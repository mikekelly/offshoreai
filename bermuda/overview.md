# Bermuda — Overview

## What Bermuda is

**Bermuda** is a **British Overseas Territory** in the **North
Atlantic**, approximately **700 miles east of North Carolina**:

- **British Overseas Territory**;
- **UK Parliament** can legislate (rarely does);
- **Bermuda Parliament** (legislature);
- **Privy Council** as final appeal court;
- **Single island group** of approximately 138 islands.

## Population and economy

- **Population**: approximately **64,000**;
- **Capital**: **Hamilton**;
- **Currency**: **Bermudian dollar (BMD)** pegged 1:1 to USD;
- **Economy**: **dominated by international business**
  (especially insurance / reinsurance) plus tourism.

## Bermuda is the global insurance hub

The **defining feature** of Bermuda:

- **Largest** captive insurance jurisdiction globally;
- **Major** reinsurance market (one of the top 3 globally
  with Lloyd's and Munich);
- **Specialty** insurance specialty;
- **ILS / catastrophe bond** centre;
- **Major** insurers head-quartered (RenaissanceRe, Arch,
  Axis, Argo, Hiscox Bermuda, others);
- **Substantial** Lloyd's-related activity.

## Why Bermuda for insurance

- **Established** market since 1960s;
- **Depth** of professional infrastructure (actuaries, brokers,
  lawyers, accountants);
- **Tax** treatment historically favourable;
- **BMA** as sophisticated regulator;
- **Solvency II** equivalence with EU;
- **NAIC qualified jurisdiction** for US purposes;
- **Time zone** alignment with US East Coast;
- **Specialist** reinsurance and ILS expertise.

## Constitutional / political

Bermuda:

- Has its own **Parliament** (House of Assembly and Senate);
- Has its own **judiciary**;
- Has its own **regulator** (BMA);
- **Limited** local revenue from international business
  (income tax not applied);
- **UK Government** retains responsibility for defence,
  external relations, some good-governance matters.

## Financial-services sector

Beyond insurance:

### Trusts

- **Established** trust regime;
- **Specific** Bermuda trust structures.

### Funds

- **Significant** fund-services industry;
- **Specific** Bermuda fund categories.

### Banking

- **Limited** local banks;
- **International** private banking.

### Companies

- **Bermuda exempted company** for international structures;
- **Specific** corporate vehicles.

## Regulator

The **Bermuda Monetary Authority (BMA)**:

- **Comprehensive** financial-services regulator;
- **Established** 1969;
- **Independent**;
- **Solvency II equivalent** for insurance regulation;
- **Sophisticated** prudential framework.

## Distinctive Bermuda features

### Pillar-Two corporate income tax

In a **historic development**:

- **Bermuda introduced a corporate income tax** from 2025;
- **15% rate** on in-scope multinationals (€750m+ revenue);
- **Aligned** with OECD GloBE rules;
- **First** Bermuda corporate income tax — a watershed.

For **out-of-scope** smaller groups, **no income tax** still
applies.

### Insurance regulation

- **BMA** is widely respected;
- **Solvency II equivalent** (recognised by EU);
- **NAIC qualified jurisdiction** for US;
- **Specific** Insurance Act provisions;
- **Multiple** insurance classes (Class 1-5, Special Purpose,
  etc.).

### ILS market

Bermuda is the **leading ILS / cat bond** centre:

- **Special Purpose Insurer (SPI)** vehicle;
- **Catastrophe bonds**;
- **Sidecars**;
- **Industry loss warranties**;
- **Specific** Bermuda regulatory framework.

## Comparison with Jersey

| Feature | Jersey | Bermuda |
|---|---|---|
| Status | Crown Dependency | British Overseas Territory |
| Population | ~107k | ~64k |
| Tax (corporate) | Zero-ten | 15% from 2025 (Pillar Two) for in-scope; **none** for out-of-scope |
| Personal tax | 20% | **None** |
| Insurance specialty | Smaller | **Global leader (captives, reinsurance, ILS)** |
| Currency | Jersey pound | BMD (par USD) |
| Trust regime | TJL 1984 | Trustee Act |
| Regulator | JFSC | BMA |
