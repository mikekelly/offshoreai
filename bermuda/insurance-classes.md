# Bermuda Insurance Classes

## What this covers

Bermuda is the world's **third-largest reinsurance
market** and the largest **catastrophe-reinsurance**
market. Its insurance regulatory regime — under the
**Insurance Act 1978** (as amended) — categorises
licensed insurers into **classes** based on the nature
and scale of business they conduct.

Understanding the classes is essential when:

- Structuring a **captive insurance** programme;
- Setting up a **reinsurance** or **special-purpose
  insurance** vehicle;
- Comparing Bermuda with **Cayman** or **Guernsey**
  captive regimes;
- Considering **ILS** structures.

## The Bermuda insurance classes

The Insurance Act 1978 establishes the following classes
(consolidated and indicative — current regulations
should be checked for definitive parameters):

### General business (P&C)

| Class | Typical use |
|---|---|
| **Class 1** | Pure single-parent captive (only writes risks of parent / group) |
| **Class 2** | Multi-owner / group captive (writes group risks plus limited unrelated risks) |
| **Class 3** | Captive writing significant unrelated business — up to specified thresholds |
| **Class 3A** | Mid-sized commercial insurer / reinsurer (specific gross-premium thresholds) |
| **Class 3B** | Large commercial insurer / reinsurer (above Class 3A thresholds) |
| **Class 4** | Largest commercial market insurers / reinsurers; minimum capital US$100m |

### Long-term business (life)

| Class | Typical use |
|---|---|
| **Class A** | Long-term captive |
| **Class B** | Long-term multi-owner / group captive |
| **Class C** | Long-term commercial insurer (smaller) |
| **Class D** | Long-term commercial insurer (mid-tier) |
| **Class E** | Long-term commercial insurer (largest) |

### Special purpose insurer (SPI)

A **Class 3B / 4 / Special Purpose Insurer (SPI)** is
used principally for **ILS structures** (cat bonds,
collateralised reinsurance, sidecars) — see
[`ils.md`](ils.md).

The SPI is the most regulated-light class for genuinely
collateralised, fully-funded structures.

### Insurance manager and ancillary licences

Beyond insurers themselves:

- **Insurance managers** — licensed under Part IIIA to
  manage captives / smaller insurers;
- **Insurance brokers** — licensed for intermediary
  activities;
- **Insurance agents** — for distribution;
- **Insurance salespeople** — registered;
- **Loss adjusters** — registered.

## Why the classes matter

Each class has different:

- **Minimum capital** requirements (rising sharply
  through the scale);
- **Solvency** requirements;
- **Reporting** intensity;
- **Audit** requirements;
- **BMA supervisory** engagement;
- **Permitted business** scope.

Sponsors pick the **lowest class consistent with their
business** to minimise regulatory overhead. Misclassifying
risks regulatory action.

## The Class 4 commercial reinsurance market

**Class 4** is the iconic Bermuda commercial reinsurance
class:

- **US$100m minimum** capital;
- **Largest insurance** companies in Bermuda;
- **Substantial substance** — Bermuda offices, staff,
  underwriting teams;
- **Major catastrophe** reinsurance writers, specialty
  reinsurers, global insurance carriers' Bermuda
  subsidiaries.

The Class 4 cluster — RenaissanceRe, Everest, Arch,
Axis, and equivalents — is the global catastrophe-
reinsurance market in concentrated form.

## Captive insurance

Bermuda is the world's leading domicile for captive
insurance. Captives:

- **Class 1**: pure single-parent — typical for a large
  corporate self-insuring own risks;
- **Class 2**: group / association captives — multi-
  parent or trade-association structures;
- **Class 3**: captives writing partly unrelated business
  (commercial third-party);
- **SAC**: captives operated through a Bermuda
  Segregated Accounts Company for multi-line / multi-
  programme structures — see
  [`segregated-accounts-company.md`](segregated-accounts-company.md).

## ILS — Class 3 / Class 3B / SPI

Bermuda dominates the ILS market through:

- **Class 3 SPI** vehicles for catastrophe bonds and
  collateralised reinsurance;
- **SAC structures** allowing repeat sponsors to issue
  multiple bond series through one licensed entity;
- **Sidecars** — special-purpose reinsurance vehicles
  used by sponsors to share risk with investors on
  specific portfolios.

See [`ils.md`](ils.md).

## Solvency regime

Bermuda's solvency regime aligns with **Solvency II
equivalence**:

- **Bermuda Solvency Capital Requirement (BSCR)** for
  commercial classes;
- **Specific** capital factors for class;
- **Recognised** by the EU as equivalent — Bermuda
  reinsurers benefit from reduced collateral
  requirements when reinsuring EU cedants.

This equivalence is a significant Bermuda competitive
advantage.

## Comparison with other captive jurisdictions

| Domicile | Classes | Strengths | Weaknesses |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Bermuda** | Class 1-4 + SPI | Largest captive market; Class 4 cat-re depth; Solvency II equivalence | Higher BMA scrutiny; substance requirements |
| **Cayman** | Class A-E (under Insurance Act 2010) | Healthcare captive specialism; lighter touch | Less reinsurance depth |
| **Guernsey** | PCC-based captive market | PCC invention; mature captive market for European parents | Smaller scale than Bermuda |
| **Vermont (US)** | US captive | US-onshore preference for some structures | US tax regime |

Bermuda is typically the choice for **largest captives**,
**catastrophe reinsurance**, and **ILS** structures;
Cayman for **healthcare** captives; Guernsey for **European
parents** wanting an EU-near structure.
