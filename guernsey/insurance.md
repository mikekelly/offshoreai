# Guernsey Insurance — Captive Insurance Centre

## Why Guernsey for insurance

Guernsey is **Europe's largest captive insurance domicile**
by number of vehicles:

- **Established** market dating to 1920s;
- **PCC invented** in Guernsey (1997) — adopted globally
  since;
- **Specialist** captive expertise;
- **GFSC** experienced regulator;
- **Cost-competitive**;
- **Established** infrastructure.

## Principal legislation

- **Insurance Business (Bailiwick of Guernsey) Law 2002** —
  domestic insurance;
- **International Insurance Business (Bailiwick of Guernsey)
  Law 2002** — captive and international;
- **Specific** subsidiary legislation.

## Categories

### Domestic insurance

- **Guernsey-resident** policyholders;
- **Stronger** consumer protection;
- **Similar** in principle to Jersey Category A.

### International / captive

- **Non-Guernsey-resident** policyholders;
- **Captive** insurers (parent / group);
- **Reinsurers**;
- **Specialty** lines (cyber, professional indemnity,
  political risk).

## PCC (Protected Cell Company)

Guernsey's **flagship** insurance vehicle:

### What a PCC is

- **Single legal entity** with **multiple cells**;
- **Cell assets** statutorily segregated from each other and
  from the core;
- **Each cell** can underwrite policies, hold assets, write
  business;
- **Failure** of one cell does not affect others.

### Applications

- **Sponsored captives** — cells let to participants;
- **Cell-based** reinsurance arrangements;
- **Structured products**;
- **Fund umbrella** vehicles.

### Significance

- **Originated in Guernsey** (1997 Companies (Guernsey)
  (Enabling Provisions) Law);
- **Adopted** in Jersey (Companies Law 1991 amendments) and
  internationally;
- **Most established** PCC market.

## ICC (Incorporated Cell Company)

A development of PCC concept:

- **Each cell** is its own body corporate;
- **Stronger** segregation than PCC;
- **More complex** governance.

## Captive types

### Pure captive

- **Wholly** owned by the parent;
- **Insures** only the parent / group risks;
- **Most common**.

### Sponsored captive

- **Sponsor** provides PCC;
- **Participants** insure through cells;
- **Cost-efficient** for medium-sized companies.

### Group captive

- **Multiple** unrelated members;
- **Insures** members' risks collectively.

### Reinsurance captive

- **Reinsures** for the parent;
- **Specific** Pillar Two and substance considerations.

## Regulation

The **GFSC** regulates Guernsey insurance:

- **Permits** for domestic and international insurers;
- **Capital** requirements proportionate to risk;
- **Substance** requirements;
- **Annual** returns and prudential reporting;
- **Specific** AML / CFT obligations.

## Comparison with Jersey

| Feature | Jersey | Guernsey |
|---|---|---|
| Statute | IBL 1996 | Insurance Business (Bailiwick of Guernsey) Law 2002 |
| Categories | Category A / B | Domestic / International |
| Captive market size | Smaller | Largest in Europe |
| PCC origin | Adopted from Guernsey | Originated 1997 |
| ICC | Yes | Yes |
| Regulator | JFSC | GFSC |

**Why Guernsey for captives**:

- **Depth** of expertise;
- **Numerical** dominance;
- **Specialist** professional infrastructure (managers,
  actuaries, lawyers);
- **Cost** competitiveness.

**Why Jersey for captives**:

- **Existing** relationships with Jersey advisers;
- **Specific** Jersey advantages in some configurations;
- **Smaller** but quality market.

## International insurance

Beyond captives, Guernsey hosts:

- **Reinsurance** vehicles;
- **Specialty** lines;
- **Cell-based** structured products;
- **Specific** Lloyd's-related vehicles.
