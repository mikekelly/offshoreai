# Bermuda Tax — Pillar Two Corporate Income Tax Introduction

## Historical position

Until **2025**, Bermuda was a **zero-tax jurisdiction**:

- **No** corporate income tax;
- **No** personal income tax;
- **No** capital gains tax;
- **No** inheritance tax;
- **No** GST / VAT.

This made Bermuda an attractive jurisdiction for international
business.

## The 2025 watershed: Corporate Income Tax

In a **historic development**, Bermuda introduced **corporate
income tax** from **1 January 2025**:

### Why introduced

- **Pillar Two** (OECD/G20 GloBE rules);
- **15% minimum** effective tax rate for **multinational
  groups** with **€750m+** revenue;
- **Bermuda** introduced its own corporate income tax to
  collect this top-up rather than ceding to other
  jurisdictions.

### Key features

- **15% corporate income tax** on **Bermudian entities** that
  are members of **in-scope multinational groups**;
- **Effective date**: 1 January 2025;
- **Specific** computation rules aligned with GloBE
  framework;
- **Limited** to in-scope groups.

### Out-of-scope groups

For **smaller groups** (€750m revenue threshold not met):

- **No** Bermuda corporate income tax;
- **Traditional** zero-tax environment continues;
- **Annual fees** and other charges only.

### Historic significance

This is **Bermuda's first** corporate income tax in modern
history. It represents:

- **Major shift** in Bermuda's tax positioning;
- **Pragmatic** response to international tax developments;
- **Acceptance** of Pillar Two framework;
- **Preservation** of Bermuda government revenue;
- **Coordination** with other offshore jurisdictions (Jersey
  MCIT, Cayman MTT, etc.).

## Other Bermuda taxes

Beyond the new corporate income tax:

### No personal income tax

- **No** personal income tax;
- **Specific** payroll taxes (employer / employee
  contributions);
- **No** capital gains tax;
- **No** inheritance / estate tax;
- **No** GST / VAT.

### Payroll tax

- **Employer** and **employee** payroll tax;
- **Specific** Bermuda employer obligations;
- **Revenue** source for Bermuda government.

### Customs duties

- **Significant** revenue source;
- **High** customs duties on imported goods.

### Land tax

- **Real property** taxes;
- **Specific** Bermuda rates.

### Other fees and duties

- **Company** annual fees;
- **Specific** transaction taxes (limited stamp duty);
- **Other** specific revenue sources.

## Economic substance

Bermuda has equivalent **economic substance** legislation:

- **Aligned** with Jersey / Guernsey / IoM / Cayman approach;
- **In-scope** activities;
- **Annual** substance return;
- **Penalties** for non-compliance.

## CRS / FATCA

- **CRS** implementation;
- **FATCA IGA** with US;
- **CbCR** for in-scope groups;
- **Aligned** transparency standards.

## Tax treaty network

Bermuda has:

- **Specific** bilateral tax treaties (limited);
- **TIEAs** with multiple jurisdictions;
- **Specific** mutual assistance arrangements;
- **Insurance-specific** arrangements (e.g., NAIC qualified
  jurisdiction for US reinsurance).

## Comparison with other offshore

| Feature | Jersey | Cayman | BVI | Bermuda |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Corporate (in-scope) | 10% (FS) | MTT 15% | MTT 15% | **15%** |
| Corporate (out-of-scope) | 0% | None | None | None |
| Personal income | 20% | None | None | None |
| GST / VAT | 5% | None | None | None |
| Substance | 2019 | 2018 | 2018 | Aligned |
| Pillar Two | MCIT | MTT | MTT | **Direct CIT** |

**Bermuda's approach** to Pillar Two — direct corporate
income tax — is **distinctive** among offshore peers (others
use specific top-up mechanisms).
