# Bermuda Exempted Company

## What it is

The **Bermuda Exempted Company** is the workhorse vehicle
of Bermuda's international business sector. Governed by
the **Companies Act 1981** (as amended), it is the form
used for:

- **(Re)insurance** companies (Bermuda's dominant
  industry);
- **Investment funds** (alongside Bermuda LPs and unit
  trusts);
- **Holding companies** at the top of multinational
  groups;
- **SPVs** for structured finance and asset securitisation;
- **Listed** holdcos on NYSE / NASDAQ / LSE (Bermuda is
  routinely accepted as a listing domicile).

It is the **default form** for Bermuda incorporation
where the company is not intended to carry on business
with the Bermuda public.

## The "exempted" label

As in Cayman and BVI, "exempted" refers to being
**exempted from the requirement to carry on business with
the public in Bermuda** — it is the international-
business form. Bermuda has **no general direct taxes**, so
"exempted" is a regulatory rather than tax classification.

A company actually carrying on local Bermuda business
would incorporate as a **Local Company**, with different
share-ownership rules (60% Bermudian ownership and
specific exemption procedures for foreign ownership).

## Formation

A Bermuda exempted company is formed by:

1. **Memorandum of Association** (the constitutional
   document, fixed at incorporation);
2. **Bye-Laws** (amended by special resolution);
3. **BMA consent** for incorporation (Bermuda Monetary
   Authority — a distinguishing feature: Bermuda
   incorporations are vetted by the regulator
   pre-formation);
4. **Beneficial ownership** disclosed to the BMA at
   incorporation;
5. **Registered office** in Bermuda;
6. **Initial directors** (at least one; minimum one
   "ordinarily resident" director or a resident
   secretary / RSP).

The **BMA pre-vetting** is the principal procedural
distinction from Cayman / BVI — Bermuda is harder to
incorporate into for the wrong sort of business, but the
filter delivers regulatory confidence to counterparties.

## Capital and shares

- **Par value** or **no-par-value** shares;
- **Multiple classes** permitted;
- **Redeemable** shares permitted;
- **Capital reduction** by court order or specified
  alternative procedures;
- **Treasury shares** permitted;
- **Share warrants / bearer shares** not permitted.

## Directors and governance

- **Minimum one director**;
- **No general residence requirement** but at least one
  director or a **resident secretary / RSP** must be
  Bermuda-based;
- **Director duties** broadly common-law fiduciary plus
  statutory codification;
- **AGMs** required unless dispensed with under the Bye-
  Laws;
- **Specific** statutory record-keeping.

## Tax

- **Bermuda**: no direct tax on corporate profits, capital
  gains, dividends, or interest (subject to the new
  Corporate Income Tax regime — see below);
- **Tax assurance**: companies may apply for a
  **government undertaking** of tax exemption to 31 March
  2035 (recently extended).

### The 2023/2025 Corporate Income Tax

In response to OECD Pillar Two, Bermuda enacted the
**Corporate Income Tax Act 2023**, introducing a **15%
corporate income tax** on Bermuda constituent entities of
in-scope multinational groups (those with consolidated
revenue ≥ EUR 750 million) **effective fiscal years
beginning on or after 1 January 2025**.

The tax operates as a **QDMTT-equivalent** — keeping the
top-up tax revenue in Bermuda rather than ceding it to
other jurisdictions under the IIR.

This dramatically reshaped Bermuda's tax narrative for
large multinationals. For groups **below** the EUR 750m
threshold, the historic no-direct-tax position continues.

## Use cases

### Insurance and reinsurance

Bermuda's primary sector. Bermuda is the world's third-
largest reinsurance market, focused on:

- **Catastrophe** reinsurance;
- **Special-purpose insurers** for ILS / cat bonds (see
  [`ils.md`](ils.md));
- **Captive insurance** for corporate self-insurance
  structures;
- **Specialty lines** (D&O, environmental, etc.).

The Bermuda exempted company is the principal vehicle
form for all these.

### Investment funds

Bermuda funds use:

- **Exempted company** for many open-ended structures;
- **Exempted Limited Partnership** for closed-ended
  PE/VC;
- **Unit trust** for specific use cases;
- **Segregated Accounts Company (SAC)** for multi-
  strategy or multi-cell funds.

Bermuda funds are regulated under the **Investment Funds
Act 2006** — see [`funds.md`](funds.md).

### Holding companies and listings

Bermuda exempted companies are routinely listed on:

- **NYSE / NASDAQ** (US listings — common for
  insurance and reinsurance groups);
- **London Stock Exchange**;
- **Toronto Stock Exchange**;
- **Bermuda Stock Exchange** itself.

The combination of regulatory rigour and tax efficiency
(historic) made Bermuda a preferred listing domicile for
many specialty businesses.

## Regulatory perimeter

A Bermuda exempted company is **not regulated** by virtue
of form alone. Regulation attaches to specific activities:

- **(Re)insurance** — Insurance Act 1978, regulated by
  BMA;
- **Investment funds** — Investment Funds Act 2006;
- **Investment business** — Investment Business Act 2003;
- **Banking** — Banks and Deposit Companies Act 1999;
- **Trust business** — Trusts (Regulation of Trust
  Business) Act 2001.

See [`bma.md`](bma.md) for the regulator.

## Substance overlay

Bermuda enacted the **Economic Substance Act 2018**
broadly aligned with the Cayman and BVI substance regimes:

- **Relevant activities** must demonstrate substantive
  presence in Bermuda;
- **CIGA** conducted in Bermuda;
- **Adequate** employees / expenditure / physical
  presence;
- **Annual** filings.

Insurance and reinsurance activities are core Bermuda
substance areas — the operating businesses typically have
deep substance already.

## Comparison

| Feature | Bermuda Exempted | Cayman Exempted | BVI BC |
|---|---|---|---|
| Statute | Companies Act 1981 | Companies Act | BCA 2004 |
| Pre-formation regulatory consent | BMA required | None | None |
| Resident director / secretary | One required | Not required | Not required |
| Pillar Two implementation | CIT 2023 (in force 2025) | MCIT (similar) | No equivalent yet |
| Reinsurance focus | Dominant | Limited | Limited |
| Fund domicile | Used (smaller than Cayman) | Dominant | Used |
