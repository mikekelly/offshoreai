# Bermuda ILS — Insurance-Linked Securities

## What ILS is

**Insurance-Linked Securities (ILS)** are **financial
instruments** whose value derives from **insurance risk**:

- **Catastrophe bonds (cat bonds)**;
- **Industry loss warranties**;
- **Sidecars** (reinsurance vehicles);
- **Specific** other ILS products.

## Why Bermuda dominates ILS

Bermuda is the **leading ILS jurisdiction** globally:

- **Established** since early 2000s;
- **Specialist** professional infrastructure;
- **Regulatory** framework optimised;
- **Time zone** alignment with capital markets;
- **Major** ILS investors based in / connected to Bermuda;
- **BMA expertise** in ILS supervision.

## Cat bonds

A **catastrophe bond**:

- **Insurer / reinsurer** transfers catastrophe risk to
  capital-markets investors;
- **Special Purpose Insurer (SPI)** in Bermuda issues notes;
- **Investors** receive principal + interest **if no
  triggering event**;
- **If triggering event** occurs (specified loss exceeds
  threshold), **investors lose principal** (in whole or
  part);
- **Insurer** receives indemnification for catastrophe loss.

## Special Purpose Insurer (SPI)

The **Bermuda SPI** is the **flagship ILS vehicle**:

- **Specifically designed** for ILS transactions;
- **Fully collateralised** typically;
- **Simplified** regulatory regime;
- **Faster** to establish than full insurance company;
- **Bankruptcy-remote** from sponsor.

### SPI features

- **Insurance Act 1978** Class — specific category;
- **BMA supervision** with appropriate proportionality;
- **Limited recourse** — investors' rights limited to SPI
  assets;
- **Trustee** holds collateral typically;
- **Specific** Bermuda regulations.

## ILS structures

### Standard cat bond

1. **Sponsor** (insurer / reinsurer) wants to transfer cat
   risk to capital markets;
2. **SPI** established in Bermuda;
3. **SPI** issues **catastrophe bonds** to investors;
4. **SPI** writes reinsurance contract to sponsor;
5. **Bond proceeds** held as collateral (typically in trust);
6. **No catastrophe** → investors paid principal + coupon;
7. **Catastrophe occurs** → collateral pays sponsor for
   reinsurance recovery; investors lose principal.

### Sidecar

- **Investors** participate in a **specific layer** of an
  insurer's underwriting;
- **Different** from standalone cat bond;
- **Specific** structure variations.

### ILW (Industry Loss Warranty)

- **Triggered** by industry-wide loss exceeding threshold;
- **Different** from indemnity-based cat bond.

## Triggers

ILS payouts can be triggered by:

### Indemnity trigger

- **Actual** loss to the specific sponsor;
- **Provides** real indemnification;
- **Subject to** detailed loss reporting.

### Industry-loss trigger

- **Industry-wide** loss exceeding specified level;
- **Less basis-risk** for investors;
- **Specific** index used.

### Parametric trigger

- **Specific** event characteristics (e.g., earthquake of
  certain magnitude in specific location);
- **Objective** trigger;
- **Faster** payout if triggered;
- **Basis-risk** for sponsor.

### Modelled-loss trigger

- **Catastrophe modelling** firm calculates loss;
- **Industry-specific** modelling.

## Investor base

ILS investors:

- **Specialist** ILS funds;
- **Pension** funds (some);
- **Hedge** funds;
- **Endowments**;
- **Reinsurers** themselves (selective);
- **Specialty** capital providers.

## Comparison with traditional reinsurance

| Feature | Traditional reinsurance | ILS |
|---|---|---|
| Provider | Reinsurance company | Capital markets via SPV |
| Capacity | Limited by reinsurer capital | Limited by investor appetite |
| Pricing | Traditional cycle | Capital-market cycle |
| Settlement | Insurance settlement | Bond mechanics |
| Counterparty risk | Reinsurer | Collateralised |

## Solvency II / NAIC treatment

ILS investments by insurers / reinsurers:

- **Solvency II** treatment specific;
- **NAIC** treatment specific for US;
- **Specific** Bermuda regulatory recognition;
- **Specific** ratings and capital relief.
