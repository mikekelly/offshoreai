# Bermuda vs Jersey — Comparison

## Constitutional / political

| Feature | Jersey | Bermuda |
|---|---|---|
| Status | Crown Dependency | British Overseas Territory |
| Population | ~107k | ~64k |
| Legislature | States Assembly | Parliament (House of Assembly and Senate) |
| Top court | Royal Court | Supreme Court of Bermuda |
| Privy Council | Yes | Yes |
| Currency | Jersey pound | BMD (par USD) |
| Time zone | UTC/UTC+1 | UTC-4 |

## Tax

| Feature | Jersey | Bermuda |
|---|---|---|
| Corporate income | Zero-ten | **15% from 2025** for €750m+ groups; **none** otherwise |
| Personal income | 20% | **None** |
| GST / VAT | 5% GST | **None** |
| CGT | None | None |
| IHT | None | None |
| Pillar Two | MCIT | **Corporate income tax 15%** introduced 2025 |

The **Bermuda 2025 corporate income tax** is a **historic
development** — Bermuda was traditionally a zero-tax
jurisdiction and **introduced** corporate income tax
specifically to satisfy Pillar Two.

For **out-of-scope** smaller groups, Bermuda remains a
**zero-tax** environment.

## Insurance — the principal difference

| Feature | Jersey | Bermuda |
|---|---|---|
| Captive market size | Smaller | **Largest globally** |
| Reinsurance market | Limited | **Top 3 global** |
| Specialty | Various | **Specialty / cat / property catastrophe** |
| ILS / cat bonds | Limited | **Leading centre** |
| Solvency II equivalence | Selective | **Full equivalence with EU** |
| NAIC qualified | Not | **Qualified jurisdiction for US** |
| Class structure | A / B | Class 1-5, Special Purpose, etc. |
| BMA expertise | Smaller | **Global authority** |

Bermuda is **the** insurance centre offshore. Jersey is a
**smaller, capable** insurance jurisdiction.

## Companies

| Feature | Jersey CJL 1991 | Bermuda Companies Act |
|---|---|---|
| Statute | CJL 1991 | Companies Act 1981 (amended) |
| Exempted company | No specific category | **Yes** — main international vehicle |
| Cell companies | PCC and ICC | Segregated Account Companies |
| LLC | LLP only | **LLC** under specific Act |
| Migration | Continuance | Continuance |

**Bermuda exempted companies** are the standard vehicle for
international insurance / financial-services.

## Trusts

| Feature | Jersey TJL 1984 | Bermuda Trustee Act |
|---|---|---|
| Statute | TJL 1984 | Trustee Act 1975 + amendments |
| Reserved powers | Article 9A | Specific provisions |
| Firewall | Article 9 | **Trusts (Special Provisions) Act 1989** |
| Purpose trusts | Article 12 | Specific Act |
| Duration | Unlimited | Subject to limits |

Both jurisdictions have **good** trust regimes. **Jersey
TJL** more developed in case law for some specific issues.
**Bermuda** trust regime well-established and used.

## Funds

| Feature | Jersey | Bermuda |
|---|---|---|
| Public funds | CIFL 1988 | Investment Funds Act 2006 |
| Expert funds | Expert Fund | Class A Exempt Fund |
| Private funds | JPF | Closed-Ended Funds |
| AIFMD | NPPR | NPPR |

Bermuda has **modest** fund-services industry compared with
Cayman and Jersey but offers competitive options especially
for **insurance-linked funds**.

## Banking

| Feature | Jersey | Bermuda |
|---|---|---|
| Sector size | Larger | Smaller |
| Retail banking | Yes | Limited (limited local banks) |
| International private | Yes | Yes |

Bermuda banking is **smaller** than Jersey banking.

## Legal heritage

| Feature | Jersey | Bermuda |
|---|---|---|
| Heritage | Norman customary | Common law |
| Customary law role | Significant | Minimal |
| English law | Persuasive | More directly applicable |

Bermuda law is **substantially common law** with Bermuda
statutory variations — similar to Cayman / BVI in this
respect.

## When to choose Bermuda

Strong factors favouring Bermuda:

- **Insurance** matters (overwhelming preference);
- **Reinsurance** entities;
- **ILS** structures;
- **NAIC qualified jurisdiction** for US-related insurance;
- **Solvency II equivalent** for EU-related insurance;
- **Specialty** insurance markets;
- **Captive** at significant scale.

## When Jersey is the natural choice

- **Trust matters** (especially with Article 9A);
- **Crown Dependency** status preferred;
- **GBP** denomination;
- **EU / UK** proximity;
- **Banking** matters;
- **Time zone** alignment with European business.

## Common combinations

For insurance groups:

- **Bermuda master** insurance / reinsurance;
- **Jersey holding** trust for ultimate beneficial ownership;
- **Other offshore** entities for specific functions.
