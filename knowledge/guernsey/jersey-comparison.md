# Guernsey vs Jersey — Comprehensive Comparison

## Why compare

For practitioners advising international clients on
**Channel Islands jurisdiction choice**, key differences
between Jersey and Guernsey matter:

- **Tax** treatment (broadly similar, some specifics differ);
- **Regulatory** features (broadly similar, some sectoral
  differences);
- **Substantive** law differences (trust, company, customary
  law);
- **Cost** and **service quality** considerations.

Most international groups have offices / counsel in **both**
jurisdictions.

## Constitutional / political

| Feature | Jersey | Guernsey |
|---|---|---|
| Status | Crown Dependency | Crown Dependency |
| Legislature | States Assembly | States of Deliberation |
| Head of judiciary | Bailiff of Jersey | Bailiff of Guernsey |
| Crown representative | Lieutenant Governor | Lieutenant Governor (separate) |
| UK government dept | Ministry of Justice | Ministry of Justice |
| Privy Council appeals | Yes | Yes |
| Currency | Jersey pound (par GBP) | Guernsey pound (par GBP) |
| Population | ~107,000 | ~63,000 |
| Land area | 119 km² | 78 km² |

## Tax

| Feature | Jersey | Guernsey |
|---|---|---|
| Corporate tax (general) | 0% (zero-ten) | 0% (zero-ten) |
| Corporate tax (financial services) | 10% | 10% |
| Corporate tax (specific categories) | 20% (utilities, large retail) | 20% (specific categories) |
| Personal tax (general) | 20% | 20% |
| GST / VAT | 5% GST | No VAT; Goods and Services Tax not implemented |
| CGT | None | None |
| IHT | None | None |
| Substance Law | 2019 | 2019 |
| Pillar Two MCIT | From 2025 | From 2025 (equivalent) |

The **GST difference** is notable: Jersey has a **5% GST**
on most goods and services; Guernsey has **no equivalent**.

## Companies law

| Feature | Jersey CJL 1991 | Guernsey CGL 2008 |
|---|---|---|
| Statute | Companies (Jersey) Law 1991 | Companies (Guernsey) Law 2008 |
| Share types | Par-value and no-par-value | Par-value and no-par-value |
| Cell companies | PCC and ICC | PCC (Guernsey originated) and ICC |
| Migration | Continuance in / out | Continuance in / out |
| Director duties | Article 74 framework | Specific sections |
| Capital maintenance | Solvency-based | Solvency-based |

Both regimes are **broadly similar** but **structurally
different** — Jersey practitioners should not assume
identical provisions.

## Trust law

| Feature | Jersey TJL 1984 | Guernsey TGL 2007 |
|---|---|---|
| Statute | Trusts (Jersey) Law 1984 | Trusts (Guernsey) Law 2007 |
| Reserved powers | Article 9A | Different provisions, similar concept |
| Firewall | Article 9 | Different provisions, similar concept |
| Article 47 mistake | TJL Article 47 (wide) | TGL provisions (slightly different) |
| Schmidt v Rosewood | Applied | Applied |
| Purpose trusts | Article 12 | Specific TGL provisions |
| Statutory cy-près | Article 47A | TGL provisions |

Both regimes are **broadly aligned** but specific provisions
differ. **Cross-jurisdictional planning** typically uses
one or the other consistently.

## Funds

| Feature | Jersey | Guernsey |
|---|---|---|
| Public funds | CIFL 1988 | The Authorised Collective Investment Schemes Rules |
| Expert funds | Expert Fund Guide | Class A, Class B, Class Q schemes |
| Private funds | Jersey Private Fund | Private Investment Funds (PIF) |
| Listed funds | Listed Fund Guide | Closed-ended Investment Schemes |
| Unregulated funds | Unregulated Funds | Unregulated funds available |
| AIFMD passporting | NPPR | NPPR |

Both jurisdictions have **competitive fund regimes**;
specific structural differences make one or the other
better suited to specific fund types.

## Insurance

| Feature | Jersey | Guernsey |
|---|---|---|
| Domestic insurance | Category A IBL 1996 | Equivalent regime |
| Captive insurance | Category B IBL 1996 | International Insurance Business Law 2002 |
| PCC | Yes (Companies Law origin) | Yes (Guernsey originated, 1997) |
| ICC | Yes | Yes |
| Market size | Smaller captive market | Largest European captive market |
| Solvency II equivalence | Selective | Selective |

**Guernsey** is the **larger captive insurance** jurisdiction
in Europe; Jersey has a smaller but significant market.

## Banking

| Feature | Jersey | Guernsey |
|---|---|---|
| Banking law | BBL 1991 | Banking Supervision (Bailiwick of Guernsey) Law 2020 |
| Top-tier policy | "Top 50" policy historically | Similar policy |
| Number of licensed banks | Larger sector | Smaller sector |
| Customer base | International deposits | International deposits |
| Regulator | JFSC | GFSC |

**Jersey banking sector** is larger by assets and number of
licensed banks.

## Financial-services regulation

| Feature | Jersey | Guernsey |
|---|---|---|
| Regulator | JFSC | GFSC |
| Authorising statute | Financial Services Commission (Jersey) Law 1998 | Financial Services Commission (Bailiwick of Guernsey) Law 1987 |
| Umbrella law | Financial Services (Jersey) Law 1998 | Regulation of Fiduciaries, Administration Businesses and Company Directors etc. (Bailiwick of Guernsey) Law 2000, plus specific sectoral laws |
| TCB regulation | FSL Article 9 | RFCB Law specifically |
| FSB regulation | FSL Article 9 | Protection of Investors (Bailiwick of Guernsey) Law 1987 |
| Investment business | FSL Article 9 | Protection of Investors (Bailiwick of Guernsey) Law 1987 |

The **structural** approach differs:

- **Jersey**: single umbrella FSL 1998 covering most
  regulated classes;
- **Guernsey**: separate statutes per regulated sector.

Substantively, the regimes are aligned in practice.

## AML / CFT

Both jurisdictions are:

- **FATF-aligned**;
- **Moneyval-evaluated** (both recently with positive results);
- **FATF-compliant** with Recommendation 24 on BO;
- **Specific** statutes (Jersey POCL 1999; Guernsey
  equivalent);
- **Specific** Handbooks issued by regulators;
- **Specific** Schedule 2 / equivalent regulated businesses.

## Customary law

| Feature | Jersey | Guernsey |
|---|---|---|
| Norman roots | Yes | Yes |
| Principal customary jurist | Le Geyt | Le Marchant |
| Customary code | Le Geyt's "Code" | Le Marchant's "Remarques et animadversions" |
| Modern application | Real property, succession, contract | Similar areas |
| Pothier influence | Yes | Yes |

## When to choose which

**Choose Jersey** for:

- **Larger banking** requirements;
- **Trust** matters where Article 9A reserved powers
  specifically attractive;
- **Specific** Jersey commercial trust law features;
- **GST-relevant** consumer-facing business (rare for
  international clients).

**Choose Guernsey** for:

- **Captive insurance** (especially substantial captive
  vehicles);
- **PCC for insurance** (Guernsey origin and depth);
- **Some specific** fund regimes;
- **Cost** considerations in some sectors.

In **practice**:

- Many international groups use **both** as parallel
  jurisdictions;
- **Specific** matters split between based on optimal fit;
- **Cooperation** between Jersey and Guernsey regulators
  facilitates cross-jurisdictional structures.
