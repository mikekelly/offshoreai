# Guernsey Image Rights Register — A Unique Offering

## What it is

Guernsey is the **only jurisdiction in the world** with
a **statutory image-rights register** for individuals,
introduced by the **Image Rights (Bailiwick of Guernsey)
Ordinance 2012**. Personalities, celebrities, and
families can register specific image attributes
("personnage") as **registered property rights**, making
them:

- **Transferable** (assigned, licensed);
- **Inheritable** (passed on death);
- **Enforceable** against unauthorised use;
- **Mortgageable** (collateralised in some cases).

The regime turns what most jurisdictions treat as a
patchwork of common-law tort claims, defamation, passing
off, and rights of publicity into a **registrable
property right** with statutory backing.

## Why it exists

Most legal systems give individuals **limited and
fragmented** protection over commercial use of their
image / persona:

- **UK**: passing off, defamation, breach of confidence
  — limited to specific facts;
- **USA**: state-by-state "right of publicity" — not
  uniform;
- **France**: right to image / personality, statutory
  but tort-based;
- **EU**: GDPR considerations for personal data, but
  not a property right per se.

For a **celebrity, athlete, or family with significant
commercial value attached to image / persona**, this
patchwork is unsatisfactory. The Guernsey regime gives a
**single, registered, enforceable property right** that
can be:

- **Used as the basis** for licensing agreements;
- **Held in a corporate or trust structure** for
  succession / estate planning;
- **Mortgaged** to raise finance;
- **Enforced** against infringers via Guernsey courts
  with the comfort of a registered title.

## What can be registered

A "personnage" can be registered, covering:

- **Name** (real, professional, stage);
- **Voice**;
- **Signature**;
- **Image** (photographs, likeness);
- **Appearance** characteristics (distinctive features);
- **Mannerisms**;
- **Other** personality-identifying attributes.

Both **natural persons** and **fictional / non-real
characters** can be associated with a personnage —
opening uses for animated characters, brand mascots, etc.

## Registration

Registration is:

- **Filed** with the Guernsey Intellectual Property
  Office;
- **Subject to** examination and publication;
- **Granted** after opposition period;
- **Renewable** every 10 years (life of person + 100
  years for some categories);
- **Public** on the register;
- **Modest** fees compared to commercial trademark
  filings.

## Who uses it

The register has been used by:

- **Sports personalities** with significant brand value;
- **Entertainment celebrities** wanting structured
  succession of image rights;
- **Estates** of deceased personalities (where image
  rights survive death);
- **Brand owners** managing celebrity-licensed product
  portfolios;
- **Family offices** wrapping image-rights assets in
  trust or corporate structures alongside other family
  IP.

## Enforcement

A registered personnage gives the holder:

- **Standing** in Guernsey courts to enforce against
  infringement;
- **Statutory remedies** — injunction, damages, account
  of profits, delivery up of infringing material;
- **Specific** Guernsey court familiarity with the
  Ordinance.

For infringers operating **outside Guernsey**, the
holder typically uses:

- **Local-jurisdiction** enforcement (trade-marks,
  passing off, right-of-publicity claims);
- **The Guernsey registration** as evidence of
  proprietary interest in support of foreign litigation;
- **Licensing** agreements requiring Guernsey-law
  governing law and forum.

## Tax and structuring

A Guernsey image-rights registration can be combined
with:

- **Trust structure** — image rights settled into a
  Guernsey trust;
- **Company holding** — image rights owned by a
  Guernsey or other holding company that licenses use;
- **Royalty flow** — licensing fees paid to the
  Guernsey holding company / trust;
- **Estate planning** — succession of image rights
  outside the personality's home-jurisdiction
  succession regime (subject to applicable conflict-of-
  laws rules).

The combination of **Guernsey image-rights registration
+ Guernsey trust structure + favourable tax** delivers
a sophisticated wealth-structuring outcome for high-
profile individuals.

## Practical limitations

- **Foreign-court recognition** is the main practical
  question — Guernsey's regime is sui generis and
  foreign courts may not give it direct effect;
- **Best used** in combination with local-jurisdiction
  IP / publicity-rights enforcement;
- **Most valuable** as the asset base for licensing
  arrangements rather than as a standalone enforcement
  tool;
- **Public registration** may not suit individuals
  wanting privacy (though specifics of the registration
  can be calibrated).

## Comparison

No other jurisdiction has a directly equivalent regime.
**Jersey** does not have an image-rights statute.
**Cayman** does not. **BVI** does not.

The closest analogues elsewhere:

- **US state-by-state rights of publicity** (especially
  California and New York) — strong rights but no
  unified register;
- **UK** common-law / equitable protections — narrower;
- **Some EU member states** with codified personality
  rights — tort-based, not registrable property.

For sophisticated celebrity-wealth structuring, the
Guernsey regime offers a unique tool. It is one of the
small number of genuinely jurisdiction-distinctive
offerings (alongside the PCC invention and the PIF
fast-track) that explains why Guernsey remains relevant
in a competitive offshore landscape.
