# Guernsey Tax — Overview

## Principal statute

The **Income Tax (Guernsey) Law 1975** is Guernsey's
principal tax statute.

## Zero-ten regime

Like Jersey, Guernsey operates a **zero-ten** corporate tax
regime:

- **0%** — most companies (the "zero" rate);
- **10%** — specified financial-services companies;
- **20%** — utilities and specific other categories.

The regime is **broadly aligned** with Jersey's, established
in similar response to EU Code of Conduct on business
taxation in the 2000s.

## Personal tax

- **20% standard rate** on personal income;
- **Specific** allowances and deductions;
- **HVR-equivalent** regime for high-net-worth migrants;
- **Similar** to Jersey personal tax.

## No general taxes

Like Jersey:

- **No** capital gains tax;
- **No** inheritance tax;
- **No** wealth tax;
- **No** gift tax (in normal sense).

## Key difference from Jersey: GST

The **principal** Jersey / Guernsey tax difference:

- **Jersey** has a **5% GST**;
- **Guernsey** has **no GST / VAT** equivalent;
- This is a **structural** Guernsey advantage for goods /
  services purchasers (though specific items might be cheaper
  in Jersey through other mechanisms).

## Substance Law

Guernsey has equivalent **economic substance** legislation
(2019):

- **Aligned** with Jersey Substance Law 2019;
- **Same** activity categories;
- **Same** substance requirements;
- **Annual** substance return.

## Pillar Two MCIT

Guernsey introduced **Multinational Top-up Tax** (MTT)
equivalent to Jersey's MCIT from 2025:

- **Same** threshold (€750m+ revenue);
- **Same** 15% minimum effective rate;
- **Aligned** with OECD GloBE rules;
- **Coordinated** with Jersey approach.

## Tax treaties

Guernsey has:

- **Specific** bilateral tax treaties;
- **Joint Statement / DTA** with UK (1955, since amended);
- **TIEAs** with multiple jurisdictions;
- **MLI** signatory.

## CRS / FATCA

Like Jersey:

- **CRS** implementation;
- **FATCA IGA** with US;
- **CbCR** for in-scope groups;
- **Aligned** transparency standards.

## Compliance

Tax compliance through:

- **Comptroller of Income Tax** — Guernsey equivalent of
  Jersey Comptroller of Revenue;
- **Annual returns** for companies and individuals;
- **Specific** tax forms and procedures;
- **Penalties** for non-compliance.
