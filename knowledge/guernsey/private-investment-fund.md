# Guernsey Private Investment Fund (PIF)

## What it is

The **Guernsey Private Investment Fund (PIF)** is
Guernsey's **fast-track private-fund regime**, designed
to compete with Jersey's JPF and Cayman's various
private-fund routes. It launched in 2016 and has been
refined since (most recently substantive Rules in 2021).

A PIF is:

- **A Guernsey fund** with **limited investor numbers**;
- **Registered** with the GFSC under a streamlined
  process;
- **Subject to** light-touch ongoing regulation;
- **Approvable** within **1 business day** in many cases.

## Three PIF routes (Route 1, 2, 3)

The PIF regime has **three distinct routes**, each with
different eligibility / governance requirements:

### Route 1 — Licensed manager

The PIF appoints a **licensed Guernsey manager** who
takes regulatory responsibility:

- **Manager** is GFSC-licensed (Investment Manager
  authorisation);
- **Manager** carries out due diligence on the fund's
  governance, structure, valuation, custody, etc.;
- **GFSC** authorises the PIF based on the licensed
  manager's role;
- **Faster** authorisation (1 business day typically);
- **Most common** route.

### Route 2 — Family / private relationship

The PIF is restricted to **family or private-relationship
investors** (a defined narrow class):

- **Up to 50 investors**;
- All must have a **clear family or private relationship**
  with each other and with the sponsor;
- **No licensed manager** required;
- **Lighter** ongoing regulation appropriate to the
  closely-held nature;
- **Used** for family-office investment pools and
  related-party structures.

### Route 3 — Qualifying private investors

The PIF is restricted to **qualifying private investors**:

- **Up to 50 investors**;
- Each must be a **professional** or **sophisticated**
  investor by defined criteria;
- **Detailed PPM** with specific disclosures;
- **No licensed manager** required (but typical);
- **More flexibility** in structuring than Routes 1 and
  2.

## Common features across routes

All PIFs:

- **Cap of 50 investors** (whichever route);
- **Closed-ended or open-ended** permitted (most are
  closed-ended PE/VC structures);
- **Audited** financial statements;
- **GFSC** ongoing supervision;
- **AML/CFT** compliance;
- **Specific** valuation, custody, and reporting
  policies.

## Vehicle forms

A PIF can use any standard Guernsey corporate / fund
form:

- **Guernsey company** (limited / cell company);
- **Guernsey limited partnership** (under LP Law);
- **Guernsey unit trust**;
- **PCC / ICC** for multi-strategy / multi-cell
  structures.

The PCC / ICC choice is particularly Guernsey-strong —
see [`protected-cell-company.md`](protected-cell-company.md)
and [`incorporated-cell-company.md`](incorporated-cell-company.md).

## Comparison with Jersey JPF

| Feature | Guernsey PIF | Jersey JPF |
|---|---|---|
| Max investors | 50 | 50 |
| Authorisation time | 1 business day (Route 1) | 48 hours |
| Routes | 3 (licensed mgr / family / qualifying) | 1 (DSP delegation) |
| Licensed manager required | Route 1 only | Not required (DSP model) |
| DSP-equivalent | No direct equivalent | Yes (the core JPF feature) |
| Audited statements | Required | Required |
| AML/CFT obligations | Yes | Yes |
| Vehicle forms | Company / LP / Trust / PCC / ICC | Company / LP / Trust |

The PIF and JPF are **competing offerings**, each tuned
to slightly different sponsor preferences:

- **PIF** offers more route flexibility (especially
  Routes 2 and 3 for family / qualifying-investor
  structures);
- **JPF** offers the DSP delegation model that some
  sponsors prefer for regulatory predictability;
- **Both** deliver fast launch with light-touch ongoing
  regulation.

## Use cases

### Mid-market PE / VC

For sponsors with ≤50 LPs and a closed-ended structure,
the PIF (typically Route 1 or Route 3) is competitive
with Jersey JPF, Cayman ELP, and BVI Private Fund.

### Family-office structures

Route 2 PIFs are particularly attractive for family-
office or related-party investor pools where the
investor base is genuinely a "family / private
relationship" group.

### Multi-strategy platforms

PIF structures using PCCs allow sponsors to run
multiple strategies under one PIF vehicle, with statutory
cell segregation.

## Migration and conversion

A PIF can be **converted** to a different Guernsey fund
class (Authorised, Registered, QIF) if it outgrows the
PIF parameters or if the sponsor wants broader marketing
flexibility. The conversion process is procedural rather
than transformative.

## Tax

- **Guernsey**: 0% standard corporate income tax
  (specific exceptions for finance, regulated business,
  property income);
- **Investors**: taxed in home jurisdiction depending on
  vehicle form and investor classification;
- **Economic substance**: investment-fund-business
  carve-out applies in most cases.

## Pitfalls

- **Wrong route choice** — Route 2 requires genuine
  family / private relationship; misuse risks
  regulatory action;
- **Investor count** breach above 50;
- **Manager licensing** — Route 1 PIFs depend on the
  licensed manager; if the manager loses licensing,
  the PIF status is at risk;
- **AML/CFT** — substantive compliance, not formal;
- **Substance** for the manager / GP entity.
