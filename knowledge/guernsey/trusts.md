# Guernsey Trusts — Overview

## Principal statute

The **Trusts (Guernsey) Law 2007 (TGL)** is the modern
Guernsey trust statute, replacing earlier 1989 legislation.

The TGL is **broadly similar to** Jersey's TJL 1984 but with
specific Guernsey features.

## Key features

### Validity and recognition

- **Trusts created** under Guernsey law are recognised;
- **Foreign trusts** also recognised;
- **Specific** Guernsey conflict-of-laws rules;
- **Similar** in principle to Jersey.

### Settlor reserved powers

- **Wide** reserved powers permitted;
- **Similar** to Jersey TJL Article 9A;
- **Specific** Guernsey wording;
- **Includes**: revocation, distribution direction, appointment
  of trustees / protectors, investment management,
  amendment.

### Firewall

- **Guernsey firewall** protecting trusts from foreign
  forced-heirship / matrimonial claims;
- **Similar** to Jersey TJL Article 9;
- **Specific** Guernsey provisions;
- **Foreign judgments** inconsistent with Guernsey law not
  enforced.

### Trustee duties

- **Specific** TGL provisions on duties;
- **Similar** to Jersey TJL Article 21 in substance;
- **Specific** Guernsey case law developing duty
  jurisprudence.

### Information rights

- **Beneficiary** information rights;
- **Similar to Schmidt v Rosewood** approach;
- **Specific** Guernsey provisions;
- **Specific** case law.

### Article 47-equivalent mistake / hastiness

- **Specific** Guernsey provisions on setting aside trustee
  decisions for mistake / failure to consider relevant
  factors;
- **Slightly different** from Jersey TJL Articles 47B-J;
- **Specific** Guernsey case law.

### Purpose trusts

- **Specific** Guernsey provisions;
- **Similar** to Jersey Article 12 in principle;
- **Specific** enforcer requirement.

### Duration

- **Unlimited** duration of trusts permitted (no rule
  against perpetuities);
- **Similar** to Jersey post-2006 position.

### Royal Court jurisdiction

- **Royal Court of Guernsey** has jurisdiction equivalent to
  Jersey Royal Court;
- **Section 69** of TGL (equivalent to Jersey Article 51) —
  directions applications;
- **Beddoe**, **blessing**, **directions** applications.

## Differences from Jersey

While **broadly similar**, specific differences:

### Specific statutory wording

- **TGL** is later (2007 vs 1984) and structurally
  different;
- **Drafting differences** can produce different outcomes in
  edge cases;
- **Cross-jurisdictional** planning typically uses one or the
  other consistently.

### Article 47 mistake

- **Jersey** Article 47 regime introduced by 2013
  amendment;
- **Guernsey** has had similar provisions since the TGL
  2007;
- **Specific** procedural and substantive differences.

### Case law

- **Separate** body of Royal Court of Guernsey case law;
- **Privy Council** appeals in both;
- **Specific** Guernsey-developed doctrines.

### Customary-law context

- **Guernsey** customary law similar to Jersey but with
  specific differences;
- **Le Marchant** Guernsey customary jurist;
- **Different** specific customary rules.

## Specific trust types

### Discretionary trust

- **Standard** Guernsey trust;
- **Specific** Guernsey rules on letters of wishes;
- **Specific** investment-management considerations.

### Charitable trust

- **Guernsey Charities Law 2008** applicable;
- **GFSC** specific charity registration;
- **Different** from Jersey CL 2014.

### Purpose trust

- **Non-charitable purpose** trusts under TGL;
- **Specific** enforcer requirements;
- **Specific** Guernsey rules.

### Foundation alternative

- **Guernsey Foundations Law 2012** introduced foundations
  as alternative to trusts;
- **Similar** to Jersey Foundations Law 2009;
- **Specific** Guernsey provisions.

## Choosing Jersey vs Guernsey trust

Both jurisdictions are **major trust centres**. Choice
depends on:

- **Existing relationships** with service providers;
- **Specific** statutory provisions favouring one
  configuration;
- **Cost** considerations;
- **Specific** legal advice for the particular structure.

For **most ordinary trusts**, the choice is largely
**indifferent** — both produce similar practical outcomes.

For **specific structures** (specific firewall scenarios,
specific information-rights configurations, etc.), specific
legal advice may favour one or the other.
