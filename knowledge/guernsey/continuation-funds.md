# Continuation Funds and GP-Led Secondaries — Guernsey Treatment

## What this is

Guernsey is the **leading European jurisdiction for
continuation funds**. The reasons are specific and
durable: regulatory speed (3-business-day single-asset
approval), favourable not-a-collective-investment-scheme
treatment for single-asset structures, mature
practitioner base, and explicit strategic positioning by
the GFSC and Guernsey Finance.

For the wider market context, current adoption
statistics, and strategic narrative, see the frontier
file at [`../frontier/continuation-funds-and-gp-led-secondaries.md`](../frontier/continuation-funds-and-gp-led-secondaries.md).
This file covers the **Guernsey-law treatment**: the
regulatory categorisation, the GFSC fast-track, the
substantive issues, and why Guernsey has emerged as the
European market leader.

## Why Guernsey leads

### Single-asset CF not-a-CIS treatment

The structurally most important feature. A **single-asset
continuation fund** in Guernsey is **not treated as a
collective investment scheme** for the purposes of the
**Protection of Investors (Bailiwick of Guernsey) Law
2020** (and the predecessor 1987 POI Law).

The reasoning: a CIS requires the **pooling of investor
contributions for collective management**. A
single-asset CF is structurally **a single co-investment
vehicle** in one specific asset — closer to a joint
venture than a managed fund. The regulator's treatment
follows from this characterisation.

The practical effect: a single-asset Guernsey CF can be
established **without** authorisation under the POI Law as
a collective investment scheme. The
[Private Investment Fund (PIF)](./private-investment-fund.md)
regime or other appropriate vehicle is used.

**Multi-asset continuation funds** are typically treated
as collective investment schemes and use the standard
authorised-fund regimes (closed-ended Authorised Collective
Investment Schemes, Class B Schemes, or the PIF Class 3).

### GFSC 3-business-day approval

For single-asset CFs that require GFSC review, the
**3-business-day approval timeline** is the fastest in
the European market. Combined with Guernsey's mature
admin / fund-counsel ecosystem, this means a sponsor can
go from term-sheet to closing in **2-3 weeks** rather than
the 4-8 weeks typical in other jurisdictions.

### GFSC strategic positioning

The GFSC has explicitly positioned Guernsey as a
**continuation-funds jurisdiction** through guidance,
practitioner engagement, and case-handling. Guernsey
Finance has marketed the product line aggressively to UK
and European sponsors.

### Practitioner base

Guernsey's fund-counsel and fund-admin practitioner base
includes the major Channel Islands firms (Mourant, Ogier,
Carey Olsen, Walkers, Appleby) and specialised admin
houses (Aztec, IQ-EQ, JTC, Sanne now Apex). They have
materially handled the volume of single-asset CF
transactions through 2024-2026.

## Choice of Guernsey fund vehicle

### Single-asset CFs — typically the PIF or a non-CIS structure

The [Guernsey Private Investment Fund (PIF) regime](./private-investment-fund.md)
is the workhorse for closed-ended private-capital
vehicles. The PIF has three classes:

- **Class 1 PIF**: requires licensed Guernsey manager;
  ≤50 investors; suitable for many CFs;
- **Class 2 PIF**: family-office route;
- **Class 3 PIF**: requires qualifying investor
  certification but no investor count cap.

For single-asset CFs structured as not-a-CIS, the vehicle
can sit outside the PIF regime entirely — often as a
straightforward Guernsey company under the
[Companies (Guernsey) Law 2008](./companies.md) with
appropriate offering and subscription documents.

### Multi-asset CFs — typically the PIF or authorised regime

Multi-asset CFs typically use the **Class 1 PIF** or an
authorised closed-ended fund. The Authorised Collective
Investment Scheme route adds documentation and timing but
provides marketing flexibility.

## Transaction structure

A typical Guernsey single-asset continuation fund involves
the same fundamental structure as the
[Jersey equivalent](../jersey/funds/continuation-funds.md) —
sponsor on both sides, independent valuation, fairness
opinion, LPAC consent, roll/cash election, carry
crystallisation. The differences are:

- **Vehicle**: Guernsey company or PIF (instead of Jersey JPF);
- **Regulator**: [GFSC](./gfsc.md) (instead of JFSC);
- **Approval timeline**: 3-business-day single-asset
  (instead of Jersey's 48-hour JPF);
- **Substance regime**: Guernsey Economic Substance Law
  (instead of Jersey's).

## Substantive regulatory and structural issues

### Conflicts of interest

As with all GP-led secondaries, the sponsor sits on both
sides. Mitigants:

- **Independent valuation and fairness opinion**;
- **LPAC consent** under the Old Fund's LPA;
- **GFSC engagement** for unusual features;
- **Independent advisor** for LPs (where market practice).

### LP election fairness

The roll/cash election mechanics are subject to the same
fairness scrutiny as Jersey. The Guernsey market practice
has converged with the international standard:

- 30-45-day election window;
- Same price for roll and cash;
- Clear and conspicuous disclosure of the conflict;
- Independent counsel for LPs where the transaction
  warrants.

### Carry crystallisation

Same dynamic as elsewhere — the sponsor crystallises Old
Fund carry; new carry arrangement applies in the
Continuation Fund.

### Substance and Economic Substance (Guernsey) Law 2018

The Guernsey Continuation Fund as a Guernsey-resident
entity carrying on fund management or holding-company
activity falls within Guernsey's Economic Substance Law
2018 (the parallel to Jersey's
[Substance Law 2019](../jersey/tax/economic-substance.md)).
Substance must be maintained.

### AIFMD and marketing

If marketed to EU investors, AIFMD NPPR routes apply.
Post-16 April 2026 the AIFMD II disclosure uplift
applies — see [`../frontier/aifmd-ii-april-2026.md`](../frontier/aifmd-ii-april-2026.md).

### Pillar Two

Guernsey has implemented its **Income Tax (Pillar Two)
(Guernsey) Law 2024** with QDMTT effective 1 January
2025. For in-scope MNE-group sponsors the 15% top-up
applies to the Guernsey Continuation Fund.

## The Guernsey market position

Through 2024-2026, Guernsey single-asset CFs have run at
**market-leading volume** for European secondaries. The
typical transaction profile:

- **Asset**: high-conviction PE asset from a 2018-2020
  vintage fund;
- **Transaction size**: EUR 100m – EUR 1bn;
- **Lead investor**: institutional secondary buyer
  (HarbourVest, Coller, Pantheon, etc.);
- **Roll rate**: 30-60% of existing LP commitments;
- **Timing**: 8-12 weeks from term-sheet to close.

Multi-asset CFs are also well-handled but the single-
asset is where Guernsey's regulatory edge is most
visible.

## Guernsey vs Jersey for continuation funds

| Feature | Guernsey | Jersey |
|---|---|---|
| Single-asset CF regulatory treatment | Not-a-CIS — fast path | JPF — 48-hour route |
| GFSC approval timeline | 3 business days | 48 hours |
| Multi-asset CF route | PIF Class 1 or authorised CIS | JPF or Expert Fund |
| Market position | Leading European CF jurisdiction | Strong but second to Guernsey |
| Practitioner base | Mature; transaction-specialised | Mature; trust + PE depth |
| Substance regime | Economic Substance (Guernsey) Law 2018 | [Economic Substance Law 2019](../jersey/tax/economic-substance.md) |

Most sponsors with **strong continuation-fund deal flow**
have defaulted to Guernsey through 2024-2026. Jersey
remains competitive especially for sponsors with existing
Jersey infrastructure or for trust-and-family-office-
adjacent transactions.

For the Jersey treatment in detail see
[`../jersey/funds/continuation-funds.md`](../jersey/funds/continuation-funds.md).

## Pitfalls

- **Assuming the Guernsey route is always faster** — for
  multi-asset CFs and unusual structures the Jersey
  route can be just as quick;
- **Underestimating substance** — Guernsey CFs still
  need Guernsey substance, not just an admin address;
- **Conflicts process shortcuts** — the GFSC will
  scrutinise transactions where the conflicts process
  is thin, regardless of the not-a-CIS speed advantage;
- **AIFMD II disclosure gap** — post-April 2026 the
  disclosure obligations are materially heavier and the
  GFSC fast-track does not exempt;
- **Assuming the not-a-CIS treatment auto-applies** —
  the structural characterisation matters; sponsors
  should engage the GFSC early on novel structures.
