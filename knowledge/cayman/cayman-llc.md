# Cayman LLC — Limited Liability Companies Act 2016

## What it is

The **Cayman Limited Liability Company (LLC)** is a
relatively new corporate form, introduced by the
**Limited Liability Companies Act 2016**. It was
specifically designed to mirror the **Delaware LLC** —
the dominant US private-company vehicle — to make Cayman
a more attractive destination for sponsors familiar with
Delaware structures and for **cross-border restructurings**
involving Delaware LLCs.

The Cayman LLC is **not** a partnership and **not** a
company — it is a **distinct legal form** combining
features of both:

- **Limited liability** for all members (no GP equivalent
  with unlimited liability);
- **Contractual flexibility** — the LLC Agreement is the
  master document, similar to a partnership LPA;
- **Members** (not shareholders or partners);
- **Managers** who can be members or external persons;
- **Statutory default rules** that are largely
  modifiable.

## Why introduce the LLC

The Cayman LLC fills a gap in Cayman's traditional
offering. Before 2016:

- **Cayman exempted company** — classic corporate form;
- **Cayman exempted limited partnership** — for tax-
  transparent fund vehicles needing a GP / LP structure;
- **Unit trust** — for specific use cases.

But sponsors used to Delaware LLCs found none of these a
direct equivalent. A Delaware LLC offers:

- Limited liability for all owners;
- Pass-through tax (US default);
- Contractual flexibility through the LLC Agreement;
- No corporate formalities (no shares, no statutory
  capital);
- Direct ownership by "members".

Cayman responded with the LLC Act 2016, producing a
Cayman-law mirror that fund sponsors and US lawyers can
work with using familiar Delaware-LLC concepts.

## Key features

| Feature | Cayman LLC |
|---|---|
| **Members** | One or more; no maximum |
| **Liability** | Limited to capital contribution and amounts agreed in LLC Agreement |
| **Management** | Member-managed by default; can elect manager-managed |
| **Constitutional documents** | LLC Agreement (private) + Registration Statement (public) |
| **Tax (Cayman)** | None directly |
| **Registration** | With the Registrar (similar to exempted company) |
| **Tax undertaking** | 50-year undertaking available |
| **Migration in/out** | Permitted |
| **Mergers / conversions** | Available, including with other Cayman entities and foreign LLCs |
| **Public filings** | Minimal |

## Cayman LLC vs Cayman Exempted Company

| Feature | Cayman LLC | Cayman Exempted Co |
|---|---|---|
| Owners | Members | Shareholders |
| Equity instrument | Membership interest | Shares |
| Statutory capital concept | None | Capital, capital reserve, share premium |
| Default management | Members | Directors |
| Constitutional document | LLC Agreement | Memorandum + Articles |
| US tax classification flexibility | Check-the-box typically available | Per-se corporation in most cases |
| Statutory formalities | Minimal | Companies-Act-style |
| Re-domicile from Delaware LLC | Direct conversion possible | Conversion to corp form first |

The LLC form is **particularly useful** when:

- **Counterparty** is a Delaware LLC and consistency is
  preferred;
- **Tax structuring** wants the check-the-box election
  for US tax purposes (default is partnership / disregarded
  entity classification);
- **Fund GPs** want a member-managed structure without
  partnership liability concerns;
- **Restructurings** involve a Delaware LLC redomiciling
  to Cayman.

## Cayman LLC vs Cayman ELP

A Cayman LLC and a Cayman ELP both:

- Have **contractual** governing documents (LLC
  Agreement / Limited Partnership Agreement);
- Offer **flexibility** in economic / governance terms;
- Are **tax-transparent or pass-through** (in most
  investor jurisdictions);
- Are used in **fund structures**.

But:

- **LLC**: limited liability for **all** members; no GP
  with unlimited liability;
- **ELP**: at least one **general partner** with
  **unlimited liability** (typically a shell company);
- **Tax classification**: ELPs are typically partnerships
  for US tax purposes by default; Cayman LLCs can elect
  partnership or association classification under US
  check-the-box rules.

In practice:

- **Closed-ended PE funds** typically use **ELPs** —
  market familiarity, GP/LP economics natural;
- **Cayman LLCs** are growing in use for **specific
  fund structures**, **JV vehicles**, and **transactions
  where Delaware-LLC parity matters**.

## Fund-vehicle use

Cayman LLCs are increasingly used:

- As **fund GP entities** (LLC member-managed by the
  sponsor; ELP fund underneath);
- As **alternative investment vehicles** for specific
  investors with US tax preferences;
- As **co-investment vehicles** in PE / VC structures;
- As **SPV** vehicles in transactions where the
  counterparty is a Delaware LLC.

For the actual fund itself (the LP-equivalent vehicle
holding the investments), the ELP remains the dominant
choice because of its market familiarity and the GP/LP
economic structure.

## US tax treatment

The Cayman LLC is **eligible for US "check-the-box"**
classification:

- **Default**: as a foreign entity owned by US tax
  persons, the LLC is by default a **partnership** (if
  >1 member) or **disregarded entity** (if 1 member);
- **Elective**: the entity can elect to be treated as a
  **corporation** instead;
- **Important** for US tax planning — partnership /
  disregarded treatment allows direct flow-through, while
  corporation treatment can be useful for blockers /
  GILTI / Subpart F planning.

This flexibility is one of the main reasons sponsors
choose the LLC form over the exempted company for
specific structures.

## Regulatory perimeter

- **No CIMA registration** for the LLC form alone;
- **Activity-triggered** regulation as for any Cayman
  entity (fund regimes, insurance, banking, trust
  business);
- **Economic substance** obligations as for any Cayman
  entity carrying on relevant activities.

## Pitfalls

- **Investor unfamiliarity** — some investors and
  counterparties don't know the form; expect to explain;
- **US tax assumption errors** — the check-the-box
  default may not be what the structure needs;
- **Member-managed governance** — without explicit
  manager-managed election, every member may have
  management authority by default;
- **LLC Agreement drafting** — the whole structure
  depends on the LLC Agreement; sloppy drafting is
  fatal;
- **Migration** from Delaware LLC has tax-residency and
  Pillar Two implications that need careful handling.
