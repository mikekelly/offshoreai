# Cayman vs Jersey — Comparison

## Constitutional / political

| Feature | Jersey | Cayman |
|---|---|---|
| Status | Crown Dependency | **British Overseas Territory** |
| UK Parliament | Cannot legislate without consent | **Can** legislate directly (rarely does) |
| Governor | Lieutenant Governor | Governor (more active role) |
| Top court | Royal Court | Grand Court |
| Privy Council | Final appeal | Final appeal |
| Legislative body | States Assembly | Parliament of Cayman Islands |
| Population | ~107k | ~80k |
| Currency | Jersey pound (par GBP) | Cayman dollar (par USD at CI$1=US$1.20) |
| Time zone | UTC/UTC+1 | UTC-5 |

The **constitutional difference** is significant: BOTs and
Crown Dependencies have different relationships with the UK.

## Tax

| Feature | Jersey | Cayman |
|---|---|---|
| Corporate tax | Zero-ten (0/10/20%) | **None** |
| Personal tax | 20% | **None** |
| GST / VAT | 5% GST | **None** |
| Capital gains | None | None |
| Inheritance | None | None |
| Pillar Two | MCIT 2025 | **MTT 2025** (Cayman did implement Pillar Two equivalent) |
| Substance Law | 2019 | Equivalent 2019 |
| Customs duties | Limited | Significant (revenue source) |
| Stamp duty | Limited | Significant on real estate |

**Cayman** is a **true zero-tax** environment for income taxes
on both personal and corporate. Government revenue comes from
**customs duties, fees, and stamp duty**.

## Companies law

| Feature | Jersey CJL 1991 | Cayman Companies Act |
|---|---|---|
| Statute | CJL 1991 | Companies Act (1961, extensively amended) |
| Exempted companies | No specific category | **Yes** — main international vehicle |
| LLC | No (use LLP) | **Yes** — Limited Liability Companies Act 2016 |
| Cell companies | PCC and ICC | **SPC** (Segregated Portfolio Company) |
| Migration | Continuance in/out | Continuance in/out |
| Filing requirements | Annual return | Annual return |

**Cayman exempted companies** are the principal vehicle for
international business — exempt from local taxes,
restrictions on doing local business.

## Trust law

| Feature | Jersey | Cayman |
|---|---|---|
| Statute | TJL 1984 | Trusts Act (various amendments) |
| Firewall | Article 9 | Trusts (Foreign Element) Act 1987 |
| Reserved powers | Article 9A | **Specifically allowed** under amendments |
| Purpose trusts | Article 12 | **STAR trusts** (alternative regime) |
| Duration | Unlimited | Generally limited (subject to specific structures) |
| Beneficiary information | Schmidt v Rosewood | Cayman version with specific rules |

**STAR trusts** are the Cayman distinctive feature —
**Special Trusts Alternative Regime** for non-charitable
purpose trusts with enforcer-based enforcement.

## Funds

| Feature | Jersey | Cayman |
|---|---|---|
| Hedge funds | JPF, Expert Fund | **Globally dominant** |
| Private equity | LP / Jersey LP variants | **Major centre** |
| Public funds | CIFL 1988 | Mutual Funds Law |
| Speed of establishment | Days to weeks | **Hours to days** |
| Fund services | Significant | **Major specialty** |
| AIFMD | NPPR | NPPR |

**Cayman dominates** global hedge fund and private-equity fund
domiciliation. Jersey's fund market is significant but
substantially smaller than Cayman.

## Insurance

| Feature | Jersey | Cayman |
|---|---|---|
| Captive market | Smaller | Significant |
| PCC | Yes | **SPC** (Segregated Portfolio Company) |
| Specialty | Various | Reinsurance, healthcare captives |
| Regulator | JFSC | CIMA |

**Cayman** has a significant captive market but smaller than
Bermuda. Specific specialties (healthcare risk, reinsurance).

## Banking

| Feature | Jersey | Cayman |
|---|---|---|
| Sector size | Larger relative | Significant; wholesale focus |
| Retail banking | Yes | Limited |
| International private | Yes | Yes |
| Specific specialty | Various | Wholesale, fund-services banking |

**Cayman banking** is more **wholesale-focused** than Jersey's
mixed retail / wholesale market.

## Legal heritage

| Feature | Jersey | Cayman |
|---|---|---|
| Heritage | Norman customary | British colonial / common law |
| Customary law role | Significant | Minimal |
| English law | Persuasive | **More directly applicable** |
| Pothier | Cited | Not relevant |

Cayman law is **closer to English law** than Jersey law in
many areas. The civil-law / Pothier dimension of Jersey is
absent in Cayman.

## When to choose Cayman

Strong factors favouring Cayman:

- **Hedge fund** structures (overwhelming preference);
- **Private equity** structures (overwhelming preference);
- **Bond issuances** and structured finance;
- **Speed** of fund / company establishment;
- **STAR trust** for purpose-trust structures;
- **Cost** for high-volume structures;
- **Existing** Cayman-based investor base.

## When Jersey is the natural choice

Strong factors favouring Jersey:

- **Trust matters** for **private family wealth** (especially
  with Article 9A reserved powers);
- **Crown Dependency** status preferred (some clients/markets
  prefer this);
- **GBP-denominated** operations;
- **EU / UK** proximity;
- **Time zone** alignment with European business;
- **Specific** Jersey legal advantages for structure;
- **Substance** at scale (Jersey has good substance
  capacity).

## Common combinations

Many international structures use **both**:

- **Cayman master fund** + **Jersey feeder** (or vice versa);
- **Cayman portfolio holdings** with **Jersey family trust**
  at top;
- **Jersey HVR resident** with **Cayman fund** investments;
- **Specific** sectoral combinations.
