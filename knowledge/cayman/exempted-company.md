# Cayman Exempted Company

## What it is

The **Cayman Exempted Company** is the workhorse vehicle
of Cayman corporate structuring. It is the form used for:

- **Offshore fund** structures (open- and closed-ended);
- **Holding companies** at the top of group structures;
- **SPV** orphan vehicles in structured finance;
- **Pre-IPO** holdcos;
- **Joint-venture** vehicles between sponsors and
  investors.

It is the **default form** for Cayman incorporation where
the company is not intended to carry on business with the
public in the Cayman Islands.

## Statutory basis

Companies Act (Cayman Islands), Part XV — Exempted
Companies. The Companies Act has been amended many times;
the current consolidated text is the operative source.

## Distinguishing features

| Feature | Exempted Company |
|---|---|
| **Carrying on business with the public in Cayman** | **Prohibited** (except as ancillary to non-Cayman business) |
| **Tax undertaking** | Can apply for **20-year undertaking** from the Government against future tax imposition (renewable to 30 years) |
| **Register of shareholders** | Not required to be publicly available |
| **Annual general meeting** | Not required |
| **Disclosure of nominees** | Beneficial-ownership register exists but not publicly accessible |
| **Migration** | Re-domiciliation in / out permitted |
| **Public filings** | Limited |

## The "exempted" label

"Exempted" refers to being **exempted from carrying on
business** with the public in Cayman, **not** to tax
exemption per se. Cayman has no general direct taxes — so
the tax-exemption framing is misleading. What "exempted"
really means: this is the form for **internationally-
focused business**, not local commerce.

A company actually carrying on business with the Cayman
public would incorporate as an **Ordinary Resident
Company** instead.

## Formation

A Cayman exempted company is formed by:

1. **Memorandum of Association** filed with the Registrar
   of Companies;
2. **Articles of Association** adopted;
3. **Specified objects** (or unlimited objects clause);
4. **Initial subscriber(s)**;
5. **Initial directors** appointed;
6. **Registered office** in Cayman (provided by a CIMA-
   licensed corporate services provider);
7. **Government fee** paid (annual fee structure based on
   share capital).

Typical timeline: **1-2 days** for a clean incorporation.

## Capital structure

Highly flexible:

- **Par value** or **no-par-value** shares (no-par is
  uncommon — most Cayman exempteds use par-value);
- **Multiple classes** of shares (voting / non-voting,
  preferred / common, founder / investor);
- **Redeemable** shares (often used for fund vehicles);
- **Treasury** shares permitted;
- **Capital reduction** procedures available;
- **No** statutory capital maintenance regime equivalent
  to UK requirements.

The capital flexibility is a major reason for Cayman's
fund-vehicle dominance — fund structures need redemption
mechanics, multi-class share structures, and capital
movement that the Cayman Act accommodates with minimal
formality.

## Directors

- **Minimum one** director (no Cayman-residence
  requirement);
- **Corporate directors** permitted (a Cayman-resident
  director is **not** mandatory but is increasingly
  expected by service providers and counterparties);
- **No** statutory requirement for AGMs;
- **Specific** Companies Act director duties (broadly
  reflecting common-law fiduciary duties);
- **Indemnification** of directors permitted, subject to
  fraud / dishonesty exceptions.

## Shareholders

- **Single shareholder** permitted;
- **No residence requirements**;
- **Beneficial ownership register** required but not
  publicly available — searchable by Cayman authorities
  and accessible under specific information-sharing
  arrangements.

## Use cases

### Offshore funds

The **dominant use case**. Cayman is the world's largest
domicile for offshore funds. Open-ended hedge funds and
closed-ended PE / VC vehicles routinely use the exempted
company form, often combined with a Cayman ELP for
limited-partner vehicles.

### Holding companies

Cayman exempted companies sit at the top of group
structures for:

- **Pre-IPO** structures (frequently combined with US
  listing via SEC Form F-1 / F-4);
- **Group holdcos** for tax-efficient repatriation;
- **Joint venture** holdcos with multiple sponsors.

### Orphan SPVs

For structured finance and asset-backed transactions —
often combined with a STAR trust over the SPV shares for
true orphan status (see [`star-trusts.md`](./star-trusts.md)).

### Pre-IPO and listing

Many US-listed companies with operations in Asia / EMEA
list as Cayman exempted companies (the Alibaba / TikTok
holdco pattern). Cayman is one of three jurisdictions
(alongside BVI and Delaware) routinely accepted by major
exchanges as listing vehicles.

## Regulatory perimeter

A Cayman exempted company is **not regulated by CIMA**
by virtue of its form alone. It becomes regulated when
it carries on a regulated activity, such as:

- Operating as a **mutual fund** under the Mutual Funds
  Act;
- Operating as a **private fund** under the Private
  Funds Act 2020;
- Carrying on **insurance**, **banking**, or **trust
  business**;
- Acting as **CIMA-licensed** investment manager.

See [`funds.md`](./funds.md) and [`cima.md`](./cima.md).

## Annual obligations

- **Annual return** to the Registrar with annual fee;
- **Beneficial-ownership** information up to date;
- **Economic-substance** filings (if required under the
  Economic Substance Act 2018);
- **Specific** filings for regulated activities.

## Migration and exit

A Cayman exempted company can:

- **Re-domicile** to another jurisdiction (continuation
  out) — receiving jurisdiction's law applies post-
  migration;
- **Re-domicile** into Cayman from another jurisdiction
  (continuation in);
- **Merge** with another Cayman company or with a non-
  Cayman company in a permitted merger jurisdiction;
- **Wind up** voluntarily or by court;
- **Strike off** for inactivity.

The migration regime is meaningfully easier than many
onshore jurisdictions, supporting tax / regulatory
restructurings.

## Economic substance overlay

Under the **International Tax Co-operation (Economic
Substance) Act 2018**, exempted companies carrying on
relevant activities (banking, distribution and service
centre, finance and leasing, fund management, headquarters,
holding company, insurance, intellectual property,
shipping) must demonstrate adequate substance in Cayman:

- **CIGA** (Core Income-Generating Activities) conducted
  in Cayman;
- **Adequate employees / expenditure / physical
  presence**;
- **Annual** filings to the Tax Information Authority.

Holding companies and pure equity-holding companies are
subject to a **reduced substance test**. Other activities
have substantive obligations.

## Comparison

| Feature | Cayman Exempted | BVI BC | Jersey Co (CJL 1991) | Delaware Corp |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Tax | No direct tax | No direct tax | 0% (zero-ten) | State / federal tax |
| Substance regime | ESA 2018 | ESA 2018 | Substance Law 2019 | Limited |
| Listing acceptance | NYSE, NASDAQ, HKEx | NYSE, NASDAQ, HKEx (more limited) | LSE primarily | NYSE, NASDAQ |
| Fund-vehicle use | Dominant | Common | Selective (esp. JPF) | Less common offshore |
| Beneficial-owner register | Yes, non-public | Yes, non-public | Yes, non-public | Federal (CTA) |
| Re-domiciliation | Permitted | Permitted | Permitted | More restrictive |
