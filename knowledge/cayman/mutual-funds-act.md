# Cayman Mutual Funds Act

## What it does

The **Mutual Funds Act** regulates **open-ended** funds
domiciled or operated in the Cayman Islands. It is the
older Cayman fund regime, predating the Private Funds
Act 2020 by decades. Open-ended funds — typically hedge
funds and other strategies with **investor redemption
rights** — are within scope.

A "mutual fund" is broadly an entity:

- That **issues equity interests**;
- Whose **purpose** is to enable investors to receive
  profits or gains from acquiring, holding, managing, or
  disposing of investments;
- **Where investors have redemption rights** (the open-
  ended characteristic).

Together with the **Private Funds Act 2020** (closed-
ended), the Mutual Funds Act now provides full
regulatory coverage of the Cayman collective-investment
universe.

## Categories of mutual fund

The Act creates four categories with different regulatory
intensities:

### Section 4(1) — Licensed

A fully licensed mutual fund. Highest regulatory burden:

- **CIMA approval** of constitutional documents;
- **CIMA-approved** directors and operator;
- **Significant** fitness-and-propriety scrutiny;
- **Specific** capital and operational standards.

Rare in practice — most sponsors prefer lighter-touch
categories.

### Section 4(3) — Administered

A mutual fund whose **principal office in Cayman is
provided by a licensed administrator**:

- Lighter regulatory burden;
- **Administrator** acts as the de facto regulatory
  interface;
- **CIMA** can inspect via the administrator.

Used for **larger funds** wanting a Cayman administrative
hub.

### Section 4(4) — Registered (the most common)

The workhorse category:

- **Minimum investment** per investor of US$100,000 (or
  equivalent);
- **Audited financial statements** by a CIMA-approved
  auditor;
- **Offering memorandum** filed with CIMA;
- **Annual return** and fee.

Most Cayman hedge funds operate as Section 4(4) registered
mutual funds. The US$100,000 minimum investment is the
key gating mechanism — it limits the fund to relatively
sophisticated / well-capitalised investors.

### Section 4(4A) — Listed

Mutual funds whose interests are **listed** on a CIMA-
approved stock exchange:

- **Reduced filings** on the basis that the listing
  exchange provides oversight;
- **Specific** Cayman Islands Stock Exchange listings
  (and other approved exchanges) qualify.

## Master / feeder structures

The Mutual Funds Act explicitly contemplates **master /
feeder** structures common in the hedge-fund world:

- **Master fund** — holds the actual investments;
- **Feeder funds** — collect investor capital and invest
  into the master fund;
- **Typical pattern** — Cayman master, with a Cayman
  feeder for non-US investors and a Delaware feeder for
  US-taxable investors.

Master funds may be regulated separately depending on
their structure and operations.

## CIMA-approved auditors

All registered mutual funds must engage a CIMA-approved
auditor for annual audits. The auditor list is published
and updated by CIMA. Audited financial statements must be
filed with CIMA annually.

## Operational requirements

Mutual funds must have:

- **CIMA-approved offering memorandum**;
- **Audited financial statements** annually;
- **Specific** custody arrangements;
- **Valuation policy**;
- **AML / CFT** compliance;
- **Conflicts of interest** policies;
- **Operator** (director, GP, trustee depending on
  vehicle form) appointed.

These obligations broadly mirror the Private Funds Act
2020 but with regime-specific differences in detail.

## Common structures

The Mutual Funds Act doesn't dictate the vehicle form.
Common combinations:

- **Cayman exempted company** (the most common Section
  4(4) vehicle) — investors hold redeemable shares;
- **Cayman segregated portfolio company** — for multi-
  strategy funds with portfolio-level segregation;
- **Cayman unit trust** — used for some Asian-investor-
  focused funds.

Cayman LPs are typically **closed-ended** (PE/VC) and
fall under the **Private Funds Act 2020** rather than
the Mutual Funds Act.

## Master Fund deemed Mutual Fund

If a feeder is a Cayman mutual fund and feeds into a non-
Cayman master, the master may still be deemed within the
Cayman regulatory perimeter via specific connected-fund
provisions. Practitioners check this carefully when
structuring.

## Tax

- **Cayman**: no direct tax on the fund or investors;
- **Investor jurisdiction**: typically the redemption
  rights and pass-through characteristics drive
  classification;
- **CRS / FATCA**: full reporting at fund level;
- **Economic substance**: investment-fund-business is
  carved out of the substance test (a sensible carve-out
  given the global investor base).

## Comparison

| Feature | Mutual Funds Act | Private Funds Act 2020 |
|---|---|---|
| Open / closed | Open-ended | Closed-ended |
| Categories | 4 categories | Single category |
| Typical use | Hedge funds | PE / VC / RE / credit |
| Investor minimum | US$100,000 (s4(4)) | None statutory |
| Audited statements | Required | Required |
| Custody | Required | Required |
| Annual fee | Yes | Yes |
