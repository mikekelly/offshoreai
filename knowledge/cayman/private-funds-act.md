# Cayman Private Funds Act 2020

## What it does

The **Private Funds Act 2020** (PFA) extended Cayman's
regulatory perimeter to **closed-ended private funds**.
Before 2020, Cayman regulated only **open-ended mutual
funds** (via the Mutual Funds Act). PE, VC, real-estate,
infrastructure, credit, and other closed-ended structures
operated **outside CIMA's reach**.

The PFA 2020 changed that — closed-ended funds vehicling
through Cayman that meet the statutory test now require
**CIMA registration** and ongoing oversight. Cayman moved
from being a "wide-open" closed-fund domicile to one with
substantive (though still light-touch) regulation.

## What counts as a "private fund"

A "private fund" under the PFA is broadly:

- An **entity** (company, partnership, unit trust);
- Whose **principal business** is the offering or issuing
  of investment interests;
- Whose **purpose** is the **pooling of investor funds**
  for **collective investment** with the aim of profit /
  gain;
- That is **not** a mutual fund (which falls under the
  Mutual Funds Act);
- That is **not** otherwise excluded.

Carve-outs include:

- **Single-investor** vehicles;
- **Joint ventures** between identified parties (not
  pooled investment);
- **Pension funds** for the employees of a specific
  employer;
- **Securitisation special-purpose vehicles**;
- **Certain holding** structures.

The "pooling" requirement is critical — a vehicle that
isn't pooling third-party capital is generally outside.

## Registration

A private fund must:

1. **Register** with CIMA before accepting investor
   capital;
2. **Pay** a registration fee;
3. **Provide** prescribed information (constitutional
   documents, offering memorandum, GP / Investment
   Manager details);
4. **Confirm** ongoing compliance commitments.

Registration is typically achieved within days — Cayman
designed the PFA to preserve speed-to-market for fund
sponsors.

## Ongoing obligations

Once registered:

### Annual obligations

- **Audited financial statements** by a CIMA-approved
  auditor;
- **Annual return** to CIMA;
- **Annual fee** to CIMA;
- **Beneficial-ownership** information up to date.

### Operational obligations

- **Valuation policy** — independent or with appropriate
  oversight;
- **Safekeeping of assets** — segregation, custodian
  arrangements;
- **Cash monitoring** by an appropriate person;
- **Identification** of securities (record-keeping);
- **Title verification** for asset purchases;
- **AML / CFT** compliance and AML officer appointed;
- **Risk management** framework;
- **Conflicts of interest** policies.

These obligations are **less onerous** than EU AIFMD but
substantively more than the pre-2020 unregulated regime.

## Structures captured

The PFA covers:

- **Cayman exempted limited partnerships** (the typical
  PE/VC fund vehicle) — see [`exempted-limited-partnership.md`](./exempted-limited-partnership.md);
- **Cayman exempted companies** operated as closed-ended
  funds;
- **Cayman segregated portfolio companies** (each
  portfolio that is a private fund);
- **Cayman unit trusts**;
- **Foreign entities** that are deemed private funds
  by virtue of their operations being centred in Cayman.

## Comparison with Mutual Funds Act

| Feature | Mutual Funds Act | Private Funds Act 2020 |
|---|---|---|
| Type | Open-ended | Closed-ended |
| Investors | Redemption rights | Restricted withdrawal |
| Categories | Master / feeder / standalone | Single category |
| Registration | Yes | Yes |
| Audited statements | Yes | Yes |
| Custody | Required | Required |
| Valuation | Required | Required |
| AML | Required | Required |

The two regimes operate in **parallel** — together they
cover essentially all collective-investment vehicles in
Cayman.

## Practical impact on sponsors

Pre-2020, a Cayman closed-ended fund could be set up and
operated with minimal CIMA contact. Post-2020:

- **Registration** is mandatory before launch;
- **CIMA-approved auditor** must be engaged;
- **Operational policies** (valuation, custody, AML)
  must be in place;
- **Annual return** and fee;
- **CIMA inspections** possible (but rare).

For sponsors used to the pre-2020 regime, the transition
required upgrading governance and operational practice.
For sponsors used to other regulated regimes (EU AIFMD,
US Advisers Act), the PFA is **lighter touch**.

## Exemption strategy

Some structures attempt to fall **outside the PFA**:

- **Family / employer pension** vehicles;
- **Securitisation** SPVs (though specific Cayman
  securitisation legislation also applies);
- **Single-LP** vehicles;
- **JV** partnerships between named parties;
- **Specific** holding structures.

Each exemption requires careful analysis — incorrect
reliance can result in unauthorised fund operation
penalties.

## Recent developments

The PFA has been amended since 2020 to refine the regime:

- **Definitional** clarifications;
- **Fee** updates;
- **Reporting** detail;
- **AML/CFT** integration.

Current consolidated Act is the operative source.
