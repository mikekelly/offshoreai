# Cayman Tax — Zero-Tax Environment

## Cayman has no income tax

The Cayman Islands has **no income tax**:

- **No** corporate income tax;
- **No** personal income tax;
- **No** withholding tax;
- **No** capital gains tax;
- **No** inheritance / estate tax;
- **No** gift tax;
- **No** GST / VAT.

This is a **defining feature** of Cayman as a financial
centre.

## Government revenue

Cayman government revenue comes from:

### Customs duties

- **Substantial** revenue from goods imports;
- **Tourism**-related duties;
- **Specific** product-level duties.

### Fees

- **Companies Registry** fees;
- **CIMA** licence fees;
- **Annual** company fees;
- **Trust** fees;
- **Fund** annual fees.

### Stamp duty

- **Real estate** transactions;
- **Specific** transfers;
- **Significant** government revenue source.

### Specific tourism / travel duties

- **Departure tax**;
- **Cruise ship** taxes;
- **Hotel** accommodation taxes.

## Tax neutrality certificates

For long-term structures:

- **20-30 year exemption certificates** available for exempted
  companies and trusts;
- **Guarantee** that no future Cayman tax will apply during
  the certificate period;
- **Subject to** specific application and fees.

## Pillar Two — Cayman implementation

Cayman implemented **Pillar Two** equivalent:

- **Multinational Top-up Tax (MTT)** from 2025;
- **15%** minimum effective rate for €750m+ groups;
- **Aligned** with OECD GloBE rules;
- **Significant** development for Cayman (first
  income-like tax for in-scope groups).

This represents a **historic shift** in Cayman tax — Cayman's
first material income-tax-equivalent for in-scope multinational
groups.

## Substance Law

Cayman has equivalent **economic substance** legislation
(2018):

- **Aligned** with Jersey / Guernsey / IoM Substance Laws;
- **Same** activity categories;
- **Substance requirements**;
- **Annual** substance return.

## CRS / FATCA

- **CRS** implementation;
- **FATCA IGA** with US;
- **CbCR** for in-scope groups;
- **Aligned** transparency standards.

## Tax treaty network

Cayman has a **limited** treaty network:

- **TIEAs** with multiple jurisdictions;
- **Limited** full DTAs;
- **Specific** mutual assistance arrangements;
- **Less developed** than Jersey treaty network.

## Practical tax considerations

For Cayman structures:

### Cayman-side

- **No Cayman tax** on income / gains (subject to MTT);
- **Specific** annual fees;
- **Substance** if in-scope.

### Foreign side

- **CFC** rules in investor jurisdictions;
- **Specific** US tax considerations for US investors
  (especially PFIC rules);
- **Specific** UK tax considerations;
- **Specific** other jurisdictions.

The **Cayman zero tax** is **only relevant from Cayman's
perspective** — foreign tax authorities tax their own
residents on Cayman-source income.

## Comparison with Jersey

| Feature | Jersey | Cayman |
|---|---|---|
| Corporate income | Zero-ten (0/10/20) | **None** |
| Personal income | 20% | **None** |
| Capital gains | None | None |
| Inheritance | None | None |
| GST / VAT | 5% GST | **None** |
| Customs duties | Limited | Significant |
| Stamp duty | Limited | Significant |
| Pillar Two | MCIT 2025 | MTT 2025 |
| Substance Law | 2019 | 2018 |
