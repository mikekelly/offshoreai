# STAR Trusts — Cayman Special Trusts Alternative Regime

## What STAR trusts are

**STAR trusts** (Special Trusts — Alternative Regime) are
**Cayman's distinctive trust offering**:

- **Established** by Special Trusts (Alternative Regime) Act
  **1997**;
- **Non-charitable purpose** trusts;
- **No requirement** for beneficiaries (purpose trusts);
- **Enforcer**-based enforcement;
- **Wide** purpose permitted (not just charitable).

## Why STAR matters

Traditional trust law requires:

- **Beneficiaries** for non-charitable trusts;
- **Charitable purposes** as alternative;
- **Non-charitable purpose** trusts historically problematic.

**STAR trusts** solve this by:

- **Allowing** non-charitable purposes;
- **Using enforcers** instead of beneficiaries;
- **Wide** flexibility in purpose definition.

## STAR features

### Purpose

A STAR trust can be created for:

- **Any** lawful purpose (not limited to charitable);
- **Mixed** purposes (some beneficiaries plus some purposes);
- **Family-governance** purposes;
- **Commercial** purposes;
- **Specific** philanthropic-like purposes that aren't strictly
  charitable.

### Enforcer

The **enforcer** is a person appointed to enforce the trust:

- **Mandatory** for STAR trusts;
- **Stands in place of beneficiaries** for enforcement
  purposes;
- **Has** power to call trustee to account;
- **Specific** Cayman provisions on enforcer rights /
  duties.

### Duration

STAR trusts can have **unlimited duration**:

- **Specific** STAR provisions;
- **Different** from standard Cayman trusts (which have
  duration limits).

### Trustees

- **Specific** STAR trust trustees;
- **At least one** Cayman-licensed trust company typically
  required;
- **Standard** fiduciary duties;
- **Specific** STAR-trust trustee rules.

## Common STAR uses

### Orphan structures

The **classic STAR use**:

- **SPV** in a securitisation / structured-finance
  transaction needs orphan ownership;
- **STAR trust** holds the SPV shares;
- **No beneficiaries** to consolidate;
- **Bankruptcy-remote** from any settlor.

### Family-governance vehicles

- **Family council** through a STAR trust;
- **Multi-generational** family-governance vehicles;
- **Specific** family purposes (education, philanthropy,
  property maintenance).

### PTC (Private Trust Company) holding

- **STAR trust** holds shares in a PTC;
- **Orphan** PTC structure;
- **Common** alongside Jersey purpose-trust equivalents.

### Charitable-like vehicles

- Where strict charitable status doesn't fit;
- **STAR** allows wider charitable-like purposes;
- **Tax considerations** in beneficiary jurisdictions.

### Specific commercial purposes

- **Specific** lawful purposes that don't fit charitable
  or beneficiary trust;
- **Commercial** holding arrangements.

## Comparison with Jersey purpose trusts

| Feature | Jersey Article 12 | Cayman STAR |
|---|---|---|
| Statute | TJL Article 12 | STAR Act 1997 |
| Enforcer | Required | Required |
| Duration | Unlimited | Unlimited |
| Mixed beneficiary / purpose | Specific provisions | Specifically permitted |
| Trustee | Jersey-licensed | Cayman-licensed |
| Subject to | Trust Law generally | Specific STAR Act |

Both are **functionally similar** for orphan structures and
family-governance vehicles. The **statutory regimes differ**
in detail.

## Drafting STAR trusts

A STAR trust deed:

- **Identifies** the purposes specifically;
- **Names** the enforcer (and successor enforcers);
- **Specifies** trustee duties relating to the purposes;
- **Specifies** distribution / application rules;
- **Specifies** dissolution mechanisms;
- **Specific** STAR-compliant clauses.

## Tax considerations

- **Cayman**: no income tax;
- **Settlor's jurisdiction**: subject to local tax rules
  (CFC, attribution, etc.);
- **Specific** characterisation for US grantor-trust
  purposes;
- **Specific** consideration for OECD / Pillar Two if
  applicable.
