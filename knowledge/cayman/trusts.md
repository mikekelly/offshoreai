# Cayman Trusts — Overview

## Principal legislation

Cayman trust law is governed by:

- **Trusts Act** (principal statute, much amended);
- **Trusts (Foreign Element) Act** — firewall provisions;
- **Special Trusts (Alternative Regime) Act 1997** — STAR
  trusts;
- **Specific** other acts.

## Standard Cayman trusts

### Express trusts

- **Standard** trust principles;
- **Common-law** foundation;
- **Specific** Cayman statutory overlay.

### Charitable trusts

- **Recognised** under standard trust principles;
- **Specific** Cayman provisions;
- **Tax treatment** advantageous.

### Discretionary trusts

- **Standard** discretionary regime;
- **Specific** Cayman approaches.

## STAR trusts

The **Special Trusts — Alternative Regime** is **Cayman's
distinctive trust offering**:

- **Non-charitable purpose** trusts;
- **Enforcer-based** enforcement (no beneficiary needed);
- **Used** for orphan structures, family-governance,
  charitable-like vehicles;
- **Specific** STAR Act 1997 with subsequent amendments.

See [`star-trusts.md`](./star-trusts.md).

## Firewall

The **Trusts (Foreign Element) Act 1987** provides firewall
protection:

- **Foreign forced-heirship** claims excluded;
- **Foreign matrimonial** claims limited;
- **Foreign judgments** inconsistent with Cayman trust law
  not enforced;
- **Similar** in principle to Jersey Article 9.

## Reserved powers

Cayman trust law permits **settlor reserved powers**:

- **Specifically allowed** by statutory amendment;
- **Wide** menu of powers;
- **Similar** to Jersey Article 9A;
- **Specific** Cayman provisions.

## Duration

Cayman trusts have **specific duration rules**:

- **Generally** subject to perpetuities-type limits (though
  modernised);
- **STAR trusts** have specific duration provisions;
- **Specific** Cayman statutory rules.

## Trustee duties

Cayman trustees:

- **Common-law** fiduciary duties;
- **Specific** statutory provisions;
- **Cayman case law** developing;
- **Similar** to English / Jersey approach.

## Information rights

- **Beneficiary information** rights;
- **Similar to** Schmidt v Rosewood approach;
- **Specific** Cayman provisions.

## Specific Cayman trust uses

### Hedge fund / PE structures

- **Cayman trusts** often used in fund-related structures
  (e.g., holding entity for fund manager equity, carry
  vehicles);
- **Specific** Cayman trust types tailored for these uses.

### Private wealth

- **Cayman trusts** for international families;
- **STAR trusts** for family governance;
- **Specific** Cayman wealth structures.

### Bond / structured finance

- **STAR trusts** as orphan SPVs in structured finance;
- **Trustee** holding shares in issuer SPV.

## Comparison with Jersey

| Feature | Jersey TJL 1984 | Cayman Trusts Act |
|---|---|---|
| Statute | TJL 1984 | Trusts Act + amendments |
| Reserved powers | Article 9A | Specific provisions |
| Firewall | Article 9 | Foreign Element Act 1987 |
| Purpose trusts | Article 12 | STAR Act 1997 |
| Duration | Unlimited | Subject to limits (some exceptions) |
| Customary law overlay | Norman | Common-law only |

Both are **major trust jurisdictions** with **strong** legal
frameworks.

## When to use Cayman trusts

Common scenarios:

- **Fund-related** trust structures (carry, manager
  equity);
- **STAR trust** orphan structures;
- **US-tax-purposes** structures (Cayman characterisation
  preferred in some configurations);
- **Existing** Cayman-based wealth or counterparties;
- **Specific** Cayman structural advantages.
