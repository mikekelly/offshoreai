# Isle of Man Trusts — Overview

## Principal legislation

IoM trust law is governed by:

- **Trustee Act 2001** — principal trustee duties statute;
- **Purpose Trusts Act 1996** — non-charitable purpose trusts;
- **Trust (Amendment) Act 2015** — firewall provisions;
- **Specific** other statutes (Variation of Trusts Act,
  etc.);
- **Customary** law overlay.

## Key features

### Trust validity and recognition

- **Express trusts** recognised;
- **Foreign trusts** recognised under conflict-of-laws
  principles;
- **Specific** IoM rules.

### Reserved powers

The IoM permits **settlor reserved powers** similar to Jersey
Article 9A:

- **Revocation**;
- **Distribution** direction;
- **Appointment** of trustees / protectors;
- **Investment** management;
- **Amendment**;
- **Specific** IoM provisions.

### Firewall

The **Trust (Amendment) Act 2015** introduced firewall
provisions:

- **Foreign forced-heirship** claims excluded;
- **Foreign matrimonial** claims limited;
- **Foreign judgments** inconsistent with IoM trust law not
  enforced;
- **Similar** in principle to Jersey Article 9;
- **Specific** IoM provisions.

### Trustee duties

The **Trustee Act 2001** sets out:

- **Statutory** duty of care;
- **Investment** powers and duties;
- **Delegation** rules;
- **Specific** professional trustee rules;
- **Modelled** on English Trustee Act 2000 with IoM
  adaptations.

### Information rights

- **Beneficiary information** rights;
- **Similar to** Schmidt v Rosewood approach;
- **IoM** case law developing.

### Purpose trusts

The **Purpose Trusts Act 1996**:

- **Non-charitable purpose** trusts;
- **Enforcer** requirement;
- **Subject to** specific IoM rules;
- **Used** for orphan structures, family-governance.

### Duration

- **Unlimited duration** permitted (no perpetuities rule);
- **Specific** IoM rules.

### Variation

- **Variation of Trusts Act** for court-approved variations;
- **Similar** to UK Variation of Trusts Act 1958.

## Comparison with Jersey

| Feature | Jersey TJL 1984 | IoM Trustee Act 2001 etc. |
|---|---|---|
| Statute date | 1984 | 2001 (Trustee Act); various |
| Reserved powers | Article 9A | Specific IoM provisions |
| Firewall | Article 9 | Trust (Amendment) Act 2015 |
| Schmidt v Rosewood | Applied | Applied |
| Purpose trusts | Article 12 | Purpose Trusts Act 1996 |
| Mistake / hastiness | Article 47B-J | Specific IoM provisions |
| Variation | Article 47 (Court) | Variation of Trusts Act |

Both regimes are **broadly aligned**. **Jersey** TJL 1984 has
been more extensively used in international structures and has
more developed case law. **IoM** trust regime has been
modernised in stages and is well-developed.

## When to use IoM trusts

Common scenarios:

- **eGaming** business structures (group context);
- **Insurance** structuring with IoM captive;
- **Specific** IoM advisory relationships;
- **UK-IoM** cross-border (IoM in UK customs union);
- **Specific** IoM tax / regulatory advantages in narrow
  configurations.

## Choosing between Jersey, Guernsey, IoM trusts

For trust structures, the choice between the three Crown
Dependencies is **largely indifferent** for most purposes.
Specific drivers:

- **Existing relationships**;
- **Specific** statutory advantages;
- **Sectoral** preferences;
- **Specific** structural needs.
