# IoM Life Assurance — The International Industry

## What this is

The Isle of Man is the world's leading offshore **life-
assurance** jurisdiction — particularly for **portfolio
bond** and **investment-linked life-assurance** products
sold to:

- **High-net-worth** individuals in the UK and EU
  jurisdictions;
- **Expatriates** wanting a tax-efficient investment
  wrapper;
- **Family-wealth** structuring vehicles;
- **Pension-equivalent** retirement-planning products.

The industry is regulated by the **Isle of Man Financial
Services Authority (IOMFSA)** under the **Insurance Act
2008** and its subsidiary regulations. The IoM has
**Solvency II equivalence** status with the EU, giving its
insurers regulatory standing in EU markets.

## Why IoM for life assurance

IoM's strengths in this sector:

- **Long-established** industry — major UK, European,
  and global insurers have IoM subsidiaries dating back
  decades;
- **Specialist** regulatory expertise — IOMFSA
  understands the international life-assurance business
  in depth;
- **Solvency II equivalence** — IoM-regulated insurers
  can passport into EU markets under specific
  arrangements;
- **Tax-neutral** for the insurer (IoM corporate income
  tax for life-assurance business is structured to be
  neutral);
- **Reputable** jurisdiction with mature financial-
  services infrastructure.

## Principal product: portfolio bonds

The **portfolio bond** is the IoM's defining life-
assurance product:

- **Single-premium** investment vehicle wrapped in a
  life-assurance policy;
- **Investment** in a wide range of underlying assets
  (funds, equities, fixed income, alternatives);
- **Policyholder** designates investment strategy (often
  via an investment manager);
- **Life-assurance** wrapper provides:
  - **Tax deferral** on growth (depending on policyholder
    jurisdiction);
  - **Estate-planning** efficiency in some jurisdictions;
  - **Specific** tax treatment for HNW expatriates and
    UK-based investors (the 5% rule);
- **Policy administration** by the IoM insurer;
- **Surrender** flexibility (with penalties depending on
  product).

UK-based investors use IoM portfolio bonds extensively
for tax-efficient long-term investment alongside ISAs and
pensions.

## Tax-driver: UK 5% rule

A defining UK tax mechanism for IoM (and other offshore)
life-assurance bonds:

- **5% of the original premium** can be withdrawn each
  year **tax-deferred** for 20 years;
- **At final encashment**, the "chargeable event" is
  treated as deferred income;
- **Top-slicing** relief reduces the effective tax rate
  for higher-rate / additional-rate payers;
- **Specific** statutory rules (Chapter 9 of Part 4 ITTOIA
  2005 in the UK).

This delivers materially better cash-flow tax treatment
than ordinary investment for HNW UK-residents — and the
IoM is the dominant jurisdiction for the wrapping
insurer.

## Capital and solvency

IoM insurers are subject to:

- **Solvency Capital Requirement** under the
  Solvency II-equivalent framework;
- **Minimum Capital Requirement** floor;
- **Specific** life-assurance technical-provision rules;
- **Reporting** to IOMFSA;
- **Stress testing**.

The Solvency II equivalence is a substantial competitive
advantage — IoM insurers can interact with EU markets in
ways that non-equivalent jurisdictions cannot.

## Major industry players

The IoM life-assurance sector is dominated by:

- **UK insurance group** subsidiaries (Quilter, Standard
  Life International, Royal London, Canada Life
  International, Utmost, others);
- **Specialist** IoM life-assurance carriers;
- **International** group platforms.

The industry employs several thousand people on the
Island — one of its largest private-sector employment
clusters.

## Conduct of business

IoM life-assurance is sold predominantly through
**intermediaries**:

- **UK FCA-authorised** advisers;
- **Specialist** international financial advisers (IFAs)
  for cross-border clients;
- **Private banks** as introducer / adviser to HNW
  clients;
- **Trust companies** in fund / estate-planning
  contexts.

The IoM insurer typically deals with the intermediary
rather than the policyholder directly. Anti-money-
laundering compliance flows through both the
intermediary and the IoM insurer.

## Comparison with other jurisdictions

| Jurisdiction | Life-assurance role |
|---|---|
| **Isle of Man** | Largest offshore life-assurance domicile; Solvency II equivalence; portfolio-bond specialism |
| **Ireland (Dublin)** | EU-domiciled life-assurance for cross-border passporting (different model — Dublin is inside the EU) |
| **Luxembourg** | EU-domiciled life-assurance; specialist HNW / cross-border |
| **Bermuda** | Reinsurance of life business; less common as primary writer to international consumers |
| **Guernsey** | Specialty captive life vehicles; less mass-market |
| **Jersey** | Some specialty; smaller than IoM |

The IoM and EU-domiciled markets are partly
complementary: post-Brexit, IoM remains a competitive
alternative for UK-focused business; Dublin / Luxembourg
remain key for EU-focused.

## Recent developments

- **Solvency II equivalence** review and re-
  certification — ongoing;
- **OECD Pillar Two** considerations for IoM life-
  assurance groups within in-scope multinationals;
- **Conduct-of-business** rule refinements following
  UK / EU consumer-protection trends.

## Pitfalls

- **Misclassification** of products in policyholder
  tax jurisdictions;
- **Adviser** suitability concerns for non-sophisticated
  retail investors;
- **Solvency II** equivalence status changes (unlikely
  but watch-list);
- **AML/CFT** in the intermediary chain — IoM insurer
  bears regulatory exposure.
