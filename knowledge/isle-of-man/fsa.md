# Isle of Man FSA — Financial Services Authority

## What the FSA is

The **Isle of Man Financial Services Authority (FSA)** is the
**unified financial services regulator** for the Isle of Man:

- **Established** by the Financial Services Act 2008;
- **Merged** Financial Supervision Commission and Insurance
  and Pensions Authority in **2015**;
- **Independent** statutory regulator;
- **Funded** through industry fees;
- **Reports** to Tynwald.

## Functions

The FSA:

- **Licenses** financial-services businesses;
- **Supervises** ongoing compliance;
- **Investigates** breaches;
- **Enforces** regulatory standards;
- **Issues** Rule Book and guidance;
- **Operates** the Isle of Man Companies Registry (in
  collaboration with other agencies);
- **Cooperates** with international regulators.

## Sectors regulated

The FSA regulates:

### Banking

- **Banks** under specific banking legislation;
- **Basel III** aligned prudential rules.

### Insurance

- **Long-term (life) insurance** (Class 3);
- **General insurance** including captives (Class 2);
- **Domestic** insurance (Class 1);
- **Insurance intermediaries**.

### Investment business / funds

- **Authorised** and **regulated** funds;
- **Investment business** licensees;
- **Specialist** and **qualifying** funds.

### Fiduciary services

- **Trust and corporate** services (TCB equivalent);
- **Specific** IoM fiduciary regulation;
- **Similar** to Jersey TCB / Guernsey fiduciary regulation.

### Money transmission

- **Money services**;
- **Designated** money transmission businesses.

### Pension services

- **Pension** scheme administrators;
- **International** pension structures.

### Cryptocurrency / digital assets

- **Specific** IoM crypto regime;
- **AML / CFT** registration;
- **Specific** Rule Book provisions.

## Cooperation

The FSA cooperates with:

- **JFSC** and **GFSC** — sister Crown Dependencies;
- **UK FCA / PRA**;
- **EU** regulators (selectively post-Brexit);
- **Foreign regulators** through MoUs;
- **IOSCO**, **IAIS**, **FATF** through international
  participation.

## Rule Book

The FSA maintains a **Rule Book**:

- **Sectoral** rules;
- **Specific** to each regulated activity;
- **Detailed** conduct requirements;
- **Enforceable**;
- **Updated** periodically.

## eGaming — separate

**Online gaming** is regulated separately:

- By the **Gambling Supervision Commission (GSC)**;
- Under the **Online Gambling Regulation Act 2001**;
- **Not** under FSA;
- **Specific** Manx eGaming licensing regime.

See [`egaming.md`](./egaming.md).

| Year founded | 1998 | 1987 | 2015 (merged) |
