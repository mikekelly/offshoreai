# Isle of Man Legal System — Overview

## Court structure

### High Court

The IoM **High Court** is the principal civil and criminal
court:

- **First Deemster** as head of judiciary;
- **Deemsters** as principal judges;
- **Specific** Manx terminology;
- **Comparable** in role to Jersey Royal Court.

### Court of Appeal

- IoM **Staff of Government Division** (Court of Appeal);
- **Hears** appeals from High Court;
- **Specific** procedural rules.

### Magistrate's Court

- **Lower court** for minor matters;
- **Magistrate** for criminal and civil minor matters.

### Privy Council

- **Final appeal** from IoM;
- **Same** as Jersey / Guernsey position.

## Sources of law

IoM law sources:

1. **Statute** (Manx legislation — Acts of Tynwald);
2. **Case law** (High Court, Court of Appeal, Privy Council
   in Manx appeals);
3. **English common law** (highly persuasive, more so than
   in Jersey for some areas);
4. **Manx customary law** (specific areas);
5. **Privy Council** decisions binding when in Manx appeals.

## Manx legal heritage

The Isle of Man has **distinct legal heritage**:

- **Norse / Viking** influences (Tynwald goes back over
  1,000 years);
- **Celtic** influences;
- **Specific** Manx customary law;
- **English common law** introduction over centuries;
- **Modern** Manx statutes.

## Manx language

- **Manx Gaelic** has historic significance;
- **Statute** language is English;
- **Specific** Manx terminology in legal documents (Deemster,
  Tynwald, etc.);
- **Ceremonial** use of Manx (Tynwald Day).

## Legal profession

### Manx Bar — Advocates

- **Advocates** of the High Court of Isle of Man;
- **Specific** admission requirements;
- **Distinct** from Jersey advocates;
- **Cross-qualification** routes available.

### Solicitors

- **Manx solicitors** can practise in specified ways;
- **Specific** Manx admission rules.

## English law comparison

IoM legal practice often closer to **English law** than Jersey:

- **Manx law** sometimes labelled "**English law with Manx
  variations**";
- **English case law** more directly applicable in some areas;
- **Less** civil-law influence than Jersey;
- **Manx customary** law less central than Jersey customary
  law.

## Comparison with Jersey

| Feature | Jersey | IoM |
|---|---|---|
| Top court | Royal Court | High Court |
| Head | Bailiff | First Deemster |
| Lay judges | Jurats | No equivalent |
| Legal heritage | Norman | Norse / Celtic / English |
| Customary law role | Substantial (real property, succession, contract) | Limited |
| English law | Persuasive | More directly applicable |
| Procedure | Royal Court Rules 2004 | Rules of High Court |

## Procedure

IoM civil procedure:

- **Rules of the High Court** (IoM);
- **Modelled** closely on English CPR;
- **Less** distinctive procedural concepts than Jersey;
- **Standard** common-law-style procedures.

## Specific Manx institutions

### Tynwald

- **Legislature**;
- **Tripartite** structure;
- **Continuous** for over 1,000 years (claimed);
- **Tynwald Day** annual ceremony.

### Deemsters

- **Principal judges**;
- **Historic** Manx office;
- **Specific** appointment process;
- **Crown** appointed.
