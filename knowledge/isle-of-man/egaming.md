# Isle of Man eGaming — Online Gambling Hub

## Why IoM for eGaming

The Isle of Man is a **leading offshore eGaming
jurisdiction**:

- **Established** since early 2000s;
- **Reputable** licensing regime;
- **Specialist** professional infrastructure;
- **Tax-attractive** for operators;
- **EU** market access constraints (post-Brexit limitations);
- **UK** market via UK Gambling Commission licensing (in
  addition to IoM).

## Principal legislation

- **Online Gambling Regulation Act 2001** — principal
  statute;
- **Subsequent** amendments (2007, 2009, 2015, 2020+);
- **Specific** regulations and guidance;
- **AML / CFT** integration.

## Regulator

The **Gambling Supervision Commission (GSC)**:

- **Independent** of the FSA;
- **Specific** gambling regulation expertise;
- **Issues** licences;
- **Supervises** compliance;
- **Enforces** standards.

## Licensing categories

### Full licence

- **Comprehensive** online gambling operations;
- **All** product types (casino, sports betting, poker,
  lottery);
- **Subject to** full compliance regime.

### Sub-licence

- **Operating under** a full licensee;
- **Limited** scope;
- **Lighter** initial scrutiny.

### Specific product licences

- **B2B licences** for software / platform providers;
- **Network services** licences;
- **Specific** sector licences.

## Licensing requirements

A licence applicant must:

- **Demonstrate** financial soundness;
- **Pass** fit-and-proper assessment of principals;
- **Comply** with technical standards (game fairness, RNG,
  payout);
- **Comply** with AML / CFT obligations;
- **Have** local presence;
- **Meet** player-protection standards;
- **Specific** capital and bond requirements;
- **Specific** technical compliance.

## Tax treatment

For licensed operators:

- **Corporate tax** at IoM standard rates (typically 0%);
- **Gaming Duty** at specific rates per product type;
- **Specific** sectoral tax considerations.

## Market access

IoM-licensed operators can market to:

- **IoM** customers (subject to IoM rules);
- **UK** customers (subject to UK Gambling Commission
  separately licensing);
- **Specific** other jurisdictions (subject to local rules);
- **Cannot** market into countries that prohibit online
  gambling.

## Comparison with other gambling jurisdictions

| Jurisdiction | Status | Specialty |
|---|---|---|
| IoM | UK customs union; established | Comprehensive offering |
| Gibraltar | UK overseas territory | Major operator base |
| Malta | EU member state | EU market access |
| Curaçao | Dutch Caribbean | Cheaper, lighter regime |
| Alderney | Channel Islands | Specific eGaming |
| Jersey / Guernsey | Limited eGaming | Not specialty |

IoM is **competitive** for established operators wanting a
**reputable**, **UK-customs-friendly** jurisdiction.

## Compliance themes

### AML / CFT

- **Strong** AML compliance expected;
- **Specific** Gambling Commission rules;
- **Reporting** obligations;
- **Specific** consideration of high-value players.

### Player protection

- **Responsible gambling** rules;
- **Self-exclusion** mechanisms;
- **Specific** advertising restrictions;
- **Specific** age verification.

### Game fairness

- **Approved** game providers and software;
- **Independent** testing of RNG and payout;
- **Audit** trail of game outcomes;
- **Player** dispute resolution.

### Data protection

- **Specific** GDPR / IoM DP rules;
- **Cross-border** data transfers;
- **Specific** player data handling.

## Industry

The IoM eGaming sector hosts:

- **Major operators** with significant employee bases;
- **Software providers** developing gambling technology;
- **Service providers** (legal, accounting, payment, technical);
- **Specific** Tech / Manx eGaming hubs in Douglas.

## Jersey comparison

Jersey has **limited** equivalent activity:

- **Some** specific gambling-related activities;
- **No** major eGaming specialty;
- **Lottery** governance in Jersey separately;
- **Specific** Channel Islands lottery arrangements (with
  Guernsey, Alderney).
