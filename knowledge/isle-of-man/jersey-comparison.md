# Isle of Man vs Jersey — Comparison

## Constitutional / political

| Feature | Jersey | Isle of Man |
|---|---|---|
| Status | Crown Dependency | Crown Dependency |
| Legislature | States Assembly | Tynwald (tripartite) |
| Head of judiciary | Bailiff | First Deemster |
| Crown representative | Lieutenant Governor | Lieutenant Governor |
| Privy Council appeals | Yes | Yes |
| Currency | Jersey pound (par GBP) | Manx pound (par GBP) |
| Population | ~107k | ~85k |
| Legal heritage | Norman customary | Norse / Manx customary plus English common law |

## Tax

| Feature | Jersey | Isle of Man |
|---|---|---|
| Corporate tax (general) | 0% (zero-ten) | 0% (general) |
| Corporate tax (financial services) | 10% | 0% for most; 10% for banking |
| Corporate tax (specific categories) | 20% (utilities, large retail) | 10% for land / property income; some sector-specific rates |
| Personal tax | 20% standard | 10%/20% bands |
| GST / VAT | 5% Jersey GST | UK VAT 20% (customs union with UK) |
| CGT | None | None |
| IHT | None | None |
| Pillar Two | MCIT 2025 | Specific IoM Pillar Two implementation |

**Personal tax cap** — IoM has a notable cap on personal tax
liability (currently around £200k+ for very high earners),
which Jersey does not have in the same form.

**VAT vs GST** — A major structural difference: IoM is in
**UK VAT customs union** (so 20% VAT applies); Jersey has its
own **5% GST**.

## Companies law

| Feature | Jersey | Isle of Man |
|---|---|---|
| Statute | CJL 1991 | Companies Acts 1931 and 2006 (parallel regimes) |
| Share types | Par / no-par | Par / no-par |
| Cell companies | PCC and ICC | Protected Cell Companies (2004) |
| Specific features | Article 115 cash-flow solvency | 2006 Act offers "modern" company structure with reduced filings |

The **IoM 2006 Companies Act** creates a parallel modern
companies regime — companies can choose between the 1931 Act
(traditional) and 2006 Act (simpler, more flexible) at
incorporation.

## Trust law

| Feature | Jersey | Isle of Man |
|---|---|---|
| Statute | TJL 1984 | Trustee Act 2001 plus customary law |
| Reserved powers | Article 9A | IoM provisions (similar) |
| Firewall | Article 9 | Trust (Amendment) Act 2015 (firewall) |
| Mistake / hastiness | Article 47B-J | IoM provisions (similar) |
| Purpose trusts | Article 12 | Purpose Trusts Act 1996 |

Both jurisdictions have **competitive trust regimes**.
**Jersey** has more developed case law on Article 9A and
Article 47 mistake; **IoM** has specific provisions in some
areas.

## Insurance

| Feature | Jersey | Isle of Man |
|---|---|---|
| Statute | IBL 1996 | Insurance Act 2008 |
| Captive market | Smaller | Significant (larger than Jersey) |
| Specialty | Various | **Life insurance / wealth protection** specialty |
| PCC | Yes | Yes |

**IoM** has a particular specialty in **life insurance** for
wealth-protection / inheritance-planning products — a
distinctive offering.

## Funds

| Feature | Jersey | Isle of Man |
|---|---|---|
| Public funds | CIFL 1988 | Collective Investment Schemes Act 2008 |
| Expert funds | Expert Fund | Specialist Fund |
| Private funds | JPF | Qualifying Fund |
| Listed funds | Listed Fund Guide | Specific listed fund regime |
| AIFMD passporting | NPPR | NPPR |

Both jurisdictions have **competitive fund regimes**;
specific suitability varies by structure.

## Banking

| Feature | Jersey | Isle of Man |
|---|---|---|
| Sector size | Larger | Smaller |
| Number of licensed banks | More | Fewer |
| Specialist offerings | International private banking | International private banking |
| Regulator | JFSC | FSA |

## eGaming (IoM speciality)

- **Online Gambling Regulation Act 2001** plus 2009
  amendments;
- **Gambling Supervision Commission** as regulator;
- **Online gambling** licensed under Manx law for global
  operators;
- **Major** revenue source for IoM;
- Jersey has limited equivalent activity.

## Customary law

| Feature | Jersey | Isle of Man |
|---|---|---|
| Heritage | Norman / French | Norse / Celtic / Manx |
| Customary text | Le Geyt | Manx Statutes Roll, customary materials |
| Language | Historic French | Historic Manx Gaelic |
| Modern application | Real property, succession | Specific areas |

## Legal profession

- **Jersey**: Advocates of the Royal Court;
- **IoM**: Advocates of the High Court (Manx Bar);
- **Different** admission processes;
- **Cross-qualification** routes available;
- **Major** firms have offices in both (some).

## When to choose IoM specifically

Strong factors favouring IoM:

- **eGaming** business (specific specialty);
- **Captive insurance** at certain scales;
- **Life insurance** / wealth-protection products;
- **Personal tax cap** for very high earners;
- **VAT regime** — for businesses needing UK VAT compatibility;
- **Specific** existing IoM relationships.

## When Jersey is the natural choice

- **Trust matters** with developed Article 9 / 9A case law;
- **No GST cap** — Jersey GST at 5% lower than IoM VAT 20%
  for non-VAT-recoverable scenarios;
- **Banking** sector depth;
- **HVR** programme for residence + tax;
- **Specific** existing Jersey relationships.
