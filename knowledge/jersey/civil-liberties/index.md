# Civil Liberties — Jersey

## What this section is about

Jersey's civil-liberties framework — the statutory
structure that:

- **Compels** transparency by public authorities
  (Freedom of Information);
- **Regulates** State surveillance, interception and
  covert investigation (RIPL);
- **Protects** individual rights generally (ECHR via
  the Human Rights Law 2000);
- **Specific** other rights-protection regimes (data
  protection, discrimination, etc.).

Two principal statutes:

- **Freedom of Information (Jersey) Law 2011** (FOI 2011)
  — public-information access right;
- **Regulation of Investigatory Powers (Jersey) Law 2005**
  (RIPL 2005) — control of State surveillance.

Both interact with the **Human Rights (Jersey) Law 2000**
(ECHR Articles 8, 10, 14 in particular) and with the
**Data Protection (Jersey) Law 2018** (DPL).

  information;
- **Anyone subject** to police / regulatory surveillance;
- **Lawyers** advising clients on surveillance / FOI
  issues.

## At a glance

| Topic | Position |
|---|---|
| Public-information access | Freedom of Information (Jersey) Law 2011 |
| Public-authority duty | Scheduled Public Authorities under FOI |
| Default position | Right of access, subject to specified exemptions |
| Exemption types | Absolute (e.g. personal data) and Qualified (subject to public-interest test) |
| Regulator | Jersey Office of the Information Commissioner (JOIC) |
| FOI appeal route | Internal review → Information Commissioner → Royal Court (final) |
| State surveillance | Regulation of Investigatory Powers (Jersey) Law 2005 |
| Surveillance categories | Interception of communications; communications data; covert surveillance; covert human intelligence sources |
| Surveillance authorisation | Codes of Practice; specified ranks of authoriser; warrants for interception |
| Oversight | Investigatory Powers Commissioner (a Court of Appeal judge) |
| Annual report | To the Bailiff |
