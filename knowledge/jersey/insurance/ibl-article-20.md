# IBL 1996 Article 20 — Captive Insurance Permits

Article 20 provides the framework for **captive insurance
permits** — the regime under which most Jersey insurers
operate.

## The provision

A **captive insurer** is an insurer:

- Owned by a parent (or group) for the purpose of insuring the
  risks of the parent / group;
- Operating under a **Category B permit** (typically) with
  captive-specific conditions;
- Subject to **proportionate** prudential supervision.

## Types of captive

### Pure captive

- **Wholly** owned by the parent;
- Insures **only** the parent's / group's risks;
- Most common structure.

### Group captive

- **Owned** by multiple unrelated companies;
- Insures the **owners' risks** collectively;
- Membership-based.

### Sponsored captive

- A **sponsor** company makes the captive available to
  participants;
- Participants insure their risks through "cells" or accounts
  within the captive;
- See PCC / ICC structures
  ([`ibl-part-6-captives.md`](./ibl-part-6-captives.md)).

### Reinsurance captive

- Operates as a **reinsurer** for the parent's risks;
- Reinsures a fronting insurer that issues the policies;
- Particularly useful for cross-jurisdictional risk management.

## Why captives

Captives offer:

- **Risk management** consolidation within a group;
- **Cost efficiency** by avoiding commercial insurer load
  (acquisition costs, profit margin);
- **Access** to reinsurance markets at wholesale rates;
- **Tailored cover** for unique risks;
- **Capital efficiency** through group capital optimisation;
- **Tax efficiency** (subject to home-country rules — captive
  jurisdictions are scrutinised under BEPS and Pillar Two).

## Captive permit specifics

The JFSC's captive permit regime:

- **Proportionate** capital — typically £100,000+ supplemented
  by parent support;
- **Simpler** reporting than full commercial insurers;
- **Faster** licensing — often 3-6 months;
- **Lower** ongoing fees;
- **Captive-specific** governance expectations.

## Jersey's captive market

Jersey is a recognised **captive domicile** alongside:

- **Guernsey** (the largest UK/CD captive domicile);
- **Bermuda** (the global leader);
- **Cayman Islands**;
- **Isle of Man**;
- **Malta** (EU access);
- **US** captive domiciles (Vermont, Hawaii, Utah, etc.).

Jersey's market is smaller than Guernsey's but offers:

- **Quality** regulator (JFSC);
- **Established** professional infrastructure;
- **Tax neutrality** (0% under zero-ten);
- **Reputational** standing.
