# IBL 1996 Article 4 — Permit Required to Carry on Insurance Business

Article 4 is the **operative prohibition** of the IBL.

## The provision

A person **shall not** carry on insurance business in or from
within Jersey unless they hold a **permit** under the Law.

## Territorial scope

The prohibition applies to:

- Insurance business **carried on in Jersey**;
- Insurance business **carried on from within Jersey**
  (where Jersey is the operational base);
- **Jersey-incorporated** insurers writing internationally
  (subject to bilateral and host-country rules).

## Penalty for breach

Unlicensed insurance business:

- Is a **criminal offence**;
- Carries substantial **fines**;
- Carries **imprisonment** up to **7 years** for individual
  perpetrators;
- Triggers **civil consequences** (policies may be voidable;
  recoveries possible against the unlicensed insurer).

## Types of permit

A permit specifies:

- **Category** (A or B per Article 3);
- **Class** of business (long-term / general / both);
- **Specific** lines within class;
- **Conditions** imposed;
- **Effective date** and duration.

## Application

Application is made under **Article 5** with substantive
content including:

- Constitutional documents of the applicant;
- Business plan (lines of business, geography, projected
  volumes);
- Capital (initial and maintained);
- Reinsurance arrangements;
- Reserving methodology;
- Investment policy;
- Principal-person fit-and-proper information;
- Group structure;
- AML / CFT programme (insurers are Schedule 2 supervised);
- Local presence (registered office, management);
- Auditor details;
- Actuary engagement (for long-term and complex general);
- Fees.

## Substantive grant criteria (Article 6)

The JFSC will grant a permit if satisfied that:

- The applicant is **fit and proper**;
- The applicant has **adequate capital**;
- The applicant has **adequate technical provisions / reserves**;
- The applicant's **principal persons** are fit and proper;
- The applicant's **business plan** is sound;
- The applicant's **reinsurance / risk management** is
  adequate;
- The applicant's **systems and controls** are adequate;
- It is **in the public interest** to grant.
