# IBL 1996 Article 6 — Grant of Permit

Article 6 sets out the **substantive conditions** for grant of
an insurance permit.

## The provision

The JFSC may grant a permit if satisfied that:

- The applicant is **fit and proper** to carry on insurance
  business;
- The applicant has **adequate financial resources** (capital
  plus expected reserves);
- The applicant has **adequate technical provisions** for the
  proposed lines of business;
- The applicant's **principal persons** are fit and proper;
- The applicant has **adequate systems and controls**;
- The applicant has **adequate reinsurance / risk transfer**
  arrangements;
- The applicant's **investment policy** is consistent with
  the liabilities;
- The applicant is appropriately **organised in Jersey**
  (registered office, key personnel);
- It is **in the public interest** to grant the permit.

## Fit and proper — applicant

The applicant institution must demonstrate:

- **Good standing** as a corporate entity;
- **Clean regulatory history** with home authority (for
  group-affiliated applicants);
- **Adequate parent / group support** (for captives —
  parental support letter or capital commitment);
- **Reasonable business case** — not opportunistic or
  speculative.

## Fit and proper — principals

Directors, senior officers, controllers, and other principal
persons must be:

- Of **good character and honesty**;
- **Competent** in insurance;
- **Experienced** appropriately to their role;
- of **adequate financial standing**;
- **JFSC-notified** through fit-and-proper notification.

## Capital and solvency

The applicant must meet:

- **Initial capital** at or above prescribed minimum
  (varies by class — long-term, general, captive);
- **Solvency margin** continuing — assets over technical
  provisions and liabilities;
- For Category A and significant Category B: alignment with
  **Solvency II equivalent** principles where the JFSC
  applies them;
- For captives: **proportionate** solvency requirements based
  on group support.

## Technical provisions

The applicant must hold **technical provisions** sufficient
for:

- **Outstanding claims** reserves;
- **Incurred-but-not-reported** (IBNR) reserves;
- **Unearned premium** reserves;
- **Unexpired risk** provisions;
- For long-term: **mathematical reserves** computed
  actuarially.

## Reinsurance

The reinsurance programme must:

- **Adequately** transfer risks beyond the insurer's appetite;
- Use **acceptable counterparties** (rated; or group entities
  with explicit support);
- **Document** retention and treaty terms;
- Be **monitored** for counterparty risk;
- Be **stress-tested** for major loss scenarios.

## Investment policy

Assets backing technical provisions must:

- Be **suitable** to the liabilities (duration, currency,
  liquidity matching);
- Be **diversified**;
- Be **secure** (credit quality);
- Be **liquid** as needed for expected claims patterns;
- Comply with **investment restrictions** in the IBL and
  Codes.
