# Category A Insurance Permit — Jersey

A **Category A permit** under the Insurance Business (Jersey) Law
1996 authorises an insurer to write **general business in
Jersey** — retail and commercial general insurance to Jersey
customers.

## Who needs Category A

- **Retail insurers** offering motor, home, travel, pet, and
  similar general insurance to Jersey residents;
- **Commercial insurers** offering property, liability,
  professional indemnity, and other commercial cover to Jersey
  businesses;
- **Insurers participating** in the local market in any capacity
  involving direct Jersey-customer business.

## Regulatory expectations

Category A insurers face the **highest level** of JFSC scrutiny
within the insurance regime, because of:

- **Direct retail exposure** — Jersey consumers rely on the
  insurer to pay claims;
- **Conduct considerations** — fair treatment of policyholders,
  clear documentation, prompt claims handling;
- **Solvency** — adequate capital and reserves to meet claims;
- **Continuity** — the insurer's continued ability to write and
  service business in Jersey.

## Comparison with Category B

| Feature | Category A | Category B |
|---|---|---|
| Customer base | Jersey retail / commercial | International, captive, specialist |
| Conduct expectations | High | Proportionate to client sophistication |
| Solvency calibration | Retail-protection oriented | Risk-based, tailored |
| Typical structures | Standalone insurers | Captives, cells, reinsurance |
