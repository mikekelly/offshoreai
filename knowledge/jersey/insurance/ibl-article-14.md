# IBL 1996 Article 14 — Technical Provisions

Article 14 requires insurers to hold **technical provisions**
sufficient to meet obligations to policyholders.

## General insurance provisions

For general (non-life) business:

### Outstanding claims reserves (OCR)

- **Known but unpaid** claims;
- **Individually** valued where appropriate;
- **Aggregate** for high-volume small claims.

### Incurred but not reported (IBNR)

- Claims that have **occurred** but **not yet been reported**;
- **Actuarial** estimation;
- **Specific** triangle / development methods.

### Loss adjustment expenses (LAE)

- **Costs** of settling claims;
- **Allocated** and unallocated LAE;
- **Specific** methodology.

### Unearned premium reserves (UPR)

- **Premiums received** for cover yet to expire;
- **Pro-rata** allocation typically.

### Unexpired risk provision (URR)

- **Additional reserve** where future claims will exceed UPR;
- **Specific** Jersey actuarial standards.

## Long-term (life) provisions

For long-term business:

### Mathematical reserves

- **Actuarially-determined** liabilities to policyholders;
- **Best estimate** of policyholder benefits;
- **Margin** for adverse experience;
- **Specific** Jersey actuarial standards.

### Unallocated divisible surplus

- For **with-profit policies**;
- **Specific** Jersey rules.

### Bonus reserves

- For **performance-linked** products.

## Methodology

Technical provisions must be:

- **Calculated** using prudent actuarial / statistical
  methods;
- **Documented** in reserving methodology papers;
- **Reviewed** by external auditors;
- For long-term: **certified** by appointed actuary;
- **Stress-tested** under adverse scenarios.

## JFSC oversight

- **JFSC reviews** reserving in supervisory visits;
- **Specific** Jersey actuarial standards;
- **Specific** sectoral guidance;
- **Specific** stress-test scenarios.
