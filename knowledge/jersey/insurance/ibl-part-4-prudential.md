# IBL 1996 Part 4 — Prudential (residual)

Article 13 (capital and solvency) has its own deep file. This
cluster covers Articles 14-16.

## Article 14 — Technical provisions

An insurer must hold **technical provisions** sufficient to
meet obligations to policyholders:

### General business technical provisions

- **Outstanding claims reserves (OCR)** — known but unpaid
  claims;
- **Incurred-but-not-reported (IBNR)** — claims that have
  occurred but not yet been reported;
- **Loss adjustment expenses (LAE)** — costs of settling
  claims;
- **Unearned premium reserves (UPR)** — premiums received for
  cover yet to expire;
- **Unexpired risk provision (URR)** — additional reserve
  where future claims will exceed UPR.

### Long-term business technical provisions

- **Mathematical reserves** — actuarially-determined
  liabilities to policyholders;
- **Unallocated divisible surplus** — for with-profit
  policies;
- **Bonus reserves** for performance-linked products;
- **Margin** for adverse experience.

### Methodology

Technical provisions must be:

- **Calculated** using prudent actuarial / statistical
  methods;
- **Documented** in reserving methodology papers;
- **Reviewed** by external auditors and (for long-term) by
  the appointed actuary;
- **Stress-tested** under adverse scenarios.

## Article 15 — Investment of assets

Assets backing technical provisions must:

### Match liabilities

- **Currency** matching to liability currencies;
- **Duration** matching to liability profile;
- **Liquidity** matching to expected claim patterns;
- **Inflation** matching for inflation-linked liabilities.

### Be diversified

- **Counterparty** diversification (limits on single
  counterparty);
- **Asset class** diversification;
- **Geographic** diversification;
- **Sector** diversification.

### Be of acceptable quality

- **Investment-grade** credit ratings for fixed-income
  (with exceptions);
- **Established** equity markets;
- **Marketable** real estate;
- **Custody** with regulated custodians.

### Restricted categories

The IBL Code restricts:

- **Concentrated** equity holdings;
- **Speculative** instruments;
- **Connected-party** investments (subject to limits and
  approval);
- **Illiquid** assets above thresholds.

## Article 16 — Reinsurance

The insurer must arrange **adequate reinsurance**:

### Risk transfer

- **Retention** within the insurer's appetite and capital;
- **Layers** above retention transferred to reinsurers;
- **Catastrophe** cover for tail risks;
- **Aggregate** stop-loss for cumulative losses.

### Counterparty risk

- **Rated** reinsurers (typically A- or better);
- **Group reinsurers** subject to parental support
  documentation;
- **Concentration** limits on single reinsurer;
- **Collateral** for non-rated counterparties.

### Treaty terms

- **Documented** in reinsurance treaties;
- **Coverage** clearly defined;
- **Exclusions** understood;
- **Reporting** to reinsurers timely.

### Reinsurance asset valuation

- **Recoverables** from reinsurers form assets backing
  technical provisions;
- Valued with appropriate **credit risk haircuts**;
- **Concentration** disclosure required.
