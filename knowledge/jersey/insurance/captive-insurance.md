# Captive Insurance — Jersey

A **captive insurer** is an insurer **owned by the entity (or
group) whose risks it primarily insures**. Captives are a form
of **structured self-insurance**.

## Why use a captive

Captives serve several strategic purposes:

- **Risk financing** — formalising self-insurance reserves;
- **Coverage for risks** that commercial markets won't write or
  price favourably;
- **Tax efficiency** — premiums may be deductible in the
  insured's home jurisdiction;
- **Risk-pricing discipline** — internal cost of risk becomes
  visible;
- **Access to reinsurance** markets that prefer to deal with
  insurers rather than direct corporates;
- **Multi-line consolidation** — pooling diverse risks across
  group entities.

## Captive structures in Jersey

Jersey hosts several captive structures:

### Pure captive

- **Owned by one parent**;
- Insures that **parent's risks only**;
- Standard Category B permit;
- Most common captive type.

### Group / association captive

- **Owned by multiple unrelated companies** in the same industry
  or with similar risks;
- Pools their **collective risk exposure**;
- More complex governance.

### PCC captive (rent-a-captive)

- A **Protected Cell Company** establishes individual cells for
  separate captive programmes;
- Each cell is effectively a **virtual captive** for its owner;
- **Shared overhead** across cells;
- The PCC structure preserves **legal segregation** between
  cells (Article 127YT of the Companies Law).

### ICC captive

- An **Incorporated Cell Company** with each cell as a
  separately-incorporated body;
- Used where **legal-personality segregation** is preferred over
  PCC statutory segregation (e.g. for foreign-law recognition);
- Each cell has its own JFSC permit.

## Why Jersey for captives

Jersey is competitive for captives because:

- **Established regulatory regime** with experienced insurance
  supervisors at the JFSC;
- **Cell-company flexibility** (Part 18D Companies Law) enabling
  efficient PCC / ICC captive structures;
- **Strong service-provider infrastructure** (captive managers,
  actuaries, lawyers, auditors);
- **Tax efficiency** under zero-ten;
- **Reputation** as a well-regulated jurisdiction;
- **English-language, English-law-influenced** environment.

Major competitors include Guernsey, the Isle of Man, Bermuda,
Cayman, and (for European groups) Luxembourg.
