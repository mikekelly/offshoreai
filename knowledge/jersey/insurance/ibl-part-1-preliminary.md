# IBL 1996 Part 1 — Preliminary

## Article 1 — Interpretation

Article 1 defines key terms:

- **"Insurance business"** — defined in Article 2 by reference
  to contracts of insurance;
- **"Permit"** — authorisation under the Law to carry on
  insurance business (Category A or Category B);
- **"Category A"** — domestic / Jersey-policyholder business
  permit;
- **"Category B"** — international / non-Jersey-policyholder
  permit (captive / international insurer);
- **"Insurer"** — a person carrying on insurance business
  under a permit;
- **"Long-term business"** — life, annuity, permanent health,
  capital redemption;
- **"General business"** — non-life classes;
- **"Reinsurance"** — insurance of insurers;
- **"Captive insurer"** — an insurer underwriting risks of its
  parent / group;
- **"Cell company"** — a Jersey protected-cell or
  incorporated-cell company underwriting through cells.

The Law and JFSC Code distinguish between:

- **Permit Category A** — see [category-a-permit.md](./category-a-permit.md);
- **Permit Category B** — see [category-b-permit.md](./category-b-permit.md).
