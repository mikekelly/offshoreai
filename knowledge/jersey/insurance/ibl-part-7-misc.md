# IBL 1996 Part 7 — Enforcement and Miscellaneous

Part 7 consolidates enforcement, administrative architecture,
and citation.

## Civil penalties

The JFSC may impose **civil penalties** for:

- **Breach** of permit conditions;
- **Breach** of continuing obligations;
- **Failure** to comply with directions;
- **Material** AML / CFT failures;
- **Failure** to provide information / cooperate.

Penalties are calibrated to severity, duration, customer harm,
and institution size.

## Public statements

The JFSC may issue **public statements**:

- Naming enforced firms / individuals;
- Setting out findings;
- Issuing warnings about unauthorised activity.

## Criminal offences

Specific offences include:

- **Unlicensed** insurance business (Article 4);
- **False statements** in applications / notifications;
- **Failure** to provide accurate returns;
- **Failure** to comply with directions;
- **Insurance fraud** (separate from the general criminal
  law).

## Appeals

Decisions are appealable to the **Royal Court**.

## Regulations

The Minister can make **regulations** for:

- **Forms** and **fees**;
- **Specific** prudential standards;
- **Conduct** rules;
- **Implementation** of international standards (IAIS, IOPS).

## JFSC Insurance Code of Practice

The Code is the operational standard for Jersey insurers:

- **Prudential** rules and ratios;
- **Governance** expectations;
- **Conduct** rules (more detailed than Part 5 of the Law);
- **Outsourcing** standards;
- **Captive-specific** provisions.

## Insurance intermediaries

Beyond insurers, **intermediaries** (brokers, agents,
managers) are regulated under the:

- **Financial Services (Jersey) Law 1998** for **General
  Insurance Mediation Business (GIMB)** registration;
- Separate from the IBL but interconnected.

See [`../financial-regulation/gimb.md`](../../../jersey/financial-regulation/gimb.md)
*(if exists)* or the FSL Article-9 wiki.

## Citation

The Law is cited as the **"Insurance Business (Jersey) Law
1996"**.

It came into force on **1 January 1996** with substantial
amendments through 2020s aligning with IAIS / Solvency II /
JFSC supervisory enhancements.

## International cooperation

The JFSC participates in:

- **International Association of Insurance Supervisors (IAIS)**
  as a member;
- **MoUs** with overseas regulators;
- **Information sharing** under bilateral arrangements;
- **Group supervision** for international groups.
