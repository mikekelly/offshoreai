# IBL 1996 Part 3 — Supervision

## Article 9 — Continuing obligations

An insurer must, on a continuing basis:

- **Maintain** the conditions of its permit;
- **Hold** adequate capital and technical provisions;
- **Maintain** systems and controls;
- **Conduct** business in accordance with the JFSC Insurance
  Code of Practice;
- **Submit** prescribed returns:
  - **Annual** audited financial statements;
  - **Annual** prudential return (solvency, technical
    provisions, reinsurance);
  - **Quarterly** management accounts for larger or higher-
    risk insurers;
  - **Actuarial** report (long-term mandatory; general
    increasingly required);
- **Notify** material changes (principals, scope, capital,
  reinsurance, ownership);
- **Cooperate** with JFSC supervision.

## Article 10 — Information powers

The JFSC may require an insurer to:

- **Produce documents**;
- **Provide information**;
- **Explain** records or transactions;
- **Confirm** specified facts;
- **Within** a specified timeframe.

Failure to comply is an offence and grounds for supervisory
action.

## Article 11 — Inspections

The JFSC may inspect:

- **Premises** of the insurer;
- **Records** and IT systems;
- **Staff** (interviews);
- **Outsourced functions** at provider locations.

Inspections may be:

- **Routine** (typically every 1-3 years);
- **Themed** (sector-wide on specific topics);
- **Cause-based**;
- **Joint** with home regulator for groups.

## Article 12 — Directions

The JFSC may issue **directions** including:

- **Cease** specified activities;
- **Take** specified steps;
- **Cease writing** new business;
- **Run off** existing business;
- **Strengthen** capital, reinsurance, or governance;
- **Reorganise** outsourcing arrangements.

Directions are enforceable and subject to appeal to the Royal
Court.

## Insurance Code of Practice

The JFSC Insurance Code of Practice supplements the Law:

- **Detailed** prudential requirements;
- **Conduct of business** rules;
- **Governance** expectations;
- **Outsourcing** standards;
- **Treating customers fairly** principles (where applicable
  to Category A);
- **Captive-specific** provisions.

The Code is **enforceable** and is the principal operational
standard for Jersey insurers.
