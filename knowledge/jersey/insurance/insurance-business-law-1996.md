# Insurance Business (Jersey) Law 1996 — Insurance-Sector View

This file covers the **Insurance Business (Jersey) Law 1996** from
the **insurance-sector operational perspective**. For the
**regulator-framework perspective**, see
[`../financial-regulation/insurance-business-law-1996.md`](../financial-regulation/insurance-business-law-1996.md).

## Permit categories

The Law distinguishes:

- **Category A permit** — for insurers writing **general
  business in Jersey** (retail and commercial general insurance
  to Jersey customers);
- **Category B permit** — for insurers writing **specified
  international or specialist business** — most commonly
  **captive insurance** structures;
- **Specific permits** for **long-term business** (life
  insurance, pensions);
- **Reinsurance** permits.

## Operating obligations

Jersey insurers must:

- Maintain **adequate solvency capital** proportionate to the
  risk written;
- Establish appropriate **technical provisions** (reserves);
- Implement **risk-management frameworks**;
- Have **fit-and-proper** senior management and underwriting
  staff;
- Comply with the **Insurance Code of Practice**;
- File regular **regulatory returns** to the JFSC;
- Pay annual **JFSC fees**.

## Captive insurance — Jersey's strength

Jersey is particularly strong in **captive insurance**:

- **Pure captives** for single-parent risks;
- **Group captives** for industry consortia;
- **Cell-company captives** using PCC / ICC structures;
- **Reinsurance captives**.

Many of these are **Category B** permits.

## The reinsurance market

Jersey hosts a notable **reinsurance sector**, serving:

- **Group reinsurance** programmes;
- **Specialty reinsurance** for niche risks;
- **Run-off** management for closed reinsurance portfolios.
