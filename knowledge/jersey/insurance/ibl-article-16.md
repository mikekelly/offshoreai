# IBL 1996 Article 16 — Reinsurance

Article 16 requires Jersey insurers to maintain **adequate
reinsurance**.

## Risk transfer

The reinsurance programme must:

- **Adequately transfer** risks beyond the insurer's appetite;
- **Retention** within the insurer's capital and risk
  appetite;
- **Layers** above retention transferred to reinsurers;
- **Catastrophe cover** for tail risks;
- **Aggregate stop-loss** for cumulative losses.

## Counterparty risk

Reinsurance counterparties:

- **Rated reinsurers** (typically A- or better);
- **Group reinsurers** subject to parental support
  documentation;
- **Concentration limits** on single reinsurer;
- **Collateral** for non-rated counterparties.

## Treaty terms

- **Documented** in reinsurance treaties;
- **Coverage** clearly defined;
- **Exclusions** understood;
- **Reporting** to reinsurers timely.

## Reinsurance recoverables

Recoverables from reinsurers form assets backing technical
provisions:

- **Valued** with appropriate credit-risk haircuts;
- **Concentration** disclosure required;
- **Aging** monitoring;
- **Specific** Jersey actuarial standards.
