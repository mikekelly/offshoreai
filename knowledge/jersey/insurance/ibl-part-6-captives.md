# IBL 1996 Part 6 — Captive Insurance (residual: Article 21)

Article 20 (captive permits) has its own deep file. This
cluster covers Article 21 and the cell-company captive
arrangements.

## Article 21 — PCC / ICC captives

Jersey's **Protected Cell Companies (PCC)** and **Incorporated
Cell Companies (ICC)** regimes (under the Companies Law and
related legislation) are used extensively for captives:

### Protected cell companies (PCC)

- **Single legal entity** with multiple "cells";
- **Cell assets** statutorily segregated from other cells'
  and from the core's assets;
- Each cell can issue policies, hold assets, write business;
- **Cell-specific** capital and reserves;
- Insolvency of one cell does not propagate to other cells.

### Incorporated cell companies (ICC)

- **Multiple incorporated cells**, each itself a body
  corporate;
- **Separate legal personality** for each cell;
- Even stronger segregation than PCC;
- More complex governance (each cell has directors).

## Captive cell uses

### Sponsored captive

- A sponsor company establishes a PCC / ICC;
- **Participants** insure their risks through a cell;
- Each cell's underwriting is **legally segregated** from
  other cells;
- Cost-efficient route for medium-sized companies to
  access captive benefits without the cost of a wholly-owned
  captive.

### Rent-a-captive (legacy term)

- Synonym for sponsored captive;
- Less commonly used now.

### Segregated portfolio company (US / Cayman)

- US / Cayman equivalent of PCC;
- Recognised internationally.

## Permit structure for cells

The IBL requires:

- **Permit** for the cell company (core);
- **Cell-specific** approval for each cell's activity;
- **Capital** and **technical provisions** at cell level;
- **Reporting** at cell level;
- **Governance** at cell level (committees, decisions).

## Advantages

- **Lower** entry cost than standalone captive;
- **Quicker** to establish;
- **Flexible** for participants of varying scale;
- **Independent** legal segregation (key in insolvency
  scenarios).

## Disadvantages / cautions

- **Sponsor dependence** — quality of sponsor matters;
- **Less control** for cell owners than wholly-owned captive;
- **Tax characterisation** — cells need careful
  characterisation in home jurisdictions (especially US look-
  through rules);
- **Substance** considerations — cells may have minimal
  substance, which can be problematic under modern substance
  rules.
