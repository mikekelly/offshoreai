# IBL 1996 Part 5 — Conduct of Business

Part 5 governs **conduct of business** — the consumer / policy-
holder-facing rules.

## Article 17 — Conduct of business

An insurer must:

- **Treat policyholders fairly** (TCF principle);
- **Avoid conflicts** between insurer interests and policy-
  holder interests, or manage them transparently;
- **Communicate** clearly and accurately;
- **Sell** appropriate products to policyholders;
- **Disclose** material features and exclusions;
- **Maintain** policyholder records;
- **Handle** complaints fairly and promptly.

## Article 18 — Information to policyholders

The insurer must provide:

### Pre-sale

- **Product information**;
- **Premiums** and how calculated;
- **Cover** scope and exclusions;
- **Cancellation** rights (cooling-off period for some
  products);
- **Complaints** procedure;
- **Compensation** scheme details (where applicable).

### At sale

- **Policy document**;
- **Schedule** showing specific cover;
- **Premium** receipt;
- **Renewal** terms.

### During the term

- **Material changes** to terms;
- **Renewal** invitations;
- **Premium** statements;
- **Policy** updates and endorsements;
- **Claims** procedures.

### At claim

- **Claims** acknowledgment;
- **Decisions** on cover within reasonable timeframe;
- **Reasons** for declined claims;
- **Complaints** route if claim disputed.

## Article 19 — Claims handling

Claims must be handled:

- **Promptly** — acknowledgments within prescribed periods;
- **Fairly** — applying policy terms reasonably;
- **Transparently** — communicating decisions and reasons;
- **Without** unreasonable delay;
- With **records** maintained for review.

## Category A vs Category B

The conduct rules apply with **different intensity**:

### Category A (domestic)

- **Full retail** protection regime;
- **TCF** principle applied robustly;
- **Vulnerability** considerations;
- **Strict** complaints handling.

### Category B (international)

- **Reduced** retail consumer protection (no Jersey retail
  policyholders);
- **Commercial / institutional** policyholder protections
  primarily;
- **Captive context** — parent / group as policyholder, very
  different dynamics.

## Complaints

A complaint procedure must:

- Provide for **easy access** by policyholders;
- **Investigate** complaints promptly;
- **Resolve** or escalate;
- **Report** complaint statistics to the JFSC;
- **Refer** appropriately to the **Channel Islands Financial
  Ombudsman** for eligible disputes.
