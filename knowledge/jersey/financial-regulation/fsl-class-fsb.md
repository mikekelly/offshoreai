# FSL 1998 — Fund Services Business (FSB)

Fund Services Business (FSB) covers **services provided to
collective investment funds** — managers, administrators,
custodians, and other functionaries.

## Activities within scope

FSB includes services **to a fund** as defined in the CIFL
1988:

### Manager

- **Acting as manager** of a fund (operator);
- **Investment management** of fund assets (often the same
  entity or a separately appointed investment manager);
- **Strategic decisions** on behalf of the fund.

### Administrator

- **NAV calculation**;
- **Investor register**;
- **Subscription / redemption processing**;
- **Investor reporting**;
- **AML / CFT on investors**.

### Custodian / depositary

- **Asset safekeeping**;
- **Settlement of trades**;
- **Cash management**;
- For AIFMD-compliant funds, **oversight** role over manager.

### Registrar / transfer agent

- **Investor register maintenance**;
- **Transfer processing**;
- **Distribution**.

### Other prescribed services

- **Distributor / placement agent**;
- **Trustee** (for unit trust funds);
- **Fund accountant**;
- **Compliance officer** for fund (in some structures).

## Activities outside scope

- **Services to non-fund clients** — those are Investment
  Business or TCB depending on nature;
- **Self-managed corporate fund directors** — within TCB
  rather than FSB in some configurations (factual analysis);
- **Banking activities** for fund assets — within Banking
  Business Law 1991;
- **Auditing** — within accountancy regulation, not FSL;
- **Legal services** — within legal-profession regulation.

## Coverage of fund regimes

FSB authorisation is required to provide services to:

### CIFL public funds

- **Article 7 of CIFL** also requires permit for functionaries
  of public funds — typically held alongside FSB
  registration;
- **Cross-cutting** authorisations.

### Non-public funds

- **Expert Fund** — FSB required for service providers;
- **Eligible Investor Fund** — FSB required;
- **Jersey Private Fund** — FSB required (or specific
  exemptions);
- **Listed Fund** — FSB required;
- **Unregulated funds** — limited service-provider regulation.

## Registration requirements

An FSB applicant must:

- **Register** under Article 7-9 of FSL;
- Be **fit and proper** (corporate and principal persons);
- Maintain **adequate capital** (proportionate to scale);
- Have **adequate systems and controls**;
- Have **Jersey substance** (registered office, qualifying
  staff);
- Comply with **AML / CFT** programme requirements;
- Comply with **continuing obligations**;
- Pay **application and annual fees**.

## Code of Practice for FSB

The JFSC issues a **Code of Practice for Fund Services
Business**. It addresses:

- **Governance** of FSBs;
- **Risk management**;
- **Conduct of business** for fund services;
- **CDD / AML / CFT**;
- **Custody / depositary** standards;
- **Outsourcing**;
- **Pricing-error / fair-valuation** practices;
- **Cross-border** considerations.

## Specific FSB issues

### AIFMD compliance for EU distribution

- Jersey-based FSBs servicing EU-distributed funds operate
  under **NPPR** (National Private Placement Regime) regimes
  in EU Member States;
- **No passporting** — country-by-country compliance;
- **Specific** AIFMD-equivalent rules apply (depositary
  oversight, disclosure, transparency);
- **JFSC opt-in** AIFMD regime available for Jersey FSBs.

### Group structures

- Many Jersey FSBs are **subsidiaries** of international fund
  services groups;
- **Group-wide** AML / risk programmes operate alongside
  Jersey-specific compliance;
- **Outsourcing** to non-Jersey group affiliates subject to
  Code requirements.

### Substance considerations

- FSB is **in-scope** for the Substance Law 2019 (as "fund
  management" relevant activity);
- **Substance requirements** — directed in Jersey, qualified
  employees, premises, CIGAs in Jersey;
- **Annual substance return** to Comptroller of Revenue.
