# FSL 1998 Article 2 — Meaning of "Financial Service Business"

Article 2 is the **gateway** to the FSL regime — defining
which activities are regulated.

## The provision

A person carries on **financial service business** if they
carry on, by way of business, any activity in one of the
**five regulated activity classes**:

- **Investment business** (Schedule 2 activities);
- **Trust company business** (Schedule 3 activities);
- **Fund services business** (Schedule 4 activities);
- **General insurance mediation business**;
- **Money service business**.

Each class has its own scope, threshold, and exemptions.

## The five classes

### Investment business

See [`fsl-class-investment-business.md`](./fsl-class-investment-business.md).

Includes:

- **Investment advice** to clients;
- **Discretionary investment management** of client portfolios;
- **Dealing in investments** as principal or agent;
- **Arranging deals** in investments;
- **Safeguarding and administering** investments (custody).

Excludes: services to **collective investment funds** (which
fall within FSB), advice strictly **incidental** to other
professional services, and various specific exemptions.

### Trust company business (TCB)

See [`fsl-class-tcb.md`](./fsl-class-tcb.md).

Includes:

- **Acting as trustee** of an express trust;
- **Acting as administrator** of trusts;
- **Acting as a foundation council member or guardian**;
- **Acting as director / secretary** of a company on
  commercial basis;
- **Acting as registered office provider** on commercial
  basis;
- **Acting as nominee** for commercial purposes.

The TCB class captures most of the **Jersey fiduciary industry**
(Mourant, Ogier Global, Carey Olsen Trust, IQ-EQ, Hawksford,
etc.).

### Fund services business (FSB)

See [`fsl-class-fsb.md`](./fsl-class-fsb.md).

Includes services **to collective investment funds**:

- **Manager**;
- **Investment manager / adviser**;
- **Administrator**;
- **Custodian / depositary**;
- **Registrar / transfer agent**;
- **Other prescribed services**.

### General insurance mediation business (GIMB)

See [`fsl-class-gimb.md`](./fsl-class-gimb.md).

Insurance broking — arranging, advising on, or otherwise
mediating insurance contracts.

### Money service business (MSB)

See [`fsl-class-msb.md`](./fsl-class-msb.md).

Money transmission, FX, payment services that are not
banking.

## "By way of business"

A common feature of all five classes: the activity must be
**by way of business**:

- **Repeated** / habitual;
- **For remuneration** (direct or indirect);
- Conducted in a **commercial** manner;
- Distinguished from **occasional** / **gratuitous** acts.

The "by way of business" threshold means **one-off
transactions** for a family member, or **gratuitous advice**
without commercial structure, generally falls outside the
regime.

## Exclusions

Article 2 (with Schedules and Orders) **excludes**:

- Activities by **specific exempt persons** (Crown bodies,
  certain professionals acting in their primary capacity);
- **Banking activities** (regulated under BBL 1991);
- **Insurance underwriting** (regulated under IBL 1996);
- **Specific intra-group** activities;
- **De minimis** thresholds for certain activities.

## Consequences of being financial service business

Once an activity is within Article 2:

- **Article 7 registration** is mandatory;
- **Fit and proper** assessment of principals;
- **Codes of Practice** apply;
- **AML / CFT** regime applies (FSL businesses are Schedule
  2 supervised);
- **JFSC supervision** and enforcement;
- **Annual fees and reporting**.

## Penalty for unlicensed activity

Carrying on financial service business without registration
is a **criminal offence** with substantial penalties (Article
7) and triggers civil consequences (contracts may be voidable;
disgorgement of profits).
