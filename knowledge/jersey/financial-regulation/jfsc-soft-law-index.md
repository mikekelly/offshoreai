# JFSC Soft Law — Index

The **JFSC's soft-law surface** is the body of publications
the JFSC produces that **shape supervisory expectations**
without being directly enforceable as statute or as a
[Code of Practice](./codes-of-practice.md). It is the largest
single signal of the regulator's current focus, and a regulated
business's compliance programme is incomplete without it.

This is the **hub** for the soft-law layer. Per-surface
indexes below organise the JFSC's publications by type.

## What counts as soft law

| Surface | Indexed at | Effect |
|---|---|---|
| **Thematic reviews** | [`./jfsc-thematic-reviews-index.md`](./jfsc-thematic-reviews-index.md) | Findings from supervisory sampling across the industry; sets the bar for what the JFSC considers adequate practice |
| **Public statements / enforcement notices** | [`./jfsc-public-statements-index.md`](./jfsc-public-statements-index.md) | Outcomes of enforcement action against named firms or individuals; the clearest signal of what crosses the line |
| **Industry updates** | [`./jfsc-industry-updates-index.md`](./jfsc-industry-updates-index.md) | Periodic newsletters communicating policy direction, supervisory priorities, and operational reminders |
| **Director General speeches** | [`./jfsc-dg-speeches-index.md`](./jfsc-dg-speeches-index.md) | Strategic-direction signals from JFSC leadership |
| **Consultations and feedback statements** | (treated within the affected Code or Handbook surface) | The process by which Codes and Handbook updates are developed |

## Why soft law is load-bearing

Three reasons it deserves a dedicated content surface:

### 1. Enforcement risk is mapped here

A regulated business that satisfies the statute and the Code
to the letter but operates **outside** the JFSC's currently-
articulated supervisory expectations is at material
enforcement risk. The expectations are surfaced primarily
through thematic reviews and public statements.

### 2. Demonstrates regulatory direction

The JFSC's strategic priorities — currently digitalisation,
proportionate risk-based supervision, sustainable finance,
financial-crime effectiveness — are signalled through DG
speeches and industry updates before they crystallise into
Code updates. A business that reads soft-law signals can
adapt before enforcement action.

### 3. Calibrates the Code

The JFSC's
[Codes of Practice](./codes-of-practice.md) are written at
principle-and-rule level. The **operational application** of
the Code in any specific situation is shaped by what the JFSC
has said and done since the Code was published — which lives
in the soft-law layer.

## Freshness discipline

Soft-law content is **explicitly date-sensitive**. Each entry
under the per-surface indexes carries an `as_of` date (parallel
to the frontier discipline in
[`../../frontier/`](../../frontier/)). When a public statement
is superseded, a thematic review's findings are addressed, or
a DG speech's predictions are overtaken, the entry is updated
or archived.

The JFSC website is the **authoritative source**; corpus
entries are summaries with citations.

For any specific operational question, **read the relevant
per-surface index alongside** the applicable
[Code of Practice section file](./codes-of-practice.md) and
the
[AML/CFT Handbook section file](../aml-cft/aml-cft-handbook.md) —
the three layers (Code, Handbook, soft law) together describe
what the JFSC expects.
