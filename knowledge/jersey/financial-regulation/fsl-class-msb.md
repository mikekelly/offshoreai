# FSL 1998 — Money Service Business (MSB)

MSB covers **payment-services** activities outside the
banking regime — money transmission, FX, e-money issuance
(in some configurations), and similar.

## Activities within scope

MSB includes:

- **Money transmission** — transferring funds on behalf of
  customers without holding deposits;
- **Currency exchange** — bureau de change activities;
- **Payment services** — issuing or acquiring payment
  instruments;
- **E-money issuance** in some configurations (where it does
  not engage banking law);
- **Crypto-asset** transmission may engage MSB / VASP
  considerations depending on facts.

## Activities outside scope

MSB excludes:

- **Banking** (taking deposits and lending) — regulated under
  BBL 1991;
- **Investment business** — regulated as Investment Business
  under FSL;
- **Insurance** — regulated under IBL / GIMB;
- **Specific exempt** activities (in-house treasury, low-value
  consumer activities).

## Crypto / VASP overlap

Jersey's **virtual asset service provider (VASP)** regime
sits adjacent to MSB:

- **VASPs** are regulated through specific FSL / AML rules;
- **Crypto-to-fiat** conversion is typically MSB / VASP;
- **Pure custody** of crypto can engage different rules;
- **Stablecoin** issuance can engage banking, MSB, or VASP
  depending on substance.

The regulatory landscape continues to evolve in this area.

## Registration requirements

An MSB applicant must:

- **Register** under Article 7-9 of FSL;
- Be **fit and proper**;
- Maintain **adequate** capital (calibrated to volume / risk);
- Hold **client money** appropriately (typically through
  segregated accounts at registered banks);
- Have **adequate** AML / CFT programme;
- Have **Jersey** presence;
- Comply with **continuing obligations**.

## Code of Practice

The JFSC issues guidance for MSBs:

- **Client money** segregation;
- **AML / CFT** for high-velocity transactions;
- **Settlement** standards;
- **Transaction monitoring**;
- **Cross-border** considerations;
- **Sanctions** screening.

## AML / CFT emphasis

MSBs are high-priority for AML scrutiny:

- **High transaction volumes** create monitoring challenges;
- **Cash-intensive** activity attracts ML risk;
- **Cross-border** money transmission attracts terrorist-
  financing risk;
- **Customer base** can include higher-risk individuals.

The MLO 2008 and JFSC AML / CFT Handbook apply with
particular intensity.

## Jersey market

The Jersey MSB sector is **relatively small**:

- Few **dedicated** MSB firms;
- **Bank-provided** money transmission for retail customers
  is more common (within banking);
- **Specialist** firms exist for crypto / payment services /
  bureau de change;
- The sector is **growing** with fintech innovation.
