# Code of Practice for Trust Company Business — Index

This is the agent-facing entry point for the **JFSC Code of
Practice for Trust Company Business** (current version effective
**1 January 2021**), the binding conduct standard for every Jersey
business registered to carry on trust company business under the
[Financial Services (Jersey) Law 1998](./financial-services-jersey-law-1998.md).

For the perimeter of the **TCB regulated activity class** — what
counts as TCB, who must register, who is exempt — read
[`fsl-class-tcb.md`](./fsl-class-tcb.md) first. This file is the
**conduct surface** the JFSC supervises against once a person is
registered.

## The Code's principles

The Code is structured around a set of high-level **principles**
the JFSC expects every registered TCB to satisfy. The principles
articulate the conduct standard at the level of the whole
business; the substantive sections operationalise them.

The principle headings (as currently published) are recorded in
[`cop-tcb-principles.md`](./cop-tcb-principles.md) *(stub —
verify against current JFSC publication before citing the
specific wording of any principle)*. They cover, in summary:

- **Integrity** — honest and fair conduct.
- **Skill, care and diligence** — competent execution.
- **Organisation and control** — governance, systems, and a
  designated compliance officer.
- **Risk management** — proportionate to the nature and scale
  of the business.
- **Customer treatment** — fair, suitable, and informed.
- **Financial soundness** — adequate capital and solvency.
- **Cooperation with the JFSC** — open, accurate, timely.
- **Market behaviour and integrity** — fair dealing.
