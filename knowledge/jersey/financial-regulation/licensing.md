# Licensing and Fit-and-Proper — Jersey

This file covers the **JFSC licensing / registration regime**
and the **fit-and-proper** standards applied to **principal
persons** and **key persons** of regulated entities.

## Registration vs licensing

The regulatory Laws use the term **"registration"** (FS(J)L
1998) or **"permit"** (Banking, Insurance) for what other
jurisdictions call licensing. The substance is the same — a
person needs JFSC permission to carry on the regulated activity.

## The application process

A typical application includes:

- A **completed JFSC application form** in prescribed format;
- A **business plan** describing the proposed activities;
- **Three-year financial projections**;
- **Principal-person questionnaires** for each principal person;
- **Key-person questionnaires** for each key person;
- **Group structure charts** showing ultimate beneficial
  ownership;
- **Source-of-wealth / source-of-funds** information for
  controllers;
- **Operational and risk management policies**;
- **AML / CFT policies**;
- **References** from prior employers and regulators;
- **Application fees** and prescribed forms.

## Principal persons and key persons

The regulatory Laws distinguish:

- **Principal persons** — those with significant control or
  influence over the regulated entity (controllers,
  shareholders above prescribed thresholds, ultimate beneficial
  owners);
- **Key persons** — senior management performing key functions
  (directors, compliance officer, MLRO, head of operations).

Both groups undergo **fit-and-proper assessment**.

## The fit-and-proper test

The JFSC assesses fit-and-proper status by reference to:

- **Honesty, integrity, and reputation**;
- **Competence and capability** for the role;
- **Financial soundness**;
- **Regulatory history** in any jurisdiction;
- **Criminal record**;
- **Conflicts of interest**;
- **Track record** in prior similar roles.

The test is **applied at the level of the individual** and
again at the level of the **organisation** as a whole.

## Outcomes of the assessment

The JFSC may:

- **Approve** the application;
- **Approve subject to conditions** (e.g. capital, board
  composition, monitoring);
- **Refuse** the application.

Refusals are subject to **appeal rights** in the Royal Court.

## Ongoing fit-and-proper

The test is **continuing**:

- Changes to principal persons or key persons require **prior
  JFSC notification** (in some cases prior approval);
- Adverse events (criminal conviction, regulatory action,
  insolvency) trigger fit-and-proper review;
- The JFSC can **remove individuals** from regulated roles via
  enforcement.

## Outsourcing of administration

Many small firms outsource compliance and administration to
larger providers. The Codes require:

- **Outsourcing agreement** in writing;
- **Continued responsibility** of the regulated entity for the
  outsourced function;
- **JFSC notification** for material outsourcing;
- **Right of audit** for the regulated entity and the JFSC;
- **Exit strategy** for ending the outsourcing.
