# JFSC Codes of Practice

The **JFSC Codes of Practice** are **detailed conduct standards**
issued by the JFSC under powers conferred by the principal
regulatory Laws.

## Where Codes sit in the regulatory hierarchy

The regulatory framework has **layers**:

1. **Principal Laws** — FS(J)L 1998, Banking Business Law 1991,
   CIF Law 1988, Insurance Business Law 1996, JFSC Law 1998;
2. **Regulations and Orders** — subordinate legislation made by
   the States or Minister;
3. **JFSC Codes of Practice** — JFSC-issued conduct standards;
4. **JFSC Guidance** — non-mandatory guidance on interpretation
   and application.

Codes are **mandatory** for registered persons — breach attracts
enforcement consequences.

## Codes by class

The JFSC issues separate Codes for each regulated class:

- **Code of Practice for Banking Business**;
- **Code of Practice for Investment Business**;
- **Code of Practice for Trust Company Business**;
- **Code of Practice for Fund Services Business**;
- **Code of Practice for General Insurance Mediation Business**;
- **Code of Practice for Money Service Business**;
- **Code of Practice for Insurance Business**;
- **Code of Practice for Certified Funds**.

## Common Code structure

A typical Code addresses:

- **Principles** of conduct (integrity, due care, etc.);
- **Governance** — board composition, roles, accountability;
- **Compliance function** — designated compliance officer,
  reporting lines;
- **Risk management**;
- **Customer / client treatment** — CDD, suitability, complaints;
- **Outsourcing** — when permitted, what conditions;
- **Conflict-of-interest management**;
- **Record-keeping**;
- **Reporting** to the JFSC;
- **Capital adequacy** and financial resources;
- **AML / CFT** — overlap with the AML/CFT Handbook.

## Customer / Client Risk Profile

A common Code requirement is **customer / client risk profiling**:

- Identifying and categorising customers by risk (low / medium /
  high / unacceptable);
- Applying **proportionate due diligence** based on the
  classification;
- **Monitoring** the customer relationship for changes in risk.

## Outsourcing

Codes typically permit outsourcing of operational functions
subject to:

- **Risk assessment** of the outsourcing arrangement;
- **Continuing responsibility** of the regulated entity for the
  outsourced function;
- **JFSC consent** for outsourcing of significant functions to
  non-Jersey providers;
- **Access** by the JFSC to outsourced records and providers.

## Updates

The JFSC consults on **Code updates** periodically — typically
through formal consultation papers, industry workshops, and a
final consultation feedback statement. Updates take effect on
prescribed implementation dates.
