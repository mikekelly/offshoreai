# JFSC Public Statements & Enforcement Notices — Index

JFSC **public statements** and **enforcement notices** are
the published outcomes of enforcement action against named
firms and individuals. They are the **clearest possible
signal** of what crosses the supervisory line and what the
consequences are.

This is the **per-surface index** for these publications.
Individual entries below are seeded as the editor populates
them from the
[JFSC News page](https://www.jerseyfsc.org/news-and-events/news/).

## What this surface includes

| Publication type | What it signals |
|---|---|
| **Civil financial penalty notice** | A monetary penalty imposed under the regulatory Law; published amount + scope of breach |
| **Public statement** | Censure of conduct without monetary penalty, or alongside one |
| **Direction notice** | A binding direction issued to a regulated person to take or refrain from action |
| **Conditions on registration** | New / amended conditions imposed on the person's registration |
| **Refusal / revocation of registration** | The strongest outcome — denial of authorisation or removal from the register |
| **Prohibition order** | A bar on an individual holding a regulated function in Jersey financial services |

The enforcement framework itself is at
[`./enforcement.md`](./enforcement.md); this index records
**specific outcomes**, dated.

## Why enforcement publications are load-bearing

Three operational uses:

1. **Calibrating risk appetite** — knowing the penalty
   distribution for specific breach types informs internal
   risk-management cost-benefit decisions.
2. **Identifying live red lines** — JFSC enforcement themes
   surface what the regulator is currently prioritising
   (sanctions screening failures, PEP CDD gaps,
   beneficial-ownership failures, outsourcing weakness).
3. **Comparing against own controls** — when an enforcement
   notice describes the failure pattern at the censured firm,
   the regulated business asks "would we have done better?" —
   and adjusts controls if not.

## How entries should be structured

Each public-statement entry (when populated) should carry:

- **Date** of the notice;
- **Subject** — firm and / or individual (named in the
  notice — public-record citation is acceptable per the
  tenant-neutrality rule in CONVENTIONS.md);
- **Breach summary** — what the JFSC found;
- **Outcome** — penalty amount, conditions, prohibition, etc.;
- **Relevant Code / Handbook provisions** breached;
- **Cross-references** to thematic reviews or industry
  updates that anticipated the issue;
- **`as_of`** date.
