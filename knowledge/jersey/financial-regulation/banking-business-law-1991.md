# Banking Business (Jersey) Law 1991

The **Banking Business (Jersey) Law 1991** is the principal
statute regulating **deposit-taking businesses** in Jersey.
Banking sits **outside** the FS(J)L 1998 omnibus framework and
has its own dedicated regime.

## What is "banking business"

Banking business is defined broadly as **accepting deposits** as
a regular business activity:

- **Deposits** are sums of money received from a customer on
  terms that they will be **repaid** with or without interest;
- The exact legal definition has technical refinements (e.g.
  excluding sums received from related companies in some cases);
- The activity is regulated whether conducted by a Jersey
  company or a Jersey branch of a foreign bank.

## The registration requirement

It is an **offence** to carry on banking business in or from
within Jersey without registration under the 1991 Law. Banks
registered:

- Are typically **wholly-owned Jersey subsidiaries** of major UK,
  US, European, or Asian banking groups;
- Operate from **Jersey premises** with Jersey staff;
- Have **Jersey-resident directors** and a Jersey **registered
  office**;
- Hold a **Banking Business permit** from the JFSC.

## Regulatory expectations

The JFSC's expectations of registered banks include:

- **Capital adequacy** — Basel III-aligned regulatory capital
  requirements;
- **Liquidity** — adequate liquid asset buffers;
- **Risk management** — governance and risk frameworks
  proportionate to the institution;
- **Fit-and-proper** principals and key persons;
- **AML / CFT compliance** to the high standards expected of
  banks;
- **Conduct standards** for retail customer treatment.

These are detailed in the **JFSC Banking Code of Practice**.

## Depositor protection

Jersey operates a **deposit compensation scheme**:

- Funded through industry contributions;
- Provides limited **compensation** to retail depositors of a
  failed Jersey bank;
- Maximum compensation is set in regulations.

The scheme is less generous than the UK FSCS but covers the
core retail depositor risk.

## Why Jersey banking matters

Jersey banking serves several markets:

- **Private-banking** for HNW and family-office clients;
- **Trust-administration** banking — supporting Jersey trust
  companies in operating client trusts;
- **Fund-administration** banking — providing custody and
  banking services to Jersey funds;
- **Channel-Islands retail** — local banking services for the
  resident population.
