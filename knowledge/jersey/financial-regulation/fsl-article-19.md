# FSL 1998 Article 19 — Codes of Practice

Article 19 empowers the JFSC to **issue Codes of Practice**
setting out detailed conduct expectations for each regulated
activity class.

## The provision

The JFSC may, by Order, issue a **Code of Practice** for any
class of financial service business. The Code:

- **Sets out** standards expected of registered firms;
- **Provides guidance** on compliance with statutory
  obligations;
- **Is enforceable** — treated as evidence of expected
  standard;
- **Is binding** on registered firms.

## Codes issued

The JFSC has issued Codes for:

### Trust Company Business

- **Code of Practice for Trust Company Business** — governance,
  CDD, conflicts, conduct, records.

### Fund Services Business

- **Code of Practice for Fund Services Business** — governance,
  custody, NAV, pricing, conduct.

### Public Funds

- **Code of Practice for Certified Funds** — prospectus, NAV,
  pricing errors, conduct.

### Jersey Private Funds

- **Code of Practice for JPFs** — governance and conduct for
  closed-circle funds.

### Investment Business

- **Code of Practice for Investment Business** — suitability,
  disclosure, best execution, client money.

### GIMB

- **Code of Practice for General Insurance Mediation
  Business** — customer-facing, commissions, claims, conflicts.

### MSB

- **Guidance for Money Service Business** addressing AML,
  client money, settlement.

### Banking

- **Code of Practice for Deposit Takers** — under BBL 1991
  authority but functioning as JFSC Code.

### Insurance

- **Insurance Code of Practice** — under IBL 1996 authority.

## Code content typically covers

Common themes across Codes:

- **Senior management** responsibilities;
- **Governance** (board, committees, three lines of
  defence);
- **Risk management** framework;
- **Compliance** function and authority;
- **Internal audit** (where applicable);
- **CDD / AML / CFT**;
- **Conflicts of interest** identification and management;
- **Conduct of business** (customer treatment, suitability,
  disclosure);
- **Outsourcing** standards;
- **Records and reporting**;
- **Operational continuity**;
- **Cyber and IT** controls.

## Enforceability

The Codes are **enforceable**:

- **Breach** is a **regulatory offence** under Article 19(5)
  and related provisions;
- **Civil penalties** available for breaches;
- **Public statements** can name breaches;
- **Suspension / revocation** of registration in serious
  cases;
- Codes are **referenced in supervisory action** as evidence
  of the standard expected.

## Status as guidance vs rule

The Codes occupy a **middle position**:

- Not formal **statutory rules** in the strict sense;
- More than **mere guidance** — explicitly enforceable;
- **Treated as evidence** of the regulator's expectations;
- **Departure** from a Code requires careful documented
  justification;
- In practice, treated by firms as **the operational
  standard**.

## Updates

The JFSC updates Codes periodically:

- **Consultation** on draft changes;
- **Industry feedback** considered;
- **Implementation** with transition period typically;
- **Aligned** with international developments (FATF, IOSCO,
  IAIS).
