# Financial Services Commission (Jersey) Law 1998

The **Financial Services Commission (Jersey) Law 1998** is the
**constituting statute** of the **Jersey Financial Services
Commission (JFSC)** — the independent regulator that supervises
Jersey financial services.

## What the Law does

The Law:

- **Establishes** the JFSC as a statutory body corporate;
- Sets its **functions** — to be the regulator of financial
  services in Jersey;
- Provides for its **governance** — Board of Commissioners,
  Chair, Director General;
- Provides for its **funding** — primarily through industry fees;
- Sets its **powers** — registering financial-services businesses,
  issuing codes of practice, conducting investigations,
  imposing penalties;
- Sets the **accountability** framework — reporting to the
  Minister, the States Assembly, the public.

## JFSC functions

Under the 1998 Law, the JFSC has the following statutory
functions:

- **Regulating** financial services businesses in or from within
  Jersey;
- **Reducing the risk** of financial crime;
- **Promoting** Jersey's financial-services industry in a way
  consistent with the protection of consumers, investors, and
  Jersey's reputation;
- **Operating** the **Registry of Companies** under the Companies
  (Jersey) Law 1991 and the registries for foundations,
  partnerships, and other legal persons.

The combination of **regulatory** and **registry** functions in
the JFSC is distinctive — in many other jurisdictions these
functions are split.

## Governance

The JFSC is governed by:

- A **Board of Commissioners** including non-executive
  commissioners appointed by the Minister;
- A **Chair** of the Board;
- A **Director General** as the principal executive;
- **Divisions** organised by sector and function (Banking,
  Investment Business, Fiduciary Business, Funds, Insurance,
  Registry, Enforcement, Policy).

## Funding

The JFSC is **funded principally by industry fees**:

- **Registration fees** under regulated-activity Laws;
- **Annual fees** for ongoing supervision;
- **Search and document fees** from the registries;
- **Some States funding** for specified functions.

It is **not** funded by general Jersey taxation in the main, which
is consistent with the user-pays principle of financial-services
regulation.

## Information powers and confidentiality

The 1998 Law gives the JFSC strong **information-gathering
powers** to support its regulatory functions, with corresponding
**confidentiality** obligations and **information-exchange**
gateways for cooperation with other regulators (domestic and
foreign).
