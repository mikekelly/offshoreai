# Jersey Innovation Council

## What it is

The **Innovation Council** is the strategic-coordination
body established by the Government of Jersey in **late
2025** to drive Jersey's positioning in **digital assets**,
**tokenisation**, and **broader financial-services
innovation**.

The Council brings together the Government of Jersey
(particularly the Treasury and the Minister for External
Relations and Financial Services), the [JFSC](./jfsc.md),
Jersey Finance, Digital Jersey, the legal and accounting
professions, and industry practitioners. It operates as
the **strategic-direction layer** that sits above the
regulator's tactical guidance and the industry's
commercial responses.

## Why it exists

Through 2023-2025 Jersey faced a clear strategic question:
**how aggressively should it pursue digital-assets growth
given the regulatory and reputational complexity**.
Practitioners and the regulator could each move at their
own pace, but a coordinated multi-stakeholder body was
needed to:

- Set strategic priorities;
- Coordinate the JFSC's tactical guidance with industry
  development;
- Engage internationally (with OECD, FATF, the EU on MiCA-
  compatibility, and bilateral counterparts);
- Sponsor industry pilots and proof-of-concept work;
- Articulate Jersey's public positioning.

The Innovation Council is the answer. It launched in late
2025 in the run-up to the
[JFSC 2026-2030 Strategy](./jfsc-strategy-2026-2030.md)
which formalised digital assets and tokenisation as a
strategic growth vertical.

## What it does

### Strategic direction

- Sets the **digital-assets vertical strategy** for
  Jersey;
- Aligns government, regulator, and industry on the
  direction-of-travel;
- Engages OECD, FATF, and bilateral counterparts on
  innovation-friendly regulatory positioning.

### Coordination

- Brings the [JFSC](./jfsc.md) tactical guidance into
  alignment with industry capability;
- Surfaces practitioner pain points to the regulator;
- Sponsors industry working groups on specific topics
  (tokenised funds, stablecoins, security tokens,
  carbon-credit tokenisation, identity);
- Coordinates with Digital Jersey on infrastructure and
  skills.

### Industry pilots

- Sponsors **proof-of-concept pilots** in tokenised real-
  world assets, tokenised fund interests, and tokenised
  carbon credits;
- Coordinates with established Jersey institutions
  (banks, TCBs, fund administrators) on adoption
  pathways.

### International engagement

- Represents Jersey's position in international forums on
  digital-assets regulation;
- Maintains **MiCA-compatible posture** with the EU
  Markets in Crypto-Assets Regulation despite Jersey not
  being in MiCA scope;
- Coordinates with FATF on VASP supervision and travel
  rule implementation.

## How it relates to the regulator and the industry

The Innovation Council does **not regulate** — it sets
strategic direction. Regulation remains the
[JFSC's](./jfsc.md) responsibility under existing
financial-services legislation
([Financial Services (Jersey) Law 1998](./financial-services-jersey-law-1998.md))
and the [VASP register](../registries/vasp-register.md).
Industry execution remains in the practitioner sector.

The Innovation Council is therefore the **third leg of the
stool**:

| Body | Role | Activities |
|---|---|---|
| Innovation Council | Strategic direction | Vision, coordination, international engagement |
| [JFSC](./jfsc.md) | Regulation | Authorisation, supervision, enforcement |
| Industry | Execution | Product development, transaction execution |

## The strategic positioning

The Innovation Council's positioning of Jersey on digital
assets:

- **Regulated** — Jersey is not pursuing a "anything-goes"
  digital-assets strategy; the [VASP regime](../registries/vasp-register.md),
  AML/CFT, and substance requirements all apply;
- **MiCA-compatible** — Jersey will not diverge from the
  EU framework in a way that creates regulatory arbitrage;
- **Selective** — Jersey is targeting **specific verticals**
  (tokenised RWAs, tokenised fund interests, security
  tokens) rather than retail-crypto or speculative-token
  flows;
- **Sponsor-led** — Jersey is positioning to be the
  domicile of choice for established sponsors moving into
  tokenisation, not for early-stage retail crypto;
- **Substance-required** — the Substance Law 2019 applies
  to digital-assets businesses as it does to any other.

By Oct 2025 there were **14 registered VASPs** in Jersey —
see [`../registries/vasp-register.md`](../registries/vasp-register.md).

## Cross-Crown-Dependencies coordination

The Crown Dependencies are coordinating on digital-assets
regulation through informal channels and through
the Crown Dependencies' Financial Services Forum.
Guernsey and Isle of Man are pursuing parallel
strategies. The Innovation Council represents Jersey's
specific positioning within the cluster.

## What's coming

- **Continued tokenisation pilots** through 2026-2027;
- **Possible specific tokenisation legislation** — if
  the existing framework proves inadequate, the Council
  may sponsor specific tokenisation provisions
  (currently the framework operates under existing
  financial-services law);
- **SFDR 2.0 / sustainable-finance interplay** with
  tokenised sustainable assets;
- **International engagement** as OECD and FATF
  finalise digital-assets supervisory frameworks.

## Pitfalls

- **Confusing the Council with the regulator** — the
  Council sets strategy; the JFSC regulates;
- **Treating "Innovation Council exists" as a green
  light** — regulatory compliance still applies;
- **Overstating speed-to-market** — Jersey's approach is
  selective and substance-driven;
- **Ignoring the MiCA-compatible posture** — Jersey is
  not pursuing regulatory arbitrage against the EU.
