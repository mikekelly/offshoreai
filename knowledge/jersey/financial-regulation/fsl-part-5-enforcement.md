# FSL 1998 Part 5 — Enforcement

Part 5 sets out the JFSC's **enforcement powers** for breaches
of FSL obligations.

## Civil penalties

Under the **Civil Financial Penalties (Financial Services
Business) (Jersey) Order 2015** (issued under FSL authority):

- **Three bands** of penalty severity (1, 2, 3);
- **Band 1**: lesser breaches — typically £10k-£100k;
- **Band 2**: material breaches — typically £100k-£500k;
- **Band 3**: most serious breaches — typically £500k+ with
  no statutory upper limit;
- **Mitigation**: cooperation, remediation, self-reporting
  can reduce penalty;
- **Aggravation**: repeated breaches, customer harm,
  concealment can increase.

Penalties are imposed on **firms** principally, but **individual
penalties** are increasingly used for senior managers.

## Public statements

The JFSC may issue **public statements**:

- Naming **firms** subject to enforcement;
- Naming **individuals** in serious cases;
- Setting out **findings**;
- Issuing **warnings** about unregistered activity;
- **Education** purposes (helping industry understand
  standards).

Public statements are a **significant reputational sanction**
distinct from financial penalty.

## Criminal offences

The Law creates **criminal offences**:

- **Unregistered** financial service business (Article 7);
- **False statements** in applications / notifications;
- **Failure to comply** with directions or notices;
- **Tipping off** in AML contexts (cross-referenced with
  POCL);
- **Obstruction** of inspections;
- **Specific** breaches by individuals.

Criminal proceedings are brought by the **Attorney General**.

## Disqualification

Individuals may be **disqualified**:

- From acting as **principal person** in regulated business;
- For specified periods (typically years);
- Following adverse findings of dishonesty, incompetence, or
  serious breach.

Disqualification can be **career-ending** for industry
professionals.

## Decision-making process

Enforcement decisions follow:

- **Notice** of intent with proposed findings and sanctions;
- **Right** to make representations;
- **Decision Making Committee** — JFSC commissioners
  separate from investigators;
- **Reasoned** decision;
- **Right of appeal** to Royal Court.

## Skilled persons in enforcement

The JFSC may require **skilled persons** to:

- **Investigate** specific matters in lieu of internal JFSC
  investigation;
- **Report** findings to the JFSC;
- Costs **borne by the firm**.

## Appeals

Decisions are subject to **appeal**:

- To the **Royal Court** within prescribed period;
- On **grounds** of procedural fairness, error of law, or
  unreasonableness;
- The Royal Court can **confirm, vary, or set aside**;
- **Further appeal** to **Court of Appeal**;
- **Final appeal** (with leave) to **Privy Council**.

## Recent enforcement themes

Areas of recent JFSC enforcement focus:

- **AML / CFT** failures — particularly around BO
  identification and EDD;
- **Outsourcing** governance;
- **Conduct** failures in TCB;
- **Conflicts of interest** management;
- **Records and reporting** quality;
- **Individual accountability** for senior managers.
