# Jersey Financial Services Commission (JFSC)

The **Jersey Financial Services Commission (JFSC)** is Jersey's
financial-services regulator. The JFSC describes itself as "the
regulator for the financial services industry in Jersey".
([jerseyfsc.org][jfsc])

It is an **independent statutory body** established by the **Financial
Services Commission (Jersey) Law 1998**, funded by industry fees, with
its own Board of Commissioners and an executive Director General.
([JFSC legislation page][jfsc-legis])

## Functions

The JFSC's operational areas, as presented on its own site, are:

- **Supervision** of regulated entities;
- **Authorisation** (licensing of new entrants and key persons);
- **Enforcement** against regulated entities and individuals;
- **Policy and risk**;
- **Registry** management (companies, foundations, business names,
  partnerships, virtual-asset service providers);
- **Intelligence** (financial-intelligence functions in conjunction
  with the Jersey Financial Intelligence Unit).

It is *not* a prudential resolution authority in the bank-run sense;
deposit-compensation arrangements sit under the **Banking Business
(Depositors Compensation) (Jersey) Regulations 2009** — see the
[banking section](../banking/index.md) once written.

## Statutory basis

The constituting statute is the **[Financial Services Commission
(Jersey) Law 1998][fsc-law]**. Under it, the JFSC:

- is a body corporate with perpetual succession;
- is governed by a Board of Commissioners (including independent
  members, a Chair, and the Director General);
- is empowered to issue **codes of practice** under the principal
  regulatory Laws and to direct and discipline regulated entities for
  breaches of the codes and the Laws;
- is funded by **fees** from the regulated population.

The detail of the Board's composition, the appointments process, and
the JFSC's powers and immunities is treated in
[jfsc-law-1998.md](jfsc-law-1998.md) *(stub at this revision)*.

## What the JFSC administers

The JFSC administers a wide set of Laws and Orders. The headline list:

| Class of business | Principal Law |
|---|---|
| Deposit-taking (banking) | [Banking Business (Jersey) Law 1991][bbl] |
| Public collective investment funds | [Collective Investment Funds (Jersey) Law 1988][cif] |
| Alternative investment funds (AIFs / AIFMs) | [Alternative Investment Funds (Jersey) Regulations 2012][aif] |
| Insurance | [Insurance Business (Jersey) Law 1996][insl] |
| Investment business, trust-company business, fund-services business, general-insurance mediation, money-services business | [Financial Services (Jersey) Law 1998][fsjl] |
| Beneficial-ownership / persons-with-significant-control disclosure | [Financial Services (Disclosure and Provision of Information) (Jersey) Law 2020][fsdl] |
| Schedule 2 "supervised business" AML/CFT scope | [Proceeds of Crime (Jersey) Law 1999][pocl] |

The full list (with every order, regulation and code) is on the JFSC
[all-legislation page][jfsc-legis] and a curated index of canonical
URLs is in [sources.md](../sources.md).

## Codes of practice

For each regulated class of business, the JFSC issues a **Code of
Practice** under power conferred by the principal regulatory Law. The
Codes are not statute but are treated by the Royal Court as evidence
of the regulatory expectation, and breach can give rise to JFSC
enforcement and (where relevant) to civil financial penalties.

The Codes — Trust Company Business, Fund Services Business, Investment
Business, Banking, Insurance, etc. — are listed on the JFSC's codes
pages and indexed for this corpus in
[codes-of-practice.md](codes-of-practice.md) *(stub at this revision)*.

## Registries

The JFSC operates the Jersey registries for:

- companies, foundations, LLPs and partnerships;
- business names;
- virtual-asset service providers;
- the (non-public) **beneficial-ownership** register required under
  the FSDI Law 2020;
- public statements of regulatory action.

For the registry deep-dive see
[registries/index.md](../registries/index.md).
