# FSL 1998 Part 6 — Miscellaneous

Part 6 consolidates administrative architecture, regulation-
making, fees, citation, and cooperation provisions.

## Regulation-making power

The Minister may make **Orders** under the Law to:

- **Prescribe** classes of business and their scope;
- **Specify** capital, liquidity, and prudential requirements;
- **Implement** international standards (FATF, IOSCO, IAIS);
- **Set** application and ongoing fees;
- **Address** specific risks (crypto, e-money, fintech);
- **Define** "investment" and other key terms;
- **Update** Schedules.

## Codes of Practice

## Forms and fees

- **Application forms** published by JFSC;
- **Fee schedule** annually reviewed;
- **Different** fees per class and per activity scope;
- **Special** fees for inspections and skilled persons;
- **Late** fees for delayed filings.

## Information sharing

The Law authorises **information sharing** with:

- **UK regulators** (FCA, PRA) — historic close relationship;
- **EU regulators** through cooperation protocols
  (where compatible post-Brexit);
- **Other foreign regulators** through MoUs;
- **AML / FIU** channels (Jersey FIU, Egmont Group);
- **Other domestic** authorities (Comptroller of Revenue,
  States of Jersey Police);
- **IOSCO, IAIS** through international cooperation
  agreements.

Information sharing is subject to **confidentiality
safeguards**.

## International cooperation

The JFSC participates in:

- **IOSCO MMoU** (International Organization of Securities
  Commissions);
- **IAIS** (insurance supervisors);
- **GIFCS** (Group of International Finance Centre
  Supervisors);
- **EBA / BIS / FSB observer** participation in some
  capacities;
- **Bilateral MoUs** with major foreign regulators.

## Citation

The Law is cited as the **"Financial Services (Jersey) Law
1998"**.

It came into force on **1 July 1998** (different parts at
different times) and has been amended many times to keep pace
with:

- **AIFMD** transitional provisions;
- **MiFID II** equivalent provisions;
- **FATF** standards;
- **JFSC supervisory** enhancements;
- **VASP / crypto** regulatory regime introduction;
- **Outsourcing** standard updates;
- **MCIT** integration considerations (2025).
