# FSL 1998 Article 21 — Suspension of Registration

Article 21 enables the JFSC to **suspend** the registration
of a financial-services business.

## Grounds for suspension

The JFSC may suspend for:

### Breach of conditions

- Failure to comply with **registration conditions**
  (Article 10);
- **Material** breaches;
- **Specific** failures.

### Pending investigation

- **JFSC investigation** of suspected breaches;
- **Pending** completion;
- **Specific** procedural protections.

### Public-interest reasons

- **Suspected** illegal activity;
- **Reputational** concerns affecting Jersey financial-
  services sector;
- **Specific** public-interest grounds.

### Prudential concerns

- **Capital** or **liquidity** material concerns;
- **Going-concern** issues;
- **Specific** prudential thresholds breached.

### AML / CFT failures

- **Material** AML / CFT compliance failures;
- **Specific** Schedule 2 obligations breached;
- **Specific** JFSC AML / CFT Handbook obligations.

## Effect

A suspended firm:

- **Cannot** carry on the regulated activity during
  suspension;
- **Must** comply with **emergency directions** issued by
  the JFSC;
- **Must continue** to pay fees and report;
- **May** continue to wind-down operations under JFSC
  supervision;
- **Specific** existing client matters subject to specific
  directions.

## Procedure

The JFSC suspension process:

1. **Notice** of intent to suspend with grounds;
2. **Right** to make representations;
3. **JFSC decision-making** committee;
4. **Reasoned** decision;
5. **Right of appeal** to Royal Court;
6. **Specific** stay considerations.

## Emergency suspension

In urgent cases:

- **Emergency** suspension possible with shorter notice;
- **Specific** grounds for urgency (consumer protection,
  market integrity);
- **Right** to subsequent representations;
- **Right of appeal**.

## Outcome

After suspension:

- **Lifting** if breach remedied;
- **Conditional** lifting (specific conditions imposed);
- **Revocation** if breach not remedied or is serious;
- **Specific** transition arrangements.
