# TCB Code of Practice — Principles

The JFSC Code of Practice for Trust Company Business opens with
a set of **high-level Principles** that every registered trust
company business must satisfy. The Principles articulate the
**conduct standard** the JFSC supervises against and frame every
substantive obligation in the rest of the Code.

The exact wording of each Principle and any subordinate guidance
**must be verified against the current published Code on the
[JFSC's website](https://www.jerseyfsc.org/industry/codes-of-practice/)**
before quoting in an authoritative answer. This file describes
the Principles' structure and load-bearing themes at the level
of a practitioner orientation. The current version of the TCB
Code was issued effective **1 January 2021**.

## The Principles in summary

The TCB Code follows the JFSC's standard FSL-class Code
structure of **eight Principles**, applied to the fiduciary
context. In summary terms they cover:

1. **Integrity** — registered persons must conduct business
   with integrity. This is the substrate for every other
   Principle.
2. **Skill, care and diligence** — competent execution of
   fiduciary functions, allocated to suitable persons.
3. **Organisation and control** — adequate governance,
   systems, controls, and a designated compliance function.
   This is where the JFSC's expectation of a three-lines-of-
   defence framing for the business sits.
4. **Risk management** — proportionate identification,
   assessment, and mitigation of risk; a business-wide risk
   assessment that drives policies and procedures.
5. **Customer treatment** — fair treatment of clients,
   including disclosure of material information, suitability
   of the services provided, and proper handling of conflicts.
6. **Financial soundness** — adequate capital and resources to
   meet liabilities as they fall due; appropriate professional
   indemnity insurance.
7. **Cooperation with the JFSC** — open, accurate and timely
   dealings with the regulator, including notification of
   material events.
8. **Market integrity** — fair dealing in markets; behaviour
   that does not harm the integrity of the Jersey market.

## Why the Principles matter operationally

The Principles are **not aspirational** — they are mandatory.
Breach of a Principle is itself enforceable under the
[Financial Services (Jersey) Law 1998](./financial-services-jersey-law-1998.md),
independently of breach of a more specific Code rule. This
means:

- The JFSC can take enforcement action against a TCB whose
  conduct breaches a Principle even where no specific Code
  subsection is contravened.
- Civil financial penalties under the FSL can be calibrated
  by reference to which Principles were breached and how
  severely.
- A compliance programme that satisfies the detailed Code
  rules but fails on Principle 1 (integrity) or Principle 3
  (organisation and control) is still in breach.

The substantive sections of the Code operationalise the
Principles — for example,
[`cop-tcb-customer-onboarding.md`](./cop-tcb-customer-onboarding.md)
*(stub)* operationalises Principle 5 (customer treatment) and
[`cop-tcb-compliance-officer.md`](./cop-tcb-compliance-officer.md)
*(stub)* operationalises Principle 3 (organisation and control).

## Interaction with the FSL fit-and-proper test

The Principles supplement, rather than replace, the
fit-and-proper test under
[Article 9 of the FSL](./fsl-article-9.md). Fit-and-proper is a
**pre-condition for being on the register**; the Principles
govern **how a registered person conducts itself**. A
registered person whose conduct breaches a Principle may
**cease to be fit and proper** — but the Principles can be
breached without crossing that threshold, and the JFSC can act
on Principle breach short of de-registration through public
statements, financial penalties, directions, or conditions.

## Interaction with the AML/CFT framework

The Principles do not duplicate AML/CFT obligations. Principle
5 (customer treatment) and Principle 3 (organisation and
control) sit alongside — and refer to — the obligations under
the
[Money Laundering (Jersey) Order 2008](../aml-cft/money-laundering-order-2008.md)
and the
[JFSC AML/CFT Handbook](../aml-cft/aml-cft-handbook.md). A TCB
satisfying the Code's customer-treatment expectations must
**also** satisfy the MLO 2008 CDD/EDD/ongoing-monitoring
obligations and the Handbook's operational expectations.
