# Financial Services (Jersey) Law 1998

The **Financial Services (Jersey) Law 1998** (FS(J)L 1998) is the
**omnibus regulatory statute** for most Jersey financial services
businesses. It is the principal source of authority for the JFSC's
regulation of:

- **Investment business**;
- **Trust company business (TCB)**;
- **Fund services business (FSB)**;
- **General insurance mediation business (GIMB)**;
- **Money services business (MSB)**.

(Banking is regulated separately under the Banking Business
(Jersey) Law 1991; underwriting insurance under the Insurance
Business (Jersey) Law 1996.)

## Structure

The Law is structured around a **regulated-activity gateway**:

- It is an **offence** to carry on a regulated activity in or from
  within Jersey **without registration** (Article 7);
- Registration is granted by the JFSC subject to **fit-and-proper**
  and other tests;
- Registered persons must comply with the relevant **Code of
  Practice**;
- The JFSC has wide **supervisory and enforcement** powers.

## Regulated activity classes

### Trust company business (TCB)

Class for **professional trustees, foundation administrators,
company services providers**, and similar fiduciary businesses.

Covers:

- Acting as trustee;
- Acting as a foundation council member or guardian
  (qualified-member capacity);
- Acting as company director, secretary, or registered office
  provider on a commercial basis;
- Acting as administrator for trusts, foundations, and companies.

Predominantly applies to the **Jersey fiduciary industry** — the
trust companies and corporate-services firms.

### Fund services business (FSB)

Class for **fund administrators, managers, custodians, and other
fund-services providers**.

Covers a fund vehicle's:

- **Management** (investment-management decisions);
- **Administration** (back-office, NAV, accounting);
- **Custody** (asset safekeeping);
- **Registrar / transfer agency** services;
- **Other prescribed fund-services**.

### Investment business

Class for **investment advisers, dealers, and asset managers**
serving clients other than collective investment funds.

Covers:

- Investment advice;
- Discretionary investment management;
- Dealing in investments as principal or agent;
- Arranging deals;
- Other prescribed investment activities.

### General insurance mediation business (GIMB)

Class for **insurance brokers and intermediaries**.

### Money services business (MSB)

Class for **money transmission, currency exchange, and similar**
financial-services activities outside the principal banking
regime.

## The registration process

Applicants must:

- Submit a **detailed application** to the JFSC;
- Demonstrate **fit-and-proper** status for principal and key
  persons;
- Demonstrate **adequate resources** (capital, systems, staff);
- Show **understanding of regulatory requirements**;
- Pay **application fees** and ongoing **annual fees**.

The JFSC may **grant**, **grant with conditions**, or **refuse**
registration. Refusals are appealable.

## Conduct expectations

Each regulated class has a **Code of Practice** issued by the
JFSC under FS(J)L 1998 powers. The Codes contain detailed conduct
expectations covering:

- **Governance and risk management**;
- **Client treatment** (CDD, complaints, suitability);
- **Outsourcing**;
- **Conflict-of-interest management**;
- **Record-keeping**;
- **Reporting** to the JFSC.

## Enforcement

The JFSC has wide enforcement powers:

- **Directions** requiring specific conduct;
- **Public statements** naming non-compliance;
- **Civil financial penalties** under the Civil Financial
  Penalties (Financial Services Business) (Jersey) Order 2015;
- **Conditions** on registration;
- **Suspension** of registration;
- **Revocation** of registration.
