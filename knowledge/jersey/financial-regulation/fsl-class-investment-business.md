# FSL 1998 — Investment Business

Investment Business covers **services to clients other than
collective investment funds** — discretionary management,
advice, dealing, custody.

## Activities within scope

Investment business includes:

### Investment advice

- **Personal recommendations** on specific investments;
- **Discretionary** vs **non-discretionary** advice models;
- **Pension** and **wealth management** advice.

### Discretionary investment management

- **Managing portfolios** on behalf of clients with
  discretion over investment decisions;
- **Family office** investment management;
- **High-net-worth** portfolio management.

### Dealing in investments

- **As principal** — trading on own account with clients;
- **As agent** — executing trades on clients' behalf;
- **Market-making** in some structures.

### Arranging deals

- **Brokerage** — bringing buyer / seller together;
- **Placement** — primary market activity;
- **Introducing** clients to other investment business
  providers.

### Custody

- **Safeguarding** client investments;
- **Administering** investments on clients' behalf;
- **Settlement** of trades.

## "Investment" — wide definition

Under FSL "**investment**" includes:

- **Securities** (shares, debt securities);
- **Units in collective investment schemes**;
- **Derivatives** (futures, options, swaps);
- **Contracts for differences**;
- **Certificates representing investments**;
- **Other** prescribed instruments;
- **Crypto-assets** subject to specific JFSC guidance.

## Activities outside scope

Investment business excludes:

- **Services to collective investment funds** — those are FSB;
- **Advice strictly incidental** to other professional
  services — e.g., a lawyer advising on tax implications of
  a specific investment without ongoing advisory
  relationship;
- **In-house treasury** activity for related-party groups;
- **One-off** transactions in limited circumstances;
- **Specific exempt** activities.

## Who provides investment business

Jersey investment-business firms include:

- **Bank-owned** investment management firms;
- **Independent** wealth managers;
- **Family-office** investment management arms;
- **Specialist** boutique investment advisers;
- **Stockbrokers** (limited Jersey market — most retail
  broking is through UK platforms);
- **Crypto-asset** advisers and dealers (where in scope).

## Registration requirements

An investment business applicant must:

- **Register** under Article 7-9 of FSL;
- Be **fit and proper**;
- Maintain **adequate capital** (significantly higher for
  principals dealing on own account);
- Have **adequate** systems and controls;
- Have **Jersey presence** and qualifying staff;
- Comply with **AML / CFT**;
- Comply with **conduct rules** (Code of Practice).

## Code of Practice for Investment Business

The Code addresses:

- **Suitability** of recommendations (KYC, risk profile,
  objectives);
- **Disclosure** of fees, commissions, conflicts;
- **Best execution** in dealing;
- **Client money** segregation;
- **Custody** standards;
- **Complaints** handling;
- **Treating customers fairly**.

## Cross-border investment activity

Jersey-based investment business firms serving:

- **UK clients** — subject to FCA reciprocity considerations
  and MiFID-equivalent compliance;
- **EU clients** — subject to country-by-country NPPR /
  equivalent regime;
- **US clients** — subject to SEC / FINRA considerations;
- **Other** clients — host-country rules typically apply.
