# FSL 1998 Article 10 — Conditions Imposed on Registration

Article 10 empowers the JFSC to impose **conditions** on
registration — restrictions or requirements specific to the
registered firm.

## The provision

The JFSC may impose **such conditions on registration** as it
considers appropriate, including:

- **Scope** restrictions;
- **Capital** maintenance;
- **Specific reporting** requirements;
- **Operational** restrictions;
- **Senior management** approval requirements;
- **Geographical** restrictions;
- **Customer type** restrictions;
- **Time-limited** approvals.

## Standard conditions

Most registrations carry **standard conditions**:

- **Compliance** with the relevant Code of Practice;
- **Annual returns** and prescribed reporting;
- **Notification** of material changes;
- **Cooperation** with JFSC supervision;
- **Capital maintenance**;
- **MLRO** designated and notified;
- **Compliance Officer** designated.

## Firm-specific conditions

Beyond standard conditions, common firm-specific conditions:

### Scope conditions

- **Limited classes of business** within a class — e.g., a
  TCB authorised only for "trustee and administrator" but not
  "directorship";
- **Limited customer types** — e.g., institutional only;
- **Limited products** — specific fund types;
- **Limited jurisdictions** for distribution.

### Capital conditions

- **Minimum capital** above the JFSC default for the class;
- **Sub-component** requirements (e.g., liquidity buffer
  specifically);
- **Group support** documentation requirements.

### Reporting conditions

- **Enhanced** reporting (quarterly vs annual);
- **Specific risk** indicators;
- **Concentration** monitoring;
- **Operational metrics** above standard.

### Governance conditions

- **Senior management** approval required for specific
  matters (new product, material outsourcing, principal
  changes);
- **Independent** review of specific activities;
- **External audit** beyond statutory;
- **Skilled persons** review at intervals.

### Operational conditions

- **Specific** outsourcing approvals;
- **IT** continuity standards above default;
- **AML / CFT** enhanced procedures;
- **Cyber** standards.

## Sources of conditions

Conditions arise from:

- **Application review** — JFSC identifies risks during
  assessment;
- **Pre-conditions** to grant — must be met before
  registration takes effect;
- **Subsequent variation** under Article 11 — based on
  developments;
- **Supervisory action** — conditions added in response to
  issues.

## Enforceability

Conditions are **enforceable**:

- **Breach** is a regulatory offence;
- **Grounds** for enforcement (civil penalty, public
  statement);
- **Grounds** for suspension or revocation in serious cases;
- **Senior management** personal accountability.

## Variation of conditions

Either party may seek **variation**:

- The **firm** may apply to relax conditions as it matures;
- The **JFSC** may add or tighten conditions in response to
  risk or events;
- Variations subject to **notice and representations**
  process;
- **Appeals** to Royal Court available.

## Practical implications

For Jersey-based regulated firms:

- **Condition compliance** is operationally significant;
- **Conditions register** maintained alongside core policies;
- **Senior management** aware of conditions and how they
  affect business decisions;
- **Material change** considerations include condition impact;
- **JFSC engagement** for variation when business evolves.
