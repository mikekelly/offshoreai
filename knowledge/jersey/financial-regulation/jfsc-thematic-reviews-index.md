# JFSC Thematic Reviews — Index

A **thematic review** is a JFSC supervisory exercise across a
sample of regulated businesses focused on a specific theme or
risk. The published findings communicate **what the JFSC found**
and **what it expects to be different** — making thematic
reviews the clearest single signal of supervisory expectation
short of named enforcement action.

This is the **per-surface index** for thematic reviews.
Individual entries below are seeded as the editor populates
them from the
[JFSC website](https://www.jerseyfsc.org/industry/).

## Why thematic-review content matters operationally

When the JFSC publishes a thematic review:

- The regulated businesses **sampled** receive specific
  feedback;
- The **published findings** become the JFSC's stated
  expectation across the industry;
- Practice **inconsistent with the findings** is at
  enforcement risk on subsequent inspection;
- The findings often **precede Code or Handbook updates** in
  the relevant area.

A compliance programme that does not track thematic-review
findings has a blind spot to the regulator's current focus.

## How entries should be structured

Each thematic-review entry (when populated) should carry:

- **Date** of the review and of the published findings;
- **Theme** — what was reviewed (e.g. "TCB CDD on PEP
  relationships", "FSB outsourcing controls");
- **Sectors** covered;
- **Sample size** and methodology;
- **Key findings** — what the JFSC found in practice;
- **JFSC expectations** — what good practice looks like
  according to the review;
- **Cross-references** to the relevant
  [Code of Practice section](./codes-of-practice.md) or
  [AML/CFT Handbook section](../aml-cft/aml-cft-handbook.md);
- **`as_of`** date (for refresh discipline).
