# FSB Code of Practice — JPF Designated Service Provider

The **Jersey Private Fund (JPF)** is the flagship Jersey fund
vehicle for closed-ended private capital (PE, VC, family-
office, infrastructure). The JPF regime delegates substantial
regulatory responsibility from the fund itself to a
**Designated Service Provider (DSP)** — a JFSC-regulated FSB
that takes on specific Code-defined obligations on behalf of
the JPF.

This file is the orientation surface for what the DSP role
involves. For the JPF regime itself, see
[`../funds/jersey-private-fund.md`](../funds/jersey-private-fund.md).

## What the JPF DSP is

When a JPF is established, the JFSC requires it to appoint a
**Designated Service Provider** — a Jersey-licensed fund
services business that:

- Performs prescribed regulatory functions for the JPF;
- Acts as the **JFSC's primary contact** for the JPF;
- Is **accountable to the JFSC** for the JPF's regulatory
  compliance.

The DSP model is the **regulatory anchor** that enables the
JPF regime's speed-to-market (48-hour establishment), light-
touch ongoing regulation, and limited direct JFSC interaction
with the fund itself.

## Why the DSP model matters operationally

The JPF regime is fast and operationally efficient because
the DSP carries the regulatory lift. For a JPF:

- **No JFSC fund-level authorisation** — the JPF is
  registered, not authorised;
- **Light JPF-level ongoing supervision** — JFSC
  supervisory engagement runs primarily through the DSP;
- **DSP-handled regulatory matters** — notifications,
  filings, fit-and-proper assessments of fund officials,
  ongoing-compliance attestation.

This compresses the regulatory perimeter from the fund
itself to its DSP — making JPFs cost-efficient for managers
running multiple parallel funds.

## DSP obligations under the FSB Code

The Code's JPF-DSP-specific provisions typically cover:

### Fit-and-proper assessment

The DSP assesses fit-and-proper standing of the JPF's
**General Partner** (where structured as an LP), **directors**
(where structured as a company), or equivalent governing
body. The assessment uses the same fit-and-proper criteria
the JFSC would apply if it were doing the assessment
directly.

### Anti-money-laundering

The DSP is responsible for ensuring the JPF's AML/CFT
compliance — applying the
[Money Laundering Order 2008](../aml-cft/money-laundering-order-2008.md)
and the
[JFSC AML/CFT Handbook](../aml-cft/aml-cft-handbook.md) to
the JPF's investors and any underlying investments where
AML touchpoints apply.

In practice this means the DSP:

- Performs CDD on the JPF's investors at subscription;
- Maintains ongoing monitoring of investor relationships;
- Files SARs where suspicion arises;
- Maintains the JPF's AML/CFT records.

### Ongoing notifications

The DSP notifies the JFSC of material events at the JPF —
changes in GP / directors, material amendments to fund
constitutional documents, change-of-control events,
breaches of fund terms.

### Annual compliance return

The DSP files an **annual compliance return** for the JPF
confirming ongoing compliance with the JPF Guide's terms.

### Regulator interaction

The DSP is the JFSC's first point of contact for
supervisory matters concerning the JPF — thematic-review
participation, ad-hoc enquiries, breach notifications.

## Eligibility to act as DSP

To act as a DSP, an FSB must:

- Hold a relevant **JFSC FSB licence** under the
  [FSL 1998](./financial-services-jersey-law-1998.md);
- Have **sufficient capacity** to discharge DSP
  responsibilities (the JFSC considers the DSP's overall
  workload and resourcing);
- Have **specific JPF expertise** — the DSP must understand
  the JPF regime and its operational specifics;
- Satisfy ongoing FSB Code obligations applied to the DSP
  role.

The Jersey market has a number of FSB firms that
specialise in JPF DSP work — making it a recognisable
service line within the Jersey fund-services landscape.

## DSP-JPF agreement

The DSP and the JPF (or its GP / manager) enter into a
written agreement covering:

- **Scope of DSP services** — precisely defined;
- **Information flow** between the DSP and the JPF's
  managers / advisers / service providers;
- **Indemnities** for the DSP's regulatory exposure;
- **Termination** — and the regulatory implications of
  termination (a JPF without a DSP is in breach of the JPF
  Guide and faces operational consequences);
- **Fees** and fee escalation;
- **Governing law** (typically Jersey).

## Common DSP role failure points

JFSC-related themes:

- **Inadequate CDD on investors** — particularly where the
  JPF accepts subscriptions from intermediated investor
  vehicles where look-through CDD is challenging;
- **Late notifications** of material events;
- **Annual compliance return inaccuracies**;
- **Capacity overload** at the DSP firm — too many JPFs per
  DSP officer, leading to thin oversight;
- **Termination handling** — JPF left without a DSP for
  longer than acceptable;
- **Conflicts** where the DSP is also providing other
  services to the JPF (administration, custody) — conflict
  management within the FSB Code's
  [conflicts](./cop-fsb-conflicts.md) *(stub)* section.

## Interaction with AIFMD-track funds

The JPF is **not** typically AIFMD-marketed into the EU —
the JPF Guide constrains investor base in ways that
generally keep the JPF outside AIFMD perimeter. JPFs that
do market into EU member states under NPPR are subject to
the
[AIFMD interface](./cop-fsb-aifmd-interface.md)
obligations layered on top of the JPF DSP framework.

## Why this matters for wealth management

For wealth-management businesses with **fund-services
adjacencies** — running PE funds for HNW client groups,
family-office co-investment vehicles, structured products —
the JPF regime is often the right vehicle and the DSP role
is the regulatory architecture. Understanding the DSP role
is essential for:

- **Cost-modelling** of fund structures (DSP fees are a
  recurring overhead);
- **Choice of DSP** for new fund structures;
- **Operational design** of fund-management workflows that
  interlock with DSP responsibilities;
- **Risk allocation** between manager / GP and DSP.
