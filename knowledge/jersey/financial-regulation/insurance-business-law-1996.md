# Insurance Business (Jersey) Law 1996

The **Insurance Business (Jersey) Law 1996** is the principal
statute regulating **insurance underwriting** in or from within
Jersey.

## Insurance vs intermediation

Two distinct activities are regulated separately:

- **Underwriting** (accepting insurance risk) → Insurance
  Business (Jersey) Law 1996;
- **Intermediation** (broking, arranging) → FS(J)L 1998 General
  Insurance Mediation Business (GIMB) regime.

## Categories of insurance permit

The 1996 Law distinguishes:

- **Category A permits** — for insurers writing **general
  business in Jersey** (typically retail / commercial general
  insurance);
- **Category B permits** — for insurers writing **specified types
  of business**, often international risks or captive structures;
- Specific permit types for **long-term business** (life
  insurance, pensions).

## Captive insurance

Jersey is a **well-established captive insurance jurisdiction**:

- A **captive** is an insurer owned by (and primarily serving)
  the **risk-bearing parent** group — i.e. a self-insurance
  vehicle structured as a regulated insurer;
- Captives are typically **Category B**;
- Jersey's flexible captive regime accommodates:
  - **Pure captives** for single-parent risks;
  - **Group captives** for industry consortia;
  - **Cell-company captives** using PCC / ICC structures
    (Part 18D Companies Law);
  - **Reinsurance** structures.

## Regulatory expectations

The JFSC's expectations of insurers include:

- **Solvency** — adequate capital and reserves proportionate to
  risk;
- **Reserving** — appropriate technical provisions;
- **Reinsurance** — appropriate reinsurance arrangements;
- **Governance** — fit-and-proper management and underwriting
  controls;
- **Conduct** — fair treatment of policyholders;
- **Reporting** — regular returns to the JFSC.

Detail is in the **JFSC Insurance Codes of Practice**.
