# FSL 1998 — Trust Company Business (TCB)

Trust Company Business (TCB) is the **largest class** under
the FSL by participant numbers in Jersey. It captures the
**fiduciary industry** — trust companies, foundation
administrators, corporate-service providers.

## Activities within scope

TCB includes:

### Trustee services

- **Acting as trustee** of an express trust;
- **Acting as protector / enforcer** in a commercial capacity;
- **Administering** trusts as trustee.

### Foundation services

- **Acting as a foundation council member**, particularly the
  qualified member of a Jersey foundation;
- **Acting as guardian** of a foundation in commercial capacity;
- **Administering** foundations.

### Company services

- **Acting as director** of a company on a commercial basis
  (i.e., as a service provider, not as an executive of a
  group);
- **Acting as secretary** of a company on commercial basis;
- **Providing registered office services** on commercial
  basis;
- **Acting as nominee shareholder** for clients;
- **Acting as treasury / cash management agent** in narrow
  cases.

### Administration

- **Trust administration** — bookkeeping, distribution
  processing, beneficiary reporting;
- **Foundation administration**;
- **Company administration** — secretarial work, filings,
  meetings.

## Activities outside scope

Outside TCB:

- **In-house trustee** for a related-party group (not by way of
  business);
- **One-off** trustee acts for family / friends (no commercial
  structure);
- **Lawyer / accountant** acting in primary professional
  capacity (some exemptions — but acting as trustee is
  usually within TCB regardless of primary qualification);
- **Specific exempt** activities by JFSC order.

## Who provides TCB

Jersey TCBs include:

- **Major firms** — Mourant Trust, Ogier Global, Carey Olsen
  Trust, Bedell Cristin Trust, Walkers, Appleby Trust;
- **Specialist independents** — IQ-EQ, Vistra, Apex Group
  (Sanne legacy), Hawksford, Suntera Global, Maples;
- **Smaller / boutique** firms — many specialist or family-
  office-focused;
- **Bank-owned trust companies** — historical model now less
  common;
- **In-house / single-family-office** TCBs (some structures
  involve a TCB providing services to a single family-office
  group).

## Registration requirements

A TCB applicant must:

- **Register** under Article 7-9 of FSL;
- Be **fit and proper** (corporate and principal persons);
- Maintain **adequate capital** (typically a multiple of
  operating costs, with minimum thresholds);
- Have **adequate systems and controls**;
- Have **Jersey presence** — registered office, qualifying
  staff;
- Comply with **AML / CFT** programme requirements;
- Pay **application and annual fees**;
- Comply with **continuing obligations**.

## Code of Practice for TCB

The JFSC issues the **Code of Practice for Trust Company
Business** under Article 19. It addresses:

- **Governance** — board composition, senior management
  responsibilities;
- **Risk management** — first / second / third line of
  defence;
- **CDD** — coordinated with MLO 2008;
- **Conflicts of interest** — between TCB and clients,
  between clients;
- **Outsourcing** — controls on third-party arrangements;
- **Conduct of business** — treating customers fairly;
- **Records** — content and retention.

## Specific TCB issues

### Acting as director on commercial basis

A common scenario:

- TCB principal acts as **director of client-administered
  companies**;
- **Within TCB scope** because commercial / by way of business;
- **Fiduciary duties** of directorship apply alongside TCB
  obligations;
- TCB Code addresses **standards** for such directorships.

### Family office structures

- A TCB providing services to a single family may still be
  within TCB scope;
- **Substance and substance considerations** under the
  Substance Law 2019;
- **AML / CFT** considerations same as for any TCB;
- **Reduced** retail-protection focus given sophisticated
  client.

### Private Trust Companies (PTCs)

- A PTC may be **exempt** from full TCB registration if
  acting as trustee of a single trust / family of related
  trusts;
- **Exemption** subject to specific conditions (no public
  marketing, related-trust scope);
- See [`../trusts/ptc.md`](../use-cases/trust-officer/ptc.md) *(if exists)* or
  [`../use-cases/trust-officer/ptc.md`](../use-cases/trust-officer/ptc.md).
