# FSL 1998 Article 9 — Grant of Registration; Fit and Proper

Article 9 is the **substantive grant** provision — the JFSC's
power to register applicants who meet **fit-and-proper** and
related criteria.

## The provision

The JFSC **may grant** registration if satisfied:

- The applicant is **fit and proper** to carry on the
  proposed financial service business;
- Each **principal person** of the applicant is **fit and
  proper**;
- The applicant has **adequate resources** (capital,
  personnel, premises, systems);
- The applicant has **adequate systems and controls**;
- The applicant's **business plan** is appropriate;
- It is **in the public interest** to grant registration.

## Fit-and-proper criteria

Schedule 1 of the Law sets out fit-and-proper criteria:

### Honesty, integrity, reputation

- **Honesty** in dealings;
- **No criminal record** for dishonesty / financial crime;
- **No** material regulatory sanctions;
- **No** bankruptcy / insolvency history (in some cases);
- **Truthfulness** in dealings with regulators.

### Competence and capability

- **Qualifications** appropriate to the role;
- **Experience** in similar regulated activity;
- **Ongoing professional development**;
- **Track record** of competent performance;
- **Knowledge** of Jersey regulatory framework.

### Financial soundness

- **Personal financial standing** (for principals);
- **Capital adequacy** (for applicant);
- **No undischarged** material liabilities;
- **Resources** appropriate to scale.

### Time commitment

- **Sufficient time** to perform the role;
- **Other commitments** disclosed;
- **Conflicts of interest** managed.

## Continuing fit-and-proper

Registration is **not a one-time check**:

- Principal persons must **remain fit and proper** at all
  times;
- **Changes** notified to the JFSC under Article 13;
- **Re-assessment** triggered by adverse events (criminal
  charges, financial difficulties, regulatory action,
  conflict events);
- **Loss of fit-and-proper** is grounds for suspension /
  revocation.

## Group / parent considerations

For applicants in **international groups**:

- **Parent / group** fit-and-proper considered;
- **Home regulator** standing relevant;
- **Group AML / risk** programmes considered;
- **Resolution** arrangements relevant (for banking-adjacent);
- **Information sharing** MoUs with home regulators.

## Resources

Adequate resources means:

- **Capital** at level prescribed by class / JFSC;
- **Liquidity** to operate;
- **Staff** with appropriate skill set in Jersey;
- **Premises** in Jersey;
- **IT** infrastructure;
- **Group support** documented if relied upon.

## Systems and controls

Adequate systems means:

- **Three lines of defence** risk management;
- **Compliance** function;
- **Internal audit** function;
- **MLRO** and AML systems;
- **IT and cyber** controls;
- **Outsourcing** governance;
- **Business continuity**;
- **Records management**.

## Public interest

Beyond technical criteria:

- **Jersey financial-services sector** quality;
- **Reputational** considerations;
- **Capacity** of existing market for new entrants;
- **International** standards alignment (FATF, IOSCO, IAIS);
- **Demand** for the proposed services.

## Conditional grant

The JFSC frequently grants **subject to conditions**:

- **Specific** capital injection;
- **Specific** principal-person appointment;
- **Limitation** on initial scope;
- **Reporting** during early operation;
- **Six-month** review post-launch.

## Refusal

The JFSC may **refuse** for:

- Failure to meet any of the Article 9 conditions;
- Material concerns about applicant or principals;
- Public-interest grounds;
- **Reasoned** decision with right of appeal to Royal Court.
