# TCB Code of Practice — Customer Onboarding

Customer onboarding is the **operational gateway** through
which every TCB relationship passes. The Code's customer-
onboarding section operationalises Principle 5 (customer
treatment) and interlocks with the AML/CFT
[CDD obligations](../aml-cft/amlh-cdd.md) — a Jersey TCB
must satisfy **both** the customer-treatment expectations of
the TCB Code **and** the CDD obligations of the
[Money Laundering Order 2008](../aml-cft/money-laundering-order-2008.md)
+ [AML/CFT Handbook](../aml-cft/aml-cft-handbook.md) before
opening any client relationship.

## What the Code expects at onboarding

The Code requires that a TCB:

1. **Understand the prospective client** — who they are,
   what their proposed structure is, what they want the TCB
   to do;
2. **Assess the client for fit with the TCB's risk appetite**
   — not every prospective client is acceptable, and the TCB
   should be deliberate about which it takes;
3. **Document the assessment** — a file that survives
   inspection;
4. **Apply appropriate CDD** at the intensity the customer
   risk rating demands;
5. **Secure senior-management approval** where the customer
   is higher-risk or otherwise outside standard parameters;
6. **Communicate clearly** with the client about the
   relationship, fees, services, and expectations.

## The onboarding workflow

A typical Code-compliant onboarding workflow runs:

### Stage 1 — Initial enquiry / introduction

The prospective client makes contact (directly, via referral,
or via introducer). The TCB:

- Records the introduction source (introducer evidence
  matters for the
  [CDD reliance regime](../aml-cft/amlh-cdd.md));
- Captures initial information about the proposed
  relationship — type of structure, asset profile,
  beneficiary class;
- Performs **preliminary screening** — quick PEP / sanctions
  / adverse-media check before significant resources are
  invested.

### Stage 2 — Initial risk assessment

Before deep diligence, the TCB:

- Applies the [BWRA](../aml-cft/amlh-risk-assessment.md)
  factors to the prospective relationship — geography,
  product / service, customer type, delivery channel;
- Forms a preliminary customer risk rating;
- Decides whether the relationship is within risk appetite
  and worth pursuing.

A relationship that fails preliminary risk assessment is
declined here — the TCB does not progress to full
diligence.

### Stage 3 — Customer due diligence (CDD)

Per the [MLO 2008 CDD obligations](../aml-cft/mlo-part-3-cdd.md)
and the [AML/CFT Handbook CDD section](../aml-cft/amlh-cdd.md):

- **Identify the customer** — the relevant natural and legal
  persons in the proposed structure;
- **Verify identity** on reliable and independent evidence;
- **Identify the beneficial owners** (typically natural
  persons controlling 25%+ or otherwise exercising control);
- **Understand purpose and intended nature** of the
  relationship;
- **Understand source of funds** for the proposed
  transactions;
- **Apply EDD** where the
  [EDD triggers](../aml-cft/amlh-edd.md) apply (PEPs, high-
  risk countries, complex / opaque structures, customer
  risk rated higher, etc.);
- **Type-specific CDD** for trusts, foundations, companies,
  partnerships, charities — see
  [`amlh-customer-types.md`](../aml-cft/amlh-customer-types.md).

### Stage 4 — Suitability and fit assessment

Distinct from CDD: the **TCB-specific fit assessment**.

- Does the proposed structure make sense for the client's
  stated objectives?
- Is the TCB the right service provider for this structure
  (skill, capacity, conflicts)?
- Are the proposed beneficiaries / classes appropriate?
- Does the structure raise any **integrity** concerns under
  Principle 1?

A structure that satisfies AML/CFT CDD may still fail the
TCB-specific fit assessment — for example, where the
structure's purpose is unclear, the family circumstances
indicate disputes, or the proposed timetable is unrealistic.
Declining at this stage is normal and protective.

### Stage 5 — Senior-management approval

Before opening the relationship, the TCB obtains the
appropriate level of internal approval:

- **Standard relationships** — typically approved at
  designated senior-management level (e.g. relationship
  director + compliance director);
- **EDD relationships** — board or designated senior
  committee approval, with a documented rationale;
- **PEP relationships** — board or equivalent senior-most
  level, per the
  [PEP regime in the Handbook](../aml-cft/amlh-peps.md).

### Stage 6 — Engagement, fees, terms

Documented in:

- **Engagement letter / client agreement** — defines the
  services, fees, termination terms, applicable law (Jersey),
  dispute resolution;
- **Trust deed** (or equivalent constitutional document)
  for the structure;
- **Letter of wishes** where the trust is discretionary;
- **Investment management agreement** where investment-
  management services are within the engagement;
- **Confidentiality / data-protection** terms.

The Code expects **fair, clear, and not misleading**
communication — fees disclosed in full (including cost
escalation triggers), services scoped, expectations managed.

### Stage 7 — Ongoing monitoring set-up

Onboarding is not complete until the **ongoing-monitoring
arrangements** are in place:

- Periodic-review cadence calibrated to the customer risk
  rating (see [`amlh-ongoing-monitoring.md`](../aml-cft/amlh-ongoing-monitoring.md));
- Transaction-monitoring rules covering the expected
  activity profile;
- Trigger-event watchlists for the customer (PEP
  re-screening, sanctions re-screening, adverse-media
  monitoring).

## Common onboarding failure points

JFSC enforcement records surface recurring failures:

- **Insufficient CDD on beneficial ownership** —
  identification stopped at the immediate entity rather
  than working through to natural persons;
- **Source-of-wealth weak** — declaration-only or generic
  narrative without documentary evidence (see
  [`amlh-edd-source-of-wealth.md`](../aml-cft/amlh-edd-source-of-wealth.md));
- **Onboarding completed on incomplete CDD** with verbal
  commitment to "follow up" — the JFSC's position is that
  CDD must be complete **before** the relationship is
  opened, with narrow exceptions documented;
- **Senior-management approval pro-forma** — sign-off
  without evidence the approver actually reviewed the file;
- **Engagement-letter ambiguity** — fees or services
  insufficiently specified, leading to later disputes;
- **No documented decision rationale** — particularly for
  relationships within risk appetite but at the edge;
- **Fit assessment skipped** — CDD applied but TCB-specific
  fit assessment treated as automatic.

A onboarding programme that addresses each of these will
pass thematic-review scrutiny.

## Particular onboarding scenarios

### Multi-jurisdiction family structure

For HNW families with branches in multiple jurisdictions, the
onboarding spans **multiple persons across multiple home
jurisdictions**, each requiring CDD. Practical
considerations:

- The CDD discipline operates separately for each natural
  person beneficial owner;
- Country-risk assessment is multi-dimensional;
- Engagement letter often involves multiple family members
  signing — consents, conflicts among family branches.

### Successor trustee for an existing trust

Where the TCB is being appointed as **successor trustee** of
a trust that already exists (replacing a retiring or
outgoing trustee):

- CDD on the **trust** is a fresh exercise — not reliance
  on the predecessor's records;
- **Outgoing trustee indemnity** is part of the engagement
  package (see
  [`../trusts/outgoing-trustee.md`](../trusts/outgoing-trustee.md));
- **Transfer of trust property** is structured carefully —
  Royal Court blessing where the change is contested or
  involves a momentous decision.

### Private Trust Company (PTC) onboarding

Where the TCB is acting as administrator of a PTC (not
itself the trustee):

- CDD on the **PTC's underlying family** rather than the
  PTC entity alone;
- Verification of the PTC's exempt status under the
  prescribed conditions (single family, no public
  marketing — see
  [`./fsl-class-tcb.md`](./fsl-class-tcb.md));
- Engagement with the PTC's directors (some family
  members) about the TCB's role boundaries.
