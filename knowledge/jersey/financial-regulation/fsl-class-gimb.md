# FSL 1998 — General Insurance Mediation Business (GIMB)

GIMB covers **insurance broking and mediation** — services
arranging insurance between policyholders and insurers.

## Activities within scope

GIMB includes:

- **Insurance broking** — arranging insurance for clients;
- **Insurance advice** — recommending specific cover;
- **Claims handling** in some configurations;
- **Reinsurance broking** in some cases;
- **Captive consultancy** where it includes mediation
  elements.

## Activities outside scope

GIMB excludes:

- **Insurance underwriting** — regulated under the Insurance
  Business (Jersey) Law 1996 (IBL);
- **Reinsurance underwriting** — IBL;
- **Captive management** that doesn't involve mediation;
- **Specific exempt** activities (e.g., introductions only,
  ancillary services).

## Who provides GIMB

Jersey GIMB firms include:

- **Bank-owned** insurance broking arms;
- **Independent** brokers serving Jersey retail / commercial
  market;
- **Specialist** brokers for high-net-worth, marine, aviation,
  specialty;
- **Captive managers** acting as broker for captive
  participants;
- **Affinity** schemes (employee benefits, group cover).

## Registration requirements

A GIMB applicant must:

- **Register** under Article 7-9 of FSL;
- Be **fit and proper**;
- Maintain **adequate** capital (relatively lower than
  investment business);
- Have **professional indemnity** insurance;
- Have **Jersey** presence;
- Have **adequate AML / CFT** programme;
- Comply with **continuing obligations**.

## Code of Practice for GIMB

The Code addresses:

- **Customer-facing** standards (suitability, disclosure);
- **Commissions and fees** — transparency;
- **Claims handling** — fair and prompt;
- **Conflicts of interest** — manager vs broker conflicts;
- **Complaints** handling;
- **Client money** if held in fiduciary capacity.

## Cross-border insurance mediation

Jersey-based brokers serving:

- **Jersey** retail customers — full regime applies;
- **UK** customers — FCA mediation rules considerations;
- **EU** customers — country-by-country rules;
- **Captive** schemes for international groups — typically
  permissible under bespoke arrangements.

## Relationship with IBL

GIMB and the **Insurance Business (Jersey) Law 1996** intersect:

- **GIMB** governs mediators (brokers);
- **IBL** governs underwriters;
- A single firm doing **both** activities needs **both**
  authorisations;
- **Captive managers** providing both broker and management
  services may need careful authorisation mapping.
