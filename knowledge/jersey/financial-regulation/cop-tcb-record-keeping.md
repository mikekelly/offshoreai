# TCB Code of Practice — Record-Keeping

Record-keeping is the **evidentiary backbone** of every
other TCB obligation. The Code's record-keeping discipline
makes the firm's conduct **auditable** — by the JFSC, by
the firm's own management, by law enforcement, and (in
litigation) by the Royal Court and beneficiaries.

Two regimes apply in parallel:

- **TCB Code** record-keeping for general TCB conduct;
- **AML/CFT** record-keeping under
  [MLO 2008 Article 17](../aml-cft/mlo-article-17.md) and
  [Handbook](../aml-cft/amlh-record-keeping.md).

The two largely overlap but have distinct retention periods
and specifications.

## What records must be kept

The Code expects records of:

### Customer-relationship records

- **Onboarding pack** — all CDD documentation, risk
  assessment, senior-management approval evidence;
- **Engagement letter / client agreement** and any
  amendments;
- **Trust deed / foundation charter / company constitution**
  for the structures the TCB administers;
- **Letter of wishes** where applicable;
- **Beneficiary information** and family-structure mapping;
- **Investment management agreement** where applicable.

### Decision records

- **Board / committee minutes** for the TCB firm's decisions;
- **Trustee meeting minutes** for trustee decisions on each
  trust;
- **Director meeting minutes** for company directorships;
- **Distribution decision rationales** documented;
- **Investment decision rationales** documented;
- **Conflict-management decisions** documented.

### Operational records

- **Transaction records** for all trust / client transactions;
- **Bank statements** and reconciliations;
- **Custody confirmations**;
- **Counterparty correspondence**;
- **Internal compliance reviews** and findings.

### Regulatory records

- **JFSC notifications** and correspondence;
- **JFSC inspection records**;
- **AML/CFT records** per the parallel discipline;
- **Tax-reporting records** (CRS, FATCA, Annex IV);
- **MLRO / MLCO** appointment and change records.

## Retention periods

The Code's standard expectations (subject to verification
against the current publication):

| Record type | Retention |
|---|---|
| **CDD records** | At least 5 years from end of business relationship |
| **Transaction records** | At least 5 years from date of transaction |
| **SAR-related records** | At least 5 years from filing (often longer in practice) |
| **Trustee decision records** | Throughout the trust's life + a defined post-termination period |
| **Director decision records** | Throughout the directorship + a defined post-resignation period |
| **Litigation-relevant records** | For the duration of relevant limitation periods |
| **JFSC supervisory records** | At least 5 years from creation |

**Longer where required** by law-enforcement request,
pending investigation, or contractual obligation to the
client.

## Form

The Code permits **electronic record-keeping** provided:

- Records are **retrievable** in usable form;
- Records are **legible** (text searchable / readable
  formats);
- Records are **tamper-evident** — write-once or
  cryptographically-protected storage where appropriate;
- **Original signed documents** are preserved either in
  physical form or in evidentiary-quality electronic form
  (some legal documents require originals for evidentiary
  purposes in litigation).

## Retrievability and access

The Code's underlying purpose: records must be **available
on demand**. Operational expectations:

- **The full client file** can be assembled within a
  reasonable time on request;
- **JFSC inspection access** is supported through documented
  procedures;
- **Law-enforcement access** — pursuant to lawful request —
  is provided promptly;
- **Cross-jurisdictional record-access** for records held
  outside Jersey via outsourced storage is reliably
  enforceable.

## Particular record-keeping scenarios

### Long-established trust

For a trust that has been administered for decades:

- **Multi-format records** spanning paper, microfilm,
  early-digital, modern-digital systems;
- **Predecessor-trustee records** transferred on successor
  appointment;
- **Format-migration evidence** documenting that records
  have been preserved across system changes.

### Trust with sensitive beneficiary information

Beneficiary information may include health, sexuality,
political affiliations, family conflicts. The records must:

- **Protect confidentiality** consistent with the
  [Data Protection Law 2018](../data-protection/index.md);
- **Restrict access** to authorised personnel;
- **Survive RM / staff turnover** with continuity of
  appropriate-need-to-know.

### Records relevant to ongoing litigation

Where the TCB or its trusts are in actual or contemplated
litigation:

- **Litigation hold** procedures apply — no destruction of
  relevant records;
- **Privileged communications** identified and treated
  separately;
- **Records-of-records** — meta-information about what
  records exist and where — is itself preserved.

### Cross-border records

Where records are held outside Jersey (e.g. on cloud servers
in third-country jurisdictions):

- **Data-protection compliance** for cross-border data
  flows;
- **Sovereign-access risk** — where records sit in a
  jurisdiction whose authorities may access them under
  local law;
- **JFSC enforceable access rights** must be preserved
  through contract.

## Common record-keeping failure modes

JFSC enforcement themes:

- **Decision rationales not documented** — minutes record
  the decision but not the reasoning;
- **Multi-format archives unreliable** — older records
  effectively inaccessible;
- **Email and chat records** not captured systematically;
- **Retention destruction** premature — records destroyed
  before the retention period expires;
- **Litigation hold breaches** — destruction of records
  during litigation;
- **Cross-border access** untested until needed;
- **Subsidiary / group records** held inconsistently
  across entities.
