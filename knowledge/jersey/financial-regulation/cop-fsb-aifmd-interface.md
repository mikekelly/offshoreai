# FSB Code of Practice — AIFMD Interface

A Jersey fund-services business that acts as **Alternative
Investment Fund Manager (AIFM)** for a Jersey AIF — or that
provides services to an AIF marketed into the EU under
National Private Placement Regimes (NPPR) — operates at the
**intersection of the FSB Code and the AIFMD framework**.
This file is the orientation surface for that intersection.

For the substance of:

- The AIFMD framework itself, see
  [`../funds/aifmd-nppr.md`](../funds/aifmd-nppr.md) and the
  [Alternative Investment Funds (Jersey) Regulations 2012](https://www.jerseylaw.je/laws/current/Pages/17.245.51.aspx);
- **AIFMD II** (most requirements effective 16 April 2026),
  see the frontier file
  [`../../frontier/aifmd-ii-april-2026.md`](../../frontier/aifmd-ii-april-2026.md);
- The Jersey AIF Code itself, see
  [`./cop-aif-index.md`](./cop-aif-index.md).

## What "AIFMD interface" means

The FSB Code is **Jersey-side**. AIFMD is **EU-side** (with
NPPR carve-outs for non-EU AIFMs marketing into individual
member states). For a Jersey-based AIFM, the operational
reality is **both regimes apply simultaneously**:

- **FSB Code** governs the JFSC-regulated activity in
  Jersey;
- **AIFMD requirements** apply via the
  [AIF (Jersey) Regulations 2012](https://www.jerseylaw.je/laws/current/Pages/17.245.51.aspx)
  for the AIFM function itself, and via NPPR rules in each
  EU member state where the AIF is marketed.

The FSB Code's AIFMD-interface section addresses how
JFSC-side conduct standards interact with the AIFMD-style
obligations.

## Jersey's NPPR positioning

Jersey is **not an EU member state**. Post-Brexit, Jersey
maintains AIF marketing into EU member states via **NPPR**
(national private placement regimes) negotiated bilaterally
with each EU jurisdiction. Key facts:

- **No AIFMD passport** — Jersey AIFMs marketing into EU
  jurisdictions must satisfy each member state's specific
  NPPR rules;
- **Article 42 framework** — the AIFMD Article 42
  conditions form the baseline NPPR substance: depositary,
  remuneration, leverage limits, Annex IV reporting;
- **Member-state variations** — France, Germany, Sweden,
  others have specific local rules layered on top.

For a Jersey AIFM marketing into multiple EU member states,
operational compliance spans **each member state's NPPR
regime** simultaneously.

## Annex IV reporting

The AIFMD framework requires AIFMs to file **Annex IV
reports** with national competent authorities for each
member state into which they market. For Jersey AIFMs, this
operates via:

- **Periodic reporting** (typically quarterly for larger
  AIFs, semi-annually or annually for smaller) on AIF
  positions, leverage, risk exposures, principal markets;
- **Filed through the JFSC** as the home regulator, with
  forwarding to relevant ESMA / member-state authorities;
- **Significant operational uplift** — Annex IV is data-
  intensive and the systems / process for compliant filing
  is a material undertaking.

AIFMD II (16 April 2026) materially extends Annex IV
reporting obligations — see
[`../../frontier/aifmd-ii-april-2026.md`](../../frontier/aifmd-ii-april-2026.md).
The Jersey AIF Code
([`./cop-aif-index.md`](./cop-aif-index.md), effective same
date) aligns with these uplifted reporting requirements.

## Depositary requirements

For AIFs marketed into EU member states under Article 42
NPPR, **depositary arrangements** are required. The Jersey
position:

- The depositary is typically a **Jersey-licensed bank or
  custody business** (or an EU-domiciled depositary where
  the marketing strategy requires);
- The depositary's role includes safekeeping of assets,
  cash-flow monitoring, and oversight of the AIFM's
  compliance;
- The depositary must have **arrangements with the AIFM**
  on access, reporting, and information flow.

## Remuneration disclosure

The AIFMD remuneration provisions apply to AIFMs. Key
requirements:

- **Identified staff** whose professional activities have a
  material impact on the AIF's risk profile;
- **Remuneration policy** covering identified staff;
- **Variable / fixed ratio** controls;
- **Deferral and clawback** provisions;
- **Annual disclosure** of aggregate remuneration data;
- **Alignment** between AIFM staff remuneration and AIF
  investor interests.

For smaller Jersey AIFMs, the **proportionality principle**
may reduce the formality of compliance, but the substantive
obligation applies.

## Leverage limits and reporting

AIFs have **leverage limits** disclosed to investors and
reported to the regulator. The AIFM:

- Calculates leverage using prescribed methods (gross /
  commitment methods);
- Discloses leverage limits in the AIF's offering documents;
- Reports actual leverage in Annex IV;
- Has internal procedures for breach detection and
  remediation.

## Service-provider arrangements

A Jersey FSB providing services to an AIF (administration,
depositary, custody, transfer agency) operates at the
intersection of:

- **The FSB Code** — its own conduct-of-business
  obligations;
- **The AIFM's regulatory obligations** — the AIFM is
  ultimately responsible for the AIF's compliance;
- **The AIF's constitutional documents and offering
  materials** — service-provider obligations specified at
  the fund level.

The interlocking nature means **a Jersey FSB cannot rely
solely on its own Code** — its obligations under the
service-provider agreement and the AIFM's regulatory
position both shape its operations.

## Common interface failure points

JFSC-related themes:

- **Annex IV data quality** — reports filed but data
  inaccuracies or omissions later surfaced;
- **Depositary oversight gaps** — depositary appointed but
  the substantive oversight relationship is light;
- **Remuneration policy formality** — policy documented but
  not applied substantively;
- **NPPR rule variations** missed across member states —
  the AIFM operates to a generic AIFMD standard rather than
  member-state-specific NPPR;
- **AIFMD II transition** — for the 16 April 2026 changes,
  inadequate transition planning is the foreseeable failure
  mode in the next reporting cycle.

## Status

Draft. AIFMD II (16 April 2026) is recent; the operational
implementation across NPPR member states is still
evolving. Verify against current JFSC AIF Code, ESMA
guidance, and member-state NPPR rules for any specific
operational application.
