# JFSC Enforcement Powers

The JFSC has a **graduated set of enforcement powers** for
addressing non-compliance by regulated entities:

| Power | Description |
|---|---|
| **Direction** | Order requiring specific conduct or remediation |
| **Public statement** | Public naming of misconduct |
| **Civil financial penalty (CFP)** | Money penalty up to prescribed limits |
| **Conditions** | Restrictions on registration |
| **Suspension** | Temporary withdrawal of registration |
| **Revocation** | Permanent withdrawal of registration |
| **Disqualification** | Removal of specific individuals from regulated roles |
| **Prosecution referral** | Criminal cases referred to the Attorney General |

## Civil Financial Penalties

The **Civil Financial Penalties (Financial Services Business)
(Jersey) Order 2015** allows the JFSC to impose **money
penalties** without going to court.

Penalty amounts are calibrated by:

- **Type of regulated entity** (banks face higher caps than
  smaller firms);
- **Seriousness** of the breach;
- **Whether the breach was deliberate or negligent**;
- **Repeat offences**;
- **Cooperation** with the JFSC investigation.

The CFP regime is **administratively efficient** — most cases
are resolved through:

- **JFSC investigation**;
- **Provisional findings** issued to the firm;
- **Firm representations** considered;
- **Final notice** with penalty determination;
- **Right of appeal** to the Royal Court.

## Public statements

The JFSC issues **public statements** for:

- **Final notices** of enforcement action against named firms;
- **Industry-wide warnings** about emerging risks;
- **Specific guidance** on regulatory expectations.

Public statements are a **significant reputational consequence**
beyond the formal penalty.

## Directions

A **direction** is an order requiring:

- **Specific actions** to remediate breaches (e.g. enhance
  systems, change personnel);
- **Restrictions** on activities (e.g. no new business);
- **Reporting** to the JFSC on progress.

Directions are commonly the **first step** in a graduated
response — public statements and penalties follow if
directions are not complied with.

## Suspension and revocation

For serious non-compliance:

- **Suspension** of registration for a defined period;
- **Revocation** permanently — equivalent to expulsion from the
  industry.

Suspension and revocation are reserved for the most serious
cases — they effectively put the firm out of business.

## Individual sanctions

The JFSC can also act against **individuals**:

- **Disqualification** of an individual from regulated roles
  under prescribed powers;
- **Withdrawal** of fit-and-proper status;
- **Personal CFP** in some circumstances.

## Cooperation with other authorities

The JFSC works with:

- **Police** and **Attorney General** for criminal matters;
- **Comptroller of Revenue** for tax-related issues;
- **Foreign regulators** for cross-border matters under
  Memoranda of Understanding (MoUs) and IOSCO MMoU.
