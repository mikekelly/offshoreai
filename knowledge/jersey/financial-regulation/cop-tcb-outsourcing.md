# TCB Code of Practice — Outsourcing

A Jersey TCB may outsource operational functions to third
parties — domestic or non-Jersey — subject to defined controls.
The Code's outsourcing section sets out **when outsourcing is
permissible**, **what notifications are required**, and **what
continuing responsibilities remain with the regulated TCB**.

## The principle — non-delegation of responsibility

The Code's foundational outsourcing principle: **a TCB can
outsource the function but not the responsibility**. The
regulated TCB remains accountable to the JFSC for every
outsourced activity, regardless of who actually performs it.

This means:

- **The TCB chooses the provider carefully** — capability,
  capacity, control environment, regulatory standing;
- **The TCB designs the arrangement** — contractual rights,
  service levels, performance reporting, audit rights, exit
  rights;
- **The TCB monitors performance** continuously — provider
  failures are TCB failures from the JFSC's perspective;
- **The TCB can terminate** the arrangement and bring the
  function back in-house.

## Categories of outsourcing

The Code distinguishes (broadly) two categories, with
different controls:

### Material outsourcing

Functions that, if performed inadequately, would have a
material adverse impact on the TCB's ability to:

- Meet its Code obligations;
- Manage its principal risks;
- Maintain customer service.

Material outsourcing typically requires:

- **JFSC notification** (and in some cases consent) before
  the arrangement is entered into;
- A written **outsourcing agreement** with prescribed
  content;
- **Specific board approval** of the arrangement;
- **Ongoing reporting** to the board on performance;
- **Annual review** of the arrangement.

### Non-material outsourcing

Functions where outsourcing has a lower impact (e.g.
secretarial services, IT helpdesk, premises services).
Lighter controls apply, but the foundational principle of
non-delegation of responsibility still applies.

The line between material and non-material is **fact-
sensitive**. A function that is non-material in a small TCB
may be material in a larger one, and vice versa. The TCB
documents its categorisation methodology and applies it
consistently.

## Required controls — material outsourcing

The Code's expectations on material outsourcing typically
cover:

### Provider selection and due diligence

- **Capability assessment** — does the provider have the
  skill, capacity, and infrastructure to deliver?
- **Reputation and regulatory standing** of the provider;
- **Financial soundness** — provider failure risk;
- **Control environment** — provider's own governance, risk
  management, AML/CFT controls (if relevant to the
  outsourced function);
- **Conflict-of-interest screening**;
- **References** from existing customers where appropriate.

### Written agreement content

The outsourcing agreement should cover:

- **Scope of services** — precisely defined;
- **Service levels** with measurable targets;
- **Reporting** by the provider to the TCB;
- **Performance escalation** procedures;
- **Confidentiality and data protection** obligations;
- **JFSC access rights** — the regulator can require
  access to outsourced records and to the provider's
  premises;
- **Records retention** consistent with the TCB's own
  obligations;
- **Termination rights** — for cause and at notice;
- **Exit transition** — how a function would return
  in-house or to an alternative provider;
- **Indemnities and liability** allocation;
- **Governing law** (typically Jersey).

### Ongoing monitoring

- **Periodic performance reviews** against service levels;
- **Risk-event reporting** — provider-side incidents flowed
  through to the TCB;
- **Annual deep review** by senior management;
- **Trigger-event reassessment** — material change in
  provider circumstance (acquisition, regulatory action,
  capability change).

## Cross-border outsourcing

Outsourcing to non-Jersey providers adds layers:

- **Equivalent regulatory standards** of the provider's
  jurisdiction matter (particularly for AML/CFT-relevant
  functions);
- **JFSC consent** is often required for material cross-
  border outsourcing — verify against current Code
  publication;
- **Data protection** — cross-border data flows must comply
  with the [Data Protection (Jersey) Law 2018](../data-protection/index.md);
- **JFSC access rights** must be enforceable in the
  provider's jurisdiction (memoranda of cooperation between
  regulators where applicable);
- **Sanctions screening** of the provider's jurisdiction
  itself — high-risk-country outsourcing typically not
  acceptable.

## Common outsourcing scenarios for TCBs

### Fund administration outsourced to a non-Jersey administrator

Common for fund-services-adjacent TCBs. The TCB retains
fiduciary responsibility for the trust / structure; the
administrator performs operational tasks (bookkeeping,
reporting). Cross-references the
[FSB Code](./cop-fsb-index.md) where the administrator is
itself JFSC-regulated, or external regulator regimes
otherwise.

### Custody outsourced to a non-Jersey custodian

Sub-custody arrangements where the legal owner of the asset
(typically a Jersey-incorporated company below the trust)
holds via a custody chain. The TCB monitors the chain's
operational integrity.

### IT and infrastructure outsourcing

Increasingly cloud-based. The Code is technology-neutral but
expects the same controls — capability, contract,
monitoring, access, exit. Particular attention to:

- Data residency and cross-border data flow;
- Service-availability and disaster-recovery;
- Provider lock-in and exit-cost considerations.

### Compliance / MLRO outsourcing

A TCB can outsource specific compliance tasks but **cannot
outsource the MLRO function itself** — the MLRO is a
designated principal person under the
[FSL 1998](./fsl-class-tcb.md) and the
[MLO 2008 Article 16](../aml-cft/mlo-article-16.md), and the
function must reside in a Jersey-employed individual at
appropriate seniority. Sub-tasks (transaction monitoring,
sanctions screening operations) can be outsourced subject
to the MLRO's continuing oversight.

## Common outsourcing failure points

JFSC enforcement and thematic-review patterns surface:

- **Outsourcing without notification** where notification was
  required;
- **Material outsourcing categorised as non-material** —
  inadequate controls applied to material functions;
- **Provider monitoring inadequate** — initial diligence good,
  ongoing oversight light;
- **Exit rights unenforceable in practice** — the contract
  permits termination but the operational dependence makes
  termination impractical;
- **JFSC access blocked at the provider** — the contractual
  right exists but the practical implementation does not;
- **Sub-outsourcing not controlled** — the provider sub-
  contracts and the TCB's controls do not flow through.

A outsourcing programme that addresses each of these will
withstand thematic-review scrutiny.

## Interaction with the FCA Handbook (group context)

For Jersey TCBs that are part of groups with UK FCA-
authorised subsidiaries — see
[`../external-rules/fca-handbook-jersey-touchpoints.md`](../external-rules/fca-handbook-jersey-touchpoints.md) —
intra-group outsourcing between Jersey and UK affiliates
must satisfy **both** Jersey JFSC outsourcing controls and
UK FCA SYSC 8 outsourcing controls. The two regimes overlap
in substance but have distinct documentation and reporting
requirements.
