# JFSC Director General Speeches & Strategic Communications — Index

JFSC leadership — the **Director General** and other Board /
senior-management figures — periodically speak at industry
events and publish strategic communications. These speeches
are an **early signal of strategic direction** and supervisory
priority shifts.

This is the **per-surface index**.

## Why DG speeches matter

Three operational uses:

1. **Strategic direction-of-travel** — speeches typically
   pre-announce themes that later land in formal publications
   (Codes, Handbook, strategy documents). The lead time can
   be 12–24 months.
2. **Tone-setting** — supervisory tone (proportionate /
   risk-based vs prescriptive / formalistic) is calibrated
   through speech-level communication before policy-level
   publication.
3. **Cross-jurisdictional context** — DG speeches at FATF,
   IOSCO, IAIS, and similar fora signal international
   pressures Jersey is navigating, which surface in domestic
   supervision 12–24 months later.

## How entries should be structured

Each speech entry (when populated) should carry:

- **Date** and **venue**;
- **Speaker** (DG, Chair of Board, other senior figure —
  named in primary-source-citation capacity);
- **Theme** — what supervisory or strategic topic was
  addressed;
- **Substantive points** — the speech's load-bearing claims;
- **Forward-looking signals** — what changes the speech
  pre-announces;
- **Cross-references** to the
  [JFSC 2026–2030 Strategy](./jfsc-strategy-2026-2030.md),
  relevant Codes, and Handbook surfaces;
- **`as_of`** date.
