# BDL 1990 Part 3 — Effect on the Debtor and Property

## Article 10 — Vesting (detail)

The vesting in the Viscount under Article 6 is **automatic**
and **comprehensive**:

- **All property** the debtor owned at declaration;
- **Property** acquired during désastre subject to specific
  rules;
- **Property** held on trust **does not vest** (insolvency-
  remote);
- **Property** subject to a **valid security interest** vests
  subject to the security;
- **Foreign property** vests as far as Jersey law applies;
  enforcement abroad requires recognition.

## Article 11 — Property excluded

Excluded from vesting:

- **Tools of trade** below prescribed value;
- **Necessary clothing**;
- **Basic household effects**;
- **Specific personal items** of limited value;
- **Property held in fiduciary capacity** (trustee, nominee,
  agent — but only what is genuinely fiduciary).

## Article 12 — Debtor's capacity

The debtor:

- **Cannot dispose of property** that has vested;
- **Cannot incur debts** without disclosing désastre;
- **Cannot trade** as principal without disclosure;
- **Cannot act** as director or controller of regulated
  business without Court / regulator approval;
- **Cannot accept** trust appointments during désastre;
- Has **limited cash** for living expenses (subject to
  Viscount approval).

## Article 13 — Restrictions on regulated activity

A debtor en désastre:

- **Cannot** act as a director (subject to Court permission);
- **Cannot** hold registration as financial-services
  principal person;
- **Cannot** hold specific public-trust offices;
- **Restrictions** continue until discharge.

## Article 14 — Notifications

The Viscount notifies:

- **Banks** holding accounts of the debtor;
- **Companies Registry** (for company debtors);
- **JFSC** (for regulated debtors);
- **Comptroller of Revenue**;
- **Other** relevant authorities.

## Article 15 — Powers of investigation

The Viscount may:

- **Examine** the debtor (compulsory examination);
- **Examine** spouse, business associates, others with
  knowledge;
- **Require** production of documents;
- **Enter premises** with Court authority;
- **Require** banks to disclose accounts.

## Article 16 — Set-off and netting

**Set-off** between the debtor's claims and mutual claims of
creditors:

- **Mutual debts** subject to **netting** under statutory
  rules;
- **Provable claims** can be set off against debts owed by
  the debtor;
- **Bank** depositor with overdraft / loan is set off mutually;
- **Specific protections** for financial-market contracts
  (close-out netting).
