# BDL 1990 Article 6 — Effect of Declaration of Désastre

Article 6 sets out the **legal consequences** of a Royal Court
declaration of désastre.

## The provision

On declaration:

- The debtor's **property vests in the Viscount**;
- The debtor's **capacity to deal with property** is
  suspended;
- **Creditors' enforcement actions** against the debtor are
  **stayed**;
- The debtor must **cooperate** with the Viscount;
- The Viscount **assumes** statutory functions.

## Vesting

All of the debtor's property (subject to limited exclusions)
**vests in the Viscount**:

- **Real property** (Jersey and overseas, subject to
  jurisdiction issues for foreign property);
- **Personal property** (cash, equities, investments, vehicles,
  chattels);
- **Contractual rights** (subject to non-assignment
  restrictions);
- **Causes of action** the debtor has;
- **Future property** acquired during désastre (in some
  cases).

Property **excluded** from vesting (typically narrow):

- **Tools of trade** necessary for earning a living (limited
  value);
- **Basic household effects**;
- **Specific exempt categories** prescribed by Order;
- **Trust property** held by the debtor as trustee (does not
  vest);
- **Property** that has already vested in another office-
  holder (e.g., earlier-appointed receiver).

## Suspension of debtor's capacity

The debtor:

- **Cannot transfer** property without Viscount consent;
- **Cannot incur debts** beyond modest living expenses;
- **Cannot act as director** of companies during désastre;
- **Cannot hold** specific office (e.g., regulated industry
  principal person);
- **Must obtain** Viscount approval for specified actions.

For **companies**, désastre typically leads to **dissolution**;
the company effectively ceases operating activities.

## Stay of creditor enforcement

Once declared:

- **Existing legal actions** against the debtor are stayed
  (cannot proceed without leave of Court);
- **Enforcement** of judgments suspended;
- **Levy** on assets prohibited;
- **Counterclaim** in pending litigation continues but
  subject to désastre control;
- **Crown debts** (taxes, customs) subject to same stay as
  general creditors.

## Cooperation obligations

The debtor must:

- **Disclose** all property to the Viscount;
- **Provide** records (accounts, contracts, correspondence);
- **Attend** examinations and meetings as required;
- **Answer questions** truthfully;
- **Surrender** specific items (passport in some cases,
  business records);
- **Inform** the Viscount of property acquired during
  désastre.

Non-cooperation is a **criminal offence** and grounds for
extension of désastre period or denial of discharge.

## Notice to third parties

The declaration is:

- **Recorded** in the Royal Court records;
- **Published** by the Viscount;
- **Notified** to specified parties (banks holding accounts,
  Companies Registry for company debtors, JFSC for regulated
  debtors);
- **Effective** against third parties from notification.

A bank, customer, or counterparty dealing with the debtor
after notification of désastre acts at risk — they cannot
acquire good title against the Viscount.
