# BDL 1990 Part 1 — Preliminary

## Article 1 — Interpretation

Key definitions:

- **"Court"** — the Royal Court of Jersey;
- **"Debtor"** — the person whose property is en désastre;
- **"Désastre"** — the statutory insolvency status;
- **"Property"** — defined widely (real, personal, in
  possession, in action; tangible and intangible);
- **"Viscount"** — the executive officer of the Royal Court;
- **"Creditor"** — a person to whom the debtor owes money or
  has a contractual obligation;
- **"Discharge"** — the formal release of a debtor at the
  end of the désastre period;
- **"Transaction at an undervalue"** — a transaction for
  consideration significantly less than market value;
- **"Preference"** — a transaction favouring one creditor
  over others;
- **"Property exempt from désastre"** — limited categories of
  property the debtor retains (tools of trade, basic clothing,
  etc.).

## Article 2 — Application

The Law applies to:

- **Individuals** ordinarily resident in Jersey;
- **Companies** incorporated in Jersey;
- **Foreign companies** with **sufficient nexus** to Jersey
  (operations, assets, contracts);
- **Foundations** in some circumstances;
- **Partnerships** in some circumstances;
- **Other legal persons** as the Court determines.

The Law does **not** apply to:

- **Crown bodies**;
- **Authorities** with specific statutory exemption;
- **Foreign individuals** without Jersey nexus (their home-
  jurisdiction insolvency applies).

For **trustees** of a trust:

- Trustee's **personal estate** is subject to BDL;
- Trustee's **trust property** is **not** part of the
  personal estate (insolvency-remote);
- This distinction is **critical** for trust practice — see
  [`../trusts/trustee-estate-and-insolvency.md`](../trusts/trustee-estate-and-insolvency.md).
