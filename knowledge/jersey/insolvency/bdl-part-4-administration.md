# BDL 1990 Part 4 — Administration

Article 17 (Viscount's functions) has its own deep file. This
cluster covers Articles 18-25.

## Article 18 — Public examinations

The Viscount may apply for a **public examination** of:

- The **debtor**;
- **Officers** of a corporate debtor (directors, secretary,
  managers);
- **Spouse** or family member with knowledge;
- **Business associates**;
- **Persons** who have transacted with the debtor.

The examination:

- Is **on oath**;
- **In open Court** or in chambers as ordered;
- Subjects the examinee to **questioning** by the Viscount;
- **Records** can be used in subsequent proceedings;
- **Failure to attend** is contempt of Court.

## Article 19 — Production of documents

The Viscount may require:

- **Debtor** to produce records;
- **Banks** to produce account records;
- **Solicitors** to produce client records (subject to
  privilege);
- **Accountants** to produce work papers;
- **Third parties** with relevant material.

**Legal professional privilege** is **preserved** but the
**crime-fraud exception** may apply to dishonest transactions.

## Article 20 — Records

The Viscount must maintain:

- **Inventory** of property received;
- **Schedule** of sales and realisations;
- **Receipts and disbursements** ledger;
- **Adjudication** records for creditor claims;
- **Distribution** records;
- **Correspondence** with creditors and Court;
- **Records retained** post-discharge.

## Article 21 — Creditors meetings

The Viscount may convene **creditors' meetings**:

- To **report** on progress;
- To **seek views** on contentious matters;
- To **report** on proposed compromises;
- To **vote** on specific matters where required.

Creditors' meetings are **less formal** than in English
insolvency — much of désastre administration is by Viscount
authority subject to Court oversight rather than by creditor
vote.

## Article 22 — Proofs of debt

Creditors **prove** their claims:

- **Submit proof** in prescribed form;
- **Document** the debt (contracts, invoices, judgments);
- **Confirm** any security held;
- **Confirm** any set-off applicable;
- **Submit** within prescribed timeframe.

The Viscount **adjudicates**:

- **Admits** valid proofs;
- **Rejects** invalid proofs with reasons;
- **Adjourns** for further evidence if needed;
- **Subject** to Court appeal.

## Article 23 — Continuation of business

Where appropriate:

- The Viscount may **continue** the debtor's business
  temporarily (e.g., to preserve going-concern value);
- **Subject** to Court approval;
- **Subject** to specific safeguards.

This is **less common** than in some other jurisdictions —
Jersey désastre is typically a **liquidation**-style process
rather than a **reorganisation**.

## Article 24 — Sale of assets

The Viscount may sell:

- **By auction**, **private treaty**, or **tender**;
- At **market value** or best obtainable price;
- **Engaging** professional advisers as appropriate;
- **Subject** to Court approval for contentious or unusual
  sales;
- **Subject** to **secured creditors' interests** (sale subject
  to security or with security paid out of proceeds).

## Article 25 — Specific transaction approval

For specified transactions, the Viscount must seek **Court
approval**:

- **Sale of real property** above prescribed value;
- **Sale** to connected parties (debtor's relatives,
  associates);
- **Compromise** of major claims;
- **Continuation** of business beyond specified period;
- **Other** contentious matters.
