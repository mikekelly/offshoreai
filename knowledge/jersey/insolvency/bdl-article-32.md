# BDL 1990 Article 32 — Priority of Claims

Article 32 sets out the **statutory priority** for distribution
of désastre estate proceeds to creditors.

## The priority waterfall

### 1. Costs and expenses

**First**: costs of the désastre itself:

- **Viscount's fees** and expenses;
- **Court fees**;
- **Costs** of legal proceedings necessary to the désastre
  (e.g., avoidance actions, asset recovery);
- **Costs** of preserving and realising assets;
- **Costs** of essential business continuity (where business
  is continued).

These rank **ahead of all other claims** including secured
creditors' deficiencies.

### 2. Secured creditors (to extent of security)

**Secured creditors** are paid:

- **Out of their security** in priority;
- **Up to** the value of the security;
- **Excess** of debt over security value reverts to
  **unsecured** ranking;
- **Excess** of security value over debt reverts to estate
  for general distribution.

Security includes:

- **Mortgages and hypothecs** over Jersey real property;
- **Security interests** over personal property under the
  Security Interests (Jersey) Law 2012;
- **Specific liens** at common law / customary law;
- **Charges** under Jersey or foreign law.

### 3. Preferential creditors

**Preferential creditors** rank **above unsecured** creditors
in the estate:

- **Recent employee wages** — typically last 4 months
  capped at a per-employee statutory limit;
- **Holiday pay** for employees;
- **Pension contributions** deducted but unpaid;
- **Specific tax obligations** — recent VAT-equivalent and
  income-tax deductions in some cases;
- **Specific Crown** debts as prescribed.

Preferential creditors are **paid in priority** to unsecured
but **after** secured (subject to their security).

### 4. Unsecured creditors

The **bulk of trade creditors** rank as unsecured:

- **Trade debt** owed;
- **Loans** without security;
- **Damages** for breach of contract;
- **Tort** judgments;
- **Tax** beyond preferential ranks;
- **Most** other claims.

Unsecured creditors are paid **pari passu** — proportionate
share of available funds. If only 30 pence in the pound is
available, each unsecured creditor receives 30% of their debt.

### 5. Subordinated creditors

**Subordinated** creditors rank below ordinary unsecured:

- **Contractually subordinated** debt (e.g., shareholder
  loans subordinated to bank lending);
- **Connected-party** loans where the connection or
  circumstances justify subordination;
- **Statutorily subordinated** debts (e.g., certain
  shareholder claims).

### 6. Members / shareholders

Last in line:

- **Residual equity** distribution;
- **Only paid** if all higher classes are paid in full;
- **Rarely** receive anything in practice.

## Set-off

Before distribution, **set-off** applies:

- **Mutual debts** between debtor and creditor are netted;
- **Resulting balance** (one way or the other) determines
  the claim or property;
- **Financial market** close-out netting protected under
  specific legislation.

## Foreign creditors

Foreign creditors:

- **Rank pari passu** with Jersey unsecured creditors
  (typically);
- **Submit proofs** under same rules;
- **Receive distributions** in the same proportions;
- **Subject to** any specific treaty arrangements.

## Trust assets

**Trust property** held by the debtor as **trustee** is:

- **Not part of** the estate;
- **Returned to** the trust (or new trustee);
- **Not available** to creditors of the debtor personally.
