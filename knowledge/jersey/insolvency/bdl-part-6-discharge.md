# BDL 1990 Part 6 — Distribution and Discharge (residual)

Article 32 (priority of claims) has its own deep file. This
cluster covers Articles 26-31 (distribution mechanics) and
33-40 (discharge).

## Distribution mechanics (Articles 26-31)

### Receipt of proofs

The Viscount:

- **Publishes** notice of désastre with proof-submission
  details;
- **Sets** deadline for proofs;
- **Receives** proofs in prescribed form;
- **Adjudicates** validity.

### Calculation of distribution

- **Total** estate proceeds calculated after costs;
- **Allocated** to priority classes per Article 32;
- **Pari passu** distribution within unsecured class;
- **Notice** to creditors of proposed distribution;
- **Opportunity** to object;
- **Court approval** for material distributions.

### Interim and final distributions

- **Interim** distributions may be made as assets realised;
- **Reserves** maintained for contingent claims;
- **Final** distribution after all assets realised and claims
  adjudicated;
- **Surplus** (rare) returned to debtor / members.

## Discharge (Articles 33-40)

### Automatic discharge

For **individual debtors**:

- **Automatic discharge** after **4 years** from declaration
  (typical default);
- **Subject to** Court power to extend or refuse;
- **Court can** also discharge earlier on application;
- **Restrictions** on borrowing during désastre lifted on
  discharge.

For **company debtors**:

- No "discharge" in the personal sense;
- Désastre typically ends in **dissolution**;
- Company **struck off** the register.

### Effect of discharge

For individuals:

- **Released** from most pre-désastre debts;
- **Restored** to capacity (subject to specific exceptions);
- **No longer** subject to désastre restrictions;
- **Future earnings** are the debtor's own.

### Non-discharged debts

Some debts **survive** discharge:

- **Fraud-tainted** debts;
- **Court fines** and **criminal damages**;
- **Family-court** maintenance orders;
- **Specific tax** obligations (in some cases);
- **Debts not** properly proved (but the creditor often
  cannot enforce against a discharged debtor anyway).

### Refusal or postponement of discharge

The Court may **refuse** or **postpone** discharge if:

- The debtor has **failed to cooperate**;
- The debtor has **concealed assets**;
- The debtor has been **dishonest** in désastre;
- The debtor has **incurred new debts** during désastre
  without disclosure;
- The debtor has **subsequent désastres** showing pattern.

### Restoration to capacity

On discharge:

- **Bankruptcy restrictions** lifted;
- **Can act as director** again;
- **Can hold regulated positions** subject to fit-and-proper
  reassessment;
- **Reputation** restoration in legal sense (commercial
  reputation depends on facts).

### Re-désastre

A discharged debtor who falls into further insolvency:

- **Can be subject to new désastre**;
- **Subject to enhanced scrutiny** given history;
- **Discharge** more difficult to obtain;
- **Restrictions** more likely.
