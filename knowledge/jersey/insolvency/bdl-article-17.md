# BDL 1990 Article 17 — Viscount's Functions

Article 17 sets out the **statutory functions** of the
Viscount in désastre.

## Who the Viscount is

The **Viscount** is the **executive officer** of the Royal
Court — a Crown office of historic origin (the title is the
Norman *vicomte*). The Viscount:

- Is **appointed** by the Crown;
- **Serves** the Royal Court in various ceremonial and
  practical capacities;
- **Acts** as bailiff of the Royal Court for execution of
  orders;
- In désastre, **acts as office-holder** of the debtor's
  estate;
- **Reports to** the Royal Court.

## Functions in désastre

The Viscount must:

### Take control

- **Take possession** of the debtor's property;
- **Secure** assets against dissipation;
- **Notify** banks, registries, third parties;
- **Maintain inventory** of property received.

### Investigate

- **Examine** the debtor's records and affairs;
- **Trace** transactions in the lookback period;
- **Identify** preferences, undervalue transactions,
  fraudulent transfers;
- **Question** the debtor, family, business associates;
- **Apply to Court** for compulsory examinations;
- **Report** suspected criminal conduct to the AG.

### Realise

- **Sell** property at market value;
- **Use** appropriate sale methods (auction, private treaty,
  tender);
- **Engage** professional valuers and agents;
- **Convert** non-cash assets to cash for distribution;
- **Apply** to Court for guidance on contentious sales.

### Distribute

- **Receive** creditor proofs of debt;
- **Adjudicate** proofs (admit / reject / require further
  evidence);
- **Calculate** distributions per statutory priority;
- **Pay** dividends to creditors;
- **Report** to Court and creditors on progress.

### Litigate

- **Defend** the estate against unfounded claims;
- **Pursue** avoidance claims (preferences, undervalue,
  fraudulent transfers);
- **Recover** assets from third parties;
- **Settle** claims with Court approval;
- **Cooperate** with foreign insolvency officeholders.

### Discharge

- **Recommend** discharge to the Court when appropriate;
- **Object** to discharge if grounds exist;
- **Maintain** records post-discharge.

## Powers

The Viscount has statutory power to:

- **Sell** property;
- **Compromise** claims;
- **Continue or commence** litigation;
- **Engage** professional advisers (lawyers, accountants,
  valuers);
- **Engage** outside service providers (auctioneers,
  realtors);
- **Apply** to the Court for directions on contentious
  matters.

## Reporting and accountability

The Viscount:

- **Reports** to the Royal Court periodically;
- **Reports** to creditors;
- **Accounts** for receipts and disbursements;
- **Subject** to Court audit;
- **Subject** to creditor scrutiny.

## Funding

The Viscount's work is funded by:

- **Estate assets** (statutory fees deducted from
  realisations);
- **Public funds** to a limited extent for indigent estates;
- **Creditor advances** in some cases.
