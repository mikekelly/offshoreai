# BDL 1990 Article 4 — Grounds for Declaration of Désastre

Article 4 sets out the **substantive grounds** on which the
Royal Court will declare a debtor's estate en désastre.

## The grounds

The Court may declare désastre if **any** of the following is
established:

### Inability to pay debts

- The debtor is **unable to pay debts** as they fall due
  (cash-flow insolvency);
- The debtor's **liabilities exceed assets** (balance-sheet
  insolvency);
- The debtor has **failed** to comply with a statutory
  demand;
- The debtor has **admitted** insolvency in writing.

The cash-flow test is the **primary** practical test —
balance-sheet insolvency is harder to prove on a contested
basis.

### Voluntary acknowledgment

- The **debtor's own application** confirms insolvency;
- Court generally accepts on debtor's evidence.

### Specific failures

- **Unsatisfied judgment** — a creditor has a judgment
  against the debtor that remains unsatisfied;
- **Unable to find assets** sufficient to satisfy a judgment.

### Specific person types

- **Trustees** in their personal capacity — trust insolvency
  separately treated;
- **Trustees** in their trust capacity (trust insolvency) —
  rare in Jersey practice as trust property is
  insolvency-remote.

## The Court's discretion (Article 5)

Even if grounds exist, the Court has **discretion** to refuse:

- If the application is an **abuse of process**;
- If the debtor can **pay all debts** (despite some
  unsatisfied);
- If a **less drastic alternative** is available (e.g.,
  voluntary arrangement);
- If the application is **vexatious**;
- For other **substantial reasons**.

In practice, **voluntary** applications are generally
granted; **creditor** applications are granted unless the
debtor can show clear evidence of solvency or abuse.

## Standard of proof

The standard is **balance of probabilities** — civil standard.

For **creditor applications**:

- The applicant **bears the burden** of proving the ground;
- **Statutory demand** non-compliance is **prima facie**
  evidence of cash-flow insolvency;
- **Disputed debts** may justify adjournment for substantive
  determination first.

## Disputed debts

If the debtor disputes the underlying debt:

- The **désastre application** may be **adjourned** pending
  determination of the debt;
- The Court may **stay** désastre proceedings pending
  resolution of an underlying claim;
- **Frivolous** dispute is not a bar to désastre.

## Effect of declaration

Once declared, the consequences are set out in
[`bdl-article-6.md`](./bdl-article-6.md) — most importantly,
vesting of the debtor's property in the Viscount.
