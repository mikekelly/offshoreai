# BDL 1990 Article 17B — Transactions at Undervalue

Article 17B is an **avoidance power** for transactions made
**at undervalue** in the period before désastre.

## The provision

A transaction is at **undervalue** if:

- The debtor **makes a gift** or otherwise enters into a
  transaction with another person on terms providing for the
  other person to receive **no consideration**;
- The debtor receives consideration **significantly less than
  the value provided**; or
- The debtor enters into a transaction in **consideration of
  marriage / civil partnership** (specific category).

The transaction must have been made within the **lookback
period**.

## Lookback periods

- **Connected parties** — **5 years**;
- **Unconnected parties** — **6 months** (in some
  configurations) or **2 years** (in others);
- **Specific transaction types** may have specific lookback
  periods.

The longer lookback for connected parties reflects the
heightened ML / asset-protection risk.

## Insolvency requirement

For unconnected parties:

- The debtor must have been **insolvent** at the time of the
  transaction, **or** become insolvent **as a result**;
- The Viscount must prove this connection.

For connected parties:

- **Insolvency is presumed** (rebuttable);
- Heightened scrutiny.

## "Significantly less" — the valuation test

The undervalue test:

- Compares **consideration given** to **consideration
  received**;
- **Market value** at the time of the transaction;
- **Difference** must be **significant** (not de minimis);
- **Marriage consideration** does **not** count as
  consideration for this purpose (specific Jersey rule).

## Examples

### Clear undervalue transactions

- **Sale** of real property at well below market value to a
  family member;
- **Gift** of investments to children before désastre;
- **Settlement** of trust property at no real consideration;
- **Sale of business** at fire-sale price to associate.

### Not undervalue

- **Bona fide** sale at market value;
- **Ordinary** commercial transactions where price reflects
  market;
- **Settlement** of pre-existing claims for reasonable value.

## Defences

The recipient may defend on grounds:

- **Adequate consideration** was given;
- The debtor was **not insolvent** at the time;
- Transaction was a **bona fide commercial** arrangement;
- The transaction was made **in good faith** for **value**.

## Effect

If the Court declares a transaction at undervalue:

- The **transaction is set aside**;
- **Property** is restored to the estate;
- **Recipient** must restore the property or pay value;
- **Recipient's interest** reduced to creditor status (if any
  debt was acknowledged).

## Asset-protection structures

Article 17B is the **principal challenge route** for
asset-protection structures:

- **Pre-insolvency** trust settlements may be undervalue
  transactions;
- **Pre-insolvency** transfers to spouses / family may be
  undervalue;
- **Connected-party** transactions have **5-year** lookback —
  far longer than typical commercial planning windows;
- **Sham** transactions can be challenged separately under
  general principles.

Asset-protection planning must therefore consider:

- **Solvency at time** of planning (well before any
  insolvency event);
- **Market-value consideration** for all transfers;
- **Documentation** of commercial purpose;
- **5-year horizon** beyond likely insolvency events.
