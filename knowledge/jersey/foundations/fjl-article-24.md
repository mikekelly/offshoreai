# FJL 2009 Article 24 — Appointment, Removal, Retirement of Guardian

Article 24 governs **changes** to the guardian office.

## Appointment

A guardian is **appointed**:

- By the **charter or regulations** at establishment;
- By **resolution** of the council (where regulations so
  permit);
- By a **person specified** in the charter or regulations
  (e.g., founder, surviving spouse);
- By **court order** in default of mechanism (Article 45).

## Removal

A guardian is **removed**:

- By the **regulations' specified mechanism**;
- By **court order** (Article 45) for:
  - Breach of duty;
  - Inability to act;
  - Misconduct;
  - Other just cause;
- By **resignation** (with reasonable notice and successor in
  place).

## Retirement

A guardian may retire:

- By **notice** to the council;
- **Subject to** transition — successor in place;
- **Specific** procedures per regulations;
- **Court application** if no successor mechanism / available.

## Mandatory office

The foundation **must always** have a guardian (Article 22):

- **Transition** of guardian requires successor in place;
- **Cannot** leave foundation without a guardian;
- **Court** can appoint interim guardian if needed.

## Specific scenarios

### Founder-guardian succession

Many foundations have **founder as initial guardian**:

- **Succession** plan in regulations (e.g., spouse, then
  children, then professional);
- **Specific** trigger events (death, incapacity);
- **Clear** transition mechanism.

### Professional guardian

- **Professional** guardian (lawyer, family-office firm);
- **Term-based** appointment;
- **Replacement** through specific mechanism.

### Court intervention

If the guardian:

- Becomes **unable** to act;
- **Refuses** to act;
- **Acts** in breach of duty;

then the Court can order replacement under Article 45 / 46.

## Procedure

Changes to guardian:

- **Internally** documented;
- **Notified** to the council;
- **Specific** transitional matters;
- **Not** typically a public filing (unlike council changes
  which appear in annual return).
