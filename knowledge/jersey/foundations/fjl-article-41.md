# FJL 2009 Article 41 — Dissolution by Council

Article 41 enables the **voluntary dissolution** of a Jersey
foundation by council action.

## The provision

The council can dissolve the foundation:

- In accordance with the **charter / regulations** (typically
  requiring specific procedural steps);
- By **council resolution** specifying the dissolution date;
- **Subject to solvency** — the foundation must be able to
  pay debts as they fall due and have sufficient assets to
  meet liabilities;
- With **notification** to the Registrar;
- With **distribution** of remaining property per Article 43.

## Authorisations

Internal authorisation typically required:

### Council resolution

- **Material** resolution authorising dissolution;
- **Specific** majorities per regulations;
- **Reasons** documented (objects fulfilled, no longer
  needed, etc.).

### Guardian consent

- If regulations require, **guardian consent**;
- Common requirement for material foundation matters.

### Founder consent

- If founder reserved this power, **founder consent**;
- Specific documentation.

### Beneficiary consent

- Generally **not** required (beneficiaries do not have
  "ownership" of foundation per Article 29);
- **Unless** regulations specifically require;
- **Practical** consultation common.

## Solvency

The dissolution must be **solvent**:

- **Cash flow**: ability to pay debts as they fall due;
- **Balance sheet**: assets exceed liabilities;
- **Council declaration** of solvency;
- **Specific** professional advice helpful.

If insolvent, **dissolution by council not appropriate** —
Court-ordered dissolution (Article 42) is the route.

## Procedure

1. **Internal authorisation** (council resolution, guardian
   consent, founder consent as applicable);
2. **Notification** to Registrar;
3. **Identification** of remaining property;
4. **Payment** of debts and liabilities;
5. **Distribution** per Article 43;
6. **Final** filing with Registrar;
7. **Removal** from the register.

## Dissolution date

The dissolution becomes effective:

- On the **specified date** in the council resolution;
- **Or** on a date determined by the Registrar;
- **Specific** procedural timing.

## Post-dissolution

After dissolution:

- The foundation **ceases to exist**;
- **Records** retained per Article 32 obligation (10 years);
- **Specific** post-dissolution issues addressed by the
  qualified-member firm.

## When voluntary dissolution is used

Common scenarios:

- **Objects** fulfilled;
- **Foundation** no longer needed (family changes,
  alternative structure preferred);
- **Reorganisation** with successor structure;
- **Consolidation** with another structure;
- **Founder** withdraws support.
