# FJL 2009 Article 48 — Variation of Charter / Regulations

Article 48 enables the **Royal Court** to vary the charter or
regulations of a foundation.

## The provision

The Royal Court can **vary** a foundation's charter or
regulations:

- On application by a person with standing under Article 45;
- Where variation is **necessary or expedient** to:
  - **Deal with changed circumstances**;
  - **Enable** the foundation to carry on its objects;
  - **Remove a mistake** in drafting;
  - **Address** a deadlock;
  - **Modernise** outmoded provisions;
  - **Enable migration** out of Jersey;
  - **Address** failure of objects;
- **Subject to** procedural safeguards.

## When Court variation is used

The Court route is used when **internal mechanism fails**:

### Council deadlock

- **Council** cannot agree on amendments;
- **Internal voting** mechanism produces no result;
- **Court** intervenes.

### Founder unavailable

- **Founder** deceased without successor reserved powers;
- **No internal** authorisation mechanism for the change;
- **Court** can vary in default.

### Guardian conflicted

- **Guardian** has personal conflict with the change;
- **Internal** consent mechanism unworkable;
- **Court** can vary.

### Beneficiary disputes

- **Beneficiaries** disagree with proposed changes;
- **Court** balances interests.

## Cy-près for charitable foundations

For **charitable** foundations where **objects fail**:

- **Court** can vary objects to permit foundation to continue
  with similar charitable purposes;
- **Attorney General** convened;
- **Cy-près doctrine** applied;
- **Conservative** approach — closest practicable
  alternative purpose.

See [`../charities/cl-part-6-cy-pres.md`](../charities/cl-part-6-cy-pres.md).

## Procedure

1. **Application by representation** to Royal Court;
2. **Convenors** — all affected parties (council members,
   guardian, beneficiaries, founder or successor, Attorney
   General if charitable);
3. **Evidence** filed:
   - Need for variation;
   - Consequence of not varying;
   - Proposed wording;
   - Specific impact on stakeholders;
4. **Hearing** in Family Division or Samedi;
5. **Court order** specifying the new wording;
6. **Filing**:
   - Charter (public) — filed with Registrar;
   - Regulations (private) — internal record.

## Standing

Same as Article 45:

- Council member;
- Guardian;
- Founder;
- Beneficiary;
- Attorney General;
- Registrar;
- Person with sufficient interest.

## Scope of variation

The Court can vary:

- **Charter** (subject to Article 6 minimum content);
- **Regulations**;
- **Both**;
- **Specific** provisions only.

The Court **cannot**:

- Fundamentally **destroy** the foundation's nature;
- **Override** statutory minimum requirements;
- Act in a way **contrary to public policy**.

## Conservative approach

The Court generally:

- **Respects** the founder's intent as expressed in the
  charter / regulations;
- **Minimal** variation necessary to address the issue;
- **Specific** consultation with affected parties;
- **Reasoned** judgment.
