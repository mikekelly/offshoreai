# FJL 2009 Part 2 — Establishment (residual)

The most-cited Part 2 provisions (Articles 4-7) have their own
deep files. This cluster covers the remaining establishment
provisions (Articles 8-11).

## Article 8 — Name

A foundation's name:

- **Must end** with "Foundation" or "Fondation" (or a
  recognised abbreviation);
- **Must not** be the same as a name already on the
  Registrar's register;
- **Must not** be misleading as to the nature of the
  foundation;
- **Must not** include words / expressions prohibited by the
  Minister.

The Registrar can refuse a name; an appeal lies under Article
46.

## Article 9 — Change of name

A foundation can change its name by:

- A **resolution** of the council in accordance with the
  charter / regulations;
- **Filing** the resolution with the Registrar;
- Subject to **name controls** in Article 8;
- The new name takes effect on the date of the **Registrar's
  certificate** of name change.

## Article 10 — Address in Jersey

Every foundation must have a **registered office** in Jersey
— typically the office of the qualified member (TCB firm).

The registered office is:

- **Notified** in the charter or by separate filing;
- The address at which **service of process** is effective;
- The address at which **registers / records** are kept (unless
  otherwise notified).

## Article 11 — Initial endowment

A foundation **may**:

- Be established with an **initial endowment** of property;
  or
- Be established with **no initial property**, and receive
  endowment subsequently.

A foundation can therefore be a "**shelf foundation**" awaiting
endowment, or be fully endowed at establishment. There is no
minimum capital requirement.

Endowment after establishment:

- Property is **transferred** to the foundation;
- The transfer is **documented** in the foundation's records;
- The transfer may be made by the founder or by **third
  parties** (subject to AML / source-of-funds diligence).
