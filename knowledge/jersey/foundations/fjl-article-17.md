# FJL 2009 Article 17 — Functions and Duties of Council Members

Article 17 sets out the **statutory duties** of council
members.

## The provision

A council member must:

- Act in **good faith**;
- Act with the **care, diligence, and skill** that a
  **reasonably prudent person** would exercise in comparable
  circumstances;
- Act for the **objects** of the foundation;
- Act in accordance with the **charter and regulations**;
- **Not profit** from the foundation save as authorised by
  the charter / regulations or by the Court;
- **Not place** himself in a position of conflict between
  his duty to the foundation and a personal interest.

## Standard of care

The standard is a **prudent-person** standard, comparable
to:

- The trustee duty under **Article 21(1)** of the Trusts
  (Jersey) Law 1984;
- The director duty under **Article 74** of the Companies
  (Jersey) Law 1991.

For **professional council members** (TCB firms acting as
qualified member), the standard is heightened in the way that
*Re Manifold* and *Bartlett v Barclays Bank* heighten the
professional-trustee standard.

## Acting for the objects

Council members must act **for the foundation's objects**, not:

- For the **founder's personal interest** (unless the
  objects so direct);
- For their own personal interest;
- For the interests of **specific beneficiaries** to the
  exclusion of others (where the foundation has multiple
  beneficiary classes); or
- For **third parties** (e.g., the founder's family
  business, unless the objects so direct).

This is the **council member's equivalent of the trustee's
duty of impartiality**.

## Reliance and delegation

A council member may **rely on**:

- **Professional advice** properly obtained (legal, accounting,
  investment);
- **Delegated functions** properly delegated;
- **Information** provided by other council members or
  officers, where reasonable.

Delegation does **not absolve** the council member from
oversight responsibility. The qualified member in particular
must maintain **active engagement**, not merely sign documents.

## Conflicts

A council member with a **conflict** must:

- **Disclose** the conflict to the council;
- **Abstain** from voting on the conflicted matter (subject to
  the regulations);
- **Recuse** himself if a conflict is irreconcilable;
- **Resign** if conflicts are systemic and irreparable.

## Liability

Breach of Article 17 duties gives rise to:

- **Personal liability** to the foundation for loss;
- Potential **injunction** by the guardian or another
  authorised person;
- **Disqualification** in serious cases;
- **JFSC action** for the qualified-member firm.

Indemnity (Article 21) may protect against ordinary
negligence but **not against** wilful default or fraud.
