# FJL 2009 Article 36 — Matters Determined by Applicable Law

Article 36 specifies **what** the applicable law governs.

## The provision

The applicable law of a foundation determines:

- The **validity** of the foundation;
- The **construction** and effect of the charter and
  regulations;
- The **administration** of the foundation, including
  procedural rules;
- The **rights, powers, duties, and liabilities** of council
  members and the guardian;
- The **rights of beneficiaries** as against the foundation
  and its officers;
- The **dispositive provisions** of the foundation;
- The **dissolution and distribution** on dissolution;
- The **resignation, retirement, and replacement** of council
  members and guardian.

## What is **not** governed

The applicable law does **not** override:

- **Mandatory Jersey rules** (Article 37 firewall, public
  policy);
- **Foreign mandatory rules** that apply by reason of subject-
  matter (e.g., property situs rules, tax rules of the
  beneficiary's residence);
- **Sanctions law** of relevant jurisdictions;
- **Criminal law** of relevant jurisdictions.

## Migrating foundations

Where a foundation has **continued into Jersey** under Part 10,
the applicable law transitions:

- The **Jersey law** applies from the date of continuance;
- The **prior-jurisdiction law** ceases to apply except for
  pre-continuance matters;
- **Construction of the charter** uses Jersey-law principles
  thereafter;
- **Vested rights** of beneficiaries / council members under
  the prior law are typically preserved.

## Practical effects

For most Jersey-established foundations, the applicable law is
**Jersey law** by default — and Article 36 simply confirms the
comprehensive scope of Jersey-law governance.

For **dispute** purposes:

- A foreign court hearing a dispute about a Jersey foundation
  **should apply Jersey law** to the matters Article 36 covers;
- A **Jersey court** hearing a dispute about a foreign-law
  foundation **applies the foreign law** for those matters
  (subject to Article 37 firewall);
- **Choice-of-law clauses** in the charter / regulations are
  given effect, subject to public policy.
