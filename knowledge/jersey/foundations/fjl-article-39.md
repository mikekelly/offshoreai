# FJL 2009 Article 39 — Continuance out of Jersey

Article 39 enables a Jersey foundation to **continue
(migrate) out** of Jersey to another jurisdiction.

## The provision

A Jersey foundation can continue out where:

- The **destination-jurisdiction law** permits continuance
  in;
- **Internal authorisation** is obtained:
  - **Council resolution**;
  - **Guardian consent** if regulations so require;
  - **Founder consent** if reserved;
- **Application** to Jersey Registrar.

## Application content

- **Evidence** of destination's acceptance;
- **Statement of solvency**;
- **Declaration** that no Jersey creditors will be
  prejudiced;
- **Council resolution** authorising migration;
- **Specific** other particulars;
- **Prescribed fee**.

## Procedure

1. **Authorisation** internally (council, guardian, founder
   as applicable);
2. **Destination jurisdiction** accepts the foundation
   (typically requires application there);
3. **Application** to Jersey Registrar;
4. **Registrar review**;
5. **Certificate** of continuance out issued;
6. **Foundation** ceases to be a Jersey foundation on the
   date stated.

## Effect

On continuance out:

- The foundation **ceases** to be Jersey-governed;
- **Legal personality** continues in the destination
  jurisdiction;
- **Subject to** destination jurisdiction's law going
  forward;
- **No** discontinuity of property, rights, obligations.

## Creditor protection

The application must show **no Jersey creditors will be
prejudiced**:

- **Specific** declaration to that effect;
- **Notice** procedures may apply;
- **Specific** consideration of contingent claims.

## Internal authorisations

### Council resolution

- **Material** resolution authorising migration;
- **Specific** majorities per regulations;
- **Documented** with full reasons.

### Guardian consent

- If regulations require, **guardian consent** obtained;
- **Specific** documentation.

### Founder consent

- If founder reserved this power, **founder consent**
  obtained;
- **Specific** documentation.

### Beneficiary consent

- Generally **not** required;
- **Unless** regulations specifically require;
- **Beneficiary information rights** during migration
  process.

## Tax / regulatory considerations

### Jersey side

- **No** Jersey continuing-tax issue typically (zero-ten);
- **Pillar Two** considerations for large groups;
- **Substance** rules cease to apply.

### Destination side

- **Destination jurisdiction** rules apply;
- **Specific** acceptance procedures;
- **Specific** tax / regulatory consequences;
- **Specific** beneficial-ownership rules.

### Beneficiaries' jurisdictions

- **Foreign tax** characterisation of the migrated
  foundation;
- **Specific** consequences depending on home jurisdiction;
- **Specific** look-through rules.

## Common destinations

- **Other Crown Dependencies** (Guernsey, IoM);
- **Other offshore** centres (Cayman, BVI, Bermuda);
- **Civil-law** foundation home (Liechtenstein,
  Switzerland);
- **Specific** family-related destination.
