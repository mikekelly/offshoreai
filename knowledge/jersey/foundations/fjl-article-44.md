# FJL 2009 Article 44 — Striking Off

Article 44 enables the **Registrar** to strike a foundation
off the register for non-compliance.

## Grounds for striking off

The Registrar can strike a foundation off for:

- **Failure to file** annual returns;
- **Failure to pay** annual fees;
- **Failure to maintain** a qualified member;
- **Failure to maintain** a registered office;
- **Failure to comply** with specific orders / directions;
- **Specific** systemic non-compliance.

## Procedure

The striking-off process typically involves:

### 1. Notice

- **Written notice** to the foundation's registered office;
- **Specifying** the grounds for proposed strike-off;
- **Time** to remedy (typically 3-6 months).

### 2. Final notice

- If non-compliance persists, **final notice**;
- **Specific** remedy period.

### 3. Strike-off

- **If still** non-compliant, Registrar strikes the
  foundation off;
- **Foundation** ceases to exist as a legal person;
- **Property** vests in the Crown (subject to Article 43
  procedures);
- **Notice** published.

## Effect

A struck-off foundation:

- **Ceases to exist** as a legal person;
- **Property** vests in the Crown subject to specific
  procedures;
- **Records** retained per retention obligations;
- **Specific** consequences for liabilities (creditors may
  petition for restoration to enforce claims).

## Restoration

A struck-off foundation can be **restored**:

### Application

- **Application to the Royal Court** by interested person;
- **Reasons** for restoration;
- **Plan to remedy** the original non-compliance.

### Grounds

- **Foundation** still has property or affairs to be wound
  up;
- **Creditor** has unresolved claim;
- **Beneficiaries** with vested interests affected;
- **Specific** other interest.

### Effect

- **Foundation restored** to register;
- **Treated** as if never struck off in most respects;
- **Property** re-vested in the foundation;
- **Specific** conditions on restoration.

## Practical considerations

For a foundation at risk of strike-off:

- **Address** non-compliance promptly;
- **File** outstanding returns;
- **Pay** outstanding fees;
- **Appoint** new qualified member if needed;
- **Specific** Court application if Registrar action
  inappropriate.

## Difference from voluntary dissolution

| Aspect | Voluntary (Article 41) | Strike-off (Article 44) |
|---|---|---|
| Initiated by | Council | Registrar |
| Reason | Foundation no longer needed | Non-compliance |
| Asset distribution | Per regulations (Article 43) | Crown bona vacantia subject to restoration |
| Council control | Yes | No |
| Restoration | Cannot reverse | Possible by Court order |

## Difference from Court dissolution

| Aspect | Court (Article 42) | Strike-off (Article 44) |
|---|---|---|
| Initiated by | Various applicants | Registrar |
| Court | Royal Court order | Administrative |
| Procedure | Substantive | Administrative |
| Discretion | Court | Registrar with safeguards |
