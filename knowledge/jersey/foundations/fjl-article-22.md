# FJL 2009 Article 22 — Guardian

Article 22 establishes the **guardian** office — a mandatory
feature of every Jersey foundation.

## The provision

Every foundation **must have a guardian**. The guardian:

- Is **not a council member**;
- Has the **function** prescribed by Article 23 (broadly:
  ensuring the council carries out its functions);
- May be an **individual or a body corporate**;
- May be the **founder** (founder-as-guardian is permitted
  and common);
- May be a **professional adviser** (lawyer, accountant) or
  family member;
- May be the **qualified-member firm** — but this raises
  conflict concerns and is generally avoided in well-drafted
  structures.

## Why mandatory

The guardian requirement reflects the Jersey legislator's
view that a foundation, lacking beneficiary equitable
ownership, needs an **independent supervisor** to ensure the
council acts properly. Without a guardian, no one (other than
the Attorney General in public-interest cases) would have
standing to challenge council conduct.

## Founder-as-guardian

A common pattern: the **founder is the initial guardian** and
holds reserved powers as guardian (consent rights to specified
matters, removal of council members, etc.). This gives the
founder ongoing influence over the foundation **without
being on the council**.

On the founder's death or incapacity, a **successor guardian**
takes office per the regulations (e.g., the founder's spouse,
then a child, then a professional).

## Professional guardian

For more institutional structures, a **professional guardian**
may be appointed:

- A **lawyer** (e.g., a partner in a Jersey law firm);
- An **accountant**;
- A **trust company** other than the qualified-member firm;
- A **family-office** entity.

The professional guardian receives **fees** out of foundation
assets.

## Conflicted positions

The guardian must **avoid conflicts**:

- The **qualified-member firm as guardian** is problematic —
  it supervises itself;
- The **founder as both founder and guardian** is permitted
  but should be checked against tax / regulatory concerns
  (depending on intended treatment).
