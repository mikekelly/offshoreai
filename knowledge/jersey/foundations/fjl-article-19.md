# FJL 2009 Article 19 — Decisions of Council

Article 19 governs **how the council takes decisions**.

## Meeting decisions

Council decisions are made:

- By **resolution at a meeting**;
- **Quorum** per the regulations (default: majority of
  council members);
- **Voting** typically by simple majority unless regulations
  require special majority;
- **Chair's** casting vote per regulations;
- **Recorded** in minutes.

## Written resolutions

Decisions can be made **in writing**:

- **Signed by** all (or specified proportion of) council
  members;
- **Avoids** physical meeting;
- **Same effect** as meeting resolution;
- **Specific** regulations may require physical meeting for
  certain matters.

## Quorum

- **Default**: majority of council members;
- **Regulations** can vary;
- **Qualified member** typically required for quorum (good
  practice);
- **Specific** regulations on specific matter quorums.

## Voting

- **Simple majority** by default;
- **Casting vote** for chair;
- **Specific** matters may require higher majority
  (constitutional changes, major distributions, etc.).

## Records

Council decisions are **recorded**:

- **Minutes** of meetings;
- **Written resolutions** retained;
- **Available** to other council members and the guardian;
- **Retention** per Article 20;
- **Subject to** Court discovery in proceedings.

## Decisions binding the foundation

A properly made council decision **binds the foundation**:

- **Third parties** can rely on resolutions;
- **Specific** authority granted to council members for
  execution;
- **BFPV** protection for counterparties.

## Deadlock

Where the council is **deadlocked**:

- **Regulations** may provide tie-breaking mechanism;
- **Guardian** may have a casting role in some
  configurations;
- **Court application** under Article 46 in serious cases;
- **Specific** dispute-resolution procedures.

## Specific decision types

### Distributions

- **Council resolution** required;
- **Specific** documentation;
- **Beneficiary identification** for the distribution;
- **AML / CFT** on the recipient.

### Asset transactions

- **Material** transactions typically require council
  resolution;
- **Specific** thresholds in regulations;
- **Guardian consent** if regulations require.

### Amendments

- Charter / regulations amendments per their internal
  mechanism;
- **Specific** Court approval required for material
  amendments in some cases.
