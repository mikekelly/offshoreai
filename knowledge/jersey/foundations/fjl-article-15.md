# FJL 2009 Article 15 — Council

Article 15 establishes the **council** as the governing organ
of a Jersey foundation.

## The provision

Every foundation:

- **Must have a council**;
- The council has **one or more** members;
- The council **manages the foundation** in accordance with
  the charter and regulations;
- At least one member must be a **qualified member** (Article
  16);
- Council members may be **individuals or bodies corporate**.

## Composition

- **One-member councils** are permitted (the qualified member
  alone);
- **Multi-member councils** are common, blending professional
  members (the qualified-member firm) with **family** members
  or trusted advisers;
- **Corporate council members** are permitted — a body
  corporate can serve, in which case it acts through its
  directors / officers.

## Functions

The council:

- **Manages** the foundation;
- **Holds and disposes of** foundation property;
- **Makes distributions** (where the foundation has
  beneficiaries) or **applies funds to purposes**;
- **Engages and dismisses** agents and advisers;
- **Files** with the Registrar;
- **Maintains accounts and records**;
- **Reports** to the guardian as required.

## Relationship with guardian

The council and guardian have **distinct roles**:

- **Council** — manages and decides;
- **Guardian** — supervises and (often) consents to specified
  matters.

The guardian is **not** a council member and is **not bound**
by council decisions; equally, the council is not bound by
guardian directions (subject to the regulations).
