# FJL 2009 Part 13 — Miscellaneous

Part 13 covers the **administrative architecture** — the
Registrar, the register, fees, offences, regulation-making
power, and citation.

## Article 49 — Registrar of Foundations

The **Registrar of Foundations** is a function discharged by:

- The **JFSC** under delegation by the Minister;
- Acting through **designated officers** in the JFSC
  Registry;
- **Reporting** to the Minister and the States Assembly on
  registry matters.

The Registrar:

- **Receives** applications for establishment, continuance,
  dissolution;
- **Maintains** the register of foundations;
- **Issues** certificates (establishment, name change,
  continuance);
- **Strikes off** non-compliant foundations;
- **Investigates** compliance matters within its remit;
- **Sets fees** in conjunction with the Minister.

## Article 50 — Register of Foundations

The Registrar maintains a **register** containing:

- **Foundation name** (current and former);
- **Date of establishment**;
- **Status** (active, dissolved, struck off);
- **Registered office**;
- **Council members** (per annual return);
- **Qualified member** identification;
- **Charter** (current version) and amendments;
- **Filings** (annual returns, name changes, continuances).

## Article 51 — Inspection of register

The register is:

- **Public** in respect of the charter, name, registered
  office, council members, and qualified member;
- **Not public** in respect of the regulations;
- **Available** for inspection on the JFSC online registry;
- **Subject to** fees for copies / extracts.

This is a key **transparency / privacy compromise** in the
Jersey foundation regime — the charter is public (so the
foundation's existence and constitutional outline are
accessible), but the substantive content (regulations,
beneficiaries, dispositive rules) is private.

## Article 52 — Fees and forms

The Minister prescribes:

- **Fees** for establishment, annual return, amendments,
  continuance, dissolution, copies / extracts;
- **Forms** in which applications are made;
- **Late penalties**.

Practitioners check the current JFSC fee schedule for live
amounts.

## Article 53 — Offences

The Law creates offences for:

- **False statement** in any document filed with the
  Registrar;
- **Failure** by a qualified-member firm to comply with
  obligations;
- **Failure** by council members to maintain records;
- **Acting** as qualified member without TCB registration;
- **Breach** of specific Law provisions (e.g., Article 32
  records, Article 34 annual return).

Penalties include **fines** (in some cases substantial),
**imprisonment** (in serious cases), and **JFSC enforcement
sanctions** against the qualified member.

## Article 54 — Regulations

The Minister may make **regulations** under the Law:

- Prescribing **forms** and **fees**;
- Setting **procedural** requirements;
- Modifying or supplementing **interpretation**;
- Implementing **international standards** (FATF, OECD).

Regulations are subject to **negative procedure** in the
States Assembly typically — they come into force unless
disapproved.

## Article 55 — Citation

The Law is cited as the **"Foundations (Jersey) Law 2009"**.

It came into force on **17 July 2009**.
