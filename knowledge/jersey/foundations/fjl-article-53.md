# FJL 2009 Article 53 — Offences

Article 53 creates **criminal offences** related to
foundation regulation.

## Principal offences

### False statements

A person commits an offence by:

- **Making false statements** in:
  - Applications for establishment;
  - Annual returns;
  - Any documents filed with the Registrar;
- **Knowing** or being **reckless** as to falsity.

Penalty: **substantial fines** and **imprisonment** up to
specified term.

### Qualified-member breach

A qualified member or its principals commit offences by:

- **Failure** to comply with FJL obligations;
- **Acting** without proper TCB registration;
- **Specific** AML / CFT breaches.

### Council member breaches

Council members commit offences by:

- **Failure** to maintain records (Article 32 / 33);
- **Failure** to file annual return (Article 34);
- **Acting** as council member without proper status;
- **Specific** breaches of fiduciary duty in serious cases.

### Specific other breaches

- **Misuse** of "Foundation" name without authorisation;
- **Obstruction** of investigations;
- **Breach** of Court orders.

## Mental element

Most offences require:

- **Knowledge** or **recklessness** for serious offences;
- **Negligence** sufficient for some procedural offences;
- **Strict liability** in narrow cases.

## Penalties

- **Fines**: typically up to **£10,000 or higher** for
  serious offences;
- **Imprisonment**: up to **2-5 years** for serious offences;
- **Both** in serious cases;
- **Corporate liability** for entity offences;
- **Personal liability** for individual offenders.

## Prosecution

Prosecutions are brought by:

- The **Attorney General**;
- After **Police investigation** in serious cases;
- **JFSC referral** for regulated-entity matters;
- **Specific** offshore-related cooperation if cross-border.

## Defences

Specific defences include:

- **Due diligence** — reasonable steps taken to comply;
- **No knowledge** of the falsity / breach;
- **Acting on advice** in good faith;
- **Specific** statutory defences.

## Relationship with regulatory action

Criminal prosecution typically reserved for **serious**
matters. **Routine** non-compliance addressed through:

- **JFSC civil action** against qualified member;
- **Registrar action** (notice, fine, strike-off);
- **Court directions** under Article 45;
- **Specific** other regulatory mechanisms.

## Recent enforcement themes

- **Beneficial-ownership** false statements;
- **Specific** AML / CFT failures by qualified members;
- **Council member** misuse of foundation assets;
- **Specific** fraud / corruption cases.
