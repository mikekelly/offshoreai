# FJL 2009 Article 27 — Other Persons

Article 27 permits the charter / regulations to give **powers,
rights, or functions** to **persons other than** the founder,
council, or guardian.

## The provision

The charter / regulations may grant powers / rights /
functions to:

- **Specifically named** persons;
- **Office-holders** (e.g., family council, protector-style
  roles);
- **Trusted advisers**;
- **Future** appointees per specified mechanism.

## Typical roles created under Article 27

### Family council

- **Body** of family members with consultative or consent
  roles;
- **Specific** powers per regulations;
- **Family-governance** dimension;
- **Common** in multi-generational structures.

### Protector / monitor

- **Single individual** with specific oversight or consent
  rights;
- **Distinct from** guardian role;
- **Specific** narrower powers;
- **Subject to** Court interpretation.

### Investment adviser / committee

- **Investment direction** powers;
- **Specific** consultation rights;
- **Distinction** between advice and binding direction.

### Distribution committee

- **Specific** distribution-related powers;
- **Member-based** or family-based;
- **Specific** mechanisms.

### Letters-of-wishes recipient

- **Persuasive** influence;
- **Not binding** but considered by council;
- **Specific** family / advisory roles.

## Status of Article 27 powerholders

A person granted Article 27 power:

- Is **not** automatically a council member;
- Is **not** the guardian;
- Is **not** a beneficiary;
- **Has** purely contractual / constitutional rights per the
  regulations.

To be a **fiduciary**, the role must be designed that way.

## Duties

The duties of an Article 27 powerholder depend on:

- **Express** terms in the charter / regulations;
- **Nature** of the power (fiduciary vs personal);
- **Specific** Jersey case law (developing);
- **Comparison** with protector jurisprudence in trusts.

### Fiduciary characterisation

- **Discretion** affecting beneficiaries;
- **Powers** to be exercised in interests of objects /
  beneficiaries;
- **Drafting** doesn't carve out fiduciary duty;
- **Likely fiduciary**.

### Personal characterisation

- **Express** statement of non-fiduciary nature;
- **Personal benefit** rights;
- **No** duty to others;
- **Personal** (rare for substantive roles).

## Drafting points

For Article 27 roles:

- **Be explicit** about fiduciary vs personal nature;
- **Specify** scope and standards;
- **Provide** for succession;
- **Address** removal mechanism;
- **Specific** indemnity from foundation assets;
- **Coordinate** with guardian role to avoid conflict /
  overlap.
