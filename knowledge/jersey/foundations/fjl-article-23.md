# FJL 2009 Article 23 — Function of Guardian

Article 23 defines what the **guardian does**.

## The provision

The guardian's function is **to take such steps as are
reasonable in all the circumstances to ensure that the council
carries out its functions** — i.e., that the council manages
the foundation in accordance with the charter, regulations, and
the Law.

The guardian:

- Has **supervisory** (not managerial) authority;
- Acts in **good faith**;
- Has standing to **apply to the Royal Court** under Article
  46 if the council fails or is in breach;
- Has **information rights** (Article 25);
- May give or withhold **consent** to specified matters under
  the regulations.

## Reasonable steps

The standard is a **reasonableness** standard — the guardian
is not expected to micromanage the council. Typical guardian
activity:

- **Reviewing** council minutes and resolutions;
- **Attending** council meetings (often by invitation);
- **Receiving** annual reports and accounts;
- **Consenting** to specified matters per the regulations;
- **Raising concerns** with the council;
- **Applying to Court** in extremis.

## Consent rights

Regulations commonly require **guardian consent** for:

- **Amendment** of the charter or regulations;
- **Distributions** above a threshold;
- **Appointment / removal** of council members;
- **Material transactions** outside the ordinary course;
- **Dissolution** of the foundation;
- **Migration** out of Jersey.

The guardian's consent right is a **practical control
mechanism** — it allows the guardian (often the founder or a
family adviser) to backstop the council.

## Standing

The guardian has **standing** to:

- Apply to the Court under Article 46;
- Bring an Article 47 mistake / hastiness application;
- Seek an injunction to restrain ultra-vires acts (Article 13);
- Bring proceedings for breach of council duty (Article 17).

The guardian does **not** have standing to direct council
decisions — that would convert supervision into management.

## Liability

The guardian:

- Is **not personally liable** for council decisions (the
  guardian does not make them);
- Owes **good-faith duties** of supervision — actionable if
  breached;
- Is **entitled to indemnity** out of foundation assets for
  proper expenses;
- May be **personally liable** for fraud / wilful default.
