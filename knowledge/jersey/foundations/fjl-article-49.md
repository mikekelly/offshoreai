# FJL 2009 Article 49 — Registrar of Foundations

Article 49 establishes the **Registrar of Foundations**.

## The office

The Registrar of Foundations is:

- A **function** discharged within the **JFSC** under
  delegation by the Minister;
- Acting through **designated officers** in the JFSC
  Registry;
- **Reporting** to the Minister and States Assembly on
  registry matters.

## Functions

The Registrar:

- **Receives applications** for establishment, continuance,
  dissolution, amendment;
- **Maintains** the register of foundations;
- **Issues certificates** (establishment, name change,
  continuance);
- **Strikes off** non-compliant foundations (Article 44);
- **Investigates** compliance matters within its remit;
- **Sets fees** in conjunction with the Minister.

## Powers

The Registrar can:

- **Refuse** applications not complying with the Law;
- **Require** further information from applicants;
- **Issue** directions for compliance;
- **Strike off** under Article 44;
- **Refer** matters to JFSC enforcement or Attorney General.

## Public functions

The Registrar:

- **Maintains** the public register (Article 50);
- **Provides** copies of public documents;
- **Charges** prescribed fees for searches and copies;
- **Online** access via JFSC system.

## Engagement with foundations

For practitioners:

- **Pre-application** discussions on complex matters;
- **Specific** filings throughout foundation lifecycle;
- **Regulatory** queries and responses;
- **Specific** procedural requirements per Registrar.

## Relationship with JFSC

The Registrar is **within the JFSC**:

- **Single** integrated regulator and registry;
- **Specific** divisions for different functions;
- **JFSC AML / CFT** oversight of qualified-member firms;
- **Coordinated** approach to foundation regulation.
