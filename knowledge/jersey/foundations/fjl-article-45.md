# FJL 2009 Article 45 — Jurisdiction of Royal Court

Article 45 establishes the **Royal Court of Jersey's
jurisdiction** over Jersey foundations.

## The provision

The Royal Court has jurisdiction in respect of any matter
concerning:

- A **Jersey foundation**;
- A **foreign foundation** to the extent permitted by Jersey
  conflict-of-laws principles;
- The **administration**, **affairs**, or **constitution** of
  a foundation;
- The **rights** of any council member, guardian, founder,
  beneficiary, or other person;
- The **dissolution** or **migration** of a foundation;
- Any other matter for which the Law gives jurisdiction.

The Court's jurisdiction is **broad and supervisory**,
analogous to its trust jurisdiction.

## Standing to apply

Persons with standing to apply to the Royal Court include:

- A **council member** (current or former);
- The **guardian** (current or former);
- The **founder** (where reserved powers or interest persist);
- A **beneficiary** (designated or contingent);
- The **Attorney General** (public-interest matters);
- The **Registrar** (for compliance / strike-off matters);
- The **JFSC** (regulatory matters affecting the qualified
  member);
- A **creditor** of the foundation;
- Any other person with **sufficient interest**.

## Types of relief

The Court can grant:

- **Declarations** as to construction of the charter /
  regulations;
- **Directions** to the council under Article 46;
- **Removal** of council members or guardian;
- **Appointment** of new council members or guardian;
- **Injunctions** against ultra-vires acts;
- **Setting aside** of council decisions for mistake / breach
  of duty (Article 47);
- **Variation** of charter / regulations (Article 48);
- **Order** for accounts;
- **Compensation** for breach of duty;
- **Dissolution** (Article 42);
- Any **ancillary relief** appropriate.

## Procedure

Applications to the Royal Court are by **representation** —
the procedural vehicle for non-contentious or quasi-
contentious matters. The proceedings:

- Are heard in the **Family Division** or **Samedi** as
  appropriate;
- Often involve **Jurats** (lay judges sitting with the
  Bailiff);
- May be heard **in private** (closed court) for confidentiality;
- Can be **expedited** in appropriate cases.

## Royal Court's approach

The Court's approach to foundations follows broadly its
trust-law approach:

- **Respect** for the founder's intent as expressed in the
  charter / regulations;
- **Support** for the council in proper exercise of
  discretion;
- **Intervention** only where necessary;
- **Confidentiality** of family matters preserved where
  possible.
