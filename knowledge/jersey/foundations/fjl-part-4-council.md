# FJL 2009 Part 4 — Council (residual)

Articles 15-17 have their own deep files. This cluster covers
the remaining Part 4 provisions (Articles 18-21).

## Article 18 — Appointment, removal, retirement

A council member is **appointed**:

- By the **regulations** (initial council);
- By **resolution** of the council (subsequent appointments,
  unless the regulations otherwise provide);
- By **founder's reserved power** if so reserved;
- By **court order** in default of mechanism (Article 45).

A council member is **removed** by:

- **Resolution** of the council (with required majority);
- **Founder's reserved power** if so reserved;
- **Guardian** if the regulations so provide;
- **Court order** under Article 45.

A council member may **retire**:

- By **notice** to the council;
- Subject to **transition** so the foundation continues to
  have a qualified member.

## Article 19 — Decisions of council

Council decisions:

- Are made by **resolution** at a meeting or in writing;
- Require the **quorum** stated in the regulations
  (default: majority);
- May be made by a **simple majority** unless the regulations
  require a special majority;
- Are **recorded** in council minutes;
- **Bind** the foundation when properly made.

A **deadlock** is resolved per the regulations or, in default,
by application to the Royal Court under Article 45.

## Article 20 — Records and accounts

The council must keep:

- A **register of council members** at the registered office;
- **Accounting records** sufficient to show the foundation's
  financial position;
- A **register of beneficiaries** (where applicable);
- Other **records** prescribed by the regulations or by the
  Minister.

Records must be retained for the period prescribed (typically
**10 years**) and made available for **inspection** by:

- The **guardian**;
- The **Registrar** on request;
- The **Royal Court** on order;
- **Beneficiaries** to the extent permitted by Article 30 and
  the charter / regulations.

## Article 21 — Indemnity

A council member is **entitled to be indemnified** out of
foundation assets against:

- **Liabilities** properly incurred in acting as council member;
- **Costs** properly incurred in defending litigation;
- **Other expenses** properly incurred in the performance of
  duties.

The indemnity does **not** extend to:

- **Fraud**;
- **Wilful default**;
- **Wilful breach** of duty under Article 17;
- **Liabilities** outside the scope of council duties.

The charter / regulations can **extend or restrict** the
default indemnity, within the limits of public policy. The
qualified-member firm typically also has **contractual
indemnities** in the engagement letter.
