# FJL 2009 Part 10 — Migration and Continuance

Part 10 governs **migration** of foundations into and out of
Jersey — a process functionally analogous to the
**continuance** regime for Jersey companies under Part 18C of
the Companies Law 1991.

## Article 38 — Continuance into Jersey

A foreign foundation can **continue** into Jersey:

- The **prior-jurisdiction law** must permit migration out;
- The foreign foundation must satisfy **Jersey-law
  formation requirements** (charter, qualified member,
  guardian, objects);
- **Application** to the Jersey Registrar with:
  - Evidence of authority to migrate from the prior
    jurisdiction;
  - Proposed charter and regulations in Jersey-law form;
  - Council and guardian particulars;
  - Statement that no insolvency / liquidation is pending;
  - Prescribed fee.
- The **Registrar issues a certificate** of continuance,
  which is **conclusive evidence** of continuance.

On continuance:

- The foundation **becomes** a Jersey foundation;
- Its **legal personality** continues uninterrupted;
- Its **property, rights, and obligations** continue;
- **Jersey law** becomes the applicable law (subject to
  Article 35);
- The foundation is **subject to FJL 2009** going forward.

## Article 39 — Continuance out of Jersey

A Jersey foundation can **continue** out of Jersey:

- The **destination-jurisdiction law** must permit
  continuance in;
- **Council resolution** authorising migration;
- **Guardian consent** if regulations so require;
- **Founder consent** if reserved;
- **Application** to the Jersey Registrar with:
  - Evidence of destination's acceptance;
  - Statement of solvency;
  - Declaration that no Jersey creditors will be
    prejudiced;
  - Prescribed fee.
- The **Registrar issues a certificate** of continuance out,
  upon which the foundation **ceases to be a Jersey
  foundation**.

## Article 40 — Effect of migration

On continuance (in either direction):

- **Property, rights, obligations** of the foundation are
  preserved without break;
- **Litigation** continues in the new jurisdiction unless
  withdrawn / re-commenced;
- **Contracts** remain in force, governed by their existing
  terms;
- **Beneficiaries** retain their rights (subject to the new
  jurisdiction's law);
- **Tax** consequences depend on the relevant jurisdiction —
  a Jersey-tax-neutral migration may be **taxable** in the
  destination country.

## Reasons to migrate

Common migration drivers:

- **Family relocation** — moving the structure closer to where
  the family is based;
- **Tax treaty access** — moving to a jurisdiction with
  preferable treaty network;
- **Regulatory regime** — preference for a different
  oversight regime;
- **Cost / service quality** — moving to a jurisdiction with
  better-fit service providers;
- **Reputational** considerations.

## Common destinations

Jersey foundations have historically migrated to / from:

- **Guernsey**, **Isle of Man** — sister Crown Dependencies;
- **Liechtenstein**, **Panama** — civil-law foundation
  homes;
- **Cayman Islands**, **Bahamas** — other offshore
  jurisdictions;
- **Malta**, **Cyprus** — EU offshore jurisdictions.
