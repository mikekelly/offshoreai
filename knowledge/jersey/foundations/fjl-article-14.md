# FJL 2009 Article 14 — Powers

Article 14 specifies the **powers** of a Jersey foundation.

## The provision

A foundation has all the **powers of a natural person**:

- To **acquire, hold, dispose of** property (movable and
  immovable);
- To **contract**;
- To **borrow** money;
- To **lend** money;
- To **issue debt securities** (but **not equity** — no
  shareholders);
- To **act as trustee** of a trust;
- To **act as a council member** of another foundation
  (subject to qualified-member rules for that other
  foundation);
- To **engage in any lawful activity** consistent with its
  objects;
- To **sue and be sued** in its own name.

## No equity issuance

A foundation **cannot issue equity** because:

- The **foundation has no shareholders**;
- It is a **body corporate** with objects, not owners;
- **Beneficiaries** are not owners (no equitable interest
  under Article 29).

The foundation **can** issue:

- **Debt securities** (bonds, notes);
- **Specific** participation interests in some contexts;
- **Other** legally recognised instruments that don't
  constitute equity.

## Common uses

The wide powers enable foundations to be used as:

### Holding vehicles

- **Holding shares** in operating companies;
- **Real estate** ownership;
- **Investment portfolios**;
- **Specific** asset categories.

### PTC parent

- **Holding shares** in a Private Trust Company (PTC);
- **Common** family-office structure;
- See [`uses.md`](./uses.md).

### Purpose-trust analogue

- **Orphan SPV** in finance transactions;
- **Holding** specific assets for charitable / purpose
  reasons;
- **Distinct** from Jersey purpose trust under TJL Article
  12.

### Trustee

- A foundation **acting as trustee** of a trust;
- **Specific** uses where corporate trustee preferred;
- **Subject to** trust company business regulation if by
  way of business.

### Council member of another foundation

- A foundation **acting as council member** of another
  foundation;
- **Subject to** qualified-member rules for the other
  foundation;
- **Specific** cross-foundation structures.

## Restrictions

Powers are **subject to**:

- **Objects** of the foundation (Article 13 ultra vires);
- **Charter / regulations** internal restrictions;
- **Council duties** (Article 17);
- **External law** (regulation, AML, etc.).
