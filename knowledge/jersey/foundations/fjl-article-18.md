# FJL 2009 Article 18 — Appointment, Removal, Retirement of Council Members

Article 18 governs **changes to council membership** —
appointment, removal, and retirement of council members.

## Appointment

A council member is **appointed**:

- By the **regulations** (the initial council);
- By **resolution** of the council (subsequent appointments,
  unless the regulations otherwise provide);
- By **founder's reserved power** if so reserved in the
  charter / regulations;
- By **guardian** if the regulations so provide;
- By **court order** in default of mechanism (Article 45).

## Removal

A council member is **removed** by:

- **Resolution** of the council (with required majority per
  regulations);
- **Founder's reserved power** if so reserved;
- **Guardian** if the regulations so provide;
- **Court order** under Article 45;
- **Automatic** on death, incapacity, or specific events
  (insolvency, criminal conviction in some configurations).

## Retirement

A council member may **retire**:

- By **notice** to the council;
- **Subject to** transition (the foundation must continue to
  have a qualified member);
- **Specific** notice periods per regulations;
- **Specific** handover obligations.

## Mandatory qualified member

Through all these changes:

- The foundation **must at all times** have a **qualified
  member** (Article 16);
- **Transition** of qualified member requires successor in
  place;
- **Article 16** is the load-bearing constraint.

## Procedure

For each change:

- **Documentation** (resolution, notice, etc.);
- **Filing** with Registrar (annual return or specific
  notification);
- **Internal** records updated;
- **Banks / counterparties** notified for material changes;
- **Specific** specimen-signature updates.

## Specific scenarios

### Death of founder-council member

- Council continues with remaining members;
- **Specific** regulations may provide for successor;
- **Court application** if no mechanism for successor.

### Loss of qualified member status

If the qualified-member firm:

- **Resigns** the foundation engagement;
- **Loses** JFSC TCB authorisation;
- **Has** principal-person changes affecting fit-and-proper;
- The foundation must **appoint a successor** qualified
  member without delay.

### Court-ordered removal

The Court can remove a council member for:

- **Breach** of duty (Article 17);
- **Inability** to act (incapacity);
- **Misconduct**;
- **Specific** other grounds under Article 45.
