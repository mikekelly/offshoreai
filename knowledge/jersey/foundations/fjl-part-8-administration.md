# FJL 2009 Part 8 — Administration

Part 8 governs the **administrative obligations** of a Jersey
foundation: records, accounts, and the annual return.

## Article 32 — Accounting records

The council must maintain **accounting records** which:

- Are **sufficient** to show and explain the foundation's
  transactions;
- **Disclose** the foundation's financial position with
  reasonable accuracy at any time;
- Enable the preparation of any required accounts;
- Are **kept** at the registered office or another place
  notified to the Registrar;
- Are **preserved** for at least **10 years**.

Accounting records include:

- **Cash books** and **bank records**;
- **Receipts** and **invoices** for transactions;
- **Asset registers**;
- **Distribution records**;
- Records of **transactions with founder, guardian,
  beneficiaries, and connected parties**.

Most foundations do **not** prepare audited financial
statements unless they are large or required by the
regulations / charter. The qualified member typically prepares
**management accounts** annually.

## Article 33 — Registered office records

At the **registered office**, the council must keep:

- **Charter** and **regulations** (current versions);
- **Register of council members**;
- **Register of beneficiaries** (where applicable);
- **Council minutes** and resolutions;
- **Guardian appointments** and removals;
- **Records of distributions**.

These records must be **available** for inspection by:

- The **guardian** (Article 25);
- The **Registrar** on demand;
- The **Royal Court** by order;
- **Beneficiaries** to the extent permitted (Article 30).

## Article 34 — Annual return

Every foundation must file an **annual return** with the
Registrar:

- By the **prescribed date** (typically **end of January** for
  the preceding calendar year);
- Containing the **prescribed information** (council members,
  qualified member, registered office, etc.);
- Accompanied by the **prescribed fee**;
- **Signed** by a council member.

The annual return is **public** — it is accessible through the
Registrar's online register. This is one of the few public-
record elements of a Jersey foundation (the others being the
charter, name changes, and dissolution events).

## Late filing

Late filing of the annual return:

- Triggers **late-filing penalties** (scaled by lateness);
- Can lead to **strike-off** by the Registrar (Article 44);
- Affects the **qualified member's good standing** with the
  JFSC.
