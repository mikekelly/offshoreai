# FJL 2009 Article 38 — Continuance into Jersey

Article 38 enables a **foreign foundation** to **continue
(migrate)** into Jersey.

## The provision

A foreign foundation can continue into Jersey, becoming a
Jersey foundation, where:

- The **prior-jurisdiction law** permits migration out;
- The foreign foundation satisfies **Jersey-law formation
  requirements** (charter, qualified member, guardian,
  objects);
- An **application** is made to the Jersey Registrar.

## Application content

The application includes:

- **Evidence** of authority to migrate from prior
  jurisdiction;
- **Proposed charter and regulations** in Jersey-law form;
- **Council and guardian** particulars;
- **Statement** that no insolvency / liquidation is pending;
- **Specific** other particulars;
- **Prescribed fee**.

## Procedure

1. **Pre-application** engagement with Registrar and Jersey
   counsel;
2. **Authorisation** from prior jurisdiction obtained;
3. **Application** to Jersey Registrar with all required
   documentation;
4. **Registrar review** of application;
5. **Certificate** of continuance issued (conclusive
   evidence);
6. **Foundation** becomes Jersey foundation on the date
   stated.

## Effect

On continuance:

- The foundation **becomes** a Jersey foundation;
- **Legal personality** continues uninterrupted;
- **Property, rights, obligations** continue;
- **Jersey law** becomes the applicable law (subject to
  Article 35);
- **Subject to** FJL going forward;
- **No** discontinuity of contractual obligations.

## Authorising jurisdiction

The foreign jurisdiction must permit migration out:

- **Specific** foreign-jurisdiction approval procedures;
- **Documentation** that prior jurisdiction has authorised
  exit;
- **Coordination** with foreign counsel;
- **Specific** timing considerations.

## Practical considerations

### Choice of jurisdictions

Common source jurisdictions for migration to Jersey:

- **Other Crown Dependencies** (Guernsey, IoM);
- **BVI**, **Cayman**, **Bermuda**;
- **Liechtenstein**, **Panama** (civil-law foundation
  homes);
- **Malta**, **Cyprus**;
- **Specific** others.

### Why migrate to Jersey

- **Reputation** of Jersey;
- **Article 9 firewall** (TJL, with FJL equivalent);
- **Established** trust / foundation law;
- **Specific** family / commercial reasons;
- **Existing** Jersey advisory relationships.

### Tax considerations

- **Jersey tax** consequences on continuance (typically
  none for zero-rated foundations);
- **Home jurisdiction** tax consequences (varies);
- **Beneficiaries' jurisdictions** tax considerations;
- **Specific** Pillar Two considerations for large groups.
