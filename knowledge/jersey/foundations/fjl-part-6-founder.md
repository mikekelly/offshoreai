# FJL 2009 Part 6 — Founder and Other Persons (residual)

The founder provision (Article 26) has its own deep file. This
cluster covers Article 27 — "other persons".

## Article 27 — Other persons

The charter / regulations may give powers, rights, or
functions to **persons other than** the founder, council, or
guardian. Typical examples:

- A **protector**-type role (consent rights, although this
  function overlaps significantly with the guardian);
- A **family council** (where a body of family members has
  consultative or consent roles);
- A **trusted adviser** with specific powers (investment
  direction, distribution committee);
- A **letter-of-wishes recipient** with persuasive (not
  binding) influence;
- An **add-back power holder** (rare).

## Status of "other persons"

A person granted power under Article 27 is **not** automatically:

- A **council member** (must be appointed as such if they are
  to act in council capacity);
- A **guardian** (must be appointed as such);
- A **beneficiary** (must be designated as such).

Their position is **purely contractual** — a power granted
under the charter / regulations.

## Duties of "other persons"

A person exercising powers under Article 27:

- Must act in **good faith**;
- Must act within the **scope of the power granted**;
- Must comply with the **charter / regulations**;
- May owe **fiduciary** or **non-fiduciary** duties depending
  on the nature of the role (the analysis tracks the
  jurisprudence on Jersey trust protectors).

The role may attract a **fiduciary characterisation** if:

- It involves **discretion** affecting beneficiaries;
- It is described as a **power** to be exercised in the
  interests of objects / beneficiaries;
- The drafting **does not** carve out fiduciary duty
  expressly.

## Drafting points

- **Be explicit** about whether the power is fiduciary or
  personal;
- **Specify** the scope and standards of exercise;
- **Provide** for succession (death, incapacity, resignation);
- **Address** removal mechanism;
- **Consider** indemnity from foundation assets;
- **Coordinate** with the guardian's role to avoid overlap or
  conflict.
