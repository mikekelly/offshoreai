# FJL 2009 Article 13 — Ultra Vires

Article 13 enables the **setting aside** of acts beyond the
foundation's objects.

## The provision

Acts **outside the objects** of a foundation are **ultra vires**
and may be **set aside** by the Royal Court on application by:

- A **council member**;
- The **guardian**;
- The **Attorney General** (charitable / public-purpose
  contexts);
- A **person specified** in the charter or regulations.

## Standing

The list of who can apply is **closed** to specifically named
parties. **Counterparties** to ultra vires transactions
generally **cannot** apply themselves.

## Effect of ultra vires

Where an act is **set aside**:

- The **transaction** is unwound;
- **Property** transferred is restored;
- **Third parties** with BFPV protection may retain
  property;
- **Specific** equitable considerations.

## BFPV protection

A **bona fide purchaser for value without notice**:

- **Retains** what they acquired in good faith;
- **Specific** protection under general principles;
- **Subject to** specific facts;
- **Specific** Jersey case law.

## Modern relevance

The ultra vires doctrine has been **narrowed** in modern
practice for several reasons:

- **Capacity** under Article 12 is wide;
- **Most foundations** have **broadly drafted objects**;
- **Specific** transactions rarely truly outside objects;
- **Courts** reluctant to set aside complete transactions
  where unfair to innocent parties.

In practice, ultra vires challenge is **rare** but **available**
for clear cases.

## Compared to company ultra vires

The traditional company ultra vires doctrine has been
**significantly narrowed** in modern company law. Foundation
ultra vires retains more bite because:

- **Foundations** are object-driven (more so than typical
  modern companies);
- **Charter** is a public statement of objects;
- **Specific** beneficiary / public-purpose context.

## Practical drafting

To avoid ultra vires issues:

- **Charter objects** drafted **broadly**;
- **Specific** "and any incidental purpose" wording;
- **Regulations** authorise reasonable foundation activities;
- **Specific** review for sensitive transactions.
