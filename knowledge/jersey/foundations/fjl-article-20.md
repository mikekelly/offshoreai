# FJL 2009 Article 20 — Records and Accounts

Article 20 requires the council to maintain **records and
accounts**.

## Required records

The council must maintain at the registered office:

### Register of council members

- Current council members;
- Historical members;
- Dates of appointment / removal;
- **Qualified member** identified.

### Register of beneficiaries (where applicable)

- Beneficiary identification;
- Class definitions;
- **Specific** beneficiaries named per regulations;
- Updates as beneficiaries added / removed / disclaim.

### Council minutes

- All council meetings and resolutions;
- **Written** records;
- **Signed** by chair / participants.

### Accounting records

- Receipts and payments;
- Asset registers;
- Distribution records;
- **Sufficient** to show financial position with reasonable
  accuracy at any time.

### Charter and regulations

- Current versions;
- All amendments;
- **History** of changes.

### Distribution records

- Each distribution recorded;
- **Beneficiary** identification;
- **Amount** and **purpose**;
- **Decision-making** documentation.

### Specific other records

- **Material** transactions;
- **Service-provider** agreements;
- **AML / CDD** records (typically held by qualified-member
  firm);
- **Audit** materials if applicable.

## Retention

Records must be retained:

- For at least **10 years** (typical Jersey standard);
- **Or** longer per other obligations (AML, tax,
  litigation);
- **Even after** foundation dissolution;
- **Specific** retention schedules per record type.

## Access

Records are accessible by:

### Internally

- **Council members** (full access);
- **Guardian** (under Article 25);
- **Qualified member** firm (operational);
- **Auditor** if engaged.

### Externally

- **Registrar** on request;
- **Royal Court** by order;
- **JFSC** for regulated functions;
- **Beneficiaries** to the extent permitted by Article 30
  and the regulations.

## Format

- **Paper or electronic** acceptable;
- **Specific** integrity requirements;
- **Backed up** to protect against loss;
- **Specific** secure storage for confidential records.

## Foundation vs trust comparison

| Records | Trust (TJL) | Foundation (FJL) |
|---|---|---|
| Trustee / council | Yes | Yes |
| Beneficiaries | Yes | Yes (if any) |
| Accounting | Yes | Yes |
| Letters of wishes | Trust deed-specific | Regulations-specific |
| Public visibility | None | Charter public; regulations private |
