# FJL 2009 Article 25 — Guardian Information Rights

Article 25 gives the guardian **information rights** broader
than those of beneficiaries (Article 30).

## The provision

The guardian is entitled, on reasonable request, to:

- **Inspect** records, minutes, and accounting books;
- **Receive** copies of resolutions, accounts, and other
  documents;
- **Receive** notice of council meetings (where regulations so
  provide);
- **Be consulted** on matters per regulations;
- **Be informed** of material developments;
- **Specific** consent rights for prescribed matters.

## Why broader than beneficiary rights

The guardian's information rights are **deliberately broader**:

- **Supervisory** role requires full information;
- **Cannot effectively supervise** without records access;
- **Distinct** from beneficiary's constrained rights under
  Article 30;
- **Reflects** the guardian's responsibility (Article 23).

## Specific entitlements

### Records inspection

- **Council minutes**;
- **Accounting records**;
- **Beneficiary register**;
- **Material contracts** and transactions;
- **AML / CDD** records (subject to specific confidentiality).

### Periodic reporting

- **Annual reports** to the guardian (standard practice);
- **Material developments** notified;
- **Specific** thresholds per regulations.

### Meeting attendance

- **Notice** of council meetings (where regulations so
  provide);
- **Attendance** as observer (typical);
- **Not voting** (guardian is not a council member);
- **Specific** rights of participation per regulations.

### Specific consent

The regulations commonly require **guardian consent** for:

- **Distributions** above thresholds;
- **Material** asset transactions;
- **Constitutional** amendments;
- **Specific** other prescribed matters.

## Limits

The guardian's information rights are:

- **Reasonable** in scope (not abusive);
- **Subject to** legitimate confidentiality (e.g., specific
  beneficiary information protected from another
  beneficiary);
- **Subject to** AML / CFT considerations (specific SAR
  details not disclosed even to guardian);
- **Specific** Court can intervene for disputes.

## Practical operation

In practice:

- **Qualified-member firm** typically provides regular
  reporting to guardian;
- **Annual** comprehensive report;
- **Ad hoc** on material matters;
- **Quarterly / semi-annual** depending on activity level;
- **Guardian** typically consulted before material council
  decisions.

## Compared with beneficiary rights

| Aspect | Guardian (Article 25) | Beneficiary (Article 30) |
|---|---|---|
| Default | Broad rights | Limited unless regulations grant |
| Scope | Supervisory access | Constrained |
| Records | Direct access | Through council discretion |
| Meeting notice | Typical | Not typical |
| Consent rights | Common | Rare |
