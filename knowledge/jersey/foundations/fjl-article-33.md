# FJL 2009 Article 33 — Registered Office Records

Article 33 specifies the **records that must be kept at the
registered office**.

## The required registered-office records

At the registered office, the council must keep:

- **Charter** (current version);
- **Regulations** (current version);
- **Register of council members** (current and historical);
- **Register of beneficiaries** (where applicable);
- **Council minutes** and resolutions;
- **Guardian appointments** and removals;
- **Records of distributions**;
- **Accounting records** (Article 32);
- **Specific** other records prescribed by regulations.

## Why at the registered office

- **Central location** for foundation records;
- **Accessible** to authorities and other entitled persons;
- **Subject to** inspection on demand;
- **Practical** for ongoing administration.

## Inspection rights

The records are accessible by:

### Statutory access

- **Guardian** (Article 25);
- **Registrar** on demand;
- **Royal Court** by order;
- **JFSC** for regulated functions.

### Beneficiary access

- **Limited** under Article 30;
- **Subject to** regulations.

### Council member access

- **Full access** as office-holder.

## Specific record formats

Records can be in:

- **Paper** form;
- **Electronic** form;
- **Combination**;
- **Specific** integrity requirements;
- **Specific** confidentiality controls.

## Alternative location

The records can be kept **away from** the registered office
if:

- **Notified** to the Registrar;
- **Specified** alternative location;
- **Specific** authorisation per regulations.

In practice, records typically **stay at** the registered
office (qualified-member firm).

## Retention

Per Article 32:

- **At least 10 years** retention;
- **Longer** for specific obligations (AML, tax, litigation);
- **Even after** dissolution;
- **Specific** retention schedules.

## Practical operation

The qualified-member firm typically:

- **Maintains** all records at its office;
- **Provides** secure storage (physical and electronic);
- **Provides** access to authorised parties;
- **Manages** retention and destruction;
- **Charges** for records services as part of engagement.
