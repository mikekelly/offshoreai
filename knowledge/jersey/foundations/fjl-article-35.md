# FJL 2009 Article 35 — Applicable Law

Article 35 governs the **choice of applicable law** for a
Jersey foundation.

## The provision

The **applicable law** of a foundation is:

- The **law of Jersey** (default for any Jersey-established
  foundation); or
- Such other law as is **specified in the charter** for
  matters governed by Article 36;
- **Mandatory Jersey rules** (notably Article 37 firewall)
  apply regardless of any contrary choice.

## Why this matters

The applicable-law concept is **less central** for foundations
than for trusts (where the *Trusts (Jersey) Law 1984* governs
international trusts with proper law clauses). But it remains
relevant for:

- **Migrated foundations** continuing in from another
  jurisdiction;
- **Foundations** with multinational founders / beneficiaries;
- **Foundations** holding non-Jersey property where governing-
  law issues affect title.

## What is governed by the applicable law

Under Article 36, the applicable law governs:

- The **validity** of the foundation;
- The **construction** of the charter and regulations;
- The **administration** of the foundation;
- The **powers and duties** of council members;
- The **information rights** of beneficiaries;
- The **dissolution** procedure.
