# FJL 2009 Article 9 — Change of Name

Article 9 governs **change of name** of an existing foundation.

## Procedure

A foundation can change its name by:

- **Council resolution** in accordance with the charter and
  regulations;
- **Filing** the resolution with the Registrar;
- **Payment** of the prescribed fee;
- **Subject to** the new name complying with Article 8.

## Effective date

The new name takes effect on the **date of the Registrar's
certificate** of name change.

## Constitutional impact

- **Charter** is updated to reflect new name;
- **Specific** consequential changes (letterheads, contracts,
  registrations);
- **Existing** contractual obligations continue under the new
  name;
- **Foundation's** legal personality is **unaffected** by the
  name change.

## Specific scenarios

### Brand refresh

- **Council** approves new name;
- **Filing** with Registrar;
- **Implementation** across all stakeholder touchpoints.

### Acquisition

- Following acquisition by a different family / group;
- **New** name reflecting new ownership;
- **Specific** transitional considerations.

### Compliance-driven change

- Where existing name becomes **misleading** or **prohibited**;
- **Renaming** required;
- **Specific** Minister intervention possible.

## Notifications

The name change should be **notified** to:

- **Banks** holding accounts of the foundation;
- **Counterparties** under existing contracts;
- **Tax authorities** in relevant jurisdictions;
- **Other** registries (where foundation is registered as
  shareholder etc.).
