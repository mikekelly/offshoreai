# FJL 2009 Article 31 — Beneficiary Disclaimer

Article 31 permits a beneficiary to **disclaim** their
beneficiary status.

## The provision

A beneficiary may **disclaim** their beneficiary status by:

- **Notice** to the foundation (in writing);
- **Irrevocable** (cannot be rescinded except by Court);
- **Without** receiving consideration for the disclaimer;
- **Provided** the regulations do not prohibit disclaimer.

## Effect of disclaimer

The disclaimed beneficiary:

- **Ceases** to be a beneficiary from the date of disclaimer;
- **Is not** subsequently treated as a beneficiary for any
  purpose;
- **Loses** any information rights they may have had;
- **Cannot** later rescind except by application to the
  Royal Court (rare).

## Reasons to disclaim

### Foreign tax

The most common reason:

- **Foreign-tax** attribution to the beneficiary;
- **Worldwide income** taxation in the beneficiary's home
  jurisdiction;
- **Disclaimer** to remove the beneficiary from the regime;
- **Specific** US grantor-trust analogues for foundations.

### Family disputes

- **Withdrawing** from a family structure;
- **Breaking** family ties for personal reasons.

### Sanctions

- **Politically exposed person** wishing to avoid
  designation;
- **Pre-emptive** disclaimer.

### Inheritance decline

- **Refusing** to take from a deceased family member's
  foundation.

## Regulations may restrict

The regulations can:

- **Allow** disclaimer (default, per the Law);
- **Restrict** disclaimer to specified circumstances;
- **Provide** for **partial** disclaimer (e.g., disclaim
  distributions but retain information rights);
- **Specify** the form of notice;
- **Require** specific consents.

## Tax considerations

Disclaimer can have **foreign tax consequences**:

- **Home jurisdiction** may treat disclaimer as a deemed
  receipt;
- **Specific** advice in the disclaimer's home jurisdiction
  essential;
- **Timing** of disclaimer matters in some regimes;
- **Specific** documentation required.

## Disclaimer of specific interests

Where a beneficiary has multiple interests:

- **Specific** interests can be disclaimed separately;
- **Specific** rules per regulations;
- **Court guidance** if regulations unclear.

## Successor beneficiaries

When a beneficiary disclaims:

- **Successor beneficiaries** (per regulations) may receive;
- **Per stirpes** distribution in family-structure trusts;
- **Specific** rules in the regulations.
