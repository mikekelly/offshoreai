# FJL 2009 Article 42 — Court-Ordered Dissolution

Article 42 enables the **Royal Court** to order the
dissolution of a Jersey foundation.

## Who can apply

A Court-ordered dissolution may be applied for by:

- The **council**;
- The **guardian**;
- A **beneficiary** with sufficient standing;
- The **Attorney General** (public-interest contexts —
  charitable, public-purpose);
- The **Registrar** (for systemic non-compliance);
- A **creditor** (in insolvent dissolution).

## Grounds for Court-ordered dissolution

The Court may order dissolution for:

### Insolvency

- Foundation **cannot pay debts** as they fall due;
- **Balance sheet** insolvent;
- **Specific** insolvency tests.

### Failure of objects

- **Objects impossible** to achieve;
- **Objects illegal** or become illegal;
- **Practical failure** of the foundation's purposes;
- **Cy-près** alternative may be preferred to dissolution.

### Public-policy concerns

- **Foundation** being used for illegal purposes;
- **Specific** public-interest grounds;
- **Sham** foundation.

### Deadlock

- **Council** in deadlock that cannot be resolved;
- **Governance failure** beyond internal mechanisms;
- **Court** intervention to break deadlock through
  dissolution.

### Material breach

- **Material breach** of charter / regulations;
- **Repeated** non-compliance with statutory obligations;
- **Specific** governance failures.

### Other just cause

- **Specific** circumstances justifying dissolution;
- **Court's** broad discretion;
- **Case-specific** analysis.

## Procedure

1. **Application** by qualifying party;
2. **Affidavit** evidence of grounds;
3. **Convening** of affected parties (other beneficiaries,
   creditors, Attorney General if charitable);
4. **Hearing** before the Royal Court;
5. **Court order** for dissolution;
6. **Specific** ancillary orders (liquidator, asset
   distribution).

## Liquidator

The Court may **appoint a liquidator** to:

- **Realise** assets;
- **Pay** creditors;
- **Distribute** remaining property per Article 43 or
  Court order;
- **Make** final filings.

The liquidator is often a **specialist insolvency
practitioner** or **the Viscount** for serious cases.

## Insolvent dissolution

For insolvent foundations:

- **Specific** procedures for creditor proofs;
- **Priority** of claims (similar to corporate winding-
  up);
- **Avoidance** powers may apply;
- **Coordination** with **désastre** considerations.

## Asset distribution

The Court order specifies distribution:

- **Per charter / regulations** if solvent;
- **Per Court determination** if regulations unclear or
  insolvent;
- **Specific** charitable cy-près if charitable foundation;
- **Bona vacantia** to Crown in rare cases.

## Specific examples

### Settlor wants out

A founder seeking dissolution where:

- Family-governance no longer functional;
- Foundation purpose no longer served;
- **Possible** through Court application with consent of
  council, guardian, beneficiaries.

### Insolvent foundation

- **Creditor** application;
- **Court-supervised** wind-up;
- **Specific** insolvency procedures.

### Charitable purposes failed

- **Attorney General** application;
- **Cy-près** remedy considered first;
- **Dissolution** if cy-près not possible;
- **Property** to similar charitable purposes.
