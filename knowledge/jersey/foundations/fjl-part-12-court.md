# FJL 2009 Part 12 — Court (residual)

Articles 45-47 have their own deep files. This cluster covers
Article 48 — variation of the charter / regulations by court
order.

## Article 48 — Variation of charter / regulations

The Royal Court can **vary** a foundation's charter or
regulations:

- On application by a person with standing under Article 45;
- Where variation is necessary or expedient:
  - To **deal with changed circumstances** (e.g., objects
    have failed, become impractical);
  - To **enable** the foundation to carry on its objects;
  - To **remove** a mistake in drafting;
  - To **address** a deadlock;
  - To **modernise** outmoded provisions;
  - To enable **migration** out of Jersey;
- Subject to **procedural safeguards** (notice to affected
  parties, evidence-based hearing).

## Why this matters

Foundation governance is rooted in:

- The **charter** (public; amendment is filed publicly);
- The **regulations** (private; amendment is internal).

Both documents are normally amended by **internal mechanism**:

- **Council resolution** (subject to charter / regulations);
- **Guardian consent** (where required);
- **Founder consent** (where reserved);
- **Beneficiary consent** (rare, but possible).

When the **internal mechanism fails** — e.g., council is in
deadlock; founder is deceased and no successor reserved
powerholder; guardian is conflicted — Article 48 provides the
**court route**.

## Cy-près for charitable foundations

Where the foundation is **charitable** and its objects fail or
become impossible:

- The Court can **vary** the objects to permit the foundation
  to continue with similar charitable purposes;
- The **Attorney General** is typically convened;
- The **cy-près doctrine** as developed in Jersey / English
  charity law applies;
- The variation is **conservative** — the closest practicable
  alternative purpose is chosen.

## Procedure

Application is by **representation**:

- **Filed** with the Royal Court;
- **Convenors** include all affected parties;
- **Evidence** filed of the need for variation, the
  consequence, and the proposed wording;
- **Hearing** in the Family Division or Samedi;
- **Court order** specifies the new wording;
- **Filing** with the Registrar for the charter (public);
  internal record for the regulations (private).
