# FJL 2009 Article 8 — Name

Article 8 governs the **name** of a Jersey foundation.

## Requirements

A foundation's name **must**:

- **End with** "Foundation" or "Fondation" (or a recognised
  abbreviation);
- **Not** be the **same as** a name already on the Registrar's
  register;
- **Not** be **misleading** as to the nature of the
  foundation;
- **Not** include words or expressions **prohibited** by the
  Minister.

## Name approval

The Registrar:

- **Reviews** proposed names at registration;
- **Can refuse** a name on the statutory grounds;
- **Maintains** the register of foundation names;
- **Specific** reservation procedure available pre-
  application.

## Prohibited words

Specific words / expressions require Minister approval:

- Words implying **royal patronage**;
- Words implying **regulatory authority**;
- Words implying **specific** activities the foundation is
  not licensed for (e.g., "Bank");
- **Specific** politically sensitive terms.

## Appeals

A refused name can be appealed:

- To the **Royal Court** under Article 46;
- **Reasoned** decision required from Registrar;
- **Substitute** name typically agreed in negotiations.

## Practical considerations

- **Reserve** the name during the application process;
- **Confirm** trademark / brand alignment;
- **Specific** sensitivity around words like "Trust",
  "Charity", "International";
- **Avoid** confusion with existing entities.
