# FJL 2009 Part 7 — Beneficiaries (residual)

Articles 28-30 (defining the beneficiary regime) have their
own deep files. This cluster covers Article 31 and ancillary
points.

## Article 31 — Beneficiary disclaimer

A beneficiary may **disclaim** their status:

- By **notice** to the foundation;
- **Irrevocably**;
- **Without** receiving consideration for the disclaimer;
- Provided the **regulations** do not prohibit disclaimer.

The disclaimed beneficiary:

- Ceases to be a beneficiary from the date of disclaimer;
- Is **not** subsequently treated as a beneficiary for any
  purpose;
- Loses any **information rights** they may have had;
- Cannot later **rescind** the disclaimer except by
  application to the Royal Court.

## Why a beneficiary might disclaim

Common scenarios:

- **Foreign tax** — disclaimer to avoid attribution of trust /
  foundation income to the disclaimant under home-jurisdiction
  rules;
- **Family disputes** — withdrawing from a structure to break
  family ties;
- **Public-policy** considerations — politically exposed
  persons, sanctions concerns;
- **Inheritance** declines — refusing benefit from a deceased
  family member's foundation.

## Drafting

The regulations can:

- **Allow** disclaimer (default, per the Law);
- **Restrict** disclaimer to specified circumstances;
- **Provide** for partial disclaimer (e.g., disclaim
  distributions, retain information rights);
- **Specify** the form of notice.

## Adding / removing beneficiaries

Adding / removing beneficiaries is **not** governed by Article
31 (which is disclaimer only). The regulations should provide
mechanisms:

- **Council** power to add / remove;
- **Founder** reserved power;
- **Guardian** consent to addition / removal;
- **Family-council** input.

Addition / removal should be **documented** in the
foundation's records, and the **register of beneficiaries**
(Article 20) updated.
