# FJL 2009 Article 7 — Regulations

Article 7 governs the **regulations** — the private
constitutional document of a Jersey foundation, complementing
the public charter.

## Status

The regulations:

- Are **mandatory** — every foundation must have them;
- Are **not filed** with the Registrar;
- Are **not on public inspection**;
- Are **internal** to the foundation and binding on the council.

This privacy is one of the principal attractions of the
foundation vehicle: the sensitive governance and dispositive
content sits in the regulations, away from public view, while
the charter (which is public) contains only the high-level
constitutional matters.

## Required content

The regulations **must** address:

- The **functioning of the council** — convening meetings,
  voting, quorum, decisions;
- The **appointment, removal, and retirement** of council
  members;
- The **disposition** of foundation assets (in accordance with
  the objects);
- The **guardian's** functions in relation to the foundation;
- **Records and accounts**;
- The **dissolution** procedure (council-led under Article 41).

## Common additional content

Practitioners typically also include:

- The **identification of beneficiaries** (where the foundation
  has beneficiaries rather than purposes only);
- **Distribution policy** — discretionary, fixed, by class;
- **Letters of wishes** mechanism;
- **Reserved powers** of the founder (consent rights,
  revocability, etc.);
- **Information rights** of beneficiaries (or their exclusion
  / modification);
- **Cy-près** type provisions for charitable foundations;
- **Conflict resolution** procedures.

## Amendment

The regulations can be amended:

- By the **council** (under powers reserved in the regulations
  or charter);
- With **guardian consent** if the regulations or charter so
  require;
- By **Royal Court order** under Article 48.

Amendments to the regulations are **not filed publicly** —
they remain private. This is a significant practical advantage
over public-register entities (companies whose articles must
be filed on amendment).

## Drafting practice

- The regulations should be **comprehensive** — they are the
  operating manual of the foundation;
- They should **dovetail** with the charter (no inconsistency);
- They should **anticipate succession** issues (council member
  death, qualified-member resignation, founder's death);
- They should **specify dispute resolution** for inter-organ
  disputes (Council vs guardian, founder vs council).
