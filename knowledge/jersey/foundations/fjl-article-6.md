# FJL 2009 Article 6 — Charter

Article 6 governs the **charter** — the public constitutional
document of a Jersey foundation.

## Required content

The charter **must** state:

- The **name** of the foundation (ending in "Foundation" or
  "Fondation" or an abbreviation);
- The **objects** for which it is established (a purpose,
  charitable or non-charitable; or for beneficiaries; or both);
- The **initial council** members (and identification of the
  qualified member);
- Whether the foundation is established for **a period** or
  perpetually;
- The **endowment** at establishment (if any);
- Any other matter the Law prescribes.

## Optional content

The charter **may** also contain:

- The **dispositive rules** (though typically deferred to the
  regulations);
- The mechanism for **appointment / removal** of council
  members;
- **Reserved powers** of the founder;
- **Information rights** of beneficiaries (or their exclusion);
- The **applicable law** (typically Jersey, but provision can
  be made);
- Provisions for **migration** out of Jersey.

## Public document

The charter is **filed with the Registrar** and is **available
for public inspection** under Article 51. Practitioners
should design the charter accordingly:

- **Avoid placing sensitive information** in the charter
  (e.g., specific beneficiary names, detailed dispositive
  rules);
- **Use the regulations** (Article 7) for private matter;
- **Identify beneficiaries** by class rather than by name
  where possible.

## Amendment

The charter can be **amended**:

- By the council (under powers reserved in the charter);
- With **guardian consent** if the charter so requires (and
  often the regulations make guardian consent mandatory);
- By **Royal Court order** under Article 48.

Amendment requires **filing** with the Registrar and becomes
public.

## Practical drafting

A well-drafted charter will:

- State the objects **broadly enough** to permit future flexibility;
- Reserve **detailed dispositive rules** to the regulations;
- Provide for **guardian consent** to material amendments;
- Specify **succession** of the qualified member if it
  resigns;
- Address **dissolution** and **migration** at a high level.
