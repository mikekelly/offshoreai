# FJL 2009 Article 54 — Regulations

Article 54 grants the Minister power to make **regulations**
under the Law.

## Scope of regulation-making power

The Minister may make regulations:

- **Prescribing forms** and procedures;
- **Setting fees** (Article 52);
- **Modifying** or **supplementing** interpretation
  provisions;
- **Implementing** international standards (FATF, OECD,
  Moneyval);
- **Addressing** specific risks (substance, BO, sanctions);
- **Specific** procedural matters.

## Approval procedure

Regulations under Article 54 are typically subject to:

- **Negative procedure** in the States Assembly — come into
  force unless disapproved within a specified period;
- **Sometimes** affirmative procedure for material
  regulations;
- **Specific** procedural rules per the constitutional
  framework.

## Specific implementing regulations

Key implementing regulations under FJL include:

### Forms and Fees

- **Specific** Orders prescribing forms;
- **Specific** Orders prescribing fees;
- **Updated** annually typically.

### Substance / BO integration

- **Substance Law 2019** application to foundations;
- **FS(DPI)(J)L 2020** BO register integration;
- **Specific** Orders integrating these.

### AML / CFT alignment

- **Specific** Orders aligning FJL with MLO 2008 / POCL
  1999;
- **Schedule 2** treatment of foundations.

## Sub-regulation

Below regulations:

- **JFSC Codes of Practice** for qualified-member firms;
- **Practice Notes** from Registrar;
- **Industry** guidance;
- **Specific** procedural notices.
