# FJL 2009 Article 40 — Effect of Migration

Article 40 specifies the **legal consequences** of migration
(continuance in or out).

## Continuity of legal personality

The foundation's **legal personality continues** uninterrupted:

- **Same entity** before and after migration;
- **Same rights and obligations**;
- **Same contracts** continuing;
- **No** notional dissolution / new establishment.

## Property

- **All property** of the foundation continues to be owned;
- **No transfer** of title required (continuance is **not**
  a transfer);
- **Land registry** updates may be needed in some
  jurisdictions for clarity;
- **Specific** property-related registrations updated.

## Contracts

- **Existing contracts** continue in force;
- **Counterparty** not entitled to terminate solely due to
  continuance (subject to specific contractual provisions);
- **Specific** assignment / novation considerations for
  some contracts;
- **Notice** to counterparties typical.

## Litigation

- **Pending litigation** continues with the migrated entity
  as party;
- **Subject to** procedural rules of the relevant court;
- **Specific** considerations for service of process post-
  migration.

## Tax consequences

### Jersey side (outgoing)

- **No** Jersey tax on the migration itself typically
  (foundations are zero-rated);
- **Specific** Pillar Two considerations for in-scope groups;
- **Substance** Law obligations cease prospectively.

### Destination side (incoming)

- **Destination jurisdiction** rules apply;
- **Specific** tax recognition of incoming foundation;
- **Specific** characterisation under destination tax law.

### Beneficiaries

- **Beneficiaries' tax** in their home jurisdictions:
  - May treat continuance as **non-event**;
  - May treat as **constructive distribution / new
    establishment** in some configurations;
  - **Specific** advice in each beneficiary's home
    jurisdiction.

## Regulatory consequences

### Outgoing

- **JFSC** authorisation of qualified-member ceases for this
  foundation;
- **Foundation** no longer subject to FJL;
- **AML / CFT** Jersey-side records retained per retention
  periods.

### Incoming

- **Destination regulator** assumes oversight;
- **Specific** destination requirements apply;
- **AML / CFT** transfer of records to incoming TCB
  equivalent.

## Documentation

The migration is documented through:

- **Certificate** of continuance (in or out);
- **Updated** charter and regulations reflecting governing
  law;
- **Specific** registry filings in both jurisdictions;
- **Specific** stakeholder notifications.

## Beneficiaries' rights

Beneficiaries' rights:

- **Vested** rights typically preserved;
- **Contingent** rights subject to destination-jurisdiction
  rules;
- **Discretionary** beneficiary status continues but is
  subject to new jurisdiction's law;
- **Specific** beneficiary notifications recommended.
