# Tokenisation and Digital Assets — Jersey 2026 State of Play

## What this is

Jersey's approach to digital assets, virtual asset
service providers (VASPs), and **tokenisation of real-
world assets (RWAs)** has evolved rapidly through 2024-
2026. Key features of the current state:

- **No bespoke digital-assets primary legislation** —
  Jersey treats digital assets as another asset class
  within its existing legal and regulatory framework;
- **VASP registration regime** under existing AML
  legislation;
- **JFSC guidance** on tokenisation, stablecoins, and
  tokenised funds — updated through 2024-2025;
- **Innovation Council** established late 2025 to
  coordinate fintech and digital-assets development;
- **14 registered VASPs** as of October 2025 (a small
  but growing population).

For Crown Dependencies PE practitioners, tokenisation
matters because:

- **Fund interests** are starting to be tokenised — both
  inside and outside fund-side regulatory perimeters;
- **Tokenised securities** raise specific VASP +
  investment-business questions;
- **Asset tokenisation** (real estate, infrastructure,
  art) is a deal-shape that uses Jersey-domiciled
  issuance vehicles;
- **Stablecoin** issuance and treasury management
  affects fund cash management;
- **Trust structures** holding tokenised assets raise
  fresh Article 9 firewall and beneficiary-rights
  questions.

## Where it stands (as of 2026-05-18)

### VASP regime

Any Jersey entity conducting activities such as:

- **Exchange** of virtual assets;
- **Transfer** of virtual assets;
- **Safekeeping** of virtual assets;
- **Issuing** of virtual assets;

must register as a **VASP** with the JFSC.

Key features:

- **Not a conduct regulatory regime** in the traditional
  sense — no regulatory capital or full operational
  framework imposed by VASP status alone;
- **Rigorous approval process** — approximately **4
  months** from application to registration;
- **AML/CFT focus** — VASP registration is fundamentally
  an AML measure, ensuring KYC/CDD compliance;
- **14 registered VASPs** as of October 2025.

If the VASP is facilitating trading of tokens that are
**securities**, additional **investment-business licensing**
is needed. That brings:

- Full regulatory capital;
- Physical presence requirements;
- **Corporate income tax at 10%** (financial-services
  rate) rather than 0%.

### Tokenisation of real-world assets (RWAs)

The JFSC's August 2024 RWA tokenisation guidance was
**updated through H1 2025** on:

- **Stablecoins**;
- **VASPs**;
- **Tokenised funds**.

For a Jersey-domiciled RWA token issuer, requirements
include:

- **Jersey-incorporated company or LLC** as the issuer;
- **Administered by a regulated Jersey corporate
  services provider**;
- **JFSC regulatory consent** before issuing tokens;
- **AML/CFT/CPF compliance** with KYC/CDD on token
  purchasers and redeemers;
- **Specific** sound-business-practice guidance.

The regime is **compatible with international standards**,
including alignment with the EU's MiCA (Markets in
Crypto-Assets Regulation).

### Innovation Council

The Government of Jersey and JFSC launched a **Digital
Assets Innovation Council** in late 2025, bringing
together technology specialists and industry / JFSC
experts. The Council's role under the broader
**Competitiveness Programme** is to:

- Coordinate fintech-regulatory dialogue;
- Identify regulatory gaps and friction;
- Engage with international standard-setters;
- Promote Jersey as a digital-assets-friendly
  jurisdiction.

A government call for financial specialists for the
Council was issued in December 2025 — the Council is
**actively staffing up**.

### What's distinctive about Jersey's approach

- **No bespoke primary legislation** — Jersey chose to
  apply existing financial-services law (FSL 1998, AML
  legislation, POCL 1999) to digital assets rather than
  enact a standalone framework;
- **Pragmatic over prescriptive** — guidance-led rather
  than rule-led;
- **MiCA-compatible** but not MiCA-equivalent — Jersey
  isn't a member state and doesn't have to mirror MiCA
  in detail;
- **Lower-touch** than Cayman's VASP Act + Securities
  Investment Business Act stack;
- **Substance** required for token issuers under the
  general Substance Law 2019.

## Hot use cases

### Tokenised PE fund interests

A live and developing area:

- **Fund interest** represented as a token;
- **Improves** secondary-market liquidity for LP stakes;
- **Reduces** transfer-friction in fund-stake trading;
- **Specific** Jersey legal questions on how LP-stake
  tokenisation interacts with the fund's existing LP
  Agreement and transfer restrictions.

A Jersey JPF or PIF can in principle have tokenised
fund interests — the **technology layer** is separate
from the **fund regulatory layer**. The JFSC's tokenised
funds guidance addresses this.

### Tokenised real estate

Single-property and portfolio-property tokenisation:

- **SPV** holds the property;
- **Tokens** represent fractional beneficial interests;
- **Jersey** as the issuer jurisdiction;
- **Distribution** rights to token holders;
- **Specific** considerations on KYC, secondary trading,
  redemption.

### Stablecoins

- Specific stablecoin guidance updated H1 2025;
- **Reserve-asset** management;
- **Issuance** through Jersey vehicles;
- **MiCA-compatible** standards.

### Family-office digital-asset holdings

- HNW families with significant crypto allocations;
- **Trust structures** holding tokens (Article 9
  considerations);
- **Substance** for the holding vehicle;
- **CRS/FATCA** reporting on crypto positions.

## What practitioners are doing

- **VASP applications** — pre-application engagement
  with JFSC is essential given the 4-month process;
- **Tokenised fund design** — combining JPF/PIF
  structures with token mechanics, focus on transfer
  restrictions and KYC pathways;
- **MiCA strategy** for Jersey issuers serving EU
  investors — the compatibility-not-equivalence position
  needs careful management;
- **Innovation Council engagement** — sponsors and
  advisers are actively contributing to the Council's
  agenda-setting;
- **Comparison shopping** — Cayman, BVI, and Gibraltar
  are all competing in this space; Jersey's narrative is
  pragmatism + reputation.

## What to watch for next

- **Innovation Council** initial outputs and agenda
  publication;
- **Specific tokenised-fund guidance** refresh through
  2026;
- **MiCA equivalence** assessment — whether Jersey will
  pursue a formal route;
- **Securities-token** specific guidance — boundary
  with investment-business licensing;
- **DeFi protocol** application — Jersey hasn't yet
  developed a specific framework;
- **CBDC / central-bank** digital-asset interactions
  (Jersey's States have considered but not progressed);
- **EU LP appetite** — EU institutions adopting tokenised
  fund interests will accelerate the regulatory case for
  alignment;
- **JFSC enforcement** — first VASP enforcement actions
  will set the practical regulatory tone.

## Pitfalls

- **Assuming** VASP registration is the full regulatory
  picture — securities-token operation pulls in
  investment-business licensing;
- **Missing** the 4-month VASP approval timeline;
- **Treating** tokenised PE fund interests as outside
  the fund's regulatory perimeter — the underlying fund
  rules still apply;
- **MiCA confusion** — Jersey is compatible, not
  equivalent, and acting on the wrong assumption costs
  marketing access in the EU;
- **Substance** — Jersey-incorporated token issuer must
  meet Substance Law 2019 requirements;
- **AML/CFT** — KYC/CDD on token purchasers and
  redeemers is required and the standards are
  demanding;
- **Tax** — corporate income tax at 10% if classified
  as financial-services (e.g. securities-token VASP).
