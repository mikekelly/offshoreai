# SFDR 2.0 and Channel Islands Sustainable Finance — Frontier as of May 2026

## What this is

The **EU Sustainable Finance Disclosure Regulation
(SFDR)** is the EU's principal sustainability-disclosure
framework for asset managers and financial products
marketed in the EU. On **20 November 2025** the European
Commission published a **formal proposal for SFDR 2.0**
— a substantial overhaul. Separately, Jersey and Guernsey
have been developing **their own sustainable-finance
positioning** as competitive offerings.

For Crown Dependencies fund managers marketing into the
EU under NPPR, SFDR matters because:

- **NPPR-marketed funds** are typically **in scope** for
  SFDR;
- **SFDR classifications** (Article 6 / 8 / 9) drive
  investor expectations and marketing positioning;
- **Channel Islands** have positioned aggressively as
  sustainable-finance hubs.

## Where it stands (as of 2026-05-18)

### SFDR 1.0 (current law)

In force since 10 March 2021, with technical standards
applying since 1 January 2023.

Article-based classification:

- **Article 6** — no sustainability characteristic;
- **Article 8** — promotes ESG characteristics ("light
  green");
- **Article 9** — has sustainable investment as its
  objective ("dark green").

Plus **Principal Adverse Impact (PAI)** disclosure
requirements for AIFMs above prescribed thresholds.

### SFDR 2.0 (proposed)

The 20 November 2025 European Commission proposal would
overhaul SFDR substantially:

- **Categorical labels** replacing Article 8/9 in
  practice;
- **Simplified** disclosure regime less complex than
  current PAI structure;
- **Clearer** distinction between products with
  different ESG ambitions;
- **Reduced** ambiguity around "sustainable investment"
  definition.

Timeline:

- **Co-decision process** through 2026 — Parliament,
  Council;
- **Adoption** expected end-2026 / early 2027;
- **Implementation** running **2027-2028 at earliest**
  per the Commission's envisaged timeline.

This means **SFDR 1.0 remains in force** through 2026
and into 2027 — fund managers cannot rely on imminent
relief from current obligations.

### Channel Islands position

**Jersey** issued a sustainable-finance disclosure
framework that came into force **15 July 2021** —
deliberately structured **differently** from SFDR.
Jersey's approach:

- **Not mirroring** SFDR's three-category structure;
- **Simpler** disclosure requirements;
- **Avoiding** the worst of SFDR 1.0's complexity;
- **Pragmatic** for non-EU-domiciled funds that don't
  primarily target EU investors.

**Guernsey** has issued **anti-greenwashing guidance**
through the GFSC, focused on:

- **Substantiation** of sustainability claims in
  marketing materials;
- **Consistent** ESG language across PPMs and ongoing
  reporting;
- **Specific** sound-business-practice expectations.

### Channel Islands AUM in sustainable finance

Per Jersey Finance projections, **Jersey's sustainable
fund AUM is expected to reach £123 billion by 2026** —
a substantial growth narrative.

## How SFDR applies to Channel Islands managers

The critical operational point: **non-EU AIFMs marketing
into the EU via NPPR are within scope of SFDR for the
products they market**. This means:

- A **Jersey-domiciled AIFM** marketing an AIF into
  Germany or France via NPPR must classify the AIF
  under SFDR (Article 6 / 8 / 9);
- **Pre-investment** disclosure documents and **ongoing**
  disclosures must meet SFDR requirements for the
  marketed product;
- **PAI** considerations apply where the AIFM exceeds
  the relevant thresholds;
- **Greenwashing risk** — misclassification has
  enforcement consequences from EU competent
  authorities of marketing-target jurisdictions.

For a non-NPPR Channel Islands fund (e.g., one only
marketed to Crown Dependencies / UK / US LPs), SFDR
does **not** apply — though Jersey / Guernsey own-
regime requirements may.

## Hot operational topics

### Article 8 vs Article 9 classification

A live area for Crown Dependencies sponsors:

- **Article 8** ("light green") is the **more common**
  classification — promotes ESG characteristics
  alongside primary financial objective;
- **Article 9** ("dark green") requires sustainable
  investment as the **primary objective** — a higher
  bar and more onerous ongoing reporting;
- **Reclassifications down** (Article 9 → 8) happened
  widely in 2022-2023 as managers found the higher bar
  unmanageable;
- **Reclassifications up** (8 → 9) are rare and
  carefully documented.

### Greenwashing enforcement

EU regulator scrutiny has tightened:

- **ESMA opinion** on greenwashing risk areas;
- **BaFin** in Germany has been particularly active;
- **AMF** in France similarly;
- **Crown Dependencies** sponsors marketing into these
  jurisdictions face the enforcement exposure indirectly
  through their EU distributor / placement-agent
  chains.

### Channel Islands competitive positioning

PwC Channel Islands' commentary frames the Channel
Islands as **complementary** to EU domiciles, not
substitutes:

- **EU-domiciled** SFDR-compliant fund for European
  retail / quasi-retail capital;
- **Channel Islands** non-SFDR-bound fund for
  institutional capital wanting structural simplicity;
- **Parallel-fund** structures combining both.

For sponsors with deeply ESG-oriented investor bases,
this is a real and growing structural pattern.

## What practitioners are doing

### For Channel Islands NPPR managers

- **Annual review** of SFDR classification and disclosure;
- **PAI thresholds** tracking;
- **Marketing material** alignment with classification;
- **Side-letter** ESG provisions for major LPs;
- **Greenwashing risk** assessment under EU regulator
  guidance;
- **Updates** for SFDR 2.0 coming into view — but no
  immediate change required.

### For sponsors structuring new sustainable funds

- **Dual-structure** (Channel Islands + EU parallel)
  for broad investor reach;
- **Channel Islands single-domicile** for institutional-
  only funds;
- **EU domicile** where retail / quasi-retail SFDR
  Article 9 marketing is essential;
- **PRI / TCFD / GFANZ** alignment as voluntary
  enhancements.

### For LPs

- **Side-letter ESG provisions** increasingly common;
- **Specific** ESG reporting obligations on GP;
- **Exclusion lists** (no fossil fuels, no weapons, etc.);
- **Anti-greenwashing** comfort clauses.

## What to watch for next

- **SFDR 2.0** co-decision progress through 2026;
- **ESMA** common supervisory action on greenwashing
  outcomes;
- **CSRD / CSDDD** intersection with SFDR (the broader
  EU sustainability-reporting framework);
- **JFSC / GFSC** approach to SFDR 2.0 in due course —
  whether Channel Islands frameworks will be revised in
  response;
- **UK SDR** (Sustainability Disclosure Requirements)
  continued implementation — affects Channel Islands
  funds marketed in the UK;
- **OECD / G20** sustainability guidance evolution.

## Pitfalls

- **Assuming** SFDR doesn't apply because the AIFM is
  Channel Islands-domiciled — NPPR marketing brings the
  product into scope;
- **Misclassification** — Article 8 vs 9 is fact-
  specific and audit-trail-critical;
- **Marketing-material drift** — PPM says one thing,
  ongoing reporting says another;
- **PAI threshold breach** without reporting upgrade;
- **Greenwashing** language without substantiation;
- **Cross-border** with multiple EU marketing
  jurisdictions — each has its own competent-authority
  posture;
- **Assuming** SFDR 2.0 will simplify enough to defer
  current compliance — it's a 2027-2028 implementation
  earliest.
