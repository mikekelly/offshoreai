# Jersey Finance Industry — State of Play 2026

## What this is

A consolidated snapshot of Jersey's (and broadly Channel
Islands') financial-services industry trajectory as of
May 2026. Designed to give a senior practitioner the
**short** answer to "what's the state of the industry
right now" — including the live consultations,
strategic announcements, AUM growth, and the structural
narratives shaping the next 12-24 months.

## Headline industry data

### Jersey

- **Assets Under Administration** in Jersey grew **5%
  in 2025**;
- **Jersey Private Funds (JPFs)** — population of **~750
  as of March 2025** per Jersey Finance, with sustained
  year-over-year new-establishment flow (the Government
  competitiveness report cites ~1,500 structures since
  launch and £86.7bn administered — that figure
  aggregates structures-since-launch rather than the live
  JPF count);
- **Sustainable fund AUM projected to reach £123
  billion by 2026** (Jersey Finance projection);
- **JFSC 2026-2030 Strategy** published 23 March 2026;
- **Government 10-year Financial Services
  Competitiveness Programme** launched;
- **Innovation Council** for digital assets established
  late 2025.

### Guernsey

- **Total assets serviced reaching US$592 billion** by
  June 2025 (per Monterey Insight);
- **Strong double-digit growth** in fund industry to
  June 2025;
- **Private markets** leading the growth (PE, VC,
  infrastructure, real estate);
- **GFSC** has been particularly active in continuation-
  fund market development.

The two Channel Islands are operating as **competitive
twins** rather than head-to-head substitutes — each
plays specific roles in cross-border PE / family-
office structuring.

## What's shaping the year

### Live JFSC consultations

As of May 2026:

- **MLCO reforms** — Government of Jersey consultation
  on Money Laundering Compliance Officer role reforms
  under the Money Laundering (Jersey) Order 2008
  (consultation 30 Jan – 13 Mar 2026);
- **Schedule 2 Guidelines** — JFSC consultation on
  amendments to Article 36 Guidelines under POCL 1999
  on Schedule 2 activities;
- **EU AIF Code split** — under development per the
  AIFMD II April 2026 effective date;
- **Sustainable finance** — ongoing iteration.

### Strategic positioning

The **JFSC 2026-2030 Strategy** (published 23 March
2026) articulates the regulator's priorities. Key
themes:

- **Maintaining international standards** while
  preserving competitive positioning;
- **Digital assets and tokenisation** as a strategic
  growth vertical;
- **Sustainable finance** as a growth vertical;
- **Substance and AML/CFT** as ongoing supervisory
  priorities;
- **Outsourcing and operational resilience** as
  emerging supervisory focus.

The **Government's 10-year Financial Services
Competitiveness Programme** runs in parallel,
articulating economic-policy support for the financial-
services sector through 2035.

### Industry narrative themes

Per the Jersey Finance chairman's update (early 2026)
and law-firm commentary:

> "Year of change"

The industry's self-description for 2026. Key drivers:

1. **AIFMD II** effective 16 April 2026 — see

### Structural shifts in product mix

The Jersey industry is **moving away from blind-pooled
funds toward**:

- **Tailored investment structures** — bespoke
  vehicles for specific investor groups;
- **Corporate structuring** — non-fund corporate
  holdings (TopCos, JVs, family-office vehicles);
- **Private capital** — direct deal structuring rather
  than fund commitments;
- **Tokenisation and digital assets** — specifically
  tokenised real-world assets and tokenised fund
  interests;
- **Continuation funds** — single-asset and multi-asset
  continuation vehicles.

This shift mirrors **global PE-industry trends** where
LP capital is increasingly seeking:

- Specific deal access;
- Liquidity options;
- Tailored exposures;
- Lower fee leakage.

## The Crown Dependencies competitive positioning

| Strength | Lead jurisdiction |
|---|---|
| Largest fund-services industry by AUM | Guernsey (US$592bn) |
| Largest PE-product-line breadth | Jersey |
| Captive insurance | Guernsey |
| Life-assurance / portfolio bonds | Isle of Man |
| Continuation funds | Guernsey (aggressively) + Jersey |
| Trust law for forced-heirship defence | Jersey (Article 9 firewall) |
| Image rights | Guernsey (sole jurisdiction) |
| eGaming | Isle of Man |
| Sustainable finance | Both Jersey + Guernsey |
| Tokenisation | Jersey (Innovation Council); Cayman (VASP Act) |
| Pillar Two QDMTT + Qualified IIR | All Crown Dependencies (Jersey MCIT 2025, Guernsey + IoM equivalents; none has implemented UTPR) |

## What practitioners are doing

### Regulatory engagement

- **MLCO consultation** — industry submissions through
  Jersey Finance and trade bodies;
- **Schedule 2 Guidelines** — engagement on application
  to TCBs, fund administrators, lawyers;
- **AIFMD II EU AIF Code** — pre-publication engagement
  with JFSC;
- **Substance Law** ongoing — practitioner submissions
  on inspection experience.

### Business development

- **Continuation funds** as a dominant marketing message;
- **Sustainable finance** as a growth pitch to EU
  capital;
- **Tokenisation pilots** for forward-looking sponsors;
- **Family-office services** as a stable growth
  segment;
- **UK-departure** opportunities from UK partners
  facing the carried-interest reform.

### Internal compliance

- **AIFMD II readiness** projects;
- **Pillar Two** operational compliance;
- **AML/CFT** continued investment;
- **Operational resilience** programmes per JFSC
  emerging focus.

## What to watch for next

- **JFSC EU AIF Code** publication (expected through
  2026);
- **Industry AUM data** for full-year 2025;
- **First Pillar Two QDMTT** returns and outcomes;
- **JFSC 2026-2030 Strategy** implementation
  deliverables;
- **Continuation funds** market growth metrics;
- **Brexit impact tail** as UK's own AIFM regime
  finalises;
- **MoneyVal / FATF** assessments — periodic AML
  reviews;
- **OECD Pillar Two** further developments;
- **EU/UK** financial-services equivalence
  developments.

## Pitfalls

- **Treating** AUM growth as the full health metric —
  fund-product mix and value-add depth matter more;
- **Assuming** AIFMD II is a non-event for Crown
  Dependencies — the disclosure / Annex IV uplift is
  real;
- **Ignoring** the UK carried-interest reform impact on
  partner attraction / retention;
- **Missing** the regulatory direction-of-travel on
  digital assets — the Innovation Council is
  strategically meaningful;
- **Underestimating** continuation funds as a long-term
  market segment rather than a temporary post-COVID
  phenomenon.
