# DL 2013 Part 7 — Miscellaneous

## Articles 66-68 — Regulations

The Minister may make **Orders**:

- **Add** new protected characteristics;
- **Specify** exemptions;
- **Prescribe** procedural rules;
- **Update** Tribunal procedures;
- **Issue** Codes of Practice.

## Article 69 — Transitional

Transitional provisions:

- **Phased introduction** of characteristics (2014-onwards);
- **Specific** legacy arrangements;
- **Grandfathered** discriminatory practices being addressed.

## Article 70 — Citation

The Law is cited as the **"Discrimination (Jersey) Law
2013"**.

It came into force in **phases from 2014**:

- **2014**: race (first characteristic);
- **2015**: sex, sexual orientation, gender reassignment,
  pregnancy and maternity;
- **2016**: age;
- **2018**: disability.

Further characteristics may be added by Order.

## Codes of Practice

The Employment Forum / Minister issues Codes of Practice:

- **Employment** discrimination;
- **Service-provision** discrimination;
- **Education**;
- **Specific** topics (sexual harassment, age discrimination,
  disability adjustments).

Codes are **not strictly binding** but **persuasive** at
Tribunal — failure to follow attracts negative inference.

## Future development

Areas under consideration for protection:

- **Religion or belief**;
- **Marriage / civil partnership**;
- **Pregnancy in non-employment contexts**;
- **Caring responsibilities**;
- **Specific** intersectional protections.

Jersey's approach is **iterative** — characteristic addition
through Order rather than full statutory reform.
