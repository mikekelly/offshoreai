# DL 2013 Article 31 — Right to Equal Pay

Article 31 establishes the right to **equal pay** between
sexes.

## The principle

A woman has the right to equal pay with a man (and vice
versa) where:

- They are employed by the **same or associated employer**;
- They do the **same work**, **work rated as equivalent**, or
  **work of equal value**;
- They are at the **same establishment** or comparable
  establishments.

## Comparable employment

### Same work

- **Identical or broadly similar** duties;
- **Same level** of responsibility;
- **Same skills and experience** required.

### Work rated as equivalent

- **Formal job evaluation scheme** classifies the roles as
  equivalent;
- **Objective** evaluation criteria;
- **Documented** evaluation.

### Work of equal value

- **Different jobs** but of **equal value** in terms of:
  - **Skill**;
  - **Effort**;
  - **Decision-making**;
  - **Responsibility**;
- **Expert evidence** typically required;
- **Job evaluation expert** appointed by Tribunal.

## Comparator

The claimant must identify a **comparator**:

- **Specific** person of the opposite sex;
- **Same employer** (or associated employer);
- **Receiving** higher pay;
- **Doing** comparable work.

A **hypothetical** comparator is **generally not sufficient**
— a real comparator is required.

## Pay components

Equal pay extends to **all elements of pay**:

- **Basic pay**;
- **Bonuses**;
- **Overtime rates**;
- **Allowances**;
- **Benefits in kind**;
- **Pension** contributions;
- **Specific** other contractual payments.

## Material factor defence

The employer may defend on grounds of **material factor**:

- **Genuine** factor not based on sex;
- **Pay difference** attributable to the factor;
- **Examples**:
  - Different length of service;
  - Different skills / experience;
  - Different responsibilities not reflected in comparator
    basis;
  - Different qualifications;
  - Market forces for the specific roles.

### Indirect discrimination test

If the material factor itself indirectly discriminates:

- **Justification** required (proportionate means /
  legitimate aim);
- **Heightened** scrutiny.

## Pay transparency

DL Article 31 supports pay-transparency rights:

- **Employees** can discuss pay with colleagues for
  discrimination purposes;
- **Confidentiality clauses** preventing such discussion are
  unenforceable to that extent;
- **Tribunal** has powers to require disclosure of pay
  information.

## Remedies

Equal-pay claims:

- **Backdated pay** awards;
- **Forward** equal-pay declarations;
- **Time limit**: typically **6 years** for arrears claim;
- **Tribunal** jurisdiction.
