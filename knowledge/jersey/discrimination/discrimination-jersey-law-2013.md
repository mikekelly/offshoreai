# Discrimination (Jersey) Law 2013 — Overview

The **Discrimination (Jersey) Law 2013 (DL)** is Jersey's
**comprehensive anti-discrimination statute**. It came into
force in **phases from 2014**, with **protected
characteristics added** through Orders over time.

## Structure

The DL has a **framework / Order** structure:

- The **Law** establishes the framework, mechanisms,
  remedies;
- **Specific Orders** prescribe the **protected
  characteristics** to which the framework applies;
- **New characteristics** added by Order — no need for full
  statutory amendment.

This allows **phased implementation** as Jersey expands
equality protection.

## Comparison with UK Equality Act 2010

Substantively similar:

- Same **types of discrimination** (direct, indirect,
  harassment, victimisation);
- Same **protected characteristics** (broadly);
- Same **areas of application** (employment, services,
  education);
- Same **remedies** (compensation, declarations).

Key differences:

- Jersey's **phased** introduction of characteristics;
- **Specific** Jersey procedural rules through Jersey
  Employment and Discrimination Tribunal;
- **Specific** Jersey codes of practice;
- **Specific** Jersey exemptions in some areas.

## Forms of discrimination

### Direct discrimination

Less favourable treatment because of a protected
characteristic:

- "I don't hire women" — direct sex discrimination;
- "He's too old" — direct age discrimination;
- **Specific objective justification** for some characteristics
  in some contexts (age primarily).

### Indirect discrimination

A neutral rule that puts a protected group at a particular
disadvantage:

- "All managers must work 60 hours" — may indirectly
  discriminate against women / disabled / parents;
- **Subject to** justification (proportionate means of
  achieving a legitimate aim).

### Harassment

Unwanted conduct related to a protected characteristic that
violates dignity / creates a hostile / intimidating /
degrading / humiliating / offensive environment:

- **Workplace** sexual harassment;
- **Race-based** insulting jokes;
- **Disability** mockery.

### Victimisation

Treating a person less favourably because they have:

- **Made** a discrimination complaint;
- **Supported** a discrimination claim;
- **Given evidence** in proceedings;
- **Sought information** about possible discrimination.

### Reasonable adjustments (disability)

- **Auxiliary aids** for disabled persons;
- **Removal of physical barriers** where reasonable;
- **Modification** of practices and procedures;
- **Subject to** reasonableness;
- **Specific** focus on disability characteristic.

## Areas of application

### Employment

- **Recruitment**;
- **Terms and conditions**;
- **Promotion / training**;
- **Dismissal**;
- **All aspects** of employment relationship.

### Provision of goods and services

- **Sale of goods**;
- **Provision of services** (commercial, professional, public);
- **Access** to facilities;
- **Subject to** specific exemptions (private clubs, etc.).

### Education

- **Admissions**;
- **Treatment** within educational establishments;
- **Provision** of educational facilities;
- **Specific** rules for single-sex schools, religious schools.

### Premises

- **Disposal** of premises (sale, rent);
- **Use** of premises;
- **Termination** of tenancies;
- **Subject to** specific exemptions for owner-occupiers
  letting rooms.

## Remedies

- **Compensation** (no cap on compensation);
- **Injury to feelings** awards;
- **Declarations** of discrimination;
- **Recommendations** to remedy practices;
- **Specific** orders against future discrimination;
- **Tribunal** primary forum for employment / services;
- **Royal Court** for some claims.
