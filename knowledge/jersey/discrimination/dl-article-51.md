# DL 2013 Article 51 — Tribunal Procedure

Article 51 establishes Tribunal procedure for discrimination
claims.

## Primary forum

The **Jersey Employment and Discrimination Tribunal** is the
primary forum for discrimination claims:

- **Same Tribunal** as employment claims (EL Part 8);
- **Specific** procedural rules for discrimination claims;
- **Specific** procedural protections.

## Time limits

Time limits for claims:

- **Employment-related** discrimination: typically **8 weeks**;
- **Service-provision** discrimination: typically **6 months**;
- **Pre-claim** conciliation through JACS encouraged;
- **Specific** extensions in narrow circumstances.

## Filing

A claim is filed by:

- **Claim form** to the Tribunal;
- **Specific** required information;
- **Fee** in some cases (most cases fee-free);
- **JACS** conciliation reference where applicable.

## Defence

The respondent files:

- **Response** within prescribed period;
- **Specific** required content;
- **Counterclaims** in some cases.

## Burden of proof

The claimant must establish a **prima facie** case:

- Some evidence of less-favourable treatment / particular
  disadvantage;
- Connection to protected characteristic;

Then **burden shifts** to the respondent:

- **Provide** non-discriminatory explanation;
- If respondent cannot, Tribunal must find discrimination;
- **Article 56** specifically addresses this burden.

## Evidence

- **Documentary** evidence;
- **Witness statements**;
- **Statistical** evidence for indirect discrimination /
  equal pay;
- **Expert** evidence (job-evaluation experts, etc.);
- **Specific** procedural protocols.

## Hearing

- **Less formal** than Royal Court;
- **Self-representation** common;
- **Specific** Tribunal-specific procedures;
- **Hearing** in Jersey.

## Decision

- **Reasoned** decision in writing;
- **Findings of fact**;
- **Application of law**;
- **Orders** for remedy if claim succeeds.

## Appeals

- **Royal Court** on **point of law** only;
- **Specific** procedure;
- **Time limit**;
- **Further** appeals to Court of Appeal possible.
