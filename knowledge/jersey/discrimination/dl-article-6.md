# DL 2013 Article 6 — Victimisation

Article 6 prohibits **victimisation** — adverse treatment
because of a **protected act**.

## Definition

**Victimisation** = subjecting a person to detriment because
they have done a **protected act**.

## Protected acts

- **Bringing proceedings** under the DL;
- **Giving evidence or information** in proceedings under
  the DL;
- **Making an allegation** of discrimination (whether or not
  later proven);
- **Doing anything else** under the DL.

## Detriment

Examples of detriment:

- **Subsequent less-favourable treatment**;
- **Refusal** to provide reference;
- **Disciplinary** action;
- **Specific** retaliation in employment;
- **Service refusal**;
- **Specific** other adverse treatment.

## Causation

The detriment must be **because of** the protected act:

- **Connection** between the protected act and the
  detriment;
- **Burden of proof** on respondent to provide non-
  discriminatory explanation after prima facie case;
- **Subject to** factual analysis.

## Defence

The respondent may defend on grounds:

- **Good faith** investigation;
- **No causation** by the protected act;
- **Reasonable** explanation for the detriment unrelated to
  the protected act;
- **Specific** procedural protections used.

## Importance

Victimisation prohibition is **critical**:

- Without protection, employees would fear retaliation for
  complaining;
- **Reporting culture** depends on protection;
- **Specific** employer-side procedural protections often
  needed to demonstrate non-causation.

## Specific scenarios

### Reference refusal

- Former employee made discrimination complaint;
- Former employer refuses reference;
- **Subject to** victimisation analysis;
- **Defence**: good-faith reference policy unrelated.

### Disciplinary action

- Employee made discrimination complaint;
- Employer initiates disciplinary action;
- **Subject to** victimisation analysis;
- **Defence**: genuine unrelated conduct issue.

### Service termination

- Customer made discrimination complaint;
- Service provider terminates service;
- **Subject to** victimisation analysis;
- **Defence**: genuine unrelated service issue.
