# DL 2013 Part 6 — Enforcement

Part 6 provides the **enforcement mechanism** for
discrimination claims.

## Articles 51-55 — Tribunal procedure

The **Jersey Employment and Discrimination Tribunal** has
primary jurisdiction:

- **Same Tribunal** as for employment claims (Employment Law
  Part 8);
- **Specific** procedural rules for discrimination claims;
- **Time limit**: typically **8 weeks** for employment-related
  discrimination, **6 months** for service-provision
  discrimination;
- **Pre-claim** conciliation through JACS encouraged.

## Article 56 — Burden of proof

**Reverse burden of proof** applies after **prima facie** case:

- Claimant must establish **facts from which discrimination
  could be inferred**;
- Burden shifts to **respondent** to provide non-
  discriminatory explanation;
- If respondent cannot, **Tribunal must find discrimination**.

This is **important** — it lowers the practical burden on
claimants.

## Articles 57-60 — Remedies

### Declarations

- **Statement** that discrimination occurred;
- **Specific** finding about the conduct.

### Compensation

- **No upper cap** on compensation;
- **Material loss** (financial harm);
- **Injury to feelings** (Vento bands):
  - **Lower band**: minor / one-off (typically £1k-£12k);
  - **Middle band**: more serious / continuing (typically
    £12k-£36k);
  - **Upper band**: severe / sustained (typically £36k+);
- **Aggravated damages** for malicious / wilful conduct;
- **Exemplary damages** in extreme cases (rare).

### Recommendations

- **Specific** action by respondent to prevent future
  discrimination;
- **Policy reviews**;
- **Training**;
- **Specific** structural changes.

### Interest

- **Interest** on awards;
- **Specific** Jersey statutory rates.

## Articles 61-63 — Specific remedies

### Reinstatement / re-engagement

For employment cases:

- **Order** of reinstatement in original role;
- **Re-engagement** in alternative suitable role;
- **Subject to** practicality and parties' wishes.

### Specific orders

- **Specific performance** of equal-pay obligations;
- **Specific** orders to provide reasonable adjustments;
- **Specific** orders to remove discriminatory practices.

## Articles 64-65 — Appeals

- **Appeal to Royal Court** on point of law;
- **Specific** procedure;
- **Time limit**;
- **Further** appeals to Court of Appeal and Privy Council.

## JACS

The **Jersey Advisory and Conciliation Service** offers:

- **Pre-claim conciliation**;
- **Tribunal-stage conciliation**;
- **Settlement** facilitation;
- **Code** of practice on discrimination;
- **Training** for employers.

## Practical considerations

### For claimants

- **Document** the alleged discrimination;
- **Keep records** of incidents;
- **Engage JACS** pre-Tribunal;
- **Take legal advice** for substantial claims;
- **Time limits** are strict.

### For respondents

- **Investigate** complaints promptly;
- **Document** non-discriminatory reasons for decisions;
- **Engage** with conciliation;
- **Take advice** on settlement vs defending.

### For tribunals

- **Reasoned decisions** required;
- **Detailed** findings of fact;
- **Specific** application of burden-of-proof rules;
- **Subject to** appellate review.
