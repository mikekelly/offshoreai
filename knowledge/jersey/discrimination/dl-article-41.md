# DL 2013 Article 41 — Occupational Requirements

Article 41 provides for the **occupational requirement
exemption** — narrow circumstances where a protected
characteristic can be a job requirement.

## The provision

Direct discrimination may be lawful where:

- Having a particular protected characteristic is a **genuine
  and determining occupational requirement**;
- The requirement is a **proportionate means** of achieving
  a **legitimate aim**;
- **Specific** narrow contexts.

## Examples

### Sex-specific roles

- **Women's refuge** counsellor — female-only;
- **Specific** physical examination roles;
- **Specific** clothing-fitting models;
- **Subject to** narrow application.

### Religion-specific roles

- **Religious organisation** leadership in some
  configurations;
- **Specific** teachers in religious schools;
- **Subject to** specific Jersey rules.

### Nationality / national identity

- **Specific** national-identity-required roles;
- **Specific** language-required roles where the language
  is genuinely required (not as proxy for race);
- **Subject to** narrow application.

### Age-specific roles

- **Specific** age requirements where genuinely required;
- **Subject to** Article 43 (age) and Article 41 combined;
- **Specific** Jersey examples.

## Test

The Article 41 test:

### Genuine and determining

- **Substantive** requirement, not pretext;
- **Determining** — without the characteristic, the role
  cannot be performed;
- **Not** merely preferred or convenient.

### Proportionate means / legitimate aim

- **Legitimate aim** (business / regulatory necessity);
- **Proportionate means** — no less restrictive alternative;
- **Documented** business reason.

## Narrow application

The exemption is **narrowly construed**:

- **Not** a general license to discriminate;
- **Not** for general business preference;
- **Not** for customer preference (unless extreme contexts);
- **Specific** Jersey case law on application.

## Documentation

Employers relying on Article 41:

- **Documented** business reason;
- **Reviewed** periodically;
- **Specific** legal advice on application;
- **Audit trail** in case of challenge.

## Common misapplications

- **Customer preference** (e.g., "our customers prefer
  male sales staff") — generally **not** sufficient;
- **Convenience** of operation — generally **not** sufficient;
- **Assumptions** about ability — generally **not**
  sufficient.
