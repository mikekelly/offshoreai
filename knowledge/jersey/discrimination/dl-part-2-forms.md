# DL 2013 Part 2 — Forms of Discrimination (residual)

Articles 4 (direct / indirect) and 10 (reasonable
adjustments) have their own deep files. This cluster covers
Articles 5-9.

## Article 5 — Harassment

**Harassment** is unwanted conduct related to a protected
characteristic that:

- **Violates dignity**;
- **Creates** an intimidating, hostile, degrading,
  humiliating, or offensive environment.

### Forms of harassment

- **Sexual harassment** (related to sex);
- **Race harassment**;
- **Age harassment**;
- **Disability harassment**;
- **Other** protected characteristics.

### Conduct types

- **Verbal** (jokes, insults, comments);
- **Non-verbal** (gestures, displays of materials);
- **Physical** (unwanted touching, intimidation);
- **Online** (emails, social media, messaging);
- **Specific** sexualised conduct of any nature.

### Standard

- **Reasonable person** test for whether the environment is
  hostile etc.;
- **Subjective** perception of the claimant relevant;
- **Context** matters (workplace culture, professional
  setting);
- **One-off** incidents can be harassment if sufficiently
  serious.

## Article 6 — Victimisation

**Victimisation** = subjecting a person to detriment because
they have done a **protected act**:

### Protected acts

- **Bringing** proceedings under the DL;
- **Giving evidence or information** in proceedings;
- **Making** an allegation of discrimination;
- **Doing** anything else under the DL.

### Detriment

- **Subsequent** less-favourable treatment;
- **Refusal** to provide reference;
- **Disciplinary** action;
- **Specific** retaliation.

### Defence

- **Good faith** investigation;
- **No causation** by the protected act;
- **Reasonable** explanation for the detriment.

## Article 7 — Discrimination by association

A person can claim discrimination based on **association**
with a person having a protected characteristic:

- **Carer** of disabled person discriminated against;
- **Spouse** of person from minority race;
- **Specific** family / caring relationships.

The duty-holder discriminates against **the associate** even
if they don't personally have the characteristic.

## Article 8 — Discrimination by perception

A person can claim discrimination based on **perception** that
they have a characteristic:

- Treated as gay when actually heterosexual;
- Treated as disabled when not formally disabled;
- **Mistaken** perception still grounds the claim;
- **Specific** to the duty-holder's belief about the
  claimant.

## Article 9 — Instructing / causing discrimination

It is unlawful to:

- **Instruct** another to discriminate;
- **Cause** another to discriminate;
- **Induce** discrimination;
- **Subject to** liability for the instructor as well as the
  performer.

This addresses scenarios where a senior manager directs
discriminatory conduct by junior staff.
