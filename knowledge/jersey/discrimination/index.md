# Discrimination Law — Jersey

## What this section is about

This section covers **Jersey discrimination and equality law**
— protections against discrimination in employment, education,
services, and other areas of life.

The principal statute is the **Discrimination (Jersey) Law
2013 (DL)**, which has been **brought into force in phases**
since 2014, with **new protected characteristics added** over
time.

## Protected characteristics

As of 2026, the DL protects against discrimination on grounds
of:

- **Race** (added 2014);
- **Age** (added 2016);
- **Sex** (added 2015);
- **Sexual orientation** (added 2015);
- **Gender reassignment** (added 2015);
- **Pregnancy and maternity** (added 2015);
- **Disability** (added 2018);
- **Specific** other characteristics under amendment.

## Types of discrimination

The DL prohibits:

- **Direct discrimination**;
- **Indirect discrimination**;
- **Harassment**;
- **Victimisation**;
- **Failure to make reasonable adjustments** (disability);
- **Discrimination by association**;
- **Discrimination by perception**.

## Where it applies

The DL covers:

- **Employment** (recruitment, terms, dismissal,
  treatment);
- **Education**;
- **Provision of services** (commercial, professional, public);
- **Premises** (renting / selling);
- **Specific** public functions;
- **Limited** exemptions for specific contexts.
