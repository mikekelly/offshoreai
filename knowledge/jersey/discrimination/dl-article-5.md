# DL 2013 Article 5 — Harassment

Article 5 defines **harassment** as a form of unlawful
discrimination.

## Definition

**Harassment** is unwanted conduct related to a protected
characteristic that:

- **Violates the dignity** of the person; or
- **Creates** an intimidating, hostile, degrading,
  humiliating, or offensive environment.

## Forms

### Conduct types

- **Verbal** (jokes, insults, comments);
- **Non-verbal** (gestures, displays of materials);
- **Physical** (unwanted touching, intimidation);
- **Online** (emails, social media, messaging);
- **Specific** sexualised conduct of any nature.

### Related to characteristic

- **Direct** reference to a protected characteristic;
- **Stereotyping** based on characteristic;
- **Conduct** that the recipient perceives as related to
  their characteristic;
- **No** intent required — conduct can be harassment even if
  unintended.

## Standard

The standard is:

- **Reasonable person** in the position of the recipient;
- **Subjective perception** relevant but not decisive;
- **Context** matters (workplace, public, private);
- **One-off** incidents can be harassment if sufficiently
  serious.

## Sexual harassment

Specific category:

- **Unwanted conduct of a sexual nature**;
- **Specific** workplace focus;
- **Specific** #MeToo-era awareness;
- **Specific** Code of Practice for sexual harassment.

## Employer liability

The employer can be liable for:

- **Harassment by employees** (vicarious liability);
- **Harassment by third parties** (clients, customers) in
  some configurations;
- **Defence**: "all reasonable steps" to prevent the
  harassment.
