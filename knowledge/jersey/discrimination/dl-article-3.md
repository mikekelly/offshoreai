# DL 2013 Article 3 — Protected Characteristics

Article 3 establishes the **list of protected characteristics**
— added progressively by Order since 2014.

## The current list (as of 2026)

### Race

- Added **2014**;
- Covers **colour, nationality, ethnic or national origins**;
- **Specific** consideration of cultural / linguistic aspects;
- **Caste** in some contexts;
- **Most** discrimination claims are race-related.

### Sex

- Added **2015**;
- Covers **male and female** sex;
- **Specific** treatment of intersex persons;
- **Gender** characteristic separately covered.

### Sexual orientation

- Added **2015**;
- Covers **heterosexual, gay, lesbian, bisexual** orientation;
- **Subject to** specific exemptions for religious context
  (limited).

### Gender reassignment

- Added **2015**;
- Covers persons who **are proposing to undergo, are
  undergoing, or have undergone** gender reassignment;
- **Specific** protections in workplace and services.

### Pregnancy and maternity

- Added **2015**;
- Covers **pregnancy** and **maternity period** (typically 26
  weeks post-birth or duration of maternity leave);
- **Specific** workplace protections;
- **Automatic unfair dismissal** for pregnancy-related
  reasons.

### Age

- Added **2016**;
- Covers **any age**;
- **Direct** and **indirect** age discrimination;
- **Specific** justification defence broader than other
  characteristics (proportionate to legitimate aim);
- **Specific** exemptions for benefits, sports, occupational
  requirements.

### Disability

- Added **2018**;
- Covers **physical, mental, sensory** impairment with
  **substantial and long-term adverse effect** on day-to-day
  activities;
- **Reasonable adjustment** duty (Article 10);
- **Specific** discrimination types;
- **Wider** than direct discrimination concept.

## Order-based expansion

The DL is designed for **phased expansion**:

- **New characteristics** can be added by Minister's Order;
- **No** full statutory amendment required;
- **Specific implementing** rules per characteristic.

## Characteristics not currently protected

As of 2026, the following are **not** Jersey protected
characteristics:

- **Marriage / civil partnership**;
- **Religion or belief**;
- **Political opinion**;
- **Trade union membership**;
- **Socio-economic status**.

Some are protected indirectly through other characteristics
or through ECHR rights, but not specifically under DL.

## Multiple characteristics

A person may have **multiple** protected characteristics:

- **Intersectional** discrimination claims;
- **Each** characteristic considered;
- **Specific** to individual circumstances;
- **Compound** effects considered.

## Characteristic vs perception vs association

The DL protects:

- **Actual** protected characteristic of the claimant;
- **Perception** that the claimant has the characteristic
  (even if mistaken);
- **Association** with someone who has the characteristic
  (e.g., carer of disabled person).
