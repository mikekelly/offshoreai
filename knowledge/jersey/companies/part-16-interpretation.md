# Article 102 — Interpretation (Part 16)

Article 102 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** is the
**interpretation** Article for Part 16 (Accounts and Audits) —
defining key terms used throughout the Part.

## What Article 102 covers

Defines terms specific to Part 16, including:

- "**accounting records**";
- "**accounts**";
- "**financial period**";
- "**recognized auditor**";

— for the purposes of Part 16 on accounts and audits.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why these definitions matter

Part 16 is one of the more **technical** parts of the Companies
Law, and consistent terminology is essential:

- "**Accounting records**" — what the company must maintain
  day-to-day (Article 103);
- "**Accounts**" — the formal annual financial statements
  (Article 105);
- "**Financial period**" — the period covered by accounts (often
  but not always 12 months);
- "**Recognized auditor**" — a person registered on the JFSC
  Register of Recognized Auditors (Articles 110-112).

These terms thread through the rest of Part 16.
