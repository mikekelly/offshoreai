# Article 115B — Power of States to Amend Part 17

Article 115B of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** is the
**amendment-power** provision for Part 17 (Distributions). The
States may amend Part 17 by Regulations.

## What Article 115B says

The **States may by Regulations amend Part 17**, enabling
modifications to:

- **distribution rules**;
- **solvency-testing mechanisms**;

as commercial needs evolve.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

Distribution rules are an area where the **commercial /
regulatory balance** can shift over time:

- **OECD inclusive-framework** expectations may affect what
  distributions are considered legitimate;
- **Accounting and audit-standard** changes may need procedural
  updates;
- **Cross-border tax-driven** structures may need accommodation
  through Regulations.

The Article 115B power lets the States update Part 17 without
primary-law amendment.

## Family of amendment powers

One of several Part-specific amendment-power Articles — see also
Articles 2B, 17D, 40C, 59, 66A, 85A, 113H, 124A, 127GA, 127YN,
194A.
