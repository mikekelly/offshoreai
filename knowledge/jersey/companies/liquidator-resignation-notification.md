# Article 190 — Notification by Liquidator of Resignation, etc.

Article 190 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]**
requires the liquidator to **notify the registrar** of changes
in their office.

## What Article 190 covers

The liquidator must notify the registrar within prescribed time
of:

- **Resignation** from office;
- **Removal** by creditor or court action;
- **Other cessation** of office;
- Their **appointment** (if not already notified by another
  person).

Notice must be in **prescribed form** and accompanied by any
prescribed fees.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

The **public register** must reflect who is currently in office:

- **Counterparties** dealing with the company need to know who
  has authority;
- **Creditors** need to know whom to direct queries to;
- **Statutory notices** depend on accurate liquidator
  identification.

Article 190 ensures the register stays current.
