# Article 58C — Part 11 Redemption / Purchase Not a Reduction of Capital

Article 58C of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** is a
**procedural simplification**: redemption, purchase, or
cancellation under Part 11 is **not treated as a reduction of
capital** requiring Part 12 procedure.

## What Article 58C says

Share redemption, purchase, or cancellation transactions under
Part 11 **do not constitute a capital reduction** requiring **Part
12 court approval or solvency-statement procedure**.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

**Part 12** (Reduction of Capital, Articles 60–66A) imposes a
formal procedure including the
[Article 61A solvency-statement](./solvency-statement.md) or, in
the alternative, court confirmation. The procedure has real
operational cost.

Without Article 58C, every redemption or buyback would
theoretically trigger Part 12 procedure (because it effectively
reduces paid-up capital). That would make routine treasury
operations impractical.

Article 58C **carves out Part 11 transactions** from Part 12 —
companies can redeem, buy back, or cancel shares under the
**Part 11 streamlined regime** without invoking the Part 12
machinery.

## Practical effect

Routine operations that benefit from Article 58C:

- **Fund-vehicle redemptions** at NAV — many operations daily;
- **Treasury-share buybacks** under employee-share schemes;
- **Capital-return buybacks** for shareholder distributions;
- **Routine cancellations** of redeemed or bought-back shares.

All of these can proceed under the lighter Part 11 framework.

## When Part 12 still applies

Article 58C does not exclude Part 12 from operating in other
contexts:

- **Ordinary reductions of paid-up capital** without redemption
  or buyback;
- **Reductions of share-premium or stated-capital accounts**
  separate from share buyback;
- **Court-confirmed reductions** under Articles 62–64.

Where the company is reducing its capital base **other than**
through Part 11 mechanisms, Part 12 procedure applies — including
the solvency statement.

## Pitfalls

- **Confusing the two regimes** — Part 11 (redemption/buyback)
  is one regime; Part 12 (capital reduction) is another. Article
  58C only carves Part 11 out of Part 12; both regimes still
  exist for their respective contexts.
- **Mistaken solvency-statement omissions** — for a Part 12
  reduction, you do need the solvency statement. Article 58C
  doesn't relieve you of that.
