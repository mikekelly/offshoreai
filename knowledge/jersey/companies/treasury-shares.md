# Article 58A — Treasury Shares

Article 58A of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** permits a
Jersey company to hold redeemed or bought-back shares as
**treasury shares** rather than cancelling them. Treasury shares
sit on the company's own register but carry **no voting or
dividend rights**.

## What Article 58A says

A company may hold redeemed or purchased shares as **treasury
shares** rather than cancelling them.

Treasury shares:

- carry **no voting rights**;
- receive **no dividends**;
- may be **reissued, cancelled, or sold** later.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## How treasury shares work

When a company redeems (under [Article 55](./redeemable-shares.md))
or buys back (under [Article 57](./share-buyback.md)) its own
shares, it has two choices:

- **Cancel** the shares — they cease to exist and the issued
  share count drops;
- **Hold as treasury** — they remain in existence but are held by
  the company itself.

Treasury treatment offers operational flexibility:

- The company can **reissue** the treasury shares later without
  the cost and effort of creating new shares;
- Useful for **employee share schemes** — the company can repeat
  the buy/issue cycle as employees come and go;
- **Capital markets utility** — listed companies use treasury
  shares for share-based compensation, capital structure
  management.

## No voting, no dividends

While held as treasury:

- **No voting** — treasury shares carry no votes (preventing the
  company from effectively voting its own shares to entrench
  management);
- **No dividends** — treasury shares don't receive distributions
  (preventing the company from paying dividends to itself);
- **Counted for some purposes, not others** — treasury shares
  count toward the company's issued capital but are typically
  excluded from voting and distribution calculations.

## Subsequent disposal

The company may later:

- **Cancel** the treasury shares (reducing issued capital);
- **Sell or reissue** them (typically at market value, with the
  proceeds going to the company's capital account);
- **Use them** in employee-share-scheme operations.

Reissuing treasury shares is **not** a reduction of capital
issue — the shares continue to exist; they're just being placed
with a holder again.

## Limits

[Article 58B](./treasury-shares-limits.md) imposes **limits on the
number and nominal value** of shares that can be held as
treasury. Without limits, a company could theoretically buy back
its entire share base and hold itself, which would be absurd. The
limits prevent this.

## Not a reduction of capital

[Article 58C](./not-a-reduction.md) confirms that holding shares as
treasury is **not a reduction of capital** requiring Part 12
procedure. The shares continue to exist; only the holder has
changed.

## Pitfalls

- **Voting / dividend errors** — operational systems must
  exclude treasury shares from voting and dividend calculations.
- **Reissuance pricing** — treasury shares reissued at a discount
  to fair value can engage directors' duties and shareholder
  fairness concerns.
- **Limits compliance** — track treasury shares against the
  Article 58B thresholds.
