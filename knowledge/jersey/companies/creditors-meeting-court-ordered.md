# Article 160A — Meeting of Creditors Following Court-Ordered Creditors' Winding Up

Article 160A of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]**
covers the **creditors' meeting** following a **court-ordered**
creditors' winding up under Article 157C.

## What Article 160A covers

Where the winding up was commenced by court order, a creditors'
meeting must be convened within prescribed time (typically 14
days of the court order):

- The **provisional liquidator** (if any) or **court-appointed
  liquidator** convenes the meeting;
- Procedural mechanics largely mirror Article 160;
- Creditors may **confirm or replace** the court-appointed
  liquidator;
- The meeting **considers** the statement of affairs.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

Even in **court-ordered** winding up, **creditors retain
governance influence** through the first meeting. The court's
initial liquidator appointment is **provisional** in the sense
that creditors can change it.

This balances:

- **Court control** over commencement;
- **Creditor control** over conduct.
