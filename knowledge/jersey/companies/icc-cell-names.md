# Article 127YL — Names of Incorporated Cell Companies

Article 127YL of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** sets
out **naming requirements** for an ICC and for incorporated
cells.

## What Article 127YL covers

- The name of an **incorporated cell company (ICC)** must include
  the words **"Incorporated Cell Company"** (or the
  abbreviation **"ICC"**);
- The name of an **incorporated cell** must include words or
  abbreviation indicating that it is an incorporated cell of an
  identified ICC (often in a form like "[Cell Name] IC of [ICC
  Name] Limited" or equivalent);
- Naming is subject to the general **Article 13 requirements**
  for Jersey company names.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

The naming convention serves **counterparty-protection** and
public-register purposes:

- Anyone dealing with an "**ICC**" knows they are dealing with a
  structured umbrella entity, and that any cell of it has
  separate legal personality;
- Anyone dealing with an "**IC of [ICC]**" knows they are
  dealing with an incorporated cell — separate legal person
  with claims segregated to that cell's assets.

Compare with the **PCC naming** under Article 127YS — different
naming convention reflecting the different legal structure.
