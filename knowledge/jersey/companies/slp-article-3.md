# SLP Law 2011 Article 3 — Separate limited partnerships

**The load-bearing constitutional provision.** An SLP:

- Has **at least one GP and one LP**;
- Is a **legal person** but **NOT a body corporate**;
- May be formed for **any lawful purpose**.

## The intermediate position

The SLP sits between the LP 1994 (contractual, no separate legal personality) and the ILP 2011 (separate legal personality + body corporate):

| Vehicle | Separate legal person? | Body corporate? |
|---|---|---|
| LP 1994 ([`lp-article-3.md`](./lp-article-3.md)) | No | No |
| **SLP 2011 (this Article)** | **Yes** | **No** |
| ILP 2011 ([`ilp-article-3.md`](./ilp-article-3.md)) | Yes | Yes |

The SLP's "separate legal person without body-corporate character" is the **US-tax-classification-optimised** structure — see [`../use-cases/client-cohorts/us-person-abroad.md`](../use-cases/client-cohorts/us-person-abroad.md) for the US grantor / partnership-tax-classification rationale.
