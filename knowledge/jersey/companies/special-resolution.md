# Article 90 — Definition of Special Resolution

Article 90 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** defines
"**special resolution**" — the elevated-threshold member
resolution required for many of the Companies Law's most
significant decisions.

## What Article 90 says

A special resolution requires approval by:

> a **majority of not less than three-fourths** of such members
> as, being entitled to do so, **vote in person or, where proxies
> are allowed, by proxy**, at a meeting **of which notice
> specifying the intention to propose the resolution as a special
> resolution has been duly given**.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## The three-fourths majority

The threshold: **75% of votes cast** at a properly-noticed
meeting. Not:

- 75% of all members (a member who doesn't vote isn't counted);
- 75% of shares in issue (only voting members count);
- A simple majority (50% + 1 doesn't qualify).

## Notice requirement

A special resolution can only be passed at a meeting **of which
the notice specifies the intention** to propose the resolution
as a special resolution. The notice must:

- Be **duly given** under [Article 91](./notice-of-meetings.md);
- **Specify** that the resolution will be proposed as special;
- Include the **proposed text** of the resolution.

A resolution proposed at a meeting where notice didn't specify
its special-resolution character is **not validly passed** as
special even if 75% vote in favour.

## Where special resolution is required

Many of the Companies Law's most important member decisions
require special resolution:

- **Alteration of memorandum and articles** (Article 11);
- **Change of name** (Article 14);
- **Change of status** (public ↔ private) (Articles 16, 17);
- **Reduction of capital** (Article 61);
- **Variation of class rights** (Article 52 — though with a
  separate class-special-resolution at 75% of the class);
- **Mergers** under Part 18B;
- **Continuance** under Part 18C;
- **Winding-up** (members' winding-up under Part 21);
- Other matters specified in the deed or in specific Articles.

The **special resolution** is therefore one of the most-cited
concepts in Jersey company law.

## Written resolutions

A special resolution can also be passed as a **written
resolution** under [Article 95](./written-resolutions.md):

- All members entitled to vote (or per the deed) sign in writing;
- No meeting required;
- Threshold typically the same (75% — or unanimous, depending
  on the deed / Article 95 procedure used).

Modern Jersey private-company practice **heavily uses written
resolutions** for special-resolution business.

## Variations in the deed

The deed can:

- **Increase** the threshold (e.g., require 80% or unanimous
  approval) for specific matters;
- Add **class-specific** voting requirements alongside the
  general special resolution;
- Specify **quorum** requirements that effectively raise the
  threshold.

The deed cannot **reduce** the special-resolution threshold
below 75% — that's the statutory minimum.

## Pitfalls

- **Notice deficiency** — without specifying special-resolution
  character in the notice, the resolution isn't valid.
- **Calculating the 75%** — based on votes cast, not on members
  entitled to vote.
- **Class-rights interaction** — a special resolution affecting
  class rights also needs class-rights variation procedure.
