# Article 133 — Obstruction

Article 133 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** creates
the **offence of obstructing an inspector**.

## What Article 133 covers

A person commits an offence if the person:

- **Obstructs or hinders** an inspector in the execution of the
  inspector's functions;
- **Destroys, mutilates, defaces, falsifies or conceals**
  documents that are or might be relevant to the investigation;
- **Provides false information** to the inspector;
- Otherwise frustrates the investigation by deliberate acts.

Penalties include **imprisonment** and **fines** at significant
levels.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

The investigation regime has **no teeth** without serious
penalties for obstruction. Article 133 ensures that:

- **Document destruction** is a serious offence — not just a
  procedural nuisance;
- **Lying to inspectors** is criminal;
- **Physical obstruction** of search or examination is
  punishable.

Article 134 covers the **passive** form of non-cooperation
(failure to cooperate); Article 133 covers the **active** form
(obstruction and falsification).
