# Takeovers Panel Law 2009 Article 3 — Rule-making powers of the Panel

Article 3 is the **substantive engine** of the Law. It
empowers the Panel to make rules — the rules that form the
**Jersey Takeover Code** — and ties their substance to the
general principles articulated in the UK Companies Act 2006.

## Key provisions

- The Panel **may make rules**.
- The rules **may reflect general principles** derived from
  the Companies Act 2006 (UK).
- The rules can regulate **takeover bids**, **merger
  transactions**, and **related conduct** in connection with
  such transactions.
- Rule-making **about fees and charges** under Article 17
  must be done by the Panel itself or by a committee
  established by it.
- Other rule-making must be **done by a committee**
  established by the Panel.

## Why this matters

Article 3 is what makes the Jersey Panel's rules
**substantively coherent with the UK City Code**. By
permitting the Panel to reflect City-Code principles, the
Jersey regime mirrors the UK position for most operational
purposes — supporting the practical reality that the **UK
City Code applies to most Jersey-incorporated public
companies** by virtue of their UK market admission (see
[`../external-rules/uk-takeover-code.md`](../external-rules/uk-takeover-code.md)).

The Jersey Panel's rules fill the gaps where the UK Code
does not apply — Jersey-incorporated companies traded only
on a non-UK venue, for example.
