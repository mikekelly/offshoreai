# Takeovers Panel Law 2009 Article 1 — Interpretation

Article 1 sets out defined terms used throughout the Law.

## Defined terms

Key definitions include:

- **"City Code"** — the City Code on Takeovers and Mergers
  as published from time to time by the UK Panel on
  Takeovers and Mergers; the source of the general
  principles the Jersey Panel may adopt under
  [Article 3](./tmp-article-3.md).
- **"Commission"** — the
  [Jersey Financial Services Commission](../financial-regulation/jfsc.md).
- **"Companies Act 2006"** — the UK Companies Act 2006,
  the framework from which the Jersey Law substantively
  derives its substantive provisions.
- **"company"** — defined for the purposes of the Law in
  the manner consistent with the rules' scope.
- **"Court"** — the [Royal Court](../legal-system/royal-court.md).
- **"takeover bid"** — a public offer made to the holders
  of the securities of a company to acquire some or all of
  those securities, whether mandatory or voluntary, which
  follows or has as its objective the acquisition of
  control of that company.

## Why this matters

The "takeover bid" definition is the **scope-defining
provision** for the Law — it defines what transactions fall
within the Panel's rule-making and supervisory remit. The
"City Code" reference is the **substantive anchor** —
Article 3 permits the Panel to make rules reflecting general
principles from the City Code, importing the UK regulatory
substance into Jersey's domestic framework.
