# Article 213A — Recognition of Status of Foreign Corporations

Article 213A of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]**
provides for **recognition by Jersey law** of foreign corporations
— ensuring foreign companies can operate in Jersey and Jersey
courts can recognise their existence, capacity, and dissolution.

## What Article 213A covers

A body corporate incorporated under the law of any **foreign
jurisdiction** is:

- **Recognised** by Jersey law as having the **legal status** it
  has under the law of its place of incorporation;
- Has the **capacity** to contract, hold property, sue and be
  sued in Jersey to the same extent as that capacity exists
  under its home law;
- Is treated as **dissolved** in Jersey law when dissolved under
  its home law.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

Article 213A is a **private-international-law clarification**:

- It confirms the **lex incorporationis** principle — a foreign
  company's status is determined by its place of incorporation;
- It supports **cross-border commerce** with foreign companies
  doing business in Jersey;
- It makes **enforcement of foreign-company contracts** in Jersey
  straightforward;
- It avoids the need for **separate Jersey registration** for
  foreign companies that merely contract in Jersey (as opposed
  to establishing a place of business, which triggers external-
  company registration under Article 195).
