# Article 142 — Power for Minister or the Commission to Apply to Court

Article 142 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** extends
the unfair-prejudice remedy beyond individual members: the
**Minister** or the **JFSC** may apply to the Royal Court where
public-interest grounds are engaged.

## What Article 142 covers

The Minister or the JFSC may apply to the Royal Court for an
order under Article 143 on the same unfair-prejudice grounds as
in Article 141, where:

- The conduct affects **regulated companies** subject to JFSC
  supervision;
- The conduct affects **publicly held or market-traded
  companies** with broad shareholder constituencies;
- There is a **public-interest** dimension to the conduct beyond
  the individual prejudice to particular members;
- Members are **unable or unwilling** to bring proceedings
  themselves (e.g. controlled-company situations).

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

The individual member's right under Article 141 is **uneven** in
practice:

- Members in **widely held companies** may not have a clear
  representative;
- Members of **regulated entities** may not understand the
  technical regulatory dimension;
- The conduct may affect **future members** (e.g. structurally
  rigged share issuances) who don't yet have standing.

Article 142 gives the JFSC (and the Minister) a **public-
interest enforcement** route to address conduct that affects the
integrity of Jersey's company-law regime even where private
enforcement is weak.
