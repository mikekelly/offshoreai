# Companies — Jersey

## What this section is about

This section covers **Jersey corporate vehicles** — the legal
entities a person can use to hold assets, run a business, raise
capital, or structure a transaction under Jersey law. The headline
vehicle is the **Jersey company** under the
**[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]**; the section also covers
limited liability partnerships, the four kinds of limited
partnership, and cell-company structures (Protected Cell Company,
Incorporated Cell Company).

A Jersey company is structurally similar to an English limited
company but with **important divergences** worth knowing on landing:

- **No-par-value shares are the default** in modern practice
  ([Article 3F](no-par-value-companies.md)). Par-value still exists
  ([Article 3E](par-value-companies.md)) but the share-premium
  complications most English practitioners are used to don't apply
  to the modern Jersey vehicle.
- **Distributions are governed by a cash-flow solvency test**
  (Article 115, with the Article 61A solvency-statement mechanic),
  not by the English "profits available for distribution" test.
  Directors confirm solvency in writing; the distribution is then
  lawful.
- **Cell companies are first-class vehicles**: Part 18D of the
  Companies Law gives both PCC and ICC structures statutory
  ringfencing. See [`cell-companies.md`](cell-companies.md).
- **Continuance is statutory**: a Jersey company can be migrated
  in or out of Jersey under Part 18C without dissolving and
  re-forming.
- **Audit is not automatic for private companies**: the audit
  regime applies to public companies and certain regulated
  businesses; most private Jersey companies are not audit-required.

## How a typical Jersey company comes into existence

A short walk-through, anchor before the detail files:

1. **Choose a vehicle.** Almost always a **private no-par-value
   company limited by shares** under the Companies Law 1991. Use
   of LP, LLP, cell company, foundation, or trust is driven by
   commercial purpose.
2. **Engage a Jersey administered-service provider** — a
   JFSC-regulated trust company business (TCB) acting as
   registered agent / company secretary. In practice required for
   incorporation.
3. **Obtain JFSC consent to incorporate** under the
   [Control of Borrowing (Jersey) Order 1958][cob]. The
   application includes prospective shareholder, director, and
   beneficial-owner details.
4. **Incorporate.** Standard service is 24 hours; a 2-hour
   fast-track is available on premium fees.
5. **Run the company** within the Companies Law: meetings (Part
   15), accounts (Part 16), distributions only after passing the
   Article 61A solvency statement, directors' duties under
   Article 74.
6. **Wind up** by solvent members' winding-up (Part 21 Chapter 2)
   or, if insolvent, by creditors' winding-up or *désastre*.

The corresponding tax position for this lifecycle:
[`../tax/zero-ten.md`](../tax/zero-ten.md) — 0% by default, with
specified categories at 10% (financial-services companies under
Article 123D) or 20% (utilities, large retailers, hydrocarbon,
cannabis, Jersey-property income).
