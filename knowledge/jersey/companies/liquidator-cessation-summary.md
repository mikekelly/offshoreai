# Article 153 — Cesser of Office by Liquidator (Summary Winding Up)

Article 153 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]**
covers the **end of the liquidator's appointment** in a summary
winding up.

## What Article 153 covers

The liquidator's office ceases on:

- **Completion of the winding up** (final account filed,
  distributions made, dissolution effective);
- **Resignation** with prescribed notice;
- **Removal** by members' resolution (subject to Article 153
  procedure protections);
- **Death** of the liquidator;
- **Disqualification** under Article 188;
- **Court order** for removal (Article 175).

On cessation, the liquidator must:

- **Hand over** company records and remaining assets to a
  successor (or to the Court);
- **File final reports** as prescribed.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

Continuity of liquidator office is the **norm**, but Article 153
provides for orderly transitions where:

- The liquidator wishes to **retire** (e.g. for health, change of
  employment);
- Members lose confidence;
- A **dispute** about conduct requires removal.

The handover provisions ensure no loss of company information
during transitions.
