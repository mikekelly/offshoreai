# Article 177 — Responsibility of Persons for Wrongful Trading

Article 177 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** is one
of the **most powerful tools** in the insolvency regime: it
imposes **personal liability** on directors who knew (or ought to
have concluded) that the company could not avoid insolvent
liquidation, but continued trading.

## What Article 177 covers

If, in the course of a winding up, it appears that:

- The company has gone into insolvent winding up;
- At some time before commencement of winding up, the **director**
  (or shadow director) **knew or ought to have concluded** that
  there was **no reasonable prospect** of the company avoiding
  insolvent winding up;
- The director **did not take every step** that a reasonably
  diligent person should have taken to **minimise potential
  losses** to creditors;

the Royal Court may, on the liquidator's application, declare
that the director is **personally liable** to contribute to the
company's assets as the Court thinks fit.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

Wrongful trading liability is the **mechanism by which
directors are forced to face up to insolvency early**:

- Once "no reasonable prospect" is reached, directors must
  **act** — stop trading or take protective steps;
- Continuing to incur credit beyond that point **personally
  exposes** them;
- The standard is **objective** (what a reasonably diligent
  director should have concluded) — directors cannot rely on
  subjective optimism.

The article works alongside **fraudulent trading** (Article 178)
— wrongful trading covers negligent failure to stop, while
fraudulent trading covers deliberate carrying on with intent to
defraud creditors.

## The "every step" defence

Article 177 has an **important defence**: even where the
threshold is met, a director who **takes every step to minimise
loss** is not liable. Typical protective steps:

- Convening **early creditors' meetings**;
- **Stopping** new credit;
- **Consulting** insolvency professionals;
- **Documenting** the going-concern review;
- **Limiting** trading to that which preserves value.

The defence rewards **proactive engagement** with the deterioration.
