# Article 127YH — Incorporation of a Cell Independent of a Cell Company

Article 127YH of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]**
allows an **incorporated cell** to **separate from its ICC** and
continue as a **standalone Jersey company**.

## What Article 127YH covers

An incorporated cell may be **incorporated independently** of
its ICC by:

- **Application** to the registrar in prescribed form;
- **Amendment** of the cell's memorandum and articles to remove
  references to its being a cell of the ICC;
- **Approval** by members of both the cell and the ICC;
- Satisfaction of **creditor-protection** conditions (solvency,
  consents);
- **Registration** of the amended documents.

On registration:

- The cell **ceases** to be a cell of the ICC;
- The cell **continues** as a standalone Jersey company in its
  own right;
- The cell's **legal identity is unchanged** — it is the same
  legal person before and after (continuity).

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

This is a **commercially valuable** feature of the ICC structure:

- A **fund cell** may grow large enough to justify standalone
  status outside the umbrella;
- A **prospective acquirer** of a cell may want it as a
  standalone company rather than a cell;
- An ICC may want to **wind down** the cell structure while
  preserving the underlying cells as separate companies.

The continuity-of-legal-identity feature means **no transfer of
assets is needed** — the cell just stops being a cell and becomes
a standalone.
