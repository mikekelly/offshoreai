# Article 144A — Procedure: Winding Up of Other Companies of Limited Duration

Article 144A of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]**
extends the limited-duration winding-up regime to **other
companies of limited duration** beyond strictly "limited life"
companies under Article 3H.

## What Article 144A covers

Where a company's memorandum or articles provide for **automatic
termination** on some specified event (without the formal
"limited life company" designation), Article 144A applies a
similar automatic winding-up procedure on the occurrence of that
event:

- Winding-up **commences automatically**;
- Notice goes to the registrar;
- The applicable winding-up route (summary / creditors') is
  determined by solvency.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

This article handles the **edge cases** where a company has
duration-limiting provisions without being a formal "limited life
company". Practical examples:

- A company whose articles **require dissolution** on the
  occurrence of a specified event;
- A company with a **sunset clause** built into its
  constitutional documents.

Article 144A ensures these companies wind up automatically just
as a formal limited-life company would under Article 144.
