# Article 127YO — Interpretation (PCC Provisions)

Article 127YO of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** is
the **interpretation provision** for the **PCC-specific** part of
Part 18D (Articles 127YO–127YW).

## What Article 127YO covers

Key definitions for PCC purposes:

- **"Protected cell company" (PCC)** — a company whose
  memorandum / articles permit it to **create cells** which are
  **not separately incorporated** but whose assets and
  liabilities are segregated as a matter of statutory law;
- **"Cell"** (in PCC context) — a statutory compartment within
  the PCC, **not a separate legal person**;
- **"Cellular assets" / "cellular liabilities"** — assets and
  liabilities attributable to a specific cell;
- **"Non-cellular assets" / "non-cellular liabilities"** — assets
  and liabilities of the PCC not attributable to any cell (the
  "general account").

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

The **PCC vocabulary** is the foundation of the regime. The
critical distinction from the ICC is:

| Feature | ICC | PCC |
|---|---|---|
| Cell legal personality | Yes — each cell is a company | No — cell is a statutory compartment |
| Segregation source | Separate legal entity | Statutory operation (Article 127YT) |
| Contracting party | The cell | The PCC, acting on behalf of the cell |
| Naming | "X IC of Y ICC" | "Y PCC" with cells named within |

Article 127YO is the look-up for the PCC-specific articles.
