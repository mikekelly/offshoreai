# Article 162 — Appointment of Liquidation Committee

Article 162 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** provides
for the **liquidation committee** — a creditor-elected body that
oversees the liquidator.

## What Article 162 covers

The creditors may appoint a **liquidation committee** consisting
of:

- A specified maximum number of **creditor representatives**;
- Optionally, **member representatives** (in a typically smaller
  number);
- Drawn from those present and voting at the creditors' meeting.

The committee:

- **Oversees** the liquidator's conduct;
- **Approves** specific actions (e.g. sale of substantial assets,
  litigation funding decisions);
- **Sanctions** the liquidator's remuneration where required;
- **Receives** regular reports from the liquidator.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

The liquidation committee is the **standing creditor oversight
mechanism** between full creditors' meetings:

- Convening a full meeting is **expensive and slow**;
- Day-to-day decisions need **timely creditor input**;
- The committee provides **proportionate governance**.

In complex liquidations, the committee plays a significant role
in **strategic decisions**: which claims to pursue, how to
litigate, when to settle.
