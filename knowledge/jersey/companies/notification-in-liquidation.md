# Article 191 — Notification That Company is in Liquidation

Article 191 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]**
requires the **company's stationery and identifying documents**
to indicate that the company is in liquidation.

## What Article 191 covers

While a company is in winding up, every:

- **Invoice**, **letter**, **business document**;
- **Order form**, **website**, and **email signature**;
- **Public-facing identifying material**;

issued by or on behalf of the company must contain a statement
that the company is in **liquidation**.

The statement typically reads "in liquidation" or "(in
liquidation)" after the company name.

Failure to comply is an **offence** for which the liquidator and
others may be personally liable.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

The "in liquidation" identifier is a **counterparty warning**:

- Suppliers know **not to extend credit** in the ordinary way;
- Customers know the company may be **unable to deliver**;
- Counterparties consider **withdrawal of facilities**.

Without the identifier, the liquidator could inadvertently
cause harm by appearing to trade normally when the company is
in wind-down.
