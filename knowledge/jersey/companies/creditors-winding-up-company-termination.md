# Article 157D — Company's Application to Terminate Creditors' Winding Up

Article 157D of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** lets
the company **apply to the Royal Court** to terminate a
creditors' winding up.

## What Article 157D covers

The company (acting through its directors with appropriate
authority) may apply to the Royal Court for an order terminating
the creditors' winding up where:

- The company's affairs have been **restructured** to permit
  continued operation (e.g. through a creditor compromise);
- A **rescue financing** has been arranged;
- The **basis for the winding up has fallen away** (e.g. the
  applying creditor's claim has been satisfied);
- It is otherwise **just and equitable** to terminate.

The Court will consider:

- **Creditor consent** (typically required);
- **Solvency** of the restructured company going forward;
- **Continuing risk** to creditors generally.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

A creditors' winding up is not always **terminal**. Companies
sometimes:

- Negotiate **standstills** and compromises with creditors
  during the winding up;
- Obtain **new financing** that pays out creditors and restores
  solvency;
- Are **sold as a going concern** to a buyer who refinances them.

Article 157D allows the procedure to **reverse out** of
liquidation in these cases, restoring the company to active
status.
