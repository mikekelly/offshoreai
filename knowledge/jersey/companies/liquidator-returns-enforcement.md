# Article 187 — Enforcement of Liquidator's Duty to Make Returns

Article 187 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** lets
the Royal Court enforce the **liquidator's duty to file
returns** with the registrar and to comply with other
procedural obligations.

## What Article 187 covers

If the liquidator fails to make returns or comply with
prescribed obligations within prescribed time:

- The **registrar** or any creditor / member may apply to the
  Court;
- The Court may make orders requiring **compliance**;
- The Court may **impose penalties**;
- The Court may **remove** the liquidator for persistent
  non-compliance.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

The **public register** depends on liquidator filings being
current. Without enforcement:

- Stale records persist after the winding up should have ended;
- Creditors cannot search for current status;
- Member confidence in the insolvency regime erodes.

Article 187 is the **enforcement teeth** behind the procedural
duties.
