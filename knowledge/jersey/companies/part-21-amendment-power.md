# Article 194A — Power of States to Amend Part 21

Article 194A of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** gives
the **States of Jersey** power to **amend Part 21** (Winding
Up) by Regulations.

## What Article 194A covers

The States may by Regulations amend Part 21, including:

- **Adding or modifying** chapters;
- **Adjusting** claw-back periods and thresholds;
- **Updating** procedural mechanics;
- **Calibrating** the regime against international developments
  in insolvency law (UNCITRAL Model Law, cross-border insolvency,
  etc.);
- **Adjusting** the relationship with the désastre regime.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

Insolvency law is **fast-evolving**, with significant international
developments in:

- **Cross-border insolvency** (UNCITRAL Model Law, Hague
  Judgments Convention);
- **Restructuring tools** (UK Part 26A, EU Restructuring
  Directive);
- **Director liability** (e.g. UK CIGA 2020 reforms).

Article 194A allows Jersey to **keep pace** through Regulations
without need for full primary legislation each time.
