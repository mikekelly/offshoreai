# Article 57 — Power of Company to Purchase Its Own Limited Shares (Buyback)

Article 57 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** permits a
**limited company** to **purchase its own shares** — a share
buyback. This is structurally distinct from redemption under
Article 55 because the shares need not be issued as redeemable
in the first place.

## What Article 57 says

A **limited company** may purchase its own shares if:

- **authorised by its articles**;
- the shares are **fully paid** before acquisition.

Purchase **terms** (price, timing, conditions) are set by the
articles or a **director resolution**.

The company may **hold purchased shares as treasury shares**
under [Article 58A](treasury-shares.md) or **cancel** them.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Buyback vs redemption

Redemption ([Article 55](redeemable-shares.md)) and buyback
(Article 57) are similar but distinct:

| Feature | Redemption (Art 55) | Buyback (Art 57) |
|---|---|---|
| Original share class | Issued as redeemable | Any limited shares |
| Trigger | Per redemption terms in articles | At company's election under Article 57 framework |
| Terms | Set at issuance | Set at buyback (within articles) |
| Mandatory aspect | Often mandatory at trigger | Always discretionary |
| Common use | Fund-vehicle exits | Capital returns, employee-share-scheme operations |

## Why this matters

Buyback was **historically restricted** by common-law rules
against companies dealing in their own shares. Article 57 enables
buyback as a modern corporate-finance tool:

- **Capital returns** to shareholders without paying ordinary
  dividends;
- **Employee-share-scheme operations** where the company buys
  back vested shares;
- **Restructuring** where a group simplifies by repurchasing
  minority holdings;
- **Defensive measures** in takeover contexts.

## Conditions

The buyback must satisfy:

- **Articles authorisation** — the deed must permit buyback (most
  modern Jersey articles include this);
- **Fully-paid requirement** — only fully-paid shares can be
  bought back;
- **Director-resolution or articles-specified terms** — the
  buyback price and procedure must follow the deed;
- **Funding** — buybacks typically funded from distributable
  profits, share-premium account (subject to specific rules), or
  fresh-issue proceeds.

## Treasury treatment

Same as redemption — bought-back shares can be:

- **Cancelled** (extinguishing the shares), or
- **Held as treasury shares** under
  [Article 58A](treasury-shares.md).

Treasury treatment lets the company **re-issue** the shares
later, avoiding the cost of cancellation and re-creation.

## Not a capital reduction

[Article 58C](not-a-reduction.md) provides that buybacks under
Part 11 are **not treated as reductions of capital** requiring
Part 12 procedure. This is a significant operational simplification.

## Pitfalls

- **Articles authority missing or unclear** — older Jersey
  articles may not authorise buyback; check the deed.
- **Buyback above market value** — exposes directors to breach-
  of-duty claims; pricing should be reasonable.
- **Cross-border tax** — buyback proceeds may be characterised
  as dividend or capital in the holder's home jurisdiction;
  pre-model.
