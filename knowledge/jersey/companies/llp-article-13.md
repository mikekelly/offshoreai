# LLP Law 2017 Article 13 — Relations of partners to one another

Article 13 makes the **partnership agreement** the primary
source of inter-partner rights and duties — while preserving
the limited-liability benefit.

## Key provisions

- The **partnership agreement** determines the rights,
  duties, and obligations of partners as between themselves;
- The limited-liability benefit applies regardless of the
  partnership agreement's terms — it cannot be contracted
  away as between partners and third parties.

## Operational significance

The partnership agreement is the **operational
constitution** of a Jersey LLP. It typically covers:

- Profit-sharing arrangements;
- Capital contributions and withdrawals;
- Admission and retirement of partners;
- Decision-making and voting;
- Dispute resolution between partners.

The Law's default position is light — most of the
operational content lives in the partnership agreement.
