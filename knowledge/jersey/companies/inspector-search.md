# Article 132 — Authority for Search

Article 132 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** allows
the **Royal Court** to authorise **search of premises** in
connection with a Part 19 investigation.

## What Article 132 covers

On application by an inspector (or other authorised person), the
Royal Court may issue a **search warrant** authorising entry
onto and search of premises where:

- Records or evidence relevant to the investigation are
  reasonably believed to be located;
- Voluntary production has been refused or would defeat the
  purpose of the search;
- Other prescribed conditions are met.

The warrant typically authorises:

- **Entry** onto specified premises (with reasonable force if
  needed);
- **Search** of the premises;
- **Seizure** of documents or other items relevant to the
  investigation;
- **Securing** electronic records;
- Use of **police assistance**.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

Voluntary production assumes cooperation. Where:

- A subject has **destroyed or hidden** records;
- A subject has **fled the jurisdiction** but documents remain;
- Premises are **occupied by uncooperative third parties**;

the search warrant is the **last-resort enforcement** tool. It
brings physical force behind the inspector's powers and makes
investigation possible where it would otherwise be defeated.

## Pitfalls

- **Privilege** — searches must respect legal professional
  privilege; protocols typically segregate apparently-privileged
  materials for later determination.
- **Foreign premises** — the warrant operates in Jersey;
  searches of foreign premises require letters rogatory or
  equivalent international cooperation.
