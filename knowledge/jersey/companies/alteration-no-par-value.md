# Article 38A — Alteration of Capital (No-Par-Value Companies)

Article 38A of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** is the
**no-par-value equivalent** of [Article 38](./alteration-par-value.md).
It permits a no-par-value company to alter its share capital.

## What Article 38A permits

A no-par-value company may, by shareholder resolution:

- **Increase** authorised capital (where the deed specifies a
  share-count limit);
- **Decrease** capital;
- **Consolidate** or **sub-divide** shares;
- **Convert** shares between classes (subject to class-rights
  protections under Articles 52–54).

Changes must be **documented and registered**.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why no-par-value is operationally simpler

Compared to par-value alteration under
[Article 38](./alteration-par-value.md):

- No **par-value adjustment** complications when consolidating or
  sub-dividing — the share count changes but there's no per-share
  nominal value to recalculate;
- No **share-premium account** mechanics — the
  [stated-capital account](./stated-capital.md) is used instead and
  is more flexible;
- **Currency conversions** (under
  [Article 38B](./currency-conversion-rate.md)) are
  straightforward — the stated capital account can hold
  multi-currency amounts.

This is one of the reasons no-par-value is the modern default for
Jersey company incorporations.

## Substantive reduction — Part 12 still applies

Same caveat as for Article 38: substantive reductions of paid-up
capital are governed by **Part 12**, including the
**Article 61A solvency-statement** mechanic. Article 38A is the
operational route for **alterations**; Part 12 is the substantive
constraint when reducing.

## Pitfalls

- **Confusing share-count alteration with capital reduction** —
  reducing the share count without reducing the underlying
  stated capital is one thing; reducing the stated capital
  itself is another (Part 12).
- **Class-rights effects** — even no-par-value alterations can
  trigger class-rights variation procedures if different
  classes are affected differently.
