# Takeovers Panel Law 2009 Article 5 — Rulings

Article 5 empowers the Panel to **interpret and apply rules**
on specific facts — issuing **rulings** that have binding
effect to the extent and in the circumstances specified in
the rules.

## Key provisions

- The Panel **may rule** on the interpretation, application,
  or effect of rules.
- "**To the extent and in the circumstances specified in
  rules, subject to any review or appeal, a ruling has
  binding effect.**"

## Operational significance

Rulings are the **case-by-case mechanism** by which the
Panel applies the principles-based Code to specific
transactions. In UK Panel practice (mirrored in Jersey
Panel practice given the close substantive alignment),
rulings:

- Come at the **request of parties** to a transaction (or
  on the Panel's own motion);
- Address specific applications of rules to specific facts;
- Are binding on the parties subject to the review / appeal
  mechanism in [Article 12](./tmp-article-12.md);
- Form the **practical body of case-law** alongside the
  written rules.
