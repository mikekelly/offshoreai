# Article 99 — Inspection of Minute Books

Article 99 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** gives
**members** the right to inspect **meeting minutes** held by the
company.

## What Article 99 covers

**Members have inspection rights** regarding meeting minutes.

The company must **make these records available** for **member
examination** under specified conditions.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## What members can inspect

The Article 99 right typically extends to:

- **Minutes of general meetings** of members;
- **Resolutions** passed by members (whether at meetings or in
  writing);
- Connected **supporting records**.

It typically does **not** extend to:

- **Board minutes** (these are confidential to the board
  subject to specific deed provisions);
- **Committee minutes** for specific sub-board committees;
- **Confidential trading or commercial** records.

## Procedure

Similar to [Article 45 register inspection](./register-inspection.md):

- **Reasonable notice** to the company;
- **Inspection during business hours**;
- **Nominal fee** for copies if requested;
- **At the registered office** typically.

## Pitfalls

- **Refusing inspection without justification** — invites
  enforcement and Court applications.
- **Confusing member-meeting minutes with board minutes** — the
  inspection right is narrower than members sometimes assume.
