# Takeovers Panel Law 2009 Article 2 — Appointment of body to oversee takeovers

Article 2 is the **constitutional foundation** of the Jersey
Panel. It empowers the Minister to appoint, by Order, a body
to exercise specified functions under the Law. That body
becomes the **"Panel on Takeovers and Mergers"** for the
purposes of the Law.

## Key provisions

- The **Minister may appoint a body by Order** to act as
  the Panel.
- The appointed body **becomes "the Panel on Takeovers
  and Mergers"** for the purposes of this Law and the
  rules made under it.
- The Panel **may undertake any acts necessary or
  expedient** for the discharge of its functions.
- The Panel **may delegate** the discharge of its
  functions to committees, officers, or staff appointed by
  it.

## Operational reality

In practice, the **UK Panel on Takeovers and Mergers** has
historically been appointed to act as the Jersey Panel —
unifying the supervisory regime for transactions that
straddle the UK / Jersey jurisdictional boundary. The
exact current appointment should be verified against the
Order in force at the time of any specific transaction.
