# Part 19 — Investigations (Overview)

Part 19 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** is the
**statutory investigation regime** — the JFSC's tool of last
resort for uncovering serious corporate wrongdoing where the
normal corporate-governance mechanisms have failed.

## When Part 19 is used

- **Suspected fraud** by directors or management;
- **Material misstatements** of financial position not caught by
  audit;
- **Oppression of minorities** of a kind not adequately addressed
  by Part 20 unfair-prejudice proceedings;
- **Regulatory concerns** that warrant deeper scrutiny than
  ordinary supervision;
- **Members' formal request** for investigation backed by
  prescribed support thresholds.

## Headline features

- **Inspector-led** — independent professionals (typically
  forensic accountants and lawyers) conduct the investigation
  with statutory powers;
- **Compulsion powers** — production of documents, examination
  on oath, search of premises with court authority;
- **Criminal penalties** for obstruction and non-cooperation;
- **Reports admissible as evidence** in subsequent civil,
  criminal, and regulatory proceedings;
- **Privilege preserved** — legal professional privilege survives
  the compulsion powers.
