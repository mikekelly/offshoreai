# Article 88 — Commission's Power to Call Meeting in Default

Article 88 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** lets the
**JFSC** direct a company to convene a meeting where the company
has defaulted in holding a required meeting.

## What Article 88 says

If a company **defaults** in holding required meetings, the
**JFSC** may, **upon application**, direct the company to
**convene a meeting**.

The Commission may **appoint a person to preside** at the meeting.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## When this is used

The JFSC power is exercised where:

- A company has **failed to hold** an AGM or other required
  meeting;
- A **member or other interested party** has applied to the
  Commission;
- The Commission considers it **appropriate** to direct a
  meeting.

In practice the JFSC's power runs in parallel with the Royal
Court's power under [Article 94](court-ordered-meetings.md) —
applicants can typically choose which forum to approach.

## Practical application

The power is rarely invoked because:

- Most Jersey companies **don't default** on meeting
  requirements;
- Where defaults occur, **negotiation** between members and
  directors usually resolves the issue without regulator
  involvement;
- Directors aware of a default typically convene a meeting
  voluntarily once notified.

The JFSC's power is the **backstop** for situations where
management actively obstructs proper governance.
