# Article 115 — Restrictions on Distributions

**Article 115** of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** is
the **distribution-restriction** rule and the **second pillar**
(with Article 61A) of Jersey's distinctive **cash-flow-solvency**
approach to capital management. A company may only make a
distribution if its directors confirm the company's solvency.

## What Article 115 says

A company may only make distributions if:

1. The company is **solvent**, demonstrated through a **solvency
   statement**; and
2. **Post-distribution**, the company **remains able to pay debts
   as they become due**.

The solvency-assessment mechanism **parallels
[Article 61A](solvency-statement.md)'s** framework for capital
reductions.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## The two-limb test

For any distribution:

### Limb 1 — solvency at the time

The company must be **solvent at the time of the distribution**.
Solvency here is the same **cash-flow** standard as Article 61A —
the company can pay its debts as they fall due.

### Limb 2 — solvency post-distribution

The directors must confirm that **after the distribution**, the
company **remains able to pay debts as they become due**. This is
a **forward-looking** assessment:

- **Existing debts** and their maturities;
- **Contingent and prospective** liabilities;
- **Operating cash-flow** post-distribution;
- The **direct effect** of the distribution itself on liquidity.

The horizon is typically **12 months minimum**, mirroring the
Article 61A practice.

## The solvency statement

The mechanism is parallel to
[Article 61A](solvency-statement.md):

- **All directors** sign;
- **Statutory declaration** typically accompanies;
- **Personal liability** for false statements;
- **Filed** records support the audit trail.

## The Jersey distinctive — cash-flow not profits

Jersey's reliance on **cash-flow solvency** rather than English-
style **profits-available-for-distribution** is the **defining
feature** of Jersey corporate distributions:

| Test | Jersey (Article 115) | English (CA 2006 s.830) |
|---|---|---|
| Standard | Cash-flow ability to pay debts | Accumulated realised profits less realised losses |
| Time horizon | Forward-looking (typically 12 months) | Backward-looking accounting position |
| Loss-making company | Can distribute if cash-flow solvent | Cannot distribute (no profits available) |
| Newly-formed company | Can distribute if cash-flow solvent | Often cannot (no accumulated profits yet) |
| Group restructuring flexibility | High | Lower |

This is one of the **most-cited reasons** for using Jersey as a
TopCo jurisdiction in cross-border groups — Jersey TopCos can
**distribute on a cash-flow basis** in scenarios where an English
TopCo would be blocked.

## What types of distribution

Article 115 applies to any "distribution" within the
[Article 114](distribution-definition.md) definition:

- **Cash dividends**;
- **In-specie distributions**;
- **Buyback payments** (subject to specific Part 11 carve-outs);
- **Capital reductions** that return value (subject to Part 12
  parallel procedures);
- **Other transfers** to members.

## Unlawful distributions

A distribution made **without** the solvency statement, or where
the statement is **knowingly false**, is **unlawful** under
[Article 115A](unlawful-distribution-consequences.md):

- **Members must repay** the unlawful distribution;
- **Directors face personal liability**;
- The distribution may be **unwound** by the court.

## Court order — Article 115ZA

[Article 115ZA](distribution-retrospective-order.md) provides for
a **court order treating a distribution as made in accordance
with Article 115** — useful for **rectifying** technical
deficiencies in the procedural compliance with the solvency-
statement framework where the substantive solvency position was
satisfied.

## Pitfalls

- **Inadequate cash-flow analysis** — a balance-sheet-only
  review is not the Jersey test. Look at future cash flow.
- **Contingent-liability blind spots** — pending litigation,
  guarantee exposures, tax assessments. Account for these.
- **Reluctant-director signatures** — all directors must
  genuinely assess and sign. Pressure-signing creates personal
  exposure.
- **Treating ad-hoc payments as non-distributions** — payments
  to members in their member capacity (e.g., loans without
  commercial terms, gratuitous transfers) are distributions in
  substance.
