# Article 196 — Registrar and Other Officers

Article 196 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]**
establishes the **office of the Registrar of Companies** for
Jersey.

## What Article 196 covers

- The **Registrar of Companies** is the principal office
  responsible for the public register and the administration of
  the Companies Law;
- The Registrar is **appointed** by (or within) the JFSC under
  the JFSC's governing legislation;
- The Registrar may be **assisted** by deputy registrars and
  staff;
- The Registrar acts under the **general supervision** of the
  JFSC.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

The Registrar is the **gatekeeper of corporate existence**:

- Companies come into existence by registration;
- Public information about companies depends on the Registrar's
  records;
- Statutory filings (annual confirmation statements, accounts,
  director changes) flow through the Registrar.

The institutional integration with the JFSC reflects the
**regulatory dimension** of the Registrar's role — companies
formation is not just clerical record-keeping but a public-
interest function.
