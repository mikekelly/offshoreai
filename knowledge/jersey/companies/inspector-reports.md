# Article 135 — Inspectors' Reports

Article 135 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** sets
out **how inspectors report** on the conclusion of an
investigation.

## What Article 135 covers

Inspectors must:

- **Submit a final report** to the JFSC (or other appointing
  authority) on completion of the investigation;
- **Set out findings** of fact, conclusions, and recommendations
  in the report;
- **Submit interim reports** where the JFSC requires (e.g. on
  matters demanding urgent action);
- **Identify** persons who have been the subject of adverse
  findings, with **fair opportunity** to respond (in line with
  procedural-fairness principles).

The JFSC may:

- **Publish** the report (in whole or in part), having regard to
  the public interest and the rights of identified persons;
- **Use the report** as the basis for regulatory action,
  prosecution referrals, or civil proceedings;
- **Forward the report** to other authorities (police, foreign
  regulators, professional bodies).

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

The inspector's report is the **end product** of the
investigation. Its quality, fairness, and admissibility (Article
138) determine whether the investigation achieves its purposes:

- **Regulatory action** against bad actors;
- **Criminal prosecution** for offences;
- **Civil recovery** by victims;
- **Lessons learned** for future supervision.

The "fair opportunity to respond" element matters: inspectors'
adverse findings can be **reputationally devastating**, and
procedural fairness is essential to legitimacy.
