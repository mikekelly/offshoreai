# Article 195 — Power to Make Regulations as to External Companies

Article 195 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** is the
**entirety of Part 22** — a single delegated power to make
Regulations governing **external companies** (foreign companies
operating in Jersey).

## What Article 195 covers

The States may by Regulations provide for the **registration
and regulation** of external companies — i.e. companies
incorporated outside Jersey but with a **place of business** or
other connection within Jersey requiring local regulatory
oversight.

The Regulations typically prescribe:

- **Registration requirements** — companies establishing in
  Jersey must register;
- **Filing obligations** — annual returns, accounts (where
  applicable), changes in directors/registered office;
- **Naming rules** for trading in Jersey;
- **Compliance** with Jersey financial-services and AML rules;
- **Service of process** mechanisms;
- **Strike-off** for non-compliant external companies;
- **Application** of other Companies Law provisions (e.g.
  investigations under Article 140) to external companies.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl] and the
relevant Regulations.)

## Why this matters

Jersey has been the **base of operations** for many foreign
companies — particularly investment funds, holding companies, and
financial-services structures that incorporate elsewhere but
have **economic substance** in Jersey.

Without external-company regulation:

- Such companies would **escape Jersey oversight** despite
  operating here;
- Local **counterparties** would have no public record of who
  they are dealing with;
- **Service of process** in Jersey courts would be difficult.

Article 195 (and the implementing Regulations) close this gap.
