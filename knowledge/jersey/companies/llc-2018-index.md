# Limited Liability Companies (Jersey) Law 2018 — Index

The **Limited Liability Companies (Jersey) Law 2018** creates
the Jersey **Limited Liability Company (LLC)** — a Jersey
**body corporate** with **flexible internal economics** modeled
substantively on the **Delaware LLC**.

The LLC is Jersey's response to global demand for Delaware-LLC-
equivalent vehicles. It sits alongside the
[Cayman LLC](../../cayman/cayman-llc.md) (introduced under the
Cayman LLC Act 2016) and is conceptually similar.

This file is the structural entry point. Per-Article files are
scaffolded as stubs.

## How the Jersey LLC differs from the Jersey company

A Jersey company under
[CJL 1991](./companies-law-1991.md) has:

- **Shareholders** with prescribed rights;
- **Directors** with fiduciary duties under Article 74;
- **Share capital** structured around par-value or no-par-value
  shares;
- **A board / management** structure aligned with the
  shareholders' rights.

A Jersey LLC has:

- **Members** (not shareholders) with rights set by the **LLC
  agreement** — flexible economic and governance terms;
- **Managers** (or member-management) where management
  structure is similarly flexible;
- **Members' interests** that are not "shares" — economic
  rights, voting rights, and rights on liquidation are
  set by the LLC agreement;
- A **body-corporate character** with limited liability for
  members.

The flexibility of the LLC agreement is the **principal draw**.
Where the Delaware-LLC pattern is the comparable mental model,
the Jersey LLC is the equivalent vehicle without needing US
incorporation.

## Use cases

The Jersey LLC is typically chosen where:

- The structurer wants **Delaware-LLC-like flexibility** in
  the operating agreement;
- The structure has **US connections** that would benefit from
  LLC familiarity;
- **Bespoke economic arrangements** between participants are
  needed beyond what CJL 1991 share structures permit;
- The structure is being used as a **fund vehicle** where the
  LLC operating-agreement flexibility supports tailored
  investor terms (less common than LP but increasing).

The Jersey LLC has been adopted more slowly than the Cayman
LLC because (a) the Cayman LLC has first-mover-advantage in
the global market, and (b) Jersey already has a deep
LP / ILP / SLP / company toolkit for most structuring needs.
For specific cases where the LLC's flexibility is decisive,
the Jersey LLC is the right choice.
