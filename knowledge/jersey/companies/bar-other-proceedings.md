# Article 193 — Bar Against Other Proceedings in Bankruptcy

Article 193 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]**
prevents **parallel insolvency proceedings** — once a company is
in winding up under Part 21, désastre cannot be commenced (and
vice versa) save under the bridging articles.

## What Article 193 covers

A company that is in:

- **Summary winding up** under Chapter 2;
- **Just-and-equitable winding up** under Chapter 3;
- **Creditors' winding up** under Chapter 4;

may not be subject to **separate désastre** proceedings under
the Bankruptcy (Désastre) (Jersey) Law 1990, except via the
bridging articles (154A or 185B).

Conversely, a company in désastre cannot be put into winding up
under Part 21 in parallel.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

Parallel insolvency proceedings create **chaos**:

- **Multiple officeholders** with competing claims to control;
- **Inconsistent treatment** of creditors;
- **Wasted costs** of duplicated effort;
- **Conflict of laws** between two regimes within the same
  jurisdiction.

Article 193 channels insolvency proceedings into a **single
regime per company**. The bridging articles allow transitions
between regimes but **not concurrent** proceedings.
