# LP Law 1994 Article 3 — Limited partnership

Article 3 is the **constitutional foundation** — permits
formation of LPs for any lawful purpose with the prescribed
partner structure.

## Provision

An LP may be formed for any lawful purpose. The LP must
have:

- **One or more general partners** (with unlimited
  liability for LP obligations); and
- **One or more limited partners** (with liability capped
  at capital contributed, subject to safe harbours).

No upper limit on either category.

## Operational significance

The "**at least one of each**" requirement is structural —
an entity without a GP is not an LP; an entity without an
LP partner is not an LP. The "any lawful purpose" framing
is what enables the LP's wide use across PE / VC funds,
family-office investment partnerships, carry vehicles,
co-investment vehicles, and asset-holding structures.
