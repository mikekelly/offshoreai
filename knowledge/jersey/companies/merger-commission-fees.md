# Article 127FG — Fees, Expenses and Security

Article 127FG of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]**
covers the **financial mechanics** of obtaining JFSC consent for a
cross-border merger under Article 127FF: fees payable, recovery
of expenses, and security for those expenses.

## What Article 127FG covers

In connection with a JFSC consent application under Article 127FF,
the Commission may:

- Charge **prescribed fees** for processing the application;
- **Recover its expenses** (including the cost of any external
  advisors it engages);
- Require **security** for fees and expenses in advance.

The Commission's fees and expenses are recoverable from the
applicant **as a civil debt**.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

Cross-border merger applications can involve **substantial JFSC
work** — review of foreign law, due diligence on the foreign
body, structural review of the merger. Article 127FG ensures the
**JFSC is fully reimbursed** so that the cost falls on the
applicant rather than on Jersey taxpayers.

The **security** requirement protects the JFSC against
applicants who might walk away if the answer is unfavourable.
