# Article 113B — Auditor's Duties and Powers

Article 113B of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** sets
out the **duties and powers** of an auditor in conducting an
audit of a Jersey company.

## What Article 113B covers

Auditors possess:

- **rights to access** company records;
- the right to **obtain explanations** from officers.

Auditors must:

- **plan audits** to detect material misstatements from
  **fraud or error**;
- **report significant findings** to **management** and **those
  charged with governance**.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Rights of access

The auditor has **statutory access** to:

- All **accounting records**;
- **Supporting documentation**;
- **Officer explanations** of records and decisions;
- **Information** from group-related parties;
- **Third-party documents** relevant to the audit.

Refusal to give the auditor access is an offence under
[Article 113C](false-statements-to-auditors.md).

## Audit planning

The auditor's duty: **plan and conduct** the audit to detect:

- **Fraud** — intentional misstatement or theft;
- **Error** — unintentional but material inaccuracy;
- **Going-concern** issues;
- **Disclosure** failures.

The audit is **risk-based** — the auditor designs procedures
proportionate to the risks identified.

## Reporting

The auditor reports significant findings to:

- **Management** (the directors and officers running the
  company);
- **Those charged with governance** (the board, often via the
  audit committee for larger companies).

Reports include:

- The **formal audit opinion** in the accounts;
- **Management letters** identifying control weaknesses;
- **Specific** going-concern or fraud-risk communications.

## Pitfalls

- **Inadequate audit planning** — exposes the auditor to claims
  of negligence.
- **Failure to escalate findings** — material findings should
  be communicated promptly.
