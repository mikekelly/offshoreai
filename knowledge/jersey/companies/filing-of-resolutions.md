# Article 100 — Filing of Resolutions

Article 100 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** requires
**certain resolutions** to be **filed with the Registrar** —
ensuring official registration and public record of key company
decisions.

## What Article 100 covers

Resolutions passed at meetings **require filing with the
Registrar** to ensure:

- **Official registration**;
- **Public record** of company decisions.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Which resolutions

Not every resolution must be filed — the Article (and its
companion provisions) specify which. Typical filing requirements:

- **Special resolutions** under [Article 90](special-resolution.md)
  — virtually always required;
- **Resolutions altering the memorandum or articles** — to
  effect the change at the Registry;
- **Resolutions on capital matters** — reductions, alterations,
  etc.;
- **Resolutions on name changes** — to update the registered
  name;
- **Resolutions on wind-up** — to register the wind-up status;
- **Resolutions designated by the deed or by specific Articles**.

**Ordinary resolutions** on routine business are generally
**not filed**.

## Filing procedure

A typical filing:

- **Resolution text** delivered to the Registrar;
- **Within the time specified** by the relevant Article
  (often 14 or 28 days from the resolution);
- **Filing fee** as prescribed;
- **Effective** on filing (subject to the specific Article's
  effective-date rules).

## Public availability

Filed resolutions become **part of the public Registry
record**:

- Counterparties dealing with the company can verify the
  resolution;
- Third parties can confirm specific changes (name, status,
  capital);
- Regulators can review the company's governance history.

## Pitfalls

- **Failure to file** required resolutions — exposes the
  company and directors to offences and may invalidate the
  intended effect.
- **Late filing** — may incur penalties and operational delays.
