# Article 48 — Trusts Not to Be Entered on Register

Article 48 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** establishes
that **trusts are not entered on the register of members**. The
company treats the **registered legal holder** as the member,
regardless of any underlying beneficial-ownership or trust
arrangement.

## What Article 48 says

The company **need not recognise trusts** affecting shares.

It **treats registered members as owners** regardless of
beneficial-ownership arrangements.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## What this means in practice

Where shares are held by a **trustee** for **beneficiaries**:

- The **trustee** is entered on the register as the legal holder;
- The fact that the holding is **on trust** is **not recorded**;
- The company treats the **trustee** as the member for all
  membership purposes (voting, dividends, notices, meetings);
- The **beneficiaries** are not on the register and have no
  direct relationship with the company qua company.

This is sometimes called the **"clean register"** rule — the
register shows legal title only.

## Practical consequences

- The company **discharges its obligations** by dealing with the
  registered trustee;
- The **trustee** acts as conduit for any communications or
  rights flow-through to beneficiaries (per the trust deed);
- The **company is not obliged** to investigate the trustee's
  internal authority or comply with trust-side requirements;
- The **AML/CFT obligations** of the registered agent and
  underlying-trust trustee handle the beneficial-ownership
  disclosure separately under the FSDI Law 2020.

## Beneficial-ownership transparency

Article 48 is **not** about hiding beneficial ownership — it's
about **register simplicity**. Modern Jersey beneficial-ownership
transparency is maintained through:

- The **FSDI Law 2020 beneficial-ownership register** —
  non-public but available to regulators and law enforcement;
- The **JFSC's regulatory regime** for the trust-company
  business that administers the trust;
- The **CRS and FATCA reporting** by the trust to relevant tax
  authorities.

The shares-register clean-handed rule sits alongside this
broader transparency architecture.

## Foreign-law trusts

A foreign-law trust holding Jersey-company shares is treated the
same way — the registered holder (the foreign trustee) is on the
register; the trust details are not.

The Jersey company doesn't need to apply foreign-trust-law tests
to its register-keeping; the register is purely legal-title-based.

## Pitfalls

- **Treating the register as a beneficial-ownership record** —
  it's not. BO data is separate.
- **Refusing to honour trustee actions** because they're on
  trust — the company should treat the trustee as the full
  member. The trustee's internal authority is the trust's
  problem, not the company's.
