# Article 69 — Company's Name to Appear in Correspondence

Article 69 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** requires
a Jersey company to **display its registered name** on all
business correspondence and official publications.

## What Article 69 says

A company must **display its registered name legibly** on all
business correspondence, including:

- **letters**;
- **notices**;
- **advertisements**;
- **other official publications**.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

Counterparties dealing with a company need to know:

- The **company's identity** — its registered legal name;
- That they are dealing with a **company** rather than an
  individual or partnership;
- The **liability status** (limited / unlimited) — indicated by
  the suffix.

The Article 69 requirement is the **disclosure mechanism** that
ensures this information appears on the company's public-facing
communications.

## What counts as correspondence

The Article is broad:

- **Physical letters** and printed correspondence;
- **Email** signatures and standardised messaging;
- **Notices** to members or third parties;
- **Marketing materials** including advertisements;
- **Websites** and other online presence;
- **Contracts and engagement letters**;
- **Invoices**.

The "legibly displayed" requirement means the name must be
**visible and readable**, not buried in fine print.

## The "Limited" / "Ltd" suffix

A limited company's name must include the suffix
("Limited", "Ltd", "avec responsabilité limitée", or "a.r.l." —
see [Article 13](name-requirements.md)). The Article 69 display
requirement extends to the **full registered name including the
suffix**.

A limited company that drops the "Limited" / "Ltd" in its
correspondence is in breach — and officers may face personal
liability on contracts as a consequence.

## Companion provision — Article 70

[Article 70](particulars-in-correspondence.md) adds additional
**particulars** that must appear on certain business documents —
typically the company's **registered number** and **registered
office address**. Article 69 covers the **name**; Article 70
covers **the broader business-document particulars**.

## Pitfalls

- **Inconsistent use** of the name on different documents — some
  with suffix, some without. Standardise.
- **Trading name vs registered name** — a company may use a
  trading name (different from its registered name) for marketing,
  but the **registered name** must still appear on formal
  correspondence.
- **Subsidiary signatures** for related entities — each entity
  must show its own name; combined letterhead for groups can be
  confusing.
