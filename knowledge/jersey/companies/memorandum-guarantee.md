# Article 4B — Memorandum of Company with Guarantor Members

Article 4B specifies the additional memorandum content for a
**guarantee company** under [Article 3G](./guarantee-companies.md).
Guarantee companies have **no share capital**; members are
**guarantors** who undertake to contribute up to a specified
amount on wind-up.

## What Article 4B says

The memorandum must:

- state that each guarantor member undertakes to **contribute up
  to a maximum specified amount** towards:
  - the **payment of the debts and liabilities** of the company;
    and
  - the **payment of the costs, charges and expenses of winding
    up**;
- state, **against each guarantor member's name**:
  - the **guarantor member designation**; and
  - the **maximum amount** specified for that member.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Practical content

A typical guarantee-company memorandum will state, for each
guarantor:

> [Name of guarantor], guarantor member, hereby undertakes to
> contribute up to the maximum sum of £[X] to the assets of the
> company in the event of its being wound up while the guarantor
> is a member, or within one year after ceasing to be a member,
> for payment of the debts and liabilities of the company
> contracted before the guarantor ceases to be a member, and of
> the costs, charges and expenses of winding up.

The **typical guaranteed amount** is nominal — often £1, £10, or
£100 per member. The function is to provide a **wind-up
contribution mechanism**, not to capitalise the company.
Operational funding comes from subscriptions, donations, or
external sources.

## Why use a guarantee company

Common uses:

- **Charitable or not-for-profit companies** — the absence of
  share capital reflects the absence of profit motive;
- **Mutual associations** — clubs, professional bodies,
  industry associations;
- **Orphan structures** — analogous to (though distinct from)
  non-charitable purpose trusts in
  [`../trusts/purpose-trusts.md`](../trusts/purpose-trusts.md).

For charitable use, the company will typically also register as a
charity under the **Charities (Jersey) Law 2014** (a separate
step from Companies Law incorporation).

## Pitfalls

- **Setting too high a guarantee amount** can deter members from
  joining; **too low** can leave the company without resources on
  wind-up. The "nominal but real" range (£1 to £100 per member) is
  typical.
- **Hybrid attempts** — a company "limited by guarantee and
  having a share capital" is **not** permitted under Jersey
  Article 3G; a Jersey guarantee company has no share capital.
  For hybrid commercial-charitable structures, use separate
  vehicles for each function.
