# Article 127FN — Effect of Completion of Merger

Article 127FN of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** is
the **substantive effect provision** for a Part 18B merger — what
actually happens, in law, on completion. This is the article that
makes the merger regime **commercially valuable**.

## What Article 127FN covers

On the effective date of the merger:

- The **merging bodies that do not survive** are dissolved
  **without winding up**;
- All **property, rights, assets and interests** of the merging
  bodies **vest in the merged body** by **operation of law**, no
  transfer or conveyance being required;
- All **liabilities and obligations** of the merging bodies
  **become liabilities and obligations of the merged body**;
- All **contracts, deeds, bonds, agreements and other
  instruments** to which any merging body was a party have
  **effect** as if the merged body were a party in place of the
  merging body;
- All **legal proceedings** pending by or against any merging
  body **continue** by or against the merged body;
- The **members of the merging bodies** become members of the
  merged body in accordance with the merger agreement.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

The doctrine of **universal succession** by operation of law is
what makes Part 18B mergers **powerful**:

- **No bilateral transfers needed** — assets pass without
  conveyance, contracts continue without novation, employees
  transfer without separate consents;
- **No "blocking" counterparties** — third parties cannot withhold
  consent because there's no transfer event for them to block;
- **Legal proceedings continue** — pending litigation just
  transfers across.

This contrasts with **asset acquisitions** (which require
individual asset transfers and counterparty consents) and
**share acquisitions** (which keep the original entities alive
but under new ownership).

## Practical considerations

- **Foreign-law effects** — universal succession works for assets
  governed by Jersey law and (after the merger is recognised
  abroad) often for assets governed by foreign law of cooperating
  jurisdictions. Assets governed by uncooperative foreign law may
  need separate transfer steps.
- **Change-of-control clauses** — a clause that triggers on
  "change of control" or "transfer of assets" may still be
  triggered by a merger, even though there is no transfer in
  Jersey law. Read the relevant contract.
- **Licences and consents** — regulatory licences and similar
  consents may or may not pass with universal succession.
