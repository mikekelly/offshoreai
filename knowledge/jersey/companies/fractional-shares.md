# Article 40 — Power to Issue Fractions of Shares

Article 40 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** permits a
Jersey company to **issue fractions of shares** — a feature that
some other jurisdictions don't permit.

## What Article 40 enables

A Jersey company may **issue fractional shares** — e.g., 0.5
shares, 0.25 shares, 1.75 shares.

Fractional shares carry **proportional rights**:

- Voting rights typically proportional (subject to deed);
- Dividend entitlement proportional;
- Capital entitlement proportional on wind-up.

The deed must address how fractional rights are exercised —
particularly voting where the fractional vote might not produce
a whole number.

## Why fractional shares are useful

Common scenarios:

- **Fund vehicles** issuing investor participations at NAV — the
  investor's subscription doesn't always divide evenly into whole
  shares; fractional allotments handle the remainder.
- **Dividend reinvestment plans** — accrued dividends used to buy
  more shares often produce fractional positions.
- **Stock splits, consolidations, and corporate actions** —
  arithmetic of conversions may produce fractional results.
- **Employee share schemes** — vesting in fractional amounts.

Without Article 40, a Jersey company would have to round to whole
shares (paying cash for fractions) which is operationally
clunkier.

## Voting and dividend mechanics

The deed typically specifies:

- **Voting** — fractional shares either have **proportional
  votes** (rare — requires fractional vote counting) or
  **aggregate up** at the holder level (so a holder of 1.5 shares
  has 1 vote and 0.5 of a vote that's effectively rounded);
- **Dividends** — distributed proportionally to fraction held;
- **Wind-up distribution** — proportional to fraction held.

## Multi-currency / multi-class interaction

In a no-par-value fund-vehicle company with multiple share classes
in different currencies, fractional shares are common in each
class. The Article 40 permission combines with the
multi-currency permission of [Article 4A](./memorandum-shares.md)
and [Article 38B](./currency-conversion-rate.md) to give Jersey
fund vehicles considerable structural flexibility.

## Pitfalls

- **Voting arithmetic** — companies with fractional shares need
  clear rules in the deed for how fractional votes are counted.
- **Listing-rule conflicts** — some listing venues don't accept
  fractional listed shares; check before listing a Jersey
  company.
- **Foreign-jurisdiction operational issues** — some foreign
  registries or banks don't handle fractional positions well;
  factor in if the company will have foreign-resident members.
