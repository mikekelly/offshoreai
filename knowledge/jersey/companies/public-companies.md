# Article 3A — Public Companies

Article 3A of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** defines a
**public company** by reference to its **memorandum of association**.

## What Article 3A says

A company is a **public company** if its memorandum states that it
is a public company.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

The public/private distinction is fundamental in Jersey company law:

| Feature | Public company | Private company |
|---|---|---|
| Memorandum statement | "Public" | "Private" |
| Minimum incorporators (Article 3) | 2 | 1 |
| Maximum members | None | None (after Article 17D Order — historically 30) |
| Listing eligible | Yes | No (without prior status change) |
| Audit requirement | Required | Generally not required, subject to specific cases |
| Prospectus rules | Apply | Apply only on public offer |
| Status change | Article 16 | Article 17 |

The **declaration in the memorandum** is the trigger — not the
substance of the company's activities. A Jersey company whose
memorandum states it is public **is** public, even if it has never
offered shares publicly.

## Typical use cases for public form

- **Pre-IPO holding company** — where a Jersey TopCo will be
  publicly listed (often on London, NASDAQ, or other major
  exchanges) and benefits from being in public form from
  inception;
- **Existing public group** subsidiaries that need public form for
  group-structure reasons;
- **Fund structures** intended to be widely held by retail or
  professional investors;
- **Public listings** of debt or equity instruments on Jersey or
  international markets.

Jersey is a popular jurisdiction for **TopCo** structures of
companies going public on the LSE because:

- Jersey company law provides a familiar UK-style structure;
- there is no Jersey corporate income tax on the holding company
  (zero-ten regime — see [`../tax/zero-ten.md`](../tax/zero-ten.md));
- Jersey is in a [customs union with the UK](../uk-relationship.md)
  for goods (less relevant for holdcos);
- the Royal Court is a respected forum for shareholder disputes.

## Status change — Article 16

A public company can be converted to a private company under
[Article 16](./public-to-private.md). The procedure requires a special
resolution and a memorandum amendment.

The reverse — private to public — is under
[Article 17](./private-to-public.md).

## Auditing

Public companies are subject to the **mandatory audit** regime in
Part 16. A private company may or may not require an audit depending
on its size, regulated status, and member resolutions; a public
company always does.
