# Article 131 — Power of Inspectors to Call for Directors' Bank Accounts

Article 131 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** gives
inspectors specific power to require **production of directors'
personal bank account information** in defined circumstances.

## What Article 131 covers

An inspector may require a **director (or former director)** to
produce records of any **bank account** maintained or operated
in the name of the director, where the inspector has reasonable
grounds for believing the account was used in connection with:

- **Misconduct** in relation to the affairs of the company;
- **Receipt of money** in connection with transactions of the
  company that were not properly accounted for;
- **Transactions in breach of duty** (Article 74) or undisclosed
  conflicts (Article 75);
- **Fraudulent or improper purposes** related to the company.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

Director self-dealing and secret commissions are common
manifestations of corporate fraud. **Following the money** into
personal bank accounts is often essential to:

- **Confirm** that funds the company paid to suspect counterparties
  ended up with the director;
- **Quantify** undisclosed benefits;
- **Identify** further conduits.

Article 131 narrowly authorises this intrusion into directors'
personal finances when the **specific suspicion** justifies it.

## Pitfalls

- **Privacy concerns** — directors' personal finances are
  ordinarily private. The inspector must have **reasonable
  grounds** for the specific belief; a fishing expedition is
  outside the article.
- **Foreign bank accounts** — Article 131 reaches accounts in
  the director's name wherever held, but enforcement depends on
  cooperation with foreign banks.
