# Article 127YQ — Membership of Protected Cell Company

Article 127YQ of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** sets
out how **membership of a PCC** works given the cell structure.

## What Article 127YQ covers

A PCC has:

- **Core members** — holders of shares not attributable to any
  cell (the "core" or "general" shares);
- **Cell shareholders** — holders of shares whose rights are
  attributable to a specific cell (e.g. fund investors in a
  specific sub-fund);
- A unified **register of members** that records both categories,
  with cell attribution clearly identified.

Cell shareholders typically:

- Have economic interests only in the **performance of the
  relevant cell**;
- Have voting rights specified in the PCC's articles (sometimes
  restricted to cell-level matters);
- May redeem or transfer their shares according to cell-specific
  terms.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

The membership structure makes the PCC a popular vehicle for
**multi-class** or **multi-strategy** funds:

- Each cell represents a **separate investment strategy**;
- Investors subscribe for shares **of the cell** of interest;
- Their economic exposure is **segregated** to the chosen
  strategy.

The dual-layer membership (core + cell) is essential to making
this work in a single corporate entity.
