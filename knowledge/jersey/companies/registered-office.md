# Article 67 — Registered Office

Article 67 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** requires
every Jersey company to maintain a **registered office in Jersey**
— the physical point of contact for the company and the location
where statutory records are held.

## What Article 67 says

A company must have a **registered office in Jersey** where:

- **documents and records** are kept; and
- which is **available for inspection during business hours**.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why the registered office matters

The registered office is the company's **legal address** for:

- **Service of documents** under
  [Article 72](./service-of-documents.md);
- **Statutory inspection** of records (memorandum, articles,
  register of members, register of directors);
- **Regulator contact** — the JFSC, Revenue Jersey, and other
  Jersey authorities;
- **Public visibility** — the registered office address appears
  on the company's certificate of incorporation and the public
  Registry record.

A company **without** a registered office is in breach. The
Registrar can ultimately strike the company off under Part 23.

## Where the office is

In modern Jersey practice, the registered office is **at the
office of the registered agent** — a JFSC-regulated trust company
business (TCB), law firm, or similar service provider.

This works because:

- The registered agent **maintains the company's statutory
  records** day-to-day;
- The registered agent has the **systems and staff** to receive
  service and respond to regulator queries;
- The address is a **professional location** suitable for the
  company's formal presence.

A company can in principle have its **own physical office** as
the registered office, but in practice most do not — the
registered-agent model is standard.

## Notification of changes

A change of registered office must be **notified to the
Registrar**. The notification takes effect on filing (or such
later date as specified).

## Pitfalls

- **No registered office** — strict breach; companies should
  always have one.
- **Inactive registered office** — the address must be **real**
  and **operational**, not just a mail-drop.
- **Change without notification** — operational moves between
  registered agents need prompt Registrar filing.
