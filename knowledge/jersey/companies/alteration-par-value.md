# Article 38 — Alteration of Capital (Par-Value Companies)

Article 38 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** governs
how a **par-value** Jersey company alters its share capital —
increasing by new issues, decreasing by cancellation, consolidating
or sub-dividing shares.

## What Article 38 permits

A par-value company may, by resolution:

- **Increase** its share capital by issuing new shares;
- **Decrease** its share capital through **cancellation** of
  unissued shares (or shares not subject to creditor protections);
- **Consolidate** shares (combine smaller-denomination shares
  into larger ones);
- **Sub-divide** shares (split larger-denomination shares into
  smaller ones);
- **Convert** shares between classes (subject to class-rights
  protections under Articles 52–54).

The **Registrar must be notified** of any alteration, which
typically becomes effective on registration.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Comparison with no-par-value

The Article 38 mechanism for par-value is **partly mirrored** by
[Article 38A](./alteration-no-par-value.md) for no-par-value
companies. Differences:

| Feature | Article 38 (par-value) | Article 38A (no-par-value) |
|---|---|---|
| Headline metric | Par-value × number of shares | Total stated capital |
| Increase mechanism | New shares with stated par value | New shares (no nominal value) |
| Consolidation / sub-division | Adjusts par value | Adjusts share count |
| Conversion | Permitted within class-rights rules | Permitted within class-rights rules |
| Registrar notification | Required | Required |

## Capital reduction — Article 38 vs Part 12

Article 38 covers **simple alterations** — increases, cancellation
of unissued shares, consolidation, sub-division. **Substantive
capital reduction** (reducing paid-up share capital, share premium,
or stated capital below historical levels) is governed by **Part
12** of the Companies Law:

- [Article 61](./reduction-of-capital-accounts.md) — reduction of
  capital accounts;
- [Article 61A](./solvency-statement.md) — the **solvency-statement**
  mechanic that is the centrepiece of Jersey's capital-management
  regime;
- [Article 62 and 63](./court-confirmation.md) — Court confirmation
  procedure where it is required.

So Article 38 is the **operational mechanism** for routine
alterations; Part 12 is the **substantive constraint** when
reducing paid-up capital materially.

## Resolution and registration

The Article requires the alteration to be by **resolution**.
Typically:

- **Ordinary resolution** for routine increases of authorised
  capital;
- **Special resolution** for changes to memorandum terms
  affecting capital (depending on the deed);
- **Class-rights variation procedure** where the alteration
  affects different share classes.

The altered memorandum must be filed with the Registrar; the
alteration takes effect on registration.

## Pitfalls

- **Confusing Article 38 alterations with Part 12 reductions** —
  the two have different procedural requirements.
- **Class-rights triggers** — an alteration that affects only
  some shareholders' rights may be a class-rights variation
  requiring Article 52 procedure, not just Article 38.
