# Limited Partnerships (Jersey) Law 1994 — Index

The **Limited Partnerships (Jersey) Law 1994** is the original
Jersey partnership statute, establishing the **Jersey Limited
Partnership (LP)** — the most commonly used Jersey fund and
private-investment vehicle. Together with the **Incorporated
Limited Partnerships Law 2011**
([`ilp-2011-index.md`](./ilp-2011-index.md)) and the
**Separate Limited Partnerships Law 2011**
([`slp-2011-index.md`](./slp-2011-index.md)), the LP 1994 sits
at the centre of Jersey's fund-vehicle infrastructure.

This file is the structural entry point for the statute. The
per-Article files referenced below are scaffolded as stubs
pending a primary-source pass against the current consolidated
text on
[jerseylaw.je](https://www.jerseylaw.je/laws/current/l_22_1994).

## The Jersey LP at a glance

A Jersey LP under the 1994 Law has:

- **At least one General Partner (GP)** — unlimited liability
  for the partnership's obligations; manages the LP.
- **One or more Limited Partners (LPs)** — liability capped at
  capital contributed (subject to the safe-harbour list of
  permitted activities that do not lose limited-liability
  protection).
- **No separate legal personality** — the LP is a contractual
  arrangement between the partners, not an incorporated body.
  Property is held by the GP on behalf of the partnership.
- **No registered office requirement separate from the GP**
  but a registered representative is required.
- **Registration** with the JFSC Registry.

The contractual nature (no separate legal personality) is the
key feature distinguishing the LP from the
[**Incorporated LP (ILP)**](./ilp-2011-index.md) (separate
legal personality with body-corporate character) and the
[**Separate LP (SLP)**](./slp-2011-index.md) (separate legal
personality without body-corporate character).

## Use cases

The Jersey LP is the **default vehicle** for:

- **Closed-ended private equity funds** — the partnership
  structure aligns with the GP-fund manager / LP-investor
  capital allocation model.
- **Venture capital funds**.
- **Family-office investment partnerships**.
- **Carry vehicles and management-incentive structures** —
  often layered on top of a primary fund LP.
- **Co-investment vehicles**.

The LP regime competes (and increasingly co-exists) with the
[Cayman Exempted Limited Partnership (ELP)](../../cayman/exempted-limited-partnership.md)
which is the dominant global PE vehicle. Choice is often driven
by investor familiarity, manager domicile, and tax
considerations — Jersey LP is increasingly chosen by managers
with EU/UK investor focus and AIFMD/NPPR-marketing needs.

## Interaction with other vehicles

- **vs ILP 2011** — [`ilp-2011-index.md`](./ilp-2011-index.md)
  has separate legal personality + body-corporate character;
  ICCs of partnerships become possible; useful where the LP
  needs to contract in its own name.
- **vs SLP 2011** — [`slp-2011-index.md`](./slp-2011-index.md)
  has separate legal personality without body-corporate
  character; intermediate position; primarily used to bridge
  US tax-classification preferences with Jersey LP economics.
- **vs LLP 2017** — [`llp-2017-index.md`](./llp-2017-index.md)
  is for professional-services use cases; partner liability is
  the focus rather than fund-vehicle mechanics.
- **vs LLC 2018** — [`llc-2018-index.md`](./llc-2018-index.md)
  is Jersey's response to demand for Cayman-LLC-equivalent
  vehicles; member-managed body-corporate; useful where the
  Delaware-LLC pattern is the comparable mental model.
