# Article 73 — Directors

Article 73 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** is the
foundational provision on **directors** — every Jersey company
must have at least one, and directors may be natural persons or
corporate bodies.

## What Article 73 says

- A company must have **at least one director**.
- Directors may be **natural persons** or **corporate bodies**
  meeting specific requirements under Article 73(4).

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Minimum of one

The Jersey minimum is **one director**. This is lower than many
other jurisdictions (the UK requires two for public companies;
some EU jurisdictions require multiple). The single-director
minimum suits:

- **Wholly-owned subsidiaries** in group structures;
- **Family-office vehicles** with concentrated decision-making;
- **PTC** structures where the family controls via a single
  director.

For public companies, the deed will typically require **at least
three or more** directors — driven by listing rules and
governance best practice — even though the statutory minimum is
one.

## Corporate directors

A **corporate body** may serve as a director, subject to specific
requirements under Article 73(4). This is sometimes called a
"corporate director" arrangement. Common in:

- **Trust-company-business administered structures** — the TCB
  acts as corporate director of multiple client companies;
- **PTC structures** — the PTC has a corporate director that is
  itself a Jersey-licensed entity;
- **Specific group structures** where insertion of a corporate
  director simplifies governance.

The **Article 73(4) requirements** typically include conditions
on the corporate director's own constitution, regulation, and
suitability — the corporate director must itself be properly
established and able to act through identified individuals.

## Director particulars

Articles 84 and 84A require **particulars of directors** to be
registered — different requirements for natural-person and
corporate directors. See

## Pitfalls

- **Director vacancy** — a company with no director is in
  breach. Plan for replacement on resignation.
- **Corporate-director regulatory consequences** — the corporate
  director may itself be a regulated entity; ensure compliance
  with its own regulatory framework.
