# Takeovers Panel Law 2009 Article 7 — Power to require documents and information

Article 7 gives the Panel **investigative powers** — it can
require any person to produce documents or to provide
information that is reasonably necessary for the Panel to
discharge its functions.

## Key provisions

- The Panel may **by written notice** require a person to
  produce documents or provide information.
- The notice must specify a **reasonable period** for
  compliance.
- The Panel may **authenticate documents**, **verify
  information**, and **take copies** of documents produced.
- The Panel **cannot compel disclosure** of materials
  subject to **legal professional privilege**.

## Operational use

Article 7 is the investigative mechanism that supports
Panel decision-making. Typical use:

- Requiring a bidder to produce funding-confirmation
  evidence;
- Requiring target-board records on defensive-action
  decisions;
- Requiring market participants to disclose dealings or
  positions.

## Interaction with self-incrimination and confidentiality

Article 7 operates alongside:

- **[Article 8](./tmp-article-8.md)** — restriction on
  Panel disclosure of information received;
- **[Article 22](./tmp-article-22.md)** — privilege against
  self-incrimination — statements made in response to
  Article 7 requirements cannot be used in subsequent
  criminal proceedings (except perjury).
