# Article 127W — Statements of Solvency in Respect of Continuance

Article 127W of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** sets
out the **solvency-statement requirement** for both inbound and
outbound continuance.

## What Article 127W covers

The directors of the body / company must make a **solvency
statement** in **prescribed form** that, having made a full
inquiry into the affairs of the body:

- They are of the **opinion** that the body, after the
  continuance becomes effective, will be able to **pay its
  debts as they fall due**;
- The opinion is based on **reasonable grounds**;
- No matter known to the directors **contradicts** the opinion.

The form mirrors the **Article 61A solvency statement** used for
reductions of capital and other transactions.

Falsity without reasonable grounds is an **offence** with severe
penalties.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

Continuance changes the **legal environment** of the body without
changing its **economic position**. Creditors should be no worse
off in commercial substance — but they must accept changes in
the **legal regime** around their claims.

The solvency statement is the **directors' personal attestation**
that:

- Creditors can be paid as their debts mature;
- The post-continuance entity has the economic capacity to
  service those debts;
- Directors have inquired and have grounds for that conclusion.

This is the **central creditor-protection mechanism** in the
continuance regime, supported by the JFSC consent regime and by
creditor notice requirements (Article 127R).
