# Article 41 — Register of Members

Article 41 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** requires
every Jersey company to maintain a **register of members** — the
canonical record of who owns the company.

## What Article 41 says

**Every company must maintain a register** showing:

- **members' names**;
- **members' addresses**;
- members' **share holdings**.

The register is **prima facie evidence** of membership details.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## What the register records

The register typically records, for each member:

- **Name** (legal name as accepted on registration);
- **Address** (registered address for service);
- **Number and class of shares** held;
- **Date** each parcel of shares was acquired (and disposed of);
- **Status** — full member / fractional / joint holder / etc.

Where the company has **multiple classes of shares**, the
register tracks holdings by class.

## Prima facie evidence

A name on the register is **prima facie evidence** of membership.
This means:

- **Third parties dealing with the company** can rely on the
  register;
- The company can treat the registered holder as the member for
  all purposes;
- The registered holder can exercise membership rights without
  needing to prove additional facts.

"Prima facie" — not conclusive. The register can be:

- **Rectified** under [Article 47](register-rectification.md) if
  incorrect;
- **Challenged** in subsequent proceedings if title evidence
  shows otherwise.

## Beneficial ownership

The register shows the **legal title** to shares — not the
beneficial owner. Where a trustee, nominee, or custodian holds
shares for a beneficial owner:

- the **registered holder** is the trustee / nominee / custodian;
- the **beneficial owner** is not on the register;
- the company is **entitled to treat the registered holder as
  the full member** (see [Article 48](no-trusts-on-register.md)).

The **beneficial-ownership data** is recorded separately under
the FSDI Law 2020 and is **not public** — see
[`../registries/beneficial-ownership.md`](../registries/beneficial-ownership.md)
*(stub)*.

## Where the register is kept

[Article 44](register-location.md) governs where the register
must be kept — typically at the registered office or another
location notified to the Registrar.

[Article 45](register-inspection.md) governs members' right to
inspect.

## Pitfalls

- **Out-of-date register** — common in small companies that
  don't actively maintain it. Failure to update on transfers
  creates evidential problems and may invalidate distributions
  to incorrectly-registered persons.
- **Joint-holder treatment** — register typically shows joint
  members; the deed should specify who exercises voting and
  dividend rights.
- **Misnamed members** — should be corrected promptly via
  Article 47 rectification.
