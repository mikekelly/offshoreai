# Article 127GB — Demergers

Article 127GB of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]**
introduces a **statutory demerger regime** — a court-free
mechanism by which a Jersey company may split its business and
assets into two or more **separate companies** that continue as
distinct entities.

## What Article 127GB covers

Article 127GB is the **enabling article** for the **statutory
demerger** regime. The detailed mechanics are set out in
**Demerger Regulations** made under the article.

In outline, the demerger regime allows a Jersey company (the
"demerging company") to:

- **Divide** its property, rights and liabilities between two or
  more **continuing or new companies**;
- Cause **shares in the resulting companies** to be issued to the
  members of the demerging company (or to specified members) in
  the appropriate proportions;
- Cease to exist (in the case of a full division) or continue as
  one of the resulting entities (in the case of a partial
  division).

The demerger takes effect on **registration** of the demerger
documents with the registrar — there is no Court sanction
requirement.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl] and the
Demerger Regulations.)

## Why this matters

Demergers serve **business-separation** objectives:

- **Spin-offs** — separating a non-core division into its own
  listed company;
- **Splitting joint ventures** that have run their course;
- **Family / shareholder separations** — splitting a family
  company so each branch holds its own vehicle;
- **Regulatory separations** — separating regulated and
  unregulated businesses;
- **Pre-sale preparation** — extracting an unwanted operation
  before selling the core business.

Before the statutory demerger regime, equivalent outcomes had to
be achieved through **schemes of arrangement** (Article 125),
**reductions of capital** (Part 12), or **distributions in
specie** (Part 17). Article 127GB provides a **purpose-built**,
court-free alternative.

## The demerger statement

The directors of the demerging company must make a **statutory
demerger statement** broadly equivalent to the solvency statement
under Article 61A — that each of the **resulting companies** will
be able to **pay its debts as they fall due** for a period after
the demerger.

Falsity of the statement is an **offence** with severe penalties.

## Approval process

Typical approval steps (as set out in the Regulations):

1. **Directors approve** the demerger and make the demerger
   statement;
2. **Demerger documents** prepared — describing what assets and
   liabilities go where;
3. **Members approve** by special resolution;
4. **Creditor protection** — prescribed period during which
   creditors can object;
5. **Registration** with the registrar;
6. **Effective date** — assets, liabilities and contracts vest in
   the resulting companies by operation of law.

## Pitfalls

- **Foreign-asset recognition** — assets located outside Jersey
  may need separate transfer steps to the resulting companies;
  the statutory vesting works in Jersey law but not necessarily
  in foreign law.
- **Tax treatment** — demergers can have material tax
  consequences in each shareholder's tax jurisdiction; advance
  clearances commonly sought.
- **Regulated business consents** — financial services consents
  and licences may need bespoke arrangements alongside the
  demerger.
