# Takeovers Panel Law 2009 Article 21 — Exemption from liability in damages

Article 21 protects the **Panel, its members, officers, and
staff** from personal liability in damages for the
discharge of Panel functions.

## Key provisions

- Neither the Panel nor its members, officers, or staff are
  liable in damages for anything done or omitted in the
  discharge or purported discharge of Panel functions.
- **Exceptions** — liability is preserved for:
  - Acts or omissions in **bad faith**;
  - Acts or omissions infringing **rights under the
    Human Rights (Jersey) Law 2000**.

## Operational significance

The exemption enables Panel members and staff to discharge
their functions without fear of personal liability for
ordinary mistakes or judgement calls. This is consistent
with the equivalent UK Panel protection.

The carve-outs:

- **Bad faith** — wilful misconduct or knowing breach;
- **Human Rights Law** — particularly Article 6 ECHR
  (fair-trial rights), preserving the rule-of-law overlay
  on Panel decision-making.
