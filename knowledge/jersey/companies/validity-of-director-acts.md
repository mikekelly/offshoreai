# Article 80 — Validity of Acts of Director

Article 80 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** is a
**third-party-protection** provision: acts of a director are
**not invalidated** by reason only of defects in the director's
appointment or qualification that are discovered later.

## What Article 80 covers

Acts of a director are **valid** in respect of third parties
even where there is later discovered:

- a **defect in the director's appointment**;
- a **disqualification** that was unknown at the time;
- another formal **defect** affecting the director's
  position;

— provided the third party acted in **good faith** without
notice of the defect.

(For the verbatim text see [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

A third party dealing with a company sees the **director acting
on the company's behalf**. The third party should not be at risk
because of internal corporate defects:

- The director was **never properly appointed** but acted as one;
- The director was **disqualified** but continued to act;
- The director's appointment **was procedurally defective** (e.g.,
  the appointing resolution had a quorum problem);
- The director's term had **technically expired** but they
  continued.

Article 80 protects the third party in such cases — the
director's acts remain valid as against the third party.

## Pairs with Article 19 and Article 67C

The provision works alongside:

- [Article 19](./no-implied-notice.md) — no implied notice of
  Registry records, so the third party isn't deemed to know of
  internal defects;
- [Article 67C](./evidence-of-authorization.md) — evidence of
  authorisation via seal or authorised signature;
- [Article 18](./corporate-capacity.md) — corporate-capacity
  unlimited by deed.

The four together form the **third-party-protection package** in
Part 5 / Part 14 of the Companies Law: counterparties dealing
with apparently-authorised company representatives don't have to
investigate the internal authorisation chain.

## Internal consequences

Article 80 doesn't cure the **internal** consequences:

- A director who **acts while disqualified** still faces
  [Article 79](./disqualified-personal-liability.md) personal
  liability;
- An **improperly-appointed director** may still have personal
  liability to the company;
- The **company's internal records** still need correction.

The Article protects the **external relationship** with third
parties; internal-relationship issues remain.

## Pitfalls

- **Treating Article 80 as curing internal defects** — it
  doesn't. The director still faces internal consequences for
  acting beyond authority.
- **Third-party knowledge** — Article 80 protection requires
  the third party to be without notice. A third party who
  knows of the defect cannot rely on Article 80.
