# Takeovers Panel Law 2009 Article 12 — Hearings and appeals

Article 12 requires the **rules** made by the Panel to
provide for a **review-and-appeal architecture**:

- A **Hearings Committee** that reviews Panel decisions; and
- A **Takeover Appeal Board** that hears appeals from
  Hearings Committee decisions.

## Key provisions

- The rules **must** provide for the review of Panel
  decisions by a **Hearings Committee**.
- The rules **must** provide for the appeal of Hearings
  Committee decisions to an **independent Takeover Appeal
  Board**.
- Rules govern **procedures, evidence, and enforcement**
  of these bodies' processes.
- Panel staff **cannot serve** on the review committees.
- Review-committee members **cannot act** in the underlying
  Panel proceedings.

## Operational significance

The two-tier review architecture mirrors the UK Panel's
structure — Hearings Committee internally, Takeover Appeal
Board externally. This is the **due-process backbone** of
the Panel's enforcement powers.

The independence of the Appeal Board is the key safeguard:
parties subject to Panel rulings have access to an
independent appellate forum before any Court enforcement.
