# Article 12 — Copies of Memorandum and Articles for Members

Article 12 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** is the
**member-disclosure** rule for constitutional documents: a member
has the right to a copy of the memorandum and articles on request.

## What Article 12 says

- A company **shall, on being so required by a member**, send to
  the member a copy of the memorandum and of the articles;
- Subject to **payment of such sum (if any), not exceeding the
  published maximum**, as the company may require;
- **Failure constitutes an offence**.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

Members are bound by the memorandum and articles under
[Article 10](effect-of-memorandum-articles.md), but they need to
**know what binds them**. Article 12 gives a statutory right to a
copy — they cannot be denied access to the document that defines
their rights and obligations.

In modern practice this rarely surfaces as a dispute because:

- The memorandum is **publicly available** at the JFSC Registry;
- The articles are **also publicly available** at the Registry;
- Members typically receive the documents on becoming members.

The right matters most in:

- **Disputes** where a member is contesting the company's
  position and wants to verify the deed's terms;
- **Long-running companies** where the deed has been amended
  multiple times and the member wants the **current** consolidated
  version;
- **Family-company contexts** where access to constitutional
  documents has been informally restricted.

## The fee

The Article permits the company to charge "a sum not exceeding
the published maximum". The "published maximum" refers to a
maximum prescribed by the JFSC or by Order. The fee is typically
nominal — copy-cost equivalent.

In practice most companies provide copies free of charge to
members on request.

## Failure as an offence

A company that fails to comply with a member's Article 12 request
commits an offence under the Companies Law (typically a fineable
offence under Schedule 1). The offence can be charged against the
company and against officers in default.

## Modern practice

Most Jersey companies satisfy Article 12 by:

- **Maintaining current articles** in the company's records;
- **Providing copies** to members on becoming members and on
  any subsequent amendment;
- **Posting copies** on the company's intranet or shareholder
  portal (for larger groups);
- **Providing copies promptly** when a member specifically asks.

## Pitfalls

- **Refusing to provide for the wrong reason** — e.g.,
  withholding because the member is in a dispute with the
  company. The Article 12 right exists regardless of the
  member's standing in any dispute.
- **Providing only the original deed** when subsequent
  amendments have changed it — the member is entitled to the
  **current** consolidated version (or to be told of the
  amendments).
- **Charging an excessive fee** — the fee is capped by the
  prescribed maximum.
