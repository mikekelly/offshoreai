# Article 167 — Arrangement When Binding on Creditors

Article 167 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** allows
the liquidator (with appropriate consents) to enter into an
**arrangement with creditors** that binds dissenting creditors.

## What Article 167 covers

In a creditors' winding up, the liquidator may negotiate an
**arrangement** with creditors that is binding on the entire
creditor body if approved by:

- A **prescribed majority** of creditors (commonly 75% in value
  of those voting); and
- The **liquidation committee** or **Court sanction** as
  prescribed.

The arrangement may cover:

- **Compromise** of disputed claims;
- **Variation of priorities**;
- **Continued trading** rather than realisation;
- **Distribution** of assets in specified ways.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

Sometimes the best outcome for creditors is **not** strict
realisation:

- A **continued trading** arrangement may produce better returns
  than a fire sale;
- **Settlement** of complex claims may save litigation costs;
- A **mixed asset/cash distribution** may serve some creditors
  better.

Article 167 gives the liquidator **flexibility** to negotiate
such arrangements with binding effect — without requiring
unanimous creditor consent (impossible in large estates) or
falling back on a full scheme of arrangement under Article 125.
