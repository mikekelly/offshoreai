# Article 157C — Order of Court Commencing Creditors' Winding Up

Article 157C of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** is
the **Court order** that places a company into creditors'
winding up following a creditor's application.

## What Article 157C covers

On hearing the Article 157A application, if the Court is
satisfied that the grounds are made out, it may make an order:

- **Placing the company in creditors' winding up**;
- **Appointing a liquidator** (or directing creditors to do so
  at a creditors' meeting);
- Making **incidental and consequential orders** as appropriate.

The order **commences** the winding up from the date specified
in the order.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

The Article 157C order is the **judicial equivalent** of the
member resolution under Article 157. It places the company in
creditors' winding up by court action, overriding the directors'
and members' inaction (or opposition).
