# ILP Law 2011 Article 3 — Incorporated limited partnership

The **load-bearing constitutional provision** of the ILP
Law:

- An ILP is a **body corporate**;
- With **separate legal personality**;
- With **perpetual succession**;
- With **unlimited capacity** (within the limits of its
  business / purpose);
- Must have **one or more general partners** and **one or
  more limited partners**.

## How this differs from LP 1994

The
[LP 1994](./lp-article-3.md) LP is a **contractual
arrangement** between partners without separate legal
personality. The ILP is **incorporated** — with body-
corporate character and the full attributes of a Jersey
body corporate. This is the structural distinction between
the two vehicle types.
