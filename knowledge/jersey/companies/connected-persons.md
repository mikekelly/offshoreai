# Article 74ZA — Connected Persons for Article 74 Purposes

Article 74ZA of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** defines
**persons connected with a director** for the purposes of Article
74. Connected persons are deemed to share the director's
interests for conflict-of-interest analysis.

## What Article 74ZA says

For [Article 74](directors-duties.md) purposes, **persons
connected** with a director include:

- **Spouses**;
- **Minor children**;
- **Trustees of settlements** benefiting the director;
- **Partnerships** where the director is a partner.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

A director cannot avoid the **conflict-of-interest** rules by
acting through a closely-connected person. If the director's
spouse benefits, that's the director benefiting indirectly; the
Article 74 duty engages.

The **connected-persons list** captures the typical workarounds:

- The director's **spouse** receives a payment, contract, or
  benefit — treated as the director receiving it;
- The director's **minor children** are made beneficiaries — same
  treatment;
- A **trust of which the director is a beneficiary** receives
  the benefit — same treatment (subject to the specifics of the
  trust);
- A **partnership** in which the director is a partner receives
  the benefit — same treatment.

## What it does

The connected-person rules engage:

- The [Article 75](director-disclosure.md) **disclosure duty** —
  the director must disclose interests of connected persons,
  not just their own personal interests;
- The Article 74 **conflict-of-interest** duty — a connected
  person's interest is treated as the director's;
- The **no-profit rule** — profits to connected persons are
  treated as profits to the director.

## Not exhaustive

The Article 74ZA list is **typical** but not necessarily
exhaustive of all connection situations. Courts may treat other
close relationships as creating de facto connection — e.g., a
director's **adult dependent** with whom the director shares a
household; a **closely-controlled company** owned by the director.

The substance of the relationship matters; mechanical reading of
the list isn't sufficient if a clear substance-over-form
connection exists.

## Pitfalls

- **Spouse business dealings** — particularly common: the
  director's spouse owns a service-provider business that the
  company contracts with. Article 74ZA captures this as a
  connected-party transaction needing disclosure.
- **Children's trust beneficiaries** — minor children as trust
  beneficiaries can be connected even if the director is not
  themselves a beneficiary.
- **Closely-held companies of the director** — the director's
  own corporate vehicles may be substantively connected even if
  not on the explicit list.
