# Article 127YM — Restriction on Alteration of Memorandum or Articles

Article 127YM of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]**
restricts a cell's ability to alter its memorandum and articles
in ways that would compromise the **cell-ICC relationship**.

## What Article 127YM covers

An incorporated cell may not alter its memorandum or articles
to:

- Remove the **"cell of [ICC]"** identification — without going
  through the Article 127YH (separation) or 127YI (transfer)
  process;
- Change the **company type** in a way inconsistent with its
  status as a cell of the ICC;
- Make other alterations that **conflict with structural
  requirements** of being a cell.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

Without Article 127YM, a cell could **detach itself from the
ICC** by simple member resolution — bypassing the creditor-
protection and ICC-consent requirements of Article 127YH.

The restriction channels detachment through the **proper
procedures**, ensuring:

- **ICC consent** for changes affecting the umbrella;
- **Creditor protection** during structural changes;
- **Registration** of structural changes for public notice.
