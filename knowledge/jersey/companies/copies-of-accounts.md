# Article 107 — Copies of Accounts

Article 107 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** requires
companies to **send copies of accounts** to members and file with
the Registrar, with variations based on company size and
classification.

## What Article 107 covers

Companies must:

- **send copies of accounts** to **members**;
- **file** accounts with the **Registrar**;

— with **variations** based on **company size and classification**.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Who gets a copy

Members typically receive:

- **Annual accounts** as a matter of course;
- **Auditor's report** where audit applies;
- **Directors' report** for public companies.

The mechanism is typically **postal delivery** or **electronic
delivery** per the deed.

## Variations by company type

**Public companies** typically have:

- **Full annual accounts** sent to members;
- **Publicly-filed** versions for the Registrar;
- **Auditor's report** included;
- **Listing-specific** requirements where listed.

**Private companies** may have:

- **Abbreviated accounts** filed with the Registrar (in some
  cases);
- **Less elaborate disclosure** of certain items;
- **Audit-relief** for smaller companies.
