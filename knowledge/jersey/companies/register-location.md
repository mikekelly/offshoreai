# Article 44 — Location of Register of Members

Article 44 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** specifies
where a Jersey company must **keep** its register of members.

## What Article 44 says

The register must be kept:

- at the **registered office**; or
- at **another location** satisfying the **Registrar's
  requirements**.

Companies must **notify the Registrar** of any location changes.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Default location — registered office

The default and most common location is the **registered office**
— typically the office of the registered agent (a Jersey TCB or
law firm). Keeping the register at the registered office:

- Provides a clear point of reference for members exercising
  Article 45 inspection rights;
- Aligns the register with the company's principal Jersey-law
  contact point;
- Is operationally convenient for the registered agent who
  maintains the register day-to-day.

## Alternative locations

The Registrar may approve **alternative locations**, typically
where:

- The **registered agent** holds the register at a different
  office;
- A **specialist registrar provider** maintains the register
  electronically;
- An **overseas branch register** under
  [Article 49](overseas-branch-register.md) supplements the
  principal register.

In all cases, the **principal register** must be in Jersey;
overseas branch registers under Article 49 don't displace this.

## Notification of changes

A change of register location requires **notification to the
Registrar**. The notification:

- Identifies the **new location**;
- Becomes effective on **filing with the Registrar** (or such
  later date as the notification specifies);
- Should be communicated to **members** also, since it affects
  their Article 45 inspection rights.

## Electronic registers

A register kept **electronically** satisfies the Article 44
requirements provided the electronic system is **accessible** in
the manner contemplated by the Article and the Registrar has
accepted the arrangement.

Most modern Jersey companies maintain electronic registers
through registered-agent systems.

## Pitfalls

- **Failure to notify** of location change — exposes the
  company to inspection-right problems and possible offences.
- **Loss or corruption** of the register — the company has a
  statutory obligation to maintain it; loss should trigger
  reconstruction from supporting records and Registrar
  notification.
