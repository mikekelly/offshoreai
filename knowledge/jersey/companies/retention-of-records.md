# Article 104 — Retention of Records

Article 104 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** sets the
**minimum retention period** for accounting records.

## What Article 104 says

Accounting records must be kept for **at least five years** from
the date they are made.

Companies may **request shorter retention** in certain
circumstances.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## The five-year minimum

The default: **five years from the date of the record**.

The five-year period aligns with:

- **AML/CFT** record-retention requirements under the Money
  Laundering (Jersey) Order 2008;
- **Tax record** retention expectations of Revenue Jersey;
- **Limitation** periods for many civil claims.

## Shorter retention

The Article permits **shorter retention** in certain
circumstances — typically subject to the Registrar's approval or
specific Regulations.

Most Jersey companies retain records for **at least five years**
and many for longer:

- **Listed-company** accounts often kept indefinitely;
- **Fund-vehicle** accounts kept for the fund's life plus a
  margin;
- **Records relating to pending litigation** retained until
  resolution.

## Form of retention

Records can be:

- **Physical** documents;
- **Electronic** files with appropriate backup;
- **Hybrid** with primary documents electronic and supporting
  paper.

The key is **availability** — the records must be retrievable
and intelligible within the retention period.

## Pitfalls

- **Premature destruction** — five years from the record date
  is the minimum, not a target for disposal.
- **Inaccessible electronic records** — files in legacy formats
  that can't be read invite the same problems as physical
  destruction.
