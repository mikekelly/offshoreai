# Article 221 — Transitional Provisions

Article 221 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]**
introduces the **transitional provisions** that govern how the
1991 Law interacted with pre-existing companies and rules when
it came into force.

## What Article 221 covers

The transitional provisions:

- **Continue** companies incorporated under previous legislation
  (the 1861 Loi sur les Sociétés à Responsabilité Limitée and
  subsequent amendments);
- **Map** terminology and structures of the old regime to the
  new one;
- **Preserve** validity of acts done under the old regime;
- **Provide** transition periods for compliance with new
  requirements;
- **Detailed provisions** are set out in **Schedule 2** of the
  Law.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl] and Schedule
2.)

## Why this matters

The 1991 Law was a **major modernisation** of Jersey company
law:

- It replaced the framework dating from 1861;
- It introduced concepts like par-value/no-par-value, public/
  private split, modern director-duty provisions;
- It needed to apply to **existing companies** without invalidating
  their past acts.

Article 221 (and Schedule 2) provide the **bridge** from the old
regime to the new.
