# Article 127YR — Additional Duties of Directors of Protected Cell Companies

Article 127YR of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]**
imposes **PCC-specific duties on directors** in addition to the
general Article 74 duties.

## What Article 127YR covers

Directors of a PCC must:

- **Keep cellular and non-cellular assets segregated** in fact
  (not just on paper) — maintain accounting and operational
  separation;
- **Identify, in respect of each contract entered into by the
  PCC**, the cell (if any) on whose behalf it is entered into;
- **Disclose** in dealings with counterparties that the PCC is
  acting on behalf of a specific cell, where applicable;
- **Allocate** income, expenses, assets, and liabilities to the
  appropriate cell or to non-cellular accounts;
- **Act fairly between cells** — no cell may be unfairly
  prejudiced relative to others;
- **Not transfer** cellular assets to non-cellular accounts (or
  to another cell) without proper consideration and authority.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

The **statutory segregation** in Article 127YT only works if
directors **maintain it operationally**. If directors:

- Commingle assets;
- Fail to identify which cell is contracting;
- Transfer value between cells improperly;

the segregation breaks down in practice, exposing cells to
cross-claims and undermining the regime.

Article 127YR creates **personal liability** for directors who
fail to maintain segregation — backing up Article 127YT's
statutory effect with a director-level obligation.
