# Article 127FI — Decisions and Appeals

Article 127FI of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** sets
out how the JFSC **decides** Article 127FF consent applications
and the **appeal rights** of dissatisfied applicants.

## What Article 127FI covers

The JFSC must:

- **Decide** the application within a prescribed or reasonable
  period;
- **Notify** the applicant of its decision, giving **reasons**;
- Identify any **conditions** to which a consent is subject.

The applicant may **appeal** the decision (refusal or onerous
conditions) to the **Royal Court** within a prescribed period.
The Court considers whether the JFSC's decision was:

- **Lawful** (within powers);
- **Procedurally fair**;
- **Reasonable** on the material before the JFSC.

The Court may **affirm**, **reverse**, or **vary** the JFSC's
decision.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

JFSC consent is a significant **commercial gating event**.
Without a clear appeal mechanism, applicants would be **at the
mercy** of administrative decision-making. Article 127FI
preserves the basic **right to judicial review** of administrative
decisions affecting commercial rights, in a focused statutory
appeal form.

In practice, appeals are rare — JFSC and applicants typically
**negotiate** conditions during the consent process — but the
appeal right is the long-stop protection.
