# Article 5 — Articles of Association

Article 5 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** establishes
the **articles of association** — the company's internal-governance
rulebook, sitting alongside the memorandum as the second
constitutional document.

## What Article 5 says

The articles must:

- be **registered** alongside the memorandum;
- specify **the regulations of the company**;
- be in **English or French**;
- be **printed** (subject to the electronic-delivery exception);
- be **divided into paragraphs numbered consecutively**;
- be **signed by each subscriber in the presence of at least one
  witness**, who attests the signature and inserts the witness's
  own name and address.

The **Electronic Communications (Jersey) Law 2000** exemption
applies — electronically delivered articles need not be printed or
witnessed.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## What the articles cover

The articles set out the **internal-governance rules** of the
company. Typical content:

- **Share rights** (where the company has shares) — voting,
  dividend, distribution, redemption rights of each class;
- **Share-transfer mechanics** — pre-emption rights, board
  consent requirements, drag/tag-along provisions;
- **Member meetings** — quorum, notice, voting procedures (under
  Part 15 of the Companies Law);
- **Director appointment, removal, and proceedings** — number of
  directors, eligibility, term, board procedures;
- **Director conflict and disclosure** — operationalising
  Articles 74 and 75 of the Companies Law;
- **Distribution mechanics** — operationalising
  [Article 115](./distribution-restrictions.md) *(planned)* solvency
  test;
- **Indemnification** of directors and officers — operationalising
  [Article 77](./officer-indemnity.md) *(planned)*;
- **Winding-up** procedural detail.

A company **may adopt all or part** of the
[Standard Table](./standard-table.md) *(planned — Article 6)* — the
Minister-prescribed model articles — and customise from there.

## Memorandum vs articles — what goes where

Practitioners sometimes ask which provisions go in the memorandum
vs the articles. As a rough rule:

| Memorandum | Articles |
|---|---|
| Constitutional identity (name, type, status) | Operational governance |
| Public-facing key facts | Internal rules |
| Things that bind third parties dealing with the company | Things that bind members and directors among themselves |
| Limited-duration triggers (Article 4C, when used) | Procedural detail for executing those triggers |
| Capital structure summary | Capital-mechanics detail |

The **legal effect** of registered memorandum and articles is the
same under [Article 10](./effect-of-memorandum-articles.md) *(planned)*
— both bind the company and its members as if signed covenants.

## Alteration

Both the memorandum and the articles can be **altered by special
resolution** under [Article 11](./alteration-of-memorandum-articles.md)
*(planned)*. Alterations cannot increase member liability without
written consent.

## Modern practice

In practice:

- Articles are drafted from a **standard template** by the
  registered agent;
- Customisations cover client-specific governance, share-class
  structures, and any commercial requirements (e.g., investor
  protections in a fund vehicle's articles);
- Articles are **filed electronically** under the Electronic
  Communications Law exemption; physical printing-and-witnessing
  is rare.

## Pitfalls

- **Defaulting to the Standard Table without checking fit** — the
  Standard Table is suitable for **par-value companies without
  unlimited shares or guarantor members**; companies outside that
  description (most modern Jersey companies) need bespoke articles.
- **Memorandum-articles inconsistency** — provisions duplicated
  in both documents can create interpretive friction. Each
  provision should have one canonical home.
- **Failure to update articles** when the law changes — modern
  articles should reflect contemporary Jersey-law features
  (no-par-value mechanics, cell-company provisions where
  relevant, post-2019 substance considerations).
