# Article 127FD — Company to Apply to Court if Solvency Statement Not Made

Article 127FD of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]**
provides that **where directors cannot give the solvency
statement** for a Part 18B merger, the company **must apply to
the Royal Court** for the merger to proceed.

## What Article 127FD covers

If the directors of any merging body do **not** make a solvency
statement (under Article 127E) — for example because they cannot
honestly attest to post-merger solvency — the merger may
nevertheless proceed only if the **Court** sanctions it.

The application is made to the Royal Court and the Court may:

- **Sanction the merger** on terms (including required creditor
  protections);
- **Refuse to sanction** the merger;
- Order that **objecting creditors** be **paid out, secured, or
  given a guarantee** as a condition of the merger.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

The default Part 18B regime is **court-free** — but that
court-freeness rests on the directors' solvency certificate. Where
the directors cannot or will not certify solvency:

- Creditors **cannot rely on the statutory protection** of the
  directors' attestation;
- Court oversight is **substituted** — equivalent in protective
  function to the Court's role in a scheme of arrangement.

This is the safety valve that makes the regime workable for
**distressed mergers** (e.g. restructurings) while keeping the
fast track available for healthy mergers.
