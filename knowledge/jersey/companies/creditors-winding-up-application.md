# Article 156 — Application of Chapter 4 (Creditors' Winding Up)

Article 156 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** sets
out **when Chapter 4 (creditors' winding up) applies** — the
insolvency-driven route.

## What Article 156 covers

Chapter 4 applies where:

- The company is **insolvent** (or the directors cannot make a
  solvency statement under Article 146);
- A **creditor or other authorised person** has applied for
  creditors' winding up under Article 157A;
- The Court has **ordered** creditors' winding up under Article
  157C;
- A summary winding up has **converted** to creditors' winding up
  under Article 151.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

Creditors' winding up has become the **dominant insolvency
route** for Jersey companies — increasingly preferred over
désastre for company insolvency because:

- It provides a **company-law framework** familiar to international
  practitioners;
- It allows for **specialist liquidators** chosen by creditors;
- Its claw-back provisions (transactions at undervalue,
  preferences, wrongful trading) are well developed;
- It interfaces well with **cross-border insolvency** regimes.

Chapter 4 is the **insolvency core** of Part 21, comprising
Articles 156–186.
