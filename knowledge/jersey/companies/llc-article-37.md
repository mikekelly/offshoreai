# LLC Law 2018 Article 37 — Limitation on distribution

Article 37 imposes a **statutory solvency constraint**
on LLC distributions analogous to — but distinct from —
the [Article 115 cash-flow solvency test](./article-115-solvency-test.md)
under the Companies Law 1991.

## Substance

An LLC must **not make a distribution** if, after giving
effect to it:

1. the LLC would be **unable to pay its debts** as they
   fall due in the ordinary course of business; or
2. the **realisable value of the LLC's assets** would
   be less than the sum of its **liabilities** (other
   than members' contributions and accruals to members
   in respect of their interests).

The test applies at the time of the distribution. A
manager (or member-manager) who authorises a
distribution in breach of the limitation is potentially
liable to the LLC for the amount distributed in excess
of what was permissible.

## Comparison

| Provision | Cash-flow limb | Net-asset limb |
|---|---|---|
| **LLC Law Art 37** | Yes — debts as they fall due | Yes — assets vs liabilities excluding member contributions |
| **Companies Law Art 115** | Yes — debts as they fall due in 12 months | No standalone net-asset limb |

The LLC test is **bilaterally stricter** than the
Companies Law test: it adds the net-asset limb that
Companies Law 1991 dropped in the 2008 reforms.
