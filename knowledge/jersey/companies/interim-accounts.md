# Article 106 — Publication of Interim Accounts

Article 106 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** permits a
Jersey company to publish **interim accounts** during a financial
period — subject to the same standards as annual accounts where
applicable.

## What Article 106 covers

The Article permits companies to **publish interim accounts**
during a financial period, **subject to the same requirements
as annual accounts** where applicable.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## When interim accounts are published

Common scenarios:

- **Listed Jersey companies** publishing half-year or quarterly
  interim accounts per market-listing rules;
- **Fund vehicles** publishing periodic NAV-related accounts;
- **Bond-issuer companies** publishing interim financials per
  indenture covenants;
- **Specific transactional contexts** — e.g., interim accounts
  supporting a capital reduction or distribution decision.

## Standards

Interim accounts are typically **less detailed** than annual
accounts but follow the **same principles** — accuracy, fairness,
proper accounting standards. They are typically **unaudited**
(audit is annual).

## Pitfalls

- **Treating interim accounts as fully audited** when they're
  unaudited — disclose the audit status clearly.
- **Inconsistency** between interim and annual frameworks —
  use the same accounting policies.
