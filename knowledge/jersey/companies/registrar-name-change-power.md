# Article 15 — Power to Require Change of Name

Article 15 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** is the
**Registrar's post-incorporation name-change power**: where a
company's existing name turns out to be misleading or undesirable,
the Registrar can require the company to change it.

## What Article 15 says

- The **Registrar may direct** a company to change its name where
  the name is **misleading** or **undesirable**;
- The company has **three months to comply** (or longer if
  permitted);
- The company **may appeal** within **21 days**;
- Court confirmation requires a minimum **28-day** compliance
  period if the Court upholds the direction.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## When the power is used

The Registrar's veto in [Article 13](name-requirements.md) catches
problematic names **before incorporation**. Article 15 is the
catch-up power for names that:

- **passed Article 13** because the issue wasn't apparent at
  incorporation;
- **became misleading** later because of a change in the
  company's circumstances (e.g., a company licensed for a
  regulated activity loses its licence but keeps "Bank" in its
  name);
- **become similar to another name** because of subsequent
  incorporations or third-party trade-mark issues.

In practice the power is rarely exercised — Article 13 catches
most issues at the gate. Article 15 directions tend to follow
specific events:

- **Loss of regulated status** — a name containing "Bank",
  "Trust", "Insurance" etc. without the underlying licence;
- **Successful trade-mark challenge** — though typically resolved
  by Court order rather than Registrar direction;
- **Public-interest concern** emerging after incorporation.

## Procedure

1. **Registrar's direction** — written notice to the company
   specifying the issue and the required change.
2. **Three-month compliance window** (or longer if the Registrar
   extends).
3. **Appeal** within 21 days to the Court if the company
   disagrees.
4. **Court confirmation or override** — Court can confirm,
   modify, or set aside the direction.
5. **Implementation** — if confirmed, the company changes its
   name following the [Article 14](change-of-name.md) procedure.

## Appeal grounds

The Article expressly permits appeal on grounds that the direction
is **unreasonable**. The Court's review:

- Considers the **public-interest grounds** advanced by the
  Registrar;
- Considers the **practical and commercial burden** on the
  company;
- Balances the two.

A direction will be set aside if it is **disproportionate** to the
problem identified, or if the Registrar's reasoning is flawed.

## Pitfalls

- **Ignoring the direction**. Non-compliance after the
  compliance window can expose officers to offences under the
  Companies Law and can lead the Registrar to **strike the
  company off** under
  [Article 205](registrar-strike-off.md) *(planned)*.
- **Treating it as administrative when it should be appealed** —
  if the company has a substantive defence, file the appeal in
  the 21-day window.
- **Forgetting trade-mark issues**. A direction triggered by
  trade-mark conflict suggests the underlying conflict needs
  resolution before reincorporation under any name similar to
  the one the Registrar found problematic.
