# Article 166 — Application of the Law Relating to Désastre

Article 166 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** applies
specified **provisions of the désastre law** to creditors'
winding up, supplementing the companies-law mechanics with
established insolvency-law tools.

## What Article 166 covers

Certain provisions of the **Bankruptcy (Désastre) (Jersey) Law
1990** apply to creditors' winding up, including:

- **Proof of debt** procedures and admission/rejection of
  claims;
- **Set-off** rules where the company and a creditor have mutual
  dealings;
- **Preferential debts** ranking;
- **Disclaimer** of unprofitable assets (mirroring Article 171);
- **Avoidance** of transactions in specified circumstances.

The détail is set out in regulations and rules of court.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

Creditors' winding up benefits from the **well-developed
mechanics** of the désastre regime without having to duplicate
them in the Companies Law:

- **Proof of debt** procedures are technical and benefit from
  consistent application;
- **Set-off** rules need careful definition;
- **Cross-regime** consistency reduces forum-shopping incentives.

Article 166 keeps Jersey insolvency law **coherent** by sharing
foundational concepts between the two regimes.
