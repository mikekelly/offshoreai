# Article 81 — Secretary

Article 81 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** requires
a Jersey company to have a **secretary** — a specific officer
distinct from the directors.

## What Article 81 covers

A company must have a **secretary**.

The secretary's role typically includes:

- **Maintaining** the company's statutory records;
- **Convening** board and member meetings;
- **Keeping** minutes;
- **Filing** documents with the Registrar;
- **Compliance** monitoring for company-law obligations;
- **Acting as a contact point** for the Registrar and other
  authorities.

(For the verbatim text see [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## The secretary's position

The secretary is an **officer** of the company (within the
[Article 1 "officer" definition](./companies-interpretation.md)) but
is **distinct from directors**:

- Secretaries don't have **director-style fiduciary duties** of
  the same broad type;
- Secretaries are not part of the **directorial decision-making**
  body;
- The secretary's role is **administrative and compliance-
  oriented**.

But the secretary does have specific Companies Law
**responsibilities** for filings, meetings, and records — and
breach can produce offences.

## Who can be secretary

Article 82 covers qualifications. In practice the secretary is:

- A **JFSC-regulated trust company business** providing
  secretarial services as part of the registered-agent role;
- A **specialist company secretary firm**;
- An **individual** with appropriate qualifications and
  experience;
- Where appropriate, a **director** acting as secretary
  concurrently (though in some contexts a separation is
  expected).

## Single-secretary vs joint

A company may have **one or more** secretaries. In small Jersey
companies, a single secretary is typical. In larger or
listed-company contexts, a **deputy** or **assistant**
secretarial structure may operate, with the principal secretary
having ultimate responsibility.

## Pitfalls

- **No secretary** — strict breach. Companies should always
  have one. The registered agent typically fills the role by
  default.
- **Confusing secretary role with director role** — they are
  distinct officers with different duties.
