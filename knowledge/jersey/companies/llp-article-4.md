# LLP Law 2017 Article 4 — Liability of a limited liability partnership

Article 4 establishes that the **LLP itself is liable** for
debts and losses that would otherwise bind an ordinary
partnership — and that this liability is satisfied from LLP
property.

## Key provisions

- The LLP is liable for any debt or other liability that
  would, in the case of an ordinary partnership, bind the
  partners;
- Such liability is **satisfied from LLP property**;
- Where LLP property is insufficient, the
  [Article 12 claw-back](./llp-article-12.md) provisions
  may apply to recover unauthorised partner withdrawals.

## Operational significance

The LLP-as-debtor design is what gives the LLP its "limited
liability" character — claims against the partnership flow
to LLP property, not to partner personal estates. Partner
liability is addressed in [Article 5](./llp-article-5.md).
