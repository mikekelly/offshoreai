# Article 165 — Costs of Creditors' Winding Up

Article 165 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** sets
the **priority of liquidation costs and expenses** in a
creditors' winding up.

## What Article 165 covers

In creditors' winding up, costs and expenses are paid in the
following priority:

1. **Liquidator's remuneration**;
2. **Liquidator's expenses** (necessary outlays);
3. **Legal and professional costs** incurred by the liquidator;
4. **Costs of the application** for winding up (if court-
   ordered);
5. **Costs of any creditor or member** awarded costs by the
   Court.

Costs are paid **out of the company's assets** in priority to
ordinary unsecured creditors but **after** secured creditors
realising their security.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

The **priority of liquidation costs** is what makes professional
liquidator appointment commercially viable:

- Without cost priority, no insolvency practitioner would take
  on an insolvent liquidation;
- The creditor body **collectively benefits** from a competent
  liquidator (recovery, claw-back, distribution administration);
- The cost is **proportionate** to assets — small estates have
  smaller liquidator fees.
