# Article 17A — Calculation of Number of Members

Article 17A of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** specifies
**how members are counted** for the purposes of the 30-member
thresholds in Articles 3, 16, and 17. The Article carves out
employee-related members and treats joint shareholders as one.

## What Article 17A says

**Excluded** from the member count:

- **Directors or employees** of the company;
- **Directors or employees** of the company's **subsidiaries** or
  **related entities**;
- **Former employees** of any of the above who remain members.

**Joint shareholders** count as a **single member**.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why the carve-outs

The 30-member threshold is intended to distinguish:

- a **close-held private company** with a small group of genuine
  owners; from
- a **company with a wider membership** that resembles a public
  participant.

**Employee-shareholders** don't move the company along that
spectrum — they're holding shares because of their employment, not
because they're external investors. So they're excluded from the
count.

The **joint-shareholder** rule treats a holding under multiple
names (e.g., spouses jointly holding a single share parcel) as one
person for member-counting, not as multiple.

## Practical implications

For a typical Jersey private company:

- **Founders / external shareholders** — count.
- **Employee-shareholders under an option scheme** — don't count.
- **Joint holders** (e.g., family trusts where two trustees hold
  jointly) — count as one.
- **Bare nominees** — typically count as one (the underlying
  beneficial holder is what matters, but the legal title structure
  matters for register entries).
- **Former employees still on the register** — don't count.

## Interaction with the 30-member thresholds

The 30-member triggers in:

- **Article 3** (formation — Registrar can restrict applications
  >30 unless JFSC approval);
- **Article 16** (public → private conversion threshold);
- **Article 17** (private → public auto-conversion threshold);

all reference the Article 17A counting rules. So a company with
100 employee-shareholders, 5 external founders, and 2 joint
holders counts as **6 members** (5 founders + 1 joint pair) for
these thresholds, not 107.

## Article 17D Order — abolition of the 30-member limit

The States has the power under
[Article 17D](./30-member-limit-abolition.md) to abolish the
30-member limit for private companies. That Order has historically
been made. Check the current state of the Order before applying
the 30-member rule.

## Pitfalls

- **Counting employee-shareholders** in the threshold by
  mistake — they're excluded. A company that thinks it has 35
  members may actually have only 5 for Article 17 purposes.
- **Joint-holder treatment** in family contexts — joint holdings
  by trustees, spouses, or family-office structures count as
  one, but be careful with the deed's interpretation.
- **Former-employee complications** — someone who was an
  employee and is now retired but retains their shares is still
  excluded. Their status as a former employee continues to drive
  the carve-out.
