# Article 113Q — Application of Part 19 to Market Traded Companies

Article 113Q of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** extends
the **investigative provisions of Part 19** (Investigations) to
**market-traded companies**.

## What Article 113Q covers

**Investigative provisions in Part 19** apply to **market-traded
companies** with appropriate modifications.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

Part 19 of the Companies Law governs **JFSC investigations** of
companies generally. Article 113Q ensures these investigative
powers also apply to **market-traded** Jersey companies (those
whose shares are admitted to trading on a recognised market):

- Listed Jersey companies on the LSE, Euronext, NASDAQ, etc.;
- AIM-listed Jersey companies;
- Jersey companies with admission on other recognised exchanges.

The application is with **appropriate modifications** to fit the
listed-company context.

## Pitfalls

- Listed Jersey companies subject to **multiple regulatory
  regimes** — Jersey, market, and home-of-listing rules can
  overlap.
