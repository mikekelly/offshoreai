# Article 103 — Accounting Records

Article 103 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** requires
every Jersey company to maintain **adequate accounting records**
— the operational ledgers and books that document transactions.

## What Article 103 says

Every company must maintain **adequate accounting records**
sufficient to:

- **show and explain transactions**;
- record **financial position**;
- record **details of assets, liabilities**;
- record **goods sold and purchased**.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## What's required

The standard is "**adequate to show and explain**":

- **Transaction-level** records — each financial event;
- **Asset and liability** records — balance-sheet items;
- **Goods purchased and sold** — for trading companies;
- **Sufficient detail** to support preparation of statutory
  accounts and an audit (where required).

The Companies Law doesn't prescribe **specific accounting
standards** — the company chooses an appropriate framework
(typically IFRS, UK GAAP, US GAAP, or another generally-accepted
standard).

## Modern practice

Most Jersey companies maintain accounting records:

- **Electronically** through accounting software;
- Updated **monthly or more frequently**;
- Held by the **registered agent** or by a specialist
  accounting firm;
- **Backed up** to satisfy reconstruction needs.
