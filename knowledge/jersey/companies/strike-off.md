# Article 205 — Registrar's Powers to Strike Companies Off Register

Article 205 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** gives
the Registrar power to **strike companies off the register** for
non-compliance and other prescribed grounds.

## What Article 205 covers

The Registrar may strike a company off the register where:

- The company has **failed to file** annual confirmation
  statements or other required filings for a prescribed period;
- The Registrar has **reason to believe** the company is no
  longer carrying on business;
- The company has **failed to pay** annual fees;
- Other prescribed grounds apply.

Before striking off, the Registrar must:

- **Give notice** to the company at its registered office;
- **Give notice to directors** at their addresses;
- **Publish notice** of intention to strike off;
- Allow a **prescribed period** for objection or remedy.

On strike-off, the company is **dissolved** — it ceases to
exist as a legal person.

**Restoration** is possible by court order on application of any
person with a legitimate interest, within a prescribed period.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

Strike-off is the **non-insolvency dissolution** route:

- For companies that have stopped trading without formal
  winding up;
- For companies that have been **abandoned** by their owners;
- For **defunct companies** clogging the register.

It is **simpler and cheaper** than formal winding up, but:

- It carries the **risk** that assets and liabilities survive
  the dissolution in unhelpful ways;
- It can be **misused** to evade obligations (though restoration
  is available);
- **Bona vacantia** may pass assets to the Crown.
