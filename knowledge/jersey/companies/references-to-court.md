# Article 186A — References to the Court

Article 186A of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** is a
**procedural interpretation provision**: it explains how
references to "the Court" in Part 21 are construed.

## What Article 186A covers

References in Part 21 to "the Court" mean the **Royal Court** of
Jersey, exercising its winding-up jurisdiction. The Royal Court
acts through:

- A **single judge** (Bailiff, Deputy Bailiff, or Commissioner)
  in routine winding-up matters;
- A **bench** (judge plus Jurats) in more substantive matters.

The Royal Court's **Insolvency Division** (or equivalent in
practice) deals with winding-up applications, claw-back
litigation, and supervisory matters.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

A consolidated reference rule prevents the need to keep
specifying which court is meant in every other article. It also
confirms that **only the Royal Court** has jurisdiction over
Jersey winding-up matters (not magistrate or lower courts).
