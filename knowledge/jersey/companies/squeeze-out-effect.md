# Article 118 — Effect of Notice under Article 117

Article 118 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** sets out
**what happens once an Article 117 squeeze-out notice has been
served**: how title to the dissenting shares passes to the
offeror, how the consideration is paid, and what protections
remain for the dissenting shareholder.

## What Article 118 covers

Six weeks after the **Article 117 notice** is served (provided no
successful application has been made to the Court under Article
121), the **offeror is entitled and bound** to acquire the
dissenting shares on the offer terms.

The mechanics:

- The offeror **pays the consideration** to the company (the
  target) to hold on trust for the dissenting shareholder;
- The offeror **delivers an instrument of transfer** (executed on
  behalf of the dissenting shareholder) to the target;
- The target **registers the transfer** so that the offeror
  becomes the registered holder;
- The consideration is **held by the target** until the dissenting
  shareholder claims it (or until it is paid into court after a
  prescribed period).

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

The Article 118 mechanism is what makes the squeeze-out **self-
executing**:

- No further cooperation from the dissenting shareholder is
  needed — the offeror executes the transfer "on their behalf".
- The dissenting shareholder is **fully protected on the money
  side**: the consideration is held on **statutory trust** by the
  target, separate from the target's own assets.
- The transaction is **not held hostage** by lost shareholders,
  estates without an executor, or simple refusals.

## After Article 118 takes effect

The dissenting shareholder ceases to be a shareholder of the
target. Their rights are now **money rights against the trust
held by the target**:

- They may claim the consideration at any time during the trust
  period;
- After a prescribed period of unclaimed amounts, the target
  typically pays the amounts into **court** or to a designated
  agency in line with the regulations.

## Pitfalls

- **Procedural failures** — failure to serve in prescribed form,
  miscount of 90%, or premature execution before the 6-week
  window can void the squeeze-out.
- **Court application during the 6-week window** — Article 121
  applications **stay** the squeeze-out pending the court's
  decision.
