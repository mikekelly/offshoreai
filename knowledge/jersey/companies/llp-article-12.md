# LLP Law 2017 Article 12 — Specified solvency statement

Article 12 establishes the **claw-back regime** — a partner
withdrawing property from the LLP must do so against a
documented solvency statement, or face repayment obligation
if the withdrawal was unauthorised or made when the LLP was
insolvent.

## Key provisions

- Partners may make **specified solvency declarations**
  before authorising withdrawals;
- Withdrawals **without a current solvency statement** are
  restricted;
- **Repayment obligations** arise for unauthorised
  withdrawals — partners must repay sums improperly
  withdrawn;
- Insolvency at the time of withdrawal triggers retroactive
  repayment obligations regardless of the solvency
  statement.

## Operational significance

This is the **partner-protection counterpart** to the
limited-liability shield in [Article 5](./llp-article-5.md).
The shield does not protect partners who have taken
distributions inconsistent with the LLP's solvency
position — claw-back ensures creditors are not left
without recourse where the LLP was effectively insolvent
at the time of distribution.

Comparable to (but mechanically distinct from) the
[CJL 1991 Article 115 cash-flow solvency test](./companies-law-1991.md)
for company distributions.
