# Article 148 — Effect on Status of Company (Summary Winding Up)

Article 148 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]**
specifies the **legal status of the company** during summary
winding up.

## What Article 148 covers

During summary winding up:

- The company **continues in existence** as a legal entity;
- The company **may continue to carry on business** only so far as
  is necessary for the **beneficial winding up**;
- The corporate **powers** continue but are exercised by the
  liquidator;
- The **directors' powers cease** on commencement, except as
  delegated by the liquidator;
- The **registered office and corporate identity** continue.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

The company is **not dissolved** by commencement of winding up —
dissolution comes only at the **end** of the winding up (Article
150). During the winding up, the company:

- **Owns** its assets (which the liquidator realises);
- **Owes** its debts (which the liquidator pays);
- **Holds** its contracts (which the liquidator performs,
  disclaims, or terminates);
- **Continues** as a defendant in litigation.

This continuity allows the liquidator to **operate effectively**
— it would be impractical to wind up an entity that had ceased
to exist.
