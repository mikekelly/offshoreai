# Article 127R — Notice to Creditors of Application for Continuance Overseas

Article 127R of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** is
the **creditor-notification** step for an outbound continuance —
ensuring creditors have a chance to react before their debtor
leaves Jersey.

## What Article 127R covers

A Jersey company applying to continue overseas must give
**notice** to its creditors:

- In **prescribed form**;
- Within a prescribed period of the application;
- By **direct notice** to known creditors above a prescribed
  threshold;
- By **public notice** (e.g. publication in the JEP and on the
  registrar's website) to other creditors.

The notice typically includes:

- The fact and destination of the continuance;
- The expected effective date;
- The creditor's right to **object** to the JFSC;
- The basis on which the company can continue (solvency
  statement, etc.).

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

Outbound continuance means creditors will, after the effective
date, be **dealing with a foreign entity** under **foreign law**:

- Enforcement of claims may require foreign-court action;
- Insolvency proceedings would be governed by foreign law;
- Foreign tax / regulatory regimes may complicate recovery.

Creditors have a **legitimate interest** in knowing this is
happening and in being able to:

- **Object** to the JFSC (raising specific concerns);
- **Accelerate** outstanding debts under contractual provisions
  triggered by redomicile;
- **Negotiate** security or guarantees as a condition of
  continuing the relationship.

Article 127R is the **disclosure backbone** that enables all of
these.
