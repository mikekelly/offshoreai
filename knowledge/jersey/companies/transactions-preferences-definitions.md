# Article 176B — Definitions Relating to Transactions at an Undervalue and Preferences

Article 176B of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** is
the **interpretation provision** for the claw-back regime —
particularly the "connected persons" definition.

## What Article 176B covers

Key definitions include:

- **"Connected person"** — broadly:
  - A **director** of the company (or a person who was a
    director within a prescribed period before the transaction);
  - An **associate** of a director;
  - A **shadow director**;
  - A **holding body, subsidiary, or fellow subsidiary** of the
    company;
  - **Associates** of the connected persons above (family,
    business partners, trustees, etc.).
- **"Associate"** — typically includes spouses, civil partners,
  cohabitees, parents, children, siblings, business partners,
  and entities controlled by such persons.
- **"Relevant period"** — the time before commencement of
  winding up during which transactions are vulnerable:
  - **5 years** for connected-person transactions at undervalue;
  - **6 months** for unconnected-person transactions at
    undervalue;
  - **5 years** for connected-person preferences;
  - **12 months** for unconnected-person preferences.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

The **connected-person concept** is the **key analytical step**
in any claw-back inquiry:

- It **extends the claw-back period** significantly (often 10×
  for transactions at undervalue);
- It **flips the burden** on insolvency (presumption of
  insolvency for undervalue) and on desire (presumption for
  preferences);
- It captures **economic substance** of insider dealing even
  through nominees or controlled entities.

The breadth of the definition reflects **policy hostility**
toward insider transactions in the run-up to insolvency.
