# Article 95ZA — Circulation of Written Resolutions Proposed by Directors

Article 95ZA of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** provides
the mechanism for **directors to propose written resolutions** for
member approval — circulated to members with explanatory
materials.

## What Article 95ZA says

Directors may **propose written resolutions** to members.

The company must **circulate the proposal** to all eligible
members with **explanatory materials**.

Members then have a **specified period** to **approve or reject**
the proposal in writing.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## The mechanism

1. **Directors propose** a resolution — typically by board
   resolution authorising circulation.
2. **Company circulates** the proposed resolution to all
   eligible members, with:
   - The **proposed text**;
   - **Explanatory materials** explaining the context, rationale,
     and director recommendation;
   - **Voting procedures** — how members signify approval or
     rejection;
   - **Deadline** for response.
3. **Members respond** in writing within the deadline.
4. **Result** — the resolution passes if it reaches the
   required threshold (typically the same as for a meeting-passed
   resolution).

## Difference from Article 95

The basic [Article 95](./written-resolutions.md) mechanism requires
**unanimous member consent** — every member must sign. The
Article 95ZA mechanism requires only the **threshold for the
resolution type** (e.g., 75% for special resolutions, simple
majority for ordinary).

Article 95ZA is therefore the **operationally lighter** route
for companies with many members.

## When used

Common scenarios:

- **Public-company decisions** that need member approval but
  where convening an AGM is impractical;
- **Listed-company** matters between AGMs;
- **Multi-member private companies** where unanimous Article 95
  signing isn't realistic.
