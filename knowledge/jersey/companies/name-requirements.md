# Article 13 — Requirements as to Names

Article 13 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** sets the
**naming rules** for Jersey companies — what names are permitted,
what designations are required, and the Registrar's power to refuse
inappropriate names.

## What Article 13 says

### Registrar's veto

The Registrar **may refuse to register** a name that is:

- **misleading** (e.g., suggesting a regulated business the
  company is not licensed for);
- **undesirable** (e.g., offensive, contrary to public policy,
  or too similar to an existing company name).

### Mandatory designations

**Limited companies** must use one of:

- "**Limited**";
- "**Ltd**";
- "**avec responsabilité limitée**" (the French equivalent);
- "**a.r.l.**" (abbreviation).

**Public limited companies** may use:

- "**public limited company**"; or
- "**PLC**" / "**plc**".

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why the designations matter

The "**Limited**" / "**Ltd**" suffix is a **third-party warning**:
it tells any counterparty that the company's members enjoy
limited liability and that recourse on the company's debts is
generally limited to the company's own assets (subject to
guarantees, personal undertakings, etc.).

A limited Jersey company that **omits the suffix** in its
correspondence or contracts may face problems under
[Article 69](./name-on-correspondence.md) *(planned — name on
correspondence)*. Officers can be personally liable on the
contract.

## French-language permission

The French-language designation ("avec responsabilité limitée" or
"a.r.l.") reflects Jersey's bilingual heritage. In modern practice
this is rarely used — most Jersey companies adopt English
suffixes — but the option remains for clients with civil-law
heritage or specific preferences.

## Restricted words

In addition to the Article 13 statutory tests, the Registrar
maintains a list of **restricted words** that require specific
clearance:

- Words suggesting **regulated business** (Bank, Insurance,
  Trust, Trustee, etc.) — typically require evidence of the
  underlying regulatory permission;
- Words suggesting **government or royal connection** ("Royal",
  "Crown", "Jersey" used in misleading ways);
- Words suggesting **chartered or professional** status
  (Chartered, Institute, etc.) without underlying basis.

The Registrar's published guidance lists current restrictions.
Check before proposing a name.

## Name-availability check

The first step in any Jersey incorporation:

1. **Check name availability** on the JFSC online portal — the
   Registrar's database is searchable.
2. **Reserve the name** if appropriate (a separate process
   permits reservation pending incorporation).
3. **Submit the incorporation** with the reserved or available
   name.

## Pitfalls

- **Name too similar to existing** — most-frequent reason for
  rejection. The Registrar's similarity test is contextual but
  conservative.
- **Restricted-word usage without clearance** — proposing
  "X Trust Limited" without trust-company licence registration
  is rejected.
- **Omitting suffix in correspondence** after incorporation —
  the suffix must appear in the company's name on letterhead,
  contracts, websites; see Article 69.
