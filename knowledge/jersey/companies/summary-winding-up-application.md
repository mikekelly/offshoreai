# Article 145 — Application of Chapter 2 (Summary Winding Up)

Article 145 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** sets
out **when Chapter 2 (summary winding up) applies** — the
solvent-liquidation route.

## What Article 145 covers

Chapter 2 applies where a Jersey company is to be wound up as a
**solvent company**:

- The company can **pay its debts in full** within a prescribed
  period (typically 12 months) of commencement;
- The members **resolve** to wind it up under Chapter 2;
- The directors give a **solvency statement** supporting the
  procedure.

If the company is **not solvent**, summary winding up is
unavailable; the creditors' winding up under Chapter 4 must be
used instead.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

Summary winding up is the **standard route for solvent
liquidation** — the simplest, fastest, cheapest way to wind up
a company that has served its purpose:

- **Member-driven** (special resolution);
- **Member-appointed liquidator**;
- **No court involvement** in the normal case;
- **Final distribution** to members after creditors paid.

A Jersey company **at the end of its useful life** that has
positive net assets and can pay all creditors typically uses
summary winding up.
