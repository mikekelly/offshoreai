# Takeovers Panel Law 2009 Article 15 — Enforcement by the Court

Article 15 is the **Court enforcement mechanism** — when
Panel sanctions or directions are not complied with, or
where the Panel anticipates contravention, the Panel may
apply to the [Royal Court](../legal-system/royal-court.md)
for an order.

## Key provisions

- On Panel application, the Court may make orders if:
  - "There is a reasonable likelihood that a person will
    contravene a requirement" under the Law, **or**
  - A person has contravened such a requirement.
- The Court may grant **injunctions** and **other orders**
  it considers appropriate.
- **Only the Panel** may apply for these orders — private
  parties cannot directly seek Court enforcement of Panel
  rules.

## Operational significance

Court enforcement is the **last-resort tool** in the Panel
arsenal. Most rule-compliance issues are resolved within the
Panel process — through directions, rulings, hearings, and
sanctions. Court enforcement is reserved for:

- Anticipatory injunctions where pre-emptive intervention is
  needed;
- Post-hoc enforcement where Panel sanctions have not
  produced compliance;
- Recovery of compensation under
  [Article 14](./tmp-article-14.md) orders unpaid.

The **Panel-only standing** is significant — parties to a
takeover cannot bypass the Panel by going directly to the
Royal Court on Code matters.
