# Article 192 — Liability as Contributories of Present and Past Members

Article 192 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]**
defines the **contributory liability** of present and past
members of the company in winding up.

## What Article 192 covers

Present and past members are **liable as contributories** to the
extent of:

- **Unpaid amounts on their shares** (for **par-value
  companies**);
- The **amount unpaid** on their commitments (for **guarantee
  companies**);
- **Other liabilities** specified in the memorandum / articles.

Past members:

- Are liable **only if** they ceased to be members **within 12
  months** before commencement of winding up;
- Are liable **only for debts** contracted while they were
  members.

For **limited companies**, members are **not liable** for amounts
beyond their unpaid commitments — this is the **central
protection** of limited liability.

For **unlimited companies**, members may be **personally liable**
for company debts without limit.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

The contributory regime is what makes **partly paid shares**
and **guarantee companies** work in insolvency:

- The liquidator can **call up** unpaid amounts on shares to
  pay creditors;
- The liquidator can **demand** members' guarantee
  contributions.

For **fully paid shares in a limited company**, there is no
further contributory liability — which is the **classic
shareholder protection**.
