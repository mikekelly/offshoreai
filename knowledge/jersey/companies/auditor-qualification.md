# Article 112 — Qualification Under Rules of Recognized Professional Bodies

Article 112 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** addresses
qualification of auditors through **recognised professional
bodies**.

## What Article 112 covers

Individuals qualifying as auditors through rules of **recognised
professional bodies** (such as accounting institutes) may
**register with the Commission**.

The Commission assesses whether such qualifications **meet
standards equivalent** to those it would otherwise require for
auditor registration and approval.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Recognised professional bodies

Typical recognised bodies whose qualifications are accepted
include:

- **ICAEW** (Institute of Chartered Accountants in England and
  Wales);
- **ICAS** (Institute of Chartered Accountants of Scotland);
- **Chartered Accountants Ireland**;
- **ACCA** (Association of Chartered Certified Accountants);
- **Other international accountancy bodies** as recognised by
  the JFSC.

The exact list of recognised bodies is set by JFSC published
guidance; it evolves over time.

## Equivalence assessment

The Commission assesses whether the foreign / non-listed
qualification meets the standards expected. The assessment looks
at:

- **Training and examination** standards;
- **Ongoing CPD** requirements;
- **Disciplinary regime** for breaches;
- **Independence** requirements applied by the home body.

## Pitfalls

- **Non-recognised qualifications** — won't support Article 112
  registration; the alternative-competence route under Article
  111 must be used.
