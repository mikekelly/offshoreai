# Article 194 — Disposal of Records

Article 194 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** covers
**what happens to the company's records** after dissolution.

## What Article 194 covers

After dissolution of a company following winding up:

- The liquidator must **retain** the company's records for a
  prescribed period (commonly 10 years);
- After the retention period, the records may be **destroyed**
  or **transferred** to the registrar / public archives;
- **Some records** may be subject to longer retention for
  regulatory or tax purposes;
- Liquidator personal **records of the liquidation** may have
  separate retention requirements under professional rules.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

After dissolution:

- The company no longer exists, but its **historical records**
  may still be relevant for:
  - **Litigation** (e.g. by former counterparties);
  - **Tax investigations** of former officers;
  - **Regulatory inquiries**;
  - **Historical research** or due diligence.

Article 194 balances **record availability** against **storage
costs** through the retention period framework.
