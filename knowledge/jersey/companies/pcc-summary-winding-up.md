# Article 127YV — Effect of Commencement of Summary Winding Up of Protected Cell Company

Article 127YV of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]**
specifies what happens on a **summary winding-up of a PCC** —
preserving the segregation principle in the liquidation context.

## What Article 127YV covers

On commencement of a summary winding-up of a PCC:

- The **liquidator** takes control of all the PCC's assets;
- The liquidator must **maintain segregation** of cellular and
  non-cellular assets;
- **Cellular liabilities** are paid from the relevant cell's
  cellular assets first;
- **Non-cellular liabilities** are paid from non-cellular assets
  first;
- **Surplus cellular assets** (after meeting cellular liabilities)
  are typically distributed to cellular members;
- **Surplus non-cellular assets** are distributed to core members;
- **Shortfalls** in cellular assets to meet cellular liabilities
  may be made up from non-cellular assets (if the articles so
  provide) — otherwise cellular creditors take a haircut.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

The **single most important moment** for testing PCC
segregation is at the point of insolvency / winding-up:

- **Creditors of failed cells** want to claim against other
  cells' assets — Article 127YV says they cannot;
- **Creditors of healthy cells** want to know they are
  protected — Article 127YV gives that protection;
- **Members of healthy cells** want to receive their share of
  surplus — Article 127YV ensures cell-specific surpluses go to
  cell-specific members.

The article is **what makes the PCC commercially viable** — the
segregation must hold up in liquidation or the regime is
worthless.

## Cell-only winding-up

Some PCC articles provide for **winding-up of a single cell**
without winding up the whole PCC. The mechanics (set out in
Regulations or articles) typically involve:

- A **cell-specific resolution**;
- **Cellular creditor notice and payment**;
- **Distribution** of cellular surplus to cellular members;
- **Termination** of the cell as a statutory compartment without
  affecting the PCC or other cells.
