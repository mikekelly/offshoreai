# Article 127FB — Objection by Member

Article 127FB of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** is
the **member's right to challenge a merger in the Royal Court**.

## What Article 127FB covers

A **member of a merging body** (whether they voted for, against,
or did not vote on the special resolution) may apply to the
**Royal Court** within a prescribed period (commonly 21 days
after the resolution) for an order that the merger should not be
made, or should be made on different terms.

The Court may make any order it thinks fit, including:

- **Cancellation** of the merger;
- **Modification** of the merger terms;
- **Acquisition** of the objecting member's shares on terms set
  by the Court (a buyout exit).

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

A merger approved by the requisite super-majority would otherwise
bind dissentients with no further oversight. Article 127FB is the
**safety valve**: a court hearing where the objecting member can
show that:

- The merger was approved with **inadequate or misleading
  information**;
- The merger **unfairly prejudices** their interests;
- The merger is **outside the powers** of the company;
- The directors **breached duty** in recommending it.

In practice, the most common outcome of a successful objection is
not cancellation but a **court-ordered buyout** — the objector
takes cash at a court-set price and the merger proceeds.
