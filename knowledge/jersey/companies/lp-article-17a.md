# LP Law 1994 Article 17A — Remedies against partner

Partnership agreements may impose **enforceable sanctions**
on partners for breach of obligations — without restriction
based on the sanctions being "penal" in character.

This is significant because Jersey law (like English law)
generally treats "penal" contractual provisions as
unenforceable. Article 17A disapplies that limitation in
the LP context, supporting the use of:

- **Default-interest** clauses for missed capital calls;
- **Forced-sale** mechanisms for defaulting partners;
- **Forfeiture** of partnership interest;
- **Liquidated-damages** clauses.

These are standard provisions in PE-LP agreements
internationally; Article 17A confirms their Jersey-law
enforceability.
