# Article 211 — Relief for Private Companies

Article 211 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]**
provides **specific reliefs and exemptions** for **private
companies** from some general Companies Law requirements.

## What Article 211 covers

Private companies (as defined in Article 3B) are exempt from or
have modified treatment of:

- **Prospectus** requirements (Articles 29 onwards) — private
  companies generally cannot offer securities to the public;
- **Some filing obligations** (e.g. simplified annual confirmation
  statement filings);
- **Members' written-resolution mechanisms** (some apply only to
  private companies);
- **Other prescribed reliefs** appropriate to the private-company
  context.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

Private companies make up the **vast majority** of Jersey
companies:

- They have **closed shareholder bases**;
- They do not engage with **public capital markets**;
- They have **simpler governance** needs.

Applying the full panoply of public-company requirements would
be **disproportionate**. Article 211 calibrates the regime to fit
private-company realities.
