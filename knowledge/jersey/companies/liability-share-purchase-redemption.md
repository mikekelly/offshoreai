# Article 181 — Liability in Respect of Purchase or Redemption of Shares

Article 181 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** imposes
**potential liability** in respect of pre-winding-up **share
purchases or redemptions** that, in hindsight, prejudiced
creditors.

## What Article 181 covers

Where the company:

- **Purchased or redeemed** its own shares (under Article 55 or
  Article 57) within a prescribed period before winding up;
- Subsequently goes into **insolvent winding up**;
- The directors' **solvency statement** for the purchase/
  redemption is shown to have lacked reasonable grounds;

the Court may make orders for:

- **Recovery from selling shareholders** of the consideration paid
  to them;
- **Personal liability of directors** who gave the solvency
  statement;
- **Contribution to the assets** of the company.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

Buybacks and redemptions are **distributions to members** that
in substance return capital. If made when the company was already
struggling:

- **Selling shareholders** were preferred over creditors;
- The company's **going-concern position** was made worse;
- The director **solvency statement** was wrong.

Article 181 lets the liquidator unwind these transactions and
recover the value for creditors. Selling shareholders may have
to repay sums they had treated as theirs.
