# Article 113J — Exemption from Liability for Damages

Article 113J of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** provides
a **limited liability exemption** for recognised auditors acting
in good faith.

## What Article 113J covers

Recognised auditors receive **protection from damages liability**
when acting **reasonably in compliance** with:

- Commission regulations; or
- professional-body rules.

**Except** in cases of:

- **gross negligence**; or
- **wilful misconduct**.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## The protection

The Article shields auditors from damages claims **for ordinary
errors** committed while:

- Acting in **compliance with applicable rules**;
- In **good faith**;
- With **reasonable** professional judgment.

This recognises that audit is **judgment-based** — auditors who
make reasonable calls that turn out to be wrong shouldn't face
unlimited liability for honest mistakes.

## The irreducible core

The protection does **not** extend to:

- **Gross negligence** — substantial departures from
  professional standards;
- **Wilful misconduct** — deliberate misbehaviour;
- **Fraud** — by general principle.

Audit firms still need **professional indemnity insurance** —
the Article 113J protection is a narrow shield, not comprehensive
immunity.

## Pitfalls

- **Treating Article 113J as broad immunity** — it isn't. Gross
  negligence remains actionable.
