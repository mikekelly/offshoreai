# Article 220 — General Provisions as to Regulations and Orders

Article 220 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** is a
**general provision** governing **Regulations and Orders** made
under the Companies Law.

## What Article 220 covers

Regulations and Orders may:

- Be **specific or general**;
- Make **different provision for different cases**;
- Include **incidental, supplementary, transitional, and
  consequential** provisions;
- **Amend, repeal, or restate** earlier Regulations or Orders;
- Refer to **external documents** (specifications, professional
  standards) as updated from time to time.

Procedural rules typically include:

- **Publication** requirements;
- **Tabling** before the States;
- **Coming-into-effect** mechanics;
- **Amendment and revocation** processes.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

Delegated legislation is the **detail-filling layer** of the
Companies Law. Article 220 ensures:

- **Drafting flexibility** for the legislator;
- **Procedural consistency** across the various powers;
- **Public visibility** through publication;
- **Democratic oversight** through States procedures.
