# Article 127FA — Simplified Approval of Mergers Involving Subsidiaries

Article 127FA of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]**
provides a **simplified approval procedure** for mergers within
**wholly-owned corporate groups** — where the costly special-
resolution process under Article 127F adds little protective
value.

## What Article 127FA covers

Where a merger involves:

- A **parent and one or more wholly-owned subsidiaries** (vertical
  merger); or
- **Two or more wholly-owned subsidiaries** of a common parent
  (horizontal sister-merger);

the **special-resolution requirement** in Article 127F is
**disapplied** for the wholly-owned subsidiaries — provided the
directors and the parent give the prescribed resolutions and
certificates.

Conditions typically include:

- All affected shares are held by the parent or another wholly-
  owned subsidiary in the same group;
- The merger does not involve any change to **non-parent
  shareholders'** rights;
- Directors' certificates as to solvency are made in the normal
  way.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

Group simplification is a routine corporate-housekeeping exercise.
Where a parent already owns 100% of the subsidiaries to be
merged, a member-protection mechanism aimed at minorities is
**superfluous** — there are no minorities.

Article 127FA streamlines the process while keeping the
**directors' duties** and **solvency-certificate** protections
intact.

## Pitfalls

- **Hidden non-wholly-owned interests** — a single non-group
  share (employee, option-holder, trust) takes the case outside
  the simplified procedure.
- **Foreign-subsidiary mergers** — simplified procedure
  availability depends on whether all merging bodies are
  wholly-owned Jersey companies.
