# Article 125 — Power of Company to Compromise with Creditors and Members

Article 125 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** is the
foundation of the **Jersey scheme of arrangement** — a court-
sanctioned compromise or arrangement between a company and its
**creditors** or its **members** (or any class of either).

## What Article 125 covers

Where a **compromise or arrangement** is proposed between a
company and its **creditors** (or any class) or its **members**
(or any class), the **Royal Court** may, on the application of
the company, a creditor, a member, the liquidator (if any), or
prescribed others:

- **Order a meeting** of the relevant creditors / members / class
  to consider the proposal;
- If at the meeting the proposal is approved by the **prescribed
  majority** of those present and voting (typically **a majority
  in number representing 75% in value**) within each class — and
  the Court subsequently **sanctions** the scheme — the
  compromise or arrangement is **binding on all** affected
  creditors / members / class members, including those who voted
  against or did not vote.

The Court's sanction is **filed with the registrar** and on filing
the scheme takes effect.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

Article 125 schemes are the **principal Jersey restructuring
tool** outside formal insolvency. They are used for:

- **Takeover transactions** as an alternative to a Part 18
  takeover offer — particularly where the bidder wants
  certainty of 100% acquisition without dealing with the 90%
  threshold and squeeze-out;
- **Solvent reorganisations** — group simplifications,
  introduction of new holding companies, debt-for-equity
  conversions;
- **Distressed restructurings** — compromises with bondholders
  and other creditors to deleverage without insolvency;
- **Cross-border restructurings** — particularly where Jersey is
  used as a restructuring jurisdiction for international groups.

## The scheme process

1. **Convening hearing** — the Court considers the constitution
   of classes and orders meetings on appropriate terms;
2. **Explanatory statement** circulated to all affected
   creditors / members (see Article 126);
3. **Class meetings** held; each class votes;
4. **Sanction hearing** — the Court considers whether to
   sanction the scheme. The Court looks at:
   - Whether **statutory requirements** have been met (notice,
     classes correctly constituted, majorities reached);
   - Whether the scheme is one that an **intelligent and honest
     person**, acting in respect of his interest, might
     reasonably approve;
   - Whether the scheme is **fair and reasonable**;
   - Whether there is any **blot on the scheme** (e.g. illegality
     or impossibility);
5. **Filing** of the Court's act with the registrar — the
   scheme takes effect.

## Class composition

Class composition is the most-litigated issue. A class consists
of those whose rights are **so similar** that they can consult
together with a view to their common interest. Differences in
**commercial interest** can also matter — e.g. a creditor who
will be repaid in full under the scheme has a different interest
from one who will be partially compromised.

If classes are wrongly constituted, the Court will not sanction
the scheme.

## Scheme vs Part 18 takeover offer

| Feature | Scheme (Art 125) | Takeover offer (Part 18) |
|---|---|---|
| **Initiator** | Target's directors | Bidder |
| **Threshold** | 75% in value + majority in number per class | 90% acceptance |
| **Court involvement** | Mandatory sanction hearing | Available on dissenting application |
| **Effect** | Court-ordered, binds all | Compulsory acquisition under Art 117 |
| **Outcome** | 100% acquisition certain if sanctioned | 100% acquisition contingent on hitting 90% |
| **Tax / stamp** | Different cross-border tax footprint | Different cross-border tax footprint |

Both routes are commonly used; the choice depends on the bidder's
relationship with the target board, certainty appetite, and the
profile of the shareholder base.
