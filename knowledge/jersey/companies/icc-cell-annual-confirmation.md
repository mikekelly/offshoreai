# Article 127YE — Annual Confirmation Statement in Respect of Cells

Article 127YE of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]**
applies the **annual confirmation-statement obligation** to
incorporated cells with appropriate modifications.

## What Article 127YE covers

Each incorporated cell must, like any Jersey company:

- File an **annual confirmation statement** with the registrar;
- Confirm or update the prescribed particulars (members,
  directors, registered office, etc.);
- Pay the **prescribed annual fee** for the cell.

Some practical modifications:

- The statement may be **filed by the ICC's secretary** on behalf
  of the cell;
- The **registered office** is shared with the ICC and need not
  be separately filed;
- The fee schedule for cells may differ from that for standalone
  companies (typically lower per-cell fees).

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

The annual confirmation statement is the **central recurring
filing** that keeps the public register accurate. For ICCs, with
potentially many cells, the annual filing obligation can be a
significant administrative burden — but it is essential to:

- **Maintain good standing** of each cell;
- **Keep the public register current**;
- **Trigger annual fees** that fund the registrar.

ICC service providers typically have **bulk filing systems** to
manage the multiple-cell filing obligation.
