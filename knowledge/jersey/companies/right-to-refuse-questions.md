# Article 210 — Right to Refuse to Answer Questions

Article 210 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** sets
out **limits** on the compulsion to answer questions under the
Companies Law's compulsion powers — typically by preserving
privilege against self-incrimination in specified circumstances.

## What Article 210 covers

A person required to answer questions or provide information
under specified powers in the Companies Law may **refuse** where:

- The answer would **incriminate** the person in proceedings for
  an offence (subject to use-immunity exceptions);
- The information is **legally privileged**;
- Other prescribed protections apply.

Where the **use-immunity** model applies, the person must
answer, but the answer cannot be used against them in
subsequent criminal proceedings (subject to specified
exceptions, e.g. perjury).

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

The **balance between compulsion and protection** is a key
calibration of the regime:

- **Too much compulsion** without protection violates fundamental
  rights;
- **Too little compulsion** defeats investigation;
- **Use immunity** is a compromise that allows information to be
  gathered while preserving criminal-trial fairness.

Article 210 sets the limits on Jersey's compulsion powers in
this area.
