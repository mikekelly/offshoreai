# Article 127G — Offences Relating to Merger

Article 127G of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]**
creates **criminal offences** for false or deficient conduct in
connection with a Part 18B merger.

## What Article 127G covers

Offences include:

- **Making a solvency certificate** under Article 127E without
  having reasonable grounds for the opinion expressed;
- **Knowingly delivering false documents** to the registrar in
  connection with a merger;
- **Failing to give creditor notice** required by Article 127FC;
- **Concealing creditors or liabilities** from the directors when
  the solvency certificate is being prepared.

Penalties include **imprisonment and fines** at significant
levels — these are not regulatory slap-on-the-wrist offences.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

The Part 18B regime trusts directors to make the **solvency
certificate** — the foundation of the court-free process. If
that trust is misplaced, creditors can be left with claims
against an insolvent merged body and no recovery.

Article 127G creates **strong personal accountability** for
directors:

- The solvency certificate is not a formality; it's a **criminal
  statement**;
- Directors who sign without reasonable grounds **commit an
  offence** regardless of the company's subsequent solvency
  outcome.

This drives directors to take the certificate seriously — get
financial advice, model the post-merger cash position, consider
covenant compliance — which protects creditors in turn.
