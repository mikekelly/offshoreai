# Article 105 — Accounts

Article 105 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** requires
Jersey companies to prepare **annual accounts** showing financial
position at year-end, including a **profit and loss account** and
**balance sheet**.

## What Article 105 says

Companies must **prepare annual accounts** showing financial
position at year-end.

These accounts must include:

- a **profit and loss account**;
- a **balance sheet**;

— reflecting company affairs according to **proper accounting
principles**.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## What "proper accounting principles"

The Companies Law doesn't mandate a specific accounting framework
— the company chooses an appropriate framework. Common choices for
Jersey companies:

- **IFRS** (International Financial Reporting Standards) — most
  common for fund vehicles and cross-border-group entities;
- **UK GAAP** (specifically FRS 102) — common where the company
  has UK operating-business links;
- **US GAAP** — for companies with US parent or operations;
- **Industry-specific frameworks** — for funds, banks, insurance.

The framework chosen must be **consistently applied** year over
year. Changes require disclosure and explanation.

## Content of accounts

A typical Jersey company's accounts include:

- **Balance sheet** (statement of financial position);
- **Profit and loss account** (statement of comprehensive
  income);
- **Cash-flow statement**;
- **Statement of changes in equity**;
- **Notes** explaining accounting policies and detail items;
- **Directors' report** (especially for public companies);
- **Auditor's report** (where audit is required).

## Audit

**Audit** is required for:

- **Public companies** (Article 87 / Part 16);
- **Specific regulated businesses** (banks, large funds,
  insurance);
- **Companies whose deeds or member resolutions require it**;

Audit is **optional** for many private Jersey companies, which
is one of Jersey's commercial attractions for private group
structures.

The audit, where required, follows
[Article 113A](./auditor-report.md).

## Pitfalls

- **Late accounts** — missed deadlines invite offences and
  Registrar enforcement.
- **Inconsistent frameworks** between years — changes need
  proper disclosure and may need restatement of comparatives.
- **Forgetting audit** where the company has crossed into a
  category requiring audit (e.g., became public, took on
  regulated activities).
