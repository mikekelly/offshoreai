# Article 173 — Powers of Court in Respect of Disclaimed Property

Article 173 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** gives
the **Royal Court** power to make orders concerning **disclaimed
property** — to resolve issues that disclaimer creates.

## What Article 173 covers

Affected parties may apply to the Royal Court for orders
including:

- **Vesting** of disclaimed property in another person (e.g.
  vesting a disclaimed lease in a guarantor who would otherwise
  remain liable);
- **Compensation** orders for parties prejudiced by disclaimer;
- **Determination** of disputes about the scope or effect of
  disclaimer;
- Such other relief as is **just**.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

Disclaimer can create **complex third-party effects**:

- **Guarantors** of disclaimed leases may want the lease vested
  in them so they get the property they will end up paying for;
- **Sub-tenants** of disclaimed leases may want to retain their
  sublease;
- **Co-owners** of disclaimed shared property may want
  resolution.

Article 173 gives the Court power to **clean up the
consequences** of disclaimer fairly.
