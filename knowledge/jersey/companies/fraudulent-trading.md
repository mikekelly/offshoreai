# Article 178 — Responsibility for Fraudulent Trading

Article 178 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** imposes
**personal liability** (and criminal exposure) on those who
**knowingly carry on the business of the company with intent to
defraud creditors**.

## What Article 178 covers

If, in the course of a winding up, it appears that the company's
business has been carried on:

- With **intent to defraud creditors** of the company or of any
  other person; or
- For any **fraudulent purpose**;

the Royal Court may, on the liquidator's application, declare any
**person** who was **knowingly a party** to the fraudulent
trading to be **personally liable** to contribute to the
company's assets.

The article reaches:

- **Directors and officers**;
- **Shadow directors**;
- **Other persons** (e.g. advisers, employees, related parties)
  who were knowingly involved.

Fraudulent trading is also a **criminal offence** with
significant penalties.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

Fraudulent trading is the **culpable counterpart** to wrongful
trading:

- **Wrongful trading** (Art 177) — negligent failure to face
  insolvency; standard is objective; civil liability only;
- **Fraudulent trading** (Art 178) — deliberate carrying on with
  intent to defraud; standard requires actual knowledge / intent;
  both civil and criminal liability.

The breadth of "any person knowingly a party" allows the
liquidator to reach beyond directors to **co-conspirators**:

- **Family members** complicit in fraudulent asset transfers;
- **Professional advisers** who participated in fraudulent
  structures;
- **Recipients** who knowingly took fraudulently transferred
  assets.

## Pitfalls

- The **intent / knowledge standard** is high — mere imprudence
  is not fraudulent trading;
- The **criminal-standard burden** applies to criminal
  proceedings; civil claims under Article 178 can be proved to
  the lower **civil standard** (balance of probabilities) but
  still require evidence of dishonesty.
