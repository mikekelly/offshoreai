# Article 110 — Commission to Maintain Register of Recognized Auditors

Article 110 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** requires
the **JFSC** to maintain a **Register of Recognized Auditors** —
the canonical list of persons eligible to audit Jersey companies.

## What Article 110 says

The **Commission** must establish and maintain a **register of
auditors** meeting prescribed qualifications and regulatory
standards.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

A Jersey company that requires an audit must have it conducted
by a **recognised auditor** — a person on the JFSC's register.
The register:

- **Identifies** who can validly audit Jersey companies;
- Provides **public verification** that the auditor is properly
  qualified;
- Subjects auditors to **regulatory oversight** by the JFSC;
- Allows for **enforcement** against auditors who breach standards.

## Pitfalls

- **Using a non-registered "auditor"** — the audit may not
  satisfy Article 113A and the audit report may be ineffective
  for Companies Law purposes.
