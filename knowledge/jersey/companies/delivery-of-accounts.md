# Article 108 — Delivery of Accounts to Registrar

Article 108 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** requires
**filing of accounts with the Registrar** within specified
timeframes.

## What Article 108 says

Requires **filing of accounts** with the Registrar within
specified timeframes:

- **typically within nine months** of financial period end for
  most companies.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## The nine-month default

The standard filing deadline is **nine months from the end of the
financial period**. So a 31-December year-end requires filing by
**30 September** the following year.

**Variations** apply for:

- **Public companies** — may have shorter deadlines under
  listing-rule requirements;
- **Specific regulated businesses** — different filing schedules
  from JFSC;
- **First financial period** — sometimes longer windows for
  newly-incorporated companies.

## Filing mechanism

Accounts are filed:

- Through the **JFSC online portal**;
- With **accompanying** auditor's report (where audit applies);
- With the **directors' report** (for public companies);
- With **filing fees** as prescribed.

## Public availability

Filed accounts are typically **available on the public Registry**
for:

- **Public companies** — full annual accounts;
- **Specific categories** of private companies — subject to the
  variations in Article 107;
- **Some private companies** can file **abbreviated accounts**
  with reduced public disclosure.

## Failure to file

[Article 109](accounts-compliance-failure.md) addresses the
consequences of failing to comply with the filing requirements:

- **Offences** for the company and directors;
- **Civil financial penalties**;
- Potential **strike-off** under Part 23 in chronic non-
  compliance.

## Pitfalls

- **Missed deadlines** — strict liability for directors;
  flexibility from the Registrar is limited.
- **Inadequate documents** for filing (e.g., missing
  signatures) — typically returned for correction with knock-on
  delays.
