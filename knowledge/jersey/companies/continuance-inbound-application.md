# Article 127K — Application to Commission for Continuance Within Jersey

Article 127K of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** sets
out the **application requirements** for a foreign body wishing
to continue **into Jersey** — i.e. to become a Jersey company.

## What Article 127K covers

The applicant must deliver to the JFSC:

- Application in **prescribed form**;
- The body's **current constitutional documents** under its home
  jurisdiction's law;
- A **certificate of good standing** (or equivalent) from the
  home jurisdiction;
- The **proposed articles of continuance** for the Jersey
  company (see Article 127L);
- A **proposed name** for the continuing Jersey company (see
  Article 127M);
- A **solvency statement** (see Article 127W);
- **Beneficial-owner** and source-of-funds information;
- **Foreign-law opinion** confirming that the home jurisdiction
  permits the continuance and that the body will cease to exist
  there on completion;
- **Prescribed fees** and security (Article 127J).

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

The inbound application is a **substantial documentation
exercise** — what the JFSC needs is a complete factual picture
of:

- **Who** is migrating in;
- **What** they bring with them (business, assets, liabilities);
- **Where** they came from and what controls applied there;
- **Why** they want to be in Jersey;
- **Whether** the home jurisdiction will fully release the body
  on completion.

The completeness of the application materially drives the
**timetable**.
