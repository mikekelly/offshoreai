# Article 83 — Register of Directors and Secretaries

Article 83 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** requires
every Jersey company to maintain a **register of directors and
secretaries** — the canonical record of who currently holds these
offices.

## What Article 83 covers

A company must maintain a **register of directors and
secretaries** recording:

- the **current** directors and secretaries;
- **particulars** specified in Articles 84, 84A, and 85;
- **changes** in office-holders as they occur.

(For the verbatim text see [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## What gets recorded

- For each **natural-person director**: full particulars under
  [Article 84](./director-particulars-natural.md);
- For each **corporate director**: full particulars under
  [Article 84A](./director-particulars-corporate.md);
- For each **secretary**: full particulars under
  [Article 85](./secretary-particulars.md);
- Dates of **appointment** and **cessation** of office.

## Where it's kept

Typically at the **registered office** alongside the register of
members. The two registers may be combined in practice into a
single statutory-records book.

## Public access

Director and secretary particulars are typically **public** at
the Registrar level — counterparties can search the Registry to
see current directors of a Jersey company. This is distinct from
beneficial-ownership data, which is non-public.

## Updating

The register must be **updated** within specified timeframes
when:

- A **director** is **appointed**, **resigns**, or **dies**;
- A **secretary** is changed;
- A **director's particulars** change (e.g., change of address,
  change of name).

The **Registrar must be notified** of changes (typically through
the JFSC's online filing system).

## Pitfalls

- **Out-of-date register** — directors come and go without
  being recorded. The legal position can be unclear on later
  challenge.
- **Failure to notify** Registrar of changes — exposes the
  company to offences and creates Registry discrepancies.
