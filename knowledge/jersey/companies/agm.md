# Article 87 — Annual General Meeting

Article 87 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** requires
Jersey companies to hold **annual general meetings (AGMs)**, with
the requirement varying between public and private companies.

## What Article 87 says

Every company must hold an **annual general meeting**.

**Public companies** must hold one:

- within **18 months of incorporation**; and
- subsequently in **each calendar year**.

**Private companies** have **similar requirements**, **unless
relieved** by:

- **special resolution**; or
- **exemption** under the Law.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Public-company AGM

A public company's AGM is the **principal annual member event**:

- **Accounts** are presented to members;
- **Auditors** are appointed or reappointed;
- **Directors** retire by rotation (per the deed);
- **Resolutions** on company business are passed;
- **Members raise questions** on the company's affairs.

The AGM is typically a **substantial event** for listed Jersey
companies.

## Private-company AGM

For private Jersey companies, the AGM requirement is often
**relieved by special resolution**. Many family-office, SPV, and
holding-structure private companies operate without an AGM —
substituting:

- **Written resolutions** under Articles 95 / 95ZA / 95ZB;
- **Informal member-director communication**;
- **Ad-hoc meetings** as specific business arises.

The relief is **routine** in modern Jersey private-company
practice. Check the articles for the relief provision.

## Default if no AGM

A company that **fails** to hold a required AGM:

- May face **directors' offences** under the Companies Law;
- The **JFSC may direct** a meeting be called under
  [Article 88](jfsc-meeting-power.md);
- The **Court may order** a meeting under
  [Article 94](court-ordered-meetings.md).

## Pitfalls

- **Forgetting AGM** in early years for public companies —
  monitor the 18-month window after incorporation.
- **Missing private-company exemption** — confirm the relief
  is properly recorded in the articles or by special resolution.
