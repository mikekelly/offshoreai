# Article 146 — Procedure (Summary Winding Up)

Article 146 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** sets
out the **step-by-step procedure** for commencing a summary
winding up.

## What Article 146 covers

The summary winding-up procedure:

1. **Directors' solvency statement** — directors give a statement
   in prescribed form that they have inquired into the
   company's affairs and are of the opinion the company will be
   able to **pay its debts in full within 12 months** of the
   commencement of winding up;
2. **Special resolution** of members to wind up the company under
   Chapter 2;
3. **Notice to the registrar** of the special resolution and the
   solvency statement;
4. **Publication** as prescribed;
5. **Appointment of liquidator** (Article 149).

The solvency statement is the **core protection** — falsity
without reasonable grounds is an offence (mirroring Article 61A).

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

The procedure is simple but the **solvency statement is non-
trivial**. Directors should:

- **Review** the company's accounts and known liabilities;
- **Identify** all creditors (trade, finance, tax, lease, etc.);
- **Consider** contingent and prospective liabilities (warranty
  claims, litigation, tax assessments);
- **Document** the basis for the solvency opinion.

If the inquiry is **inadequate**, the directors are exposed to
offence liability under Article 146 (and personal liability if
the company turns out insolvent).
