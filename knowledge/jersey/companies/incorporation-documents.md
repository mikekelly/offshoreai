# Article 7 — Documents to Be Delivered to Registrar

Article 7 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** sets out
the **paperwork required** on incorporation in addition to the
memorandum and articles. It specifies registered-office details,
director particulars, and the signing/delivery requirements.

## What Article 7 says

The documents to be delivered include:

### A statement of particulars

A statement specifying:

- the **intended address** of the company's registered office on
  incorporation;
- any **other particulars** required to be published;

signed **by or on behalf of the subscribers**.

### Director particulars — natural persons (public companies)

For each natural-person director, the document must record:

- **present forenames and surname**;
- **any former forenames or surname**;
- **business or usual residential address**;
- **nationality**;
- **business occupation**;
- **date of birth**.

### Director particulars — corporate directors

For each corporate director:

- the **name** under which the corporate director is registered;
- the **registered-office address**;
- the **jurisdiction of incorporation**;
- the **registered number** (if any).

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## How this fits with incorporation

In practice the Article 7 delivery happens in parallel with:

- delivery of the memorandum (Article 4) and articles (Article 5);
- the **JFSC consent to incorporate** under the [Control of
  Borrowing (Jersey) Order 1958][cob];
- the **fee** payment.

All of this is handled by the **registered agent** — a
JFSC-regulated trust company business (TCB) or law firm — using
the JFSC's online incorporation portal.

## Beneficial-ownership reporting

Although Article 7 itself does not require beneficial-ownership
information, modern Jersey incorporations also generate
**beneficial-ownership and Significant Persons** disclosures
required under the **Financial Services (Disclosure and Provision
of Information) (Jersey) Law 2020** (FSDI Law). The JFSC's online
incorporation portal collects this data alongside the Article 7
particulars.

The beneficial-ownership register is **not public** but is
available to law-enforcement and regulators — see
[`../registries/beneficial-ownership.md`](../registries/beneficial-ownership.md)
*(stub)*.

## Public-company vs private-company particulars

The Article distinguishes the level of director-particulars
required:

- **Public companies** — full director particulars per the list
  above;
- **Private companies** — a reduced set, with corporate directors
  also accommodated.

Public companies have higher transparency obligations throughout
the Companies Law, and Article 7 reflects that.

## Updating particulars after incorporation

Once incorporated, changes to director particulars or registered
office must be notified to the Registrar — see
[`registered-office.md`](registered-office.md) *(planned —
Article 67)* and [`register-of-directors-secretaries.md`](register-of-directors-secretaries.md)
*(planned — Article 83)*.

## Pitfalls

- **Inaccurate director particulars** — leads to Registrar
  queries and may delay incorporation. Natural-person directors
  in particular need their full name history (incl. former
  surnames) recorded accurately.
- **Wrong registered-office address** — the registered office
  must be a Jersey address (the registered agent's office is
  typical). A foreign or invalid address will be rejected.
- **Missing or incomplete beneficial-ownership data** — the
  separate FSDI Law requirement may not get rejected at
  incorporation but will surface later.
