# Article 138 — Inspectors' Report to be Evidence

Article 138 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** makes
an inspector's report **admissible as evidence** in subsequent
proceedings.

## What Article 138 covers

In any proceedings:

- A **copy of the inspector's report** is **admissible**
  evidence of its contents;
- A copy is treated as **conclusive evidence** that the
  inspector formed the opinions and conclusions set out in the
  report (without prejudice to the court's power to draw its
  own conclusions on the underlying facts);
- The **inspector's findings of fact** are typically admitted as
  prima facie evidence of those facts.

This applies in:

- **Criminal proceedings** based on conduct uncovered;
- **Civil proceedings** brought by the company, JFSC, or others;
- **Regulatory proceedings** (disciplinary, deregistration);
- **Disqualification proceedings** against directors.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

Without Article 138, an inspector's report would just be **hearsay**:

- Each fact in the report would need to be re-proved by primary
  evidence;
- Witnesses examined by inspectors would need to be re-examined
  in court;
- Costs and delay would multiply.

Article 138 makes the **investigation directly useful** in
subsequent proceedings without forcing re-litigation of the
factual basis. The other side can still **challenge specific
findings** — Article 138 establishes admissibility, not
infallibility.
