# Article 127X — Provisions Relating to Continuance

Article 127X of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]**
contains **supplementary and ancillary provisions** for the Part
18C continuance regime, including the regulation-making power
that fills in the detail.

## What Article 127X covers

Supplementary provisions cover matters such as:

- **Recognised jurisdictions** — the JFSC's power (or the
  Minister's, by Order or Regulation) to specify which foreign
  jurisdictions are recognised for continuance purposes;
- **Prescribed forms** for applications, certificates, and
  filings;
- **Procedural timing** — periods for objections, response
  deadlines, registration windows;
- **Fees and security** detail;
- **Treatment of share capital** continuity across the boundary;
- **Continuity of corporate records** — what records need to be
  re-created or maintained on continuance.

These detailed mechanics are typically set out in Regulations
made under the Article rather than in the principal Law.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

The continuance regime is **operationally detailed** and changes
as international practice evolves. Putting the detail in
Regulations:

- Allows **agility** when foreign jurisdictions change their
  laws;
- Enables **recognition lists** to be updated without primary
  legislation;
- Keeps the principal Law **focused on principles** and
  consequence.

Practitioners should always **check current Regulations**
alongside the principal Law when advising on continuance.
