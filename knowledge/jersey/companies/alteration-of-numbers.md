# Article 17C — Alteration of Numbers (the 30-member threshold)

Article 17C of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** confers
on the **Minister** a delegated power to **adjust the 30-member
threshold** that appears in Articles 3(3), 16, and 17.

## What Article 17C says

The **Minister may by Order** adjust the 30-member threshold
referenced in:

- **Article 3(3)** (Registrar's discretion to restrict
  applications with more than 30 incorporators);
- **Article 16** (public → private threshold);
- **Article 17** (private → public auto-conversion threshold).

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this provision exists

The 30-member figure is **legacy policy** — it reflects historical
Jersey thinking about what distinguishes a "close" private company
from one that resembles a public participant. The figure may not
always be the right policy answer.

Article 17C lets the Minister update the threshold by **Order**
(a faster process than primary legislation) when circumstances
change. The States Assembly retains oversight of the Order.

## Relationship with Article 17D

Article 17C lets the Minister **adjust** the threshold. The more
radical [Article 17D](30-member-limit-abolition.md) gives the
**States** a power to **abolish** the private-company member limit
entirely. The States has historically exercised the Article 17D
power, abolishing the limit for private companies in modern
Jersey practice — but the 30-member triggers in Articles 16 and
17 (for status-conversion purposes) remain in force unless
specifically modified.

## Pitfalls

- **Treating the 30-member figure as permanent**. Check the
  current Order before applying it. The States have made
  amendments by Order over the years.
