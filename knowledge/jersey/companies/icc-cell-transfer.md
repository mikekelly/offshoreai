# Article 127YI — Transfer of Cell

Article 127YI of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]**
allows an **incorporated cell** to be **transferred from one ICC
to another** — i.e. to change its "host" ICC while remaining the
same legal person.

## What Article 127YI covers

An incorporated cell may be transferred to a different ICC by:

- **Application** to the registrar in prescribed form;
- **Approval** by members of the cell, the existing ICC, and the
  receiving ICC;
- **Amendment** of the cell's memorandum and articles to identify
  the **new** ICC;
- Satisfaction of **creditor-protection** conditions;
- **Registration** of the amended documents.

On registration:

- The cell **ceases** to be a cell of the original ICC;
- The cell **becomes** a cell of the receiving ICC;
- The cell's **legal identity is unchanged**.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

Cell-transfer is used when:

- A **fund sponsor** sells an umbrella structure to another
  sponsor — the individual cells move across to the new umbrella
  without redocumentation of underlying contracts;
- A **service-provider migration** is effected at the umbrella
  level rather than the cell level;
- A **restructuring of multiple umbrellas** consolidates cells
  into a single ICC.

The **continuity-of-legal-identity** principle preserves the
cell's contracts, assets and counterparty relationships during
the transfer — only the relationship with the host ICC changes.
