# Article 154 — Termination of Summary Winding Up

Article 154 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** allows
a summary winding up to be **terminated** before dissolution if
circumstances change.

## What Article 154 covers

The summary winding up may be **terminated** before dissolution
by:

- A **special resolution** of members; or
- A **court order** on application of a member, creditor, or
  other interested party.

On termination:

- The company **returns to active status**;
- The liquidator's authority ends;
- The directors' powers are **restored**;
- Notice is given to the registrar.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

A summary winding up may need to be **stopped** where:

- A **buyer emerges** for the company's business — sale as a
  going concern is preferable to piecemeal liquidation;
- The reason for winding up **ceases to apply** (e.g. a tax
  liability is resolved that had prompted the wind-down);
- The members **change their minds** before the dissolution
  becomes irreversible.

Article 154 provides the **off-ramp** — termination before final
dissolution leaves the company alive and trading.
