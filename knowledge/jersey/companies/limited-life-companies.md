# Article 3H — Limited Life Companies

Article 3H of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** defines a
**limited life company** — a company whose memorandum includes
**provisions for winding up upon specific events or time periods**.

## What Article 3H says

A limited life company is one whose memorandum includes provisions
for **winding up upon specific events or after a stated time
period**.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## What "limited life" means

A limited-life company terminates **automatically** on:

- the **expiry of a stated duration** (e.g., 25 years from
  incorporation);
- the occurrence of a **stated event** (e.g., the completion of a
  defined transaction; the sale of a specified asset; the death
  of the founder);
- the **fulfilment of a specified purpose** (in some structures —
  similar to a purpose trust ending on completion of its purpose);
- some combination of the above.

This contrasts with the default position — a Jersey company has
**indefinite duration** unless its memorandum says otherwise.

## Why use a limited life form

- **Special purpose vehicles (SPVs)** for a defined transaction
  with a definite end-date — the limited-life provision ensures
  the company terminates cleanly on completion;
- **Fund GP and management vehicles** matched to fund duration —
  a fund GP company with a life matched to the fund's term;
- **Joint-venture entities** with a planned wind-up at the
  termination of the JV;
- **Family-office structures** with planned termination on a
  specific event (e.g., second-generation succession);
- **Aviation, shipping and similar finance** structures where the
  SPV's life matches the financed asset's economic life.

## Procedure on triggering event

On the trigger event:

- the company **enters a winding-up process** — typically a
  **summary winding up** under [Article 144 (limited duration)](winding-up.md)
  *(planned cluster)* / [Article 145](winding-up.md);
- the **liquidator** (often the directors or a designated person
  under the memorandum) realises the assets and distributes them;
- the company is **dissolved** on completion of the wind-up.

The mechanism is statutorily integrated — no separate Court
application is needed unless the wind-up is contentious.

## Drafting

The memorandum of a limited-life Jersey company should:

- **clearly specify** the trigger events;
- specify the **wind-up procedure** (typically pointing to the
  Article 144 summary procedure);
- specify the **distribution order** of remaining assets;
- consider **extension mechanisms** (member resolution to extend
  the duration before expiry, where commercially desired).

## Conversion to indefinite life

A limited-life company can typically be **converted to indefinite
life** by amending the memorandum — usually requiring a special
resolution. This is a useful flexibility for SPVs whose original
purpose has evolved.
