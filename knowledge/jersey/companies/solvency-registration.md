# Article 61B — Registration of Solvency Statement and Minute of Reduction

Article 61B of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** is the
**filing requirement** for a solvency-statement capital reduction.
The solvency statement and the **minute of reduction** (the board
resolution authorising the reduction) are filed together at the
Registrar.

## What Article 61B says

The **solvency statement** and the **minute of reduction** must
be **delivered to the registrar together**.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## What the documents contain

### Solvency statement

Per [Article 61A](./solvency-statement.md):

- Identification of the company;
- Date;
- Signature of **each director**;
- Statutory declaration.

### Minute of reduction

The board resolution authorising the capital reduction:

- States the **amount** of the reduction;
- Identifies which **capital account** is being reduced (nominal,
  share premium, stated capital);
- References the **special resolution** of members authorising
  the reduction;
- Specifies the **effective date** (typically registration date).

## Effective date

The reduction takes effect on **registration** of the documents
with the Registrar (subject to any later date specified in the
minute itself).

Until registration, the reduction is **not effective** — even
though the directors have signed the solvency statement and the
members have passed the special resolution. Filing is the
**operative step**.

## Practical timing

The typical reduction sequence:

1. **Board considers** the proposed reduction;
2. **Board minute** authorising the reduction (subject to member
   resolution and solvency statement);
3. **Special resolution** of members under
   [Article 61](./reduction-of-capital-accounts.md);
4. **Solvency statement** signed by all directors (Article 61A);
5. **Statutory declaration** sworn by a director;
6. **Filing** with the Registrar (Article 61B);
7. **Effective date** — on registration.

The whole process can typically be completed in **a few business
days** once the underlying analysis is done.

## Failure to file

Failure to file the documents:

- Renders the reduction **ineffective**;
- May expose directors to offences under the Companies Law;
- Creates **uncertainty** about the company's capital position.

File **promptly** after the underlying steps are complete.

## Pitfalls

- **Stale solvency statement** — file promptly after signing.
  An old statement based on out-of-date analysis may not support
  the reduction.
- **Mismatched minute and statement** — the documents should be
  consistent on the reduction amount and effective date.
- **Filing fee oversights** — Registrar fees apply; budget for
  these.
