# Article 127Q — Approval by Company and Members of Proposal for Continuance Overseas

Article 127Q of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]**
requires **members' approval** for a Jersey company to **continue
overseas** (outbound continuance / redomiciliation out of Jersey).

## What Article 127Q covers

A Jersey company may not proceed with an outbound continuance
application unless:

- The directors **propose** the continuance with **reasons**;
- The members approve by **special resolution** (or such other
  majority as the articles require);
- Where the continuance prejudices any **class of members**, that
  class separately approves;
- The proposal sets out the **destination jurisdiction**, the
  proposed **continuing constitution**, and **post-continuance
  governance**.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

Outbound continuance fundamentally changes the **legal
environment** of the company:

- The **applicable corporate law** changes (Jersey Companies Law
  no longer applies);
- The **jurisdiction of courts** for company-law disputes
  changes;
- The **regulatory environment** (JFSC, tax, AML) changes;
- The **enforcement and creditor-protection regime** changes.

These changes can materially affect members' interests. The
special-resolution requirement gives **minorities a voice** —
two-thirds super-majority is the bar.

The **class-approval** requirement protects classes that may be
disproportionately affected (e.g. preference shareholders whose
rights translate differently into the destination jurisdiction's
law).
