# Article 37 — Provision for Different Amounts to Be Paid on Shares

Article 37 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** permits
a Jersey company to provide for **different amounts to be paid**
on different shares — i.e., the company can issue shares with
different paid-up amounts even within the same class.

## What Article 37 enables

A Jersey company can:

- **Issue shares as fully paid** (the simple case — purchaser pays
  the full subscription price on allotment);
- **Issue shares as partly paid** — purchaser pays part of the
  subscription price, with the **balance callable** later under
  the deed's call provisions;
- **Have different paid-up amounts on shares within the same
  class** — one share fully paid, another partly paid, even
  though both carry the same nominal rights.

The articles of association typically specify:

- The **call mechanism** — how and when unpaid amounts can be
  called from members;
- **Forfeiture provisions** — if a member fails to pay a call,
  the share may be forfeited under Article 60.

## Modern practice

In modern Jersey commercial practice **partly-paid shares are
unusual** for several reasons:

- Most no-par-value Jersey companies issue shares **fully paid**
  on subscription;
- **Operational simplicity** — fully-paid shares don't need call
  tracking;
- **AML/CFT considerations** — partly-paid arrangements can
  raise questions about future-payment sources.

Partly-paid shares are sometimes used in:

- **Fund GP vehicles** where capital is called from LPs over
  time (though typically the GP is structured as a limited
  partnership not a company);
- **Employee share schemes** with vesting tied to payment;
- **Specialised financing structures** where deferred
  consideration is part of the design.

## Distinction from unpaid liability

A **partly-paid share** is one where the member has agreed to
pay a specific further amount on call. This is **not the same as**
unlimited member liability:

- Partly-paid: member's liability is **the unpaid amount**;
- Unlimited (under Article 3D): member's liability is **unlimited**.

A member of a limited company with partly-paid shares faces
liability **capped** at the unpaid amount.

## Pitfalls

- **Confusing partly-paid with unlimited** — entirely different
  regimes.
- **Operational tracking** — companies with partly-paid shares
  need robust systems for calls and forfeiture.
- **AML scrutiny** — be ready to explain the commercial reason
  for partly-paid structures in regulator queries.
