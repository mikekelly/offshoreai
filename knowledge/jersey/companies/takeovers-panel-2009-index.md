# Companies (Takeovers and Mergers Panel) (Jersey) Law 2009 — Index

The **Companies (Takeovers and Mergers Panel) (Jersey) Law
2009** is the Jersey-domestic statute establishing a **Jersey
Takeovers and Mergers Panel** with statutory power to issue and
administer a Jersey **Takeover Code** for takeovers and mergers
of Jersey-incorporated companies that are not subject to (or are
only partially subject to) the
[UK City Code](../external-rules/uk-takeover-code.md).

as **stubs** pending a primary-source pass against the current

## What the statute does

The 2009 Law:

1. **Establishes the Jersey Takeovers and Mergers Panel** — a
   Jersey-domestic statutory Panel with appointment, governance,
   and decision-making provisions.
2. **Authorises the Panel to issue a Code** — a Jersey
   Takeover Code with substantive rules on the conduct of
   takeovers and mergers of Jersey-incorporated companies in
   scope.
3. **Defines the scope** of transactions to which the Code
   applies — typically Jersey-incorporated companies that are
   **not** within the UK City Code's jurisdiction by virtue of
   their listing venue, or specific situations where Jersey-
   internal protection is sought.
4. **Provides for sanctions** for breach of the Jersey Code,
   including potential consequences for parties and advisers.

## Why this statute exists alongside the UK Code

Most Jersey-incorporated companies that go to public markets list
on UK markets (AIM, Main Market) or on the Channel Islands
exchange (TISE) — all of which bring the **UK City Code** into
play. See
[`../external-rules/uk-takeover-code.md`](../external-rules/uk-takeover-code.md)
for the City Code's applicability test.

But Jersey has Jersey-incorporated public companies that are
**not** within the UK Code's jurisdiction — companies whose
shares are traded only in Jersey, on overseas exchanges
out-of-scope of the Code, or in private trading arrangements that
nonetheless involve broad shareholder bases. The 2009 Law fills
that gap, ensuring that Jersey-domestic takeover situations have
a Jersey statutory framework rather than relying purely on
common-law and CJL 1991 mechanics.

The statute also gives Jersey a **constitutional position** in
takeover regulation: even where the UK Code applies, Jersey is
not delegating its takeover policy to the UK Panel — it has its
own statutory Panel that can take a parallel view.

## Interaction with the UK Panel

In practice, the **UK City Code is by far the more frequently
applicable regime** for Jersey-incorporated public companies.
The Jersey Panel's role on a transaction within UK Code
jurisdiction is largely supportive — it does not replace the UK
Panel's primary role.

The cleanest applicability summary:

- **Jersey-incorporated, UK market admission** → UK City Code
  applies (see [`../external-rules/uk-takeover-code.md`](../external-rules/uk-takeover-code.md));
  Jersey Panel role is supplementary.
- **Jersey-incorporated, no relevant UK market admission, public
  shareholder base** → Jersey Code applies; the 2009 Law is the
  primary framework.
- **Jersey-incorporated, private** → neither Code applies; the
  transaction is governed by [CJL 1991](./companies-law-1991.md)
  contract and articles-of-association mechanics, contractual
  arrangements, and (where relevant) the
  [scheme of arrangement](./scheme-of-arrangement.md) regime.

## Interaction with CJL 1991 takeover mechanics

The 2009 Law sits **alongside** the **CJL 1991 takeover
provisions** — Parts 18 (takeovers, squeeze-out, sell-out) and
the [scheme of arrangement](./scheme-of-arrangement.md) regime
in Part 18A. The 2009 Law is about **process and conduct** of
the takeover (the Code that the Panel administers); the CJL
1991 provisions are about **statutory mechanics** (compulsory
acquisition thresholds, scheme sanction by the Royal Court,
member objection rights).

Article-level coverage of the 2009 Law is **scaffolded** below.

### Parts

The Law is structured into **three Parts** (corrected from
the earlier scaffold guess of five):

- **Part 1 — Interpretation** (Article 1).
- **Part 2 — Takeover panel** (Articles 2–22; the Panel's
  appointment, rule-making, rulings, directions, information
  powers, confidentiality, hearings, sanctions, compensation,
  enforcement, fees, levy, and operational matters).
- **Part 3 — Miscellaneous and closing** (Articles 23–25;
  interaction with other legislation, regulations / orders,
  citation).
