# Article 175 — Appointment or Removal of Liquidator by the Court

Article 175 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** gives
the **Royal Court** power to appoint or remove liquidators.

## What Article 175 covers

The Royal Court may, on application of a creditor, member, the
JFSC, the Minister, or other interested party:

- **Appoint** a liquidator where there is none in office;
- **Replace** an existing liquidator on cause shown (misconduct,
  lack of independence, incapacity);
- **Confirm** an appointment that is challenged;
- Make **consequential orders** about handover, fees, and
  continuity.

Grounds for **removal** typically include:

- Demonstrable **misconduct or breach of duty**;
- **Conflict of interest**;
- **Incompetence** harming the liquidation;
- **Loss of confidence** by the creditor body.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

Article 175 is the **judicial oversight** of the liquidator
appointment process — the final backstop against:

- A **deadlocked creditors' meeting** with no liquidator
  appointed;
- A **dishonest or compromised** liquidator whom creditors
  cannot remove by ordinary means;
- A **liquidator becoming unable to act** through illness or
  other incapacity.

The Court rarely intervenes; the threat of intervention helps
keep the liquidator-creditor relationship aligned.
