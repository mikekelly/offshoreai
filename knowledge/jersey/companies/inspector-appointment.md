# Article 128 — Appointment of Inspectors

Article 128 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** is the
**gateway provision** for Part 19 investigations: the power to
**appoint inspectors** to investigate a company's affairs.

## What Article 128 covers

The **JFSC** (and in some cases the **Minister**) may appoint
one or more **inspectors** to investigate the affairs of a
Jersey company. Triggering circumstances typically include:

- **On application** by members holding a prescribed percentage
  of the issued share capital;
- **On application** by the company itself (board resolution);
- **On the JFSC's own initiative** where the JFSC has reasonable
  grounds for concern;
- **On the order of the Royal Court**;
- Where there are **circumstances suggesting** fraud, misconduct
  by management, oppression of minorities, or other matters
  warranting investigation.

The inspector(s):

- Must be **competent persons** (often forensic accountants,
  lawyers, or specialised investigators);
- May be assisted by other professionals;
- Have the powers set out in Articles 129–134;
- Report under Article 135.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

Part 19 is the Companies Law's **investigative regime** — used
where the normal corporate-governance tools (member oversight,
auditor scrutiny, board accountability) have failed or are
inadequate to uncover serious wrongdoing.

Inspector appointments are **relatively rare** but powerful:

- Inspectors can compel **production of documents**;
- Inspectors can require **examination on oath**;
- Inspectors can search premises under court authority;
- Inspectors' reports are **admissible evidence** in proceedings.
