# Article 127FC — Notice to Creditors

Article 127FC of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** is
the **creditor-notification step** in the Part 18B merger
process.

## What Article 127FC covers

Each merging body must give **notice of the proposed merger** to
its **creditors**, in prescribed form, within the prescribed
period before the merger is registered. The notice typically
includes:

- The fact and proposed effective date of the merger;
- The identity of the **merged body** that will assume the
  liabilities;
- Information about how the creditor can **object**;
- Reference to the directors' **solvency certificates**.

Modes of notice may include:

- **Direct written notice** to known creditors above a prescribed
  threshold;
- **Publication** in the JEP (or a prescribed publication) for
  other creditors;
- **Posting** at the registered office.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

Part 18B mergers cause **liabilities to transfer by operation of
law** to the merged body. Creditors of the merging body
**involuntarily exchange** their counterparty — they end up with
a claim against the merged body rather than their original
debtor.

This can be **prejudicial** where:

- The merged body is **less creditworthy** than the original
  debtor;
- The merged body is in a **less favourable jurisdiction**;
- The creditor's **security position** is materially weakened by
  the merger.

Article 127FC ensures creditors are **informed** so they can
exercise their objection rights under Articles 127FD and 127FE.
