# Article 126 — Information as to Compromise to be Circulated

Article 126 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** is the
**disclosure backbone** of the scheme of arrangement: it requires
that creditors and members called to vote on a scheme receive
**adequate information** to make an informed decision.

## What Article 126 covers

Where a meeting is summoned under Article 125, the **notice of
meeting** must be accompanied by a **statement explaining**:

- The **effect** of the compromise or arrangement;
- In particular, any **material interests** of directors
  (whether as directors, members, or creditors of the company,
  or otherwise);
- The **effect** of the compromise on those interests insofar as
  it differs from the effect on similar interests of other
  persons.

For schemes involving **trustees** for debenture-holders or other
secured creditors, the trustees' material interests must also be
disclosed.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

A scheme of arrangement binds **dissenting and non-voting
creditors / members** because the Court is satisfied the requisite
majorities approved the scheme with **adequate information**. If
the explanatory statement is materially deficient or misleading,
the Court will refuse sanction.

The **directors' interests** disclosure is particularly important
where directors:

- Are continuing in office after the scheme;
- Hold shares that will be cancelled, transferred or
  exchanged differently from other shares;
- Are receiving **management equity** in a new structure;
- Are receiving **payments** in connection with the scheme.

These disclosures form a core part of the explanatory statement
and any deficiency is fatal to sanction.

## Pitfalls

- **Omissions about taxation** — explanatory statements should
  disclose material tax consequences for affected stakeholders.
- **Unrealistic financial projections** — projections supporting
  the scheme need a reasonable basis; over-optimistic projections
  may invalidate consent.
- **Class-specific disclosures missing** — different classes need
  information tailored to their position.
