# Article 147 — Commencement of Summary Winding Up

Article 147 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** sets
the **commencement date** of a summary winding up.

## What Article 147 covers

The summary winding up commences:

- On the **passing of the special resolution** under Article 146;
  or
- On any **later date** specified in the resolution.

From commencement:

- The company **continues to exist** but only for the purpose of
  winding up;
- The **directors' powers cease** (subject to liquidator
  delegation);
- The **liquidator takes control**.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

The commencement date is the **moment from which** various legal
consequences flow:

- Directors lose authority;
- Liquidator's powers crystallise;
- Various claw-back periods are calculated by reference to it;
- Notifications must be given.

Specifying a **later commencement date** in the resolution is
sometimes useful — e.g. to allow time to put liquidation
arrangements in place before the cut-over.
