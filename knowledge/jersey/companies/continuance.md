# Part 18C — Continuance (Overview)

Part 18C of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** is the
**continuance** (a.k.a. **migration** or **redomiciliation**)
regime: a body corporate moves its corporate seat from one
jurisdiction to another **without losing its legal identity**.

## What continuance achieves

Continuance changes the **governing corporate law** of an entity
while leaving the entity itself intact:

- Same legal person before and after;
- Existing contracts continue (no novation);
- Existing assets remain owned by the same entity;
- Existing litigation continues;
- Members continue as members (on terms set out in the articles
  of continuance / continuation document).

This contrasts with:

- **Dissolution and re-incorporation** — different entity, no
  contract continuity;
- **Merger** — universal succession but entity-identity changes;
- **Share transfer** — ownership changes, not corporate seat.

## When Part 18C is used

- **Inbound migration** — a foreign body becomes a Jersey company,
  often to take advantage of Jersey corporate law, regulatory
  standing, tax positioning, or proximity to UK financial
  services markets;
- **Outbound migration** — a Jersey company becomes a foreign
  body, e.g. to follow operating businesses to onshore
  jurisdictions, to take advantage of foreign listing
  requirements, or to consolidate global groups under a single
  jurisdiction.

## Headline features

- **Two-direction regime** — Articles 127K–127P (inbound) and
  Articles 127Q–127V (outbound) mirror each other;
- **JFSC consent gating** in both directions;
- **Solvency-statement protection** for creditors (Article 127W);
- **Special-resolution approval** by members for outbound;
- **Member-objection** right (Article 127S);
- **Creditor notice** (Article 127R) for outbound;
- **Continuity of legal identity** at the centre of both
  directions.

## Approval and timing flow

### Inbound

1. Application to JFSC (Article 127K) with articles of
   continuance (127L), name (127M), solvency statement (127W);
2. JFSC determines (Article 127N) — grant / conditions / refusal;
3. Certificate of continuance issued (Article 127O);
4. Effect from effective date (Article 127P) — body becomes a
   Jersey company.

### Outbound

1. Member approval by special resolution (Article 127Q);
2. Creditor notice (Article 127R);
3. Member objection window (Article 127S — 21 days);
4. Application to JFSC (Article 127T) with solvency statement
   (127W);
5. JFSC determines (Article 127U) — grant / conditions / refusal;
6. Effective date (Article 127V) — body ceases to be a Jersey
   company.

## Choosing between continuance, merger, and other tools

| Outcome desired | Best route |
|---|---|
| Move legal seat while keeping same entity | **Continuance** (Part 18C) |
| Combine two entities into one | **Merger** (Part 18B) or **scheme** (Article 125) |
| Move assets to a new entity in a new jurisdiction | **Asset transfer** or **continuance + restructure** |
| Move legal seat where destination doesn't recognise continuance | **Outbound merger** into a destination entity |
