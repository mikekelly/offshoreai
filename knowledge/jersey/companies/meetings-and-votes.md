# Article 92 — General Provisions as to Meetings and Votes

Article 92 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** sets the
**default voting and procedural rules** for general meetings of a
Jersey company.

## What Article 92 covers

The Article establishes default procedures:

- **Reasonable notice** to members before meetings;
- **Show-of-hands voting** initially, with **polls** available
  upon demand under [Article 97](./poll-demand.md);
- Members **present in person or by proxy** vote per the deed;
- The **chair has a casting vote** in case of ties on a show of
  hands.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Show-of-hands voting

The default mechanism for routine resolutions:

- Each **member present** has **one vote** regardless of share
  count;
- The **chair declares** the result based on votes shown;
- A **poll** can be demanded under Article 97 to convert to
  shareholding-based voting.

For most routine business in close Jersey companies, show-of-
hands voting is sufficient. For **contested or significant**
matters, a poll is typically demanded so that voting reflects
shareholdings.

## Chair's casting vote

If a show-of-hands vote ties:

- The **chair has a casting vote**;
- This is **separate** from any vote the chair has as a member;
- The chair's casting vote is **discretionary** — the chair can
  abstain.

The chair's casting vote applies only to **show-of-hands** ties.
On a **poll**, ties are resolved by the shareholding-weighted
count without a separate casting vote.

## Voting per the deed

The detailed voting rights — what shares carry votes, how
multiple-class voting works, special voting arrangements — come
from the **articles of association**. Article 92 provides the
defaults; the deed customises.

## Pitfalls

- **Mixing show-of-hands and poll** results — once a poll is
  demanded, the show-of-hands result is superseded.
- **Chair's casting vote on poll** — not available; the poll
  determines the outcome based on shareholdings.
