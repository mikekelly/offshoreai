# Article 140 — Investigation of External Companies

Article 140 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** extends
the **Part 19 investigation regime** to **external companies** —
foreign companies that have established a place of business in
Jersey.

## What Article 140 covers

Inspectors may be appointed under Article 128 to investigate the
**Jersey affairs** of an external company, with the powers in
Articles 129–139 applying with appropriate modifications. The
modifications recognise that:

- Only the **Jersey activities** of the external company are
  within the investigation's primary scope;
- Compulsion powers extend only to **Jersey persons** and
  **Jersey-located records**;
- **Foreign-located records** require international cooperation
  via the home regulator or letters rogatory.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

External companies (i.e. foreign companies registered to operate
in Jersey) can be:

- The **operational vehicle** of significant Jersey business;
- The **conduit** for transactions affecting Jersey
  counterparties;
- The **subject** of complaints by Jersey investors.

Without Article 140, the JFSC could investigate Jersey companies
but not foreign companies trading in Jersey — a significant gap.
