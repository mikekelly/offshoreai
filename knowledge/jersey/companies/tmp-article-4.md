# Takeovers Panel Law 2009 Article 4 — Further provisions about rules

Article 4 elaborates on the rule-making power in
[Article 3](./tmp-article-3.md), addressing:

- Differentiation by purpose;
- Inclusion of exceptions and modifications;
- Authority for the Panel to vary application;
- Procedural requirements for rule-making;
- Publication requirements for the rule text;
- The consequence of inadequate publication.

## Key provisions

- Rules may **differentiate** by reference to purpose,
  category of person, type of transaction, or other
  appropriate criteria.
- Rules may **include exceptions** and authorise the Panel
  to modify the application of rules in specified
  circumstances.
- Rules must be **made in writing**.
- The text of the rules must be **publicly available**.
- A person is **not in non-compliance** if the relevant text
  of the rule was not publicly accessible at the relevant
  time.

## Why this matters

Article 4 makes the Code's text **operationally enforceable**
— it must be in writing and publicly accessible. A rule that
has not been published in accessible form cannot be used as
the basis for a finding of non-compliance against a party.

This is the **due-process underpinning** of the Code's
enforceability and is consistent with the City Code's own
publication discipline.
