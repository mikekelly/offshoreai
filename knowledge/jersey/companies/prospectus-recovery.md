# Article 32 — Recovery of Compensation

Article 32 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** addresses
procedural aspects of recovery on a successful
[Article 30](prospectus-compensation.md) claim. It clarifies that
**shareholder-claimants retain full compensation rights** despite
their membership, and that compensation is **debt** rather than a
member-contribution issue in wind-up.

## What Article 32 says

- Shareholders and prospective shareholders **retain full
  compensation rights** regardless of their membership status;
- Compensation sums are treated as **ordinary debts**, not
  member-contribution issues;
- This applies both **during winding-up** and otherwise.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## The members-as-creditors point

A historical concern with prospectus-compensation claims by
**existing members** of the company:

- The member's claim is for compensation from the company;
- Members traditionally rank **behind creditors** in wind-up;
- If the member is a creditor on the prospectus claim, do they
  rank with creditors or with members?

Article 32 clarifies: a member's prospectus claim is treated as
a **debt** (creditor claim), **not** as a member-contribution
issue. The member claiming compensation has the rights of an
**ordinary creditor** in the company's wind-up.

This matters in insolvency: if the company that issued a
defective prospectus subsequently winds up, prospectus claimants
who happen to be members rank with the unsecured creditors,
**ahead of equity holders generally**.

## Procedure

The Article 30 claim is brought as **ordinary civil litigation**
in the Royal Court. The procedure:

- **Pleadings** identifying the misstatement, the loss, the
  causal connection;
- **Defence** typically relying on Article 31 grounds;
- **Discovery** of the verification file and supporting evidence;
- **Trial** before the Royal Court (Samedi Division for civil
  cases of value, or the Inferior Number where appropriate).

Class-action-style or representative procedures are available
under the Royal Court Rules for multiple-claimant scenarios.

## Limitation

The Article does not specify a limitation period; the general
Jersey-law civil-claim limitation applies. For prospectus claims,
this is typically a **few years from discovery** of the
misstatement, subject to the standard limitation framework.

## Pitfalls

- **Treating the claim as an equity claim** — it's a debt
  claim, with creditor-rank consequences for the claimant in
  any subsequent wind-up.
- **Mixing the prospectus claim with shareholder-rights
  claims** — they can run in parallel but have different
  remedies (compensation vs unfair-prejudice-style remedies under
  Part 20).
