# Article 113I — Power to Make Regulations in Respect of Recognized Auditors

Article 113I of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** gives
the **JFSC** rule-making authority over recognised auditors.

## What Article 113I covers

The Commission has authority to establish regulations governing:

1. **Recognised-auditor eligibility** and **registration**;
2. **Audit-conduct standards**;
3. **Auditor-independence** requirements;
4. **Professional-body recognition** criteria;
5. Other matters relating to **auditor oversight**.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this regulatory authority

The JFSC needs operational rule-making power to:

- **Set standards** for audits of Jersey companies;
- **Update** standards as international audit practice evolves;
- **Recognise** new professional bodies (or de-recognise
  existing ones);
- **Specify** procedural details for auditor registration and
  oversight.

The regulations sit **alongside** the primary Companies Law
provisions in Part 16 and operationalise them.

## Pitfalls

- **Treating Article 113I as primary law** — the regulations
  flesh out the Part 16 framework; both apply.
