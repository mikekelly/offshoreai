# Article 143A — Power for Minister for Treasury and Resources to Apply to Court

Article 143A of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** is
the Companies-Law side of the **economic substance** enforcement
regime — it allows the **Minister for Treasury and Resources** to
apply to the Royal Court for orders against companies that fail
the substance test.

## What Article 143A covers

Where a Jersey company has been determined (under the
**Taxation (Companies — Economic Substance) (Jersey) Law 2019**
or its successors) to have **failed the economic substance test**
for one or more financial periods, the Minister may apply to the
**Royal Court** for an order under Article 143B.

The application is typically made after:

- Tax / regulatory determinations confirming the substance
  failure;
- Penalties already imposed under the substance legislation;
- Persistent failures despite warnings.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

The **economic substance regime** was introduced in Jersey
(effective 2019) in response to **EU Code of Conduct Group** and
**OECD BEPS** demands that low-tax jurisdictions require
companies to have **real economic activity** in the jurisdiction
proportionate to the income they book there.

Failing the substance test has several layers of consequence:

- **Tax penalties** under the 2019 Law;
- **Information exchange** with relevant foreign tax authorities;
- **Companies Law enforcement** under Part 20A — the most severe
  consequence, potentially including **striking off** or other
  court-ordered consequences.

Part 20A gives the substance regime **real teeth** through court
orders that affect the company's legal status, not just its tax
position.
