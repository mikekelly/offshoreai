# Article 74A — Contracts with Sole Members Who Are Also Directors

Article 74A of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** relaxes
the strict **self-dealing** prohibition in
[Article 74](./directors-duties.md) for the narrow case of a
company with **only one member who is also the sole director**.

## What Article 74A says

When a company has:

- **only one member**; and
- that member is **also the sole director**;

[Article 74](./directors-duties.md) restrictions on **self-dealing
contracts** are **relaxed** — provided the contract terms are
**recorded in writing** or **confirmed in the company's records**.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this exception exists

In a typical single-shareholder-single-director company:

- The "company" and the "director" are effectively the same
  person commercially;
- The strict Article 74 self-dealing prohibition would catch
  routine transactions like the director paying themselves
  salary, leasing assets to the company, or providing services;
- The **disclosure** and **avoidance** rules don't make sense
  when there's no one to disclose to.

Article 74A acknowledges this **one-person-company reality** and
relaxes the rules, while retaining the **documentation
requirement** to preserve a record.

## The documentation requirement

The relaxation is **conditional on documentation**:

- **Contract terms in writing**, or
- **Confirmation in the company's records** (typically by
  director's minute or written resolution).

The documentation:

- Preserves an **audit trail** for tax, regulatory, and creditor
  purposes;
- Provides **evidence** in any later challenge (e.g., on
  insolvency where a creditor questions whether a transaction
  was at arm's length);
- Allows **regulators** to verify the company's affairs.

## Limits

Article 74A does **not** relax:

- The **broader directors' duties** (good faith, proper purpose,
  no-profit, care) — these continue to apply;
- The **solvency-statement requirements** for distributions
  under [Article 115](./distribution-restrictions.md) *(planned)*
  — the director must still confirm solvency;
- **Tax obligations** — Revenue Jersey expects arm's-length-type
  documentation even in single-member companies;
- **AML/CFT** obligations of the registered agent administering
  the company.

So Article 74A's relaxation is **narrow**: it addresses the
specific self-dealing prohibition; other rules continue to
operate.

## Pitfalls

- **Treating Article 74A as carte blanche** — it isn't. Broader
  duties continue.
- **Forgetting the documentation** — without records, the
  relaxation doesn't apply and the transaction may be
  challengeable under Article 74.
- **Multi-member transitions** — if the company adds a second
  member (becoming a two-member company), Article 74A no longer
  applies to subsequent transactions. Plan for the transition.
