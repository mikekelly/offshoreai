# Article 127M — Proposed Name

Article 127M of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]**
requires the inbound-continuance applicant to specify a
**proposed name** for the continuing Jersey company.

## What Article 127M covers

The proposed name:

- Must comply with **Article 13 name requirements** for a Jersey
  company;
- May be the **same** as the body's existing name (subject to
  Jersey rules);
- May be a **different** name if the existing name is
  unavailable or unsuitable in Jersey;
- Must reflect any **company-type suffixes** required for Jersey
  (e.g. "Limited", "PCC", "ICC").

The JFSC may object to a name that is misleading, offensive,
already in use, or otherwise non-compliant.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

The name is the **public face** of the continuing company.
Continuing under the **same name** is often desired to preserve
brand and counterparty relationships — but is sometimes blocked
by clashes on the Jersey register or by Jersey naming rules
(e.g. suffix requirements).

A change of name on continuance may require:

- **Counterparty notifications**;
- **Updating of contracts** to reflect the new name (although
  legal identity is preserved);
- **Re-issuance of certificates** and other documents.
