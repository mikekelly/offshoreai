# Article 96 — Proxies

Article 96 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** governs
the **appointment of proxies** by members to attend and vote at
general meetings on their behalf.

## What Article 96 says

Members may **appoint proxies** to attend and vote at meetings.

The **articles govern** proxy appointment procedures and voting-
rights delegation.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## The proxy mechanism

A proxy is an **agent** appointed by a member to:

- **Attend** a meeting on the member's behalf;
- **Vote** as instructed by the member (or with the member's
  discretion);
- **Generally exercise** the member's rights at that meeting.

## When proxies are used

- **Geographically-dispersed members** who cannot attend in
  person;
- **Institutional members** delegating to a representative
  (though a corporate representative under Article 93 is often
  preferred);
- **Family-office structures** where the actual owner sends a
  professional adviser as proxy;
- **Listed-company AGMs** where individual shareholders appoint
  proxy to vote on their behalf.

## Appointment procedure

The articles typically specify:

- **Form of proxy** — paper, electronic, or telephone;
- **Deadline** for proxy appointment (typically 48 hours before
  the meeting);
- **Multiple proxies** — whether a member can appoint multiple
  proxies for different shares;
- **Revocation** procedure;
- **Discretionary vs directed** proxies — whether the proxy
  must vote as instructed or has discretion.

## Voting via proxy

A proxy votes **on the member's behalf**:

- For show-of-hands voting, the proxy has **one vote** (same as
  any individual attendee);
- For a **poll**, the proxy can exercise **all the member's
  voting rights** based on shareholding;
- The proxy can **speak** at the meeting if the deed permits.

## Comparison with corporate representative

For **corporate members**, an [Article 93 corporate
representative](corporate-representation.md) is typically
preferable to a proxy:

- **Broader authority** as if the corporate member itself
  attended;
- **No proxy-form formalities**;
- More **operationally efficient**.

## Pitfalls

- **Late proxy** — submitted after the deadline. Typically
  invalid.
- **Defective proxy form** — proxies inconsistent with the
  deed's required form may be rejected.
