# Article 127FK — Pre-Registration Steps: Where Merged Body is Not a Company

Article 127FK of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** sets
out the **pre-registration document bundle** for an **outbound
merger** — where the merged body (the surviving entity) is **not a
Jersey company**.

## What Article 127FK covers

Where the merged body is to be a **foreign body corporate**
(i.e. the merger effectively redomiciles the merged business out
of Jersey), the parties must deliver to the registrar (in
addition to the documents required under Article 127FJ):

- Evidence of the **JFSC's consent** under Article 127FF;
- Evidence that the **foreign jurisdiction will recognise** the
  merger and accept the merged body;
- A **foreign-law opinion** confirming the merger's effect
  abroad;
- **Tax clearances** as required by Comptroller of Revenue;
- Confirmation of **discharge of Jersey-specific liabilities**.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

An outbound merger is, in substance, a **redomiciliation** —
the Jersey companies cease to exist in Jersey law and the
business continues as a foreign entity. The registrar's
gatekeeping is therefore stricter:

- It needs to confirm that the **foreign jurisdiction will
  accept** the merged body (otherwise the business ends up in
  legal limbo);
- It needs to confirm that **Jersey creditors** are not stranded
  with no surviving Jersey debtor;
- It needs **tax confirmation** that Jersey taxes have been
  accounted for.

This article works alongside the **continuance regime** in
Part 18C — outbound mergers and outbound continuance both
achieve redomiciliation, with different mechanics.
