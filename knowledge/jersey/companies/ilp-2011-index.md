# Incorporated Limited Partnerships (Jersey) Law 2011 — Index

The **Incorporated Limited Partnerships (Jersey) Law 2011**
creates the **Incorporated Limited Partnership (ILP)** — a
Jersey LP with **separate legal personality** and **body-
corporate character**. It supplements (does not replace) the
[Limited Partnerships Law 1994](./lp-1994-index.md), which
remains the underlying contract-LP statute.

This file is the structural entry point for the statute.
Per-Article files are scaffolded as stubs pending a primary-
source pass.

## How the ILP differs from the LP and the SLP

The Jersey partnership-vehicle family operates on a **two-axis
distinction**:

| Vehicle | Separate legal personality? | Body-corporate character? |
|---|---|---|
| LP 1994 ([`lp-1994-index.md`](./lp-1994-index.md)) | No | No |
| SLP 2011 ([`slp-2011-index.md`](./slp-2011-index.md)) | Yes | No |
| ILP 2011 (this statute) | Yes | Yes |
| LLP 2017 ([`llp-2017-index.md`](./llp-2017-index.md)) | Yes | Yes (with full-shield partner liability) |

**Separate legal personality** means the partnership can hold
property in its own name, sue and be sued in its own name, and
generally operate as a distinct legal person.

**Body-corporate character** means the partnership is treated
as a body corporate for most legal purposes — distinct from a
natural-person aggregation and distinct from a contractual
arrangement.

The ILP has **both**. This makes it useful where the
partnership economics (GP / LP capital allocation) are wanted
but the operational benefits of a body corporate are also
needed.

## Use cases

The ILP is typically chosen over the LP where:

- The partnership needs to **contract or hold property in its
  own name** (rather than through the GP) — useful for some
  real-estate fund and infrastructure-fund structures;
- A **continuing legal personality** is wanted across changes
  in partner composition;
- The **counterparty environment** prefers dealing with a
  body-corporate-like vehicle;
- The fund structure includes **incorporated cell**
  arrangements (ICC of partnerships are possible under the
  ILP regime).

The ILP is less common than the LP in market practice — most
Jersey funds use the LP 1994 because the GP-acting-on-behalf-
of-LP model is well-understood by investors and counterparties.
