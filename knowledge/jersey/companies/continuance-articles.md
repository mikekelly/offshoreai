# Article 127L — Articles of Continuance

Article 127L of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]**
requires the inbound-continuance applicant to deliver **articles
of continuance** — the Jersey-form constitutional documents that
will govern the body after it becomes a Jersey company.

## What Article 127L covers

The articles of continuance must:

- Be in a **form prescribed** for memorandum and articles of a
  Jersey company under Article 4 / Article 5;
- State the **company type** (par-value / no-par-value, limited /
  unlimited, guarantee, private / public, etc.);
- Reproduce or replace the **share capital structure** of the
  body so that the existing shareholders' rights translate to
  the Jersey company on continuance;
- Comply with all **Jersey company law requirements** that apply
  to memoranda and articles (Articles 4–7).

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

On continuance, the body **ceases to be governed by its old
constitutional documents** and **becomes governed by the
articles of continuance**. The articles of continuance therefore
need to:

- **Translate** the existing capital structure into Jersey form
  (e.g. par-value shares into Jersey par-value shares, or
  conversion to no-par-value);
- **Preserve** existing class rights;
- **Match** existing shareholder agreements where appropriate;
- **Comply** with mandatory Jersey provisions (e.g. directors'
  duties under Article 74 — which cannot be opted out of).

Mistakes here can leave shareholders with **changed rights**
without realising it.
