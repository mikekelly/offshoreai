# Article 127J — Security for Commission's Expenses

Article 127J of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** lets
the JFSC require **security for its expenses** in connection with
continuance applications.

## What Article 127J covers

The JFSC may require an applicant to:

- **Lodge security** in cash or other prescribed form for
  estimated processing expenses;
- **Indemnify** the JFSC for additional costs incurred during
  investigation of the application.

If the application is withdrawn or refused, costs are deducted
from the security and the balance returned.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

Continuance applications can involve **substantial JFSC effort**:

- Review of foreign-law opinions;
- Verification of good standing of the body in its home
  jurisdiction;
- Beneficial-ownership and source-of-funds checks;
- Inter-jurisdictional regulator liaison.

Article 127J ensures Jersey does not subsidise that work.
