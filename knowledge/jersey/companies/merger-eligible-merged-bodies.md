# Article 127C — Bodies Eligible to be Merged Bodies

Article 127C of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** says
**which bodies are eligible to be the *merged body*** — the
single surviving entity after a Part 18B merger.

## What Article 127C covers

The merged body must be:

- A **Jersey company** (existing or newly formed in connection
  with the merger); or
- A **foreign body corporate** of a prescribed description
  (where Regulations and JFSC consent permit).

Where the merged body is to be a **newly formed Jersey company**,
the merger documents must include the **memorandum and articles**
of the new company.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

Strategic choice of merged-body identity drives the **commercial
result** of the merger:

- **Inbound migrations** — foreign company becomes a Jersey
  merged body (taking advantage of Jersey corporate law, tax,
  regulatory environment);
- **Outbound migrations** — Jersey company merged into a foreign
  body (subject to foreign-jurisdiction consent and equivalence);
- **Equal mergers** — two Jersey companies combine into a new
  Jersey company born of the merger;
- **Group simplifications** — parent-subsidiary mergers (often
  using the simplified procedure under Article 127FA).
