# Article 58B — Limits on Treasury Shares

Article 58B of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** caps the
**number and nominal value** of shares a Jersey company may hold
as treasury shares.

## What Article 58B says

Treasury shares cannot exceed:

- the **number**; or
- the **nominal value**;

**specified in regulations**, ensuring companies maintain **minimum
capital requirements** while holding shares.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why limits exist

Without limits, a company could in theory:

- **Buy back its entire share base**, leaving itself as the sole
  holder of its own shares — an absurdity;
- **Hold treasury shares indefinitely**, distorting its capital
  structure;
- **Use treasury shares to thwart minority protections** —
  buying back minority holdings without genuine cancellation.

The Article 58B limits prevent these abuses while preserving the
operational utility of treasury shares.

## The current limits

The specific numerical or value limits are set by **Regulations**
made under the Article. Check the **current Regulations** for the
applicable caps — the Regulations are periodically updated.

Typical limits cap treasury holdings at **a percentage of issued
share capital** (e.g., 10% in some periods) and provide that
treasury shares **counted against issued capital** for purposes
of various Companies Law thresholds.

## Exceeding the limits

A company that exceeds the treasury-share limits is in
breach. The typical consequences:

- **Cancellation** of the excess treasury shares;
- **Offences** under the Companies Law for the directors;
- **Operational disruption** to the company's capital structure.

Track treasury holdings against the limits and **plan
disposals** before approaching the cap.

## Pitfalls

- **Slow drift** above the limit through gradual buybacks. Set
  internal thresholds well below the statutory cap.
- **Counting** — the limit is on **the company's holding**;
  shares held by subsidiaries for the parent are typically
  counted in some way under the Regulations. Check.
