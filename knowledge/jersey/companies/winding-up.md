# Part 21 — Winding Up of Companies (Overview)

Part 21 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** is the
**winding-up regime** for Jersey companies — the longest single
Part of the Law, comprising five chapters covering five
different routes by which a Jersey company can be wound up.

## The five routes

| Chapter | Articles | Route | Triggered by |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | 144–144A | Limited-duration winding up | Time / event limit in articles |
| 2 | 145–154A | **Summary** winding up | Solvent company; member-led |
| 3 | 155 | **Just-and-equitable** winding up | Member application to Court |
| 4 | 156–186 | **Creditors'** winding up | Insolvency; creditor-led |
| 5 | 186A–194A | General provisions | Cross-cutting |

## Relationship with the désastre regime

Jersey insolvency law has a **dual structure**:

- **Winding up under the Companies Law (Part 21)** — court-
  driven or member-driven processes governed by this Part;
- **Désastre under the Bankruptcy (Désastre) (Jersey) Law 1990**
  — a separate, older régime where the Viscount takes control
  of the company's affairs.

Several Articles (151, 154A, 166, 185B) bridge the two regimes,
allowing for transfer from winding up to désastre where
appropriate.

## Choosing the right route

- **Solvent solvent liquidation** — Chapter 2 summary winding up
  is the standard route. Member resolution, member-appointed
  liquidator, distribution to members after creditors paid in
  full.
- **Solvent but disputed** — Chapter 3 just-and-equitable allows
  the Court to wind up a company even though it can pay its
  debts, where there are disputes that make continuing
  operations impossible (quasi-partnership breakdown, deadlock,
  fraud).
- **Insolvent** — Chapter 4 creditors' winding up, increasingly
  the dominant route. Triggered by creditor application or by
  the company itself realising it cannot pay its debts.
- **Limited-duration company** — Chapter 1 specialised procedure
  for companies whose constitutions specify a termination event.

## Key features

- **Liquidator-led** — once winding up commences, the liquidator
  takes control of the company's affairs;
- **Directors' powers cease** (Article 163) — directors lose
  their authority on commencement of winding up;
- **Creditor protection** through claw-back: transactions at an
  undervalue (Art 176), preferences (Art 176A), wrongful trading
  (Art 177), fraudulent trading (Art 178);
- **Final dissolution** of the company after distribution.

## Article 51 distributions (trust law analogy)

Part 21 is the **company-law** insolvency regime. Trust
practitioners should remember the parallel **Article 51 (Trusts
(Jersey) Law 1984) directions** as their court-application
route.
