# Article 52 — Variation of Class Rights

Article 52 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** sets the
procedure for **varying rights attached to a class of shares** in a
Jersey company. Class rights cannot be altered by a simple
majority of all members — the **class itself** must consent.

## What Article 52 says

A company may **vary class rights** by:

- **Written consent of holders of three-fourths** of the shares
  in the class; or
- **Approval at a separate class meeting** of the class members.

"Variation" includes both **modification** and **complete
elimination** of class rights.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why class-rights protection exists

A class of shares typically has **specific rights** that
distinguish it from other classes — e.g.:

- **Preference shares** with priority dividend rights;
- **Voting / non-voting** classes;
- **Currency-specific** classes;
- **Conversion** rights into other classes;
- **Liquidation preference** in wind-up.

Without Article 52, a **simple majority of all members** could
change these rights. Article 52 ensures that **the affected
class controls its own destiny** — the class's own holders must
consent at the 75% threshold.

## The two routes

### Three-fourths written consent

The **written-consent route** doesn't require a meeting. Holders
of **75% of the affected class** can consent in writing to the
variation. This is operationally cleaner for:

- **Closely-held companies** with a small number of class
  holders;
- **Time-sensitive** variations where convening a meeting would
  delay;
- **Pre-cleared** variations where consent has been canvassed
  before the formal step.

### Class meeting with class-special-resolution

The **class-meeting route** requires:

- **Notice** to all class members;
- A **quorum** at the meeting;
- A **75% majority** vote of those present and voting.

This is the route for **larger classes** or **disputed
variations** where a formal procedure is appropriate.

## What counts as a "variation"

Variation is broader than just a deed amendment to the class
rights. The Royal Court has applied the concept to capture:

- **Express changes** to the class's rights clause;
- **Indirect changes** that effectively modify class rights
  (e.g., creating a new class with priority over the existing
  class);
- **Changes to the deed generally** that have a disproportionate
  effect on one class.

A reorganisation, share issue, or capital alteration that
affects classes differently may be a class-rights variation
needing Article 52 procedure.

## Pitfalls

- **Treating a class-rights change as a general deed amendment**
  — needs the Article 52 procedure, not just Article 11
  special resolution.
- **Indirect variation** through new class issuance —
  consider whether the new class effectively modifies existing
  class rights.
- **Class-meeting quorum failures** — convening proper class
  meetings with proper notice is a procedural minefield;
  written-consent route is often cleaner where feasible.
