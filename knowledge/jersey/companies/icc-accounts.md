# Article 127YG — Accounts of Cell Companies

Article 127YG of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]**
modifies the **Part 16 accounting regime** to fit the cell-
company structure.

## What Article 127YG covers

Each ICC and each of its incorporated cells must prepare
accounts in accordance with Part 16 (subject to modifications):

- Each **incorporated cell** prepares its **own accounts** as a
  separate company;
- The **ICC** typically prepares its own accounts (relating to
  its own assets and the management of the cell structure);
- **Group / aggregate** accounts may also be required where the
  ICC consolidates its cells in substance.

Specific modifications may include:

- **Inter-cell transactions** disclosure;
- **Allocation of common expenses** between ICC and cells;
- **Auditor rotation** considerations across the cell structure.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

The **financial-segregation principle** that underpins the cell
structure requires **clear accounting**. If a cell's assets and
liabilities are not properly tracked, the legal-segregation
principle can be undermined in practice:

- Creditors of one cell may struggle to identify cell-specific
  assets;
- Inter-cell loans and balances need transparent recording;
- Cross-cell guarantees need clear disclosure.

Article 127YG ensures the **accounting backbone** matches the
legal structure.
