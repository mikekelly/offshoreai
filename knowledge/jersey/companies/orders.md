# Article 219 — Orders

Article 219 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** provides
for the making of **Orders** by the Minister (or other authorised
person) where the Companies Law contemplates Orders rather than
Regulations.

## What Article 219 covers

Where a provision of the Companies Law refers to an Order, the
Minister:

- Has power to **make** the Order;
- May **vary or revoke** previously made Orders;
- Must follow any **procedural requirements** in the relevant
  provision (e.g. consultation, publication).

Orders are typically used for **more granular** or **more
specific** delegated legislation than Regulations.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

The Companies Law has a **hierarchy of delegated legislation**:

- **Regulations** — made by the States, used for substantive
  rules;
- **Orders** — made by the Minister, used for more specific
  matters;
- **Rules** — made by the Royal Court / Chief Minister, used for
  procedure.

Article 219 anchors the Order-making power.
