# Article 25 — Definition of "Member"

Article 25 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** defines
who is a **member** of a Jersey company. The definition is more
nuanced than just "shareholder" and threads through the rest of
the Law.

## What Article 25 says

- **Subscribers to the memorandum** are deemed to have agreed to
  become members; on registration, they are entered in the
  register of members.
- After incorporation, "**every other person who agrees to become
  a member of a company, and whose name is entered in its register
  of members, is a member of the company**".
- **Exception**: protected-cell-company membership rules under
  [Article 127YQ](protected-cell-company-membership.md) *(planned)*
  may modify the position for PCC cells.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## The two requirements

To be a member, a person must:

1. **Agree to become a member** — typically by subscribing, by
   accepting a share allotment, or by accepting a transfer; and
2. **Be entered in the register of members** — the formal record
   under [Article 41](register-of-members.md) *(planned)*.

Both are required. A person who has paid for shares but is not yet
on the register is **not yet a member** (though may have a
contractual right to be registered). A person on the register who
hasn't agreed to membership has an unsound entry.

## Membership vs beneficial ownership

A Jersey company's register of members is **not** a register of
beneficial owners. The register records:

- the **legal title** to the shares;
- typically the **registered holder** as named.

Where shares are held by a **trustee**, **nominee**, or
**custodian** for a beneficial owner, the **trustee/nominee** is
on the register and is the "member" — the beneficial owner is not.

The beneficial-owner question is handled separately under the
**Financial Services (Disclosure and Provision of Information)
(Jersey) Law 2020** (FSDI Law) — see
[`../registries/beneficial-ownership.md`](../registries/beneficial-ownership.md)
*(stub)*.

## Article 48 — "trusts not to be entered on register"

[Article 48](no-trusts-on-register.md) *(planned)* expressly
states that "trusts" are not to be entered on the register. The
register shows the **legal holder**, not the underlying
beneficial-ownership structure.

This means:

- A **trustee** is registered in their own name;
- The fact that they hold the shares **as trustee** is not on the
  public register;
- The company is entitled to treat the registered holder as the
  full member with all rights.

This is a practical rule that simplifies share-register
administration; the beneficial-ownership data is captured
separately and is not public.

## Membership rights

A member typically has:

- **Voting rights** (depending on share class);
- **Dividend rights** (depending on share class);
- **Right to a copy of the deed** (Article 12);
- **Right to inspect the register** (Article 45);
- **Right to information** under various Articles;
- **Right to attend AGMs and other general meetings** (Part 15);
- **Right to bring statutory remedies** — unfair prejudice (Part
  20), just-and-equitable winding-up (Article 155).

## PCC carve-out

Protected Cell Companies under Part 18D have specific membership
mechanics — the PCC itself has members, but cells may have their
own member relationships. See
[`cell-companies.md`](cell-companies.md) for the broader context,
and Article 127YQ for the specific PCC rules.

## Pitfalls

- **Conflating registered member with beneficial owner**. Members
  are the registered legal holders; the BO data is separate.
- **Acting on un-registered share rights**. A buyer who has
  paid but is not yet on the register is not yet a member and
  cannot exercise membership rights (subject to specific deed
  provisions).
- **Joint registered holders** — the register typically shows
  joint members; voting and dividend rules follow the deed.
