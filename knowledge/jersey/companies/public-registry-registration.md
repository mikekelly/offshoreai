# Article 214 — Registration in the Public Registry

Article 214 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]**
addresses **registration in the Public Registry** (the registry
for Jersey **immovable property** transactions, separate from
the Companies Registry).

## What Article 214 covers

Where a Jersey company:

- Acquires, holds, or disposes of **immovable property** in
  Jersey;
- Grants **hypothecs** (Jersey-law security over immovable
  property);
- Otherwise engages in transactions affecting the Jersey land
  register;

the relevant **registration in the Public Registry** must be
effected in accordance with the **Loi (1880) sur la propriété
foncière** and related real-property legislation.

The Companies Law provisions for **directors' authority** and
**company seals** support proper execution of such registrations.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

Jersey's **Public Registry** is the **land register** for the
island — a separate institution from the **Companies Registry**.
Real estate transactions follow distinct procedures rooted in
**Jersey's customary law**:

- **Heritable** (héritable) property contracts are passed before
  the Royal Court;
- **Hypothecs** are registered in the Public Registry;
- **Foreign-law mortgages** do not work for Jersey land — the
  Jersey hypothec is required.

Article 214 ensures **Companies Law mechanics** dovetail with
Jersey real-property law where companies own Jersey land.
