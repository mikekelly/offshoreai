# Takeovers Panel Law 2009 Article 16 — No action for breach of statutory duty etc.

Article 16 is the **private-litigation immunity** clause.

## Key provisions

- A contravention of a requirement under the Law gives
  **no right of action** for breach of statutory duty.
- A contravention of a rule under the Law **does not void**
  a transaction or otherwise affect its validity, unless
  the rules so provide.

## Operational significance

Two important consequences:

### Enforcement channel through the Panel only

Parties affected by another party's breach of Code rules
cannot bring private claims for breach-of-statutory-duty
damages. The enforcement channel runs through the **Panel
itself**, then via the Court (under
[Article 15](./tmp-article-15.md)) where Panel processes
do not produce compliance.

### Transaction validity preserved

A takeover bid completed in breach of a Code rule is **not
automatically void**. The completed transaction stands; the
remedy is at the Panel level (sanctions, compensation), not
at the contract-validity level. This protects market
certainty — a completed acquisition does not unwind because
of a procedural Code breach.

The "**unless the rules so provide**" caveat permits the
Panel's rules themselves to specify particular contractual
consequences in particular cases.
