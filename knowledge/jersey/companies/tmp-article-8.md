# Takeovers Panel Law 2009 Article 8 — Restriction on disclosure

Article 8 imposes a **confidentiality regime** on information
received by the Panel — prohibiting disclosure of information
relating to private affairs of an individual or particular
business without consent, save for specific exceptions.

## Key provisions

Information about an individual's private affairs or
business cannot be disclosed by the Panel without consent,
**except**:

- Disclosures facilitating Panel functions;
- Disclosures to persons specified in **Part 1 of the
  Schedule** (typically regulators and similar bodies);
- Disclosures of **types specified in Part 2 of the
  Schedule** (typically anonymised / aggregate
  information);
- Disclosures under **Part 3 of the Schedule** (specific
  authorised disclosure types);
- Information already in the public domain.

The States may amend the Schedule subject to restrictions
on who may be specified. The provisions respect data-
protection requirements.
