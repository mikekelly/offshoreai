# Article 168 — Meetings of Company and Creditors

Article 168 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** sets
out the **regime for subsequent meetings** of the company and
creditors during a creditors' winding up.

## What Article 168 covers

The liquidator must convene meetings:

- **Annually** to report on progress;
- **On request** by a prescribed proportion of creditors;
- **At the end of each accounting period** to consider accounts;
- **At conclusion** of the winding up (Article 169 final
  meeting).

Meetings are open to:

- **All creditors** known and proven;
- **Members** (with non-voting or limited rights, given members'
  subordinated interests in insolvency).

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

Long-running liquidations need **ongoing creditor governance**.
Article 168 ensures creditors retain visibility and influence
throughout the procedure — not just at the start.
