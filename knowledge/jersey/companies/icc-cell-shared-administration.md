# Article 127YDA — Requirements in Relation to Secretaries, Directors, Registered Offices and Registers

Article 127YDA of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]**
provides for **shared and aligned administration** between an
ICC and its incorporated cells.

## What Article 127YDA covers

Typical requirements include:

- The **secretary** of the ICC must (or may be required to) also
  be a secretary of each cell;
- The **registered office** of each cell must be the same as the
  registered office of the ICC;
- The **register of members**, **register of directors**, and
  other prescribed registers of each cell must be available at
  the ICC's registered office (or accessible through it);
- Certain **directors** of the cell may be required to be directors
  of the ICC, or vice versa.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

While ICC cells have **separate legal personality**, in practice
they are commonly:

- Administered by the **same service provider** as the ICC;
- Located at the **same physical office**;
- Subject to **common governance** at the practical level.

Article 127YDA reflects this commercial reality and ensures that:

- **Notices and process** can be served effectively (registered
  office of the ICC works for all cells);
- The **public register** remains coherent and searchable;
- Common-administration economies are preserved.
