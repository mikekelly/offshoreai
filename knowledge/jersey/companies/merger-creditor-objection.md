# Article 127FE — Objection by Creditor if All Solvency Statements Made

Article 127FE of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** is
the **creditor's right of objection** to a Part 18B merger, even
where the directors have made the solvency statement.

## What Article 127FE covers

Where all merging bodies have made the required solvency
statements, a **creditor** of any merging body may, within a
prescribed period (commonly 21 days of notice), apply to the
**Royal Court** for an order that the merger should not be made,
or should be made on different terms — or that the creditor
should be **paid, secured, or guaranteed** before the merger
proceeds.

The Court may make any order it thinks just, including:

- **Cancellation** of the merger;
- **Conditions** to be satisfied before registration (payment of
  the creditor, security, parent guarantee);
- **No order** if satisfied that the merged body's solvency is
  adequately protected.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

Even with the directors' solvency certificate, the **transfer of
liabilities** to the merged body can prejudice individual
creditors:

- A **creditor of a strong company** may end up with a claim
  against a weaker merged body;
- A **secured creditor's collateral** may be eroded by combined
  asset / liability structure;
- A creditor with **guarantees or covenants** specific to its
  original debtor may lose protection.

Article 127FE gives that creditor a **case-by-case Court remedy**.
Courts will typically not block a merger that is properly approved
and where directors' solvency certificates are reasonable — but
will routinely **impose protective conditions** (payment, security,
guarantee) where a specific creditor's position is materially
prejudiced.

## Pitfalls

- **Tight time limit** — creditors must move quickly within the
  21-day period.
- **Burden on creditor** to show actual prejudice — generic
  worries about the merged body do not normally suffice.
