# Article 28 — Prohibition of Minors etc. from Membership

Article 28 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** prevents
**minors and certain protected persons** from becoming members of a
Jersey company, with a narrow exception for automatic transmission
on the death of a previous member.

## What Article 28 says

**Minors** and **certain protected persons** cannot become company
members.

**Exception**: where membership rights **transfer automatically
following the death of the previous member** (e.g., to a minor
heir by will or intestacy).

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Who is a "minor"

Under Jersey law, a minor is generally a person who has not yet
reached the **age of majority** (currently 18). The reference also
captures **protected persons** — those under formal incapacity
(*interdiction* or its modern equivalent).

The policy reason: a minor cannot validly contract, so cannot
validly **agree to become a member** under
[Article 25](./definition-of-member.md).

## How structures accommodate minor beneficiaries

Where a family wants a Jersey company structure to benefit a
minor, Article 28 prevents direct registration of the minor as a
member. Common workarounds:

- **Trust holding**: a trust holds the shares; the trustee is the
  registered member; the minor is a beneficiary. Article 28 does
  not apply because the trustee is the member.
- **Holding company structure**: an adult holds the
  intermediating company's shares; the minor inherits later.
- **Guardian / *tuteur* structure**: under Jersey customary law,
  a *tuteur* may be appointed to hold property for a minor.

For most modern Jersey wealth-planning structures, a **Jersey
trust** is the natural vehicle for minor beneficial interests —
the trust's legal mechanics accommodate minor beneficiaries
through the trustee's intermediating role.

## Transmission on death exception

The exception preserves the operational reality of **inheritance**.
If a member dies and their shares pass to a minor heir (by will,
intestacy, or other transmission mechanism), Article 28 does not
prevent that transmission. The minor becomes a member; their
**rights are typically exercised by their *tuteur* or guardian**
until the minor reaches majority.

The exception is narrow — it covers **automatic transmission**
following death, not **voluntary transfer** to a minor.

## Pitfalls

- **Direct gift of shares to a minor** is not within the
  Article 28 exception. The transfer is at risk; use a trust or
  hold-for-minor structure instead.
- **Subscription by a minor** is not effective. If a parent
  inadvertently includes a minor as a subscriber, the company
  formation may be defective.
