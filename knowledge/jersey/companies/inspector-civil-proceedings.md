# Article 136 — Power to Bring Civil Proceedings on Behalf of Body Corporate

Article 136 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** gives
the **JFSC** power to **bring civil proceedings on behalf of a
company** investigated under Part 19, where the company itself
is unwilling or unable to pursue claims that the investigation
has uncovered.

## What Article 136 covers

Where an inspector's report discloses:

- **Misconduct by directors or officers** giving the company a
  cause of action against them;
- **Wrongful diversion of company assets**;
- **Other claims** the company has against third parties;

the JFSC may bring those civil proceedings **in the name of the
company** — typically when:

- The current directors are themselves the wrongdoers (and so
  will not authorise proceedings);
- The company is in financial difficulty and cannot fund
  proceedings;
- It is in the **public interest** for the proceedings to be
  brought.

The proceeds (after costs) **belong to the company**.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

This is a powerful tool because:

- **Director wrongdoers** cannot block recovery by withholding
  authorisation;
- **Minority shareholders** who would otherwise have to fund a
  derivative action are spared the cost;
- The **JFSC's expertise** and resources are brought to bear
  on complex litigation.

Article 136 is the **enforcement bridge** between the
investigation regime and civil recovery.
