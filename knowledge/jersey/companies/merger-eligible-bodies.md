# Article 127B — Bodies Eligible to Merge

Article 127B of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** says
**which bodies corporate can use the Part 18B merger regime** as
a merging body.

## What Article 127B covers

A body corporate is eligible to merge under Part 18B where it is:

- A **Jersey company** under the Companies Law; or
- A **foreign body corporate** of a description prescribed by
  Regulations (typically requiring that the foreign jurisdiction
  recognises mergers and that there is an equivalent solvency
  protection regime).

The merger must involve **at least one Jersey company** as a
merging body — Part 18B is not available for an entirely-foreign
merger.

Subject to specified restrictions:

- Bodies in **liquidation, administration or equivalent
  insolvency proceedings** are typically excluded;
- Bodies under prescribed **regulatory action** may be excluded
  or subject to additional consents.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

Cross-border statutory mergers are powerful corporate tools:

- A Jersey company can **merge with a foreign company** with
  assets vesting by operation of law in the merged body;
- The merger is **court-free** in Jersey (subject to JFSC consent
  where required);
- The result is a **single surviving legal entity**, not the
  layered group that would result from an acquisition.

Article 127B is the **gating provision** — only eligible bodies
can participate.
