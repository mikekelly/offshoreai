# Article 123 — Associates

Article 123 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** defines
**associates** for the purposes of Part 18 — the persons whose
shareholdings and acquisitions are aggregated with the offeror's
for the 90% threshold and other Part 18 calculations.

## What Article 123 covers

A person is an **associate** of the offeror where, broadly:

- They are a **subsidiary or holding body** of the offeror (or a
  fellow subsidiary);
- They are an **officer, partner, employee or shareholder** of
  the offeror with significant participation;
- They have entered into an **agreement or understanding** with
  the offeror to acquire shares in the target on a coordinated
  basis (concert party);
- They are a **nominee** of the offeror;
- Other prescribed close-relationship categories.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

The squeeze-out threshold is **90% of the shares to which the
offer relates** — i.e. shares **not already held** by the offeror.
Without an associates rule, a bidder could:

- Park 10% of the shares with a related party;
- Make a takeover offer for the remaining 90%;
- Quickly hit the squeeze-out threshold even though only a small
  proportion of the truly independent shareholders accepted.

Article 123 prevents that by treating shares held or acquired by
associates as held by the offeror (and therefore **excluded** from
the "shares to which the offer relates" — meaning the 90% must be
reached without leaning on associate-held shares).

## Symmetric effect

Conversely, for the **sell-out** under Article 119:

- The offeror's holding **plus** associates' holdings must reach
  90% before the sell-out is triggered;
- This **broadens** the sell-out trigger and protects more
  minority shareholders.

So the associates rule cuts both ways — it constrains the
offeror's squeeze-out path while widening the minority's sell-out
path.

## Pitfalls

- **Inadvertent concert parties** — informal coordination between
  a bidder and a major shareholder can render the major a
  Part 18 associate, even without a written agreement.
- **Family and trust structures** — trustees, beneficiaries, and
  controlled entities can fall within the associates net.
