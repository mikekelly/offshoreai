# Article 157A — Application for Creditors' Winding Up by Creditor

Article 157A of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** gives
**creditors** the power to apply to the Royal Court for an order
placing the company in creditors' winding up.

## What Article 157A covers

A creditor may apply to the Royal Court for an order under
Article 157C placing the company in creditors' winding up where
the creditor can show that:

- The company is **unable to pay its debts** (insolvent);
- The creditor has a **valid claim** against the company that
  has not been paid;
- A statutory **demand or judgment** has gone unsatisfied (in
  many cases);
- It is **just and equitable** for the Court to make the order.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

The creditor application is the **principal compulsory winding-
up tool** for unpaid creditors. Without it:

- Creditors of insolvent companies would be stranded with no
  recovery mechanism;
- Directors could keep an insolvent company operational
  indefinitely (continuing to harm new creditors);
- The orderly distribution of remaining assets would be left to
  ad hoc action.

The creditor application gives the **Court control** of an
insolvent company even where its directors and members are
unwilling to put it into liquidation.
