# Article 158 — Notice of Winding Up

Article 158 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** sets
out **notice requirements** on commencement of creditors'
winding up.

## What Article 158 covers

Within prescribed periods of commencement of creditors' winding
up, notice must be:

- **Filed with the registrar**;
- **Published** as prescribed (typically in the JEP and on the
  registrar's website);
- **Sent to creditors** known to the company.

The notice typically contains:

- The fact and date of commencement;
- Identity of the liquidator (where appointed);
- Information about the **first creditors' meeting**;
- The cut-off date for **proofs of debt**.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

Without proper notice, creditors cannot:

- **Submit proofs of debt** in time to participate in
  distributions;
- **Attend** creditors' meetings;
- **Object** to liquidator appointments;
- **Stop** giving the company further credit.

Article 158 ensures the **public-record** and **creditor-
notification** functions are performed promptly.
