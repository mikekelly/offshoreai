# Article 113D — Ineligibility to Act as Auditor

Article 113D of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** lists
who **cannot serve as an auditor** of a Jersey company.

## What Article 113D covers

Persons who **cannot serve as auditor**:

- those **lacking required qualifications**;
- those with **conflicts of interest**;
- those **subject to professional sanctions**;
- **directors and employees** of the audited company;
- individuals with **disqualification orders**;
- those **lacking necessary professional standing**.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Categories of ineligibility

### Insufficient qualifications

A person without the qualifications required under
[Articles 111](auditor-registration.md) and
[112](auditor-qualification.md) is not on the recognised-auditor
register and cannot validly act.

### Conflicts of interest

A person with a substantive conflict — typically a financial
interest in the audited company — is ineligible. The
**independence** rules in [Article 113E](auditor-independence.md)
expand on this.

### Professional sanctions

A person currently subject to disciplinary sanctions by their
professional body — suspension, exclusion, conditions on
practice — is ineligible.

### Director or employee

A **director or employee** of the audited company cannot serve as
its auditor. This is the most basic independence rule.

### Disqualified persons

A person subject to a Companies Law
[Article 78](disqualification-orders.md) disqualification order
cannot serve as auditor.

### Lack of professional standing

A broader catch-all — a person who is not in good standing in
their profession.

## Pitfalls

- **Cross-relationship issues** — a partner of an audit firm
  who has personal ties to the audited company can compromise
  the firm's eligibility.
- **Past employment** — recent former employees may face
  cooling-off requirements.
