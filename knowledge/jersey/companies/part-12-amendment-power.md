# Article 66A — Power of States to Amend Part 12

Article 66A of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** is the
**amendment-power** provision for Part 12 (Reduction of Capital).

## What Article 66A says

The **States may amend this Part by Regulations**, allowing
legislative modification of capital-reduction procedures.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

Capital-reduction procedure is a sensitive area where:

- **Commercial practice** evolves (e.g., new mechanisms for
  group reorganisations);
- **OECD inclusive-framework expectations** can affect what
  reductions are considered legitimate;
- **Operational efficiency** improvements may be appropriate.

The States can update Part 12 by Regulations without primary-law
amendment — keeping the Jersey regime responsive without the
heavy lift of full legislation.

## Family of amendment powers

One of several Part-specific amendment-power Articles — see also
Articles 2B, 17D, 40C, 59, 85A, 113H, 115B, 124A, 127GA, 127YN,
194A. Each is limited to its specific Part.
