# Takeovers Panel Law 2009 Article 23 — Other legislation relating to takeovers and mergers

Article 23 is a **preservation clause** — confirming that
the 2009 Law operates **alongside**, not in displacement of,
other Jersey legislation relating to takeovers and mergers.

## Key provisions

The Law does not affect:

- Provisions of the
  [Companies (Jersey) Law 1991](./companies-law-1991.md)
  regarding takeover bids (including Articles 117–122
  squeeze-out / sell-out, Part 18A schemes of arrangement,
  Part 18B statutory mergers);
- Provisions of the
  [Competition (Jersey) Law 2005](../international/index.md)
  regarding takeover-relevant competition matters.

## Operational significance

A Jersey-incorporated takeover transaction operates within
**three regulatory layers** simultaneously:

1. **Substantive Jersey company law** — CJL 1991 mechanics
   for offer / scheme / squeeze-out / sell-out
   ([`./squeeze-out.md`](./squeeze-out.md),
   [`./sell-out.md`](./sell-out.md),
   [`./scheme-of-arrangement.md`](./scheme-of-arrangement.md));
2. **Takeovers Panel Law / Jersey Code** — conduct of the
   bid;
3. **UK City Code** (where applicable) — see
   [`../external-rules/uk-takeover-code.md`](../external-rules/uk-takeover-code.md).

Article 23 confirms that the Panel Law does not replace
layers 1 and 3.
