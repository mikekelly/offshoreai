# Article 144 — Procedure: Winding Up of Limited Life Companies

Article 144 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** sets
out the **automatic winding-up procedure** for **limited life
companies** when the limiting event occurs.

## What Article 144 covers

When the **limiting event** specified in a limited life company's
memorandum or articles occurs (e.g. expiry of a fixed term, death
of a specified person, occurrence of a specified event):

- The company **automatically commences winding up** on that
  date;
- The winding-up follows the **summary or creditors' winding-up
  procedure** as appropriate to the company's solvency;
- The directors must give **notice to the registrar** of the
  commencement.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

Limited life companies (Article 3H) are used in transactional
structures requiring **automatic termination** — joint ventures
with fixed terms, single-purpose vehicles tied to specific
events, fund vehicles for closed-end strategies.

Article 144 makes the **termination automatic** rather than
discretionary — the limiting event triggers the wind-down without
need for further member resolution.
