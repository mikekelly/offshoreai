# Article 77 — Indemnity of Officers and Former Officers

Article 77 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** governs
the company's ability to **indemnify** its officers and former
officers against liabilities incurred in their corporate role.

## What Article 77 covers

A Jersey company may indemnify its **officers and former
officers** against:

- liabilities incurred **in the course of their duties**;
- legal **costs** of defending claims (subject to specific
  rules);
- other costs associated with the corporate role.

Indemnities are typically provided in the **articles of
association** or by separate **deed of indemnity** with the
officer.

(For the verbatim text see [jerseylaw.je][cjl]; the consolidated
text accessed for this corpus is brief on the specific scope
limits.)

## What can and cannot be indemnified

The Article 77 indemnity typically:

- **Can cover** liability to third parties for acts done
  honestly in the corporate role;
- **Can cover** defence costs in proceedings;
- **Can cover** liabilities arising from civil claims where the
  officer acted in good faith;
- **Cannot cover** liability **to the company itself** for
  breach of duty (the company can't indemnify the officer
  against claims it is itself bringing);
- **Cannot cover** liability for **fraud**, **wilful misconduct**,
  or **dishonest** acts (matching the irreducible-core principle).

The detailed scope is set by the **articles** of the company —
modern Jersey deeds typically include detailed indemnity
provisions tracking the Article 77 limits.

## Insurance

In addition to indemnity from the company, officers typically
have **directors and officers (D&O) liability insurance**:

- **Underwritten** by commercial insurers;
- **Pays defence costs** and judgment amounts;
- Has its own **exclusions** (typically tracking the
  irreducible-core: fraud, wilful misconduct);
- Provides **cover beyond the company's indemnity** where the
  company itself is the claimant (e.g., insolvent company
  pursuing its own former directors).

D&O insurance is **standard** for professional directors and
for boards of substantive Jersey companies.

## Indemnity for former officers

The Article expressly covers **former officers** — typically:

- **Resigned directors** who face subsequent claims for acts
  during their tenure;
- **Removed directors** challenged after departure;
- **Directors of dissolved companies** where corporate
  successors continue the indemnity obligation.

The indemnity survives the officer's departure, subject to its
own terms.

## Pitfalls

- **Indemnity scope drift** — articles indemnities that purport
  to cover fraud or wilful misconduct are **void** as to those
  matters.
- **Insufficient D&O insurance** — relying on company indemnity
  alone is risky in a company that may become insolvent.
- **Former-officer paperwork** — ensure the indemnity continues
  by deed on the officer's departure.
