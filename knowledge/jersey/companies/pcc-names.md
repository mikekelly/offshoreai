# Article 127YS — Names of Protected Cell Companies

Article 127YS of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** sets
**naming requirements** for PCCs and their cells.

## What Article 127YS covers

- The name of a **protected cell company** must include the
  words **"Protected Cell Company"** (or the abbreviation
  **"PCC"**);
- Each **cell** of the PCC must have a **name or identifier**
  that distinguishes it from other cells of the PCC;
- Cells are commonly referred to as **"Cell X of [PCC Name]"**
  or by a descriptive name (e.g. "Growth Fund Cell of [PCC
  Name]").

The naming is subject to **Article 13 general name rules**.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

The "**PCC**" designator alerts counterparties that:

- They are dealing with a **single legal entity** that operates
  through multiple statutory cells;
- The PCC's assets are subject to **cell segregation**;
- They need to identify **which cell** they are contracting with
  to understand which assets are available to satisfy claims.

The cell-identifier:

- Helps directors meet the **disclosure-of-cell-identity** duty
  (Article 127YR);
- Helps counterparties identify their **cell exposure**;
- Supports **accounting segregation** at the operational level.
