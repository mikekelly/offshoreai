# Article 54 — Registration of Particulars of Special Rights

Article 54 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** requires
companies to **register particulars** of any **special rights**
attached to share classes — making the existence and broad terms
of class rights public information.

## What Article 54 says

Companies must **register detailed information** about any
**special rights** attached to share classes with the **Registrar**.

This applies to:

- **Preferential voting rights**;
- **Dividend privileges**;
- **Redemption features**;
- Other class-specific rights.

The registered particulars serve as **official notice to all
stakeholders and the public** regarding such arrangements.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## What gets registered

Where a class of shares carries special rights, the company
must file particulars with the Registrar — typically:

- The **class designation** (e.g., "Class B Preference Shares");
- The **number of shares** in the class authorised and issued;
- The **rights** attached — voting, dividend, capital,
  conversion, redemption;
- Any **conditions** or **triggers** on those rights.

The filed particulars become **part of the public record** at the
JFSC Registry.

## Why this matters

Public registration of class rights serves:

- **Counterparty due diligence** — a party dealing with the
  company can check what classes exist and what rights they
  carry;
- **Investor information** — prospective investors can confirm
  the rights they would acquire;
- **Creditor protection** — creditors can identify priority
  arrangements affecting wind-up distribution;
- **Regulatory transparency** — the JFSC and other regulators
  can see the company's capital structure.

## Interaction with the memorandum and articles

The **substantive class rights** typically live in:

- the **memorandum** — for high-level capital structure;
- the **articles** — for detailed rights (voting, dividend,
  etc.).

The Article 54 **registration of particulars** is a separate
filing — it provides a **summary** for the public record without
requiring the public to read the full memorandum and articles.

For **simple capital structures** (e.g., a single class of
ordinary shares), Article 54 filing is light. For **complex
structures** (multiple classes, conversion rights, preference
features), it is more substantial.

## Updating particulars

Where the rights are **varied** under
[Article 52](./variation-of-class-rights.md), the registered
particulars must be **updated** to reflect the new position.
Failure to update is an offence.

## Pitfalls

- **Failure to register** particulars at incorporation —
  basic compliance failure.
- **Failure to update** after variation — common when
  class-rights changes happen without process discipline.
- **Inconsistency** between registered particulars and the
  deed — confusing for users; the deed prevails as the legal
  source but the registration should reflect it.
