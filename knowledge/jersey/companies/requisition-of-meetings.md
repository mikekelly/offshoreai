# Article 89 — Requisition of Meetings

Article 89 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** gives a
**substantial minority of members** the power to **requisition**
a general meeting — forcing the directors to call one even where
they would prefer not to.

## What Article 89 says

Members holding **not less than one-tenth (10%)** of paid-up
capital carrying voting rights may **requisition the directors**
to call a general meeting, **specifying the business** to be
transacted.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## The 10% threshold

A **10% minority** can force a meeting. The threshold is:

- **Of paid-up capital carrying voting rights** — not 10% of
  total members or 10% of all shares;
- **Aggregate** — the 10% can be made up of several smaller
  members acting together;
- **At the requisition date** — measured at the time of the
  requisition, not at any subsequent date.

## What the requisition must include

- **Names** of the requisitioning members;
- **Their voting rights** showing the 10% threshold met;
- **The business** to be transacted (specific resolutions
  proposed);
- **Compliance** with any deed-specified procedural requirements.

## Effect

On receipt of a valid requisition:

- The **directors must call a meeting** within the time period
  specified by the Companies Law or the deed (typically a few
  weeks);
- If the directors **fail** to call the meeting, the
  **requisitioners themselves can call it** at the company's
  expense;
- The **business specified** in the requisition is the agenda.

## Use cases

Requisitioned meetings are typical mechanisms for:

- **Dissident minority** members forcing a vote on directors;
- **Activist investors** in listed Jersey companies pushing
  governance changes;
- **Family-business disputes** where one branch wants to force a
  vote;
- **Shareholder protection** against entrenched management.

## Pitfalls

- **Failure to meet the 10% threshold** — invalidates the
  requisition.
- **Inadequate specification** of business — directors may
  treat the requisition as invalid.
- **Directors ignoring valid requisition** — they may face
  personal liability for the requisitioners' costs in self-
  calling the meeting.
