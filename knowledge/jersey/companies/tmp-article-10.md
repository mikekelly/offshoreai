# Takeovers Panel Law 2009 Article 10 — Panel's duty of co-operation

Article 10 imposes a **statutory duty of co-operation** on
the Panel, both with the JFSC and with regulatory bodies
outside Jersey performing similar functions.

## Key provisions

The Panel must, to the extent it considers appropriate, co-
operate with:

- The **[Commission (JFSC)](../financial-regulation/jfsc.md)**;
- Any **regulatory body** outside Jersey exercising
  functions similar to the Panel's.

Co-operation may include the sharing of information not
restricted by [Article 8](./tmp-article-8.md).

## Operational significance

The cross-border co-operation duty is the **mechanism** by
which the Jersey Panel coordinates with the UK Panel on
transactions touching both jurisdictions — the typical case
where a Jersey-incorporated company is admitted to a UK
market. The duty enables seamless practical handling of
joint-jurisdiction transactions.
