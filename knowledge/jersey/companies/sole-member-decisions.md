# Article 95A — Recording of Decisions by Sole Member

Article 95A of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** is the
**sole-member-company** variant: where a private company has only
one member, that member's decisions must be **recorded in
writing**.

## What Article 95A says

Where a private company has **only one member**, any decision
taken by that member shall be **recorded in writing**.

The recording constitutes **evidence of the decision**.

These records must be **maintained with other company
documents**.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

A sole-member private company is structurally simpler than a
multi-member company:

- **No need for meetings** — the member alone can make decisions;
- **No need for circulation** — Article 95ZA / ZB / ZC don't
  apply;
- **No quorum issues**;
- **Decisions** are effective when made by the sole member.

But the **paper trail** is critical:

- For **audit trail** purposes;
- For **regulatory** verification (JFSC, Revenue Jersey);
- For **subsequent transactions** with third parties needing
  evidence of company decisions;
- For **wind-up** scrutiny.

Article 95A requires the **writing**.

## What gets recorded

The sole member's decisions on:

- **Director appointments and removals**;
- **Memorandum and articles amendments** (subject to special
  resolution requirements — even for a sole member, a special
  resolution recorded in writing);
- **Major transactions**;
- **Distributions** (with the Article 115 solvency-statement
  process);
- **Wind-up or restructuring** decisions.

## Form of recording

The Article doesn't prescribe a specific form. Typical
documentation:

- **Written resolution** signed by the sole member;
- **Minute** of a "decision" (in lieu of a meeting);
- **Letter or memorandum** from the member.

The key is that the decision is **in writing** with the date,
the substance, and the member's signature.

## Pitfalls

- **Oral or informal decisions** — not effective; the writing
  is required.
- **Lost records** — recreate from supporting evidence if
  possible; but the original should be retained.
