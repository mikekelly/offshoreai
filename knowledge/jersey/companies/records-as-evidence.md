# Article 207 — Examination of Records and Admissibility of Evidence

Article 207 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** sets
out how the **records of a company** can be **examined and used
as evidence** in proceedings.

## What Article 207 covers

- Records of a company are **admissible** in legal proceedings as
  evidence of the matters they record;
- The records may be **proved** by production of the original or
  a certified copy;
- Examiners (including inspectors under Part 19 and liquidators)
  have rights to **inspect and copy** records as needed;
- **Privilege** rules apply to limit access to legally
  privileged material.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

Company records are the **primary evidence** of:

- **Member relationships** (register of members);
- **Director appointments** (register of directors);
- **Resolutions and decisions** (minute books);
- **Financial position** (accounts and accounting records).

Article 207 ensures these records are usable in court and other
proceedings without the cumbersome formality of certifying every
page through a custodian witness.
