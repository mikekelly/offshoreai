# Article 127FJ — Pre-Registration Steps: Where All Merging Bodies are Companies

Article 127FJ of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** sets
out the **pre-registration document bundle** that must be filed
with the registrar where **all merging bodies are Jersey
companies**.

## What Article 127FJ covers

Before registering a merger of Jersey companies, the parties must
deliver to the registrar:

- The **merger agreement**;
- The **directors' resolutions and certificates** under Article
  127E (including the solvency certificates);
- Evidence of **members' approval** (the special resolution under
  Article 127F, or the simplified approval under 127FA);
- Evidence of **creditor notice** under Article 127FC;
- Any **Court orders** relevant to the merger;
- The **memorandum and articles** of the merged body (if newly
  formed);
- Prescribed **fees**.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

The registrar's role is **gatekeeper of the public register**. By
requiring the full document bundle, the registrar can:

- Verify on the face of the documents that the **statutory
  prerequisites** have been satisfied;
- Maintain a **complete record** of the merger for future search;
- Refuse to register where the documents are **deficient**.

This article covers the **simplest case** (all-Jersey merging
bodies). Different document bundles apply for cross-border
mergers under Articles 127FK and 127FL.
