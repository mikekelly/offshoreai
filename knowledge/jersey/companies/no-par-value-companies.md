# Article 3F — No Par Value Companies

Article 3F of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** defines a
**no par value company** — a company whose shares are "**not
expressed as having nominal value**". This is the **modern default**
for Jersey company incorporation.

## What Article 3F says

A no par value company is registered with shares **not expressed as
having nominal value** — they are issued for whatever the company
decides to charge, with the full proceeds going to the
**stated capital account**.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why no-par-value is preferred

No-par-value share capital has several operational advantages over
par-value:

### 1. No share premium complications

Par-value companies have **share premium accounts** that hold the
excess of issue price over nominal value. The share premium
account has specific use restrictions (it can be used for certain
purposes but not freely distributed). No-par-value companies have
a **stated capital account** ([Article 39A](./stated-capital.md))
that is more flexible.

### 2. Simpler distributions

Distributions from a no-par-value company are governed by the
cash-flow solvency test in [Article 115](./distribution-restrictions.md)
with **no separate test** for the source (premium vs profits) —
the directors simply confirm solvency and the distribution can
proceed.

### 3. Currency flexibility

A no-par-value company can issue shares in any currency the
directors choose, without the par-value-based currency-conversion
complications. [Article 38B](./currency-conversion-rate.md) provides
the conversion mechanics.

### 4. International familiarity

Cayman, BVI, Bermuda and other major offshore jurisdictions have
moved to no-par-value defaults. Jersey's no-par-value option is
familiar to international counterparties.

### 5. Conversion flexibility

Jersey companies can be **converted between par-value and no-par-
value** under Articles 40A and 40B. Older par-value companies
routinely convert to no-par-value to access the operational
benefits.

## Mechanics

A no-par-value company's memorandum:

- states it is a no-par-value company;
- specifies the **number of shares authorised** (or states the
  number is unlimited);
- specifies any **share classes** and their respective rights;
- does **not state a nominal value** for the shares.

Issued shares are recorded at the **subscription price** in the
stated capital account. The directors set the subscription price for
each issue.

## Modern Jersey practice

A newly-incorporated Jersey private company is almost always:

- **private** ([Article 3B](./private-companies.md));
- **limited by shares** ([Article 3C](./limited-companies.md));
- **no-par-value** (Article 3F).

This is the operational default; departures need a specific
reason.
