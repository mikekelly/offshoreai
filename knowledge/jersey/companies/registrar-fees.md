# Article 201 — Fees and Charges

Article 201 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** allows
the Registrar to **levy fees and charges** for services provided
under the Companies Law.

## What Article 201 covers

Fees may be prescribed for:

- **Incorporation** of a company;
- **Annual confirmation statement** filing;
- **Document inspection and copy provision**;
- **Search certificates**;
- **Express service** (faster turnaround);
- **Specialised filings** (mergers, continuances, cell creations);
- **Strike-off applications**.

Fee schedules are typically set out in **Regulations** rather
than in the principal Law itself.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

Registrar fees fund the **operation of the registry**:

- Without fee income, the Registry would depend on taxpayer
  funding;
- Fee-funded operations align costs with users (companies and
  searchers);
- Surplus fees contribute to **regulatory functions** of the
  JFSC.

Jersey's company-incorporation fees are competitive relative to
other major financial centres but contribute meaningfully to
JFSC budget.
