# Article 97 — Demand for Poll

Article 97 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** governs
the **right to demand a poll** at general meetings — converting
show-of-hands voting to shareholding-based voting.

## What Article 97 covers

A **poll may be demanded** at company meetings.

The **articles typically specify**:

- Who may **demand a poll**;
- Under what **circumstances** such demands are valid.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why a poll matters

The default voting at a Jersey company meeting is **show of
hands** — each member present has **one vote** regardless of
shareholding. This works for routine business but not where
shareholdings are unequal.

A **poll** converts to **shareholding-weighted voting**:

- Each member votes the **number of votes attaching to their
  shares**;
- A holder of 60% of voting shares has 60% of the votes on a
  poll;
- A holder of 1 share has 1 vote (or the share's specified
  number of votes per the deed).

For **contested votes** or **important business**, a poll is
typically demanded to ensure shareholdings determine the outcome.

## Who can demand

The articles typically permit a poll to be demanded by:

- The **chair** of the meeting;
- A **specified number of members** (often three or five);
- Members holding a **specified proportion** of voting rights
  (often 5% or 10%);
- **Specific named persons** under the deed.

## Effect of poll

Once a poll is demanded:

- The **show-of-hands result is set aside**;
- **Polling is conducted** by collection of voting forms or
  electronic recording;
- The **result is determined** by share-weighted votes;
- The **chair's casting vote** under Article 92 doesn't apply
  to polls.

## Pitfalls

- **Failing to demand** in time — poll demands typically must
  be made promptly after the show-of-hands result.
- **Inadequate demand** — must satisfy the deed's specific
  requirements (e.g., the right threshold of members or
  shares).
