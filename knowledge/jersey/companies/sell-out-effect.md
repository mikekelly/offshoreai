# Article 120 — Effect of Requirement under Article 119

Article 120 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** sets out
**what happens when a minority shareholder exercises the sell-out
right** under Article 119 — how the offeror is required to acquire
the shares, on what terms, and what default terms apply.

## What Article 120 covers

Where a shareholder has served a valid Article 119 demand:

- The **offeror is entitled and bound** to acquire those shares;
- The acquisition is on the **terms of the takeover offer** (or
  on such other terms as may be agreed between offeror and
  shareholder);
- Where the original offer contained **alternative
  considerations** (e.g. cash or securities), the dissenting
  shareholder may choose; failing a timely choice, the **default
  alternative** specified in the offer applies.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

The minority shareholder exercising the sell-out is not stuck with
whatever the offeror happens to offer **after the offer closed** —
they are entitled to the **same terms** that everyone else got.

Selecting between alternatives:

- The minority shareholder typically can choose the same cash /
  securities / mixed mix as was offered;
- If they don't choose within the deadline, the **default** in the
  offer terms applies — usually the **cash alternative**, which
  is also the default in many UK-style takeover regimes.

## Court application

Either side can apply to the **Royal Court** under Article 121
where there is dispute about:

- Whether the sell-out demand was validly served;
- The terms on which the acquisition is to occur;
- Computation of the consideration in mixed-currency or share-
  exchange offers.
