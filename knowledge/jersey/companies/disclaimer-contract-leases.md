# Article 172 — Disclaimer of Contract Leases

Article 172 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** is a
**special-purpose disclaimer** provision for **leases**.

## What Article 172 covers

A lease (whether commercial property, equipment lease, or similar)
may be disclaimed by the liquidator with specific mechanics:

- **Notice** to the landlord / lessor;
- **Liability for rent** to date of disclaimer;
- **Landlord's claim** for future rent / breach damages provable
  in the winding up;
- **Re-entry rights** of landlord on disclaimer.

The article addresses the **specific complications** of lease
disclaimer:

- The continuing **leasehold interest** until disclaimer takes
  effect;
- The **landlord's compensation claim** for early termination;
- **Sub-tenants** and their position on disclaimer of the head
  lease.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

Leases are **commercially significant onerous property** in
many liquidations:

- A long-lease for premises the company no longer needs is a
  major ongoing liability;
- Equipment leases for unused machinery similarly burden the
  estate;
- Without disclaimer mechanics, lease creditors would dominate
  the unsecured-creditor body and depress recoveries for trade
  creditors.

Article 172 provides the **specialised mechanic** to deal with
leases efficiently.
