# Article 205A — Registrar May Strike Company Off Register at End of Duration

Article 205A of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** lets
the Registrar strike a company off the register at the **end of
its specified duration** (for limited life / limited duration
companies).

## What Article 205A covers

Where a company has a **specified maximum duration** in its
memorandum / articles and that duration has expired:

- The Registrar may **strike off** the company on the basis that
  it should no longer exist;
- This is **distinct** from the Article 144 automatic winding-up
  procedure — Article 205A operates where formal winding-up
  procedures have not been followed.

The Registrar follows similar **notice and objection
procedures** as for general Article 205 strike-off.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

A **limited-duration company** whose duration has expired but
that has not been properly wound up creates **register
inconsistency**:

- The company's articles say it should have terminated;
- But it still appears as active on the register.

Article 205A lets the Registrar **clean this up** unilaterally
where the directors have failed to do so.
