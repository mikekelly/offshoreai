# Article 127YIA — Company May Become Cell of Cell Company

Article 127YIA of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]**
allows a **standalone Jersey company** to **become an
incorporated cell** of an ICC — the mirror-image of Article 127YH.

## What Article 127YIA covers

A standalone Jersey company may apply to become a cell of an ICC
by:

- **Application** to the registrar in prescribed form;
- **Amendment** of the company's memorandum and articles to
  identify it as a cell of the ICC;
- **Approval** by members of both the company and the ICC;
- Satisfaction of **creditor-protection** conditions;
- **Registration** of the amended documents.

On registration:

- The company **becomes a cell** of the ICC;
- The company's **legal identity is unchanged**;
- The cell now operates within the ICC's umbrella structure.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

This is the **rationalisation route** — a sponsor with multiple
standalone Jersey companies can bring them under a single ICC
umbrella, gaining:

- **Common service-provider economies**;
- **Aligned administration** (shared registered office, common
  secretary);
- **Coordinated reporting** at umbrella level;
- **Streamlined cross-cell arrangements** within the same
  structure.

The continuity-of-identity preservation means counterparty
contracts and assets continue with the same legal entity.
