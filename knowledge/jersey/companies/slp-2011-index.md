# Separate Limited Partnerships (Jersey) Law 2011 — Index

The **Separate Limited Partnerships (Jersey) Law 2011**
creates the **Separate Limited Partnership (SLP)** — a Jersey
LP with **separate legal personality** but **without body-
corporate character**. It sits between the
[LP 1994](./lp-1994-index.md) (no separate personality, no
body-corporate character) and the
[ILP 2011](./ilp-2011-index.md) (separate personality + body-
corporate character) in the partnership-vehicle taxonomy.

This file is the structural entry point for the statute.

## Why the intermediate position

The SLP exists primarily to satisfy a **US-tax-classification
preference**. US "check-the-box" entity-classification rules
treat partnerships and corporations differently for federal tax.
For a non-US partnership to be reliably classified as a
partnership (rather than a corporation) for US tax purposes,
it typically must lack body-corporate characteristics — even
where it has separate legal personality.

The SLP gives the partnership the operational benefits of
**separate legal personality** (contracting and holding
property in its own name) **without** the body-corporate
character that would risk US-corporation classification. For
funds with significant US-investor LP capital, this is the
preferred Jersey vehicle.

## Use cases

The SLP is typically chosen where:

- The fund has **material US LP capital** and US-tax-
  classification certainty is a structural requirement;
- The fund **does not have** US LP capital but mirrors the
  Cayman ELP shape closely enough that converging on the SLP
  simplifies cross-Atlantic structures;
- The fund's operations benefit from **separate legal
  personality** for contracting / property-holding but the
  manager / structurer wants to avoid body-corporate
  characterisation.

For Jersey funds with predominantly EU / UK LP capital, the
LP 1994 remains the more commonly chosen vehicle —
[`./lp-1994-index.md`](./lp-1994-index.md). The SLP is a
specialist choice driven by US-tax considerations or US-
investor familiarity.
