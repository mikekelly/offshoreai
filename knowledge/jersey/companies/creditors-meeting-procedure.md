# Article 169A — Procedure at Creditors' Meeting

Article 169A of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]**
prescribes the **procedural rules** for creditors' meetings in a
creditors' winding up.

## What Article 169A covers

The procedural rules cover:

- **Notice** of the meeting (form, content, delivery);
- **Chairing** of the meeting (typically the liquidator or court
  appointee);
- **Voting** (typically by value of admitted claim, with
  contested claims subject to chairman's provisional decision);
- **Adjournment** procedures;
- **Minutes** and record-keeping.

Detailed mechanics are typically in **Insolvency Rules** made
under the regulation-making powers.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

Creditors' meetings can be **contentious** with disputed claims,
strategic voting blocs, and high-value decisions on the table.
Clear procedural rules prevent the meetings from breaking down
and ensure decisions taken are **legally robust**.
