# LLP Law 2017 Article 11 — Accounts and audit

Article 11 addresses accounts and audit:

- **Audit is at the LLP's discretion** — there is no
  statutory audit requirement for Jersey LLPs;
- The LLP must maintain **adequate accounting records**;
- Accounting records must be provided to the secretary
  within prescribed timeframes for record-keeping under
  [Article 9](./llp-article-9.md).

## Operational significance

The discretionary-audit position contrasts with Jersey
public companies under the
[CJL 1991](./companies-law-1991.md) (which typically
require audit) and with regulated entities under JFSC
supervision (which may require audit by regulatory rule
even where the underlying corporate law does not). For
professional-services LLPs, audit is typically conducted
even though not statutorily required — for partner-
governance purposes.
