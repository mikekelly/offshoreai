# Article 186 — Distribution of Company's Property

Article 186 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** sets
the **distribution waterfall** for the company's property in a
creditors' winding up — who gets paid in what order.

## What Article 186 covers

The liquidator distributes the company's property in the
following order:

1. **Secured creditors** — out of their security (or by surrender
   to the liquidator and proof for any shortfall);
2. **Costs and expenses** of the winding up (Article 165);
3. **Preferential debts** — wages and salaries (subject to caps),
   social security, certain taxes, and other prescribed
   categories;
4. **Ordinary unsecured creditors** — pari passu (proportionally
   if the estate is insufficient);
5. **Subordinated creditors** — those whose claims rank below
   unsecured by contract or statute;
6. **Members** — to the extent of any surplus, in accordance with
   the articles and class rights.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

The waterfall is the **central economic mechanism** of
insolvency law. It determines:

- **Who gets paid** when there isn't enough to go round;
- **Who bears the loss** of insolvency;
- **What contracts and security** are worth in distress.

The waterfall is hardwired into commercial structuring: secured
lenders pay less interest because they get paid first; trade
creditors charge higher prices because they bear unsecured risk;
members buy shares cheaply because they bear residual risk.

## Pari passu and exceptions

The default rule is **pari passu** among unsecured creditors —
they share equally in proportion to admitted claims. Exceptions:

- **Set-off** — a creditor with a counter-claim sets off and
  recovers in full to that extent;
- **Trust assets** — assets held by the company on trust do not
  fall into the estate;
- **Retention of title** — assets sold under retention of title
  arrangements may not be the company's;
- **Statutory subrogation** — certain payments are deemed to
  occur in a different order.
