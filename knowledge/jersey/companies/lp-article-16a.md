# LP Law 1994 Article 16A — Third party rights

Article 16A allows partnership agreements to **grant
enforceable rights to non-partners** — third parties named
in or identified by the agreement.

The Article also provides for variation and extinction
procedures for such third-party rights.

This is the LP-law analogue of the English Contracts
(Rights of Third Parties) Act 1999 — useful for fund
structures with co-investment, side-letter, or trustee
rights vesting in persons not formally party to the LP
agreement.
