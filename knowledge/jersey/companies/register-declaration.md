# Article 46 — Declaration (Lost Share Certificates)

Article 46 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** addresses
the practical problem of **lost share certificates**: where a
share certificate is lost or destroyed, the company may require
the member to swear a **statutory declaration** before issuing a
replacement.

## What Article 46 says

Where share certificates are **lost**, the company **may require
statutory declarations** before issuing replacements.

The declaration protects against **fraudulent claims** — a
malicious person claiming a "lost" certificate to extract a
duplicate that could then be used to claim entitlements twice.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Typical declaration content

A typical Article 46 declaration would set out:

- The **member's identity** and shareholding;
- That the **original certificate has been lost or destroyed**;
- The **circumstances of the loss** (so far as known);
- That the member **has not sold, transferred, or otherwise
  parted with** the lost certificate;
- An **indemnity** to the company against claims arising from
  issue of the replacement.

The declaration is typically sworn before:

- A **Commissioner for Oaths** in Jersey;
- A **Notary Public** outside Jersey;
- Equivalent local officials in the member's home jurisdiction.

## Indemnity

In practice the company typically requires both:

- A **statutory declaration** (under Article 46); and
- An **indemnity bond** from the member (and sometimes a bank
  or insurer) to cover any loss the company suffers from issuing
  the replacement.

The indemnity is a **belt-and-braces** measure — the declaration
is the statutory minimum; the indemnity is a practical
contractual protection.

## Modern alternative — uncertificated shares

Many modern Jersey companies maintain shares in
**uncertificated form** under
[Article 51A](./uncertificated-securities.md) — the share is
recorded electronically in the register and **no physical
certificate exists** to be lost in the first place.

For uncertificated shares, Article 46 is **not engaged** —
there's nothing to replace.

## Pitfalls

- **Waiving the declaration** for convenience — exposes the
  company to fraud risk. Always require the declaration.
- **Inadequate indemnity** — the declaration alone may not
  provide sufficient protection; consider an indemnity bond for
  high-value certificates.
