# Article 35 — Rule of Law on Issuing Shares at Discount Abolished

Article 35 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** abolishes
the common-law / equitable rule that prohibited a company from
issuing shares **at a discount** to their par value.

## What Article 35 says

- **Article 35(1)**: applies to shares issued at discount and to
  capital-money applications for commissions;
- **Article 35(2)**: repealing former provisions does **not
  retroactively criminalise** prior discount issuances or similar
  transactions lawful under the old regime.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## What the old rule was

At common law (historically), a company with **par-value shares**
could not issue them for **less than their par value**. The rule
protected creditors and other shareholders: par value was
treated as a notional minimum capital contribution, and a
discount-issue would have undermined the value of the par-value
"floor".

The rule never applied to **no-par-value** companies in the same
way, because there was no nominal floor to undercut.

## Why it was abolished

The discount rule was:

- **Easily evaded** in practice (e.g., by issuing shares for
  inadequate non-cash consideration);
- **A relic of historical par-value thinking** that doesn't fit
  modern share-capital practice;
- **Inconsistent with no-par-value share regimes** that Jersey
  permits in parallel.

Article 35 abolishes the rule. A Jersey company can now issue
shares at any price, including below par value where the company
is a par-value company.

The **practical safeguard** for creditors and other shareholders
isn't the old discount rule — it's:

- the **directors' duties** under Article 74 (no improper share
  issues);
- the **disclosure / valuation** requirements where non-cash
  consideration is involved;
- the **Article 115 cash-flow solvency test** for distributions
  (any return of capital still requires solvency).

## Modern practice

In modern Jersey practice:

- **No-par-value companies** (the default — Article 3F) don't
  encounter the issue at all;
- **Par-value companies** can issue at discount if commercially
  justified, with the directors' fiduciary duties as the
  governing constraint.

## Non-retroactivity

Article 35(2) makes clear that the abolition is **not
retroactive**. Discount issues that were unlawful under the
pre-1991 regime are not retroactively legalised; conduct that was
lawful under the old regime is not retroactively criminalised.

## Pitfalls

- **Treating discount issues as unconstrained**. The abolition
  doesn't remove directors' duties; an improper discount issue
  can still ground a breach-of-duty claim.
- **Forgetting valuation** for non-cash consideration. Where
  shares are issued for non-cash consideration, the directors
  need to satisfy themselves the consideration is real and of
  appropriate value.
