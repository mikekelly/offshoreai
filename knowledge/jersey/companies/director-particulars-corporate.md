# Article 84A — Particulars of Directors (Corporate Directors)

Article 84A of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** specifies
the **particulars** that must be recorded for **corporate
directors** — i.e., companies that themselves serve as directors
of other Jersey companies.

## What gets recorded

For each corporate director:

- **Name** under which the corporate director is registered;
- **Registered-office address**;
- **Jurisdiction of incorporation**;
- **Registered number** (if any).

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Common corporate-director scenarios

Corporate-director arrangements are common in Jersey practice:

- **Trust-company businesses** serving as corporate director of
  many client companies — typical for special-purpose vehicles
  and family-office structures;
- **PTC structures** where the PTC has a corporate director that
  is itself a regulated entity;
- **Foreign-incorporated parent** as a director of a Jersey
  subsidiary in some group structures.

## Why these particulars

The particulars allow:

- **Identification** of the corporate director;
- **Verification** of the corporate director's own existence and
  standing in its home jurisdiction;
- **Cross-jurisdictional registry checks** (e.g., confirming the
  Cayman parent really exists by reference to the Cayman
  Registry);
- **Service** of process at the corporate director's registered
  office.

## Public availability

Same as natural-person director particulars — typically **public**
at the Registrar level.

## Acting through

The corporate director acts through its own **officers and
authorised representatives**. The Article 84A registration
identifies the **corporate director itself**; the underlying
people through whom the corporate director operates are not
captured at this level — they show up at the corporate director's
own corporate level.

## Pitfalls

- **Stale registered-office data** for foreign corporate
  directors — keep aligned with the foreign Registry.
- **Wrong jurisdiction** — confirm the corporate director is
  in good standing in its home jurisdiction.
