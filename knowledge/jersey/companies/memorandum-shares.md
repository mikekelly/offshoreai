# Article 4A — Memorandum of Company with Shares

Article 4A specifies the **additional content** required in the
memorandum of a Jersey company **with shares** — i.e., a company
under [Article 3C](./limited-companies.md) (limited by shares) or
[Article 3D](./unlimited-companies.md) (unlimited with shares).

## What Article 4A says

### Par-value companies (Article 3E)

Must state:

- the **amount of share capital** with which the company is to be
  registered; and
- the **amounts** into which the shares of each class are divided
  (i.e., the par value of each share).

### No-par-value companies (Article 3F)

Must state:

- the **limit (if any) on the number of shares of each class**
  the company may issue.

### All share companies

Must state:

- whether **share liability is limited** or **unlimited**;
- **against each subscriber's name**:
  - the **number of limited shares** of each class to be taken; and
  - the **number of unlimited shares** of each class to be taken.

Share values may be **stated in any unit or part of a unit of any
currency** (so a memorandum can state shares in £, $, €, ¥, etc.,
or in fractions like 0.01 of a currency unit).

**Minimum subscription**: each subscriber must take **at least one
share**.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

The Article 4A requirements differ depending on **par-value vs
no-par-value** choice:

| Element | Par-value (Article 3E) | No-par-value (Article 3F) |
|---|---|---|
| Total authorised capital | Required (a specific currency amount) | Not required — but may state a number-of-shares ceiling |
| Nominal value per share | Required | Not applicable |
| Currency | Stated as part of par value | Stated per share class or implied |
| Subscribers' shareholdings | Required | Required |

This drives the **drafting differences** between par-value and
no-par-value memoranda — see
[`par-value-companies.md`](./par-value-companies.md) and
[`no-par-value-companies.md`](./no-par-value-companies.md).

## Multi-currency capital

A Jersey company can have **share capital denominated in any
currency**, and can have **different share classes in different
currencies** (e.g., a fund company with classes in GBP, USD, EUR).
The Article 38B currency-conversion mechanic deals with the
operational implications.

## Pitfalls

- **Misalignment** between the memorandum's stated capital and
  what's actually issued — leads to validity questions on the
  excess issues.
- **Mixing par-value and no-par-value classes** within the same
  company is not permitted; a company is one or the other under
  Articles 3E / 3F.
- **Subscribers taking zero shares** — not permitted; minimum is
  one share per subscriber.
