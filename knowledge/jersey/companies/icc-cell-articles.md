# Article 127YB — Memorandum and Articles of Cells

Article 127YB of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** sets
out the requirements for the **memorandum and articles** of an
**incorporated cell** within an ICC structure.

## What Article 127YB covers

The cell's memorandum and articles must:

- Comply with the **general requirements** for a Jersey company's
  memorandum and articles (Articles 4–5);
- State the **fact** that the cell is an **incorporated cell** of
  a specified ICC;
- Identify the **cell company** of which it is a cell;
- Set out the **share capital structure** of the cell;
- State the **company type** within the ICC structure (par-value
  / no-par-value, limited / unlimited, etc.);
- Comply with **Article 127YM restrictions** on alteration.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

The cell has its **own legal personality** — it can contract,
hold assets, sue and be sued. So it needs **its own
constitutional documents** distinct from the ICC's. But it also
has a **structural relationship** with the ICC (it can only exist
as a cell of that ICC), so the documents identify that
relationship.

The **alteration restrictions** in Article 127YM reflect the
structural relationship — certain changes (such as removing the
"cell of [ICC]" identification) require the ICC's involvement.
