# Article 183 — Duty to Co-operate with Liquidator

Article 183 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** imposes
a **legal duty** on the company's directors, officers, employees,
and others to **cooperate with the liquidator**.

## What Article 183 covers

Persons covered include:

- **Present and past directors** and officers;
- **Present and past employees** with relevant knowledge;
- **Auditors and other professional advisers** (subject to
  privilege);
- **Members or others** with custody or knowledge of company
  property.

Required cooperation includes:

- **Providing information** about the company's affairs;
- **Producing books, records, and documents**;
- **Attending** to be interviewed;
- **Verifying** statements of affairs;
- **Assisting** the liquidator's investigations generally.

Non-cooperation is an **offence** (Article 134 framework
applies).

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

The liquidator depends on **information from those who ran the
company**:

- Where assets are located;
- What liabilities exist;
- What recoveries are possible;
- What conduct led to insolvency.

Without legal teeth behind cooperation duties, former
management could **passively defeat** the liquidation by
withholding information. Article 183 prevents that.
