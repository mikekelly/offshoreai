# Article 185B — Declaration under Désastre Law (Creditors' Winding Up)

Article 185B of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** is
the **bridge from creditors' winding up to désastre** (the
mirror of Article 154A for summary winding ups).

## What Article 185B covers

Where a creditors' winding up is in progress and circumstances
make a transfer to the désastre regime appropriate, the
liquidator or other interested party may apply to the Royal
Court for a **declaration of désastre** under the
**Bankruptcy (Désastre) (Jersey) Law 1990**.

On declaration:

- The **Viscount** takes control of the company's affairs;
- The creditors' winding up is **superseded** by the désastre
  proceedings;
- The liquidator's office may **cease** or be reconfigured to
  support the désastre.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

Reasons to switch from creditors' winding up to désastre might
include:

- **Cross-border complexity** that benefits from Viscount
  involvement;
- **Limited estate** where a private liquidator is uneconomic;
- **Specific Viscount powers** that the situation requires;
- **Strategic considerations** of the creditor body.

Article 185B preserves **flexibility** between the two regimes
through the life of the winding up, not just at commencement.
