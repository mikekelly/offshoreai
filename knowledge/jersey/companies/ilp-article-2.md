# ILP Law 2011 Article 2 — Meaning of "insolvent" and "solvent"

An ILP is **insolvent** when **unable to discharge debts
and obligations as they fall due** — excluding liabilities
to partners regarding partnership interests.

Differs slightly in formulation from the LP 1994 solvency
test ([`lp-article-2.md`](./lp-article-2.md)), which keys on
the GP's ability to discharge from partnership assets alone.
The ILP's separate legal personality means the test is more
directly an LLP-like ability-to-pay test.
