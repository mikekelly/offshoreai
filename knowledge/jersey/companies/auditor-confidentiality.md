# Article 113P — Confidentiality

Article 113P of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** protects
**information obtained during Commission investigations** of
auditors.

## What Article 113P covers

Information obtained during Commission investigations receives
**confidentiality protection**.

**Subject to specified exceptions** for:

- legal proceedings;
- regulatory cooperation.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

Effective auditor oversight requires the JFSC to obtain
sensitive information — about audited clients, audit working
papers, disciplinary findings. If that information were freely
disclosed:

- **Audit clients** would lose protection of legitimate
  commercial confidentiality;
- **Auditors** would be deterred from cooperating with
  investigations;
- The **regulatory function** would be undermined.

Article 113P provides the confidentiality baseline.

## Exceptions

The confidentiality is **not absolute**. Exceptions for:

- **Legal proceedings** — where the JFSC is itself a party or
  where the information is properly disclosable in court;
- **Regulatory cooperation** — sharing with foreign regulators
  or other Jersey regulators on a controlled basis;
- **Public interest** disclosures where appropriate.

## Pitfalls

- **Inadvertent disclosure** in connection with multiple
  regulatory relationships — manage carefully.
