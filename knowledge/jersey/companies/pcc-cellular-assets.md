# Article 127YU — Protection of Cellular and Non-Cellular Assets of Protected Cell Companies

Article 127YU of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** sets
out the **protective mechanics** for cellular and non-cellular
assets — extending Article 127YT's segregation principle.

## What Article 127YU covers

The article provides for:

- **Identification** of assets as cellular or non-cellular;
- **Statutory protection** against claims that are not properly
  attributable to the relevant pool;
- **Trustee duties** on directors / officers in dealing with
  cellular and non-cellular assets;
- **Remedies** for misallocation or misappropriation between
  pools;
- The principle that **cellular assets are held by the PCC for
  the benefit of the cell** — not as the PCC's own absolute
  property.

Some PCCs are structured with cellular assets held by the PCC
**effectively in trust** for the cell's investors, reinforcing
the segregation.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

The segregation in Article 127YT is the **doctrine**; Article
127YU is the **operational protection**. It addresses risks like:

- **Misallocation** by careless directors;
- **Commingling** in operational accounts;
- **Inadvertent cross-claims** through poor record-keeping;
- **Third-party attempts** to assert claims against the wrong
  asset pool.

The article makes the segregation **robust against operational
slippage**.
