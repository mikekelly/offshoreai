# Article 124A — Power of States to Amend Part 18

Article 124A of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** gives
the **States of Jersey** power to **amend Part 18** (Takeovers)
by Regulations.

## What Article 124A covers

The **States** may by **Regulations** amend Part 18, including
adjusting:

- The **90% threshold** for squeeze-out and sell-out;
- The **same-terms requirement** and permitted derogations;
- The **time limits** for notices, court applications, and
  acquisitions;
- The **associates** rule and concert-party definitions.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

The takeover regime is sensitive to market structure and the
practice of comparable jurisdictions (notably the **UK**). As the
UK Takeover Code and Companies Act 2006 evolve, Jersey may wish
to keep pace without primary legislation each time.

Article 124A is a **delegated power**: an amendment Regulation
laid before the States can update Part 18 to reflect modern
takeover practice.

## Pitfalls

- **Track Regulations as well as the principal Law** — Part 18
  is one of the more amenable parts of the Law to subordinate-
  legislative tuning, so checking only the principal Law can miss
  recent changes.
