# Schedule 1 — Punishment of Offences

Schedule 1 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** is the
**offences schedule** — the lookup table of penalties for the
various offences scattered throughout the Companies Law.

## What Schedule 1 covers

Schedule 1 lists, for each offence-creating article in the
Companies Law:

- The **article** creating the offence;
- The **description** of the offence;
- The **maximum penalty** on summary conviction;
- The **maximum penalty** on conviction on indictment.

Penalties range from:

- **Modest fines** (Level 1 or 2 fines under the Criminal Justice
  (Standard Scale of Fines) (Jersey) Law) for filing offences and
  minor procedural breaches;
- **Substantial fines and short imprisonment** (up to 2 years)
  for mid-tier offences (false statements, certain disclosure
  failures);
- **Significant fines and longer imprisonment** (up to 5 years)
  for serious offences (fraudulent trading, false solvency
  statements, falsification of records).

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

Centralising penalties in Schedule 1:

- Makes **enforcement consistent** — the same offence carries
  the same maximum regardless of where it appears in the Law;
- Allows the States to **update penalties** (e.g. for inflation)
  without amending every Article;
- Provides **practitioners** with a single reference for penalty
  exposure.
