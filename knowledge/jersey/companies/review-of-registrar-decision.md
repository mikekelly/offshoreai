# Article 67B — Review of Registrar's Decision

Article 67B of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** is the
**Royal Court review** mechanism for decisions of the **Registrar
of Companies**.

## What Article 67B says

A **person affected by the Registrar's decision** may apply to
the **Court for review** within **specified timeframes**.

The Court may **confirm or overturn** the Registrar's decision.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## What decisions can be reviewed

The Article 67B review applies to a range of **Registrar
decisions**, including:

- **Refusal to register** a memorandum or other document;
- **Refusal of consent** to incorporate;
- **Directions** under specific Articles (e.g., name-change
  direction under Article 15);
- **Strike-off decisions** under Part 23;
- Other **administrative determinations** by the Registrar.

## Procedure

The applicant must apply within the **specified timeframe** —
typically a short window (e.g., 21 or 28 days). Procedure
follows ordinary Royal Court Representation practice in the
Samedi Division.

The Court considers:

- The **legal basis** of the Registrar's decision;
- The **factual evidence** supporting it;
- **Procedural fairness** in the Registrar's process;
- **Reasonableness** in the *Wednesbury* sense — was the
  decision within the range of decisions a reasonable Registrar
  could have made?

## Court powers

The Court can:

- **Confirm** the decision (no relief);
- **Overturn** the decision and direct a different outcome;
- **Remit** the matter to the Registrar with directions on the
  approach to take;
- Impose **conditions** on any remitted decision.

## Cross-reference

The mechanism is similar to public-law judicial review in other
common-law jurisdictions but is **statutorily integrated** into
the Companies Law for Registrar decisions specifically.

## Pitfalls

- **Missing the timeframe** — applications outside the window
  are typically dismissed.
- **Bringing review for tactical delay** without substantive
  grounds — costs consequences likely.
