# Article 84 — Particulars of Directors (Natural Persons)

Article 84 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** specifies
the **particulars** that must be recorded for **natural-person
directors** in the [register of directors and secretaries](./register-of-directors-secretaries.md).

## What gets recorded

For each natural-person director:

- **Full name** (present forenames and surname);
- **Any former names** (e.g., maiden name, former marital name);
- **Business or usual residential address**;
- **Nationality**;
- **Business occupation**;
- **Date of birth**.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why these particulars

The particulars enable:

- **Identification** of the director (full name, date of birth);
- **Service** of legal process (address);
- **Cross-referencing** with other Jersey company directorships
  (multi-board directors are visible);
- **Regulatory and law-enforcement** access to director
  information.

## Public availability

The particulars are typically **public** at the Registrar level.
Anyone can search the Registry and see who currently directs a
Jersey company.

This is one element of Jersey's **transparency framework** —
director identity is public even though beneficial-ownership data
is not.

## Updating

Particulars must be **updated promptly** on changes:

- **Change of name** (e.g., on marriage);
- **Change of address**;
- **Change of business occupation** (in some cases);
- **Cessation of office** (the director is removed from the
  register).

## Pitfalls

- **Inaccurate addresses** — out-of-date residential addresses
  for directors who have moved. Update promptly.
- **Former-names omissions** — a director who has changed name
  must record the former name.
