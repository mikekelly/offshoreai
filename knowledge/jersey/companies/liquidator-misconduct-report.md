# Article 184 — Liquidator to Report Possible Misconduct

Article 184 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** imposes
a **mandatory reporting duty** on the liquidator: where the
liquidator forms the view that a person has engaged in possible
misconduct, the liquidator must report it.

## What Article 184 covers

If the liquidator forms the **opinion** that:

- An **offence** has been committed in relation to the company
  by a director or officer;
- A director or officer is **otherwise unfit** to be a director;
- Other prescribed misconduct has occurred;

the liquidator **must report** to the JFSC (and in some cases
the Attorney General).

The report may lead to:

- **Criminal investigation** and prosecution;
- **Disqualification proceedings** under Article 78;
- **Civil recovery** on behalf of the company.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

The liquidator is in a **unique position** to identify
misconduct:

- They review the company's books;
- They interview former officers;
- They reconstruct pre-liquidation events;
- They identify claw-back targets.

Article 184 makes this information feed into the **enforcement
system**. Without the duty, liquidators might confine themselves
to asset realisation and ignore the broader regulatory dimension.
