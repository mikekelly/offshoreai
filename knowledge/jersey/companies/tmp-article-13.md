# Takeovers Panel Law 2009 Article 13 — Sanctions

Article 13 enables the Panel — through rules — to **impose
sanctions** for rule breaches or non-compliance with
directions.

## Key provisions

- Rules **may authorise the Panel** to impose sanctions on
  any person who has acted in breach of rules or has not
  complied with a direction.
- For sanctions of a type **not previously imposed**, the
  Panel must publish a **policy statement** before doing
  so.
- The policy statement must address:
  - The type of sanction;
  - The criteria for imposing it;
  - For financial penalties: the **amount** range (with
    factors for setting amounts including breach
    seriousness, whether deliberate, and whether the
    subject is an individual or a body).

## Sanctions in practice

Typical sanctions under the analogous UK Panel regime
include:

- **Public statements of censure** against named persons;
- **Cold-shouldering** — restricting the regulated
  community's interaction with the censured person;
- **Compensation orders** — see
  [Article 14](./tmp-article-14.md);
- **Financial penalties** — subject to the policy-statement
  publication requirement.
