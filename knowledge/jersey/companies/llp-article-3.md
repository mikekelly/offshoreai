# LLP Law 2017 Article 3 — Limited liability partnership property

Article 3 defines what constitutes LLP property and specifies
that property of the LLP **vests in the LLP itself** (not in
the partners individually).

## Key provisions

- All property acquired by the LLP, or by the partners on
  behalf of the LLP, is **LLP property**;
- LLP property **vests in the LLP**;
- The partners hold their **partnership interests** rather
  than direct rights in LLP property.

## Operational significance

This is the **load-bearing separation** between LLP and
partners — a creditor of an individual partner cannot
directly attach LLP property, and an LLP creditor cannot
directly attach partners' personal property (subject to the
[Article 5](./llp-article-5.md) liability rules).
