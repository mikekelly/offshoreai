# Takeovers Panel Law 2009 Article 6 — Directions

Article 6 empowers the Panel to issue **directions** — formal
instructions to parties — for three purposes:

1. **Restraining a person** from acting (or continuing to
   act) in breach of rules;
2. **Restraining a person** from any conduct pending the
   Panel's determination of an issue; and
3. **Requiring a person** to do anything specified that is
   necessary or expedient to secure compliance with rules.

## Operational use

Directions are a **real-time intervention tool** — they
allow the Panel to manage conduct during a live transaction
without waiting for the formal sanctions process
([Article 13](./tmp-article-13.md)) or Court enforcement
([Article 15](./tmp-article-15.md)).

Typical situations:

- Restraining a bidder from making market purchases
  inconsistent with the Code;
- Restraining target-board defensive action pending Panel
  ruling on its consistency with Code requirements;
- Requiring disclosure of information the Panel considers
  necessary to fair-market operation.

Directions are made under and authorised by rules — Article
6 does not itself impose specific direction-making powers
beyond what the rules permit.
