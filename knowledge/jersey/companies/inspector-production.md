# Article 130 — Production of Records and Evidence to Inspectors

Article 130 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** sets
out the **duties of persons** to produce records and provide
evidence to inspectors.

## What Article 130 covers

A person required by an inspector must:

- **Produce** to the inspector all documents relating to the
  company that are in the person's custody or power;
- **Attend** before the inspector when required;
- **Answer questions** truthfully;
- **Otherwise assist** the inspector to the best of the person's
  ability.

Persons covered include:

- **Present and past officers** of the company;
- **Present and past employees**;
- **Agents** (lawyers, accountants, bankers — subject to
  privilege provisions in Article 139);
- **Auditors** (present and past);
- **Other persons** who the inspector has reasonable grounds to
  believe have relevant information.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

Article 130 is the **practical core** of the inspector's
investigation. Without robust production duties, the
inspector's powers in Article 129 would be theoretical.

The duty extends to:

- **Former officers and employees** — important because by the
  time an investigation starts, the people best placed to know
  what happened may have left;
- **Professional advisers** — within the limits of privilege;
- **Third parties** with custody of company documents.
