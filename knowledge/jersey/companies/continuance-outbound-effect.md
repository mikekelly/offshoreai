# Article 127V — Effect of Continuance Overseas

Article 127V of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** is the
**substantive effect provision** for outbound continuance — what
happens when a Jersey company successfully migrates to a foreign
jurisdiction.

## What Article 127V covers

On the effective date of the outbound continuance:

- The body **ceases to be a Jersey company** under the Companies
  Law;
- The body **continues to exist** as a body corporate in the
  destination jurisdiction;
- The body's **legal identity is unchanged** (continuity of
  entity);
- All **property, rights, assets and liabilities** of the body
  continue to be its property — **no transfer or conveyance** is
  needed (subject to destination-jurisdiction recognition);
- All **legal proceedings** by or against the body continue
  unaffected (subject to destination-jurisdiction recognition);
- The **register of companies** is updated to record the
  continuance overseas and the body is **struck off** the active
  register (typically marked as "continued overseas" rather than
  "dissolved").

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

Outbound continuance achieves the mirror-image of inbound
continuance: **preservation of legal identity** while changing
the governing jurisdiction.

Crucially:

- The body **does not dissolve** in Jersey — it ceases to be
  Jersey-resident as a matter of company law, but persists in
  the destination jurisdiction;
- **Existing contracts and obligations** continue;
- **Existing litigation** continues with the same body as a
  party (now under a foreign jurisdiction).

## Pitfalls

- **Asymmetric recognition** — Jersey recognises the continuance,
  but the destination jurisdiction must also recognise it.
  Mismatched recognition can leave the body in a **conflict-of-
  laws limbo** where neither jurisdiction's law fully applies.
- **Foreign-law re-domicile clauses** — counterparty contracts
  may treat the continuance as a triggering event.
- **Tax** — the move may have material Jersey tax (and
  destination-country tax) consequences.
