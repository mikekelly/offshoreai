# Article 116 — Takeover Offers

Article 116 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** defines
what counts as a **takeover offer** for the purposes of Part 18 of
the Law — the gateway concept that triggers the **squeeze-out**
(Article 117) and **sell-out** (Article 119) rights.

## What Article 116 covers

A **takeover offer** is an offer to acquire **all the shares**
(or all the shares of a class) in a Jersey company **other than**
shares already held by the offeror at the date of the offer.

Key features:

- The offer must be on **the same terms** for all shares (or all
  shares of the class) to which the offer relates;
- Limited specified exceptions are permitted (e.g. legal or
  practical bars on the same terms applying in certain
  jurisdictions);
- An offer to acquire shares of a particular class is treated as
  a separate offer for the purposes of Part 18.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

Part 18 of the Companies Law is the **statutory squeeze-out / sell-
out regime**. It gives a successful takeover bidder the right to
compulsorily acquire the shares of a small holdout minority — and
gives a minority shareholder the right to insist on being bought
out by an overwhelmingly successful bidder.

The whole regime hinges on **whether there is a "takeover offer"
within Article 116**:

- If yes, Articles 117–124 apply.
- If no — for example because the offer is selective rather than
  to all shareholders — the bidder cannot use the statutory
  squeeze-out and must rely on a different transaction structure
  (typically a **scheme of arrangement** under Article 125 or a
  **statutory merger** under Part 18B).

## How a takeover offer is structured

A typical takeover offer:

- Is announced publicly (and, where the target is listed, in
  accordance with the relevant **Takeover Code** of the home
  market);
- Is open for acceptance for a fixed period;
- Specifies the consideration (cash, shares, mixed);
- Is conditional on a minimum acceptance level (commonly **90% of
  the shares to which the offer relates** so that the squeeze-out
  threshold is reached).

## Same-terms requirement

The offer must be on **the same terms** for all shares to which
it relates. Permitted differences:

- Differences arising **solely from variations in the date of
  acceptance**;
- Differences necessary to **comply with foreign law** preventing
  the offer being made on identical terms in another jurisdiction;
- **Cash alternatives** offered as an option in addition to a
  securities-exchange offer (these do not by themselves break the
  same-terms requirement).

If the offer is **not** on the same terms, it falls outside
Article 116 and the statutory squeeze-out is unavailable.
