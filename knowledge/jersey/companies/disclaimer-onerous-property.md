# Article 171 — Power to Disclaim Onerous Property

Article 171 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** gives
the liquidator power to **disclaim onerous property** — i.e.
property whose retention would prejudice the winding up.

## What Article 171 covers

The liquidator may disclaim:

- **Property unsaleable** or not readily saleable;
- Property that gives rise to **liabilities** in excess of its
  value;
- **Contracts** where ongoing performance would harm the
  liquidation;
- **Leases**, **shares**, or other interests that are onerous to
  retain.

Disclaimer:

- **Terminates** the company's rights and obligations in the
  property from the date of disclaimer;
- Does **not affect** the rights of third parties except so far
  as is necessary to release the company;
- Triggers **compensation claims** by affected third parties
  (provable in the winding up).

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

Without disclaimer:

- The company would remain liable on **uneconomic contracts**;
- **Onerous leases** would continue to accrue rent and service
  charges;
- **Loss-making businesses** could not be quickly shut down;
- The liquidation could be **dragged out** indefinitely by
  unwanted assets.

Disclaimer is the **clean cut** that lets the liquidator shed
problem assets and focus on realising valuable ones.
