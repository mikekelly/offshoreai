# Article 127A — Interpretation (Part 18B Mergers)

Article 127A of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** is the
**interpretation provision** for Part 18B — the **statutory
merger regime** between bodies corporate, of which at least one
is a Jersey company.

## What Article 127A covers

Key definitions used throughout Part 18B include:

- **"Merging body"** — each body corporate party to the proposed
  merger;
- **"Merged body"** — the body corporate (existing or newly
  formed) that continues after the merger absorbs all the merging
  bodies;
- **"Merger agreement"** — the agreement between the merging
  bodies setting out the terms of the merger;
- **"Solvency statement"** — the directors' statement in
  prescribed form, broadly equivalent to the Article 61A
  solvency statement, that the merged body will be able to pay
  its debts;
- **"Foreign body"** — body corporate incorporated outside Jersey.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

Part 18B is the **statutory merger regime**: a court-free
mechanism by which two or more bodies corporate merge to form a
single surviving entity, with **assets, liabilities and contracts
vesting by operation of law** in the merged body.

The vocabulary of merging-body / merged-body / merger agreement
is used throughout Part 18B; Article 127A is the look-up.
