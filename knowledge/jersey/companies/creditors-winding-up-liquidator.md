# Article 161 — Appointment of Liquidator (Creditors' Winding Up)

Article 161 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** sets
out how the **liquidator is appointed** in a creditors' winding
up.

## What Article 161 covers

The liquidator may be appointed by:

- **The members** in the resolution under Article 157
  (subject to creditor confirmation at the first meeting);
- **The creditors** at the first creditors' meeting under
  Article 160 or 160A;
- **The Royal Court** under Article 157C or Article 175 (in
  court-ordered or court-supervised cases).

If members and creditors disagree on the appointee, the
**creditors' choice prevails** — this is fundamental to the
creditors'-winding-up regime.

The liquidator must:

- Meet **qualification requirements** (Article 188);
- **Consent** to act;
- **File notice** of appointment.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

The **creditor primacy** in liquidator selection is the
hallmark of creditors' winding up:

- Members' interests are subordinate in insolvency, so members'
  appointment authority is contingent;
- Creditors who will bear the losses get to choose the
  professional who will administer the realisation;
- This drives **alignment** between liquidator and creditor body.
