# Article 98 — Minutes

Article 98 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** requires
Jersey companies to **maintain records of meeting proceedings** —
the company's "minute book".

## What Article 98 covers

Companies must **maintain records of meeting proceedings**.

These **minutes serve as official documentation** of decisions
and resolutions passed.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## What gets recorded

Typical minute-book content:

- **Date and place** of meeting;
- **Names of attendees** (members, directors, proxies, others);
- **Resolutions proposed and passed** (with vote counts);
- **Material discussion points** (less detailed for routine
  meetings, more for significant decisions);
- **Signature of chair** authenticating the minutes.

The minute book covers:

- **General meetings** of members;
- **Board meetings** of directors (kept in a separate book
  typically);
- **Class meetings** for class-rights variations;
- **Committee meetings** of board sub-committees.

## Evidential value

Minutes that are:

- **Signed by the chair**;
- **Kept in the company's records**;

are **prima facie evidence** of the proceedings and decisions
recorded.

This means in subsequent disputes:

- The **burden falls** on the challenger to show the minutes
  don't accurately reflect what happened;
- **Members and directors are bound** by the recorded decisions
  even if they later dispute them;
- **Third parties dealing with the company** can rely on the
  minute record (combined with [Article 67C](evidence-of-authorization.md)
  evidence rules).

## Pitfalls

- **Late minute preparation** — minutes prepared months after
  the meeting are evidentially weaker.
- **Inadequate detail** for significant decisions — important
  business deserves substantive minute coverage.
- **Conflicting drafts** — finalise minutes before circulation
  to avoid version confusion.
