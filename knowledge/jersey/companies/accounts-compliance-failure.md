# Article 109 — Failure to Comply with Accounting Requirements

Article 109 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** is the
**enforcement** provision for accounting and filing failures.
Companies that don't meet Part 16 requirements face penalties and
their directors face personal exposure.

## What Article 109 covers

Companies failing to meet **accounting and filing requirements**
face penalties, including potential **offence status** for
**directors or officers** responsible.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## What can be breached

The Article covers failures under any of:

- [Article 103](accounting-records.md) — accounting records;
- [Article 104](retention-of-records.md) — retention;
- [Article 105](accounts.md) — accounts preparation;
- [Article 106](interim-accounts.md) — interim accounts (where
  published);
- [Article 107](copies-of-accounts.md) — copies to members;
- [Article 108](delivery-of-accounts.md) — delivery to Registrar.

## Consequences

- **Offences** under the Companies Law (with penalties in
  Schedule 1);
- **Personal director liability** — directors / officers can be
  charged for the offence;
- **Civil financial penalties** through JFSC enforcement;
- **Strike-off** under Part 23 in chronic cases;
- **Reputational damage** for professional directors.

## Defences

Typical defences:

- **Reasonable cause** for the failure;
- **Unavoidable circumstances** under Article 67A;
- **Reliance on professional advisers** in specific limited
  cases.

The defences are **narrow**. The expectation is timely
compliance.

## Pitfalls

- **Cumulative breaches** — Late accounts plus inadequate
  records plus missing AGM compounds the exposure.
- **Director liability** — even where the company has
  professional administration, directors retain personal
  responsibility.
