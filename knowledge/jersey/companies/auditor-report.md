# Article 113A — Auditor's Report

Article 113A of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]**
specifies the **content** of the auditor's report — what an
auditor must report on for a Jersey company.

## What Article 113A covers

Auditors must report on:

- whether financial statements present a **true and fair view**
  of company affairs;
- whether auditors **obtained necessary information and
  explanations**;
- **confirmation of independence**;
- any **material uncertainties** about **going concern** status.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## True and fair view

The "**true and fair view**" standard is the auditor's primary
attestation. The auditor confirms (or qualifies / disclaims) that
the financial statements:

- **fairly reflect** the company's financial position;
- are **prepared in accordance with** applicable accounting
  framework;
- contain **no material misstatement** (whether from fraud or
  error).

If the auditor cannot give a clean opinion, they qualify the
report — disclosing the specific issues.

## Information obtained

The auditor confirms they **obtained the information** they
needed. A statement of inability to obtain information is a
**serious flag** to readers of the accounts.

## Independence

The auditor **confirms independence** — that they are not
disqualified under [Article 113D](auditor-ineligibility.md) and
maintained independence under [Article 113E](auditor-independence.md).

## Going concern

The auditor addresses **going concern** — whether the company is
likely to continue as a going concern for at least the next 12
months. Concerns raised here are significant for users of the
accounts.

## Pitfalls

- **Qualified opinions** — investors and counterparties read
  these carefully; significant qualifications affect the
  company's market reputation.
- **Late-discovered issues** — the auditor's report should
  reflect what was known at the time of signing; subsequent
  emergence of issues may need restatement.
