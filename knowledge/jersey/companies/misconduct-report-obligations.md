# Article 185 — Obligations Arising Under Article 184

Article 185 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** sets
out the **obligations of the liquidator and other persons**
flowing from an Article 184 misconduct report.

## What Article 185 covers

Once a misconduct report has been made:

- The liquidator must **cooperate** with subsequent
  investigation or prosecution;
- The liquidator must **provide further information** as
  requested by the JFSC or Attorney General;
- **Other persons** with knowledge must cooperate with the
  enforcement process;
- **Privilege** is preserved (Article 139 framework).

Failure to comply is an **offence**.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

A misconduct report is the **starting point** for enforcement
action. Article 185 ensures the **follow-through**:

- Investigations require continued access to the liquidator's
  records and analysis;
- Witnesses (including the liquidator) need to be available;
- Documents must be preserved and produced.

Without the follow-through obligations, the Article 184 report
would be a dead end.
