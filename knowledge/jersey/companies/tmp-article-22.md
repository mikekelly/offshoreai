# Takeovers Panel Law 2009 Article 22 — Privilege against self-incrimination

Article 22 protects persons compelled to respond to Panel
requirements from having their statements used against them
in **criminal proceedings**.

## Key provisions

Statements made in response to Panel requirements (e.g.
under [Article 7](./tmp-article-7.md)) **cannot be used in
criminal proceedings**, except for proceedings on a charge
of **perjury** (giving false statements under the
compulsion).

## Operational significance

The provision is consistent with general principles of
self-incrimination privilege adapted to a compulsory
regulatory information-gathering context. Parties responding
to Panel notices know that:

- They **cannot decline to provide information** on
  self-incrimination grounds (the Panel's powers are
  compulsory);
- But the **information cannot be used** in subsequent
  criminal proceedings against them, except where the
  charge is that the statement itself was false.

This balances regulatory effectiveness with criminal-
procedure rights.
