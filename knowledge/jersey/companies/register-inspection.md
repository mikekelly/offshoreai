# Article 45 — Inspection of Register

Article 45 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** gives
**members** a right to inspect the register and obtain copies, on
reasonable notice and at a reasonable fee.

## What Article 45 says

Members **may inspect the register during business hours**.

The company may require **reasonable notice** and may charge a
**modest fee**.

Companies must **provide copies on request**.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Who has inspection rights

The Article gives the right to **members**. In practice the
right is also extended to:

- **Persons authorised** by the company (e.g., the company
  secretary, the registered agent);
- **Court-appointed officers** (e.g., a Liquidator on wind-up);
- **Regulators** (the JFSC) under specific statutory powers
  independent of Article 45.

**Non-members** typically don't have a public right to inspect a
private company's register. Public-company registers are subject
to a wider set of disclosure rules under listing requirements.

## Reasonable notice

The Article allows the company to require **reasonable notice** —
typically a few business days. This lets the registered agent
prepare the register and make staff available.

A member who arrives without notice and demands immediate
inspection has no absolute right to it; the company can require
a reasonable scheduling.

## Reasonable fee

The fee for inspection or copying is **capped at "reasonable"** —
in practice nominal (copy-cost equivalent). Excessive fees can
be challenged.

## What the member can see

The register itself — names, addresses, shareholdings of all
members. The member is **not** entitled (under Article 45) to:

- Beneficial-ownership data (separate FSDI Law regime);
- Director or officer particulars (separate Article 83 register);
- Trustee deeds or other corporate documents (separate
  Article 12 right to memorandum and articles).

## Pitfalls

- **Refusing inspection without basis** — an offence and may
  prompt a Royal Court order compelling inspection.
- **Excessive fees** — challengeable; keep them nominal.
- **Confusing register inspection with director-particulars
  inspection** — they're separate rights.
