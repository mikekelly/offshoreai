# Article 113 — Appointment and Removal of Auditors

Article 113 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** governs
the **appointment** and **removal** of auditors of a Jersey
company.

## What Article 113 covers

Companies must **appoint auditors at their AGM**.

Auditors may be **reappointed or removed** by **ordinary
resolution**.

The **Commission** may remove recognised auditors from the
register for breach of professional standards or failure to meet
independence requirements.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## AGM appointment

For companies requiring audit:

- The **auditor is appointed** at the AGM (or at a meeting where
  audit is first required);
- **Reappointment** is annual unless otherwise resolved;
- **Auditor terms** are typically until the next AGM unless the
  resolution specifies otherwise.

## Ordinary resolution

The threshold is **ordinary resolution** — a simple majority of
votes cast. This is **lower** than the special-resolution
threshold for many other matters.

## Removal

An auditor can be **removed** by ordinary resolution. Typical
reasons:

- **Loss of confidence** in the auditor;
- **Independence concerns**;
- **Cost or service issues**;
- **Strategic alignment** with a different audit firm.

Mid-term removal raises specific concerns about auditor
independence — an auditor removed for refusing to support a
favourable view on a matter has rights and protections.

## JFSC removal from register

Separately, the **JFSC** can remove an auditor from the recognised-
auditor register for:

- **Breach of professional standards**;
- **Independence failures** ([Article 113E](auditor-independence.md));
- **Discipline issues** at the auditor's professional body;
- Other **fit-and-proper** concerns.

A removed auditor cannot continue to audit Jersey companies until
re-registered.

## Pitfalls

- **Mid-year removal disputes** — auditor disputes pre-AGM removal
  can be procedurally complex.
- **Independence issues** — appointment of an auditor with
  independence problems can invalidate the audit.
