# Article 163 — Remuneration of Liquidator, Cesser of Directors' Powers, and Vacancy in Office of Liquidator

Article 163 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** bundles
three matters that arise on liquidator appointment in a
creditors' winding up: **remuneration**, **directors' powers
ceasing**, and **vacancy filling**.

## What Article 163 covers

**Remuneration**:

- Determined by the **liquidation committee** (or by creditors
  generally if no committee), often by reference to scale or
  hourly rate;
- May be reviewed by the **Royal Court** if disputed.

**Cesser of directors' powers**:

- On appointment of the liquidator, the **directors' powers
  cease** except so far as the liquidator (or liquidation
  committee or Court) **sanctions** their continuance;
- Directors retain **limited statutory obligations** (e.g.
  cooperation with the liquidator under Article 183);
- Directors are **not removed from office** but lose authority
  to act.

**Vacancy**:

- A vacancy in the liquidator's office is filled by **creditors'
  meeting** or by Court order (Article 175).

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

The **cesser of directors' powers** is a sharp moment in
corporate insolvency. Directors who continued running the business
up to liquidation suddenly lose all authority. Important practical
implications:

- **Bank accounts** transfer to liquidator control;
- **Counterparty notifications** of new authority signatories;
- **Employees**' employment terms are now liquidator-handled;
- **Existing director contracts** with the company may need
  unwinding or assumption by the liquidator.
