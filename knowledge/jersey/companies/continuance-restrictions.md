# Article 127I — Restrictions on Continuance

Article 127I of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** sets
out **restrictions and disqualifications** for the Part 18C
continuance regime.

## What Article 127I covers

Continuance is **restricted or prohibited** where:

- The body is **insolvent** or in formal insolvency proceedings;
- The body is the subject of **regulatory action** in Jersey or
  the home jurisdiction;
- The continuance would **prejudice creditors** materially;
- The body has **outstanding statutory obligations** in Jersey
  (taxes, fees, regulatory filings);
- The continuance is to or from a jurisdiction that does not
  meet prescribed **standards** (e.g. AML / regulatory standards);
- Specified **public-interest** grounds apply.

For continuance into Jersey, additional restrictions may apply
where the body's activities require **regulatory consent**
(financial services, etc.) — the continuance can proceed only
with the relevant Jersey consent.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

Continuance is **easily misused** without restrictions:

- A body in financial trouble could continue out of Jersey to
  evade Jersey insolvency proceedings;
- A body could continue **into** Jersey to escape regulatory
  enforcement abroad;
- A body could redomicile to **launder** disreputable history.

Article 127I is the substantive **policy filter** behind the
continuance regime, supported by JFSC consent gating in
Article 127N (inbound) and Article 127U (outbound).
