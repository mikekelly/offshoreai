# Article 182 — Resolutions Passed at Adjourned Meetings

Article 182 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** treats
**resolutions passed at adjourned meetings** in a winding up as
having been passed on the date of the adjourned meeting (not the
original date).

## What Article 182 covers

Where a meeting (of members or creditors) in connection with a
winding up is **adjourned** to a later date and the resolution is
passed at the adjourned meeting:

- The resolution is **deemed passed on the date** of the adjourned
  meeting;
- Time periods (notice, filings, etc.) run from that **later
  date**;
- The procedural validity of the resolution depends on the
  adjourned meeting being **properly convened**.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

The default rule under general meeting law (Article 101) is that
resolutions at adjourned meetings are deemed passed at the
adjourned date — Article 182 confirms this rule specifically for
winding-up proceedings, where the question can otherwise be
unclear.
