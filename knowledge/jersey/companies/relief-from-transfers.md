# Article 39B — Relief from Premium / Stated-Capital Transfer Requirements

Article 39B of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** is the
**relief gateway**: the States may provide by Regulations that the
ordinary requirements to credit premium or stated capital to a
separate account are **relaxed** in specified circumstances.

## What Article 39B says

The **States may by Regulations** provide **relief** from the
requirements to maintain separate **share-premium accounts** (for
par-value companies) or **stated-capital accounts** (for
no-par-value companies) under **specified circumstances**.

This streamlines compliance where the ordinary capital-account
formalities don't add useful protection.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## When relief is typically given

The States has exercised the Article 39B power in connection with:

- **Group reorganisations** — where an internal group
  restructuring would otherwise generate large share-premium
  account balances that serve no creditor-protection purpose;
- **Statutory mergers** under Part 18B — where merger accounting
  mechanics would force impractical premium / stated-capital
  treatment;
- **Demergers** under Part 18BA — similar accounting
  simplification;
- **Continuance** under Part 18C — where a foreign company
  redomiciling to Jersey would have to convert its existing
  capital structure into Jersey premium / stated-capital format
  by default.

The relief is typically **conditional** — the Regulations specify
the circumstances and the conditions under which relief is
available.

## The English-law parallel

This is loosely analogous to the **group reconstruction relief**
and **merger relief** in English company law — sections 611–613
of the UK Companies Act 2006 — which excuse companies from the
share-premium-account requirement on certain reorganisations.
The Jersey Article 39B mechanism plays a similar role.

## Practical relevance

Modern Jersey transactional practice routinely relies on
Article 39B Regulations to:

- Avoid the creation of **artificial share-premium balances**
  on intra-group reorganisations;
- Permit **acquirer companies** in mergers to allocate
  acquisition consideration without forcing premium-account
  bloat;
- Facilitate **continuance-in** of foreign companies without
  needing to restructure their capital accounts.

Check the **current Order** made under Article 39B for the
specific relief available; the Regulations have been amended
periodically.

## Pitfalls

- **Assuming relief is automatic** — it isn't; the Regulations
  specify the circumstances. Confirm a transaction qualifies
  before relying on relief.
- **Not documenting the relief reliance** — accounts and filings
  should reflect the relief being relied on for audit-trail
  purposes.
