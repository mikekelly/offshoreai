# Article 6 — Standard Table

Article 6 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** authorises
the Minister to prescribe a **Standard Table** of model articles
of association — and provides that those model articles apply by
default to eligible companies that don't register their own.

## What Article 6 says

- The **Minister may prescribe** a set of model articles in a
  Standard Table;
- The Standard Table is suitable for **par-value companies
  without unlimited shares and without guarantor members** (i.e.,
  the "vanilla" Article 3E par-value-limited-by-shares form);
- A company **may adopt the whole or any part** of the Standard
  Table for its articles, "to the extent that it is appropriate
  to do so";
- For eligible companies where **no articles are registered**,
  the Standard Table **applies as if articles in the form of the
  Table had been duly registered**.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why the Standard Table exists

The Standard Table is a **default safety net**: if a company
incorporates without filing its own articles (rare in modern
practice), it nevertheless has a complete set of operational
governance rules.

Historically the Standard Table also served as a **drafting
starting point** — agents would start from the Standard Table and
customise.

## Modern practice

In modern practice almost every Jersey company:

- **Files its own articles** rather than relying on the Standard
  Table by default;
- **May incorporate parts of the Standard Table by reference**,
  but typically uses bespoke or registered-agent template articles
  tailored to:
  - **no-par-value share capital** (Standard Table is par-value
    only);
  - **modern cell-company / fund / family-office** structures;
  - **specific commercial provisions** (investor protections,
    drag/tag, pre-emption).

A company that is **not** a par-value-limited-by-shares form
(e.g., guarantee, unlimited, no-par-value, cell company) **cannot
rely on the Standard Table** — the Table is by design only suited
to the vanilla case.

## Where to find the current Standard Table

The Standard Table is prescribed by **Order of the Minister** and
is published on jerseylaw.je as a subordinate instrument under the
Companies Law. Look up the current Order (the form has been
updated periodically) on [jerseylaw.je][cjl-orders] before relying
on the content for a specific company.

## Pitfalls

- **Treating the Standard Table as universal**. It applies only to
  vanilla par-value companies; modern Jersey vehicles (no-par,
  cell, fund-related) need bespoke articles.
- **Mixing the Standard Table with bespoke articles** without a
  clear primacy rule. Resolve conflicts in the bespoke articles
  with an express clause.
- **Old-version Standard Table** — the form has been amended;
  check the current consolidated Order before adopting.
