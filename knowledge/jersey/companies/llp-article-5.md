# LLP Law 2017 Article 5 — Liability of a partner or former partner

Article 5 is the **partner protection clause** — the core
of what "limited liability" means for the partners.

## Key provisions

- A partner or former partner is **not liable** for LLP
  debts or other LLP liabilities solely by virtue of being
  (or having been) a partner;
- **Exceptions** — partner liability is preserved for:
  - The partner's **own personal debts** and personal
    liabilities;
  - **Unauthorised property withdrawals** — see
    [Article 12](./llp-article-12.md);
  - Any other express provision of the Law.

## Operational significance

The "solely by virtue of being a partner" formulation means
partners do not assume joint-and-several liability for the
LLP's contractual or tortious liabilities. A partner can
still be **personally liable** for their own torts (a
professional providing negligent advice, for example,
remains personally liable for that negligence — and the LLP
may also be liable as principal under
[Article 17](./llp-article-17.md)).
