# Article 95 — Resolutions in Writing

Article 95 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** permits
member resolutions to be passed **without a formal meeting** by
**written consent**. Written resolutions are the **modern
operational default** for many Jersey private companies.

## What Article 95 says

Members may pass resolutions **without formal meetings** through
**written consent**:

- **All members** must agree to the proposed resolution
  (subject to the Article 95ZA/ZB/ZC variants);
- The resolution becomes **effective** when the **required
  number of members** have signed;
- Companies must **keep records** of written resolutions.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why written resolutions are heavily used

For **private Jersey companies**, written resolutions are typical
because:

- **No meeting** needs to be convened — saves time and
  administration;
- **Asynchronous** signing — members in different time zones can
  sign at their convenience;
- **Same legal effect** as a resolution passed at a meeting;
- **Can pass special resolutions** as well as ordinary;
- **Cleaner record-keeping** — signed copies in the company's
  files are unambiguous.

Many modern Jersey private companies operate **almost
exclusively** by written resolution.

## Unanimity vs simplified procedure

The basic Article 95 written-resolution mechanism requires
**unanimous member consent**. The **simplified procedures** in
Articles 95ZA, 95ZB, and 95ZC permit:

- **Director-proposed** written resolutions ([Article 95ZA](./directors-written-resolutions.md))
  — circulated to members for consideration;
- **Member-proposed** written resolutions
  ([Article 95ZB](./members-written-resolution.md)) — members can
  initiate;
- **Statements** accompanying resolutions
  ([Article 95ZC](./written-resolution-statement.md)).

These variants allow **threshold approvals** (e.g., 75% for
special resolutions) without requiring unanimous signing.

## Sole-member companies

A **sole-member** company uses the **simpler procedure** in
[Article 95A](./sole-member-decisions.md) — the single member
records decisions in writing.

## Effect — same as meeting-passed

A written resolution has the **same legal effect** as a
resolution passed at a properly-convened meeting:

- **Ordinary written resolutions** are ordinary resolutions;
- **Special written resolutions** are special resolutions (with
  same 75% threshold or unanimity per the procedure used);
- **Class written resolutions** are class resolutions.

## Record-keeping

The signed written resolution is kept in the **company's
records** alongside meeting minutes. The record:

- Shows the **text** of the resolution passed;
- **Names** of signing members;
- **Date** the resolution became effective;
- Any **accompanying statements** (under Article 95ZC).

## Pitfalls

- **Missing signatures** — a written resolution that doesn't
  reach the required threshold is not validly passed.
- **Mixing meeting and written-resolution procedures** — choose
  one and follow it through.
- **Special-resolution thresholds** — note that the simplified-
  procedure variants may have their own threshold rules.
