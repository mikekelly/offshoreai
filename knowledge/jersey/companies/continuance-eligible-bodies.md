# Article 127H — Bodies Corporate Eligible for Continuance

Article 127H of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** sets
out **which bodies corporate are eligible to use the continuance
regime** to become a Jersey company without losing their corporate
existence.

## What Article 127H covers

A body corporate is eligible for **continuance into Jersey** if:

- It is incorporated under the law of a **recognised
  jurisdiction** (typically a jurisdiction whose corporate law
  permits outbound migration);
- The **constitutional documents** of the body permit
  redomiciliation;
- It is in **good standing** in its home jurisdiction;
- It is **not** in liquidation, administration or equivalent
  proceedings;
- It meets prescribed **fit-and-proper** criteria for ownership
  and management.

A Jersey company is eligible for **continuance out of Jersey** if:

- It is in good standing in Jersey;
- It is **not** in liquidation, administration or equivalent
  proceedings;
- The chosen destination jurisdiction recognises **continuance**
  as a legal concept;
- Specified additional conditions are met (see Article 127I
  restrictions).

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

Continuance — also called **migration** or **redomiciliation** —
moves a body corporate from one legal jurisdiction to another
**without dissolving and reincorporating**:

- **Same legal entity** survives the move (no change of
  contractual counterparty);
- **Existing contracts continue** automatically;
- **Existing litigation continues** in the same entity;
- **Existing assets** remain in the same legal owner.

This is materially different from:

- **Liquidation and re-incorporation** (a different entity with
  no contract continuity);
- **Merger with a new vehicle** (works by universal succession in
  some jurisdictions but creates entity-identity questions);
- **Share transfer to a new parent** (changes ownership not seat).

Article 127H is the eligibility gateway.
