# Article 127S — Objections by Members to Continuance Overseas

Article 127S of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** is the
**member's right to challenge an outbound continuance** in the
Royal Court.

## What Article 127S covers

A member of a Jersey company who:

- Voted against the special resolution under Article 127Q; or
- Was not entitled to vote on it;

may, within a prescribed period (commonly 21 days), apply to the
**Royal Court** for an order that the continuance should not
proceed or should proceed on different terms.

The Court may make any order it thinks fit, including:

- **Cancellation** of the continuance proposal;
- **Modification** of the proposed terms;
- A **buyout order** — the company or other members buy out the
  objector at a court-set price.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

Outbound continuance materially changes the **legal regime**
under which members hold their shares:

- Minority-protection rules differ between jurisdictions;
- Unfair-prejudice rights and similar may be weaker abroad;
- Court jurisdiction shifts.

Members who **value Jersey's specific corporate-law features**
have a legitimate concern about losing them. Article 127S gives
them a court remedy.

In practice, as with merger objections under Article 127FB, the
most common outcome is a **court-ordered buyout** — the objector
takes cash and the continuance proceeds.
