# Article 117 — Right of Offeror to Buy Out Minority Shareholders

Article 117 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** is the
**statutory squeeze-out**: where a takeover offer has been
accepted by holders of **90% or more** of the shares to which the
offer relates, the offeror may **compulsorily acquire** the
remaining minority shares on the same terms as the offer.

## What Article 117 covers

Where a takeover offer under Article 116 has been **accepted by
holders of not less than 90%** (in value) of the shares to which
the offer relates — and, for shares carrying votes, 90% of those
voting rights — the offeror **may give notice** to the dissenting
minority that it **wishes to acquire** their shares on the offer
terms.

Notice must be:

- In the **prescribed form**;
- Given within a **prescribed time** of the 90% threshold being
  reached (commonly within a few months of the offer becoming
  unconditional);
- Given to **each holder** of the shares to which the notice
  relates.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

The squeeze-out is the principal reason takeover offers are
structured as offers under Part 18 rather than as bilateral
contracts. Without it, a single holdout shareholder could **block
delisting / privatisation** by refusing to sell.

The 90% threshold reflects a policy balance:

- **Public-interest principle** — once an overwhelming majority
  has accepted, the bidder has effectively become the owner and
  should be able to consolidate the rest;
- **Minority-protection principle** — the threshold is high
  enough that minority shareholders are not lightly dispossessed,
  and the same-terms requirement protects against discounted
  buyouts.

## Mechanics

1. Offeror makes a Part 18-compliant **takeover offer**;
2. Offer is accepted by **≥ 90%** of the shares to which it
   relates (and 90% of the voting rights);
3. Offeror serves **Article 117 notice** on dissenting
   shareholders;
4. Six weeks pass without successful court application (see
   Article 121);
5. **Compulsory acquisition** takes effect — see Article 118 for
   the mechanism by which legal title transfers.

## Computing the 90%

The 90% is computed by reference to the shares **to which the
offer relates**, excluding:

- Shares already held by the offeror at the date of the offer;
- Shares held by **associates** of the offeror (Article 123)
  acquired during the offer, in certain circumstances.

Where the offer relates to a **class** of shares, the 90% is
computed within that class.

## Dissenting shareholder's protection

The dissenting shareholder can apply to the **Royal Court** under
Article 121 within 6 weeks of the Article 117 notice for an order
that the shares should **not** be acquired or should be acquired
on **different terms**.

The court has wide power but in practice will not interfere with
the offer terms unless the minority can show that the offer was
**unfair** — a high bar, given that 90% of the shares to which
the offer related accepted those same terms.
