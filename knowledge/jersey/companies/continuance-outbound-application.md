# Article 127T — Application to Commission for Authorization to Seek Continuance Overseas

Article 127T of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** sets
out the **application** a Jersey company must make to the **JFSC**
for **authorization** to seek continuance overseas.

## What Article 127T covers

The applicant must deliver to the JFSC:

- Application in **prescribed form**;
- Evidence of **member approval** under Article 127Q;
- Evidence of **creditor notice** under Article 127R;
- A **solvency statement** under Article 127W;
- The proposed **destination jurisdiction** and confirmation that
  it accepts continuance;
- A **foreign-law opinion** confirming that the destination
  jurisdiction will accept the continuance and treat the body
  as continuing in existence;
- **Tax clearance** from the Comptroller of Revenue (or
  equivalent);
- Confirmation of **discharge** of outstanding Jersey statutory
  obligations (filings, fees);
- **Prescribed fees** and security under Article 127J.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

The outbound application is the **substantive checkpoint** before
the JFSC authorises the company to be released from Jersey:

- The company will **cease to be a Jersey company** on completion;
- Jersey law will **lose direct control** over the body;
- Jersey creditors and members will **depend on foreign law** for
  protection going forward.

The JFSC needs **complete information** about the destination,
the reasons for the move, and the post-continuance arrangements
before it can approve.
