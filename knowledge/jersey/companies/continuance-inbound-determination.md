# Article 127N — Determination of Application for Continuance Within Jersey

Article 127N of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** sets
out how the **JFSC determines** an application for **inbound
continuance**.

## What Article 127N covers

The JFSC must consider whether the body and the proposed
continuance:

- Meet the **eligibility requirements** in Article 127H;
- Do not fall within **restrictions** in Article 127I;
- Have **complete and accurate** application documents;
- Are accompanied by **valid solvency statements**;
- Reflect **proper notice** to creditors (where required) and
  approval by the body's members in accordance with its home law;
- Pass **fit-and-proper** standards for owners and directors;
- Have proper **foreign-law confirmation** that the body will
  cease to exist in its home jurisdiction on completion.

The JFSC may:

- **Grant** the application;
- **Grant with conditions**;
- **Refuse** the application (with reasons).

Refusals are appealable to the Royal Court.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

The JFSC determination is the **substantive vetting** of the
inbound migration. The Commission acts as gatekeeper for Jersey's
reputation as a corporate domicile.

In practice, applicants and the JFSC engage in **iterative
correspondence** during the determination period to clarify
issues and (where possible) negotiate conditions.
