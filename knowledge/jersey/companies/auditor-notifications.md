# Article 113K — Matters to Be Notified to the Commission

Article 113K of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** sets
**ongoing reporting obligations** for recognised auditors to the
JFSC.

## What Article 113K covers

Recognised auditors must **notify the Commission** regarding:

1. **Changes in registration status**;
2. **Disciplinary actions** taken against them;
3. **Resignation or removal**;
4. **Investigation initiation**;
5. Other **material circumstances** affecting their fitness to
   practice.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why these notifications

The JFSC needs **current information** about the auditor
population:

- Who is **still registered** and eligible;
- Who is **under investigation** or disciplinary action;
- Who has **resigned** or been removed from engagements;
- Other **fit-and-proper** changes.

This lets the JFSC:

- **Maintain the accuracy** of the auditor register;
- **Identify trends** in audit quality issues;
- **Act on emerging concerns** before they affect Jersey
  companies.

## Timing

Notifications must be **prompt** — typically within stated
timeframes after the relevant event.

## Pitfalls

- **Forgotten notifications** — easy to overlook when an
  auditor's home-body disciplinary action is the trigger.
