# Article 127O — Issue of Certificate of Continuance Within Jersey

Article 127O of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** is the
**issue mechanic** for the certificate that completes an
inbound continuance.

## What Article 127O covers

On a successful inbound application:

- The JFSC issues a **certificate of continuance**;
- The certificate specifies an **effective date** for the
  continuance;
- The certificate is **registered** with the registrar of
  companies;
- The body is **deemed registered** as a Jersey company from the
  effective date;
- The articles of continuance become the **memorandum and
  articles** of the Jersey company.

The certificate is **conclusive evidence** that the requirements
of Part 18C have been met.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

The certificate marks the **legal moment** of the migration. From
the effective date:

- Jersey law governs the body;
- The body is a Jersey company with all the corresponding
  obligations (annual filings, fees, JFSC oversight if regulated);
- The home jurisdiction's law typically ceases to apply (subject
  to foreign-law confirmations).
