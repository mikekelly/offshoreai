# Flying Freeholds and Share Transfer Property in Jersey

## The Jersey apartment problem

Jersey customary real-property law assumed **terrestrial
ownership** — *terre et soleil*, ground and sun. Apartment
ownership doesn't fit naturally because:

- **A unit** has no ground footprint of its own;
- **Customary law** struggles with sideways / upwards
  ownership;
- **Repairs / common parts** require enforceable
  obligations between owners.

Three solutions emerged:

1. **Share transfer** — own a share in a company that owns
   the building;
2. **Flying freehold** under the 1991 Law — statutory
   apartment-ownership regime;
3. **Long leasehold** — long lease of the apartment, with
   freehold retained by management entity.

## Share transfer property

### Structure

- **Company** owns the freehold of the building;
- **Shares** carry a right to **occupy a specified flat**;
- **Articles** govern occupation rights, service charges,
  repair obligations;
- **Transfer** of the flat = transfer of the shares.

### Advantages

- **Avoids** Royal Court passing for unit transfer;
- **Quick** transfer (share-purchase mechanics);
- **Lower** stamp duty (share transactions);
- **Flexible** governance via articles;
- **Historical** market — many older Jersey blocks.

### Disadvantages

- **No statutory** framework specifically tailored;
- **Company law** complexity;
- **Mortgage / hypothec** challenges (share security
  required, not real-property security);
- **Specific** Jersey practice has developed standard
  documentation;
- **CHWL** restrictions apply (Article 10).

### Lending against share-transfer flats

- **Banks** historically reluctant;
- **Specific** Jersey practice now well-established;
- **Share charge** (Security Interests Law 2012);
- **Company-articles** typically allow charging.

## Flying freehold (Loi 1991)

### Structure

The Loi (1991) sur la copropriété des immeubles bâtis
provides a **statutory apartment regime**:

- **Individual unit** owned in freehold;
- **Common parts** held by an **association of co-owners**
  (*association des copropriétaires*);
- **Réglements** govern occupation, service charges, repair;
- **Specific** statutory regime.

### Advantages

- **Real-property** title (rather than shares);
- **Familiar** to common-law lawyers;
- **Standard** banking / mortgage treatment;
- **Statutory** framework reduces ambiguity.

### Disadvantages

- **Royal Court** passing required for each unit transfer;
- **Stamp duty** at real-property rates;
- **Slower** transactional process;
- **Specific** Loi 1991 compliance for new developments.

## Choosing between structures

For a new Jersey apartment development:

- **Flying freehold** — preferred for new schemes (standard
  banking acceptance);
- **Share transfer** — historically dominant; many older
  blocks remain;
- **Long leasehold** — used in specific niches.

For an existing building converting:

- **Conversion** from share transfer to flying freehold —
  complex but possible;
- **Specific** Jersey case law and practice;
- **Tax / stamp duty** implications.

## Common parts and management

Both structures require:

- **Defined** common parts;
- **Service-charge** mechanisms;
- **Reserve funds** for major repairs;
- **Voting** rights on association decisions;
- **Specific** Jersey rules on quorum and majorities.

## Occupation rights (CHWL overlay)

Apartment ownership is **subject to** the Control of
Housing and Work (Jersey) Law 2012:

- **Entitled / Licensed / Registered** status governs
  occupation;
- **Article 10** restrictions on non-Entitled buyers;
- **Specific** rules on second homes / lodging-house use;
- **Share transfer** and **flying freehold** both subject to
  CHWL.

## Transfer mechanics

### Share transfer

- **Stock transfer form**;
- **Board approval** (typically per articles);
- **Updated share register**;
- **Disclosure** to mortgagee / chargee;
- **AML / CDD** by company administrator.

### Flying freehold

- **Contract** drafted (acquéreurs / vendeurs);
- **Royal Court passing** (Friday afternoon);
- **Registration** in Public Registry;
- **Hypothec** registered if mortgage involved;
- **Specific** Jersey advocate practice.

## Tax and stamp duty

- **Stamp duty** at different rates for share vs flying-
  freehold transfers;
- **Specific** Jersey rates and thresholds;
- **GST / ISE** — generally not on residential transfers;
- **Higher rates** for non-Entitled buyers (where permitted);
- **HVR** buyers — specific tax treatment.

## Specific Jersey contexts

### St Helier apartment blocks

- **Mix** of share-transfer and flying-freehold;
- **Older blocks** typically share transfer;
- **New developments** typically flying freehold.

### Rural / off-island

- **Less common** for apartments;
- **Specific** considerations for rural converted buildings.

### Second homes / Air-BnB

- **CHWL** restrictions limit short-let use;
- **Specific** lodging-house permission required;
- **Specific** planning considerations.

## Pitfalls

- **Misunderstanding** share-transfer mechanics (it's a
  share deal, not property deal);
- **Missing** company-articles compliance on transfer;
- **Drafting** share-transfer security incorrectly;
- **Missing** CHWL Article 10 compliance;
- **Underestimating** Royal Court passing for flying
  freehold;
- **Missing** stamp-duty difference between structures.
