# Easements and Rights of Way in Jersey

## Terminology

In Jersey customary law:

- **Servitude** = the general term (≈ easement);
- **Servitude active** (positive) — right enjoyed over
  another's land;
- **Servitude passive** (negative) — restriction on use of
  one's own land;
- **Fonds dominant** = dominant tenement (benefiting);
- **Fonds servient** = servient tenement (burdened).

This file focuses specifically on **rights of way** and
other **access servitudes**; see [`servitudes.md`](./servitudes.md)
for the general framework.

## Categories of right of way

Jersey rights of way include:

- **Chemin** — a public road;
- **Chemin privé** — a private road;
- **Chasse / charrière** — a customary track for vehicles;
- **Sentier** — a footpath;
- **Banque** — bank / verge alongside a way;
- **Specific** Norman terminology preserved.

## Creation

Rights of way are created by:

- **Express grant** in a contrat passed before the Royal
  Court;
- **Express reservation** when subdividing land;
- **Implied** grant (by necessity, common intention);
- **Prescription** (long use establishing a right);
- **Statute** (specific Jersey statutory provisions);
- **Customary use** (specific historical rights).

## Express grant or reservation

A well-drafted servitude clause specifies:

- **Dominant** and servient tenements identified;
- **Width / dimensions** of the way;
- **Permitted uses** (pedestrian / vehicular / agricultural);
- **Hours** of access (if restricted);
- **Maintenance** responsibility;
- **Specific** prohibitions (parking, gates).

## Implied creation

A right of way may be implied:

- **Necessity** — landlocked parcel needs access;
- **Common intention** — based on circumstances at
  conveyance;
- **Specific** Jersey case law on implication;
- **Pothier-aligned** good-faith considerations.

## Prescription

Long use may establish a right by prescription:

- **Long, continuous, peaceful, uninterrupted** use;
- **As of right** (not by permission);
- **Specific** Jersey statutory periods (typically 40 years);
- **Specific** customary requirements;
- **Distinguishable from** English Prescription Act 1832.

## Scope of right

The scope is determined by:

- **Express terms** of grant;
- **Purpose** of grant (purpose-limiting);
- **Reasonable** development of the dominant tenement;
- **Specific** Jersey case law on excessive use.

## Maintenance

Default rules:

- **Dominant** owner typically maintains the way (unless
  otherwise specified);
- **Servient** owner cannot obstruct use;
- **Specific** contractual provisions override defaults;
- **Specific** Jersey case law on repair obligations.

## Excessive use

A dominant owner may not:

- **Exceed** the scope of the granted right;
- **Use** for purposes outside the original grant;
- **Cause** disproportionate burden on the servient
  tenement;
- **Specific** Jersey case law on excessive use.

For example, a right of way for "agricultural purposes"
typically cannot be used for industrial traffic.

## Obstruction

A servient owner may not:

- **Obstruct** the way;
- **Reduce** its dimensions below those granted;
- **Erect** structures interfering with use;
- **Subject to** reasonable variation (gates, etc.);
- **Specific** Jersey case law on permissible variations.

## Extinguishment

Rights of way may be extinguished by:

- **Express release** in a contrat;
- **Merger** (unity of ownership);
- **Abandonment** (with intent);
- **Statutory** procedures;
- **Specific** Jersey rules.

Mere **non-use** does not extinguish — intent to abandon is
typically required.

## Specific Jersey contexts

### Rural / agricultural

- **Customary** access tracks;
- **Right of way** to fields, woods, beaches;
- **Specific** Norman terminology;
- **Specific** Jersey case law on agricultural rights.

### Urban / residential

- **Shared driveways**;
- **Side-entrance** rights;
- **Pedestrian** access ways;
- **Specific** Jersey practice in modern developments.

### Coastal

- **Beach access** rights;
- **Specific** Jersey rules on coastal customary rights;
- **Specific** public-access considerations.

### Service-related

- **Drainage** servitudes;
- **Cable / pipe** servitudes;
- **Water-flow** rights;
- **Specific** modern infrastructure servitudes.

## Public vs private rights

- **Public footpaths** — accessible to all;
- **Public roads** — by statute / customary dedication;
- **Private rights of way** — between identified parties;
- **Specific** Jersey law on public dedication.

## Disputes

Common rights-of-way disputes:

- **Existence** of the right (proof);
- **Scope** of permitted use;
- **Obstruction** by servient owner;
- **Excessive use** by dominant owner;
- **Maintenance** responsibility;
- **Specific** Jersey procedural considerations.

## Procedure

Royal Court applications:

- **Order of Justice** typically;
- **Survey evidence** important;
- **Title-deed** evidence;
- **Historic** photograph / map evidence;
- **Witness** evidence of historic use;
- **Specific** Jersey procedural rules.

## Remedies

- **Declaration** of right;
- **Injunction** against obstruction / excessive use;
- **Damages** for interference;
- **Specific performance** of repair / restoration;
- **Specific** Jersey case law.

## Pitfalls

- **Drafting** ambiguous servitude grants;
- **Missing** Royal Court passing for creation /
  extinguishment;
- **Failing** to specify scope / maintenance;
- **Confusing** prescription with abandonment;
- **Underestimating** litigation costs;
- **Ignoring** customary-law context.

## Drafting recommendations

- **Identify** parties and tenements precisely;
- **Specify** dimensions, route, purpose, hours;
- **Address** maintenance responsibility;
- **Include** dispute-resolution provisions;
- **Engage** Jersey advocates for passing.
