# Restrictive Covenants on Jersey Real Property

## What this covers

A **restrictive covenant** on Jersey real property is a
**negative obligation** restricting use:

- **Common in** developments (estate covenants);
- **Recorded** in the contrat passing the property;
- **Specific** Jersey rules on enforceability and running with
  the land;
- **Distinguishable from** positive covenants (more
  problematic to enforce);
- **Distinguishable from** servitudes (rights, not
  restrictions).

## Sources

- **Customary law** (Norman-derived);
- **Contracts** passed in Royal Court;
- **Specific** statutory provisions;
- **Modern** Jersey case law.

## Typical restrictive covenants

Common categories:

- **User** restrictions (residential only / no commercial);
- **Density** restrictions (single dwelling);
- **Aesthetic** restrictions (materials, colours, height);
- **Build-line** restrictions;
- **No-let** restrictions;
- **No alteration** without consent;
- **Specific** estate-management rules.

## Creation

Restrictive covenants are created by:

- **Contract** passed in Royal Court;
- **Specific** wording in the conveyance;
- **Registered** in the Public Registry;
- **Identification** of benefited and burdened land
  essential.

## Running with the land

A restrictive covenant runs with the land if:

- **Identified** dominant and servient tenements;
- **Touches and concerns** the land (not personal);
- **Negative** character (positive covenants are more
  problematic);
- **Properly recorded** in the contrat;
- **Specific** Jersey case law on requirements.

## Enforcement

A covenant may be enforced by:

- **Original** benefiting party (against successors);
- **Successor** benefiting party (against successor
  burdened party);
- **Royal Court** injunction;
- **Damages** in lieu of injunction;
- **Specific performance** of preventative measures.

## Discharge and modification

Restrictive covenants may be:

- **Released** by deed of release;
- **Modified** by agreement of benefiting party;
- **Set aside** by Royal Court on application;
- **Specific** Jersey statutory provisions;
- **Specific** discharge / modification procedures.

## Grounds for discharge / modification

Common grounds include:

- **Obsolescence** — covenant no longer serves its purpose;
- **Changes** in neighbourhood character;
- **Impeding reasonable use** of the property;
- **No benefit** to the benefiting party;
- **Public interest** considerations;
- **Specific** Jersey case law and statutory provisions.

## Specific contexts

### New developments

- **Standard** estate covenants;
- **Developer** typically retains benefit during
  development;
- **Transfer** to management entity / residents'
  association;
- **Specific** Jersey practice.

### Heritage / conservation

- **Specific** covenants on listed buildings;
- **Planning law** overlay;
- **Specific** Jersey heritage rules.

### Coastal / amenity

- **Specific** covenants on coastal land;
- **Visual amenity** protections;
- **Specific** Jersey customary rules.

### Top-50 properties

- **Specific** covenants on Top-50 housing-policy properties;
- **Jersey Property Holdings** considerations;
- **Specific** Jersey practice.

## Positive covenants

Positive covenants (requiring action) are more problematic:

- **Generally** do not run with the land in Jersey;
- **Specific** workarounds (deed of arrangement, chain of
  indemnity);
- **Modern** developments use estate-management companies
  to enforce positive obligations;
- **Specific** Jersey case law.

## Estate-management companies

A common workaround for positive covenants:

- **Management company** holds common parts / enforces
  rules;
- **Each unit owner** becomes member / shareholder;
- **Contractual** obligations to management company;
- **Specific** Jersey practice and standard documentation;
- **Service-charge** mechanisms.

## Servitudes vs restrictive covenants

| Feature | Servitude | Restrictive covenant |
|---|---|---|
| Nature | Right | Restriction |
| Direction | Dominant has right | Burdened has restriction |
| Customary basis | Yes | Yes |
| Running with land | Yes | Yes (if properly created) |
| Discharge | Specific procedures | Specific procedures |

## Title due diligence

When acquiring Jersey real property:

- **Title search** to identify covenants;
- **Public Registry** searches;
- **Survey** for compliance with covenants;
- **Specific** advice on enforceability;
- **Insurance** for covenant risks (specific products).

## Pitfalls

- **Drafting** ambiguous covenants;
- **Failing** to identify benefited / burdened land
  clearly;
- **Mixing** positive and negative obligations without
  clarity;
- **Missing** registration in Public Registry;
- **Failing** to check covenants on purchase;
- **Ignoring** discharge / modification options when
  redeveloping.

## Drafting recommendations

- **Specify** benefited land and benefiting parties clearly;
- **Distinguish** negative from positive obligations;
- **Include** modification / release mechanisms;
- **Specify** enforcement and remedies;
- **Consider** estate-management company structure for
  positive obligations;
- **Engage** Jersey advocates for drafting and passing.
