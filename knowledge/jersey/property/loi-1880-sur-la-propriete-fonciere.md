# Loi (1880) sur la propriété foncière

The **Loi (1880) sur la propriété foncière** is the **principal
statute** governing real property in Jersey. Despite its age
(1880), it remains the **foundational** real-property
statute, modified by subsequent legislation.

## Structure

The Law has been amended many times since 1880 but retains its
core structure:

### Part 1 — Ownership and division

- **Concept** of land ownership;
- **Vesting** of property;
- **Division** of property among co-owners;
- **Specific** Jersey doctrines.

### Part 2 — Hypothecs

- **Creation** of hypothecs;
- **Priority** rules;
- **Enforcement** through dégrèvement;
- See [`hypothec.md`](./hypothec.md).

### Part 3 — Rentes

- **Creation** of rentes (historic);
- **Conversion** to modern instruments;
- **Specific** rente rules.

### Part 4 — Servitudes

- **Creation** of servitudes;
- **Specific** customary servitudes preserved;
- **Statutory** servitudes.

### Part 5 — Specific procedures

- **Dégrèvement** (foreclosure-like remedy);
- **Adjudication** procedures;
- **Specific** Royal Court processes.

## Why 1880 still matters

Despite its age:

- **Core principles** of Jersey real-property law remain
  here;
- **Substantial** body of Royal Court case law applying it;
- **Specific** Jersey concepts (hypothec, rente) governed by
  this Law;
- **Modernisation** by amendment rather than full re-
  codification.

## Key concepts

### Pleine propriété

Full ownership — the closest Jersey concept to English
freehold. The Loi 1880 sets out:

- **Acquisition** of pleine propriété;
- **Limits** (servitudes, hypothecs);
- **Transfer** mechanisms.

### Quote-part (co-ownership)

- **Co-ownership** of real property;
- **Specific** Jersey customary rules;
- **Division** procedures.

### Hypothec

- **Real-property security** (Part 2);
- **Conventional** hypothec by contract passed before Royal
  Court;
- **Judicial** hypothec arising from court judgment;
- **Légal** hypothec arising by operation of law (e.g.,
  spousal in some cases);
- **Priority** rules and enforcement.

### Rente

- **Historic** mechanism for purchase-money financing;
- **Largely obsolete** in modern practice;
- **Some legacy** rentes still extant;
- **Conversion** procedures.

## Procedural framework

### Contract passing

Property transactions still **pass before the Royal Court**:

- **Loi 1880** preserves this procedure;
- **Greffier** records;
- **Public registry** entry.

### Public registry

- **Maintained** by the Royal Court Greffier;
- **Records** transactions, hypothecs, servitudes;
- **Available** for searches;
- **Critical** for title investigation.

### Dégrèvement

- **Foreclosure-like** remedy for hypothec enforcement;
- **Specific** Royal Court process;
- **Public** auction in some cases;
- **Strict** procedural requirements.

## Subsequent legislation

The Loi 1880 has been supplemented by:

- **Loi (1862) sur les Hypothèques** — pre-existing
  hypothec rules retained;
- **Loi (1991) sur la Vente** — sale modernisation;
- **Specific** subsequent statutes;
- **Security Interests (Jersey) Law 2012** — for personal
  property (not within Loi 1880 scope).

## Practical significance

For practitioners:

- **Loi 1880** remains a daily reference for real-property
  practitioners;
- **Specific** sections frequently cited;
- **Royal Court** decisions extensively interpret it;
- **Customary** law principles preserved alongside statute.
