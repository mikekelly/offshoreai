# Hypothec — Jersey Real Property Security

## What a hypothec is

A **hypothec** is the **Jersey form of security** over real
property — analogous to but distinct from an English mortgage.

## Key features

### No transfer of legal title

Unlike older English mortgages (mortgage by demise):

- The **debtor retains ownership** of the property;
- The hypothec is a **charge** registered against the
  property;
- Only **on enforcement** does the lender potentially acquire
  the property.

### Registered in the Public Registry

- **All hypothecs** registered;
- **Priority** determined by registration order;
- **Searches** through the Public Registry reveal hypothecs;
- **Critical** to conveyancing — buyer takes subject to
  registered hypothecs.

### Created by passing before Royal Court

- **Conventional hypothec** — created by **passing a contract**
  before the Royal Court;
- **Greffier** records the hypothec;
- **Specific** Royal Court procedure;
- **Public** event.

## Types of hypothec

### Conventional hypothec

The standard commercial hypothec:

- **Agreed** between debtor and creditor;
- **Loan agreement** plus the contract passed before Court;
- **Most common** for residential mortgages, commercial
  property finance;
- **Subject to** specific Royal Court procedures.

### Judicial hypothec

A hypothec arising from a **court judgment**:

- Where a court orders payment of money;
- **Registered** as a hypothec against debtor's property;
- **Priority** dates from registration;
- **Enforcement** through standard hypothec procedures.

### Legal hypothec

Arising **by operation of law** in specific circumstances:

- **Spouse** hypothec for certain matrimonial obligations
  (historic; less common in modern law);
- **Specific** statutory hypothecs;
- **Workmen's** hypothec for unpaid labour in some cases.

## Priority

Priority of hypothecs is determined by:

- **Registration date** — first in time, first in priority;
- **Specific** legal hypothec priorities by statute;
- **Specific** workmen's privilege priorities.

## Enforcement — dégrèvement

The principal hypothec **enforcement remedy** is **dégrèvement**:

### What dégrèvement is

A **specific Royal Court process** for hypothec enforcement:

- **Public** procedure;
- **Multi-stage** process;
- **Strict** procedural requirements;
- **Can result in** property being **dégrevé** (stripped of
  hypothec) by sale, or vested in the senior creditor.

### Procedure

1. **Default** by debtor;
2. **Application** to Royal Court by senior creditor;
3. **Notice** to debtor and other creditors;
4. **Court process** with submissions;
5. **Adjudication** on priority and quantum;
6. **Sale** of property or vesting in senior creditor;
7. **Distribution** of proceeds per priority.

### Effects

- **Property sold free** of subordinate hypothecs (after
  satisfaction);
- **Specific** treatment of competing creditors;
- **Surplus** to debtor.

## Practical commercial considerations

For commercial property finance:

- **Hypothec** is the primary security;
- **Additional** security (personal guarantees, debenture
  over chattels) common;
- **Priority** searches essential;
- **Specific** Jersey advocate involvement essential.

For residential mortgages:

- **Hypothec** secures the borrower's primary residence;
- **Specific** consumer-protection rules (limited compared
  with UK);
- **Specific** dispute mechanisms.

## Comparison with English mortgage

| Feature | Jersey hypothec | English mortgage |
|---|---|---|
| Legal title transfer | No | (Historically yes; now charge by way of legal mortgage) |
| Procedure | Contract passing before Royal Court | Deed registered with HM Land Registry |
| Enforcement | Dégrèvement (court process) | Possession order plus sale (LPA 1925) |
| Documentation | Pass before Court | Mortgage deed |
| Registration | Public Registry (Royal Court) | HM Land Registry |
| Priority | Registration date | Priority of charges register |
