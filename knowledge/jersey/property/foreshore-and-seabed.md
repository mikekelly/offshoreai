# Foreshore, Seabed, and Coastal Rights in Jersey

## Foundational position

Jersey's foreshore and seabed are subject to a distinctive
customary-law and statutory regime:

- **The Crown** holds the seabed and foreshore in right of
  Jersey;
- **Public rights** of access historically preserved;
- **Specific** customary rights ("droit de varech",
  fishing, etc.);
- **Modern** statutory regulation supplements customary law.

## Definitions

- **Foreshore** — area between high-water mark and low-water
  mark (intertidal zone);
- **Seabed** — area below low-water mark;
- **Territorial seabed** — within Jersey's territorial
  waters;
- **Coastal land** — terrestrial land adjacent to the
  foreshore.

## Crown ownership

The Crown holds:

- **Foreshore** (intertidal);
- **Seabed** within territorial waters;
- **Specific** Jersey administration via Crown departments;
- **Historic** customary law preserved.

## Customary public rights

Jersey customary law preserves:

- **Public access** to foreshore for traditional uses;
- **Beach access** along customary routes;
- **Droit de varech** (right to collect seaweed);
- **Fishing** rights from foreshore;
- **Specific** Jersey customary uses.

## Statutory framework

Modern Jersey statutes:

- **Public Lands (Jersey) Law 1934** — management of public
  lands;
- **Coastal-protection** statutes;
- **Marine resources** regulation;
- **Specific** environmental statutes.

## Coastal land ownership

For terrestrial coastal land:

- **Private ownership** typical (subject to customary public
  rights);
- **Title** by passing in Royal Court;
- **Specific** Jersey rules on coastal boundaries;
- **High-water mark** as common boundary.

## High-water mark issues

The high-water mark is significant because:

- **Boundary** between private land and Crown foreshore;
- **Shifts** over time (erosion, accretion);
- **Specific** Jersey customary rules on accretion;
- **Title** considerations.

## Reclamation

Where land is reclaimed from the sea:

- **Title** to reclaimed land — specific Jersey rules;
- **Crown** may retain rights;
- **Specific** statutory provisions for major reclamation;
- **Specific** Jersey case law.

## Coastal-access rights

Public rights to access the coast:

- **Customary** access ways preserved;
- **Specific** Jersey case law on traditional routes;
- **Modern** statutory dedication of paths;
- **Specific** considerations for landowner-public balance.

## Mooring and anchorage

For boats:

- **Crown** consent typically required for permanent
  moorings;
- **Specific** Jersey harbour and marina rules;
- **Customary** rights for traditional fishing moorings;
- **Specific** statutory regulation.

## Marine licensing

Activities on / in the sea:

- **Marine licences** for specific activities;
- **Environmental** assessment requirements;
- **Specific** Jersey regulator;
- **EU-derived** standards applied in modified form.

## Coastal development

For development of coastal land:

- **Planning law** overlay;
- **Coastal-protection** statutory provisions;
- **Environmental** assessment;
- **Specific** Jersey rules on coastal building lines;
- **Customary** access preservation.

## Specific contexts

### Marinas

- **Specific** Jersey rules on marina development;
- **Seabed** rights from Crown;
- **Mooring** allocations.

### Tidal energy

- **Specific** Jersey rules on tidal-energy projects;
- **Seabed** licensing;
- **Environmental** considerations.

### Aquaculture

- **Specific** rules on shellfish farming, etc.;
- **Foreshore / seabed** use rights;
- **Specific** Jersey licensing.

### Coastal erosion

- **Statutory** sea-defences provisions;
- **Specific** Jersey case law on liability for erosion;
- **Specific** insurance considerations.

## Public rights vs private property

Tension between:

- **Customary public rights** of access / use;
- **Private property** rights of coastal owners;
- **Statutory** balance via planning / environmental law;
- **Specific** Jersey case law on resolution.

## Cross-border considerations

- **Specific** Jersey rules vs UK / France adjacent waters;
- **International** maritime law overlay;
- **Fishing** quotas and access;
- **Brexit** implications.

## Pitfalls

- **Assuming** UK / English coastal-law rules apply;
- **Missing** customary public rights;
- **Failing** to identify high-water-mark boundary;
- **Ignoring** Crown rights in seabed transactions;
- **Misunderstanding** accretion / erosion rules;
- **Missing** environmental / marine-licensing
  requirements.

## Practical recommendations

- **Title search** including coastal-boundary
  considerations;
- **Survey** of high-water-mark position;
- **Engage** Jersey advocates with coastal experience;
- **Address** customary rights expressly in transactions;
- **Specific** advice on marine licensing where relevant.
