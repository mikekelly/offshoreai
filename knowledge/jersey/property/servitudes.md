# Servitudes — Jersey Easements

## What a servitude is

A **servitude** is a **burden** on one parcel of land
("**fonds servant**") in favour of another parcel
("**fonds dominant**"):

- **Right** that runs with the land;
- **Binds** future owners of the burdened parcel;
- **Benefits** the dominant parcel;
- **Distinct from** personal contractual rights.

Similar to English **easements** but with **distinctive Jersey
customary law** rules.

## Types of servitude

### Right of way (chemin)

- **Vehicular** or **pedestrian**;
- **Specific** route prescribed;
- **Maintenance** obligations apportioned;
- **Specific** customary rules.

### Right of drainage

- **Surface water** drainage;
- **Sewage** drainage;
- **Specific** customary rules from agricultural Jersey;
- **Modernised** by planning law overlay.

### Right of light

- **Recognised** in Jersey;
- **Specific** customary rules;
- **Specific** statutory rules in some areas (planning).

### Right of support

- **Buildings** supporting each other;
- **Specific** Jersey customary rules;
- **Limited** modern application.

### Specific historic servitudes

- **Customary rights** preserved in particular areas;
- **Specific** historic burdens.

## Creation

### Express grant

- **Contract** creating the servitude;
- **Passed** before Royal Court typically;
- **Registered** in Public Registry;
- **Specific** servitude terms documented.

### Reservation

- **Seller** reserves servitude when selling part of larger
  parcel;
- **Documented** in the sale contract;
- **Specific** retention of rights.

### Implied servitudes

- **Specific** customary law implications;
- **Where** the use is essential to the property's use;
- **Specific** Jersey jurisprudence.

### Prescription

- **Long use** can establish a servitude:
- **Specific** prescription periods (typically **40 years**);
- **Customary** rules on **continuous, undisturbed use**;
- **Documented** evidence of use required.

## Extinguishment

A servitude can be **extinguished** by:

- **Release** by the dominant owner;
- **Merger** when one party becomes owner of both parcels;
- **Abandonment** (specific Jersey rules);
- **Statutory** displacement;
- **Court order** in specific circumstances.

## Specific Jersey nuances

### Customary servitudes

- **Specific** historic rights of way;
- **Agricultural** customary rights;
- **Coastal** access rights;
- **Specific** customary rights documented in Le Geyt and
  other sources.

### Public vs private

- **Public** rights of way separately governed (statute);
- **Private** servitudes governed by customary law plus
  Loi 1880;
- **Specific** treatment of historic public-private overlay.

## Enforcement

A servitude is enforceable through:

- **Royal Court** action by dominant owner against burdened
  owner;
- **Injunction** for interference;
- **Damages** for past interference;
- **Declaratory** relief on scope.

## Servitudes in conveyancing

In Jersey conveyancing:

- **Title** investigation reveals existing servitudes;
- **Public Registry** searches;
- **Specific** seller representations on servitudes;
- **Buyer** takes subject to existing servitudes;
- **Specific** investigations for boundary disputes.
