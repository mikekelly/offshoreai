# Contract Passing Before the Royal Court

## What "passing" means

In Jersey, **real-property contracts are passed before the
Royal Court** — a distinctive feature of Jersey conveyancing:

- The **buyer and seller** (or their lawyers) **appear**
  before the Royal Court;
- The **contract is read** or referenced;
- The **Greffier records** the transaction;
- The **transfer** is effected by this Court act;
- The **property changes hands** at that moment.

## Which Court division

The **Samedi Division** of the Royal Court is the typical
forum:

- Held **most weekdays**;
- **Property contracts** routinely passed;
- **Specific** procedural rules.

For specific complex transactions, **other divisions** may be
appropriate.

## Procedure

### Pre-passing

- **Drafting** the contract of sale by the parties' lawyers;
- **Title** investigation through Public Registry;
- **Searches** (planning, environmental, taxes);
- **Specific** Jersey conveyancing diligence;
- **Mortgage / hypothec** arrangements with lender.

### Passing day

- **Parties appear** at Royal Court (typically by lawyers
  acting as attorneys);
- **Contract read** or referenced in Court;
- **Greffier** signs and records;
- **Transfer** takes effect;
- **Possession** typically same day.

### Post-passing

- **Recording** in Public Registry;
- **Hypothec** registered if applicable;
- **Specific** post-completion administrative steps;
- **Final** accounting between parties.

## Why this procedure exists

The contract-passing procedure is a **Norman customary law**
inheritance:

- **Public** transfer of real property;
- **Royal Court** as repository of property records;
- **Certainty** about title transitions;
- **Visibility** of transactions (though specific terms not
  public);
- **Historic** importance preserving the system.

## Lawyers as attorneys

In practice:

- **Lawyers act as attorneys** for absent parties;
- **Powers of attorney** drafted for this purpose;
- **Specific** rules for who can pass for parties;
- **Specific** Royal Court protocols.

This allows transactions to be **completed even where parties
cannot attend personally** — common for non-Jersey-resident
buyers / sellers.

## Public visibility

The fact that a contract has **passed** is public:

- **Royal Court records** (Acts of Court);
- **Public Registry** entries;
- **Parties' identities** typically visible;
- **Specific transaction terms** (price) typically private.

This is **different from English** conveyancing, which is
largely private until completion (and even then, only the
buyer's identity and some terms appear on the Land Registry).

## Contract content

Jersey real-property contracts contain:

- **Parties** (buyer, seller, lender if applicable);
- **Property description** (with cadastral references);
- **Price** (typically expressed in £);
- **Hypothec** terms (if applicable);
- **Servitudes** (creating or referencing existing);
- **Specific** Jersey customary clauses;
- **Specific** statutory references.

## Specific transaction types

### Sale

- **Standard** passing procedure;
- **Title transfer** to buyer;
- **Hypothec** of buyer's lender registered.

### Hypothec only

- **Where** an existing owner takes out a loan secured by
  hypothec;
- **Contract** passed creating the hypothec;
- **No title transfer**.

### Gift / transfer for nominal value

- **Specific** Jersey customary rules;
- **Stamp duty** / land transfer fees considerations;
- **Specific** procedural variations.

### Inheritance

- **Customary inheritance** rules for real property;
- **Specific** procedures different from sale;
- **Probate** and customary-law overlay.

### Lease

- **Long leases** may be passed before Royal Court;
- **Short leases** typically not (contractual only);
- **Specific** thresholds.
