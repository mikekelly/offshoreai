# Security Interests (Jersey) Law 2012 — Personal Property Security

The **Security Interests (Jersey) Law 2012 (SIL)** modernised
Jersey's **personal property** (non-real-estate) security
regime, replacing the earlier piecemeal system.

## What SIL covers

Security interests over **personal (movable) property**:

- **Receivables** (debts owed to the debtor);
- **Investment securities** (shares, bonds, fund units);
- **Bank accounts**;
- **Intellectual property** rights;
- **Specific** other intangible property;
- **Goods** and **chattels** in some configurations.

SIL **does not cover** security over **real property** —
hypothecs under the Loi 1880 remain the regime for real
property.

## Why SIL was introduced

Pre-2012 Jersey law:

- Had **fragmented** personal-property security rules;
- **Specific** statutes for specific asset types;
- **Customary** law overlay;
- **Awkward** for modern finance practice.

SIL aligned Jersey with **modern international standards**:

- Similar to **US UCC Article 9** in approach;
- **Functional** approach to security (any transaction
  creating a security interest, regardless of label);
- **Registration-based** priority;
- **Clearer** enforcement procedures.

## Key concepts

### Security interest

A **security interest** is any interest in personal property
that secures **payment or performance** of an obligation:

- **Includes** pledges, hypothecs over movables, charges,
  assignments by way of security;
- **Functional** approach — looks at substance;
- **Includes** financial collateral arrangements.

### Attachment

A security interest **attaches** when:

- **Value given** by the secured party;
- **Debtor** has rights in the collateral;
- **Agreement** in writing satisfying SIL requirements.

Once attached, the security interest is enforceable between
the parties.

### Perfection

A security interest is **perfected** by:

- **Registration** in the SIR (Security Interests Register);
- Or **control** of the collateral (for some types — e.g.,
  bank accounts);
- Or **possession** of the collateral (for goods).

Perfection determines **priority** against third parties.

### Security Interests Register (SIR)

- **Public register** of security interests;
- **Searchable** by debtor name and collateral;
- **Online** filing system;
- **Priority** determined by registration order.

## Priority rules

Standard priority:

- **First in time** of perfection;
- **Specific exceptions** for:
  - **Purchase-money security interests** (PMSI) — priority
    if perfected within prescribed window;
  - **Control-based** perfection (for bank accounts,
    securities) — often higher priority than registration-
    based;
  - **Specific** statutory priorities.

## Enforcement

On debtor default:

- **Notice** to debtor and other secured parties;
- **Disposal** of collateral by sale, lease, or other commercial
  reasonable means;
- **Proceeds** applied to:
  - Costs of enforcement;
  - Senior secured creditors;
  - Junior secured creditors;
  - Surplus to debtor.

Or:

- **Acceptance** of collateral in satisfaction (with consent
  in some cases);
- **Specific** judicial procedures for contested matters.

## Specific applications

### Financial collateral

The **Security Interests (Jersey) Law 2012** plus specific
**Financial Collateral (Jersey) Regulations**:

- **Implementing** principles similar to EU Financial
  Collateral Directive;
- **Specific** rules for securities, cash collateral;
- **Close-out** netting recognition;
- **Critical** for derivatives, repo, securities lending.

### Receivables financing

- **Factoring** and similar receivables-purchase
  arrangements;
- **Specific** SIL treatment;
- **Notice** to underlying obligors typically required;
- **Specific** consumer-protection considerations.

### Asset-based lending

- **Inventory** and equipment finance;
- **Specific** PMSI rules;
- **Specific** asset-pool security.

### Trust assets as security

- **Trust property** can be subject to security in some
  configurations;
- **Trustee** authorisation typically required;
- **Specific** rules for security over trust beneficial
  interests.

## Interaction with insolvency

- **Perfected** security interests **survive** désastre;
- **Secured creditors** paid out of secured collateral
  ahead of unsecured creditors (subject to specific
  priorities);
- **Unperfected** interests may be **avoided** in insolvency;
- **Avoidance** for late perfection in some configurations.
