# Deeds of Arrangement on Jersey Real Property

## What this is

A **deed of arrangement** in Jersey property is a
**contractual instrument** between co-owners or adjacent
owners regulating:

- **Use** of property;
- **Repair** and maintenance;
- **Shared services**;
- **Boundary** matters;
- **Easements** / servitudes;
- **Specific** Jersey-customary arrangements.

It is **distinct from** the conveyance itself but typically
passed alongside or recorded with reference to the contrat.

## When used

Deeds of arrangement are common in:

- **Co-ownership** situations (siblings inheriting jointly);
- **Adjacent properties** sharing services / boundary
  features;
- **Sub-divisions** of larger properties;
- **Estate developments** (positive obligations workaround);
- **Specific** Jersey property contexts.

## Form

A deed of arrangement is typically:

- **Written** contract;
- **Passed** before the Royal Court if real-property
  interests created;
- **Registered** in the Public Registry where appropriate;
- **Specific** Jersey conveyancing-practice requirements.

## Contents

Standard provisions include:

- **Parties** and their interests;
- **Property** description;
- **Specific** arrangements (use, repair, services);
- **Cost** allocation;
- **Dispute resolution**;
- **Successors** binding;
- **Termination** / variation mechanisms.

## Running with the land

For obligations to bind successors:

- **Real-property** character required;
- **Passing** before Royal Court;
- **Registration** in Public Registry;
- **Specific** Jersey rules on which obligations run;
- **Personal** obligations bind only original parties.

## Positive obligations

Deeds of arrangement are often used for **positive
obligations** (requiring action):

- **Chain of indemnity** — each successor agrees to take on
  the obligation;
- **Estate-management company** holding obligations;
- **Specific** workaround for Jersey customary rule that
  positive covenants don't run with land;
- **Specific** Jersey practice.

## Specific use cases

### Sub-divided property

When dividing a larger property:

- **Service** access between units;
- **Shared** access ways;
- **Joint** maintenance of shared features;
- **Specific** Jersey rules on sub-division;
- **Royal Court** passing for each sub-division.

### Co-owned inherited property

- **Specific** customary inheritance rules (real property);
- **Légitime** considerations;
- **Specific** arrangements between heirs;
- **Specific** Jersey case law.

### Estate developments

- **Standard** estate deeds of arrangement;
- **Estate-management** company structure;
- **Service charges** and reserves;
- **Specific** Jersey practice.

### Mixed-use developments

- **Residential / commercial** mix;
- **Specific** Jersey rules on mixed-use;
- **Service-charge** apportionment;
- **Planning law** overlay.

## Dispute resolution

Deeds of arrangement typically include:

- **Mediation** clause;
- **Arbitration** option;
- **Royal Court** ultimate jurisdiction;
- **Specific** Jersey procedural considerations;
- **Costs** allocation.

## Variation and termination

- **By agreement** of all parties;
- **Specific** Jersey rules on variation of real-property
  interests;
- **Royal Court** approval for some variations;
- **Specific** provisions in original deed.

## Specific Jersey concepts

### Parage

Historical Jersey concept where multiple co-owners hold
land:

- **Specific** customary rules;
- **Replaced** in many contexts by modern deed of
  arrangement;
- **Specific** Jersey case law.

### Aînesse

Historical primogeniture rule:

- **Customary** preference for eldest in real-property
  succession;
- **Modern** statutory modifications;
- **Specific** Jersey rules.

### Customary access

- **Specific** Jersey rules on traditional access ways;
- **Servitude** issues;
- **Specific** customary-law preservation.

## Drafting recommendations

- **Identify** parties and interests precisely;
- **Specify** property by reference to title;
- **Distinguish** personal from real obligations;
- **Address** successors expressly;
- **Include** variation / termination mechanisms;
- **Include** dispute resolution;
- **Engage** Jersey advocates for drafting and passing.

## Pitfalls

- **Drafting** ambiguously between personal and real
  obligations;
- **Missing** Royal Court passing for real-property
  effects;
- **Failing** to register in Public Registry;
- **Inadequate** dispute-resolution mechanisms;
- **Ignoring** customary-law context;
- **Missing** estate-management company structure for
  positive obligations.
