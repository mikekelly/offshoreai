# Real Property Succession — Customary Rules

## Why this is distinctive

Jersey **real-property succession** is governed by
**customary law** rather than the modern Wills and Successions
Law 1993 (which covers movable property):

- **Historic** customary rules preserved;
- **Specific** Jersey doctrines;
- **Distinct** from movable-property succession;
- **Complex** for international families.

## Customary inheritance rules

### Principal heir (héritier)

In customary law:

- **Eldest son** historically the principal heir to real
  property;
- **Modern** position: equal division between all children
  is the **default** (per modern customary practice);
- **Specific** customary rules in particular contexts.

### Division between children

- **Equal** shares between children generally;
- **Per stirpes** for predeceased children;
- **Specific** customary treatment of family parishes.

### Spouse / surviving parent

- **Specific** customary rights;
- **Usufruit** (life-tenure-like right) in some
  configurations;
- **Specific** Jersey customary terms.

## Différence from movables

| Issue | Movable property (WSL) | Real property (customary) |
|---|---|---|
| Governing law | WSL 1993 | Norman customary law plus specific statutes |
| Légitime | Statutory protected share | Different customary rules |
| Intestacy | WSL Part 4 rules | Customary rules (equal division typically) |
| Will dispositions | Subject to légitime | Subject to customary rules |
| Children's share | Statutory légitime | Customary equal division |
| Surviving spouse | Statutory légitime | Usufruit (life-use) typically |

## Devolution

### On death intestate

Real property devolves:

- **By customary law** to qualifying heirs;
- **Equal** division typically among descendants;
- **Specific** customary roles for specific family members;
- **Royal Court** can adjudicate disputes.

### On death testate

If there is a will affecting real property:

- **Cannot fully override** customary rights to specific extent;
- **Specific** rules on disposing of real property by will;
- **Customary** restrictions on disinheriting key heirs;
- **Specific** Royal Court interpretation.

## Inheritance and Loi 1880

The **Loi 1880** does not displace customary inheritance:

- **Loi 1880** addresses ownership, transfer, security;
- **Customary law** addresses inheritance flow;
- **Subsequent statutes** modernise specific aspects.

## Practical implications

### Local Jersey families

- **Customary** rules apply largely undisturbed;
- **Family** typically aware of customary norms;
- **Royal Court** familiar with customary disputes.

### International families

- **Customary** rules can be **surprising** for international
  testators;
- **Foreign-domiciled** testator's foreign will may not
  effectively dispose of Jersey real property in expected way;
- **Specific** Jersey advice essential;
- **Lifetime planning** (e.g., trust holding) often used to
  avoid customary inheritance complications.

### Foreign property owners

- **Non-Jersey-domiciled** owners of Jersey real property are
  subject to Jersey customary inheritance rules for that
  property;
- **Foreign succession** law applies to other assets;
- **Coordinated** planning across jurisdictions essential.

## Modern reform considerations

The bifurcation between:

- **Modern statutory** succession for movables; and
- **Customary** succession for immovables

is **historically rooted** but increasingly criticised:

- **Complexity** for practitioners;
- **Unintended** outcomes for international families;
- **Specific** reform proposals from time to time;
- **No** major recent reform.
