# Jersey Lease Law

## What a lease is

A **lease (bail)** in Jersey is a **contract** by which an
**owner (lessor)** grants to a **tenant (lessee)** the right
to **use property** for a **defined period** in return for
**rent** or other consideration:

- **Real property** (most leases of real property);
- **Specific** rules for short, medium, and long-term leases;
- **Combination** of customary law and statute.

## Length-based categories

Jersey law distinguishes leases by **duration**:

### Short-term residential lease

- **Up to 9 years**;
- **Contractual** primarily;
- **Specific** Residential Tenancy (Jersey) Law 2011 rules
  for residential;
- **Not** required to pass before Royal Court.

### Medium-term lease

- **9 to 21 years** (broadly);
- **Specific** rules;
- **Specific** registration considerations.

### Long-term lease

- **Over 21 years**;
- **Closer** to ownership;
- **Specific** registration requirements;
- **May pass** before Royal Court;
- **Specific** tax / stamp duty considerations.

### Emphyteotic / building leases

- **Very long-term** (typically 99+ years);
- **Closer** to ownership;
- **Specific** rules.

## Residential leases

The **Residential Tenancy (Jersey) Law 2011** governs:

- **Statutory protections** for tenants;
- **Notice** periods for termination;
- **Rent** controls / dispute mechanisms;
- **Security** of tenure;
- **Conditions** of the premises;
- **Specific** Jersey rules.

## Commercial leases

- **More contractual freedom** than residential;
- **Specific** commercial considerations (rent reviews, break
  clauses, alteration clauses);
- **Specific** Jersey practices;
- **Specific** dispute mechanisms.

## Tenant rights and obligations

### Standard tenant rights

- **Quiet enjoyment** of the premises;
- **Repair** obligations on landlord (mostly);
- **Specific** statutory rights (residential);
- **Specific** notice provisions.

### Standard tenant obligations

- **Pay rent**;
- **Use premises** appropriately;
- **Repair** obligations (typically internal / fair wear);
- **Comply with** lease terms;
- **Specific** sub-letting rules.

## Landlord rights and obligations

### Standard landlord rights

- **Receive rent**;
- **Re-enter** for breach (subject to procedure);
- **Recover possession** at end of term;
- **Specific** rights under lease terms.

### Standard landlord obligations

- **Provide quiet enjoyment**;
- **Repair** structure and exterior (typically);
- **Comply** with statutory standards (residential);
- **Specific** landlord obligations.

## Termination

A lease can be terminated by:

- **Expiry** of the term;
- **Notice** as required by lease and statute;
- **Forfeiture** for breach (subject to procedure);
- **Surrender** by mutual agreement;
- **Specific** statutory termination rights;
- **Frustration** in extreme circumstances.

## Lease passing

Long leases may be **passed before Royal Court**:

- **Specific** rules;
- **Specific** thresholds;
- **Public Registry** entry.

Short leases are **purely contractual** and not passed.

## Specific Jersey concepts

### Bail à ferme

A **customary lease form**:

- **Agricultural** leases historically;
- **Specific** customary terms;
- **Modern** application limited.

### Bail à locataire (residential)

- **Modern residential** lease;
- **Subject to** Residential Tenancy Law 2011;
- **Standard** form.

### Bail commercial

- **Commercial** leases;
- **Greater** contractual freedom;
- **Specific** practices.

## Tax considerations

- **Stamp duty** / land transfer fees on creation in some
  cases;
- **Income tax** on rental income;
- **Specific** non-resident landlord rules;
- **GST** considerations on commercial rent.
