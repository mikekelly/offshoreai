# Voisinage — The Jersey Doctrine of Good Neighbourhood

## What voisinage is

**Voisinage** is a **Norman customary doctrine** governing
the relationship between neighbouring landowners. It
requires each owner to use their property in a way that does
not cause **unreasonable harm** to neighbours.

- **Customary origin** — preserved in Jersey, abandoned in
  most other modern legal systems;
- **Pothier-aligned** with broader civil-law neighbour-
  responsibility doctrines;
- **Modern application** by the Royal Court;
- **Distinct from** English nuisance, although overlapping
  in result.

## Sources

The doctrine derives from:

- **Norman customary law** (Le Geyt, Pothier);
- **Royal Court jurisprudence**;
- **Specific** Jersey case law developing the doctrine;
- **Persuasive** modern French neighbour-relations
  jurisprudence.

## The duty

A property owner must not:

- **Cause excessive harm** to neighbours;
- **Use property unreasonably**;
- **Exceed normal** inconveniences of neighbourhood;
- **Without justification** or proportionate purpose.

## Threshold of liability

Voisinage liability requires:

- **Harm** (objectively identifiable);
- **Caused by** defendant's use of property;
- **Exceeding** ordinary inconveniences of neighbourhood;
- **Without** adequate justification.

## Comparison with English nuisance

Similarities:

- **Both** address neighbour-to-neighbour interference;
- **Both** require unreasonable interference;
- **Both** offer injunctive and damages remedies.

Differences:

- **Voisinage** can apply without fault (objective);
- **No requirement** of "smell, smoke, noise" enumerated
  categories;
- **Broader** range of activities potentially captured;
- **Specific** Jersey customary-law foundation.

## Typical claims

Voisinage may apply to:

- **Excessive noise** from neighbouring property;
- **Smells** from livestock / activities;
- **Smoke / fumes** from neighbouring use;
- **Vibrations** from machinery / construction;
- **Surface water / drainage** issues;
- **Tree roots / overhanging branches**;
- **Light obstruction** (specific Jersey rules);
- **Specific** Jersey case law on each category.

## Defences

- **Reasonable use** of property;
- **Pre-existing** state (limited);
- **Consent / acquiescence** of claimant;
- **Statutory authority** for use;
- **Prescription** (limited).

## Remedies

The Royal Court may grant:

- **Injunction** restraining the activity;
- **Damages** for harm caused;
- **Specific performance** of preventative measures;
- **Specific** Jersey procedural requirements.

## Specific Jersey contexts

### Rural / agricultural

- **Livestock smells / noise** — specific Jersey rules;
- **Manure / slurry** spreading;
- **Agricultural** machinery noise;
- **Specific** case law.

### Construction

- **Construction noise / dust** typically temporary
  inconvenience;
- **Excessive / prolonged** may breach voisinage;
- **Specific** Jersey construction-site practice.

### Restaurants / commercial premises

- **Kitchen smells / fumes**;
- **Late-night noise**;
- **Refuse / pest** issues;
- **Specific** Jersey case law on commercial-residential
  interface.

### Apartments

- **Inter-unit** noise / smells;
- **Common parts** issues;
- **Specific** flying-freehold / share-transfer
  considerations;
- **Service-charge** mechanisms may interact.

## Relationship with planning / regulation

- **Planning permission** does not exclude voisinage;
- **Regulatory** compliance may be evidence of reasonable
  use;
- **Specific** Jersey rules on parallel regulatory and
  voisinage claims.

## Relationship with servitudes

- **Servitudes** (easements) may permit specific
  activities;
- **Voisinage** does not override valid servitudes;
- **Excessive use** of servitude may still breach
  voisinage.

## Procedure

Voisinage claims typically proceed by:

- **Initial correspondence**;
- **Mediation** often pursued;
- **Order of Justice** if litigation needed;
- **Royal Court** civil procedure;
- **Expert evidence** (acoustic, environmental, etc.);
- **Site visit** common;
- **Specific** Jersey procedural rules.

## Pitfalls

- **Assuming** English nuisance test applies wholesale;
- **Missing** customary-law foundation;
- **Failing** to characterise harm specifically;
- **Ignoring** statutory authority defences;
- **Underestimating** Royal Court costs;
- **Missing** mediation opportunities.

## Practical recommendations

- **Document** the interference (logs, photographs,
  recordings);
- **Engage** experts early (acoustic, environmental);
- **Correspondence trail** with neighbour;
- **Mediate** as first step where possible;
- **Engage** Jersey advocate familiar with voisinage.
