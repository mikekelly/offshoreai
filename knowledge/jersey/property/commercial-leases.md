# Commercial Leases in Jersey

## Framework

Commercial leases in Jersey are governed by:

- **Customary law** (Norman-derived lease principles);
- **Loi sur les contrats de bail** and related statutes;
- **Contract law** (Pothier-aligned interpretation);
- **Practice** of the Royal Court for passing of long leases.

## Categories by duration

Jersey lease law distinguishes:

- **Less than 9 years** — registered by lease document
  (paréage) without Royal Court passing;
- **9 years and over** — must be passed before the Royal
  Court (*contrat héréditaire*) and registered in the Public
  Registry;
- **Long leases** (typically 21+ years) — treated almost as
  freehold for many purposes;
- **Short-term commercial** — frequently 5- to 9-year terms
  to avoid passing.

## Royal Court passing

For leases of 9+ years:

- **Contract** drafted and approved by both advocates;
- **Passing** on a Friday afternoon at the Royal Court;
- **Two Jurats** sit to witness;
- **Registered** in the Public Registry;
- **Specific** Jersey conveyancing practice.

## Typical commercial-lease terms

### Rent and rent review

- **Initial rent** specified;
- **Rent review** clauses — typical 5-year cycles;
- **Open market** review most common;
- **Index-linked** (RPI / CPI) increasingly used;
- **Upward-only** common but not universal;
- **Specific** Jersey rent-review practice.

### Repair and maintenance

- **Tenant repair** typically for interior;
- **Landlord** typically responsible for structure (FRI
  leases shift this);
- **FRI** (full repairing and insuring) leases common for
  modern commercial premises;
- **Specific** Jersey case law on repair standards.

### Insurance

- **Landlord** typically insures the building;
- **Tenant** pays insurance rent (recharge);
- **Public liability** typically tenant's responsibility;
- **Specific** Jersey insurance-market considerations.

### Service charges

- **Multi-let** buildings — service-charge mechanisms;
- **Apportionment** rules;
- **Reserve funds** for major works;
- **Specific** Jersey service-charge practice.

### Alienation

- **Assignment** restrictions (consent of landlord, not
  unreasonably withheld);
- **Sub-letting** restrictions;
- **Change of use** restrictions;
- **Specific** Jersey rules on landlord consent.

### Break clauses

- **Tenant break** options (typical mid-term);
- **Landlord break** less common;
- **Conditions** for valid exercise;
- **Specific** Jersey case law on strict compliance.

### User clauses

- **Permitted use** specification;
- **Restriction** on change of use;
- **Planning** law overlay (CHWL / planning permission);
- **Specific** Jersey rules.

### Forfeiture

- **Re-entry** for non-payment or breach;
- **Royal Court** procedure for possession;
- **Specific** Jersey procedural requirements;
- **Relief from forfeiture** principles applied.

## Rent review mechanics

Standard open-market rent review:

- **Hypothetical lease** assumptions;
- **Assumed terms** (parity, length, repair);
- **Disregards** (tenant improvements, occupation);
- **Determination** by surveyor / arbitrator;
- **Specific** Jersey market evidence considerations.

## Indexation reviews

- **RPI / CPI** linked rent reviews;
- **Cap** and **collar** common;
- **Drafting** must specify index, base, frequency;
- **Specific** Jersey practice.

## Stamp duty and GST

- **Stamp duty** on long leases (specific rates per
  duration);
- **GST** generally not on rent for commercial property;
- **ISE** status considerations;
- **Specific** Jersey tax practice.

## Termination

Leases terminate by:

- **Expiry** of term;
- **Mutual surrender**;
- **Forfeiture** for breach;
- **Notice to quit** (specific Jersey rules);
- **Break clause** exercise;
- **Frustration** (rare in lease context);
- **Royal Court** order (rare).

## Holding over

After expiry, where tenant remains:

- **Tacit relocation** principles may apply;
- **New term** typically year-to-year;
- **Specific** Jersey rules on terminating implied tenancies;
- **Negotiation** of new lease usually pursued.

## Specific Jersey commercial-lease issues

### St Helier office market

- **Top-50** policy restrictions on tenant identity;
- **Class** of office space (Grade A vs others);
- **Specific** Jersey office-market dynamics.

### Retail premises

- **Specific** Jersey rules on shop leases;
- **Service-charge** practice in shopping centres;
- **Royal Square / King Street** prime retail.

### Industrial premises

- **Specific** environmental / planning considerations;
- **Long leases** common (50+ years for industrial);
- **Specific** rent-review and repair regimes.

### Hotel / hospitality

- **Specific** Jersey rules on licensed premises;
- **Long leases** common;
- **Specific** liquor-licensing overlay.

## Pitfalls

- **Missing** Royal Court passing for 9+ year leases;
- **Drafting** ambiguous rent-review or break clauses;
- **Failing** to consider planning / CHWL implications;
- **Ignoring** stamp duty on long leases;
- **Missing** Top-50 policy considerations for office
  premises;
- **Drafting** alienation clauses without "reasonably
  withheld" standard;
- **Inadequate** dilapidations provisions.

## Drafting recommendations

- **Specify** duration to suit Royal Court passing
  considerations;
- **Calibrate** rent review to market practice;
- **Specify** repair, insurance, service charges clearly;
- **Address** alienation, change of use, planning expressly;
- **Include** break clauses with clear conditions;
- **Engage** Jersey advocates from drafting through passing.
