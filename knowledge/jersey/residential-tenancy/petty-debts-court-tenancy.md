# Petty Debts Court — Tenancy Jurisdiction

## What it is

The **Petty Debts Court** is Jersey's small-claims and
lower-civil court. It has **exclusive original
jurisdiction** under the RTJL over residential tenancy
disputes — making it the principal forum for landlord-
tenant cases.

It also handles general civil claims under £30,000 in its
small-claims jurisdiction (subject to specific monetary
limits that change over time).

## Jurisdiction over tenancy matters

Under the RTJL, the Petty Debts Court hears:

- **Termination** of residential tenancy agreements;
- **Eviction** orders;
- **Money judgments** for arrears, damages, deposit
  disputes (subject to the Deposit Scheme route);
- **Specific performance** — orders requiring a landlord
  to repair;
- **Rent abatement** for uninhabitable premises;
- **Declarations** of rights under the agreement;
- **Stays of execution**;
- **Costs** orders.

It does **not** hear:

- **Rent-increase** disputes — Rent Tribunal (see
  [`rent-tribunal.md`](./rent-tribunal.md));
- **Discrimination** claims as such — Employment &
  Discrimination Tribunal (though it may grant tenancy-side
  relief in parallel);
- **Commercial-lease** disputes — Royal Court (see
  [`../property/commercial-leases.md`](../property/commercial-leases.md)).

## Procedure overview

- **Issuing**: file an application with the Court's
  registry; pay the fee;
- **Service**: on the other party in the proper form;
- **Directions**: short hearing to identify issues;
- **Substantive hearing**: usually one day for contested
  cases;
- **Judgment**: ex tempore or shortly reserved;
- **Orders**: termination, eviction, money, costs.

## Costs

- **Court fees** — modest, set by tariff;
- **Advocate / solicitor fees** — recoverable per Royal
  Court costs rules, scaled for Petty Debts Court;
- **Enforcement** — Viscount fees (substantial);
- **Costs against** the losing party are the default rule
  in tenancy cases.

## Self-representation

Tenants and landlords commonly represent themselves
("acting in person") in Petty Debts Court tenancy cases.
The court's procedures are intended to be accessible. For
contested or complex cases, engaging a Jersey advocate is
typically worthwhile — recoverable costs may exceed the
fees.

## Appeals

Appeals from the Petty Debts Court lie to the **Royal
Court**:

- **As of right** on a point of law within prescribed
  period (typically 28 days);
- **By leave** on matters of fact;
- **Not** automatically staying execution — separate stay
  application needed.
