# Residential Tenancy — Jersey

## What this section is about

Jersey residential tenancies — the legal relationship
between a landlord and a tenant of a self-contained home
let for value, for a term of nine years or less.

The principal statute is the **Residential Tenancy (Jersey)
Law 2011** (RTJL). It was significantly amended by the
**Residential Tenancy (Jersey) Amendment Law 2025**, which
took effect on **15 April 2026**. Any agent answering a 2026
tenancy question must apply the post-amendment regime to
new tenancies and the transitional rules to existing ones —
see [`2025-amendment-law.md`](./2025-amendment-law.md).

Disputes are heard by the **Petty Debts Court**, which has
exclusive original jurisdiction over residential tenancy
matters under the RTJL. Eviction orders are enforced by the
**Viscount**. A new **Rent Tribunal** (operating from 2026)
handles disputes over rent increases.

## Why this section exists

The Jersey corpus was originally built outward from
sophisticated finance personas (fund counsel, trust officer,
MLRO). That produced deep coverage of trusts, AML, and
regulatory law and **near-zero coverage of resident-facing
law**. The coverage audit at
[`../COVERAGE-AUDIT.md`](../COVERAGE-AUDIT.md) identified
the **Residential Tenancy (Jersey) Law 2011** as the #1
gap. This section is the first gap-fill — every other
top-20 gap follows the same approach.
