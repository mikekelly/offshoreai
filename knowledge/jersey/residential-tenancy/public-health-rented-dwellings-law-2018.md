# Public Health and Safety (Rented Dwellings) (Jersey) Law 2018

## What the law does

The **Public Health and Safety (Rented Dwellings) (Jersey)
Law 2018** establishes **public-law minimum standards**
for rented residential dwellings in Jersey:

- Sets **fitness standards** for rented accommodation;
- Empowers **Environmental Health** officers to inspect,
  serve **improvement notices**, and prosecute;
- Provides a **registration scheme** for landlords (in
  prescribed circumstances);
- Sits **alongside** the contractual RTJL 2011 framework
  but is a **separate public-law route**.

## Key distinctions

| Regime | Source | Enforcer | Remedy |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Contractual (RTJL 2011)** | The tenancy agreement + implied terms | Tenant via Petty Debts Court | Damages, specific performance, rent abatement, set-off in arrears claim |
| **Public-law (PHSRD 2018)** | The statutory minimum standards | Environmental Health | Improvement notice, prohibition notice, prosecution |

A tenant facing serious disrepair has both routes
available — and they often run in parallel.

## Minimum standards covered

The PHSRD 2018 and associated regulations set standards
on:

- **Damp and mould**;
- **Heating** adequacy;
- **Electrical safety**;
- **Gas safety** (where applicable);
- **Fire safety**;
- **Sanitation** (toilets, washing facilities);
- **Drainage**;
- **Hot water**;
- **Structural integrity**;
- **Ventilation** and lighting;
- **Pest control**;
- **Overall fitness for habitation**.

Many standards align with broadly-accepted UK Housing
Health and Safety Rating System (HHSRS) concepts but
with Jersey-specific implementation.

## Enforcement powers

### Inspection

Environmental Health officers can:

- **Inspect** rented dwellings (with notice, except in
  emergencies);
- **Test** for hazards (mould, damp, gas, electrical);
- **Interview** landlord and tenant;
- **Require documents** (gas safety certificate, electrical
  reports).

### Improvement notice

For deficiencies short of immediate danger:

- **Specifies** the deficiency;
- **Specifies** the works required;
- **Sets time** for compliance;
- **Failure** = criminal offence with fines.

### Prohibition notice

For serious / dangerous deficiencies:

- **Prohibits** continued occupation in the unfit state;
- **Effective immediately**;
- **Tenant** may need to be rehoused — landlord typically
  responsible.

### Prosecution

For serious breaches or notice non-compliance:

- **Magistrate's Court** typically;
- **Royal Court** for the most serious;
- **Fines** (substantial) and possible imprisonment.

## How a tenant uses the PHSRD 2018

For a tenant with an unresponsive landlord:

1. **Report in writing** to the landlord first;
2. **Allow reasonable time** for response;
3. **Report to Environmental Health** if no action — they
   investigate;
4. **Inspection** typically follows;
5. **Improvement notice** to the landlord if standards are
   not met;
6. **Compliance** typically follows (the notice is
   serious);
7. **Prosecution** if notice ignored.

This is a **free** route for tenants — no court fees, no
need for legal representation. Compare with the
contractual route via Petty Debts Court which is also
available but more procedurally demanding for the tenant.

## Landlord obligations

A landlord:

- Must **provide** a dwelling meeting the minimum
  standards;
- Must **maintain** the dwelling to those standards;
- Must **respond** to issues raised by tenants or
  Environmental Health;
- Must **comply with** any notice issued;
- **Cannot evict** in retaliation for an Environmental
  Health complaint (specific anti-retaliation provisions
  align with the RTJL).

## Penalties

For serious non-compliance:

- **Substantial fines** — recently strengthened;
- **Possible imprisonment** in the most serious cases;
- **Direct action** by the Department to remedy at
  landlord's expense;
- **Reputational** consequences (landlords are publicly
  identifiable).

## Interaction with the RTJL 2011

The two regimes interact:

- A **landlord's public-law breach** is generally also a
  **contractual breach** under the RTJL implied repair
  obligations;
- A tenant can use **either or both** routes;
- An **eviction notice served after an EH complaint** is
  scrutinised for retaliation;
- **Set-off** of substantiated repair costs against rent
  is available under the contractual route — see
  [`repairs-and-standards.md`](./repairs-and-standards.md).
