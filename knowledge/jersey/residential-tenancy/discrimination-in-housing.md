# Discrimination in Housing

## Why this matters in tenancies

The **Discrimination (Jersey) Law 2013** applies to
residential housing — letting, terms offered, and
termination. A landlord who refuses to let, lets on worse
terms, or terminates a tenancy on the basis of a protected
characteristic commits an actionable wrong, regardless of
RTJL compliance otherwise.

## Protected characteristics

The DL 2013 covers:

- **Race**;
- **Sex** (including gender reassignment, pregnancy /
  maternity);
- **Sexual orientation**;
- **Age**;
- **Disability**;
- **Religion or belief**.

Some characteristics were added by subsequent regulations;
see [`../discrimination/index.md`](../discrimination/index.md)
for the current list.

## Forms of discrimination

### Direct

Refusing to let, or terminating, **because of** a
protected characteristic. Examples:

- "No families with children" — direct on sex /
  maternity in some applications, and a barrier in
  practice;
- "No DSS" (Income Support recipients) — may amount to
  indirect discrimination on disability or other
  characteristic;
- "Only mature professionals" — age discrimination.

### Indirect

Applying a neutral rule that disproportionately
disadvantages a protected group:

- "Minimum income of £X" — may disadvantage women, older
  people, people with disabilities;
- "No pets" — may disadvantage disabled people with
  assistance animals (where the assistance animal is a
  reasonable adjustment).

### Harassment

Unwanted conduct related to a protected characteristic
that violates dignity or creates an intimidating
environment. Examples in housing:

- Repeated visits at unreasonable hours;
- Comments about the tenant's race, religion, sexuality;
- Differential treatment on repairs.

### Victimisation

Worse treatment because the tenant has made (or supported)
a discrimination complaint.

## In the lifecycle of a tenancy

### Letting

- **Advertisements** — must not exclude protected groups
  ("No DSS" is high-risk);
- **Selection** — cannot prefer based on protected
  characteristics;
- **Terms offered** — must be the same regardless of
  protected characteristics.

### During the tenancy

- **Repairs** must be done equally for all tenants;
- **Treatment** by landlord / agent must not differ on
  protected grounds;
- **Reasonable adjustments** for disabled tenants —
  including, in some cases, modifications to the
  property (specific Jersey rules).

### Termination

- **Cannot** terminate on a protected ground;
- A no-fault 12-month notice that follows discrimination
  may be the **discriminatory act**, not the no-fault
  ground stated;
- Tribunal / court will look at the timing and substance.

## Remedies

A tenant who establishes housing discrimination may
obtain:

- **Declaration** of unlawful discrimination;
- **Damages** — including for injury to feelings;
- **Injunction** restraining further discrimination;
- **Variation** of the tenancy terms to remove
  discriminatory effect.

The forum is typically the **Employment & Discrimination
Tribunal** for the discrimination claim itself, with the
Petty Debts Court handling any tenancy-side relief.

## Specific overlay with RTJL

Where a landlord's RTJL-compliant action (e.g. notice of
termination on a justified ground) is in substance
discriminatory:

- The **DL 2013** route succeeds even where the RTJL
  procedure was followed;
- The Tribunal may grant damages or other relief;
- The Petty Debts Court may refuse to make the eviction
  order on the same facts.

## Practical recommendations

For landlords:

- **Train** any letting agent on protected
  characteristics;
- **Avoid** discriminatory advertising language;
- **Document** non-discriminatory reasons for selection
  and termination decisions;
- **Treat all tenants equally** on repairs, access,
  enforcement of rules;
- **Make reasonable adjustments** for disabled tenants.

For tenants:

- **Document** suspected discrimination — dates, words,
  comparison evidence;
- **Use the discrimination route** alongside RTJL defences;
- **Engage** Citizens Advice Jersey or JACS for support;
- **Time limits** — see DL 2013 for the relevant tribunal
  time limit.
