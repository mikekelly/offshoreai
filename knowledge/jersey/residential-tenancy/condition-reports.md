# Condition Reports — The Mandatory Regime

## What a condition report is

A **condition report** is a document recording the
**physical condition and state of repair** of a residential
unit at the start of a tenancy. It is the principal
evidential document for end-of-tenancy deposit disputes
and any claim by the landlord that the tenant caused
damage.

## Statutory basis

- **RTJL 2011** — mandates a condition report at
  commencement;
- **Residential Tenancy (Condition Reports) (Jersey) Order
  2014** — prescribes form, content, timing;
- **2026 Amendment Order** — minor procedural updates.

## Who completes it

The **landlord** (or agent) prepares the condition report
**at or before** commencement of the tenancy. The tenant
should:

- **Inspect** the property and the report;
- **Agree** the report (signing it) or note disagreements
  in writing;
- **Retain** a copy.

If the tenant disagrees with parts of the report, those
disagreements should be set out in writing within 7 days
of receipt. Silence beyond 7 days is generally treated as
acceptance of the report as written.

## What it must cover

The report typically covers:

- **Each room** — walls, floors, ceilings, windows, doors,
  fittings;
- **Kitchen** — appliances, units, countertops, sinks;
- **Bathroom** — sanitary ware, tiles, sealants;
- **Utilities** — meters, heating, electrics;
- **Furniture and contents** (if furnished) — inventory
  with condition notes;
- **Garden and outdoor areas** (if applicable);
- **Photographs** — increasingly standard and effectively
  necessary for serious deposit disputes.

## End-of-tenancy comparison

At the end of the tenancy, the landlord must:

- Confirm within **7 days of vacation** whether the unit
  has been vacated in **substantially the same or better
  condition** than at commencement (allowing for fair
  wear and tear);
- **Identify** any damage with reference to the original
  condition report;
- **Quantify** any proposed deduction from the deposit.

## Fair wear and tear

The standard is **substantially the same or better
condition**, with **allowance for fair wear and tear**.
Fair wear and tear includes:

- **Normal occupancy** marks (scuffs on paintwork);
- **Aging** of carpets and fittings;
- **Settlement** cracks;
- **General** patina from reasonable use.

It does **not** include:

- **Holes** in walls;
- **Burns** or stains from misuse;
- **Broken** fittings from misuse;
- **Pet damage** (where pets were not authorised);
- **Cleaning failures** at the end of the term.

## Penalties for non-compliance

- **Failure to complete** a condition report at all —
  fine up to **£10,000**;
- **Failure to provide a copy** to the tenant — offence;
- **Failure to issue end-of-tenancy comparison within 7
  days** — deposit deductions challengeable.

## Practical recommendations

For landlords:

- **Use a standard template** — many letting agents provide
  one;
- **Include dated, time-stamped photographs** for every
  room;
- **Walk through** with the tenant where possible;
- **Sign and countersign** the report;
- **Repeat** at end of tenancy with the same level of
  detail.

For tenants:

- **Inspect carefully** at commencement and don't sign a
  report you disagree with;
- **Take your own photographs** at start and end;
- **Document** anything that develops during the tenancy
  with dated photographs;
- **Compare** end-of-tenancy report to start-of-tenancy
  report and challenge specifically.
