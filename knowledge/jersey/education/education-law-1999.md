# Education (Jersey) Law 1999 — Overview

## What the law does

The **Education (Jersey) Law 1999** (EJL 1999) is the
principal statute governing schooling in Jersey:

- Sets the **compulsory school age**;
- Imposes a **duty** on the Minister to provide education;
- Establishes **state schools** and regulates **private
  schools**;
- Provides for **special educational needs (SEN)**
  provision;
- Regulates **school attendance** and consequences for
  non-attendance;
- Provides for **exclusions** and appeals;
- Establishes **school governance** arrangements.

## Compulsory school age

A child is of compulsory school age:

- **From** the first day of the school term in which the
  child's **fifth birthday** falls;
- **Until** **30 June** of the school year in which the
  child attains the age of **16 years**.

Parents have a **statutory duty** to ensure that a child
of compulsory school age receives full-time education
appropriate to age, ability, and aptitude — either at a
school or "otherwise" (home education, subject to
satisfaction of the Minister).

## Duty to provide education

The Minister must ensure that:

- **Full-time education** is available for every child of
  compulsory school age;
- The education is **appropriate** to age, ability, and
  aptitude;
- Special educational needs are met;
- Schools comply with prescribed standards.

## School attendance

Parents must ensure regular attendance. The Department
has powers to:

- **Investigate** non-attendance;
- **Serve notices** requiring attendance;
- **Prosecute** parents for persistent unjustified
  non-attendance;
- **Apply** to the Family Division for orders in serious
  cases (potentially overlapping with welfare /
  child-protection routes under the Children (Jersey)
  Law 2002).

Fines and other sanctions are available for serious
non-compliance.

## Exclusion

A pupil may be excluded:

- **Fixed-period** exclusion — short-term;
- **Permanent** exclusion — for serious or repeated
  breaches of school discipline.

Parents have rights to:

- **Be informed** of the reasons;
- **Make representations**;
- **Appeal** to a panel / committee;
- **Appeal** further to specific statutory bodies.

Permanent exclusion is a last resort and is closely
scrutinised on appeal.

## Special educational needs (SEN)

The EJL 1999 (and subsequent amendments) provide for SEN
provision:

- **Identification** of children with SEN;
- **Statementing** / formal record of needs;
- **Educational provision** appropriate to the SEN;
- **Specialist** schools / units for some categories;
- **Parental** involvement in decisions;
- **Appeal** rights against decisions.

The framework aligns broadly with similar UK SEN regimes
but with Jersey-specific provisions.

## Home education

Parents who wish to educate at home:

- **Must notify** the Minister;
- **Education must be** suitable to age, ability, and
  aptitude;
- **Monitoring** by the Department in some cases;
- **Specific** procedural requirements.

## School types

- **States primary schools**;
- **States secondary schools**;
- **Specialist** schools / units (SEN);
- **Private** schools — registered and regulated;
- **Selective entry** schools (specific arrangements
  for the grammar-school tradition).

## Discrimination overlay

Schools are subject to the **Discrimination (Jersey) Law
2013** — race, sex, sexual orientation, age, disability,
religion or belief. SEN exclusion that in reality reflects
disability discrimination may be unlawful.

## Children Law 2002 overlay

Decisions affecting children's welfare engage the
**Children (Jersey) Law 2002** principles:

- **Welfare paramountcy**;
- **Specific issue orders** for school-choice disputes
  between parents;
- **Care orders** in serious child-protection situations
  arising from school context.

## Practical recommendations

For parents:

- **Engage** with the school early on attendance or
  behavioural concerns;
- **Seek SEN identification** if your child needs it;
- **Use** the appeal route for exclusion / SEN
  decisions;
- **Specific** legal support available via Citizens
  Advice Jersey and specialist advocates.

For schools:

- **Document** decisions carefully (especially
  exclusions);
- **Consult** parents at appropriate stages;
- **Comply** with SEN procedures;
- **Avoid** discrimination — DL 2013 overlay applies.
