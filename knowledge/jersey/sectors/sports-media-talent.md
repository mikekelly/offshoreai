# Sector — Sports and Media Talent

## What this sector covers

Structuring for **high-earning sports professionals**
(footballers, golfers, F1 drivers, tennis players, boxers,
emerging-sport stars) and **media talent** (actors, musicians,
presenters, influencers, podcasters). These clients typically
have:

- **Concentrated, time-limited income** from active career
  earnings (prize money, salary, image rights, sponsorship,
  appearance fees);
- **Multi-jurisdictional source-of-income** profile (tax
  arises in each performance jurisdiction);
- **Career-arc planning** — the high-income period is
  finite; structures must accommodate post-career income
  fall.

## Why a Jersey structure

For this sector, common drivers:

1. **Career-arc wealth-preservation** — Jersey trusts as
   long-term wealth vehicles spanning active-career through
   retirement;
2. **Family-governance** — for talent with young families and
   significant wealth, multi-generational planning;
3. **Image rights / IP ownership** — separating personal IP
   from earnings income, with related licensing arrangements;
4. **Concentration management** — diversification structures
   funded by peak-earning years;
5. **Tax-residence transition** — many talent clients are
   nomadic across multiple tax-residences over a career;
   structures need to anticipate residency changes;
6. **Forced-heirship defence** for talent from forced-
   heirship-jurisdiction backgrounds.

## Load-bearing considerations

### Image rights

For sports / media talent, **image rights** can be a
substantial separate income stream from performance income —
sponsorship, endorsement, licensing of name, likeness, and
playing-image. Structuring image rights typically uses:

- A **Jersey-incorporated image-rights company** holding the
  IP and licensing it back to commercial counterparties;
- **Jersey trust** above the image-rights company for
  long-term ownership.

Note: [**Guernsey** is the only jurisdiction in the world
with a statutory image-rights register](../../guernsey/image-rights-register.md);
some talent structuring uses the Guernsey register alongside
Jersey-side wealth holdings.

### Multi-jurisdictional source-of-income

Performance income may arise in dozens of jurisdictions in a
single year (a tennis player playing tournaments in 20
countries, for instance). Each performance-state has its own
withholding rules. The Jersey structure does **not** typically
solve performance-state withholding; it provides the
**ongoing wealth vehicle** that holds net earnings after
source-state tax.

### Endorsement / sponsorship income

Often paid into a Jersey-incorporated commercial entity that
contracts with the talent (rather than to the talent
personally). Structuring choices interact with home-country
tax rules on personal-services-company arrangements.

## Sector-specific risks

- **Career-ending injury / early retirement** — wealth-
  structure design must contemplate sharp income-drop
  scenarios;
- **Personal-services-company anti-avoidance** in home
  jurisdictions (UK IR35, similar rules elsewhere) may
  challenge the Jersey commercial entity if it lacks
  substance;
- **Reputation management** — talent clients have
  asymmetric reputational risk; the structure should
  withstand public scrutiny;
- **Tax disputes** — high-profile talent clients are
  audit-attractive in source jurisdictions; structures
  should be defensible on substance;
- **Endorsement-contract obligations** — sponsors may
  require performance through the talent personally (not
  via a corporate intermediary) for some contracts.

## Reporting overlay

- **CRS** — Jersey reports financial-account information to
  the talent's tax-residence jurisdiction(s);
- **Performance-state withholding** — typically operates at
  source and is the source state's revenue authority's
  position;
- **UK Image Rights specific** — historic UK HMRC challenges
  to image-rights structures; specific UK tax advice
  essential for UK-resident or UK-touching talent.
