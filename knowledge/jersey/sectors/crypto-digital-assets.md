# Sector — Crypto and Digital Assets

## What this sector covers

Structuring around **crypto-assets, tokens, NFTs, DeFi
positions, and tokenised securities** held by individuals or
managed through funds. Includes both **passive holding** (the
client owns crypto) and **active management** (the client
runs a crypto-focused fund or investment business).

## Why a Jersey structure

For this sector, common drivers:

1. **Investment fund vehicle** — Jersey-licensed VASP and
   fund-services regime can accommodate tokenised funds and
   crypto-strategy funds;
2. **Succession and family-governance** — concentrated crypto
   wealth (often illiquid private-key-held) needs structuring
   for next-generation transfer;
3. **Tokenisation of traditional assets** — the
   [Jersey tokenisation surface](../../frontier/tokenisation-jersey-2026.md)
   is a live frontier topic;
4. **Custody and security** — Jersey-regulated custody
   providers offer institutional-grade key management;
5. **Reporting position** — clarity ahead of CARF
   implementation (see [`../international/carf.md`](../international/carf.md)).

## Load-bearing rules

### JFSC VASP regime

Virtual Asset Service Providers (VASPs) are within the JFSC
registration perimeter under the
[FSL 1998](../financial-regulation/financial-services-jersey-law-1998.md)
and supervised under the AML/CFT framework. Activities in
scope typically include:

- Exchange between virtual assets and fiat;
- Exchange between one or more forms of virtual asset;
- Transfer of virtual assets;
- Safekeeping / administration of virtual assets;
- Participation in / provision of financial services
  related to virtual-asset offerings.

The JFSC's view is that VASPs are **inherently high-risk**
under FATF and JFSC framing and EDD is the default — see
[`../aml-cft/amlh-risk-assessment.md`](../aml-cft/amlh-risk-assessment.md).

### CARF (Crypto-Asset Reporting Framework)

The OECD's CARF — the crypto-asset reporting equivalent of
CRS — comes into force on 1 January 2026 with first reports
in 2027. Jersey has committed to implementation. See
[`../international/carf.md`](../international/carf.md).

This is a **near-term operational reality** for any structure
holding crypto-assets — reporting visibility increases
materially.

### Tokenisation

The Jersey legal framework accommodates tokenised securities
and tokenised fund interests. Specific Jersey-incorporated
tokenisation vehicles are increasingly used by Web3-adjacent
clients. See the frontier file
[`../../frontier/tokenisation-jersey-2026.md`](../../frontier/tokenisation-jersey-2026.md).

## Sector-specific risks

- **Source-of-wealth verification** — crypto wealth is often
  difficult to source-document; SoW evidentiary standard
  under [EDD](../aml-cft/amlh-edd.md) requires
  reconstruction.
- **Custody / private-key risk** — operational risk
  materially different from traditional assets; structuring
  must align with custody arrangements.
- **Sanctions exposure** — DeFi protocols and crypto
  counterparties may be sanctioned (e.g. Tornado Cash);
  structures need active sanctions screening.
- **Volatility** — valuation for trust / company accounting
  purposes is fact-sensitive.
- **CARF reporting** — emerging; the structure must
  anticipate reporting obligations.

## Reporting overlay

- **CARF** from 1 January 2026 (first reports 2027) — see
  [`../international/carf.md`](../international/carf.md);
- **CRS** for any fiat / traditional-asset component of the
  structure;
- **FATCA** for US-person investors;
- **JFSC VASP supervisory reporting** for VASPs.
