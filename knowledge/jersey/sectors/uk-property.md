# Sector — UK Real Estate

## What this sector covers

Holding **UK residential or commercial property** through
Jersey-incorporated vehicles or via Jersey trusts. The
historic structuring rationale was UK IHT efficiency for
non-UK-domiciled owners using corporate envelopes; **multiple
waves of UK reform** (2013, 2015, 2017, and the 2024–2025
non-dom reforms) have tightened this materially. The sector
remains relevant but with materially different mechanics
than pre-2017.

## Why a Jersey structure now

Common drivers in the current regime:

1. **Commercial real estate** — where Jersey corporate
   wrappers remain useful for non-UK-tax-resident owners;
2. **Trust-held UK property** for specific succession or
   asset-protection use cases;
3. **Co-investment vehicles** for UK-property strategies
   (especially institutional / fund structures);
4. **Pre-purchase planning** where ownership profile is
   uncertain.

The "Jersey company holds UK residential property for IHT
shelter" pattern that drove much of the historic market is
**largely obsolete** post-2017 changes (the deemed
domicile / non-dom reforms brought non-UK-resident corporate
owners of UK residential property within UK IHT scope).
Wealth-management RMs should be careful not to recommend
historic patterns without current UK tax advice.

## Load-bearing UK rules to know

For any UK-property structuring:

- **ATED (Annual Tax on Enveloped Dwellings)** — annual
  charge on UK residential property valued >£500,000 held
  by a "non-natural person" (company, partnership with
  corporate partner, certain CIVs);
- **SDLT 15% rate** — Stamp Duty Land Tax penal rate on
  acquisition of UK residential property by non-natural
  persons above thresholds;
- **NRCGT (Non-Resident CGT)** — UK CGT on disposal of UK
  property by non-residents (residential since 2015,
  commercial since 2019);
- **UK IHT on UK-residential-property-holding-entities** —
  Jersey companies holding UK residential property are
  effectively within UK IHT scope post-2017;
- **Post-2025 non-dom reforms** — see [`../use-cases/client-cohorts/uk-non-dom.md`](../use-cases/client-cohorts/uk-non-dom.md)
  for the broader UK reform context.

The Jersey side does not solve these UK-side issues; it
provides a **wrapper**, but the UK tax position must be
understood under current rules.

## Sector-specific risks

- **Historic structures** that worked under pre-2017 rules
  may now generate UK tax liability — review legacy
  structures against current rules before refreshing.
- **Acquisition timing** — structuring decisions must
  precede the acquisition; post-acquisition restructuring
  is taxable.
- **Beneficial-ownership disclosure** — UK property is
  subject to the UK Register of Overseas Entities; Jersey-
  incorporated owners must register and disclose beneficial
  ownership.

## Reporting overlay

- **UK Register of Overseas Entities (ROE)** — Jersey
  companies holding UK property are within scope;
- **ATED return** annually where applicable;
- **NRCGT return** on disposal;
- **CRS** on the Jersey side.
