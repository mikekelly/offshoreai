# Sector — Family Business Succession

## What this sector covers

Structuring for **multi-generational ownership and governance
of family-owned operating businesses** — a use case where the
business itself is held in a Jersey trust or foundation, with
the family acting as beneficiaries and the operating business
managed under family-board oversight.

This is **the deepest structuring conversation** in private
wealth — typically involving multiple family branches across
multiple jurisdictions, an active operating business, and a
multi-decade horizon.

## Why a Jersey structure

For family-business succession, common drivers:

1. **Continuity of ownership** across generations without
   triggering succession events on each death;
2. **Family-governance discipline** — formal structures for
   family-board decision-making, branch representation,
   dispute resolution;
3. **Forced-heirship defence** for families from forced-
   heirship jurisdictions where shares of the business would
   otherwise be split per local rules;
4. **Asset protection** for the business from individual
   family-member exposure (divorce, bankruptcy, litigation);
5. **Phased transition** — gradually moving operational
   control from senior to junior generations under structured
   trust / foundation governance;
6. **Tax-residence flexibility** for the family across
   generations.

## Two principal structural patterns

### Trust over holding company

The most common pattern:

- **Operating business** held in an operating company (in
  whatever jurisdiction makes operational sense);
- **Operating company shares** held by a **Jersey holding
  company**;
- **Holding company shares** held by a **Jersey
  discretionary trust**;
- **Family** are beneficiaries of the trust;
- **Trust governance** includes a **protector** /
  **protector council** with family representation;
- **Article 9A reserved powers** retained by senior-
  generation family members (often the founder) until
  transition;
- **Letter of wishes** sets out family expectations for
  trustee decisions.

### Foundation alternative

For families with a **civil-law-flavour preference** (some
European, Latin American, Middle Eastern families), the
[Jersey foundation](../foundations/index.md) provides similar
governance:

- **Council members** (with prescribed Jersey qualified
  member);
- **Guardian** with oversight role;
- **Beneficiaries** (or charter-defined purpose for purpose
  foundations);
- **Founder reserved powers** equivalent to trust reserved
  powers.

### Private Trust Company (PTC) hybrid

For larger families wanting **family control of the trustee**:

- A **Jersey-incorporated private trust company** acts as
  trustee of the family trust;
- **PTC board** includes family members alongside
  professional directors;
- PTC is **exempt from full TCB registration** under
  prescribed conditions (single trust / family-of-related-
  trusts, no public marketing).

## Common structural questions

| Question | Discussion |
|---|---|
| Trust or foundation? | Trust is dominant; foundation suits civil-law-flavour families or where beneficiary rights need to be more constrained than discretionary-trust default. |
| When to transition? | Often phased — settle shares, retain Article 9A reserved powers, gradually release control over 5–20 years. Specific to family circumstances. |
| Who's on the protector council? | Typically family branches + independent professional + lawyer. Mechanism for disputes is critical. |
| What about non-family senior management? | Usually outside the trust structure — they have employment / option arrangements with the operating company, not beneficial interests in the trust. |
| What if the business is sold? | Trust holds the proceeds; structure continues with realised cash / replacement assets. The structure outlives the business. |

## Sector-specific risks

- **Family-disagreement breakdown** — the structure must
  accommodate disagreement between family branches without
  forcing fracture; this is where governance mechanisms earn
  their keep.
- **Operating-business performance dependence** — if the
  business fails, the trust holds depreciated / worthless
  shares. Diversification structures sit alongside the family-
  business trust.
- **Tax-residence migration of family members** — over
  generations, family branches move; structuring must
  accommodate UK / US / EU / GCC / Asian residence outcomes.
- **Information rights of beneficiaries** — across
  generations, beneficiary information demands can grow;
  Jersey law has developed mechanisms (see
  [`../trusts/article-29-disclosure.md`](../trusts/article-29-disclosure.md))
  but specific drafting matters.
- **Royal Court applications** — major decisions
  (distribution policy changes, dispute resolution, sale of
  the business) often go to the Royal Court for blessing
  under Article 51; the structure should anticipate this.

## Reporting overlay

- **CRS** — family-member account information reported to
  resident states.
- **Beneficial-ownership registers** — Jersey holding company
  is in scope of the FSDI Law 2020.
- **Operating-business jurisdiction** — usually has its own
  reporting; the Jersey side does not displace it.
