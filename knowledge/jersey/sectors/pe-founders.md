# Sector — PE Founders / Carry / Pre-IPO Equity

## What this sector covers

Three related asset profiles:

1. **PE / VC founders' carry interests** — performance fees
   from fund management;
2. **Founder equity** — concentrated equity stakes in
   private operating companies, pre-listing;
3. **Pre-IPO equity** — late-stage private holdings where
   liquidity is approaching.

All three share characteristics that drive Jersey-structuring
conversations: **concentrated value**, **timing-sensitive
realisation**, **family / succession exposure**, and (for
carry specifically) **shifting home-jurisdiction tax rules**.

## Why a Jersey structure

For this sector, common drivers:

1. **Pre-realisation family planning** — transferring future
   upside to a Jersey trust before a liquidity event
   crystallises a much larger taxable position;
2. **Tax-residence transition** — structuring around an
   anticipated change in the founder's tax residence
   (e.g. UK departure or arrival);
3. **Concentration management** — diversification structures
   funded by realised gains, held in Jersey wrappers;
4. **UK carry reform navigation** — the 2026 UK reforms (see
   [`../../frontier/uk-carried-interest-reform-2026.md`](../../frontier/uk-carried-interest-reform-2026.md))
   have changed the calculus for UK-resident carry recipients,
   surfacing structuring opportunities for non-UK-resident
   recipients;
5. **Forced-heirship defence** for founders with family in
   forced-heirship jurisdictions.

## Common timing question — when to settle

The **single most consequential structuring decision** for
this sector is **timing**. Settling shares into a trust
before they appreciate materially has a different home-
country-tax profile than settling after appreciation.
Specific home-jurisdiction advice (UK / US / EU) is essential.

The corpus does not provide that advice — but the agent can
flag the **timing-criticality** to the user and direct them
to specific tax advice before settling.

## Sector-specific risks

- **Concentration risk** — pre-realisation values are
  illiquid and the structuring decisions are made under
  imperfect information;
- **Vesting and forfeiture** — carry and founder equity
  often have vesting / good-leaver / bad-leaver mechanics
  that intersect awkwardly with trust structuring;
- **Co-founder / GP-partner relationships** — a founder's
  carry / equity restructuring may require partner / GP
  consent under partnership / shareholder agreements;
- **Pre-IPO regulatory disclosure** — listed-company
  pre-IPO disclosure of beneficial ownership may require
  the trust structure to be disclosed in the prospectus
  / admission document;
- **UK carry reform 2026** — material change in the
  position for UK-resident carry recipients; see
  [`../../frontier/uk-carried-interest-reform-2026.md`](../../frontier/uk-carried-interest-reform-2026.md).

## Reporting and information-exchange overlay

- **CRS** — Jersey trust / company reports beneficial-owner
  account information.
- **Home-country reporting** — typically substantial (UK,
  US, EU) — the structuring is for tax-efficient planning
  within reported visibility.
- **AIM / Main Market disclosure** at IPO — beneficial
  ownership including via Jersey trust may be reportable.
