# Sector — Middle East Property

## What this sector covers

Holding real estate in **GCC jurisdictions** — UAE (Dubai
and Abu Dhabi predominantly), Saudi Arabia (post-Vision 2030
foreign ownership reforms), Qatar, Bahrain, Oman, Kuwait —
through Jersey structures, primarily for **succession and
asset-protection** purposes.

This sector overlaps with the
[UAE expat cohort](../use-cases/client-cohorts/uae-expat.md)
and the [GCC national cohort](../use-cases/client-cohorts/gcc-national.md);
the cohort files give the home-jurisdiction context and this
file gives the asset-class-specific structuring view.

## Why a Jersey structure

For Middle East property, common drivers:

1. **Sharia / forced-heirship defence** — the primary
   motivation. GCC inheritance applies Sharia by default to
   Muslim individuals; non-Jersey wrappers may not
   adequately disapply Sharia at the validity-and-effects
   level. [Article 9 firewall](../trusts/firewall.md) is the
   load-bearing tool.
2. **Family-business succession** — GCC families often have
   commercial real estate held in family-business structures
   alongside residential property; the integrated
   structuring conversation typically extends to
   [family business succession](./family-business-succession.md).
3. **Cross-jurisdictional asset-portfolio** — for clients
   with property in multiple GCC states plus UK / EU
   property, the Jersey wrapper provides a consistent
   governance vehicle.
4. **DIFC / ADGM common-law overlay** — UAE non-Muslim
   expats may register wills in DIFC / ADGM jurisdictions
   that apply common-law succession; the Jersey trust can
   sit alongside the DIFC/ADGM will for full coverage.

## GCC-specific load-bearing facts

- **Foreign-ownership restrictions** vary by GCC state and
  property type. Dubai has the most liberal regime (most
  freehold areas open to foreign ownership); Saudi Arabia
  has progressively opened post-Vision 2030. Verify against
  the specific GCC jurisdiction for current rules.
- **Local nominee structures** are common in some GCC states
  where foreign ownership is restricted; the Jersey wrapper
  may sit above the local nominee.
- **Sharia inheritance** applies by default to Muslim owners
  on death.
- **Wasiyyah** (one-third testamentary freedom) limits what
  can be done by will under Sharia.

## Sector-specific risks

- **Local ownership rules** — verifying that the Jersey
  structure can validly hold the property under the GCC
  state's foreign-ownership regime; in some jurisdictions
  this requires local sub-structures or nominee arrangements.
- **Local tax** — some GCC states apply property-transaction
  taxes / municipal fees / annual charges; the Jersey
  structure does not avoid these.
- **Enforcement uncertainty** — if a Sharia court were to
  rule against the Jersey trust's claim to the property, the
  Article 9 firewall protects the Jersey-side validity but
  GCC-side enforcement is harder. In practice the structure
  is robust where the property is held via a company the
  trust controls (the trust does not directly appear in the
  GCC land register).

## Reporting overlay

- **CRS** — GCC-Jersey reporting where applicable.
- **GCC beneficial-ownership** registers (Dubai, Abu Dhabi,
  others) — increasing transparency.
