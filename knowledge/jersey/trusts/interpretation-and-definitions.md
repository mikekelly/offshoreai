# Article 1 — Interpretation and Definitions

Article 1 of the **[Trusts (Jersey) Law 1984][tjl]** is the
interpretation section. It defines the terms used throughout the
Law and contains a critical disclaimer of codification.

## Disclaimer of codification

Article 1 expressly states that the Law "shall not be construed as a
codification of laws regarding trusts." ([Article 1, Trusts (Jersey)
Law 1984][tjl])

This matters: where the Law is silent, Jersey common law and case law
fill the gap. The Royal Court has repeatedly relied on this clause to
import equitable doctrines (e.g., the constructive trust at large, the
trustee's duty of confidentiality, knowing receipt and dishonest
assistance) into Jersey trust practice without needing to point to
statute.

## Key defined terms

The verbatim definitions (per the consolidated text) include:

| Term | Definition |
|---|---|
| **beneficiary** | "a person entitled to benefit under a trust or in whose favour a discretion to distribute property held on trust may be exercised" |
| **breach of trust** | "a breach of any duty imposed on a trustee by this Law or by the terms of the trust" |
| **corporate trustee** | "a trustee which is a corporation" |
| **court** | "the Inferior Number of the Royal Court" |
| **enforcer** | the person appointed under Article 13 to enforce a non-charitable purpose trust |
| **foreign trust** | "a trust whose proper law is the law of some jurisdiction other than Jersey" |
| **Jersey trust** | "a trust whose proper law is the law of Jersey" |
| **minor** | a person who has not reached legal capacity (Jersey or domicile) |
| **personal representative** | executor, administrator, or principal heir |
| **professional trustee** | a trustee registered with the JFSC for trust company business |
| **settlor** | "a person who provides trust property or makes a testamentary disposition on trust or to a trust" |
| **trust property** | property held within a trust at any given time |
| **unit trust** | a trust providing investment-participation facilities for beneficiaries |

(Source: [Article 1, Trusts (Jersey) Law 1984][tjl])

## Points to flag

- **"Court" = Inferior Number of the Royal Court.** Routine trust
  applications under Article 51 (directions) and Articles 47/47B–H
  (variation / mistake) are *not* before a single judge in the
  Samedi Division — they are before the Inferior Number formation
  of the Royal Court, i.e., the Bailiff or Deputy Bailiff with Jurats.
  In practice the Inferior Number sits as required for trust
  business; see [`../legal-system/royal-court.md`](../legal-system/royal-court.md).
- **"Professional trustee" = registered with the JFSC.** The label
  is a regulatory marker. A "private trust company" (PTC) operating
  under an exemption from the JFSC trust-company-business
  registration is **not** a professional trustee within Article 1.
- **"Settlor" includes testamentary settlors.** The definition is
  wide enough to capture a person making a will-trust as well as a
  lifetime settlor.
- **"Beneficiary" includes a discretionary object.** The wording
  "in whose favour a discretion to distribute property held on trust
  may be exercised" deliberately captures discretionary objects, who
  have no fixed entitlement but are within the supervisory
  jurisdiction of the Royal Court (see
  [`beneficiary-rights.md`](beneficiary-rights.md)).
- **"Breach of trust" includes breach of Law-imposed duties.** This
  is the foundation for, e.g., Article 21 (general trustee duties)
  to ground breach-of-trust liability under Article 30 even where
  the trust instrument is silent on the duty.

## What Article 1 does **not** define

- **"Trust"** is not defined in Article 1; its existence is
  established by [Article 2](existence-of-a-trust.md) rather than by
  definitional fiat.
- **"Protector"** is not defined; the protector role is recognised
  in practice and in the case law but not in Article 1.
- **"Letter of wishes"** is not defined; its treatment flows from
  case law on confidentiality (see
  [`beneficiary-rights.md`](beneficiary-rights.md)).
