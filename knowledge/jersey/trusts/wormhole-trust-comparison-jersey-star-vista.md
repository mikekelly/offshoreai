# Jersey trust vs Cayman STAR vs BVI VISTA — compiled comparison

> **What this is.** A compiled, citation-complete distillation of the
> classic cross-border trust-vehicle choice. Derived from the three
> regimes' canonical files; `draft` status — verify the cited primary
> files before relying on it. For the wider matrix see
> [`../../CROSS-JURISDICTIONAL-MAP.md`](../../CROSS-JURISDICTIONAL-MAP.md).

## The three regimes at a glance

| | Jersey trust | Cayman STAR | BVI VISTA |
|---|---|---|---|
| Core form | Classic **discretionary trust** under the **Trusts (Jersey) Law 1984** | **Purpose** (or mixed person/purpose) trust under the STAR regime | Ordinary trust with the **VISTA share-ownership** overlay |
| Defining feature | **Article 9 firewall** + **Article 9A reserved powers** | **Enforcer**-based; beneficiaries have no standing to enforce — the enforcer does | Designed to **hold company shares**; disapplies the trustee's prudent-investment/intervention duty |
| Best for | Maximum forced-heirship defence; broad settlor control with deep jurisprudence | Orphan SPV / no-beneficiary purpose holding | Family-business succession with a deliberately **disengaged** trustee |

## When to use which

- **Jersey** — the default for **wealth protection and forced-heirship
  defence**. Article 9 directs the Royal Court to apply Jersey law only and
  to **disregard foreign forced-heirship rules**; Article 9(4) makes a
  foreign judgment inconsistent with Article 9 unenforceable in Jersey.
  Article 9A lets the settlor **reserve broad powers** (investment,
  appointment, revocation) without invalidating the trust. Choose Jersey
  when the driver is *defending the structure against foreign claims* and
  you want settled case law.
  ([`./firewall.md`](./firewall.md), [`./reserved-powers.md`](./reserved-powers.md))

- **Cayman STAR** — choose when the structure has **no beneficiaries to
  serve**, or a *purpose* dominates: an orphan SPV, a holding vehicle for a
  PTC, a philanthropic or commercial purpose. Enforcement runs through an
  **enforcer**, not beneficiaries.
  ([`../../cayman/star-trusts.md`](../../cayman/star-trusts.md))

- **BVI VISTA** — choose when the trust's job is to **hold the shares of a
  family/operating company** and the family wants the trustee to **stay out
  of the company's management**. VISTA removes the trustee's duty to monitor
  and intervene, so the directors run the business.
  ([`../../bvi/vista-trusts.md`](../../bvi/vista-trusts.md))

## The decision in one line

Forced-heirship / control / jurisprudence → **Jersey**; no-beneficiary or
purpose-led → **Cayman STAR**; hold-company-shares with a hands-off trustee
→ **BVI VISTA**.
