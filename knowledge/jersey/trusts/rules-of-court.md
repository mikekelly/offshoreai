# Article 60 — Rules of Court

Article 60 of the **[Trusts (Jersey) Law 1984][tjl]** is a
**rule-making power**. It confirms that the Royal Court's
rule-making authority under the **Royal Court (Jersey) Law 1948**
includes the power to make **procedural rules for trust matters**.

## What Article 60 says

The Royal Court (Jersey) Law 1948 authority to make rules **includes
procedural rules for trust matters**.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][tjl].)

## What rules exist

The **Royal Court Rules** (made under the 1948 Law and subsequent
authority) contain detailed procedural rules for:

- **Representations** in trust matters (the Article 51 directions
  vehicle);
- **service** on parties (including representation of minor and
  unborn beneficiaries via guardian ad litem);
- **costs** in friendly proceedings;
- **anonymisation** of family trust matters;
- specific orders and notices required for trust applications.

The current Rules should be checked against the latest version
published by the Judicial Greffe; for a procedural point in a live
matter the practitioner should refer to the current Rules
directly.
