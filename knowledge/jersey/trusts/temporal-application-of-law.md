# Article 58 — Application of This Law (Temporal)

Article 58 of the **[Trusts (Jersey) Law 1984][tjl]** is the
**temporal-scope** provision. The Law applies to trusts created
**before or after** its commencement date.

## What Article 58 says

The statute applies to trusts created **before or after** its
commencement date.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][tjl].)

## Why this matters

A statute of broad application is normally presumed to apply only
to matters arising after its commencement. Article 58 displaces
that presumption for the Trusts Law — the substantive provisions
catch:

- **trusts created on or after the commencement date** (1984);
- **trusts created before 1984** that continue in existence on or
  after the commencement date;
- **subsequent amendments** to the Law (which generally apply to
  all trusts subject to the Law, subject to specific savings).

## Pre-1984 trusts

A Jersey trust created before 1984 is **brought within** the modern
statutory framework on commencement of the Law, subject to:

- **savings provisions** in [Article 59](./savings.md) preserving
  pre-existing rights and powers;
- **specific transitional rules** in subsequent amending Laws —
  notably the perpetuity-abolition transition for trusts created
  before / after the relevant 2006 date (see
  [`duration.md`](./duration.md));
- the **mistake regime** in Articles 47B–H, introduced by the 2013
  amendment, which applies prospectively from its commencement.

## Subsequent amendments

The Trusts Law has been amended multiple times since 1984. Each
amendment has its own application provisions:

- **Trusts (Amendment No. 4) (Jersey) Law 2006** — introduced
  Article 9A reserved powers, abolished perpetuities for new
  trusts. Pre-2006 trusts retain pre-amendment rules in some
  respects.
- **Trusts (Amendment No. 6) (Jersey) Law 2013** — introduced
  Articles 47B–H mistake regime. Applies to applications brought
  after commencement.
- Subsequent amendments — apply per their own commencement
  provisions.

A trustee should always check the **commencement and saving
provisions** of any amendment cited in a particular issue.

## Pitfalls

- **Assuming modern rules apply retroactively.** Article 58's
  general retroactive application has specific limits in later
  amendments. Don't assume the 2006 perpetuity abolition applies
  to a pre-2006 trust without checking.
- **Old-deed interpretation.** A pre-1984 Jersey trust deed may
  use language and concepts pre-dating the modern statute.
  Interpretation requires reference to both the deed's own context
  and the modern statutory overlay.
