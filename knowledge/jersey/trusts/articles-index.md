# Trusts (Jersey) Law 1984 — Articles Index

## What this is

A complete, navigable index of **every Article** in the
**[Trusts (Jersey) Law 1984][tjl]** — Jersey's principal trust
statute. For each Article there is a one-sentence description of what
the Article does and a link to the deep concept file. An agent
looking for "what does Article X say" should land here and click
through.

The Trusts Law has **5 Parts** and **61 Articles** (plus two
repealed Articles). Of the 61, the 47B–47J mistake regime is
consolidated into a single concept file because the Articles work
as one statutory mechanism; every other Article has its own file.

## How to use this index

- **Looking for an Article by number?** Use Ctrl-F.
- **Looking for a concept?** The descriptions are written so a cold
  reader can identify whether an Article is relevant. If the
  description matches what you're looking for, click through.
- **Looking by theme?** Go to the [section index](index.md), which
  groups the same Articles by **conceptual theme** rather than
  statutory order.
- **Need orientation on what a Jersey trust *is*?** Read [the
  section index](index.md) first.

## Naming and citation

The Law's short title is "**Trusts (Jersey) Law 1984**" (per
Article 61). In this corpus we abbreviate as "**TJL**" or "the
Trusts Law" after the first mention; we cite Articles as
"**Article X TJL**" or, where clear from context, "Article X".

The consolidated text on [jerseylaw.je][tjl] is the canonical
reference. The Law has been amended many times — most importantly by
**Amendment No. 4 of 2006** (reserved powers and abolition of
perpetuities) and **Amendment No. 6 of 2013** (statutory mistake
regime).

## Part 1 — General

Foundational provisions that apply to **all** trusts (Jersey-proper-
law and foreign), defining what a trust is, when Jersey recognises
one, what its proper law is, and when the Royal Court has
jurisdiction over it.

| Article | What it does | Deep file |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Interpretation.** Defines all key terms in the Law — "trustee", "beneficiary", "settlor", "Jersey trust", "foreign trust", "professional trustee" (= regulated TCB), "court" (= Inferior Number of the Royal Court). Includes the critical disclaimer that the Law is **not a codification**. | [interpretation-and-definitions.md](interpretation-and-definitions.md) |
| 2 | **Existence test for a trust.** A trust exists when a trustee holds property "not in their own right" for the benefit of beneficiaries or a purpose. The legal/beneficial-ownership split is the defining feature. | [existence-of-a-trust.md](existence-of-a-trust.md) |
| 3 | **Default recognition.** Jersey recognises a trust as "valid and enforceable" subject to the Law's other provisions. The one-sentence anchor for the whole regime. | [recognition-of-a-trust.md](recognition-of-a-trust.md) |
| 4 | **Proper law of a trust.** Tells you whether a trust is a "Jersey trust" (engaging the firewall and Part 2 substantive rules) or a foreign trust. Tested by express choice → implied choice → closest connection. | [proper-law.md](proper-law.md) |
| 5 | **Royal Court jurisdiction.** When the Court has jurisdiction over a trust — Jersey trusts always; foreign trusts with Jersey trustee, Jersey assets, or Jersey administration. | [court-jurisdiction-over-trusts.md](court-jurisdiction-over-trusts.md) |

## Part 2 — Provisions applicable only to a Jersey trust

The substantive heart of the Law. Applies only where the proper law
is Jersey law. Covers creation and validity of the trust, the
**firewall**, **reserved powers**, **purpose trusts**, trusteeship
mechanics, trustee duties and powers, breach-of-trust liability,
court intervention, and the modern statutory mistake regime.
