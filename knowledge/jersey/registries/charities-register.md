# Jersey Charities Register

The **Jersey Charities Register** is maintained by the **Jersey
Charity Commissioner** under the **Charities (Jersey) Law 2014**
(in force in part since 2018).

## Categories of registration

The Law provides for three categories:

- **Registered Charity** — full registration with public-register
  status;
- **Restricted Section** — for charities whose details are not
  to be made public (e.g. for security or operational reasons);
- **General Section** — the main category, fully public.

## What is recorded

For each registered charity:

- **Name** of the charity;
- **Charitable purposes**;
- **Registered office / contact details**;
- **Governing document** (often charity-by-laws or trust deed);
- **Trustees / council members** (varies by structure);
- **Annual financial information** (income, expenditure, total
  assets);
- **Activities** report.

## Charity definition

The 2014 Law defines a charity by reference to:

- **Charitable purposes** — prescribed list (relief of poverty,
  advancement of education, religion, health, citizenship,
  arts, amateur sport, etc.);
- **Public benefit** — the activities must benefit the public
  generally or a sufficient section of the public;
- **Restriction** on private benefit.

The definition broadly follows English charity-law principles
with Jersey-specific calibration.

## Why registration matters

- **Charity-tax-status** (for the limited Jersey tax benefits
  available);
- **Donor confidence** that the entity is independently
  supervised;
- **Public accountability** through registered information;
- **Access to charity-specific benefits** (e.g. certain exemptions
  in legislation).
