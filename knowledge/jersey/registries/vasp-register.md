# Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP) Register — Jersey

Jersey maintains a **register of Virtual Asset Service Providers
(VASPs)** as part of its **FATF-compliant** approach to the crypto
sector.

## What a VASP is

A **VASP** is a person who, as a business, conducts one or more
of the following for or on behalf of another person:

- **Exchange** between virtual assets and fiat currencies;
- **Exchange** between virtual assets;
- **Transfer** of virtual assets;
- **Safekeeping or administration** of virtual assets;
- **Participation** in financial services related to an issuer's
  offer or sale of virtual assets.

This mirrors the **FATF VASP definition** in Recommendation 15.

## Registration requirement

VASPs operating in or from within Jersey must:

- **Register** with the JFSC;
- Comply with **AML / CFT** obligations equivalent to other
  Schedule 2 supervised businesses;
- Meet **fit-and-proper** standards for principal and key persons;
- **Pay** registration and ongoing fees;
- **Report** to the JFSC under prescribed schedules.

## AML / CFT specifics

VASPs are subject to:

- **Travel rule** — requirement to share originator / beneficiary
  information for virtual-asset transfers above prescribed
  thresholds;
- **CDD** on virtual-asset customers (often requiring blockchain-
  analysis tools);
- **Suspicious activity reporting** to the FIU;
- **Sanctions screening** in the virtual-asset context.

## Why Jersey

Jersey's approach to virtual assets:

- **Cautious but pragmatic** — registering legitimate VASPs while
  excluding bad actors;
- **AML-first** — strong CDD and reporting expectations;
- **Coordinated with JFSC's broader supervision** of regulated
  businesses;
- **Aligned with FATF** standards to maintain Jersey's
  reputation.
