# Jersey Land Registry and Public Registry

Jersey maintains **two interlinked registers** for **immovable
property** (land and buildings):

- **The Public Registry** — the **register of contracts**
  affecting immovable property (passations);
- **The Land Registry** — for **freehold and qualifying interests**
  recorded under modernised land-title legislation.

The administrative system is run by the **Judicial Greffe** and
related offices.

## What is registered

For immovable property:

- **Conveyances** (passations) of freehold property;
- **Hypothecs** — Jersey-law security over immovable property
  (functionally similar to mortgages but with civil-law
  mechanics);
- **Leases** of qualifying terms;
- **Easements** and other recorded interests;
- **Judicial conveyances** following court orders.

## Mechanics

Jersey's **customary law** of immovable property has distinctive
features:

- Heritable property contracts are **passed before the Royal
  Court** (the *passation*) — a public ceremony of contract
  execution before judges (Jurats);
- **Hypothecs** are registered as a separate act;
- **Freehold title** is established by **chain of registered
  contracts** rather than by central register-of-title (though
  modernised land-registration introduces a register-of-title
  element for qualifying property).

## Why this matters

- Companies and trusts holding **Jersey immovable property** have
  Jersey-law-specific registration obligations;
- **Foreign-law mortgages** do not work for Jersey land — Jersey
  hypothecs are required;
- **Stamp duty / LTT** is assessed in connection with land-
  transaction registration.
