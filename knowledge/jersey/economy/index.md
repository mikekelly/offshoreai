# Jersey Economy — Overview

## What this is

A high-level overview of Jersey's economy as of 2026, with
the GDP partition by sector and short summaries of each
significant sector. Designed to give an agent or reader a
defensible answer to *"how is Jersey's economy structured?"*
without defaulting to *"it's just finance"*.

For the **deep sector history**, see
[`../history/`](../history/). The finance sector has the
fullest historical narrative at
[`../history/finance/`](../history/finance/). Other sectors
have only this overview page so far — see the bottom of
this file for the expansion roadmap.

## Headline data

Per **Statistics Jersey — Jersey's Economy 2024** (October
2025):

- **Total Gross Value Added (GVA)** ~ £5.5-6 billion;
- **Financial services as a share of national income**:
  **38.5%** in 2024;
- **Population** ~ 103,000;
- **GVA per capita** among the highest in the world;
- **Government revenue** dominated by personal income tax,
  GST, corporate tax (Zero-Ten + MCIT 2025), and property
  duties.

## GDP partition by sector (2024, approximate)

The approximate share of Gross Value Added by sector, per
Statistics Jersey:

| Sector | Share of GVA | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Financial services** | ~38-40% | Banking, funds, trust, insurance — the dominant sector |
| **Real estate activities** | ~8-10% | High weight driven by the wealth-management story and Jersey property values |
| **Public administration, education, health** | ~9-11% | States of Jersey, schools, hospitals, social care |
| **Wholesale and retail trade** | ~6-8% | Imports / retail; long-arc story through changing duty regime |
| **Construction** | ~5-7% | Tied to property and population dynamics |
| **Information and communication (digital)** | ~4-5% | The Digital Jersey / FinTech / tokenisation growth story |
| **Hospitality / tourism** | ~3-4% | Long-term decline from 1960s-80s peak |
| **Professional, scientific, technical activities** | ~3-4% | Law firms, accounting, consulting (some overlap with finance) |
| **Transport and storage** | ~2-3% | Ports, airport, freight |
| **Agriculture, forestry, fishing** | ~1-2% | Iconic but small (Jersey Royal potatoes, dairy) |
| **Manufacturing** | <1% | Very small |
| **Other services** | balance | Personal services, arts, recreation |

Sector definitions follow the Statistics Jersey GVA
classification (broadly aligned with the UK ONS Standard
Industrial Classification framework). Exact splits move
year-on-year; the table reflects 2024 approximate weight.

## Sector summaries

### Financial services (~38-40%)

The dominant sector by some distance. Covers banking, fund
services, trust and corporate services, insurance, fund
management, and adjacent professional services. Has its own
deep history at [`../history/finance/`](../history/finance/)
covering the last 25 years of regulatory and industry
evolution. Frontier developments tracked at
[`../frontier/jersey-finance-industry-2026.md`](../frontier/jersey-finance-industry-2026.md).

The sector's economic weight has been **broadly stable** at
the 38-45% range for many years. Its growth trajectory is
covered in detail in
[`../history/finance/trajectory.md`](../history/finance/trajectory.md).

### Real estate activities (~8-10%)

Second-largest sector by GVA. Includes ownership and
operation of real estate, real-estate agency services, and
property-related professional services. The high weight
reflects both:

- **High Jersey property values** (housing-affordability
  is a persistent political issue);
- **Real-estate intermediation** for the international
  wealth-management client base;
- **Property holding structures** (jersey TopCos, family-
  office vehicles, investment-property SPVs).

Real estate intersects with financial services through the
wealth-management product line and with the population /
housing policy debate.

**Historical narrative not yet built** in this corpus — see
the expansion roadmap below.

### Public administration, education, health (~9-11%)

Government of Jersey, States Assembly, the judicial system,
schools, hospitals, social care, regulatory bodies (JFSC,
Comptroller of Taxes, etc.). Funded primarily by taxation.

A stable, large employer. Its long-term direction is shaped
by the **demographic ageing** of the Jersey population and
the **health-and-social-care funding** debate.

### Wholesale and retail trade (~6-8%)

Imports, distribution, retail. Long arc through:

- **Changing import-duty regime** (GST introduced 2008 at
  3%; raised to 5% in 2011);
- **Online-retail disruption** (post-COVID, accelerated);
- **High-street decline** in St Helier;
- **Specialist / luxury retail** holding up better than
  general merchandise.

**Historical narrative not yet built** — see roadmap.

### Construction (~5-7%)

Building and civil-engineering work. Tied closely to the
property cycle, the housing-affordability debate, and
population policy. Major projects include the **new
hospital** (long-running political question) and **St
Helier regeneration**. Significant employer of skilled
trades.

**Historical narrative not yet built** — see roadmap.

### Information and communication (digital) (~4-5%)

The digital / FinTech / technology sector. Anchored by:

- **Digital Jersey** (industry body founded 2013);
- **Innovation Council** (launched 2025 — see
  [`../financial-regulation/innovation-council.md`](../financial-regulation/innovation-council.md));
- **VASP registrations** (14 by Oct 2025);
- **JFSC tokenisation guidance** (multiple updates 2024-26).

Intersects with financial services via tokenisation,
FinTech, and the Innovation Council. Has a growth narrative
that may warrant its own history file in time.

See [`../digital-assets/index.md`](../digital-assets/index.md)
for the doctrinal layer.

### Hospitality / tourism (~3-4%)

Hotels, restaurants, accommodation. **Long-term decline
narrative** from a 1960s-80s peak when Jersey was a
significant package-holiday and short-break destination.
Cheap European air travel and changing holiday preferences
have shrunk the sector materially. Some recovery in higher-
end / experiential tourism.

### Professional, scientific, technical activities (~3-4%)

Law, accounting, management consulting, advertising,
scientific R&D. Significant overlap with financial services
(many law firms and accountancy firms are primarily
financial-services-facing). Partly captured under the
financial-services umbrella in informal classifications.

### Transport and storage (~2-3%)

Ports (Jersey Harbours), airport (Jersey Airport), freight,
inter-island and France ferries, logistics. Critical
infrastructure for an island economy.

### Agriculture, forestry, fishing (~1-2%)

**Iconic but small.** Jersey Royal potatoes, Jersey-breed
dairy, some niche horticulture. The marine sector (fishing,
oyster farming) is meaningful regionally. Politically and
culturally important well beyond its economic weight.

Long arc:

- **Mid-20th-century**: dominant industry;
- **Post-1960s**: progressively displaced by financial
  services;
- **2020s**: small but iconic; the **post-Brexit fishing
  arrangements** with France were a meaningful political
  flashpoint.

### Manufacturing (<1%)

Very small. Jersey is not a manufacturing economy.

## What this means for the agent

For an agent answering questions about **Jersey's economy**:

- **Finance is dominant but not the whole story** — at
  ~40%, it is dominant; at ~60%, the rest is meaningful;
- **Real estate** is the second-largest sector and has a
  story worth telling that intersects with finance;
- **Digital** is the growth vertical, articulated through
  the [Innovation Council](../financial-regulation/innovation-council.md)
  and the [JFSC 2026-2030 Strategy](../financial-regulation/jfsc-strategy-2026-2030.md);
- **Tourism, agriculture, manufacturing** are small but
  culturally / historically significant;
- **Public sector and construction** are economically
  significant and tied to the **population-policy debate**
  (Jersey's small-island demographic strategy).
