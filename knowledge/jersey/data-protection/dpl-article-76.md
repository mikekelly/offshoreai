# DPL 2018 Article 76 — Administrative Fines

Article 76 empowers the JOIC to impose **administrative
fines** for breaches of the DPL.

## Fine bands

Two tiers of maximum fines:

### Lower tier

For breaches of administrative / procedural obligations:

- **£10 million** or **2% of global turnover** (higher);
- Covers Articles relating to records, DPIA, controller /
  processor obligations, breach notification, etc.

### Upper tier

For breaches of fundamental principles:

- **£20 million** or **4% of global turnover** (higher);
- Covers data-protection principles, lawful bases,
  data-subject rights, international transfers, fundamental
  breaches.

## Factors for fine determination

JOIC must consider:

- **Nature, gravity and duration** of infringement;
- **Number** of data subjects affected;
- **Damage** suffered;
- **Intentional or negligent** character;
- **Mitigation** measures taken;
- **Previous infringements**;
- **Cooperation** with the JOIC;
- **Categories** of personal data;
- **Manner** in which the infringement came to the JOIC's
  attention;
- **Compliance** with previous orders;
- **Adherence** to codes / certification.

## Procedure

JOIC must:

- **Investigate** before imposing fine;
- **Notice of intention** to the controller / processor;
- **Right to make representations**;
- **Reasoned decision**;
- **Right of appeal** to the Royal Court (Article 79).

## Enforcement

- **Fine** payable to the JOIC / Treasurer;
- **Recoverable** as civil debt;
- **Specific** enforcement procedures.

## Practical issues

For Jersey-regulated entities:

- **JOIC** has not imposed top-band fines to date;
- **Cooperation** typically reduces fines;
- **AML / regulatory** parallel enforcement possible;
- **Specific** Jersey context — smaller than EU jurisdictions.
