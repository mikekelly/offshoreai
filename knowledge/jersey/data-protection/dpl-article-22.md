# DPL 2018 Article 22 — Right to Object

Article 22 gives the data subject the **right to object** to
certain processing.

## Right

A data subject may object to processing based on:

- **Legitimate interests** (Article 8(1)(f));
- **Public-interest task** (Article 8(1)(e));
- **Profiling** based on the above;
- **Direct marketing** (absolute right);
- **Scientific / historical research** (subject to public-
  interest qualifications).

## Effect of objection

Where the data subject objects:

- **Direct marketing** — processing must stop **immediately**
  and unconditionally;
- **Other processing** — controller must stop **unless** it
  demonstrates **compelling legitimate grounds** overriding
  the data subject's interests, or processing is necessary
  for legal claims.

## Controller obligations

- **Inform** data subject of right to object at first
  communication;
- **Inform** clearly and separately from other information;
- **Provide** mechanism for objection (automated where
  feasible);
- **Cease** processing where objection upheld.

## Compelling legitimate grounds

To continue processing despite objection, controller must
show:

- **Compelling** (not merely legitimate) interest;
- **Overrides** data subject's interests, rights, freedoms;
- **Documented** assessment;
- **Specific** to that processing.

## Practical issues

For Jersey financial-services entities:

- **AML / regulatory** processing typically based on legal
  obligation (Article 22 right does not apply);
- **Marketing** subject to absolute objection right;
- **Risk-management** legitimate-interests processing —
  objection may be overridden by compelling grounds.
