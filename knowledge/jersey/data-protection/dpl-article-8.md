# DPL 2018 Article 8 — Lawful Bases for Processing

Article 8 establishes the **six lawful bases** for processing
personal data — analogous to GDPR Article 6.

## The six bases

Processing is lawful **only if at least one** of the
following applies:

### 1. Consent

The data subject has given **consent** to processing for one
or more specific purposes:

- **Freely given** (no coercion);
- **Specific** (purpose-bound);
- **Informed** (clear information);
- **Unambiguous** (clear affirmative action);
- **Withdrawable** at any time.

### 2. Contract

Processing is necessary for:

- The **performance of a contract** with the data subject;
- **Pre-contractual steps** at the data subject's request.

### 3. Legal obligation

Processing is necessary for compliance with a **legal
obligation** of the controller:

- **AML / CFT** obligations (POCL, MLO);
- **Tax reporting** (CRS, FATCA, Substance Law);
- **Companies Law** filings;
- **Specific** statutory requirements.

This basis is **heavily used** by Jersey regulated firms.

### 4. Vital interests

Processing is necessary to protect **vital interests** of the
data subject or another natural person:

- **Life-or-death** situations;
- **Medical emergencies**;
- **Narrow** application.

### 5. Public task

Processing is necessary for:

- **Performance of a task** carried out in the public
  interest;
- **Exercise of official authority** vested in the
  controller.

Mainly relevant for **government** controllers; less common
for private sector.

### 6. Legitimate interests

Processing is necessary for the **legitimate interests**
pursued by the controller or third party, **except** where
overridden by the data subject's interests / rights /
freedoms:

- **Most flexible** but most contested basis;
- **Balancing exercise** required;
- **Documented** legitimate interest assessment (LIA)
  expected;
- Cannot be relied upon by **public authorities** for their
  tasks.

## Choosing a basis

The choice depends on:

- The **purpose** of processing;
- The **relationship** with the data subject;
- The **risk** to data subjects;
- **Specific legal requirements** for the activity.

For **AML / CFT processing**: typically **legal obligation**
(Article 8(c)) is the basis, supplemented where needed.

For **client onboarding**: typically **contract** (Article
8(b)) with elements of legal obligation.

For **marketing**: typically **consent** (Article 8(a)) or
**legitimate interests** (Article 8(f)) with appropriate
balancing.

## Special category data

For **special category data** (Article 9):

- **Additional** legal basis needed (beyond Article 8);
- **Explicit consent**, **substantial public interest**,
  **employment law**, **legal claims**, **medical**, **public
  health**, **research**, **archiving** in public interest;
- More **restrictive** than general personal data.

## Documenting the basis

Controllers should **document**:

- The **basis** chosen for each processing purpose;
- The **rationale** for the choice;
- For legitimate interests: the **LIA**;
- **Available** to data subjects on request;
- **Available** to the JOIC on enforcement.
