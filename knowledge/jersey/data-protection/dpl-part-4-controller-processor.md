# DPL 2018 Part 4 — Controller and Processor

Part 4 governs the **operational obligations** of controllers
and processors.

## Article 28 — Responsibility of controller

The controller must:

- **Implement** appropriate technical and organisational
  measures;
- **Demonstrate** compliance with the principles;
- **Review and update** measures;
- **Document** processing;
- **Cooperate** with the JOIC.

## Articles 29-31 — Data protection by design and default

- **Privacy considered** from project inception;
- **Default settings** minimise data collection / processing;
- **Pseudonymisation** where appropriate;
- **Documented** through DPIAs and design reviews.

## Article 32 — Joint controllers

Where two or more controllers **jointly determine** purposes
and means:

- **Joint controller agreement** required;
- **Allocation** of responsibilities;
- **Transparency** to data subjects;
- **Liability** to data subjects (can be joint or several).

## Articles 33-35 — Processor

Processors must:

- Act **only on documented instructions** of the controller;
- **Confidentiality** undertakings by personnel;
- **Security** measures (Article 38);
- **Sub-processor** appointments only with controller consent;
- **Cooperate** with controller's responses to data subject
  rights;
- **Notify** breaches to controller;
- **Return / delete** data on end of processing;
- **Make available** information for audit.

## Article 36 — Records of processing activities

Controllers and processors must maintain **records**:

### Controllers' records

- **Name and contact** details of controller / DPO / etc.;
- **Purposes** of processing;
- **Categories** of data subjects and data;
- **Recipients** of data;
- **International transfers** and safeguards;
- **Retention** schedules;
- **Security** measures (general description).

### Processors' records

- **Name and contact** details;
- **Each controller** for which processor acts;
- **Categories** of processing activities;
- **International transfers** and safeguards;
- **Security** measures.

Records must be **available to the JOIC** on request.

## Article 37 — Cooperation with Commissioner

Controllers and processors must **cooperate** with the JOIC:

- **Information** requests;
- **Investigations**;
- **Compliance reviews**;
- **Specific** notifications under Article 43.

## Article 38 — Security of processing

**Appropriate technical and organisational measures**:

- **Encryption** of personal data;
- **Ongoing confidentiality, integrity, availability,
  resilience**;
- **Restoration** ability in case of incident;
- **Regular testing** of effectiveness;
- **Risk-based** approach.

## Articles 39-41 — DPIA

A **Data Protection Impact Assessment (DPIA)** is required
when processing is **likely to result in high risk**:

- **Systematic** evaluation / scoring;
- **Large-scale** special category data;
- **Systematic** monitoring of public area;
- **New technologies** (AI, biometrics);
- **Other** prescribed processing.

DPIA content:

- **Description** of processing;
- **Necessity / proportionality**;
- **Risks** to data subjects;
- **Measures** to address risks;
- **Consultation** with DPO if appointed;
- **Prior consultation** with JOIC if high residual risk.

## Article 42 — Data Protection Officer (DPO)

DPO **required** for:

- **Public authorities**;
- **Core activities** involving systematic monitoring;
- **Core activities** involving large-scale special category
  processing;
- **Specific** Jersey rules.

DPO tasks:

- **Inform / advise** the controller;
- **Monitor** compliance;
- **Provide** DPIA advice;
- **Cooperate** with JOIC;
- **Contact point** for data subjects;
- **Independent** in the role;
- **Sufficient resources**.
