# DPL 2018 Article 9 — Special Category Data

Article 9 provides **enhanced protection** for **special
category personal data** — sensitive data requiring an
additional legal basis beyond Article 8.

## What is special category data

- **Racial or ethnic origin**;
- **Political opinions**;
- **Religious or philosophical beliefs**;
- **Trade union membership**;
- **Genetic data**;
- **Biometric data** for identification purposes;
- **Health data**;
- **Sex life** data;
- **Sexual orientation** data.

**Criminal convictions and offences** data has **separate
specific protection** (Article 10).

## Default prohibition

Processing of special category data is **prohibited**
**unless** one of the specific Article 9 conditions applies.

## Article 9 conditions

Processing is lawful if **one or more** of the following
applies:

### Explicit consent

- **Explicit** consent (higher standard than general
  consent);
- **Specific** to one or more processing purposes;
- **Documented** clearly.

### Employment / social security

- Necessary for **employment, social security, or social
  protection** obligations / rights.

### Vital interests

- Where the data subject is **physically or legally
  incapable** of giving consent;
- **Life-or-death** circumstances.

### Member organisation

- Processing by **not-for-profit** body (e.g., trade union,
  religious organisation) for its members on their special
  category data;
- Subject to specific safeguards.

### Manifestly public data

- Data **manifestly made public** by the data subject.

### Legal claims

- Processing for **establishment, exercise, or defence of
  legal claims**;
- **Court** proceedings.

### Substantial public interest

- Processing necessary for **substantial public interest**;
- **Specific** statutory basis required;
- **Proportionality** demonstrated.

### Health / medical care

- **Medical diagnosis**, **healthcare provision**, **occupational
  medicine**;
- Subject to professional secrecy obligations.

### Public health

- **Public health interests** including disease control;
- **Specific safeguards** apply.

### Archiving / research

- **Public-interest archiving**, **scientific or historical
  research**, **statistical purposes**;
- Subject to **safeguards** and **specific safeguards** for
  the data subject.

## Practical applications

### AML / CFT contexts

- **Source of wealth** investigations may reveal special
  category data (medical inheritance, political donations,
  religious organisation membership);
- **Legal obligation** (Article 8(c)) **plus** specific
  Article 9 basis needed;
- **AML / CFT public interest** can support Article 9
  processing.

### Insurance underwriting

- **Health data** for underwriting requires Article 9
  basis;
- Typically **explicit consent** or **specific contractual**
  basis.

### Employee management

- **Health data** for employee management requires Article 9
  basis;
- **Employment / social security** condition typically.

### Marketing

- Special category data should **rarely** be used for
  marketing;
- **Explicit consent** required if so used;
- **Heightened scrutiny** of legitimacy.
