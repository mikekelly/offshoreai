# DPL 2018 Part 1 — Preliminary

## Article 1 — Interpretation

Definitions mirror GDPR:

- **"Personal data"** — information relating to an
  identified or identifiable natural person;
- **"Data subject"** — the natural person to whom personal
  data relates;
- **"Controller"** — determines purposes and means of
  processing;
- **"Processor"** — processes on behalf of the controller;
- **"Processing"** — any operation on personal data
  (collection, storage, use, disclosure, deletion, etc.);
- **"Recipient"** — entity to which personal data are
  disclosed;
- **"Third party"** — entity other than the data subject,
  controller, processor, and authorised persons;
- **"Consent"** — freely given, specific, informed, unambiguous
  indication;
- **"Special category data"** — sensitive data per Article 9;
- **"Personal data breach"** — security breach causing loss /
  unauthorised access etc.;
- **"Pseudonymisation"** — processing such that data cannot be
  attributed to a specific data subject without additional
  information;
- **"DPO"** — Data Protection Officer (where required);
- **"Authority" / "Commissioner"** — the JOIC.

## Articles 3-4 — Personal data scope

**Personal data** is broadly defined:

- **Direct identifiers** (name, ID number);
- **Indirect identifiers** (combination of factors revealing
  identity);
- **Online identifiers** (IP, cookies, device IDs in context);
- **Location data**;
- **Behavioural data** that could identify;
- **Pseudonymous data** if re-identification possible.

**Not** personal data:

- **Truly anonymous** data (irreversibly anonymised);
- **Aggregated** data not revealing individuals;
- **Data on deceased** persons (subject to specific
  exceptions).

## Article 5 — Controller defined

Controller is the person who **determines purposes and means**:

- **Single** controller in most cases;
- **Joint** controllers possible (with allocated
  responsibilities);
- **Co-controllers** in specific configurations;
- **Determined** by **factual reality** not formal label.

## Article 6 — Processor defined

Processor processes **on behalf of the controller**:

- **Outsourced** services (IT, cloud, mailing);
- **Limited** discretion;
- **Bound by** controller's instructions and contract;
- **Specific obligations** to controller (security, sub-
  processors, breach notification).

## Article 7 — Geographical application

The DPL applies to processing:

- By **Jersey-established** controllers / processors;
- **Wherever** the processing occurs;
- For **Jersey-located** data subjects (offering goods /
  services or monitoring behaviour);
- Subject to **specific carve-outs** for purely personal
  activity.
