# DPL 2018 Part 3 — Data Subject Rights

Part 3 sets out the **eight rights** of data subjects under
the DPL — mirroring GDPR Articles 12-22.

## Article 16 — Information rights

Data subjects must be **informed** about processing:

- **Identity** of the controller;
- **Purposes** of processing and legal basis;
- **Recipients** of personal data;
- **International transfer** information;
- **Retention** periods;
- **Their rights** under the DPL;
- **Right to complain** to the JOIC.

**Privacy notices** are the standard mechanism.

## Article 17 — Right of access

Data subjects have the right to:

- **Confirmation** of whether personal data is being processed;
- **Access** to the personal data;
- **Information** about purposes, recipients, retention,
  rights;
- **Copy** of the personal data;
- **First copy free** (additional copies may attract reasonable
  fee).

The **subject access request (SAR)** is the principal
mechanism — responding generally within **1 month**.

## Article 18 — Right of rectification

Data subjects have the right to:

- **Correct** inaccurate personal data;
- **Complete** incomplete data;
- **Without undue delay**.

## Article 19 — Right of erasure ("right to be forgotten")

Data subjects have the right to **erasure** in specific
circumstances:

- Data **no longer necessary** for the original purpose;
- **Consent withdrawn** and no other basis;
- **Objection** under Article 22 with no overriding legitimate
  ground;
- **Unlawful processing**;
- **Legal obligation** for erasure.

**Limited** by overriding rights / interests (free expression,
public interest, legal claims).

## Article 20 — Right to restrict processing

Data subjects have the right to **restrict** processing:

- During **accuracy disputes**;
- Where processing **unlawful** but subject prefers
  restriction;
- Where data **no longer needed** but subject needs it for
  claims;
- Pending **objection** resolution under Article 22.

## Article 21 — Right of portability

Data subjects have the right to receive personal data:

- **In structured, commonly used, machine-readable format**;
- **Transmit** to another controller;
- Where processing is **based on consent** or **contract**;
- And **carried out by automated means**.

## Article 22 — Right to object

Data subjects have the right to **object** to processing:

- **Legitimate interests** processing (controller must
  demonstrate compelling grounds to continue);
- **Direct marketing** (absolute right to object);
- **Research / statistical** processing (subject to public-
  interest test).

## Article 23 — Rights related to automated decisions

Data subjects have the right **not to be subject** to
decisions:

- **Solely** based on automated processing;
- That produce **legal or similarly significant effects**;
- **Exceptions** for contract necessity, legal authority,
  explicit consent;
- **Safeguards** — human intervention, contest, express
  opinion;
- **Profile / AI** decisions in scope.

## Articles 24-27 — Procedure

- **Verification** of identity for SAR / requests;
- **Free** for routine requests;
- **Fee** for excessive / repetitive requests;
- **Refusal** with reasons in specified cases;
- **Right of complaint** to the JOIC;
- **Time limits** (1 month, extendable for complex cases).
