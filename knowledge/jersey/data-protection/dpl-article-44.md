# DPL 2018 Article 44 — International Transfers

Article 44 establishes the general regime for **transferring
personal data outside Jersey**.

## General principle

Personal data may be transferred to a third country only if:

- **Adequate level of protection** is ensured in the
  receiving country; or
- **Appropriate safeguards** are in place; or
- **Specific derogation** applies.

## Adequate countries

Transfers are unrestricted to countries with **adequacy
decisions**:

- **EU / EEA** countries (mutual adequacy);
- **UK** (post-Brexit adequacy);
- **Other** countries with Jersey adequacy decisions;
- **JOIC** designations.

## Appropriate safeguards

Where adequacy not available, transfers may rely on:

- **Standard Contractual Clauses** (SCCs);
- **Binding Corporate Rules** (BCRs);
- **Approved codes of conduct** with binding commitments;
- **Approved certification** mechanisms;
- **Ad-hoc** contractual clauses approved by the JOIC.

## Derogations (specific situations)

Article 44 derogations include:

- **Explicit consent** of the data subject;
- **Necessary** for performance of contract with data
  subject;
- **Important public-interest** reasons;
- **Legal claims** purposes;
- **Vital interests** of the data subject;
- **Public register** information.

## Onward transfers

Where data is transferred to a third country with
safeguards:

- **Onward transfers** are subject to the same regime;
- **Specific** SCC / BCR provisions on onward transfers;
- **JOIC** supervises Jersey-originated chains.

## Practical issues

For Jersey TCBs / banks:

- **Group structures** — onward transfers to parent /
  affiliates;
- **Cloud services** — many vendors host outside Jersey;
- **AML / tax exchange** — typically not "transfers" in DPL
  sense (specific exemption);
- **Post-Brexit** — UK transfers unrestricted under
  adequacy.
