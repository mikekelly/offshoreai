# DPL 2018 Article 17 — Right of Access (SAR)

Article 17 establishes the **Subject Access Request (SAR)**
right — a data subject's right to access their personal
data.

## Right

A data subject has the right to:

- **Confirmation** of whether personal data is being
  processed;
- **Access** to the personal data;
- **Information** about purposes, recipients, retention,
  rights;
- **Copy** of the personal data;
- **First copy free**;
- **Additional copies** may attract reasonable fee.

## Information accompanying access

When responding to a SAR, the controller must provide:

- **Purposes** of processing;
- **Categories** of personal data processed;
- **Recipients** of personal data;
- **Retention** period or criteria;
- **Data subject's rights** under the DPL;
- **Right to complain** to the JOIC;
- **Source** of the data if not collected directly;
- **Automated decision-making** information if applicable;
- **International transfers** information.

## Response time

- **Within 1 month** of receiving the request;
- **Extendable to 3 months** for complex requests (with
  notification of delay);
- **Specific** procedural extensions.

## Identity verification

The controller may:

- **Verify** the requestor's identity;
- **Specific** verification methods;
- **Cannot** demand excessive verification;
- **Must** balance verification with accessibility.

## Refusal grounds

The controller may refuse / restrict access:

- **AML / CFT** investigation prejudice (subject to specific
  conditions);
- **Crime detection** prejudice;
- **Legal professional privilege**;
- **Specific** statutory exemptions;
- **Confidentiality** of third parties;
- **Negotiating position** in some narrow contexts;
- **Disproportionate effort** for specific categories.

## SAR scope

A SAR covers:

- **Personal data** about the data subject;
- **Not** information about other people (subject to specific
  redaction);
- **Not** purely internal management information;
- **Not** information subject to legal privilege.

## Refusal procedure

Where refused:

- **Reasoned** refusal in writing;
- **Specific** statutory basis for refusal;
- **Right** of data subject to complain to JOIC;
- **Right** of judicial review / Royal Court application.

## Practical issues for service providers

For Jersey TCBs / banks responding to client SARs:

- **AML / SAR** information typically protected from
  disclosure;
- **Standard** client information typically disclosed;
- **Specific** considerations for joint clients (only
  individual's own data);
- **Beneficiary** SARs in trust context — specific
  considerations.
