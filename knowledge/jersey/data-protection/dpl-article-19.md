# DPL 2018 Article 19 — Right of Erasure

Article 19 establishes the data subject's **right to
erasure** ("right to be forgotten").

## Right

A data subject may obtain erasure where:

- **No longer necessary** for the purposes for which
  collected;
- **Consent** withdrawn and no other lawful basis;
- **Successful objection** under Article 22;
- **Unlawful processing**;
- **Legal obligation** to erase;
- **Children's data** collected in information-society
  services context.

## Controller obligations

When erasure applies, the controller must:

- **Erase** the personal data without undue delay;
- **Inform** other controllers (including those to whom the
  data was disclosed);
- **Take reasonable steps** to inform downstream
  processors / controllers;
- **Account** for technical limitations.

## Exceptions

Erasure does not apply where processing is necessary for:

- **Freedom of expression / information**;
- **Legal obligation** or public-interest task;
- **Public health** purposes;
- **Archiving in the public interest**, research, statistics;
- **Establishment / exercise / defence of legal claims**;
- **AML / regulatory** retention obligations.

## Interaction with regulatory retention

For Jersey TCBs / banks:

- **AML records** must be retained per MLO 2008 (5 / 10
  years);
- **Tax records** retention under ITL 1961;
- **Regulatory** record-keeping obligations;
- Erasure rights **do not override** these.

## Practical issues

- **Active records** typically not eligible (still
  necessary);
- **Archived records** eligible if retention period elapsed;
- **Backup tapes** — practical erasure problems;
- **Specific** procedures for confirming erasure to data
  subject.
