# DPL 2018 Part 7 — Enforcement

## Articles 76-79 — Administrative fines

The JOIC may impose **administrative fines** for DPL
breaches:

### Tier 1 fines (lower)

- **Up to £5 million** OR **2% of global annual turnover**
  (whichever higher);
- For breaches of:
  - Controller / processor obligations;
  - Records of processing;
  - Cooperation with JOIC;
  - DPIA obligations;
  - Specific procedural breaches.

### Tier 2 fines (higher)

- **Up to £10 million** OR **4% of global annual turnover**
  (whichever higher);
- For breaches of:
  - **Principles** (Articles 10-15);
  - **Lawful basis** (Article 8);
  - **Data subject rights** (Articles 16-23);
  - **International transfers** (Articles 44-56);
  - **Non-compliance** with Commissioner orders.

These mirror **GDPR penalty tiers**.

### Mitigation factors

- **Cooperation** with the JOIC;
- **Self-reporting**;
- **Remediation**;
- **First-offender** status;
- **No customer harm**;
- **Proactive** compliance program.

### Aggravation factors

- **Wilful** or **negligent** conduct;
- **Customer harm**;
- **Repeated** breaches;
- **Concealment**;
- **Scale** of breach;
- **Special category** data involved.

## Articles 80-83 — Criminal offences

Specific criminal offences:

- **Intentional / reckless** unauthorised disclosure of
  personal data;
- **Re-identification** of pseudonymised data without
  authorisation;
- **Obstruction** of JOIC investigations;
- **False statements** to JOIC;
- **Non-compliance** with directions or court orders.

Penalties:

- **Imprisonment** up to **5 years**;
- **Unlimited fines**;
- **Disqualification** in some cases.

## Article 84 — Compensation

Data subjects may claim **compensation** from controllers
/ processors:

- For **material damage** (financial loss);
- For **non-material damage** (distress, anxiety, damage to
  reputation);
- **Joint and several** liability of joint controllers /
  processors;
- **Court** proceedings in Royal Court.

## Article 85 — Public statements

The JOIC may issue **public statements**:

- About **enforcement action**;
- About **non-compliant** controllers / processors;
- About **themes** in compliance;
- **Reputational sanction** distinct from financial.

## Articles 86-87 — Appeals

Decisions appealable to **Royal Court**:

- **Within** prescribed period;
- On **grounds** of procedural fairness, error of law,
  proportionality;
- **Stay** of decision possible pending appeal;
- **Further appeals** to Court of Appeal and Privy Council.

## Trends in enforcement

Common JOIC enforcement themes:

- **AML / CDD** retention beyond necessity;
- **Inadequate** breach response;
- **Inadequate** privacy notices;
- **International transfers** without proper safeguards;
- **SAR response** failures;
- **Specific** healthcare / public-sector data issues.
