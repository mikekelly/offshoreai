# CL 2014 Article 13 — Restricted Section

Article 13 establishes the **restricted section** of the
Jersey charity register — for smaller / private charities.

## What the restricted section is for

The restricted section accommodates:

- **Small** charities with limited public engagement;
- **Family** charitable trusts and foundations;
- **Private** charitable structures;
- **Charities** with primarily private donor base;
- **Charities** not engaged in public fundraising.

## Features

### Limited public disclosure

- **Less** information made public than general section;
- **More privacy** for family-led / private structures;
- **Specific** Commissioner discretion on what is public.

### Reduced reporting obligations

- **Lighter** annual reporting than general section;
- **Specific** thresholds;
- **Specific** Commissioner accommodation.

### No public fundraising

- **Restriction** on public fundraising in restricted
  section;
- **Specific** scope of permitted activities;
- **Distinction** from general section.

## Migration

A charity can:

- **Migrate** between sections as circumstances change;
- **General to restricted** if scaling back public activity;
- **Restricted to general** if expanding public activity;
- **Specific** Commissioner approval.

## Common users

The restricted section commonly used by:

### Family charitable foundations

- **Family-funded** giving;
- **Family-directed** purposes;
- **Privacy** important;
- **Specific** family-governance considerations.

### Small charitable trusts

- **Limited** assets;
- **Specific** beneficiary classes;
- **No** public fundraising.

### Specific private charitable purposes

- **Donor-restricted** purposes;
- **Specific** family / community focus;
- **Limited** scope.

## Comparison

| Section | Public access | Reporting | Public fundraising |
|---|---|---|---|
| General | Full | Annual returns | Permitted |
| Restricted | Limited | Reduced | Not permitted |
| Historic | Lighter | Lighter | Subject to specific rules |
