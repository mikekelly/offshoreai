# CL 2014 Part 1 — Preliminary

## Article 1 — Interpretation

Key definitions:

- **"Charity"** — an entity established for **charitable
  purposes** (Article 5) providing **public benefit** (Article
  6);
- **"Charitable purposes"** — purposes within the statutory
  list;
- **"Charity trustee"** — person responsible for governing a
  charity (board member, council member, director of charity-
  company);
- **"Commissioner"** — the **Charity Commissioner**;
- **"General section"** — main charity register section;
- **"Public benefit"** — defined under Article 6;
- **"Registered charity"** — charity entered in the register;
- **"Restricted section"** — register section for private /
  small charities.

## Article 2 — Application

The Law applies to:

- **All** Jersey-resident charities (regardless of legal form
  — trust, foundation, company);
- **Foreign charities** with Jersey operations or fund-raising
  (subject to specific rules);
- **Persons** acting as charity trustees in Jersey;
- **Specific** exemptions for very small charities or
  intra-group arrangements.

## Article 3 — Meaning of "charity"

A **charity** is an entity that:

- Is established for **charitable purposes** (Article 5);
- **Provides** public benefit (Article 6);
- Is **not for private benefit** of identified individuals
  (subject to specific exceptions);
- **Devotes** its assets to charitable purposes.

## Article 4 — Registered charity

A **registered charity** is one entered on the **charity
register** by the Commissioner:

- **Distinct** from a charity that has not registered
  (unregistered charities have **no Jersey tax status** and
  may face other consequences);
- **Mandatory** for most Jersey charities meeting threshold;
- **Public benefit** of the register includes transparency
  and donor confidence.

## Three register sections

The register has three sections:

### General section

- **Standard** Jersey charities;
- **Public** register entry;
- **Standard** compliance and reporting;
- **Open** to public fund-raising.

### Restricted section

- **Smaller** charities with limited public engagement;
- **Family** charitable trusts and foundations;
- **Limited** public disclosure (more privacy);
- **No public fund-raising** typically;
- **Reduced** annual obligations.

### Historic section

- **Pre-CL** charitable entities grandfathered;
- **Transitional** classification;
- **Lighter** regulatory regime;
- **Phasing out** over time.
