# Charities (Jersey) Law 2014 — Overview

The **Charities (Jersey) Law 2014 (CL)** is Jersey's
**principal modern charity statute**. It came into force in
**phases** from 2014 to 2018 and established:

- The **Charity Commissioner** as the supervisor;
- The **charity register** (public);
- **Definitions** of charitable purpose;
- **Public benefit** as a statutory test;
- **Trustee duties** for charity trustees;
- **Investigation** powers.

The CL **modernised** Jersey charity law along lines similar
to the UK Charities Act 2011 but with **Jersey-specific**
features.

## Key features

### Charity Commissioner

A statutory office:

- **Independent** office holder appointed by the States
  Assembly;
- **Supervises** registered charities;
- **Maintains** the charity register;
- **Investigates** complaints and concerns;
- **Provides guidance** to charities;
- **Reports** to the States.

### Registration

Jersey charities **must register** with the Commissioner:

- **Three sections** of the register:
  - **General section** — most charities;
  - **Restricted section** — small / private charities with
    limited public exposure;
  - **Historic section** — pre-CL charities with grandfathered
    status.

Registration is **mandatory** for charities meeting threshold
criteria.

### Charitable purposes

Specific **statutory list** of charitable purposes:

- **Prevention or relief** of poverty;
- **Advancement** of education;
- **Advancement** of religion;
- **Advancement** of health;
- **Advancement** of citizenship / community development;
- **Advancement** of arts, culture, heritage, science;
- **Advancement** of amateur sport;
- **Advancement** of human rights, conflict resolution,
  religious or racial harmony;
- **Advancement** of environmental protection;
- **Relief of those in need** by age, disability, financial
  hardship, etc.;
- **Advancement** of animal welfare;
- **Other** purposes recognised at common law / customary
  law.

### Public benefit test

Every charitable purpose must demonstrate **public benefit**:

- **Identifiable** benefit (not just notional);
- **To the public** or a sufficient section of the public;
- **Not** outweighed by detriments;
- **Substantive** evidence in registration process;
- **Continuing** demonstration through ongoing operations.

### Trustee duties

Charity trustees:

- **Owe fiduciary duties** to the charity;
- Must **act in best interests** of the charity;
- Must **avoid conflicts**;
- Must **comply with the charitable purposes**;
- **Stewards** of the charity assets;
- **Subject to** Commissioner oversight.

## Comparison with UK regime

Similar to **UK Charities Act 2011** but:

- **Smaller scale** — Jersey's charity sector is small in
  number but significant in international assets;
- **Specific Jersey** features (qualifying for charitable tax
  exemption);
- **International** charity work prominent;
- **Light-touch** regime for genuinely private / family
  charities (restricted section).

## Tax status

A registered Jersey charity:

- **Tax exempt** for **income tax** purposes (Article 115
  of the Income Tax (Jersey) Law 1961);
- **Tax exempt** for **GST**;
- **Eligible** for various international tax exemptions in
  donor jurisdictions (subject to local rules);
- **Charity tax relief** for Jersey donors.
