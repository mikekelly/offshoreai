# CL 2014 Part 6 — Cy-près and Modification

Part 6 deals with **modifying charitable purposes** when
circumstances change — the **cy-près** doctrine and related
mechanisms.

## Articles 49-50 — Cy-près

The Royal Court may order **cy-près** modification:

- **Where** the original charitable purpose has:
  - **Failed** (impossible to fulfil);
  - Become **inappropriate** (changed circumstances);
  - Become **impractical**;
  - **Provided adequately** for its objects;
  - **Original** terms outdated.
- To **modify** the purposes to **as close to** the original
  as practicable;
- **Subject to** Commissioner's view;
- **Subject to** Attorney General's view.

## Article 51 — Application

A cy-près application may be made by:

- The **charity trustees**;
- The **Commissioner**;
- The **Attorney General**;
- A **person** with sufficient interest.

## Article 52 — Procedure

The cy-près process:

- **Application** to the Royal Court;
- **Representation** of all interests;
- **Evidence** of failure of original purposes;
- **Proposals** for modified purposes;
- **Hearing** with submissions;
- **Court order** specifying the new purposes;
- **Notification** to the Commissioner and registration.

## Article 53 — Alternative purposes

The Court considers **alternative purposes**:

- **Closest** to original intent;
- **Practical** to administer;
- **Charitable** under Article 5;
- **Public benefit** demonstrable;
- **Aligned** with original donor / settlor intentions if
  ascertainable.

## Articles 54-55 — Other modification mechanisms

### Constitutional amendment

- **Trustees** may amend constitutional documents within the
  trustees' powers;
- **Subject to** any limits in the original document;
- **Subject to** Commissioner notification.

### Schemes

The Commissioner may approve **schemes** for:

- **Smaller** modifications not requiring Court order;
- **Routine** updates;
- **Standardised** modifications;
- **Subject to** Commissioner discretion.

### Dissolution

Where modification is not feasible:

- **Dissolution** of the charity;
- **Surplus assets** distributed to similar charitable
  purposes (cy-près on dissolution);
- **Subject to** Court / Commissioner approval.

## Specific scenarios

### Original purpose redundant

- Charity established to **cure a specific disease**;
- Disease **eradicated** or alternative treatments make
  charity unnecessary;
- Cy-près to **related medical research** or **similar
  conditions**.

### Original beneficiary class diminished

- Charity for **war widows of a specific war**;
- **Beneficiaries** all deceased;
- Cy-près to **war widows generally** or **veterans'
  families**.

### Restrictive terms outdated

- Charity providing **bursaries** to specific school no
  longer existing;
- Cy-près to **education in the same area** or **same type
  of pupil**.
