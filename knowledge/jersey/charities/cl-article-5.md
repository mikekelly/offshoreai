# CL 2014 Article 5 — Charitable Purposes

Article 5 sets out the **statutory list** of **charitable
purposes** — the threshold for charitable status.

## The provision

A purpose is **charitable** if it falls within one or more of
the listed categories AND provides **public benefit**
(Article 6).

## The statutory list

### Relief of poverty

- **Relief or prevention** of poverty;
- Includes **financial support** to those in poverty;
- **Food banks, shelters, debt relief**;
- **Beyond Jersey** — international development relief
  qualifies;
- Historic charitable head (was common law).

### Advancement of education

- **Education** of children;
- **Adult education**;
- **Research** (subject to public-benefit considerations);
- **Schools, universities, libraries**;
- **Vocational training**;
- **Specific** educational charities.

### Advancement of religion

- **Religious worship** and observance;
- **Religious education**;
- **Maintenance** of religious buildings;
- **Religious community** support;
- **All** recognised religions covered (not just Christianity);
- Subject to **public benefit** specifically (not just internal
  group benefit).

### Advancement of health

- **Saving lives**;
- **Promotion** of health;
- **Prevention** of illness;
- **Medical research**;
- **Mental health** support;
- **Specific** diseases / conditions advocacy and support.

### Advancement of citizenship / community development

- **Civic responsibility**;
- **Volunteering**;
- **Community building**;
- **Urban / rural** development;
- **Specific community** organisations.

### Advancement of arts, culture, heritage, science

- **Arts** organisations (visual, performing);
- **Cultural** preservation;
- **Heritage** sites and conservation;
- **Scientific research** (with public-benefit aspect);
- **Museums, galleries**.

### Advancement of amateur sport

- **Sports** participation (not professional);
- **Sports facilities** for community;
- **Subject to** specific rules on professional involvement.

### Advancement of human rights, conflict resolution, religious / racial harmony

- **Human rights** advocacy;
- **Conflict resolution**;
- **Anti-discrimination** work;
- **Promotion** of harmony between groups.

### Advancement of environmental protection

- **Conservation** of environment;
- **Climate change** mitigation;
- **Wildlife** protection;
- **Sustainable** development;
- **Specific** environmental charities.

### Relief of those in need

- By **age, disability, financial hardship, other disadvantage**;
- **Elderly care**;
- **Disability** support;
- **Refugee / asylum** support;
- **Specific** beneficiary groups.

### Advancement of animal welfare

- **Animal welfare** charities;
- **Wildlife** protection;
- **Veterinary** services for charitable purposes.

### Other purposes recognised at common law / customary law

- **Catch-all** for purposes recognised before the CL;
- **Customary** Jersey charitable purposes;
- **Specific** local traditions (e.g., support for ancient
  Jersey institutions).

## Mixed purposes

A charity can pursue **multiple** charitable purposes:

- **Stated** in the constitutional documents;
- **Each** purpose tested separately for public benefit;
- **Charitable** even if some purposes non-substantive (de
  minimis).

## Non-charitable purposes

Common purposes that are **NOT charitable**:

- **Political purposes** (campaigning for legislative change
  is generally not charitable, though research with policy
  recommendations may be);
- **Private benefit** purposes (benefiting specific named
  individuals);
- **Commercial** purposes (profit-making without charitable
  application);
- **Mixed** purposes where charitable element is incidental
  to non-charitable element.
