# CL 2014 Part 7 — Offences and Appeals

## Articles 56-58 — Criminal offences

Specific criminal offences include:

### Holding out as charity

- **Holding out** as a charity without registration is an
  offence;
- **Misleading** representations about charitable status;
- **Use** of "charity" or similar in trading style without
  registration;
- **Fraudulent** charity solicitation.

### Failure to register

- A qualifying entity **failing to register** when required;
- **Persistent** failure to register;
- **Civil penalties** alongside criminal liability.

### False statements

- **False** statements in registration applications;
- **False** statements in annual returns;
- **False** statements in response to investigation;
- **Misleading** the Commissioner.

### Misconduct

- **Knowing or reckless** misconduct by trustees;
- **Misappropriation** of charity funds;
- **Fraudulent** use of charity for non-charitable purposes;
- **Specific** fundraising offences.

### Failure to comply with directions

- **Failure** to comply with Commissioner directions;
- **Failure** to comply with Court orders;
- **Contempt**.

## Article 59 — Penalties

- **Imprisonment** up to **5 years** for serious offences;
- **Unlimited fines**;
- **Disqualification** as charity trustee;
- **Confiscation** of misappropriated property;
- **Civil penalties** by the Commissioner (limited).

## Articles 60-61 — Civil penalties

The Commissioner may impose **civil penalties**:

- **Calibrated** to severity;
- **Faster** process than criminal;
- **Available** for specific compliance breaches;
- **Subject** to appeal.

## Articles 62-63 — Appeals

Decisions are appealable to the **Royal Court**:

- **Refusal** of registration;
- **Conditions** imposed;
- **Removal** from register;
- **Trustee disqualification**;
- **Directions**;
- **Civil penalties**;
- **Subject** to procedural protections.

Further appeals lie to the **Court of Appeal** and **Privy
Council** (with leave) on points of law.
