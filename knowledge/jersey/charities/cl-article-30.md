# CL 2014 Article 30 — Charity Trustee Duties

Article 30 sets out **specific duties** of charity trustees,
layered on top of general trustee / director duties.

## Core duties

Charity trustees must:

- Act in the **best interests** of the charity;
- Act **only for** the charity's charitable purposes;
- Comply with the **constitutional documents**;
- **Avoid conflicts** of interest;
- Not derive **personal benefit** beyond reasonable
  reimbursement / remuneration;
- Act with **due care and diligence**;
- **Cooperate** with the Commissioner;
- **Comply** with statutory and regulatory standards.

## Layered duties

These duties are **on top of**:

- **Trustee** duties under TJL 1984 (charitable trust);
- **Council member** duties under FJL 2009 (charitable
  foundation);
- **Director** duties under CJL 1991 (charitable company);
- **Specific** entity-specific obligations.

## Acting only for charity

The "charity-only" duty:

- **Personal benefit** prohibited beyond reasonable;
- **Family-member benefit** subject to specific scrutiny;
- **Conflict** transactions subject to disclosure / approval;
- **Specific** Commissioner oversight.

## Specific obligations

### Investment

- **Prudent** investment;
- **Diversification**;
- **Subject to** charity's specific investment policy;
- **Specific** ethical / sustainable considerations.

### Records

- **Trustees' decisions** documented;
- **Financial records**;
- **Charity activity** records;
- **Subject to** Commissioner access.

### Reporting

- **Annual** returns to Commissioner;
- **Material event** notification;
- **Specific** transparency to donors / beneficiaries.

### Governance

- **Effective** board / trustee body;
- **Specific** committee structures for larger charities;
- **Conflict management** procedures;
- **Specific** training and capacity.

## Breach consequences

For breach of Article 30 duties:

- **Personal liability** to the charity for losses;
- **Commissioner action** (directions, removal);
- **Court** intervention in serious cases;
- **Disqualification** as charity trustee;
- **Specific** criminal liability for serious breach.

## Indemnity

Charity trustees:

- **Statutory indemnity** for proper performance of duties;
- **No indemnity** for fraud, wilful default, gross
  negligence;
- **Insurance** funded by charity (subject to conditions);
- **Personal liability** for serious breaches.
