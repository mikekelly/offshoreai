# CL 2014 Part 4 — Charity Trustees

Part 4 sets out **specific duties** of charity trustees —
applicable on top of any general trustee / director duties
under the underlying entity's law.

## Article 29 — Definition

A **charity trustee** is a person who has **general control
and management** of a charity:

- For a **charitable trust** — the trustees;
- For a **charitable foundation** — the council members;
- For a **charitable company** — the directors;
- For a **charitable association** — the committee members;
- Other persons exercising **effective control**.

## Article 30 — Duties

Charity trustees must:

- Act in the **best interests** of the charity;
- Act **only for** the charity's charitable purposes;
- Comply with the **constitutional documents**;
- **Avoid conflicts** of interest;
- Not derive **personal benefit** beyond reasonable
  reimbursement / remuneration;
- Act with **due care and diligence**;
- **Cooperate** with the Commissioner;
- **Comply with** statutory and regulatory standards.

These duties are **layered on top** of the trustee / director
duties under the underlying entity's law (TJL, FJL, Companies
Law).

## Article 31 — Conflicts of interest

Charity trustees must:

- **Identify** conflicts;
- **Disclose** them to other trustees;
- **Recuse** themselves from conflicted decisions;
- **Avoid** transactions that benefit them personally;
- **Document** conflicts and management.

Common conflict scenarios:

- **Trustee businesses** providing services to the charity;
- **Family** of trustees benefiting from the charity;
- **Other charities** in which trustees serve.

## Article 32 — Remuneration

Charity trustees:

- Generally serve **without remuneration**;
- **Reasonable reimbursement** of expenses permitted;
- **Specific remuneration** permitted in some cases (professional
  trustees, specific services with authorisation);
- **Constitutional documents** may authorise;
- **Commissioner approval** typically required for material
  remuneration.

## Article 33 — Investment

Charity trustees must:

- **Invest** charity assets prudently;
- **Diversify** (subject to size);
- **Apply** investment income to charitable purposes;
- **Consider** ethical / sustainable investment factors;
- Follow **investment policy** documented in trustees'
  records.

## Articles 34-35 — Records

Charity trustees must:

- **Maintain** trustees' decisions and meetings records;
- **Keep** financial records (accounts);
- **Retain** records for **at least** the prescribed period
  (typically 6 years);
- **Make available** to Commissioner on request.

## Article 36 — Annual accounts

Charity trustees must:

- **Prepare** annual accounts;
- **Have audited** if above the audit threshold;
- **Submit** to the Commissioner;
- **Publish** in some cases (general section charities).

## Article 37 — Removal

Charity trustees may be removed:

- **Voluntarily** (resignation);
- By **other trustees** under constitutional documents;
- By the **Commissioner** for misconduct or unfitness;
- By the **Royal Court** in serious cases;
- **Following due process** and right of representation.

## Article 38 — Indemnity

Charity trustees:

- Have **statutory indemnity** for proper performance of
  duties;
- **No indemnity** for fraud, wilful default, gross negligence;
- May have **insurance** funded by the charity (subject to
  conditions);
- **Personal liability** for serious breaches.
