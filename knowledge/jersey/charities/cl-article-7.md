# CL 2014 Article 7 — Application for Charity Registration

Article 7 specifies the **application requirements** for
Jersey charity registration.

## Application content

A complete application includes:

### Constitutional documents

- **Charity vehicle** documents (trust deed, foundation
  charter, company articles, association rules);
- **Specific** clauses on charitable purposes;
- **Specific** dissolution provisions (cy-près).

### Charitable purposes

Specifically identified per Article 5:

- **Specific** purposes per statutory list;
- **Documentation** of activities;
- **Linkage** between purposes and activities.

### Public benefit

Demonstrated per Article 6:

- **Identifiable benefit**;
- **To the public** or sufficient section;
- **Outweighing** any detriments;
- **Specific** evidence base.

### Initial trustees

- **Each charity trustee** identified;
- **Personal** information including fit-and-proper
  considerations;
- **Specific** experience and qualifications;
- **Conflicts** disclosure.

### Financial information

- **Current assets** if any;
- **Anticipated income**;
- **Source of funding**;
- **Specific** financial-sustainability analysis.

### Operational plan

- **Activities** detail;
- **Geography** (Jersey, UK, international);
- **Beneficiaries** identification;
- **Programs** proposed;
- **Reporting** plans.

### Section sought

- **General section** (public-facing charities);
- **Restricted section** (private / family / smaller);
- **Historic section** (pre-CL grandfathered).

### Fee

- **Application fee** per JFSC / Commissioner schedule.

## Pre-application

In practice:

- **Pre-application** engagement with Charity Commissioner;
- **Specific** scoping discussions for complex charities;
- **Documentation** preparation;
- **Specific** legal advice common.

## Review timeline

- **Standard** applications: weeks to months;
- **Complex** applications: longer;
- **Specific** queries from Commissioner;
- **Iterative** revisions common.
