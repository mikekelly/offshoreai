# CL 2014 Part 2 — Registration

## Article 7 — Application

A charity applies to the Charity Commissioner for
registration:

- **Constitutional documents** of the entity;
- **Charitable purposes** specified (per Article 5);
- **Public benefit** demonstrated (per Article 6);
- **Initial trustees** and key personnel;
- **Financial information** (current assets, anticipated
  income);
- **Operational plan** (activities, geography, beneficiaries);
- **Section** sought (general, restricted, historic).

## Article 8 — Sections of the register

The Commissioner decides the appropriate section:

- **General section** — most public-facing charities;
- **Restricted section** — private / family / smaller
  charities;
- **Historic section** — pre-CL grandfathered charities.

A charity can apply to **move** between sections as
circumstances change.

## Article 9 — Grant of registration

The Commissioner grants if satisfied:

- The applicant is **established for charitable purposes**;
- The purposes provide **public benefit**;
- The applicant's **governance** is adequate;
- The **trustees** are fit and proper;
- **Compliance** with statutory and prudential standards;
- **In the public interest** to register.

## Article 10 — Conditions

Registration may be subject to **conditions**:

- **Specific operational** restrictions;
- **Reporting** beyond standard requirements;
- **Specific** safeguards for public-benefit operation;
- **Trustee** requirements (independence, experience).

## Articles 11-12 — Refusal

The Commissioner may **refuse**:

- Failure to satisfy Article 9 conditions;
- **Misleading** information in application;
- **Public-interest** concerns;
- Subject to **right of appeal** to Royal Court.

## Article 13 — Restricted section

The restricted section accommodates:

- **Small** charities (limited public engagement);
- **Family** charitable foundations / trusts;
- **Private** charitable structures;
- **Limited** public disclosure;
- **Reduced** reporting obligations;
- **No public fund-raising**.

## Article 14 — Historic section

Grandfathers pre-CL charitable entities:

- **Registered** automatically on CL commencement;
- **Lighter** regulatory regime;
- **Phasing out** as charities migrate to general or
  restricted section;
- **Transitional** provision.

## Articles 15-17 — Continuing obligations

Registered charities must:

- **File annual returns**;
- **Submit** annual accounts (audited above thresholds);
- **Notify** changes in trustees, address, constitution;
- **Notify** material changes in operation;
- **Cooperate** with Commissioner oversight;
- **Pay** annual fees.

## Article 18 — Removal from register

The Commissioner may remove a charity:

- For **non-compliance** with conditions;
- **Failure** to file returns;
- **Failure** to pay fees;
- **Loss** of charitable status (purposes changed);
- **Voluntary** removal on charity's request;
- **Misconduct** by trustees;
- Subject to **due process** and **appeal**.
