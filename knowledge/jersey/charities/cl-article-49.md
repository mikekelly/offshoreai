# CL 2014 Article 49 — Cy-près

Article 49 establishes the statutory **cy-près regime** —
the power to apply charity assets to alternative purposes
where the original purposes have failed or become
impracticable.

## Cy-près occasions

Cy-près may be applied where original purposes:

- **Cannot be carried out**;
- **Provide a use** for only part of the funds available;
- **Are similar** to purposes already provided for by other
  means;
- **Have ceased** to be charitable;
- **Have ceased** to provide suitable / effective use of
  funds;
- **Have been adequately provided** for by other means;
- **Are no longer** in keeping with the spirit of the gift.

## Spirit of the gift

The alternative purposes must:

- **Reflect** the spirit of the original gift;
- **Be charitable** under Article 5;
- **Meet** the public benefit test under Article 6;
- **Be approved** by the Commissioner.

## Procedure

Cy-près application proceeds by:

- **Application** to the Charity Commissioner;
- **Trustees** typically initiate;
- **Donor consent** considered where practicable and
  recent;
- **Commissioner** approval / scheme;
- **Royal Court** application for significant matters.

## Modification of purposes

Article 49 extends to:

- **Alteration** of charitable purposes within original
  framework;
- **Variation** of restrictions;
- **Reallocation** of assets between purposes;
- **Specific** procedures for restricted-section charities.

## Donor wishes

Where the donor is living and contactable:

- **Donor consultation** appropriate;
- **Donor consent** may be required for specific
  variations;
- **Specific** procedures for substantial gifts.

## Practical issues

For Jersey charity trustees:

- **Failed purposes** — when original objectives become
  obsolete or impossible;
- **Insufficient funds** — assets too small for original
  scheme;
- **Successor charities** — merging with similar bodies;
- **Specific** Commissioner guidance on cy-près applications.

## Common cy-près situations

- **Disease eradicated** (medical research charities);
- **Beneficiary class** no longer exists;
- **Geographic area** changed in relevance;
- **Better-resourced** charities now operate;
- **Technology** has rendered purposes obsolete.
