# Listed Buildings in Jersey

## What listing means

A **listed building** is a building of **special
architectural or historic interest** designated under the
Planning and Building (Jersey) Law 2002. Listing protects:

- The building **and its setting**;
- **External and internal features** of special interest;
- **Curtilage** structures (ancillary buildings within the
  property);
- **Fixed contents** of historic significance.

The listing is **registered** against the property and
runs with the title.

## Grades

| Grade | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Grade 1** | Buildings of exceptional importance — international / national significance |
| **Grade 2** | Buildings of special interest |
| **Grade 3 / additional categories** | Lower-tier buildings; ancillary structures; sites of archaeological interest |

The grade affects the sensitivity of the building and the
likelihood of consent for proposed works.

## What requires listed-building consent

Listed-building consent (LBC) is needed for any work that
would **affect the character** of the listed building as
a building of special interest. This includes:

- **Demolition** (in whole or part);
- **Alteration or extension** affecting character;
- **Internal alterations** affecting historic interiors —
  often surprising scope (panelling, fireplaces,
  staircases, plasterwork, original doors);
- **External alterations** (windows, doors, materials,
  paint colours in some cases);
- **Roof works** affecting profile or materials;
- **Service installations** (heating, electrical) that
  alter fabric;
- **Curtilage** structures.

A common surprise: **even internal works** on a listed
building typically need consent. The list isn't only the
facade.

## What does NOT need LBC

- **Like-for-like repairs** with original materials —
  generally accepted as not "affecting character";
- **Routine maintenance** — gutter clearing, painting in
  original colours (in many cases);
- **Decoration** in original schemes;
- **Works to non-listed parts** of the curtilage (specific
  determination needed).

When in doubt, **ask** — the Historic Environment Team
can advise.

## Application process

A listed-building consent application:

- **Separate application** from ordinary planning
  permission (often submitted together);
- **Specialist statements** — heritage statement,
  significance assessment, design and access statement;
- **Specialist consultees** — Historic Environment Team,
  Jersey Heritage, Société Jersiaise;
- **Higher fees** in some categories;
- **Standard timelines** — 8-13 weeks target.

For a listed building you typically need **both**
planning permission **and** LBC. Each must be granted.

## Sensitivities

Common issues that lead to refusal:

- **Removing original features**;
- **Inappropriate materials** (UPVC where wood was
  original; modern doors);
- **Inappropriate scale** of extensions;
- **Solar panels** in sensitive locations;
- **External insulation** changing the appearance;
- **Replacement windows** with double-glazing that
  alters proportions;
- **Modern interventions** without sympathy for
  character.

## Specialist advice

For a listed building:

- **Engage a conservation-specialist architect** —
  invaluable;
- **Heritage consultant** for the significance statement;
- **Pre-application engagement** with Historic
  Environment Team strongly recommended;
- **Specialist contractors** experienced in listed
  buildings;
- **Specialist surveyors** for condition reports.

## Enforcement

Unauthorised works to a listed building are a **serious
criminal offence**:

- **Unlimited fines**;
- **Possible imprisonment**;
- **Enforcement notices** requiring reversal of works;
- **Reputation damage** for the owner / contractor.

The Historic Environment Team actively monitors. Removal
of historic features by an owner or contractor unaware
of the listing is a regular enforcement scenario.

## Buying a listed building

Due diligence essential:

- **Confirm** the listing entry and grade;
- **Check** the schedule of significance;
- **Identify** any unauthorised works by previous owners
  (could become your enforcement problem);
- **Survey** with a listed-building specialist;
- **Plan** for higher maintenance costs;
- **Plan** for restricted alteration options.

## Insurance and finance

- **Insurance** — listed buildings need specialist cover;
  premiums typically higher;
- **Mortgages** — most lenders fund listed properties but
  some restrict;
- **Re-instatement** costs typically higher than open-
  market value.
