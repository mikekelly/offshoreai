# Extensions, Conversions, and Outbuildings

The most common planning questions in Jersey involve
domestic extensions, conversions, and outbuildings. This
file collects the practical positions.

## Rear extensions

Typical single-storey rear extension:

- **Permitted development** within the GD Order 2011
  limits — typically a defined floor area / depth / height;
- **Beyond those limits** — full planning application
  required;
- **Conservation areas** — typically need full
  application even if within thresholds;
- **Listed buildings** — listed-building consent always
  required;
- **Boundary distance** matters — close to boundary
  triggers neighbour-rights issues.

For a typical Jersey-sized property a small rear extension
(e.g. 3-4m projection, single storey) may be permitted
development; anything larger or two-storey requires
application.

## Side extensions

- **Narrow side extensions** sometimes permitted
  development;
- **Wider** side extensions need application;
- **Materials** typically must match the existing
  dwelling.

## Two-storey extensions

- **Always requires application** under current rules;
- **Materials**, **roof line**, **window position**
  carefully considered;
- **Neighbour amenity** (overlooking, daylight) is a
  major consideration.

## Loft conversions

- **Internal loft conversion** — typically permitted
  development for unlisted, non-conservation properties,
  subject to specific conditions;
- **Dormer windows** — subject to size, position, and
  design conditions;
- **Mansards / large dormers** — typically need
  application;
- **Skylights / Velux** within roof slope — generally
  permitted.

## Conservatories

- **Modest conservatories** at the rear — typically
  permitted development within size limits;
- **Side conservatories** — narrower exemptions;
- **Larger conservatories** — application needed;
- **Heating / insulation** — even if planning is OK,
  building approval rules may apply.

## Garden offices / sheds / outbuildings

- **Small sheds and outbuildings** — permitted
  development subject to:
  - Height (typically 2.5m flat, up to 4m pitched);
  - Floor area limits;
  - Distance from main building and boundaries;
  - **Not for residential use** (i.e. not a separate
    living space);
- **Garden office** for home working — generally OK if
  modest and incidental;
- **Annexe** for a family member — substantial
  outbuildings need application and typically include
  conditions preventing independent letting.

## Conservatories / orangeries / pergolas

- **Pergolas** without solid roof — often outside the
  planning definition;
- **Orangery** with solid roof — likely "building
  operations";
- **Glazed structures** — fall within building
  operations regardless of "conservatory" label.

## Solar panels and heat pumps

- **Solar panels** on a dwelling — typically permitted
  development within size / position limits;
- **Ground-mounted solar** — often needs application;
- **Air-source heat pumps** — permitted in many cases,
  subject to noise and position rules;
- **Conservation area** restrictions apply.

## Driveways and hard standing

- **Permeable hardstanding** for parking — often
  permitted development within size limits;
- **Impermeable hardstanding** — may have specific
  drainage requirements;
- **New vehicular access** to a highway — typically
  needs full planning application and highway approval.

## Garage conversions

- **Internal conversion** of an existing garage to
  habitable space — internal works are generally
  outside the planning definition, but:
  - **Building approval** is needed for habitable-space
    standards;
  - **Loss of parking** may be a planning issue if it
    triggers conditions on the original consent;
- **External alterations** (new windows, doors) — may
  need application.

## Change of use

A common question: can I rent my house on AirBnB?

- **Occasional letting** — typically not material;
- **Regular short-term commercial letting** — likely a
  material change of use to "C3" (or similar
  short-let class) requiring permission;
- **Jersey has been tightening** rules in this area to
  protect housing supply;
- See [`change-of-use.md`](./change-of-use.md).

## Conservation areas and listed buildings

Everything narrows substantially:

- **Permitted-development rights restricted**;
- **External works** typically need consent;
- **Materials and design** scrutinised;
- **Listed building consent** is separate from planning
  permission — both may be needed;
- **Demolition** typically requires consent.

See [`listed-buildings.md`](./listed-buildings.md) and
[`conservation-areas.md`](./conservation-areas.md).

## Practical sequence

For a typical Jersey extension:

1. **Check** if it's permitted development (limits, area,
   distances);
2. **If unclear**, apply for a **Certificate of Lawful
   Development**;
3. **If outside PD**, take **pre-application advice**;
4. **Engage** an architect / planning consultant for
   anything beyond the most modest;
5. **Submit application** with complete drawings and
   statements;
6. **Receive decision** in 8 weeks (target);
7. **Apply for building approval** separately;
8. **Wait** the 28-day appeal window before commencement;
9. **Build to approved drawings only**;
10. **Notify** completion for sign-off.

## Pitfalls

- **Assuming** "small" = permitted (the GD Order has
  specific limits, often more restrictive than
  intuition suggests);
- **Ignoring** the conservation-area / listed-building
  overlay;
- **Building before** appeal window closes — third-party
  appeal could overturn approval;
- **Forgetting** building approval — both are needed;
- **Departing** from approved drawings — even small
  changes can attract enforcement;
- **Title covenants** — private restrictive covenants
  can prohibit works even if planning grants them.
