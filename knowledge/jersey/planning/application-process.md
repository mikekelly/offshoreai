# Planning Application Process

## Step-by-step

A standard planning application runs through:

| Step | What happens | Timing |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Pre-application advice** (free drop-in or paid pre-app) | Days-to-weeks |
| 2 | **Application submitted** with fee, drawings, supporting documents | — |
| 3 | **Validation** by the Department | 1-2 weeks |
| 4 | **Public notice** on-site sign + Jersey Gazette listing | 21 days |
| 5 | **Consultation** — neighbours, statutory consultees | 21 days (overlapping notice) |
| 6 | **Officer assessment** — desktop review, site visit, design assessment | Within target |
| 7 | **Decision** — approval, refusal, or conditional approval | 8 weeks (minor) / 13 weeks (major) target |
| 8 | **Decision notice issued** | — |
| 9 | **28-day appeal window** | 28 days post-decision |

## Step 1 — Pre-application advice

Strongly recommended for anything beyond the most minor
works:

- **Free drop-in** service at the Department for the
  Environment;
- **Paid pre-application** with formal written advice
  for complex schemes;
- **Architect involvement** typically valuable for
  extensions / new builds;
- **Engagement** with the planning officer reduces the
  risk of refusal and clarifies what evidence is needed.

## Step 2 — The application

A complete application typically requires:

- **Application form** (paper or via the planning
  portal);
- **Site plan** showing location, boundaries, access;
- **Existing and proposed plans / elevations**;
- **Existing and proposed floor plans**;
- **Supporting design and access statement** for
  non-trivial schemes;
- **Specialist reports** for sensitive sites (heritage
  impact, ecology, drainage);
- **Application fee** — based on type and scale (e.g.
  small dwellings approx. £5/m²; satellite dishes
  approx. £58).

## Step 3 — Validation

The Department checks the application is complete:

- Correct fee;
- All required drawings and documents;
- Ownership and notice requirements addressed.

Invalid applications are returned, restarting the
process.

## Step 4 — Public notice

Once validated:

- A **public notice site board** is placed at the
  property for **21 days**;
- The application is **listed in the Jersey Gazette**;
- The application is **published on the planning portal**;
- Neighbours **directly affected** receive written
  notice.

## Step 5 — Consultation

Statutory and discretionary consultees comment within the
21-day period. Common consultees:

- **Neighbours** (within proximity defined by the
  Department);
- **Parish authorities** (Connétable / parish officials);
- **Statutory bodies** — Historic Environment Team
  (for listed / conservation), Transport, Environmental
  Health;
- **Special-interest** consultees — Société Jersiaise,
  Jersey Heritage, National Trust for Jersey, etc.;
- **The public** — anyone can comment.

Objections must be **specific** — generic "I don't like
it" objections carry less weight than objections
referring to specific Island Plan policies or material
considerations.

## Step 6 — Officer assessment

The planning officer:

- **Reviews** the application against the Island Plan;
- **Considers** objections and consultation responses;
- **Visits the site** in most cases;
- **Considers design** — referral to the **Independent
  Architecture Commission** for significant schemes;
- **Negotiates amendments** with the applicant if needed;
- **Prepares a recommendation** report.

## Step 7 — Decision

Three possible outcomes:

- **Approval** — application granted;
- **Conditional approval** — granted subject to specific
  conditions (most common);
- **Refusal** — application denied, with reasons stated.

**Decision-maker**:

| Application | Decision-maker |
|---|---|
| Routine, no significant objections | **Officer** under delegated authority |
| Politically significant, public interest, controversial | **Planning Committee** (panel of States members) |
| Departure from Island Plan; called-in | **Minister for the Environment** |

## Step 8 — Decision notice

The decision notice includes:

- The decision (approval / refusal / conditional);
- **Conditions** if granted conditionally — must comply
  with each;
- **Reasons** if refused;
- **Time limits** for commencement (typically 3 years
  from decision);
- **Appeal information**.

## Step 9 — Appeals (28 days)

Within 28 days of the decision:

- **Applicant** may appeal a refusal or onerous
  conditions;
- **Third parties** may appeal against an approval
  (limited grounds);
- **Royal Court** judicial-review route exists for
  procedural challenges.

See [`appeals.md`](./appeals.md).

## Common conditions

Standard planning conditions include:

- **Commencement** within 3 years;
- **Materials and finishes** to match approved samples;
- **Landscaping** scheme to be implemented;
- **Hours of construction**;
- **Highway visibility splays**;
- **Drainage**;
- **Working from approved drawings only**;
- **Compliance with approved boundary treatment**.

Conditions are **legally binding** — breach is an
enforcement matter.

## Building approval — separately

Once you have planning permission, you also need
**building approval** under the Building Bye-Laws 2007.
This is a **separate process** with:

- Different fees;
- Different decision-maker;
- Different timelines;
- Technical (not aesthetic) focus.

You **cannot start work** until both consents are in
place. See [`building-bye-laws.md`](./building-bye-laws.md).

## Fees

Application fees scale by:

- **Type of development**;
- **Floor area** (typically £/m²);
- **Number of dwellings**;
- **Special categories** (advertisements, listed
  building consent, change of use).

Fees are non-refundable if the application is refused.

## Timelines — what to expect

Indicative real-world timelines:

| Stage | Minor application | Major application |
|---|---|---|
| Pre-application | 2-4 weeks | 4-8 weeks |
| Submission to validation | 1-2 weeks | 2-3 weeks |
| Validation to decision target | 8 weeks | 13 weeks |
| Realistic decision (target sometimes missed) | 8-12 weeks | 13-26 weeks |
| Appeal window | 28 days | 28 days |
| Total: idea → safe-to-start | **3-5 months** | **6-12 months** |

Plan accordingly. Don't enter contracts assuming a
planning permission you don't yet have.

## Pitfalls

- **Starting work** before both planning and building
  consents — enforcement risk;
- **Submitting incomplete** application — restarts the
  validation clock;
- **Treating permitted-development limits casually** —
  exceeding by a small amount still needs full
  application;
- **Ignoring consultation responses** — better to
  engage and address than to ignore;
- **Missing conditions** in granted approvals — each
  condition is binding;
- **Missing the 28-day appeal window**.
