# The Island Plan

## What it is

The **Island Plan** is Jersey's statutory planning policy
framework. It sets the policies against which all planning
applications are assessed — what's allowed, where, and on
what terms.

The current Island Plan is the **2022 Island Plan**,
adopted by the States Assembly and reviewed periodically.

## Status

Under the Planning and Building (Jersey) Law 2002, the
Island Plan:

- Is a **statutory document** with legal force;
- Must be **considered** in every planning decision;
- Sets the **default position** — applications complying
  with policies are favoured;
- Allows for **departures** in specific circumstances
  decided by the Minister.

## Key themes (2022 Island Plan)

The 2022 Plan addresses:

- **Housing supply** — meeting demand without losing
  countryside / amenity;
- **Affordable housing** — policies on contributions and
  delivery;
- **Climate emergency** — energy efficiency, net-zero
  alignment;
- **Countryside protection** — the Green Zone, Coastal
  National Park, key landscape areas;
- **Heritage** — listed buildings, conservation areas,
  archaeological sites;
- **Town centres** — St Helier and the parish villages;
- **Economic development** — commercial, agricultural,
  tourism;
- **Transport** — encouraging active travel, restricting
  car-dependent development;
- **Infrastructure** — drainage, utilities, schools.

## Geographical zoning

The Island is divided into policy zones:

- **Built-up area** — where the bulk of development is
  directed;
- **Green Zone** — countryside; tight restrictions on
  development;
- **Coastal National Park** — additional protections for
  the coast;
- **Coastal Edge Zone** — transitional protection;
- **Areas of Special Site Importance / Special Landscape
  Importance**.

Each zone has specific policies determining what
development is acceptable.

## Specific policy areas

The Plan contains policies on:

- **Extensions** — design, scale, materials;
- **New dwellings** — supply allocations, design standards;
- **Conversions** — agricultural to residential, office
  to residential;
- **Outbuildings** — scale, position, materials;
- **Listed buildings** — sensitive alterations;
- **Conservation areas** — preserving character;
- **Heritage** — archaeological evaluation;
- **Trees** — protection, replacement;
- **Drainage and flooding** — surface-water management;
- **Energy** — solar, heat pumps, wind;
- **Parking** — standards by use class;
- **Highways** — visibility, access design.

## How decisions use the Plan

A planning officer assessing an application:

1. **Identifies the relevant policies**;
2. **Tests** the application against each;
3. **Weighs** compliance / non-compliance against
   material considerations;
4. **Reaches** a recommendation.

Where an application **departs** from the Plan, the
default is **refusal** — unless the Minister exercises
the power to grant a departure on stated grounds (typically
in the wider public interest).

## Updating the Plan

The Island Plan is reviewed periodically — typically every
10 years with intermediate revisions. The process involves:

- **Public consultation** at multiple stages;
- **Examination** by an independent inspector;
- **States Assembly adoption** by debate and vote;
- **Implementation** including transitional arrangements.

## Practical implications for applicants

For a planning applicant:

- **Read the relevant policies** before applying;
- **Demonstrate compliance** in your supporting
  statement;
- **Address non-compliance** head-on (mitigations,
  exceptional circumstances);
- **Pre-application advice** is most useful where Island
  Plan compliance is borderline;
- **Departures** are possible but require strong
  justification.

For an objector:

- **Cite specific policies** in your objection;
- **Material considerations** matter; generic objection
  carries little weight;
- **Specific evidence** strengthens the case.

## Where to find it

- The full Island Plan is published on **gov.je**;
- A summary and policy maps are available;
- The **planning portal** provides policy references on
  individual applications.
