# Immigration & Residency — Jersey

Jersey controls access to housing and to local employment through the
**Control of Housing and Work (Jersey) Law 2012**, which establishes
the four residential and employment status categories — **Entitled**,
**Licensed**, **Entitled for Work**, and **Registered**. Individual
status determines whether a person can buy or rent particular
categories of property and whether an employer can employ them. The
**2(1)(e) High-Value Residency (HVR)** route grants Licensed (and in
practice Entitled-style) status to incoming high-net-worth
individuals, with a bespoke tax regime — see
[tax/high-value-residency-tax.md](../tax/high-value-residency-tax.md).
