# CHWL 2012 Part 5 — Enforcement

## Articles 27-30 — Criminal offences

The Law creates **criminal offences** for:

- **Occupying** qualified property without proper status or
  consent;
- **Letting** qualified property to unqualified tenants;
- **Employing** persons without proper status;
- **Working** without proper status;
- **False statements** in applications;
- **Failure** to comply with notices.

## Articles 31-33 — Civil penalties

The Population Office may impose **civil financial
penalties**:

- **Calibrated** to severity;
- **Available** for compliance breaches;
- **Faster** process than criminal;
- **Mitigation** for cooperation;
- **Subject to** appeal.

## Article 34 — Eviction

Where occupation is unlawful:

- **Eviction orders** can be sought through the Royal Court;
- **Specific** notice and procedural requirements;
- **Compensation** considerations for innocent occupiers;
- **Time** for relocation.

## Article 35 — Recovery of property

- **Civil** recovery of property occupied unlawfully;
- **Compensation** for loss to Population Office costs;
- **Specific** procedural rules.

## Article 36 — Appeals

Decisions appealable to **Royal Court**:

- **Refusal** of consent / permission;
- **Civil penalties**;
- **Status** determinations;
- **Other** Population Office decisions;
- **Procedural fairness** standards.

## Articles 37-38 — Enforcement bodies

Enforcement is conducted by:

- **Population Office** principally;
- **Population Office** investigators;
- **States Police** in serious cases;
- **AG** for criminal prosecutions;
- **Royal Court** for orders and appeals.

## Practical enforcement themes

Common themes in CHWL enforcement:

- **Landlords** letting to unqualified tenants;
- **Employers** failing to verify worker status;
- **Workers** continuing past licence expiry;
- **Sham marriages** to acquire status;
- **Status documentation** fraud.
