# High-Value Residency — Jersey 2(1)(e) Route

The **High-Value Residency (HVR)** route — formally **regulation
2(1)(e)** under the Control of Housing and Work (Jersey)
Regulations 2013 — is Jersey's **principal inward-migration
route** for **high-net-worth individuals**.

## What 2(1)(e) provides

A 2(1)(e) applicant who is approved becomes:

- **Licensed** under the Control of Housing and Work Law;
- **Able to acquire residential property** in Jersey (which is
  otherwise restricted);
- **Subject to a bespoke tax regime** — see
  [`../tax/high-value-residency-tax.md`](../tax/high-value-residency-tax.md);
- **Permitted to bring** immediate family (with appropriate
  derivative status).

## Eligibility criteria

The Government's published criteria typically include:

- **Significant economic and social contribution** to Jersey;
- A specified **annual Jersey tax contribution** (a minimum-tax
  floor, set in negotiation);
- Acceptable **source of wealth** and reputation;
- Acquisition of **qualifying residential property** within
  prescribed timeframes;
- **Personal characteristics** appropriate to Jersey residence
  (good character, no relevant criminal record, etc.).

## Process

Applications typically flow through:

- **Initial enquiry** with **Locate Jersey** — the Government
  body marketing Jersey to inward investors;
- **Detailed application** with supporting documentation;
- **Due diligence** by Locate Jersey, the Population Office, and
  relevant departments;
- **Submission** to the **Chief Minister** for approval;
- **Approval** subject to compliance with conditions;
- **Property acquisition** and **registration** of status.

## Tax negotiation

The **annual minimum tax** floor is **negotiated** as part of
the 2(1)(e) approval — typically a minimum amount tied to
projected Jersey-taxable income at the **bespoke HVR rates**
(see the tax file).

## Why HVR matters

The HVR route is a **significant contributor** to Jersey's
economy:

- **Tax revenue** from HVR residents at the negotiated minimum;
- **Investment** in Jersey real estate;
- **Connections** brought to the Jersey financial sector by
  HVR residents (often international business owners, family-
  office principals);
- **Lifestyle and cultural** contributions to the community.

It is also **politically sensitive** — balancing the **economic
contribution** of new HVR residents against **local concerns**
about property prices and population.
