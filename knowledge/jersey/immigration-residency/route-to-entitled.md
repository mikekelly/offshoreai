# Route to Entitled Status — Jersey

A person arriving in Jersey as **Registered** can progress
**through Entitled for Work to Entitled** over qualifying
periods of Jersey residence.

## The progression

The typical progression:

1. **Registered** (Year 1) — short-term status, limited housing.
2. **Continuing residence** with qualifying employment — accruing
   qualifying time.
3. **Entitled for Work** — typically after a prescribed period
   (commonly **5+ years** of qualifying residence).
4. **Entitled** — typically after **10+ years** of continuous
   qualifying residence.

The exact qualifying periods and conditions are set out in the
**Control of Housing and Work (Residential and Employment
Status) (Jersey) Regulations** as updated from time to time.

## Qualifying residence

Not all residence counts:

- **Periods of absence** beyond prescribed thresholds may
  **interrupt** the qualifying period;
- **Unauthorised residence** does not count;
- **Specific exclusions** may apply for certain status changes.

## Why it matters

- **Long-term residents** of Jersey gain progressively more
  housing and employment access;
- The system provides **predictability** for those investing
  long-term in Jersey;
- It **distinguishes** between short-term migrants and committed
  residents;
- It **rewards** continuity and contribution to the Island.

## Practical points

- **Documentation** of residence is essential — keep records of
  presence, employment, addresses;
- **Population Office records** form the primary basis for
  status determinations;
- **Status applications** are submitted through the Population
  Office with supporting evidence.
