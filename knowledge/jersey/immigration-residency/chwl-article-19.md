# CHWL 2012 Article 19 — Restrictions on Employment

Article 19 governs **access to Jersey employment** by status
category.

## The provision

Employment in Jersey is **subject to status restrictions**:

- **Entitled** and **Entitled for work** — full work rights;
- **Licensed** — work for specified employer / sector in
  accordance with licence;
- **Registered** — work permission tied to specific
  employer / business;
- **Employer obligations** to verify status.

## Employer obligations

Employers must:

- **Verify** each employee's status before engagement;
- **Maintain** records of status verification;
- **Not employ** persons without proper status;
- **Notify** Population Office of new hires in some
  circumstances;
- **Comply** with sectoral quotas in some sectors.

## Sectoral employment

Certain sectors have **specific employment rules**:

- **Construction** — registered-status workers possible
  subject to permits;
- **Hospitality** — seasonal worker rules;
- **Agriculture** — seasonal permits;
- **Specific** essential-worker sectors with sponsorship.

## Penalties

Employing without proper status:

- **Civil penalties** on the employer;
- **Criminal offences** in serious cases;
- **Repeat offenders** — escalating sanctions;
- **Contracts** of employment may be unenforceable;
- **Reporting** by competent authorities.

## Process for permission

For a non-Entitled person to take Jersey employment:

- **Employer** identifies a specific role;
- **Application** to Population Office (sponsored by employer);
- **Demonstrate** the role's importance to Jersey economy;
- **Verify** no qualified Jersey-resident candidate available;
- **Permission** granted with conditions;
- **Time-limited** typically.

## Specific scenarios

### Essential worker sponsorship

- **Employer** sponsors a foreign worker for specific role;
- **Application** to Population Office;
- **Specific** roles in shortage sectors (healthcare,
  specialist financial services, hospitality);
- **Licensed status** granted to the employee.

### HVR employment

- **HVR** Licensed status allows certain employment;
- **Cannot** displace local workers;
- **Specific** restrictions on role types;
- **Typically** investment / business activity vs traditional
  employment.

### Cross-border workers

- **Workers commuting** from UK / France to Jersey — specific
  rules;
- **Subject to** all status requirements regardless of
  residence;
- **Special** treatment for Channel Islands ferry routes.

### Self-employment / business

- **Self-employment** is "employment" for CHWL purposes;
- **Business** activities subject to specific permissions;
- **Limited** to status-permitted activities;
- **Business licence** under separate regime.
