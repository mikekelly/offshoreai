# CHWL 2012 Article 10 — Restrictions on Residential Property

Article 10 is the **operative provision** restricting access
to Jersey residential property by status category.

## The provision

Residential property in Jersey is **subject to status
restrictions**:

- **Qualified property** — restricted to **Entitled** persons
  (and specific other categories with consent);
- **Other categories** of property — various restrictions
  per the type and category of property.

## Property types and access

### Qualified property

The largest category:

- **Restricted** to Entitled persons;
- **Entitled** persons can buy, lease, occupy without
  specific consent;
- **Other status** persons need **specific Population Office
  consent** to occupy.

### Lodging accommodation

Specific category:

- **Bedsits, shared rooms** in lodging houses;
- **Available** to all status categories;
- **Registration** of the lodging house required;
- **Specific** standards.

### Non-qualified ("registered") property

Specific category:

- **Available** to all categories typically;
- **Less restrictive** access rules;
- **Lower-value** property generally.

### Specific exemptions

- **Diplomatic** residences;
- **Specific** Crown servant accommodation;
- **Particular** historic exemptions.

## Penalties

Occupying property without proper status / consent:

- **Civil penalties**;
- **Criminal offences** in serious cases;
- **Eviction** orders;
- **Contracts** may be unenforceable.

## Process for consent

A non-Entitled person seeking to occupy qualified property:

- **Application** to Population Office;
- **Justification** (essential employee, HVR, family
  circumstances);
- **Property details** and intended use;
- **Time-limited** consent typically;
- **Conditions** may apply.

## Specific scenarios

### HVR purchase

- HVR (Licensed) can purchase specific qualified property
  under Article 135A regime;
- **Subject to** HVR-specific approval;
- **Property** generally above prescribed value threshold;
- **Conditions** on principal residence.

### Employee accommodation

- Employer-sponsored accommodation for Licensed / Registered
  staff;
- **Specific** approval routes;
- **Time-limited** with employer relationship.

### Foreign investment

- Non-resident purchase of Jersey residential property is
  **substantially restricted**;
- **HVR** route is the principal channel for foreign buyers;
- **Commercial** property has different (less restrictive)
  rules.

## Real estate market implications

The CHWL housing controls:

- **Two-tier** Jersey property market (qualified vs non-
  qualified);
- **Significant price** differentials;
- **HVR** market for high-value qualified property;
- **Specific** Jersey legal advice essential for any
  acquisition.
