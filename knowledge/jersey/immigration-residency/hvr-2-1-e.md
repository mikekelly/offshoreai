# 2(1)(e) High-Value Residency — The HVR Route in Detail

## What 2(1)(e) refers to

"**2(1)(e)**" refers to **Article 2(1)(e) of the Income Tax
(Jersey) Law 1961** (in earlier statutory references) — the
provision that granted **Licensed** status for high-value
residents based on prospective tax contribution.

The modern HVR regime sits under:

- **Control of Housing and Work (Jersey) Law 2012** — for
  status (Licensed) and housing rights;
- **Income Tax (Jersey) Law 1961 Articles 135A-135B** (and
  Schedule 7) — for the special tax treatment;
- **Specific JFSC / Comptroller Orders** for procedural rules.

## Eligibility

To qualify as HVR:

### Financial requirements

- **Minimum prospective Jersey tax** contribution
  (typically **£170,000+ per year**);
- **Minimum global wealth** test (typically £10 million+
  worldwide);
- **Proof of source** of wealth.

### Personal requirements

- **Good character**;
- **No regulatory** or criminal concerns;
- **Genuine** intention to make Jersey principal residence;
- **Substance** of presence in Jersey (typically 6+ months
  per year).

### Economic / community contribution

- **Skills, experience, or business** that benefits Jersey;
- **Charitable / community** engagement potential;
- **Specific** sectoral preferences.

## Process

### Pre-application

- **Initial enquiry** with Population Office;
- **Indicative** view on likelihood of approval;
- **Identification** of suitable property to acquire;
- **Pre-application** documentation gathering.

### Application

- **Formal application** to Population Office;
- **Comprehensive** documentation:
  - Personal background;
  - Wealth and source-of-wealth;
  - Tax-contribution undertaking;
  - Intended residence patterns;
  - Property acquisition plans.
- **CDD** by Population Office;
- **Liaison** with Comptroller of Revenue (tax) and JFSC.

### Approval

- **Licensed status** granted (with HVR conditions);
- **Approval** to acquire qualified property;
- **Specific** tax regime applicable;
- **Conditions** on ongoing presence and contribution.

## Tax regime

HVR Licensed persons benefit from:

### Headline rate

- **20% income tax** on **first £1,250,000** of taxable
  income;
- **1% income tax** on income above that threshold;
- **Effective** rate decreases as income grows.

### What's taxable

- **All Jersey-source** income (rental, employment);
- **Worldwide** income subject to specific treatments;
- **Foreign-source** income subject to **specific exemptions**
  / treatments;
- **Income from trusts** subject to specific rules.

### Other taxes

- **No** capital gains tax in Jersey generally;
- **No** inheritance tax in Jersey;
- **GST** at standard rates on goods / services in Jersey.

### Tax planning

- **HVR + Jersey trust** structure common for asset
  protection;
- **Foreign-source income** carefully structured;
- **Foreign tax** planning coordinated.

## Property requirements

HVR Licensed status permits property acquisition:

- **Qualified property** at or above **prescribed value**
  (typically £1.75 million+);
- **Principal residence** in Jersey;
- **No** second-home rights typically;
- **Subject to** specific conditions on use.

## Ongoing obligations

HVR persons must:

- **Maintain** sufficient Jersey presence (typically 6+
  months per year);
- **Pay** the agreed minimum tax annually;
- **Comply** with Jersey laws generally;
- **Notify** material changes (income, residence patterns,
  family changes);
- **Subject to** periodic review.

## Loss of HVR status

HVR status can be lost through:

- **Insufficient presence** in Jersey;
- **Failure** to meet minimum tax contribution;
- **Material** change in circumstances;
- **Specific** breaches of conditions;
- **Voluntary** surrender.

## Numbers and policy

Jersey's HVR programme:

- **Limited** approvals per year (typically 30-50);
- **Selective** based on overall Jersey benefit;
- **Subject to** political considerations;
- **Periodic** policy review.
