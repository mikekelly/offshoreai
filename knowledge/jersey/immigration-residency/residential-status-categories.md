# Residential Status Categories — Jersey

The Control of Housing and Work (Jersey) Law 2012 establishes
**four residential status categories** that determine **what
housing a person may access** in Jersey.

## The categories

### Entitled

- The **most established** status;
- Typically achieved by **10+ years of residence** in Jersey;
- Can **buy and lease** essentially any property;
- **Permanent** unless lost through extended absence.

### Licensed

- Granted to **incoming high-skill workers** essential to the
  Jersey economy;
- **Linked to employment** — the licensee must remain in the
  qualifying employment;
- Can access **most housing** but with some restrictions;
- Includes **2(1)(e) HVR** (High-Value Residency) status for
  qualifying high-net-worth individuals.

### Entitled for Work

- Allows **work access** but does not grant the same housing
  rights as Entitled;
- Typical examples: spouses of Entitled persons; some long-term
  Registered persons reaching prescribed thresholds.

### Registered

- The **default** status for new arrivals;
- **Limited housing access** — generally restricted to specific
  rental property categories;
- **Not eligible** to buy property;
- **Time-limited** progression to Entitled-for-Work / Entitled
  available subject to qualifying conditions.

## Housing implications

| Status | Can buy property? | Can rent any property? |
|---|---|---|
| Entitled | Yes — any | Yes — any |
| Licensed | Generally not | Most categories accessible (varies) |
| Entitled for Work | Limited | Most categories |
| Registered | No | Limited to specific categories |

## Why this matters

The status system effectively **regulates the housing market**:

- **Entitled-status properties** are the most accessible (the
  general Jersey housing market);
- **Registered-status properties** are a much narrower market
  segment;
- **Investment / second-home** acquisition by foreigners is
  largely **closed** absent HVR.
