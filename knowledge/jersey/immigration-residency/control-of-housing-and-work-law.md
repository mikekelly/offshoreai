# Control of Housing and Work (Jersey) Law 2012

The **Control of Housing and Work (Jersey) Law 2012** is the
central statute governing **who may live and work in Jersey**.
It addresses two interlinked questions:

- **Housing** — which residential property may a person
  buy / lease / occupy?
- **Work** — which jobs may a person undertake or be employed
  in?

## Why Jersey has these controls

Jersey is a **small island with limited housing supply** and a
**capped working population**. Without controls:

- **Inward migration** would drive up housing costs;
- **Local employment opportunities** would be reduced for
  long-term residents;
- The **population balance** between long-term residents and
  newcomers would be hard to manage.

The 2012 Law calibrates **inward migration and housing access**
through a four-category status system.

## The four status categories

- **Entitled** — long-term Jersey residents who can access most
  housing and most work;
- **Licensed** — high-skill / essentially-employed migrants
  granted housing access for the duration of their employment;
- **Entitled for Work** — persons with work access (typically
  spouses of Entitled persons, or persons meeting prescribed
  qualifying criteria);
- **Registered** — short-term and lower-priority status with
  limited housing access.

(See [`residential-status-categories.md`](residential-status-categories.md)
for the housing implications and
[`employment-status-categories.md`](employment-status-categories.md)
for the work implications.)

## Administration

The Law is administered by:

- The **Population Office** of the Government of Jersey;
- **Licensing** decisions by Ministers under prescribed
  delegations;
- **Locate Jersey** as the conduit for high-skill / HVR
  applications.

## Enforcement

Operating outside the 2012 Law's status system is an offence:

- **Employers** face penalties for hiring without proper status;
- **Landlords** face penalties for letting to unqualified
  persons;
- **Individuals** may face status revocation.

The Law has **teeth** and is enforced.
