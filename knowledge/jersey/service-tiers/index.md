# Service Tiers — Wealth-Management Standardised Menus

## What this section is

A set of **industry-typical service-tier descriptions** for
Jersey-based wealth-management businesses, organised by
client-asset range. The aim is to give an agent (or an RM
preparing for a new-client conversation) a reference for
**what the typical menu looks like at each tier**, without
endorsing any specific firm's commercial offering.

Per the [tenant-neutrality rule](../../CONVENTIONS.md#tenant-neutrality):
these files describe **industry-typical** tiers, not any
specific firm's pricing or product menu. Each tenant's actual
service offering and pricing lives in the per-tenant
configuration layer, not in the canonical corpus.

## Why this surface exists

Three uses:

1. **New-business preparation** — an RM approaching a new
   prospect can calibrate the conversation to the prospect's
   tier;
2. **Existing-client review** — periodic relationship reviews
   can check whether the client is on the right service tier
   (under- and over-served are both commercial issues);
3. **Cross-sell / upsell triggers** — when a client crosses
   a tier boundary (e.g. through an inheritance or liquidity
   event), the appropriate menu shift becomes a conversation.

## The three industry-typical tiers

The Jersey wealth-management market broadly operates three
tiers, with significant overlap and firm-specific variation.
The asset-range bands are **industry-typical, not
prescriptive**.

| Tier | Asset range (industry-typical) | File |
|---|---|---|
| **Mass affluent / Standard** | £100k – £1m investable | [`standard-mass-affluent.md`](./standard-mass-affluent.md) |
| **HNW Standard** | £1m – £25m investable | [`standard-hnw.md`](./standard-hnw.md) |
| **Bespoke UHNW** | £25m+ investable | [`bespoke-uhnw.md`](./bespoke-uhnw.md) |

The boundaries are soft; clients near tier boundaries are
often served on bespoke terms.
