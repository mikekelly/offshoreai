# Service Tier — Bespoke UHNW

## Client profile

Industry-typical: **£25m+ investable assets**. Often:

- Multi-generational family enterprises;
- Founders / industrialists post-major-realisation;
- Royal / political families (with PEP and sanctions
  considerations);
- Single-family offices and embedded multi-family-office
  service;
- Family-business succession scenarios with operating
  businesses.

This tier is the **deepest practice in private wealth** —
multi-decade relationships, multi-generational family
conversations, multi-jurisdictional structures.

## Service menu — industry-typical

### Core services

- **Full family-office service** — coordination across legal,
  tax, accounting, investment, banking, philanthropy, art
  collections, real-estate portfolios, family-business
  governance;
- **Multi-generational structuring** — Jersey trust over
  holding companies for the family business; succession
  vehicles for next-generation transfer; protector / family
  council architecture;
- **Bespoke investment-management mandates** including
  direct alternatives, private credit, real assets, art and
  collectibles, sometimes including operating-business
  oversight;
- **Embedded compliance and reporting** across the family's
  jurisdictions;
- **Dedicated relationship-management team** — typically a
  named partner / principal plus team (10–20 clients per
  team at this tier);
- **Concierge / lifestyle services** — at the client's
  request, can extend to property management, employment of
  family staff, travel, education coordination, security;
- **Strategic-conversation cadence** — quarterly or more
  frequent meetings on family governance and major
  decisions.

### Distinctive services at this tier

- **Family-office set-up** — increasingly clients have an
  in-house family office, and the wealth-management business
  provides specific services rather than full discretion;
- **Royal Court applications** — momentous decisions
  ([Article 51](../trusts/article-51-directions.md))
  routinely involve Court blessing;
- **Inter-generational governance facilitation** — formal
  family-board meetings, family constitution drafting,
  next-generation engagement programmes;
- **Public-relations / reputation management** support;
- **Philanthropic-structure** management (Jersey foundations
  used for philanthropic vehicles).

## Typical structures used

- **Jersey trust over Jersey holding company over operating
  businesses** — the canonical family-business-succession
  pattern (see [`../sectors/family-business-succession.md`](../sectors/family-business-succession.md));
- **Multiple trusts** — distinct trusts for distinct family
  branches or distinct asset classes;
- **Jersey foundation** for civil-law-flavour family
  preferences or for purpose-trust applications (philanthropic
  endowments, image-rights vehicles);
- **Private Trust Company** (PTC) where family control of
  trustee is wanted (see
  [`../trusts/private-trust-company.md`](../trusts/private-trust-company.md));
- **Multi-jurisdictional underlying** — Cayman / BVI / Bermuda
  companies for specific asset classes;
- **Cell company** structures (PCC / ICC) for segregated
  family-branch assets.

## Common conversations

- **Family-business succession** — the deepest conversation,
  typically spans multiple years and multiple advisers;
- **Royal Court blessing applications** — for momentous trust
  decisions;
- **Cross-jurisdictional substance management** as family
  members move countries;
- **Pillar Two / MCIT** implications for family-business
  groups crossing thresholds;
- **Family disputes** — Trust litigation, beneficiary
  information rights, protector challenges;
- **Philanthropy** — Jersey foundations as endowment
  vehicles;
- **Reputation events** — public profile of family members
  triggering compliance and family-governance issues.

## Cross-tier movements

This is the top tier. Movement is laterally to other UHNW
providers (competitive moves) or to in-house family-office
structures (where the wealth manager continues to provide
specific services).
