# Social Security (Jersey) Law 1974 — Overview

## What the law does

The **Social Security (Jersey) Law 1974** (SSJL 1974)
establishes Jersey's principal **contributory** social-
insurance scheme. It works on the classic contribution-
benefit model:

- **Workers and employers** pay contributions;
- **Contribution record** establishes entitlement to
  benefits;
- **Benefits** are paid against contribution credits, not
  on a means-tested basis.

It sits in parallel with the **Income Support (Jersey)
Law 2007** (means-tested scheme) — most adults engage
with both at different points in life.

## Benefits funded

The contributory fund pays for:

- **Old-age pension** — the principal Jersey state
  pension;
- **Survivor's pension** — for spouse / civil partner on
  death;
- **Short-term incapacity allowance** — for illness or
  injury preventing work;
- **Long-term incapacity allowance** — for sustained
  incapacity;
- **Maternity allowance and grant** — for parents;
- **Death grant** — lump sum on death;
- **Other** specified benefits per regulation.

## Contributions

### Who pays

- **Employees**: Class 1 contributions deducted by the
  employer;
- **Employers**: matching contributions on the employee;
- **Self-employed**: Class 2 contributions paid directly
  by the individual;
- **Non-employed** (Class 3): voluntary contributions
  available in narrow circumstances.

### Rates (indicative — check current rates)

- **Employees**: **6%** on earnings up to the monthly
  earnings cap;
- **Employers**: **6.5%** on the same earnings;
- **Self-employed**: **12.5%** (covering both halves) on
  earnings within the cap;
- **Monthly earnings cap**: approximately **£4,610** as
  of recent rates (revised periodically — check current
  figure).

Contributions are **not** payable above the cap — meaning
the system has a regressive structure relative to total
earnings.

### Long-Term Care contribution

A **separate** contribution — the **Long-Term Care
contribution** — is levied alongside Social Security at
a different rate, funding the LTC scheme. See
[`long-term-care.md`](../tax/long-term-care.md).

## Pension structure — contribution-based

Jersey old-age pension is based on **the period for which
contributions are made, not the amount**. To get a full
pension, a worker needs a specific number of qualifying
years (currently in the order of 45 years for a full
pension; partial pensions for shorter records).

This differs from some defined-benefit schemes that pay
proportional to earnings. Two workers with very different
earnings but identical contribution records receive the
same pension.

## Pension age

- **Current pension age**: rising on a published schedule;
- **Variable** depending on year of birth;
- **Deferral** option — defer pension to receive higher
  rate;
- **Early drawing** option — narrow circumstances.

## Short-term incapacity allowance

Payable to workers who:

- **Are unable to work** because of illness or injury;
- **Have contributed** sufficiently in the relevant
  contribution year;
- **Provide medical certification**;
- **Have not exhausted** the benefit period (typical
  maximum around a year).

Long-term incapacity follows after specified short-term
periods.

## Maternity benefits

- **Maternity allowance** — paid to the mother for a
  prescribed period around birth;
- **Maternity grant** — lump sum on birth (subject to
  contribution conditions);
- **Adoption grant**;
- **Paternity / partner** benefits per the modern
  amendments.

## Reciprocal arrangements

Jersey has **reciprocal agreements** with various
jurisdictions covering social security:

- **UK** — reciprocal arrangement allowing contribution
  recognition;
- **Guernsey** — close reciprocal arrangement;
- **Specific** EU and Commonwealth countries;
- **EU post-Brexit** — separate arrangements with
  individual member states or via UK.

A worker who has worked in multiple jurisdictions may be
able to combine contribution records.

## Administration

Customer and Local Services (CLS) administers:

- **Contribution collection** (via Revenue Jersey);
- **Benefit payment**;
- **Records** of contributions and entitlement;
- **Decisions** on claims;
- **Reciprocal arrangements** liaison.

## Appeals

A decision can be:

- **Reviewed** internally;
- **Appealed** to the **Social Security Tribunal**;
- **Further appealed** to the Royal Court on points of
  law.

## Comparison with Income Support

| Feature | Social Security 1974 | Income Support 2007 |
|---|---|---|
| Basis | Contribution record | Means test (income + assets) |
| Eligibility | Worked / contributed | Resident + needs |
| Benefits | Pension, illness, maternity, death | Single benefit covering housing, living, health, childcare |
| Means tested? | No (subject to specific rules) | Yes |
| Time-limited? | Some benefits time-limited (incapacity); pension lifetime | Continues while eligibility met |
| Affected by partner / household | Specific rules | Household basis |

Both schemes can apply to the same person at different
life stages (or simultaneously) — typical for a low-
earning pensioner who has paid contributions but whose
pension is below the income-support threshold.
