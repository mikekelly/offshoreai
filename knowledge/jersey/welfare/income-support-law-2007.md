# Income Support (Jersey) Law 2007 — Overview

## What the law does

The **Income Support (Jersey) Law 2007** (ISL 2007)
established Jersey's principal **means-tested** benefit —
a single benefit that covers a household's needs for:

- **Housing** (rent assistance);
- **Living** costs (general living component);
- **Health** (medical / dental / optical costs);
- **Childcare** (where parents are working).

It replaced a previous patchwork of separate benefits and
is **the** safety-net benefit for Jersey residents whose
income and assets fall below thresholds.

It is administered by **Customer and Local Services**
(CLS), the States department (formerly known as "Social
Security").

## Two parallel systems

Income Support sits alongside the **Social Security
(Jersey) Law 1974** contributory system. The two:

- **IS** is **needs-based** — eligibility depends on
  income and assets being below thresholds;
- **Social Security** is **contribution-based** —
  eligibility depends on contribution record (pensions,
  short-term incapacity, etc.).

A household can be eligible for both; many pensioners
draw a Social Security old-age pension and also Income
Support if the pension is insufficient.

## Eligibility — headline tests

To claim Income Support, every adult in the household
must:

- **Meet the residence test** — 5 years' continuous
  residence in Jersey immediately preceding the claim
  **or** 10 years' cumulative residence at some point
  before;
- **Be in full-time paid work** or **be exempt** from the
  work expectation (specified categories: disability,
  caring responsibilities, certain ages, sickness);
- **Have household income and assets** within the
  thresholds.

See [`income-support-eligibility.md`](income-support-eligibility.md).

## Components of Income Support

The benefit is calculated as the sum of household needs
minus household resources:

**Needs (components):**

- **Adult component** — basic personal allowance per
  adult;
- **Child component** — per child;
- **Housing component** — rent assistance (capped by
  household size and type of accommodation);
- **Disability / special-needs component**;
- **Childcare component** — where parents are working;
- **Medical** component — co-pays at GP, dental,
  optical, prescription;
- **Other** specified components.

**Resources (deductions):**

- Earned income (with disregards for the first portion);
- Other income (pensions, contributory benefits);
- Asset value above thresholds;
- Specific deductions per the regulations.

If **needs > resources**, the difference is paid as
Income Support. If **needs ≤ resources**, the claimant
is not eligible.

## Work expectation

A central feature: every adult in the household is
expected to be in **full-time paid work** unless exempt.
Exemptions include:

- **Pension age**;
- **Disability** preventing work;
- **Sickness** documented by certification;
- **Caring** for a young child or disabled person;
- **Specific** other categories per regulation.

Failure to meet the work expectation without good cause
can result in:

- **Reduced or suspended benefit**;
- **Required engagement** with employment support;
- **Sanctions** for non-cooperation.

The work-expectation framework is a notable feature
distinguishing Jersey's Income Support from passive
income-replacement schemes.

## Special components

The ISL 2007 provides for specific situations:

- **Lone parents** — adjusted work expectations and
  childcare provision;
- **Disabled people** — disability components and
  exemptions;
- **Carers** — caring allowances and exemptions;
- **Maternity / paternity** — alignment with Social
  Security maternity benefits;
- **Cold weather / fuel** payments;
- **Christmas bonus** for specific pensioner claimants.

## Application process

1. **Online eligibility check** at gov.je;
2. **Application form** if eligible;
3. **Documents** — proof of income, identity, residence,
   rent details, household composition;
4. **Interview** at Customer and Local Services if
   required;
5. **Decision** with reasons;
6. **Right of review** and right of appeal to the
   Tribunal.

## Sanctions and overpayments

If a claimant fails to:

- **Report a change of circumstances**;
- **Cooperate with work-expectation requirements**;
- **Be available for** training or work referrals;
- **Provide truthful information**;

CLS may:

- **Suspend or reduce** benefit;
- **Recover overpayments** (deductions, debt claims);
- **Prosecute** for fraud in serious cases.

## Appeals

Decisions can be:

- **Reviewed** internally first;
- **Appealed** to the **Social Security Tribunal**;
- **Further appealed** to the Royal Court on points of
  law.

See [`appeals.md`](appeals.md).

## Practical recommendations

For those who may be eligible:

- **Use the eligibility calculator** at gov.je first;
- **Apply promptly** — benefits can be backdated only
  narrowly;
- **Keep records** of all income, rent, and household
  changes;
- **Engage with work-expectation** requirements
  positively;
- **Report changes** of circumstances within the
  required timeframes;
- **Citizens Advice Jersey** can help with applications.
