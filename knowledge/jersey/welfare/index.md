# Welfare and Social Security — Jersey

## What this section is about

Jersey welfare and social security — the statutes,
contributions, benefits and entitlements that make up the
island's safety net.

Two principal statutes:

- **Social Security (Jersey) Law 1974** — the
  **contributory** scheme. Workers and employers pay
  contributions; pensions, illness, incapacity, maternity,
  and other benefits follow from contribution record;
- **Income Support (Jersey) Law 2007** — the
  **means-tested** scheme. A single benefit covering
  housing, living, health, and childcare needs for those
  whose income and assets are below thresholds.

There are also specific schemes:

- **Long-Term Care (Jersey) Law 2012** — funded by a
  separate payroll-style contribution; pays for care-home
  and home-care needs for those who meet the test;
- **Specific** benefits and grants administered by
  Customer and Local Services (the department, formerly
  Social Security).

## Why this section exists

This is the **fifth gap-fill** from the coverage audit at
[`../COVERAGE-AUDIT.md`](../COVERAGE-AUDIT.md), addressing
gaps **#7** (Income Support 2007) and **#8** (Social
Security 1974). Everyday Jersey residents — pensioners,
new parents, the unemployed, the ill, the disabled, those
on low incomes — depend on these schemes. The corpus had
incidental references in tax and tenancy files but no
substantive coverage.

## At a glance

| Topic | Position |
|---|---|
| Contributory scheme | Social Security (Jersey) Law 1974 |
| Means-tested scheme | Income Support (Jersey) Law 2007 |
| Residence requirement for IS | 5 years continuous or 10 years cumulative |
| Contribution rates (employee / employer) | 6% / 6.5% on earnings up to monthly cap |
| Pension structure | Based on contribution record (not amount) |
| Administering department | Customer and Local Services |
| Appeals | Social Security Tribunal; Royal Court on points of law |
