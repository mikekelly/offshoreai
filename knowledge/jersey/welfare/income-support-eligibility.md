# Income Support Eligibility

## The three tests

Income Support eligibility turns on three concurrent
tests:

1. **Residence test** — must have lived in Jersey for a
   minimum period;
2. **Work expectation test** — must be in full-time work
   or exempt;
3. **Means test** — household income and assets below
   thresholds.

All three must be satisfied. Failing any one defeats the
claim.

## The residence test

To meet the residence requirement, **every adult in the
household** must have:

- **Lived in Jersey continuously for at least 5 years**
  immediately before the claim; **or**
- **Lived in Jersey continuously for at least 10 years**
  at some time in the past.

There is some flexibility:

- If you lived in Jersey continuously for 5+ years in the
  past, **moved away for less than 5 years**, then
  returned and lived here continuously for **longer than
  the time spent away** — you can re-qualify.

The test is **strict** on the "every adult in the
household" point. A couple where one spouse has 4½ years'
residence will typically not qualify until that spouse
hits 5 years.

### What counts as "continuous"

- **Continuous physical presence** with home in Jersey;
- Brief holidays / work trips do not break continuity;
- Long absences (typically 6+ months) typically do break
  continuity;
- Specific rules for students, work secondments, and
  medical treatment abroad.

### Specific exemptions

Some applicants are **exempt** from the residence test:

- **Returning Jersey-born** people who left and have
  returned;
- **Specific** humanitarian arrivals;
- **Refugees** under international protection;
- **Specific** other categories.

## The work expectation

Every adult in the household must be:

- **In full-time paid work**; or
- **Exempt** from the work expectation.

### Full-time

"Full-time" is defined in the regulations — typically a
minimum hours threshold per week. Working at a level below
the threshold means partial work-expectation engagement
(work-search, skills training).

### Exemptions

Common exemption grounds:

- **Pension age** (post-state-pension-age claimants);
- **Disability** preventing work (with appropriate
  evidence);
- **Sickness** with medical certification;
- **Caring** for a child under specified age;
- **Caring** for a disabled or elderly relative;
- **Specific** other categories.

Where one adult is exempt and another is not, the latter
remains subject to the work expectation.

### Active engagement

For adults who are subject to the work expectation but
not currently in full-time work:

- **Work-search activity** required;
- **Engagement** with Customer and Local Services'
  employment-support teams;
- **Acceptance** of suitable referrals;
- **Training** participation;
- **Specific** sanctions for non-engagement.

## The means test

Household resources are compared against household needs:

### Resources counted

- **Earned income** (wages, self-employed income) — with
  disregards for the first portion;
- **Other income** — pensions (state and private),
  contributory benefits, interest, dividends;
- **Maintenance / child maintenance**;
- **Asset value above thresholds** — specific assets are
  disregarded (e.g. main home) but others count.

### Disregards and reliefs

- **Earned-income disregards** — a portion of wages doesn't
  count, to provide a work incentive;
- **Disability earnings disregards** — higher for
  disabled workers;
- **Asset thresholds** — claimants can have assets up to
  a specified threshold without affecting the claim;
- **Main home** disregarded for most purposes.

### Needs assessed

- **Adult component** per adult;
- **Child component** per child;
- **Housing component** — rent / mortgage interest, capped
  by household size and accommodation type;
- **Disability / care components**;
- **Childcare component**;
- **Medical / health components**;
- **Other specified components**.

### Calculation

If **needs > resources**, the difference is paid as
Income Support.

If **needs ≤ resources**, the household is not eligible
(though specific health and dental components may
still apply).

## Composition of the household

The household for Income Support is:

- The **claimant**;
- The **claimant's spouse / civil partner / cohabitant**;
- **Dependent children**;
- Specific **other dependents** per regulation.

Adult children working full-time are typically separate
households. Lodgers in the household are typically
separate.

## Common eligibility traps

- **5-year residence** — every adult must qualify, not
  just one;
- **Cohabitants** treated as household — even where
  unmarried;
- **Working but earning little** — still in work
  expectation but eligible for top-up;
- **Self-employed** with variable income — needs careful
  monthly accounting;
- **Capital from house sale** — can take you over the
  asset threshold temporarily;
- **Inheritance** — affects resources and may trigger
  reassessment.

## Verification and ongoing eligibility

Once on Income Support:

- **Monthly reviews** of circumstances;
- **Mandatory reporting** of changes of circumstance —
  income, household composition, work status;
- **Annual reviews** in detail;
- **Random audits** for fraud prevention;
- **Sanctions** for non-disclosure.

## Practical recommendations

For those considering a claim:

- **Use the gov.je eligibility calculator** first;
- **Gather documents** before applying — pay slips, bank
  statements, rent agreement, ID, residence proof;
- **Don't delay** — claims can only narrowly be backdated;
- **Engage** Citizens Advice Jersey for help if needed;
- **Be honest** about all income and household
  circumstances — fraud consequences are severe.

For those on Income Support:

- **Report changes promptly** — same-day or next-day for
  major changes;
- **Keep records** of all communications with CLS;
- **Engage** with work-expectation requirements
  positively;
- **Appeal** decisions you disagree with — see
  [`appeals.md`](./appeals.md).
