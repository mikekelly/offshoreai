# Pothier and the Coutume — Civil-Law Influences in Jersey

## Who Pothier was

**Robert-Joseph Pothier** (1699-1772) was a **French jurist
and academic** from Orléans:

- **Magistrate** in Orléans (judge);
- **Professor** of French law at the University of Orléans;
- **Author** of comprehensive treatises on Roman law and
  French customary law;
- His works **systematised** French civil law in a way that
  preceded the Napoleonic Code (1804);
- Pothier's writings **shaped** continental civil law and,
  through Norman customary law tradition, **Jersey law**.

## Pothier's key works

### Traité des obligations (1761)

Pothier's most influential work — the treatise on
**obligations**:

- **Sources of obligations** (contract, tort, quasi-contract,
  quasi-tort, statute);
- **Formation** of contracts;
- **Performance** and **breach**;
- **Damages**;
- **Termination** of obligations.

This treatise was **translated into English** (William Evans,
1806) and became a **major source** for English contract law
through Lord Mansfield and others. Through Norman tradition,
it also remains a **Jersey** authoritative source.

### Other treatises

- **Traité du contrat de vente** — contracts of sale;
- **Traité du contrat de société** — partnerships;
- **Traité du contrat de mariage** — marriage contracts;
- **Traité du contrat de louage** — leases and lettings;
- **Traité de la propriété** — property;
- **Traité du contrat de dépôt** — deposit / bailment;
- **Traité du contrat de gage** — pledge.

## Why Pothier matters in Jersey

### Jersey customary law connection

The Norman customary law preserved in Jersey **drew from**
the broader French civil-law tradition that Pothier
systematised:

- **Same** Roman-law foundations;
- **Same** civil-law approach to obligations;
- **Pothier's** clear analysis was adopted by Jersey
  jurists.

### Le Geyt

**Philippe Le Geyt** (1635-1716), the **principal Jersey
customary law jurist**, wrote a code of Jersey customary law
that draws on similar sources. While Le Geyt preceded
Pothier substantively, the same intellectual tradition
underlies both.

### Modern Jersey courts

Jersey Royal Court decisions **routinely cite Pothier**
particularly in:

- **Contract** disputes;
- **Obligations** generally;
- **Performance** and **breach** analysis;
- **Damages** quantification;
- **Specific** customary-law contexts.

This makes Jersey law **distinct** from English law on some
fundamental contract issues — even where the outcomes
converge, the **reasoning** differs.

## Pothier vs English authority

When a Jersey court has both Pothier and English authority
to consider:

- **Both** are persuasive but neither strictly binding;
- **English** authority dominant in **modern commercial**
  contexts;
- **Pothier** dominant in **customary** contexts;
- **Jersey Court of Appeal** balances both for distinctly
  Jersey approaches.

## Examples of Pothier-derived Jersey doctrines

### Cause of contract

Continental civil law requires **cause** (motivation) for
contract validity:

- **Jersey law** historically required cause;
- **Modern Jersey** position contested — some cases retain
  cause requirement;
- **Mostly** practical convergence with English consideration
  doctrine;
- **Specific** Jersey cases analysing Pothier on cause.

### Obligations from quasi-contract

- **Pothier's** civil-law quasi-contracts (e.g., negotiorum
  gestio — managing another's affairs);
- **Modern** Jersey case law occasionally invoking these;
- **Convergence** with English unjust enrichment doctrines.

### Specific performance

- **Civil-law** broader specific-performance doctrine;
- **Jersey** retains aspects of this;
- **Specific** Jersey case law on remedy choice.

## Reading Pothier in modern practice

Pothier is **dated** (18th century French):

- **Archaic language** even in original;
- **English translations** available (Evans translation
  classic);
- **Modern French scholarship** building on Pothier;
- **Specific Jersey** academic writing analysing Pothier in
  Jersey context.

In Jersey practice, Pothier is cited:

- **Where** Norman customary law applies;
- **Where** Jersey contract law diverges from English;
- **Where** historical reasoning supports modern
  determination;
- **Especially** in real-property and customary contexts.
