# ECHR and Human Rights in Jersey

## The Human Rights (Jersey) Law 2000

The **Human Rights (Jersey) Law 2000** incorporates the
**European Convention on Human Rights (ECHR)** into Jersey
law:

- Mirrors the UK's **Human Rights Act 1998** approach;
- Renders Convention rights **directly enforceable** in Jersey
  courts;
- Requires courts to **interpret legislation** compatibly with
  Convention rights;
- Provides for **declarations of incompatibility** where
  legislation cannot be so interpreted;
- Renders **public authority** action unlawful if incompatible
  with Convention rights (subject to defences).

## Convention rights incorporated

The principal Convention rights now enforceable in Jersey:

- **Article 2** — right to life;
- **Article 3** — prohibition on torture and inhuman /
  degrading treatment;
- **Article 5** — right to liberty and security;
- **Article 6** — right to a fair trial;
- **Article 7** — no punishment without law;
- **Article 8** — right to respect for private and family
  life;
- **Article 9** — freedom of thought, conscience, and
  religion;
- **Article 10** — freedom of expression;
- **Article 11** — freedom of assembly and association;
- **Article 12** — right to marry;
- **Article 14** — prohibition on discrimination;
- **Protocol 1, Article 1** — peaceful enjoyment of
  possessions;
- **Protocol 1, Article 2** — right to education;
- **Protocol 1, Article 3** — right to free elections.

## Application

Convention rights apply to:

- **Public authorities** in Jersey — Council of Ministers,
  States departments, JFSC, Royal Court, police, prison
  service;
- **Bodies exercising public functions** — including some
  delegated functions to private bodies in some cases.

Convention rights do **not** generally apply directly to
private parties (horizontal effect is limited), but courts
must interpret common law / statute consistently with
Convention rights, which has indirect horizontal effect.

## Practical implications in commercial practice

### Financial regulation

- **JFSC decisions** are subject to Article 6 fair-trial
  protections;
- **Asset freezing** / restraint orders may engage Article 6
  and Protocol 1, Article 1;
- **Sanctions** designations and Jersey enforcement engage
  Article 6;
- **Enforcement** penalties (substantial fines) attract
  criminal-process protections under Article 6.

### Trust and company law

- **Beneficial ownership** registers engaging Article 8
  privacy considerations balanced against AML / tax
  transparency public interests;
- **Foreign judgments** enforcement engaging Article 6;
- **Article 47 mistake** applications occasionally engaging
  fair-trial considerations.

### Criminal practice

- **Custody** decisions, **bail**, **trial fairness** all
  engage Convention rights;
- **Sentencing** subject to Article 3 (inhuman / degrading)
  and Article 7 (legality);
- **Confiscation** orders engaging Protocol 1, Article 1.

## Strasbourg jurisdiction

Jersey is **bound by ECHR** through the UK's signatory
status:

- **European Court of Human Rights** in Strasbourg is the
  ultimate adjudicator;
- **Petitions** to Strasbourg are available after
  domestic remedies exhausted;
- **Judgments** are binding on Jersey through the UK's
  obligations.

Petitions to Strasbourg from Jersey are **rare** but have
occurred (e.g., on bailiff role separation issues, sex-
discrimination cases historically).

## Limits

The Human Rights (Jersey) Law:

- Does **not** override primary Jersey legislation (Royal-
  Court-controlled interpretation is the principal tool);
- Allows **declarations of incompatibility** but does not
  invalidate incompatible legislation;
- Requires **political follow-up** (legislative amendment) to
  address declarations.
