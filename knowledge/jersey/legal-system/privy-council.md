# Judicial Committee of the Privy Council — Jersey Appeals

The **Judicial Committee of the Privy Council (JCPC)** is the
**final court of appeal** for Jersey — sitting in London,
formally the King in Council.

## Composition

Sitting as the JCPC, the panel typically comprises:

- **Justices of the UK Supreme Court** (sitting as Privy
  Counsellors);
- **Senior Commonwealth jurists** as needed;
- **Three or five** sitting together for substantive appeals.

The same individuals are typically the Supreme Court Justices —
the institutional formality of sitting as the Privy Council
distinguishes the role.

## Appeals from Jersey

Appeals from the Jersey Court of Appeal go to the Privy Council:

- **With leave** of either the Court of Appeal or the Privy
  Council itself;
- **On constitutional or significant questions** of Jersey law;
- **Without leave** in some specified categories.

Privy Council decisions on Jersey appeals are **binding** on the
Royal Court and Court of Appeal.

## Significance

Privy Council decisions:

- **Settle Jersey law definitively** on questions before them;
- Are **frequently** the most-cited authorities in Jersey
  practice for any topic the Privy Council has addressed;
- **Trust law** — several significant Privy Council decisions
  on Jersey trust law (Schmidt v Rosewood applied; Article 47
  set-aside cases; reserved-powers issues);
- **Companies law** — though fewer Companies-Law appeals reach
  the Privy Council.

## Procedure

- **Written submissions** plus oral argument;
- **Hearing in London** at Downing Street;
- **Written reasoned judgments** delivered after hearing;
- **No further appeal** — the Privy Council's decision is final.

## Where to find judgments

Privy Council Jersey-appeal judgments are published on:

- **jcpc.uk** (the Judicial Committee's website);
- **jerseylaw.je** (Jersey law repository);
- **BAILII** (the British and Irish Legal Information Institute).
