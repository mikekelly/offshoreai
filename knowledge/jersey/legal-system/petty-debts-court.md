# Petty Debts Court — Jersey

## What this is

The **Petty Debts Court** is Jersey's small-claims and
lower-civil court. It handles:

- **Civil money claims** up to **£30,000**;
- **Residential tenancy** matters (exclusive original
  jurisdiction under the Residential Tenancy (Jersey) Law
  2011 — see [`../residential-tenancy/index.md`](../residential-tenancy/index.md));
- **Lease cancellation** matters where annual rent does
  not exceed £45,000 (with **unlimited** jurisdiction
  for rent arrears);
- **Specific** other limited-jurisdiction matters under
  statute.

Procedurally, the court is **less formal** than the Royal
Court and is intended to be accessible to litigants
without lawyers — though specialist advocates are routine
in disputed cases.

The governing legislation is the **Petty Debts Court
(Miscellaneous Provisions) (Jersey) Law 2000** and the
**Petty Debts Court Rules 2018**.

## Jurisdiction

### Monetary limit

The Petty Debts Court has jurisdiction in **all civil
causes and matters** where the claim (whether liquidated
or unliquidated), **net of any interest claimed**, does
**not exceed £30,000**.

Above £30,000, claims must be brought in the **Royal
Court**.

### Residential tenancy

The Petty Debts Court has **exclusive original
jurisdiction** under the RTJL 2011 over residential
tenancy disputes:

- Termination of agreements;
- Eviction orders;
- Money judgments for arrears;
- Deposit disputes;
- Specific performance (e.g. ordering repairs).

The £30,000 monetary cap does **not** apply to tenancy
matters within the RTJL — the court can deal with
substantial arrears or damages claims linked to a
tenancy.

### Lease cancellation

Beyond the residential tenancy context, the court can
deal with **cancellation of lease contracts** where the
annual rent does not exceed **£45,000**. With **unlimited
jurisdiction** for rent arrears in that context.

### Specific other matters

Various statutes confer specific jurisdiction on the
Petty Debts Court — for example, certain consumer
matters, specific licensing appeals, and prescribed
small-debt claims.

## The court

The Petty Debts Court is presided over by the **Petty
Debts Court Judge** (typically a senior advocate). The
court sits as required to handle the caseload.

It is **physically separate** from the Royal Court and
Magistrate's Court but is part of the broader Jersey
judicial system.

Appeals lie to the **Royal Court** (with leave).

## Procedure overview

### Pre-action

Before issuing proceedings, a plaintiff should send a
**letter of demand**:

- Setting out the **basis of the claim**;
- **Demanding payment**;
- Giving **at least 7 days** for response;
- Stating that proceedings will follow if no payment.

This is **not strictly required** by the statute, but is
expected procedure and may affect costs awards.

### Issuing the summons

If unresolved:

- **Summons** prepared stating the sum claimed, brief
  reason for the debt, interest claimed, stamp duty,
  costs, and any wages arrest sought;
- **Filed** with the court;
- **Served** on the defendant.

### Defendant's response

The defendant may:

- **Admit** the claim (in whole or part);
- **Defend** — file an Answer setting out the defence;
- **Counterclaim** — file a cross-claim against the
  plaintiff;
- **Apply** for transfer to the Royal Court (complexity
  ground).

### Trial

If contested, the case proceeds to trial:

- **Judge sits alone**;
- **Less formal** than Royal Court;
- **Evidence** typically documentary plus brief oral
  evidence;
- **Decision** usually same-day or shortly reserved;
- **Costs** typically follow the event but in modest
  amounts.

### Tenancy procedure

For residential tenancy matters specifically, see
[`../residential-tenancy/petty-debts-court-tenancy.md`](../residential-tenancy/petty-debts-court-tenancy.md).
Procedure follows similar lines but with specific
requirements from the RTJL 2011.

## Transfer to Royal Court

At any stage, the Petty Debts Court may order **transfer
to the Royal Court** if:

- **An important or complex question of law** is likely
  to arise;
- **A complex question of fact** is likely to arise;
- The **claim** exceeds the court's monetary
  jurisdiction (typically because amendment / counter-
  claim takes it over £30,000);
- **Specific** jurisdictional grounds.

Transfer typically increases costs and timeline.

## Enforcement

After judgment:

- **Voluntary payment** by the defendant;
- **Wage arrest** — deduction from earnings;
- **Bank account arrest** — freezing and attachment;
- **Goods arrest** by the Viscount;
- **Dégrèvement** — for substantial sums against a
  property-owner debtor.

The **Viscount** is the principal enforcement officer.

## Appeals

A party dissatisfied with a Petty Debts Court decision
may appeal:

- **With leave** of the Petty Debts Court Judge or the
  Bailiff;
- **To the Royal Court**;
- **Within prescribed time** (typically 28 days);
- **On points of law or sentence**; merits-rehearing in
  narrow cases.

## Practical recommendations

For a small claim:

- **Letter of demand** first — many cases settle here;
- **Modest sum + time-cost** — calculate whether worth
  pursuing;
- **DIY representation** is feasible — the court is
  designed for it;
- **Specialist advocate** for contested or larger cases.

For tenancy matters:

- See [`../residential-tenancy/petty-debts-court-tenancy.md`](../residential-tenancy/petty-debts-court-tenancy.md);
- **Strict compliance** with RTJL procedural requirements
  essential;
- **Engage** Jersey tenant-landlord specialist for
  contested eviction.

For a defendant served with a summons:

- **Read carefully** and note the deadlines;
- **Consider** admitting in part / negotiating;
- **File an Answer** if defending — within the prescribed
  time;
- **Court attendance** essential to avoid default
  judgment.
