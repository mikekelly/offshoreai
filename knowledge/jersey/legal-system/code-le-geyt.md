# Le Geyt — Jersey's Customary Law Jurist

## Who Le Geyt was

**Philippe Le Geyt** (1635-1716) was a **Jersey-born jurist
and Bailiff**:

- **Born** in Jersey;
- **Studied** law (apparently informally and through practical
  experience);
- **Served** as **Bailiff** of Jersey (1676-1714);
- **Author** of the most comprehensive **codification of
  Jersey customary law** prior to modern times.

## Le Geyt's "Code"

Le Geyt wrote (but never formally published in his lifetime)
a **systematic** statement of Jersey customary law:

- Generally referred to as **"Le Code Le Geyt"** or simply
  "Le Geyt";
- **Manuscript** circulated in Jersey legal circles;
- **Published** posthumously in **19th century**;
- **Latin / French** original;
- **Available** at Jersey legal libraries and the Jersey Law
  Library.

## Why Le Geyt matters

### Comprehensive coverage

Le Geyt covered:

- **Property** law (real and personal);
- **Succession** (movable and immovable);
- **Contract** and obligations;
- **Tort** / quasi-delict;
- **Family** law;
- **Procedure** in Royal Court;
- **Criminal** law (specific aspects);
- **Public** law principles.

### Customary law preservation

Le Geyt's work:

- **Preserves** Jersey's customary law in systematic form;
- **Adapts** Norman customary law to Jersey conditions;
- **Documents** Jersey-specific variations on broader Norman
  law;
- **Provides** authoritative source citable in modern Royal
  Court.

### Modern citations

Jersey Royal Court cases **continue to cite** Le Geyt:

- Particularly in **customary law** matters;
- In **property** disputes with customary roots;
- In **succession** matters where customary rules apply;
- For **historical** interpretation of statute.

## Le Geyt's intellectual context

Le Geyt drew on:

- **Norman customary law** sources (Grand Coutumier, Coutume
  reformée);
- **Continental civil law** authorities (early Pothier
  contemporary tradition);
- **English law** to a limited extent;
- **Jersey customary practice** he observed as Bailiff;
- **Practical experience** in Jersey courts.

## Other Jersey customary writers

Other historical Jersey jurists:

### Poingdestre

**Jean Poingdestre** (1609-1691):

- **Lieutenant Bailiff** of Jersey;
- **Predecessor** to Le Geyt;
- **Author** of "Lois et Coutumes de l'Île de Jersey";
- **Less comprehensive** than Le Geyt;
- **Still cited** in modern cases.

### Hemery

**Thomas Hemery** wrote on Jersey customary law in the early
18th century. Less foundational than Le Geyt.

### Modern academic work

Modern Jersey academic and judicial writing on customary law:

- **Jersey Law Review** articles;
- **Specific Royal Court** judgments analysing Le Geyt;
- **Treatises** by Jersey jurists.

## Reading Le Geyt today

Le Geyt's work:

- **Difficult** to read (old French / Latin);
- **English translations** for some passages exist;
- **Specific** Royal Court reliance on specific passages
  documented in case law;
- **Practitioners** typically rely on **secondary** writing
  citing Le Geyt rather than first-instance reading.
