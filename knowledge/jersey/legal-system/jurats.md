# Jurats of the Royal Court

**Jurats** are the **lay judges of fact** of the Royal Court of
Jersey — a distinctive feature of the Jersey legal system.

## What Jurats do

Jurats:

- **Determine questions of fact** in Royal Court proceedings
  (analogous to jury function in other systems);
- **Sit alongside** the Bailiff (or Deputy Bailiff /
  Commissioner) who is the **judge of law**;
- **Decide guilt** in criminal trials and **liability** in civil
  trials;
- **Set sentences** within sentencing limits (in Inferior Number
  / Superior Number criminal sittings);
- Number of Jurats depends on the type of proceeding (commonly 2
  for Samedi / Inferior Number; more for Superior Number).

## Composition

- **24 Jurats** at any time (a fixed roster);
- **Elected** for life (until age 72) by an electoral college
  comprising existing Jurats, Advocates, and members of the
  States Assembly;
- **Drawn from prominent Jersey residents** — often senior
  businesspeople, retired professionals, or experienced lay
  citizens;
- **Not legally qualified** in general — they bring **community
  judgment** to bear on facts.

## Why Jurats matter

The Jurat system:

- **Combines** legal expertise (Bailiff) with community judgment
  (Jurats) in a single panel;
- **Differs** from English jury practice (juries are
  randomly-selected one-off panels; Jurats are standing judges
  of fact);
- **Preserves** the Norman-customary tradition of community
  participation in dispute resolution;
- **Provides** continuity and experience in complex commercial
  matters.

## Modern operation

In **complex commercial cases** (trust disputes, company-law
matters, large fraud trials), the combination of legally-trained
Bailiff plus experienced Jurats provides a **bench** familiar
with the practical context as well as the law.
