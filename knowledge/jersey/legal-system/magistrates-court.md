# Magistrate's Court

## Position in the court hierarchy

The **Magistrate's Court** is the **lowest court** in Jersey,
handling:

- **Minor criminal** offences (summary jurisdiction);
- **Smaller civil** claims (petty debts court);
- **Specific** statutory matters (licensing, parking, etc.);
- **Initial hearings** for more serious matters (committed to
  Royal Court for trial).

## Personnel

The Magistrate's Court is presided over by:

- The **Magistrate** — a qualified lawyer (Jersey advocate /
  solicitor or qualified equivalent) appointed for the role;
- **Assistant Magistrates** in some cases;
- **Stipendiary Magistrate** typically full-time.

The role is **professional** — Jersey's Magistrate is not a lay
magistrate (unlike England's lay magistracy).

## Criminal jurisdiction

The Magistrate's Court hears:

- **Summary offences** (the equivalent of English summary
  offences) — minor crimes, parking, public order, minor
  assault;
- **Either-way offences** where summary trial is appropriate;
- **First hearings** for indictable offences before committal
  to Royal Court;
- **Pre-trial** custody decisions;
- **Bail** applications.

Sentencing powers are **limited**:

- **Fines** up to prescribed levels;
- **Community sentences**;
- **Imprisonment** up to **12 months** (typically);
- Cases requiring greater sentences are **committed** to Royal
  Court.

## Civil jurisdiction (Petty Debts Court)

The Petty Debts Court (part of the Magistrate's Court complex):

- Hears **civil claims** below specified thresholds (typically
  £30,000);
- **Simpler** procedure than Royal Court;
- **Quicker** resolution;
- **Cost-effective** for smaller matters;
- **Self-represented** litigants common.

## Family jurisdiction

Specific family matters:

- **Domestic violence** orders;
- **Child protection** orders (initial);
- **Maintenance** orders;
- More substantive family proceedings go to the **Family
  Division of the Royal Court**.

## Specific statutory functions

- **Licensing** (alcohol, taxis, etc.);
- **Parking** fines and appeals;
- **Traffic** offences (most);
- **Environmental** and planning enforcement (initial);
- **Public health** orders.

## Appeals

Appeals from the Magistrate's Court lie to the **Royal Court**:

- On **fact and law**;
- By **rehearing** in most cases;
- Subject to procedural requirements.

Further appeal to the **Court of Appeal** is exceptional.

## Practical points

For practitioners:

- Most **commercial litigation** is **not** in the Magistrate's
  Court — Royal Court is the principal forum;
- **Petty Debts Court** is appropriate for sub-£30k claims;
- **Criminal practice** at Magistrate's Court level is the
  starting point for serious-crime defence work.
