# Statutory Interpretation in Jersey

## The basic approach

Jersey courts apply statutory interpretation principles
**broadly similar to English** principles, with Jersey-
specific overlays:

- **Plain meaning** of the statute as the starting point;
- **Context** of the statute as a whole;
- **Purpose** of the legislation;
- **Mischief** addressed by the legislation;
- **Specific** Jersey-context considerations;
- **ECHR compatibility** (Human Rights (Jersey) Law 2000).

## The three rules

### Literal rule

- **Plain meaning** of the words;
- **Where unambiguous**, applied as written;
- **First port of call** in interpretation.

### Golden rule

- If literal meaning produces **absurdity** or
  **inconsistency**, depart from literal meaning;
- **Narrower** golden rule: only depart where literal meaning
  causes absurdity;
- **Wider** golden rule: depart to give effect to obvious
  legislative intent.

### Mischief rule

- Identify the **mischief** the statute addresses;
- **Interpret** the statute to remedy that mischief;
- Used **especially** where literal interpretation defeats
  legislative purpose.

## Modern Jersey approach

Modern Jersey courts apply the **unified purposive
approach**:

- **Identify** the purpose of the legislation;
- **Read** the statute as a whole;
- **Construe** consistent with purpose where possible;
- **Subject to** plain-meaning constraints.

## Specific Jersey considerations

### Customary-law overlay

- **Statute** does not displace customary law unless **clearly
  intended**;
- **Specific** presumptions favour preservation of customary
  rights;
- **Articles 59 of the Trusts Law** preserves customary
  jurisdictions and powers.

### ECHR compatibility

- **Human Rights (Jersey) Law 2000** requires statutes to be
  **interpreted compatibly with Convention rights** where
  possible;
- **Declarations of incompatibility** available where not
  possible;
- **Specific** Jersey case law on this.

### Bilingual statutes (rare)

- Most Jersey statutes are **in English**;
- **Historic** statutes sometimes in French / Jersey French;
- **Specific** rules for bilingual interpretation in those
  cases.

### Comparison with English statutes

- Where a Jersey statute **closely mirrors** an English
  statute, English interpretation cases are **persuasive**;
- **Specific** Jersey statutory phrasing may diverge;
- **Jersey legislative intent** prevails over English.

## Hansard / explanatory materials

In Jersey:

- **States Assembly debates** can be referred to for context;
- **Explanatory notes** to a Projet de Loi are persuasive
  but not binding;
- **Pepper v Hart** principles apply selectively;
- **More restrictive** than UK on Hansard use.

## Statutory presumptions

Jersey courts apply standard interpretation **presumptions**:

### Presumption of legislative intent

- The legislature **intended** a particular result;
- **Subject to** clear statutory text contradicting.

### Presumption against retrospectivity

- **Statutes** do not operate retrospectively unless **clearly
  intended**;
- **Particularly strong** for criminal / penal statutes;
- **Specific** Jersey case law.

### Presumption of consistency with international obligations

- Jersey statutes **interpreted consistently** with international
  obligations (ECHR, treaty obligations) where possible;
- **Subject to** clear statutory text overriding.

### Presumption against absurdity

- **Avoid** interpretations producing absurd results;
- **Subject to** plain-meaning constraints in clear cases.

## Specific provisions and Schedules

- **Schedules** of a statute are **part of the statute**;
- **Headings** and **section titles** are **aids**, not binding;
- **Marginal notes** are aids;
- **Specific** Jersey style: customary law preserved in
  savings clauses.
