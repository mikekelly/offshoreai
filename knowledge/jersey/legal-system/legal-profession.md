# Legal Profession — Jersey

Jersey's legal profession comprises **Advocates** and
**Solicitors** of the Royal Court — a **divided profession**
analogous in some respects to the English barrister/solicitor
split but historically distinct.

## Advocates

**Advocates of the Royal Court of Jersey**:

- Have **rights of audience** in the Royal Court, Court of Appeal,
  and (with leave) Privy Council;
- Are typically **Jersey-trained lawyers** who have completed the
  required apprenticeship and examinations;
- May be **King's Counsel** in some cases;
- Form the **Bar** of Jersey;
- Are governed by professional rules of the **Law Society of
  Jersey**.

## Solicitors

**Solicitors of the Royal Court of Jersey**:

- Have **rights of audience** in lower courts and on specified
  applications;
- Provide a **wide range** of legal services including conveyancing,
  probate, commercial advice;
- Often work **alongside Advocates** — referring contentious
  matters to Advocates for Royal Court representation;
- Are also governed by Law Society rules.

## Training and admission

To qualify:

- **Law degree** (often from a UK university) followed by
  Jersey-specific examinations;
- **Apprenticeship** with a senior Jersey lawyer;
- **Call to the Bar** (for Advocates) by the Royal Court;
- **Continuing professional development** requirements.

## Practice structure

Jersey legal practice is dominated by **a small number of
established firms** including (without endorsement):

- **Mourant**, **Carey Olsen**, **Ogier**, **Bedell Cristin**,
  **Walkers**, **Appleby**, and others;
- These firms typically have **multi-jurisdictional offshore**
  practice (Jersey, Guernsey, Cayman, BVI, etc.);
- They cover the full range of corporate, trust, fund, banking,
  and litigation work.

## English-qualified lawyers

Many Jersey transactions involve **English-qualified solicitors
and barristers** advising on English-law aspects. Jersey-Court-
of-appeal panels typically include senior English-qualified
judges.
