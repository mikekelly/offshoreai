# Judicial Appointments

## The judicial offices

Jersey's principal judicial offices are:

- **Bailiff of Jersey** — head of the Royal Court;
- **Deputy Bailiff** — deputy head;
- **Commissioners of the Royal Court** — senior judges hearing
  specific matters;
- **Jurats** — lay judges of the Royal Court;
- **Magistrate** — head of the Magistrate's Court;
- **Court of Appeal judges** — full-time and additional
  appointments;
- **Other** specialist appointments (e.g., the Greffier).

## The Judicial Appointments Commission

Modern judicial appointments operate through the **Judicial
Appointments Commission**:

- **Independent** body recommending appointments to the
  Sovereign (for senior offices) or the appropriate
  appointing authority;
- **Membership** includes judiciary, lawyers, lay members;
- **Process** is by application, interview, and assessment
  against published criteria;
- **Reforms** in 2010s formalised and de-politicised
  appointments.

## Senior offices — Crown appointment

The **Bailiff** and **Deputy Bailiff**:

- Are **Crown appointments** by the Sovereign;
- On advice of the Privy Council;
- Following Jersey-led selection through the Judicial
  Appointments Commission;
- For an effectively **tenured** term (to retirement).

## Royal Court Commissioners

Commissioners:

- Sit on the Royal Court for **specific matters** alongside
  or in place of the Bailiff;
- Are typically **senior practitioners** or retired judges;
- Include **English / UK** senior practitioners in some cases
  for international trust / commercial matters;
- Allow the Royal Court to **draw on** wider expertise.

## Jurats

Jurats are **lay judges** of the Royal Court:

- **Elected** by an electoral college of legal practitioners,
  Crown officers, and others;
- **Serve** for a renewable term;
- **Decide questions of fact** at trial;
- **Determine** sentences in serious cases;
- **Sit** typically two or three at a time alongside the
  Bailiff or Commissioner.

The Jurat system is **distinctive** to the Channel Islands —
an example of a non-jury fact-finding tribunal blending
professional (legal) and lay judging.

## Magistrate

The Magistrate:

- Is a **professional** judicial appointment;
- **Qualified lawyer** (advocate / solicitor or equivalent);
- **Appointed** through the Judicial Appointments Commission
  process;
- **Full-time** in most cases (with deputy / assistant
  arrangements for caseload).

## Court of Appeal judges

The Jersey Court of Appeal:

- Has **full-time** Jersey judges;
- Plus **additional** judges drawn from:
  - **Guernsey** senior practitioners and former judges;
  - **English / UK** senior practitioners and former judges;
  - **Other Channel Islands** practitioners.
- Sits in **panels of three** typically.

## Criteria

Appointing authorities consider:

- **Legal competence** and **experience**;
- **Integrity** and **independence**;
- **Judicial temperament**;
- **Knowledge of Jersey law** (where relevant);
- **Public confidence**;
- **Diversity** considerations.

## Tenure and removal

- Senior judicial offices are effectively **tenured to
  retirement**;
- **Removal** is exceptional and would require Privy Council
  process (for Crown appointments) or other formal process;
- **Independence** is constitutionally protected.
