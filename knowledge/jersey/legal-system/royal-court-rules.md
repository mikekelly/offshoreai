# Royal Court Rules — Civil Procedure

## The Rules

The **Royal Court Rules 2004** (and subsequent amendments)
govern **civil procedure** in the Royal Court:

- **Detailed** procedural code;
- **Updated** periodically;
- **Modelled** on English CPR but with Jersey adaptations;
- **Specific** Jersey procedural concepts preserved.

## Structure

The Rules cover:

- **Commencement** of proceedings (Order of Justice,
  Representation);
- **Pleadings** (statements of case);
- **Disclosure** and inspection;
- **Witness statements** and evidence;
- **Interim relief**;
- **Trial** procedures;
- **Costs**;
- **Appeals**;
- **Specific** procedures (Trust applications, Beddoe,
  Article 51, etc.).

## Key procedural concepts

### Order of Justice

The **Jersey civil-action commencement** document:

- **Equivalent of** English claim form;
- **Filed** with the Judicial Greffe;
- **Served** on the defendant;
- **Triggers** the defendant's obligation to respond.

### Representation

A **non-contentious or quasi-contentious** procedure:

- Used for **trust applications**, **estate matters**,
  **directions**, **Article 51**, etc.;
- **Filed** with the Royal Court;
- **Convenors** identified;
- **Less adversarial** than full Order of Justice
  proceedings.

### Doleance

An older form of **complaint to the court** about:

- **Improper conduct** of an official;
- **Specific** procedural grievances;
- **Rare** in modern practice.

### Justice without delay

A specific procedure for:

- **Urgent** orders;
- **Specific** ex parte applications;
- **Royal Court** specific procedure.

## Practice directions

The Bailiff issues **Practice Directions** supplementing the
Rules:

- **Specific** procedural guidance;
- **Specific** topic-area procedures;
- **Updated** as needed;
- **Persuasive** authority on procedure.

## Comparison with English CPR

Areas of alignment:

- **Disclosure** principles broadly similar;
- **Witness statements** practice broadly similar;
- **Costs** principles broadly similar;
- **Case management** approach similar.

Areas of difference:

- **Order of Justice** vs claim form;
- **Representation** procedure (no direct English equivalent);
- **Specific** Jersey procedural terminology;
- **Trust applications** procedure more developed than
  English equivalent.

## Specific application types

### Article 51 directions (Trust)

- Most common type of Royal Court application;
- **Representation** procedure typically;
- **Specific** procedural requirements (convenors,
  evidence);
- **Specific** *Public Trustee v Cooper* categories applied.

### Beddoe applications

- **Beddoe orders** for trustees engaged in litigation;
- **Costs** protection for the trustee;
- **Specific** procedural rules.

### Mareva-equivalent freezing orders

- **Freezing injunctions** against assets;
- **Mareva** principles applied;
- **Specific** Jersey procedural requirements;
- **Worldwide** orders possible.

### Norwich Pharmacal orders

- **Disclosure** orders against third parties facilitating
  wrongdoing;
- **Specific** Jersey application of *Norwich Pharmacal*;
- **Important** for asset-tracing in Jersey.

## Appeals procedure

From Royal Court:

- **Court of Appeal** within prescribed time;
- **Permission** required for some appeals;
- **Specific** procedural requirements;
- **Further** appeal to Privy Council (with leave).
