# BBL 1991 Article 4 — Registration Required to Carry on Deposit-Taking Business

Article 4 is the **operative prohibition** of the BBL — no
person may carry on deposit-taking business in or from within
Jersey without **JFSC registration**.

## The provision

A person **shall not** carry on deposit-taking business in or
from within Jersey unless they are **registered** under the
Law.

## Territorial scope

The prohibition applies to:

- **Deposit-taking business** carried on **in Jersey**;
- **Deposit-taking business** carried on **from within
  Jersey** (where the activity has Jersey nexus even if some
  customer-facing aspects occur abroad);
- **Jersey-incorporated entities** carrying on deposit-taking
  business **abroad** (subject to bilateral arrangements with
  the foreign regulator).

## Penalty for breach

Carrying on deposit-taking business without registration:

- Is a **criminal offence**;
- Carries **substantial fines**;
- Carries **imprisonment** up to **7 years** for individual
  controllers;
- Triggers **civil consequences** (deposit contracts may be
  unenforceable; depositor protection issues).

## Who can register

The JFSC's policy framework (the **"Top 50 policy"**) limits
registration to:

- **Top-50 global** banking groups or their subsidiaries;
- **Specific exempted** institutions;
- **JFSC discretion** in exceptional cases.

This is a **policy** rather than a statutory requirement, but
is the operating constraint on banking entry to Jersey. See
[`top-50-policy.md`](./top-50-policy.md).

## The registration regime

Article 4 connects to:

- **Article 5** — application procedure;
- **Article 6** — grant of registration (substantive
  conditions);
- **Article 7** — conditions imposed;
- **Article 8** — variation;
- **Article 9** — refusal / suspension / revocation.

## Practical scope questions

### Treasury operations of non-banks

A multinational group's Jersey treasury operation:

- Receives funds from group entities — generally **not** "from
  the public";
- Applies them to group needs — generally **not** lending to
  the public;
- Generally **not deposit-taking business** requiring BBL
  registration.

But:

- If the treasury **borrows externally** from third-party
  banks and **on-lends** to group affiliates, this is **lending
  business** but typically not deposit-taking business;
- If the treasury **takes deposits** from third parties (e.g.,
  pension funds, third-party investors), this **may be**
  deposit-taking business — BBL applies.

### Holding-company structures

A pure **holding company** holding shares in subsidiaries:

- Not deposit-taking business;
- Not within BBL.

### Family-office activities

A **family office** holding family money:

- If only family funds (no public), not deposit-taking
  business;
- If accepts funds from external parties, could be — depends
  on facts.
