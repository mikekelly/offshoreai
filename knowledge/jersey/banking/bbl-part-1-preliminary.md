# BBL 1991 Part 1 — Preliminary

## Article 1 — Interpretation

Article 1 defines key terms:

- **"Bank"** — a person registered to carry on deposit-taking
  business under the Law;
- **"Banking Business"** — deposit-taking business (as defined
  in Article 2) carried on in or from within Jersey;
- **"Commission"** — the Jersey Financial Services Commission
  (JFSC);
- **"Deposit"** — a sum of money paid on terms of repayment
  (defined widely; see Article 2);
- **"Director"** — includes any person occupying the position
  of director, by whatever name called;
- **"Holding company"**, **"Subsidiary"** — corporate-group
  relationships defined by reference to the Companies Law;
- **"Minister"** — the Minister with responsibility for
  financial services;
- **"Principal person"** — a controller / significant
  shareholder / director / senior officer.

## Article 2 — Meaning of "deposit-taking business"

This is the **gateway provision** of the BBL — see
[`bbl-article-2.md`](bbl-article-2.md) for the deep-dive.

In summary, deposit-taking business consists of:

- **Receiving** deposits from the public;
- **Lending** money to others, or otherwise applying deposits
  for borrowers' benefit;
- Conducted by way of business.

The combination of **receiving deposits** and **lending /
applying** them is the defining feature.

## Article 3 — Excluded business categories

Certain activities are **excluded** from "deposit-taking
business" notwithstanding meeting the Article 2 test:

- **Public-sector** deposit-taking (States bodies, central
  government);
- **Specific exempted categories** prescribed by Order;
- **Interbank** transactions in narrow circumstances;
- **Solicitor / accountant client accounts** holding client
  monies in the ordinary course of professional practice;
- **Other** exempted activities by Order.

These exclusions ensure the BBL captures **banking** rather
than every form of money receipt.
