# BBL 1991 Article 16 — Large Exposures

Article 16 governs **large exposures** by registered banks.

## Single counterparty limit

- Exposure to **any single counterparty** capped at **25% of
  capital** (Tier 1 typically);
- **Connected counterparties** aggregated;
- **Group exposures** (related companies) treated as a
  single exposure.

## Connected counterparties

The aggregation rule:

- **Companies in the same group** counted as a single
  counterparty;
- **Persons** with significant influence over each other;
- **Specific** Jersey aggregation rules;
- **Subject to** specific waivers in narrow circumstances.

## Sector limits

Beyond single counterparty:

- **Industry-sector** concentration limits;
- **Geographic** concentration limits;
- **Product type** concentration limits;
- **Subject to** firm-specific internal limits.

## Reporting

Banks must:

- **Report large exposures** to JFSC;
- **Quarterly** large-exposure return;
- **Notify** breaches immediately;
- **Action plans** for remediation.

## Treatment of related entities

For corporate groups:

- **Group lending** within the banking group has specific
  treatment;
- **Subsidiaries** of the bank — specific limits;
- **Affiliated** entities — careful aggregation.

## Sovereign and central-bank exposures

- **Specific** treatment for sovereign / central-bank
  exposures;
- **Zero risk weight** for high-rated sovereigns typically;
- **Specific** Basel III adjustments.

## Connected lending

Lending to:

- **Bank's directors / senior managers** — specific stricter
  rules;
- **Controllers and connected persons** — specific
  restrictions;
- **Specific** Jersey corporate-governance considerations.

## Breach consequences

- **Specific** JFSC supervisory action;
- **Capital surcharge** in some cases;
- **Civil penalties** for material breaches;
- **Specific** corrective action required.
