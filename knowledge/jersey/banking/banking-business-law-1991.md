# Banking Business (Jersey) Law 1991 — Banking-Sector Perspective

This file covers the **Banking Business (Jersey) Law 1991** from
the **banking sector's operational perspective**. For the
**regulator's framework perspective**, see the parallel file at
[`../financial-regulation/banking-business-law-1991.md`](../financial-regulation/banking-business-law-1991.md).

## Registration as a Jersey bank

A bank operating in Jersey must:

- Be **registered** under the 1991 Law;
- Have **adequate capital** (Basel III aligned);
- Have **fit-and-proper** principal persons and key persons;
- Operate from **Jersey premises** with adequate staff;
- Comply with the **Banking Code of Practice**;
- Comply with **AML / CFT obligations**.

Banks in Jersey are typically **wholly-owned subsidiaries** of
major international banking groups, taking advantage of:

- **Established Jersey infrastructure** (legal, accounting,
  service providers);
- **Tax-efficient corporate environment**;
- **Channel-Islands financial-services brand**.

## Categories of Jersey bank

Jersey banks broadly fall into:

- **Private banks** serving high-net-worth clients with
  multi-jurisdictional wealth-management needs;
- **Corporate banks** providing trust-administration and fund-
  administration banking services;
- **Local retail banks** serving Jersey-resident population;
- **Specialist** banks (e.g. for specific client segments).

The number of Jersey banks has **consolidated** over the past two
decades as the global banking industry has rationalised — but
the remaining banks tend to be large, well-capitalised, and
strongly parent-supported.

## Operating model considerations

Jersey banks typically:

- **Operate as branches** of the parent group (legal-structure
  branch) or as **wholly-owned subsidiaries** (separate legal
  entity);
- **Subsidiary** structures bring stronger ring-fencing but more
  capital requirements;
- **Branch** structures bring closer parent-group integration
  but rely on parent solvency;
- **Risk-management** functions are often **integrated** with
  parent group while compliance is **Jersey-specific**.

## Key operational obligations

Banks must:

- **Submit regular returns** to the JFSC (capital, liquidity,
  large exposures, risk concentrations);
- **Maintain customer records** under AML / CFT and CRS / FATCA
  rules;
- **Participate** in the **deposit compensation scheme**;
- **Pay** annual JFSC fees;
- **Report** suspicious transactions to the FIU.
