# BBL 1991 Articles 18-22 — Resolution Conditions and Tools

## Article 18 — Resolution authority

The **JFSC** functions as resolution authority for Jersey
banks:

- **Coordinates** with home authority for foreign-parent
  groups;
- **Implements** resolution at Jersey-subsidiary level if
  needed;
- **Cooperates** in resolution colleges.

## Article 19 — Resolution planning

Each bank must maintain:

- **Recovery plan** (bank-led);
- **Resolution plan** (regulator-led for material banks);
- **MREL** (minimum requirement for own funds and eligible
  liabilities) where applicable;
- **Annual review**.

## Article 20 — Resolution conditions

Resolution may be triggered when:

- The bank is **failing or likely to fail**;
- **Private-sector measures** cannot reasonably prevent
  failure;
- Resolution is **in the public interest** (financial
  stability, depositor protection, critical functions
  continuity).

## Articles 21-22 — Resolution tools

### Sale of business

- **Transfer** all or part of the bank to a third-party
  acquirer;
- **Auction** process for value;
- **Continuity** for depositors and counterparties.

### Bridge institution

- **Transfer** critical functions to a JFSC-controlled
  bridge bank;
- **Temporary** vehicle pending permanent solution;
- **Operates** under banking licence.

### Bail-in

- **Write down or convert** shareholders and unsecured
  creditors;
- **Recapitalise** the bank from creditor losses;
- **Preserve** insured deposits and certain other
  liabilities;
- **MREL** ensures sufficient eligible liabilities.

### Asset separation

- **Transfer** distressed assets to a separate vehicle
  ("bad bank");
- **Managed run-off** to maximise value;
- **Crystallises** losses transparently.

### Modified insolvency

For smaller banks:

- **Modified insolvency** similar to ordinary désastre but
  with depositor protection features.

## Coordination

Resolution coordinates with:

- **UK Bank of England / PRA** for UK-parented banks;
- **ECB / Single Resolution Board** for euro-area parents;
- **Other foreign** resolution authorities;
- **Resolution colleges**.
