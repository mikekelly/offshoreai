# BBL 1991 Part 6 — Enforcement

Part 6 sets out the JFSC's **enforcement powers** for banking
regulation.

## Civil penalties

The JFSC may impose **civil financial penalties** for:

- **Breach** of registration conditions;
- **Breach** of continuing obligations (Article 10);
- **Failure** to comply with directions (Article 13);
- **Material** AML / CFT failures;
- **Failure** to provide information / cooperate.

Penalty levels are calibrated to:

- **Severity** of the breach;
- **Duration**;
- **Customer harm**;
- **Aggravating / mitigating** factors;
- **Co-operation** with the JFSC;
- The institution's **size and resources**.

For material breaches, penalties can be **substantial** —
seven-figure penalties have been imposed on Jersey banks for
serious AML / supervisory failures.

## Public statements

The JFSC may issue **public statements** about:

- **Enforcement** action taken;
- **Findings** of investigations;
- **Warnings** about unregistered activity;
- **Cease and desist** notices.

Public statements are themselves a **reputational sanction**
— "naming and shaming" — distinct from the underlying
enforcement.

## Decision Making

Enforcement decisions follow:

- **Notice** of intent with proposed decision and grounds;
- **Right** to make representations;
- **Decision-maker** independent from investigators
  (decision-making committee);
- **Reasoned** decision;
- **Right of appeal** to the Royal Court (Article 35).

## Criminal offences

The Law creates **criminal offences** for:

- **Carrying on** deposit-taking business without registration
  (Article 4);
- **False statements** in applications or notifications;
- **Failure** to comply with Court orders;
- **Specific** breaches by individuals (typically directors).

Criminal proceedings are brought by the **Attorney General**
and require **Royal Court** trial.

## Individual accountability

Senior managers and directors face:

- **Personal civil penalties** in some cases;
- **Disqualification** orders preventing further regulated
  activity;
- **Public statements** naming individuals;
- **Criminal prosecution** for serious offences.

The JFSC has expanded its focus on **individual
accountability** in alignment with FATF / Basel expectations
and UK Senior Managers Regime principles.

## Article 35 — Appeals

Decisions are subject to **appeal**:

- To the **Royal Court** within a prescribed period;
- On grounds including procedural fairness, error of law,
  and reasonableness;
- The Royal Court can **confirm, vary, or set aside** the
  decision;
- **Further appeal** lies to the **Court of Appeal**.

## Supervisory measures (non-enforcement)

Short of formal enforcement, the JFSC uses:

- **Direction letters** requiring specific actions;
- **Risk mitigation programmes**;
- **Heightened supervision**;
- **Skilled persons reviews** (independent third-party
  assessment paid by the bank);
- **Senior management** meetings and challenge;
- **Board attestations**.
