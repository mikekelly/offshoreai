# BBL 1991 Article 2 — Meaning of "Deposit-Taking Business"

Article 2 defines the **gateway** to Jersey's banking
regulatory regime — what constitutes "deposit-taking business"
and therefore triggers BBL registration.

## The provision

A person carries on **deposit-taking business** if:

- They **receive deposits** from members of the public on a
  business basis; and
- They **lend money** to other persons, or otherwise **apply
  deposits for borrowers' benefit**.

Both limbs must be present for the activity to be
"deposit-taking business":

- **Receiving deposits alone** is not banking;
- **Lending alone** is not banking;
- The **combination** — funded lending from public deposits —
  is banking.

## What is a "deposit"

A **deposit** is a sum of money paid on **terms of repayment**:

- Whether **on demand** or **with notice**;
- Whether **fixed term** or **continuing**;
- Whether interest-bearing or not;
- Whether at **par** or otherwise;
- Whether **secured or unsecured**.

Distinguishing features:

- **Repayability** is essential — money not repayable is not
  a deposit;
- **Investment** does not equal deposit — equity, debt
  securities, fund units have different regulatory treatment;
- **Customer credit balances** (e.g., utility customer
  prepayments) are not "deposits" in the BBL sense if not held
  on banking-type terms.

## "From members of the public"

Deposit-taking is regulated when conducted **with the public**:

- **Retail deposits** from individuals;
- **Corporate deposits** from non-related companies;
- **Wholesale deposits** above retail thresholds;
- **Interbank deposits** — typically not "from the public"
  if between banks.

The "public" requirement excludes:

- **Captive treasury** activity within a group (not from the
  public);
- **Specific exempted** counterparties;
- **Highly restricted** invitation-only schemes (depending on
  characterisation).

## "Lending money"

Banking requires application of deposits for **borrower
benefit**:

- **Traditional lending** to retail / commercial borrowers;
- **Money-market** lending;
- **Investment in debt securities** of borrowers (broad
  interpretation);
- **Treasury management** for affiliates (excluded if not
  on-lending publicly).

A bank that **only invests** depositor funds in equities or
treasury bonds (without lending) is at the **edge** of the
definition and may engage other regimes (investment business,
custody).

## Excluded persons / activities

Article 3 excludes:

- **Public-sector** deposit-takers;
- **Solicitors / accountants** in professional practice;
- **Interbank** transactions in defined contexts;
- **Specific exempted categories** by Order.

## Implications for non-banks

Activities that **look like** banking but may fall outside
Article 2:

- **E-money** issuance — engages e-money regulation, not BBL;
- **Crowdfunding** intermediation — engages investment-
  business regulation;
- **Stablecoin** issuance — engages VASP / DASP rules;
- **Credit union** activity — separate regulation;
- **Building society** activity (Jersey has limited
  building-society activity).

## Consequences of being deposit-taking business

Once an activity is within Article 2:

- **Registration** under Article 4 is mandatory;
- **Top 50** policy applies (the "top 50 bank" policy for
  Jersey — banks must be in or directly related to a top-50
  global banking group);
- **Capital / liquidity** requirements apply;
- **Recovery / resolution** regime applies;
- **AML / CFT** regime applies (banks are Schedule 2 supervised
  business);
- **Depositor compensation** scheme applies.
