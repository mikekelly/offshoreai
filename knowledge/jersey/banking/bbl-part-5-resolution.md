# BBL 1991 Part 5 — Recovery and Resolution

Part 5 implements **bank recovery and resolution** mechanisms
— the Jersey equivalent of the EU **BRRD** and UK **Banking Act
2009** regimes, aligned with FSB Key Attributes.

## Recovery planning

A registered bank must maintain a **recovery plan**:

- **Identifying** scenarios that would threaten viability;
- **Documenting** recovery measures (capital raising, asset
  sales, expense cuts);
- **Testing** through simulation;
- **Updating** annually or on material change;
- **Reviewed** by JFSC.

## Resolution planning

The JFSC (as resolution authority) develops a **resolution
plan**:

- For each registered bank above thresholds;
- Identifying the **preferred resolution strategy** (e.g.,
  single point of entry bail-in; multiple point of entry;
  modified insolvency);
- Identifying **resolution tools** to be deployed;
- Identifying **operational continuity** requirements;
- Identifying **MREL** (minimum requirement for own funds and
  eligible liabilities) where applicable.

## Article 18-19 — Resolution authority

The JFSC functions as **resolution authority** for Jersey
banks:

- **Coordinates** with home authority for foreign-parent
  groups (Single Resolution Board for euro-area parents; Bank
  of England for UK; FDIC for US);
- **Implements** resolution at Jersey-subsidiary level if
  needed;
- **Cooperates** in resolution colleges.

## Article 20 — Resolution conditions

Resolution may be triggered when:

- The bank is **failing or likely to fail**;
- **Private-sector measures** cannot reasonably prevent the
  failure;
- Resolution is **in the public interest** (financial
  stability, depositor protection, critical functions
  continuity).

## Articles 21-23 — Resolution tools

The JFSC may apply:

### Sale of business

- Transferring **all or part** of the bank to a third-party
  acquirer;
- **Auction** process for value;
- **Continuity** for depositors and counterparties.

### Bridge institution

- Transferring **critical functions** to a JFSC-controlled
  bridge bank;
- **Temporary** vehicle pending permanent solution;
- **Operates** under banking licence.

### Bail-in

- **Writing down** or **converting** shareholders and
  unsecured creditors;
- **Recapitalising** the bank from creditor losses;
- **Preserving** insured deposits and certain other
  liabilities;
- **MREL** ensures sufficient eligible liabilities are
  available.

### Asset separation

- Transferring **distressed assets** to a separate
  vehicle ("bad bank");
- **Managed run-off** to maximise value;
- **Crystallises** losses transparently.

### Modified insolvency

- For smaller banks, a **modified insolvency** procedure
  similar to ordinary désastre but with depositor protection
  features.

## Article 24 — Depositor protection

The **Jersey Bank Depositors Compensation Scheme**:

- Covers eligible deposits up to **£50,000 per depositor per
  bank** (current threshold);
- Funded by **levy** on participating banks;
- Pay-outs within **7 days** of failure;
- Administered by the **States Treasury** with JFSC
  involvement.

See [`depositor-compensation.md`](./depositor-compensation.md)
for the scheme overview.

## Cross-jurisdictional resolution

For Jersey banks that are part of foreign banking groups:

- **Single Resolution Mechanism** (euro-area) — Jersey
  cooperates;
- **UK Bank of England / PRA** — Jersey cooperates closely
  given UK ownership / parentage of most Jersey banks;
- **Resolution colleges** convene supervisors;
- **Information sharing** under MoUs.
