# BBL 1991 Article 6 — Grant of Registration

Article 6 sets out the **substantive conditions** for grant of
banking registration in Jersey.

## The provision

The JFSC may grant registration if it is satisfied that:

- The applicant is **fit and proper** to carry on
  deposit-taking business;
- The applicant has **adequate capital** for the proposed
  business;
- The applicant has **adequate liquidity** arrangements;
- The applicant's **principal persons** (directors, senior
  officers, controllers) are fit and proper;
- The applicant has **adequate systems and controls** for
  risk management;
- The applicant has **adequate accounting and audit**
  arrangements;
- The applicant's home regulator (if applicable) **consents**
  to the Jersey activity;
- It is **in the public interest** to grant registration.

## Fit and proper — applicant

The applicant must:

- Be of **good standing** as an institution;
- Have a **clean regulatory history** with home regulator;
- Have **no criminal** record relevant to the activity;
- Have **adequate experience** in the proposed business;
- Have **acceptable ownership** structure (no money-
  laundering / sanctions concerns at parent level).

## Fit and proper — principal persons

Each principal person must be:

- Of **good character** and **honesty**;
- **Competent** and **experienced** in banking;
- Of **adequate financial standing**;
- **Independent** of disqualifying conflicts;
- Subject to **JFSC fit-and-proper notification**.

Principal persons include:

- **Controllers** (10%+ shareholders);
- **Directors** (all);
- **Senior officers** (CEO, CFO, COO, Head of Compliance,
  MLRO);
- **Other** named persons by JFSC determination.

## Capital adequacy

The applicant must:

- Hold **regulatory capital** at the level prescribed for its
  risk profile;
- **Initial capital** typically substantial (£5m+ for new
  Jersey bank);
- **Maintain** capital ratios in line with Basel III standards
  (CET1, Tier 1, total capital);
- **Stress-test** adequacy under JFSC-approved scenarios.

## Liquidity

The applicant must have:

- **Liquidity coverage** ratio (LCR) compliance;
- **Net stable funding** ratio (NSFR) compliance;
- **Stressed liquidity** scenarios pre-modeled;
- **Funding** plan that does not over-rely on volatile sources;
- **Contingency funding** arrangements.

## Systems and controls

The applicant must have:

- **Three lines of defence** risk management;
- **Internal audit** function (typically group-supported);
- **Compliance** function with senior leadership;
- **AML / CFT** programme and MLRO;
- **IT systems** with appropriate resilience;
- **Operational continuity** planning;
- **Outsourcing** controls if relying on group services.

## Home regulator engagement

The JFSC requires:

- **Consent letter** from home regulator (for foreign
  bank subsidiaries / branches);
- **Memorandum of understanding** with home regulator if
  needed;
- **Information sharing** arrangements;
- **Resolution coordination** in distressed scenarios.

## Public interest

The JFSC considers the **broader public interest**:

- **Jersey banking sector** stability and capacity;
- **Top-50 policy** alignment (operating policy, not
  statutory);
- **Reputational** factors for Jersey;
- **Competition** considerations;
- **Demand** for the proposed services in Jersey.
