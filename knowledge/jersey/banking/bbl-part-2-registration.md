# BBL 1991 Part 2 — Registration (residual)

The principal Part 2 provisions (Articles 4 and 6) have their
own deep files. This cluster covers Articles 5, 7-9.

## Article 5 — Application for registration

The application includes:

- **Identity** of the applicant;
- **Constitutional documents** (memorandum and articles or
  equivalent);
- **Business plan** — proposed scope of activity, depositor
  base, lending plans;
- **Capital** — proposed capital base, source of capital;
- **Principal persons** — directors, senior officers,
  controllers, with fit-and-proper information;
- **Group structure** — parent banking group, sister
  affiliates;
- **Home regulator** confirmation and consent letter;
- **Local infrastructure** — Jersey premises, staff, systems;
- **AML / CFT** programme;
- **Auditors** and **internal audit** arrangements;
- **Risk management** framework;
- **Recovery / resolution** plan summary;
- **Prescribed fees**.

The application is **substantial** — typically months of
pre-application engagement with the JFSC, followed by formal
application of 100+ pages with supporting evidence.

## Article 7 — Conditions of registration

The JFSC may impose **conditions** on registration:

- **Scope** of activity (e.g., wholesale only, retail
  permitted);
- **Capital and liquidity** requirements;
- **Concentration limits** (large exposures);
- **Geographical** limits;
- **Reporting** requirements;
- **Senior management** approval requirements for material
  changes;
- **Operational** requirements (segregation, audit, IT
  resilience).

Conditions are **enforceable** — breach is a regulatory
offence and grounds for action.

## Article 8 — Variation of registration

A registered bank can apply for **variation** of:

- **Conditions** of registration;
- **Scope** of business;
- **Principal persons** approved;
- **Other** registration details.

The JFSC's response is risk-based — material changes
(expansion of scope, new products) attract more scrutiny than
administrative updates (change of address).

## Article 9 — Refusal, suspension, revocation

The JFSC may:

### Refuse

- For **failure** to meet Article 6 conditions;
- For **inadequate** fit-and-proper of principals;
- For **inadequate** capital or business plan;
- For **adverse** home-regulator information;
- For **public-interest** reasons (e.g., already-served-market
  consideration).

### Suspend

- For **breach** of conditions;
- For **breach** of continuing obligations;
- For **public-interest** reasons (e.g., suspected illegal
  activity);
- **Pending** investigation.

### Revoke

- For **material breach**;
- For **failure** to remedy breaches;
- For **loss** of fit-and-proper status;
- For **substantive** prudential failure (capital, liquidity);
- For **AML / CFT** material failure.

Refusal / suspension / revocation decisions are **subject to
appeal** to the Royal Court.

## Procedural protections

Registration / revocation decisions are subject to:

- **Notice** of intention with grounds;
- **Right** to make representations;
- **Reasoned** decision;
- **Right of appeal** to the Royal Court;
- **Stay** of decision in some cases pending appeal.
