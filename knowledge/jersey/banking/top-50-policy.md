# Top-50 Bank Policy — Jersey

The **"Top-50" policy** is Jersey's **historical preference** —
expressed in policy rather than statute — for licensing banks
that are part of **major international banking groups**, broadly
those ranked among the world's **fifty largest** banks by
relevant measure.

## Origin

The policy emerged in the late twentieth century as part of
Jersey's positioning as a **prudent, well-regulated** offshore
banking centre:

- Jersey wanted to avoid the **reputational risks** of housing
  weak or speculative banking operations;
- It preferred banks with **strong parent-group capital backing**;
- The "top-50" measure became a **shorthand** for the policy
  threshold.

## Modern operation

The policy has been **moderated** over time:

- Strict "top-50" criteria are no longer applied mechanically;
- The principle of **strong parent-group backing** persists;
- The JFSC retains **discretion** to license banks outside the
  literal top-50 if they meet the underlying quality criteria;
- New applicants face thorough **fit-and-proper** scrutiny.

## Why it matters

The policy effects show in:

- **The character of Jersey's banking sector** — dominated by
  major international names (HSBC, Barclays, Standard Chartered,
  RBS / NatWest, Santander, etc.);
- **Stability** — Jersey banking has been less exposed to
  challenger / specialist bank failures than some onshore
  jurisdictions;
- **Reputation** — Jersey is associated with established,
  well-capitalised banks rather than fringe operations.
