# BBL 1991 Part 4 — Capital and Prudential (residual)

Article 14 (capital requirements) has its own deep file. This
cluster covers Articles 15-17 — liquidity, large exposures,
and other prudential matters.

## Article 15 — Liquidity

A registered bank must maintain **adequate liquidity**:

### Liquidity Coverage Ratio (LCR)

- **High-quality liquid assets** ≥ 100% of expected 30-day
  net cash outflow under stress;
- Composition of HQLA: cash, central-bank reserves, high-
  rated sovereign debt, certain other instruments;
- **Run-off** rates assumed for funding sources;
- **Daily** monitoring.

### Net Stable Funding Ratio (NSFR)

- **Available stable funding** ≥ 100% of **required stable
  funding**;
- Calibrated over a **1-year horizon**;
- Designed to ensure funding profile is sustainable.

### Liquidity buffer

- **Internal** buffer above regulatory minimum;
- **Stress-tested** under various scenarios;
- **Contingency funding plan** documented and tested;
- **Diversified** funding sources.

### Intraday liquidity

- **Adequate** intraday cash for settlement;
- **Monitoring** systems;
- **Limits** for material exposures.

## Article 16 — Large exposures

A registered bank must limit **large exposures**:

### Single counterparty limit

- Exposure to any **single counterparty** capped at **25% of
  capital** (Tier 1 typically);
- **Connected counterparties** aggregated;
- **Group exposures** (related companies) treated as a single
  exposure.

### Sector limits

- **Industry-sector** concentration limits;
- **Geographic** concentration limits;
- **Product type** concentration limits.

### Reporting

- **Large exposure reports** to JFSC;
- **Breaches** notified immediately;
- **Action plans** for remediation.

## Article 17 — Other prudential

Other prudential requirements include:

### Counterparty credit risk

- **Internal credit assessment** for all counterparties;
- **Margin and collateral** management;
- **Wrong-way risk** identification.

### Operational risk

- **Capital** allocation for operational risk (Basel III
  standardised approach);
- **Incident** reporting and analysis;
- **Business continuity** planning;
- **IT resilience**;
- **Outsourcing** governance.

### Market risk

- **Trading book** capital;
- **VaR** or **standardised** approach;
- **Stress testing**.

### Interest rate risk in the banking book (IRRBB)

- **Economic value of equity** sensitivity;
- **Net interest income** sensitivity;
- **JFSC outlier** test (capital allocation if outlier).

### Pillar 2 / ICAAP

- **ICAAP** identifies all material risks;
- **Capital allocation** beyond Pillar 1 minimums;
- **Annual review** by senior management and JFSC.
