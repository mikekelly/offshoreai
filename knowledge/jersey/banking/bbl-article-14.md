# BBL 1991 Article 14 — Capital Requirements

Article 14 provides the **statutory basis** for the JFSC's
capital-adequacy regime — operationalised in Basel-III-aligned
implementing rules and Codes of Practice.

## The provision

A registered bank must hold **regulatory capital**:

- At the **level** prescribed by JFSC Order / Code;
- In the **composition** prescribed (CET1, Tier 1, Tier 2);
- **Calculated** by reference to the bank's risk-weighted
  exposures;
- **Reported** in prescribed returns;
- **Stress-tested** under specified scenarios.

## Composition

Regulatory capital comprises:

### Common Equity Tier 1 (CET1)

- **Paid-up ordinary share capital**;
- **Retained earnings**;
- **Disclosed reserves**;
- Subject to **deductions** (goodwill, deferred tax assets,
  certain holdings).

### Additional Tier 1 (AT1)

- **Perpetual non-cumulative** preferred instruments;
- **Contingent convertibles** (CoCos);
- Subject to **trigger** and **loss-absorption** features.

### Tier 2

- **Subordinated debt** with minimum maturity;
- **General** loan loss provisions (limited);
- **Other** specified items.

## Minimum ratios

The JFSC operates Basel-III-aligned minimum ratios:

- **CET1 ratio** — minimum 4.5% of risk-weighted assets;
- **Tier 1 ratio** — minimum 6%;
- **Total capital ratio** — minimum 8%;
- Plus **buffers**:
  - **Capital conservation buffer** — 2.5% additional CET1;
  - **Counter-cyclical buffer** — variable per JFSC
    determination (currently 0% in Jersey at most points);
  - **Systemic / D-SIB buffer** — for systemic institutions.

## Risk weighting

Exposures are **risk-weighted** by:

- **Counterparty** (sovereign, bank, corporate, retail,
  exposure type);
- **Credit risk** standardised or internal-ratings-based
  approach;
- **Market risk** (trading book exposures);
- **Operational risk** (typically Basel III standardised
  approach);
- **Other** specific risks (CCR, settlement, securitisation).

## Leverage ratio

In addition to risk-weighted ratios:

- **Leverage ratio** — Tier 1 capital divided by total
  exposure (unweighted);
- **Minimum** 3% (per Basel III standardised);
- **Cap** on leverage regardless of risk-weighting.

## Pillar 2 — additional capital

Beyond Pillar 1 (minimum ratios):

- **Pillar 2** — the JFSC may require **additional
  institution-specific capital** to address risks not fully
  captured by Pillar 1;
- Calibrated based on **ICAAP** review;
- **Confidential** but in some cases publicly disclosed.

## Stress testing

The JFSC requires:

- **Bank's own** stress testing (idiosyncratic and
  macroeconomic scenarios);
- **JFSC-specified** scenarios for supervisory comparison;
- **Reverse** stress testing (identifying scenarios that
  would render the bank non-viable);
- **Capital plans** addressing stress outcomes.
