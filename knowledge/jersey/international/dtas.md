# Double Taxation Agreements (DTAs) — Jersey

A **Double Taxation Agreement** (DTA) is a **bilateral tax
treaty** between Jersey and another jurisdiction. It allocates
taxing rights between the two jurisdictions to prevent double
taxation of the same income.

## Jersey's DTA network

Jersey has DTAs with a **selected** set of jurisdictions —
fewer than major economies but covering Jersey's key economic
partners:

- **United Kingdom** — the most important DTA, covering all
  major income types;
- **Guernsey** — Crown Dependency DTA;
- **Isle of Man** — Crown Dependency DTA;
- **Cyprus, Estonia, Luxembourg, Malta** — EU partners;
- **Hong Kong, Singapore, UAE, Qatar** — Asian / Middle Eastern
  partners;
- **A handful of others**, growing as Jersey concludes new DTAs.

The Jersey-UK DTA is **by far** the most heavily used given the
density of Jersey-UK financial and personal connections.

## What DTAs do

DTAs typically include:

- **Residence tie-breakers** for individuals and companies that
  are dual-resident;
- **Reduced withholding rates** on cross-border dividends,
  interest, royalties (often eliminating withholding entirely
  for treaty-qualified payments);
- **Permanent-establishment** rules to determine when a
  jurisdiction can tax foreign-resident business income;
- **Capital gains** allocation rules;
- **Mutual agreement procedure (MAP)** for resolving treaty
  disputes;
- **Information exchange** provisions;
- **Non-discrimination** clauses;
- **Tax credit / exemption** mechanisms for double-taxation
  relief.

## Practical Jersey-UK examples

- A **UK-resident individual** with a Jersey company shareholding —
  the DTA does not eliminate UK personal tax on Jersey dividends
  but ensures Jersey does not also tax (Article 123C(2) Jersey
  company pays no Jersey tax on dividend distributions anyway).
- A **Jersey-resident individual** with UK rental income — UK
  taxes the rental income under non-resident landlord rules;
  Jersey gives a credit for UK tax paid on the same income.
- A **Jersey company** owning UK shares — UK withholding on
  dividends is typically reduced or eliminated under the DTA.

## Limitations

Jersey's DTA network is **smaller** than that of major economies:

- **Many bilateral relationships** are governed only by TIEAs
  (information exchange) without full DTAs;
- **Tax-credit** relief is therefore the principal tool for
  double-taxation relief in non-DTA cases, but only some
  jurisdictions give unilateral credit;
- **Pillar Two** (Jersey MCIT 2024) provides a separate top-up
  framework alongside DTAs.

## Multilateral Instrument (MLI)

Jersey has joined the **OECD Multilateral Convention to Implement
Tax Treaty Related Measures to Prevent BEPS** (MLI). The MLI
modifies covered DTAs to incorporate **BEPS Action 6** anti-
avoidance provisions (principal purpose test, limitation on
benefits).

This effectively **updates** Jersey's DTA network for **anti-
abuse purposes** without renegotiating each bilateral treaty.
