# Tax Information Exchange Agreements (TIEAs) — Jersey

A **TIEA** is a bilateral treaty for **information exchange on
request** between tax authorities. TIEAs are **lighter** than
DTAs — they handle information cooperation but do **not** include
DTA-style allocation of taxing rights or withholding-rate
reductions.

## Jersey's TIEA network

Jersey has TIEAs with **over 30 jurisdictions**, including:

- **All major OECD members** — US, UK, France, Germany, Italy,
  Canada, Australia, Japan, etc.;
- **Major emerging economies** — Brazil, India, Mexico, South
  Africa;
- **Various other partners** with no full DTA but where info
  exchange is mutually desired.

The TIEA network was built **rapidly** in the 2000s as part of
Jersey's response to international pressure on offshore finance
centres.

## What TIEAs do

A typical Jersey TIEA provides for:

- **Information exchange on request** — tax authority A asks
  Jersey for information about a person of interest to A's tax
  investigation;
- **Domestic-law-equivalent powers** — Jersey gathers
  information using domestic powers (e.g. Revenue Jersey demands
  to FIs);
- **Use restrictions** — information used only for tax purposes;
- **Confidentiality** maintained between authorities.

TIEAs do **not** provide:

- **Automatic exchange** (that is CRS / FATCA);
- **Reduced withholding rates** (that is DTAs);
- **Tie-breaker rules** (that is DTAs).

## Why TIEAs mattered

In the early 2000s, **offshore finance centres** including
Jersey faced significant pressure to:

- **Cooperate** with onshore tax authorities investigating their
  citizens' offshore holdings;
- **Move off** OECD / EU "uncooperative" lists.

TIEAs were the **proportionate response** — they allowed
information sharing without committing to a full DTA's
allocation of taxing rights. Jersey's TIEA-rich network was a
key part of moving from "uncooperative" to "cooperative" status.

## TIEAs today

With **CRS** providing **automatic** exchange for financial-
account data, TIEAs are increasingly less central — most
day-to-day information flow happens automatically rather than
on request. But TIEAs remain important for:

- **Bespoke information requests** outside CRS scope (e.g. tax-
  investigation-specific information not in the CRS data set);
- **Pre-CRS-era** investigations of historical conduct;
- **Mutual assistance** in tax collection (specifically provided
  by some TIEAs).
