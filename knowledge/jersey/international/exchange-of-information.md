# Exchange of Information — International Channels

## What this is about

Jersey participates in multiple **international information-
exchange** channels covering tax, AML, and regulatory
information. This file maps the **landscape**.

## Tax information exchange

### Common Reporting Standard (CRS)

- **OECD framework** for automatic exchange of financial
  account information;
- **100+ jurisdictions** participating;
- **Jersey reports** annually on accounts held by tax-resident
  individuals / entities from CRS-partner jurisdictions to
  the relevant tax authority;
- **Comprehensive**: covers bank accounts, custodial accounts,
  cash-value insurance, certain entity accounts;
- See [`crs.md`](crs.md).

### FATCA

- **US** Foreign Account Tax Compliance Act;
- **Jersey IGA** (intergovernmental agreement) with the US;
- **Jersey reports** to the Comptroller of Revenue, which
  exchanges with the IRS;
- **Specific** to US-tied accounts / persons;
- See [`fatca.md`](fatca.md).

### Tax Information Exchange Agreements (TIEAs)

- **Bilateral** TIEAs with specific partners;
- **On-request** information exchange (not automatic);
- **Cover** specific case-by-case requests by treaty partner
  tax authorities;
- Largely **superseded** by DTAs with EOI articles, CRS, and
  multilateral CbC;
- See [`tieas.md`](tieas.md).

### DTAs — Article 26

- **DTAs** include an **Article 26** equivalent permitting
  information exchange between treaty partners;
- **On-request** plus, increasingly, **spontaneous** and
  **automatic** in some cases;
- **Subject to** treaty safeguards (relevance, fiscal
  necessity, confidentiality);
- See [`dtas.md`](dtas.md).

### Country-by-Country Reporting (CbCR)

- **Ultimate parents** of MNE groups with €750m+ revenue file
  CbCR;
- **MCAA CbC** (Multilateral Competent Authority Agreement)
  enables exchange;
- **Jersey-resident** parents file with the Comptroller of
  Revenue, which exchanges with treaty partners;
- See [`./beps-overview.md`](beps-overview.md).

### Substance reporting

- **Annual substance return** under the Substance Law 2019;
- **Exchanged** with relevant tax authorities of beneficial
  owners' jurisdictions;
- **Identifies** substance status of Jersey entities for
  foreign-tax purposes.

## AML information exchange

### FIU-to-FIU

- **Egmont Group** membership — Jersey FIU exchanges with
  170+ partner FIUs;
- **Bilateral** information requests on suspected ML / TF;
- **Spontaneous** disclosures of suspect information.

### POCL Article 36 / Tipping off carve-out

- **Regulated firms** sharing AML information under POCL
  Article 36;
- **Group-wide** and **same-customer/transaction** sharing
  permitted;
- See [`../aml-cft/pocl-article-36.md`](../aml-cft/pocl-article-36.md).

### MLA / treaty channels

- **Mutual legal assistance** in criminal matters (MLAT);
- **Crime (International Co-operation) (Jersey) Law 2001** as
  Jersey-side enabler;
- **Letters of request** for evidence in foreign criminal
  proceedings.

## Regulatory information exchange

### JFSC bilateral

- **MoUs** with foreign regulators (FCA, PRA, SEC, ECB,
  Single Resolution Board, etc.);
- **Information** exchange on supervisory matters;
- **Joint** supervision for groups;
- **Confidentiality** safeguards.

### International associations

- **IOSCO** — financial markets;
- **IAIS** — insurance supervisors;
- **GIFCS** — Group of International Finance Centre
  Supervisors;
- **Bank for International Settlements (BIS)** — banking
  supervision via Basel Committee observer participation.

### Resolution

- **Resolution colleges** for cross-border banking groups;
- **Bilateral** resolution coordination MoUs.

## Beneficial ownership

### Public access

- Jersey's **central register** of beneficial owners
  (Financial Services (Disclosure and Provision of
  Information) (Jersey) Law 2020);
- **Authorities** have access;
- **Public access** is limited per Jersey's current
  legislative framework;
- **Foreign authorities** receive information through MoU /
  treaty channels.

### Cross-jurisdictional cooperation

- Jersey's central register interfaces with **UK / EU
  registers** where compatible;
- **OECD / EU pressure** for expanded transparency continues.

## Practical implications

For Jersey-based structures:

- **Multiple information flows** — Jersey vehicles' details
  are exchanged through various channels with foreign
  authorities;
- **Comprehensive transparency** for tax purposes for the
  beneficial owners' jurisdictions;
- **Privacy** preserved against the public but not against
  authorities;
- **Compliance burden** distributed across CRS, FATCA, CbCR,
  Substance, and bilateral AML / regulatory.
