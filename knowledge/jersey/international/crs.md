# Common Reporting Standard (CRS) — Jersey

The **Common Reporting Standard (CRS)** is the **OECD-developed
framework** for **automatic exchange of financial-account
information** between participating jurisdictions. Jersey is a
**reporting jurisdiction** and has been since the CRS launched.

## What CRS does

Under CRS:

- **Financial institutions (FIs)** in a participating jurisdiction
  identify their **non-resident account holders**;
- They **classify accounts** under prescribed CRS rules;
- They **report** account holder identity, balance, income, and
  prescribed transaction data **annually** to their local tax
  authority;
- The local tax authority **forwards** the data to the relevant
  **foreign tax authority** under bilateral or multilateral
  exchange agreements;
- Foreign authorities can **match** the data against their
  domestic tax records to verify that foreign-source income is
  properly declared.

## CRS in Jersey

Jersey implementation:

- **Taxation (Implementation) (International Tax Compliance)
  (Common Reporting Standard) (Jersey) Regulations 2015** —
  domestic legal framework;
- **Revenue Jersey** receives FI reports and forwards data to
  partner jurisdictions;
- **Multilateral Convention on Mutual Administrative Assistance
  in Tax Matters (MAC)** plus the **CRS MCAA** — the international
  legal basis for exchange.

## Who reports

A **Jersey Financial Institution** for CRS purposes is broadly:

- **Custodial institutions** — banks, brokers holding financial
  assets for customers;
- **Depository institutions** — banks;
- **Investment entities** — fund administrators, fund vehicles,
  some trust companies (broad definition);
- **Specified insurance companies** — insurers with
  cash-value contracts.

The "investment entity" category is the **broadest** and captures
many **Jersey trusts and companies** that hold investment assets
managed by a manager, with significant cross-border reach.

## What is reported

For each reportable account:

- **Account holder identity** — name, address, jurisdiction of
  residence, tax ID, date of birth;
- **Account number**;
- **Account balance / value** at year-end;
- **Gross interest / dividends / proceeds** credited during the
  year;
- For **controlling persons** of certain entity accounts —
  identification of beneficial owners.

## Trust-specific complexities

CRS captures trusts in two main ways:

- **Trust as a financial institution** — many Jersey trusts are
  classified as "investment entities" under CRS;
- **Trust account holder** — settlors, beneficiaries, protectors,
  trustees of foreign trusts may be reportable persons.

Classification is **fact-sensitive** and a major **compliance
area** for Jersey trust companies.

## Penalties

Failure to comply with CRS obligations attracts:

- **Civil financial penalties** under the implementing Regulations;
- **Reputational risk** for institutions found non-compliant;
- **Risk** of further enforcement under the FS(J)L 1998 for
  regulated entities.
