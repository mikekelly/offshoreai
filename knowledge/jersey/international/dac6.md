# DAC6 — EU Mandatory Disclosure Rules and Jersey

## What DAC6 is

**DAC6** is **EU Directive 2018/822**, the **6th amendment**
to the Directive on Administrative Cooperation in Tax (DAC).
It requires **intermediaries** (and in some cases taxpayers)
to **report** cross-border tax arrangements meeting specified
**hallmarks** to their national tax authority, which then
exchanges the information across the EU.

The Directive applies to **EU intermediaries** and **EU
taxpayers** in cross-border arrangements.

## Jersey's position

Jersey is **not** an EU Member State and is **not** subject
to DAC6 directly:

- Jersey **does not implement** DAC6 domestically;
- Jersey **does not report** under DAC6 framework;
- **Jersey intermediaries** (e.g., Jersey trustees, Jersey
  service providers) **do not have DAC6 reporting obligations**
  on a stand-alone basis.

However, DAC6 affects Jersey practice in **practical** ways:

### Cross-border arrangements involving EU intermediaries

- If an **EU lawyer**, **EU accountant**, or **EU service
  provider** is involved in a cross-border arrangement with a
  Jersey nexus, that EU intermediary may have a DAC6
  reporting obligation;
- The EU intermediary will report **EU-side details**
  including the Jersey nexus;
- Jersey-side participants may need to **provide information**
  to the EU intermediary to enable the report.

### Cross-border arrangements with EU taxpayers

- If an **EU-resident taxpayer** (individual or entity) is a
  party to the cross-border arrangement, the taxpayer's
  jurisdiction may impose **secondary reporting** under
  DAC6;
- This creates **disclosure** of the arrangement to EU tax
  authorities;
- Jersey-side participants are not directly required to
  report but are aware that the arrangement is being
  disclosed.

### UK position

The **UK** initially implemented DAC6 during the transition
period, then **withdrew** most of it post-Brexit:

- UK now applies a **narrower** Mandatory Disclosure Rules
  (MDR) regime aligned with OECD recommendations;
- UK MDR is **less comprehensive** than full DAC6;
- Jersey nexus involving UK intermediaries / taxpayers is
  governed by the UK MDR, not full DAC6.

## DAC6 hallmarks (summary)

The Directive lists **5 categories** of hallmarks. Arrangements
exhibiting one or more are reportable:

### Category A — generic hallmarks linked to main benefit test

- **Confidentiality** clauses preventing disclosure of the
  tax benefit;
- **Contingent fee** for the intermediary linked to the tax
  benefit;
- **Standardised** documentation / structure used by
  multiple taxpayers without substantial customisation.

### Category B — specific hallmarks linked to main benefit test

- **Loss** acquisitions / use;
- **Income converted** to capital, gifts, or other lower-
  taxed categories;
- **Circular transactions** with no commercial purpose.

### Category C — specific cross-border transactions

- **Deductible** payment with **multiple-jurisdictional
  recipient mismatch**;
- **Double-non-taxation** through tax residency mismatches;
- **Hybrid** payments / instruments;
- Cross-border depreciation / write-offs duplication;
- Asset transfers without commercial purpose where the
  recipient has different tax base.

### Category D — Automatic Exchange of Information / Beneficial Ownership

- Arrangements **undermining CRS** reporting;
- Use of **opaque legal structures** in jurisdictions
  without beneficial-ownership transparency.

### Category E — transfer pricing

- **Unilateral safe-harbour** rules;
- **Hard-to-value intangible** transfers;
- **Cross-border** group reorganisations with significant EBIT
  shift.

## What Jersey practitioners should do

### When advising on cross-border arrangements

- **Identify** whether EU intermediaries are involved;
- **Assess** hallmarks if EU intermediaries or EU taxpayers
  are in scope;
- **Cooperate** with EU intermediaries on information
  required for their reporting;
- **Document** the analysis and rationale;
- **Privilege** considerations — EU intermediaries may have
  privilege defences modifying the reporting obligation.

### Privilege under DAC6

- **Legal professional privilege** is a recognised defence in
  some Member States;
- In practice, the **client** then takes on reporting in many
  cases;
- The interaction is complex and **jurisdiction-specific**.
