# FATCA — Jersey

The **US Foreign Account Tax Compliance Act (FATCA)** is the
**US-specific automatic information exchange framework**.
Jersey implements FATCA through an **Intergovernmental Agreement
(IGA)** with the US.

## What FATCA does

FATCA requires **non-US financial institutions** to identify
**US-person account holders** and report them to the US Internal
Revenue Service (IRS):

- Either **directly** to the IRS (Model 2 IGA jurisdictions); or
- Via their **local tax authority** which forwards to the IRS
  (Model 1 IGA jurisdictions — including Jersey).

## Jersey's Model 1 IGA

Jersey signed a **Model 1 IGA** with the US:

- Jersey Financial Institutions (FFIs) report to **Revenue
  Jersey**;
- Revenue Jersey forwards the data to the **IRS**;
- The arrangement is **bilateral** — Jersey receives reciprocal
  information about Jersey-resident persons with US accounts.

## Definition of US person

A **US person** for FATCA purposes is:

- A **US citizen** (regardless of where resident);
- A **US permanent resident** (green-card holder, in many cases);
- A **US-resident individual** under the substantial-presence
  test;
- A **US corporation, partnership, or other entity**;
- An **estate of a US person**;
- Certain **trusts** with US persons in control or as
  beneficiaries.

The definition is **broad** and captures more individuals than
might be expected (US citizens born in the US who have lived
abroad all their lives, for example).

## What is reported

Similar to CRS but focused on US persons:

- **Identity** of US-person account holders;
- **Tax Identification Number** (TIN);
- **Account balance / value**;
- **Income credited**;
- For entity accounts, **controlling US persons**.

## Penalties (the FATCA stick)

The **US-level** consequence of non-compliance is severe:

- **30% withholding tax** on **US-source income and gross
  proceeds** paid to non-compliant FFIs;
- This effectively **excludes** a non-compliant FFI from US
  capital markets.

The Jersey-level consequence of FFI non-compliance is **civil
financial penalties** under the implementing Jersey Regulations.

## Jersey FFI categories

The IGA recognises **simplified categories**:

- **Sponsored** FFIs — a sponsor reports on behalf of multiple
  entities;
- **Trustee-documented trusts** — the trustee reports for the
  trust;
- **Deemed-compliant** FFIs — entities meeting prescribed criteria
  with reduced obligations;
- **Non-reporting** FFIs — entities below the threshold or
  otherwise exempt.

Classification work is a **significant compliance task** for
Jersey trust companies and fund administrators with mixed
client books.

## Relationship with CRS

FATCA and CRS are **complementary**:

- **FATCA** — US-only, with the US 30% withholding stick;
- **CRS** — multilateral, no equivalent stick but global
  participation;
- Jersey FIs typically run **integrated compliance programmes**
  covering both.
