# BEPS — Base Erosion and Profit Shifting

## What BEPS is

**Base Erosion and Profit Shifting (BEPS)** is the **OECD/G20
project** to address aggressive tax planning by multinational
groups that **shifts profits** from high-tax jurisdictions to
low-tax / no-tax jurisdictions or **erodes the tax base** of
relevant countries.

The project began in **2013** with the OECD's BEPS Action
Plan, comprising **15 actions**. **BEPS 2.0** (the "Two
Pillars" of 2021) extended the project to global minimum tax
(Pillar Two) and revised allocation of taxing rights for
digital-economy enterprises (Pillar One).

## The 15 Original BEPS Actions

The original BEPS package (2015):

1. **Digital economy** — tax challenges of the digital
   economy;
2. **Hybrid mismatches** — neutralising tax-arbitrage from
   hybrid arrangements;
3. **CFC rules** — strengthening controlled foreign company
   rules;
4. **Interest deductions** — limiting interest deductions
   (BEPS Action 4 — ATAD / equivalent);
5. **Harmful tax practices** — substantial activity
   requirements for preferential regimes;
6. **Treaty abuse** — preventing treaty shopping (Principal
   Purpose Test, LOB clauses);
7. **Permanent establishment** — preventing PE avoidance;
8-10. **Transfer pricing** — aligning transfer pricing
    outcomes with value creation;
11. **Data and analysis** — measuring BEPS;
12. **Disclosure of aggressive tax planning**;
13. **Country-by-country reporting** (CbCR);
14. **Dispute resolution** — improving mutual agreement
    procedure;
15. **Multilateral instrument (MLI)** — implementing treaty
    measures.

## Jersey's BEPS implementation

### Substance Law 2019 (Action 5)

The **Taxation (Companies — Economic Substance) (Jersey) Law
2019** implements BEPS Action 5 requirements:

- **Substance requirements** for in-scope activities (banking,
  insurance, fund management, financing / leasing, IP,
  headquarters, shipping, distribution-and-service-centre,
  pure equity holding);
- **CIGAs** (core income-generating activities) must be
  performed in Jersey;
- **Substance evidence** — directed in Jersey, qualified
  employees, premises, expenditure;
- **Annual substance return** filed with the Comptroller of
  Revenue;
- **Penalties** for non-compliance, escalating to strike-off
  for serious cases.

### CbCR (Action 13)

Jersey implemented **country-by-country reporting**:

- **Ultimate parents** of MNE groups (€750m+ revenue) file
  CbCR;
- **Jersey-resident** parents file with the Comptroller of
  Revenue;
- **Information exchanged** with treaty partners under MCAA
  CbC.

### MLI (Action 15)

Jersey signed the **Multilateral Instrument**:

- **Modifying** Jersey's bilateral tax treaties to implement
  BEPS treaty-related measures;
- Adding **Principal Purpose Test** to most DTAs;
- **Other** treaty-abuse provisions.

### Hybrid mismatches, interest deductions, CFC

Jersey's **zero-ten** regime means most of these BEPS
mechanisms have less direct effect than in high-tax
jurisdictions. But Jersey-resident groups are subject to
**foreign-jurisdiction** BEPS rules that may attribute Jersey
income to onshore taxation:

- **CFC rules** (in UK, US, EU) attribute Jersey subsidiary
  income to onshore parents;
- **Interest deduction limits** affect cross-border lending
  structures;
- **Hybrid mismatch** rules neutralise tax-arbitrage from
  Jersey hybrid entities.

### Pillar Two (BEPS 2.0)

Jersey's MCIT (2025) implements Pillar Two for in-scope
multinational groups — see
[`pillar-two-mcit.md`](./pillar-two-mcit.md).

## Implications for Jersey IFC

### Substance is mandatory

- Pre-2019 "letterbox" structures **no longer viable** for
  in-scope activities;
- Jersey substance is **inspectable** and **enforced**;
- Substance requirements drive **real economic activity** in
  Jersey (employees, offices, IT, decision-making).

### Tax-rate neutrality still relevant for non-Pillar-Two

- For **groups below €750m** revenue, zero-ten preserved;
- Most **family-office** structures unaffected;
- **Smaller corporate** groups continue to benefit.

### Compliance burden

- **Substance reporting** — annual;
- **Pillar Two** — for in-scope groups;
- **CbCR** — for in-scope groups;
- **MLI** treaty implementation in cross-border structures.
