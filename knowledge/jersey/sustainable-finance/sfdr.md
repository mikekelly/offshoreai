# SFDR — Application to Jersey-Domiciled Funds

## What it is

The **Sustainable Finance Disclosure Regulation
(Regulation (EU) 2019/2088)** — known as **SFDR** —
is the EU regime requiring financial-market participants
to make sustainability-related disclosures at the
**entity level** and the **product level**. It entered
application in **March 2021**.

SFDR introduced the now-familiar **Article 6 / Article 8
/ Article 9** product-classification system that has
become the standard taxonomy for sustainable funds across
Europe and (by extension and adoption) globally.

## Why this matters for Jersey

Jersey is **not in EU scope** for SFDR directly. But
Jersey-domiciled funds marketed into the EU under the
[AIFMD National Private Placement Regime (NPPR)](../funds/aifmd-nppr.md)
are expected to comply with **SFDR-equivalent disclosure**
where they make sustainability claims. The reasons:

- **Market practice convergence** — EU investors expect
  SFDR-style disclosure regardless of fund domicile;
- **JFSC guidance** — adopts a MiCA-compatible posture
  on sustainability disclosure;
- **EU national-competent-authority gating** — some EU
  Member States' NCAs require SFDR-equivalent disclosure
  as a condition of NPPR registration;
- **Greenwashing risk** — making sustainability claims
  without SFDR-equivalent rigour is increasingly
  reputationally and regulatorily costly.

For most Jersey funds with EU investor base or marketing,
**treating SFDR as effectively applicable** is the
practical default.

## The three product categories

### Article 6 — non-sustainable

A fund that does **not promote ESG characteristics** and
does **not have sustainable investment as an objective**.
This is the default categorisation. Article 6 disclosure
is minimal: the manager states that the product does not
make sustainability claims.

### Article 8 — promotes ESG characteristics ("light green")

A fund that **promotes environmental or social
characteristics** without having sustainable investment
as its objective. Article 8 covers a wide range of ESG-
labelled products and is the most common category for
sustainable-marketed funds.

Article 8 requires:

- **Pre-contractual disclosure** of the ESG
  characteristics promoted;
- **Periodic reporting** (typically annual) of how those
  characteristics were met;
- **Website disclosure** of the sustainability strategy;
- **Engagement and stewardship** disclosure where
  applicable.

### Article 9 — sustainable investment objective ("dark green")

A fund that has **sustainable investment as its
objective**. Article 9 is the most demanding category and
applies typically to dedicated impact funds, climate funds,
and explicit-sustainability strategies.

Article 9 requires (in addition to Article 8):

- **All investments** to qualify as sustainable
  investments under the SFDR definition;
- **Stronger** sustainability-integration documentation;
- **Demonstrable contribution** to sustainable objectives;
- **Do-no-significant-harm (DNSH) testing**.

## How to choose the right classification

The conservative practitioner approach:

1. **Start at Article 6** unless the fund makes ESG
   claims;
2. **Move to Article 8** if any sustainability claim is
   made in marketing material;
3. **Move to Article 9 only** if the fund is structurally
   committed to all-sustainable-investments and has the
   integration depth to evidence it.

The most common practitioner error is **over-classifying**
to Article 9 to maximise marketing impact, then being
unable to evidence the substantive sustainability
integration. This is the classic greenwashing-risk
trigger.

## SFDR mechanics for a Jersey-domiciled fund

### Entity-level disclosure

The Jersey AIFM (or, in delegation arrangements, the
delegated AIFM) must publish:

- **Sustainability risks policy** — how sustainability
  risks are integrated into investment decisions;
- **Adverse impacts statement** — whether the firm
  considers principal adverse impacts (PAIs) of investment
  decisions on sustainability factors;
- **Remuneration policy** — how the firm's
  remuneration policy is consistent with sustainability
  risks integration.

### Product-level disclosure

For each fund:

- **Pre-contractual disclosure** in the offering memorandum /
  PPM (the SFDR Annex IV / V templates apply for
  Article 8 / 9);
- **Periodic disclosure** (annual report) on
  sustainability outcomes;
- **Website disclosure** of the fund's sustainability
  strategy.

### Taxonomy Regulation alignment

For funds making **environmental** sustainability claims,
the **EU Taxonomy Regulation (Regulation (EU) 2020/852)**
applies overlay disclosure: the proportion of investments
aligned with the EU Taxonomy's environmentally sustainable
economic activities. For Article 9 funds with
environmental objectives this is core.

## Practical considerations for Jersey funds

### Limited substantive impact for non-EU LPs

If a Jersey fund has **no EU LPs and no EU marketing**,
SFDR has limited substantive application. The fund can
make sustainability claims under JFSC guidance without
the full SFDR machinery — though best practice is to
adopt SFDR-style disclosure regardless.

### Co-existence with JFSC guidance

The JFSC's sustainable-finance guidance is consistent with
SFDR but more flexible on detailed-disclosure templates.
Jersey funds positioning as sustainable typically adopt
the SFDR Annex templates as the de facto standard.

### Documentation discipline

The single most important practitioner discipline is
**making the disclosure honestly match the underlying
investment activity**. Misalignment is the greenwashing
trigger.

## What's changing

**SFDR 2.0** is the dominant live development. The
European Commission published its formal proposal **20
November 2025**, with implementation expected **2027-2028**.
SFDR 2.0 is expected to:

- **Replace or evolve** the Article 6 / 8 / 9
  categorisation with a clearer category labelling
  system;
- **Tighten** sustainability claim regulation;
- **Strengthen** data architecture via the European
  Single Access Point (ESAP);
- **Address** greenwashing concerns more explicitly.

Through 2026-2027, **SFDR 1.0 remains operationally in
force**. Jersey funds should continue current compliance
while monitoring SFDR 2.0 development. See
[`../../frontier/sfdr-2-and-sustainable-finance.md`](../frontier/sfdr-2-and-sustainable-finance.md).

## Pitfalls

- **Article 9 overreach** — high greenwashing risk;
- **Inconsistency between marketing and PPM** — EU NCAs
  scrutinise this;
- **Static periodic reporting** — must reflect actual
  outcomes, not boilerplate;
- **Taxonomy claims without substantive alignment** — the
  Taxonomy Regulation requires real economic-activity
  qualification;
- **Ignoring DNSH testing for Article 9** — fundamental
  to the regime;
- **Assuming Brexit-style escape** — Jersey is not in
  EU scope but the marketing channel pulls SFDR into
  effect.
