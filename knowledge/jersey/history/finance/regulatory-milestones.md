# Key Regulatory Milestones — Historical Reference

## What this is

A consolidated historical-reference file for the three
**foundational regulatory milestones** that shape Jersey's
modern financial-services era. Each is referenced across
multiple [history](./README.md) and doctrinal files; this
file is the primary source.

The three milestones:

1. The **Edwards Report (1998)** — the policy foundation;
2. The **IMF FSAP Update (2009)** — the post-GFC
   validation;
3. The **MoneyVal 4th → 5th Round arc (2016 → 2024)** — the
   AML/CFT defensive question closed.

## Milestone 1 — Edwards Report (1998)

### What it is

**Andrew Edwards' 1998 *Review of Financial Regulation in
the Crown Dependencies*** — commissioned by HM Treasury,
conducted by Andrew Edwards CB (a senior UK Treasury
official), and published in November 1998. The Report
reviewed the financial-services regulatory frameworks of
Jersey, Guernsey, and the Isle of Man, and made
recommendations for modernisation.

### Why it matters

The Edwards Report is the **policy foundation** of Jersey's
modern regulatory architecture. Its recommendations
underpinned:

- The
  [**Financial Services (Jersey) Law 1998**](../../financial-regulation/financial-services-jersey-law-1998.md)
  consolidating financial-services regulation;
- The
  [**JFSC Law 1998**](../../financial-regulation/jfsc-law-1998.md)
  establishing the [JFSC](../../financial-regulation/jfsc.md)
  as the unified financial-services regulator;
- The build-out of **TCB regulation** (Trust Company
  Business class — see
  [`../jersey/financial-regulation/fsl-class-tcb.md`](../../financial-regulation/fsl-class-tcb.md));
- Parallel reforms in Guernsey and the Isle of Man.

### Key recommendations

- **Unified financial-services regulator** with statutory
  independence;
- **Comprehensive regulatory perimeter** covering banking,
  insurance, trust, fund services, investment business;
- **Modernised AML/CFT framework**;
- **Statutory enforcement powers**;
- **Coordination between Crown Dependencies** on regulatory
  standards.

### Aftermath

Jersey, Guernsey, and the Isle of Man each implemented the
Edwards recommendations through 1998-2002. The
**Cycle 1 / Act I — Modernisation** narrative in
[`trajectory.md`](./trajectory.md) and
[`last-25-years.md`](./last-25-years.md) is the build-out
that followed.

## Milestone 2 — IMF FSAP Update (2009)

### What it is

The **IMF Financial Sector Assessment Programme (FSAP)
Update** for Jersey, published 2009. Jersey is a
**voluntary** FSAP participant. The 2009 FSAP remains the
most recent IMF FSAP for Jersey (no successor has been
commissioned).

### Why it matters

The 2009 FSAP was a **post-Global Financial Crisis
validation** of Jersey's regulatory framework. Conducted in
the immediate aftermath of the 2008 GFC, it assessed
Jersey's banking sector, fund services, regulatory
framework, and AML/CFT against international standards.

### Headline findings

- The Jersey regulatory framework was **broadly aligned
  with international standards**;
- The post-Edwards-Report regulatory architecture (JFSC
  consolidation; statutory enforcement powers) was
  **operationally functional**;
- Certain improvement areas were identified —
  particularly around supervisory intensity, financial-
  intelligence capabilities, and stress-testing of
  banking exposures.

### Aftermath

The 2009 FSAP findings drove the **Cycle 2 / Act II —
Re-regulation** narrative (see
[`trajectory.md`](./trajectory.md)). Specific operational
responses included:

- Introduction of [**Zero-Ten corporate tax**](../../tax/zero-ten.md)
  in 2009;
- Strengthened banking supervision;
- Build-out of financial-intelligence capability;
- Preparation for FATCA (2014) and CRS implementation.

The 2009 FSAP is also the **most recent IMF assessment** of
Jersey's financial-stability framework, meaning the
post-GFC, pre-Brexit, pre-MoneyVal-5th-round picture
remains the IMF's published view.

## Milestone 3 — MoneyVal 4th → 5th Round Arc (2016 → 2024)

### The 4th Round MER (2016)

The **MoneyVal 4th-round Mutual Evaluation Report on
Jersey** assessed Jersey's AML/CFT framework against
**FATF Recommendations** and identified **meaningful
supervisory and effectiveness gaps**:

- Supervisory intensity in certain sectors;
- Financial-intelligence function resourcing;
- Risk-based-supervision build-out;
- Information-sharing mechanics.

The 2016 report did not produce a "non-compliant" finding
but identified a **substantial work programme** that drove
the **2016-2023 supervisory build-out**.

### The supervisory build-out (2016-2023)

In response to the 4th-round MER, Jersey undertook a
multi-year supervisory build-out:

- **JFSC resourcing increased substantially** (staff,
  budget, supervisory capability);
- **Enforcement powers expanded** under the
  [POCL](../../aml-cft/proceeds-of-crime-law-1999.md)
  and FSL frameworks;
- **Financial-intelligence function reinforced** (the
  Jersey FIU);
- **Risk-based supervision** matured across all
  regulated sectors;
- **Industry compliance spend** rose substantially —
  TCBs, banks, fund administrators all built out MLRO /
  MLCO infrastructure;
- **Customer due diligence** standards intensified;
- **Transaction monitoring** infrastructure built out.

The build-out cost — to both regulator and industry —
was real and substantial.

### The 5th Round MER (24 July 2024)

The **MoneyVal 5th-round Mutual Evaluation Report on
Jersey** was published **24 July 2024**. Jersey was rated
**Compliant or Largely Compliant on 39 of the 40 FATF
Recommendations** — top-tier **technical compliance**. The
**effectiveness** picture across the 11 Immediate Outcomes
was strong but not uniformly top-tier: **1 High, 6
Substantial, and 4 Moderate** effectiveness ratings (no
Low ratings).

The narrative arc:

**2016 — "identifiable gaps and substantial work programme"**

→ **2024 — "top-tier technical compliance; strong but
still-improvable effectiveness"**

The combination was strong enough to close the EU/UK
listing-risk question that had hung over Jersey's regulatory
posture for the prior period, while leaving real ongoing
work on certain effectiveness Immediate Outcomes.

For the wider regulatory significance see
[`../jersey/aml-cft/moneyval.md`](../../aml-cft/moneyval.md).

### Aftermath

The 5th-round outcome enabled the **Cycle 3 → Cycle 4
pivot**:

- The [**JFSC 2026-2030 Strategy**](../../financial-regulation/jfsc-strategy-2026-2030.md)
  shifts from defensive to offensive posture;
- The [**FSCP**](../../government/financial-services-competitiveness-programme.md)
  articulates a 10-year growth plan;
- **Growth verticals** (digital assets, sustainable
  finance) become explicit strategic priorities.

The full narrative is in [`trajectory.md`](./trajectory.md).

## The three milestones in relation

The three milestones operate as a sequence:

| Milestone | Period | Role | Outcome |
|---|---|---|---|
| Edwards Report 1998 | 1998-2001 | Policy foundation | Financial Services Commission (Jersey) Law 1998 + Financial Services (Jersey) Law 1998; JFSC established |
| IMF FSAP 2009 | 2008-2010 | Post-GFC validation | Zero-Ten + supervisory build-out |
| MoneyVal 4th→5th 2016-2024 | 2016-2024 | AML/CFT validation | Top-tier technical compliance (39/40 C/LC) with strong-but-improvable effectiveness; enabled growth-agenda pivot |

Each milestone **triggered** a cycle of regulatory and
industry response that built the foundation for the next.
The three together describe the **modern era regulatory
spine** of Jersey financial services.
