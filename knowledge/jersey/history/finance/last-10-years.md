# Jersey Finance Industry — Last 10 Years (2016-2026)

## What this covers

The structural decade in which Jersey's financial-services
industry **transformed from a primarily fund-and-trust
servicing centre into a sophisticated multi-product
private-capital and family-office jurisdiction**, against
a backdrop of intensifying international regulatory
pressure that the industry navigated successfully.

This window contains the **single cleanest narrative arc**
in the corpus: the **MoneyVal 4th round (2016) → 5th round
(2024) transition** from "identifiable gaps" to "global top
tier", reflecting a decade of disciplined regulatory build-
out.

It bridges [`last-5-years.md`](./last-5-years.md) upward to
[`last-25-years.md`](./last-25-years.md).

## The window in context

2016 is the natural starting point because it bundles three
foundational events:

- [**MoneyVal**](../../aml-cft/moneyval.md) **4th Round
  MER on Jersey** (the regulatory baseline);
- **Capital Economics' *Jersey's Value to Britain*** (the
  industry-narrative baseline);
- **The Brexit referendum** (June 2016), which set in motion
  the EU/UK financial-services equivalence question that the
  Crown Dependencies have navigated ever since — see
  [`../jersey/immigration-residency/post-brexit-cross-border.md`](../../immigration-residency/post-brexit-cross-border.md).

By 2026, the picture is materially different: MoneyVal 5th
round complete with a top-tier outcome,
[Substance Law](../../tax/economic-substance.md) in its
fifth operating year, [JPF (Jersey Private
Fund)](../../funds/jersey-private-fund.md) launched
2017 maturing into a dominant product,
[Pillar Two](../../tax/pillar-two.md)
[MCIT 2025](../../international/pillar-two-mcit.md) in
force, AIFMD II live,
[JFSC](../../financial-regulation/jfsc.md) 2026-2030
Strategy published.

## Headline structural change

### Industry composition shift

**2016**: Jersey was primarily a fund-services and trust
jurisdiction with strong banking. Funds dominated by
listed-fund-of-funds, hedge funds, and conventional PE
limited partnerships.

**2026**: Jersey is a **multi-product private-capital,
[trust](../../trusts/index.md),
[family-office](../../use-cases/family-office-adviser/index.md),
[listing-vehicle](../../use-cases/founder-entrepreneur/why-jersey-topco.md),
and tokenisation centre**. Funds are dominated by
[JPFs](../../funds/jersey-private-fund.md) (Jersey
Finance reported a JPF population of ~750 as of March 2025
with sustained year-on-year new-establishment flow — see
[`../frontier/jersey-finance-industry-2026.md`](../../frontier/jersey-finance-industry-2026.md)),
continuation funds (see
[`../frontier/continuation-funds-and-gp-led-secondaries.md`](../../../frontier/continuation-funds-and-gp-led-secondaries.md)),
single-asset vehicles, and sophisticated PE structures.

The defining structural shift is the **broadening of the
product mix** — from a 3-4-product industry to a 10+
product industry — while maintaining specialist depth in
each.

### Scale

The **2016 Capital Economics report** sized Jersey's
contribution to UK FDI / jobs / GDP at a particular level
that, by the 2026 TheCityUK update, had **materially
grown**. The Jersey-attributable 2026 figures are **~£500bn
FDI conduit, ~951,000 UK jobs, ~£62bn UK GDP** (Guernsey
quotes separate figures including ~£58bn capital flows
plus the insurance / captive contribution). The methodology
between the two reports is not exactly comparable but the
directional growth is unmistakable.

### GFCI trajectory

Across the **Z/Yen Global Financial Centres Index** rounds
since 2016, Jersey has held a **stable global top-40-to-50
position** and consistently led the offshore Crown
Dependencies cluster. **GFCI 39 (March 2026)** has Jersey
ranked **38th**, ahead of Guernsey, Isle of Man, and Cayman
in that index.

## The MoneyVal arc — the cleanest 10-year story

The single most coherent narrative across this decade is
the **MoneyVal 4th-to-5th-round transformation**.

### 2016 — 4th Round Mutual Evaluation Report

The **MoneyVal 4th-round MER on Jersey** identified
**meaningful gaps** in the AML/CFT framework — particularly
in the supervisory regime's effectiveness across certain
sectors and in the resourcing of financial-intelligence
functions. The headline rating was acceptable but the
report set out a substantial work-programme.

The report's findings drove the **2016-2023 supervisory
build-out**: JFSC resourcing increased substantially,
supervisory engagement intensified, enforcement powers
were expanded, and the financial-intelligence function
was reinforced. The cost to industry was real — compliance
spend, MLRO/MLCO investment, customer-due-diligence
build-out, transaction-monitoring infrastructure.

### 2024 — 5th Round Mutual Evaluation Report

The **MoneyVal 5th-round MER** (published 24 July 2024)
rated Jersey **Compliant or Largely Compliant on 39 of the
40 FATF Recommendations** — placing it in the **global top
tier** for AML/CFT technical compliance.

The narrative arc is:

**2016 — "identified gaps and substantial work programme"**

→ **2024 — "global top tier; effectiveness validated"**

This is the **single biggest strategic asset Jersey gained
across the decade**. It closes (for the foreseeable future)
the EU/UK listing-risk question that had hung over the
prior period.

A parallel arc applies to Guernsey, with the 2024 5th-round
on-site visit producing comparable conclusions.

## Other regulatory milestones across the decade

### 2017 — Jersey Private Fund (JPF) launch

The [**Jersey Private Fund regime**](../../funds/jersey-private-fund.md),
launched 2017, allowed up to 50 sophisticated investors with
**48-hour authorisation** and a Designated Service Provider
(DSP) delegated-compliance model. It became one of the most
successful product launches in Jersey's regulatory history;
by March 2025 the live JPF population was **~750** (per
Jersey Finance), with the Government competitiveness report
citing ~1,500 structures since launch and £86.7bn
administered (the latter being a broader structures-since-
launch metric than the live JPF count). The
operational view of JPF registration is at
[`../jersey/use-cases/fund-counsel/jpf-registration.md`](../../use-cases/fund-counsel/jpf-registration.md);
the fund-counsel persona at
[`../jersey/use-cases/fund-counsel/index.md`](../../use-cases/fund-counsel/index.md);
the wider [funds index](../../funds/index.md) covers
the comparable [Expert Fund](../../funds/expert-fund.md),
[Eligible Investor Fund](../../funds/eligible-investor-fund.md),
and [Listed Fund](../../funds/listed-fund.md) regimes.

The JPF is the **structural innovation** that defines the
decade for the funds industry.

### 2017-2019 — Beneficial-ownership transparency

The 2017 EU 4th AML Directive and subsequent expansions
brought beneficial-ownership-register requirements into
the Crown Dependencies' frameworks. Jersey, Guernsey, and
IoM all implemented registers with regulator and law-
enforcement access (and the question of public access
evolved further across the decade, with EU and UK debates
continuing into the 2020s).

This continued the **structural transparency arc** — the
opacity-led use cases that had defined the industry decades
earlier are largely closed by this transition.

### 2019 — Taxation (Economic Substance) Law

The [**Substance Law 2019**](../../tax/economic-substance.md)
was a foundational regulatory event: Jersey-resident
companies carrying on "relevant activities" (banking,
insurance, fund management, financing-and-leasing,
headquarters, distribution-and-service-centre, shipping,
holding company, IP, eGaming) must demonstrate **economic
substance** in Jersey (directors meeting in Jersey, adequate
staff and premises, core income-generating activities in
Jersey).

The parallel legislation in Guernsey, IoM, BVI, Cayman, and
Bermuda established the **Crown Dependencies + Overseas
Territories substance regime** as a cluster — see
[`../CROSS-JURISDICTIONAL-MAP.md`](../../../CROSS-JURISDICTIONAL-MAP.md).
The impact on how structures were designed was profound:
the **"empty brass plate"** model became operationally
impossible; real substance had to be located somewhere, and
Jersey's mature
[Trust Company Business](../../financial-regulation/fsl-class-tcb.md)
/ fund-administration / professional-services infrastructure
(under the
[Financial Services (Jersey) Law 1998](../../financial-regulation/financial-services-jersey-law-1998.md))
made it the natural choice for many structures. The TopCo-
specific application is at
[`../jersey/use-cases/founder-entrepreneur/substance-for-topco.md`](../../use-cases/founder-entrepreneur/substance-for-topco.md).

### 2020-2024 — BEPS implementation and Pillar Two negotiation

The **BEPS programme** continued through the decade with
the **OECD Pillar Two** architecture emerging as the
defining international-tax development of the period.
Jersey participated in the OECD process from early stages
and signalled QDMTT adoption from 2022-2023 — a strategic
decision that secured Jersey's position as a compliant
jurisdiction rather than a target.

### 2025 — MCIT 2025 / Bermuda CIT 2023 effective

The implementation of Pillar Two QDMTT regimes across
Jersey, Guernsey, IoM, and Bermuda effective 2025 closed
the Pillar Two arc. See [`last-5-years.md`](./last-5-years.md)
and [`last-2-years.md`](./last-2-years.md).

### 2026 — Strategic framework consolidation

The **JFSC 2026-2030 Strategy** and the Government FSCP
articulate the next-five-and-ten-year direction. AIFMD II
and UK carry reform take effect. The decade closes with
the regulatory framework **maximally consolidated** and the
industry pivoting from defensive to offensive posture.

## Industry developments across the decade

### Funds — JPF dominance and product diversification

The **JPF launch (2017)** is the dominant funds-industry
event of the decade. Alongside it, the Expert Fund regime
matured, the Eligible Investor Fund regime adjusted, and
the Unregulated Eligible Investor Fund (UEIF) and
Unregulated Exchange-Traded Fund (UETF) regimes were
adjusted. By 2026, the **fund regulatory toolkit** is
materially wider than in 2016.

The **structural shift from blind-pool to tailored** —
described in [`last-5-years.md`](./last-5-years.md) — began
gathering pace in 2017-2020 and intensified through 2021-
2026.

### Private capital surge

Per Bain's **Global Private Equity Report** (annual since
2010), global PE AUM has roughly **doubled across the
decade 2016-2026** (driven by yield-search, fundraising
boom, and the public-to-private rotation). Jersey and
Guernsey are net beneficiaries of this expansion via
fund-domicile and structuring work.

The **continuation-fund and GP-led secondaries category**
emerged within this decade and became, by 2025-26, one of
the most consequential product lines in private capital
(see [`../frontier/continuation-funds-and-gp-led-secondaries.md`](../../../frontier/continuation-funds-and-gp-led-secondaries.md)).

### Wealth management

The **family-office segment** grew steadily across the
decade, driven by the generational wealth transfer and the
demand for tailored structures. The
[Article 9 firewall](../../trusts/firewall.md),
[Article 9A reserved powers](../../trusts/reserved-powers.md),
the [Article 47B mistake regime](../../trusts/article-47-set-aside.md),
and the [foundation product](../../foundations/index.md)
all matured. **Private Trust Company (PTC)** structures
became standard for ultra-HNW families — see
[`../jersey/use-cases/family-office-adviser/when-ptc.md`](../../use-cases/family-office-adviser/when-ptc.md).
The full operational worked example is at
[`../jersey/use-cases/family-office-adviser/worked-example-international-family-wealth.md`](../../use-cases/family-office-adviser/worked-example-international-family-wealth.md).

### Tokenisation and digital assets

The decade saw tokenisation move through three phases:
**2016-2019 (ICO era and conceptual exploration)**;
**2019-2023 (R&D, regulatory scoping, VASP regime
development)**; **2024-2026 (deployment, Innovation Council,
14 registered VASPs — see
[`../jersey/registries/vasp-register.md`](../../registries/vasp-register.md)
and the
[MLRO view](../../use-cases/compliance-mlro/vasp-crypto-customers.md))**.
Full frontier coverage at
[`../frontier/tokenisation-jersey-2026.md`](../../frontier/tokenisation-jersey-2026.md).

### Banking

Jersey's banking deposit base remained substantial across
the decade. The Capital Economics 2016 baseline of Jersey
banking deposits and the 2024-2026 Jersey Finance Annual
Review figures show **growth in absolute terms** with the
deposit base broadening across more institution types
(major-UK-bank deposits, Asian-bank deposits, US-bank-
subsidiary deposits).

## The counterweight perspective

The **Tax Justice Network's Financial Secrecy Index** ranked
Jersey at **#19 globally in 2022** and **#8 globally in
2024**. The rise is partly methodological (the index covers
more variables now) and partly real (Jersey's size
matters more as some secrecy reduces elsewhere). The
counterweight view holds that despite the regulatory build-
out, the Crown Dependencies remain meaningful actors in
international financial secrecy.

The corpus view is that this perspective should be
**triangulated against** — not ignored, but also not
accepted uncritically. The MoneyVal 2024 outcome, the
substance regime, the CRS / FATCA / OECD-MDR transparency
architecture, and the public-register debates all argue
that Jersey is **more transparent than at any prior point
in its modern history**, and the methodological choices
in the TJN index are contested by Jersey Finance and
others. Both sides have substance.

## Structural patterns across the decade

### Pattern 1 — Defensive-to-offensive transition

The decade started with the industry primarily **defending**
its position against EU listing risk, FATF scrutiny, BEPS,
and reputational pressure. By 2026, the posture has
**reversed**: Jersey is articulating offensive growth
strategies (JFSC 2026-2030 Strategy, FSCP) and pitching
PE / family-office / sustainable / digital growth verticals.

### Pattern 2 — Defensible niche, not maximum scale

Jersey did **not become the largest** offshore fund
jurisdiction (Cayman is) or the largest captive
jurisdiction (Bermuda is). Its **defensible niche** is
high-complexity structuring (PE, TopCo / listing
structures, trust-law certainty), product-mix breadth,
and the post-MoneyVal-2024 compliance credibility — a
combination Jersey is well-positioned to compete on but
which is not guaranteed to hold share against scale rivals
or lower-friction IFCs going forward.

### Pattern 3 — The Crown Dependencies as a cluster

The Channel Islands + IoM increasingly operated as a
**cluster with specialised twins** rather than as
substitutes. This was visible across the substance regime
(parallel laws), the Pillar Two QDMTT implementation
(parallel timelines), and the MoneyVal 5th round
(parallel outcomes).

### Pattern 4 — The transparency arc

Beneficial-ownership registers, CRS expansion, OECD MDR / DAC6-style mandatory-disclosure commitments,
country-by-country reporting, and the CARF crypto-asset
reporting framework all moved transparency forward across
the decade. The remaining Jersey use cases are uniformly
**substance-and-structure-led**, not secrecy-led.

## What this means going into the 25-year horizon

The 10-year arc is the **build-and-validate cycle** of the
modern era: the supervisory framework was built (2016-
2023), the validation arrived (MoneyVal 2024), and the
next-cycle strategic framework articulated (JFSC 2026-2030
Strategy, FSCP).

The wider [`last-25-years.md`](./last-25-years.md) covers
the **prior cycle** — the 2001-2015 modernisation that
created the foundation on which the 2016-2026 build-and-
validate cycle stood.
