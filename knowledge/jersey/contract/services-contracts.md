# Services Contracts under Jersey Law

## Framework

A services contract in Jersey is governed by:

- **General Jersey contract law** (Pothier, formation,
  performance);
- **Supply of Goods and Services (Jersey) Law 2009** —
  service-specific provisions;
- **Specific** Jersey statutes for regulated services (TCB,
  legal, audit);
- **Tort / délit** for negligent service delivery.

## Implied terms (services)

The 2009 Law implies that a service provider:

- **Will carry out** the service with reasonable care and
  skill;
- **Will perform** the service within a reasonable time
  (absent agreement);
- **Will charge** a reasonable price (absent agreement);
- **Specific** Jersey rules on quality of work.

## Reasonable care and skill

The standard:

- **Objective** — that of a reasonably competent provider
  of the service;
- **Profession-specific** — higher for specialist services;
- **Industry standards** consulted;
- **Specific** Jersey case law on professional negligence.

## Consumer vs commercial services

- **Consumer** services — implied terms non-excludable;
- **Commercial** services — exclusions subject to
  reasonableness test;
- **Specific** Jersey consumer-protection rules;
- **Cross-border** services — choice of law applies.

## Specific regulated services

### Trust company business

- **FSL 1998** licensing for TCBs;
- **JFSC** Codes of Practice — supplement contractual
  obligations;
- **TJL 1984** trustee duties;
- **Specific** TCB indemnity / liability rules.

### Legal services

- **Legal profession** regulated under specific Jersey
  statutes;
- **Advocates / Solicitors** professional rules;
- **Engagement letters** — standard structure;
- **Specific** liability provisions (PI insurance
  mandatory).

### Audit / accountancy

- **JFSC** RAS regime for auditors of regulated bodies;
- **Specific** Jersey audit standards;
- **Auditor liability** — specific case law.

### Financial advisory

- **FSL 1998** investment-business class;
- **JFSC** Code of Practice;
- **Specific** suitability / disclosure rules.

## Engagement letters

For professional services, the engagement letter typically:

- **Defines scope** of services;
- **Specifies fees / disbursements**;
- **Limits liability** (subject to law);
- **Addresses confidentiality / conflicts**;
- **Specifies termination** rights;
- **Includes** governing law and forum;
- **Addresses** AML / CDD obligations.

## Liability limitation

Limitation clauses in Jersey services contracts:

- **Generally enforced** for commercial parties;
- **Subject to** reasonableness test;
- **Cannot exclude** fraud or specific statutory liability;
- **Specific** professional rules may set minimum levels
  (PI insurance);
- **Drafting** should specify scope, cap, exclusions.

## Termination

Standard provisions cover:

- **Termination for convenience** (with notice);
- **Termination for cause** (material breach,
  insolvency);
- **Effect** of termination (fees, work product,
  confidentiality);
- **Specific** professional rules on file return /
  succession.

## Specific Jersey contexts

### Conflicts of interest

- **Profession-specific** rules apply;
- **Disclosure / consent** mechanisms;
- **Specific** TCB / legal-profession requirements.

### Confidentiality

- **Implied** duty of confidentiality;
- **Statutory** carve-outs (AML reporting, court orders);
- **Trust** professional secrecy;
- **DPL** processor / controller distinction.

## Drafting recommendations

- **Define scope** precisely (in-scope / out-of-scope);
- **Address** fees, disbursements, billing cadence;
- **Calibrate** liability limits to insurance;
- **Include** termination and survival provisions;
- **Address** AML / CDD as ongoing obligations;
- **Engage** Jersey counsel for opinion-level review.

## Pitfalls

- **Excluding** professional standard of care (often void);
- **Drafting** ambiguous scope (leads to scope creep
  disputes);
- **Missing** Jersey-specific professional rules;
- **Failing** to address conflicts / confidentiality
  expressly;
- **Ignoring** AML reporting carve-outs from confidentiality.
