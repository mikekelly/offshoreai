# Arbitration in Jersey

## The framework

Arbitration in Jersey is governed by:

- **Arbitration (Jersey) Law 1998** — the principal statute;
- **New York Convention** (NYC) — applies via the 1998 Law
  and UK extension;
- **Royal Court** supervisory jurisdiction;
- **Specific** Jersey procedural rules.

## Arbitration agreements

The 1998 Law requires:

- **Written** arbitration agreement;
- **Specific** subject matter capable of arbitration;
- **Specific** persons / parties identified;
- **Specific** scope (all disputes / specific disputes).

## Arbitrable matters

Generally arbitrable in Jersey:

- **Commercial** disputes;
- **Trust** disputes (with limitations);
- **Contract** breaches;
- **Joint venture** / shareholder disputes;
- **Insurance** claims (with specific exceptions).

Generally **non-arbitrable**:

- **Insolvency** statutory proceedings;
- **Specific** regulatory matters (JFSC enforcement);
- **Real-property** transfer (passing must occur in Royal
  Court);
- **Criminal** matters;
- **Public-law** matters.

## Stay of court proceedings

Where a party sues in breach of an arbitration agreement:

- **Stay** of Royal Court proceedings on application;
- **Mandatory stay** under Article 5 of the 1998 Law (NYC
  obligation);
- **Discretionary** stay in other cases;
- **Specific** procedural requirements.

## Arbitral procedure

The 1998 Law provides default rules supplemented by:

- **Party agreement** (institutional rules — LCIA, ICC,
  UNCITRAL, etc.);
- **Tribunal** procedural discretion;
- **Jersey** as seat permits Royal Court support;
- **Specific** Jersey arbitration rules where applicable.

## Royal Court supervisory jurisdiction

The Royal Court may:

- **Appoint** arbitrators on default;
- **Remove** arbitrators for misconduct;
- **Determine** preliminary points of law;
- **Issue** orders supporting the arbitration (evidence,
  freezing);
- **Set aside** awards on specific grounds;
- **Enforce** awards.

## Setting aside awards

Limited grounds under the 1998 Law:

- **Lack** of jurisdiction;
- **Procedural** unfairness;
- **Fraud** or corruption;
- **Public policy** of Jersey;
- **No appeal** on the merits unless parties have so agreed.

## Enforcement of awards

Foreign awards enforced in Jersey under:

- **New York Convention** (157+ states);
- **Royal Court** procedures;
- **Limited** defences (NYC Article V);
- **Specific** Jersey procedural rules.

## Trust arbitration

A specific Jersey context:

- **TJL 1984** does not prohibit trust-arbitration clauses;
- **Royal Court** has supervisory jurisdiction over trusts
  (Article 51);
- **Specific** issues — minor / unborn beneficiaries, public
  interest;
- **Specific** Jersey case law developing.

## Specific arbitral institutions

Jersey-seated arbitrations commonly use:

- **LCIA** rules;
- **ICC** rules;
- **UNCITRAL** rules (ad hoc);
- **Specific** Jersey arbitration providers.

## Drafting recommendations

- **Specify** the seat (Jersey) clearly;
- **Specify** rules (institutional or ad hoc);
- **Specify** number of arbitrators;
- **Specify** language;
- **Carve out** non-arbitrable matters;
- **Consider** confidentiality, costs, appeal rights.

## Pitfalls

- **Confusing** seat with hearing venue;
- **Choosing** rules incompatible with seat;
- **Failing** to specify governing law for arbitration
  agreement;
- **Drafting** ambiguous "arbitration in Jersey" clauses
  without rules;
- **Missing** carve-outs for emergency relief.
