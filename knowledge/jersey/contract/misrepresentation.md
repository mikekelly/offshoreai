# Misrepresentation in Jersey Contract Law

## What misrepresentation is

A **misrepresentation** is:

- A **false statement** of fact;
- Made by one party to the contract (or their agent);
- **Inducing** the other party to enter the contract;
- **Material** to the contract.

It is a **vitiating factor** affecting **consent** under
Jersey contract law.

## Categories of misrepresentation

### Fraudulent misrepresentation

A statement made:

- **Knowingly false**, **or**
- **Without belief in its truth**, **or**
- **Recklessly** without caring whether true or false.

(*Derry v Peek* test, persuasive in Jersey.)

### Negligent misrepresentation

A statement made:

- **Without reasonable grounds** for believing its truth;
- **Tort-style** liability through breach of duty of care;
- **Specific** Jersey case law on this (developing).

### Innocent misrepresentation

A statement made:

- **Honestly believing** it true;
- **With reasonable grounds**;
- But which turns out to be false.

## Elements

For an actionable misrepresentation:

### Statement of fact

- **Fact**, not **opinion** (subject to specific
  qualifications);
- **Not mere puff**;
- **Not** statement of future intention (subject to specific
  rules);
- **Not** general advertising claims.

### False

- **Untrue** at the time made;
- **Becomes false** subsequently if relied upon and not
  corrected.

### Material

- **Significant** to the decision;
- **Subject to** reasonableness from the perspective of the
  representee.

### Induced reliance

- **The representee relied** on the statement;
- **Wholly or partly** induced to contract;
- **Specific** Jersey rules on partial inducement.

## Remedies

### Rescission

- **Setting aside** the contract;
- **Available** for all three categories of
  misrepresentation;
- **Subject to** bars to rescission:
  - **Affirmation** of the contract;
  - **Lapse** of time;
  - **Innocent third-party rights**;
  - **Impossibility** of restitution.

### Damages

- **Fraudulent**: damages in tort of deceit;
- **Negligent**: damages in tort of negligent
  misstatement;
- **Innocent**: damages in lieu of rescission (in
  discretion);
- **Specific** Jersey rules on quantum.

### Indemnity

- **Specific** indemnity for expenses incurred in reliance;
- **Available** alongside rescission;
- **Specific** Jersey rules.

## Specific scenarios

### Pre-contractual statements

- **Statements made in negotiations** about facts can be
  misrepresentation;
- **Subject to** entire-agreement clauses (which can
  exclude liability for misrepresentation, but cannot
  generally exclude liability for fraud);
- **Specific** Jersey case law.

### Silence as misrepresentation

- **General rule**: silence is **not misrepresentation**;
- **Exceptions**:
  - **Half-truths**;
  - **Fiduciary** relationships requiring disclosure;
  - **Specific** statutory duties;
  - **Changing** circumstances making earlier statement
    misleading;
- **Specific** Jersey rules in particular contexts.

### Misrepresentation in financial-services context

- **Specific** FSL conduct rules;
- **Code of Practice** standards;
- **JFSC enforcement** alongside contract remedies;
- **Specific** Jersey financial-services rules.

### Misrepresentation in trust / estate context

- **Trustee** duties of disclosure;
- **Settlor** misrepresentation re trust property;
- **Specific** Jersey trust-law rules;
- **Crime-fraud** considerations.

## Exclusion of liability

Contractual exclusion of misrepresentation liability:

- **Generally permitted** subject to:
  - **Cannot exclude** liability for fraud;
  - **Unfair Contract Terms** considerations (Jersey
    equivalent rules);
  - **Specific** consumer-protection rules;
  - **Public policy** constraints.
