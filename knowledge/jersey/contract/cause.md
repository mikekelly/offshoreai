# Cause in Jersey Contract Law

## What "cause" is

**Cause** is a civil-law concept — the **motive or reason**
for entering into a contract.

In Roman / French / Norman civil-law tradition, **cause** is
the **fourth essential element** of contract (alongside
consent, capacity, and object).

## The traditional Jersey requirement

Historically, Jersey contract law required cause:

- **Pothier** identified cause as essential;
- **Le Geyt** recognised it for Jersey;
- **Older Jersey cases** required cause;
- **Distinct from** English consideration.

## What cause requires

For a contract to be valid:

- The **motive** must be **legitimate** (not contrary to
  public policy);
- The **motive** must be **real** (not fictitious);
- The **motive** must be **specific** (sufficiently
  ascertainable).

Examples:

- **Sale**: cause = the price (for seller) / the goods (for
  buyer);
- **Loan**: cause = the obligation to repay (for lender) /
  the use of money (for borrower);
- **Gift**: cause = the donative intent (libéralité).

## Cause vs consideration

| Feature | Jersey cause | English consideration |
|---|---|---|
| Origin | Civil-law / Norman | Common-law / equity |
| Required for | Validity of contract | Validity of simple contract |
| Gift contracts | Cause can be donative intent | No consideration → contract not enforceable as simple contract (deed needed) |
| Past consideration | Cause typically requires concurrent motive | Past consideration generally not valid |
| Adequacy | Less concerned with adequacy | Court will not normally inquire into adequacy |

## The modern debate

**Jersey courts have debated** the continuing role of cause:

### View 1: Cause still required

- **Customary law** preserves the requirement;
- **Pothier** authoritative;
- **Specific** Jersey cases applying cause;
- **Distinctively Jersey**.

### View 2: Cause merged with English consideration

- **Practical convergence** with English law;
- **Modern commercial** contracts treated like English
  contracts;
- **Cause** rarely contested;
- **Specific** academic and judicial commentary.

### Current position

The position is **unsettled**:

- **Most** Jersey commercial cases proceed without explicit
  cause analysis (treating consideration / cause as
  functionally equivalent);
- **Specific** cases occasionally raise cause specifically;
- **No** authoritative Privy Council decision resolving the
  doctrine.

## Cause in gift contracts

A **gift** without cause (in the English sense, no
consideration) is:

- **Enforceable** in Jersey if proper Jersey customary
  procedures followed;
- **Subject to** specific Jersey rules on gifts;
- **Different from** English position requiring deed for
  enforceable gift.

For commercial purposes, the distinction is **less practically
important** than the historical doctrine suggests.

## Cause in restitution / unjust enrichment

The **absence of cause** is a key element in **civil-law
unjust enrichment**:

- **No cause** for the transfer;
- **Restitution** appropriate;
- **Jersey** has both civil-law (no cause) and English-style
  (unjust factors) approaches to unjust enrichment;
- See [`unjust-enrichment.md`](./unjust-enrichment.md).

## Drafting implications

In Jersey contracts:

- **Include consideration / payment** terms explicitly;
- **Specify** the parties' motives where helpful;
- **Avoid** the "cause vs consideration" debate by clear
  drafting;
- **Specific** consideration helps under both regimes.

For **gift / no-consideration** documents:

- **Use deed-equivalent** Jersey form (passing before Royal
  Court for real property; specific documentation for
  movables);
- **Jersey practice** has its own conventions;
- **Specific** Jersey advice essential.
