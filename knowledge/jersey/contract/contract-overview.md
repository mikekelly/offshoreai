# Jersey Contract Law — Overview

## The distinctive Jersey approach

Jersey contract law is **distinctively neither English nor
French** but draws from both:

- **Norman customary law** roots (Pothier, Le Geyt);
- **Civil-law** approach to formation and obligations;
- **Practical** convergence with English law on commercial
  outcomes;
- **Specific** Jersey doctrines preserved (e.g., cause).

For most **commercial** contracts in Jersey, the practical
**outcome** is often similar to English law — but the
**reasoning** and **specific doctrines** can differ.

## Sources of Jersey contract law

### Customary law

- **Norman customary law** as preserved by Le Geyt and
  cited in Royal Court cases;
- **Pothier's treatises** as authoritative;
- **Modern Jersey case law** developing customary principles.

### Statute

- **No general Jersey "contract law"** statute;
- **Specific** statutes for **specific contract types**:
  - **Sale of Goods (Jersey) Law 2010**;
  - **Loi (1991) sur la Vente** (real property);
  - **Specific** consumer-protection statutes;
  - **Specific** sectoral statutes (insurance, employment).

### Persuasive authority

- **English law** highly persuasive especially in modern
  commercial contexts;
- **Pothier** persuasive on customary aspects;
- **Privy Council** decisions in Jersey appeals binding.

## Sources of obligations (Pothier framework)

Following Pothier:

1. **Contract** (express agreement);
2. **Quasi-contract** (implied obligation without
   agreement — negotiorum gestio, unjust enrichment);
3. **Délit** (intentional civil wrong / tort);
4. **Quasi-délit** (negligent civil wrong);
5. **Statute** (modern statutory duties).

Modern Jersey practitioners typically frame:

- **Contract** in similar terms to English law;
- **Tort** ≈ Délit / Quasi-délit;
- **Unjust enrichment** ≈ Quasi-contract.

## Key contract concepts

### Offer and acceptance

- **Offer** by one party;
- **Acceptance** by the other party;
- **Mirror image** rule (acceptance must match offer);
- **Specific** Jersey rules.

### Intention to be legally bound

- **Same** as English law generally;
- **Presumed** in commercial contexts;
- **Negated** in social / domestic contexts.

### Cause

A **distinctive Jersey feature** — civil-law style:

- **Cause** = the motive / reason for the contract;
- **Historically** required for contract validity;
- **Modern** Jersey position contested;
- **Practical** convergence with English consideration in
  most cases;
- See [`cause.md`](./cause.md).

### Consent

- **Real consent** required;
- **Vitiated** by mistake, duress, fraud, undue influence;
- **Specific** Jersey rules on each.

### Capacity

- **Adult** capacity required;
- **Specific** rules for minors, mental incapacity, corporate
  authority.

### Specific subject matter

- **Possible** subject;
- **Lawful** subject;
- **Determinable** (or determinate) subject.

## Form

Most Jersey contracts:

- **No specific form** required (oral, written, by conduct);
- **Specific exceptions**:
  - **Real property** must pass before Royal Court;
  - **Specific** contracts requiring writing under statute;
  - **Specific** consumer-protection requirements.

## Performance and breach

### Performance

- **Per the terms**;
- **Good faith** in performance (specific Jersey view);
- **Specific** Jersey rules on tender of performance,
  delivery, payment.

### Breach

- **Repudiatory** breach (fundamental);
- **Breach of warranty** (less fundamental);
- **Specific** Jersey treatment of conditions / warranties /
  innominate terms;
- **Remedies** for breach (damages, specific performance,
  termination).

## Remedies for breach

### Damages

- **Compensatory** principle;
- **Expectation** loss typically;
- **Reliance** loss in some cases;
- **Causation, remoteness, mitigation** principles similar to
  English law;
- **Specific** Jersey treatment of mental-distress damages.

### Specific performance

- **Broader** in Jersey than English equity;
- **Civil-law** influence — specific performance is the
  primary remedy in continental thinking;
- **Specific** Jersey case law;
- See [`specific-performance.md`](./specific-performance.md).

### Termination

- **Repudiatory** breach permits termination;
- **Specific** notice requirements;
- **Affirmation** can preclude termination.

### Restitution

- **Restitution** of benefits where contract avoided;
- **Specific** Jersey rules;
- **Quasi-contract** principles overlap.
