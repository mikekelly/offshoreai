# Boilerplate Clauses — Jersey Contracts

## What this is

"Boilerplate" — the standard machinery at the back of any
commercial contract. Jersey courts generally enforce these
along familiar common-law lines, but with **Pothier-aligned
construction** and several Jersey-specific wrinkles.

## Entire-agreement clauses

Standard form ("This document constitutes the entire
agreement between the parties..."):

- **Enforced** in Jersey, but **not absolute**;
- **Does not exclude** liability for fraudulent
  misrepresentation;
- **Does not exclude** statutory duties (consumer,
  regulatory);
- **Specific drafting** may exclude representations *as
  representations* (relied-upon-doctrine);
- **Specific** Jersey case law on construction.

## Severance / severability

Standard clause preserves the rest of the contract if one
provision is unenforceable:

- **Enforced** routinely;
- **Severance** of unenforceable parts (Pothier-aligned);
- **Blue-pencil** approach available;
- **Specific** Jersey rules on penal clauses, restraint of
  trade.

## No-waiver clauses

"Failure to enforce a right does not constitute a waiver":

- **Generally enforced**;
- **Specific** Jersey rules on estoppel-equivalent doctrines;
- **Cumulative remedies** language commonly included;
- **Drafting** should distinguish waiver from variation.

## Variation clauses

"This agreement may only be varied in writing signed by
both parties":

- **Generally enforced** but Jersey recognises informal
  variation in specific circumstances;
- **Conduct-based** variation possible if clear;
- **Specific** Jersey case law on oral variation.

## Notices clauses

Critical for performance, default, termination:

- **Method** of service (post, email, courier);
- **Address** specification (and updating mechanism);
- **Deemed receipt** rules;
- **Specific** issues for cross-border service;
- **Time-zone** specification.

## Counterparts and electronic execution

- **Counterparts** routinely upheld;
- **Electronic signatures** under Electronic Communications
  (Jersey) Law 2000 — broadly enforceable;
- **Specific** exceptions (wills, real property);
- **DocuSign / Adobe Sign** routine in Jersey practice.

## Assignment / novation

- **Assignment** of contractual rights generally permitted
  absent restriction;
- **Specific** Jersey rules on notice to debtor;
- **Novation** requires consent of all parties;
- **Trust** assets — specific TJL provisions;
- **Property** — specific Jersey property-transfer rules.

## Third-party rights

Jersey **does not have** an equivalent of the UK Contracts
(Rights of Third Parties) Act 1999 of general application:

- **Privity** generally prevails;
- **Specific** Jersey exceptions (stipulation pour autrui in
  some contexts);
- **Drafting** should not rely on UK third-party rights;
- **Trust** structures often used to confer benefits on
  third parties.

## Set-off

- **Common-law** set-off recognised;
- **Contractual** set-off enforced per terms;
- **Insolvency** set-off subject to BDL / désastre rules;
- **Specific** Jersey rules on banker's right of set-off.

## Indemnities

- **Enforced** per terms;
- **Specific** Jersey rules on consequential / indirect loss;
- **Construction** strict (against indemnitee in case of
  ambiguity);
- **Drafting** should specify scope, cap, exclusions.

## Limitation / exclusion of liability

- **Generally enforced** for commercial parties;
- **Specific** Jersey rules on consumer contracts;
- **Cannot exclude** liability for fraud;
- **Cannot exclude** liability for personal injury (specific
  contexts);
- **Specific** Jersey case law on "unreasonable" exclusions.

## Confidentiality

- **Enforced** per terms;
- **Specific** Jersey rules on disclosure obligations
  (regulatory, court orders);
- **Drafting** should carve out mandatory disclosure;
- **Trust / professional secrecy** overlap.

## Costs and interest

- **Costs** clauses enforced;
- **Default interest** — penalty doctrine considerations;
- **Specific** Jersey statutory interest rates (Royal Court);
- **Drafting** should avoid penal effect.

## Practical drafting recommendations

- **Tailor** boilerplate to Jersey context;
- **Don't** import wholesale from English templates without
  review;
- **Address** specific Jersey wrinkles (third-party rights,
  electronic execution exceptions);
- **Engage** Jersey counsel for opinion-level review.

## Pitfalls

- **Relying** on UK CRTPA-style third-party rights;
- **Drafting** entire-agreement clauses that arguably waive
  representations (Jersey approach varies);
- **Assuming** identical penalty / liquidated-damages
  treatment to English law;
- **Missing** Jersey-specific real-property carve-outs;
- **Ignoring** Pothier-aligned construction approach.
