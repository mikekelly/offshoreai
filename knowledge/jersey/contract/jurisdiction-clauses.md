# Jurisdiction Clauses — Jersey Contracts

## What this is

A **jurisdiction clause** specifies the courts that will
hear disputes arising under the contract. Jersey contracts
routinely contain either:

- **Exclusive jurisdiction** clauses (one forum only);
- **Non-exclusive jurisdiction** clauses (parties may sue
  elsewhere);
- **Asymmetric** clauses (binding on one party, optional for
  the other — common in finance).

## Jersey courts' approach

Jersey courts respect the parties' chosen forum:

- **Exclusive jurisdiction** clauses generally enforced;
- **Non-exclusive** clauses respected but Jersey may take
  jurisdiction on other grounds;
- **Forum non conveniens** doctrine applied to Jersey-
  jurisdiction clauses;
- **Anti-suit injunctions** may be granted to enforce
  Jersey-jurisdiction clauses.

## Service-of-process gateway

For a Jersey-jurisdiction clause to operate, service is
required. The **Royal Court Rules 2004** provide:

- **Service in Jersey** as of right;
- **Service out** with leave on specified gateways;
- **Submission to jurisdiction** by clause (Rule 6 / 7);
- **Specific** procedural requirements.

## Enforcement of foreign judgments

A Jersey-resident defendant sued under a foreign-
jurisdiction clause may face enforcement in Jersey:

- **Judgments (Reciprocal Enforcement) (Jersey) Law 1960**
  — UK, IoM, Guernsey judgments;
- **Common law** enforcement for other foreign judgments;
- **Specific** procedural rules;
- **Defences** include lack of jurisdiction, fraud, public
  policy.

## Asymmetric clauses

Common in finance documents (LMA, ISDA):

- **Borrower / counterparty** bound to a single forum;
- **Lender / bank** free to sue anywhere;
- **Generally upheld** by Jersey courts;
- **Specific** anti-suit considerations.

## Anti-suit injunctions

Jersey courts may grant **anti-suit injunctions** to:

- **Enforce** Jersey-jurisdiction clauses;
- **Restrain** vexatious foreign proceedings;
- **Specific** *Aerospatiale* / *Turner* principles applied;
- **Privy Council** authority applied in Jersey context.

## Specific Jersey contexts

### Trust litigation

- **Forum-clause** in trust instrument routinely upheld;
- **TJL** Article 9 — Jersey law governs validity etc.;
- **Trust deeds** with Jersey-jurisdiction clauses common.

### Real-property disputes

- **Jersey jurisdiction** exclusive by subject matter (*lex
  rei sitae*);
- **Cannot** be displaced by contractual clause.

### Regulatory enforcement

- **JFSC** powers exercised in Jersey regardless of
  contractual clause.

## Drafting recommendations

- **Express** jurisdiction clause aligned with choice of law;
- **Decide** exclusive / non-exclusive / asymmetric;
- **Service-of-process agent** appointed where party is
  outside the forum;
- **Carve out** real-property and regulatory matters where
  appropriate;
- **Consider** alignment with anti-suit risk.

## Pitfalls

- **Mismatch** between choice of law and jurisdiction
  clauses;
- **Missing** service-of-process appointment;
- **Drafting** ambiguous "non-exclusive" provisions;
- **Ignoring** mandatory forum rules (real property,
  regulatory);
- **Asymmetric clauses** without considering enforceability
  in counterparty's jurisdiction.
