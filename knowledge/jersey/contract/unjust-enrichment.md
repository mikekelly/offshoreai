# Unjust Enrichment in Jersey Law

## The Jersey approach

Jersey law on unjust enrichment draws from **both** civil-law
and common-law traditions:

- **Civil-law** (Pothier) quasi-contract concepts;
- **English** unjust-enrichment doctrines (*Sempra Metals*,
  *Lipkin Gorman*) — persuasive;
- **Specific** Jersey case law developing distinctive
  approach.

## The civil-law framework

Following Pothier, **quasi-contracts** are obligations arising
**without** agreement:

### Negotiorum gestio

- **Managing another's affairs** without their authority but
  for their benefit;
- **Recovery** of expenses by the manager;
- **Specific** Jersey rules;
- Less common in modern commercial practice.

### Solutio indebiti

- **Payment of a non-existing debt**;
- **Recovery** by the payer;
- **Subject to** specific defences (change of position, BFPV,
  etc.).

### Versio in rem

- **Direct enrichment** at another's expense;
- **No** agreement or quasi-contract narrowly defined;
- **Restitution** as primary remedy.

## The English unjust-enrichment framework

Modern English law structures unjust enrichment around:

### Elements

1. **Enrichment** of the defendant;
2. **At the expense of** the claimant;
3. **Unjust factor**:
   - **Mistake**;
   - **Failure of consideration**;
   - **Compulsion / duress**;
   - **Necessity**;
   - **Specific** other unjust factors.

### Defences

- **Change of position**;
- **BFPV** without notice;
- **Estoppel**;
- **Specific** statutory defences;
- **Limitation**.

## Jersey blending

Jersey courts apply elements of **both** frameworks:

- **Pothier-style** analysis where appropriate;
- **English-style** unjust factors;
- **Specific** Jersey case law (e.g., *In re B Trust* and
  similar developing Jersey jurisprudence);
- **Practical convergence** with English outcomes in most
  cases.

## Common scenarios

### Mistaken payment

- **Bank** mistakenly credits an account;
- **Claimant** seeks recovery;
- **Defendant's** possible defences (change of position,
  BFPV);
- **Specific** Jersey rules.

### Failure of consideration

- **Payment made** for which consideration fails;
- **Examples**:
  - **Contract** void / avoided;
  - **Specific** purpose not achieved;
  - **Deposit** on contract that does not proceed.

### Tracing in trust contexts

- **Trust property** wrongly mixed / dissipated;
- **Tracing** to identify;
- **Restitution** of traced property;
- **Specific** Jersey trust law rules under TJL Article 33
  (constructive trust).

### Wrongful enrichment

- **Property** acquired through wrongdoing;
- **Constructive trust** in favour of claimant;
- **Specific** Jersey rules.

## Remedies

### Personal claim

- **Restitution** of value of enrichment;
- **Money** paid for restitution;
- **Specific** Jersey rules on quantum.

### Proprietary claim

- **Specific property** identified through tracing;
- **Restitution** of the property itself or its proceeds;
- **Priority** over general creditors of the defendant;
- **Specific** Jersey rules.

### Subrogation

- **Replacing** another's claim;
- **Specific** in commercial / insurance contexts;
- **Specific** Jersey rules.

## Limitation

- **Jersey statutory** limitation periods apply;
- **Typically 10 years** for unjust enrichment claims;
- **Specific** Jersey case law on when time runs;
- **Specific** rules for trust contexts.
