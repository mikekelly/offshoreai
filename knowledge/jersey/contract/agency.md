# Agency under Jersey Law

## The concept

**Agency** in Jersey draws on:

- **Pothier** — *mandat* (gratuitous or paid agency
  commission);
- **Norman customary law**;
- **Modern statutes** (Powers of Attorney Law 1995, etc.);
- **English law** — heavily persuasive in commercial agency
  context.

## Types of agency

- **Express** agency (by contract / power of attorney);
- **Implied** agency (from circumstances / conduct);
- **Ostensible** agency (by representation to third party);
- **Agency of necessity** (specific limited circumstances);
- **Ratification** (retrospective adoption).

## Creation

Express agency may be created by:

- **Written** mandate / contract;
- **Power of attorney** (formal instrument);
- **Oral** appointment (where formality not required);
- **Conduct** showing intent.

## Powers of Attorney (Jersey) Law 1995

The 1995 Law provides:

- **Formal requirements** for general / specific powers of
  attorney;
- **Witnessing** and execution rules;
- **Lasting / enduring** powers (Capacity and Self-
  Determination (Jersey) Law 2016);
- **Revocation** rules;
- **Specific** Jersey forms.

## Agent's duties

An agent owes:

- **Loyalty** — act in principal's best interest;
- **Care** — exercise reasonable skill and care;
- **No-conflict** rule — avoid conflicts of interest;
- **No-profit** rule — no secret profit;
- **Account** — proper accounting to principal;
- **Compliance** with mandate scope.

## Principal's obligations

A principal owes:

- **Remuneration** per agreement (or reasonable if implied);
- **Indemnity** for expenses / liabilities lawfully
  incurred;
- **Cooperation** in performance of mandate;
- **Specific** Jersey rules on commercial-agency commissions.

## Authority

The agent's authority may be:

- **Actual** — express or implied from terms;
- **Ostensible / apparent** — based on representation;
- **Usual** — incidental to the office held;
- **Ratified** — adopted retrospectively by principal.

## Binding the principal

Third parties may hold the principal bound where:

- **Actual authority** existed;
- **Ostensible authority** based on principal's
  representation;
- **Holding out** as agent;
- **Specific** Jersey case law on ostensible authority.

## Termination

Agency terminates by:

- **Performance** of mandate;
- **Mutual** agreement;
- **Revocation** by principal (subject to irrevocable
  mandates);
- **Renunciation** by agent;
- **Operation of law** (death, insolvency, capacity loss);
- **Specific** statutory provisions.

## Commercial agency

For agency in commercial contexts:

- **Compensation** on termination — Jersey practice draws on
  English law (no EU Commercial Agents Directive equivalent);
- **Restraints** on competition — subject to Jersey
  unreasonableness review;
- **Specific** Jersey case law developing.

## Specific Jersey contexts

### Trustees as agents

- **Trustees** are not agents but fiduciaries;
- **Distinct** TJL rules apply;
- **Specific** delegation provisions.

### Directors

- **Directors** are agents of the company;
- **CJL 1991** specific provisions on director authority;
- **Article 74A** — third-party protection.

### Lawyers / advocates

- **Advocate** as agent for client;
- **Specific** legal-profession rules;
- **Authority** in litigation context;
- **Apparent authority** in settlement.

## Cross-border agency

Jersey-law agency operating across borders:

- **Choice of law** governs agent / principal relationship;
- **Lex loci** may govern third-party effects;
- **Specific** Jersey rules on foreign-PoA recognition.

## Drafting recommendations

- **Define scope** of authority precisely;
- **Specify** remuneration and indemnity;
- **Address** conflicts and no-secret-profit rule expressly;
- **Specify** termination rights and post-termination
  obligations;
- **Include** governing law and forum;
- **For PoA** — comply with 1995 Law formalities.

## Pitfalls

- **Drafting** broad ostensible-authority representations
  inadvertently;
- **Missing** PoA formalities (formal requirements strictly
  enforced);
- **Confusing** agent with independent contractor / trustee;
- **Failing** to address commission / compensation on
  termination;
- **Ignoring** Jersey-specific commercial-agency practice.
