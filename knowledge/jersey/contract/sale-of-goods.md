# Sale of Goods under Jersey Law

## The framework

Jersey sale-of-goods law sits across three layers:

- **Pothier-derived** customary contract law (formation,
  cause, performance);
- **Loi (1991) sur la Vente** — older specific sales statute
  for certain transactions;
- **Supply of Goods and Services (Jersey) Law 2009** —
  modern consumer-focused statute;
- **General Pothier** rules where statute silent.

## Loi (1991) sur la Vente

Selected provisions of the 1991 Law cover:

- **Specific** Jersey rules on sale-formation;
- **Passing of property** rules;
- **Risk** allocation;
- **Specific** older Norman concepts preserved.

## Supply of Goods and Services (Jersey) Law 2009

The modern statute provides:

- **Implied terms** as to title, description, quality;
- **Implied terms** as to fitness for purpose;
- **Implied terms** as to sample;
- **Consumer protections** — non-excludable in consumer
  contracts;
- **Specific** remedies (repair, replace, refund).

## Implied terms

The 2009 Law implies:

- **Title** — seller has the right to sell;
- **Description** — goods match description;
- **Satisfactory quality** — durable, free from defects, fit
  for purpose;
- **Fitness for purpose** — where buyer relies on seller's
  skill;
- **Sample** — bulk matches sample.

## Consumer vs commercial contracts

The 2009 Law treats consumer contracts more protectively:

- **Non-excludable** implied terms for consumers;
- **Exclusion** possible (with limits) in B2B contracts;
- **Specific** Jersey consumer-protection rules apply;
- **Reasonableness** test on exclusions.

## Passing of property and risk

Default rules under Jersey law:

- **Specific goods** — property passes at formation unless
  contrary intent;
- **Unascertained goods** — passes on ascertainment;
- **Risk follows property** unless contract specifies
  otherwise;
- **Retention of title** clauses enforceable per terms.

## Remedies for breach

### Buyer remedies

- **Reject** non-conforming goods;
- **Damages**;
- **Specific performance** (broader than English);
- **Repair / replacement** under the 2009 Law;
- **Refund** in specific consumer contexts.

### Seller remedies

- **Damages** for non-payment;
- **Retention of title** to repossess;
- **Resale** of rejected goods (mitigation);
- **Specific performance** in specific cases.

## Cross-border sales

Jersey sales with international parties:

- **Choice of law** — see [`choice-of-law.md`](./choice-of-law.md);
- **CISG** — Jersey is not a CISG state;
- **Incoterms** routinely used;
- **Specific** Jersey customs / GST issues.

## Specific Jersey contexts

### Real-property sales

- **Not** governed by the 2009 Law (Loi 1880 applies);
- **Royal Court passing** required;
- See [`../property/real-property-overview.md`](../property/real-property-overview.md).

### Bulk / commodities sales

- **Specific** Jersey rules on bulk title;
- **Commercial** practice often based on standard-form
  international contracts.

### Distance / online sales

- **Specific** Jersey consumer rules on distance contracts;
- **EU-derived** concepts applied in modified form.

## Drafting recommendations

- **Identify** consumer / commercial nature;
- **Specify** title, risk, delivery, payment terms clearly;
- **Address** retention of title where relevant;
- **Include** boilerplate (force majeure, choice of law,
  jurisdiction);
- **Calibrate** exclusions to reasonableness test.

## Pitfalls

- **Excluding** implied terms in consumer contracts (void);
- **Ignoring** Loi 1880 for real property;
- **Assuming** CISG applies (it does not in Jersey);
- **Missing** Jersey GST / ISE considerations;
- **Drafting** ambiguous retention-of-title provisions.
