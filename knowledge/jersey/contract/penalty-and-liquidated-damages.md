# Penalty Clauses and Liquidated Damages in Jersey

## The question

A contract specifies a pre-agreed sum payable on breach. Is
it enforced as agreed, or struck down as a penalty? Jersey
sits between the civil-law and common-law approaches.

## Pothier and the civil-law roots

Pothier recognises the **clause pénale** — a pre-agreed
sum payable on breach:

- **Civil-law** tradition permits broad enforceability;
- **Court power** to reduce manifestly excessive penalties;
- **Modern French** Code Civil retains this approach.

## Common-law penalty doctrine

The English (and historically Jersey) common-law approach
distinguishes:

- **Liquidated damages** — genuine pre-estimate of loss;
  enforced;
- **Penalty** — sum designed to deter breach; unenforced
  beyond actual loss.

## Modern Jersey approach

Jersey courts have **blended** the two traditions:

- **Pre-estimate** test as a starting point;
- **Pothier-aligned** consideration of fairness;
- **Cavendish v Makdessi** (UK Supreme Court 2015) approach
  influential — focus on "legitimate commercial interest"
  and "extravagant or unconscionable" sums;
- **Specific** Jersey case law developing.

## Distinguishing factors

A clause is more likely to be enforced if:

- **Pre-estimate** is genuine at time of contracting;
- **Difficulty** of quantifying actual loss at the time;
- **Commercial bargain** between sophisticated parties;
- **Proportionate** to legitimate commercial interest;
- **Not extravagant** or unconscionable.

A clause is more vulnerable if:

- **Same amount** payable on different types of breach;
- **Triggered** on minor breach as well as major;
- **Greatly exceeds** any plausible loss;
- **Purely deterrent** in character;
- **Unconscionable** in context.

## Specific Jersey contexts

### Real-property contracts

- **Deposit forfeiture** on buyer default — generally
  enforced;
- **Specific** Jersey rules on percentage thresholds;
- **Royal Court** scrutiny in exceptional cases.

### Employment

- **Garden leave** / restraint of trade — penalty doctrine
  may apply;
- **Repayment** of training costs — enforced if
  proportionate;
- **Specific** EJL 2003 protections override.

### Finance contracts

- **Default interest** — penalty considerations;
- **Make-whole** / break-funding — generally enforced if
  proportionate;
- **Specific** Jersey rules on banker's calculations.

### Trust contracts

- **Trustee** indemnities — specific TJL provisions;
- **Specific** Jersey rules on trustee remuneration / costs.

## Drafting recommendations

- **State** the rationale in the clause (legitimate
  commercial interest);
- **Calibrate** to actual likely loss;
- **Avoid** "one-size-fits-all" triggers;
- **Consider** tiered structures (different sums for
  different breaches);
- **Document** the negotiation showing pre-estimate at
  formation.

## Pitfalls

- **Copying** English standard-form clauses without Jersey-
  context review;
- **Triggering** the same large sum on trivial and serious
  breach;
- **Failing** to document the pre-estimate rationale;
- **Assuming** Jersey identical to English / French;
- **Missing** the Pothier-aligned fairness overlay.

## Court power to reduce

Where a clause is held to be penal:

- **Jersey courts** historically followed English approach
  (unenforced beyond actual loss);
- **Pothier-aligned** approach allows reduction rather than
  total invalidity in some contexts;
- **Specific** Jersey case law on the boundaries.
