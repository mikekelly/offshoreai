# Specific Performance in Jersey Law

## The Jersey approach

Jersey law on specific performance is **distinct from
English** approach due to **civil-law influences**:

- **Civil-law tradition** treats specific performance as the
  **primary** remedy;
- **English equity** treats damages as primary, specific
  performance as exceptional;
- **Jersey** position sits between the two — specific
  performance more available than in English law but not
  automatically primary.

## When specific performance is available

### Real property contracts

- **Specific performance** is the standard remedy for sale of
  Jersey real property;
- **Each property** is unique — damages are inadequate;
- **Royal Court** routinely orders specific performance for
  property sales.

### Unique chattels

- **Specific performance** for unique items (art, antiques,
  specific equipment);
- **Subject to** evidence of uniqueness.

### Long-term commercial contracts

- **Specific performance** available where damages inadequate;
- **Especially** for performance over time (services, supply
  agreements);
- **Subject to** practicability of supervision.

### Family / personal arrangements

- **Specific performance** for marriage contracts, family
  settlements;
- **Specific** Jersey rules.

## When specific performance is NOT available

### Personal services

- **Cannot** specifically enforce a contract of personal
  service;
- **Employment** contracts not typically enforceable by
  specific performance against either party;
- **Subject to** specific exceptions and injunctive relief.

### Where damages are adequate

- **Standard** test;
- **Subject to** Jersey's broader approach making specific
  performance more readily available than under English law.

### Where supervision impractical

- **Court** will not order if continuous supervision required;
- **Specific** complex performance.

### Where mutuality lacking

- **Other party** could not be ordered to perform;
- **Specific** Jersey rules.

### Discretionary refusal

- **Hardship** to the defendant;
- **Unconscionable** conduct by the claimant;
- **Specific** equitable defences.

## Differences from English law

### Default vs exceptional remedy

- **English law**: damages primary, specific performance
  exceptional;
- **Jersey law**: specific performance more readily available
  for many contract types.

### Civil-law influence

- **Pothier** treated specific performance as the primary
  remedy;
- **Norman customary** law allowed specific performance widely;
- **Modern Jersey** has retained more of this approach.

### Specific contexts

- **Real property** — similar in both (specific performance
  standard);
- **Sale of goods** — broader specific performance in Jersey
  for unique / valuable goods;
- **Commercial services** — broader Jersey availability.

## Procedure

A specific-performance claim:

- **Royal Court** action;
- **Pleaded** specifically;
- **Evidence** of breach and entitlement to specific
  performance;
- **Court order** specifying the performance required;
- **Enforcement** through standard Royal Court enforcement
  procedures.

## Specific performance with damages

- **Combined** remedies possible;
- **Damages** for breach to date plus specific performance
  going forward;
- **Specific** Jersey rules on combined remedies.

## Enforcement

A specific-performance order is enforced through:

- **Contempt of court** for non-compliance;
- **Specific** Royal Court enforcement orders;
- **Sequestration** of assets in extreme cases;
- **Specific** Jersey procedural rules.
