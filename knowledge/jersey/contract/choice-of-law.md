# Choice of Law in Jersey Contracts

## The question

Counterparties are negotiating a contract with a Jersey
nexus (Jersey party, Jersey assets, Jersey trustee). Which
law should govern? What does Jersey law recognise? When will
a Jersey court apply foreign law?

## The Jersey conflicts framework

Jersey is **not subject to Rome I** (the EU regulation on
contractual obligations) but its **conflicts rules are
broadly aligned** with common-law and Rome I principles:

- **Party autonomy** is the starting point;
- **Express choice** of law generally upheld;
- **Implied choice** may be inferred from terms / context;
- **Absent choice** — characteristic-performance / closest-
  connection test;
- **Mandatory rules** of the forum may override.

## Express choice of law

The parties may choose any law to govern:

- **Foreign law** (English, Delaware, New York, etc.) —
  routinely upheld;
- **Jersey law** — appropriate for Jersey-asset / Jersey-
  party deals;
- **Severance** — different parts of the contract may be
  governed by different laws (specific use cases);
- **Bona fides** — choice must not be a sham / evasion.

## Implied choice

In the absence of an express clause, choice may be inferred
from:

- **Choice of forum** clause (often persuasive);
- **Language** of the contract;
- **Currency** of payment;
- **Standard-form** contracts (e.g., LMA, ISDA);
- **Reference** to specific statutes / regulations.

## Absent any choice

Where no choice is expressed or implied, Jersey applies the
**closest-connection** test:

- **Characteristic performance** is a strong indicator;
- **Place of contracting**;
- **Place of performance**;
- **Residence / business** of the parties;
- **Subject matter** (real property, intangibles, etc.);
- **Specific** Jersey rules for certain contract types.

## Mandatory Jersey rules

Even where foreign law is chosen, Jersey **mandatory rules**
may override:

- **Public policy** of Jersey (narrow concept);
- **Statute** specifically extending to Jersey contracts
  (e.g., consumer protection, employment, regulatory);
- **Real property** in Jersey — Jersey law governs *lex
  rei sitae*;
- **Trusts** — Article 9 firewall provisions of the TJL
  override foreign forced-heirship;
- **AML / sanctions** — mandatory regardless of governing
  law;
- **JFSC** regulatory rules for Jersey-licensed activity.

## Specific contract types

### Real property in Jersey

- **Jersey law** governs by *lex rei sitae*;
- **Contract passing** before the Royal Court is mandatory;
- **Choice of foreign law** ineffective for transfer effect.

### Trusts

- **TJL 1984** governs Jersey trusts;
- **Article 9** firewall — foreign succession / matrimonial
  rules disregarded;
- **Specific** anti-suit / proper-law rules apply.

### Employment

- **Employment (Jersey) Law 2003** — mandatory protections
  for Jersey employees;
- **Choice of foreign law** ineffective to displace
  statutory minimums.

### Consumer contracts

- **Specific** Jersey consumer-protection rules may apply;
- **Foreign-law** choice cannot displace mandatory consumer
  protections in some contexts.

### Banking / regulated activity

- **JFSC** regulatory rules apply to licensed business in
  Jersey regardless of foreign-law governing clause for
  individual contracts.

## Severability of foreign law

Where foreign-law clauses are partially unenforceable in
Jersey:

- **Severance** preserves enforceable parts;
- **Specific** Jersey procedural rules apply;
- **Pleading** foreign law — burden on the party relying on
  it.

## Foreign-law evidence

A Jersey court applies foreign law as **fact** (not law):

- **Expert evidence** required (foreign-law expert);
- **Pleaded** with particularity;
- **Default** to Jersey law if foreign law not adequately
  pleaded / proved.

## Pitfalls

- **Assuming** Jersey law is identical to English law;
- **Choosing** a foreign law for a Jersey real-property
  matter (ineffective for transfer);
- **Not providing** for sanctions / public-policy carve-outs;
- **Failing** to align choice of law with choice of forum;
- **Misunderstanding** that Jersey courts apply foreign law
  as fact.

## Drafting recommendations

- **Express** choice of law in a stand-alone clause;
- **Align** with jurisdiction / arbitration clause;
- **Consider** carve-outs for specific issues;
- **Specify** Jersey law for Jersey-asset / Jersey-party
  matters;
- **Engage** Jersey counsel for opinions on enforceability.
