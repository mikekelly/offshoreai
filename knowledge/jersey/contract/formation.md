# Jersey Contract Formation

## The four classical requirements (Pothier)

Following Pothier and Jersey customary law, a valid contract
requires:

1. **Consent** (real and uncoerced);
2. **Capacity** (parties able to contract);
3. **Object** (subject matter — possible, lawful,
   determinable);
4. **Cause** (the motive / reason for the contract — see
   [`cause.md`](./cause.md)).

These overlap with the **English** requirements of offer,
acceptance, consideration, and intention to be bound — but
the framing is **civil-law**.

## Offer and acceptance

### Offer

An **offer** is a statement of terms intended to be
**binding on acceptance**:

- **Specific** terms;
- **Communicated** to the offeree;
- **Manifesting intention** to be bound;
- **Distinguished from** invitations to treat (advertisements,
  shop displays, etc.).

### Acceptance

An **acceptance**:

- **Mirrors** the offer (mirror-image rule);
- **Communicated** to the offeror;
- **Unequivocal** and **unconditional**;
- **Method** typically that prescribed by the offeror.

### Counter-offer

A **counter-offer** is a new offer:

- **Terminates** the original offer;
- **Awaits** acceptance from the original offeror;
- **Modifications** by acceptance typically counter-offer.

### Revocation

An offer may be **revoked** before acceptance:

- **Communicated** to the offeree;
- **Before** acceptance is effective;
- **Specific** rules on postal acceptance and revocation
  timing.

## Intention to be legally bound

The parties must intend the agreement to be **legally
binding**:

- **Presumed** in **commercial** contexts;
- **Presumed against** in **social / domestic** contexts;
- **Specific** indicators (express provisions, formality,
  context).

## Capacity

Parties must have **capacity**:

### Adult / minor

- **18+** for adult capacity;
- **Minors** can contract for **necessaries** and other
  specified things;
- **Specific** Jersey rules on minor's contracts.

### Mental capacity

- **Sound mind** at the time of contracting;
- **Specific** rules for proving incapacity;
- **Protected** persons under specific Jersey regimes.

### Corporate capacity

- **Companies** contract through authorised agents;
- **Foundations** through their council;
- **Trusts** through trustees;
- **Specific** rules on capacity and authority.

## Consent

Consent must be:

### Real

- **Voluntary** agreement;
- **Without** mistake, duress, fraud, undue influence;
- **Specific** Jersey rules on each vitiating factor.

### Mistake

- **Common mistake** (both parties wrong);
- **Mutual mistake** (parties at cross-purposes);
- **Unilateral mistake** (one party only);
- **Subject to** specific Jersey rules;
- **Effect**: may render contract void / voidable.

### Duress

- **Coercion** or **threats**;
- **Economic duress** in commercial contexts;
- **Effect**: contract voidable.

### Fraud

- **Knowingly false** representation;
- **Inducing** the contract;
- **Effect**: contract voidable; damages possible.

### Undue influence

- **Improper pressure** by one party;
- **Specific** relationships giving rise to presumptions;
- **Effect**: contract voidable.

## Object

The subject matter must be:

- **Possible** (physically and legally);
- **Lawful** (not contrary to public policy or statute);
- **Determinable** (sufficiently certain).

### Common categories

- **Sale of goods / property**;
- **Services**;
- **Forbearance**;
- **Confidentiality / restraint** (subject to public policy
  on restraint of trade);
- **Specific** financial obligations.

## Form

Most Jersey contracts:

- **No specific form** required;
- **Oral, written, by conduct** all possible;
- **Statutory exceptions** specific:
  - **Real property** (must pass before Royal Court);
  - **Some** insurance contracts;
  - **Some** consumer contracts;
  - **Powers of attorney** (specific form rules);
  - **Wills** (specific WSL formalities).

## Time of formation

- **Offer accepted** (instantaneous communications);
- **Postal rule** for delayed communications;
- **Specific** Jersey rules on electronic acceptance,
  email, etc.
