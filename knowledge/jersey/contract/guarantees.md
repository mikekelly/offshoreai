# Guarantees and Indemnities under Jersey Law

## The distinction

A **guarantee** (*cautionnement* in Pothier) and an
**indemnity** are conceptually distinct in Jersey:

- **Guarantee** — secondary obligation; surety liable only
  if principal defaults; surety has defences arising from
  principal's discharge;
- **Indemnity** — primary obligation; indemnitor liable
  directly on specified triggers; no derivative defences.

## Pothier and *cautionnement*

Following Pothier, Jersey recognises:

- **Cautionnement** as a specific suretyship contract;
- **Subsidiary** character — surety stands behind principal;
- **Specific** Norman / Pothier-derived defences;
- **Modern** convergence with English suretyship.

## Formation

A guarantee requires:

- **Agreement** between surety and creditor;
- **Identification** of principal debtor;
- **Identification** of guaranteed obligation;
- **Cause** (in the Pothier sense — typically commercial
  consideration);
- **Writing** for evidentiary purposes (no statutory writing
  requirement equivalent to UK Statute of Frauds, but
  practical necessity).

## Formalities

Jersey does **not** have an exact equivalent of the UK
Statute of Frauds s.4 requiring guarantees in writing:

- **Writing** practical for proof;
- **Specific** Jersey cases on oral guarantees;
- **Recommended** as written deed or contract.

## Surety's defences

A guarantor has defences arising from:

- **Principal's discharge** — co-extensive liability;
- **Variation** of principal obligation without surety
  consent;
- **Release** of co-sureties;
- **Material breach** by creditor;
- **Misrepresentation** by creditor at formation;
- **Specific** Pothier-derived defences (benefit of
  discussion, benefit of division — narrowly preserved).

## "Indemnity" structure to avoid defences

Lenders / creditors often draft as **indemnity** to avoid
suretyship defences:

- **Primary obligation** — indemnitor pays on trigger;
- **No** derivative defences;
- **Continues** despite principal discharge;
- **Drafting** must be clear ("I will indemnify you against
  losses" not "I guarantee");
- **Jersey courts** look to substance, not just label.

## Construction principles

Jersey courts construe guarantees:

- **Strictly** against the creditor (*contra proferentem*);
- **In favour of** the surety in case of ambiguity;
- **In context** of commercial purpose;
- **Specific** modern Jersey case law.

## All-monies clauses

- **Generally enforced**;
- **Drafting** must be clear;
- **Subject to** strict construction;
- **Specific** Jersey case law on scope.

## Continuing vs limited guarantees

- **Continuing** — covers fluctuating debt over time;
- **Limited** — covers specific transaction / amount;
- **Drafting** should specify clearly;
- **Specific** Jersey rules on termination.

## Termination by surety

A continuing guarantee may be terminable:

- **By notice** per terms;
- **By death** of surety (subject to terms);
- **By specific** Jersey rules on novation / variation;
- **Crystallisation** of liability at termination.

## Joint and several guarantees

- **Multiple sureties** may be joint, several, or both;
- **Drafting** should specify;
- **Specific** Jersey rules on contribution between
  co-sureties;
- **Effect** of release of one co-surety on others.

## Bank guarantees

Standard finance-context guarantees:

- **On-demand** vs **conditional** guarantees;
- **Performance** vs **payment** guarantees;
- **Independent** guarantees (LMA-style);
- **Specific** drafting for cross-border enforcement.

## Specific Jersey contexts

### Personal guarantees

- **Spousal** guarantees — Jersey case law on undue
  influence;
- **Specific** disclosure / independent-advice
  considerations;
- **Practical** banking practice in Jersey.

### Group guarantees

- **Upstream** and **cross-stream** guarantees common in
  group finance;
- **CJL** corporate-benefit considerations;
- **Specific** financial-assistance / capital-maintenance
  rules.

### Trust guarantees

- **Trustee** giving guarantee — TJL considerations;
- **Beneficiary** indemnity (*Hardoon v Belilios*-equivalent
  principles);
- **Specific** Jersey case law.

## Pitfalls

- **Mislabelling** indemnity as guarantee (or vice versa);
- **Failing** to address variation / release rules;
- **Drafting** ambiguous scope (limited vs continuing);
- **Missing** corporate-benefit / financial-assistance
  considerations;
- **Inadequate** independent advice for personal
  guarantees;
- **Ignoring** undue-influence / unconscionability defences.

## Drafting recommendations

- **Distinguish** guarantee from indemnity clearly;
- **Specify** scope, amount, duration;
- **Include** anti-defence provisions ("primary obligation",
  "irrespective of...");
- **Address** variation, release, co-surety contribution;
- **Include** governing law and jurisdiction;
- **Document** independent legal advice for personal
  guarantors.
