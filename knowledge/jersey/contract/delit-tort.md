# Délit / Tort in Jersey Law

## The Jersey approach

Jersey tort (délit) law draws from **both** civil-law and
common-law traditions:

- **Délit** in Pothier's framework — intentional civil wrongs;
- **Quasi-délit** in Pothier's framework — negligent civil
  wrongs;
- **English tort** law highly persuasive in modern Jersey
  practice;
- **Specific** Jersey doctrines preserved.

## Pothier framework

Pothier divided civil wrongs into:

### Délit

- **Intentional** wrongs;
- **Wilful** harm;
- **Examples**: assault, fraud, defamation.

### Quasi-délit

- **Negligent** wrongs;
- **Careless** harm;
- **Examples**: negligent injury, professional negligence.

## Modern Jersey tort framework

In practice, Jersey courts use **English-style** tort
categories with Pothier-influenced reasoning where relevant:

### Negligence

- **Duty of care**;
- **Breach** of duty;
- **Causation**;
- **Foreseeable damage**;
- **Specific** Jersey treatment of professional, occupier,
  and other duties.

### Trespass

- **Land** trespass;
- **Goods** trespass;
- **Person** (assault, battery, false imprisonment);
- **Specific** Jersey customary aspects.

### Nuisance

- **Private nuisance**;
- **Public nuisance**;
- **Specific** Jersey rules on neighbour obligations.

### Defamation

- **Libel** (written);
- **Slander** (spoken);
- **Defamatory imputation**;
- **Defences**: truth, fair comment, privilege;
- **Specific** Jersey rules on damages.

### Misrepresentation (tort form)

- **Negligent misstatement** (*Hedley Byrne*);
- **Fraudulent misrepresentation** (deceit);
- **Specific** Jersey applications;
- **Overlap with contract** misrepresentation.

### Interference with contractual / business relations

- **Inducement** to breach of contract;
- **Tortious** interference;
- **Specific** Jersey rules.

### Other torts

- **Conspiracy**;
- **Conversion** of goods;
- **Detinue**;
- **Specific** Jersey customary-law actions.

## Specific Jersey doctrines

### Voisinage

A **distinctive Jersey customary** concept:

- **Neighbour obligation**;
- **Duty** not to cause unreasonable harm to neighbouring
  property;
- **Strict liability** elements;
- **Specific** Jersey case law (*Rockhampton Apartments*
  and similar);
- **Different from** English nuisance in some respects.

### Specific customary torts

- **Specific** Jersey customary causes of action;
- **Documented** in Le Geyt and modern case law;
- **Limited** modern application.

## Negligence — the principal tort

Modern Jersey negligence law:

### Duty of care

- *Donoghue v Stevenson* persuasive;
- **Specific** categories of duty (occupier, professional,
  manufacturer);
- **Specific** Jersey rules in trust / fiduciary contexts.

### Breach

- **Standard of reasonable care**;
- **Specific** professional / specialist standards;
- **Specific** considerations for skilled defendants.

### Causation

- **'But for'** test;
- **Material contribution**;
- **Intervening factors**;
- **Specific** Jersey case law.

### Damage

- **Foreseeable** type of damage;
- **Subject to** *Wagon Mound* persuasive authority;
- **Specific** Jersey treatment of psychiatric harm,
  economic loss.

### Defences

- **Contributory negligence** (apportionment);
- **Volenti non fit injuria** (consent);
- **Ex turpi causa** (illegality);
- **Specific** Jersey rules.

## Remedies

### Damages

- **Compensatory** principle;
- **Pecuniary** and **non-pecuniary** loss;
- **Specific** Jersey rules on quantum;
- **Specific** rules for personal injury (no statutory
  damages cap in Jersey).

### Injunction

- **Restraining** future tortious conduct;
- **Mandatory** in some cases;
- **Specific** Jersey procedural rules.

### Specific orders

- **Account** of profits in some cases;
- **Specific** orders for specific torts;
- **Subject to** equitable considerations.

## Limitation

- **Jersey statutory** limitation periods apply;
- **Personal injury**: typically **3 years** from accrual;
- **Other torts**: typically **3-6 years**;
- **Specific** Jersey case law on accrual.

## Practical considerations

For practitioners:

- **Tort** claims through Royal Court typically;
- **Specific** Jersey damages quantification;
- **PI insurance** considerations;
- **Specific** Jersey procedural rules.

For commercial:

- **Tort** claims often parallel contract claims (where
  pleaded together);
- **Specific** Jersey rules on concurrent claims;
- **Limitation** considerations may favour one over the
  other.
