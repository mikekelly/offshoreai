# Restraint of Trade under Jersey Law

## The question

A Jersey contract restricts a party's freedom to compete,
solicit, or use information after termination. When will
Jersey courts enforce — and when strike down — restrictive
covenants?

## The general principle

Jersey, following Pothier and the broader common-law
tradition:

- **Restraints of trade** are **prima facie unenforceable**;
- **May be enforced** if reasonable in scope, duration,
  geography, and in the public interest;
- **Burden** on the party seeking enforcement;
- **Construed strictly** against the party benefiting.

## The reasonableness test

A restraint is enforceable if it is:

- **No wider** than necessary to protect a legitimate
  interest;
- **Reasonable** as between the parties;
- **Not contrary** to the public interest;
- **Specific** Jersey case law tracks the English approach
  with Pothier-aligned fairness overlay.

## Legitimate interests

The party imposing the restraint must show a **legitimate
business interest** to protect:

- **Trade secrets** / confidential information;
- **Customer connections**;
- **Workforce stability**;
- **Goodwill** of a business sold;
- **Specific** Jersey context (smaller market, specific
  industries).

## Scope of the restraint

Reasonableness assessed against:

- **Activities** covered (specific role / general business);
- **Geographic scope** (Jersey-only / Channel Islands /
  wider);
- **Duration** (months / years);
- **Identifiability** of customers / clients restricted.

## Specific contexts

### Employment contracts

- **Higher scrutiny** than sale-of-business restraints;
- **Specific** Jersey approach reflects bargaining
  imbalance;
- **Typical durations** — 3 to 12 months for senior roles;
- **Specific** Jersey case law on financial-services
  restraints;
- **EJL 2003** specific provisions on enforceability.

### Sale of business

- **Greater latitude** for purchaser-protective covenants;
- **Reasonable durations** longer than employment;
- **Geographic** scope often Channel Islands / wider;
- **Specific** Jersey case law on goodwill protection.

### Shareholder agreements

- **Restraints** on selling shareholders post-exit;
- **Reasonable** to protect remaining shareholders;
- **Specific** Jersey case law.

### Trust deeds

- **Specific** Jersey rules on trustee restraints;
- **Specific** considerations for protector / committee
  restraints.

## Types of restraint

### Non-competition

- **Highest scrutiny**;
- **Must protect** identifiable legitimate interest;
- **Narrow** activity / geography preferred.

### Non-solicitation of customers

- **More likely enforceable** than non-competition;
- **Specific** customer-list / contact-period limits;
- **Connection-based** test (Jersey case law).

### Non-solicitation of employees

- **Generally enforceable** in commercial contexts;
- **Specific** Jersey TCB / financial-services practice.

### Non-dealing

- **Stricter** than non-solicitation;
- **Specific** drafting considerations.

### Confidentiality

- **Lower scrutiny** than competitive restraints;
- **Implied** duty of confidentiality;
- **Specific** Jersey rules on duration and scope.

## Severance / blue pencil

Where a restraint is partly unreasonable:

- **Severance** may preserve enforceable parts;
- **Specific** Jersey case law on "blue-pencil" approach;
- **Not** rewriting — only deleting;
- **Drafting** can facilitate severance with separate sub-
  clauses.

## Construction

Jersey courts construe restraints:

- **Strictly** against the party benefiting;
- **In context** of commercial purpose;
- **By reference to** legitimate interest;
- **At time** of formation, not at enforcement.

## Remedies for breach

- **Injunction** — primary remedy;
- **Damages** — typically difficult to quantify;
- **Account of profits** in specific cases;
- **Specific performance** — broader in Jersey than
  English equity;
- **Royal Court** procedure for urgent injunctive relief.

## Pitfalls

- **Drafting** "kitchen sink" restraints with no
  reasonableness consideration;
- **Importing** English templates without Jersey
  adaptation;
- **Failing** to articulate legitimate interest in the
  drafting;
- **Mixing** non-solicitation and non-competition without
  clear separation;
- **Missing** Jersey-specific market considerations.

## Drafting recommendations

- **Identify** legitimate interest in recital / preamble;
- **Calibrate** scope, duration, geography to that interest;
- **Use separate sub-clauses** to facilitate severance;
- **Specify** definitions (customer, prospective customer,
  competing business);
- **Include** garden leave / set-off arrangements;
- **Document** the negotiation;
- **Engage** Jersey counsel for opinion.
