# Glossary — Jersey

Jersey-specific terms, names, and abbreviations. Where a term has a full
treatment file, the entry links to it.

**Additional terms** from later corpus expansions (new statute wikis,
adjacent jurisdictions, customary law, contract / property law) are in
[`glossary-extension.md`](glossary-extension.md).

## A

**Advocate.** A member of the senior branch of the Jersey legal profession,
admitted by the Royal Court, with full rights of audience before all
Jersey courts. Contrast with **Solicitor**.

**AIF.** Alternative Investment Fund. Regulated in Jersey under the
[Alternative Investment Funds (Jersey) Regulations 2012](sources.md). The
AIFM is the manager of an AIF.

**Article 9 (of the Trusts Law).** The Jersey trust **firewall**:
Jersey law governs questions of validity, capacity to settle, and
the rights and powers of trustees, beneficiaries and protectors; foreign
forced-heirship rules are not applied by Jersey courts. See
[trusts/firewall](trusts/firewall.md).

**Article 9A.** The settlor-reserved-powers provision — a wide menu of
powers the settlor of a Jersey trust may reserve without invalidating the
trust. See [trusts/reserved-powers](trusts/reserved-powers.md).

**Article 47.** The Royal Court's statutory jurisdiction to set aside the
exercise of a fiduciary power for mistake, replacing the common-law
*Hastings-Bass* rule as adopted by the Jersey courts. See
[trusts/article-47-set-aside](trusts/article-47-set-aside.md).

**Article 123C.** The 0% standard corporate income-tax rate provision in
the [Income Tax (Jersey) Law 1961](tax/zero-ten.md).

**Article 123D.** The 10% rate for "financial services companies".

**Article 123I.** The 20% rate (with taper) for large corporate retailers.

## B

**Bailiff.** The presiding civic, parliamentary and judicial officer of
Jersey. President of the Royal Court and of the States Assembly. See
[legal-system/bailiff](legal-system/bailiff.md).

## C

**CIGA.** Core Income-Generating Activities, the operational test under
the [Taxation (Companies — Economic Substance) (Jersey) Law
2019](tax/economic-substance.md).

**Connétable.** The elected head of one of the 12 parishes; sits ex
officio in the [States Assembly](government/states-assembly.md).

**CRS.** Common Reporting Standard. The OECD automatic-exchange standard,
implemented in Jersey by regulation. See [international/crs](international/crs.md).

**Crown Dependency.** Constitutional status of Jersey, Guernsey and the
Isle of Man — internally self-governing, but the King is head of state
and the UK Government carries formal responsibility for defence and (to
some extent) foreign affairs. See [government/crown-dependency](government/crown-dependency.md).

## D

**Désastre.** Jersey's customary-law personal insolvency / corporate
wind-up procedure, initiated by application to the Royal Court.

**Deputy.** One of 37 elected members of the States Assembly, returned
from constituencies. The number rises if Senators return at the 2026
election.

**DTA.** Double Taxation Agreement.

## E

**Economic substance.** The requirement under the Taxation (Companies —
Economic Substance) (Jersey) Law 2019 that Jersey-resident companies
carrying on certain relevant activities are directed and managed in
Jersey and conduct CIGA there. See [tax/economic-substance](tax/economic-substance.md).

**Entitled / Licensed / Entitled for Work / Registered.** The four
residential and employment categories under the Control of Housing and
Work (Jersey) Law 2012. See [immigration-residency/index](immigration-residency/index.md).

## F

**FATCA.** The US Foreign Account Tax Compliance Act, implemented in
Jersey under a Model 1 IGA. See [international/fatca](international/fatca.md).

**FSB.** Fund Services Business — a class of regulated business under
the Financial Services (Jersey) Law 1998 covering administrators,
custodians, registrars and certain other functionaries of funds.

## G

**GST.** Goods and Services Tax. Jersey's broad-based consumption tax,
levied at 5%. See [tax/gst](tax/gst.md).

## H

**HVR.** High-Value Residency, the 2(1)(e) residency route for
high-net-worth individuals. See
[immigration-residency/high-value-residency](immigration-residency/high-value-residency.md).

## I

**ICC.** Incorporated Cell Company — a cell company structure in which
each cell has its own legal personality. Contrast **PCC**.

**ISE.** International Services Entity, the GST regime that lets
finance-business entities pay a fixed annual fee and disregard GST on
supplies to non-residents. See [tax/gst-international-services-entity](tax/gst-international-services-entity.md).

## J

**JCPC.** The Judicial Committee of the Privy Council, the final court
of appeal from Jersey.

**JFSC.** The Jersey Financial Services Commission, established by the
[Financial Services Commission (Jersey) Law 1998](financial-regulation/jfsc-law-1998.md).

**JPF.** Jersey Private Fund. See [funds/jersey-private-fund](funds/jersey-private-fund.md).

**Jurat.** A lay judge of the Royal Court, elected for life (until age
72) by an Electoral College. Jurats are judges of fact; the Bailiff or
Deputy Bailiff is judge of law. See [legal-system/jurats](legal-system/jurats.md).

## L

**Lieutenant-Governor.** The King's personal representative in Jersey.
See [government/lieutenant-governor](government/lieutenant-governor.md).

**LTC.** Long-Term Care contribution, a separate payroll-style
contribution collected alongside income tax.

## M

**MLO.** The Money Laundering (Jersey) Order 2008, the principal AML/CFT
rules for relevant persons.

## N

**NPPR.** National Private Placement Regime — the marketing route under
AIFMD for non-EU AIFs to be marketed into individual EU member states.

## P

**Parish.** One of the twelve civil parishes that together cover the
whole of Jersey — Grouville, St Brelade, St Clement, St Helier, St John,
St Lawrence, St Martin, St Mary, St Ouen, St Peter, St Saviour, Trinity.
Each has an elected **Connétable**.

**PCC.** Protected Cell Company — a single legal entity divided into
cells whose assets and liabilities are segregated by statute.

**Privy Council.** See **JCPC**.

## R

**Revenue Jersey.** The agency within the Treasury that administers
Jersey's income tax, GST, and related taxes. See
[tax/revenue-jersey](tax/revenue-jersey.md).

**Royal Court.** Jersey's superior court of first instance and appellate
court for the Magistrate's Court. Sits in three principal "Numbers" —
**Samedi Division** (single-judge civil work), **Inferior Number**
(Bailiff plus two Jurats for trial work) and **Superior Number**
(Bailiff plus five or more Jurats for serious sentencing and
constitutional matters). See [legal-system/royal-court](legal-system/royal-court.md).

## S

**Senator.** Member of the States Assembly elected on an island-wide
mandate. The role was abolished for the 2022 election and is scheduled
to return at the 2026 election following an Assembly decision.

**States Assembly.** Jersey's unicameral legislature. See
[government/states-assembly](government/states-assembly.md).

## T

**TCB.** Trust Company Business — the class of regulated activity under
Article 2 of the [Financial Services (Jersey) Law 1998][fsjl] covering
the provision of trustee, foundation-administration, company-management
and similar services in the course of business.

**TCA.** UK–EU Trade and Cooperation Agreement, the post-Brexit
goods-and-fisheries framework that Jersey participates in following the
fall-away of Protocol 3.

**TIEA.** Tax Information Exchange Agreement.

## Z

**Zero-ten.** Shorthand for Jersey's corporate income-tax structure —
0% standard, 10% for financial services companies, 20% for utility
companies, large corporate retailers, hydrocarbon trade, quarrying,
cannabis trade, property income, and certain regulated funds. See
[tax/zero-ten](tax/zero-ten.md).

[fsjl]: https://www.jerseylaw.je/laws/unofficialconsolidated/Pages/13.225.aspx
