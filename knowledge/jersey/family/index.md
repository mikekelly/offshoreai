# Family Law — Jersey

## What this section is about

Jersey family law — divorce, ancillary relief, child
arrangements, care and protection of children, civil
partnerships, cohabitation, adoption. The forum is the
**Royal Court — Family Division** (formerly the
Matrimonial Causes Division), staffed by Family Judges.

The principal statutes are:

- **Matrimonial Causes (Jersey) Law 1949** — divorce,
  judicial separation, ancillary relief;
- **Children (Jersey) Law 2002** — care orders, contact /
  residence (now "child arrangements"), parental
  responsibility, public-law protection;
- **Civil Partnership (Jersey) Law 2012** — same-sex civil
  partnerships;
- **Marriage and Civil Status (Jersey) Order 2018** — same-
  sex marriage;
- **Adoption (Jersey) Law 1961** — adoption;
- **Child Custody (Jurisdiction) (Jersey) Law 2005** —
  cross-border child-jurisdiction rules.

## Reform pipeline (as of May 2026)

Several reforms are **drafted but not yet enacted**:

- **No-fault divorce** — would remove fault grounds, the
  3-year bar, and permit joint applications;
- **Pension lump-sum** orders — would allow lump-sum
  payments from pensions in ancillary relief (pension
  sharing remains absent);
- **Mandatory NCDR** — would require parties to engage in
  non-court dispute resolution before litigation;
- **Children and Civil Status (Amendments) (Jersey) Law
  2024** — surrogacy parental orders; not yet in force.

Until these come into force, the **current law applies** —
including the 3-year bar and the fault grounds.
