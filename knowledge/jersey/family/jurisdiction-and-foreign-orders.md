# Jurisdiction, Recognition, and Foreign Orders

## Divorce jurisdiction

The Family Division can hear a divorce petition if:

- **Both parties** are **domiciled in Jersey** at the
  petition date; **or**
- **Either party** is **habitually resident in Jersey
  throughout the year** ending with the petition.

**Nationality is irrelevant**. *Forum non conveniens*
principles apply where competing jurisdictions are
available — no automatic "jurisdiction race" since
Jersey is not bound by EU Brussels II.

## Children jurisdiction

Under the **Child Custody (Jurisdiction) (Jersey) Law
2005**, the Family Division has jurisdiction over children
matters if:

- The child is **habitually resident in Jersey**; **or**
- The child is **present in Jersey** but **not habitually
  resident** elsewhere or in the UK, and immediate
  exercise of power is required; **or**
- **Matrimonial proceedings** are continuing in Jersey
  for the parents.

## Foreign matrimonial orders

**Mirror order procedure**:

- Foreign financial orders are **not automatically
  enforceable** in Jersey;
- The Royal Court considers a **mirror order**
  application;
- Not a full rehearing — guided by **comity**;
- Royal Court typically follows the substance of the
  foreign order unless contrary to Jersey public policy
  or fairness.

**Pension sharing orders** from foreign jurisdictions:

- **Unlikely** to be registered/enforced in Jersey;
- Jersey has **no equivalent** order;
- May need to rely on offsetting elsewhere.

## Foreign contact / custody orders

- **Part 1 Family Law Act 1986** orders (England & Wales)
  — register in Jersey **without review**;
- **Other foreign orders** — require **mirror order**
  application;
- **1996 Hague Convention** — Jersey **not party**, so
  foreign PR orders are not automatically recognised.

## Children abduction — 1980 Hague Convention

Jersey **is** party (through UK extension):

- **Wrongful removal / retention** triggers return
  applications;
- **Under-16 children**;
- **Expedited** procedure;
- **Limited defences** — consent, settled, grave risk,
  child's objection;
- **Central authority** based in Jersey.

## No-claims-after-foreign-divorce rule

A spouse divorced in a foreign jurisdiction **cannot**
subsequently bring financial claims in Jersey, even
where:

- They are now resident in Jersey;
- Jersey assets exist;
- The foreign jurisdiction did not deal adequately with
  finances.

This is a significant gap. The remedy is to bring
financial claims **in the foreign jurisdiction** (or
contest jurisdiction in the first place) — not Jersey.

## Forum disputes

Where multiple jurisdictions could hear a divorce:

- **Forum non conveniens** principles apply;
- The court considers:
  - **Habitual residence** of parties;
  - **Domicile**;
  - **Location of assets**;
  - **Location of children**;
  - **Witnesses**;
  - **Speed** of proceedings;
  - **Applicable law**;
  - **Costs**.

Forum disputes are typically resolved early in the
proceedings to avoid wasted effort.

## Service abroad

Service in Hague Convention states follows Convention
rules. Non-Convention states require:

- **Diplomatic channels**;
- **Translated documents** for non-English jurisdictions;
- **Substantial lead time** in the timetable;
- **Substituted service** if formal service fails.

## Recognition of foreign marriages and civil
partnerships

Recognised:

- **Marriages** valid in country of celebration;
- **Same-sex marriages** from jurisdictions that allow
  them;
- **Civil partnerships** valid in country of formation;
- **Polygamous marriages** — in some narrow contexts
  (recognition without effect for some purposes).

Not recognised:

- **Customary marriages** without civil component;
- **Religious-only marriages** without civil registration
  in jurisdictions requiring it.

## Recognition of foreign divorces

Foreign divorces are recognised if:

- **Validly granted** in the country of origin per its
  law;
- **No public policy** objection;
- **Parties had connection** to the foreign jurisdiction.

Recognition issues arise particularly with:

- **Talaq** divorces;
- **Mail-order** divorces from third countries;
- **Non-consensual** divorces.

## Pitfalls

- **Assuming** Jersey will recognise a foreign PR order
  — it may not (no Hague 1996);
- **Forum-shopping** without specialist advice;
- **Waiting** to bring financial claims after foreign
  divorce — no remedy available later;
- **Service abroad** delays leading to procedural
  problems;
- **Mirror order** under-budgeting in cross-border cases.
