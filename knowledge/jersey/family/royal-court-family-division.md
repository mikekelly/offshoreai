# Royal Court — Family Division

## What it is

The **Family Division** of the **Royal Court of Jersey**
hears:

- **Divorce** and **judicial separation** petitions;
- **Ancillary relief** — financial orders;
- **Children matters** — private and public law under
  the CJL 2002;
- **Civil partnership** dissolution;
- **Adoption** orders;
- **Nullity** of marriage / civil partnership;
- **Cross-border** family applications (mirror orders,
  Hague Convention return applications).

It is **not** the Magistrate's Court (which handles only
criminal and some civil claims) — it is a division of the
Royal Court, the superior court of Jersey.

The Family Division is also sometimes referred to as the
**Matrimonial Causes Division** in older documents, or
informally as the "Family Court."

## Composition

A typical Family Division hearing is before:

- A **Family Judge** — a Royal Court judge with family-
  law experience (usually a Master, the Bailiff, the
  Deputy Bailiff, or a Commissioner);
- **Sitting alone** for most family work;
- **No Jurats** typically (unlike Royal Court general
  civil work).

## Procedure

Family Division procedure is governed by:

- **Matrimonial Causes Rules 2005** — divorce and
  related;
- **Royal Court Rules 2004** — general civil procedure
  baseline;
- **Practice Directions** issued by the Bailiff.

Procedure is generally **less formal** than general
Royal Court civil work and is intended to be accessible.

## Confidentiality

Family Division proceedings are conducted in **private**:

- **Closed court** — no public;
- **No press access**;
- **Anonymised** judgments where published;
- **Strict** restrictions on outside disclosure;
- **Contempt** sanctions for breaches.

This is a critical operational reality: a divorce case
that would be public in other courts is private here.

### Children — private law

Procedure for child arrangements orders:

1. Application filed;
2. First Hearing Dispute Resolution Appointment (FHDRA)
   — early triage;
3. JFCAS welfare report ordered if needed;
4. Fact-finding hearing where allegations of harm;
5. Final hearing;
6. Order.

### Children — public law

Care and supervision proceedings:

1. Pre-proceedings letter from the Minister;
2. Application;
3. Case management — guardian appointed via JFCAS;
4. Expert assessments commissioned;
5. Issues resolution hearing;
6. Final hearing within 26 weeks ideally;
7. Care plan approved;
8. Order.

## Practitioners

Family-law work is concentrated in a small number of
specialist advocates' practices in Jersey. Mediation is
offered by the **Family Foundation** (free service) and
private mediators.

## Appeals

Appeals from the Family Division lie to the:

- **Court of Appeal** for substantive issues;
- **Royal Court** (en bloc) in narrow cases;
- **Privy Council** with leave for further appeal.

Appeals are time-limited (typically 28 days) and require
permission for some grounds.

## Costs

- **Each party** typically pays own costs in family
  matters absent litigation misconduct;
- **Public funding** available in some cases (children,
  domestic violence);
- **Costs orders** rare unless one party has behaved
  badly.
