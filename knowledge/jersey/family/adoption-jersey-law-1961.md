# Adoption (Jersey) Law 1961 — Overview

## What it does

The **Adoption (Jersey) Law 1961** regulates adoption in
Jersey:

- **Eligibility** of adopters;
- **Consent** of birth parents;
- **Procedure** through the Royal Court Family Division;
- **Effect** of an adoption order;
- **International** adoption recognition (limited).

It has been amended over the decades — notably to permit
adoption by same-sex couples, civil partners, and
cohabiting couples.

## Who can adopt

Adoption is available to:

- **Married couples** (same- or opposite-sex);
- **Civil partners**;
- **Cohabiting couples** (same- or opposite-sex);
- **Single individuals**;
- **Step-parents** (in narrow cases — adoption by a
  step-parent extinguishes the other birth parent's
  status).

At least one applicant must be **domiciled in the
British Isles**.

## Eligibility — age and status

An adopter must be:

- The child's **parent** or **father** (e.g.
  step-parent); **or**
- A **relative** aged **20 or older**; **or**
- Anyone **aged 25 or older**.

## Residence requirement

The child must have **lived with the applicant for at
least 3 months** before the application — to ensure
adoption is well-founded.

## Consent of birth parents

Adoption requires the **consent of each person with
parental responsibility** unless:

- The child has been **freed for adoption** by previous
  order; **or**
- The court **dispenses with consent** because the parent:
  - **Cannot be found**;
  - Is **incapable** of giving consent;
  - **Unreasonably withholds** consent;
  - **Persistently fails** to exercise parental rights;
  - **Abandons or neglects** the child;
  - Is **incapable** of caring for the child.

The "unreasonably withholds" ground is the most litigated.
The court applies an **objective standard** — would a
reasonable parent in these circumstances refuse?

## The child's voice

- **Wishes and feelings** considered, weighted by age and
  understanding;
- Children aged **14 or over** must **agree in writing**
  unless the court determines they cannot give agreement
  (e.g. capacity reasons).

## Procedure

1. **Application** to the Royal Court Family Division;
2. **JFCAS welfare report** ordered;
3. **Birth parents** notified and consent obtained or
   dispensation sought;
4. **Hearing** in private;
5. **Adoption order** if granted.

## Effect of an adoption order

A full adoption order:

- **Transfers parental responsibility** from birth
  parents to adopters;
- **Extinguishes** birth parents' legal status as parents
  for nearly all purposes;
- The adopters become the legal parents in law (birth
  certificate amended);
- **Succession** flows through the adopters thereafter,
  not the birth family;
- **Cannot be revoked** save in extraordinary cases.

## Step-parent adoption

A step-parent may adopt their spouse / civil partner's
child:

- **Effect**: step-parent becomes legal parent;
- **Other birth parent's** status is **extinguished**
  (subject to consent or dispensation);
- **Significant decision** — alternative is a Parental
  Responsibility Agreement / Order, which gives the
  step-parent PR without extinguishing the other birth
  parent;
- Courts scrutinise step-parent adoption carefully
  given the **extinction** effect.

## International adoption

Adoption from overseas is **complex**:

- **Eligibility** of foreign adoption depends on
  recognition rules;
- **British Isles domicile** of one adopter typically
  required;
- **1993 Hague Convention** on Intercountry Adoption —
  Jersey position uncertain (review case-by-case);
- **Specialist** advice essential.

## Adoption from care

In public-law cases (see
[`care-and-supervision.md`](./care-and-supervision.md)), the
care plan may be adoption:

- **Placement order** authorises the Minister to place
  the child for adoption;
- **Strict scrutiny** — *Re B-S* / "nothing else will
  do" standard;
- **Birth parents** may oppose throughout;
- **Adoption order** ultimately ends parental status.

## Recent and forthcoming reforms

The **Children and Civil Status (Amendments) (Jersey)
Law 2024** is drafted but not in force. It will affect:

- **Surrogacy parental orders** — a new route distinct
  from adoption;
- **Legal status** of children born via fertility
  treatment.

## Pitfalls

- **Step-parent adoption** without considering the
  alternative (PR agreement / order);
- **International adoption** without specialist advice;
- **Consent issues** — birth parents whose consent is
  needed but cannot be found;
- **Failure to engage** with JFCAS welfare process;
- **Adoption disclosure** — adopted persons may seek
  records as adults, a process governed by Jersey rules.
