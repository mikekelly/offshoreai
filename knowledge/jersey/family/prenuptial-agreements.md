# Pre-Nuptial and Post-Nuptial Agreements in Jersey

## The Jersey position

Jersey follows **Radmacher v Granatino** [2010] UKSC 42:
prenuptial and postnuptial agreements are **not strictly
binding** on the Family Division, but the court will
**uphold them unless it would be unfair to do so**.

## The Radmacher test

The court will give **decisive weight** to a nuptial
agreement provided:

- It was **freely entered** — no duress, undue influence,
  or material misrepresentation;
- The parties **understood the implications**;
- It is **fair** at the time of enforcement — not just at
  the time of signing.

The court's **statutory duty** to ensure a fair outcome
**cannot be fettered** by the parties' contract. The
agreement is a strong **factor**, not a binding contract.

## Best practice for enforceable agreements

To maximise the chance of an agreement being upheld:

- **Sign at least 28 days before the wedding** (no late
  pressure);
- **Both parties** have **independent legal advice**;
- **Full financial disclosure** by both parties;
- **No duress, threats, or coercion**;
- **Reasonable provision** — leaving the financially-
  weaker spouse not destitute;
- **Periodic review** — provision for review on
  significant change (children, illness, long marriage);
- **Jersey-law governing clause** and Jersey forum.

## Post-nuptial agreements

Post-nuptial agreements are treated similarly. They are
typically used:

- After a major change (inheritance, business sale);
- To reflect a renegotiated understanding;
- To address concerns about the durability of a
  pre-nuptial agreement.

## What an agreement can do

- Specify **how matrimonial assets** will be divided;
- **Carve out** pre-marital wealth, inheritance, business
  interests;
- Set **maintenance** levels or termination;
- Address **specific assets** (the family home,
  particular investments);
- **Cross-reference** trust structures.

## What an agreement cannot do

- Bind the court to **a specific outcome** if doing so
  would be unfair;
- **Oust** the court's jurisdiction;
- **Exclude** child welfare considerations or child
  maintenance;
- Override **forced-heirship** rights on death (those
  end on divorce anyway);
- Override **mandatory** statutory protections.

## Effect of children

The arrival of children typically **strengthens** the
court's willingness to depart from a prenuptial agreement
if needed:

- **Child welfare** is paramount;
- **Housing** for the resident parent and children
  trumps capital-preservation clauses;
- **Maintenance** for children cannot be excluded.

Many agreements **provide for review** on the birth of a
child for this reason.

## International dimensions

Prenuptial agreements signed in other jurisdictions:

- **Recognised** by Jersey courts under the same Radmacher
  framework;
- **Choice of law** clause important;
- **Practical** issues if assets / parties span multiple
  jurisdictions;
- **Forum-shopping** considerations — court may apply
  *forum non conveniens*.

## Pitfalls

- **Last-minute signing** — invites duress arguments;
- **No independent advice** — vitiates the agreement;
- **No disclosure** — vitiates;
- **Inadequate provision** — court will depart from
  agreement to meet needs;
- **No review provision** — long marriages without review
  invite challenge;
- **Drafting** without specialist family-law input.
