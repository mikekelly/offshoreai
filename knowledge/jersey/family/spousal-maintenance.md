# Spousal Maintenance under Jersey Ancillary Relief

## Overview

Spousal maintenance (periodical payments) is one of the
financial orders available in ancillary relief. It pays
one spouse a regular sum from the other's income, typically
to meet ongoing needs after divorce.

## When it's ordered

The court orders maintenance where:

- **Needs** cannot be met from capital alone;
- **Clean break** is not appropriate;
- **Earning capacity** is limited (age, health, childcare,
  career-sacrifice);
- **Compensation** for marital disadvantage is required.

Where capital is sufficient to meet both parties' needs,
the court strongly prefers a **clean break** — a one-off
settlement ending all financial ties.

## Duration

Modern Jersey practice tracks English authority:

- **Time-limited** orders are now the norm;
- **Joint lives** orders (continuing for life) are rare;
- **Term orders** typically run a few years to a decade —
  long enough for the receiving spouse to retrain or
  re-establish earning capacity;
- **Deferred clean break** — capital adjustment now plus
  time-limited maintenance, ending in a clean break.

## Amount

The court considers:

- The **receiving spouse's needs** for income;
- The **paying spouse's ability** to pay;
- The **marital standard of living**;
- Any **earning capacity** of the receiving spouse;
- **Index-linking** for inflation;
- **Child maintenance** is calculated separately.

There is no formula. The court exercises **broad
discretion** weighing the Article 25-31 factors.

## Variation and termination

Maintenance orders can be varied:

- **Up or down** on change of circumstances;
- **Capitalised** into a lump sum (commutation);
- **Discharged** if no longer required;
- **Automatic termination** on death of either party or
  remarriage of the receiving spouse;
- **Cohabitation** of the receiving spouse — may justify
  variation but does not automatically terminate.

## Interim maintenance

Under Article 31, the court may order **interim
maintenance** pending substantive resolution:

- Covers basic living costs;
- Smaller than likely final amounts;
- May include **interim legal costs** — but applicant
  must show alternative funding has been explored.

## Pitfalls

- **Assuming** joint-lives orders — court avoids them now;
- **Failing** to plan for termination — receiving spouse
  needs an income strategy;
- **Cohabiting** without informing the court — risk of
  variation application;
- **Remarriage** terminating the order — receiving spouse
  may lose income at a vulnerable time.
