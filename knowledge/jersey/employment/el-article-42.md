# EL 2003 Article 42 — Summary Dismissal

Article 42 deals with **summary dismissal** — dismissal
**without notice**.

## Permitted basis

Summary dismissal is permitted for **gross misconduct**:

- **Conduct** so serious as to fundamentally undermine the
  employment relationship;
- **Examples**:
  - **Theft** from the employer;
  - **Violence** at work;
  - **Gross insubordination**;
  - **Serious dishonesty**;
  - **Serious safety breaches**;
  - **Specific** other serious misconduct.

## Fact-specific

What counts as gross misconduct **depends on circumstances**:

- **Specific** workplace context;
- **Specific** position of the employee;
- **Specific** policies of the employer;
- **Specific** seriousness of the conduct;
- **Tribunal scrutinises** carefully.

## Procedural fairness required

Even for gross misconduct, procedural fairness applies:

- **Investigation** before dismissal;
- **Specific** allegations put to the employee;
- **Opportunity** to respond;
- **Reasonable** time to prepare;
- **Right of appeal** in some cases;
- **Specific** ACAS-equivalent code expectations.

A summary dismissal **with no procedural fairness** is
generally **unfair** even if gross misconduct in fact occurred.

## Effect of summary dismissal

- **No notice** required;
- **No payment** in lieu of notice;
- **Specific** accrued holiday pay still due;
- **Specific** pension considerations;
- **Restrictive covenants** continue to bind.

## Risks for employer

- **Wrongful dismissal** claim if not gross misconduct (no
  notice required would be wrong);
- **Unfair dismissal** claim if procedure unfair;
- **Specific** discrimination claims if dismissal taints by
  protected characteristic;
- **Reputational** risks.

## Specific scenarios

### Theft from employer

- **Investigation** with police if material;
- **Specific** documentary evidence;
- **Specific** procedural fairness;
- **Specific** restrictive covenant considerations.

### Workplace violence

- **Suspension** typically pending investigation;
- **Police** involvement if criminal;
- **Specific** Health and Safety considerations.

### Gross insubordination

- **Specific** documented refusal of lawful instructions;
- **Specific** procedural fairness;
- **Specific** context evaluation.
