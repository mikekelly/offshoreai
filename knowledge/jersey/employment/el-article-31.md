# EL 2003 Article 31 — Maternity Leave

Article 31 establishes **maternity-leave** entitlement.

## Entitlement

A pregnant employee is entitled to:

- **Maternity leave** — up to **52 weeks** total;
- **Two phases**:
  - **Ordinary maternity leave** (first portion);
  - **Additional maternity leave** (extended portion).

## Eligibility

- **No qualifying period** for ordinary maternity leave;
- **Specific** length of service for additional leave;
- **Notification** to employer required.

## Pay during leave

- **Statutory** maternity benefit through Social Security
  Jersey (separate from EL);
- **Contractual** enhancement often (better than statutory);
- **Specific** Social Security qualifying period for
  maternity benefit;
- **Some pay** during ordinary maternity leave typically.

## Right to return

- **Right to return** to same job after ordinary maternity
  leave;
- **Right to return** to **same or suitable** job after
  additional maternity leave;
- **Specific** Jersey protections;
- **Continuity** of employment.

## Protection from dismissal

- **Pregnancy / maternity** is automatic unfair dismissal
  ground (Article 64);
- **No qualifying period** needed for the claim;
- **Specific** burden-of-proof considerations favouring
  the employee.

## Notification

The employee must:

- **Notify** employer of pregnancy;
- **Specific** notification of maternity-leave dates;
- **Updates** if dates change.

## Specific protections

During pregnancy / leave:

- **Reasonable adjustments** to working conditions;
- **Antenatal appointments** paid time off;
- **Health and safety** assessments;
- **Specific** Jersey practical considerations.
