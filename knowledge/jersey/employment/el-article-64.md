# EL 2003 Article 64 — Right Not to Be Unfairly Dismissed

Article 64 establishes the **fundamental statutory right** —
employees with qualifying service have the right **not to be
unfairly dismissed**.

## The provision

An employee has the right not to be unfairly dismissed by
their employer, where:

- Employee has **completed the qualifying period** (typically
  **52 weeks** continuous service);
- The **dismissal** is unfair under Articles 64-78;
- **Subject to** specific exclusions.

## Five potentially fair reasons

A dismissal is **potentially fair** if for one of:

### 1. Capability or qualifications

- **Lack of skill**, aptitude, or ability;
- **Health** preventing the work;
- **Lack of qualifications**;
- **Subject to** reasonable accommodation, training,
  alternative roles.

### 2. Conduct

- **Misconduct** by the employee;
- **Gross misconduct** can warrant summary dismissal;
- **Ordinary misconduct** typically requires warnings and
  procedural fairness.

### 3. Redundancy

- **Genuine** reduction in work / role;
- **Subject to** fair selection and consultation;
- See [`el-part-7-redundancy.md`](el-part-7-redundancy.md).

### 4. Contravention of statute

- Where continuing to employ would breach statute (e.g.,
  loss of right to work, regulatory disqualification);
- **Subject to** consideration of alternatives.

### 5. Some other substantial reason (SOSR)

- **Catch-all** for fair reasons not in the other four;
- **Specific** to circumstances;
- **Examples**: irretrievable breakdown of relationship,
  business reorganisation, third-party pressure.

## Procedural fairness

Even for a fair reason, the dismissal must be **fairly
conducted**:

- **Reasonable investigation** of the issue;
- **Notice** to the employee of the issue;
- **Opportunity** for the employee to respond;
- **Reasonable consideration** of the response;
- **Right of appeal**;
- **Consistency** with treatment of others;
- **Proportionality** of dismissal to the issue.

## Automatic unfair dismissal

Some dismissals are **automatically unfair** with **no
qualifying period**:

- **Pregnancy** / **maternity-related**;
- **Family-leave related**;
- **Trade union** activity;
- **Health and safety** activity;
- **Whistleblowing** (protected disclosure);
- **Asserting** a statutory right;
- **Working time** complaints;
- **Discrimination-related** (see Discrimination Law).

## Remedies

If unfair dismissal proven:

### Reinstatement

- **Same** role on same terms;
- **Back-pay** to date of dismissal;
- **Continuity** preserved;
- **Tribunal** can order but employer not compelled to
  comply.

### Re-engagement

- **Similar** role with same employer;
- **Compensation** for any reduction;
- **Tribunal** can order.

### Compensation

- **Basic award** based on service / pay (similar to
  redundancy);
- **Compensatory award** for loss suffered;
- **Cap** on compensatory award (prescribed annually);
- **Uplift** for failure to follow procedures.

## Time limit

Claims must be filed in the **Employment Tribunal**:

- **Within 8 weeks** of the effective date of termination;
- **Strict** time limit;
- **Specific** exceptions for not reasonably practicable
  cases.
