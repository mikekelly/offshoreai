# EL 2003 Part 2 — Written Particulars and Pay (residual)

Article 3 (written particulars) has its own deep file. This
cluster covers Articles 4-15.

## Article 4 — Itemised pay statement

Each employee must receive an **itemised pay statement** with
each pay:

- **Gross** earnings;
- **Variable** deductions (income tax, social security);
- **Fixed** deductions;
- **Net** pay;
- **Method** of payment;
- **Hours** for hourly pay.

## Articles 5-9 — Deductions

Pay **deductions** are permitted only:

- **Required by law** (income tax, social security);
- **Authorised** by contract;
- **Consented to** in writing by the employee;
- **Specific** exceptions (e.g., recovery of overpaid wages).

**Unlawful deductions** are recoverable by the employee
through the Tribunal.

## Article 10 — Payment of wages

Wages must be paid:

- **In money** (cash, cheque, or electronic transfer);
- **By the contractually agreed date**;
- **In full** (subject only to lawful deductions);
- **By appropriate method** (typically electronic transfer in
  modern practice).

## Articles 11-14 — Specific pay rules

### Pay during sickness

- **Statutory sick pay** (Social Security regime, not EL
  directly);
- **Contractual sick pay** can supplement;
- **Documentation** of sickness requirements.

### Pay during suspension

- **Disciplinary** suspensions typically paid;
- **Specific** rules for medical / regulatory suspensions.

### Holiday pay

- See [`el-part-4-leave.md`](./el-part-4-leave.md);
- **Calculation** at average pay rate;
- **Include** regular overtime in some configurations.

### Maternity / paternity / adoption pay

- See [`el-part-4-leave.md`](./el-part-4-leave.md);
- **Statutory rates**;
- **Contractual enhancement** common.

## Article 15 — Records

Employers must maintain pay records:

- **Hours worked**;
- **Pay** calculation;
- **Deductions**;
- **Holiday** records;
- **Retain** for **prescribed period** (typically 6+ years).
