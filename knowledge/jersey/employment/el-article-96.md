# EL 2003 Article 96 — Tribunal Jurisdiction

Article 96 establishes the **Jersey Employment and
Discrimination Tribunal** and its jurisdiction.

## The Tribunal

Constituted by:

- **Chair** — qualified lawyer (advocate or solicitor);
- **Two lay members** — typically one with employer-side
  experience, one with employee-side / worker
  representative background;
- **Specific** disability / other expertise where relevant;
- **Sitting** in panels of three typically.

## Jurisdiction

The Tribunal hears:

### Employment claims

- **Unfair dismissal** (Article 64);
- **Redundancy pay** claims (Article 79);
- **Unauthorised deductions** from wages;
- **Minimum wage** claims;
- **Family-leave** claims;
- **Whistleblowing** detriment claims;
- **Specific** other statutory rights;
- **Working time** claims (some);
- **Specific** other Article 96 jurisdiction.

### Discrimination claims

- **Discrimination (Jersey) Law 2013** claims;
- **Direct and indirect** discrimination;
- **Harassment**;
- **Victimisation**;
- **Equal pay**;
- **Reasonable adjustments**;
- **Specific** other DL claims.

## Outside Tribunal jurisdiction

The Tribunal does **not** hear:

- **Pure breach of contract** claims (Royal Court);
- **Tort** claims (Royal Court);
- **Specific** claims requiring criminal process;
- **Specific** matters requiring Royal Court jurisdiction.

## Comparison with Royal Court

| Feature | Tribunal | Royal Court |
|---|---|---|
| Forum | Specialist | Court of general jurisdiction |
| Procedure | Less formal | Formal |
| Cost | Lower typically | Higher |
| Composition | Chair + 2 lay | Bailiff / Judge + Jurats |
| Appeals | Royal Court | Court of Appeal |

## Specific procedural features

- **Self-representation** common;
- **Lower** formality than Royal Court;
- **Specific** witness statement procedures;
- **Specific** disclosure rules;
- **Hearing** in Jersey.
