# EL 2003 Part 9 — Miscellaneous

## Articles 106-108 — Regulations

The Minister may make **Orders** for:

- **Minimum wage** rates;
- **Statutory leave** durations;
- **Forms** and **fees** for Tribunal;
- **Specific** procedural rules;
- **Specific** sectoral applications.

The **Employment Forum** advises on regulation.

## Article 109 — Transitional

Transitional provisions:

- **Pre-2003** employments brought into scope;
- **Continuous service** rules for transitioning;
- **Grandfathered** rights;
- **Specific** legacy arrangements.

## Article 110 — Citation

The Law is cited as the **"Employment (Jersey) Law 2003"**.

It came into force in **stages from 2005 onwards** as the
various Parts were brought into operation. Amendments have
covered:

- **Family-friendly** rights enhancement;
- **Discrimination** law alignment;
- **Whistleblowing** strengthening;
- **Working time** updates;
- **Specific** sectoral rules.

## Related legislation

The EL operates alongside:

- **Discrimination (Jersey) Law 2013** — discrimination claims;
- **Health and Safety at Work (Jersey) Law 1989** — workplace
  safety;
- **Social Security (Jersey) Law 1974** — benefits and
  contributions;
- **Control of Housing and Work (Jersey) Law 2012** —
  status-based work access;
- **Specific** pension legislation;
- **JACS** through specific establishing Law;
- **Employment Forum** through specific Order.

## Codes of Practice

- **Disciplinary and grievance procedures** (Employment Forum
  Code);
- **Sexual harassment** in workplace;
- **Specific** sectoral codes;
- **Specific** equality codes.

Codes are **not strictly binding** but **persuasive** at
Tribunal — failure to follow can attract uplift.
