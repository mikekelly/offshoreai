# EL 2003 Part 7 — Redundancy

Part 7 governs **statutory redundancy** — the right to
redundancy pay and procedures.

## What is redundancy

A **redundancy situation** exists where:

- The **employer ceases business** (closure);
- The **employer ceases business at the place** where the
  employee was employed;
- The **requirements** for employees to do work of a
  particular kind has **ceased or diminished**.

The reason must be **genuine** — sham redundancies to dismiss
specific employees are challengeable.

## Articles 79-83 — Right to redundancy pay

An employee made redundant has the right to statutory
**redundancy payment** if:

- They have **at least 2 years** continuous service;
- They are within scope of the EL;
- They are **made redundant** (not dismissed for other
  reasons).

## Calculation

The statutory formula is:

- **Weekly pay** × **years of service** (capped at 20 years);
- **Age factor** (older employees get higher multiplier);
- **Weekly pay capped** at the statutory maximum (prescribed
  annually).

Specific Jersey formula varies somewhat from UK.

## Articles 84-88 — Selection

### Pool

- **Identify** appropriate pool of potentially redundant
  employees;
- **Fair** comparison group;
- **Specific** sectoral / role considerations.

### Selection criteria

- **Objective** criteria (skills, experience, performance,
  attendance, disciplinary record);
- **Avoidance** of discriminatory criteria;
- **Application** consistently across pool;
- **Subject to** discrimination law (cannot use protected
  characteristics).

### Process

- **Score** each employee in pool;
- **Identify** those at risk;
- **Consult** with at-risk employees;
- **Final** selection following consultation.

## Articles 89-91 — Consultation

### Individual consultation

For each at-risk employee:

- **Meaningful** consultation about the proposal;
- **Information** about the redundancy criteria;
- **Opportunity** to suggest alternatives;
- **Consideration** of alternative employment;
- **Specific** trial periods for alternatives.

### Collective consultation

Where **20+ employees** at risk:

- **Specific** information and consultation obligations
  before the dismissals;
- **Time-limited** consultation period;
- **Specific** notification to Employment Forum / Minister;
- **Trade union** consultation where recognised.

## Articles 92-95 — Alternative employment

The employer should:

- **Identify** suitable alternative roles;
- **Offer** alternatives before redundancy;
- **Specific** trial period for alternatives;
- **Refusal** of suitable alternative may disqualify
  redundancy entitlement;
- **Unsuitable** alternative does not disqualify.

## Practical considerations

For employers:

- **Plan** redundancies carefully;
- **Document** the business reasons;
- **Follow** Jersey ACAS-equivalent code;
- **Take** legal advice for complex cases;
- **Mitigate** liability through proper process.

For employees:

- **Engage** in consultation actively;
- **Suggest** alternatives;
- **Document** the process;
- **Consider** Tribunal claim if process unfair.
