# EL 2003 Article 51 — Qualifying Period for Unfair Dismissal

Article 51 establishes the **qualifying period** for unfair-
dismissal protection.

## Standard qualifying period

The standard qualifying period is **52 weeks** of continuous
employment:

- Below 52 weeks: **no** unfair-dismissal protection;
- 52+ weeks: **full** unfair-dismissal protection (Article
  64);
- **Subject to** automatic-unfair-dismissal exceptions
  (Article 65+) which require no qualifying period.

## Continuous employment

Continuous employment includes:

- **Specific** periods with the same employer;
- **Transferred** employment under specific TUPE-equivalent
  rules;
- **Specific** suspensions / leaves preserving continuity;
- **Subject to** breaks that reset the clock.

## Effective date of termination

The qualifying period is calculated to the **effective date
of termination (EDT)**:

- For **notice-given** termination: end of notice period;
- For **summary dismissal**: date of dismissal;
- For **constructive dismissal**: date employee resigned in
  response;
- For **PILON** dismissal: date specified per contract.

## Excluded categories

Even with 52+ weeks, some categories excluded:

- **Specific** statutory exclusions;
- **Specific** sectoral exclusions (e.g., specific Crown
  servants);
- **Specific** worker types.

## Automatic unfair dismissal — no qualifying period

For specific protected categories, **no qualifying period**:

- **Pregnancy / maternity-related** dismissal;
- **Trade-union** activity;
- **Whistleblowing** (protected disclosure);
- **Health and safety** activity;
- **Asserting** a statutory right;
- **Working time** complaints;
- **Discrimination-related** (under Discrimination Law).

The qualifying period is **important** in determining whether
the protection applies — Article 51 frames the general rule;
Articles 65+ provide the exceptions.
