# EL 2003 Article 23 — Working Time

Article 23 establishes **working-time** limits and rest
requirements.

## Maximum weekly hours

- **Average** hours worked over a reference period;
- **Default cap** at prescribed level (typically 48 hours
  per week);
- **Opt-out** available with worker consent;
- **Specific** exemptions for senior management, household
  staff, transport workers.

## Rest periods

- **Daily rest**: minimum **11 hours** between working
  periods;
- **Weekly rest**: minimum **24 hours** per week (or 48
  hours fortnightly);
- **Rest breaks** during shifts over a threshold (typically
  6+ hours).

## Night work

- **Limits** on night work hours;
- **Health assessment** for night workers;
- **Specific** sectoral rules.

## Records

Employers must maintain records of:

- **Hours worked**;
- **Rest periods taken**;
- **Night-work shifts**;
- **Opt-outs** by workers;
- **Specific** retention requirements.

## Specific exemptions

The Article 23 regime does **not** apply to:

- **Specific** senior managers with autonomy over their
  hours;
- **Household** staff in some configurations;
- **Specific** transport workers (subject to separate rules);
- **Family members** in family business.

## Opt-out

A worker can opt out:

- **Voluntary** written consent;
- **Specific** form requirements;
- **Can be withdrawn**;
- **Subject to** anti-pressure protections.
