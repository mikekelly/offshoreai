# EL 2003 Article 41 — Wrongful Dismissal

Article 41 deals with **wrongful dismissal** — termination
in breach of contract.

## Definition

**Wrongful dismissal** = **breach of contract** by the
employer:

- Dismissal **without** proper notice;
- Dismissal **without** valid contractual reason;
- **Summary dismissal** without proper basis (gross
  misconduct not in fact present);
- **Other** specific contractual breaches.

## Distinct from unfair dismissal

| Feature | Wrongful (Article 41) | Unfair (Article 64) |
|---|---|---|
| Basis | Breach of contract | Statutory right |
| Qualifying period | None | 52 weeks typically |
| Damages | Loss of notice pay typically | Compensation including future loss |
| Forum | Royal Court typically | Tribunal |
| Specific remedies | Damages | Reinstatement, re-engagement, compensation |

## Remedy

For wrongful dismissal:

- **Damages** for lost pay during notice period that was
  not given;
- **Limited** to the notice period typically;
- **Mitigation** required;
- **No** automatic right to reinstatement.

## Procedure

- **Royal Court** civil action typically;
- **Tribunal** jurisdiction in some specific configurations;
- **Pre-action** correspondence often resolves;
- **Settlement** common.

## Repudiatory breach

For a serious breach by the employer:

- Employee may treat the contract as **at an end** ("accept
  the repudiation");
- **Resignation** in response constitutes "constructive
  dismissal" — actionable as wrongful and unfair;
- **Specific** legal advice essential for employee on
  acceptance vs affirmation.

## Common scenarios

### No notice given

- **Employer** dismisses without notice;
- **No** gross misconduct justifying summary dismissal;
- **Damages** = notice-period pay.

### Specific contractual breaches

- **Demotion** without consent;
- **Pay reduction** without consent;
- **Material variation** of terms;
- **Specific** other fundamental breaches.

### Summary dismissal where no gross misconduct

- Employer **summary dismisses** but conduct doesn't meet
  gross-misconduct threshold;
- **Notice period damages** payable;
- **Plus** potential unfair-dismissal claim.
