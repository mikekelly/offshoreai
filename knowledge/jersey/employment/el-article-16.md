# EL 2003 Article 16 — Minimum Wage

Article 16 establishes Jersey's **statutory minimum wage**.

## The provision

Every worker is entitled to receive **at least** the
**statutory minimum wage** for hours worked.

The minimum wage rate is **set by Order** and **reviewed
annually** by the Minister, on the advice of the Employment
Forum.

## Rate setting

Jersey minimum wage is **typically set in line with**:

- **Cost of living** in Jersey;
- **Comparison** with UK National Living Wage (Jersey often
  comparable or higher reflecting Jersey cost of living);
- **Employment Forum** recommendations;
- **Industry / sectoral** considerations.

The **Employment Forum** (a tripartite body — government,
employers, unions) advises on the rate.

## Different rates

There may be **different rates** for:

- **Adult workers** (typically 25+ or 18+);
- **Trainees / apprentices**;
- **Specific sectoral** rates in some industries;
- **Young workers** (with separate rates).

## What counts as wages

For minimum-wage compliance:

- **Basic pay** counts;
- **Tips and gratuities** **do not count** (must be **on top**
  of minimum wage);
- **Benefits in kind** generally do not count;
- **Accommodation provided** has specific rules;
- **Specific exclusions** for items not constituting "wages".

## Hours worked

Includes:

- **Time at work** even if not actively working (downtime,
  on-call in some configurations);
- **Travel** time during work day (not commuting);
- **Training** time;
- **Specific** sectoral rules.

## Enforcement

Non-payment of minimum wage:

- **Civil claim** by the worker through the Tribunal;
- **Inspection** by Employment Department;
- **Criminal offence** for persistent failure;
- **Civil penalties** payable to the Crown;
- **Records** required to demonstrate compliance.

## Specific scenarios

### Tipping in hospitality

- Tips received by hospitality workers are **not** part of
  minimum wage;
- Employer cannot count tips towards minimum wage compliance;
- Specific Jersey rules on tip distribution.

### Accommodation deduction

- Employer providing accommodation can deduct from pay (per
  contract);
- **Cap** on the deduction at a prescribed daily / weekly
  rate;
- Cannot reduce pay below minimum wage net of allowable
  deduction.

### Apprentice / trainee rates

- **Lower** minimum wage for apprentices in their first year;
- **Standard rate** thereafter;
- **Specific** documentation of apprenticeship required.
