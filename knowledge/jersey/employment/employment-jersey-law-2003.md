# Employment (Jersey) Law 2003 — Overview

The **Employment (Jersey) Law 2003 (EL)** is Jersey's
**principal employment statute**. It came into force in
**2005** (in phases) and consolidated previously fragmented
Jersey employment rules into a single modern framework.

## Coverage

The EL covers:

- **Written terms** of employment;
- **Minimum wage**;
- **Working time** and **holiday**;
- **Pay rules** (deductions, itemised payslips);
- **Family-friendly rights** (maternity, paternity, adoption,
  parental leave, time off for dependants);
- **Unfair dismissal** protections;
- **Redundancy** procedures and pay;
- **Insolvency** payments to employees;
- **Employment tribunal** procedure;
- **Specific** sectoral rules.

The Law applies to **most** Jersey employment, with **limited
exceptions** (e.g., specific Crown servants, sailors on
specific routes).

## Key features

### Comparison with UK Employment Rights Act 1996

The EL is **broadly similar** to UK Employment Rights Act
1996 but with Jersey-specific provisions:

- **Lower threshold** for some rights (e.g., unfair-dismissal
  qualifying period historically shorter);
- **Specific** Jersey leave entitlements;
- **Specific** Jersey redundancy formula;
- **Jersey Employment Tribunal** rather than UK ET.

### Written terms of employment

Every employee must receive **written terms** within **4
weeks** of starting:

- **Identity** of parties;
- **Date** of employment start;
- **Pay** rate and frequency;
- **Hours of work**;
- **Holiday** entitlement;
- **Sick pay** terms;
- **Pension** information;
- **Notice** periods;
- **Job description** / title.

### Minimum wage

Jersey has a **statutory minimum wage**:

- **Reviewed annually**;
- **Different rates** for adults, trainees, apprentices;
- **Enforced** through Employment Tribunal and prosecutions.

### Working time

- **Maximum working week** (with opt-out);
- **Rest breaks**;
- **Daily and weekly rest**;
- **Night work** restrictions;
- **Specific** sectoral rules.

### Holiday entitlement

- **Statutory minimum** annual leave;
- **Bank holidays** treatment;
- **Pro-rata** for part-time;
- **Pay** during leave.

### Family-friendly rights

- **Maternity leave** (52 weeks);
- **Paternity leave**;
- **Adoption leave**;
- **Parental leave** (unpaid);
- **Time off** for dependants;
- **Flexible working** requests (right to request).

### Unfair dismissal

After a **qualifying period** (typically 52 weeks):

- Employee has **right not to be unfairly dismissed**;
- Dismissal must be for a **fair reason** and **fairly
  conducted**;
- **Remedies**: reinstatement, re-engagement, compensation;
- **Specific** automatic-unfair-dismissal categories (no
  qualifying period needed — pregnancy, whistleblowing,
  health-and-safety, trade-union activity).

### Redundancy

- **Statutory redundancy pay** formula;
- **Consultation** requirements (collective redundancies);
- **Selection** procedures must be fair;
- **Alternative employment** offers considered.

## Employment Tribunal

The **Jersey Employment and Discrimination Tribunal**:

- **Independent** tribunal;
- **Lay members** plus legal chair;
- **Jurisdiction** over employment and discrimination claims;
- **Procedures** less formal than Royal Court;
- **Awards** of compensation and orders.
