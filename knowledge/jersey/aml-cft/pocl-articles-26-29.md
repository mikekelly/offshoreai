# POCL 1999 Articles 26-29 — Customer Info / Monitoring / Search / Disclosure

## Article 26 — Customer information orders

A specific form of production order:

- Compels **bank or similar institution** to confirm:
  - Whether the **suspect holds an account**;
  - **Account numbers**;
  - **Identifying** information about account holder;
- **Without** transaction detail;
- **Used** in **scoping phase** of investigation.

## Article 27 — Account monitoring orders

The Court can order **monitoring of an account**:

- For a **specified period** (often 90 days);
- **Reporting** specified transactions as they occur;
- **Without** alerting the account holder (tipping-off
  exception built in);
- **Used** to track suspect transactions in real time.

## Article 28 — Search warrants

The Court can issue search warrants:

- On **reasonable grounds**;
- For **investigating** specified financial crimes;
- Subject to **safeguards** (notice where practical, time of
  day, etc.);
- Permitting **seizure** of specified items.

## Article 29 — Disclosure orders

In an investigation, the Court can order a person to:

- **Answer questions** under oath;
- **Provide information**;
- **Produce documents**;
- Subject to **LPP**;
- Subject to **excluded material** (medical, journalistic
  in some cases).

## Mental element for compliance

The orders are compulsory:

- **Failure** to comply is a criminal offence;
- **Specific** defences (privilege, no possession);
- **Court** can hear privilege disputes;
- **Specific** procedural protections.

## Practical implications

For Jersey service providers facing these orders:

- **Quick response** required;
- **Compliance officer** typically handles;
- **Legal advice** for complex / privileged matters;
- **Specific** tipping-off considerations;
- **Documentation** of compliance.
