# POCL 1999 Part 7 — Miscellaneous

Part 7 contains **forfeiture**, **civil recovery**, and other
miscellaneous provisions.

## Article 42 — Cash forfeiture

The Court may order **forfeiture of cash** that is suspected
to be:

- **Proceeds of crime**; or
- **Intended for use in unlawful conduct**.

Cash can be **seized** and **detained** by police / customs:

- Initial **48-hour detention** without court order;
- **Further detention** for up to 3 months on court order;
- **Forfeiture proceedings** initiated within the detention
  period.

The standard is **balance of probabilities** — civil standard.

Cash forfeiture is **non-conviction-based** — it applies even
where no prosecution is brought.

## Article 43 — Civil recovery

Property identified as **recoverable** (i.e., proceeds of
criminal conduct):

- Can be the **subject of civil proceedings** by the Attorney
  General;
- **Standard** is balance of probabilities;
- **Remedies** include freezing orders, receivership, vesting
  orders;
- **Property is recovered** for the benefit of the Crown
  (with discretion to compensate victims).

Civil recovery in Jersey is **narrower** than under the UK
POCA 2002 but exists as a tool for **non-conviction-based**
asset recovery.

## Article 44 — Forfeiture of listed assets

Specific provision for forfeiture of:

- **Listed assets** (luxury goods, precious metals);
- **Vehicles**, **vessels**, **aircraft**;
- **Other** specified high-value items associated with
  criminal conduct.

## Article 45 — International cooperation

The Law works with:

- The **Crime (International Co-operation) (Jersey) Law
  2001** for cross-border investigation and enforcement;
- **Bilateral mutual legal assistance treaties**;
- **Egmont Group** for FIU-to-FIU information exchange;
- **Recognition** of foreign restraint / confiscation orders.

## Articles 46-48 — General

- **Regulation-making powers** for the Minister;
- **Forms and fees**;
- **Citation** — "Proceeds of Crime (Jersey) Law 1999".

## Practical issues

- **Forfeiture proceedings** are increasingly used as a
  **non-prosecution** alternative — recovering tainted assets
  without the evidential burden of a criminal case;
- **Civil recovery** is being expanded as Jersey aligns with
  global AML trends (FATF, Moneyval recommendations);
- **International coordination** is increasingly important —
  most major Jersey AML cases involve cross-border elements.
