# POCL 1999 Part 4 — Investigations

Part 4 governs **investigative powers** for financial-crime
investigations — the powers used by the FIU and law enforcement
to gather information.

## Article 25 — Production orders

The Royal Court may order a person to:

- **Produce** specified documents / records to the
  investigator;
- **Provide** information about specified matters;
- Within a **specified timeframe**.

Grounds:

- There are **reasonable grounds** to suspect a person has
  benefited from criminal conduct or has committed an offence;
- The material is **likely to be of substantial value** to
  the investigation;
- It is **in the public interest** for the order to be made
  (weighing the interest of investigation against confidentiality
  / privacy).

A production order is **directed at a specific person**
(typically a bank, TCB firm, or other regulated business
holding records).

## Article 26 — Customer information orders

A specific form of production order requiring a **bank or
similar institution** to confirm:

- Whether the **suspect holds an account** at the institution;
- Account **numbers**;
- **Identifying** information about the account holder;
- **Without** providing transaction detail.

Customer information orders are used in the **scoping phase**
of investigation — to identify accounts before specific
production orders are made.

## Article 27 — Account monitoring orders

The Court may order an institution to **monitor an account**:

- For a **specified period** (often 90 days);
- Reporting **specified transactions** as they occur;
- Without alerting the account holder (tipping-off
  exception built in for the institution's
  compliance officer).

Used to track **suspect transactions** in real time during an
active investigation.

## Article 28 — Search warrants

The Court can issue a **warrant** for entry and search of
premises:

- On **reasonable grounds**;
- For the purpose of **investigating** specified financial
  crimes;
- Subject to **safeguards** (notice if practical, time of
  day, etc.);
- Permitting **seizure** of specified items.

## Article 29 — Disclosure orders

In an **investigation**, the Court can order a person to:

- **Answer questions**;
- **Provide information**;
- **Produce documents**;
- Subject to **legal professional privilege** (preserved);
- Subject to **excluded material** (medical, journalistic in
  some cases).

The disclosure order is a powerful tool for compelling
cooperation — non-compliance is a criminal offence.

## Practical issues for service providers

When served with an order under Part 4:

- **Comply within the time limit** (extension only by
  application);
- **Respect tipping-off rules** — do not warn the subject;
- **Identify privileged material** carefully — privileged
  documents need not be produced, but the existence of
  privileged material may need to be acknowledged;
- **Document** the response;
- **Apply for variation** if the order is unduly burdensome.
