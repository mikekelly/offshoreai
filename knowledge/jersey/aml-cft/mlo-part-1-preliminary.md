# MLO 2008 Part 1 — Preliminary

## Article 1 — Interpretation

Key definitions:

- **"Beneficial owner"** — the natural person(s) who
  ultimately own or control the customer, or on whose behalf
  a transaction is conducted. For corporate customers, a
  threshold of **25%** ownership / control is generally used;
- **"Business relationship"** — a relationship that has, or
  is expected to have, an element of duration (one-off
  transactions are subject to occasional-transaction rules);
- **"Customer"** — the person establishing or conducting the
  business relationship or transaction with the supervised
  business;
- **"PEP" (politically exposed person)** — a person entrusted
  with prominent public functions, including family members
  and close associates;
- **"Reliable independent source"** — government-issued ID,
  certified copies, regulated electronic verification systems;
- **"Senior management"** — board / executive level
  appropriate to take risk-based decisions.

## Article 2 — Application

The Order applies to **persons carrying on supervised
business** as listed in **Schedule 2 of POCL 1999**:

- **Financial institutions** (banks, FSBs, TCBs, etc.);
- **DNFBPs** (designated non-financial businesses and
  professions — lawyers, accountants, estate agents,
  high-value dealers, casinos);
- **VASPs** (virtual asset service providers).

The Order does **not apply** to:

- Persons **not in the regulated sector**;
- **Public-sector** activities not within Schedule 2;
- **Personal** transactions of individuals.

The Order applies **regardless of the size** of the supervised
business — small TCB firms have the same obligations as large
ones (the **proportionality** principle adjusts implementation,
not the obligation itself).

## Effective date

The Order came into force on **1 January 2008** and has been
amended on several occasions to align with FATF / Moneyval
expectations.
