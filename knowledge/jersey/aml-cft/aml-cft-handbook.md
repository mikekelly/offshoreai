# JFSC AML/CFT Handbook

The **JFSC AML/CFT Handbook** is the regulator's **detailed
guidance** on how supervised businesses should meet their
obligations under the Money Laundering (Jersey) Order 2008 and
the Proceeds of Crime (Jersey) Law 1999.

## Status of the Handbook

The Handbook is:

- **Issued by the JFSC** as guidance under its statutory powers;
- **Not statutory** in itself, but treated as **evidence of the
  regulatory standard** expected;
- **Mandatory in effect** — JFSC enforcement action typically
  references Handbook standards;
- **Updated periodically** through formal consultation.

Following Handbook standards generally satisfies the underlying
obligations; deviating requires good reasons and risk-management
documentation.

## Structure

The Handbook is structured into sections covering:

- **Risk assessment** — business-wide and customer-specific;
- **CDD** — standard, simplified, enhanced;
- **Specific customer types** — trusts, foundations, companies,
  partnerships, charities, governmental bodies;
- **Specific risks** — PEPs, high-risk jurisdictions, complex
  structures, virtual assets;
- **Ongoing monitoring**;
- **Internal controls** — governance, MLRO, training, record-
  keeping;
- **SAR reporting** — internal and external;
- **Sanctions and asset-freezing**;
- **Sector-specific guidance** — banking, TCB, funds, insurance,
  legal, accounting.

## Key practical sections

### Risk assessment

The Handbook expects businesses to:

- Conduct a **business-wide risk assessment** (BWRA);
- Update it **at least annually**;
- Map **customer**, **product**, **jurisdiction**, and **delivery**
  risk factors;
- Use the BWRA to drive **policies and procedures**.

### CDD on trusts

Trusts present specific CDD challenges:

- Identify **settlor**, **trustees**, **protector** (if any),
  **named beneficiaries**, and **objects** of discretionary
  classes;
- Understand the **purpose and intended nature** of the trust;
- Apply **enhanced due diligence** for trusts settled by PEPs or
  from high-risk jurisdictions.

### CDD on foundations

Similar to trusts:

- Identify **founder**, **council members**, **guardian**,
  **beneficiaries** or class of objects;
- Apply EDD where indicated.

### PEPs (Politically Exposed Persons)

PEPs and their family members and close associates require
**enhanced due diligence** including:

- **Source-of-wealth verification**;
- **Senior management approval** of the relationship;
- **Enhanced ongoing monitoring**;
- **Periodic review** at higher cadence than standard.

The PEP regime applies to **foreign and domestic** PEPs (with
some differences in scope).
