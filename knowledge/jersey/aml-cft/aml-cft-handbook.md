# JFSC AML/CFT Handbook

The **JFSC AML/CFT Handbook** is the regulator's **detailed
guidance** on how supervised businesses should meet their
obligations under the
[Money Laundering (Jersey) Order 2008](./money-laundering-order-2008.md)
and the
[Proceeds of Crime (Jersey) Law 1999](./proceeds-of-crime-law-1999.md).

This file is the **hub**: it states the Handbook's status,
sketches the section structure, and points at per-section
indexes that take the substantive coverage forward. Each
per-section file declares its own sub-section structure and
treats the section files (where they exist) as the canonical
landing for a specific operational question.

## Status of the Handbook

The Handbook is:

- **Issued by the JFSC** as guidance, not legislation;
- **Not statutory in itself**, but treated as **evidence of the
  regulatory standard** expected of supervised businesses;
- **Quasi-mandatory in effect** — JFSC enforcement action
  routinely references Handbook standards as the benchmark
  against which conduct is judged;
- **Updated periodically** through formal consultation with
  prescribed implementation dates.

Following Handbook standards generally **satisfies** the
underlying Money Laundering Order 2008 obligations; deviating
from Handbook standards requires **good reasons documented in
risk-management and policy material**.

This soft-law-with-bite character is why the Handbook sits
alongside the
[JFSC Codes of Practice](../financial-regulation/codes-of-practice.md)
(also issued by the JFSC, but **directly enforceable** under the
underlying regulatory Law) and above ordinary JFSC guidance.

## How an agent should use the Handbook coverage

The per-section index is the canonical landing for a Handbook-
operational question. A typical retrieval pattern:

1. Identify the operational question (e.g. "EDD for a UAE
   client with a PEP family member") and the relevant section
   index (here: `amlh-edd.md` + `amlh-peps.md`).
2. Read the per-section index for the structural answer.
3. Cross-reference the underlying MLO 2008 article (here:
   [`mlo-article-15.md`](./mlo-article-15.md) on EDD).
4. Cross-reference the relevant Code of Practice section for
   the regulated business (here: [TCB](../financial-regulation/cop-tcb-index.md)
   or [IB](../financial-regulation/cop-ib-index.md)
   customer-onboarding section).
5. Where the question turns on the **exact Handbook
   requirement**, cite the JFSC's current Handbook
   publication directly rather than the section file — the
   section file is for orientation, not authoritative quotation.

## How the Handbook updates

The JFSC consults on Handbook changes through formal
consultation papers, industry workshops, and a final feedback
statement, with prescribed implementation dates. The current
Handbook URL is the canonical source; files in this section
carry the **effective date of the version they describe** in
their `last_verified` field. When the Handbook is replaced or
materially amended, section files are updated in place — old
versions remain in git history.
