# Investigation of Fraud (Jersey) Law 1991

The **Investigation of Fraud (Jersey) Law 1991** equips the
**Attorney General** with **information-gathering powers** to
investigate serious or complex fraud — analogous (in function,
not in detail) to the powers of the UK Serious Fraud Office
under the Criminal Justice Act 1987.

## What the Law does

The Law:

1. Empowers the **Attorney General** (and persons designated
   by the AG) to **require production of documents** and
   **answers to questions** in the investigation of suspected
   serious or complex fraud.
2. Permits the AG to authorise **searches and seizures** in
   the investigation.
3. Provides for **enforcement** of compliance with notices
   issued under the Law.
4. Operates **in parallel with the criminal justice process**
   — the Law is investigative, not in itself prosecutorial.

## Why this Law matters for AML/CFT practitioners

The information-gathering powers under this Law are a
significant intersection point between Jersey-supervised
businesses and law enforcement:

- A **production notice** under the Law compels the business
  to produce documents relating to a customer or transaction;
- The business must respond without **tipping-off** the
  customer (interaction with the
  [POCL Article 35](./pocl-article-35.md) prohibition);
- Production typically requires **legal-privilege screening**
  before disclosure — material protected by legal professional
  privilege is generally outside scope;
- Response timelines are typically tight; the business should
  have **predefined operational procedures** for responding.

The Handbook treats production-notice response as part of the
broader cooperation-with-law-enforcement and record-keeping
discipline.

## Interaction with international cooperation

Where the underlying fraud has an international dimension,
production notices may flow alongside requests under the
[Criminal Justice (International Co-operation) Law 2001](./cicl-articles-index.md)
from foreign authorities routed through the Jersey Attorney
General. The two regimes interlock.
