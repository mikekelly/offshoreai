# MLO 2008 Article 14 — Internal Disclosures

Article 14 requires the supervised business to have an
**internal disclosure mechanism** for AML / CFT concerns.

## Requirement

The business must require its **staff** to make an internal
disclosure to the MLRO when they:

- **Know or suspect**;
- Have **reasonable grounds for knowing or suspecting**;
- That another person is engaged in money laundering or
  terrorist financing.

## Reporting channel

The supervised business must establish:

- **Confidential channel** to the MLRO;
- **Protected** from retaliation (POCL Article 35A);
- **Documented** in the firm's procedures;
- **Trained** to staff during onboarding and refreshers;
- **Accessible** to all staff.

## Threshold

The threshold is **low**:

- Staff do **not need to be certain**;
- Staff do **not need to investigate** before reporting (that
  is the MLRO's role);
- Staff should **err on the side of reporting** when in doubt;
- **Subjective suspicion or objective reasonable grounds**.

## Confidentiality

Internal disclosures are **highly confidential**:

- Limited to **MLRO** and authorised deputies;
- Not disclosed to the **subject** of the disclosure;
- Not discussed in **routine forums** (board meetings, team
  meetings);
- **Tipping-off** rules (POCL Article 35) apply.

## Internal disclosure form

Typical content:

- **Reporter** identity and role;
- **Subject** of the suspicion;
- **Nature** of the suspicion;
- **Facts** giving rise;
- **Documents** referenced;
- **Recommendation** (where appropriate);
- **Date**.

## MLRO obligation

On receipt of internal disclosure:

- **Acknowledge** receipt;
- **Assess** the matter;
- **Decide** whether external SAR appropriate;
- **Document** the assessment;
- **Specific** Article 34A POCL obligations apply.

## Failure to disclose

Staff failure to make internal disclosure when threshold met:

- **POCL Article 34** offence (regulated sector);
- **Personal liability**;
- **Disciplinary** action by employer;
- **Specific** training and culture supporting compliance.
