# MLO 2008 Article 13 — Reliance on Third Parties

Article 13 permits a supervised business to **rely on a third
party** to perform CDD, subject to safeguards.

## The provision

A supervised business may rely on a **third party** to apply
some or all of the CDD measures:

- Provided the **third party** is itself subject to AML
  regulation in an **equivalent jurisdiction**;
- Provided the **third party consents** to providing the CDD
  documentation;
- Provided **immediate access** to CDD records on request is
  available;
- Provided the **supervised business retains responsibility**
  for compliance.

## Acceptable third parties

Permitted reliance is typically on:

- **Other JFSC-regulated** firms;
- **UK-regulated** firms (FCA, PRA);
- **Equivalent EU / EEA** regulated firms;
- **Other equivalent-jurisdiction** regulated firms (US, EU,
  OECD members with AML supervision);
- **Group affiliates** in equivalent jurisdictions.

**Not** permitted reliance on:

- **Unregulated** entities;
- **High-risk jurisdiction** entities;
- **Mere agents** of the customer.

## Documentation required

- **Written agreement** with the third party;
- **Confirmation** of third-party regulatory status;
- **CDD documents** themselves (not just confirmation that CDD
  was done);
- **Periodic review** of the third party's reliability;
- **Process** for immediate access to records.

## Limits

The supervised business **retains responsibility**:

- **Reliance does not transfer** the CDD obligation;
- The supervised business is **liable** for any CDD failure;
- The supervised business must **review** the relied-upon CDD;
- The supervised business must **supplement** where the
  third party's CDD is inadequate.

If the third party **fails** or **refuses** to provide
documents, the supervised business must:

- **Apply its own CDD**; or
- **Decline / terminate** the relationship.

## Outsourcing vs reliance

**Outsourcing** is different from reliance:

- **Outsourcing** — engaging a service provider to perform
  CDD on behalf of the supervised business (the supervised
  business retains full direct responsibility);
- **Reliance** — relying on another regulated firm's CDD
  performed for **its own** purposes.

The MLO Article 13 addresses **reliance**; outsourcing is
governed by general supervisory expectations on third-party
arrangements.

## Practical scenarios

### Bank-introduced trust client

- Jersey TCB receives a new trust client via a UK bank;
- The UK bank has performed CDD on the underlying client;
- The TCB can **rely on** the UK bank's CDD under Article 13;
- The TCB must obtain the **CDD documents** from the UK bank,
  not just confirmation.

### Group CDD

- Jersey TCB is part of a group with affiliates in Switzerland
  and Singapore;
- CDD performed by the Swiss / Singapore affiliates on group-
  wide clients can be **relied upon** under Article 13;
- The TCB must have **access** to the underlying records.

### Lawyer-introduced trust client

- Foreign law firm introduces a trust client;
- Whether the law firm is "equivalent regulated" depends on
  the jurisdiction;
- For **UK / EU lawyers**, generally yes;
- For **non-FATF-compliant jurisdictions**, no — direct CDD
  required.
