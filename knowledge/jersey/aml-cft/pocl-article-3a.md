# POCL 1999 Article 3A — When a Confiscation Order May Be Made

Article 3A specifies **when** the Royal Court may make a
confiscation order.

## Conditions

The Court may make a confiscation order if:

- The defendant has been **convicted** of an offence to which
  Part 2 applies (broadly: indictable offences);
- The prosecutor (or Court) considers a confiscation order
  appropriate;
- The Court is satisfied that the defendant has **benefited
  from criminal conduct**.

## Triggering offences

Part 2 applies to:

- **Indictable** offences generally;
- **Specific** drug trafficking offences;
- **Money laundering** offences (Articles 30-32);
- **Fraud and dishonesty** offences;
- **Sanctions breaches**;
- **Specific** other categories prescribed.

## Procedure

After conviction:

1. **Prosecutor** considers confiscation;
2. **Statement of information** filed identifying defendant's
   property and benefit;
3. **Court hearing** on confiscation;
4. **Defendant** can challenge prosecution evidence;
5. **Court** determines benefit and recoverable amount;
6. **Confiscation order** made.

## Timing

Confiscation order made:

- **After conviction** in same proceedings;
- **Specific** procedural rules for separate confiscation
  hearings;
- **Time limits** for application post-conviction.
