# Terrorism Law 2002 Article 16A — Insuring against payments made in response to terrorist demands

Article 16A targets **kidnap-and-ransom insurance**
arrangements that effectively underwrite payments to
terrorists. It is an offence for an **insurer** to make
a payment under an insurance contract in respect of any
money or property paid in response to a terrorist demand,
where the insurer knows or has reasonable cause to
suspect that the money or property was paid as a result
of the demand.
