# AML/CFT Handbook — Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD)

EDD is the **higher-intensity CDD** the Handbook requires for
higher-risk customers and relationships. It supplements (does
not replace) the standard
[CDD obligations](./amlh-cdd.md) and is mandated by both the
[Money Laundering Order 2008](./mlo-article-15.md) and the
Handbook in the situations summarised below.

This file is the section index. Sub-section content is stub
pending an editorial pass.

## When EDD is triggered

The Handbook treats the following situations as **mandatory
EDD triggers**:

1. **PEPs, their family members, and known close
   associates** — foreign and domestic. See
   [`./amlh-peps.md`](./amlh-peps.md).
2. **Customers from, or with assets in, high-risk
   jurisdictions** — FATF Black List, FATF Grey List, JFSC-
   designated high-risk countries. See
   [`./amlh-high-risk-countries.md`](./amlh-high-risk-countries.md).
3. **Customers identified as higher risk** by the business's
   own risk-rating methodology under the
   [BWRA](./amlh-risk-assessment.md).
4. **Complex or unusual structures** that obscure beneficial
   ownership without a clear economic or lawful purpose —
   layered companies, opaque nominees, multi-jurisdiction
   trusts without clear rationale.
5. **Non-face-to-face onboarding** in elevated-risk
   circumstances (the Handbook conditions this triggered by
   risk; not every remote onboarding requires EDD).
6. **Where a SAR has previously been filed** in respect of
   the customer or a related party.
7. **Where unusual transaction patterns emerge** during the
   relationship (an ongoing-monitoring trigger — see
   [`./amlh-ongoing-monitoring.md`](./amlh-ongoing-monitoring.md)).

## What EDD requires

Compared to standard CDD, EDD typically requires **all of the
following** unless the Handbook permits otherwise on documented
risk grounds:

### Additional evidence

- **Source-of-wealth (SoW) evidence** — distinct from source-
  of-funds (SoF); SoW asks where the customer's *cumulative
  wealth* originated, not merely the funds for the specific
  transaction. Verification through documented evidence:
  employment / business / inheritance / sale of asset records,
  not customer declarations alone.
- **Source-of-funds evidence** to a higher evidentiary
  standard than for standard CDD — typically primary
  documentation (bank statements, audited accounts, sale
  contracts).
- **Verification of stated purpose** on independent evidence
  (e.g. legal opinion confirming a structure's purpose, where
  the customer's stated purpose appears unusual).

### Additional process

- **Senior management approval** of the relationship before
  onboarding (and at periodic refresh) — the Handbook is
  specific about the seniority required (typically board or
  designated senior committee, not first-line management).
- **Enhanced ongoing monitoring** — more frequent transaction
  review, more frequent periodic refresh of CDD, lower
  thresholds for transaction-monitoring alerts.
- **Periodic refresh at higher cadence** — the Handbook
  expects refresh more often than the standard cycle (often
  annually for high-risk PEPs versus 3-yearly for standard
  customers).

### Additional documentation

- The EDD file documents **why EDD was triggered**, **what
  additional measures were applied**, and **who approved**
  the relationship. The JFSC will sample EDD files in any
  thematic review or supervisory visit.

## Source-of-wealth — the load-bearing piece

Source-of-wealth (SoW) verification is the **single most
common EDD failure point** in JFSC enforcement records.
Practitioners frequently confuse SoF (where did *these funds*
come from) with SoW (where did *the cumulative wealth* come
from). For a high-net-worth client:

- SoF for a £5m investment might be evidenced by a bank
  statement showing a £5m incoming wire.
- SoW for the same client requires evidence of how the client
  built £50m+ of cumulative wealth — employment history,
  business sale records, inheritance documentation,
  investment-portfolio history.

The Handbook expects **documented evidence**, not customer
declarations. For PEPs, the SoW evidentiary standard is at its
highest.

## Permissible EDD limitations

Where the Handbook permits a lighter application (e.g. for a
domestic PEP in narrowly-defined circumstances, or where the
SoW evidence is genuinely impossible to obtain), the business
must:

- Document the limitation;
- Document the **risk mitigation** applied in lieu (heightened
  monitoring, lower transaction thresholds, additional
  approval gates);
- Have senior-management sign-off on the limitation.

Operating with reduced EDD without documentation is an
enforcement risk regardless of the underlying risk profile.

## Interaction with the Codes of Practice

The relevant Code of Practice — TCB, FSB, IB — references the
EDD obligation and adds **business-conduct expectations**
around it (governance of the EDD function, conflicts when
senior management is also the beneficiary of the relationship,
records). See [`../financial-regulation/cop-tcb-index.md`](../financial-regulation/cop-tcb-index.md),
[`../financial-regulation/cop-fsb-index.md`](../financial-regulation/cop-fsb-index.md),
