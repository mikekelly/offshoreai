# POCL 1999 Articles 38-41 — Supervised Business Registration and Supervision

## Article 38 — Register of supervised businesses

The **Registrar** (typically JFSC for financial-services
sectors) maintains a **register of supervised businesses**:

- **Names** of supervised businesses;
- **Permitted activities** within Schedule 2;
- **MLRO** and **Compliance Officer** designations;
- **Status** (active, suspended, struck off);
- **Specific** other prescribed information.

## Article 39 — Application and registration

A person carrying on supervised business **must register**:

- **Before** commencing supervised business;
- **By application** to the JFSC (or relevant supervisor);
- With **fit-and-proper assessment** of principals;
- **Payment** of prescribed fees;
- **Continuing obligations** (annual returns, ongoing
  compliance).

For TCBs, FSBs, etc., AML registration is **integrated** with
FSL Article 9 / 10 authorisation.

## Article 40 — Supervision and inspection

The JFSC (and other supervisors):

- **Conducts on-site visits** and off-site supervision;
- **Issues** the **AML / CFT Handbook** with practical
  guidance;
- **Investigates** failures and breaches;
- **Tests** AML / CFT compliance through file reviews;
- **Specific** supervisory tools.

## Article 41 — Enforcement

Enforcement tools:

- **Civil penalties** for AML / CFT failures;
- **Public statements** for material breaches;
- **Conditions** on registration;
- **Suspension** of registration;
- **Revocation** of registration;
- **Specific** criminal prosecution for serious cases.

## Civil penalties

JFSC fines for AML breaches can be **substantial**:

- **£100k+** for material failures;
- **Multi-million** for major failures;
- **Specific** Jersey Civil Financial Penalties regime;
- **Aggravating / mitigating** factors considered.

## Themes of enforcement

Common JFSC enforcement themes:

- **Inadequate** CDD on complex structures;
- **Failure** to identify beneficial owners;
- **Inadequate** ongoing monitoring;
- **PEP** screening failures;
- **Tipping-off** breaches in some cases;
- **Specific** sectoral themes.
