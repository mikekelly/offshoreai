# POCL 1999 Article 32 — Acquisition, Use, Possession

Article 32 captures the **end use** of criminal property —
receiving, using, or holding it.

## The provision

A person commits an offence if they:

- **Acquire** criminal property;
- **Use** criminal property; or
- Have **possession** of criminal property.

## Mental element

The person must **know or suspect** that the property
constitutes a benefit from criminal conduct.

## Adequate consideration defence

A person is **not** guilty if they acquired, used, or had
possession of the property **for adequate consideration**:

- The defence protects **bona fide purchasers** in arm's-
  length transactions;
- "Adequate consideration" is **market value** or close to it;
- **Inadequate consideration** (gift, undervalue purchase)
  means no defence — the recipient takes subject to the
  criminal-property taint;
- The defence is **not available** to someone who knows the
  property is criminal — knowledge defeats the adequate-
  consideration defence.

## Penalty

Same as Articles 30 and 31: up to **14 years' imprisonment**
and unlimited fine on indictment.

## Authorised-disclosure defence

A person can avoid Article 32 liability by:

- Making an **authorised disclosure** (SAR) and obtaining
  consent before doing the prohibited act;
- Or having a **reasonable excuse** for not having made an
  authorised disclosure.

## Practical scenarios

- **Beneficiary distributions** — a beneficiary receiving a
  distribution from a Jersey trust whose assets are tainted
  may commit Article 32 if they know the source. Adequate
  consideration is rare for gifts / distributions.
- **Real-estate purchase** with tainted funds — Article 32 by
  the recipient of the funds (typically the seller's bank)
  unless adequate consideration is given. The transaction may
  also engage Article 30 by the buyer.
- **Employee receiving salary** funded from criminal proceeds
  — the salary is criminal property; the employee may be
  protected by **adequate consideration** (work done) if not
  aware.
- **Charity receiving donation** funded by criminal proceeds —
  the charity acquires criminal property; the adequate-
  consideration defence may apply if a real exchange of
  benefit, otherwise SAR / freezing of funds is needed.
