# Terrorism Law 2002 Article 19 — General duty of disclosure of information

Article 19 imposes a **general SAR duty** in respect of
terrorism financing — the CFT analogue of the POCL
Article 34A general SAR duty.

## The duty

A person commits an offence if the person:

1. **believes or suspects** that another person has
   committed an offence under
   [Articles 15, 16, or 16A](./terrorism-article-15.md);
2. **bases that belief or suspicion on information** that
   came to the person's attention in the **course of a
   trade, profession, business or employment**; and
3. **does not disclose** the information to the FIU as
   soon as is reasonably practicable.

## Defence

It is a defence to show the person had a **reasonable
excuse** for not disclosing.

## Relationship to Article 21

For **financial institutions and persons in regulated
business** (as defined in Schedule 2 to POCL 1999),
[Article 21](./terrorism-article-21.md) imposes a
**stricter "reasonable grounds for suspicion"** test —
catching negligent failures to spot suspicion that
Article 19 would not reach.
