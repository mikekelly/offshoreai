# MLO 2008 Article 20 — Offences

Article 20 creates **offences** for non-compliance with MLO
2008 obligations.

## Principal offences

The Order creates offences for:

- **Failure** to comply with CDD obligations (Articles 5-13);
- **Failure** to maintain required records (Article 17);
- **Failure** to designate MLRO (Article 16);
- **Failure** by MLRO to comply with reporting obligations
  (Article 16, alongside POCL Article 34A);
- **Failure** to conduct BWRA (Article 3);
- **Failure** to establish procedures (Article 4);
- **Failure** to train staff (Article 19);
- **Specific** other compliance breaches.

## Penalties

- **Fines** (substantial for material breaches);
- **JFSC enforcement** action (civil penalties, supervisory
  measures);
- **Criminal prosecution** in serious cases;
- **Imprisonment** for individual offenders in serious
  cases;
- **Disqualification** of individuals.

## Civil penalties (JFSC)

Most enforcement is through **JFSC civil penalties** rather
than criminal prosecution:

- **Calibrated** to severity;
- **Faster** than criminal;
- **Substantial** fines possible (six- and seven-figure);
- **Public statements** accompanying;
- **Mitigation** for cooperation, remediation.

## Criminal prosecution

Reserved for:

- **Deliberate** non-compliance;
- **Flagrant** breaches;
- **Repeat** offenders;
- **Specific** Attorney General determination.

## Defences

Specific defences:

- **Due diligence** — reasonable steps taken;
- **No knowledge** of the breach;
- **Acting on advice** in good faith;
- **Specific** statutory defences.

## Corporate vs individual liability

- **Firm** primary liability for compliance;
- **Senior managers** personal liability for specific
  breaches;
- **MLRO** specific personal accountability;
- **Compliance Officer** specific accountability;
- **Specific** Jersey case law on senior-manager
  responsibilities.
