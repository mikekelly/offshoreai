# SAFL 2014 Article 20 — Criminal Offences

Article 20 creates the **criminal offence** structure for
sanctions breaches.

## The principal offences

### Breach of asset-freezing (Article 10)

A person who **deals** with frozen funds / resources of a
designated person, **knowing or having reasonable cause to
suspect** the asset-freezing prohibition applies, commits an
offence.

### Making funds available (Article 11)

A person who makes funds / resources available to / for the
benefit of a designated person, **knowing or having reasonable
cause to suspect** the prohibition applies, commits an
offence.

### Sectoral prohibitions (Articles 12-14)

Breach of specific sectoral prohibitions, with similar
**knowledge / reasonable cause to suspect** mental element.

### Failure to comply with notices

- **Failure to provide information** required by a notice;
- **Failure to comply** with directions;
- **Obstruction** of investigations.

### False statements

- **False statements** in applications for licences;
- **False statements** in reports to authorities;
- **Concealment** of relevant facts.

## Mental element

The offences require **knowledge or reasonable cause to
suspect**:

- **Knowledge** — actual awareness of the designation;
- **Reasonable cause to suspect** — objective test; would a
  reasonable person in the defendant's position have suspected?

The **reasonable cause** standard means that **failing to
screen** properly can lead to liability — ignorance of
designations is not a defence if a competent screening
process would have identified the match.

## Penalties

On conviction:

- **Imprisonment** up to **7 years**;
- **Unlimited fines**;
- Both **individual** and **corporate** liability;
- **Senior managers** personally liable in some cases;
- **Confiscation** of proceeds.

## Defences

The defendant may show:

- **No knowledge** and **no reasonable cause to suspect**
  (high bar for regulated professionals);
- **Reliance** on official guidance reasonably interpreted;
- **Licence** authorising the transaction;
- **Specific exception** applies;
- **Duress** or **coercion** in some cases.

## Civil penalties

Beyond criminal prosecution, the JFSC may impose **civil
penalties** under Article 21 — typically faster and less
stigmatising than criminal proceedings but still substantial.

## Prosecutions in practice

Jersey sanctions prosecutions are **relatively rare**:

- **Most** breaches are addressed by **JFSC civil action**
  rather than criminal prosecution;
- **Criminal** prosecution reserved for **deliberate** /
  **flagrant** breaches;
- **Cross-jurisdictional** investigations common — Jersey
  cooperation with UK / EU / US authorities;
- **Asset recovery** under POCL Article 42-45 may parallel
  sanctions prosecution.
