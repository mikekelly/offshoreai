# CICL 2001 Article 3 — Letters of Request from Foreign Authority

Article 3 is the **gateway** for foreign requests for MLA in
Jersey — the formal mechanism by which a foreign authority
seeks Jersey cooperation in a criminal matter.

## The provision

A foreign authority may, through a **letter of request**, seek
Jersey assistance in:

- **Obtaining evidence** for use in foreign criminal
  proceedings;
- **Locating** witnesses, suspects, or assets in Jersey;
- **Serving documents** in Jersey;
- **Searching premises** for evidence;
- **Compelling production** of documents;
- **Freezing assets** pending foreign proceedings;
- **Enforcing** foreign restraint or confiscation orders;
- **Other** specified types of assistance.

## Receipt of letters of request

Letters of request are received by the **Attorney General**:

- **Central authority** for Jersey MLA;
- **Reviews** the request for compliance with Jersey law;
- **Coordinates** Jersey's response;
- **Engages** appropriate Jersey authorities (Royal Court,
  JFSC, States Police).

## Formal requirements

A valid letter of request must:

- **Identify** the requesting authority;
- **State** the offence being investigated / prosecuted;
- **Specify** the assistance sought;
- **Provide** factual context;
- **Confirm** that the conduct alleged would be an offence
  in Jersey if it occurred in Jersey (dual criminality);
- **Specify** any specific procedures the foreign authority
  needs (e.g., specific form of evidence taking);
- **Confirm** confidentiality requirements;
- **Be** properly **authenticated** through diplomatic /
  treaty channels typically.

## Refusal grounds

The Attorney General may refuse / decline assistance:

- **Dual criminality** not satisfied — the conduct is not
  criminal in Jersey;
- **Political offence** exception;
- **Discrimination** concerns (request appears to target
  persons on basis of race, religion, political opinion);
- **Sovereignty / public policy** concerns;
- **Inadequate** information provided;
- **Disproportionate** request (request for information far
  exceeding what's needed);
- **Conflict** with Jersey criminal proceedings;
- **Treaty** provisions allowing refusal.

## Standard MLA examples

### Bank records

- Foreign authority requests **bank records** for an account
  held in Jersey;
- Jersey AG reviews — typical request granted;
- **Production order** sought through Royal Court;
- Bank produces records to AG;
- AG transmits to foreign authority.

### Witness evidence

- Foreign authority requests **deposition** of a Jersey-
  resident witness;
- Jersey AG arranges examination;
- Witness gives evidence in Jersey under Article 5
  procedure;
- Evidence transmitted to foreign authority.

### Search

- Foreign authority seeks **search of Jersey premises**;
- Jersey AG arranges judicial warrant;
- States Police execute search;
- Seized materials evaluated and transmitted under MLA.

### Asset freezing

- Foreign authority seeks **freezing** of Jersey assets
  pending foreign criminal proceedings;
- Jersey AG seeks **restraint order** in Royal Court (linked
  to CICL Article 13);
- Order made on satisfactory evidence.

## Confidentiality

MLA requests are typically **confidential**:

- **Subject** of investigation may not know about the
  request;
- Disclosure could **prejudice** the foreign investigation;
- **Tipping-off** considerations apply if a Jersey regulated
  firm becomes aware;
- **Court orders** may specifically restrict disclosure.
