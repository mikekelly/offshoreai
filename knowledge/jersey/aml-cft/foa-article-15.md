# Forfeiture Law 2018 Article 15 — Forfeiture of tainted property: general procedure

Article 15 is the **substantive contested forfeiture
procedure**. It applies to:

- Property other than cash (real property, financial-
  account balances, shares, vehicles, valuables);
- Cash where a notice of objection has been served under
  the summary procedure;
- Any case where the Attorney General considers the
  general procedure appropriate.

## The hearing

The Royal Court determines, on the **balance of
probabilities**, whether the property is **tainted
property** within
[Article 2](./foa-article-2.md). If so, the Court may
order **forfeiture to the Crown**.

The Court must consider any third-party interests
asserted, and may make orders preserving the interests
of innocent owners.

## Civil-standard significance

The balance-of-probabilities standard — combined with the
absence of a conviction requirement — makes the Forfeiture
Law 2018 a **substantially lower-friction recovery tool**
than the POCL 1999 confiscation regime. It is the
principal Jersey civil-recovery statute and a key
delivery vehicle for the MoneyVal-era effectiveness
agenda.
