# POCL 1999 Article 18 — Effect of Restraint Order

Article 18 sets out the **legal effect** of a restraint order.

## Effect

The order **prohibits dealings** with realisable property:

- **Disposing** of property is prohibited;
- **Diminishing value** (e.g., wasting, gifting) is
  prohibited;
- **Removing from jurisdiction** typically prohibited
  (worldwide effect on Jersey defendant);
- **Specific** other prohibited acts as specified.

## Who is bound

The order **binds**:

- The **defendant**;
- **Persons holding** the defendant's property (banks,
  custodians, nominees);
- **Persons notified** of the order;
- **The world** after public notice in some cases.

## Carve-outs

The order typically permits:

### Reasonable living expenses

- **Living expenses** of defendant and dependants;
- **Specific** monthly allowance set by Court;
- **Subject to** review.

### Legal expenses

- **Defending criminal proceedings** (including the
  underlying offence);
- **Challenging the restraint order** itself;
- **Reasonable fees** for legal services;
- **Specific** rate limits in some cases.

### Ordinary trade / business expenses

- **Specific** business operating costs;
- **Subject to** Court approval;
- **Specific** monitoring.

### Specific other carve-outs

- **Tax payments** required by law;
- **Specific** family-court obligations;
- **Specific** emergency expenses.

## Bank obligations

Banks holding restrained assets must:

- **Freeze** the account;
- **Notify** other parties (Attorney General, defendant) of
  the freeze;
- **Process** carve-out payments per Court order;
- **Specific** internal procedures;
- **AML / CFT** considerations parallel.

## Breach

Breach of restraint order is:

- **Contempt of Court**;
- **Specific** criminal offences for some breaches;
- **Specific** civil consequences;
- **Personal liability** for officers / directors of
  corporate holders.
