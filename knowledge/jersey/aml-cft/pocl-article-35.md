# POCL 1999 Article 35 — Tipping Off

Article 35 creates the **tipping-off offence** — disclosing
the existence of a SAR or an investigation in a way that could
prejudice it.

## The provision

A person commits an offence if they:

- **Disclose** information likely to **prejudice** any
  investigation that **might be conducted** following an
  authorised disclosure;
- They **knew or suspected** that an authorised disclosure
  had been made or was likely; **or**
- They **disclose information** likely to prejudice any
  **ongoing investigation** they knew or suspected was under
  way.

## Limbs

The offence has **two main limbs**:

1. **SAR-aware tipping** — knowing of a SAR (or that one is
   likely), disclosing in a way that prejudices investigation;
2. **Investigation-aware tipping** — knowing of an ongoing
   investigation, disclosing in a way that prejudices it.

Both limbs require **disclosure** that is likely to
**prejudice**.

## What counts as "disclosure"

Disclosure can be:

- **Express** — "the police are looking into this";
- **Implied** — declining a transaction with hints as to why;
- **Conduct-based** — abruptly ending a relationship in
  suspicious circumstances;
- **Third-party** — telling a colleague who tells the suspect.

## Mental element

The person must:

- **Know or suspect** that a SAR has been / will be made or an
  investigation is under way;
- The disclosure must be **objectively likely** to prejudice;
- **Intent** to prejudice is not required — but specific
  knowledge of likely prejudice is needed.

## Penalty

- **Fine** (unlimited on indictment);
- **Imprisonment** up to **5 years** on indictment.

## Defences

- **Permitted disclosures** within the regulated sector
  (between firms in the same group, between firms for AML
  purposes per Article 36);
- **Permitted disclosures** to authorities / regulators;
- **Disclosure** that has been **specifically authorised** by
  the FIU;
- **No knowledge or suspicion** of likely prejudice;
- **Disclosure** to client's legal adviser for the purpose of
  obtaining legal advice (limited LPP exception);
- **Reasonable excuse**.

## Practical scenarios

- **Account closure** after SAR — declining further business
  without explaining is permitted; explaining the SAR is
  tipping off;
- **Group communications** — telling a group affiliate of the
  SAR is permitted under Article 36;
- **Cross-border** — telling a foreign trustee / co-counsel
  about Jersey SAR; permitted only under specific safeguards;
- **Client meetings** — when a client asks why transactions
  are delayed, MLRO must not reveal SAR existence;
- **Litigation** — disclosing SAR in litigation pleadings is
  tipping off unless court-ordered or party-permitted;
- **Insolvency** — disclosure to insolvency practitioner needs
  careful framing.

## Coordinating with foreign counsel

A common challenge:

- **Jersey trustee** has filed SAR on suspect funds;
- **Foreign counsel** is advising the client on a related
  matter and needs information;
- **Disclosure to foreign counsel** about specific SAR is
  potentially tipping off;
- **Generic AML concerns** can be discussed in the legal-
  advice context (privileged) but not specific SAR detail;
- **Information sharing** is best done through the **regulated-
  to-regulated channel** (Article 36).
