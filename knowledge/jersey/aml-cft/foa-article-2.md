# Forfeiture Law 2018 Article 2 — Meaning of "tainted property"

Article 2 is the **load-bearing definition** of the
civil-forfeiture regime. All forfeiture under the Law
turns on whether property is "tainted property" within
this Article.

## The definition

**Tainted property** is property:

1. **obtained through unlawful conduct** (proceeds of
   criminal activity, anywhere); or
2. **intended for use in unlawful conduct**; or
3. that **represents** property within (1) or (2) — i.e.
   it traces through transformations and substitutions.

"Unlawful conduct" is conduct that is unlawful under
Jersey criminal law, or that is unlawful under the
criminal law of another country and would be unlawful
under Jersey criminal law if it occurred in Jersey
(**dual-criminality test**).

## Why this matters

Unlike the POCL 1999 criminal confiscation regime —
which requires a **conviction** — the Forfeiture Law
2018 permits forfeiture on **civil-standard proof**
that the property is tainted. The Article 2 definition
is therefore the operative threshold for the entire
civil-recovery regime.
