# POCL 1999 Articles 13-16 — Variation, Reconsideration, Compensation

## Article 13 — Increase by reason of newly discovered assets

If after the original order, **newly discovered assets** of the
defendant emerge:

- **Prosecutor** can apply for increase in confiscation
  order;
- Court can increase the recoverable amount up to the
  benefit;
- **Specific** lookback for application;
- **Subject to** procedural fairness.

## Article 14 — Decrease

If the defendant's assets are **less than originally
calculated**:

- **Defendant** can apply for decrease;
- **Specific** evidence of unrealisable property;
- **Court** can reduce recoverable amount;
- **Subject to** good faith of defendant.

## Article 15 — Reconsideration

In limited cases, the **fundamental basis** of the order can
be reconsidered:

- **New evidence** showing benefit calculation was wrong;
- **Procedural** errors in original determination;
- **Specific** Court discretion;
- **Time limits** apply.

## Article 16 — Compensation to victims

Where appropriate:

- **Compensation orders** to victims;
- **Paid** before confiscation typically;
- **Specific** Royal Court procedures;
- **Coordinated** with other recovery mechanisms;
- **Specific** Jersey rules on victim compensation.

## Civil debts

Specific provisions for **civil debts** of the defendant:

- **Some** civil debts paid in priority to confiscation;
- **Subject to** specific rules;
- **Specific** Royal Court determination.
