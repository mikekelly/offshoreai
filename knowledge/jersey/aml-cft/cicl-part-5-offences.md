# CICL 2001 Part 5 — Specific Offences

Part 5 creates **specific offences** related to overseas crime
and cross-border cooperation.

## Article 31 — Foreign proceeds-of-crime offences

It is an offence in Jersey to:

- **Deal with** property knowing or suspecting it represents
  proceeds of **foreign criminal conduct**;
- **Conceal**, **transfer**, or **convert** such property;
- **Assist** another in dealing with such property;
- **Acquire** such property without adequate consideration.

This **parallels POCL Article 30-32** (Jersey domestic ML
offences) but specifically targets **foreign-crime** proceeds.

The dual-criminality test applies — the foreign conduct must
be criminal in Jersey if done in Jersey.

## Article 32 — Foreign bribery / corruption

Specific offences for **foreign-bribery** scenarios:

- **Paying bribes** to foreign officials;
- **Receiving bribes** in foreign-official-related capacity;
- **Facilitation payments** generally not exempted (unlike US
  FCPA);
- **Corporate liability** for failure to prevent foreign
  bribery in some configurations.

These offences supplement Jersey's general **Corruption
(Jersey) Law 2006** for cross-border bribery scenarios.

## Article 33 — Foreign tax evasion (facilitation)

It is an offence to **facilitate** foreign tax evasion:

- **Knowing** that the conduct will evade foreign tax;
- **Actively facilitating** the evasion;
- **Subject to** reasonable-prevention defence (had procedures
  to prevent);
- **Specific** corporate liability for failure to prevent.

This is broadly parallel to UK's **Criminal Finances Act 2017**
facilitation offences.

## Article 34 — Terrorist financing (cross-border)

Specific offences for **terrorist financing** with cross-
border element:

- **Funding terrorism** abroad;
- **Receiving** funds for terrorist purposes;
- **Coordinated** with UN counter-terrorism resolutions and
  UK CTF framework.

## Article 35 — Sanctions evasion

Specific offences relating to **sanctions evasion**:

- **Structuring transactions** to evade sanctions;
- **False statements** about beneficial ownership to evade
  sanctions;
- **Use of complex structures** specifically to defeat
  sanctions screening;
- **Coordination** with SAFL Article 20 offences.

## Article 36 — Trafficking-related offences

Specific offences for:

- **Human trafficking** with cross-border element;
- **People smuggling**;
- **Modern slavery** with cross-border element.

## Mental element

The offences typically require **knowledge or suspicion**:

- **Knowledge** — actual awareness of the criminal source;
- **Suspicion** — more than mere conjecture;
- **Reasonable cause to suspect** — objective test in some
  offences;
- **Constructive knowledge** through wilful blindness.

## Defences

- **Authorised disclosure** (parallel to POCL SAR procedure);
- **Reasonable cause** to believe transaction lawful;
- **Specific defences** for regulated firms with proper
  procedures;
- **Adequate consideration** for acquisitions.

## Penalties

- **Imprisonment** up to **14 years** for principal offences;
- **Unlimited fines**;
- **Confiscation** of proceeds;
- **Corporate** penalties for entities.
