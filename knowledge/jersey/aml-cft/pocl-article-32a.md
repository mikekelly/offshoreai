# POCL 1999 Article 32A — Defences Common to Articles 30-32

Article 32A consolidates the **statutory defences** available
to a person charged under Articles 30, 31, or 32.

## The provision

A person does **not** commit an offence under Articles 30-32
if:

- They make an **authorised disclosure** to a constable, the
  FIU, or (if they are in the regulated sector) to a
  nominated officer; **and**
- They have the **appropriate consent** to do the prohibited
  act; **or**
- They **intended** to make a disclosure but had a **reasonable
  excuse** for not doing so.

## Authorised disclosure

An authorised disclosure is:

- Made to the **FIU** (the Financial Intelligence Unit at
  States of Jersey Police); or
- Made to a **constable**; or
- Made internally to a **nominated officer** (MLRO) of the
  reporter's regulated business — who must then make the
  external disclosure to the FIU.

The disclosure must:

- Be **made as soon as practicable** after the suspicion
  arises;
- Identify the **relevant property** and the basis of
  suspicion;
- Be **in good faith**.

## Appropriate consent

After making an authorised disclosure, the person should
**request consent** to proceed with the prohibited act:

- The **FIU has 7 working days** to respond ("notice period");
- If the FIU **refuses** within the notice period, the person
  must wait a further **31 days** ("moratorium period") during
  which the prohibited act remains an offence;
- If the FIU **does not respond** within the notice period,
  consent is **deemed given**;
- If the FIU **consents**, the prohibited act may be done.

This is the **regulatory equivalent of seeking permission** —
the FIU effectively decides whether the suspect transaction
should proceed.

## Reasonable excuse

A person with a **reasonable excuse** for not disclosing is
not guilty. Reasonable excuse is **narrow**:

- Genuine inability to disclose (e.g., extreme illness);
- **Legal privilege** preventing disclosure;
- **Duress** or **coercion**.

Mere inconvenience or commercial preference is **not** a
reasonable excuse.

## Practical regime for Jersey MLROs

A Jersey TCB or bank MLRO confronting suspicion of ML:

1. **Receive internal disclosure** from a staff member;
2. **Assess** whether knowledge or suspicion is held (see
   Article 34 / `internal-disclosure-assessment.md`);
3. If suspicion is held, **file external SAR** with the FIU;
4. **Request consent** to any specific transaction the firm
   needs to do;
5. **Suspend** any prohibited acts pending FIU response;
6. **Document** the entire process in the SAR / MLRO log;
7. **Continue compliance with tipping-off rules** (Article 35)
   throughout.

## Continuing activity post-disclosure

After disclosure, the firm may need to:

- **Continue** routine administration of a client account
  (fees, statutory payments) — typically permitted under FIU
  consent;
- **Receive incoming funds** — generally permitted but
  documented;
- **Decline** outgoing transfers without consent;
- **Document** ongoing handling.

The MLRO must avoid both **proceeding without consent** (which
would convert continued administration into an offence) and
**tipping off** the client.
