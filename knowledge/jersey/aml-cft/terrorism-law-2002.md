# Terrorism (Jersey) Law 2002

The **Terrorism (Jersey) Law 2002** is Jersey's principal
counter-terrorism financing statute. It creates the substantive
**terrorism financing offences**, the terrorism-related
**asset-freezing regime**, and the **reporting obligations** for
suspected terrorist financing — the CFT (counter-financing-of-
terrorism) counterpart to the ML (money-laundering) framework
in the
[Proceeds of Crime (Jersey) Law 1999](./proceeds-of-crime-law-1999.md).

## Why this Law sits separately from the POCL 1999

The POCL 1999 addresses **proceeds-of-crime** money-laundering
— funds derived from prior criminal activity. The Terrorism Law
2002 addresses **terrorism financing** — funds intended to be
used for terrorism, regardless of source. The two regimes have
different mental models:

- POCL is **retrospective** (proceeds of a crime already
  committed).
- Terrorism Law is **prospective** (funds destined for terrorism
  that has not yet occurred).

The reporting and AML/CFT operational obligations practitioners
face are **largely harmonised** in the
[Money Laundering Order 2008](./money-laundering-order-2008.md)
and the
[JFSC AML/CFT Handbook](./aml-cft-handbook.md) — staff training,
SAR processes, and screening cover both regimes — but the
substantive offences and asset-freezing powers come from the
separate Terrorism Law 2002.

## What the Law does

The Law provides for:

1. **Substantive terrorism-financing offences** — providing
   funds, receiving funds, fundraising, money-laundering for
   terrorism purposes.
2. **Asset-freezing of terrorism-related funds** with criminal
   sanctions for breaching freezing measures.
3. **Reporting obligations** for persons in regulated business
   to report suspected terrorism financing — interlocking with
   the SAR regime under [POCL Article 34](./pocl-article-34.md)
   and with the JFSC AML/CFT Handbook
   [SAR section](./amlh-sars.md).
4. **Information disclosure** and law-enforcement powers in
   relation to suspected terrorism financing.
5. **Designation and listing** of terrorism-related persons —
   running alongside the
   [Sanctions and Asset-Freezing Law 2019](./safl-articles-index.md)
   designations regime for the broader sanctions framework.

## Practitioner interaction

For a JFSC-supervised business, the operational reality is
that:

- **CFT screening** runs alongside sanctions and PEP
  screening;
- **Terrorism-related SARs** flow through the same internal
  pipeline as ML SARs but with heightened urgency;
- **Asset-freezing notices** under the Terrorism Law trigger
  the same freezing and reporting workflow as sanctions
  designations under the
  [SAFL 2019](./safl-articles-index.md);
- **Defence-against-money-laundering (DAML) consent** under
  POCL has an analogue under the Terrorism Law for terrorism-
  related transactions.

The
[JFSC AML/CFT Handbook section on SARs](./amlh-sars.md) treats
the two regimes in parallel.
