# CICL 2001 Article 5 — Evidence in Jersey for Foreign Proceedings

Article 5 provides the **procedural framework** for obtaining
**evidence in Jersey** to use in **foreign criminal
proceedings**.

## The provision

The Attorney General, having received a letter of request,
may apply to the **Royal Court** for an order:

- **Compelling witnesses** to give evidence;
- **Compelling production** of documents;
- **Authorising** the taking of evidence on commission;
- **Specifying** procedures for the evidence-taking.

## Procedure

### Application

- **AG** applies to the Royal Court;
- **Affidavit** evidence summarising the letter of request;
- **Specifies** witnesses, documents, premises;
- **Identifies** any specific procedural requirements from
  the foreign authority.

### Hearing

- The Royal Court considers the application;
- May **hear** affected parties (e.g., the witness can
  contest);
- **Makes orders** if satisfied of the grounds;
- **Subject** to any limits prescribed in the order.

### Evidence taking

- Conducted under **Jersey procedural rules** by default;
- **Specific foreign procedures** can be adopted if compatible
  with Jersey public policy;
- **Witness sworn** under Jersey procedure;
- **Counsel** for the foreign authority may attend;
- **Witness** may be represented;
- **Record** of evidence made.

### Transmission

- **Record** of evidence transmitted to the AG;
- **AG** transmits to the foreign authority;
- **Confidentiality** preserved as required.

## Witness rights

A Jersey witness has rights:

- **Privilege against self-incrimination** under Jersey law;
- **Legal professional privilege** preserved;
- **Public-interest** immunity available;
- **Right** to legal representation;
- **Reasonable** notice and accommodation;
- **No double jeopardy** for cooperating in foreign
  proceedings.

## Documents and records

Production orders may compel:

- **Bank records**;
- **Company records**;
- **Trust records** (subject to specific safeguards);
- **Legal records** (subject to LPP);
- **Personal records**;
- **Electronic records** including emails, messages, IT logs.

## Specific Jersey considerations

### Trust confidentiality

For requests touching **trust matters**:

- **Trustee** may have **duties of confidentiality** to
  beneficiaries;
- **Court** considers balance between MLA cooperation and
  trust confidentiality;
- **Targeted** production typically ordered (specific
  documents, not blanket disclosure);
- **Notification** to beneficiaries may or may not be ordered
  depending on circumstances.

### Bank secrecy (historical context)

- Jersey **no longer** has historic bank secrecy;
- **AML / CFT** and transparency regime applies;
- **Bank records** routinely produced under proper MLA
  process;
- **No** automatic refusal on confidentiality grounds.

### Sanctions / freezing intersection

- MLA requests may **accompany** sanctions / freezing
  measures;
- **POCL Article 36** information sharing may also apply;
- **Coordinated** approach typical for major investigations.
