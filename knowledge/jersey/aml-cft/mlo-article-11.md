# MLO 2008 Article 11 — Simplified CDD

Article 11 permits **simplified due diligence (SDD)** for
certain **lower-risk** customer categories.

## When SDD is permitted

SDD may be applied where:

- The customer falls into a **prescribed lower-risk category**;
- The supervised business **risk-rates** the relationship as
  low overall (BWRA-driven);
- **No suspicion** of ML / TF arises;
- **No higher-risk** indicators are present (e.g., PEP
  exposure, high-risk jurisdiction nexus).

## Eligible categories

Common SDD-eligible categories:

### Regulated financial institutions

- **JFSC-regulated** firms (TCB, FSB, banks, etc.) as the
  **customer**;
- **Equivalent-regulated** firms in **equivalent
  jurisdictions** (FATF-compliant, OECD members);
- The reasoning: the regulated firm is itself subject to AML
  obligations and supervisory oversight.

### Listed companies

- **Companies listed** on **recognised exchanges** (LSE Main
  Market, NYSE, NASDAQ, Euronext, etc.);
- Listed companies have **disclosure obligations** that
  function as quasi-CDD;
- **Material shareholders** may need separate consideration
  for higher-risk exchanges.

### Public-sector customers

- **Government bodies** in established democracies;
- **State-owned enterprises** of equivalent jurisdictions
  (with caution — state-owned can be PEP-adjacent).

### Pension funds

- **UK / EU / equivalent pension** funds with regulatory
  supervision;
- **Member-led occupational** schemes.

### Other lower-risk categories

- **Charities** of recognised standing and regulatory
  registration;
- **Specific product types** with limited ML risk (e.g., very
  low-value pre-paid products).

## What SDD reduces

SDD permits:

- **Reduced** identification and verification (e.g.,
  reliance on regulator's published register rather than
  documentary ID for each director);
- **Reduced** beneficial-ownership analysis (e.g., not
  drilling through listed-company shareholders);
- **Reduced** source-of-funds verification (where source is
  evidently regulated);
- **Lighter** ongoing monitoring frequency;
- **Standard** rather than enhanced screening.

SDD does **not** eliminate:

- The **base CDD obligation** (identification still required);
- **Ongoing monitoring** for unusual activity;
- **Sanctions screening**;
- **SAR obligation** if suspicion arises.

## When SDD must be abandoned

If during the relationship:

- **Higher-risk indicators** emerge (e.g., adverse media,
  unusual transactions);
- **Suspicion** of ML / TF arises;
- **The customer's category** changes;
- **The supervised business's BWRA** changes;

then SDD must be **upgraded to standard or EDD** as
appropriate.

## JFSC scrutiny

The JFSC examines **SDD application** carefully:

- **Documentation** of the SDD basis is essential;
- **Periodic review** that SDD is still appropriate;
- **Senior management** approval typically required for SDD
  application;
- **Audit trail** of the rationale.
