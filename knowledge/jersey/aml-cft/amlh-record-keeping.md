# AML/CFT Handbook — Record-Keeping

The Handbook operationalises the
[MLO 2008 Article 17](./mlo-article-17.md) record-keeping
obligation — the discipline of retaining CDD records,
transaction records, internal SARs, and policy documents for
prescribed periods in a form available to the JFSC and law
enforcement.

## What records must be kept

- **CDD records** — identification documents, verification
  evidence, beneficial-ownership analysis, risk rating,
  source-of-funds and (for EDD) source-of-wealth evidence,
  PEP screening results, sanctions screening results.
- **Transaction records** — for transactions undertaken in
  the course of the business relationship.
- **Internal SAR records** — every internal SAR raised, with
  the MLRO's investigation notes and disposition rationale.
- **External SAR records** — copies of SARs filed with the
  FIU.
- **Policy and training records** — the BWRA, AML/CFT
  policies and procedures, training attendance, board reports.
- **MLRO / MLCO appointment records** and notifications to
  the JFSC.

## For how long

The standard Handbook expectation (mirroring the MLO 2008):

- **At least five years** from the **end of the business
  relationship** for CDD records;
- **At least five years** from the **date of the transaction**
  for transaction records;
- **At least five years** for SAR-related records;
- **Longer where required** by law-enforcement request or
  pending investigation.

A regulated business should be able to **retrieve the full
file on demand** for the JFSC or the FIU. Off-site / cloud
storage is acceptable provided retrieval is timely and the
arrangement satisfies the outsourcing controls.

## Form

The Handbook permits **electronic record-keeping** provided
records are **retrievable, legible, and tamper-evident**.
Original-document retention is not generally required for
records held electronically with appropriate controls.
