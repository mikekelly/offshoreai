# MLO 2008 Article 12 — Enhanced CDD (PEPs and High-Risk)

Article 12 requires **enhanced due diligence (EDD)** for
higher-risk customers and transactions.

## When EDD is required

EDD **must** be applied when:

- The customer is a **politically exposed person (PEP)** or a
  family member or close associate of one;
- The customer has a nexus to a **high-risk jurisdiction**
  (FATF-listed, EU-blacklisted, sanctioned country);
- The customer or transaction is **otherwise higher-risk** per
  the BWRA;
- **Complex or unusually large** transactions are involved;
- **Unusual patterns** of activity with no apparent economic or
  legal purpose;
- **Non-face-to-face** customers in higher-risk circumstances;
- **Anonymous** or pseudonymous services are used (where
  permitted at all).

## PEPs

A **PEP** is a natural person entrusted with **prominent
public functions**:

- **Heads of state**, heads of government, senior politicians;
- **Senior government officials**;
- **Senior judicial / military officials**;
- **Senior executives** of state-owned enterprises;
- **Senior political party** officials.

PEP status extends to:

- **Family members** (parents, spouse, siblings, children, and
  their spouses);
- **Close associates** (joint beneficial owners of legal
  entities, close personal relationships).

PEPs are **not presumed corrupt** — they are subject to
heightened scrutiny because of the **risk** of corruption /
abuse of office.

### PEP categories

- **Foreign PEPs** — highest sensitivity;
- **Domestic PEPs** (Jersey / UK) — significant sensitivity
  though lower than foreign;
- **International organisation PEPs** — significant
  sensitivity;
- **Former PEPs** — heightened sensitivity for typically 18-24
  months after leaving office, longer if continuing
  influence.

## High-risk jurisdictions

The Order references:

- **FATF "high-risk jurisdictions subject to a call for
  action"** (the FATF "black list");
- **FATF "jurisdictions under increased monitoring"** (the
  "grey list");
- **EU AML high-risk third-country list**;
- **Sanctions** jurisdictions (UN, UK, US, EU);
- **Jersey's own** assessment in the BWRA.

## EDD content

EDD requires:

- **Enhanced identification** — more documentary evidence,
  independent confirmation;
- **Source of wealth** — comprehensive understanding of how
  the customer accumulated their wealth (not just current
  source of funds);
- **Source of funds** — verification of source for specific
  transactions;
- **Senior management approval** for the relationship;
- **Enhanced ongoing monitoring** — more frequent review,
  lower thresholds for flag, dedicated analyst attention;
- **Adverse media** monitoring more frequently;
- **Periodic refresh** more frequently (e.g., annually for
  PEPs).

## Source of wealth — the harder discipline

Source of wealth is **comprehensive** — not just the funds
involved in any single transaction:

- **Inheritance** — supported by death certificates, wills,
  probate, valuation;
- **Employment** — supported by employment history, contracts,
  P60s / tax returns;
- **Business proceeds** — supported by company accounts, share
  sale documents, dividend history;
- **Investment returns** — supported by portfolio statements,
  fund prospectuses;
- **Gifts** — supported by donor identification and source of
  funds of donor;
- **Other** — supported by appropriate documentation.

## Decline and exit

If EDD cannot be satisfied:

- The relationship / transaction **must not be entered into**;
- Or **must be terminated**;
- **SAR consideration** is virtually automatic — failed EDD
  often indicates concerns.
