# MLO 2008 Part 6 — Training and Miscellaneous

Part 6 contains training, offences, and citation provisions.

## Article 19 — Training

The supervised business must:

- **Train** all relevant staff in AML / CFT;
- **Refresh** training periodically (typically annually);
- **Tailor** training to roles (front-office, back-office,
  MLRO, board);
- **Test** comprehension where appropriate;
- **Record** training completion.

### Training content

Effective AML training covers:

- **Substantive offences** (POCL Articles 30-32);
- **Failure to disclose** (POCL Articles 34, 34A);
- **Tipping off** (POCL Article 35);
- **CDD obligations** (MLO Articles 5-12);
- **Internal disclosure** procedures;
- **Sanctions** awareness;
- **PEPs** and high-risk indicators;
- **Real-world examples** of typologies relevant to the firm's
  business;
- **Recent JFSC enforcement** cases;
- **Recent FATF / Moneyval** themes.

### Training delivery

- **Induction** training for new starters before client-facing
  work;
- **Annual refresher** for all relevant staff;
- **Ad hoc** for material legal / regulatory changes;
- **Targeted** for high-risk roles or new product launches;
- **Formats** — online modules, classroom sessions, case-
  study workshops.

### Records

- **Attendance** logs;
- **Test results**;
- **Refresher** dates;
- **Curriculum** retained for audit.

## Article 20 — Offences

The Order creates offences for:

- **Failure** to comply with CDD obligations;
- **Failure** to maintain required records;
- **Failure** to designate MLRO;
- **Failure** by MLRO to comply with reporting obligations;
- **Other** specific compliance breaches.

Penalties include:

- **Fines** (substantial for material breaches);
- **JFSC enforcement** action (civil penalties, supervisory
  measures);
- **Criminal prosecution** in serious cases;
- **Disqualification** of individuals.

## Article 21 — Citation

The Order is cited as the **"Money Laundering (Jersey) Order
2008"**.

It came into force on **1 January 2008** and has been amended
several times to align with FATF and EU AML directives.

## Practical training pitfalls

Common training failings the JFSC criticises:

- **Generic** training not tailored to the firm's risk;
- **Stale** content not updated;
- **Tick-box** completion without comprehension testing;
- **Inadequate coverage** of MLRO and senior management;
- **Poor** training records.
