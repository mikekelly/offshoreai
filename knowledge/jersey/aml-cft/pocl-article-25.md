# POCL 1999 Article 25 — Production Orders

Article 25 enables the Royal Court to order **production of
documents** for financial-crime investigations.

## Grounds

The Court may order production where:

- There are **reasonable grounds** to suspect a person has
  benefited from criminal conduct **or** has committed an
  offence;
- The material is **likely to be of substantial value** to
  the investigation;
- It is **in the public interest** to make the order;
- The material **cannot be obtained** through routine means.

## Procedure

1. **Application** by Attorney General / investigator;
2. **Affidavit** evidence of grounds;
3. **Court hearing** (often ex parte to preserve
   investigation);
4. **Order** made specifying documents and timeframe;
5. **Service** on the person required to produce.

## Effect

The person served must:

- **Produce** the specified documents within the timeframe;
- **Provide** information about specified matters;
- **Subject to** privilege and other defences;
- **Failure** is contempt of Court.

## Who is typically served

- **Banks** for account records;
- **TCB firms** for client / trust / company records;
- **Law firms** for client records (subject to LPP);
- **Accountants** for work papers;
- **Other professional service providers**.

## Privilege

**Legal professional privilege** preserved:

- **Privileged documents** need not be produced;
- **Existence** of privileged material may need to be
  acknowledged;
- **Crime-fraud exception** for documents in furtherance of
  crime.

## Confidentiality

Production orders typically include:

- **Confidentiality** obligation on the served person;
- **No tipping off** the subject of investigation;
- **Specific** carve-outs for legal advice.

## Service provider response

For a Jersey TCB served with a production order:

- **Comply within the timeframe**;
- **Engage legal advice** for scope / privilege issues;
- **Document** the production;
- **Maintain confidentiality** as required;
- **Apply to vary** if unduly burdensome.
