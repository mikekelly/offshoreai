# SAFL 2014 Article 15 — Licences

Article 15 provides for **licences** — permissions to engage
in transactions that would otherwise be prohibited under the
SAFL.

## The provision

The Minister (or delegated authority) may grant a **licence**
authorising:

- **Specific** transactions with designated persons;
- **Specific** dealings with frozen assets;
- **Specific** payments for permitted purposes;
- **Specific** services for permitted purposes.

Licences are typically granted by the **JFSC** or **Treasury**
under Ministerial delegation.

## Licence categories

### Humanitarian

- **Basic needs** — food, rent, medical care for designated
  persons and dependants;
- **Specific support** for essential humanitarian purposes;
- **Charitable** activity in sanctioned regions.

### Legal fees

- **Legal representation** of designated persons (including
  in proceedings challenging the designation);
- **Reasonable fees** for legal services;
- **Specific costs** of compliance with sanctions.

### Basic transactions

- **Specific payments** for essential utilities, food,
  housing;
- **Maintenance** of frozen property (taxes, insurance);
- **Specific** salary payments to designated persons (in
  some cases);
- **Specific** family-court orders.

### Specific transactional

- **Pre-existing contracts** — performance of contracts
  entered into before designation;
- **Specific** asset disposals;
- **Specific** restructurings;
- **Bankruptcy / insolvency** matters involving designated
  persons.

### Sectoral

- **Specific exemptions** from sectoral restrictions;
- **Permitted** trade in humanitarian goods;
- **Permitted** services for sectoral entities.

## Application process

A licence application:

- **Submitted** to the JFSC (or Minister directly for major
  cases);
- **Specifies** the proposed transaction in detail;
- **Sets out** the basis for licence (which category);
- **Demonstrates** that no broader benefit flows to the
  designated person beyond the permitted purpose;
- **Includes** all relevant documentation;
- **Pays** any prescribed fee.

## Timing

- **Routine** licences: weeks to months;
- **Complex** licences: months;
- **Coordinated** with UK / EU equivalent licensing in
  cross-border cases;
- **Emergency** licences possible for urgent humanitarian
  cases.

## Conditions

Licences typically carry **conditions**:

- **Specific transactions** authorised, not blanket
  permission;
- **Time limit** on the licence;
- **Reporting** of transactions under the licence;
- **Caps** on amounts;
- **Specific destinations** of funds;
- **Compliance** monitoring.

## Coordination

Cross-border transactions may require:

- **Jersey** licence under SAFL;
- **UK OFSI** licence under UK sanctions;
- **EU** licence under EU sanctions (where applicable);
- **US OFAC** licence for USD transactions;
- **Joint** processing in some cases.

## Practical advice

For applicants:

- **Apply early** — licence processing takes time;
- **Be comprehensive** — include all relevant facts;
- **Distinguish** what's permitted from what's not;
- **Plan** the transaction with licence conditions in mind;
- **Document** post-licence compliance with conditions;
- **Coordinate** with cross-border licensing.
