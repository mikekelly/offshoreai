# MLO 2008 Part 4 — Reporting

Part 4 sets out the **internal disclosure** (employee → MLRO)
and **external SAR** (MLRO → FIU) regimes that operationalise
POCL Articles 34-34A.

## Article 14 — Internal disclosures

A supervised business must require its **staff** to make an
**internal disclosure** to the MLRO when they:

- **Know or suspect**;
- Have **reasonable grounds for knowing or suspecting**;
- That another person is engaged in money laundering or
  terrorist financing.

### Reporting channel

- **Confidential channel** to MLRO;
- **Protected** from retaliation;
- **Documented** in the firm's procedures;
- **Trained** to staff during onboarding and refreshers.

### Threshold

The threshold is **subjective suspicion or objective reasonable
grounds**:

- Staff do not need to be **certain**;
- Staff do not need to **investigate** before reporting (that
  is the MLRO's role);
- Staff should **err on the side of reporting** when in doubt.

### Confidentiality

Internal disclosures are **highly confidential**:

- Limited to **MLRO** and authorised deputies;
- Not disclosed to **subject** of the disclosure;
- Not discussed in **routine forums** (board meetings, team
  meetings);
- **Tipping-off** rules apply.

## Article 15 — External SARs

The MLRO must make an **external SAR** to the FIU when:

- Internal disclosure has been received;
- The MLRO has **assessed** the matter;
- The MLRO **knows, suspects, or has reasonable grounds for
  suspecting** ML / TF;
- **As soon as practicable** after the MLRO's determination.

### SAR content

A SAR should contain:

- **Subject details** — name, identifiers, address;
- **Reporter** details — firm, MLRO, contact;
- **Nature** of the suspicion;
- **Activity** giving rise to the suspicion;
- **Source** of the information;
- **Documents** referenced;
- **Consent request** if needed for a specific transaction;
- **Other relevant context**.

### Format

The FIU prescribes **electronic SAR formats** through the
ELMER system (or equivalent). Manual submissions may be
accepted in exceptional cases.

### Consent procedure

Where the SAR relates to a transaction requiring consent:

- **Specify** the transaction;
- **Request consent** to proceed;
- **Provide** the FIU 7 working days (notice period);
- **Wait** for response or deemed consent.

## Documentation

The MLRO must maintain:

- **Internal disclosure log** — all received internal
  disclosures;
- **Decision log** — assessment and disposition of each;
- **SAR log** — external SARs filed;
- **Consent log** — consent requests and responses;
- **Retention** per Article 17 (minimum 5 years, often
  longer in practice).
