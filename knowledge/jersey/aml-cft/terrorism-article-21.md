# Terrorism Law 2002 Article 21 — Failure to disclose: financial institutions

Article 21 is the **financial-institution-specific
failure-to-disclose offence** — the equivalent of
[POCL Article 34A](./pocl-article-34a.md) for the
terrorism-financing context.

## The offence

A person commits an offence if:

1. the person knows, suspects, **or has reasonable
   grounds for knowing or suspecting** that another
   person has committed an offence under
   [Articles 15, 16, or 16A](./terrorism-article-15.md);
2. the information came to the person's attention in the
   course of a **regulated business** (Schedule 2 to POCL
   1999); and
3. the person does not disclose to the FIU as soon as is
   reasonably practicable.

## The "reasonable grounds" enhancement

The objective limb — "**reasonable grounds for knowing
or suspecting**" — is the critical addition. Whereas
Article 19 catches actual subjective suspicion, Article
21 catches **negligent failure** to identify the
suspicious facts in front of the regulated business.

This is the practical compliance trigger that drives the
JFSC AML/CFT Handbook's CDD, monitoring, and SAR
disciplines for CFT.

## Defence

Reasonable excuse, including legal professional
privilege carve-out.
