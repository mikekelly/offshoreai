# Terrorism Law 2002 Article 35 — Tipping off and interference with material

Article 35 is the **CFT tipping-off offence** — the
counterpart to [POCL Article 35A](./pocl-article-35a.md).

## The offence

A person commits an offence if the person:

1. **knows or has reasonable cause to suspect** that an
   investigation under this Law is taking place or is
   about to take place; and
2. **discloses** to another person anything that is
   likely to **prejudice** the investigation; or
3. **falsifies, conceals, destroys, or disposes of**
   material that is likely to be relevant to the
   investigation.

## Defence

It is a defence to show the defendant did not know or
suspect that the disclosure was likely to prejudice the
investigation, or had a reasonable excuse.

## Penalty

Liable on indictment to imprisonment for a term not
exceeding **5 years** and / or a fine.

## Practitioner consequence

For an MLRO or compliance officer who has filed a CFT
SAR, the Article 35 offence is the principal **statutory
constraint** on subsequent client communication —
mirroring the operational discipline already applied to
POCL Article 35A tipping-off.
