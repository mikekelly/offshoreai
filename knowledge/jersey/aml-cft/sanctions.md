# Sanctions — Jersey

Jersey implements **financial sanctions** under the **Sanctions
and Asset-Freezing (Jersey) Law 2014**, with operational
co-ordination with UK and UN sanctions regimes.

## Sources of Jersey sanctions

Jersey sanctions law incorporates:

- **UN sanctions** — Jersey gives effect to UN Security Council
  resolutions through Jersey legislation and Orders;
- **UK sanctions** — Jersey typically aligns with UK sanctions
  regimes (the **Constitutional relationship** with the UK
  means Jersey's foreign-policy alignment is generally with the
  UK);
- **Bespoke Jersey designations** — possible but rare;
- **Pre-2014** sanctions Orders that continue in force.

## What the regime requires

Jersey businesses must:

- **Screen** customers, beneficial owners, and counterparties
  against current sanctions lists;
- **Freeze** assets of designated persons;
- **Refuse** to deal with designated persons;
- **Report** sanctions matches and freezes to the JFSC and
  competent authorities;
- **Apply for licences** where dealings with designated persons
  are necessary (humanitarian, legal-fee, basic-needs
  exceptions).

## Penalties

Sanctions breaches attract serious consequences:

- **Criminal offences** with imprisonment of up to several years;
- **Civil financial penalties**;
- **Asset confiscation**;
- **Regulatory action** against licensed entities.

## OFSI / OFAC equivalence

In practice, most Jersey businesses also need to comply with:

- **UK OFSI** sanctions (often the source of Jersey-recognised
  measures);
- **EU sanctions** (where the customer or counterparty has EU
  links);
- **US OFAC** sanctions (extraterritorial application to USD
  transactions and US-person involvement);
- **Other national** regimes as relevant to the business.

Jersey businesses with international clients typically run
**integrated sanctions screening** programmes covering multiple
regimes.
