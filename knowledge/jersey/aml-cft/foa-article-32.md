# Forfeiture Law 2018 Article 32 — Offence of tipping off or interference

Article 32 is the **civil-forfeiture-specific tipping-off
offence**, paralleling
[POCL Article 35A](./pocl-article-35a.md) (ML) and
[Terrorism Law Article 35](./terrorism-article-35.md)
(CFT).

## The offence

A person commits an offence if the person:

1. **knows or has reasonable cause to suspect** that a
   civil forfeiture investigation is taking place or is
   about to take place; and
2. **discloses** to another person anything likely to
   prejudice the investigation; or
3. **falsifies, conceals, destroys, or disposes of**
   material likely to be relevant.

## Practitioner consequence

Receipt of an
[Article 19 production order](./foa-article-19.md), an
[Article 22 customer information order](./foa-article-22.md),
or an
[Article 23 account monitoring order](./foa-article-23.md)
triggers the same Article 32 tipping-off discipline that
applies to POCL SARs and Terrorism Law SARs — the
business must respond without alerting the customer.

## Defence

The defendant did not know or suspect that the disclosure
was likely to prejudice the investigation; or had a
reasonable excuse.
