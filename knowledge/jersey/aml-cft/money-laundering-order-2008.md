# Money Laundering (Jersey) Order 2008

The **Money Laundering (Jersey) Order 2008** is the **operative
compliance regulation** of Jersey's AML / CFT regime. It sets out
**what supervised businesses must actually do** in practice.

## What the Order covers

The Order imposes obligations on **supervised business** in five
main areas:

1. **Customer Due Diligence (CDD)** — identifying and verifying
   customers, beneficial owners, source of funds and wealth;
2. **Record-keeping** — preserving CDD documentation and
   transaction records for prescribed periods;
3. **Internal controls** — designating a Money Laundering
   Reporting Officer (MLRO), establishing policies and
   procedures, monitoring transactions;
4. **Reporting** — internal escalation to MLRO; external
   Suspicious Activity Reports (SARs) to the FIU;
5. **Training** — initial and ongoing training of staff in AML
   awareness and obligations.

## CDD — the heart of the Order

CDD obligations include:

- **Identification** of the customer (and any agent acting on
  the customer's behalf);
- **Verification** of identity using reliable independent sources
  (passport, certified copies, etc.);
- **Identification of beneficial owners** for entity customers;
- **Source of funds** for transactions;
- **Source of wealth** for higher-risk customers;
- **Ongoing monitoring** through the relationship;
- **Enhanced due diligence (EDD)** for **politically exposed
  persons (PEPs)**, **high-risk jurisdictions**, **complex
  structures**, and other risk categories.

For **simplified due diligence (SDD)**, prescribed lower-risk
categories qualify for a lighter regime — e.g. listed companies,
regulated financial institutions in equivalent jurisdictions.

## Risk-based approach

The Order requires a **risk-based approach**:

- **Risk assessment** of the business and individual
  relationships;
- **Calibrated CDD** to match the risk;
- **Periodic review** of risk profile;
- **Documentation** of risk decisions.

This contrasts with prescriptive "tick-box" approaches and
allows resources to be focused on **higher-risk** relationships.

## The MLRO

The Money Laundering Reporting Officer:

- Receives **internal disclosures** from staff suspecting money
  laundering;
- Assesses whether to make an **external SAR** to the FIU;
- Maintains the **AML compliance programme**;
- Reports to the **board and senior management**;
- Liaises with the **JFSC** and **FIU**.

The MLRO must be **fit and proper**, have sufficient seniority,
and be a Jersey-resident senior employee or officer.
