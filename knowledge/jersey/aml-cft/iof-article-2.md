# Investigation of Fraud Law 1991 Article 2 — Attorney General's powers of investigation

Article 2 is the **load-bearing substantive provision**
of the Law. It is the source of the Attorney General's
direct information-gathering powers in fraud
investigations and is the AG's analogue to the UK Serious
Fraud Office's "section 2" powers under the Criminal
Justice Act 1987.

## When the power arises

The AG's powers are exercisable where there is a
**suspected offence involving serious or complex fraud**,
**wherever committed**, and where there is **good reason
to investigate**.

## The powers

The AG (or any other person authorised by the AG by
writing) may **by notice in writing** require:

1. **A person under investigation** or **any other person
   reasonably believed to have relevant information** to:
   - **answer questions** on a specified matter;
   - **furnish information** on a specified matter; or
   - **produce specified documents** appearing to relate
     to the investigation.
2. Documents and information produced may be **retained,
   copied, and used** for the purposes of the
   investigation.

## Privilege and self-incrimination

- **Legal professional privilege** is broadly preserved
  — privileged material is generally not required to be
  produced.
- **Banker / professional-secrecy** obligations are
  overridden by a notice under this Law (the override is
  one of the principal practical functions of the
  statute).
- A statement made in answer to a question put under the
  power is **not generally admissible against the maker**
  in subsequent criminal proceedings, save in narrow
  circumstances (perjury, prosecution under this Law).

## Practitioner consequence

For a Jersey-supervised business, receipt of an Article 2
notice triggers:

- A **legal-privilege review** of responsive documents;
- A **tipping-off discipline** under
  [POCL Article 35](./pocl-article-35.md) — the business
  must not alert the customer that an investigation is
  underway;
- A **prompt response** typically within the timetable
  specified by the AG (often tight);
- **Record-keeping** of the notice, the response, and the
  privilege analysis.

## Offences

It is an offence to **fail to comply** with a notice
under Article 2 without reasonable excuse, or to
knowingly **make a false statement** in response.
