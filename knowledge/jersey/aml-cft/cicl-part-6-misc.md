# CICL 2001 Part 6 — Miscellaneous

Part 6 consolidates administrative matters, treaty
arrangements, and citation.

## Articles 37-39 — Refusal grounds (consolidated)

The Attorney General may **refuse** MLA / extradition / asset
recovery cooperation:

### Political offence

- Offences of a **political character** (treason, political
  conspiracy);
- **Not** ordinary crimes with political context (murder of
  political figure is murder, not political offence);
- **Specific exception** for serious crimes (terrorism, war
  crimes, genocide) — even if politically motivated.

### Discrimination

- Request appears to **target** the person on basis of race,
  religion, nationality, political opinion;
- The **purpose** is to prejudice the person on such grounds.

### Public policy / sovereignty

- **Public policy** of Jersey;
- **Fundamental** Jersey sovereignty;
- **Constitutional** principles.

### Procedural fairness

- **Foreign proceedings** lacking basic fairness (no notice,
  no representation, secret tribunals);
- **Article 6 ECHR** equivalent standards;
- **Specific** safeguards in capital cases.

### Double jeopardy

- Person previously **tried** for same offence;
- **Specific** double-jeopardy exceptions (new evidence, etc.).

### Statute of limitations

- **Jersey statute of limitations** for the equivalent offence
  has expired;
- **Foreign statute of limitations** has expired.

## Articles 40-44 — Treaty arrangements

Jersey's MLA / cooperation operates through:

### Bilateral treaties

Jersey has direct treaty relationships with:

- **United States** (MLAT);
- **Various** other states with specific arrangements;
- **Most** UK treaties extend to Jersey through Orders in
  Council.

### Multilateral conventions

Jersey participates in:

- **UN Convention against Transnational Organized Crime
  (UNTOC)** — extended through UK;
- **UN Convention against Corruption (UNCAC)** — extended
  through UK;
- **OECD Anti-Bribery Convention** — extended;
- **Council of Europe** conventions on cybercrime,
  trafficking, etc. — extended.

### Bilateral arrangements

Less formal arrangements with:

- **Various tax authorities** for tax-related cooperation;
- **Foreign FIUs** for AML cooperation;
- **Foreign regulators** for regulatory cooperation.

## Articles 45-47 — Cooperation channels

Cooperation channels include:

- **Attorney General** — central MLA authority;
- **JFSC** — regulatory cooperation;
- **FIU** — AML / SAR cooperation through Egmont;
- **States Police** — operational law-enforcement;
- **Royal Court** — judicial cooperation.

## Article 48 — Regulations

The Minister can make Orders for:

- **Designated countries** for specific MLA arrangements;
- **Specific** treaty implementation;
- **Procedural** requirements;
- **Forms and fees**.

## Article 49 — Cooperation with international organisations

Cooperation with:

- **Interpol** for operational matters;
- **Europol** for European matters;
- **Eurojust** for prosecution coordination;
- **Specific** UN bodies for sanctions and counter-
  terrorism.

## Article 50 — Citation

The Law is cited as the **"Crime (International Co-operation)
(Jersey) Law 2001"**.

It came into force in **2001** with amendments since to keep
pace with international developments (CFT post-2001, ML / asset
recovery, sanctions interface).
