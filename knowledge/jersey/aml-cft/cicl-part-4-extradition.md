# CICL 2001 Part 4 — Extradition and Surrender

Part 4 covers **extradition** of persons from Jersey to face
foreign criminal proceedings.

## Constitutional position

Jersey **extradition** is constitutionally complex:

- **UK extradition treaties** generally extend to Jersey
  through Orders in Council;
- **Jersey-specific** extradition arrangements are limited;
- **Persons sought** for extradition from Jersey typically
  pass through UK procedures with Jersey-specific
  application;
- **Foreign requests** for extradition route through the UK-
  Jersey constitutional framework.

## Articles 23-25 — Foreign requests

When a foreign authority seeks extradition of a person in
Jersey:

- **Request** routed through the UK Foreign Office and / or
  direct to Jersey AG depending on treaty;
- **Provisional arrest** may be sought pending full request;
- **Detention** of the person pending hearing;
- **Hearing** before the Royal Court;
- **Order** for surrender or release.

## Articles 26-28 — Procedure

The extradition procedure:

- **Initial hearing** addresses identity, treaty basis,
  prima facie case (depending on category of treaty);
- **Substantive hearing** addresses grounds for refusal;
- **Refusal grounds** include political offence,
  discrimination, double jeopardy, prejudicial nature of
  foreign justice system, breach of human rights;
- **Final order** of the Royal Court can be appealed.

## Article 29 — Specific safeguards

Safeguards include:

- **Specialty** rule — surrendered person can only be
  prosecuted for the specified offence (not other
  offences);
- **Re-extradition** consent required for onward
  extradition to third state;
- **Capital punishment** assurance required (no capital
  sentence executed);
- **Human rights** compliance (Article 6 fair trial; Article
  3 inhuman treatment).

## Article 30 — UK coordination

Jersey extradition decisions involve:

- **UK Home Office** approval typically;
- **UK courts** in some cases (where Jersey is part of UK
  extradition);
- **Crown Dependencies** coordination;
- **Foreign counsel** through diplomatic channels.

## Practical scenarios

Jersey extraditions are **rare**:

- **Most** persons sought from Jersey relocate to UK first;
- **Jersey-specific** extraditions occur but are exceptional;
- **High-profile** cases occur (fraud, financial-crime
  suspects);
- **Coordination** with UK / EU / US procedures common.
