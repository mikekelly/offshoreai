# POCL 1999 Article 31 — Arranging

Article 31 captures **facilitation** of money laundering —
becoming concerned in an arrangement that facilitates use or
control of criminal property.

## The provision

A person commits an offence if they:

- Enter into, or become **concerned in**, an arrangement;
- Which they **know or suspect** facilitates (by whatever
  means) the **acquisition, retention, use or control** of
  criminal property;
- By or on behalf of another person.

## Why this is the **MLRO's most important offence**

For Jersey trust company businesses, banks, and other
regulated firms, **Article 31 is typically the most relevant
substantive offence**:

- The firm itself does not "conceal" property (Article 30) —
  the property is held in trust or for the client;
- The firm does not personally "acquire" the property
  (Article 32);
- But the firm **does** become "concerned in arrangements"
  — opening accounts, settling trusts, transferring funds,
  receiving instructions — that may facilitate use of criminal
  property by clients.

Article 31 is therefore the **principal offence** captured by
SAR practice.

## Mental element

The person must **know or suspect** that:

- The other person's property is criminal property; and
- The arrangement will facilitate its use, control, acquisition,
  or retention.

The suspicion threshold is **the same as Article 30** — more
than mere speculation, less than reasonable belief.

## "Concerned in" — wide reach

"Concerned in" captures:

- **Direct participation** — being a party to the arrangement;
- **Indirect** — facilitating, advising, executing;
- **Continuing involvement** — even after the arrangement is
  set up, ongoing administration is involvement;
- **Knowing assistance** — providing services in the knowledge
  / suspicion that they facilitate.

A Jersey TCB firm administering a client trust **is concerned
in** the arrangement embodied by the trust. If the firm comes
to know or suspect that trust property is criminal property,
**continuation of administration** can constitute Article 31.

## Penalty

Same as Article 30: up to **14 years' imprisonment** and
unlimited fine on indictment.

## Defences

- **Authorised disclosure** with consent (Article 32A);
- **Reasonable excuse** for not making timely disclosure;
- **Good faith** compliance with a duty.

The standard MLRO response on Article 31 suspicion:

1. **Internal disclosure** to the MLRO;
2. **MLRO assessment** of whether to make external SAR;
3. **SAR** to the FIU if knowledge / suspicion is held;
4. **Consent request** for any specific transaction the firm
   needs to do;
5. **Continued involvement** suspended pending FIU consent.

## Practical issues

- **Account closure** — closing an account in suspicious
  circumstances **without** SAR may be Article 31 (because
  closing facilitates the client's removal of suspect funds);
- **Resignation as trustee** — similar concern; the firm may
  not exit the relationship without proper handling;
- **Continuing administration** while SAR is filed — typically
  permitted under FIU consent;
- **Cross-border** facilitation — Jersey firm involved in
  arrangement spanning multiple jurisdictions is captured
  under Article 31 in Jersey.
