# MLO 2008 Article 9 — Purpose and Nature of Business Relationship

Article 9 requires the supervised business to obtain
information on the **purpose and intended nature** of the
business relationship.

## Required information

The supervised business must obtain:

- **Purpose** of the relationship (e.g., wealth holding,
  trading, philanthropy);
- **Intended nature** (volume, frequency, geography of
  transactions);
- **Source of funds** to be used initially;
- **Source of wealth** for higher-risk customers;
- **Expected pattern** of activity.

## Why this matters

This information:

- **Anchors** ongoing monitoring (you can only spot
  anomalies if you understand the baseline);
- **Informs** the risk rating;
- **Drives** EDD decisions;
- **Supports** SAR decisions if patterns later deviate.

## Documenting the purpose

- **Customer onboarding** captures purpose narrative;
- **Documented** in client file;
- **Updated** as the relationship evolves;
- **Reviewed** periodically.

## Specific purposes

### Wealth holding

- **Long-term** asset preservation;
- **Investment** management;
- **Specific** wealth-structuring purpose.

### Trading

- **Commercial** trading activity;
- **Specific** business sectors;
- **Cross-border** trade flows.

### Family-office

- **Multi-generational** family wealth;
- **Specific** family-governance functions;
- **Coordination** with related entities.

### Charitable / philanthropic

- **Charitable** giving;
- **Public-benefit** purposes;
- **Specific** philanthropic strategies.

### Investment fund

- **Pooled** investment vehicle;
- **Specific** investor base;
- **Investment strategy**.

## Source of funds vs source of wealth

### Source of funds

- **Specific** funds for the transaction;
- **Where** the money came from immediately;
- **Examples**: sale of property, employment income,
  inheritance.

### Source of wealth

- **Cumulative** explanation of the customer's wealth;
- **How** the customer accumulated their assets over time;
- **Required** for higher-risk customers (PEPs, large
  transactions).
