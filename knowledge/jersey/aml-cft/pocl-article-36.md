# POCL 1999 Article 36 — Information Sharing Within Regulated Sector

Article 36 carves out **permitted information sharing** within
the regulated sector — protecting cooperation between
regulated firms from tipping-off liability.

## The provision

Article 35 (tipping off) does **not** apply to a disclosure
made:

- **Between firms** in the regulated sector;
- **For the purpose** of preventing money laundering or
  terrorist financing;
- **In good faith**;
- **Subject to safeguards** (only ML / TF prevention purpose;
  disclosure not used to facilitate ML).

## Three permitted channels

### Within a group

A regulated firm can share with **other regulated firms in
the same corporate group**:

- For **group-wide AML programme** purposes;
- For **common customer due diligence**;
- For **internal SAR / risk-management**;
- Without breaching tipping off.

### Between unrelated regulated firms

A regulated firm can share with **another regulated firm**:

- That is **involved in the same transaction or customer**;
- For the **purpose** of preventing ML / TF;
- In **good faith**;
- **Without** disclosing the existence of any SAR (which would
  remain tipping off);
- Communications must focus on **AML / risk indicators**, not
  on SAR status.

### Pursuant to specific authority

A regulated firm can share where:

- **FIU consents** to specific disclosure;
- **Court order** requires or permits disclosure;
- **JFSC** directs disclosure as part of supervision;
- **Foreign FIU** request through Egmont channels.

## "Same customer or transaction"

The same-customer / same-transaction limitation:

- Bank A and Bank B both have the customer — sharing is
  permitted;
- Trustee A and Trustee B both administer trusts for the same
  settlor — sharing about the settlor is permitted;
- Cross-border firms involved in a common transaction (e.g.,
  Jersey trustee + foreign administrator) — sharing about the
  transaction is permitted;
- **Unrelated firms** with **no common nexus** — sharing is
  not within Article 36.

## What can be shared

Article 36 protects disclosure of:

- **Customer identity** and verification details;
- **Source of funds** information;
- **Transaction patterns** identified as suspicious;
- **AML / risk concerns**;
- **CDD** outcomes;
- **Adverse media** findings.

Article 36 does **not** protect disclosure of:

- The **existence** of a specific SAR;
- The **content** of a specific SAR;
- Specific **investigation** details from law enforcement;
- Information whose **disclosure is otherwise prohibited**.

## Practical drafting

When sharing under Article 36:

- **Identify** the basis (AML / TF prevention);
- **Avoid** mentioning SAR existence;
- **Document** the basis and content of the disclosure;
- **Specify** the recipient's confidentiality obligations;
- **Coordinate** with MLRO before significant disclosures.

## Cross-border sharing

Article 36 is **Jersey-law specific**:

- **Jersey-to-Jersey** sharing within regulated firms is
  clearly within Article 36;
- **Jersey-to-foreign-regulated** sharing requires Jersey-law
  Article 36 compliance **plus** foreign-jurisdiction
  compliance with that jurisdiction's tipping-off / privacy
  rules;
- **FIU-to-foreign-FIU** sharing uses Egmont Group channels
  and is supervised by the FIU.
