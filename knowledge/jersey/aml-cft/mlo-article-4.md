# MLO 2008 Article 4 — Internal Policies, Procedures, and Controls

Article 4 requires every supervised business to establish and
maintain **internal AML / CFT policies, procedures, and
controls**.

## The provision

A supervised business **must**:

- **Adopt** policies, procedures, and controls to:
  - **Prevent** ML / TF;
  - **Detect** ML / TF;
  - **Report** ML / TF where required;
- **Communicate** them to staff;
- **Apply** them in practice;
- **Review** them regularly;
- **Update** them to reflect changes in risk, law, or
  practice.

## Required content

The internal policies cover:

### Customer onboarding

- **CDD procedures**;
- **Risk-rating** at onboarding;
- **Approval** authorities for higher-risk customers;
- **Documentation** standards.

### Ongoing monitoring

- **Transaction monitoring** systems;
- **Periodic CDD refresh**;
- **Trigger events** prompting re-review;
- **Escalation** thresholds.

### Internal disclosure

- **Reporting line** to MLRO;
- **Decision-making** on external SARs;
- **Confidentiality** of disclosures;
- **Tipping-off** controls.

### Sanctions

- **Sanctions screening** at onboarding and ongoing;
- **Match handling** procedures;
- **Reporting** to authorities;
- **Licensing** mechanism.

### Record keeping

- **What records** are kept;
- **How long** records are retained;
- **Where** records are stored;
- **How** records are accessed and protected.

### Training

- **Frequency** and **content** of staff training;
- **Records** of training completion;
- **Refresher** training schedule.

### Governance

- **Board oversight** of AML compliance;
- **MLRO** appointment and authority;
- **Annual reporting** to the board;
- **Compliance** review and audit.

## Proportionality

The policies must be **proportionate** to:

- The **size** of the business;
- The **complexity** of services;
- The **risk profile** (per the BWRA);
- The **regulatory regime** applicable.

A small TCB firm does not need the same procedural depth as a
large international bank, but it must have **proportionate**
controls.

## JFSC review

The JFSC will:

- **Review** policies and procedures during visits;
- **Test** their implementation through file review and
  interviews;
- **Compare** them to the AML / CFT Handbook expectations;
- **Take action** on inadequate or under-applied controls.
