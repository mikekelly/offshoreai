# AML/CFT Handbook — Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs)

A **Politically Exposed Person (PEP)** is a person entrusted
with a prominent public function. The Handbook imposes
**mandatory enhanced due diligence** ([EDD](./amlh-edd.md)) on
PEPs, on their **family members**, and on their **known close
associates**.

This file is the section index.

## Who is a PEP

The Handbook scope follows the FATF / EU definition family.
Categories typically include:

### Foreign PEPs

Individuals entrusted with prominent public functions by a
foreign state — heads of state, heads of government, senior
politicians, senior government / judicial / military officials,
senior executives of state-owned enterprises, important
political party officials.

### Domestic PEPs

The same categories of role within the **United Kingdom**
(Jersey aligns with UK domestic-PEP practice given the
constitutional relationship, though the Handbook treats Jersey
proper as the domestic context for some applications — verify
against the current publication).

### International organisation PEPs

Senior officials of international organisations — UN, IMF,
World Bank, regional development banks, EU institutions, FATF,
etc.

### Family members and close associates

PEPs' regime extends to:

- **Family members** — typically spouse / partner, children
  and their spouses / partners, parents, siblings (the exact
  list varies by Handbook edition; verify);
- **Known close associates** — natural persons known to have
  joint beneficial ownership of legal entities or
  arrangements with the PEP, or other close business
  relationships.

Domestic-PEP and foreign-PEP treatment can differ in the
threshold and intensity of EDD applied (verify against the
current Handbook for the precise treatment).

## What the PEP regime requires

For every confirmed PEP relationship, mandatory:

1. **Senior management approval** of the relationship (at
   onboarding and at periodic refresh).
2. **Source-of-wealth verification** to enhanced evidentiary
   standard — see
   [`./amlh-edd.md`](./amlh-edd.md) on the SoW load-bearing
   point.
3. **Source-of-funds verification** for transactions, also at
   enhanced standard.
4. **Enhanced ongoing monitoring** — more frequent transaction
   review, lower thresholds for monitoring alerts, more
   frequent periodic CDD refresh.

For PEPs of more than ordinary risk (high-profile politicians
in high-corruption jurisdictions, sanctions-adjacent figures,
adverse-media exposure), the Handbook expects further
mitigation including possible relationship termination.

## How a PEP is identified

The Handbook expects identification through:

- **Customer self-declaration** at onboarding (insufficient
  alone — the customer's incentive to disclose is weak);
- **Public-domain searches** (news, government registers,
  parliamentary registers, charity registers);
- **Commercial PEP-screening tools** (Refinitiv, Dow Jones
  Risk & Compliance, LexisNexis, ComplyAdvantage, etc.) — the
  expected baseline for any regulated business with material
  customer flow;
- **Adverse-media screening** alongside PEP screening;
- **Ongoing screening** — re-screening as new information
  emerges or at periodic refresh.

PEP screening failures (missed identification of a PEP at
onboarding) are a recurring JFSC enforcement theme.

## When PEP status ends

The Handbook generally provides that PEP status continues for
**at least one year** after the PEP ceases to hold the
qualifying function. After the minimum period, the business can
re-assess on documented risk grounds whether residual PEP risk
remains (e.g. the former PEP's influence may continue to drive
risk). The decision to exit PEP treatment is documented.

For close associates and family members, the PEP-derived risk
ends when the underlying PEP relationship ends, subject to the
same residual-risk assessment.

## Wealth-management context

For an international wealth-management business serving high-
net-worth clients in jurisdictions with significant political
involvement (Middle East, parts of Asia, parts of Africa, parts
of Latin America), the PEP population in the book is **non-
trivial**. The wealth-management RM needs:

- Robust screening at onboarding;
- A documented escalation process to senior-management approval;
- An SoW evidentiary process the JFSC will accept on
  inspection;
- Periodic-refresh discipline that catches both new PEP
  designations and changes in adverse-media exposure.
