# MLO 2008 Article 6 — When CDD Applies

Article 6 specifies the **trigger events** at which CDD must
be applied.

## Mandatory CDD trigger events

CDD **must** be applied:

### Establishing a business relationship

The standard event:

- **Before** the relationship begins (typically);
- **In all cases** (no threshold);
- Including **dormant** accounts being reactivated;
- Including **migrating** relationships (e.g., taking over a
  trust from another administrator).

### Occasional transactions

For one-off transactions (no continuing relationship):

- **Above the threshold** — typically **€15,000 / £10,000** in
  Jersey, but read the current MLO;
- **Linked transactions** must be aggregated where designed to
  evade the threshold;
- **Below threshold** transactions trigger CDD if **suspicion**
  arises.

### Suspicion of ML / TF

Suspicion triggers CDD **irrespective of any threshold**:

- The supervised business **must** apply CDD when it suspects;
- The CDD may **inform** the SAR (or remove suspicion);
- The customer must **not be tipped off** during this process.

### Doubt about existing CDD

If the supervised business has **doubt** about the adequacy or
veracity of previously obtained CDD:

- **Refresh** the CDD;
- **Verify** identity afresh if needed;
- **Update** source-of-funds information;
- **Document** the reasons for the doubt.

### Change of risk profile

- **Customer** changes (e.g., new beneficial owner);
- **Activity** changes (e.g., dramatic increase in
  transaction volume);
- **Geographic** changes (e.g., customer relocates to a
  high-risk jurisdiction);
- **Adverse media** appears.

## Timing — when CDD must be complete

CDD must be **completed before** the relationship begins or
the transaction is processed, **except**:

- **De minimis pre-completion** activity (e.g., opening but
  not funding an account);
- **Where completion is delayed** for objective reasons (e.g.,
  awaiting certified copies in transit);
- **For exempt categories** (Article 11) where reduced
  verification is permitted.

In all cases, the transaction or relationship **must not
proceed** beyond preliminary steps until CDD is complete.

## CDD for legacy customers

For relationships established **before** the Order came into
force or before specific amendments:

- **Bring** legacy customers to current CDD standard on
  refresh cycle;
- **Prioritise** higher-risk legacy customers;
- **Document** progress and any gaps;
- **Exit** relationships where current CDD cannot be obtained.
