# POCL 1999 Article 17 — When a Restraint Order May Be Made

Article 17 specifies **when** the Royal Court can make a
restraint order — freezing the suspect's assets pending
investigation or trial.

## Conditions

A restraint order may be made if:

- **Criminal proceedings** have begun **or are about to
  begin**; or
- An **investigation** has been started or is about to begin
  for an offence to which Part 3 applies; **and**
- There is **reasonable cause to believe** the defendant has
  benefited from criminal conduct.

## Standard

The standard is **low**:

- **Reasonable cause to believe** is below balance of
  probabilities;
- Significantly below criminal proof standard;
- **Designed** to preserve assets pending substantive
  determination.

## Applicants

Restraint orders typically sought by:

- **Attorney General** (Jersey prosecution);
- **Specific** police investigators;
- **Foreign authority** through CICL 2001 mechanism for
  cross-border.

## Pre-charge applications

Restraint orders can be made:

- **Before** formal charges are filed;
- **During** investigation phase;
- **Critical** for preserving assets that may dissipate;
- **Specific** safeguards to prevent abuse.

## Effect

Once made, the restraint order:

- **Freezes** the defendant's realisable property;
- **Prohibits** dealings;
- **Binds** holders of the property (banks, custodians, etc.);
- **Subject to** specific carve-outs (Article 18).
