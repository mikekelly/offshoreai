# SAFL 2014 Part 5 — Enforcement and Miscellaneous

Article 20 (criminal offences) has its own deep file. This
cluster covers Articles 21-30.

## Article 21 — Civil penalties

The JFSC may impose **civil financial penalties** for sanctions
breaches:

- **Calibrated** to severity and circumstances;
- **Without** requiring criminal prosecution;
- **Faster** process than criminal;
- **Less stigmatising** but **still substantial** financial
  penalties;
- **Mitigation** for cooperation, self-reporting, remediation;
- **Public statements** can accompany penalties.

## Article 22 — Public statements

The JFSC may issue **public statements**:

- **Naming** firms / individuals breaching sanctions;
- **Setting out** findings;
- **Issuing warnings** about specific risks;
- **Education** for industry.

## Article 23 — Information powers

Authorities may require:

- **Production** of documents;
- **Provision** of information;
- **Compulsory examinations**;
- **Reports** of frozen assets;
- **Cooperation** with investigations.

Sanctions reporting obligations are **mandatory** — failure to
report is itself an offence.

## Article 24 — Investigations

Sanctions investigations are conducted by:

- **JFSC** for regulated entities;
- **Attorney General** for criminal matters;
- **States of Jersey Police** for investigative support;
- **FIU** for AML-adjacent matters;
- **Joint** with foreign authorities for cross-border cases.

## Article 25 — Appeals

Designations and decisions are appealable to the **Royal
Court**:

- **Within** prescribed period;
- **On grounds** of procedural fairness, error of law,
  proportionality;
- **Standard of review** considers human-rights considerations
  (Article 6 ECHR, A1P1);
- **Further appeal** to Court of Appeal and Privy Council
  (with leave).

## Articles 26-30 — Miscellaneous

### Regulations

The Minister may make **Orders** under the Law:

- **Implementing** specific UN / UK sanctions regimes;
- **Setting** procedural requirements;
- **Establishing** licensing procedures;
- **Defining** terms.

### Cooperation

Jersey cooperates with:

- **UK OFSI** principally;
- **EU** sanctions authorities (where relevant);
- **US OFAC** for cross-border issues;
- **Foreign FIUs** for AML-adjacent sanctions intelligence.

### Reporting

Regulated firms must report:

- **Sanctions matches** identified;
- **Frozen assets** quarterly (or as prescribed);
- **Suspicious circumstances** suggesting evasion;
- **Specific** licence-required transactions.

### Citation

The Law is cited as the **"Sanctions and Asset-Freezing
(Jersey) Law 2014"**.

It came into force in **2014** with subsequent amendments,
including:

- **Post-Brexit** updates (2020-2021) for UK autonomous
  sanctions framework;
- **Russia sanctions** expansion (2022);
- **Specific regime** updates;
- **Procedural** refinements.
