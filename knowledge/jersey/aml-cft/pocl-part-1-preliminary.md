# POCL 1999 Part 1 — Preliminary and Interpretation

Part 1 of the Proceeds of Crime (Jersey) Law 1999 contains
**threshold definitions** that govern the substantive offences,
confiscation, and supervised-business regimes.

## Article 1 — Interpretation

Article 1 defines key terms including:

- **"Authorised disclosure"** — a SAR made to a constable or
  the FIU in accordance with Article 32A or Article 34;
- **"Constable"** — a member of the States of Jersey Police
  (or designated officer);
- **"Court"** — the Royal Court of Jersey, save where context
  requires otherwise;
- **"Criminal conduct"** — conduct that constitutes an offence
  in Jersey, **or** conduct that would constitute an offence in
  Jersey if done in Jersey;
- **"Criminal property"** — property which is, or represents
  (directly or indirectly), a benefit from criminal conduct,
  where the alleged offender knows or suspects it constitutes
  such benefit;
- **"FIU"** — the Financial Intelligence Unit, part of the
  States of Jersey Police;
- **"Nominated officer"** — a person (typically MLRO)
  nominated by a regulated business to receive internal
  disclosures;
- **"Regulated sector"** — businesses listed in Schedule 2;
- **"Property"** — defined widely (real, personal, in
  possession, in action).

## Article 1A — Criminal conduct

Article 1A is the **all-crimes anchor** of the POCL regime. It
provides that "criminal conduct" includes **any** conduct that
constitutes an offence, including:

- Drug offences;
- Tax evasion (key for offshore practice);
- Fraud and dishonesty;
- Sanctions breach;
- Corruption / bribery;
- Theft;
- Money-laundering itself;
- Any other indictable offence.

See [`pocl-article-1a.md`](./pocl-article-1a.md) for the
deep-dive.

## Article 2 — Realisable property and gifts

"Realisable property" is property:

- Held by the **defendant**;
- Or held by a person to whom the defendant has made a
  **gift caught by this Law** (transfers at undervalue, gifts
  to family / connected persons within statutory lookback
  period).

The concept is central to **confiscation**: the Court can reach
property the defendant has dissipated through gifts. The
**lookback period** is typically **6 years** for confiscation
purposes.

## Article 3 — Benefit from criminal conduct

A person **benefits from criminal conduct** if they:

- **Obtain** property as a result of or in connection with
  the conduct;
- **Obtain** a pecuniary advantage;
- **Reduce** a liability (e.g., tax evasion — the unpaid tax
  is the "benefit").

The concept of **benefit** is calculated **gross** of expenses
— the launderer does not get to deduct the costs of running
the criminal enterprise.

For **continuing offences** (e.g., long-running fraud), benefit
is the **aggregate** over the period.
