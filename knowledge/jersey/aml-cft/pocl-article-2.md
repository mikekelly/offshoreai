# POCL 1999 Article 2 — Realisable Property and Gifts

Article 2 defines **realisable property** — the property that
the Royal Court can reach for confiscation.

## Definition

**Realisable property** is:

- Property held by the **defendant** (the person convicted);
- Or held by a person to whom the **defendant has made a
  gift caught by this Law** (a "**caught gift**");
- Subject to specific exclusions.

## Caught gifts

A **caught gift** is one made:

- By the defendant;
- Within a **lookback period** (typically **6 years** for
  confiscation);
- At an **undervalue** (typically gifts or transactions
  significantly below market value);
- To **family**, **connected persons**, **associates**, or
  **other parties** within the catchment.

## Why this matters

The caught-gift mechanism allows the Court to **reach property
the defendant has dissipated** through gifts:

- **Defendants** cannot avoid confiscation by gifting assets;
- **Lookback** captures historic transfers;
- **Recipient** may have to disgorge value;
- **BFPV** for value receives some protection.

## Practical implications

For asset-recovery cases:

- **All assets** held by defendant at conviction reachable;
- **Plus** caught gifts to family / associates within the
  lookback;
- **Detailed tracing** required to identify dissipated
  assets;
- **Specific** Royal Court procedures.
