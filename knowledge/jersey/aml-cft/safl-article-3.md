# SAFL 2014 Article 3 — Designation by the Minister

Article 3 empowers the Minister to **autonomously designate**
persons as sanctions targets — independent of UN, UK, or EU
listings.

## The provision

The Minister may **designate** a person if satisfied that:

- The person is **involved in** specified conduct (terrorism,
  weapons proliferation, human rights abuses, etc.);
- Designation is **necessary** to give effect to UK / UN
  obligations or to protect Jersey's interests;
- Designation is **proportionate** to the conduct concerned.

The Minister's discretion is **broad** but **subject to**:

- **Review** procedures;
- **Court appeal**;
- **Proportionality** principles;
- **Periodic review** of designation.

## When autonomous designation is used

In practice, Jersey **rarely** uses autonomous designation:

- Most Jersey designations are **direct or indirect** from UN
  or UK lists (Articles 4-5);
- **Bespoke** Jersey designations are uncommon — Jersey's
  small size and foreign-policy alignment with the UK means
  it generally follows UK leads;
- **Specific case-by-case** Jersey designations occur for
  particular threats or regional concerns.

## Content of a designation

A designation order specifies:

- **Identity** of the person (name, identifiers, address);
- **Grounds** for designation;
- **Effect** (asset-freezing under Article 10, making
  available under Article 11, sectoral measures);
- **Duration** (often indefinite subject to review);
- **Review mechanism**.

## Effect of designation

Once designated, the person is subject to:

- **Asset freezing** (Article 10);
- **Prohibition** on making funds / resources available
  (Article 11);
- **Specific sectoral** prohibitions (Articles 12-14);
- **Reporting** requirements by Jersey businesses;
- **Inclusion** in the Jersey sanctions list.

## Review

A designation:

- **Subject to** periodic review (typically annually);
- **Designated person** can apply for review at any time;
- **Court appeal** available on grounds of procedural
  fairness, error of law;
- **Lifted** when grounds no longer apply.
