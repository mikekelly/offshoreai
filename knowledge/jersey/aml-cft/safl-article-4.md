# SAFL 2014 Article 4 — UN-Listed Persons

Article 4 provides for **automatic designation** of persons
listed by the **United Nations Security Council**.

## The provision

A person is **automatically designated** for Jersey purposes
if they are:

- **Listed by the UN Security Council** under Chapter VII
  resolutions (1267, 1373, etc.);
- **Listed by a UN Sanctions Committee** for specific regimes
  (Al-Qaida, ISIL/Daesh, Taliban, North Korea, Iran, etc.);
- **Listed by reference** to a UN consolidated list.

The designation is **automatic** — Jersey does not need to
separately designate; the UN listing flows through directly.

## Why this matters

UN sanctions are **binding** on Member States under the UN
Charter. The UK (and Jersey through the UK) is bound to give
effect to UN sanctions.

Article 4 ensures that Jersey **complies** with UN sanctions
**without** requiring case-by-case Jersey decision:

- **Immediate** effect when UN designates;
- **No** delay for Jersey administrative action;
- **Comprehensive** coverage of UN-listed persons.

## Operational practice

Jersey businesses screen against:

- **UN consolidated list** (sanctions.un.org);
- **Updated** as the UN updates;
- **Specific UN sanctions regimes** as relevant to the
  business / customer.

## Delisting

A UN-listed person can be **delisted** through:

- **UN Sanctions Committee** procedures;
- **UN Ombudsperson** (for the 1267/1989/2253 ISIL/Al-Qaida
  regime);
- **Direct petitions** to relevant committees.

Jersey **automatically lifts** the designation when the UN
delists.

## Coordination with UK

UK sanctions law also covers UN listings. In practice:

- **Same** UN-listed persons appear on both UK and Jersey
  lists;
- **Coordination** is automatic through UN feed;
- **Jersey** consolidated sanctions list reflects UN +
  UK + EU + autonomous Jersey.
