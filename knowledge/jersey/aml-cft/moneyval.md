# Moneyval Evaluations of Jersey

**Moneyval** is the **Council of Europe's anti-money-laundering
mutual-evaluation body**. Jersey, as a UK Crown Dependency, is
evaluated periodically by Moneyval under the **FATF
methodology**.

## What Moneyval does

Moneyval conducts **periodic mutual evaluations** of member
jurisdictions assessing:

- **Technical compliance** with FATF's 40 Recommendations
  (how well the legal/regulatory framework matches FATF
  standards);
- **Effectiveness** of the AML/CFT regime in practice
  (whether the framework actually achieves AML outcomes —
  the 11 Immediate Outcomes).

Evaluations are conducted by a peer-review team and result in a
**Mutual Evaluation Report (MER)** published publicly.

## Why Moneyval matters for Jersey

Jersey is **dependent on its reputation** as a clean
international finance centre. A poor Moneyval evaluation would
trigger:

- **FATF grey-listing** or worse;
- **Banking-correspondent withdrawal** by foreign banks;
- **Reputational damage** in the international finance
  community;
- **Loss of business** to better-evaluated jurisdictions.

Conversely, a **strong Moneyval evaluation** helps Jersey
maintain its position as a credible, well-regulated jurisdiction.

## Recent evaluations

Jersey has been evaluated multiple times under successive FATF
methodologies. Each evaluation has driven **legislative and
operational improvements**:

- Strengthening of the **Schedule 2 supervised-business** scope;
- Expansion of **beneficial-ownership** transparency;
- Enhanced **enforcement** powers and resources;
- Improved **inter-agency cooperation**.

The next Moneyval evaluation will assess Jersey's continuing
compliance and effectiveness.
