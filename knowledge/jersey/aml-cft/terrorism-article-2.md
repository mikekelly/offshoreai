# Terrorism Law 2002 Article 2 — Meaning of "terrorism"

Article 2 is the **load-bearing definition** of the Law.
Every offence in Parts 2, 3, 5 and 6, and every reporting
duty in Part 3, turns on whether conduct falls within
"terrorism" as defined here.

## The three-element test

"Terrorism" means the **use or threat of action** where:

1. **The action falls within Article 2(2)** — serious
   violence against a person; serious damage to property;
   endangering life (other than the user's own); creating
   serious risk to public health or safety; serious
   interference with or disruption of an electronic
   system; etc.
2. **The use or threat is designed to influence the
   Government or any international governmental
   organisation, or to intimidate the public or a section
   of it**; and
3. **The use or threat is made for the purpose of
   advancing a political, religious, racial, or
   ideological cause**.

Where the use or threat involves **firearms or
explosives**, the second element ("designed to influence /
intimidate") is **deemed satisfied** without further proof.

## The international scope

The definition applies regardless of where the action
occurs and regardless of which government is sought to be
influenced — covering Jersey and overseas terrorism alike.

## Practitioner consequence

For an AML/CFT compliance function, the Article 2
definition is the **threshold test** for whether a SAR
under [Article 19](./terrorism-article-19.md) or
[Article 21](./terrorism-article-21.md) is required. The
broad reach — particularly the "political / religious /
racial / ideological cause" requirement combined with
threat of property damage — means that a wide range of
conduct can fall within "terrorism" for screening
purposes.
