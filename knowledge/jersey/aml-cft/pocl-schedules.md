# POCL 1999 — Schedules

The Proceeds of Crime (Jersey) Law 1999 has two principal
Schedules.

## Schedule 1 — Procedural Requirements

Schedule 1 contains:

- **Procedural rules** for confiscation, restraint, and
  investigative orders;
- **Notice** and **service** requirements;
- **Default sentences** for non-payment of confiscation orders;
- **Court fees** and time limits;
- **Form** requirements.

Schedule 1 is **technical** and is mainly relevant to
practitioners conducting Royal Court proceedings under the
Law.

## Schedule 2 — Supervised Business

Schedule 2 is the **substantive** schedule — the list of
**supervised business** activities. See
[`schedule-2-businesses.md`](schedule-2-businesses.md) for the
full list and explanatory notes.

The Schedule has been **extensively amended** since 1999:

- **2008** — alignment with FATF revisions, expansion of
  professional services scope;
- **2010s** — addition of designated non-financial
  businesses and professions (DNFBPs);
- **2020s** — VASPs and crypto businesses;
- **2026** — ongoing alignment with FATF Recommendation 22
  and Moneyval feedback.

## Practical points

- The **Minister** can update Schedule 2 by order;
- The **business of "consultants"** has been progressively
  included over time;
- **High-value dealers** are within scope at increasingly
  lower thresholds;
- **Crypto / VASP** inclusion was a major 2022-2024 update.
