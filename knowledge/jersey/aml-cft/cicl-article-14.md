# CICL 2001 Article 14 — Recognition of Foreign Confiscation Orders

Article 14 enables Jersey to **recognise and enforce foreign
confiscation orders** — final orders requiring payment of
amounts representing proceeds of foreign crime.

## The provision

The Attorney General may apply to the Royal Court for
**registration** of a foreign confiscation order:

- A foreign court has **made a confiscation order** following
  criminal conviction;
- The order requires payment of a specified amount or
  transfer of specific property;
- The defendant has Jersey property or assets;
- The foreign authority **requests** Jersey enforcement.

## Procedure

### Application

- **AG** applies with the foreign confiscation order;
- **Affidavit** evidence of the foreign conviction and
  confiscation;
- **Authentication** of foreign documents;
- **Identification** of Jersey property.

### Registration

If satisfied, the Royal Court **registers** the foreign order:

- **Equivalent** to a Jersey confiscation order under POCL;
- **Enforceable** through Jersey procedures;
- **Asset realisation** through the Viscount or other
  appropriate officer.

### Realisation

The Viscount or appointed officer:

- **Takes possession** of Jersey property;
- **Realises** it through sale;
- **Subject** to third-party rights and procedural fairness;
- **Distributes** proceeds per the registration order.

## Asset-sharing

For proceeds recovered:

- **Subject to** treaty arrangements between Jersey and the
  foreign jurisdiction;
- **Typically** shared between Jersey and the requesting
  state on agreed basis;
- **Specific arrangements** for major recoveries;
- **Compensation** to victims where appropriate.

## Grounds for refusal

The Royal Court may refuse registration:

- **Procedural defects** in foreign proceedings (no proper
  notice, no opportunity to defend);
- **Dual criminality** not satisfied;
- **Conduct not criminal** in Jersey;
- **Public policy** concerns;
- **Disproportionate** confiscation;
- **Specific** treaty provisions allowing refusal;
- **Statute of limitations** issues;
- **Previous Jersey proceedings** addressing the same matter.

## Third-party protections

Third parties with interests in the property:

- **Notice** of registration proceedings;
- **Right to be heard**;
- **Specific protections** for BFPV without notice;
- **Right** to retain property if BFPV protection applies;
- **Right** to claim from foreign authority for legitimate
  interests.

## Coordination with sanctions

A property subject to:

- **Sanctions freezing** (SAFL Article 10); and
- **Confiscation order** (CICL Article 14)

— the relationship between the two regimes is **complex**.
Typically confiscation supersedes sanctions freezing once
finalised, but careful coordination is needed.

## High-value cases

Foreign confiscation orders enforced in Jersey have included:

- **Multi-jurisdictional fraud** confiscations;
- **Russia / Russia-related** asset recovery;
- **US Department of Justice** fraud / corruption
  confiscations;
- **UK** confiscation orders post-criminal conviction in UK.

Jersey's cooperation is **well-established** with major
common-law and treaty jurisdictions.
