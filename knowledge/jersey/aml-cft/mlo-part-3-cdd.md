# MLO 2008 Part 3 — CDD (residual: Articles 9-10)

The core CDD provisions (Articles 5, 6, 7, 8) have their own
deep files. This cluster covers Articles 9-10 (purpose / nature
of relationship; ongoing monitoring).

## Article 9 — Purpose and intended nature of the business relationship

The supervised business must obtain **information on**:

- The **purpose** of the relationship (e.g., wealth holding,
  trading, philanthropy);
- The **intended nature** (volume, frequency, geography of
  transactions);
- The **source of funds** to be used initially;
- The **source of wealth** for higher-risk customers;
- The **expected pattern** of activity.

This information:

- **Anchors** ongoing monitoring (you can only spot
  anomalies if you understand the baseline);
- **Informs** the risk rating;
- **Drives** EDD decisions;
- **Supports** SAR decisions if patterns later deviate.

## Article 10 — Ongoing monitoring

Ongoing monitoring includes:

### Transaction monitoring

- **Compare** transactions against expected pattern;
- **Flag** unusual / anomalous transactions;
- **Investigate** flagged transactions;
- **Escalate** to MLRO where unresolved.

### Periodic refresh

- **Identity** information current and accurate;
- **Beneficial ownership** still as recorded;
- **Sanctions / PEP** status current;
- **Source of funds / wealth** consistent with expected.

### Trigger-based review

- **Adverse media**;
- **Customer changes** (address, occupation, structure);
- **Transaction anomalies**;
- **Risk score** changes.

### System-supported monitoring

- **Transaction monitoring** tools applying rules / models;
- **Sanctions screening** systems;
- **Adverse media** monitoring (regular WorldCheck-style
  checks);
- **Behavioural analytics** for higher-risk relationships.

## Documentation of monitoring

- **System outputs** — flags, alerts, dispositions;
- **Investigator notes** on flagged matters;
- **Resolutions** — false positive, escalate, SAR;
- **MLRO oversight** of monitoring effectiveness.

## Proportionality

Monitoring is **calibrated to risk**:

- **High-risk** relationships — more frequent review, more
  thresholds, more analyst attention;
- **Standard** — automated monitoring with periodic review;
- **Low-risk** — automated only, with periodic refresh.
