# POCL 1999 Article 37 — Supervised Business

Article 37 defines **supervised business** for AML / CFT
purposes — the businesses subject to the MLO 2008 regime.

## Definition

A business is **supervised business** if it falls within
**Schedule 2** of POCL — which lists:

### Financial-services businesses

- **Banks** under BBL 1991;
- **TCBs** under FSL 1998;
- **FSBs** under FSL 1998 and CIFL 1988;
- **Investment businesses**;
- **General Insurance Mediation Business (GIMB)**;
- **Money Service Business (MSB)**;
- **Insurance underwriters** under IBL 1996.

### Designated non-financial businesses and professions (DNFBPs)

- **Lawyers** acting in specified activities;
- **Accountants** acting in specified activities;
- **Estate agents**;
- **High-value dealers**;
- **Casinos** and gaming operators;
- **Specific other** professionals on prescribed activities.

### VASPs / digital assets

- **Virtual Asset Service Providers**;
- **Crypto** businesses within scope;
- **Specific** subcategories.

## Effect

Supervised businesses are subject to:

- **AML / CFT compliance** obligations under MLO 2008;
- **Supervision** by the JFSC (typically);
- **Registration** with the JFSC;
- **Reporting** obligations;
- **Specific** record-keeping requirements;
- **Failure-to-disclose** offences under POCL Articles 34,
  34A.
