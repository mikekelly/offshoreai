# MLO 2008 Article 3 — Risk Assessment

Article 3 requires every supervised business to conduct and
maintain a **business risk assessment** (BWRA — business-wide
risk assessment).

## The provision

A supervised business **must**:

- **Identify** the money-laundering and terrorist-financing
  risks to which it is subject;
- **Assess** those risks;
- **Document** the assessment;
- **Keep it up to date** — typically reviewed at least
  annually and on material change;
- **Make it available** to the JFSC on request.

## Risk factors

The BWRA considers:

### Customer risk

- **Customer types** (individuals, entities, complex
  structures);
- **Geographic** distribution of customers;
- **PEP exposure**;
- **Source of wealth / funds** profiles;
- **Industry sectors** of customers.

### Geographic risk

- **High-risk jurisdictions** per FATF / EU lists;
- **Sanctions** jurisdictions;
- **Jurisdictions** with weak AML supervision;
- **Tax-haven** characterisations.

### Service / product risk

- **Cash-intensive** services;
- **High-value** transactions;
- **Anonymous** or pseudonymous services;
- **Cross-border** services;
- **New** or **innovative** products (e.g., crypto);
- **Complex structures** (multi-tier trusts, holding companies).

### Delivery channel risk

- **Non-face-to-face** onboarding;
- **Intermediary** introductions;
- **Online** services;
- **Reliance** on third-party CDD.

## Documentation

The BWRA document typically contains:

- **Methodology** — how risks are identified and rated;
- **Risk register** — identified risks and their rating;
- **Mitigation** — controls in place to address each risk;
- **Residual risk** assessment;
- **Action plan** for gaps;
- **Approval** by senior management / board.

## Use of the BWRA

The BWRA drives:

- **AML procedures** (Article 4) — calibrated to the risks
  identified;
- **CDD approach** — risk-based calibration;
- **Training** content — focused on identified risks;
- **Monitoring** programmes — proportionate to risks.

## JFSC engagement

The JFSC will:

- **Review** the BWRA during supervisory visits;
- **Compare** it with its own sectoral risk understanding;
- **Challenge** it if material risks appear unaddressed;
- **Take action** if the BWRA is missing, inadequate, or
  out of date.
