# AML/CFT Handbook — CDD on a Jersey Company Customer

A Jersey company under
[CJL 1991](../companies/companies-law-1991.md) is one of
the most common customer types for Jersey supervised
businesses. CDD on a Jersey-company customer combines:

- **Entity-level identification** — registry, constitutional
  documents, directors;
- **Beneficial-owner identification** — natural persons
  ultimately owning or controlling 25%+ (with the senior-
  managing-official fallback);
- **Purpose-and-intended-nature** assessment specific to the
  company's business.

## Identification — entity level

For a Jersey company customer:

- **Legal name** and registered number from the JFSC
  Registry;
- **Type of company** — public / private; par-value / no-
  par-value; standard / cell company (PCC / ICC);
- **Date and method of incorporation**;
- **Registered office** and **company secretary**;
- **Directors** — full list with identity verification
  appropriate to risk for each;
- **Memorandum and articles** (certified copies);
- **Recent annual return** confirming ongoing existence;
- **Substance and purpose** under the
  [Economic Substance Law 2019](../tax/economic-substance.md)
  where applicable.

## Identification — beneficial owner level

Per the
[25% test](./amlh-cdd-identification.md):

### Direct shareholders 25%+

Identified and verified. For nominee shareholders, the
**underlying beneficial owner** is identified (the
nominee is a layer to look through, not a stopping point).

### Indirect 25%+

Trace through layered ownership to natural persons. For a
Jersey company owned 100% by a BVI company owned 50% each
by two individuals, each individual is **50% indirect
beneficial owner** of the Jersey company.

### Senior managing official fallback

Where **no natural person meets the 25% threshold** — e.g.
a widely-held listed-company subsidiary, a multi-
contributor charitable arrangement — the **senior managing
official** of the company (typically the CEO or equivalent)
is identified as beneficial-owner-equivalent.

### Control by other means

The "other means" extension captures control patterns not
flowing through formal ownership:

- **Voting agreements** between shareholders;
- **Veto rights** held by a minority shareholder;
- **Founder reserved powers** in family-company arrangements;
- **Family-relationship control** patterns;
- **Significant economic interest** through structuring
  arrangements not on the cap table.

## Purpose and intended nature

Beyond identification:

- **Why the company exists** — operating business / holding
  company / family investment vehicle / SPV / etc.;
- **What the company does** — sector, activity, geographies;
- **Why the company is opening the relationship** —
  banking, custody, investment management, trustee
  services;
- **Expected transaction profile**;
- **Substance considerations** — does the company have
  Jersey substance per the Economic Substance Law where
  it carries on a "relevant activity"?

## Source of funds and source of wealth

- **For initial transactions** — source of funds with
  evidence appropriate to risk;
- **For the company itself** — where the company is a
  recently-formed SPV, SoW analysis flows through to the
  ultimate beneficial owners;
- **For long-established operating companies** — operating
  history, audited accounts, business-purpose evidence.

## Particular Jersey-company customer scenarios

### Operating-business company

- **Trade history** documented through accounts and
  business activity records;
- **Beneficial-owner SoW** if HNW;
- **Industry-sector risk** considered (financial-services
  / crypto / cannabis / cash-intensive sectors elevated);
- **Substance** under the Substance Law if relevant
  activity.

### Holding company (passive)

- **Purpose of the holding** documented;
- **Beneficial-owner identification** load-bearing because
  the company itself is passive;
- **Underlying assets** — what does the holding company
  hold? — informs the AML risk.

### Family investment company

- **Family structure mapping** — who's in the family, how
  are they related;
- **Trust above the company** in many cases (see
  [`./amlh-customer-jersey-trust.md`](./amlh-customer-jersey-trust.md));
- **Distribution patterns** documented.

### SPV for specific transaction

- **Transaction purpose** documented;
- **Beneficial owner of the SPV** identified through to
  the natural persons behind it;
- **Limited expected lifespan** noted — SPV CDD typically
  more streamlined than ongoing-relationship CDD.

### Cell company (PCC / ICC) cell

For a [PCC or ICC](../companies/cell-companies.md) cell:

- The **core / company** level identification;
- The **specific cell** level — the cell is the customer
  for most CDD purposes;
- **Cellular assets and liabilities** tracked separately;
- For ICC cells, the cell is itself an incorporated body
  with its own beneficial-owner analysis.

### Newly-incorporated SPV without history

Common for transactional vehicles:

- **Pre-incorporation diligence** on the persons behind
  the proposed SPV;
- **Purpose** clearly documented from the outset;
- **SoW** of the natural-person beneficial owners
  documented before the SPV's first material transaction.

## Interaction with the FSDI Law 2020 beneficial-ownership register

The
[FSDI Law 2020](../registries/fs-dpi-law-2020.md)
beneficial-ownership register obligations interact with
but are not identical to the AML/CFT Handbook's CDD
framework:

- **FSDI** applies to specific Jersey-incorporated entity
  types;
- **Handbook CDD** is the regulated business's operational
  discipline;
- **Both** use the 25% + control-by-other-means standard;
- **Reconciliation** between the two is part of the
  regulated business's discipline.

## Common Jersey-company-CDD failure modes

- **Stopping the beneficial-owner trace at the immediate
  shareholder** rather than continuing to natural persons;
- **Senior-managing-official identification skipped** in
  no-25%+ situations;
- **Director-level CDD light** — directors identified by
  name but not verified to the same standard as
  beneficial owners;
- **Articles not reviewed** for unusual provisions
  (reserved powers, share-class differential rights);
- **Annual-confirmation update** missed when company
  changes occur (director changes, share transfers).
