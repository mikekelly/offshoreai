# POCL 1999 Articles 8-12 — Confiscation Order Mechanics

Articles 8-12 set out the **operational mechanics** of
confiscation orders.

## Article 8 — Order content

The confiscation order specifies:

- **Amount** to be paid (the recoverable amount);
- **Period** within which payment must be made;
- **Default sentence** for non-payment;
- **Interest** on default;
- **Specific** other terms.

## Article 9 — Time for payment

The Court specifies a **time for payment**:

- **Typically 6 months** from order;
- **Extendable** by Court on application;
- **Specific** circumstances justifying extension;
- **Interest** accrues from end of payment period.

## Article 10 — Default sentence

For **non-payment** of confiscation order:

- **Default term of imprisonment** specified;
- **Additional** to original criminal sentence;
- **Served** consecutively typically;
- **Reduces** if partial payment made.

The default sentence is **substantial**:

- Up to **10 years** for major orders;
- **Scaled** with the unpaid amount;
- **Specific** Jersey schedule.

## Article 11 — Interest

Interest on unpaid amount:

- **Statutory rate** or rate specified by Court;
- **Accrues** from end of payment period;
- **Adds** to total payable;
- **Cumulative** over default period.

## Article 12 — Enforcement

The Court can enforce through:

- **Direct payment** by defendant;
- **Realisation** of defendant's property by Viscount;
- **Charging orders** on specific assets;
- **Receiver** appointment;
- **International** cooperation for foreign assets;
- **Default sentence** if non-payment persists.

## Court's continuing role

The Court has **continuing jurisdiction** over the confiscation
order:

- **Variation** if circumstances change;
- **Reconsideration** if new property emerges;
- **Specific** orders on enforcement disputes;
- **Specific** orders for ancillary matters.

## Practical realisation

The Viscount typically:

- **Receives** payment directly from defendant;
- **Realises** assets if defendant non-cooperative;
- **Distributes** proceeds (after costs) to the Crown;
- **Returns surplus** if any to defendant.
