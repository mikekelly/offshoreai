# POCL 1999 Article 3 — Benefit from Criminal Conduct

Article 3 defines **benefit from criminal conduct** — the
quantum the Court can confiscate.

## Definition

A person **benefits from criminal conduct** if they:

- **Obtain property** as a result of or in connection with
  the conduct;
- **Obtain a pecuniary advantage** (e.g., tax savings,
  reduced costs);
- **Reduce a liability** (e.g., tax evasion — unpaid tax is
  the "benefit").

## Gross benefit

Benefit is calculated **gross** of expenses:

- The defendant **cannot deduct** the costs of running the
  criminal enterprise;
- **Specific** harshness to the defendant;
- **Discourages** treating crime as a business cost-of-
  goods-sold.

## Continuing offences

For **continuing offences** (long-running fraud, ongoing
laundering):

- Benefit is the **aggregate** over the period;
- **Substantial** sums often;
- **Specific** Royal Court calculation methodologies.

## Criminal lifestyle assumptions

Where the defendant has a **criminal lifestyle**:

- The Court can make **assumptions** about benefit:
  - **All property** acquired in lookback period was from
    criminal conduct;
  - **All income** in lookback period was from criminal
    conduct;
  - **Six-year lookback** typical.
- Defendant must **rebut** with evidence of legitimate
  source.

## Specific benefit types

### Drug trafficking

- **Value of drugs** distributed;
- **Cash proceeds**;
- **Specific** drug-related benefit categories.

### Fraud

- **Value of fraudulent gains**;
- **Specific** investor / customer losses included;
- **Specific** Jersey case law.

### Tax evasion

- **Unpaid tax** is the benefit;
- **Interest** and penalties on unpaid tax included.

### Corruption

- **Value of bribes** received;
- **Contracts** obtained through corruption (value
  reckoning).

## Calculation in practice

Confiscation orders calculate:

- **Total benefit** (potentially large);
- **Recoverable amount** = lesser of benefit and **available
  amount** (assets practically realisable);
- **Recoverable amount** is what defendant must pay.
