# MLO 2008 Article 5 — Customer Due Diligence Generally

Article 5 sets out the **core customer due diligence (CDD)
obligation** — the foundational requirement that makes
supervised business AML-compliant.

## The provision

A supervised business **must apply CDD measures** in the
circumstances specified in Article 6, comprising:

- **Identification** of the customer (and the agent acting on
  behalf, if any);
- **Verification** of the customer's identity using **reliable
  independent sources**;
- **Identification** of the **beneficial owner** (where the
  customer is not an individual);
- **Verification** of the beneficial owner's identity to a
  **reasonable extent**;
- **Information** on the **purpose and intended nature** of
  the business relationship;
- **Ongoing monitoring** of the relationship.

## CDD as a continuing obligation

CDD is **not a one-time check at onboarding** — it is
continuous:

- **Initial CDD** at the start of the relationship;
- **Refresh** on **trigger events** (changes, suspicious
  activity, periodic review);
- **Periodic refresh** even without trigger events (typically
  every 1-3 years depending on risk);
- **Source-of-funds** verification on every significant
  transaction;
- **Adverse-news** monitoring continuously.

## Risk-based calibration

The depth of CDD is **calibrated to risk**:

- **Standard** CDD — most customers;
- **Simplified** CDD (Article 11) — low-risk categories;
- **Enhanced** CDD (Article 12) — PEPs, high-risk
  jurisdictions, complex structures.

## When CDD must be done

Article 6 specifies the **trigger events**:

- **At the establishment** of a business relationship;
- For **occasional transactions** above the prescribed
  threshold (typically €15,000 / £10,000);
- When there is **suspicion** of ML / TF (regardless of
  threshold);
- When there are **doubts** about previously obtained CDD;
- When the **risk profile** of the customer changes.

## What "reliable independent source" means

Reliable independent sources include:

- **Government-issued ID** (passport, driving licence) — with
  certified copies;
- **Public registers** (Companies House, JFSC register,
  foundation registers);
- **Regulated electronic verification** systems (LexisNexis,
  WorldCheck, etc.);
- **Independent professional** certifications.

A copy provided by the customer alone is **not** an
independent source — independence requires verification
through a third channel.

## Failure to complete CDD

If CDD cannot be completed:

- The supervised business **must not enter** into the business
  relationship;
- Or **must terminate** the existing relationship;
- Or **must not carry out** the transaction;
- And must **consider** whether to file a SAR (the failure of
  CDD itself may be a suspicion indicator).
