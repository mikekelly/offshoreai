# POCL 1999 Article 30 — Concealing Criminal Property

Article 30 is **the principal substantive money-laundering
offence** in Jersey law — the offence of **concealing**,
**disguising**, **converting**, **transferring** or **removing
from the jurisdiction** criminal property.

## The provision

A person commits an offence if they:

- **Conceal** criminal property;
- **Disguise** criminal property (typically by altering its
  apparent nature or origin);
- **Convert** criminal property (changing its form, e.g., cash
  to bank credit, equities to property);
- **Transfer** criminal property; or
- **Remove** criminal property from Jersey.

"Concealing or disguising" includes concealing or disguising
its **nature**, **source**, **location**, **disposition**,
**movement**, or **ownership**.

## Mental element

The person must:

- **Know or suspect** that the property constitutes a
  benefit from criminal conduct;
- Suspicion alone is sufficient — actual knowledge is not
  required;
- "Suspicion" is more than mere speculation but less than
  reasonable belief — *K Ltd v National Westminster Bank* and
  Jersey case-law line follow.

## Penalty

The offence is punishable by:

- A **fine** (unlimited on indictment);
- **Imprisonment** up to **14 years** on indictment.

## Defences

A person is **not guilty** of the offence if:

- They **make an authorised disclosure** (SAR to the FIU)
  and obtain consent before doing the prohibited act;
- They **intended** to make an authorised disclosure but had
  a reasonable excuse for not having done so;
- The act was done in **good faith** in compliance with a
  duty under the Law.

See [`pocl-article-32a.md`](pocl-article-32a.md) for the
common defences regime.

## Practical scenarios

For Jersey TCB and bank MLROs:

- **Transfer of suspect funds** — refusal to transfer (or
  pre-transfer SAR with consent request) is the standard
  response;
- **Restructuring** a client structure where source-of-funds
  concerns exist — typically requires SAR before any
  restructuring;
- **Account closure** — careful handling to avoid concealing
  if there are suspicions (closure with notice to the FIU is
  typical);
- **Cross-border transfer instructions** from clients with
  unclear source — requires source-of-funds verification
  before complying.

## Linkage with other offences

Article 30 overlaps with:

- **Article 31** (arranging) — the launderer's facilitator;
- **Article 32** (acquisition, use, possession) — the
  recipient;
- A single transaction may engage **multiple offences** in
  combination;
- **Failure to disclose** (Article 34) is a separate
  offence for the regulated-sector reporter.
