# SAFL 2014 Part 4 — Licensing and Exceptions

Article 15 (licences generally) has its own deep file. This
cluster covers Articles 16-19 — specific exceptions and
licence categories.

## Article 16 — Humanitarian exception

For **humanitarian** purposes:

- **Basic-needs** payments to designated persons / dependants
  permitted under licence;
- **Specific charitable** activities permitted;
- **Aid agencies** can deliver humanitarian aid to sanctioned
  regions under licence;
- **Medical** treatment of designated persons can be paid;
- **Educational** payments for designated person's children
  in some cases.

These exceptions reflect **public-policy** considerations —
sanctions should target wrongful conduct, not deny basic
welfare.

## Article 17 — Legal fees exception

Designated persons may use frozen funds for **legal fees**:

- **Defence** of criminal charges;
- **Challenge** to the designation itself;
- **Other legal proceedings**;
- **Subject to** licence specifying reasonable fees;
- **Reasonable rates** — not excessive;
- **Specified** law firms / counsel.

This is a key **rule-of-law** principle — designated persons
have the right to legal representation, including to
challenge the designation.

## Article 18 — Basic transactions exception

Specific **routine transactions** are typically permitted:

- **Tax payments** on frozen property;
- **Insurance premiums** on frozen property;
- **Utility bills** for frozen real estate;
- **Maintenance costs** to preserve value;
- **Pre-existing** contractual obligations performed in
  ordinary course.

These exceptions prevent frozen assets from **deteriorating**
during freezing — a designated person's property should not
be confiscated, just frozen.

## Article 19 — Other specific licences

Other licence categories address:

- **Specific transactional** matters (asset transfers,
  restructurings);
- **Specific sectoral** dealings under sectoral sanctions;
- **Specific** insolvency-related dealings;
- **Court-supervised** transactions involving designated
  persons.

## Cross-border licensing

For transactions touching multiple jurisdictions:

- **Jersey** licence under SAFL Article 15-19;
- **UK** licence under OFSI / SAMLA;
- **EU** licence under EU sanctions regulations;
- **US** OFAC licence for USD transactions;
- **Joint** coordination through relevant authorities.

A transaction often needs **multiple** licences in parallel.
