# AML/CFT Handbook — Staff Training

The Handbook operationalises the
[MLO 2008 Article 19](./mlo-article-19.md) training obligation.
Every supervised business must train its staff on AML/CFT
obligations — both at induction and on an ongoing basis.

## Scope

Training applies to:

- **All staff** in roles that bring them into contact with
  customers, transactions, or compliance processes;
- **Senior management** including the board;
- **Outsourced service providers** where they undertake
  customer-facing or compliance-relevant functions.

The depth and frequency vary by role — front-office RMs require
more frequent and more detailed training than back-office
operations staff.

## Content expectations

The Handbook expects training to cover at minimum:

- The **business's BWRA** and the customer-risk methodology;
- **CDD and EDD obligations** at the level needed for the
  role;
- **PEP identification and handling**;
- **Sanctions screening and reporting**;
- **Suspicious-activity recognition** — typology examples
  relevant to the business;
- **SAR internal-reporting process** — how to escalate to the
  MLRO;
- **Tipping-off offence** — what staff must not say to
  customers;
- **Record-keeping obligations**;
- **Updates** — significant Handbook, MLO 2008, or POCL
  changes since the last training cycle.

## Frequency and evidence

The Handbook expects:

- **Induction training** at onboarding for every new staff
  member;
- **Annual refresher training** at minimum (more frequent for
  higher-risk roles);
- **Documented evidence** of completion per staff member —
  attendance records, content evidence, knowledge-check
  results;
- **Senior-management reporting** of training coverage and
  outcomes.

Training records are JFSC inspection material.
