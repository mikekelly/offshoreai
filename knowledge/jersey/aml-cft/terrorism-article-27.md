# Terrorism Law 2002 Article 27 — Forfeiture of property used for terrorism

The Court may order **forfeiture** of property where a
person has been convicted of an offence under
[Articles 12–16A](./terrorism-article-12.md), where the
property was:

- in the convicted person's **possession or under their
  control** at the time of the offence; and
- either intended for use, or **had been used**, for the
  purposes of terrorism, or was otherwise terrorist
  property.

The Court must give third parties with an interest in
the property an opportunity to be heard before making
the order.

The Article 27 forfeiture power runs alongside the
**civil forfeiture** regime under the
[Forfeiture of Assets (Civil Proceedings) Law 2018](./forfeiture-of-assets-law-2018.md)
— the latter does not require a criminal conviction.
