# POCL 1999 Part 5 — Money Laundering Offences (residual)

The principal offences (Articles 30-32, 34-34A, 35-36) have
their own deep files. This cluster covers Article 35A —
**protected disclosures**.

## Article 35A — Protected disclosures

Article 35A provides **statutory protection** for the person
making a disclosure:

- **From civil liability** — no action lies against the
  discloser for breach of confidence, defamation, or other
  civil duty;
- **From contractual liability** — the discloser is **not in
  breach** of any contractual confidentiality obligation;
- **From employment-related sanctions** — the discloser
  cannot be **dismissed**, **disciplined**, or **detrimentally
  treated** by their employer for making the disclosure;
- **Subject to** the disclosure being made **in good faith**.

## Why this matters

The protection regime supports the **reporting culture** that
underpins POCL:

- Without protection, employees would face risk of suit by
  affected customers, breach-of-contract claims by clients,
  and dismissal pressure;
- The Law provides employees and firms a **safe harbour** for
  acting on knowledge / suspicion;
- The protection is **conditional on good faith** — malicious
  or reckless disclosures are not protected.

## Whistleblower aspects

While POCL focuses on ML disclosures, the same protections
apply where:

- An employee makes a **disclosure to their MLRO**;
- An MLRO makes a **disclosure to the FIU**;
- A regulated firm shares **AML information** with another
  regulated firm under Article 36.

The protection regime is **complemented** by:

- The **Public Interest Disclosure (Jersey) Law 2008** for
  broader whistleblowing protection;
- **JFSC** whistleblowing mechanisms for regulatory concerns;
- **Employment** protections in the Employment (Jersey) Law
  2003.

## Operational practice

For Jersey firms:

- **Train** staff on disclosure obligations and protections;
- **Maintain** confidential reporting channels (employee →
  MLRO);
- **Document** all disclosures and their handling;
- **Reinforce** that disclosure in good faith is protected;
- **Avoid** any retaliation (which would itself be unlawful
  and reputationally damaging).
