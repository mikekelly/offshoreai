# MLO 2008 Article 8 — Beneficial Ownership

Article 8 governs **beneficial-owner (BO) identification** —
the critical step of looking through corporate / trust /
foundation veils to the **natural persons** who ultimately
own or control the customer.

## The provision

For each customer that is not an individual, the supervised
business must:

- **Identify** the beneficial owner(s);
- **Verify** their identity to a reasonable extent;
- **Document** the BO analysis;
- **Refresh** the BO analysis as relationships and structures
  change.

## Who is a beneficial owner

### For companies

A BO is a natural person who:

- **Owns or controls** more than **25%** of the shares or
  voting rights (the standard threshold);
- **Otherwise exercises control** over the company
  (controlling director, controlling shareholder agreement,
  significant influencer);
- Acts on behalf of someone who would meet either of those
  tests (e.g., a nominee for a controlling person).

For **listed companies** on recognised exchanges:

- BOs are not generally required at shareholder level (the
  listed company itself can be treated as the BO);
- **Material shareholders** (typically 5%+) should still be
  understood.

### For trusts

For a **trust** customer, BOs include:

- The **settlor**;
- The **trustees** (each one);
- The **protector** (if any);
- The **beneficiaries** — named beneficiaries, plus the
  class for discretionary trusts;
- Any **other person exercising effective control** (e.g.,
  via reserved powers).

### For foundations

For a **foundation** customer, BOs include:

- The **founder**;
- The **council members** (each one);
- The **guardian**;
- The **beneficiaries** — named beneficiaries, plus the
  class;
- Any **other person exercising effective control**.

### For partnerships

BOs include:

- **General partners**;
- **Limited partners** with **25%+ interest**;
- Any **partner exercising control** beyond economic share.

## Multi-tier structures

For **multi-tier structures** (e.g., trust → holding company →
operating company):

- **Trace through** each layer;
- **Identify** the natural persons at the top;
- **Document** the layered ownership chart;
- **Note** any tiers obscuring the analysis.

## Verification "to a reasonable extent"

The verification standard is **proportionate**:

- **Lower** for transparent / regulated entities at the top;
- **Higher** for complex / opaque structures;
- **Highest** for PEPs as BOs or high-risk-jurisdiction BOs.

Common verification methods:

- **Government-issued ID** of natural persons;
- **Shareholder registers** (corporate);
- **Trust deeds** (trust);
- **Charter / regulations** (foundation);
- **Statutory declarations** from intermediaries;
- **Electronic verification** systems.

## Documentation

The BO record should include:

- **Identity** of each BO;
- **Role** (settlor, beneficiary, director, etc.);
- **Ownership / control** percentage and rationale;
- **Verification** documents obtained;
- **Sanctions / PEP** screening results;
- **Date** of last refresh.

## Refresh

BO information must be **refreshed**:

- On **trigger events** (changes in ownership, transactions);
- **Periodically** (typically annually for higher risk,
  biennially for medium, triennially for lower);
- On **adverse media**;
- Where **doubt** arises about previous information.
