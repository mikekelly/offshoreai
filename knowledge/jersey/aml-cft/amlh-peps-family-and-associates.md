# AML/CFT Handbook — PEP Family Members and Close Associates

The PEP regime extends beyond the PEP themselves to two
adjacent populations: **family members** and **known close
associates**. Identifying these populations correctly is one
of the more nuanced operational PEP-screening tasks — and a
recurring JFSC enforcement theme.

For the PEP definition itself, see
[`./amlh-peps.md`](./amlh-peps.md). For screening operations,
see [`./amlh-peps-screening.md`](./amlh-peps-screening.md).

## Why the extension exists

The rationale: a PEP's family members and close associates
often **share economic interests** with the PEP. Funds that
might otherwise flow through the PEP directly can flow
through family or associates instead. The international
AML/CFT standards (FATF, EU AMLD) treat the extension as
necessary to capture the **true PEP-related risk** rather
than just the formally-named PEP.

## Family members — typical scope

The Handbook typically defines PEP family members to include:

### Direct family

- **Spouse** or **registered partner**;
- **Children** (including step-children and adopted
  children);
- **Children's spouses or registered partners** (i.e. the
  PEP's in-law children);
- **Parents** of the PEP.

### Extended family in some jurisdictions

Some Handbook editions and some international standards
extend to:

- **Siblings**;
- **Sibling's spouses**;
- **Grandparents** and **grandchildren** in some applications.

The Jersey Handbook follows the FATF / EU framework with
Jersey-specific elaboration; **verify against the current
publication** for the precise list applicable to a specific
PEP relationship.

### "Family" in family-business / dynastic contexts

For UHNW family-business contexts where multiple
generations are economically interlocked, the family-member
scope can extend beyond the formal definition in practice —
the firm's own risk-based approach may identify additional
family branches as PEP-risk-relevant even where they fall
outside the formal definition.

## Close associates — typical scope

"Known close associates" is **harder to define** than family
members because the relationship is not registry-recorded.
The Handbook captures typical categories:

### Joint beneficial ownership

Natural persons known to **share beneficial ownership** of:

- **Legal entities** (companies, partnerships);
- **Trust or other arrangements**;
- **Specific assets** (property, art, securities holdings).

The "joint beneficial ownership" link is the clearest close-
associate category because it surfaces through CDD on the
related entity / trust / arrangement.

### Close business relationships

Natural persons known to have **close business
relationships** with the PEP:

- **Long-standing business partners**;
- **Senior executives** of companies the PEP controls;
- **Trusted advisers** (where the relationship goes beyond
  professional service);
- **Frontmen / nominee arrangements** where evidence
  suggests the PEP is the underlying beneficial party.

### "Known" — the qualifier

The "known" qualifier means the firm acts on **information it
actually has or could reasonably obtain** — not speculative
associations. Sources of "knowledge":

- **Customer disclosure** at onboarding (typically light
  unless directly questioned);
- **Public-domain searches** — news, business registers,
  political-funding records, company filings;
- **Commercial PEP databases** — major providers maintain
  close-associate records alongside PEP records;
- **Investigative journalism / NGO research** — particularly
  for higher-profile PEPs;
- **Internal pattern-recognition** — multiple customers
  identified as connected to the same PEP.

## How family / associate status is identified

### Onboarding

The customer self-declaration captures family relationships
to PEPs in some cases. Commercial PEP-screening tools
include family-member and close-associate links to the
known PEP database.

### Ongoing screening

Re-screening on database updates surfaces new family /
associate links as the PEP database is enriched (e.g. a
high-profile PEP's family-member list is updated as the
PEP's career develops).

### Pattern-discovery investigations

Compliance teams pursuing other matters (e.g. a SAR
investigation) often surface family / associate links not
previously identified. These trigger re-screening of the
relationship and EDD application.

## EDD application

For confirmed family-member or close-associate
relationships, the **EDD regime applies** (see
[`./amlh-edd.md`](./amlh-edd.md)):

- Senior-management approval of the relationship;
- Source-of-wealth verification at enhanced standard;
- Enhanced ongoing monitoring;
- Periodic refresh at higher cadence.

For family members and close associates, the **PEP-derived
risk** is the focus — the question is whether their
relationship to the PEP creates a structural channel for
PEP-derived funds.

## Cessation

When the underlying PEP relationship ends (the PEP ceases to
hold the qualifying role), the family-member and close-
associate derived risk also ends, subject to:

- The **minimum continuing period** typically applied to
  the original PEP (e.g. one year minimum);
- **Residual-risk assessment** — does the relationship
  continue to carry elevated risk on documented grounds?

The family member or close associate is not
"de-PEP-categorised" automatically — the documented residual-
risk assessment is required.

## Common failure modes

JFSC enforcement themes:

- **Family-member identification limited to immediate
  family** — extended family in dynastic contexts not
  captured;
- **Close-associate identification weak** — the qualifier
  "known" applied too narrowly, treating speculative
  associations as outside scope when investigation would
  have revealed them;
- **No re-screening for newly-emerged links** — initial
  screening fixed at onboarding;
- **Mass cessation** when the PEP role ends — automatic
  treatment as no-longer-PEP-derived risk without
  documented residual assessment;
- **Inconsistency across multiple customers** linked to the
  same PEP — different EDD treatment for what should be
  equivalent risk profiles.

## Wealth-management practical reality

For international wealth-management businesses serving HNW
families from politically-active jurisdictions (MENA,
South Asia, Latin America, parts of Europe), the
**family-member and close-associate population is
substantial**:

- Multiple generations of a political family may all be
  in the wealth-management book;
- Business-partner relationships may surface through
  commercial intermediaries that themselves become
  customers;
- The PEP-relationship mapping is **complex and dynamic**.

A robust PEP-family-and-associate identification programme
is not a peripheral compliance function — it is **central
to the operational discipline** of serving this clientele.
