# AML/CFT Handbook — Ongoing Monitoring

CDD does not end at onboarding. The Handbook requires
**ongoing monitoring** of the business relationship throughout
its life — both transaction-by-transaction and through
periodic CDD refresh.

This file is the section index.

## The two strands of ongoing monitoring

### Transaction monitoring

Reviewing transactions during the relationship to identify
those that are **inconsistent with the customer's known
profile** (purpose, source-of-funds expectation, transaction
patterns documented at onboarding). The Handbook expects:

- **Automated transaction screening** for material customer
  flows — typically rules-based plus pattern-based.
- **Alerts investigated** by the second line of defence
  (compliance / MLRO).
- **Decisions documented** — disposed of as no concern,
  escalated for further investigation, or escalated to SAR.
- **Outcomes fed back into the customer risk rating** when
  warranted.

For a wealth-management business with discretionary mandates,
the transaction monitoring is heavily augmented by the **RM
relationship knowledge** — the RM is the natural first observer
of customer behaviour inconsistent with profile.

### Periodic CDD refresh

The customer file is **refreshed periodically** at a cadence
driven by the customer's risk rating:

- **High-risk** — typically annually;
- **Medium-risk** — typically every 2–3 years;
- **Low-risk** — typically every 3–5 years;
- **Trigger-event refresh** — outside the periodic cycle when
  an event changes the customer's risk profile.

Each refresh re-verifies the customer's identity (where
documentation has expired), re-confirms source-of-funds / 
source-of-wealth, captures any change in PEP / sanctions
status, and re-rates the relationship.

## Trigger events for off-cycle refresh

Events that should prompt an off-cycle CDD refresh:

- The customer **becomes a PEP** or a PEP relationship is
  newly identified;
- A new **adverse media** signal;
- A change in **beneficial ownership** of an entity customer;
- A change in the customer's **country of residence** to a
  higher-risk jurisdiction;
- A **transaction pattern shift** that suggests changed
  underlying activity;
- A **regulatory enforcement** development affecting the
  customer or a related party;
- **Sanctions designation** of a related party (the customer
  may not be designated but a connected person becoming so
  changes the risk).

## Interaction with the risk-based approach

Ongoing-monitoring intensity tracks the customer risk rating
established at onboarding and refresh. A **misaligned
intensity** (high-risk customer with low-risk monitoring) is
a JFSC enforcement theme.

## Documentation

The Handbook expects the monitoring programme to be **documented
operationally** — alert rules, alert volumes, alert dispositions,
SAR escalation outcomes — and **available to the JFSC** in
thematic-review or supervisory contexts. A monitoring programme
that operates effectively but is not documented is treated as
not operating effectively for enforcement purposes.
