# POCL 1999 Articles 42-48 — Cash Forfeiture, Civil Recovery, Miscellaneous

## Article 42 — Cash forfeiture

The Court may forfeit **cash** suspected to be:

- **Proceeds** of crime; or
- **Intended for use** in unlawful conduct.

### Procedure

- **Cash seized** by police / customs (48-hour initial
  detention);
- **Further detention** up to 3 months on Court order;
- **Forfeiture proceedings** initiated within detention
  period;
- **Civil standard** (balance of probabilities).

### Non-conviction-based

- **No** criminal conviction required;
- **Powerful tool** for cash recovery;
- **Specific** Jersey applications.

## Article 43 — Civil recovery

Property identifiable as **proceeds of crime**:

- **Subject** to civil proceedings by Attorney General;
- **Standard**: balance of probabilities;
- **Remedies**: freezing orders, receivership, vesting
  orders;
- **Property recovered** for Crown (with victim
  compensation discretion).

Jersey civil recovery is **narrower** than UK POCA 2002 but
exists as a tool for **non-conviction-based** asset
recovery.

## Article 44 — Forfeiture of listed assets

Specific forfeiture for:

- **Listed assets** (luxury goods, precious metals);
- **Vehicles**, **vessels**, **aircraft**;
- **Other** specified high-value items associated with
  criminal conduct;
- **Specific** procedural rules.

## Article 45 — International cooperation

Cross-border cooperation through:

- **Crime (International Co-operation) (Jersey) Law 2001**
  (CICL);
- **Bilateral mutual legal assistance treaties**;
- **Egmont Group** for FIU-to-FIU sharing;
- **Recognition** of foreign restraint / confiscation
  orders;
- See [`cicl-articles-index.md`](cicl-articles-index.md).

## Articles 46-48 — General

- **Regulation-making powers** for Minister;
- **Forms and fees**;
- **Citation** — "Proceeds of Crime (Jersey) Law 1999";
- **Specific** other administrative matters.

## Forfeiture in practice

Cash forfeiture and civil recovery:

- **Increasingly used** as **non-prosecution** alternatives;
- **Recovering** tainted assets without criminal-trial
  burden;
- **Civil standard** is easier to satisfy;
- **Specific** Jersey jurisprudence developing;
- **Coordination** with international authorities.
