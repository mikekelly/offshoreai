# POCL 1999 Part 3 — Restraint and Charging Orders

Part 3 governs **pre-conviction restraint orders** — the Royal
Court's power to freeze a suspect's assets pending investigation
or trial.

## Article 17 — When a restraint order may be made

The Court may make a restraint order if:

- **Criminal proceedings** have begun or are about to begin;
  or
- **An investigation** has been started or is about to begin
  for an offence to which Part 3 applies;
- And there is **reasonable cause to believe** the defendant
  has benefited from criminal conduct.

The standard for making an order is **low** — the prosecutor /
investigator does not need to prove guilt; only that benefit
is reasonably suspected.

## Article 18 — Effect of restraint order

The order **prohibits dealings** with realisable property:

- **Disposing** of property is prohibited;
- **Diminishing** value (e.g., wasting, gifting away) is
  prohibited;
- **Removing** from the jurisdiction is typically prohibited;
- **Exceptions** allowed for reasonable living expenses, legal
  expenses, ordinary trade / business expenses (typically by
  variation).

The order **binds**:

- The defendant;
- Persons who hold the defendant's property (banks,
  custodians, nominees);
- Persons who are notified of the order;
- The world generally (after public proclamation in some
  cases).

## Article 19 — Application and discharge

Application is by:

- The **Attorney General** (prosecution);
- The **defendant** (for discharge / variation);
- A **third party** with interest in restrained property.

The Court can **discharge or vary** the order if:

- Proceedings are concluded without conviction;
- The order is no longer needed;
- It would be **just and reasonable** to vary it;
- Third-party interests require it.

## Articles 20-24 — Ancillary powers

The Court can make orders for:

- **Receivership** — appointing a receiver to manage
  restrained property;
- **Charging orders** — securing restrained property by way
  of charge;
- **Disclosure orders** — requiring the defendant to disclose
  assets;
- **Search and seizure** — physical taking of property in
  some cases;
- **International cooperation** — recognising and enforcing
  foreign restraint orders (typically through Article 41 of
  the Crime (International Co-operation) (Jersey) Law 2001).

## Practical issues for service providers

When a Jersey TCB or bank is **served** with notice of a
restraint order over client assets:

- **Comply immediately** — do not deal with the assets;
- **Notify** the client (subject to tipping-off
  considerations — typically allowed for restraint orders
  which are themselves public);
- **Seek legal advice** on permitted dealings (operational
  expenses, fees, etc.);
- **Apply to vary** if necessary;
- **Document** the process.
