# POCL 1999 Articles 19-24 — Restraint Order Ancillary Provisions

## Article 19 — Variation and discharge

The Court can vary or discharge a restraint order:

### Variation

- **Adjust** specific terms;
- **Add or remove** specific carve-outs;
- **Modify** the scope (specific assets only);
- **Subject to** application by interested parties.

### Discharge

- **When** criminal proceedings concluded without conviction;
- **When** the order is no longer needed;
- **Just and reasonable** to discharge;
- **Subject to** specific procedural requirements.

## Article 20 — Receivership

Where assets need active management:

- Court can appoint a **receiver**;
- **Manages** the property pending substantive
  determination;
- **Subject to** Court oversight;
- **Costs** paid from restrained property typically.

## Article 21 — Charging orders

The Court can make **charging orders**:

- **Securing** restrained property by way of charge;
- **Priority** over subsequent dealings;
- **Specific** registration requirements;
- **Subject to** specific procedural rules.

## Article 22 — Disclosure orders

The Court can require the defendant to **disclose assets**:

- **Comprehensive** disclosure of holdings;
- **Subject to** privilege against self-incrimination
  considerations;
- **Sworn statement**;
- **Specific** time limits.

## Article 23 — Search and seizure

The Court can authorise:

- **Search** of premises for evidence;
- **Seizure** of evidence of the offence and identifying
  assets;
- **Specific** procedural safeguards;
- **Notice** requirements.

## Article 24 — International cooperation

For cross-border restraint:

- **Foreign restraint orders** can be **recognised and
  enforced** in Jersey through CICL 2001 Article 13;
- **Jersey restraint orders** can be sought for enforcement
  abroad;
- **Specific** cooperation channels.

## Practical issues for service providers

When a Jersey TCB or bank is **served** with a restraint
order:

- **Comply immediately** — do not deal with the restrained
  property;
- **Notify** the client (subject to tipping-off
  considerations — restraint orders are typically public so
  tipping-off less concerning);
- **Seek legal advice** on permitted dealings (carve-outs);
- **Apply to vary** if practical issues arise;
- **Document** the process throughout.
