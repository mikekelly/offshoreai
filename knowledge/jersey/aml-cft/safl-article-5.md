# SAFL 2014 Article 5 — UK-Listed Persons

Article 5 provides for **designation by reference to UK
sanctions lists** — the principal route by which UK sanctions
take effect in Jersey.

## The provision

A person is **designated** for Jersey purposes if:

- **Listed by the UK** under the Sanctions and Anti-Money
  Laundering Act 2018 (SAMLA);
- **Listed in regulations** made under SAMLA;
- **Listed by OFSI** (Office of Financial Sanctions
  Implementation, part of HM Treasury);
- **Subject to** UK Magnitsky-style human-rights sanctions;
- **Subject to** UK cyber sanctions;
- **Subject to** other UK sanctions regimes.

The designation may be **automatic** (mirroring UK listing
directly) or by **specific Jersey order** giving effect to UK
listings.

## Post-Brexit UK regime

Since Brexit, the UK has its own autonomous sanctions framework:

- **Russia sanctions** (2022 expansion);
- **Iran sanctions** (separate from EU and US);
- **DPRK sanctions**;
- **Syria, Myanmar, Venezuela** regimes;
- **Belarus** sanctions;
- **Global Human Rights Sanctions Regulations**;
- **Global Anti-Corruption Sanctions Regulations**;
- **Cyber Sanctions**;
- **Specific country / regime** sanctions.

Jersey gives effect to these through Article 5.

## Constitutional position

Jersey's foreign-policy alignment with the UK means:

- Jersey **generally** follows UK sanctions decisions;
- **Constitutional convention** that Jersey does not pursue
  inconsistent foreign-policy stances;
- **Practical** integration through joint UK-Jersey systems
  (consolidated lists, OFSI guidance);
- **JFSC** publishes Jersey-specific implementing guidance.

## OFSI as practical authority

UK OFSI is the **practical authority** for:

- **Maintenance** of the consolidated list;
- **Publication** of designations and updates;
- **Licensing** decisions (UK side);
- **Guidance** to industry;
- **Enforcement** of UK sanctions (separately from Jersey
  enforcement).

Jersey businesses typically screen against:

- **Jersey** consolidated list;
- **UK / OFSI** consolidated list;
- Often **EU** consolidated list;
- Often **US OFAC** list (especially for USD transactions).

## Delisting

A UK-listed person can be **delisted** through:

- **OFSI** procedures;
- **Court** appeal in UK;
- **Application** for variation.

Jersey **follows** UK delisting decisions in most cases.

## Practical implications

For Jersey businesses:

- **Screen** against UK list as a primary obligation;
- **Update** screening promptly when UK adds / removes
  designations;
- **Joint compliance** for cross-border businesses serving
  UK customers;
- **JFSC** notification of significant sanctions matches.
