# AML/CFT Handbook — Sanctions

The Handbook covers the **AML/CFT-side** of sanctions —
screening obligations, reporting obligations, and the
interaction with the customer-onboarding and ongoing-monitoring
processes. The **substantive sanctions law** sits in the
[Sanctions and Asset-Freezing (Jersey) Law 2019](./safl-articles-index.md)
and the dedicated overview at
[`./sanctions.md`](./sanctions.md).

This section index treats the Handbook lens; cite the SAFL
articles wiki or the sanctions overview for the substantive
position.

## What the Handbook expects

### Screening

Every supervised business operates **sanctions screening**
against the applicable sanctions lists:

- **Jersey** designations (made by the Minister under the
  SAFL 2019);
- **UN Security Council** designations (binding via Jersey
  implementation);
- **UK OFSI** consolidated list (where the business has UK
  customer or counterparty exposure);
- **EU** sanctions lists (where the business has EU
  exposure);
- **US OFAC** lists (where the business has US-dollar-clearing
  exposure or US-person customers).

Screening at onboarding, at every subsequent transaction, and
at periodic refresh — supported by automated tools with
appropriate fuzzy-matching and false-positive management.

### Reporting

Where the business identifies a **match** to a designated
person:

1. **Freeze** the assets / refuse the transaction in
   accordance with the SAFL prohibitions
   ([Article 10](./safl-article-10.md));
2. **Report** the match to the Minister (or designated
   reporting channel — verify against current SAFL guidance);
3. **Apply for licence** if the business needs to perform any
   otherwise-prohibited act in respect of the designated
   person (Article 15);
4. **Maintain records**.

### Ongoing list management

Sanctions lists update frequently. The Handbook expects:

- **Automated re-screening** of the customer base on list
  updates;
- **Clear procedures** for handling new matches;
- **Senior-management awareness** of sanctions-related
  actions.

## The country-risk distinction

A non-designated person in a sanctioned country is not
automatically caught by the sanctions regime — but is likely
to be **high country-risk** under
[`./amlh-high-risk-countries.md`](./amlh-high-risk-countries.md)
and require [EDD](./amlh-edd.md). The two regimes apply
together but address different things.

## Wealth-management practical reality

A wealth-management business with international customer flow
operates **multiple parallel sanctions regimes** (Jersey + UK +
EU + US + UN). The operational consequence is significant — a
robust screening platform, false-positive management, and
documented procedures for handling matches across regimes.
