# CICL 2001 Article 13 — Recognition of Foreign Restraint Orders

Article 13 enables Jersey to **recognise and enforce foreign
restraint orders** — orders preventing dealings with property
pending foreign criminal proceedings.

## The provision

The Attorney General may apply to the Royal Court for an
order **giving effect** to a foreign restraint order:

- A foreign court has **made a restraint order**;
- The defendant has been (or is about to be) charged with a
  qualifying offence in the foreign jurisdiction;
- The property is **in Jersey** or held by a Jersey-connected
  person;
- The foreign authority has **requested** Jersey enforcement.

## Procedure

### Application

- **AG** applies to the Royal Court;
- **Provides** foreign court order with authentication;
- **Affidavit** evidence of foreign proceedings;
- **Identification** of Jersey property to be restrained.

### Hearing

- Royal Court considers application;
- May hear from affected parties;
- **Makes order** giving effect to the foreign restraint;
- **Specifies** the Jersey property covered.

### Effect

- **Jersey property restrained**;
- **No dealings** without Court permission;
- **Banks, custodians** notified;
- **Continues** until foreign proceedings concluded or order
  varied.

## Grounds for refusal

The Royal Court may refuse to enforce:

- **Foreign order procedurally improper** (no proper notice
  to defendant, no opportunity to respond);
- **Dual criminality** not satisfied;
- **Public policy** concerns;
- **Disproportionate** to the underlying offence;
- **Third-party** rights inadequately considered.

## Variation

The restraint order can be **varied**:

- To **release** funds for reasonable living expenses;
- To **release** funds for legal fees in foreign proceedings;
- To **release** funds for legitimate business expenses;
- For **third-party** legitimate claims;
- **Subject to** AG / foreign authority being heard.

## Practical scenarios

### Russia / Russia-related restraint

- Foreign authority (UK or other) has restrained Russia-
  connected oligarch's assets;
- Some assets are in Jersey (real estate, accounts, trust
  property);
- Foreign authority seeks Jersey recognition;
- Jersey Royal Court considers under CICL Article 13 and
  enforces.

### US grand jury investigation

- US grand jury investigates fraud;
- US Federal Court issues restraining order;
- US authorities request Jersey enforcement;
- Jersey applies and Royal Court considers.

### Money laundering investigations

- Foreign ML investigation identifies Jersey accounts;
- Foreign court issues restraint;
- Coordinated MLA / sanctions / CICL action in Jersey.

## Differences from sanctions

Article 13 restraint is different from sanctions freezing:

| Feature | CICL Article 13 | SAFL Article 10 |
|---|---|---|
| Trigger | Foreign criminal proceedings | Designation (UN/UK/etc.) |
| Procedure | Court order | Automatic on designation |
| Duration | Until foreign proceedings concluded | Until delisting |
| Public | Court order public | Designation public |
| Scope | Specific property | All assets of designated person |
| Source | Foreign judiciary | Executive (Minister, OFSI, UN) |
