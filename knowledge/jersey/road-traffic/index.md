# Road Traffic — Jersey

## What this section is about

Jersey road traffic law — the statutes, offences, enforcement
mechanisms and forums that apply to driving and owning a
vehicle on Jersey roads.

The principal statute is the **Road Traffic (Jersey) Law
1956** (RTJL 1956), supplemented by the **Motor Traffic
(Third Party Insurance) (Jersey) Law 1948**, the **Motor
Vehicle Registration (Jersey) Law 1993**, and the
**Highways (Jersey) Law 1956**. Each of these has been
substantially amended over the decades.

What makes Jersey's road traffic enforcement distinctive is
the **Parish Hall Enquiry** system — a pre-court forum run
by **Centeniers** (honorary police officers) which resolves
the majority of routine motoring offences without going
near the Magistrate's Court. This is a layer that has no
direct UK equivalent and is critical for understanding how
a typical speeding ticket or careless-driving allegation is
actually handled in practice.

## Why this section exists

This was the **third gap-fill** from the coverage audit at
[`../COVERAGE-AUDIT.md`](../COVERAGE-AUDIT.md), addressing
gap #2 (Road Traffic 1956). Every Jersey motorist — and
the audit estimated roughly two-thirds of working-age
residents — has questions about how this regime operates,
and the corpus had no coverage at all.

## At-a-glance — what every Jersey driver should know

| Topic | Position |
|---|---|
| Drink-drive limit | 80mg / 100ml blood; 35μg / 100ml breath (same as historic UK; not the Scottish 50mg) |
| Drug-drive limit | **No statutory limit** as of May 2026 (reform proposed Feb 2026) |
| Speeding > 15 mph excess | Cannot be dealt with at Parish Hall; goes to Magistrate's Court |
| Speeding fine bands | £50-£495 by tariff, based on excess speed and zone |
| Roadside enforcement | **Honorary Police** (Centeniers, Vingteniers) plus States of Jersey Police |
| Forum for routine offences | **Parish Hall Enquiry** — Centenier deals with admission; max £200 fine |
| Right to opt out of Parish Hall | Yes — the defendant can always demand Magistrate's Court instead |
| Forum for contested matters | Magistrate's Court |
| Forum for serious offences | Magistrate's Court initially; Royal Court for the most serious |
| Appeals from Magistrate's Court | Royal Court |
| Insurance | Third-party minimum compulsory (Motor Traffic (TPI) Law 1948) |
| Driving licence | Jersey issues its own; UK licences exchangeable; specific rules for EU and non-EEA |
