# Court Procedure for Traffic Offences

## The forum hierarchy

| Forum | Handles |
|---|---|
| **Parish Hall Enquiry** | Minor motoring offences with admission; max £200 fine; Centenier-led |
| **Magistrate's Court** | Contested cases, serious offences, disqualification-eligible offences |
| **Royal Court** | The most serious offences (causing death by driving); appeals from Magistrate's Court |
| **Court of Appeal** | Further appeals on points of law |

See [`parish-hall-enquiries.md`](./parish-hall-enquiries.md)
for the distinctive Jersey Parish Hall layer.

## The Magistrate's Court route

A typical contested or serious motoring case at the
Magistrate's Court runs:

1. **Charge / Summons** — issued by the Centenier or
   directly by the Magistrate's Court on application;
2. **First appearance** — typically a brief listing;
3. **Plea entered** — guilty or not guilty;
4. **Adjournment** if not guilty — for trial preparation;
5. **Trial** — evidence, cross-examination, submissions
   (in front of the Magistrate sitting alone);
6. **Verdict** — same day or briefly reserved;
7. **Sentencing** — same day or adjourned for reports.

The Magistrate sits alone, without Jurats. The proceedings
are formal but accessible.

## Right to representation

A defendant has the right to:

- **Self-represent** ("act in person");
- **Engage a Jersey advocate** — strongly recommended for
  serious cases;
- **Engage a Jersey solicitor** for advisory work and
  document preparation (a solicitor cannot conduct trial
  in the Magistrate's Court in the same way as an
  advocate);
- **Legal Aid** in narrow circumstances — generally not
  for routine motoring offences.

## Evidence

Prosecution evidence typically includes:

- **Officer statements** (States Police and / or Honorary
  Police);
- **Witness statements** from members of the public;
- **Technical evidence** — speed-measurement device
  calibration certificates, breath-test machine
  certificates;
- **Documentary evidence** — vehicle records, insurance
  records;
- **Photographs / video** where available.

Defence evidence may include:

- **The defendant's own evidence** (optional — defendant
  can decline to give evidence);
- **Defence witnesses**;
- **Expert evidence** (rare in routine cases; common in
  drink-driving procedural disputes);
- **Documentary defences** (insurance schedules, medical
  certificates).

## Sentencing

After conviction the Magistrate considers:

- The **statutory penalty** range;
- **Sentencing Guidelines** for the offence;
- **The driver's record**;
- **Aggravating factors** (accident, vulnerable road
  users, intoxication, evasion);
- **Mitigating factors** (clean record, prompt
  cooperation, remorse, character references);
- **Personal circumstances** (employment, family,
  financial means).

Sentencing typically combines:

- **Fine** — payable within prescribed time;
- **Endorsement** of licence;
- **Disqualification** — mandatory or discretionary;
- **Re-test order** in some cases;
- **Prison** in serious cases;
- **Costs** order against the convicted defendant.

## Appeals

### From Parish Hall

A defendant unhappy with a Parish Hall outcome can ask for
**Magistrate's Court rehearing**. This is **not strictly
an appeal** — it's a fresh look at the matter. The
Magistrate's Court is not bound by the Centenier's
finding.

### From Magistrate's Court

Appeals lie to the **Royal Court**:

- **As of right** on a point of law (28-day time limit);
- **By leave** on fact / sentence;
- **Notice** filed at the court;
- **Hearing** before a Royal Court judge.

Appeals on **sentence severity** are common after
disqualification.

### From Royal Court

Further appeals lie to the **Court of Appeal** on points
of law of general importance, then potentially to the
**Judicial Committee of the Privy Council**.

## Costs

- **Court fees** — modest;
- **Advocate fees** — typically several hundred to a few
  thousand pounds for routine cases;
- **Prosecution costs** — awarded against the convicted
  defendant (typical);
- **Witness expenses** — covered in narrow cases;
- **Expert fees** — defendant typically bears their own.

## Procedure when going abroad / time-sensitive

For visiting drivers facing charges in Jersey:

- **Parish Hall** can dispose same day if admission;
- **Magistrate's Court** can list quickly with admission;
- **Contested matters** may require return for trial;
- **Out-of-jurisdiction** sentence service available in
  narrow cases.

## Pitfalls

For defendants:

- **Not turning up** — court proceeds, often more
  severely;
- **Self-representing** in serious matters — costly
  mistake;
- **Pleading guilty** to gain speed when defences exist;
- **Failing** to take advantage of mitigation;
- **Underestimating** the impact of endorsement /
  disqualification on insurance and employment.
