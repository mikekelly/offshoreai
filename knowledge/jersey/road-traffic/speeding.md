# Speeding Offences in Jersey

## The offence

Driving at a speed exceeding the **lawful speed limit** for
the road / zone is an offence under the **Road Traffic
(Jersey) Law 1956**. Speed limits are prescribed by orders
made under the RTJL and indicated by traffic signs.

There is a statutory evidential protection: a person
prosecuted for speeding **cannot be convicted on the
evidence of a single witness alone** (one police officer's
opinion is not enough). Corroboration is required — either
a second witness, a calibrated speed-measurement device, or
an admission.

## Speed limits in Jersey

Jersey speed limits are lower than UK equivalents in many
zones. Typical:

| Zone | Limit |
|---|---|
| Urban / Green Lane | 15 mph |
| Built-up areas | 20 mph |
| Default rural roads | 30 mph |
| Selected main routes | 40 mph |

The 15 mph green lanes are a distinctive Jersey feature —
designated rural routes shared with pedestrians,
cyclists, and horses, with strict speed control.

## Penalty band — fines

The Magistrate's Court Sentencing Guidelines provide a
matrix mapping **excess speed** against **zone limit** to
suggest:

- **Fine bands** ranging from **£50 to £495**;
- **Endorsement** consideration (for moderate excess);
- **Disqualification** consideration (for higher excess).

Indicative pattern (specifics vary by zone):

| Excess mph | Suggested outcome |
|---|---|
| 5 mph over | £50-£100; consider endorsement |
| 10 mph over | £100-£200; endorse |
| 15 mph over | £200-£300; endorse / consider disqualification |
| 20 mph over | £300-£400; endorse / disqualify |
| 25+ mph over | £400-£495; disqualify |
| 35+ mph over | Top of scale; mandatory consideration of substantial disqualification |

These are **guidelines**, not statutory tariffs — the
Magistrate exercises discretion. Aggravating factors
(weather, pedestrians present, accident risk) push the
sentence up; mitigating factors (clean record, prompt
admission) push it down.

## Forum

| Speed | Forum |
|---|---|
| **Under 15 mph excess** | Can be dealt with at **Parish Hall Enquiry** by the Centenier (admission required, max £200 fine) |
| **15 mph or more excess** | **Cannot** be dealt with at Parish Hall — referred to Magistrate's Court by summons |
| **Excessive speed with risk** | May be charged as **dangerous driving** (Article 23 RTJL) — Magistrate's or Royal Court |

A defendant called to Parish Hall can always **demand
Magistrate's Court** instead — but if the excess is small,
Parish Hall is usually faster and cheaper.

## Endorsement and totting up

Jersey **endorses** driving licences for speeding offences
above a threshold. Endorsements remain on the licence for
a set period.

Jersey does **not** operate a UK-style "12 points → 6 month
disqualification" totting-up scheme. Instead:

- The court considers the driver's **history** when
  sentencing;
- **Multiple endorsements** can be a strong factor in
  imposing disqualification;
- A driver with a clean record will be treated more
  favourably than one with prior endorsements.

## Disqualification

Disqualification may be:

- **Mandatory** in the most serious speeding cases
  (substantial excess, dangerous-driving territory);
- **Discretionary** for serious but not extreme excess;
- **For a fixed period** — days, weeks, months, occasionally
  years for repeat offenders.

A disqualified driver who drives commits the separate
offence of **driving while disqualified** — much more
serious.

## Evidence

Speed evidence typically comes from:

- **Calibrated radar / laser** devices used by Honorary
  Police or States Police;
- **Average-speed cameras** in some locations;
- **VASCAR / pursuit-vehicle** measurement;
- **Officer's calibrated speedometer** (with corroboration).

The statutory protection against single-witness conviction
means uncorroborated officer opinion alone is insufficient.

## Defences and mitigation

Common defences:

- **Identity** — was the driver the named defendant?
- **Calibration** — was the speed-measurement device
  properly calibrated and operated?
- **Sign visibility** — was the speed limit properly
  indicated?
- **Emergency** — a narrow defence in exceptional
  circumstances;
- **Necessity / duress** — even narrower.

Common mitigation (admitting the offence but seeking
lighter penalty):

- **Clean record**;
- **Prompt admission and cooperation**;
- **Genuine remorse**;
- **Loss of licence consequences** (occupation, family
  circumstances, dependants);
- **Character references**.

## Notable Jersey-specific points

- **Green lanes** — particular scrutiny of speeds even
  modestly above 15 mph because of the shared-use nature;
- **Tourism context** — visiting drivers caught speeding
  are commonly dealt with at Parish Hall to allow speedy
  resolution before departure;
- **Hire-car drivers** — hire-companies typically pass
  penalty notices to the renter;
- **Foreign drivers** — Jersey endorsements may or may not
  follow to a foreign licence depending on the issuing
  country's recognition arrangements.
