# Compulsory Admission under the MHJL 2016

## Three principal admission routes

The Mental Health (Jersey) Law 2016 provides three routes
for compulsory hospital admission:

| Route | Article | Duration | When used |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Emergency** | 17 | Up to 72 hours | Immediate risk; allows quick removal to safety pending fuller assessment |
| **Assessment** | 21 | Up to 28 days | Diagnostic assessment of suspected mental disorder |
| **Treatment** | 22 | Up to 6 months, then renewable | Sustained treatment of diagnosed mental disorder |

Plus criminal-justice routes (hospital orders post-
conviction, etc.) covered in
[`mental-health-law-2016.md`](./mental-health-law-2016.md).

## Article 17 — Emergency admission

### Threshold

An approved practitioner forms the opinion that:

- The patient is **likely to be suffering** from mental
  disorder; **and**
- **Allowing the patient to remain at liberty** would
  endanger the patient's safety or that of others.

### Procedure

- **Authorisation** by the approved practitioner alone;
- **No second medical opinion** required at this stage;
- **Patient taken** to an approved establishment;
- **Detention period** — **72 hours from the time the
  opinion is formed**;
- **Cannot be extended** — within 72 hours either:
  - Patient discharged; or
  - Article 21 or 22 detention initiated.

### Purpose

To deal with **immediate risk** — the 72-hour window
allows fuller assessment to determine whether longer
detention is justified under Article 21 or 22.

## Article 21 — Assessment detention

### Threshold

- The patient is **suspected of mental disorder of a
  nature or degree** which warrants detention for at
  least a limited period;
- **Detention is necessary** in the patient's interests
  or for the protection of others.

### Procedure

- **Application** by an AMHP (Approved Mental Health
  Practitioner) or by the **Nearest Relative**;
- **Supported by two medical recommendations** — one from
  an AMP (Approved Medical Practitioner), one from another
  doctor who has examined the patient;
- **Patient taken** to an approved establishment;
- **Duration** — up to **28 days**;
- **Cannot be extended** beyond 28 days — converts to
  Article 22 if treatment detention is needed.

### Appeals

- Available within the **first 14 days** of detention;
- **Mental Health Review Tribunal** is the forum.

## Article 22 — Treatment detention

### Threshold

- The patient is **suffering from mental disorder of a
  nature or degree which warrants detention**;
- **Treatment is necessary** in the patient's interests
  or for the protection of others;
- **Cannot be provided** without detention.

### Procedure

- **Application** by an AMHP or Nearest Relative;
- **Supported by two medical recommendations** — one from
  an AMP, one from another doctor;
- **Specific medical evidence** of the need for treatment;
- **Patient taken** to an approved establishment;
- **Duration** — initial **6 months**;
- **Renewable** for further **6 months** (one renewal),
  then for **12 months** (subsequent renewals);
- **Each renewal** requires fresh medical evidence and
  review.

### Appeals

- Available **at any time** during detention;
- **Limit** of one application per six-month period;
- **Tribunal** is the forum.

## The Nearest Relative

The MHJL 2016 establishes a hierarchy of Nearest
Relative:

1. **Spouse / civil partner / cohabitant** of 6+ months;
2. **Eldest** son or daughter aged 18+;
3. **Parent**;
4. **Eldest sibling** aged 18+;
5. **Grandparent**;
6. **Grandchild aged 18+**;
7. **Other defined relatives**.

A person can be **displaced** as Nearest Relative on
application where they:

- **Are not** in fact the most appropriate person;
- **Have not consulted** in the patient's interests;
- **Are unsuitable** in defined circumstances.

The Nearest Relative has rights to:

- **Make** an application for admission;
- **Discharge** the patient in some cases (subject to
  responsible-clinician objection);
- **Receive information** about care;
- **Apply** to the Tribunal on the patient's behalf.

## Process at admission

When a patient is brought to the approved establishment:

- **Medical examination** confirming the grounds;
- **Documentation** in prescribed form;
- **Patient informed** of rights (in accessible form);
- **Nearest Relative informed**;
- **Independent Mental Health Advocate (IMHA)**
  available on request;
- **Custody / hospital record** maintained.

## Patient rights during detention

Detained patients have rights to:

- **Information** about their detention and rights;
- **IMHA** (advocate);
- **Legal representation** for Tribunal hearings
  (publicly funded);
- **Tribunal review**;
- **Visits** from family / friends (subject to specific
  rules);
- **Communication** with the outside world (subject to
  defined limits);
- **Treatment refusal** in defined circumstances;
- **Specific** safeguards for serious treatments.

## Treatment without consent

For specified detained patients (typically Article 22
treatment-detention):

- **Some treatments** can be given without consent
  (Section 1 / Section 2 of the Code of Practice
  framework);
- **Other treatments** require:
  - **Patient consent** if capacitous; or
  - **Second-opinion authorisation**;
- **ECT, sterilisation, specific medications** — enhanced
  safeguards;
- **Code of Practice** binding on professionals.

For Article 17 / 21 patients, the treatment rules differ
— typically lower-threshold treatments only, with
emergency exceptions.

## Discharge

A patient can be discharged by:

- **Responsible Clinician** — at any time on clinical
  grounds;
- **Hospital Managers' Review**;
- **Nearest Relative** — subject to clinician objection;
- **Mental Health Review Tribunal**;
- **Article periods expiring** without renewal.

After discharge:

- **Aftercare** under specific provisions of the law;
- **Community treatment orders** in some cases (modern
  alternative to repeat detention);
- **Voluntary care** if accepted.
