# Mental Health and Capacity — Jersey

## What this section is about

Two interlocking 2016 statutes addressing when the State
can intervene in healthcare decisions, and how individuals
can plan for their own future incapacity:

- **Mental Health (Jersey) Law 2016** (MHJL 2016) — when
  a person can be compulsorily admitted and treated for
  mental disorder against their wishes;
- **Capacity and Self-Determination (Jersey) Law 2016**
  (CSDJL 2016) — Lasting Powers of Attorney, advance
  decisions, court-appointed delegates, and the test of
  mental capacity for everyday decisions.

Both statutes came into force in 2018, modernising what
had been a deeply outdated regime in Jersey. They draw on
English Mental Health Act / Mental Capacity Act
principles but with Jersey-specific provisions and
procedural overlays.

## Why this section exists

**Seventh gap-fill** from the coverage audit at
[`../COVERAGE-AUDIT.md`](../COVERAGE-AUDIT.md), closing
audit gaps **#11** (MHJL 2016) and **#12** (CSDJL 2016).
These statutes affect:

- Anyone with **mental illness** in Jersey;
- **Carers** and **families** of vulnerable adults;
- Everyone planning for **aging-related** incapacity
  (LPAs);
- **Patients** facing serious medical treatment decisions
  with advance-decision implications.

The corpus had only passing references in the family-law
context.

## At a glance

| Topic | Position |
|---|---|
| Mental-health statute | Mental Health (Jersey) Law 2016 |
| Capacity statute | Capacity and Self-Determination (Jersey) Law 2016 |
| Emergency admission (Art 17 MHJL) | 72 hours, by approved practitioner |
| Assessment admission (Art 21 MHJL) | Up to 28 days |
| Treatment admission (Art 22 MHJL) | Up to 6 months, extendable |
| Appeal forum | Mental Health Review Tribunal |
| LPA — health & welfare | Covers medical treatment, life-sustaining treatment |
| LPA — property & finance | Covers debts, maintenance, financial gifts |
| Advance decisions | Refusal of specified treatments in advance |
| Delegates | Court-appointed for those without LPA who lack capacity |
| Court forum | Royal Court — Protection Division |
| Code of Practice | Statutory codes for both laws; binding on practitioners |
