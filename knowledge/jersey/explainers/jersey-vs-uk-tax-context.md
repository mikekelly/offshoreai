# Jersey vs UK Tax — What Changes For International Clients

## The short version

Jersey is **not** the UK. Jersey is a separate jurisdiction
with its own tax system. The headline differences for
international clients:

| | Jersey | UK |
|---|---|---|
| **Personal income tax** | 20% flat rate (with marginal relief) | Progressive bands up to 45% |
| **Capital gains tax** | None | Yes (up to 24% residential property; 24% other) |
| **Inheritance tax** | None | Yes (40% above threshold) |
| **Corporate income tax** | 0% standard ("[zero-ten](../tax/zero-ten.md)") with 10% / 20% for specific sectors | 25% (small profits 19%) |
| **Wealth / property tax** | None | Council tax / SDLT on property transactions |

**Important caveat:** these are **Jersey rules**. If you are
**UK tax-resident**, the **UK rules dominate your overall tax
position**, regardless of where your assets are held. A Jersey
trust holding non-UK assets doesn't escape UK tax for a
UK-resident beneficiary — see
[the UK non-dom explainer](../use-cases/client-cohorts/uk-non-dom.md)
for the framework.

## What "tax-resident in Jersey" means

You're tax-resident in Jersey if you spend more than 183
days a year in Jersey, or otherwise establish a permanent
home there. Jersey tax-residence is **not the same as Jersey
nationality / British nationality / UK domicile**.

For Jersey tax-residents:

- **20% income tax** on worldwide income (with marginal
  relief for low incomes);
- **5% GST** (Goods and Services Tax — the Jersey equivalent
  of UK VAT, but at a much lower rate);
- **Social security contributions** (Class 1 / Class 2);
- **Long-Term Care contributions**;
- **No CGT, IHT, or wealth tax**.

The **High Value Residency (HVR / 2(1)(e))** scheme is a
specific regime for very-high-net-worth individuals — a
£1.25m+ annual Jersey tax payment in exchange for resident
status. See [`../immigration-residency/`](../immigration-residency/index.md)
for details.

## What "Jersey tax" means for a UK resident

If you're UK tax-resident and use Jersey structures, the
**UK rules dominate**. The Jersey-side position matters but
is secondary. Typical UK rules that govern:

- **Income tax** on your worldwide income (subject to the
  FIG regime for new arrivers — see
  [`../use-cases/client-cohorts/uk-non-dom.md`](../use-cases/client-cohorts/uk-non-dom.md));
- **CGT** on your worldwide gains;
- **IHT** on your worldwide estate (subject to residence-
  based rules post-2025);
- **Anti-avoidance rules** that may attribute Jersey-
  structure income to you regardless of the structure's
  legal form.

Jersey doesn't override UK rules. What Jersey **does** offer
is:

- **Legal certainty** of structure (Jersey trust law,
  Jersey company law);
- **No additional Jersey-level tax** if the structure is
  designed correctly (so the same wealth isn't double-taxed
  in Jersey and the UK);
- **No CGT / IHT at the Jersey level** which can reduce
  total friction;
- **Specific cross-border benefits** (international
  reporting frameworks, the
  [Jersey-UK DTA](../international/dtas.md)).

## CRS and FATCA reporting

A common misconception: that Jersey structures hide assets
from tax authorities. They don't.

- **CRS** — Jersey reports financial-account information to
  your country of tax-residence automatically (annually).
  See [`../international/crs.md`](../international/crs.md).
- **FATCA** — if you're a US person, Jersey reports to the
  IRS via the Comptroller of Revenue's FATCA reporting.

The structure is for **tax-efficient planning within
reported visibility**. Not for opacity.

## Common scenarios

### Scenario 1: UK non-dom moving to UK

The FIG regime gives you four years of relief from UK tax
on foreign income and gains. After four years, worldwide UK
tax applies. A Jersey trust holding non-UK assets has limited
benefit during the FIG window (relief already applies) and
the post-FIG benefits are materially narrower than they were
pre-2025.

### Scenario 2: UK resident with Jersey trust

The UK has specific rules that may attribute Jersey trust
income to you as the settlor (settlor-interested rules),
transferor (transfer of assets abroad rules), or beneficiary
(distribution rules). The Jersey-side position is generally
neutral; the UK-side rules determine outcome.

### Scenario 3: Non-UK resident with Jersey trust

If you're not UK tax-resident, your overall position depends
on **your home jurisdiction's** rules, not the UK's. Jersey's
zero CGT / IHT / wealth tax is a benefit; your home
jurisdiction's rules may apply differently.

### Scenario 4: Resident of a jurisdiction with no income tax (UAE, Cayman)

Your home jurisdiction has no income tax. Jersey doesn't
add Jersey-level tax (assuming the structure is properly
designed). Total tax friction can be very low — but **CRS
reporting** to your home jurisdiction still applies, and
your **other potential future tax-residence jurisdictions**
matter.

## What to ask your adviser

Five good questions:

1. **What's my current tax-residence?** (And how stable is
   it?)
2. **What's my home jurisdiction's treatment of Jersey
   structures?** (This is the load-bearing question — Jersey's
   own rules are secondary for most international clients.)
3. **How does the Jersey structure fit my anticipated
   tax-residence trajectory?** (Many international clients
   move countries; structures should anticipate.)
4. **What reporting obligations do I personally have?**
   (CRS / FATCA / home-country trust-reporting / personal
   tax returns.)
5. **What's the total cost (Jersey side + home-country side)
   of the structure?**
