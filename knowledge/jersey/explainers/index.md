# Plain-Language Explainers — Jersey

## What this section is

A set of **client-readable explainers** on common Jersey
structuring concepts. Written for the **end client** — not for
the lawyer, the RM, or the agent. Short, free of legalese
where possible, organised around questions a client actually
asks.

This is a **different style** from the rest of the corpus.
Doctrinal files speak in a treatise voice ("Article 9 of the
Trusts (Jersey) Law 1984 governs..."). Persona files speak in
an operator voice ("if you're a trust officer, here's what to
do..."). Explainers speak in a **client voice** — same accuracy
discipline, same citation discipline, but stripped of
legal-register where possible.

## Why this surface exists

Three uses:

1. **Client-meeting preparation** — an RM can hand a client
   a pre-meeting explainer to set context for the
   conversation;
2. **Post-meeting follow-up** — after explaining a concept
   verbally, the client receives a written explainer that
   reinforces the conversation;
3. **Productised research / marketing** (with care per
   tenant-neutrality) — the explainers can be the source
   material for client-facing thought-leadership content
   the wealth-management business publishes.

Per [tenant-neutrality](../../CONVENTIONS.md#tenant-neutrality):
these files are **generic** — they describe concepts, not
specific firms or specific clients. The tenant configuration
layer may customise the voice and add branding for client-
facing deployment.

## Editorial discipline

The explainer style differs from doctrinal corpus style:

| Doctrinal style | Explainer style |
|---|---|
| "Article 9 TJL 1984 governs proper-law and validity..." | "Jersey law has a built-in rule that makes Jersey law govern your trust — even if someone in another country tries to challenge it." |
| Numbered Articles, statutory citations | Concept names; citations still required (the explainer is honest, just plain-language) |
| Heavy cross-linking with full link text | Light cross-linking; cross-links explained inline |
| Length: 200-800 lines | Length: 50-200 lines, typically |

Every explainer **still cites** the doctrinal concept files
and the primary sources. The discipline is **plain-language
presentation of accurate content** — not simplification at the
cost of accuracy.
