# Client Cohort — South Africa Expat / SA HNW

## Who's in this cohort

Two related sub-cohorts:

1. **SA-resident HNW** — individuals tax-resident in SA with
   wealth profile motivating offshore structuring;
2. **SA expat** — former SA tax-residents who have ceased
   SA residence and emigrated (formally or informally), now
   resident in UK / EU / UAE / Mauritius / elsewhere.

Both interact with **SA exchange control** and **SARS
worldwide-income basis** in ways that shape Jersey structuring
choices.

## Home-jurisdiction load-bearing facts

### SA tax

- SA taxes **worldwide income** of residents (similar to UK
  pre-FIG-regime model);
- **Capital gains** taxable (inclusion rate × marginal rate);
- **Estate duty** on death (worldwide assets for SA residents,
  SA-situated assets for non-residents);
- **Donations tax** on lifetime gifts above threshold;
- **No general wealth tax**.

### SA exchange control

The **South African Reserve Bank (SARB)** operates an
**exchange-control framework** governing capital movement
out of SA. Key elements:

- **Single Discretionary Allowance** — annual amount that can
  be transferred abroad without SARB approval (current
  threshold to be verified);
- **Foreign Investment Allowance** — larger amount with tax-
  clearance certificate;
- **Special applications** for amounts exceeding allowances;
- **Tax-emigration** process when ceasing SA residence — the
  former "financial emigration" replaced post-2021 by a tax-
  residence-cessation focus.

For a structuring conversation:

- **Funds leaving SA must be allowance-compliant** (or
  pre-existing offshore funds untouched);
- **Pre-emigration planning** is materially constrained by
  these rules;
- **Post-emigration** structures hold previously-permitted
  outflows or non-SA-derived assets.

### Reporting

- **CRS** — SA-Jersey reporting in both directions;
- **SARS reporting** — SA residents report worldwide income;
  offshore structures may trigger specific SA reporting (Form
  IT3, controlled-foreign-company rules).

## Why Jersey

For SA clients, Jersey structuring typically addresses:

1. **Currency diversification** — SA rand exposure / sovereign-
   risk hedging;
2. **Estate-planning vehicles** — Jersey trusts as multi-
   generational structures sitting outside SA estate-duty
   reach for non-SA assets in defined circumstances;
3. **Investment platform** — access to multi-currency
   managed accounts and global investment products;
4. **Pre-IPO equity** — for founders / entrepreneurs;
5. **Post-emigration planning** — structures positioned
   ahead of tax-residence cessation.

## Common objections and responses

| Objection | Response |
|---|---|
| "How do I get the money out under exchange control?" | Allowance-compliant outflows over time, or specific SARB approval for larger amounts. Pre-existing offshore funds (legitimately held) can be re-structured into Jersey vehicles without new outflow. |
| "Will SARS see the structure?" | Yes — via CRS reporting. The structure is for **tax-efficient succession planning** within reported visibility. |
| "What about CFC rules?" | SA's Section 9D CFC rules attribute foreign-company income to SA-resident shareholders above thresholds. Trust attribution rules also apply. **Specific SA tax advice essential.** |
| "What happens when I emigrate?" | Tax-residence cessation triggers an "exit tax" (deemed disposal of worldwide assets at fair value). Pre-emigration planning is essential. Post-emigration, the structure's tax position changes materially. |

## Reporting and information-exchange exposure

- **CRS** — Jersey reports SA-resident account information
  to SARS.
- **SARS controlled-foreign-company rules** — apply to
  SA-resident shareholders of foreign companies above
  ownership thresholds.
- **Trust attribution rules** — SA-resident settlors of
  foreign trusts may be attributed trust income / gains.

## Common red flags / EDD triggers

- **Unverifiable offshore funds** that pre-date the client's
  CRS / exchange-control compliance history — significant
  SoW issue;
- **Politically exposed person** status (SA has a substantial
  domestic PEP population);
- **State-capture / fraud exposure** indicators in SA
  corporate or political history;
- **Sanctions adjacency** through SA business relationships.
