# Client Cohort — Non-Resident Indian (NRI)

## Who's in this cohort

"NRI" is a defined Indian-law category — an Indian citizen or
a Person of Indian Origin (PIO) / Overseas Citizen of India
(OCI) resident outside India for tax purposes.

The category spans **diverse home professions and source-of-
wealth profiles**: GCC-based professionals, US / UK / Canada-
based founders, Singapore / HK-based finance executives,
European industrialists, and the substantial Indian-diaspora
HNW population globally.

## Home-jurisdiction load-bearing facts

### Indian tax for NRIs

- **Indian-source income** is taxable in India (employment in
  India, India-property rental, India-business profits,
  India-listed equity dividends / gains);
- **Foreign-source income** is **not** Indian-taxable for an
  NRI (unlike US's worldwide-citizen-based approach);
- **Returning to India** triggers Indian residence with
  worldwide-income tax — the **R(NOR)** (Resident but Not
  Ordinarily Resident) transition status provides limited
  shelter for two further years before full residence kicks
  in.

### FEMA (Foreign Exchange Management Act)

The **RBI / FEMA framework** governs cross-border financial
flows involving Indian residents and NRIs. Key NRI account
types:

- **NRE (Non-Resident External) account** — repatriable;
  funds in foreign currency converted to INR; interest tax-
  free in India;
- **NRO (Non-Resident Ordinary) account** — rupee account for
  Indian-source income; limited repatriability (up to USD 1m
  per financial year via specific process);
- **FCNR (Foreign Currency Non-Resident) account** — foreign-
  currency deposit with Indian banks;
- **Liberalised Remittance Scheme (LRS)** — for residents
  sending money abroad, USD 250,000 per financial year (the
  NRI return-side equivalent).

### Indian succession

- **Hindu Succession Act** — for Hindus (broad category) with
  fixed inheritance shares;
- **Muslim Personal Law (Shariat) Application Act** — for
  Muslims;
- **Indian Succession Act** — residual / Christian /
  Zoroastrian / mixed-religion families;
- **Hindu Undivided Family (HUF)** — coparcenary structure
  for joint-family property under Hindu law.

The **family structure (HUF particularly)** intersects with
Jersey trusts in specific ways for some Indian client groups.

### Reporting

- **CRS** — India is a CRS participant; Jersey reports NRI
  account information to India (which then routes through
  the home / source country triangulation).
- **India-Jersey TIEA** — Tax Information Exchange Agreement
  in place; specific exchange under request.
- **Form 67 / 15CA / 15CB** for India-side reporting of
  certain outbound payments.

## Why Jersey

For NRI clients, common Jersey structuring drivers:

1. **Pre-return tax planning** — many NRIs eventually return
   to India and worldwide-income tax kicks in; pre-return
   Jersey structures can be calibrated for the transition;
2. **Family-governance vehicles** spanning multiple branches
   of family across countries (India + GCC + UK + US);
3. **Investment platform** for non-India assets;
4. **Inheritance / succession planning** for non-India
   assets, particularly where the family religious / personal
   law would apply forced-heirship-style distribution to
   Indian assets;
5. **Pre-IPO equity** — for founder clients with shares in
   non-Indian companies likely to list;
6. **Repatriation flexibility** — non-Indian assets can be
   structured for flexible repatriation depending on the
   eventual residence and tax position.

## Common objections and responses

| Objection | Response |
|---|---|
| "I pay no Indian tax as an NRI — why structure?" | Tax-efficiency is one driver. **Succession protection** and **return-planning** are typically primary. Many NRIs do eventually return and worldwide-income tax kicks in. Pre-return Jersey planning is materially easier than post-return remedy. |
| "Won't India see the structure via CRS?" | Yes. The structure is for tax-efficient planning within reported visibility, not for opacity. **The reported visibility is one of the design features**, not a problem. |
| "What about FEMA / RBI compliance?" | Funds flowing into the Jersey structure must come through allowance-compliant channels (LRS for residents, NRE / FCNR balances for NRIs). Once outside India, FEMA does not apply. **On return**, the worldwide-asset position is reportable. |
| "What about HUF property?" | Jersey trusts and HUF coparcenary structures coexist in specific ways. **Specific Indian succession / Hindu-law advice is essential** before structuring HUF assets into Jersey vehicles. |
| "Will Indian succession law (Sharia / Hindu Succession Act) override the trust?" | The [Article 9 firewall](../../trusts/firewall.md) is specifically designed to disapply foreign succession / forced-heirship rules at the validity-and-effects level for Jersey-law trusts. The protection is **fact-sensitive** to specific assets and family circumstances. |

## Reporting and information-exchange exposure

- **CRS** — Jersey reports NRI account information to India.
- **India-Jersey TIEA** — exchange under request on specific
  matters.
- **Form 15CA / 15CB** for cross-border payments.

## Common red flags / EDD triggers

- **Source-of-funds unverifiability** from Indian
  pre-NRI-status period — significant SoW issue;
- **PEP status** — substantial Indian PEP population
  (politicians, regulators, board members of state-owned
  enterprises);
- **Black-money / undisclosed-income** historical concerns —
  if the source-of-wealth has not been historically reported
  in India, the structure is unlikely to proceed without
  remediation of the underlying position;
- **High-risk-country adjacency** through business operations.
