# Client Cohorts — Jersey Wealth and Structuring Map

## What this section is

A set of **client-cohort persona files** describing the
home-jurisdiction tax, succession, and reporting context that
shapes Jersey-structuring conversations for high-net-worth
clients in specific cohorts.

This is **cohort-shaped, not role-shaped** — distinct from the
[use-cases section](../index.md), where files are organised by
the user's professional role. Here the orientation is by the
**client's home-jurisdiction profile** — UK non-dom, UAE
expat, NRI, etc. — because the relevant Jersey structuring
choices follow from the cohort's home rules and reporting
exposure.

These files are designed for the **international wealth
relationship manager** (or financial-planning adviser) who
needs to:

1. Understand the home-jurisdiction tax / succession /
   reporting context for a client in a given cohort;
2. Identify which Jersey structures fit the cohort;
3. Anticipate common objections and information-exchange
   implications.

For the substantive Jersey law each cohort connects into, the
files cross-reference the doctrinal corpus
([trusts](../../trusts/index.md),
[companies](../../companies/index.md),
[tax](../../tax/index.md),
[international](../../international/index.md)).

## Cohorts covered

| Cohort | File | Home-jurisdiction load-bearing facts |
|---|---|---|
| **UK non-domiciled** | [`uk-non-dom.md`](./uk-non-dom.md) | Remittance basis legacy, FIG regime (post-2025), IHT for excluded property, UK trust taxation |
| **UAE-resident expat** | [`uae-expat.md`](./uae-expat.md) | UAE has no personal income tax, no IHT, no CGT; Sharia succession and DIFC / ADGM common-law islands; CRS reporting; potential future home-country tax (if returning) |
| **South Africa expat / SA-resident HNW** | [`sa-expat.md`](./sa-expat.md) | SA exchange-control regime; SA tax-residency cessation; double-tax treaty Jersey-SA position |
| **US person abroad** | [`us-person-abroad.md`](./us-person-abroad.md) | Citizenship-based tax; FATCA reporting; PFIC rules; grantor-trust rules; Form 3520 / 8938 / 5471 reporting; CFC; estate tax |
| **HK / Singapore-resident expat** | [`hk-singapore-expat.md`](./hk-singapore-expat.md) | Territorial tax regimes; no IHT (HK abolished 2006, SG never had); CRS; permanent-residence transitions |
| **NRI (Non-Resident Indian)** | [`nri.md`](./nri.md) | RBI / FEMA regime; NRO / NRE / FCNR account structures; Indian succession and Hindu / Muslim personal law; India-Jersey TIEA |
| **GCC national** | [`gcc-national.md`](./gcc-national.md) | Sharia succession; family / business interlock; multi-jurisdiction asset profile across GCC + UK + EU |

The list is **representative, not exhaustive**. Each cohort
file follows the same shape so additional cohorts can be added
by an editor without architectural change.

## Common shape of a cohort file

Each cohort file covers:

1. **Who's in the cohort** — definitional precision (e.g. "UK
   tax-resident, non-UK-domiciled" is distinct from "UK
   citizen resident abroad"; "UAE expat" is distinct from
   "UAE national").
2. **Home-jurisdiction load-bearing facts** — tax,
   succession, reporting / information-exchange, exchange-
   control where relevant.
3. **Why Jersey** — what the cohort is typically seeking from
   a Jersey structure.
4. **Jersey-structure menu** — common structuring options
   for the cohort, with cross-references to the doctrinal
   corpus.
5. **Common objections and how to address them** — the
   questions clients (or their existing advisers) typically
   raise, and the corpus-cited responses.
6. **Reporting and information-exchange exposure** — CRS,
   FATCA, CARF, any cohort-specific reporting.
7. **Common red flags / risk markers** that should drive
   different structuring choices or trigger EDD under the
   AML/CFT framework.

## How this section interlocks with the doctrinal corpus

The cohort files **do not restate** Jersey law — they
**reference** it. A cohort file might say "an Article 9
firewall protects against forced-heirship claims arising
under [home jurisdiction] law" and link to
[`../../trusts/firewall.md`](../../trusts/firewall.md) for
the substantive Article 9 treatment.

This is the
[concept-vs-use-case separation](../../../KNOWLEDGE-BASE-PRINCIPLES.md)
applied at the cohort level: doctrinal files answer
*"what is the rule?"*; cohort files answer *"for this
client, what's the menu?"*.

## Tenant-neutrality

Per the
[Tenant neutrality section](../../../CONVENTIONS.md) of
CONVENTIONS.md, these files describe **cohort shapes**, not
specific clients of any firm. Worked examples use stylised /
anonymised facts ("UAE-resident founder, pre-IPO equity, kids
in UK and SA"). The cohort files travel across every tenant
deployment without modification.
