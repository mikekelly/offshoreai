# Client Cohort — UAE-Resident Expatriate

## Who's in this cohort

UAE-resident expatriates ("UAE expats") — non-UAE nationals
who hold long-term residence visas in the UAE (typically
investor / employment / Golden Visa basis) and are tax-resident
under UAE rules. This cohort is **distinct from UAE nationals**
(see [`./gcc-national.md`](./gcc-national.md)) — UAE nationals
have different succession and Sharia-application
considerations.

The cohort includes a wide professional profile — energy
executives, financial-services professionals, founders and
entrepreneurs, family-office principals. Many maintain
connections to a **prior or future home jurisdiction** (UK,
India, Pakistan, Egypt, Lebanon, South Africa, etc.) which
shapes structuring.

## Home-jurisdiction load-bearing facts

### Personal taxation in the UAE

- **No personal income tax** on individual earnings,
  investment income, or capital gains.
- **No personal IHT or wealth tax**.
- **No personal CGT**.
- **Corporate tax** (introduced 2023) applies to UAE-resident
  companies and to non-UAE companies with UAE PE; rate is
  9% above AED 375,000 of taxable income. For high-net-worth
  expat structuring, the corporate tax affects holding-vehicle
  choice but not (yet) individual planning directly.

### Succession

UAE applies **Sharia inheritance** by default to assets of
Muslim individuals — fixed-share distribution to defined heirs.

For **non-Muslim** expatriates, two routes exist:

1. **Personal Status Law option** — non-Muslim UAE residents
   can elect for their home-country law to apply to
   succession of UAE assets (subject to specific
   registration);
2. **DIFC / ADGM common-law islands** — the Dubai
   International Financial Centre and Abu Dhabi Global
   Market operate as common-law jurisdictions within the
   UAE federal structure. Wills registered with the DIFC
   Wills Service or ADGM equivalent apply common-law
   succession to assets within the relevant jurisdiction.

The interaction between these options and **Jersey trusts**
holding non-UAE assets is a frequent practitioner question.

### Reporting

- **CRS** — UAE is a CRS participant since 2017–2018; financial
  accounts in UAE are reported to the resident's home tax
  authorities where applicable (and Jersey reports to the
  UAE in respect of UAE-resident account holders).
- **No automatic FATCA reporting** of UAE accounts to the US
  unless the account holder is a US person.
- **Beneficial-ownership disclosure** in UAE-domiciled
  vehicles is increasing under federal AML rules.

### Exchange control

- **No exchange-control restrictions** for outbound transfers.

## Why Jersey

For UAE expats, Jersey structuring typically addresses:

1. **Forced-heirship defence** for Muslim expats whose home
   jurisdiction may apply Sharia or other forced-heirship
   rules — the [Article 9 firewall](../../trusts/firewall.md)
   is the load-bearing piece.
2. **Family-governance vehicles** — Jersey foundations and
   trusts as multi-generational holding structures across
   moves between countries.
3. **Pre-departure / post-return planning** — many UAE expats
   expect to return to a tax-applying jurisdiction (UK, EU,
   India, etc.); Jersey structures positioned ahead of that
   return.
4. **Pre-IPO equity** — for founder / entrepreneur clients
   with shares in companies likely to list.
5. **Currency diversification and custody** — Jersey banking
   /[investment business](../../financial-regulation/cop-ib-index.md)
   relationships offering multi-currency platforms.

## Common objections and responses

| Objection | Response |
|---|---|
| "I don't pay tax — why do I need a structure?" | Structures are for **succession protection** (forced-heirship), **family governance**, and **future-state planning** (return to a tax-applying jurisdiction). Tax efficiency is one driver; not the only one. |
| "Won't Sharia courts in my home country override a Jersey trust?" | The [Article 9 firewall](../../trusts/firewall.md) is specifically designed to disapply forced-heirship rules — including Sharia — at the validity and effects level for Jersey-law trusts. The protection is robust but **fact-sensitive** to the specific assets and family circumstances. |
| "What about UAE residency reporting?" | Jersey reports financial accounts to UAE under CRS. A UAE-resident beneficiary's information is reported back to UAE. The structure is **not** an opacity tool. |
| "What if I move back to [home country]?" | Pre-move planning is essential. The structure's tax / reporting position can change materially based on settlor / beneficiary residence. **Specific home-country advice** is required — Jersey doesn't substitute for the home-country tax adviser. |

## Reporting and information-exchange exposure

- **CRS** — UAE-Jersey bilateral reporting.
- **Home-jurisdiction reporting** — if the client retains UK
  / EU / India / SA tax-residence linkage, home-country
  reporting may apply concurrently.
- **DIFC / ADGM wills** — visible to UAE authorities and to
  any party with standing to litigate.

## Common red flags / EDD triggers

- **Politically exposed person** status of the client or
  family members (large overlap between UAE expat population
  and politically connected families across MENA);
- **Sanctions exposure** — UAE has business relationships with
  several countries under UK / EU / US sanctions; structuring
  involving any sanctions-touching counterparty triggers EDD;
- **Source-of-wealth opacity** — large transfers from
  pre-UAE-residency jurisdictions where SoW is hard to
  document;
- **High-risk-jurisdiction nexus** under the
  [Handbook country-risk list](../../aml-cft/amlh-high-risk-countries.md).
