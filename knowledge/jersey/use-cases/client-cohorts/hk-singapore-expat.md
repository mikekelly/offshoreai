# Client Cohort — HK / Singapore Expatriate

## Who's in this cohort

Expatriates resident in **Hong Kong SAR** or **Singapore** —
two of Asia's principal financial centres and home to large
international professional populations.

Common professional profiles: senior financial-services
executives, family-office principals, Asian-focused PE / VC
managers, regional tech founders, multi-jurisdictional
HNW families.

## Home-jurisdiction load-bearing facts

### Tax regimes

Both HK and Singapore operate **territorial tax** systems —
broadly, only income sourced in the jurisdiction is taxable
there. This is the **most distinctive cohort fact**.

**Hong Kong:**

- **Salaries tax** on HK-source employment income;
- **Profits tax** on HK-source profits of a trade / business;
- **Property tax** on HK-source rental income;
- **No CGT** (general, with limited "trading-gains" exceptions
  for property);
- **No IHT / estate duty** (abolished 2006);
- **No GST / VAT**.

**Singapore:**

- **Income tax** on SG-source income (with specific rules on
  foreign-source income received in SG);
- **No CGT** generally;
- **No IHT / estate duty** (never had);
- **GST** at 9% (different from CGT considerations).

### Permanent residence and citizenship

Many HK / Singapore expats hold **permanent residence** or
**citizenship** in a different jurisdiction (UK, US, India,
EU member state). This **dual-status** profile shapes
structuring — the home-jurisdiction-of-citizenship may have
worldwide-income rules even where current residence does not.

### Reporting

- **CRS** — both HK and Singapore are CRS participants;
  Jersey reports financial-account information to both.
- **FATCA** — both HK and Singapore have IGAs with the US;
  US-person reporting applies.
- **Beneficial-ownership** transparency increasing in both
  jurisdictions.

## Why Jersey

For HK / Singapore expats, common drivers:

1. **Sovereign / political risk hedging** — particularly
   relevant for HK residents post-2020;
2. **Long-term wealth-preservation structures** outside Asian
   regional rules;
3. **Family-governance vehicles** for multi-jurisdiction
   family members;
4. **Currency diversification** beyond HKD / SGD / USD;
5. **Forced-heirship defence** where the family's country of
   citizenship / origin applies forced-heirship rules
   ([`../../trusts/firewall.md`](../../trusts/firewall.md));
6. **Pre-IPO equity** — for tech-founder / financial-
   executive clients with shares in companies likely to list.

## Common objections and responses

| Objection | Response |
|---|---|
| "I don't pay tax in HK / SG on offshore income — why structure?" | Tax-efficiency is one driver, not the only one. **Family governance**, **succession protection**, and **sovereign-risk hedging** are typically the primary motivators in this cohort. |
| "Does the citizenship country (UK / US / etc.) tax this?" | Citizenship-based-tax jurisdictions (US, Eritrea) tax worldwide regardless of residence — see [`./us-person-abroad.md`](./us-person-abroad.md). For non-citizenship-based countries (UK / EU / India), the position depends on the client's residence trajectory and prior-residence linkages. |
| "What about Asian sovereign-risk concerns?" | Jersey structures hold non-Asian assets in a stable common-law jurisdiction with [Privy Council final appeal](../../legal-system/court-of-appeal.md). The constitutional anchor in the UK (subject to JCPC review) is a relevant comfort point. |
| "Reporting back to HK / SG?" | Yes via CRS. Visibility is part of the design, not a problem to be solved. |

## Reporting and information-exchange exposure

- **CRS** — Jersey reports HK-resident and SG-resident
  account information to the IRD / IRAS respectively.
- **FATCA** — applies to US-person account holders.
- **CARF** (when in force) — crypto-asset reporting will add
  layer.

## Common red flags / EDD triggers

- **PEP status** — common in HK / SG financial-services
  population (regulator board roles, listed-company
  directorships);
- **Sanctions adjacency** through business relationships in
  high-risk Asian jurisdictions;
- **High-risk-country nexus** through business operations
  in jurisdictions on the
  [Handbook country-risk list](../../aml-cft/amlh-high-risk-countries.md).
