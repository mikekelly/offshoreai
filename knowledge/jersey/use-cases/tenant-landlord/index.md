# Tenant / Landlord — Use Cases

## Who this section is for

You are either a **Jersey residential tenant** or a **Jersey
residential landlord**, dealing with day-to-day questions
about a tenancy. You might be:

- A tenant facing a rent rise, a notice to quit, a deposit
  dispute, a repair request that's been ignored, or eviction
  proceedings;
- A landlord trying to recover possession, manage arrears,
  comply with the condition-report regime, or work out what
  you can and can't put in a tenancy agreement;
- A letting agent trying to do all of the above on behalf of
  a landlord.

Your primary statute is the **Residential Tenancy (Jersey)
Law 2011** (RTJL), with the 2025 amendment law and the 2014
Condition Reports Order, 2026 Rent Tribunal Order, and other
secondary instruments. The Petty Debts Court is the usual
forum for tenancy disputes.

This is the **first resident-facing persona** being built out
in detail — it's the trigger for the coverage audit at
[`../../COVERAGE-AUDIT.md`](../../COVERAGE-AUDIT.md).
