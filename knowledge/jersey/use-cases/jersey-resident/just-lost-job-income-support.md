# Q: Can I claim Income Support if I've just lost my job?

## The short answer

**Possibly — but it depends on three things**, all of
which must be true for every adult in your household:

1. **You meet the 5-year residence test** (or 10 years
   cumulatively) — Income Support under the Income
   Support (Jersey) Law 2007 has a substantial residence
   requirement;
2. **You're either looking for work actively, or
   exempt** from the work expectation;
3. **Your household income and assets are below the
   thresholds** — Income Support is means-tested.

If you've contributed Social Security as an employee,
you may **also** be entitled to **short-term incapacity
allowance** (if you're unwell rather than just
unemployed) or contributory benefits under the Social
Security (Jersey) Law 1974 — these don't require a means
test but require a contribution record.

## The proper answer

### Step 1 — Check the residence test

This is the most common stumbling block. To claim Income
Support, **every adult** in your household must have:

- **Lived in Jersey continuously for at least 5 years**
  immediately before the claim; **or**
- **Lived in Jersey for 10 years cumulatively** at some
  point in the past.

If you're a recent arrival in Jersey (under 5 years),
you typically **cannot** claim Income Support — and this
applies even if you have a Jersey job, paid taxes here,
and have a Jersey-born partner. The 5-year rule is
strict.

If you're a returning Jersey resident, specific
flexibility may apply — see
[`../../welfare/income-support-eligibility.md`](../../welfare/income-support-eligibility.md).

### Step 2 — The work expectation

Income Support is **not** an unemployment benefit in the
old sense. Adults in the household are expected to:

- **Be in full-time paid work**; or
- **Be looking for work actively** (engaging with
  Customer and Local Services employment support); or
- **Be exempt** from the work expectation (pension age,
  disability, sickness, caring responsibilities).

If you've just lost your job and are job-searching,
you remain within the work-expectation framework but
will be required to:

- **Engage** with CLS employment-support officers;
- **Accept** referrals to suitable jobs;
- **Participate** in training where directed;
- **Demonstrate** active job-search.

Non-cooperation can lead to **sanctions** — reduction or
suspension of benefit.

### Step 3 — The means test

Your household's resources (income, assets) are compared
against your household's needs:

**Resources** counted include:

- **Any earned income** of any adult in the household;
- **Pensions, contributory benefits**;
- **Savings and investments above thresholds**;
- **Other income** (rents, dividends).

**Disregards**:

- A portion of earned income is disregarded (work
  incentive);
- The family home is disregarded;
- Specific personal assets disregarded.

**Needs** include:

- Adult component(s);
- Child component(s);
- Housing component (rent assistance);
- Health, childcare, disability components as
  applicable.

If **needs > resources**, the difference is paid as
Income Support.

### Other benefits to consider

Depending on circumstances:

- **Short-term incapacity allowance** — if you're unwell
  rather than just unemployed, and have a contribution
  record;
- **Contributory pension** — if you're at pension age;
- **Maternity allowance** — if you're a new parent;
- **Carer's allowance / disability components** — if
  applicable.

These contribute-record-based benefits **don't require a
means test** but do require a sufficient contribution
record under the Social Security (Jersey) Law 1974.

### Practical sequence

1. **Use** the eligibility calculator at gov.je;
2. **Gather documents** — proof of identity, residence,
   recent income, household composition, rent details;
3. **Apply** online or in person at CLS;
4. **Interview** at CLS if required;
5. **Decision** within prescribed timescales;
6. **Engage** with employment support if subject to the
   work expectation;
7. **Right of review and appeal** if refused — see
   [`../../welfare/appeals.md`](../../welfare/appeals.md).

### Timing

Income Support claims can be backdated only narrowly.
**Apply promptly** — ideally before the previous month's
wages run out — to avoid a gap.

### What if you don't qualify?

If you fail the residence test, you may have access to
narrower help:

- **Citizens Advice Jersey** — emergency advice and
  signposting;
- **Charity grants** — Jersey has several long-standing
  charities for those in temporary hardship;
- **Parish welfare** — historic parish-level support, in
  some cases;
- **Discretionary CLS payments** — for emergencies
  outside the formal Income Support scheme.

## Common situations

### "I've just been made redundant"

- Check **redundancy pay** under your contract and EJL
  2003 — see [`../../employment/index.md`](../../employment/index.md);
- Apply for **Income Support** if you meet the tests;
- Engage **CLS employment support**;
- Update **insurance** if vehicle / private health
  policies were employer-funded.

### "I'm unwell and can't work"

- Get a **medical certificate**;
- Apply for **short-term incapacity allowance** under SS
  1974 (contribution-record based, no means test);
- Apply for **Income Support** if your household income
  is below threshold;
- Engage with **CLS Disability/Carers team** if relevant.

### "My partner has a good job — am I still eligible?"

- Income Support is **household-based**;
- Your partner's income counts against your needs;
- May still qualify for specific components (e.g.
  childcare, medical);
- **Eligibility calculator** at gov.je will give an
  indication.

### "I'm new to Jersey, just lost my visa-sponsoring job"

- **Residence test** typically not met;
- **Income Support** unlikely to be available;
- **Immigration status** may be at risk — see
  [`../../immigration-residency/index.md`](../../immigration-residency/index.md);
- Specialist advice essential.

## What to do tomorrow morning

1. **Use the gov.je eligibility calculator** — gives an
   indication in 15 minutes;
2. **Gather your documents**;
3. **Apply** online or visit Customer and Local Services;
4. **Get medical certification** if relevant for short-
   term incapacity allowance;
5. **Engage** Citizens Advice Jersey if you need help
   with the application;
6. **Check** redundancy entitlement under your contract
   and the Employment Law 2003;
7. **Don't delay** — claims can only narrowly be
   backdated.
