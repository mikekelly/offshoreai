# Royal Court Procedure for Non-Jersey Lawyers

## The question

You're a non-Jersey lawyer with a matter involving the **Jersey
Royal Court**. What procedure should you expect?

## The short answer

The Royal Court is Jersey's principal court, presided over by
the **Bailiff** (judge of law) with **Jurats** (judges of fact)
in many matters:

- **Originating processes**: Order of Justice (most civil),
  Representation (trust / probate / administrative);
- **Divisions**: Samedi (civil), Inferior Number (criminal /
  trial), Superior Number (serious criminal);
- **Procedure**: similar to English High Court but with Jersey-
  specific terminology and customary-law influences;
- **Rights of audience**: only Jersey Advocates (and Solicitors
  in some matters);
- **Appeal**: Jersey Court of Appeal, then Privy Council.

You **must instruct Jersey counsel** — no rights of audience
for non-Jersey lawyers.

## The proper answer

### Court structure

- **Royal Court** sits with:
  - **Bailiff / Deputy Bailiff / Commissioner** as presiding
    judge of law;
  - **Jurats** as judges of fact (typically 2 for Samedi /
    Inferior Number; more for Superior Number);
- **Court of Appeal**: senior English-qualified judges;
- **Privy Council**: final appeal.

### Originating processes

#### Order of Justice (OOJ)

- **Civil proceedings** typically begun by OOJ;
- **Hostile** in tone — equivalent to claim form;
- **Service** on defendant;
- **Pleadings** follow.

#### Representation

- **Non-hostile** or **administrative** proceedings;
- **Trust applications** (Article 51, Article 47, etc.);
- **Probate** matters;
- **Court applications** for directions / blessings.

### Procedural overview

#### Pleadings

- **OOJ** and **answer**;
- **Reply** if relevant;
- **Particulars** of any specific matter;
- **Affidavits** of evidence.

#### Disclosure

- **Standard disclosure** of documents per Royal Court rules;
- **Specific disclosure** orders as needed;
- **Privilege** protected.

#### Interlocutory applications

- **Summons** for specific orders;
- **Mareva / freezing orders**;
- **Norwich Pharmacal** (third-party disclosure);
- **Anton Piller** equivalents;
- **Heard** in chambers or open court depending on substance.

#### Trial

- **Witness evidence** typically by affidavit, with cross-
  examination on stand;
- **Documentary evidence** through agreed bundles;
- **Skeleton arguments** filed in advance;
- **Oral submissions**;
- **Reserved judgment** typical.

#### Appeal

- **From Royal Court** to Court of Appeal;
- **Leave** required for some matters;
- **From Court of Appeal** to Privy Council with leave.

### Specific Jersey concepts

#### Beddoe relief

- **Trustee's cost-protection** application;
- **Specific Jersey practice**;
- **Apply** before incurring significant litigation costs.

#### Article 51 directions

- **Trust-administration guidance**;
- **Standard procedure** for momentous trust decisions;
- **Public Trustee v Cooper** categories.

#### Article 47 mistake

- **Set aside** of mistaken trust decisions;
- **Wider** than English *Pitt v Holt*;
- **Tax-trigger** mistakes commonly addressed.

### Costs

- **Indemnity costs** more common in trust litigation than
  English practice;
- **Beddoe-protected** trustee costs from fund;
- **Standard basis** for hostile civil litigation;
- **Hostile parties** typically face cost orders if
  unsuccessful.

### Engagement with Jersey counsel

For non-Jersey lawyers:

#### Instructing

- **Jersey Advocate** for Royal Court advocacy;
- **Jersey Solicitor** for some matters and procedural support;
- **Major firms** (Mourant, Carey Olsen, Ogier, Bedell,
  Walkers, Appleby, etc.);
- **Boutique** firms for specific matters.

#### Working relationship

- **Lead counsel** typically Jersey;
- **You may attend** hearings as observer;
- **Joint drafting** of pleadings and witness statements;
- **Local-law issues** require Jersey-counsel sign-off.

### Foreign-court coordination

For multi-jurisdictional matters:

- **Letters rogatory** for evidence in Jersey;
- **Recognition of foreign judgments** through Royal Court
  process;
- **Jersey-jurisdiction clauses** generally honoured (post-
  Crociani);
- **Article 9 firewall** for trust matters.

### Timeline expectations

- **Simple interlocutory** matters: weeks;
- **Standard civil trial**: 12–24 months;
- **Trust directions** applications: 1–3 months typically;
- **Complex commercial trial**: 18–36 months;
- **Appeal**: 6–12 months;
- **Privy Council**: 12–18 months.

### Costs reality

Jersey litigation is **expensive**:

- **Senior Advocate** rates £400–600+ per hour;
- **Senior Solicitor** similar;
- **Trial**: typically £100k–£500k+ in costs;
- **Complex case**: well into seven figures.

## What to do next

For a Jersey matter:

1. **Engage** Jersey counsel early;
2. **Brief** them on the foreign-jurisdiction context;
3. **Coordinate** strategy;
4. **Establish** working protocols;
5. **Manage** client expectations on timeline / cost.

## Pitfalls

- **Treating Royal Court as identical to English High Court**:
  important differences;
- **Right-of-audience** mistakes;
- **Procedural rules** missed;
- **Cost shock** for clients unfamiliar with Jersey rates.
