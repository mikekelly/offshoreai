# Beneficiary Information Rights — Schmidt v Rosewood Applied in Jersey

## The question

You're advising a **beneficiary** seeking trust information from
a Jersey trustee. What does Schmidt v Rosewood mean in Jersey?

## The short answer

The Privy Council's **Schmidt v Rosewood (2003)** decision is
the foundation of Jersey law on beneficiary information rights:

- **No absolute right** to trust documents;
- **Royal Court supervisory jurisdiction** to decide;
- **Discretionary** balancing of beneficiary interests vs trust
  / other-beneficiary confidentiality;
- **Trust deed**: presumption **for** disclosure (constitutional
  document);
- **Letters of wishes**: presumption **against** disclosure
  (private to trustee's discretion);
- **Accounts**: presumption **for** disclosure;
- **Other documents**: case-by-case.

Beneficiaries seek information through the **Royal Court**'s
supervisory jurisdiction.

## The proper answer

### Schmidt v Rosewood

The Privy Council on appeal from Isle of Man — adopted in
Jersey:

- **No proprietary right** of beneficiary to trust documents;
- **Information access** is governed by **Court supervisory
  jurisdiction**;
- **Discretion** applied case-by-case;
- **Multiple factors** balanced.

### Jersey application

Jersey Royal Court has applied Schmidt extensively:

- *Re Internine Trust* and subsequent cases;
- **Document-by-document** approach;
- **Presumptions** by document type;
- **Confidentiality** of co-beneficiaries considered;
- **Trustee discretion** respected within limits.

### Document categories

#### Trust deed

- **Presumption FOR disclosure**;
- Constitutional document of the trust;
- **Beneficiary entitled** typically.

#### Trust accounts

- **Presumption FOR disclosure**;
- Reasonable transparency on financial state.

#### Letters of wishes

- **Presumption AGAINST disclosure**;
- **Private** to trustee's discretion;
- **Settlor's confidential** guidance.

#### Trustee minutes / deliberations

- **Generally NOT disclosed**;
- **Confidentiality** of trustee process;
- **Specific examples** may be disclosed.

#### Investment / distribution records

- **Variable**;
- **Specific transactions** affecting the beneficiary often
  yes;
- **Confidential** about other beneficiaries' affairs.

### Factors the Court considers

- **Class of beneficiary** (named vs discretionary);
- **Information sought** and purpose;
- **Confidentiality** affecting other beneficiaries;
- **Trustee's reasons** for refusing;
- **Settlor's** intentions;
- **Practical impact** of disclosure.

### Procedural route

1. **Request** to trustee for documents;
2. **Trustee response** (provide / refuse / partial);
3. If refused or inadequate: **Royal Court application**;
4. **Representation** with affidavit;
5. **Court determines** in supervisory jurisdiction;
6. **Order** for disclosure / refusal / specified documents.

### Data Protection (Jersey) Law 2018 overlap

For **personal data** of the beneficiary:

- **DPJL SAR** available;
- **Schmidt-protected** documents may still be exempt;
- **Coordinated** approach;
- See [`../trust-officer/dpjl-vs-schmidt.md`](../trust-officer/dpjl-vs-schmidt.md).

### Foreign-lawyer considerations

For non-Jersey counsel advising a beneficiary:

- **Don't promise** absolute disclosure rights — discretionary;
- **Engage Jersey Advocate** for any litigation;
- **Coordinate** with foreign-proceedings strategy;
- **Document-by-document** approach in any application.

### Common scenarios

#### Beneficiary requesting trust deed

- **Typically disclosed**;
- **Foundation** for any further claim.

#### Beneficiary requesting trust accounts

- **Typically disclosed**;
- **Financial transparency** expected.

#### Beneficiary requesting letters of wishes

- **Typically not** disclosed;
- **Settlor's confidential** guidance protected.

#### Beneficiary requesting distribution decisions

- **Variable**;
- **Reasons for refusal** sometimes provided;
- **Court application** may achieve more disclosure.

#### Beneficiary alleging breach

- **Pre-claim disclosure** required to substantiate;
- **Court application** for tailored disclosure relevant to
  alleged breach.

### Beneficiary's evidence

To support disclosure application:

- **Status** as beneficiary;
- **Purpose** of information sought;
- **Specificity** of request (broad fishing disfavoured);
- **Trustee's response** to the request.

## What to do next

For a beneficiary information matter:

1. **Make formal request** to trustee;
2. **Receive response**;
3. **Assess** whether to litigate;
4. **Engage Jersey counsel** for application;
5. **File Royal Court application** if needed;
6. **Manage** post-disclosure use.

## Pitfalls

- **Overbroad requests**: less likely to succeed;
- **Fishing expedition**: courts reluctant;
- **Strategic disclosure use**: can backfire;
- **Foreign-court coordination**: Schmidt application may
  interact with parallel proceedings.
