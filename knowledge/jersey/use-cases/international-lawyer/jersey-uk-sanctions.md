# Jersey vs UK Sanctions Regimes — Divergences

## The question

Your client's matter involves **UK sanctions** and **Jersey
sanctions** simultaneously (typical for UK-Jersey connected
structures). Where do they differ?

## The short answer

Jersey and UK sanctions are **closely aligned** but not
identical:

- **Jersey sanctions** under the **Sanctions and Asset-Freezing
  (Jersey) Law 2014** and Orders;
- **UK sanctions** under the **Sanctions and Anti-Money
  Laundering Act 2018 (SAMLA)** and Regulations;
- **UN sanctions** implemented in both;
- **Jersey aligns** with UK sanctions in practice but Jersey
  is **technically separate**;
- **Specific Jersey designations** possible (rare);
- **OFSI** is UK authority; **JFSC and AG** are Jersey
  authorities.

For practitioners: assume **substantive alignment** but
**check specific Jersey Orders** for any divergences.

## The proper answer

### Constitutional position

- Jersey is a **Crown Dependency** with **separate
  legislative competence**;
- **UK extends** some sanctions to Jersey through Orders in
  Council;
- **Jersey legislates** its own sanctions framework under SAFL
  2014;
- **Practical alignment** with UK is policy choice;
- **Theoretical divergence** is possible.

### SAFL 2014 framework

Jersey's principal sanctions statute:

- **Implements** UN sanctions in Jersey law;
- **Implements** UK sanctions to which Jersey is party (most);
- **Standalone Jersey** designations possible;
- **Enforcement** through Royal Court and JFSC.

### Common features

Both regimes:

- **Designate** specific persons / entities;
- **Freeze** assets;
- **Prohibit** dealings;
- **Licence** specific exceptions;
- **Criminal penalties** for breach.

### Divergences in practice

#### Designation timing

- **UK designation** typically precedes Jersey;
- **Jersey adopts** within short window;
- **Brief window** of mismatch.

#### Specific Jersey designations

- **Rare** but possible;
- **Locally-specific** concerns (e.g. local-fraud
  designations);
- **Practitioners check** Jersey-specific lists.

#### Licensing

- **OFSI licences** apply within UK regulatory perimeter;
- **Jersey licences** through Jersey authority;
- **May not be coterminous** — licence-by-licence check needed.

#### Enforcement

- **OFSI** enforces UK in UK;
- **JFSC + Attorney General** in Jersey;
- **Civil financial penalties** structure differs.

### Practical compliance

For dual-regime exposure:

#### Screening

- **Multi-list screening** including Jersey + UK + UN + EU + US;
- **Specialist tools** typically used (Refinitiv, Dow Jones,
  etc.);
- **Ongoing re-screening**.

#### Match handling

- **Both jurisdictions** notified of match;
- **Coordinated** freeze and reporting;
- **Licence applications** in both as needed.

#### Cross-jurisdictional transactions

- **Comply with both** regimes;
- **Most-restrictive** applies as practical rule;
- **Group entities** in different jurisdictions coordinate.

### Cross-jurisdictional issues

#### Group with UK + Jersey entities

- **Same designation** treated by both entities;
- **Asset freeze** coordinated;
- **Licence** in one may not cover dealings in the other.

#### Trust with UK + Jersey beneficiaries

- **Each beneficiary** screened against both regimes;
- **Distribution** to designated beneficiary blocked under
  both;
- **Coordinated** information-sharing.

#### Service-provider exposure

- **Jersey provider** to UK clients: dual exposure;
- **Multi-jurisdictional** clientele requires multi-list
  awareness;
- **OFAC** also typically relevant (US extraterritorial
  reach).

### Foreign-lawyer practical advice

For UK-Jersey cross-border matters:

- **Don't assume identity** of regimes — close but not same;
- **Check specific designations** in each;
- **Coordinate** licence applications;
- **Engage Jersey counsel** for Jersey-specific issues;
- **Maintain** dual screening / monitoring.

### Recent evolution

Post-Brexit:

- **UK** developed independent sanctions framework (SAMLA
  2018);
- **Jersey** aligned with UK rather than EU;
- **EU** has separate framework;
- **Triple alignment** (UN + UK + Jersey) common.

### Specific scenarios

#### Russia-related sanctions

- **UK Russia regulations** extensive;
- **Jersey** implements parallel measures;
- **Asset-freeze** coordination;
- **Licence** for specific dealings (legal fees, basic needs).

#### Designated PEP

- **UK designation** as PEP / sanctions target;
- **Jersey adopts** quickly;
- **Coordinated** management.

#### Trade sanctions

- **Goods exports** restrictions;
- **Jersey + UK** typically aligned;
- **Different** national approaches possible.

## What to do next

For dual-regime matter:

1. **Map** Jersey and UK sanctions exposure;
2. **Screen** against both lists;
3. **Coordinate** any freeze / reporting;
4. **Apply** for licences in both as needed;
5. **Document** the compliance approach.

## Pitfalls

- **Assuming identity**: regimes close but distinct;
- **Specific designation** mismatches;
- **Licence** coverage gaps;
- **Reporting** to one but not the other.
