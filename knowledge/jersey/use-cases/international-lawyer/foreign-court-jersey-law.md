# Foreign Court Applying Jersey Law — What to Expect

## The question

Your foreign-court proceeding involves a **Jersey-law point**
(governing law of a contract, of a trust, of a company). How
will the foreign court approach it?

## The short answer

The foreign court:

- **Applies its own choice-of-law rules** to determine that
  Jersey law applies;
- **Treats Jersey law as a matter of fact** to be proved by
  expert evidence;
- **Receives Jersey-law expert opinions** from each party
  (typically Jersey Advocates / King's Counsel);
- **Decides** the Jersey-law point based on the expert evidence
  and its own analysis.

**Expert evidence** is key — both sides instruct Jersey-qualified
experts who provide written reports and oral testimony.

## The proper answer

### Choice of law

For a contract / trust / company governed by Jersey law:

- **Foreign court** applies its own choice-of-law rules;
- **Express choice** of Jersey law usually respected;
- **Closest connection** in absence of express choice;
- **Foreign-court-of-seat** rules apply.

If Jersey law applies, the foreign court must **determine**
the Jersey-law point.

### Foreign law as fact

In most common-law jurisdictions:

- **Foreign law** is treated as a **matter of fact**;
- **Must be pleaded** by parties relying on it;
- **Must be proved** by evidence — typically expert testimony;
- **Court doesn't take judicial notice** of foreign law
  (with limited exceptions for accessible UK / Jersey law).

In some civil-law jurisdictions:

- **Foreign law** is a **matter of law** the court applies;
- **Court may research** foreign law itself;
- **Expert evidence** still typically supplied.

### Expert evidence

#### Choosing the expert

- **Jersey Advocate** (preferred) or **English barrister**
  qualified in Jersey law;
- **Senior practitioner**: King's Counsel, partner-level
  Advocate;
- **Experience** with the specific Jersey-law area;
- **Independent** from the substantive matter.

#### Expert role

- **Written expert report**;
- **Joint statements** with opposing experts (in many
  procedures);
- **Cross-examination** at trial;
- **Court ultimately decides** but expert assists.

#### Joint expert reports

- **Joint statement** of agreed points;
- **Areas of disagreement** identified;
- **Court** focuses on disputed issues.

### Specific scenarios

#### Trust dispute in English Family Division

- **Jersey trust**: Article 9 firewall analysis;
- **English court** typically receives expert Jersey-law
  evidence;
- **Familiar** Jersey concepts: Article 9, Schmidt, Article
  47;
- **English court** decides Jersey law applies but may apply
  English orders personally to settlor / beneficiary.

#### Commercial contract dispute (Jersey-law governed)

- **English Commercial Court** or other forum;
- **Jersey-law expert** evidence;
- **Construction principles** of Jersey contract law;
- **Foreign-court applies** Jersey law as proved.

#### M&A dispute over Jersey TopCo

- **Foreign court** (acquirer's jurisdiction);
- **Jersey-law-specific** corporate-law points;
- **Companies Law 1991** provisions interpreted by expert;
- **Foreign court** applies as proved.

### Expert opinion structure

A typical expert report addresses:

- **Expert qualifications** and experience;
- **Documents reviewed**;
- **Specific Jersey-law questions** the court has identified;
- **Jersey-law principles** explained for foreign court;
- **Application** of those principles to the facts;
- **Opinion** on the legal outcome under Jersey law;
- **Sources** (statutes, cases, commentary).

### Common pitfalls

#### For party arguing Jersey-law point

- **Inadequate expert** (junior, unfamiliar with area);
- **Failure to plead** Jersey law applies;
- **Reliance on English** law assumptions where Jersey diverges;
- **Underestimating** Article 9 / Article 47 / specific
  doctrines.

#### For foreign court

- **Difficulty** with Jersey-specific terminology;
- **Inadvertent** application of foreign law where Jersey
  differs;
- **Comity considerations** in trust matters.

### Where Jersey diverges from English law

Key areas:

- **Article 9 firewall** (trusts) — wider than English;
- **Article 47 mistake** (trusts) — wider than English Pitt v
  Holt;
- **Article 9A reserved powers** (trusts) — explicit Jersey
  recognition;
- **Companies Law** — solvency-statement-based capital
  decisions vs UK CA 2006;
- **Customary-law** influences in property and succession.

### Practical strategy

For a Jersey-law point in foreign-court litigation:

- **Engage Jersey counsel early** to identify the substantive
  Jersey-law position;
- **Choose expert** carefully;
- **Plead** Jersey-law applicability clearly;
- **Brief** the foreign-court judge with adequate background;
- **Coordinate** between substantive lead counsel and Jersey
  expert.

## What to do next

For a foreign-court Jersey-law issue:

1. **Identify** the Jersey-law point;
2. **Engage** Jersey counsel;
3. **Choose** an expert;
4. **Plead and prove** Jersey law applies;
5. **Coordinate** between counsel and expert;
6. **Present** the Jersey-law analysis to foreign court.

## Pitfalls

- **Treating Jersey as English** without checking divergences;
- **Inadequate expert evidence**;
- **Pleading failures** on choice-of-law;
- **Court confusion** without proper Jersey-law background.
