# Sharing AML Information with Jersey Trustees / Counsel

## The question

You're advising on a matter with **AML / suspected money-
laundering** dimensions. You need to share information with the
**Jersey trustee or Jersey counsel**. What's the regime?

## The short answer

Jersey law permits **regulated-sector information sharing**
under POCL 1999 with appropriate safeguards:

- **MLRO-to-MLRO** sharing within the regulated sector;
- **Legal-professional-privilege** preserved;
- **Tipping-off** rules apply — careful drafting essential;
- **Authorisation** for specific disclosures sometimes needed;
- **Cross-border** information sharing through specified
  channels.

Don't share AML / SAR-related information freely — engage Jersey
MLRO / counsel and follow proper procedures.

## The proper answer

### POCL section 36 (information sharing)

Jersey's Proceeds of Crime Law 1999 permits **regulated-sector
information sharing**:

- **Between regulated firms** in defined circumstances;
- **For specified purposes** (preventing money laundering /
  terrorist financing);
- **Subject to confidentiality** obligations;
- **Without breaching tipping-off** if proper.

### Tipping off

The principal constraint:

- **Don't disclose** to subject of SAR / investigation that one
  has been made / is contemplated;
- **Don't disclose** to subject that an investigation might be
  underway;
- **Even indirect** disclosure can be tipping off.

For cross-jurisdictional communication:

- **Substance of the SAR** generally not shared with parties
  outside the regulated chain;
- **Operational facts** shared with counsel as needed for
  advice;
- **Document** the basis for any communication.

### Legal professional privilege

Privilege survives the AML framework:

- **Communications** between client and lawyer for legal advice
  remain privileged;
- **Privilege** prevails over information-sharing obligations in
  most cases;
- **Crime-fraud exception**: privilege doesn't cover
  communications **in furtherance** of crime / fraud.

### Specific scenarios

#### Foreign-counsel-to-Jersey-trustee on suspected matter

- **Don't** ask the trustee directly about specific SAR status;
- **General concern** can be raised in legal-advice context;
- **Trustee MLRO** handles the internal Jersey side;
- **Cross-border coordination** through proper channels.

#### Foreign-lawyer-to-Jersey-counsel

- **Legal-privilege protected** communication for legal advice
  purposes;
- **Substantive AML information** subject to careful handling;
- **Jersey counsel** advises on Jersey-side compliance.

#### Trust restructuring with potential AML history

- **Disclosure of past concerns** with care;
- **Jersey trustee MLRO** processes any internal review;
- **Don't tip off** affected family members;
- **Document** the diligence process.

### Information exchange channels

#### Regulator-to-regulator

- **JFSC ↔ foreign regulators** through MoUs and information-
  exchange agreements;
- **FIU ↔ foreign FIUs** through Egmont Group and bilateral
  channels;
- **Court-supervised** in some matters.

#### Firm-to-firm

- **Regulated firms** can share under POCL section 36;
- **Specific safeguards** apply;
- **Documented purposes**.

#### Lawyer-to-lawyer

- **Legal privilege** preserved;
- **Substantive AML info** with care;
- **Coordination** for cross-border AML strategy.

### Specific JFSC guidance

The JFSC AML / CFT Handbook addresses cross-border sharing:

- **Group entities** can share within the group;
- **Specific outside-firm** sharing under safeguards;
- **Don't compromise** SARs or FIU processes;
- **Document** the basis.

### Practical advice

For cross-jurisdictional matters with AML dimensions:

- **Engage Jersey counsel** early;
- **Discuss** information-flow protocols at the outset;
- **Document** what's shared and why;
- **Respect** tipping-off rules;
- **Preserve privilege**.

### Common pitfalls

#### Over-sharing

- Tipping-off can be inadvertent through detailed disclosure;
- **Less is more** in cross-jurisdictional AML communication;
- **Specific** discussions through proper channels.

#### Under-sharing

- Critical information missed because of over-caution;
- **Regulated firms** are permitted to share for AML purposes;
- **Don't paralyse** cross-jurisdictional cooperation.

#### Privilege confusion

- Privilege complicates information sharing;
- **Crime-fraud exception** narrow but real;
- **Document** the legal-advice purpose of communications.

## What to do next

For an active matter:

1. **Engage Jersey counsel** and Jersey MLRO if applicable;
2. **Agree** information-flow protocol;
3. **Share** within proper framework;
4. **Document** the sharing;
5. **Respect** tipping-off and privilege rules.

## Pitfalls

- **Tipping off** through casual conversation;
- **Privilege waiver** through inappropriate disclosure;
- **Over-sharing** with unrelated third parties;
- **Under-sharing** paralysing investigation.
