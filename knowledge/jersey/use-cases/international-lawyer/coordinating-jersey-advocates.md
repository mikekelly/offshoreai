# Coordinating with Jersey Advocates — Workflow and Expectations

## The question

You're a foreign-lawyer leading a matter with a **Jersey
litigation / advisory dimension**. How do you coordinate
effectively with **Jersey Advocates / Solicitors**?

## The short answer

For effective coordination:

- **Engage** a Jersey firm — typically partner-led;
- **Set clear scope** for Jersey-side work;
- **Establish** communication cadence (weekly / as needed);
- **Coordinate** strategy with substantive lead;
- **Allow Jersey lead** on Jersey-procedural and Jersey-law-
  substantive issues;
- **Track** costs and deliverables.

Major Jersey firms are **accustomed** to working with foreign
leading counsel — established workflows exist.

## The proper answer

### Engagement structure

#### Lead vs supporting role

Typically:

- **Foreign-jurisdiction lead** counsel runs overall strategy;
- **Jersey counsel** leads on Jersey-law and Jersey-court
  matters;
- **Coordinated team** approach;
- **Single client-facing** lead typically.

For pure Jersey matters:

- **Jersey counsel** leads;
- **Foreign counsel** supports / advises client on broader
  context.

### Initial engagement

#### Scope setting

- **Identify** Jersey-specific issues;
- **Engage** appropriate Jersey firm / fee-earner;
- **Engagement letter** with Jersey firm;
- **Allocate** work between foreign and Jersey teams.

#### Conflicts

- **Jersey conflict check** required;
- **Many parties** in offshore world means more conflicts;
- **May require** different Jersey firm than first choice.

### Workflow

#### Regular communication

- **Weekly calls** typical for active matters;
- **Monthly updates** for steady matters;
- **Email** for routine matters;
- **Document-sharing platform** for joint working.

#### Document drafting

- **Jersey-law documents** drafted by Jersey counsel;
- **Foreign-law documents** by foreign counsel;
- **Joint documents** (SPAs, settlement agreements) drafted
  collaboratively;
- **Final review** by both teams.

#### Royal Court litigation

- **Jersey Advocate** runs advocacy;
- **Foreign counsel** observes / supports;
- **Joint preparation** of witness evidence;
- **Strategy coordination**.

### Cost management

#### Setting expectations

- **Estimate at engagement** based on known scope;
- **Update** as matter evolves;
- **Cap** specific work if appropriate;
- **Track** against budget.

#### Billing

- **Hourly rates** typical;
- **Senior Advocate** £500–800+;
- **Senior Solicitor** £400–600+;
- **Junior fee-earner** £200–400;
- **Disbursements** (court fees, experts) separately.

#### Cost review

- **Monthly** billing review;
- **Discuss** any scope changes;
- **Address** concerns promptly.

### Strategy coordination

#### Areas of overlap

- **Cross-border strategy**: foreign counsel leads;
- **Jersey-procedural**: Jersey counsel leads;
- **Substantive Jersey law**: Jersey counsel provides;
- **Client communication**: typically lead counsel.

#### Strategic decisions

- **Joint analysis** by both teams;
- **Lead counsel decides** with Jersey input;
- **Document** decisions for client.

### Client-facing approach

- **Lead counsel** typically client-facing;
- **Jersey counsel** for specific Jersey matters;
- **Joint calls / meetings** for major decisions;
- **Client knows** both teams.

### Specific scenarios

#### Cross-border trust litigation

- **Foreign court** proceedings led by foreign counsel;
- **Jersey court** proceedings led by Jersey Advocate;
- **Coordinated** strategy;
- **Shared** discovery and witness preparation.

#### M&A transaction

- **English / NY law SPA** lead-drafted by foreign counsel;
- **Jersey-law-specific** opinions / documents by Jersey
  counsel;
- **Closing** coordinated.

#### Restructuring

- **Multi-jurisdictional** restructuring with Jersey component;
- **Jersey procedural** (scheme of arrangement / continuance)
  by Jersey counsel;
- **Foreign-jurisdiction procedures** by local counsel;
- **Tightly coordinated**.

### Common workflow issues

#### Time zones

- **Jersey + UK**: same time zone, easy;
- **Jersey + US**: 5–8 hour gap;
- **Jersey + Asia**: significant gap.

#### Capacity

- **Senior Jersey** fee-earners are in high demand;
- **Plan** for their availability;
- **Don't expect** instant turnaround on complex matters.

#### Cultural

- **Jersey firms** can be more formal than some foreign
  counterparts;
- **Document everything**;
- **Professional courtesy** matters.

### Building long-term relationships

For repeat-business foreign counsel:

- **Develop** ongoing relationship with specific Jersey firm /
  fee-earner;
- **Joint training** / knowledge-sharing;
- **Reciprocal referrals**;
- **Trust-based** working relationship.

This produces **better outcomes** than transactional one-off
engagements.

## What to do next

For an active matter:

1. **Engage** appropriate Jersey firm;
2. **Set scope** and budget;
3. **Establish** communication;
4. **Coordinate** on strategy and deliverables;
5. **Manage** costs and timelines.

For ongoing practice:

- **Build** key Jersey relationships;
- **Stay current** on Jersey-law developments;
- **Joint training** with Jersey teams.

## Pitfalls

- **Inadequate scoping**: open-ended Jersey work expensive;
- **Communication gaps**: leads to duplication / errors;
- **Cost surprises**: budget discipline essential;
- **Strategy mismatch**: foreign-led strategy ignoring Jersey-
  specific factors.
