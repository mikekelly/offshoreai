# Jersey TopCo in UK / US M&A — What to Know

## The question

You're advising on an M&A transaction where the target /
acquirer has a **Jersey TopCo**. What Jersey-specific points
should you know?

## The short answer

Jersey TopCos are routine in UK / US M&A. Key Jersey points:

- **No Jersey CGT** on share-sale gain;
- **No Jersey stamp duty** on share transfers;
- **Share-transfer mechanics** under Article 42 — straightforward;
- **Squeeze-out** under Part 18 if 90% acceptance threshold met;
- **Statutory merger** under Part 18B (sometimes used);
- **Continuance** under Part 18C for jurisdiction migration;
- **Companies Law 1991** provisions vs UK Companies Act 2006
  differences in some areas.

For most M&A: instruct Jersey counsel for the Jersey-specific
legal opinions and documents.

## The proper answer

### Common transaction types

#### Share-purchase of Jersey TopCo

- **Standard route**;
- **SPA governed** typically by English / NY law;
- **Jersey-law-specific** points handled through Jersey counsel;
- **Closing** via share-transfer in Jersey company.

#### Take-private of listed Jersey TopCo

- **Tender offer** + squeeze-out at 90% (Article 117);
- **Scheme of arrangement** under Article 125 as alternative
  (with 75% / majority threshold);
- **Both routes** routinely used for Jersey listed companies.

#### Reverse merger / RTO

- **Jersey TopCo** acquires public-listed shell or vice versa;
- **Standard mechanics**;
- **Jersey-law-specific** corporate steps.

#### Strategic merger of equals

- **Part 18B statutory merger** sometimes used;
- **Or** share-exchange / scheme;
- **JFSC consent** if cross-border merger.

### Jersey legal opinions

For M&A involving Jersey TopCo:

- **Capacity opinion** confirming TopCo has authority to enter
  documents;
- **Authority opinion** on signatories;
- **Solvency opinion** if relevant;
- **Tax opinion** confirming Jersey position;
- **Enforceability opinion** on Jersey-law documents.

Jersey opinions follow **TIBJL standards** (Jersey law-firm
practice).

### Specific Jersey provisions to map

#### Distributions

- **Article 115** distribution mechanism (solvency statement);
- **No UK profits-available-for-distribution** test;
- **More flexibility** for distributions.

#### Capital reductions

- **Article 61A** solvency-statement-based reduction;
- **No court approval** needed if solvency statement;
- **Faster** than UK equivalent.

#### Mergers

- **Part 18B statutory merger** — court-free in most cases;
- **JFSC consent** if cross-border;
- **Cleaner** than UK CA 2006 mergers in some respects.

#### Squeeze-out

- **90% acceptance threshold** (Article 117);
- **Same as UK 90%**;
- **Procedural** similar to UK Companies Act 2006 Part 28
  Chapter 3.

### Pre-acquisition due diligence

#### Standard Jersey points

- **Memorandum and articles** verification;
- **Share register** and **register of charges**;
- **Director / secretary** identities;
- **Annual confirmation statements** filed;
- **Beneficial-ownership** filings (FS(DPI)(J)L 2020);
- **CRS / FATCA** classifications;
- **JFSC registrations** for any regulated activity;
- **Economic substance** compliance.

#### Litigation / regulatory checks

- **Royal Court** search;
- **JFSC** enforcement record;
- **Public Registry** charges and registered interests.

### Tax considerations

#### At Jersey level

- **0% Jersey rate** on most TopCos;
- **No CGT** on share-sale;
- **No stamp duty** on share transfers.

#### Shareholder-level

- **UK CGT** for UK-resident sellers;
- **US capital gains** for US-person sellers;
- **Foreign-jurisdiction** tax for each shareholder.

#### Acquirer-level

- **No Jersey tax** on acquisition;
- **Acquirer-jurisdiction** tax — group consolidation,
  withholding etc.

### Post-acquisition integration

Options:

- **Maintain** Jersey TopCo (most common);
- **Migrate** via continuance to acquirer's jurisdiction;
- **Merge** via statutory merger;
- **Wind up** if not needed.

### Common pitfalls

#### Pre-deal

- **CDD gaps**: beneficial-ownership filings sometimes
  imperfect;
- **Substance shortfalls**: relevant for tax positioning;
- **JFSC consents**: needed for some changes of control.

#### Closing

- **Share-transfer documentation**: must be Jersey-form;
- **Register update**: timely;
- **Counterparty notifications**: bank, regulators, etc.

#### Post-closing

- **Integration timing** for restructuring;
- **Tax-step-up** issues at acquirer level vary by jurisdiction;
- **Continued Jersey compliance** during transition.

### Listed-target specifics

For listed Jersey TopCo M&A:

- **Takeover Code** of listing venue applies (UK Code if LSE);
- **Jersey-law-specific** procedural overlays;
- **Squeeze-out / scheme** as primary mechanisms;
- **Joint engagement** of UK + Jersey counsel typical.

## What to do next

For an M&A transaction:

1. **Engage** Jersey counsel for Jersey-specific work;
2. **Coordinate** with UK / US / other lead counsel;
3. **Jersey opinions** drafted as standard;
4. **Specific Jersey-law** documents as required;
5. **Close** with proper procedural compliance.

## Pitfalls

- **Treating Jersey as English law**: similar but different;
- **Procedural gaps** at closing;
- **Tax-modelling** failures for cross-border shareholders;
- **Beneficial-ownership** discovery gaps in DD.
