# Expert Evidence on Jersey Law — How It Works

## The question

You need **expert evidence on Jersey law** in foreign-court
proceedings. Who can give it, what does it cover, and how is it
managed?

## The short answer

Expert evidence on Jersey law:

- **Given by** Jersey-qualified Advocate (preferred) or
  English-qualified barrister with Jersey-law experience;
- **Senior level**: King's Counsel, senior Advocate, or
  partner-level practitioner;
- **Written report** as primary form;
- **Oral testimony** at trial as needed;
- **Joint expert** report common to narrow issues;
- **Cross-examination** standard.

Choose expert with **specific subject-matter expertise** —
trust law, company law, tax, etc.

## The proper answer

### Who can give expert evidence

#### Jersey Advocates

- **Senior practitioners** at Jersey law firms;
- **Established reputation** in the specific area;
- **Royal Court** experience;
- **Standard choice** for Jersey-law expertise.

#### English barristers / KCs

- **Some KCs** with Jersey practice;
- **Often instructed** alongside Jersey Advocate;
- **Particularly** for cross-border-influenced areas
  (Article 9, trust law);
- **English bar** familiarity for foreign-court purposes.

#### Academic experts

- **Less common** for Jersey law (small academic community);
- **Some respected academic** commentary available;
- **Practitioner expertise** typically preferred.

### Choosing your expert

#### Subject-matter expertise

Match expert to topic:

- **Trust law**: Advocate with trust-litigation experience;
- **Company law**: Advocate with corporate experience;
- **Tax**: Tax-specialist Advocate or tax adviser;
- **Property**: Conveyancing-experienced Advocate;
- **Criminal**: Specialist criminal Advocate.

#### Independence

- **Not** previously involved in the underlying matter;
- **Not** acting for either party in the substantive dispute;
- **Genuine independence** to give objective opinion.

#### Reputation

- **Court-accepted** prior expert opinions;
- **Credibility** with foreign-court judges;
- **Senior status** in Jersey legal community.

#### Practical considerations

- **Availability** for the litigation timeline;
- **Hourly rate** and total fee estimate;
- **Willingness** to be cross-examined;
- **Communication** skills for court presentation.

### Expert-report structure

A standard Jersey-law expert report:

#### Front matter

- **Expert qualifications and experience**;
- **Documents reviewed**;
- **Questions** the court has identified;
- **Methodology**.

#### Substantive

- **Jersey-law background** for foreign-court judge;
- **Specific Jersey-law principles** relevant;
- **Application** to the facts;
- **Sources** (statutes, cases, commentary);
- **Opinion** on the legal outcome.

#### Back matter

- **CV** of expert;
- **Statement** of compliance with court's rules on expert
  evidence;
- **Declaration** of independence.

### Joint reports

In many procedures:

- **Experts meet** before trial;
- **Identify** agreed and disagreed points;
- **Joint statement** of agreed conclusions;
- **Individual statements** on disagreed points.

This **narrows the issues** for the court.

### Cross-examination

At trial:

- **Lead counsel** cross-examines opposing expert;
- **Tests** the expert's qualifications, reasoning, and
  conclusions;
- **Challenges** the soundness of the opinion;
- **Court** weighs expert evidence in final analysis.

### Foreign-court procedural rules

Each foreign court has its own procedural rules for expert
evidence:

#### English High Court

- **CPR Part 35** governs expert evidence;
- **Permission** typically needed before expert reports
  exchanged;
- **Limits** on number of experts per party;
- **Joint statements** routinely ordered.

#### US Federal Court

- **FRCP Rule 26** governs;
- **Daubert** standard for admissibility;
- **Discovery** of expert opinions;
- **Cross-examination** central.

#### Other jurisdictions

- Each has its own rules — engage local counsel familiar with
  procedural requirements.

### Common practical issues

#### Cost

- **Senior expert**: £500–1,000+ per hour;
- **Total cost**: £25,000–100,000+ for major case;
- **Joint-expert work** can reduce cost.

#### Timing

- **Allow time** for report preparation (typically 6–12 weeks);
- **Joint-expert process** adds time;
- **Schedule** around trial dates carefully.

#### Coordination

- **Lead counsel** brief expert thoroughly;
- **Documents** provided as needed;
- **Expert independence** preserved;
- **Don't coach** but ensure factual completeness.

### Specific Jersey-law areas needing expert evidence

#### Trust law

- **Article 9 firewall**;
- **Article 47 mistake**;
- **Article 9A reserved powers**;
- **Schmidt-Rosewood information rights**;
- **Beneficiary classes** and discretionary powers.

#### Company law

- **Squeeze-out / scheme** mechanisms;
- **Article 74 directors' duties**;
- **Solvency-statement** distributions;
- **Mergers / continuance**.

#### Tax

- **Zero-ten** classification;
- **Substance compliance**;
- **Distribution rules**;
- **Pillar Two** application.

#### Customary law

- **Property** succession;
- **Specific Norman-customary** survivals.

## What to do next

For expert evidence engagement:

1. **Identify** Jersey-law point and required expertise;
2. **Shortlist** experts;
3. **Engage** the chosen expert;
4. **Brief** with materials;
5. **Manage** report drafting and joint-expert process;
6. **Prepare** for cross-examination.

## Pitfalls

- **Junior expert**: opinion may be discounted;
- **Inadequate independence**: opinion struck;
- **Late engagement**: insufficient prep time;
- **Cross-examination weakness**: undermines case.
