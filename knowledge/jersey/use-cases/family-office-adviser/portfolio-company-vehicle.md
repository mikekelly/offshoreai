# Underlying Portfolio Company — Jersey LLC, Partnership, or Company?

## The question

The family trust / foundation needs to **hold investment
portfolio assets** through an underlying vehicle. Should it be
a **Jersey company**, **Jersey LP**, **Jersey LLC**, or
something else?

## The short answer

For a typical family-investment portfolio held below a trust /
foundation:

- **Jersey company** (no-par-value private company) — most
  common, simple, zero-ten 0% rate at company level;
- **Jersey LP / SLP / ILP** — useful where partnership tax
  characterisation matters (especially with US-investor
  family);
- **Jersey LLC** (recent Jersey form combining corporate and
  partnership features) — increasingly used for specific tax
  characterisations.

Choose based on **home-country tax characterisation** of family
investors and **operational simplicity** preferences.

## The proper answer

### Jersey company

A typical structure:

- **Trust holds 100% of shares** in a Jersey company;
- **Company holds investments**;
- **Distributes dividends** up to trust as cash needed;
- **Company taxed at 0%** under zero-ten;
- **Operational vehicle** for asset management.

#### Advantages

- **Simple** — well-understood;
- **0% Jersey tax** under zero-ten;
- **Limited liability** at vehicle level;
- **Familiar** to counterparties;
- **Easy** to contract / hold accounts.

#### Disadvantages

- **Foreign-CFC rules** may attribute company income to family
  members in their home jurisdictions;
- **PFIC** issues for US-person beneficiaries;
- **Substance** requirements under 2019 Substance Law if
  holding passive investments — generally meet "pure equity-
  holding" reduced test, but check.

### Jersey LP (limited partnership)

Structure:

- **Trust as limited partner** with most economic interest;
- **Trustee-owned LLC or company as general partner**;
- **LP holds investments**;
- **Tax-transparent** in most home jurisdictions.

#### Advantages

- **Tax-transparent** for many investor jurisdictions;
- **No CFC** treatment if partnership classification respected;
- **No PFIC** for US persons if partnership treatment;
- **Flexibility** in distribution mechanics.

#### Disadvantages

- **General-partner unlimited liability** (mitigated by
  corporate GP);
- **Less familiar** to some counterparties;
- **More complex** documentation.

### Jersey LLC

The Jersey **LLC** (Limited Liability Company) was introduced
relatively recently:

- **Body corporate** with separate legal personality;
- Can be **tax-transparent** in some jurisdictions (default to
  pass-through similar to US LLC);
- **Familiar** to US-investor families used to US LLC structures;
- **Increasingly used** for cross-border family structures.

#### Advantages

- **Familiar** to US-investor families;
- **Tax-transparent** in many cases;
- **Limited liability**;
- **Operational simplicity**.

#### Disadvantages

- **Newer** — less case law and counterparty familiarity;
- **Foreign-jurisdiction recognition** of LLC tax-transparency
  varies.

### Decision factors

| Factor | Jersey Co | Jersey LP | Jersey LLC |
|---|---|---|---|
| Tax transparency | No | Yes typically | Often |
| Familiarity | High | Moderate | Lower |
| US-person investors | CFC / PFIC issues | Tax-transparent | Tax-transparent (typically) |
| UK-resident investors | Foreign-Co rules | Partnership rules | Variable |
| Operational simplicity | High | Moderate | High |
| Family-office familiarity | High | Mid | Lower |

### Specific scenarios

#### UK-resident family trust below

- **Jersey company** typical;
- UK CFC / income attribution applies;
- **Manage** distributions for UK tax efficiency.

#### US-person beneficiaries

- **LP or LLC** often preferred for pass-through;
- **Jersey company** triggers PFIC for US-persons;
- **Foreign grantor trust** above company may mitigate but
  complex.

#### Mixed UK / US / EU family

- **Compromise structure** depending on dominant tax
  jurisdictions;
- Sometimes **multiple parallel vehicles** for different
  investor classes;
- **Substance considerations** for each.

### Substance considerations

Under the Substance Law 2019:

- **Pure equity-holding** companies meet a **reduced substance
  test** (basic company-law compliance + adequate people /
  premises);
- **Other relevant activities** (financing, IP, headquarters)
  need fuller substance;
- A typical family-holding company is **pure equity-holding** —
  reduced test.

### Investment-management arrangement

Usually:

- **Investment manager** (Jersey-regulated or external)
  appointed;
- **Discretionary mandate** with risk parameters;
- **Trustee oversight** of overall investment strategy;
- **Periodic reporting**.

## What to do next

For a new portfolio vehicle:

1. **Confirm** investor / beneficiary jurisdictions;
2. **Model** tax characterisation of each option;
3. **Choose** vehicle type;
4. **Engage** Jersey counsel for setup;
5. **Establish** investment-management arrangements;
6. **Operate**.

For existing structures:

- **Review** characterisation periodically;
- **Restructure** if tax landscape changes materially.

## Pitfalls

- **Single-jurisdiction tax modelling**: multi-juris families
  need multi-juris analysis;
- **Substance under-attention**: even reduced test requires
  compliance;
- **Operational complexity** of LPs underestimated;
- **LLC novelty** in some jurisdictions.
