# Jersey vs Guernsey — Choosing Between Sister Jurisdictions

## The question

You're a family-office adviser considering whether to use
**Jersey** or **Guernsey** for a particular structure. Both
are Channel Islands Crown Dependencies with similar regimes.
What drives the choice?

## The short answer

For **most matters**, the choice is **largely indifferent**
— both produce similar practical outcomes. Specific drivers:

- **Existing relationships** with service providers in one
  jurisdiction;
- **Specific** legal advantages (Article 9A reserved powers
  in Jersey; PCC origin in Guernsey);
- **Sectoral** preferences (Jersey banking is larger;
  Guernsey captives are larger);
- **Cost** considerations in some configurations;
- **Specific** client preferences.

Most international families use **one** jurisdiction
consistently for their main structures, with the other
considered for specific niches.

## The proper answer

### Constitutional / political

Both jurisdictions are:

- **Crown Dependencies** (parallel status);
- **Outside UK and EU**;
- **Self-governing** for domestic matters;
- **Privy Council** appeals;
- **Similar** UK relationship and constitutional independence.

No material political difference between Jersey and Guernsey
for client purposes.

### Tax

| Feature | Jersey | Guernsey |
|---|---|---|
| Corporate tax | Zero-ten | Zero-ten |
| Personal tax | 20% | 20% |
| Substance Law | 2019 | 2019 |
| Pillar Two | MCIT 2025 | MTT 2025 |
| CRS / FATCA | Yes | Yes |
| **GST** | **5%** | **None** |
| CGT | None | None |
| IHT | None | None |

The **GST difference** is the principal tax difference —
Jersey has 5% GST on most goods / services; Guernsey has
none. For most family-office structures (holding investment
assets, not consuming local goods / services), the GST
difference is **immaterial**.

### Trust law

Both jurisdictions have:

- **Modern** trust statutes (Jersey TJL 1984, Guernsey TGL
  2007);
- **Reserved powers** for settlors;
- **Firewall** protection from foreign claims;
- **Mistake / hastiness** judicial relief;
- **Schmidt v Rosewood** information rights principles.

**Specific** Jersey advantages:

- **Article 9A** is well-developed with significant case law;
- **Article 9 firewall** robust and tested;
- **Article 47B-J** mistake regime is wider than Guernsey
  equivalent in some respects.

**Specific** Guernsey advantages:

- **TGL 2007** structurally more modern;
- **Specific** Guernsey statutory provisions newer.

For **most family-office trust structures**, both jurisdictions
work.

### Foundation law

Both have foundations:

- **Jersey** FJL 2009;
- **Guernsey** FGL 2012 (later);
- **Similar** core features.

Both work for **family / philanthropic** foundation
purposes.

### Companies

| Feature | Jersey | Guernsey |
|---|---|---|
| Statute | CJL 1991 | CGL 2008 |
| Share types | Par / no-par | Par / no-par |
| Cell companies | PCC / ICC | PCC (origin) / ICC |
| Specific features | Article 115 cash-flow solvency | Specific Guernsey provisions |

For **holding companies** in family-office context:

- **Either** works;
- **Cell companies** for specific segregation needs.

### Funds

| Feature | Jersey | Guernsey |
|---|---|---|
| Public funds | CIFL 1988 | PoIL 2020 |
| Expert funds | Expert Fund | Class Q |
| Private funds | JPF | PIF |
| AIFMD | NPPR | NPPR |

For **family investment vehicles**:

- **Either** works;
- **JPF / PIF** comparable for closed-circle family
  investments.

### Banking

| Feature | Jersey | Guernsey |
|---|---|---|
| Sector size | Larger | Smaller |
| Major banks | More international banks | Fewer |
| Specialist private banking | Strong | Strong |

For **family-office banking**:

- **Jersey** has larger banking sector with more options;
- **Guernsey** has substantial private-banking offering;
- **Specific** bank choice matters more than jurisdiction.

### Insurance / captives

| Feature | Jersey | Guernsey |
|---|---|---|
| Captive market | Smaller | **Largest in Europe** |
| PCC origin | Adopted | **Originated 1997** |
| Specialist captive | Smaller industry | Larger industry |

For **family-office captive insurance**:

- **Guernsey** is typically preferred;
- **Larger** specialist market;
- **More** experienced advisers;
- **Cost** advantages in some cases.

### Service providers

Most **major firms** have offices in **both** jurisdictions:

- **Mourant** — both;
- **Carey Olsen** — both;
- **Ogier** — both;
- **Walkers** — both;
- **Bedell Cristin** — Jersey-only currently;
- **Appleby** — both;
- **Specialist** independents in each;
- **Specific** Guernsey-only firms (Babbé, Collas Crill,
  others).

This **cross-jurisdictional** capability means clients can
often work with the **same advisers** regardless of which
jurisdiction is chosen.

### Regulators

| Feature | JFSC | GFSC |
|---|---|---|
| Approach | Substantively aligned with international standards | Substantively aligned with international standards |
| Speed | Comparable | Comparable |
| Quality | High | High |
| Cooperation | Strong with GFSC | Strong with JFSC |

No material difference in regulator quality.

### Costs

**Comparable** between jurisdictions:

- **TCB / FSB** fees similar;
- **Royal Court** fees similar;
- **Professional advice** fees similar;
- **Specific** cost differences in narrow areas.

### Specific client preferences

Some clients prefer one over the other based on:

- **Personal** relationships in one;
- **Specific** previous structures in one;
- **Cultural** preferences;
- **Travel** convenience (Jersey has more daily flights to
  London).

## When to consider Guernsey specifically

Strong factors favouring Guernsey:

- **Captive insurance** plans (specialist market);
- **Specific** existing Guernsey advisory relationships;
- **Specific** Guernsey legal advantages for the structure;
- **PCC for insurance** (Guernsey origin);
- **Cost** considerations in specific configurations.

## When Jersey is the natural choice

Strong factors favouring Jersey:

- **Article 9A reserved powers** for trust planning;
- **Specific** Jersey trust case law authoritative;
- **Larger banking** sector with more options;
- **Specific** existing Jersey relationships;
- **HVR** programme for family principal residence in Jersey;
- **Specific** Jersey legal advantages for the structure.

## Mixed approach

Many international families use **both**:

- **Jersey** for principal trusts and holding companies;
- **Guernsey** for captive insurance;
- **Jersey** for specific operating subsidiaries;
- **Guernsey** for specific fund vehicles.

This **mixed approach** maximises advantages of each.

## What to do next

For a specific family-office matter:

1. **Identify** the specific vehicles needed;
2. **Compare** Jersey vs Guernsey for each;
3. **Consider** specific legal / commercial advantages;
4. **Consider** existing relationships and continuity;
5. **Decide** on the principal jurisdiction;
6. **Consider** mixed approach for specific niches;
7. **Engage** appropriate advisers in chosen jurisdiction(s).

## Pitfalls

- **Defaulting** to one jurisdiction without analysis;
- **Mixing** without coordination (different jurisdictions
  for same family can complicate);
- **Over-emphasising** minor differences;
- **Under-emphasising** existing relationships and continuity;
- **Specific** unintended interactions between Jersey and
  Guernsey structures.
