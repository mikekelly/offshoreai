# When is a Private Trust Company (PTC) Right?

## The question

The client family is considering a **Private Trust Company
(PTC)** as trustee. When is the PTC the right vehicle vs a
professional independent trustee?

## The short answer

A PTC works well when the family:

- Wants **direct control** over trustee decisions;
- Is **large enough** to support professional governance
  internally;
- Has **multiple trusts** that benefit from common governance;
- Values **continuity** beyond any specific service provider;
- Can bear **administrative cost** of running the PTC.

Independent trustee is better when:

- Family is **small / simple**;
- **Independence** is valued over control;
- **Cost-efficiency** is paramount;
- Family lacks **governance capacity** internally.

## The proper answer

### PTC structure recap

```
[Foundation OR purpose trust] (orphan vehicle)
    |
    owns shares of
    |
[PTC — Jersey company]
    |
    is trustee of
    |
[Family trust(s)]
```

The **orphan vehicle** ensures the PTC is **independently
controlled** at the legal level, despite family directors.

### Sizing thresholds

PTCs typically make sense for families with:

- **Multi-generation** wealth (3+ generations);
- **Multiple trusts** to administer;
- **Substantial asset base** (often £50–100m+ to justify
  fixed costs);
- **Engaged family principals** willing to serve as directors;
- **Complex** wealth structures benefiting from coordinated
  governance.

### Cost analysis

PTC costs:

- **Jersey company** annual fees;
- **JFSC exemption** filings;
- **Orphan-vehicle** maintenance;
- **Service-provider** support (regulated TCB providing
  administration);
- **Director fees** (independent / professional directors);
- **Accounting and audit**;
- **Legal and compliance**;

Total typically **£50,000–200,000 annually** for a moderate
PTC, more for complex structures.

Compare with **independent trustee fees** (typically 0.5–1% of
trust assets, capped at higher levels). PTC starts to be
cost-effective above **£20–50m** of assets.

### Governance considerations

The PTC needs:

- **Board of directors** mixing family, advisers, professionals;
- **Decision-making discipline**;
- **Meeting cadence** (typically quarterly minimum);
- **Documented decisions** for fiduciary defence;
- **Conflict-management** when family members are directors and
  beneficiaries.

### Advantages

#### Family control

- **Family members at the table** for trustee decisions;
- **Family priorities** can be articulated and respected;
- **Speed** of decision-making for family matters;
- **Knowledge** of family-specific context.

#### Multi-trust coordination

- **One PTC** administers multiple family trusts;
- **Coordinated governance** across trusts;
- **Cost-sharing** for administration;
- **Strategic** family-wealth planning across trusts.

#### Continuity

- **PTC persists** beyond any service-provider change;
- **Family-governance arrangement** stable across generations;
- **Brand and identity** of the family wealth structure.

### Disadvantages

#### Cost

- **Fixed costs** higher than independent trustee at smaller
  scales;
- **Multiple parties** to coordinate;
- **Administrative burden** on family.

#### Governance demands

- **Family director qualifications** needed;
- **Decision-making discipline** required;
- **Conflict management** more complex when family is involved;
- **Succession planning** for family-director roles.

#### Regulatory exposure

- **JFSC exemption** must be maintained;
- **Director personal liability** for misconduct;
- **Fit-and-proper** standards for family directors;
- **AML / CFT** obligations.

### Common scenarios

#### Multi-generation wealth family

- **Strong case** for PTC;
- Coordinated governance across multiple trusts;
- Family principals engaged.

#### Operating-business family

- PTC holds shares in family operating company / holding
  vehicles;
- Coordinated governance of business and personal wealth.

#### Single discretionary family trust under £20m

- **PTC overkill**;
- Independent trustee more cost-effective.

#### Pre-IPO family with future complex needs

- PTC may make sense **anticipating** complexity;
- Setup before scale to embed governance.

### Setting up the PTC

1. **Structure design** with Jersey counsel;
2. **Orphan vehicle** (foundation or non-charitable purpose
   trust);
3. **PTC incorporation** as Jersey company;
4. **Family directors** and professional directors identified;
5. **JFSC exemption** application (for family-only PTC);
6. **Regulated-TCB administration** arrangement;
7. **Existing trusts** transferred to PTC trusteeship.

### Working with a PTC

For an adviser to a PTC family:

- **Coordinate** with PTC directors;
- **Coordinate** with regulated-TCB administrator;
- **Document** family wishes for the PTC to consider;
- **Participate** in governance where appropriate.

## What to do next

For a PTC consideration:

1. **Assess** family governance capacity;
2. **Model** cost vs independent trustee;
3. **Identify** family directors and advisers;
4. **Engage** Jersey counsel;
5. **Set up** orphan vehicle, PTC, and administration;
6. **Migrate** existing trusts.

## Pitfalls

- **Family-director capability**: must understand fiduciary
  duties;
- **Service-provider drift**: PTC needs strong administrative
  partner;
- **Drift to wider client base**: family-only exemption lost;
- **Succession**: family-director transitions need planning.
