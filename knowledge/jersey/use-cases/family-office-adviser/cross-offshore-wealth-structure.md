# Cross-Offshore Wealth Structure for HNW Family

## The question

You're advising a **high-net-worth international family** on a
**multi-offshore wealth structure** — using Jersey, plus
specific other jurisdictions (Cayman, Bermuda, BVI, Guernsey,
IoM). How do you optimise?

## The short answer

**Allocate by function**:

- **Jersey** — main trust at top (Article 9A reserved
  powers), banking, HVR residence if applicable;
- **Cayman** — investment fund vehicles, STAR trusts for
  orphan structures;
- **Bermuda** — captive insurance if material;
- **BVI** — holding companies for specific assets;
- **Guernsey** — captive insurance, alternative trust
  jurisdiction;
- **IoM** — eGaming or life insurance if applicable.

**Avoid over-complication** — most families need only **2-3
jurisdictions** in practice.

## The proper answer

### Function-to-jurisdiction matching

For a typical HNW family structure, common functions:

#### Top-level governance / trust

The **ultimate trust** at the top of the structure:

- **Jersey** typical choice — Article 9A reserved powers,
  Article 9 firewall, established case law;
- **Guernsey** as alternative — similar features;
- **Cayman STAR** possible for orphan-style governance;
- **IoM** alternative.

**Driver**: settlor reserved powers, firewall against
foreign claims, family-governance flexibility.

#### Investment vehicles

For **investment holding**:

- **Jersey** companies for sophisticated structures;
- **BVI BCs** for volume / cost-efficient holdings;
- **Cayman** for fund-style vehicles;
- **Specific** jurisdiction for specific assets.

**Driver**: cost, speed, regulatory simplicity.

#### Operating businesses

For **operating businesses** the family owns:

- **Jurisdiction-specific** (often onshore where the
  business operates);
- **Offshore holding company** above the operating business;
- **Specific** Jersey topco common for IPO-ready structures.

#### Captive insurance

For **family-office captive insurance**:

- **Bermuda** for material captives;
- **Guernsey** for established / smaller;
- **Cayman** for specific types;
- **Jersey / IoM** for niche;
- **Driver**: scale, specialty.

#### Banking

For **family banking relationships**:

- **Jersey** for European-focused banking;
- **Singapore / Switzerland** for Asia / Europe;
- **Cayman** for USD-heavy;
- **Specific** private banks selected.

#### Real estate holdings

For **family real-estate portfolio**:

- **Jurisdiction-specific** holding companies;
- **Jersey property** through specific Jersey vehicles
  (subject to CHWL);
- **Specific** UK/EU real estate through appropriate
  structures.

#### Charitable giving

For **family philanthropy**:

- **Jersey foundation** or **charitable trust**;
- **Guernsey** alternative;
- **UK charity** for UK donors;
- **US charity** for US donors;
- **Specific** to family's giving pattern.

### Typical family-office structure

A **representative** HNW family structure:

```
Top: Jersey Family Trust (Article 9A reserved powers)
     |
     +-- Jersey holding co (sophisticated)
     |   |
     |   +-- Jersey-managed family office
     |   +-- Specific operating businesses
     |
     +-- Cayman investment fund (carry / co-invest)
     |
     +-- BVI BC 1 (specific asset 1)
     +-- BVI BC 2 (specific asset 2)
     +-- BVI BC 3 (specific asset 3)
     |
     +-- Bermuda captive insurance (if material)
     |
     +-- Charitable foundation (Jersey or other)
```

### Considerations by family circumstance

#### Family principally European

- **Jersey** dominant;
- **Guernsey** alternatives;
- **EU integration** considerations.

#### Family principally US

- **Cayman** for fund interests;
- **BVI** for holding;
- **Bermuda** for insurance;
- **Jersey** less common but possible;
- **Specific** US tax considerations (grantor trust,
  PFIC, etc.).

#### Family principally Asian

- **Cayman** for hedge fund interests;
- **BVI** for holding (especially Hong Kong / China
  connections);
- **Jersey** less common but used for some Hong Kong /
  Singapore HNW;
- **Singapore** as onshore base often.

#### Family multi-regional

- **Diversified** structure across multiple offshore;
- **Specific** functions per jurisdiction.

### Tax considerations

#### Foreign tax base

- Settlors / beneficiaries pay tax in their home
  jurisdictions on income / gains;
- Offshore structure does not avoid home-country tax in
  most cases;
- **Specific** CFC, attribution, deemed-distribution rules
  apply.

#### Substance

- **All** in-scope entities need substance;
- **Significant** ongoing cost;
- **Cannot** be ignored;
- **Substance** requirements differ by activity / location.

#### Pillar Two

- **For €750m+ groups**, 15% minimum;
- **Family-office** structures rarely reach this threshold;
- **Specific** consideration for very large families.

#### Tax treaty access

- **Limited** offshore treaty networks;
- **Specific** treaties available for specific routes;
- **MLI** PPT considerations for treaty-shopping.

### Substance and governance

For each in-scope entity:

- **Local director(s)**;
- **Local board** meetings;
- **Local** decision-making documentation;
- **Local** office and personnel;
- **Specific** activity in-jurisdiction.

This is **expensive** but **mandatory** for in-scope
activities.

### Costs

A typical HNW cross-offshore structure:

- **Establishment**: £50k - £500k+ depending on complexity;
- **Annual maintenance**: £100k - £1m+ depending on entities;
- **Significant** professional fees;
- **Specific** sub-fund / specific-activity costs.

### Common mistakes

- **Over-complication** — too many jurisdictions, too many
  entities;
- **Inadequate substance** in any jurisdiction;
- **Failure** to coordinate across jurisdictions;
- **Specific** unintended tax implications;
- **Family** unfamiliarity with complexity;
- **Insufficient** ongoing governance.

### Sequence of decisions

When advising a family on new structure:

1. **Understand** family goals, scale, distribution;
2. **Identify** principal jurisdictions per function;
3. **Design** specific entities;
4. **Coordinate** across jurisdictions;
5. **Substance** plans for each entity;
6. **Implementation** sequence;
7. **Ongoing** governance and compliance plans.

## What to do next

For a new cross-offshore family structure:

1. **Engage** family in vision and goals;
2. **Develop** structure proposal;
3. **Coordinate** legal advice across jurisdictions;
4. **Cost** the structure (establishment and ongoing);
5. **Implement** in sequenced manner;
6. **Govern** the structure post-implementation.

## Pitfalls

- **Over-engineering** complexity;
- **Inadequate** substance planning;
- **Inconsistent** cross-border documentation;
- **Specific** unintended tax / regulatory issues;
- **Family** governance under-developed;
- **Inadequate** ongoing administration.
