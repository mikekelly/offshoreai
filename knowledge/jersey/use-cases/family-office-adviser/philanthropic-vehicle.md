# Family Philanthropic Vehicle — Foundation or Charitable Trust?

## The question

The family wants to establish a **philanthropic vehicle** in
Jersey. Should it be a **Jersey foundation** (charitable purpose)
or a **Jersey charitable trust**?

## The short answer

Both work for family philanthropy. The choice turns on:

- **Body-corporate preference**: foundation wins;
- **Civil-law settlor**: foundation often more comfortable;
- **Common-law trust preference**: charitable trust;
- **Public-charter / private-regulations split**: foundation
  unique;
- **Operating vs grant-making**: both work, but foundation's
  body-corporate status is operationally cleaner.

Register either with the **Jersey Charity Commissioner** under
the **Charities (Jersey) Law 2014** for charity-status benefits.

## The proper answer

### Operational difference

#### Charitable foundation

- **Body corporate** — owns assets in own name;
- **Council manages** — including potentially family members;
- **Guardian supervises**;
- **Public charter** — publishes the charitable purpose;
- **Continuity** as a body corporate;
- **Direct operations** (running programmes) or grant-making.

#### Charitable trust

- **Trust relationship** — trustee holds for charitable purposes;
- **Trustees manage** — family or professional;
- **Trust deed** governs (private);
- **Less public-corporate identity**;
- **Operations** through trustee acting in trust capacity.

### Charity registration

The **Charities (Jersey) Law 2014** provides for **Jersey
Charity Commissioner registration**:

- **Registered Charity** status confers public recognition;
- **General Section** (full disclosure) or **Restricted Section**
  (limited disclosure for sensitive cases);
- **Charity Commissioner oversight** of operations;
- **Reporting** obligations annually;
- **Donor confidence** through registration.

Both Jersey foundations and Jersey trusts can register if they
meet charitable criteria.

### Charitable criteria

Under the 2014 Law:

- **Charitable purposes** — prescribed list (relief of poverty,
  advancement of education / religion / health / citizenship /
  arts / amateur sport / etc.);
- **Public benefit** — must benefit public generally or a
  sufficient section;
- **Limited private benefit**.

### Tax advantages

Charity status confers:

- **Limited Jersey tax exemptions** (Jersey tax is already
  light);
- **Foreign-jurisdiction recognition** — important for cross-
  border philanthropy and donor tax-deductibility;
- **Donor tax-deductibility** in foreign jurisdictions varies
  by jurisdiction.

For UK-tax-deductibility purposes, Jersey charities may need
**EU/UK** equivalent recognition through specific procedures.

### Grant-making vs operating

#### Grant-making

- The vehicle **gives grants** to other charities / causes;
- **Simpler operations** — typically board approves grants;
- **Foundation works well**;
- **Trust works well**.

#### Operating

- The vehicle **directly runs** programmes (e.g. school,
  hospital, research institute);
- **Body-corporate** (foundation or charity-company) operationally
  cleaner;
- **Employment, contracts, property** in the entity's name.

### Common scenarios

#### Family wealth deployed to philanthropy

- **Significant endowment** (£10–100m+);
- **Family directs** grant-making priorities;
- **Foundation** often chosen for body-corporate identity and
  public charter articulating family-philanthropic-mission.

#### Memorial / legacy philanthropy

- **One-time** or **finite-term** charity;
- **Trust** with finite term works;
- **Or** foundation with dissolution provision.

#### Direct-service philanthropic operation

- **School, hospital, museum, research institute**;
- **Foundation** typically;
- **Operating company** with charitable purposes another route.

#### Cross-border philanthropic activity

- Need for **recognition in donor / beneficiary jurisdictions**;
- **Jersey charity status** plus equivalent registrations
  abroad;
- **Tax-deductibility** for donors in their home jurisdictions
  requires bespoke advice.

### Setting up

#### Foundation route

1. **Establish** Jersey foundation with charitable purposes
   in charter;
2. **Council** including family + advisers;
3. **Guardian** independent oversight;
4. **Register** with Charity Commissioner;
5. **Fund** with initial endowment;
6. **Operate** the philanthropic mission.

#### Charitable trust route

1. **Draft** charitable trust deed;
2. **Appoint** trustees (family / professional);
3. **Register** with Charity Commissioner;
4. **Fund**;
5. **Operate**.

### Family involvement

For both:

- **Family members** can serve as council / trustees;
- **Family-mission** can be articulated;
- **Family-decision-making** on grant priorities;
- **Continuity** across generations through governance succession;
- **Younger generation** can be trained on philanthropy through
  the vehicle.

This is **valuable family-development** alongside the
philanthropic mission.

## What to do next

For a philanthropic-vehicle establishment:

1. **Articulate** family philanthropic mission;
2. **Choose** foundation or charitable trust;
3. **Engage** Jersey counsel;
4. **Establish** the vehicle;
5. **Apply** for Charity Commissioner registration;
6. **Fund** with endowment;
7. **Operate** the mission with family governance.

## Pitfalls

- **Inadequate mission articulation**: vague philanthropic aims
  produce drift;
- **Family-mission-vs-charity-test mismatch**: not all family
  values qualify as "charitable" under 2014 Law;
- **Charity-status complications** in foreign jurisdictions;
- **Governance neglect**: charity needs ongoing engagement.
