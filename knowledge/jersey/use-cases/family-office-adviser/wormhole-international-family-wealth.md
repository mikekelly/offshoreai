# Italian HNW family → forced-heirship-proof Jersey trust (compiled)

> **What this is.** A compiled, citation-complete distillation of the
> international-family-wealth structuring task: a Jersey trust protecting an
> Italian-domiciled family with EU / US / Swiss / Caribbean assets against
> forced heirship. `draft` status — verify the cited primary files. Full
> worked detail: [`./worked-example-international-family-wealth.md`](./worked-example-international-family-wealth.md).

## The structure

1. **Discretionary trust under the Trusts (Jersey) Law 1984** — settled by
   the family with a Jersey professional trustee; discretionary form gives
   the trustee distributive flexibility and keeps no beneficiary with a
   fixed entitlement an Italian court could attach.
   ([`../../trusts/firewall.md`](../../trusts/firewall.md))

2. **Article 9 firewall vs Italian *legittima*** — the **Article 9
   firewall** directs the Royal Court to apply **Jersey law only** to
   questions of the trust's validity and administration and to **disregard
   foreign forced-heirship rules** (Italian *legittima*). **Article 9(4)**
   makes a foreign judgment that contradicts Article 9 **unenforceable in
   Jersey**. This is the core protection — note its practical limits:
   it binds the Jersey court and Jersey-situs assets, not foreign courts
   over foreign-situs assets.
   ([`../../trusts/firewall.md`](../../trusts/firewall.md))

3. **Article 9A reserved powers** — the settlor may **reserve powers**
   (investment direction, appointment/removal of trustees, veto over
   distributions) without invalidating the trust, giving the family comfort
   that they retain influence.
   ([`../../trusts/reserved-powers.md`](../../trusts/reserved-powers.md))

4. **Underlying holding companies** — assets are held through one or more
   underlying companies beneath the trust, segregating asset classes and
   jurisdictions (e.g. a company per asset type / country).
   ([`./worked-example-international-family-wealth.md`](./worked-example-international-family-wealth.md))

5. **US blocker for US equities** — US-situs equities are held through a
   **non-US blocker company** beneath the trust to manage US estate-tax and
   US tax-reporting exposure for the non-US family.
   ([`./worked-example-international-family-wealth.md`](./worked-example-international-family-wealth.md))

6. **UK-resident beneficiary considerations** — if any beneficiary is
   UK-resident, UK anti-avoidance (settlements code, transfer-of-assets
   abroad, IHT relevant-property regime) must be planned around; distributions
   and benefits to UK beneficiaries carry UK tax consequences.
   ([`./worked-example-international-family-wealth.md`](./worked-example-international-family-wealth.md))

7. **Reporting: CRS / FATCA / DAC6 / TRS** — the structure is reportable:
   **CRS** automatic exchange on the trust and underlying entities,
   **FATCA** for any US-reportable persons, **DAC6** hallmarks on
   cross-border arrangements where an EU intermediary is involved, and the
   **UK Trust Registration Service** if there is a UK nexus. Transparency,
   not secrecy, is the operating assumption.
   ([`../../international/crs.md`](../../international/crs.md),
   [`../../international/fatca.md`](../../international/fatca.md),
   [`../../international/dac6.md`](../../international/dac6.md))

## The point in one line

A Jersey **discretionary trust** with the **Article 9 firewall** (disregards
Italian *legittima*; Art 9(4) blocks inconsistent foreign judgments) and
**Article 9A reserved powers**, holding assets through underlying companies
(US blocker for US equities), fully **CRS/FATCA/DAC6/TRS-reported**.
