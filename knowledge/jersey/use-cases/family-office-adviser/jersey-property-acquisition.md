# Jersey Property Acquisition by HVR Family

## The question

The family member has been approved for **HVR status** and
needs to acquire Jersey residential property. What are the
considerations?

## The short answer

- **Property must qualify** for HVR purposes (typically higher
  value bands);
- **Within prescribed timeframe** after approval (often 12
  months);
- **Acquisition** via standard Jersey conveyancing through the
  Royal Court (*passation*);
- **Stamp duty / Land Transactions Tax** on acquisition;
- **Ongoing** Jersey-property occupancy expectations.

Plan property search **in parallel with HVR application** —
qualifying properties are scarce.

## The proper answer

### HVR property qualification

The HVR regime specifies:

- **Minimum property value** (varies — high six / low seven
  figures typically);
- **Residential character** (not commercial);
- **Adequate size and quality** for family residence;
- **Genuine intent to occupy** as principal home.

The exact qualification thresholds change over time; check
**Locate Jersey** current guidance.

### Property market reality

The Jersey HVR-grade property market is:

- **Limited supply** of qualifying properties;
- **High prices** (often £3m–£15m+ for HVR-quality properties);
- **Slow turnover** — premium properties don't change hands
  often;
- **Specific districts** preferred (St Helier outskirts, St
  Brelade, Trinity, etc.);
- **Family-office demand** consistently exceeds supply.

### Acquisition timing

After HVR approval:

- **Approval typically conditions** include acquiring qualifying
  property within prescribed period (often 12 months);
- **Don't wait** to start property search — begin **in parallel**
  with HVR application;
- **Bridge arrangements** if HVR approved before suitable
  property found.

### Acquisition process

Jersey property acquisition through:

1. **Search and offer** through estate agents;
2. **Negotiation** and conditional contract;
3. **Title due diligence** by Jersey lawyers;
4. **Tax / planning** verifications;
5. **Conveyancing contract** (passation) executed **before the
   Royal Court**;
6. **Registration** in the Public Registry;
7. **Stamp duty / LTT** paid.

The *passation* before the Royal Court is a distinctive Jersey
feature.

### Costs

Beyond the purchase price:

- **Legal fees** (Jersey lawyer);
- **Stamp duty** (scaled — typically 5–10%+ for high-value);
- **Land Transactions Tax** (for share-transfer property);
- **Registration fees**;
- **Property condition / survey costs**.

### Ongoing occupation

HVR licence terms typically include:

- **Genuine occupation** of the property;
- **Principal residence** in Jersey;
- **Family occupancy** (spouse, dependants);
- **Not** rental investment.

Long absence or non-occupation can affect status.

### Property structures

The property is typically:

- **Held directly** by the HVR resident; or
- **Through a Jersey-resident vehicle** (less common for
  principal home);
- **Joint ownership** with spouse common;
- **Family trust** typically **not** holding the principal home
  (would compromise HVR genuine-occupation, may affect tax).

### Second-home considerations

Some HVR families also acquire:

- **Second / holiday property** in Jersey;
- **UK / European property** for travel base;
- **Different ownership structures** for non-principal homes.

### Practical tips

- **Engage** Locate Jersey early for HVR application;
- **Engage** Jersey real-estate lawyer in parallel;
- **Identify** family priorities (location, size,
  characteristics);
- **Be prepared** to act quickly when suitable property
  emerges;
- **Don't compromise** on HVR-qualifying criteria;
- **Plan** transition logistics (children's schools, moving
  family, etc.).

## What to do next

For an active HVR family:

1. **Coordinate** HVR application and property search;
2. **Engage** Jersey lawyer for conveyancing;
3. **Identify** properties in qualifying band;
4. **Move quickly** when suitable property appears;
5. **Complete** acquisition within HVR timeframe;
6. **Settle** family into the property.

## Pitfalls

- **Property scarcity**: starting search late means missing
  approval-window deadlines;
- **Non-qualifying property**: doesn't satisfy HVR criteria;
- **Family-trust ownership**: complications for HVR purposes;
- **Stamp-duty surprise**: budget includes acquisition taxes.
