# Jersey Succession Planning — Wills and Forced Heirship

## The question

For a Jersey-resident family member or someone with **Jersey
assets**, what succession-law considerations apply? What about
**Jersey's customary-law forced heirship** rules?

## The short answer

Jersey succession is governed by:

- **Wills and Successions (Jersey) Law 1993** — modern statute
  governing wills and intestate succession;
- **Customary-law residuals** — forced shares for spouse /
  children on **movable estate**, less in modern practice;
- **Immovable property** (real estate) has separate succession
  rules — historically restrictive but modernised.

Jersey wills are **testamentary freedom-respecting** to a greater
extent than civil-law jurisdictions but **with some constraints**
on movable estate. Plan with a Jersey-qualified lawyer.

## The proper answer

### The Wills and Successions Law 1993

The **Wills and Successions (Jersey) Law 1993** modernised
Jersey succession:

- **Greater testamentary freedom** than under prior customary
  law;
- **Specific provisions** for spousal / children's claims;
- **Intestate-succession** rules for those dying without a
  valid will;
- **Probate** procedures.

### Movable vs immovable

Historic Jersey law distinguished **movable estate** (personal
property) and **immovable estate** (Jersey real estate):

- Different succession rules;
- Different forms of will;
- Different formalities.

The 1993 Law modified but didn't eliminate the distinction. In
practice:

- **Will of movable estate** — typical personal property,
  bank accounts, investments;
- **Will of immovable estate** — Jersey-situated real estate;
- Some testators have **separate wills** for each;
- Combined wills also work but with care.

### Légitime (forced heirship)

Historic Jersey customary law had **légitime** — forced shares:

- **Spouse**: traditionally entitled to forced share;
- **Children**: traditionally entitled to forced share;
- The 1993 Law **modified** but didn't fully eliminate these
  in some categories.

Modern position:

- **Greater testamentary freedom** for testators;
- **Some forced-share** elements remain for spouse / children
  on movable estate;
- **Court application** by aggrieved family member possible.

### Wills formality

A Jersey will must:

- Be **in writing**;
- **Signed** by the testator;
- **Witnessed** appropriately;
- Some specific formalities for immovable estate.

**Foreign wills** by Jersey-domiciled persons can be valid but
formalities should be considered.

### Intestate succession

If no valid will:

- **Statutory shares** to spouse, children, other relatives;
- **Detailed rules** in the 1993 Law;
- **Estate administration** through Royal Court probate
  process.

### Probate

For administered estates:

- **Probate granted** by Royal Court Probate Division;
- **Executor / personal representative** administers;
- **Probate fee** payable (modest, based on estate value);
- **Distribution** in accordance with will / intestacy rules.

### Trust-based avoidance

Many wealthy families use **Jersey trusts** to manage succession
outside the probate / forced-heirship framework:

- **Lifetime settlement** of assets into trust;
- **Trust survives** the settlor's death;
- **Trust distribution** independent of succession rules;
- Subject to **forced-heirship arguments** that family members
  could make (but Article 9 firewall provides protection).

### Cross-border succession

For families with international connections:

- **Multiple wills** in different jurisdictions for different
  assets;
- **EU Succession Regulation** considerations (where
  applicable);
- **Forced heirship** in family members' home jurisdictions;
- **Tax-residence** mismatches.

The **Article 9 firewall** of the Trusts Law strongly protects
Jersey trusts against foreign forced-heirship claims — see
[`../trust-officer/forced-heirship.md`](../trust-officer/forced-heirship.md).

### Common scenarios

#### Long-time Jersey resident dies

- **Jersey will** governs Jersey estate;
- **Probate** through Royal Court;
- **Family members** may have forced-share claims on movable
  estate;
- **Trust assets** (if separate) governed by trust deed.

#### Family member with Jersey real estate but foreign domicile

- **Jersey will** for Jersey real estate;
- **Foreign will** for foreign-situated assets;
- **Coordinate** to avoid conflicts.

#### Jersey-resident HVR family member

- **Jersey domicile** acquired over time;
- **Jersey wills regime** governs estate;
- **Pre-immigration** assets sometimes treated separately.

## What to do next

For an estate-planning engagement:

1. **Identify** family member's domicile / residence
   trajectory;
2. **Inventory** assets by situs (Jersey / foreign);
3. **Engage** Jersey-qualified lawyer for will drafting;
4. **Coordinate** with foreign counsel for non-Jersey assets;
5. **Consider** trust-based planning for material wealth;
6. **Document** the family-succession strategy.

## Pitfalls

- **Single will** for international assets — risk of conflicts;
- **Customary-law surprise**: forced-heirship rights
  unfamiliar to common-law families;
- **Probate delays** for ill-drafted estates;
- **Trust-as-avoidance** challenges from disappointed family
  members.
