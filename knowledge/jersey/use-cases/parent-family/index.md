# Parent / Family — Use Cases

## Who this section is for

You are dealing with a **Jersey family-law question** — as
a parent, spouse, civil partner, or family member. You might
be:

- Marrying, divorcing, or separating;
- Working out child arrangements after a separation;
- Concerned about a child's welfare (or the subject of a
  child-protection investigation);
- Adopting or fostering;
- Making a will or sorting out a Jersey estate;
- Setting up a Lasting Power of Attorney for an aging
  relative.

Your primary statutes are the **Matrimonial Causes (Jersey)
Law 1949**, the **Marriage and Civil Status (Jersey) Law
2001**, the **Civil Partnership (Jersey) Law 2012**, the
**Children (Jersey) Law 2002**, the **Adoption (Jersey) Law
1961**, the **Capacity and Self-Determination (Jersey) Law
2016**, and the **Wills and Successions (Jersey) Law 1993**
(the last of which has article-level coverage already).

This persona is currently **a scaffold** — three of the
above statutes are top-15 audit gaps.
