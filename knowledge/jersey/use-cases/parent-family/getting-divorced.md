# Q: I want to divorce my spouse in Jersey. How do I start?

## The question

You want to get divorced in Jersey. What's the procedure,
how long does it take, what does it cost, and what
happens to the money and the children?

## The short answer

A Jersey divorce is governed by the **Matrimonial Causes
(Jersey) Law 1949** (still the operative regime — the
no-fault reform is drafted but not in force). The
mechanics:

1. **Check jurisdiction** — Jersey can hear the petition
   if both spouses are domiciled here or one has been
   habitually resident for at least 1 year.
2. **Check the 3-year bar** — petitions cannot be filed
   within 3 years of the marriage save in exceptional
   circumstances.
3. **Identify a ground** under Article 7 — fault
   (adultery, desertion, unreasonable behaviour, unsound
   mind, imprisonment) or non-fault (1-year separation
   with consent, or 2-year separation without).
4. **File a petition** at the Royal Court Family
   Division.
5. **Serve** the respondent (4 weeks before hearing).
6. **Decree nisi** → wait 6 weeks 1 day → **decree
   absolute**.
7. **Sort out the money** (ancillary relief) and the
   **children** (child arrangements) in parallel.

**Minimum timeline**: ~12 weeks to decree absolute on an
undefended petition. **Real-world timeline**: 6-18
months including financial settlement.

## The proper answer

### Jurisdiction

The Royal Court can hear your petition if **either**:

- **Both you and your spouse** are domiciled in Jersey;
  **or**
- **Either of you** has been **habitually resident** in
  Jersey for the **full year** preceding the petition.

If you're an international family with options, choosing
where to divorce can matter — Jersey is not bound by EU
Brussels II, so no "jurisdiction race." If competing
jurisdictions are available, see
[`../../family/jurisdiction-and-foreign-orders.md`](../../family/jurisdiction-and-foreign-orders.md).

### Ground for divorce

You need to satisfy one of Article 7's seven grounds:

**Fault** (immediate, no separation period):

- **Adultery** (with intolerability) — opposite-sex only
  under the statute;
- **Desertion** for 2+ years;
- **Unreasonable behaviour** — most commonly pleaded;
  covers a wide range of conduct;
- **Incurable unsound mind** (5+ years care);
- **Imprisonment** (life or 15+ years).

**Non-fault** (require separation):

- **1 year apart with consent** of the respondent;
- **2 years apart** without consent needed.

For most divorces in practice: either **unreasonable
behaviour** (for fault-based) or **2-year separation**
(for non-fault). See
[`../../family/divorce-grounds.md`](../../family/divorce-grounds.md).

### The 3-year bar

If you've been married **less than 3 years**, you can
only petition in **exceptional circumstances** — typically
extreme hardship or exceptional depravity. Reform pending
but not in force.

### Procedure

The standard procedure:

| Step | Time |
|---|---|
| Petition filed | Day 0 |
| Service on respondent | Within reasonable time |
| Respondent's Acknowledgement | 8 days after service |
| Setting down for hearing | Petitioner applies |
| Decree nisi pronounced | At undefended session |
| Wait | At least 6 weeks 1 day |
| Decree absolute applied for | By petitioner |
| Marriage ends | On decree absolute |

**Minimum timeline**: ~12 weeks. **Common practical
timeline**: longer, because financial settlement is
usually completed before decree absolute.

### Money — ancillary relief

The court has broad powers under Articles 25-31:

- **Maintenance** (interim, periodic, secured);
- **Lump sum** payments;
- **Property transfer / settlement / sale**;
- **Variation** of nuptial settlements.

**No pension sharing** is available in Jersey — only
**offsetting**. If pensions are your main asset, get
specialist advice. Reform pending.

Procedure:

- **Form E** financial disclosure by both parties;
- **First Appointment** — case management;
- **Financial Dispute Resolution (FDR)** — judge-led
  settlement attempt;
- **Final hearing** if not settled.

The **sharing principle** starts at 50:50 of matrimonial
acquest. Departures from equal are justified by needs.
Non-matrimonial assets (pre-marital, inherited, gifted)
are not automatically shared but may be drawn upon for
needs.

See [`../../family/ancillary-relief.md`](../../family/ancillary-relief.md).

### Children

If there are minor children:

- **Statement of Arrangements** must be filed with the
  petition;
- **Child arrangements orders** under Article 10 CJL 2002
  may be needed if parents cannot agree on
  residence/contact;
- **Welfare paramountcy** — child's welfare overrides
  all other considerations;
- **JFCAS welfare report** typically ordered in
  contested cases;
- **Mediation** strongly encouraged before litigation.

See [`../../family/child-arrangements-orders.md`](../../family/child-arrangements-orders.md).

### Pre-nuptial agreements

If you have a prenuptial / postnuptial agreement, it
will be a **strong factor** but not strictly binding —
Jersey follows *Radmacher v Granatino*. The court can
depart from it if necessary to ensure fairness. See
[`../../family/prenuptial-agreements.md`](../../family/prenuptial-agreements.md).

### Costs

- **Court fees** — modest;
- **Solicitor / advocate fees** — typically several
  thousand pounds for an undefended divorce, much more
  for contested or complex ancillary relief;
- **Costs orders** — typically each party pays own in
  family matters;
- **Public funding** available in narrow cases.

## What to do tomorrow morning

1. **Confirm jurisdiction** — are you / your spouse
   domiciled or habitually resident in Jersey for 1+
   year? Document this.
2. **Check 3-year bar** — when were you married?
3. **Identify your ground** — what facts do you rely on?
4. **Engage a Jersey family advocate** — fees for
   initial advice are modest; specialist insight is
   high-value.
5. **Gather financial information** — bank statements,
   pension statements, property valuations, investment
   schedules. You'll need to disclose all of this.
6. **Consider mediation first** — if there's any
   prospect of agreement on money and children, this is
   cheaper and less stressful than litigation. The
   Family Foundation offers a **free service**.
7. **Update your will** — divorce affects forced-
   heirship entitlements; a new will is essential.
8. **Plan around decree absolute timing** — sometimes
   better to delay decree absolute until financial
   settlement is concluded.

## Common mistakes

- **Pre-empting** decree absolute before financial
  settlement — risks losing entitlements;
- **Failing to disclose** — court takes a very dim view;
- **Acting unilaterally** on children — risks abduction
  proceedings if removing from Jersey;
- **Underestimating** Jersey's pension treatment
  (offsetting, not sharing);
- **Failing** to update your will after divorce.
