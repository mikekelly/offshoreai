# JPF for Hedge / Private Equity / Real Estate?

## The question

Can a **Jersey Private Fund (JPF)** be used for a **hedge fund**,
**private equity fund**, or **real estate fund**? Are there
constraints?

## The short answer

**Yes**, the JPF regime is **strategy-agnostic** — it accommodates
any of these strategies subject to the **50-investor cap** and
the **investor-qualification** rules.

- **PE and RE**: natural fit — closed-ended, professional
  investors;
- **Hedge funds**: works if investor count is manageable;
  often Expert Fund is used at scale;
- **Multi-class umbrella** for various strategies: cell-company
  JPF structure works.

The main constraint is **investor count and qualification**, not
strategy.

## The proper answer

### Strategy considerations

#### Private equity / venture capital

- **JPF is the default** for Jersey PE/VC vehicles;
- **Closed-ended LP** structure typical;
- **GP**: Jersey or foreign entity;
- **LPs**: limited number of institutional / HNW investors;
- **JPF works** comfortably within the 50-cap for most fund
  sizes.

#### Real estate funds

- **JPF works** for property-investment vehicles;
- **Single-asset** or **portfolio** structures;
- **Co-investment** vehicles common;
- **Investors** typically institutional / professional.

#### Hedge funds

- **JPF works** if investor count is bounded;
- **Master-feeder** structures can use multiple JPFs (a master
  + feeder count separately);
- For **larger investor bases**, Expert Fund is more typical;
- For very large hedge funds, listing on TISE or other exchange
  combines with Listed-Fund regime.

#### Credit / direct lending

- **JPF works** for closed-end credit vehicles;
- **Cell company** structure useful for separate
  loan-origination programmes within one umbrella;
- **Cross-jurisdictional** financing through Jersey LP common.

### The 50-investor question

**"50 investors"** is the key constraint:

- Counts at **the JPF level** — sub-funds within a JPF umbrella
  may count separately depending on structure;
- **Feeder funds** can stack — master with one feeder, feeder
  has 50, master has the feeder + others;
- **Sophisticated families** as single investors — often
  efficient.

For funds expecting **more than 50 investors**, Expert Fund is
the natural next step.

### Multi-class umbrella

A **cell-company JPF** can host multiple strategies:

- Each **cell** is a separate strategy / sub-fund;
- **PCC** statutory segregation works for fund-context if the
  manager is happy with PCC mechanics;
- **ICC** for greater legal-personality segregation;
- **One JPF authorisation** covers the umbrella; cells operate
  within.

### Investor type alignment

| Strategy | Typical investor mix | JPF fit |
|---|---|---|
| Private equity | Pension funds, sovereigns, family offices | Excellent |
| Real estate | Insurance, pension, family offices | Excellent |
| Hedge funds | Family offices, smaller institutions | Good (bounded) |
| Credit | Insurance, pension, sovereigns | Excellent |
| Multi-strategy umbrella | Mixed | Good (with cells) |

## What to do next

1. **Confirm** strategy and investor count;
2. **Choose** vehicle type (LP / company / unit trust / cell);
3. **Engage** DSP;
4. **Apply** for JPF authorisation;
5. **Document** the fund with PPM and supporting docs;
6. **Launch**.

## Pitfalls

- **Strategy-creep over investor cap**: a successful PE fund
  that scales beyond 50 needs to convert to a heavier regime;
- **Cell-company mechanics**: ensure investors understand the
  cell structure;
- **Master-feeder counting**: confirm with the JFSC how the
  50-cap applies to your specific stack.
