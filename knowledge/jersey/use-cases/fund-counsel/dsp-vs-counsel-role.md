# DSP and Fund Counsel — Who Does What?

## The question

Your JPF has a **Designated Service Provider (DSP)** — a
Jersey-licensed administrator with substantive responsibilities.
What does the DSP do, and what's your role as fund counsel /
GP vs the DSP?

## The short answer

The **DSP**:

- **Investor CDD / AML** — primary regulatory responsibility;
- **JFSC liaison** as the regulated entity;
- **Books and records** maintenance;
- **Routine administration** (NAV calculation, subscriptions /
  redemptions);
- **Reporting** under CRS / FATCA / BO obligations.

**Fund counsel / GP** retains:

- **Investment decisions**;
- **Strategic governance** (LP relations, fund-management
  business);
- **Fund-document drafting**;
- **Investor communications** beyond administration;
- **Decisions on accepting / declining** investors based on
  risk assessment.

The line is **regulatory responsibility** (DSP) vs **commercial
direction** (counsel / GP).

## The proper answer

### DSP responsibilities

The DSP is **the JFSC's regulated point of contact** for the JPF.
Specific responsibilities:

#### Regulatory compliance

- **JFSC filings and notifications**;
- **AML / CFT compliance** for the fund;
- **Code of Practice** adherence;
- **JFSC inspection** preparation and management;
- **Regulatory breach reporting**.

#### Investor administration

- **CDD on investors** at onboarding;
- **Investor-eligibility verification**;
- **Sanctions and PEP screening**;
- **Investor records** maintenance;
- **Subscription / redemption** processing.

#### Financial administration

- **NAV calculation** (typically with auditor input);
- **Subscription / redemption** payment processing;
- **Investor reporting** (statements, capital accounts);
- **Distribution** processing;
- **Tax-classification** filings (CRS / FATCA / BO).

#### Records

- **Books and records** of the fund;
- **Minute books** for fund-vehicle meetings;
- **Document retention** under regulatory standards.

### Fund counsel / GP responsibilities

#### Investment decisions

- **Investment strategy** execution;
- **Asset selection** and management;
- **Disposal decisions**;
- **Portfolio risk management**.

#### Fund strategy

- **Fund formation** and structure;
- **Capital raising** with new investors;
- **LP relations**;
- **Strategic governance** of the fund-management business.

#### Document drafting

- **PPM** drafting (in coordination with counsel);
- **Subscription documents** (DSP feeds into operational
  elements);
- **Side letters** with specific investors;
- **Reports to investors** on portfolio performance.

#### Risk decisions

- **Accept / decline** specific investors (DSP can flag risk;
  GP decides);
- **Pursue / decline** specific investment opportunities;
- **Manage** conflicts of interest at the GP level.

### Where the roles intersect

#### Investor onboarding

- **DSP**: CDD and eligibility verification, technical
  compliance;
- **GP**: commercial decision whether to admit;
- **Coordination**: GP advises on commercial questions; DSP
  on regulatory.

#### PPM drafting

- **GP / counsel**: substantive content (strategy, terms,
  team);
- **DSP**: operational elements (subscription mechanics,
  reporting schedule);
- **Iteration**: drafts go back and forth.

#### Regulatory engagement

- **DSP**: primary JFSC contact for the fund;
- **GP**: engagement on strategic / capital-raising / sponsor
  matters where the GP is itself regulated;
- **Coordination**: for material matters affecting both, joint
  engagement.

#### Reporting to investors

- **DSP**: financial / administrative reports (NAV, capital
  accounts, transactions);
- **GP**: commentary, portfolio narrative, strategic updates;
- **Joint product**: typical investor reports combine both.

### Service-level agreements

The DSP relationship is governed by an **administration
agreement**:

- **Scope of services** clearly defined;
- **SLA** for routine activities;
- **Fees** typically asset-based or fixed;
- **Escalation** procedures for issues;
- **Termination** rights.

### Working effectively

For an effective DSP / GP relationship:

- **Clear scope** at outset;
- **Regular communication** (weekly / monthly cadence);
- **Joint reviews** of compliance issues;
- **Transparent escalation** of problems;
- **Shared performance metrics**.

## What to do next

For a new JPF:

1. **Engage** the DSP early in fund design;
2. **Negotiate** the administration agreement;
3. **Coordinate** on fund-document drafting;
4. **Establish** working protocols;
5. **Operate** with regular communication.

For an existing JPF:

- **Review** the DSP relationship periodically;
- **Refine** workflows based on experience;
- **Escalate** any service issues promptly.

## Pitfalls

- **Treating DSP as commodity**: a strong DSP relationship
  drives fund quality;
- **Over-relying on DSP** for commercial decisions;
- **Under-engaging** on regulatory matters — GP should know
  the regime even when DSP handles operations;
- **Friction**: friction between GP and DSP harms the fund.
