# How is My Fund Taxed Under Zero-Ten?

## The question

You're advising on a Jersey fund. What is the Jersey-tax
position of the fund itself?

## The short answer

Jersey funds are generally **tax-efficient** under the zero-ten
regime:

- **Jersey-company funds**: 0% corporate tax under Article 123A
  (with limited exceptions);
- **Jersey LP funds**: tax-transparent — investors taxed in
  their home jurisdictions;
- **Jersey unit trusts**: tax-transparent for most purposes;
- **Eligible certified funds**: specific Article 118C exemption
  for some categories.

The fund pays **little or no Jersey tax**. The substantive tax
arises at **investor level** in investor jurisdictions.

## The proper answer

### Jersey-company fund

A fund structured as a **Jersey company** (e.g. OEIC, closed-
end company):

- **Jersey-tax-resident** under Article 123;
- **0% corporate tax** under Article 123A (most cases);
- **No Jersey withholding** on dividends to investors;
- **No Jersey CGT** on asset disposals (Jersey has none);
- **Annual return** filed with Revenue Jersey at 0%.

### Jersey LP fund

A fund structured as a **Jersey limited partnership**:

- **Tax-transparent** — not a separate Jersey-tax entity;
- **Income flows through** to partners;
- **Each partner** taxed in their home jurisdiction on their
  share;
- **Jersey filing** typically minimal (informational).

This is the **most common** structure for closed-end PE / VC /
RE funds, capital preserved at investor level.

### Jersey unit trust

A fund structured as a **Jersey unit trust**:

- **Tax-transparent** for most purposes;
- **Unitholders** taxed in home jurisdictions on income they
  receive;
- **Jersey-property income** (Schedule A) taxed at trust level
  at 20% if held directly;
- **Trustee filings** as applicable.

### Article 118C — Certified Funds

The Income Tax Law's **Article 118C** provides specific
exemption / treatment for **certified collective investment
schemes**:

- Applies to specific categories of regulated funds;
- Provides explicit Jersey-tax-exempt status;
- Used for public-distributed funds primarily.

### Distribution rules

The **Article 81 distribution regime** targets Jersey-resident
shareholders of zero-rate Jersey companies:

- A fund company distributing to Jersey-resident shareholders
  triggers Article 81 mechanics;
- For typical fund structures with **non-Jersey-resident
  investors**, Article 81 doesn't apply.

### Pillar Two consideration

For **large MNE groups** (€750m+ revenue):

- Pillar Two **15% minimum** applies to Jersey-resident entities;
- For a Jersey fund within such a group, the **MCIT** applies;
- **Below-threshold** funds are unaffected.

Most funds operate **standalone or in smaller fund-management
groups** below the threshold.

### Substance considerations

The **Substance Law 2019** applies to Jersey-tax-resident
companies carrying on **fund management** as a relevant activity:

- A Jersey-resident fund-management company must meet substance
  test;
- For **fund vehicles** themselves (the AIF), substance issues
  are less direct but still relevant for "holding company" or
  "investment entity" classification.

### Practical compliance

For a Jersey fund:

- **Determine** the legal form (company / LP / unit trust);
- **Confirm** Jersey-tax position (0% / transparent / exempt);
- **File** annual returns as required;
- **Apply** Article 118C if certified-fund status applies;
- **Monitor** Pillar Two threshold if part of large group;
- **Comply** with substance for management companies.

## What to do next

For a new fund:

1. **Choose** legal form considering tax characterisation;
2. **Model** Jersey-tax position;
3. **Apply** for Article 118C if appropriate;
4. **Set up** tax-compliance arrangements (typically through
   administrator);
5. **File** annual returns.

## Pitfalls

- **Conflating fund tax with investor tax**: fund-level
  position is light; investor tax is the substantive question;
- **Missing Pillar Two**: large groups can suddenly be in
  scope;
- **Substance neglect**: fund-management entity needs Jersey
  substance for the manager;
- **Real-estate within unit trust**: triggers Schedule A
  liability at trust level.
