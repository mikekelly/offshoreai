# Cell-Company Structures for Fund Umbrellas — When and How

## The question

You're running a multi-strategy or multi-class fund platform.
When does a **cell-company structure** (PCC or ICC) make sense
for the fund, and how is it set up?

## The short answer

Cell structures work for fund managers with:

- **Multiple strategies** under one umbrella;
- **Multi-class** investor offerings (e.g. retail / institutional
  / accredited classes);
- **Master-feeder** structures;
- **Cost-sharing** across small / mid-sized strategies.

The choice between **PCC** (statutory segregation) and **ICC**
(separate legal personality per cell) turns on:

- **Foreign-law recognition** needs (ICC better recognised
  abroad);
- **Operational simplicity** (PCC simpler);
- **Counterparty preference**.

## The proper answer

### PCC vs ICC recap

| Feature | PCC | ICC |
|---|---|---|
| Cell legal personality | Statutory compartment | Separate legal person |
| Segregation source | Article 127YT statutory | Separate-entity structural |
| Contracts | PCC contracts on behalf | Cell contracts in own name |
| Foreign-law recognition | Variable | Generally cleaner |
| Naming | "Y PCC" with cells named within | "X IC of Y ICC" |
| Cost | Lower | Higher |

### When PCC works

PCC structures fit:

- **Hedge-fund managers** with multiple strategies;
- **Multi-class** vehicles offering different fee / liquidity
  terms to investor segments;
- **Specific-mandate** carve-outs for institutional investors;
- **Cost-conscious** platforms where the per-cell cost matters.

The **statutory segregation** under Article 127YT is robust
within Jersey law and increasingly recognised abroad.

### When ICC works

ICC structures fit:

- **Cross-border** structures where foreign-law recognition
  matters;
- **Insurance / regulatory** contexts where the cell-as-separate-
  entity simplifies licensing;
- **Future-flexibility** needs — cells can convert to standalone
  companies (Article 127YH) or transfer between ICCs (Article
  127YI);
- **Investor preference** for separate-entity structure.

### Setting up a cell fund

#### PCC route

1. **Incorporate the PCC** as a Jersey company;
2. **Designate** as PCC in the memorandum and articles;
3. **Apply** for JFSC fund authorisation at PCC level;
4. **Create individual cells** as needed for strategies;
5. **Cell-specific documentation** (cell terms in regulations
   or articles);
6. **Cellular accounting** to maintain segregation.

#### ICC route

1. **Incorporate the ICC** as a Jersey company;
2. **Designate** as ICC in the memorandum and articles;
3. **Apply** for JFSC ICC authorisation;
4. **Apply for individual cell incorporations** under Article
   127YA;
5. **Each cell** is separately incorporated with its own
   memorandum and articles;
6. **Cells operate** as separate legal persons under the ICC
   umbrella.

### Fund-regime overlay

A cell fund can operate as:

- **JPF** at the PCC / ICC level (each cell within the 50-cap
  or the whole PCC as one JPF);
- **Expert Fund** for larger investor bases;
- **Listed Fund** for listed cell structures;
- **Public Fund** for retail.

### Service-provider commonality

Cell funds typically share:

- **Administrator** across cells;
- **Custodian** across cells;
- **Auditor** (often the same firm) per cell;
- **Investment manager** may differ by strategy;
- **Compliance / AML** functions integrated.

This is the **cost-efficiency** of the cell model.

### Cellular segregation in practice

For PCC:

- **Separate cellular bank accounts** for each cell;
- **Separate accounting** by cell;
- **Contracts attribute** to specific cells;
- **Disclosure to counterparties** that they are dealing with
  a cell of a PCC.

For ICC:

- **Cells separately incorporated** — segregation is structural;
- **Each cell's own bank accounts** in its own name;
- **Each cell's own contracts**.

### Pitfalls

- **Segregation breakdown**: poor record-keeping can undermine
  PCC segregation;
- **Counterparty confusion**: failure to clearly identify which
  cell is contracting;
- **Cross-cell exposure**: directors must avoid creating
  cross-cell guarantees inadvertently;
- **Foreign-law recognition**: PCC segregation may not hold in
  uncooperative jurisdictions.

## What to do next

1. **Choose** PCC or ICC based on factors above;
2. **Engage** Jersey counsel for structure design;
3. **Apply** for JFSC authorisations;
4. **Set up** cellular accounting and segregation
   infrastructure;
5. **Document** cell-specific terms;
6. **Operate** with proper segregation discipline.
