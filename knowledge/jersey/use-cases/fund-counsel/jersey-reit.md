# When Does a Jersey REIT / Property Fund Make Sense?

## The question

You're advising on a real-estate fund structure. When is a
**Jersey REIT** or **Jersey-domiciled property fund** the right
vehicle?

## The short answer

Jersey is a strong choice for **UK-focused listed property
funds** (REITs / investment trusts) because:

- **Tax efficiency** — zero-ten 0% rate plus no Jersey CGT;
- **No Jersey withholding** on dividends paid to investors;
- **LSE-listing acceptance** — Jersey companies routinely list
  on Main Market / AIM;
- **UK REIT recognition** — Jersey REITs can elect into the **UK
  REIT regime** for UK property holdings;
- **Familiar corporate law** to UK / international investors;
- **No stamp duty** on Jersey share transfers (subject to LTT
  for property-share property).

For **EU-focused** property funds, other jurisdictions
(Luxembourg) may have better treaty / regulatory positioning.

## The proper answer

### The Jersey REIT model

A typical Jersey REIT:

- **Jersey-incorporated** holding company (Listed Fund regime);
- **Listed** on LSE Main Market, AIM, or TISE;
- **Owns** UK or international property assets (sometimes
  through SPVs);
- **Elects** into the **UK REIT regime** for UK-property
  holdings (tax-transparent treatment in UK);
- **Distributes** 90%+ of UK property income as PIDs under the
  UK regime.

The Jersey corporate level is **tax-efficient**; the UK level is
tax-effective through the REIT election.

### Tax advantages

#### At Jersey level

- **0% corporate tax** on most income under zero-ten;
- **No Jersey CGT** on asset disposals;
- **No Jersey withholding** on dividends to investors;
- **No Jersey stamp duty** on share transfers (subject to LTT
  exception).

#### At UK level (if UK REIT elected)

- **UK property income** is tax-transparent (no UK corporation
  tax at REIT level on property income / gains);
- **PIDs** subject to UK 20% withholding to investors (with
  exemptions for certain investors);
- **Non-PID dividends** treated as ordinary dividends.

### Why Jersey vs UK domicile

A UK REIT vs a Jersey REIT investing in UK property:

| Feature | UK REIT | Jersey REIT (UK-REIT-elected) |
|---|---|---|
| Corporate tax on property | 0% (regime) | 0% (Jersey + UK regime) |
| CGT on asset disposals | 0% (regime) | 0% (Jersey law) |
| Withholding on PIDs | 20% (UK) | 20% (UK as recipient is non-UK-domiciled) |
| Stamp duty on share trades | 0.5% (UK SDRT) | 0% Jersey shares (general) |
| Corporate law flexibility | UK CA 2006 | Jersey CL 1991 (more flexible) |
| Listing acceptance | High | Equally high |

The **stamp-duty advantage** is the most-cited reason for
Jersey domicile of UK-property REITs.

### When Jersey doesn't win

- **EU-focused property**: EU-domiciled vehicles may have
  better access / treaty positioning;
- **Pure US-property**: US-resident vehicles common;
- **Small REITs** below the cost-effective domiciliation
  threshold;
- **Private (non-listed) property funds** — JPF / Expert Fund
  works without REIT classification.

### Structural variations

#### Jersey-listed open-ended property fund

- **Open-ended** investment company (Jersey OEIC);
- **Periodic NAV** and subscription / redemption;
- **JPF or Expert Fund** regime as appropriate.

#### Closed-end Jersey investment trust

- **Closed-ended**, listed on LSE Main Market;
- **Investment-trust tax regime** in UK (if UK-investment-trust-
  electing);
- Common structure for diversified-property holdings.

#### Property cell company

- **Cell-company JPF** with one cell per property / portfolio;
- **Segregated investor pools** for different strategies;
- **Shared administrative platform**.

### Service-provider requirements

A Jersey REIT typically needs:

- **JFSC-regulated administrator** (FSB);
- **Custodian** (FSB);
- **Property valuer** (independent);
- **Auditor**;
- **Listing-sponsor / nomad** for the LSE process;
- **UK tax adviser** for REIT-regime compliance.

## What to do next

1. **Confirm** the property strategy and target investor base;
2. **Choose** open-ended vs closed-ended structure;
3. **Engage** Jersey counsel for fund-vehicle structuring;
4. **Engage** UK tax adviser for REIT-election analysis;
5. **Apply** for JPF / Expert / Listed authorisation;
6. **Coordinate** the listing process if applicable.

## Pitfalls

- **UK REIT-regime compliance complexity**: lots of conditions
  (genuine diversity of ownership, distribution requirements);
- **PID withholding administration**: significant operational
  burden;
- **Cross-border investor tax**: investor-jurisdiction tax can
  be material.
