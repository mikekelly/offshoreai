# Article 118C Company Classification — When?

## The question

Your Jersey fund might qualify for **Article 118C** exempt
classification under the Income Tax (Jersey) Law 1961. When
does this apply, and why does it matter alongside zero-ten?

## The short answer

**Article 118C** provides a **specific exemption** for certain
collective investment schemes (CIS) and their service providers:

- Applies to **certified Jersey funds** that meet prescribed
  conditions;
- Provides **explicit tax-exempt status** independent of zero-
  ten;
- Commonly used by **public funds** for marketing certainty;
- For **private funds**, zero-ten 0% rate typically suffices —
  Article 118C is less commonly invoked.

The practical difference between Article 118C exemption and
zero-ten 0% rate is **modest** in most cases, but Article 118C
provides **clearer ground** for foreign investors who want
certified exempt status.

## The proper answer

### What Article 118C provides

Article 118C of the Income Tax (Jersey) Law 1961 provides
**exemption from Jersey income tax** for specified categories of
**certified collective investment fund** and certain related
service providers.

The exemption is **explicit** — the fund is not just at 0% but
**outside the income-tax base** for the relevant categories.

### Eligible funds

To qualify for Article 118C exemption:

- The fund must be a **collective investment scheme** as defined;
- Typically must be **registered with the JFSC** under the CIF
  Law 1988 (public funds) or appropriate regime;
- Must meet **prescribed conditions** (genuine pooled investment,
  investor diversity, etc.);
- Must **apply** to Revenue Jersey for the certification.

### Application process

To obtain Article 118C status:

1. **Determine** eligibility based on fund profile;
2. **Apply** to Revenue Jersey with supporting documentation;
3. **Receive** certification (or rejection);
4. **Operate** under exempt status;
5. **Maintain** compliance with ongoing conditions.

### Article 118C vs zero-ten 0%

For most practical purposes:

| Aspect | Article 118C exempt | Zero-ten 0% rate |
|---|---|---|
| Tax payable | None | None |
| Filing | Reduced / exempt | Full annual return |
| Certificate | Yes (Article 118C certificate) | None |
| Foreign-investor comfort | Higher (explicit exemption) | Standard |
| Conditions | Specific certified-fund conditions | Standard zero-ten |

For **most private funds (JPF / Expert)**, zero-ten 0% is
sufficient. For **public funds** with broad investor bases,
Article 118C certification is often pursued.

### Service providers

Article 118C may also extend exemption to:

- The **manager** of the certified fund;
- The **trustee** of a certified unit trust;
- **Other specified service providers** to certified funds;
- Subject to **specific conditions** in each case.

This is part of how Jersey supports the **fund-services
ecosystem** with consistent tax treatment.

### Compliance considerations

Once Article 118C status is granted:

- **Maintain** the conditions of certification;
- **Notify** Revenue Jersey of material changes;
- **File** required ongoing reports;
- **Lose status** if conditions cease to be met.

A fund that loses Article 118C status reverts to zero-ten (0%
typically) — so the consequence isn't catastrophic but the
**certification value** is lost.

## What to do next

For a new fund:

1. **Determine** whether Article 118C is appropriate;
2. **Compare** with zero-ten-only approach;
3. **Apply** if Article 118C status is desired;
4. **Maintain** ongoing compliance.

For an existing fund:

- **Review** whether Article 118C status would benefit;
- **Apply** if structural changes warrant;
- **Maintain** existing status if held.

## Pitfalls

- **Over-applying**: not every fund needs Article 118C — zero-
  ten suffices for many;
- **Conditions lapse**: losing certification through inadvertent
  non-compliance;
- **Investor expectation mismatch**: foreign investors may
  expect Article 118C even where not strictly needed.
