# PEPs / High-Risk Jurisdiction Investors — How to Handle

## The question

A prospective fund investor is a **PEP**, or based in a
**high-risk jurisdiction**. Can you admit them, and on what
conditions?

## The short answer

PEPs and high-risk-jurisdiction investors **are not prohibited**
in Jersey funds — but they require:

- **Enhanced due diligence (EDD)** at onboarding;
- **Senior-management approval** of the admission;
- **Enhanced source-of-wealth verification**;
- **Enhanced ongoing monitoring**;
- **Periodic review** at higher cadence.

The fund's **board / GP** ultimately decides whether to accept.
Many funds **decline** such investors as a policy matter for
risk-management or reputation reasons.

## The proper answer

### PEP investor mechanics

PEPs in fund context are typically:

- **HNW family-office** representatives of PEPs (the PEP's
  family office investing on their behalf);
- **Direct PEP** investors investing personal funds;
- **Sovereign wealth** entities controlled by PEP regimes.

Family-office investments **layered through entities** can
**obscure** PEP involvement — investor onboarding must reach
to the **ultimate beneficial owner** for the PEP screen.

### High-risk jurisdiction investors

"High-risk jurisdiction" typically means:

- **FATF grey/black list** jurisdictions;
- **Jersey-specified** high-risk lists;
- **Sanctions-impacted** jurisdictions (even if specific
  investors are not designated);
- **Jurisdictions of concern** for AML / financial-crime
  reasons.

Investors **from or with material links to** these jurisdictions
require EDD.

### EDD content

For PEP / high-risk investors:

#### Source of wealth — enhanced

- **Detailed narrative** of wealth accumulation;
- **Documentary evidence** for major wealth-generating events;
- **Tax compliance** in home jurisdiction;
- **Audited financials** for business-derived wealth;
- **Pre-PEP vs post-PEP** distinction in time.

#### Senior management approval

- **Board / GP approval** of the relationship;
- **Risk-committee** review for highest-risk cases;
- **Documented reasoning** for acceptance.

#### Enhanced ongoing monitoring

- **Lower threshold** transaction alerts;
- **Adverse-media ongoing screening**;
- **Annual review** instead of typical 3-yearly;
- **Trigger reviews** on political events / regime changes.

### Practical decision factors

#### When PEP / high-risk acceptable

- **Clear source of wealth** documentation;
- **Established reputation** with no adverse media;
- **Stable political environment** in PEP's jurisdiction;
- **Long-term relationship** with the fund / manager;
- **Strategic investment** size meriting EDD overhead.

#### When to decline

- **Adverse media** of corruption / sanctions risk;
- **Unstable political environment**;
- **Inadequate source-of-wealth** documentation;
- **Counterparty pressure** from other investors;
- **Fund-level risk appetite** not aligned.

### Counterparty implications

PEP / high-risk investors can affect:

- **Banking relationships** of the fund (correspondent banks
  may refuse the fund if PEP exposure is high);
- **Other investor confidence** (sophisticated investors
  scrutinise co-investor lists);
- **Regulatory standing** with the JFSC.

These second-order effects can drive **policy declines** even
where individual EDD would technically permit admission.

### Documentation

Maintain:

- **EDD file** with all source-of-wealth evidence;
- **Risk assessment** specific to the investor;
- **Senior-management approval** records;
- **Ongoing monitoring** logs;
- **Policy decision** records (if declining, document why).

## What to do next

For a PEP / high-risk applicant:

1. **Screen** to identify PEP / high-risk status;
2. **Conduct EDD**;
3. **Risk-assess** comprehensively;
4. **Escalate** to senior management;
5. **Decide** with documentation;
6. **If accepting**: set up enhanced ongoing monitoring;
7. **If declining**: communicate professionally without
   over-explaining (privacy considerations).

## Pitfalls

- **Subscription pressure**: don't let fund-close deadlines
  compromise EDD;
- **Family-office obfuscation**: ultimate beneficial owner may
  be PEP even where direct subscriber isn't;
- **Banking-relationship damage**: aggressive PEP acceptance
  can endanger fund's banking;
- **Reputational contagion**: high-profile PEP losses (regime
  changes) can damage the manager broadly.
