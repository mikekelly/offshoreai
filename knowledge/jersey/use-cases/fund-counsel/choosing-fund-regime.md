# Which Jersey Fund Regime is Right?

## The question

You are launching a Jersey fund. Which regime — JPF, Expert,
Eligible Investor, Listed, Public, or Unregulated — fits your
fund?

## The short answer

Three factors drive the choice:

1. **Investor cap and qualification** — how many investors,
   and how sophisticated?
2. **Marketing distribution** — private placement to known
   investors, or broad distribution?
3. **Regulatory cost tolerance** — light-touch + fast, or
   full-supervision + slow?

For most **closed-ended PE / VC / real estate** funds with up
to 50 professional investors, **JPF** is the default. For
**larger investor bases**, **Expert Fund** or **Listed Fund**.
For **retail distribution**, **public fund** under CIF Law
1988.

## The proper answer

### Decision tree

```
Is the fund being marketed to retail investors?
├── Yes → Public Fund (CIF Law 1988)
└── No → continue

How many investors?
├── ≤ 50 (offered to ≤ 50 potential investors) → JPF
└── > 50 → continue

Are investors all "expert"?
├── Yes → Expert Fund
└── Mixed → Eligible Investor Fund or Listed Fund

Is the fund listed on a recognised exchange?
├── Yes → Listed Fund
└── No → Expert / Eligible Investor Fund as above
```

### Comparison table

| Factor | JPF | Expert Fund | Eligible Investor | Listed Fund | Public Fund |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| Investor cap | ≤ 50 | None per se | None per se | None per se | None per se |
| Investor qualification | Professional / eligible | Expert (high net-worth) | Eligible | Listed-investor base | Retail OK |
| JFSC authorisation time | ~48 hours | Days–weeks | Days–weeks | Days–weeks | Weeks |
| Prospectus requirement | Light PPM | Offering memo | Offering memo | Listing prospectus | Full CIF prospectus |
| Service provider permits | DSP only | FSB-licensed | FSB-licensed | FSB-licensed + listing rules | Full FSB + JFSC fund permit |
| Marketing scope | Private placement, ≤ 50 offers | Wider private placement | Eligible-investor private placement | Public via listing | Full public |
| Typical use | PE, RE, club deals, single-LP | Hedge funds, larger PE | Mid-sized institutional | Investment trusts, REITs | Retail-distributed |

### Specific scenarios

- **PE / VC fund with up to 50 LPs**: **JPF**. Fast, light,
  costs aligned with closed-ended PE economics.
- **Hedge fund targeting institutional + family-office investors**:
  **Expert Fund**. AIFMD-NPPR positioning supported.
- **Real-estate fund listed on the LSE Main Market**:
  **Listed Fund**. Combines Jersey corporate / tax flexibility
  with LSE liquidity.
- **Retail UCITS-equivalent**: **Public Fund** under CIF Law.
- **Single-family vehicle for a wealthy family's investments**:
  **Unregulated Fund** or **JPF**, depending on size and
  marketing.

## What to do next

1. **Confirm investor type and number** — get clear on whether
   you can credibly cap at 50 and document the investor
   qualification.
2. **Choose the vehicle type** — Jersey company / LP / unit
   trust — separately from the regime decision (see
   [vehicle structuring](./index.md) — *planned*).
3. **Engage a Jersey-licensed administrator** as DSP (for JPF)
   or FSB-licensed service provider (other regimes).
4. **Draft the offering document** matching the regime's
   disclosure expectations.
5. **Submit to the JFSC** through the appropriate online portal
   or filing route.

## Pitfalls

- **Going too light**: a "JPF" for what should be an Expert
  Fund will be re-categorised by the JFSC if you go over the
  50-investor cap; better to size up at outset.
- **Going too heavy**: a public-fund-style filing for a club
  deal is expensive and delays time-to-market.
- **Misjudging investor qualification**: relying on "expert"
  certification that doesn't hold up under scrutiny.
