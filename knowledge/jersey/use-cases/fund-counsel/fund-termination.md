# How to Terminate a Jersey Fund

## The question

Your Jersey fund has reached the end of its life — final
investments realised, capital returned. How do you formally
terminate the fund?

## The short answer

Termination depends on fund vehicle:

- **Jersey LP**: dissolution under the Limited Partnerships
  (Jersey) Law (typically via final distribution and notice
  to the registrar);
- **Jersey company**: summary winding-up under Part 21 of the
  Companies Law (Chapter 2), then dissolution;
- **Jersey unit trust**: distribution to unitholders and trust
  termination per the trust deed;
- **Cell of a PCC / ICC**: cell-specific winding-down within
  the umbrella.

Each path requires:

- **Final realisation** of assets;
- **Final distribution** to investors;
- **Final reporting** and auditor sign-off;
- **JFSC notification** for regulated funds;
- **Registrar filings** for formal dissolution.

## The proper answer

### Pre-termination planning

Before formal termination:

- **Realise** remaining investments (or transfer to liquidating
  trust);
- **Settle** outstanding liabilities (manager fees, expenses,
  contingents);
- **Hold-back** for tail liabilities (litigation, tax assessments,
  warranty claims);
- **Final audit** of the fund's accounts;
- **Final distribution** to investors.

### LP fund termination

For a Jersey LP fund:

1. **GP makes** final distribution;
2. **LP files** notice of dissolution with the registrar;
3. **Outstanding obligations** discharged or hold-back established;
4. **Registrar removes** the LP from the active register;
5. **Final tax filings** by the LP.

The Jersey Limited Partnerships Law provides the procedural
framework.

### Company fund termination

For a Jersey company fund:

1. **Directors** make a **solvency statement** (Article 146 of
   Companies Law);
2. **Members** pass a **special resolution** for summary winding
   up;
3. **Liquidator appointed** (often a director or DSP officer);
4. **Final realisation and distribution**;
5. **Final account** filed with registrar;
6. **Company dissolved** after prescribed period.

For an insolvent fund (rare for properly-managed funds), the
**creditors' winding-up** route under Chapter 4 of Part 21
applies.

### Unit trust fund termination

For a Jersey unit trust:

1. **Trustee** follows the **trust-deed termination** procedure;
2. **Final NAV** calculation;
3. **Final distribution** to unitholders;
4. **Trustee discharge** with appropriate documentation;
5. **JFSC notification** for regulated funds.

### Cell winding-down

For a cell of a PCC / ICC:

1. **Cell-specific resolution** to wind down;
2. **Realise** cell-specific assets;
3. **Settle** cell-specific liabilities;
4. **Distribute** cellular surplus to cellular members;
5. **Cell ceases** to operate while PCC / ICC continues.

For an **ICC cell** (separately incorporated), this is like
winding up a small company; for a **PCC cell**, more
administrative.

### JFSC notification

For regulated funds:

- **Notify** the JFSC of intent to terminate;
- **Final regulatory filings**;
- **Surrender** of fund-specific registrations;
- **Coordinate** with administrator / DSP de-registration.

### Investor communications

Throughout termination:

- **Communicate** clearly with investors on timeline;
- **Provide** distribution notices and statements;
- **Explain** any hold-back arrangements;
- **Issue** final tax statements for investor jurisdictions.

### Service-provider termination

Coordinate with:

- **Administrator** — final NAV and distribution;
- **Auditor** — final audit and report;
- **Custodian** — return of any custodial assets;
- **Banks** — closing fund accounts;
- **Other** service providers (legal, tax advisers).

### Tail liabilities

Common tail-liability issues:

- **Tax assessments** that may arise post-termination;
- **Litigation** continuing past termination;
- **Warranty claims** under sale-of-portfolio-company
  arrangements;
- **Investor complaints**.

These are typically handled through:

- **Hold-back amounts** retained for tail period;
- **Insurance** for specific risks;
- **GP / manager indemnities** for post-termination matters;
- **Liquidating trust** to manage tail.

## What to do next

For fund termination:

1. **Plan** the termination timeline well in advance;
2. **Realise** remaining investments;
3. **Engage** auditors for final accounts;
4. **Establish** hold-back arrangements;
5. **Make** final distribution;
6. **File** dissolution documents;
7. **Communicate** with investors throughout.

## Pitfalls

- **Premature distribution**: distributing before tail
  liabilities settled exposes the GP;
- **Inadequate hold-back**: insufficient reserve for tail
  exposures;
- **JFSC filing gaps**: incomplete de-registration leaves the
  fund on the active register;
- **Service-provider end-of-life**: ensuring service
  continuity through termination process.
