# Jersey vs Cayman — Choosing a Fund Domicile

## The question

You're advising on the choice between **Jersey** and **Cayman**
for a new fund. Cayman dominates global hedge fund / PE
domiciliation. When does Jersey actually win?

## The short answer

- **Cayman wins by default** for **hedge funds** and **PE
  funds** marketed primarily to **US / Asian investors**;
- **Jersey wins** for funds with **EU / UK investor base**,
  **Crown Dependency** preference, **GBP** denomination, or
  **specific structural advantages**;
- **Cost / speed** favours Cayman for **standard structures**;
- **Sophisticated structures** (PCC, ICC, scheme of
  arrangement) may favour Jersey.

## The proper answer

### Where Cayman dominates

#### US-investor hedge funds

- **Cayman is industry standard** for US-investor hedge funds;
- **Investors expect** Cayman;
- **Less friction** in onboarding;
- **Established** investor familiarity.

#### Global PE / VC funds

- **Cayman exempted limited partnership** is the **global
  standard** for PE funds;
- **GP / LP** structure;
- **Tax transparency** for US LPs;
- **Carry vehicles** in Cayman.

#### Speed-critical fund launches

- **Hours to days** to incorporate in Cayman;
- **Faster** approval timelines;
- **Specific** fast-track procedures.

#### Cost-sensitive structures

- **Lower** annual fees per fund;
- **Cheaper** to administer;
- **Specific** Cayman economies of scale.

### Where Jersey wins

#### EU / UK investor base

- **Jersey** is in **Europe time zone**;
- **GBP / EUR** denomination often preferred;
- **EU / UK** familiarity (Crown Dependency status);
- **NPPR** access for EU distribution.

#### Crown Dependency preference

- Some institutional investors prefer Crown Dependency over
  British Overseas Territory;
- **Jersey** considered more "European" by some.

#### Specific structures

- **PCC** for multi-class fund umbrella (Jersey
  well-developed);
- **JPF** for closed-circle private funds (faster than Cayman
  Private Investment Fund in some cases);
- **Scheme of arrangement** for restructuring;
- **Specific** Jersey legal features.

#### Substance scaling

- Jersey has **good substance capacity** for in-scope
  activities;
- **Specific** Jersey advantages for substance-heavy
  structures.

#### Existing Jersey relationships

- **Manager** based in Jersey;
- **Existing** structures expanding;
- **Continuity** of advisers.

### Specific comparison points

#### Fund vehicles

| Vehicle | Jersey | Cayman |
|---|---|---|
| Hedge fund | JPF, Expert Fund | Exempted company / LLC under Mutual Funds Act |
| PE / VC | Jersey LP / SLP / ILP | Cayman ELP |
| Real estate | Specific JPF or LP | Various |
| Umbrella | PCC | SPC |

#### Cost

- **Cayman**: lower government fees, faster establishment;
- **Jersey**: typically higher fees but with established
  services.

#### Regulator

- **CIMA**: experienced, faster on routine matters;
- **JFSC**: experienced, similar approach.

#### Substance

- **Both** subject to substance laws;
- **Jersey** has good infrastructure for substance;
- **Cayman** depends on functions performed.

#### Investor base

- **Cayman**: US institutional and HNW dominant;
- **Jersey**: European institutional, UK investors,
  international HNW.

### Mixed approach — master / feeder

Many funds use **mixed** Cayman / Jersey:

#### Cayman master + Jersey feeder

- **Cayman master fund** holds investments;
- **Jersey feeder** for European / GBP investors;
- **Tax-transparent** flow-through;
- **Specific** structural considerations.

#### Jersey master + Cayman feeder

- **Less common** but possible;
- **Jersey master** with specific Jersey advantages;
- **Cayman feeder** for US investors.

### Decision framework

For a new fund:

1. **Investor base** primary driver:
   - US-heavy → Cayman;
   - Europe-heavy → Jersey;
   - Mixed → consider master / feeder.

2. **Strategy / asset class**:
   - Hedge fund (liquid alternatives) → Cayman default;
   - PE / VC → Cayman default for cross-border, Jersey for
     EU-focus;
   - Real estate → either depending on assets;
   - Specialty (insurance-linked) → Bermuda (separate
     analysis).

3. **Speed / cost**:
   - Cayman favoured if speed / cost matter most.

4. **Specific structural needs**:
   - Cell structures → Jersey PCC or Cayman SPC;
   - Specific legal features → assess each.

5. **Manager preferences and existing relationships**:
   - Often decisive in practice.

### Practical considerations

#### Investor preferences

Ask investors:

- Do they have specific preferences?
- Are they familiar with Cayman / Jersey?
- Will jurisdiction affect investment decision?
- US LP tax issues with non-Cayman fund?

#### Manager preferences

- Where is manager based?
- What is fund's principal market?
- What existing relationships?

#### Service providers

- Where are key service providers based?
- Cross-border service coordination?

## What to do next

For a new fund:

1. **Map** the investor base;
2. **Identify** strategy / asset class;
3. **Consider** speed / cost / structural drivers;
4. **Discuss** with investors and manager;
5. **Decide** on principal domicile;
6. **Consider** master / feeder if multi-investor-base;
7. **Engage** counsel in chosen jurisdiction(s).

## Pitfalls

- **Defaulting** to one without analysis;
- **Underestimating** investor preferences;
- **Not considering** EU investor / NPPR considerations;
- **Inadequate** master / feeder analysis;
- **Specific** structural issues unaddressed.
