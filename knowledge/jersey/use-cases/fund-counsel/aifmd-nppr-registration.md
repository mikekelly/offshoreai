# AIFMD / NPPR Registration for EU Marketing

## The question

You want to market your Jersey AIF (alternative investment fund)
into the EU under the **National Private Placement Regime
(NPPR)**. What's required?

## The short answer

To market a Jersey AIF into an EU member state under NPPR:

- The Jersey AIFM must be **JFSC-registered** under the
  AIFMD-equivalent Jersey regime;
- The AIFM must demonstrate **AIFMD-equivalent compliance** in
  Jersey (capital, risk management, depository, transparency,
  reporting);
- An **NPPR notification** must be filed with the relevant
  EU member state's competent authority;
- The AIF must meet that **member state's NPPR conditions**
  (which vary).

Each member state has **different NPPR rules**. Germany and
France are stricter than some others.

## The proper answer

### The AIFMD context

AIFMD (Alternative Investment Fund Managers Directive) is the
EU's framework for AIFMs. Jersey is **non-EU**, so direct AIFMD
passporting doesn't apply. The routes available:

1. **NPPR (country-by-country private placement)** — current
   route for Jersey AIFs;
2. **Direct AIFMD passport** (third-country) — proposed but
   not implemented;
3. **EU-domiciled feeders** — alternative structure routing
   capital through an EU vehicle.

NPPR is the practical route for Jersey AIFMs marketing into EU.

### Jersey AIFMD-equivalent regime

The JFSC operates an **AIFMD-equivalent registration** regime:

- **JFSC AIFM registration** required;
- **Capital adequacy** proportionate to AIFM size;
- **Risk-management framework** equivalent to AIFMD Art 15;
- **Liquidity management** for open-ended AIFs;
- **Depository arrangements** for AIFs marketed to EU;
- **Transparency to investors** (annual report, periodic
  reporting);
- **Regulatory reporting** to JFSC.

Once Jersey-AIFMD-registered, the AIFM can pursue NPPR
notifications.

### NPPR notification process

For each EU member state where marketing is desired:

1. **Identify** the competent authority (e.g. BaFin for
   Germany, AMF for France, FCA-equivalent for UK post-Brexit);
2. **Prepare** the NPPR notification package (varies by state);
3. **Confirm** that Jersey AIFM regime is recognised as
   equivalent by that state;
4. **Submit** notification;
5. **Await** clearance — timeline varies.

Once cleared, the AIF can be marketed **to professional
investors in that state**.

### Member-state variations

#### Germany

- Strict NPPR rules under §330 KAGB;
- **Local fact-sheet** required;
- **Local distributor** sometimes required;
- **Reporting** obligations after acceptance.

#### France

- AMF NPPR rules;
- **Detailed documentation** required;
- **Periodic reporting**;
- Specific provisions for **French-tax** investor types.

#### Netherlands / Luxembourg / Ireland

- Generally more accessible NPPR regimes;
- **Standard notification** typically sufficient.

#### UK

- **Post-Brexit** has its own NPPR (rather than EU rules);
- **FCA** is the competent authority;
- **AIFMD-equivalent compliance** demonstrated to FCA.

### Depository requirement

A Jersey AIF marketed to EU via NPPR typically requires:

- **AIFMD-equivalent depository** arrangements;
- **Custody, oversight, cash-flow monitoring** functions;
- For Jersey AIFs, often a **lighter depository** with the
  full AIFMD depository elsewhere (combined arrangement);
- **Jersey depository** must be JFSC-licensed.

### Reverse solicitation

NPPR registration is **only required for active marketing**.
**Reverse solicitation** (investor approaches the AIF
unsolicited) doesn't trigger NPPR:

- **But the documentation** must demonstrate genuine reverse
  solicitation;
- **Member-state authorities** scrutinise reverse-solicitation
  claims;
- Active outreach **disguised** as reverse solicitation can
  attract penalties.

### Brexit context

UK NPPR is now **separate** from EU NPPR:

- **Jersey AIFs marketing to UK** use UK NPPR;
- **Jersey AIFs marketing to EU member states** use each
  state's NPPR;
- **Two separate** notification streams for UK + EU exposure.

## What to do next

1. **Confirm** AIFMD-equivalent classification of the fund;
2. **Register** as Jersey AIFM with JFSC;
3. **Set up** AIFMD-equivalent compliance infrastructure;
4. **Identify** target EU / UK member states;
5. **Prepare** NPPR notification packages;
6. **Submit** notifications;
7. **Operate** marketing within NPPR limits.

## Pitfalls

- **Active marketing without notification**: serious
  enforcement risk;
- **Reverse-solicitation overclaim**: doesn't withstand
  scrutiny;
- **Depository deficiency**: lighter Jersey depository for
  EU-marketed AIFs is a regulatory mismatch;
- **Brexit confusion**: UK is now its own NPPR jurisdiction.
