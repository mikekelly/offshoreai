# Carry Vehicle Structuring for Fund-Management Teams

## The question

Your private-equity / venture-capital fund pays **carried
interest** to the management team. How should the **carry
vehicle** be structured?

## The short answer

A typical Jersey carry vehicle is:

- A **Jersey LP** with the management-team members as limited
  partners (or sometimes a Jersey company);
- The carry vehicle is itself an LP / member of the main fund
  LP;
- **Carry allocations** flow up to the carry vehicle and then
  to the management-team members;
- **Vesting and forfeiture** rules apply to align team incentives;
- **Home-jurisdiction tax** of the carry-recipients is the
  dominant consideration.

## The proper answer

### What carry is

**Carried interest** is the **performance-based participation**
of the management team in fund profits:

- Typically **20% of profits above a hurdle rate** (e.g. 8%);
- **Variable** — depends on actual fund performance;
- **Long-tail** — paid as exits realise over fund life;
- **Concentrated** — typically goes to a small senior team.

### The carry-vehicle structure

A typical structure:

```
[Management team members] 
    |
    are limited partners in
    |
[Carry Vehicle LP] (Jersey)
    |
    is a limited partner in / receives carry from
    |
[Main Fund LP] (Jersey)
```

The carry vehicle holds the **carry interest** in the main fund.
Distributions of carry flow to the carry vehicle and through to
its limited partners (the management team).

### Why Jersey for carry

Jersey is a common carry-vehicle jurisdiction because:

- **No Jersey CGT** on capital gains realised by the carry
  vehicle;
- **No Jersey tax** at carry-vehicle level for non-resident
  team members;
- **Familiar legal form** alongside the Jersey main fund;
- **Operational efficiency** — same administrator handles
  both vehicles.

### Vesting and forfeiture

The carry vehicle typically includes:

- **Vesting schedules** — team members earn their carry
  interest over service period;
- **Good leaver / bad leaver** treatment;
- **Forfeiture** on departure;
- **Bonus pool** to reward exceptional contribution.

Documented in the carry-vehicle LPA and side letters.

### Tax considerations

#### At Jersey level

- **Zero** typically (no Jersey tax on either the carry vehicle
  or its non-resident partners).

#### At team-member level

- **Home-jurisdiction tax** is the substantive issue;
- **UK**: detailed carry-tax rules (income / CGT
  characterisation, **disguised investment management fee**
  rules);
- **US**: carry as capital gain vs ordinary income (Section
  1061 and successor rules);
- **Other jurisdictions**: varies.

Carry-vehicle structuring **must integrate** with each team
member's home-jurisdiction tax position.

### Co-investment

Carry vehicles often have **co-investment** features:

- Team members **invest their own capital** alongside the
  carry;
- Co-invest may be at **fund terms** (no fee, no carry) or
  separate;
- **Aligns** team interest with LP outcomes.

### Documentation

The carry vehicle's documentation includes:

- **Carry-vehicle LPA** — governance, vesting, forfeiture,
  distribution waterfall;
- **Individual side letters** with each team member —
  vesting-specific, performance-specific terms;
- **Main-fund LPA** — defines the carry mechanic for the main
  fund.

### Operational complexity

Operating a carry vehicle requires:

- **Administrator** to maintain capital accounts per team
  member;
- **Annual reporting** to team members for their tax filings;
- **Vesting tracking** through service period;
- **Distribution mechanics** as the main fund realises carry.

## What to do next

For a new fund:

1. **Engage** Jersey counsel for fund formation including carry
   vehicle;
2. **Integrate** with home-jurisdiction tax advisers for team
   members;
3. **Document** carry-vehicle terms (vesting, forfeiture, etc.);
4. **Set up** carry-vehicle alongside main fund;
5. **Administer** through fund's administrator.

For an existing carry vehicle:

- **Refresh** terms as team composition evolves;
- **Address** new team members and departures;
- **Coordinate** with annual carry distributions.

## Pitfalls

- **Carry-tax characterisation surprise** in home jurisdictions;
- **Vesting disputes** without clear documentation;
- **Bonus pool** discretionary mechanics generating disputes;
- **Co-invest tax interaction** with carry tax.
