# JPF Registration — Documents and Process

## The question

You've decided on a **JPF** (Jersey Private Fund). What
documents do you need, and how do you get JFSC authorisation?

## The short answer

A JPF is authorised by the JFSC in **approximately 48 hours**
of submission of a complete application. The application
package includes:

- A **completed JFSC application form**;
- The fund's **PPM (Private Placement Memorandum)**;
- **Constitutional documents** of the fund vehicle;
- **Designated Service Provider (DSP) confirmation** of
  appointment;
- **Subscription documents** including investor eligibility
  certifications;
- Prescribed **fees**.

## The proper answer

### The DSP — your single most important choice

A JPF must have a **Designated Service Provider** — a
Jersey-licensed administrator that takes substantive
responsibility for:

- **CDD** on all investors;
- **AML / CFT** compliance for the fund;
- **JFSC liaison** as the regulated entity;
- **Books and records** maintenance;
- **Investor admissions and redemptions**.

The DSP is **the JFSC's regulatory hook** on a JPF. Choose a
DSP with:

- **Established Jersey funds experience**;
- **Capacity** for your fund's planned scale;
- **Compatible service-provider relationships** (custodian,
  auditor, etc.);
- **Competitive fee structure**.

### The PPM

The PPM should cover:

- **Fund objective and strategy**;
- **Manager / GP identity and track record**;
- **Investment terms** — subscription, redemption, lock-up;
- **Fees** — management fee, carry, expenses;
- **Risk factors**;
- **Conflicts of interest** disclosure;
- **Tax considerations** for investors in relevant
  jurisdictions (with appropriate disclaimers);
- **Subscription mechanics**;
- **Governance** — board / GP composition, decision-making;
- **Investor reporting** approach.

The PPM is **not JFSC-reviewed for substance** (unlike public-
fund prospectuses) but should be **professional and accurate** —
the DSP and the manager bear responsibility for its
sufficiency.

### Constitutional documents

Depending on vehicle type:

- **LP agreement** for an LP;
- **Memorandum and articles** for a company;
- **Trust deed** for a unit trust.

These should reflect the fund's investment terms and be drafted
by Jersey counsel familiar with the regime.

### Investor eligibility

JPF investors must qualify under prescribed criteria:

- **Professional investor** definitions including high-net-worth
  and institutional;
- **Eligible investor** under prescribed thresholds;
- **Self-certification** required at subscription.

The DSP verifies investor eligibility as part of CDD.

### The 50-investor cap

A JPF can be **offered to no more than 50 potential investors**:

- **"Offers"** is the key concept — not just final
  subscriptions;
- **Marketing breadth** must be controlled to stay within the
  cap;
- Going over **converts** the fund to a regulated category
  requiring different authorisation.

Careful **deal-room** and **PPM distribution** management is
essential.

### Filing and timing

- **Submit** the application via the JFSC online portal;
- **Pay** prescribed fees;
- **48-hour** authorisation target after complete submission;
- **JFSC may request** additional information for unusual
  structures.

After authorisation:

- **First investors can subscribe**;
- **DSP commences** ongoing compliance work;
- **Fund operates** within the JPF regime ongoing.

## What to do next

1. **Engage Jersey counsel** to draft constitutional documents
   and the PPM.
2. **Engage the DSP** and confirm capacity / fees.
3. **Prepare** investor materials including subscription
   documents.
4. **Submit** to the JFSC.
5. **Plan** for first-closing investor admission.

## Pitfalls

- **Over-broad marketing**: distributing the PPM to more than
  50 potential investors converts the JPF.
- **Investor eligibility shortfall**: weak certification
  evidence can be challenged later.
- **DSP mismatch**: a DSP without capacity for your fund's
  growth path creates ongoing friction.
- **Tax disclosure deficiencies**: investor jurisdictions can
  treat the fund differently from expected, requiring careful
  disclosure.
