# Jersey Fund Vehicle — Company, LP, or Unit Trust?

## The question

Once you have chosen the **regime** (JPF / Expert / Listed /
etc.), you still need to choose the **legal form** of the fund
vehicle. The principal options are:

- **Jersey company** (typically no-par-value private company);
- **Jersey limited partnership** (LP);
- **Jersey unit trust**;
- **Cell company** (PCC or ICC) for umbrella structures.

## The short answer

- **Limited partnership** is the dominant choice for **closed-
  ended PE / VC / RE** funds — tax-transparent in most investor
  jurisdictions, familiar to institutional LPs;
- **Company** is the choice for **open-ended** funds and **listed
  funds**;
- **Unit trust** is used where tax-transparency without an LP's
  partnership characterisation is needed;
- **Cell company** is the umbrella structure for **multi-strategy
  / multi-class** offerings within a single corporate shell.

## The proper answer

### Limited partnership

- A **Jersey LP** under the Limited Partnerships (Jersey) Law
  1994 (or LLLP / ILP under the **Separate Limited Partnerships**
  / **Incorporated Limited Partnerships** Laws);
- **General partner (GP)** has unlimited liability and management
  power;
- **Limited partners (LPs)** have limited liability provided they
  don't participate in management;
- **Tax-transparent** in most investor jurisdictions
  (look-through for tax purposes);
- **Common LP structures**: Jersey LP with Jersey-LLC-equivalent
  or Jersey-company GP, often with Jersey trust-company-provided
  GP services.

### Limited partnership variants

- **LP** (standard) — no separate legal personality;
- **SLP (Separate Limited Partnership)** — separate legal
  personality but maintains tax transparency;
- **ILP (Incorporated Limited Partnership)** — incorporated
  separate personality.

The choice between LP / SLP / ILP turns on **specific tax
characterisation** in investor jurisdictions and **operational
needs**.

### Company

- A **Jersey company** under the Companies Law 1991;
- **Body corporate** with own legal personality;
- **Shareholders** rather than partners;
- **Taxed as a Jersey company** (0% under zero-ten);
- **Open-ended companies** can issue and redeem shares as
  investors come and go.

Companies suit:

- **Open-ended funds** with regular subscription / redemption;
- **Listed funds** (the listed vehicle is almost always a
  company);
- **Retail funds** where partnership tax-transparency isn't a
  requirement;
- **Funds for investors** preferring corporate familiarity.

### Unit trust

- A **Jersey trust** with **units** representing investor
  interests;
- **Trustee** holds the assets on trust for unitholders;
- **Tax-transparent** in many investor jurisdictions
  (look-through);
- **Distinctive feature**: relies on Jersey trust law rather
  than corporate or partnership law.

Unit trusts suit:

- **UK-investor-oriented** funds (UK has a long tradition of
  unit trusts);
- **Specific tax-classification** needs in particular
  jurisdictions.

### Cell company (PCC / ICC)

- A **single corporate vehicle** under Part 18D Companies Law
  with multiple **cells**;
- **PCC** — statutory segregation between cells;
- **ICC** — each cell separately incorporated;
- Used for **umbrella fund structures** with multiple sub-funds.

Cell structures suit:

- **Multi-strategy** fund managers offering several strategies
  under one umbrella;
- **Master-feeder** structures;
- **Cost-efficient platforms** sharing infrastructure across
  strategies.

## Decision factors

| Factor | LP | Company | Unit Trust | Cell |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Tax transparency for investors | Yes (typically) | No (Jersey company) | Yes (typically) | Depends on cell type |
| Open-ended capable | Limited | Yes (open-ended Co.) | Yes | Yes |
| Closed-ended capable | Yes (typical) | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| Multiple strategies in one vehicle | Limited | Limited | Limited | Yes — native |
| Familiar to institutional LPs | Yes — gold standard | Less so | Variable | Yes for fund-manager platforms |
| Listing capable | No | Yes | Limited | Limited |
| Operational simplicity | Moderate | High | Moderate | Higher (multi-cell) |

## What to do next

1. **Confirm investor tax classification needs** — driven by the
   investor base's home jurisdictions.
2. **Choose the vehicle type** based on the factors above.
3. **Engage Jersey counsel** to draft the constitutional
   documents (LP agreement / articles / trust deed).
4. **Engage the JFSC service-provider** as needed (DSP for JPF,
   FSB-licensed for other regimes).

## Pitfalls

- **Tax classification mismatch**: choosing an LP for a fund
  whose investors include jurisdictions that don't recognise
  partnership transparency in the same way (e.g. some civil-law
  investor bases).
- **Operating cost**: cell structures are cost-efficient at
  scale but have minimum overhead even for small cells.
- **Listing constraints**: only companies (typically) are
  listable.
