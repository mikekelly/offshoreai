# Investor Reporting and Disclosure Obligations

## The question

What **investor reporting** and **disclosure obligations** does
a Jersey fund have? What is required by regulation vs by market
practice vs by the fund's own LPA / PPM?

## The short answer

Three layers:

1. **Regulatory** — JFSC Code of Practice requirements for fund
   service providers, plus statutory filings;
2. **Documentation-driven** — the fund's PPM / LPA typically
   commits to reports (quarterly NAV, annual audited financials,
   distribution statements);
3. **Market practice** — institutional investor expectations
   (capital account statements, attribution analysis, ESG
   reporting, side-letter compliance).

## The proper answer

### Regulatory reporting

#### Annual audited financials

- For most Jersey fund regimes (public funds, certain certified
  funds, listed funds): **audited financial statements**
  required annually;
- **Auditor** must be appropriately qualified;
- **Filed** with JFSC for regulated funds;
- **Distributed** to investors per the fund's documentation.

#### JFSC notifications

- **Material changes** (manager change, depository change,
  strategy change) notified;
- **Breach reporting** as required by Code of Practice;
- **Annual returns** for the fund vehicle and its administrator.

#### Tax / AEoI

- **CRS / FATCA** reports annually;
- **Beneficial-ownership** filings;
- **Underlying-company** tax returns where applicable.

### PPM / LPA-driven reporting

The fund's documentation typically commits to:

#### Quarterly reporting

- **NAV** as at quarter-end;
- **Performance** against benchmark;
- **Portfolio composition** at appropriate detail level;
- **Manager commentary** on the period;
- **Capital activity** (subscriptions, redemptions, dividends,
  distributions).

#### Annual reporting

- **Audited financial statements**;
- **Capital account** statements per investor (especially for
  LPs);
- **Comprehensive portfolio review**;
- **Strategy update** and forward look.

#### Specific events

- **Capital calls** in PE structures;
- **Distributions** with detail on source;
- **Material portfolio events** (acquisitions, disposals,
  defaults);
- **Side-letter** consequences.

### Market-practice reporting

#### ILPA-aligned reporting

For institutional investors, **ILPA standards** are increasingly
expected:

- **ILPA Template** for capital-call / distribution notices;
- **ILPA Reporting Template** for quarterly / annual reports;
- **Fee and expense** disclosures in ILPA format;
- **Performance** metrics (IRR, multiples, DPI, TVPI).

#### ESG reporting

Increasing market expectations:

- **SFDR-aligned** disclosures for EU-investor-facing funds;
- **TCFD-aligned** climate-related disclosures;
- **Custom ESG** metrics requested by specific investors.

#### Side-letter reporting

Many institutional investors have **side letters** requiring:

- **MFN** entitlement reports;
- **Investor-specific** representations;
- **Bespoke information** rights;
- **Compliance certificates**.

### Different reporting by fund type

#### Closed-end PE / VC / RE

- **Capital-call notices** quarterly or as needed;
- **Distribution notices** as exits occur;
- **Annual** comprehensive report;
- **Final accounting** at fund termination.

#### Open-ended hedge / multi-asset

- **Monthly NAV** typically;
- **Monthly investor letter** with portfolio commentary;
- **Annual audited** financials;
- **Subscription / redemption** statements per investor
  activity.

#### Listed funds

- **Continuing-disclosure** obligations under listing rules;
- **Periodic financial reports** to exchange;
- **Major-event notifications**;
- **Public-facing** reporting alongside investor-private.

### Operational ownership

Reporting is typically delivered by:

- **Administrator** for NAV / capital accounts / standard
  templates;
- **Investment manager** for portfolio commentary and ESG;
- **Auditor** for annual audited statements;
- **Service-provider team** integrated for delivery.

## What to do next

For a new fund:

1. **Define** the reporting commitments in the PPM / LPA;
2. **Align** with regulatory and market expectations;
3. **Set up** the administrator / manager workflow;
4. **Establish** delivery cadence and channels.

For an existing fund:

- **Review** reporting against ILPA / market evolving
  standards;
- **Address** investor requests for enhancement;
- **Refresh** as the fund matures.

## Pitfalls

- **Over-committing in PPM**: detailed reporting commitments
  are operationally expensive over decades;
- **Missing side-letter obligations**: track these carefully;
- **Auditor delays**: build in lead time for annual reporting;
- **ESG-greenwashing risk**: only commit to ESG reporting you
  can deliver and verify.
