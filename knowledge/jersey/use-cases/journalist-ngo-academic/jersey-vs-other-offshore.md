# How Does Jersey's Regulatory Regime Compare to Other Offshore Centres?

## The short answer

Jersey is generally considered:

- **Among the more transparent** and well-regulated offshore
  centres;
- **Comparable to Guernsey, Isle of Man, Cayman, Bermuda** in
  many respects;
- **More transparent than** BVI, Panama, Seychelles
  historically (though many of these have reformed);
- **Less transparent than** UK on beneficial ownership;
- **Substance regime** (2019) comparable to others under OECD
  BEPS pressure;
- **CRS / FATCA compliance** at FATF / Moneyval white-list
  level.

Each comparison is contextual — different jurisdictions
specialise in different products and have different histories.

## The proper answer

### Major offshore centres compared

#### Crown Dependencies

- **Jersey, Guernsey, Isle of Man**;
- **Similar** regulatory frameworks;
- **Common-law-influenced** legal systems;
- **Crown Dependency** status;
- **White-listed** by EU and OECD;
- **Substance** regimes implemented.

#### Cayman Islands

- **British Overseas Territory** (different from Crown
  Dependency);
- **Common-law jurisdiction**;
- **Major fund / hedge fund** centre;
- **CIMA** (Cayman Islands Monetary Authority);
- **Substance** regime;
- **Public BO** to authorities only.

#### Bermuda

- **British Overseas Territory**;
- **Common-law**;
- **Insurance** and **fund** centre;
- **BMA** (Bermuda Monetary Authority);
- **15% Pillar Two** rate adopted.

#### BVI (British Virgin Islands)

- **British Overseas Territory**;
- **Smaller** financial sector than peers;
- **IBC** vehicles historically very common;
- **Recent reforms** improving transparency;
- **Public BO** announced but implementation evolving.

#### Switzerland

- **EU-adjacent**;
- **Banking secrecy** historically (mostly dismantled);
- **Stable** legal environment;
- **Cantonal tax** competition;
- **Pillar Two** adopted.

#### Luxembourg

- **EU member**;
- **Funds and structured finance** centre;
- **Holding-company** specialist;
- **Tax-treaty** network significant;
- **Full EU transparency** rules.

#### Ireland

- **EU member**;
- **Operating** and IP-holding centre;
- **Public BO** register;
- **12.5% rate** (rising for Pillar Two);
- **Different positioning** from offshore typical.

#### Singapore / Hong Kong

- **Asian financial centres**;
- **Lower-tax** regimes;
- **Different** regulatory style;
- **Trust law** developments.

#### Panama / Bahamas / Seychelles

- **Historically** less transparent;
- **Significant reforms** post-Panama Papers;
- **Lower** standing on FATF / Moneyval evaluations historically.

### Comparison dimensions

#### Beneficial-ownership transparency

| Jurisdiction | Public BO? | Authority access? |
|---|---|---|
| UK | Yes | Yes |
| Jersey | No | Yes |
| Guernsey | No | Yes |
| Cayman | No | Yes |
| Bermuda | No | Yes |
| BVI | Evolving | Yes |
| Switzerland | Limited public | Yes |
| Luxembourg | Yes (EU rules) | Yes |
| Ireland | Yes (EU rules) | Yes |

#### Substance requirements

- **Jersey**: 2019 Substance Law;
- **Guernsey, IoM**: similar substance regimes;
- **Cayman**: International Tax Cooperation (Economic
  Substance) Law;
- **Bermuda**: Economic Substance Act;
- **BVI**: similar.

#### Corporate-law sophistication

- **Jersey, Cayman, Bermuda**: highly developed;
- **BVI**: simpler regime;
- **Luxembourg, Ireland**: civil-law-influenced;
- **Switzerland**: very developed.

#### Trust law

- **Jersey**: highly developed (1984 Law + extensive
  jurisprudence);
- **Guernsey**: similar;
- **Cayman**: developed;
- **Bermuda**: developed;
- **BVI**: developed;
- **Other** offshore: varies.

#### Fund-services

- **Cayman**: dominant for hedge funds;
- **Luxembourg**: dominant for UCITS and EU-marketed AIFs;
- **Jersey**: strong for PE / closed-end / listed funds;
- **Ireland**: rising challenger;
- **BVI**: smaller fund presence.

#### Insurance / captives

- **Bermuda**: dominant for major captives;
- **Guernsey**: significant;
- **Jersey**: established;
- **Cayman**: significant;
- **Luxembourg, Vermont**: also major.

### FATF / Moneyval evaluations

Most major offshore centres are now:

- **FATF / Moneyval compliant** at high level;
- **AML-CFT** robust;
- **Specific issues** addressed in mutual evaluations.

Differences in:

- **Effectiveness scores**;
- **Specific recommendations**;
- **Recent reform** progress.

### Cost / sophistication

- **Tier 1** (Jersey, Guernsey, Cayman, Bermuda, Luxembourg,
  Ireland, Singapore): highest cost, highest sophistication;
- **Tier 2** (BVI, IoM, Mauritius, Malta): mid-tier;
- **Tier 3** (Belize, Seychelles, others): lower cost, lower
  sophistication.

### For reporting / research

#### Avoiding oversimplification

- **Offshore is not a monolith** — jurisdictions vary
  significantly;
- **Reform trajectory** important — many "secret" jurisdictions
  have changed substantially;
- **Specific use cases** — different jurisdictions specialise.

#### Comparative reporting

- **Cite specific** features by jurisdiction;
- **Acknowledge reform** where relevant;
- **Don't aggregate** unfairly across different regimes.

#### Authoritative sources

- **OECD Global Forum** peer reviews;
- **FATF / Moneyval** mutual evaluations;
- **EU Code of Conduct Group** assessments;
- **Tax Justice Network** Financial Secrecy Index (one
  perspective);
- **Global Financial Integrity** reports.

## What to do next

For comparative research:

1. **Identify** the specific dimension to compare;
2. **Use** authoritative international sources;
3. **Recognise** reform trajectories;
4. **Report** nuance accurately.

## Pitfalls

- **Outdated stereotypes** of "tax haven" jurisdictions;
- **Conflating** different offshore regimes;
- **Ignoring** substantial reforms post-2010;
- **Bias** sources without acknowledgement.
