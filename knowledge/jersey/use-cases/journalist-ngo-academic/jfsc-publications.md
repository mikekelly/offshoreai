# JFSC Publications, Statements, and Regulatory Action

## The short answer

JFSC publishes:

- **Public statements** about specific regulatory action;
- **Codes of practice** and **AML/CFT Handbook**;
- **Industry guidance**;
- **Consultation papers**;
- **Annual reports**;
- **Enforcement records** (final notices, public statements);
- **Sector overviews** and **risk reports**.

All at **jerseyfsc.org**. Specific enforcement actions are
**public** when finalised.

## The proper answer

### JFSC website structure

**jerseyfsc.org** main sections:

- **About us**: institutional information;
- **Industry**: regulated-business resources, codes, guidance;
- **Authorisations**: registration / licensing;
- **Enforcement**: enforcement information and public statements;
- **News**: announcements and updates;
- **Publications**: reports, consultations, research.

### Public statements

When JFSC takes formal enforcement action:

- **Final notice** typically published;
- **Subject name** identified;
- **Sanctions** imposed described;
- **Reasoning** for action;
- **Industry-wide implications** sometimes flagged.

These are **valuable public records** for investigative reporting
on regulated-entity misconduct.

### Codes of practice

- **TCB Code of Practice** for trust company business;
- **FSB Code** for fund services;
- **Investment Business Code**;
- **Banking Code**;
- **Insurance Codes**;
- **MSB Code** for money services.

Each sets **regulatory expectations** for the relevant sector.

### AML / CFT Handbook

- **Comprehensive guidance** on AML compliance;
- **Updated periodically**;
- **Sector-specific** sections;
- **Practical** application of legal requirements.

### Annual reports

- **Annual review** of JFSC activities;
- **Industry statistics**;
- **Enforcement summary**;
- **Strategic priorities**.

### Consultation papers

- **Proposed changes** to rules / codes;
- **Industry input** sought;
- **Feedback statements** published.

### Risk reports

- **Sector-specific** risk assessments;
- **Industry-wide** AML / financial-crime trends;
- **Emerging risks**.

### News and announcements

- **Regulatory updates**;
- **Industry communications**;
- **Specific event** announcements.

### For investigators

#### What you can find

- **Specific enforcement** actions against named entities;
- **Industry trends** through annual reports;
- **Regulatory expectations** through codes;
- **Risk landscape** through risk reports.

#### Investigation paths

- **Search public statements** for named entities under
  investigation;
- **Annual reports** for industry statistics;
- **Consultation papers** for emerging policy trends;
- **Risk reports** for sector concerns.

#### Triangulation

JFSC publications + Royal Court judgments + Companies Registry
+ media reports = comprehensive investigation foundation for
regulated-entity matters.

### Specific accessibility

#### Free / open access

- **Codes**;
- **Handbook**;
- **Public statements** (final notices);
- **Annual reports**;
- **Consultation papers**;
- **News**.

#### Some restrictions

- **Specific investigation** detail (confidential during
  investigation);
- **Anonymised** cases in some categories;
- **Industry communications** to specific entities not public.

### Comparison with other regulators

#### UK FCA

- **More extensive** public information;
- **Decision notices** published more readily;
- **Larger volume** of public statements.

#### JFSC

- **Smaller volume** consistent with Jersey scale;
- **Generally transparent** on enforcement;
- **Industry-friendly** approach to consultations.

### Cross-jurisdictional cooperation

JFSC works with:

- **UK FCA**;
- **Foreign regulators** through MoUs;
- **FATF / Moneyval**;
- **IOSCO MMoU**;

Some information shared through these channels not directly
public but referenced in JFSC publications.

### Newsletters and updates

- **JFSC newsletter** subscriptions available;
- **Email updates** for specific topics;
- **Industry events** announcements.

### Historic information

- **Older publications** archived;
- **Trail** of regulatory evolution sometimes accessible;
- **Specific** historic enforcement may need archival research.

## What to do next

For investigation:

1. **Search** jerseyfsc.org by topic / entity name;
2. **Read** public statements for enforcement matters;
3. **Review** sector reports for context;
4. **Cross-reference** with other public sources.

## Pitfalls

- **Mistaking** confidential investigations as not yet public;
- **Reporting before** final notices issued;
- **Inferring** from limited public information.
