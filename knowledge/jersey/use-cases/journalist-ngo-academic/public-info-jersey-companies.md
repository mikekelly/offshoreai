# What Information About Jersey Companies Is Publicly Accessible?

## The question

For a Jersey company, what information can you **publicly
access**?

## The short answer

Publicly accessible:

- **Company name, registered number, status** (active /
  dissolved);
- **Date of incorporation**;
- **Company type** (private / public / cell / etc.);
- **Registered office address**;
- **Directors and secretary** (current);
- **Memorandum and articles** (for a fee);
- **Annual confirmation statement** filings;
- **Special resolutions** filed;
- **Public-filed accounts** (for public companies and certain
  categories).

**Not publicly accessible** (without authority):

- **Beneficial ownership** (held in central register but
  authority-access-only);
- **Audited accounts** of private companies (typically);
- **Internal management** records;
- **Director home addresses** (restricted);
- **Day-to-day operations**.

## The proper answer

### Publicly searchable through JFSC Registry

The **Companies Registry** at the JFSC:

- **Online portal**: jerseyfsc.org/registry;
- **Search by** name or registered number;
- **Free** basic search;
- **Paid** detailed document retrieval.

#### Free information

- **Existence and status** of company;
- **Date of incorporation**;
- **Registered number**;
- **Company type**.

#### Paid (modest fees)

- **Full company profile**;
- **Director / secretary** identities (current);
- **Registered office**;
- **Memorandum and articles**;
- **Annual confirmation statement** filings;
- **Special resolutions**;
- **Other filed documents**.

### What's not publicly accessible

#### Beneficial ownership

- **Central register** under FS(DPI)(J)L 2020;
- **Restricted access** to authorities;
- **Not publicly searchable** at this writing.

#### Private-company accounts

- Most **private Jersey companies** don't file public accounts;
- **No public access** to financials;
- **Different from** UK Companies House where private companies
  file accounts publicly.

#### Internal management

- **Board minutes**;
- **Internal correspondence**;
- **Transaction records**;
- **Audit working papers**;

All private to the company.

#### Director home addresses

- **Service address** publicly visible;
- **Home addresses** restricted (privacy protection).

### What public information reveals

#### Service-provider mapping

- **Registered office** address typically identifies service
  provider (trust company / corporate services firm);
- **Directors** often professional / nominee directors from
  service provider;
- **Pattern recognition** helps identify which providers
  service which companies.

#### Corporate history

- **Date of incorporation**;
- **Name changes** (recorded);
- **Status changes** (active / dissolved / continued);
- **Material restructuring** events filed.

#### Capital structure

- **Memorandum** shows authorised capital;
- **Articles** detail share rights, voting, etc.;
- **Specific resolutions** filed for capital changes.

### Comparison with other jurisdictions

#### UK Companies House

- **More extensive** public information;
- **PSC register** publicly accessible (with limits);
- **Private-company accounts** publicly filed;
- **Free** access to most data.

#### Cayman Islands

- **Less public** than Jersey for some categories;
- **Beneficial ownership** to authorities only;
- **No public** accounts for private companies.

#### BVI

- **Less public** than Jersey traditionally;
- **Recent reforms** improving transparency.

Jersey sits roughly **in the middle** of the offshore-
transparency spectrum.

### For journalists / NGOs

#### What you can investigate

- **Company existence, identity, structure**;
- **Service-provider relationships**;
- **Director identities** and cross-relationships;
- **Material events** filed;
- **Court proceedings** involving the company (via Royal Court
  judgments).

#### What's harder

- **Ultimate beneficial owners**: not publicly accessible;
- **Financial information** for private companies;
- **Specific transactions**;
- **Internal correspondence**.

### Investigation strategies

#### Pattern building

- **Multiple Jersey companies** with same registered office,
  same directors;
- **Trust-company portfolios** through public filings;
- **Cross-jurisdictional** relationships through corporate
  records.

#### Combining sources

- **Jersey Registry** for basic data;
- **Foreign registries** (UK Companies House, US SEC, etc.) for
  related entities;
- **Court records** for litigation involving the entity;
- **Media databases** for public information about controllers.

#### Information-request routes

- **JFSC information requests** in specific public-interest
  matters (rare for journalists directly);
- **Foreign-regulator cooperation** through proper channels;
- **Whistleblower** information (handle with care).

### Royal Court records

Public proceedings:

- **Royal Court judgments** at jerseylaw.je (full text);
- **Probate records** at Judicial Greffe;
- **Land transactions** in Public Registry (for immovable
  property).

These can reveal **substantive information** about Jersey
companies / individuals not otherwise in Companies Registry.

### Limitations

- **Public information ≠ comprehensive information**;
- **Inferences** from patterns need verification;
- **Service-provider directors** don't reveal beneficial
  owners;
- **Discretion** about reporting and inferences.

## What to do next

For an investigation:

1. **Start** with JFSC Registry search;
2. **Map** corporate structure from filings;
3. **Cross-reference** with foreign registries;
4. **Search** Royal Court judgments;
5. **Build** the picture from publicly available pieces.

## Pitfalls

- **Assuming** more is public than is;
- **Misreading** nominee structures;
- **Privacy implications** for individuals named;
- **Reporting accuracy** within public-information limits.
