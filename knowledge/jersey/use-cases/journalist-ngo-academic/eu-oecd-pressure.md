# EU and OECD Pressure on Jersey — What's Been the Response?

## The short answer

Jersey has been subject to significant **EU and OECD pressure**
on tax transparency, BEPS, and AML over decades. Jersey's
response has been **generally cooperative**:

- **TIEAs** signed extensively (early 2000s onwards);
- **CRS** participation;
- **FATCA** intergovernmental agreement;
- **Economic substance** regime (2019);
- **Beneficial-ownership** central register;
- **Pillar Two** MCIT (2024-2025);
- **Removed** from EU "uncooperative" lists.

Continuing engagement on **public BO**, fund / IP-holding
substance, and other emerging issues.

## The proper answer

### History of pressure

#### 1990s-early 2000s

- **OECD "harmful tax practices" review**;
- Jersey identified as part of broader review;
- **Commitments** to transparency;
- **TIEA negotiations** begun.

#### 2000s

- **TIEA network** extensively developed;
- **OECD Peer Reviews** of tax-transparency standards;
- Jersey moved from "concerns" to "cooperative".

#### 2010s

- **FATCA** adoption (US pressure);
- **CRS** adoption (OECD multilateral);
- **EU Code of Conduct Group** engagement;
- **OECD BEPS** package implementation.

#### 2020s

- **Substance regime** in force (2019);
- **Pillar Two** implementation (2024-2025);
- **Beneficial-ownership central register** operating;
- **Continued engagement** with evolving standards.

### Specific pressures and responses

#### EU Code of Conduct Group

- **Reviewed** Jersey tax practices;
- **Specific concerns** about substance and ring-fencing;
- **Jersey's response**: 2019 Substance Law;
- **Removed** from EU "uncooperative" list following compliance.

#### OECD inclusive framework

- **BEPS** package adoption;
- **Action plans** 1-15;
- **Specific implementations** in Jersey law:
  - Substance (Action 5);
  - Country-by-country reporting (Action 13);
  - MLI for treaty modification (Action 15);
  - Pillar Two (recently).

#### FATF / Moneyval

- **Periodic AML / CFT** evaluations;
- **Specific recommendations** addressed;
- **Generally compliant** ratings.

### Jersey's strategic approach

#### Cooperation while preserving sovereignty

- **Compliance** with international standards;
- **Sovereign legislative** competence preserved;
- **Industry consultation** on changes;
- **Reputation management** through engagement.

#### Reform priorities

- **AML / CFT** standards;
- **Tax transparency**;
- **Substance** requirements;
- **Cross-border cooperation**.

#### Where Jersey resists

- **Public BO** access — implemented authority-access but not
  public;
- **Specific tax-policy** matters within sovereign competence;
- **Regulatory cost** considerations for industry.

### Key reforms

#### Substance Law 2019

- **Direct response** to EU Code of Conduct Group;
- **Categories** of relevant activity;
- **Substance test** applied;
- **Reporting** and enforcement.

#### Pillar Two MCIT

- **OECD inclusive framework** Pillar Two adoption;
- **15% minimum** for large MNE groups;
- **2024-2025** implementation;
- **Significant** reform impact for large groups.

#### Beneficial-ownership central register

- **FS(DPI)(J)L 2020**;
- **Implements** FATF / international standards;
- **Authority-only access**;
- **Continued pressure** for public access.

#### AML / CFT expansion

- **Schedule 2** supervised-business expansion;
- **VASP** addition;
- **DNFBP** coverage improvement.

### Reputation management

#### EU lists

- **Code of Conduct Group** assessments;
- **EU "uncooperative" list** placements / removals;
- **Jersey** currently white-listed.

#### FATF / Moneyval

- **Mutual evaluation reports**;
- **Generally** positive ratings;
- **Specific** improvement actions.

#### Industry consultation

- **Jersey Finance** (industry body);
- **JFSC** consultation;
- **States Assembly** debate;
- **Strategy** consultation papers.

### NGO and academic perspectives

#### NGO critiques

- **Tax Justice Network**: Financial Secrecy Index assessments;
- **Transparency International**: corruption / BO advocacy;
- **Oxfam**: tax-justice campaigns;
- **Citizens for Tax Justice**: specific advocacy;
- **Different** methodologies and conclusions.

#### Academic

- **Limited** Jersey-specific academic;
- **Offshore-finance** academic broadly addresses;
- **Comparative** transparency studies.

### Reporting fairly

#### Avoiding caricatures

- **Outdated stereotypes** of "secret tax haven";
- **Reform trajectory** important to acknowledge;
- **Compliance levels** factual rather than rhetorical.

#### Accurate context

- **Specific** transparency measures;
- **Specific** continuing gaps;
- **Comparison** with peer jurisdictions;
- **International framework** context.

#### Critical perspectives

- **NGO assessments** as one perspective;
- **Government / industry** as another;
- **Authoritative** evaluations (FATF, Moneyval, OECD) as
  reference.

### For investigators

#### What pressures are ongoing

- **Public BO** access;
- **Substantive substance** scrutiny for fund-management,
  IP-holding;
- **Specific tax** policy elements;
- **Cross-border** enforcement cooperation.

#### Where Jersey has made progress

- **AML / CFT** framework;
- **Tax transparency**;
- **Beneficial-ownership** central register (just not public);
- **Substance** regime.

#### What might evolve

- **Public BO** if international pressure increases;
- **Further** AML enhancements;
- **Pillar Two** ongoing implementation;
- **Specific** sector-by-sector developments.

## What to do next

For investigation / reporting:

1. **Identify** the specific pressure / response area;
2. **Use** authoritative sources (OECD, FATF, Moneyval);
3. **Acknowledge** reform trajectory and current state;
4. **Report** fairly with accurate context.

## Pitfalls

- **Outdated framing**;
- **Conflation** with less-compliant jurisdictions;
- **Ignoring** reforms post-2010;
- **Cherry-picking** critiques.
