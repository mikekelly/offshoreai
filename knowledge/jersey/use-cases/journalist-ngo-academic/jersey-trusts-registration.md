# Jersey Trusts — Are They Registered?

## The short answer

**No public register**. Jersey trusts are **not registered** in
any publicly accessible way:

- **No trust register** at JFSC or elsewhere public;
- **Trust deeds** are **private** contractual documents;
- **No public record** of trust existence absent specific
  filing (e.g. in connection with property holding);
- **Beneficial-ownership central register** captures trust BO
  but **authority-only access**.

This is **different from** foundations (which have a public
charter). Trusts are **structurally private** in Jersey.

## The proper answer

### Why no trust register

Jersey trust law (Trusts (Jersey) Law 1984) doesn't require
trust registration:

- **Trust is a relationship**, not a separate legal entity;
- **Trustees hold legal title** to assets in their capacity;
- **Public information** about trust existence is incidental
  (e.g. property held in trustee name with trust noted in
  Public Registry for immovable property).

### Indirect public visibility

Trusts may surface in public records through:

#### Immovable property records

- **Property held** by trustees;
- **Public Registry** of contracts shows trustee acquiring;
- **"In trust"** may be noted;
- **Specific** trust details still private.

#### Court litigation

- **Royal Court proceedings** involving the trust;
- **Article 51 directions** sometimes filed;
- **Hostile beneficiary** claims;
- **Judgments** published on jerseylaw.je.

#### Regulatory action

- **JFSC enforcement** against trust company (rarely the trust
  itself);
- **Public statements** by regulator.

#### Foreign-court proceedings

- **Foreign litigation** involving the trust;
- **Beneficial owners** identified in foreign court records;
- **Cross-border** information surfacing.

### Beneficial-ownership central register

Trusts subject to:

- **FS(DPI)(J)L 2020** central BO register;
- **Authority-only access**;
- **Not publicly searchable**.

Trustees report:

- Settlor identity;
- Beneficiaries (named and class);
- Trustees;
- Protectors;
- Controlling persons.

### Comparison with other jurisdictions

#### UK Trust Register (TRS)

- **HMRC-maintained** trust register;
- **Authority access** to certain bodies;
- **Limited public access** for some categories;
- **More transparency** than Jersey.

#### EU directives

- **5th MLD** required trust registers in EU member states;
- **Public access** mandated for some categories;
- **Jersey not bound** as non-EU;
- **EU-equivalent** public-access not mirrored.

#### Cayman, BVI

- **Similar non-registration** approach for trusts.

Jersey trust law is **typical of offshore approach** — trust
privacy preserved.

### For journalists / NGOs

#### What you can find

- **Trustees** sometimes identifiable through service-provider
  patterns;
- **Trust-related** litigation in Royal Court;
- **Public-Registry** property records;
- **Foreign-court** trust-related materials.

#### What's hard

- **Trust existence** without specific public footprint;
- **Settlor / beneficiary** identification;
- **Trust deed terms**;
- **Internal trust operations**.

### Investigation strategies

#### Through trustees

- **Identify** trust companies as service providers to family;
- **Pattern** of trustee involvement;
- **Cross-reference** with corporate-records investigations.

#### Through litigation

- **Search jerseylaw.je** for trust-related judgments;
- **Specific case** names sometimes anonymised;
- **Detail** in published judgments can be limited.

#### Through related vehicles

- **Underlying companies** held by trust;
- **Trustee** appears as shareholder of company;
- **Pattern emerges**.

#### Through leaked records

- **Panama Papers / Pandora Papers** revealed many Jersey-trust
  structures;
- **Sourcing** through international consortium journalism.

### Trust company information

While trusts aren't registered, **trust companies** are:

- **JFSC-regulated** under FS(J)L 1998;
- **Service provider** identifiable;
- **Public information** about the trust company itself.

### Settlor / beneficiary identification

#### Limited public routes

- **Foreign-court** proceedings sometimes;
- **Tax-authority disclosures** (rarely public);
- **Voluntary disclosure** by individuals;
- **Leaked records**.

#### Inferential

- **Trustee identity** + **service-provider patterns** +
  **family circumstances** sometimes allows informed inference;
- **Verification** standards for reporting matters.

## What to do next

For investigation:

1. **Don't expect** public trust register;
2. **Pursue** indirect routes (property, litigation,
   underlying companies);
3. **Cross-reference** with leaked / foreign-court records;
4. **Recognise limits** of public information on trusts.

## Pitfalls

- **Reporting** trust existence without basis;
- **Inferring beneficiaries** unsafely;
- **Privacy law** in reporting;
- **Defamation risk** in BO speculation.
