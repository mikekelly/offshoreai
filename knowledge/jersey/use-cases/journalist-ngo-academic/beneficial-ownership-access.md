# Can I See Beneficial Ownership of a Jersey Company?

## The short answer

**Generally no, publicly**. Jersey has a **central register of
beneficial ownership** under the FS(DPI)(J)L 2020, but **access
is restricted to authorities** (JFSC, law enforcement, Revenue
Jersey, foreign tax / law enforcement through proper channels).

**No general public access** as at this writing — contrasting
with UK's PSC register which is publicly accessible (with
specific privacy exemptions).

Recent international pressure may evolve this position; check
current state.

## The proper answer

### Jersey's central register

Under the **Financial Services (Disclosure and Provision of
Information) (Jersey) Law 2020**:

- **All Jersey companies, foundations, LPs, LLPs** report
  beneficial ownership;
- **Maintained centrally** by JFSC;
- **Updated** as ownership changes;
- **Significant persons** also captured.

### Who can access

- **JFSC** (operational use);
- **Revenue Jersey** (tax purposes);
- **Law enforcement** (Jersey and foreign);
- **Foreign tax authorities** through information-exchange
  channels;
- **Other Jersey authorities** under specific permissions.

### Who cannot access

- **The general public**;
- **Journalists** (without specific permission);
- **NGOs** (without specific permission);
- **Competitors** of the company;
- **Civil litigation parties** (without Court order in proper
  cases).

### Comparison with UK PSC

| Jurisdiction | Public access | Free | Specific exemptions |
|---|---|---|---|
| UK PSC | Yes | Yes | Limited (safety, etc.) |
| Jersey BO | No | Authority-only | All authorised |
| Cayman | No | Authority-only | All authorised |
| BVI | No (changing) | Authority-only | All authorised |
| Bermuda | No | Authority-only | All authorised |

Jersey is **less transparent** than UK on BO, **more
transparent** than some other offshore centres in adoption /
verification (just not public).

### International pressure

Ongoing pressure to make BO information public:

- **EU 5th Money Laundering Directive** required EU member-state
  public registers (though Jersey not EU member);
- **FATF Recommendation 24** evolution;
- **NGO advocacy** (Transparency International, Tax Justice
  Network, ICIJ);
- **Political pressure** in UK Parliament about Crown
  Dependencies.

Jersey's position may evolve — check current state for any
recent changes.

### For journalists / NGOs

#### What you can do

- **Request access** under specific public-interest matters
  (rare success);
- **Court applications** in litigation contexts (limited);
- **Foreign-authority cooperation** through proper channels
  (you don't access directly);
- **Inferential investigation** from public information
  (service-provider patterns, director cross-references, etc.).

#### Practical advice

- **Don't expect** to access BO directly;
- **Investigate** through corporate-records patterns;
- **Pursue** stories that don't depend on BO disclosure;
- **Advocate** for transparency policy if that's your role.

### Specific journalism context

For investigative journalism:

#### What public records reveal

- **Service-provider pattern**: address, directors;
- **Corporate history**: incorporation, name changes,
  restructuring;
- **Court litigation**: where company involved;
- **Cross-jurisdictional connections**: through related
  entities.

#### Where BO information sometimes emerges

- **Leaked records** (Panama Papers, Pandora Papers, etc.);
- **Whistleblowers**;
- **Court proceedings** where BO disclosed in evidence;
- **Voluntary disclosure** by individuals.

#### Verification

- **Triangulate** information from multiple sources;
- **Public-record confirmation** where possible;
- **Editorial standards** for unverifiable claims.

### Significant persons subset

Some entities have a **limited public element** under the FS(DPI)
(J)L 2020:

- **Public Register of Persons with Significant Control** for
  some categories;
- **More limited** than UK PSC;
- **Specific entities** in scope;
- **Check current state**.

## What to do next

For an investigation needing BO:

1. **Confirm** what public information is available;
2. **Use** inferential patterns from public records;
3. **Pursue** other routes if BO essential;
4. **Don't** rely on BO access that's not available;
5. **Advocate** for transparency policy through proper
   channels.

## Pitfalls

- **Assuming** BO is accessible like UK PSC;
- **Reporting inferences** as fact;
- **Privacy law** issues in BO-related reporting;
- **Litigation risk** if reporting incorrect BO information.
