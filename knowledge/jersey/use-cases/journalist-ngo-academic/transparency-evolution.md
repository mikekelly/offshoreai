# Where Jersey Is and Isn't Transparent — Current State

## The short answer

Jersey is **transparent** on:

- **Company existence and basic identity**;
- **Foundation charter and council**;
- **Annual confirmation filings**;
- **Royal Court judgments**;
- **JFSC enforcement actions**;
- **Laws, regulations, and codes**;
- **Tax-information exchange** with authorities (CRS / FATCA);
- **Beneficial ownership** **to authorities** (but not public).

Jersey is **not transparent** publicly on:

- **Beneficial ownership** (authority-only access);
- **Private-company financials**;
- **Trust existence** as a class (no register);
- **Internal operations** of regulated entities;
- **Specific tax** information.

## The proper answer

### What's transparent

#### Companies Registry

- **Basic identity** of all Jersey companies;
- **Directors and officers** (current);
- **Memorandum and articles**;
- **Annual confirmation statements**;
- **Major filings** (resolutions, etc.).

#### Foundations Registry

- **Charter** of every foundation (public);
- **Council members**, guardian, qualified member;
- **Annual confirmations**.

#### Royal Court records

- **Judgments** published;
- **Some** anonymisation for sensitive cases;
- **Free access** at jerseylaw.je.

#### Regulatory action

- **JFSC public statements** about enforcement;
- **Final notices** named entities;
- **Industry-wide** communications.

#### Legal framework

- **All laws and regulations** at jerseylaw.je;
- **Codes of practice** at jerseyfsc.org;
- **AML/CFT Handbook**;
- **Government policy** documents.

#### Information exchange

- **CRS** automatic exchange with partner jurisdictions;
- **FATCA** with US;
- **TIEAs** with many countries;
- **Specific MoUs** with regulators.

### What's not transparent

#### Beneficial ownership

- **Central register** authority-only access;
- **Not publicly searchable** (contrast UK PSC);
- **Continuing international pressure** for public access.

#### Private-company financials

- **Most Jersey private companies** don't file public accounts;
- **Audited accounts** prepared but typically not public;
- **Different from UK** Companies House public filing.

#### Trust records

- **No public register** of trusts;
- **Trust deeds** private contractual documents;
- **Trust BO** in central register but authority-only;
- **Specific trusts** identifiable only through court /
  related-records.

#### Internal regulated-business operations

- **Specific files**, transactions, customer relationships
  private to the firm;
- **JFSC** has supervisory access;
- **No general public** access.

#### Specific tax information

- **Tax filings** of individuals and companies private;
- **Tax-authority decisions** generally private;
- **Specific rulings** not published.

### Reform trajectory

#### Recent improvements

- **Beneficial-ownership central register** (FS(DPI)(J)L 2020);
- **Substance regime** (2019);
- **Pillar Two** adoption (2024-2025);
- **Enhanced AML / CFT** framework;
- **Expanded supervised business** (Schedule 2).

#### Continuing pressure

- **Public BO** access advocacy;
- **EU Code of Conduct Group** ongoing engagement;
- **OECD inclusive framework** evolution;
- **NGO advocacy** for further transparency.

#### Jersey's response

- **Generally cooperative** with international standards;
- **Calibrated** approach to privacy / transparency balance;
- **Industry consultation** on changes;
- **Reputation management** through engagement.

### Comparison with other jurisdictions

#### More transparent than Jersey

- **UK** (public BO register);
- **Sweden / Norway** (transparent tax filings);
- **Some EU** member states.

#### Comparable to Jersey

- **Guernsey, Isle of Man**;
- **Cayman, Bermuda**;
- **Most major offshore centres**.

#### Less transparent than Jersey

- **Some non-FATF-compliant** jurisdictions;
- **Improving rapidly** as international standards spread.

### For journalists / NGOs

#### What you can investigate

- **Corporate-record-based** investigation;
- **Court-record-based** investigation;
- **Service-provider** patterns;
- **Cross-jurisdictional** cross-referencing.

#### What requires other routes

- **BO information** — needs authority cooperation or leaks;
- **Internal operations** — typically not accessible without
  whistleblowers;
- **Specific tax** matters — confidential.

#### Effective strategies

- **Triangulate** public sources;
- **International journalism consortia** (ICIJ-style) for
  cross-border;
- **FOI in Jersey-cooperating jurisdictions** for related
  information;
- **Court-record mining**;
- **Leaked records** when authoritative source.

### Specific advocacy

#### Public BO register

- **Long-running** NGO advocacy;
- **Jersey's response**: implemented central register but not
  public;
- **Continued pressure** for change.

#### Trust register

- **Less prominent** advocacy;
- **EU Trust Register** developments noted but Jersey
  unaffected.

#### Country-by-country reporting

- **OECD BEPS Action 13** for large MNEs;
- **Jersey** implements;
- **CbCR** authority-only.

### Reporting accuracy

For accurate reporting:

- **Specify** what's public vs not;
- **Cite** specific frameworks (Companies Registry, BO
  central register, etc.);
- **Acknowledge** the reform trajectory;
- **Compare fairly** with other jurisdictions.

## What to do next

For investigation / reporting:

1. **Map** what's actually public for the matter;
2. **Pursue** appropriate sources;
3. **Recognise** limits and gaps;
4. **Report** with accurate context.

## Pitfalls

- **Outdated** "secrecy haven" framing;
- **Conflation** with less-transparent jurisdictions;
- **Underestimating** authority-access provisions;
- **Misrepresenting** Jersey's actual position.
