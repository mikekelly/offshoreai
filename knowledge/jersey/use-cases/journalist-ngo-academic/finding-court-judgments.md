# Where Are Royal Court Judgments Published?

## The short answer

Jersey Royal Court judgments are published at:

- **jerseylaw.je** — primary source for Royal Court judgments
  (current and historical);
- **Court of Appeal judgments** — same source;
- **Privy Council Jersey appeals** — at jcpc.uk and on
  jerseylaw.je;
- **BAILII** (bailii.org) — also hosts UK and Commonwealth
  judgments including Jersey.

All free, searchable by case name, year, topic.

## The proper answer

### jerseylaw.je judgments

The Jersey law repository:

- **Royal Court** civil and criminal judgments;
- **Court of Appeal** judgments;
- **Privy Council** appeals from Jersey;
- **Free** access;
- **Search** by case name, year, topic;
- **Full text** typically available.

### What's included

- **Written reasoned judgments** of substantive decisions;
- **Interlocutory judgments** where reasoned;
- **Sentencing remarks** for criminal matters;
- **Costs decisions** of significance.

### What's not

- **Routine procedural** decisions without reasoning;
- **Anonymised** parts of some sensitive judgments;
- **Magistrate's Court** decisions (not Royal Court);
- **Some** confidential matters (trust applications sometimes
  anonymised).

### Anonymisation

Some judgments are **anonymised**:

- **Trust applications** sometimes (especially Article 47 / 51);
- **Family matters**;
- **Sensitive** personal information;
- **Specific orders** of the Court for confidentiality.

Anonymised judgments use **"AB v CD"** or similar.

### Searching effectively

#### By case name

- **Full name** if known;
- **Partial name** with wildcard;
- **Anonymised** cases by year and topic.

#### By year

- **All judgments** in a given year;
- **Filter** by court / division.

#### By topic

- **Subject-specific** search;
- **Cross-reference** with legal commentary.

### Citation conventions

Jersey citation:

- **[Year] JRC nnn** for Royal Court (e.g. [2024] JRC 123);
- **[Year] JCA nnn** for Court of Appeal;
- **[Year] UKPC nnn** for Privy Council;
- **Historic** other formats.

### BAILII

The British and Irish Legal Information Institute:

- **bailii.org**;
- **Free** access to UK and Commonwealth judgments;
- **Jersey** decisions included;
- **Cross-jurisdictional** search.

### Privy Council source

- **jcpc.uk** — official Privy Council website;
- **Free** transcript and reasoned judgments;
- **Jersey appeals** indexed.

### Practitioner sources

#### Subscription databases

- **Westlaw**;
- **LexisNexis**;
- **iJersey** (specialist Jersey-law platform).

#### Commentary

- **Jersey & Guernsey Law Review** publishes case commentary;
- **Major law firms** publish case-summary briefings.

### Court records office

For records not in published format:

- **Judicial Greffe** holds Royal Court records;
- **Specific orders / minutes** sometimes available;
- **Probate records** at Greffe.

### For journalists / NGOs

#### Investigation

- **Search judgments** for involvement of specific
  individuals / entities;
- **Trust matters** sometimes anonymised but topical;
- **Commercial cases** typically named;
- **Criminal cases** named (subject to specific reporting
  restrictions in some categories).

#### Reporting

- **Citation** of judgments;
- **Direct quote** from reasoned analysis;
- **Privacy considerations** especially for anonymised cases;
- **Contempt-of-court** considerations for ongoing matters.

### Reporting restrictions

In some Royal Court cases:

- **Reporting restrictions** imposed;
- **Anonymisation** of parties / witnesses;
- **Specific orders** about publication;
- **Always check** if restrictions apply before reporting.

### Historic judgments

Going back further:

- **Pre-2000** Jersey judgments less systematically published;
- **Library** archival access for older;
- **Legal history** research sometimes possible.

### Cross-jurisdictional context

For comparative research:

- **English judgments** at bailii.org;
- **Privy Council** decisions on Jersey often discussed in
  English commentary;
- **Foreign-jurisdiction** decisions applying Jersey-law
  (foreign courts applying Jersey law as fact) sometimes
  available.

## What to do next

For research:

1. **Start with** jerseylaw.je judgments search;
2. **Identify** relevant cases;
3. **Read** full text;
4. **Cross-reference** with commentary;
5. **Check** for ongoing reporting restrictions.

## Pitfalls

- **Ignoring anonymisation** in citing cases;
- **Reporting on cases** with reporting restrictions;
- **Contempt** for matters sub judice;
- **Privacy considerations** in anonymised matters.
