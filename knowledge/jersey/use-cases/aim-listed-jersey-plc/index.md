# AIM-Listed Jersey plc — Operating Reality

## What this use-case is

This persona file covers the **steady-state operating reality**
of an **AIM-listed, Jersey-incorporated public limited company**
— the most common Jersey-domiciled public-company profile. It
is shaped for the **chair, CEO, CFO, company secretary, or
general counsel** of such a group answering operational
governance questions.

The persona is **role-shaped, not group-shaped** — these are
the questions any Jersey-incorporated AIM TopCo's senior team
faces, irrespective of specific incumbent.

For **formation** of a Jersey TopCo for a UK listing (the
one-time question), read
[`../founder-entrepreneur/worked-example-jersey-topco-uk-listing.md`](../founder-entrepreneur/worked-example-jersey-topco-uk-listing.md)
in the founder-entrepreneur persona. This file picks up where
that one ends — once the listing is live and the company is in
running mode.

## The dual-jurisdiction governance picture

A Jersey-incorporated AIM-listed plc lives inside **two
overlapping rule-sets** that an agent must keep distinct:

### Jersey-side (the corpus owns this)

- [**Companies (Jersey) Law 1991**](../../companies/companies-law-1991.md)
  — the substantive company-law framework: incorporation,
  share capital, directors' duties (Article 74), accounts and
  audit (Part 16), distributions and Article 115 cash-flow
  solvency test, related-party disclosure (Article 76),
  takeover and squeeze-out (Articles 117–122),
  schemes of arrangement (Part 18A), winding-up (Part 21).
- **JFSC supervision** — applicable only to subsidiaries
  carrying on regulated activity (TCB, FSB, IB, banking,
  insurance, etc.); see [`../../financial-regulation/`](../../financial-regulation/).
  The Jersey-incorporated AIM TopCo itself is **not**
  typically JFSC-regulated.

### UK-side (the corpus has bridge files only)

The corpus does **not wikify** these — see
[`../../external-rules/index.md`](../../external-rules/index.md)
for the bridge-file principle:

- [**AIM Rules**](../../external-rules/aim-rules.md) (LSE) —
  admission, disclosure, related-party, reverse takeover,
  closed-period dealings, website disclosure.
- [**MAR**](../../external-rules/mar.md) (FCA Handbook MAR) —
  inside-information disclosure, insider lists, PDMR
  notifications, closed period.
- [**DTR 5**](../../external-rules/dtr.md) — vote-holder
  notifications (the 3% threshold + integer increments).
- [**UK Takeover Code**](../../external-rules/uk-takeover-code.md)
  — applies to AIM-listed Jersey-incorporated companies; Rule 9
  mandatory offer, Rule 21 frustrating action, scheme-of-
  arrangement alternative, irrevocables.
- [**QCA Code**](../../external-rules/qca-code.md) or
  [**UK Corporate Governance Code**](../../external-rules/uk-corporate-governance-code.md)
  — adopted under AIM Rule 26 disclosure.

The **interaction map** is the load-bearing intellectual
content — at every operational junction the chair / CFO / GC
needs to know which side governs and where the two interact.

## Annual cycle of obligations

A representative AIM-listed Jersey plc annual cycle:

| Recurring event | Frequency / timing | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Annual report and audited accounts | Within 6 months of year-end (AIM Rule 19) | [AIM Rules](../../external-rules/aim-rules.md), CJL 1991 Part 16 |
| Interim (half-yearly) report | Within 3 months of period-end (AIM Rule 18) | [AIM Rules](../../external-rules/aim-rules.md) |
| AGM | Within 18 months of incorporation; thereafter annually with no more than 18 months between AGMs (CJL 1991) | [CJL 1991 AGM provisions](../../companies/agm.md) |
| Continuing AIM Rule 26 disclosure refresh | Ongoing (immediately on material change) | [AIM Rules](../../external-rules/aim-rules.md) Rule 26 |
| QCA Code "apply and explain" update | Annual (alongside annual report) | [QCA Code](../../external-rules/qca-code.md) |
| Major-shareholder notifications recorded | Ongoing (DTR 5 trigger-driven) | [DTR](../../external-rules/dtr.md) DTR 5 |
| Insider-list maintenance | Ongoing | [MAR](../../external-rules/mar.md) Article 18 |
| PDMR notifications | Within 3 business days of transaction | [MAR](../../external-rules/mar.md) Article 19 |
| Companies Annual Return (Jersey) | Annual (calendar year basis) | [CJL 1991 register filings](../../companies/registration.md) |
| Beneficial-ownership register updates | Ongoing trigger-driven | [FSDI Law 2020](../../registries/fs-dpi-law-2020.md) |
| Confirmation of NOMAD relationship | Continuing (interruption triggers suspension) | [AIM Rules for NOMADs](../../external-rules/aim-rules.md) |

## Disclosure cadence — the four parallel triggers

A disclosable event for a Jersey-incorporated AIM issuer may
trigger **up to four** parallel obligations:

1. **MAR Article 17** — inside information disclosed without
   delay via RNS;
2. **AIM Rule 11** — price-sensitive information disclosure
   (substantively overlapping with MAR 17; same RNS workflow);
3. **AIM Rule 26 website update** — material change to website
   disclosure;
4. **DTR 6 dissemination requirements** — supplemental
   dissemination obligations.

In practice, the RNS-via-NOMAD workflow satisfies all four
simultaneously. The diligent company secretary maps each
disclosure to all applicable triggers to evidence compliance.

## Related-party regime — the load-bearing junction

Related-party transactions trigger **three parallel regimes**:

1. **AIM Rule 13** — related-party transactions above the
   5% class tests require NOMAD confirmation of "fair and
   reasonable as far as shareholders are concerned" and
   prescribed notification.
2. **CJL 1991 Article 76** — directors' duty to disclose
   material interests in transactions; see
   [`../../companies/director-disclosure.md`](../../companies/director-disclosure.md).
3. **Articles of association** — the company's own constitution
   may impose additional disclosure or approval requirements.

The three regimes have **different scopes** and **different
consequences**. A well-run company addresses all three in the
same internal process — the related-party log captures Article
76 disclosure, the NOMAD confirmation packet captures AIM Rule
13, and the articles are checked for any additional gating.

## Shareholder engagement

Two distinct strands:

### Periodic engagement

- Annual report distribution (CJL 1991 + AIM Rules);
- AGM with required ordinary and special resolutions;
- Interim reporting communications;
- Capital-markets days, investor briefings.

### Triggered engagement

- DTR 5 vote-holder notifications received → analysis under
  UK Takeover Code Rule 9 if approaching 30%;
- Shareholder requisition of a general meeting (CJL 1991
  thresholds);
- Resolution proposals from shareholders.

## Takeover and activist defence

For a Jersey-incorporated AIM company, the **UK Takeover Code
applies** — see
[`../../external-rules/uk-takeover-code.md`](../../external-rules/uk-takeover-code.md).
The chair / board need to know:

- **Rule 9** triggers at 30% — concentrated shareholder
  positions need monitoring;
- **Rule 21** frustrating-action restriction starts on bona
  fide offer communication — defensive actions need
  shareholder approval;
- **Rule 19** information disclosure throughout the offer
  period;
- **Defensive structures** (poison pills, classified boards,
  etc.) are heavily constrained under the Code regardless of
  CJL 1991 capacity;
- The **Jersey Takeovers Panel** (see
  [`../../companies/takeovers-panel-2009-index.md`](../../companies/takeovers-panel-2009-index.md))
  has a supportive role on UK-Code transactions but is not the
  primary forum.

For a **scheme-of-arrangement** alternative to a contractual
offer, see
[`../../companies/scheme-of-arrangement.md`](../../companies/scheme-of-arrangement.md).
The scheme route applies in many UK-Code-target transactions.

## Board composition

Board composition is shaped by:

- **CJL 1991** — minimum directors, validity of acts,
  fiduciary duties (Article 74); see
  [`../../companies/directors.md`](../../companies/directors.md).
- **QCA Code or UKCGC** (depending on adoption choice) —
  expected independence balance, audit committee composition,
  chair / CEO separation, board evaluation cycle.
- **Listing-segment requirements** — AIM is lighter than
  premium-listed Main Market under DTR 7.
- **Investor expectations** — what the institutional investor
  base expects of governance, even where not formally required.

The constitutional documents (memorandum and articles) should
be drafted with flexibility — see
[`../../companies/articles-of-association.md`](../../companies/articles-of-association.md)
— to allow governance choices to evolve without amendment.

## Dual-regime reporting

Where the group includes a **JFSC-regulated subsidiary** (a
TCB, FSB, IB, fund manager, etc.):

- The subsidiary reports to the **JFSC** under the relevant
  Code of Practice (see
  [`../../financial-regulation/codes-of-practice.md`](../../financial-regulation/codes-of-practice.md));
- The AIM-listed parent reports to the **LSE / FCA** under the
  AIM Rules / MAR / DTRs;
- **Group reporting** picks up consolidated obligations both
  sides;
- **Cross-regulator information flow** is governed by the
  JFSC / FCA MoU and supplementary cooperation arrangements;
- **Senior managers** in dual roles (e.g. a group officer who
  is also SMF-approved at a UK subsidiary and JFSC-approved
  at a Jersey subsidiary) have **parallel regulatory
  identities** — see
  [`../../external-rules/fca-handbook-jersey-touchpoints.md`](../../external-rules/fca-handbook-jersey-touchpoints.md).
