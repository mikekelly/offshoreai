# Distribution to a UK-Resident Beneficiary — What to Flag

## The question

A beneficiary of a Jersey trust is **UK-resident**. You are
about to make a distribution. What UK-tax issues should you
flag?

## The short answer

A distribution from a Jersey trust to a UK-resident beneficiary
typically engages **UK tax**:

- **Income** distributions: UK income tax (often at the
  beneficiary's marginal rate);
- **Capital** distributions: UK CGT and possibly UK IHT under
  the **relevant property regime** rules (10-year and exit
  charges);
- **Anti-avoidance** rules (transfer of asset abroad regime,
  ITA 2007 s.731 onwards, attribution of gains under TCGA 1992
  s.87 / s.86);
- **Reporting** obligations on the beneficiary (UK SA / Form
  50FS-equivalent).

The Jersey trustee has **no Jersey tax** on the distribution
itself but **must alert the beneficiary** to the UK position so
they can plan and report.

## The proper answer

### The UK relevant-property regime

A Jersey discretionary trust settled by a UK-domiciled person
(or now within the UK IHT net under post-2025 reforms) is
typically a **relevant property trust**:

- **10-year anniversary charges** (up to 6% of value);
- **Exit charges** on distributions (calculated by reference to
  the rate at the last 10-year anniversary or notional initial
  rate);
- **Reporting** to HMRC.

### Income / capital distinction

UK tax classification depends on whether the distribution is of:

- **Trust income** (interest, dividends, rental income) — taxed
  to the beneficiary at marginal rates with applicable credits;
- **Trust capital** (corpus, accumulated income recategorised
  as capital) — different rules, often involving **stockpiled
  gains** (TCGA s.87 attribution) or **available income**
  (ITA s.731 transfer-of-asset-abroad).

The classification can be **technical and disputed**. The
trustee's documentation is key.

### Anti-avoidance attribution

UK anti-avoidance rules can:

- **Attribute trust gains** to UK-resident settlors who can
  benefit (TCGA s.86);
- **Attribute trust income** to UK-resident transferors with
  power to enjoy (ITA s.731);
- **Tax matched capital payments** at high rates as if income.

A UK-resident beneficiary distribution can trigger these
regimes — sometimes with **supplemental charges** (1.5x or
higher effective rates).

### The trustee's role

The Jersey trustee:

- **Is not the UK taxpayer**;
- **Has no UK reporting obligation** directly for the
  distribution (though CRS and FATCA-style reporting may apply
  separately);
- **Must alert** the UK beneficiary to the likely UK tax
  consequences;
- **Should provide information** the beneficiary needs to file
  in the UK (often a **detailed distribution statement** with
  source breakdown).

### Practical flagging

When proposing a distribution to a UK-resident beneficiary:

- **Confirm** the beneficiary's UK tax status / domicile;
- **Provide** a detailed source breakdown (income / capital,
  trust-year-of-attribution);
- **Recommend** the beneficiary take UK tax advice;
- **Document** the trustee's reasonableness for trustee duty
  purposes;
- **Consider timing** — could the distribution be deferred or
  structured to reduce UK exposure?

## What to do next

1. **Confirm** beneficiary's UK tax status with documentation;
2. **Engage** UK tax advice (often via the beneficiary's
   adviser);
3. **Prepare** a source-tagged distribution statement;
4. **Inform** the beneficiary of the likely UK tax cost;
5. **Make the distribution** with proper trustee documentation;
6. **Maintain records** for future trustee defence and
   beneficiary-reporting purposes.

## Pitfalls

- **Treating Jersey trust as UK-tax-free**: it isn't for UK
  beneficiaries;
- **Missing the transfer-of-asset-abroad attribution**: this
  can tax the **settlor** even though the distribution is to a
  beneficiary;
- **Inadequate source-tagging**: makes UK filing impossible
  for the beneficiary;
- **Failing to take UK advice**: trustee may face beneficiary
  complaint if UK position is materially worse than the
  beneficiary expected.
