# Trust / Fiduciary Officer — Use Cases

## Who this section is for

You are a **trust officer** at a **Jersey-regulated trust-company
business (TCB)**, or an in-house fiduciary at a **private trust
company (PTC)** operating under a JFSC exemption. Your day involves
some combination of: administering trusts on behalf of clients;
processing distribution decisions; responding to beneficiary
inquiries; running CDD; reporting under CRS and FATCA; managing
underlying-company governance; dealing with the Royal Court on
applications; engaging with the JFSC on supervision.

This section answers the **questions you actually have**, day-to-day.
Each question links into the [trusts topic graph](../../trusts/index.md)
for the authoritative rule and into the
[financial-regulation section](../../financial-regulation/index.md)
for the regulatory framework you work under.

## Where this section sits in the regulatory frame

The **TCB regulated activity** is defined and licensed under the
**[Financial Services (Jersey) Law 1998][fsjl]** (Article 2,
"trust company business"). A person carrying on TCB in or from
within Jersey must be **registered** with the JFSC and must comply
with the JFSC's **Trust Company Business Code of Practice**.

Most users of this section will be **principal persons** or **key
persons** in a registered TCB, or will be the in-house team of a
PTC that operates under one of the JFSC's TCB exemptions
(typically the family-PTC exemption, where the PTC acts only for
trusts of a defined family group).

The substantive trust law you operate under is the
**[Trusts (Jersey) Law 1984][tjl]** — the [trusts section](../../trusts/index.md)
covers every Article. The use-cases below tell you **how to apply
that law to your daily work**.

## Where to start as a cold reader

If you arrived here cold and want to understand the persona's main
operational pressures before drilling into specific questions:

1. **[Distribution decisions and Court blessing](distribution-decisions-and-court-blessing.md)**
   — the single most-frequent decision a trust officer has to
   make: can I distribute under my own authority, or do I need to
   apply to the Royal Court for a blessing first?
2. **[Beneficiary information rights](beneficiary-information-rights.md)**
   — the second-most-frequent: when a beneficiary asks for trust
   documents, what must I provide?
3. **[Article 47 set-aside for mistake](article-47-set-aside-for-mistake.md)**
   — when something goes wrong (typically a tax mistake), can the
   Royal Court undo a past trustee decision?

Those three exercise the most-asked-about Articles of the Trusts
Law and orient on the Royal Court applications a trust officer
will see most often.

## Canonical questions a trust officer has

### Daily decisions

- [**Can I make this distribution, or do I need Court blessing?**](distribution-decisions-and-court-blessing.md) **(written)**
  — when an Article 51 application is required; the four *Public
  Trustee v Cooper* categories; the *S Settlement* three-limb test
  for blessings; pitfalls including forum-shopping risk after
  *Crociani*.
- [**The beneficiary is asking for documents — what must I provide?**](beneficiary-information-rights.md) **(written)**
  — *Schmidt v Rosewood* supervisory jurisdiction; Jersey
  presumptions by document type (deed/accounts FOR, letters of
  wishes AGAINST); how to handle a refusal.
- [**We made a mistake on a previous distribution — can we undo it?**](article-47-set-aside-for-mistake.md) **(written)**
  — Articles 47B–H regime; the *S & T Trusts* three-limb test;
  divergence from English *Pitt v Holt*; tax-trigger mistakes; what
  HMRC and IRS do with a Jersey rescission.
