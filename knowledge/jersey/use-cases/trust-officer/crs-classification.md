# Is My Trust a Financial Institution Under CRS?

## The question

You administer a Jersey trust. Is the trust itself a **Financial
Institution (FI)** for **CRS** reporting purposes?

## The short answer

Most Jersey trusts administered by a professional trustee are
**Investment Entities** — a type of FI — under CRS. The
classification typically depends on:

- **What the trust does** — managed-investment portfolio vs
  active business;
- **Who manages** the investments — professional manager
  triggers Investment-Entity status;
- **Whether** the trust holds primarily financial assets.

Once classified as an FI, the trust has CRS reporting
obligations (typically discharged through the trustee).

## The proper answer

### CRS Financial Institution categories

CRS recognises four FI categories:

- **Depository Institution** — banks taking deposits;
- **Custodial Institution** — entities holding financial assets
  for others;
- **Investment Entity** — entities managing or investing
  financial assets;
- **Specified Insurance Company** — issuers of cash-value
  contracts.

Most trusts fall into **Investment Entity** if they qualify.

### The Investment Entity test

A trust is an Investment Entity if:

- A **substantial portion** of its **gross income** is from
  **investing, reinvesting, or trading** financial assets; **and**
- It is **managed by** another FI (typically a professional
  trustee or investment manager).

The second test — **professional management** — is usually
satisfied where a JFSC-regulated trust company is the trustee
managing investment decisions.

### Specific scenarios

#### Professionally-managed investment trust

- Settlor establishes trust; portfolio of financial assets;
  professional trustee manages investment;
- **Investment Entity** — CRS applies.

#### Family discretionary trust with portfolio

- Family-discretionary trust holds investment portfolio managed
  by trustee;
- **Investment Entity** — CRS applies.

#### Real-estate-only trust

- Trust holds Jersey real estate as long-term family asset;
- **Not** an Investment Entity (real estate not financial
  assets);
- Could be **Non-Financial Entity (NFE)** instead — separate
  CRS classification.

#### Trading business trust

- Trust holds shares in family operating business;
- Depends on activity of the business — if active business, may
  be NFE Active;
- If passive holding, possibly Investment Entity.

#### Pure purpose trust

- Article 12 purpose trust with no beneficiaries;
- Classification depends on assets held;
- If financial-asset-portfolio, Investment Entity;
- If real-estate or operating-business-shares, NFE.

### NFE alternatives

If the trust is not an FI, it is an NFE:

- **Active NFE** — actively-trading business, exempt from most
  CRS reporting;
- **Passive NFE** — passive-holding (commonly trusts that don't
  meet the FI test);
- For Passive NFE, the **financial institution holding the
  account** (e.g. the trust's bank) does CRS due diligence on
  the trust's **controlling persons** (settlor, trustees,
  beneficiaries).

### Reporting obligations

If the trust is an FI:

- **Identify** controlling-person / equity-interest-holders who
  are **non-Jersey-resident** for tax purposes;
- **Apply CRS due diligence** standards to identify these
  persons;
- **Report** annually to Revenue Jersey;
- Revenue Jersey **forwards** the data to the relevant foreign
  tax authority.

The reporting typically captures:

- **Settlors**;
- **Trustees** (corporate trustees report at entity level);
- **Protectors**;
- **Beneficiaries** receiving distributions or with vested
  interests;
- For discretionary beneficiaries: those receiving distributions
  in the reporting year.

### Practical compliance

For a TCB acting as trustee:

- **Classify each trust** in your book at onboarding and at
  scheduled reviews;
- **Document** the classification reasoning;
- **Run CRS due diligence** on relevant parties;
- **Report** annually through Revenue Jersey portal;
- **Update** classifications when material changes occur (asset
  composition, beneficiary changes).

## What to do next

For each trust:

1. **Map** the trust's assets and activities;
2. **Apply the Investment Entity test**;
3. **Classify** as FI (Investment Entity) or NFE;
4. **Document** the classification;
5. **Implement** CRS due diligence as required;
6. **Schedule** annual reporting.

## Pitfalls

- **Default-FI classification**: not every trust is an
  Investment Entity; real-estate-only trusts are NFE;
- **Stale classification**: a real-estate trust that later
  acquires a financial portfolio changes classification;
- **Missing controlling persons**: settlors no longer
  beneficiaries may still be controlling persons;
- **Foreign reporting confusion**: CRS reports to Jersey first;
  Jersey forwards.
