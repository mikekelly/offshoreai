# PEP Onboarding — JFSC Expectation

## The question

A prospective client is a **politically exposed person (PEP)**
— current / former senior politician, head of state, judge,
senior military officer, central-bank governor, etc. The JFSC
expects enhanced due diligence. What's the standard?

## The short answer

Onboarding a PEP requires:

- **Senior-management approval** of the relationship;
- **Enhanced source-of-wealth verification** with documentary
  evidence;
- **Adverse-media and sanctions screening** at onboarding and
  ongoing;
- **Continuing enhanced monitoring** through the relationship;
- **Heightened reporting** considerations;
- **Annual / cyclic review** at higher cadence than standard
  clients.

PEPs are not **prohibited** — they are higher-risk. The
question is whether the TCB can adequately manage that risk.

## The proper answer

### Who is a PEP

The PEP definition draws from FATF and is implemented in
Jersey's AML rules:

- **Foreign PEPs**: senior figures in foreign governments,
  parliaments, judiciaries, military, central banks, state
  enterprises, political parties;
- **Domestic PEPs**: equivalent Jersey figures (though the
  domestic-PEP regime may have different thresholds);
- **International organisation PEPs**: senior figures at
  UN, WTO, EU institutions, etc.

**Family members and close associates** of PEPs are also
captured:

- **Spouses / civil partners**;
- **Parents, children, siblings**;
- **Business partners** and **close business associates**.

PEP status typically persists for a **period after leaving
office** (often 12–18 months for foreign PEPs).

### Why PEPs are higher-risk

PEPs:

- Have access to **state assets and decision-making**;
- Face elevated **corruption and bribery exposure**;
- Are at risk of **abuse of position** for personal gain;
- Attract **adverse media attention** more readily;
- May be subject to **sanctions** if regime change occurs.

These factors **amplify AML risk** beyond ordinary HNW
clients.

### Onboarding process for PEPs

#### 1. Identification

- Identify PEP status during CDD;
- Use **sanctions/PEP screening tools** (Refinitiv, Dow Jones,
  WorldCheck, etc.);
- **Manual review** of senior-management positions for emerging
  PEPs.

#### 2. Senior-management approval

- The TCB must have **senior-management approval** of the
  relationship;
- Decision should be **documented** with reasons;
- **Risk-committee** review for highest-risk PEPs.

#### 3. Enhanced source-of-wealth

- **Detailed narrative** of how the PEP accumulated wealth;
- **Documentary evidence**: financial statements, share
  certificates, sale agreements, inheritance documents;
- **Tax compliance** in the PEP's home jurisdiction;
- **Pre-political wealth** vs **post-political accumulation** —
  the latter requires particular scrutiny.

#### 4. Adverse media screening

- **Comprehensive search** at onboarding;
- **Ongoing screening** through the relationship;
- **Document** the search results and any flags addressed.

#### 5. Risk acceptance

- **Risk should match** the TCB's appetite;
- **Some PEPs may be excluded** under firm policy regardless of
  technical eligibility (e.g. PEPs from specific corruption-
  perceived jurisdictions);
- **Document** any policy-driven decisions.

### Ongoing monitoring

For PEP clients:

- **Enhanced transaction monitoring** — alerts at lower
  thresholds;
- **Adverse-media ongoing screening** through the
  relationship;
- **Annual review** (instead of typical 3-yearly);
- **Trigger reviews** on political-event changes (elections,
  resignations, regime changes);
- **MLRO notification** of any significant changes.

### Special PEP scenarios

#### Former PEPs

- PEP status typically persists for **prescribed period**
  after leaving office;
- **Continuing monitoring** during the transition;
- **Heritage wealth** may be cleaner than incumbent wealth.

#### PEPs from high-risk jurisdictions

- **Double-elevated** risk;
- Some TCBs decline such clients regardless of source-of-wealth
  documentation;
- If accepted, even more enhanced ongoing review.

#### Family-member PEPs

- Family members are PEPs by association;
- **Independent wealth** of the family member should be
  documented — but PEP-association persists.

## What to do next

For a PEP onboarding:

1. **Identify** PEP status via screening tools;
2. **Conduct EDD** including detailed source-of-wealth review;
3. **Adverse-media and sanctions check**;
4. **Risk-assess** the relationship;
5. **Obtain senior-management approval**;
6. **Document** the decision-making thoroughly;
7. **Set up enhanced ongoing monitoring**;
8. **Schedule** annual review.

## Pitfalls

- **Missing PEP status**: a politician's daughter who's
  building a wealth platform on her own is still a PEP;
- **Light source-of-wealth**: PEP source-of-wealth must be
  documented to a high standard;
- **No ongoing screening**: PEP status can change (new role) or
  new adverse media emerge;
- **Politicising the decision**: ad-hoc rejection of PEPs based
  on political views rather than risk is poor practice.
