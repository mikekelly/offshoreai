# Outsourcing Administration — Consent Required?

## The question

You want to **outsource** parts of trust administration —
bookkeeping, IT, compliance, AML screening, payroll. What JFSC
consent or notification do you need?

## The short answer

Under the **TCB Code of Practice**:

- **All outsourcing arrangements** are subject to **Code-of-
  Practice principles** — risk assessment, oversight, exit
  strategy;
- **Material outsourcing** (significant functions or non-Jersey
  providers) typically requires **prior JFSC consent or
  notification**;
- The **regulated entity remains responsible** for outsourced
  functions — outsourcing doesn't transfer regulatory liability;
- **AML / CFT functions** can be outsourced but not the **MLRO
  role** itself.

## The proper answer

### What outsourcing means

Outsourcing is delegating a **function** to a third party while
the regulated entity remains responsible. Common examples:

- **IT and systems** — cloud hosting, software systems;
- **Bookkeeping** — outsourced accounting providers;
- **AML / CFT screening tools** and **CDD utilities**;
- **Compliance functions** (subject to limits);
- **Payroll, HR**, other support functions;
- **Custody** of physical records;
- **Translation** and other professional services.

It is not outsourcing where the third party provides services
**to clients directly** under their own regulatory licence
(e.g. an external auditor).

### Code of Practice expectations

The TCB Code expects:

- **Pre-outsourcing risk assessment** — what could go wrong;
- **Outsourcing agreement in writing** — clear scope, SLA,
  liabilities;
- **Continuing responsibility** of the regulated entity;
- **Right of audit** by the entity and the JFSC;
- **Exit strategy** for ending the outsourcing without
  business disruption;
- **Ongoing oversight** of the outsourced function;
- **JFSC notification** of material outsourcing.

### Material outsourcing — definition

"Material" generally means:

- A **significant operational function** is outsourced;
- The outsourcing is to a **non-Jersey provider**;
- The function has **regulatory / AML implications**;
- The volume of activity is substantial.

For material outsourcing:

- **Notify the JFSC** with details;
- In some cases **prior consent** is required;
- **Contracts** should be available for JFSC inspection;
- **Annual review** of the arrangement is expected.

### What cannot be outsourced

- The **MLRO role** itself (must be a person within the entity);
- The **board / governance** functions;
- **Principal decision-making** authority;
- **Statutory responsibilities** specifically allocated by law.

### Group outsourcing

Outsourcing **within the same corporate group** is common:

- TCB in Jersey outsources to a group entity in another
  jurisdiction;
- **Lower-risk** in some respects (group governance, common
  policies);
- **Still subject** to the Code requirements;
- **Group services agreement** is the standard documentation.

### Common outsourcing scenarios

#### Cloud IT systems

- **Almost universal** in modern trust companies;
- **Material** because operationally critical;
- **JFSC notification** typically required;
- **Data residency** considerations (Jersey / EU / other);
- **Cyber resilience** expectations.

#### Compliance / AML services

- **Outsourced screening tools** are common;
- **MLRO role retained internally**;
- **Outsourced provider** does data work; MLRO does decisions.

#### Internal audit

- Often **outsourced** to specialist firms;
- **Independence** from the regulated entity required;
- **Reports to the board / audit committee** of the regulated
  entity.

## What to do next

For a planned outsourcing:

1. **Conduct** the pre-outsourcing risk assessment;
2. **Negotiate** the outsourcing agreement;
3. **Determine** materiality and notification requirements;
4. **Notify** the JFSC where required;
5. **Implement** with proper oversight arrangements;
6. **Document** the ongoing oversight regime.

For existing outsourcing:

- **Periodically review** against current Code expectations;
- **Update** notifications if scope has changed;
- **Confirm** continuing responsibility arrangements.

## Pitfalls

- **Treating outsourcing as transfer of responsibility**: the
  regulated entity remains responsible;
- **Inadequate exit strategy**: if the provider fails, business
  continuity is the entity's problem;
- **Missing notification**: material outsourcing without JFSC
  notice can be a Code breach;
- **MLRO outsourcing**: cannot outsource the role itself, only
  supporting functions.
