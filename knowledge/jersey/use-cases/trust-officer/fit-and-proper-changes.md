# Fit-and-Proper — Changes I Need to Notify

## The question

Your TCB has a change — new principal person, new director,
beneficial-owner change, adverse personal event for a key
person. What do you have to **notify the JFSC**, and when?

## The short answer

JFSC notifications fall into three broad categories:

- **Prior approval** — material changes that need JFSC consent
  **before** they take effect (typically beneficial-owner
  changes, new principal persons);
- **Notification within prescribed time** — appointments and
  changes that need to be notified **soon after** they happen
  (typically directors, MLRO, key persons);
- **Reporting of adverse events** — bankruptcy, criminal
  charges, regulatory action against a person — to be reported
  **immediately**.

When in doubt, **notify**. Better to over-report than face a
JFSC enquiry about non-disclosure.

## The proper answer

### Prior-approval changes

Typically require **JFSC consent before taking effect**:

- **Change of controller** — a new person acquiring 10%+ control
  / shareholding;
- **New principal person** — director / shareholder above
  prescribed thresholds;
- **Change of legal form** of the regulated entity;
- **Major outsourcing arrangements**.

The application typically takes several weeks to months
depending on complexity.

### Notification-within-time changes

Typically need to be notified **within prescribed time** (often
14 / 28 days):

- **New director / officer** appointments;
- **MLRO change**;
- **Resignations / removals** of principal or key persons;
- **Change of registered office**;
- **Material changes to operations** or business plan;
- **Changes in financial resources** beyond prescribed thresholds.

### Adverse-event reporting

**Immediate notification** required for:

- **Bankruptcy / insolvency** of a principal or key person;
- **Criminal charges** (in any jurisdiction) against principals
  or key persons;
- **Regulatory action** by any regulator against principals or
  key persons;
- **Director disqualification** (in any jurisdiction);
- **Loss of professional qualification** required for the role;
- **Material litigation** affecting the entity or its key
  persons.

### Specific scenarios

#### Director resignation

- Notification within prescribed time;
- File updated **Form** with the JFSC;
- Update internal records;
- Consider impact on fit-and-proper status of the entity if the
  departure leaves the entity below required staffing.

#### New director appointment

- **Fit-and-proper assessment** by the JFSC before appointment
  takes effect;
- **Principal-person questionnaire** to be completed;
- **References** and supporting documents;
- Several weeks of processing time.

#### Beneficial-owner change (e.g. M&A)

- **Prior JFSC approval** typically required;
- **Acquirer due diligence** by the JFSC;
- **Conditions** may be imposed on approval;
- **Material change** notification under multiple regimes.

#### Personal issues — director divorces, has a child, dies

- Some are non-events for JFSC purposes;
- **Death** triggers immediate notification;
- **Bankruptcy / insolvency** triggers immediate notification;
- **Routine personal events** generally don't trigger
  obligations.

### The Codes of Practice

The **TCB Code of Practice** sets out specific notification
requirements. The general principle is:

- **Transparency** with the JFSC;
- **Proactive disclosure** of material matters;
- **Prompt** notification rather than annual reporting;
- **Documentation** of internal decisions and any uncertainty.

## What to do next

For an immediate change:

1. **Identify** the category of notification required;
2. **Apply** for prior approval if required (allow timeline);
3. **Submit** notification documents through JFSC online
   portal;
4. **Update** internal records;
5. **Document** the decision and JFSC engagement.

For ongoing compliance:

- **Maintain** a register of principal and key persons;
- **Monitor** for changes triggering notification;
- **Train** staff on notification obligations;
- **Review** fit-and-proper status periodically.

## Pitfalls

- **Late notification**: even technical late filings can
  attract JFSC attention;
- **Missing adverse events**: a key person's foreign criminal
  charge that the entity didn't know about can be a
  fit-and-proper problem when discovered later;
- **Over-broad disclosure**: don't volunteer information beyond
  what's required (privacy considerations);
- **Treating notification as administrative**: the JFSC reads
  these filings carefully and may follow up.
