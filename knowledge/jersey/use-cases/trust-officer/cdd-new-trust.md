# CDD on a New Trust Client — Minimum Standard

## The question

A new trust client is coming in — settlor's family wants the
TCB to act as trustee of a newly established Jersey trust.
What's the **minimum CDD** the TCB must do?

## The short answer

Under the Money Laundering (Jersey) Order 2008 and the JFSC
AML/CFT Handbook, CDD on a new trust comprises:

- **Identify and verify** the settlor;
- **Identify and verify** all named or principal beneficiaries;
- **Identify and verify** any protector;
- **Understand** the class of beneficiaries (for discretionary
  trusts);
- **Source of funds** for the trust property;
- **Source of wealth** for the settlor (especially for HNW
  settlors);
- **Purpose and intended nature** of the trust;
- **Risk assessment** of the relationship.

For high-risk profiles (PEPs, high-risk jurisdictions, complex
structures), **enhanced due diligence** applies.

## The proper answer

### The settlor

The settlor is the **primary CDD subject** because they provide
the trust property. Required:

- **Full legal name**, date and place of birth, nationality;
- **Residential address** (current and historical for HNW);
- **Government-issued photo ID** verified (passport / national
  ID);
- **Source of wealth** — how the settlor accumulated wealth
  generally;
- **Source of funds** — the specific assets being settled;
- **Tax residence** — for CRS / FATCA classification;
- **Politically exposed person (PEP) status** screening.

For HNW settlors, expect **detailed source-of-wealth narrative**
with supporting evidence (financial statements, business
history, transaction records).

### Beneficiaries

For **named beneficiaries**:

- Full CDD on each (similar to settlor for HNW beneficiaries);
- **PEP screening** on each.

For **discretionary beneficiaries** in a class:

- **Identify the class** (e.g. "issue of the settlor");
- **Identify principal members** of the class (settlor's
  spouse, children, named individuals);
- **CDD on principal members**;
- **Sample CDD** on broader class members may be appropriate
  depending on risk.

### Protector

If a protector is appointed:

- **Full CDD** on the protector;
- Special attention if **PEP** or **settlor's connected person**;
- **Understand** the protector's role and powers.

### Source of funds and wealth

- **Source of funds (SoF)**: the immediate origin of the
  property being settled — sale of business, inheritance,
  investment proceeds, etc.;
- **Source of wealth (SoW)**: the **broader story** of how the
  settlor accumulated wealth.

For HNW settlors with complex backgrounds:

- **Audit-trail** of major life events generating wealth;
- **Tax compliance** narrative in relevant jurisdictions;
- **Background screening** for adverse media / litigation
  history.

### Purpose and intended nature

- Why is this trust being established?
- **Succession planning**, **asset protection**, **tax
  efficiency**, **family governance**, etc.;
- **Reasonable commercial / family purpose** should be evident;
- **Red flags**: pure tax avoidance, asset shielding from known
  litigation, creditor evasion.

### Trust deed

Once executed:

- **Maintain** the executed deed in the file;
- Note any **unusual provisions** (reserved powers, trustee
  immunity, jurisdiction clauses);
- **Cross-reference** to CDD on settlor and beneficiaries.

### Enhanced due diligence triggers

EDD applies where:

- Settlor is a **PEP** or **close associate**;
- Settlor / beneficiaries are from a **high-risk jurisdiction**;
- **Complex layered structure** is involved;
- **Cash-intensive** business background;
- **Negative news** or reputational concerns;
- **Unusual circumstances** in the arrangement.

EDD typically requires:

- **Senior management approval** of the relationship;
- **Enhanced source-of-wealth verification**;
- **Ongoing enhanced monitoring**;
- **Periodic review** at higher cadence.

### Ongoing CDD

CDD isn't a one-time event:

- **Refresh** at prescribed intervals (annual / 3-yearly /
  risk-based);
- **Update** for material changes (new beneficiaries,
  significant transactions);
- **Trigger reviews** on adverse events (negative news,
  sanctions matches).

## What to do next

For onboarding a new trust:

1. **Engage** with the client / introducer to gather initial
   information;
2. **Conduct** risk assessment;
3. **Gather CDD documents** for all relevant parties;
4. **Verify** identification through reliable sources;
5. **Assess** source of wealth and funds;
6. **Document** the CDD file thoroughly;
7. **Obtain** senior approval at appropriate level;
8. **Set up** ongoing monitoring;
9. **Execute** the trust documentation.

## Pitfalls

- **Light CDD on family-friend introductions**: family
  introductions don't reduce the regulatory requirement;
- **Inadequate source-of-wealth**: for HNW settlors, a thin
  SoW narrative is the most common JFSC criticism;
- **Class beneficiary gaps**: identifying just the settlor's
  spouse and children misses extended-family discretionary
  beneficiaries;
- **No purpose articulation**: "estate planning" is too generic
  to support a CDD file.
