# Does the Underlying Jersey Company Have Substance? Substance for What?

## The question

Your trust holds shares in an **underlying Jersey company**.
Does the company need to comply with **economic substance**
rules? What substance for what activities?

## The short answer

Under the **Taxation (Companies — Economic Substance) (Jersey)
Law 2019**, Jersey-tax-resident companies carrying on **relevant
activities** must meet the substance test. For trust-held
underlying companies, the most common situations:

- **Pure equity-holding** company: reduced substance test
  (basic Jersey filings + adequate people / premises);
- **Investment-holding** with trading activity: full substance
  required;
- **Family-holding** companies with limited active business:
  often pure equity-holding but check carefully.

The trustee is **responsible** for ensuring the underlying
company meets its substance obligations.

## The proper answer

### When substance applies

Substance rules apply to Jersey-tax-resident companies (and
LPs / LLCs) carrying on **relevant activities**:

- **Banking**;
- **Insurance**;
- **Fund management**;
- **Financing and leasing**;
- **Headquarters** business;
- **Shipping**;
- **Holding company business** (pure equity holding);
- **Intellectual property** business;
- **Distribution and service centre** business.

A trust-held underlying company **commonly** falls into:

- **Holding company business** (pure equity-holding) — reduced
  test;
- **Financing and leasing** if it makes loans / leases;
- **IP business** if it holds IP rights;
- **Headquarters** if it provides group management services.

### The reduced test for pure equity-holding

For pure equity-holding (passive ownership of subsidiary
shares):

- Maintain **basic Jersey company-law compliance** (registered
  office, statutory filings, directors, secretary);
- Have **adequate people** to carry out those obligations
  (typically met by the TCB administering);
- Have **adequate premises** (typically the TCB's office);
- **No CIGAs** required because none beyond holding shares.

This is the **most common** scenario for trust-held holding
companies — the substance burden is real but not onerous.

### Full substance for other activities

For other relevant activities, the company must:

- Be **directed and managed in Jersey** (board meetings here,
  decision-making here);
- Have **adequate employees** in Jersey for the activity;
- Have **adequate premises**;
- Have **adequate operating expenditure**;
- Conduct **core income-generating activities (CIGAs)** in
  Jersey.

For a trust-held company carrying on, say, **financing
activity** (intra-group lending), substance includes:

- Decision-making about lending in Jersey;
- Relevant personnel in Jersey;
- Documentation in Jersey;
- Operating costs in Jersey.

### Reporting

Each Jersey-resident company:

- **Reports annually** through the corporate tax return;
- **Self-assesses** substance compliance;
- **Provides** prescribed information (employees, premises,
  expenditure, CIGAs);
- **Identifies** the relevant activity it carries on.

### Trustee responsibility

The trustee, as **shareholder** and (often) **director** of the
underlying company:

- Should **ensure** the company classifies itself correctly;
- Should **monitor** that substance obligations are met;
- Should **escalate** substance failures (substantive or
  procedural);
- May face **TCB Code** consequences for failing trust-held-
  company supervision.

### Consequences of failure

If the company fails the substance test:

- **Civil financial penalties**;
- **Spontaneous information exchange** with foreign tax
  authorities (often the worse consequence — triggers
  scrutiny in the foreign jurisdiction);
- **Persistent failure** can lead to **Companies-Law strike-off
  / wind-up** under Articles 143A–143B;
- **Reputational damage** to the trust structure.

### Practical compliance

For trust-held underlying companies:

- **At onboarding**: classify the company's activity / activities;
- **Document** substance plan;
- **Ensure** Jersey board meetings, Jersey decision-making;
- **Track** substance metrics annually;
- **Review** classification on material changes (new activity,
  acquisitions).

## What to do next

For each underlying Jersey company:

1. **Identify** the company's activities;
2. **Classify** as pure equity-holding or other relevant activity;
3. **Apply** the appropriate substance standard;
4. **Document** the compliance plan;
5. **Monitor** annually and update as needed;
6. **Report** through the corporate tax return.

## Pitfalls

- **Misclassification**: a holding company that incidentally
  makes loans may be in financing-and-leasing category, not
  pure equity-holding;
- **Inadequate documentation**: substance must be demonstrable,
  not just asserted;
- **Foreign-tax-authority spontaneous exchange**: the worst
  consequence often comes through this channel.
