# M&A Acquirer of Jersey-Regulated Businesses — Operating Reality

## What this use-case is

This persona file covers the **buy-side acquirer's view** of
M&A involving Jersey-incorporated and / or Jersey-regulated
target businesses — the question shape *"we want to acquire a
Jersey TCB / FSB / IB / fund management business; what's the
regulatory and structural map from announcement to integration?"*

The persona is **role-shaped, not group-shaped** — corporate
development teams at any acquirer (PE buyer, strategic buyer,
roll-up acquirer) face the same fact patterns when the target
is Jersey-touching.

For the **target board's view** on a public bid (the defensive
side), see
[`../aim-listed-jersey-plc/index.md`](../aim-listed-jersey-plc/index.md)
(Takeover and activist defence section).

## The five stages of a Jersey-touching acquisition

Most Jersey-touching M&A follows a recognisable five-stage
arc. Each stage has Jersey-specific load-bearing points.

### Stage 1 — Pre-LoI: target identification and triage

The acquirer screens the target's:

- **Regulatory profile** — what JFSC licences does the target
  hold (TCB / FSB / IB / banking / insurance)? See
  [`../../financial-regulation/licensing.md`](../../financial-regulation/licensing.md)
  for the licensing framework.
- **Corporate form** — Jersey company under
  [CJL 1991](../../companies/companies-law-1991.md), LP / ILP /
  SLP under the partnership statutes, LLC under the
  [LLC Law 2018](../../companies/llc-2018-index.md)? Each has
  different M&A mechanics.
- **Listing status** — private, AIM-listed (UK Takeover Code
  applies — see
  [`../../external-rules/uk-takeover-code.md`](../../external-rules/uk-takeover-code.md)),
  TISE-listed, Main Market-listed?
- **Ownership structure** — single shareholder, concentrated,
  institutional, employee-trust-held? Drives the route choice
  (offer / scheme / SPA).
- **JFSC supervisory history** — public statements, thematic
  review findings, any unresolved supervisory concerns. See
  [`../../financial-regulation/jfsc-public-statements-index.md`](../../financial-regulation/jfsc-public-statements-index.md).

### Stage 2 — LoI, exclusivity, and structured diligence

Working assumptions for Stage 2:

- **Two parallel diligence tracks** — Jersey-side (CJL 1991
  share / property / constitutional, licensing, JFSC
  supervisory file) and target-business-side (commercial,
  financial, tax, IT, employment).
- **JFSC pre-engagement** — the acquirer typically engages
  the JFSC informally **before** the LoI is announced, to
  test the regulator's view of the proposed change of
  controller. This avoids surprise later.
- **Confidentiality and tipping-off** — the target's MLRO and
  compliance teams are kept inside a careful information
  perimeter; any AML concerns surfaced in diligence have to
  be managed without triggering tipping-off issues under
  [`../../aml-cft/pocl-article-35.md`](../../aml-cft/pocl-article-35.md).

### Stage 3 — Transaction execution

Mechanical route choices:

- **Share purchase agreement** for a private target — the
  default route.
- **Scheme of arrangement** under
  [CJL 1991 Part 18A](../../companies/scheme-of-arrangement.md)
  for a target with broad shareholder base needing court-
  sanctioned route.
- **Contractual offer under the UK Takeover Code** for an
  AIM-listed Jersey-incorporated target — see
  [`../../external-rules/uk-takeover-code.md`](../../external-rules/uk-takeover-code.md)
  and the Rule 9 / Rule 21 mechanics there.
- **Asset purchase** less commonly used for regulated targets
  because the licensing transfer is more complex than the
  change-of-control regime.

For the regulatory change of control specifically, see the
section below.

### Stage 4 — JFSC change-of-control approval

The **load-bearing regulatory gate** for a Jersey-regulated
target. JFSC shareholder-controller approval is required
before a transaction can complete. The approval process is
substantively similar across the regulated activity classes
but with class-specific elaborations.

What the acquirer submits:

- **Identification and verification** of the acquiring
  entities and their ultimate beneficial owners — the same
  CDD discipline that applies to onboarding customers
  ([`../../aml-cft/amlh-cdd.md`](../../aml-cft/amlh-cdd.md))
  is applied here by the JFSC to the acquirer.
- **Fit-and-proper assessment** of incoming principal persons
  / key persons under the relevant Code
  ([`../../financial-regulation/cop-tcb-index.md`](../../financial-regulation/cop-tcb-index.md)
  / [`-fsb-index.md`](../../financial-regulation/cop-fsb-index.md)
  / [`-ib-index.md`](../../financial-regulation/cop-ib-index.md)).
- **Business plan** — what changes the acquirer intends to
  make post-acquisition; how the business will be operated;
  what the integration timetable looks like.
- **Funding source verification** — where the acquisition
  consideration comes from.
- **Governance framework** — board composition post-completion,
  reporting lines, controls.

The JFSC's decision timeline varies. For a clean transaction
with experienced acquirer, decisions typically land within
**8–12 weeks** of complete submission; complex or
contentious transactions can take **longer**. The acquirer's
timetable management should assume the longer end of the
range.

### Stage 5 — Post-completion integration

After completion:

- **MLRO and CCO transitions** — these are JFSC-notified
  principal persons; transitions require notification.
- **Code of Practice compliance** — the integrated business
  must demonstrate ongoing Code compliance through the
  integration period; the JFSC will be attentive to controls
  during transition.
- **Customer impact** — customers must be informed of
  material changes consistent with the relevant Code's
  customer-treatment section.
- **Group reporting** — consolidated reporting upward to any
  AIM-listed parent (see
  [`../aim-listed-jersey-plc/index.md`](../aim-listed-jersey-plc/index.md))
  begins.
- **Substance** — the [Economic Substance
  Law 2019](../../tax/economic-substance.md) test continues
  to apply; the post-acquisition substance footprint should
  be designed deliberately.

## Cross-jurisdictional acquirer comparator

The acquirer often considers Jersey, the other CDs (Guernsey,
IoM), and the Caribbean cluster (Cayman, BVI, Bermuda) as
alternative target jurisdictions. See
[`../../../CROSS-JURISDICTIONAL-MAP.md`](../../../CROSS-JURISDICTIONAL-MAP.md)
for the product / vehicle landscape across the six
jurisdictions.

For the **buy-side comparison specifically** — which
jurisdiction's change-of-control regime is more / less
predictable, faster / slower, more / less prescriptive about
acquirer characteristics — the comparator is less developed
in the corpus. Each jurisdiction's regulator (JFSC, GFSC, FSA
in IoM, CIMA, BMA) operates a similar approval framework but
with significant variation in timetable, evidence
requirements, and supervisory tone. An acquirer pursuing a
multi-jurisdiction roll-up engages local counsel in each
jurisdiction for the change-of-control workstream.

## Public-target M&A specifics

Where the target is Jersey-incorporated **and** listed on a UK
market (the typical AIM TopCo case), the
[UK Takeover Code](../../external-rules/uk-takeover-code.md)
adds a second layer:

- **Rule 9 mandatory offer** if the acquisition crosses the
  30% voting-rights threshold;
- **Rule 21 frustrating-action** restriction once an offer
  is communicated to the target board;
- **Rule 19 information disclosure** throughout the offer
  period;
- **Squeeze-out** under
  [CJL 1991 Articles 117–122](../../companies/squeeze-out.md)
  for residual minority after a successful offer;
- **Scheme of arrangement** under
  [CJL 1991 Part 18A](../../companies/scheme-of-arrangement.md)
  as the most common deal structure for public M&A involving
  Jersey-incorporated targets.

## Practical operating notes

For an acquirer with multiple Jersey-touching transactions:

- **Maintain a JFSC engagement record** — repeat acquirers
  benefit from continuity of relationship with JFSC
  supervisors.
- **Standardise the submission pack** — the change-of-control
  evidence (acquirer CDD, fit-and-proper, business plan,
  funding-source verification) is repeatable across deals.
- **Engage Jersey counsel early** — local counsel's relationship
  with the JFSC and familiarity with current supervisory
  priorities shortens the approval cycle.
- **Track Jersey regulatory developments** — the
  [JFSC soft-law surfaces](../../financial-regulation/jfsc-soft-law-index.md)
  flag changes in supervisory focus that affect the
  acquirability profile of targets.
