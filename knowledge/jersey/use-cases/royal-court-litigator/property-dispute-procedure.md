# Property Disputes in the Royal Court

## The question

You're advising on a **real-property dispute** in Jersey —
contract passing, hypothec enforcement, servitudes, boundary
issues, customary inheritance, etc. What does the Royal Court
process look like?

## The short answer

- **Royal Court** has full jurisdiction;
- **Specific** Jersey property law (Loi 1880, customary
  law);
- **Distinctive** procedures for **dégrèvement** (hypothec
  enforcement);
- **Specific** Royal Court conveyancing review;
- **Property** disputes often involve **customary law**
  alongside statute;
- **Expert evidence** common for valuation, customary
  interpretation.

## The proper answer

### Common property disputes

#### Conveyancing disputes

- **Failure** to complete on agreed terms;
- **Breach** of warranty in contract;
- **Specific** Royal Court contract review;
- **Order of specific performance** common remedy
  (Jersey has broader specific performance than English law);
- **Damages** for breach.

#### Hypothec enforcement (dégrèvement)

- **Specific** Royal Court procedure;
- **Multi-stage** dégrèvement;
- **Strict** procedural requirements;
- **Public** auction in some cases;
- See [`../../property/hypothec.md`](../../property/hypothec.md).

#### Servitude disputes

- **Existence** of servitude disputed;
- **Scope** disputed (extent of right of way, etc.);
- **Interference** with servitude;
- **Specific** customary law application;
- **Expert evidence** on historic use, prescription.

#### Boundary disputes

- **Land** boundaries between parcels;
- **Customary** boundary rules;
- **Specific** survey expert evidence;
- **Royal Court** declaration.

#### Customary inheritance of real property

- **Specific** customary inheritance rules;
- **Different** from movable inheritance (WSL Article 14-15
  légitime regime);
- **Specific** Royal Court interpretation of customary
  rules;
- **Multi-generational** family disputes common.

#### CHWL disputes

- **Property** subject to CHWL restrictions;
- **Status** of buyer / seller;
- **Specific** consent applications;
- **Population Office** as party.

### Procedure

#### Initial steps

1. **Identify** the issue and applicable law (statute,
   customary, both);
2. **Title** investigation through Public Registry;
3. **Engagement** with other side (often family / neighbour);
4. **Pre-action** correspondence;
5. **Mediation** consideration.

#### Court action

If litigation:

- **Order of Justice** for contentious claims;
- **Representation** for non-contentious (e.g., directions
  on customary interpretation);
- **Specific** Royal Court Rules procedures;
- **Specific** divisions / Numbers depending on value /
  complexity.

#### Evidence

- **Documentary** evidence (Public Registry searches,
  contracts);
- **Historical** records (going back centuries in some
  customary disputes);
- **Survey** experts;
- **Customary** law experts;
- **Witness** evidence on historic use.

#### Discovery / disclosure

- **Standard** Royal Court disclosure;
- **Specific** considerations for historic property records;
- **Privilege** considerations.

#### Hearing

- **Hearing** before Royal Court;
- **Bailiff / Commissioner** plus Jurats;
- **Specific** procedural requirements;
- **Specific** property-law expertise of judiciary.

#### Remedies

- **Declaration** of rights;
- **Specific performance** of contract;
- **Damages**;
- **Injunction** against interference;
- **Orders for sale** in some cases;
- **Specific** customary remedies.

### Customary law considerations

For customary property issues:

#### Le Geyt and Norman sources

- **Authorities** on customary law cited;
- **Modern** Royal Court analysis interpreting customary
  law;
- **Specific** Jersey case law developing customary
  jurisprudence;
- See [`../../legal-system/customary-law.md`](../../legal-system/customary-law.md).

#### Expert customary evidence

- **Customary law experts** can give evidence on customary
  rules;
- **Specific** Royal Court practice on customary evidence;
- **Counsel** typically argues customary points.

### Real property succession disputes

Cross-cutting with succession:

- **Customary** real-property inheritance;
- **Statutory** légitime for movables only;
- **Both** can apply to one estate;
- **Specific** customary disputes possible;
- See [`../../property/real-property-succession.md`](../../property/real-property-succession.md).

### Specific Royal Court procedures

#### Conveyancing matters

- **Contract passing** is **Royal Court process**;
- **Specific** procedures for contested passing;
- **Specific** Greffier role.

#### Hypothec proceedings

- **Dégrèvement** is **specific Royal Court process**;
- **Multi-stage**;
- **Specific** procedural rules;
- **Public** notification typically.

#### Servitude declarations

- **Representation** to Royal Court for declaration;
- **Specific** evidence requirements;
- **Specific** Royal Court order.

## What to do next

For a property dispute:

1. **Identify** the dispute type;
2. **Identify** applicable law (statute, customary, mixed);
3. **Investigate** title and historical records;
4. **Engage** appropriate experts;
5. **Consider** mediation / alternative dispute resolution;
6. **If litigation**: prepare Order of Justice or
   Representation;
7. **Engage** Jersey property advocates specifically.

## Pitfalls

- **Underestimating** customary law role;
- **Inadequate** title / records investigation;
- **Not engaging** Jersey-specific experts;
- **Procedural** errors in Royal Court conveyancing matters;
- **Failure** to consider all applicable Jersey rules.
