# Schemes of Arrangement — Convening and Sanction Hearings

## The question

A Jersey company proposes a **scheme of arrangement** under
Article 125. What's the **court procedure** — convening hearing,
sanction hearing, and steps between?

## The short answer

Article 125 schemes require **two Royal Court hearings**:

1. **Convening hearing** — Court orders meetings of creditors /
   members in classes;
2. **Class meetings** (between hearings) — approval by **75% in
   value + majority in number** per class;
3. **Sanction hearing** — Court sanctions the scheme if
   statutory requirements met and scheme is fair;
4. **Filing** of Court Act with Registrar — scheme takes
   effect.

Typical timeline: **3–4 months** from convening hearing to
sanction.

## The proper answer

### Convening hearing

#### Application

- **Representation** to Royal Court;
- **Affidavit** describing the scheme;
- **Draft explanatory statement** (Article 126);
- **Proposed class composition**;
- **Proposed meeting arrangements**.

#### Court considerations

- **Class composition** — are the proposed classes correctly
  constituted?
- **Notice / meeting** procedural requirements;
- **Explanatory statement** content adequate;
- **Scope** of scheme.

#### Court order

If satisfied:

- **Order** for class meetings;
- **Date, venue, procedure**;
- **Notice and explanatory statement** circulation;
- **Voting procedures**.

#### Class composition

The most-litigated convening issue:

- **A class** = members whose rights are so similar they can
  consult together on common interest;
- **Different rights** = different class;
- **Different commercial interests** can also matter;
- **Court determines** at convening hearing.

### Class meetings

#### Notice

- **Period** as ordered (typically 21+ days);
- **Sent to all class members**;
- **Accompanied by** explanatory statement.

#### Explanatory statement

Must contain:

- **Effect** of scheme;
- **Material interests** of directors;
- **Detail** of consideration / terms;
- **Tax treatment**;
- **Risks**;
- **Other prescribed** information.

#### Meeting conduct

- **Chair** appointed (typically Court-approved);
- **Voting**: by show of hands or poll;
- **Approval**: majority in number representing 75% in value
  per class;
- **Independent scrutineers**;
- **Minutes** kept.

### Sanction hearing

#### Application

- **Second Representation** to Royal Court;
- **Affidavit** confirming class-meeting outcomes;
- **Class chairman's report**;
- **Confirmation** statutory requirements met.

#### Court considerations

Per Re NFU Mutual and similar:

- **Statutory requirements met**? (notice, class composition,
  majorities reached);
- **Scheme one that an intelligent and honest person, acting
  in respect of his interests, might reasonably approve**?
- **Scheme fair and reasonable**?
- **Any "blot"** on the scheme (illegality, impossibility)?

#### Court order

If satisfied:

- **Act sanctioning** the scheme;
- **Effective on filing** with Registrar.

### Common issues

#### Class composition challenge

- **Litigated** at convening hearing;
- **Court determines** based on rights / interests;
- **Wrong class** can void scheme.

#### Fairness challenge

- **At sanction hearing** by opposing parties;
- **Court considers** if substantive unfairness;
- **Bar higher** than convening hearing.

#### Procedural issues

- **Notice failures**;
- **Voting irregularities**;
- **Explanatory-statement** deficiencies.

### Cross-border schemes

For schemes with foreign elements:

- **Foreign-law recognition** important;
- **Cross-border restructuring** common in Jersey;
- **Chapter 15** (US) / equivalent recognition.

### Common scenarios

#### Takeover by scheme

- **Bidder + target** propose scheme;
- **Target shareholders** vote;
- **Court sanctions**;
- **100% acquisition** if sanctioned.

#### Debt restructuring

- **Creditor scheme** compromising debt;
- **Class composition** by creditor type / seniority;
- **Court sanctions** restructuring.

#### Group simplification

- **Internal scheme** simplifying structure;
- **Members all support** typically;
- **Quick** sanction.

#### Hostile / contested scheme

- **Opposed** by dissenting members / creditors;
- **Litigated** at convening and sanction hearings;
- **Court** carefully scrutinises.

### Timeline

Typical:

- **Convening hearing**: 2–4 weeks after filing;
- **Class meetings**: 4–6 weeks after convening;
- **Sanction hearing**: 1–2 weeks after meetings;
- **Total**: 8–12 weeks.

### Costs

- **Substantial** — schemes are expensive;
- **Specialist counsel** (Jersey + foreign typically);
- **Class meeting** logistics;
- **Two hearings**.

For a typical mid-size scheme: **£200k–£1m+** total cost.

## What to do next

For an active scheme:

1. **Engage** Jersey + foreign counsel;
2. **Plan** timeline;
3. **Draft** scheme document and explanatory statement;
4. **File** for convening hearing;
5. **Convene** class meetings;
6. **Apply** for sanction;
7. **File** Court Act with Registrar.

## Pitfalls

- **Class composition** errors;
- **Explanatory statement** deficiencies;
- **Notice** failures;
- **Voting** irregularities;
- **Last-minute** opposition.
