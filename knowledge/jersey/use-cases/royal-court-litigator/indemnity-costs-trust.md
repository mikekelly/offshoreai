# Indemnity Costs from the Trust Fund — When Available

## The question

In trust litigation, the trustee wants **indemnity costs**
recovered **from the trust fund** rather than personally. When
is this available?

## The short answer

Trustee indemnity from trust fund:

- **Trust-deed indemnity**: most modern deeds contain;
- **Statutory indemnity**: implied at common law / under Trusts
  Law for reasonable expenses;
- **Beddoe authorisation**: court-sanctioned cost protection;
- **Available** for properly-incurred litigation costs;
- **Subject to** limitations for misconduct.

For litigation: **Beddoe application** is the standard mechanism
to crystallise the indemnity in advance.

## The proper answer

### Sources of trustee indemnity

#### Trust deed

Most modern trust deeds:

- **Express indemnity** clause;
- **Reasonable expenses** including legal costs covered;
- **Subject to** misconduct exception;
- **Broader** in some deeds than others.

#### Statutory / common-law

- **Inherent right** of trustee to be indemnified for
  reasonable expenses;
- **Properly-incurred** expenses;
- **Lien** on trust property for unpaid amounts.

#### Court-ordered

- **Beddoe authorisation**;
- **Specific Court order** for cost recovery;
- **Article 51 directions** sometimes addressing costs.

### Scope

#### What's covered

- **Legal costs** of litigation;
- **Counsel's fees**;
- **Court fees**;
- **Expert evidence** costs;
- **Disbursements**;
- **Trustee's own time** sometimes (though typically not for
  professional trustees who charge ordinarily).

#### What's not covered

- **Personal misconduct** by trustee;
- **Bad-faith** action;
- **Breach of trust** that caused the litigation;
- **Unauthorised** litigation (without Beddoe protection where
  available).

### Beddoe mechanism

The standard route:

- **Application** before substantive litigation;
- **Court considers** merits and authorises;
- **Indemnity crystallised** for that litigation;
- **Trustee proceeds** with cost protection;
- **Specific scope** of authorised litigation.

### When indemnity at risk

#### Trustee misconduct

- **Breach of duty** leading to the litigation;
- **Self-dealing**;
- **Bad-faith** conduct;
- **Court may** disallow some / all costs.

#### Unauthorised litigation

- **Without Beddoe** protection where needed;
- **Personal exposure** if litigation goes badly;
- **Trustee's risk** uninsurable in many cases.

#### Failure to disclose

- **In Beddoe application** itself;
- **During litigation** to Court;
- **Material non-disclosure** can lose protection.

### Practical mechanics

#### Pre-litigation

- **Apply for Beddoe** before substantial costs;
- **Identify** anticipated scope and budget;
- **Court authorises** with terms.

#### During litigation

- **Stay within** authorised scope;
- **Return to Court** for scope expansion if needed;
- **Document** costs;
- **Maintain** trust-deed indemnity records.

#### Post-litigation

- **Cost order** as appropriate;
- **Recovery from fund** for own costs;
- **Account** to beneficiaries.

### Common scenarios

#### Trustee defending beneficiary claim

- **Beddoe authorisation**;
- **Defence costs** from fund;
- **If found** to have breached: cost protection may be lost.

#### Trustee bringing claim on trust property

- **Beddoe authorisation**;
- **Affirmative costs** from fund;
- **Recovery from defendant** if successful.

#### Cross-border trust dispute

- **Multi-jurisdictional Beddoe-like** protections needed;
- **Coordinated** with foreign-court protection;
- **Substantial** costs.

#### Article 51 directions application

- **Trustee's own** application for guidance;
- **Costs from fund** typically;
- **Beneficiary participation** costs sometimes also from fund.

### Beneficiary costs from fund

In some matters:

- **Beneficiary's helpful participation** in trustee
  application: cost from fund sometimes;
- **Representative beneficiary** costs from fund;
- **Adverse-beneficiary** costs sometimes personal.

### Court discretion

Royal Court has wide discretion:

- **Cost orders** crafted to fit case;
- **Specific provisions** for trust-fund treatment;
- **Conduct considerations**;
- **Equity** of the outcome.

## What to do next

For trust-litigation costs:

1. **Review** trust-deed indemnity;
2. **Apply for Beddoe** before significant costs;
3. **Document** costs throughout;
4. **Return to Court** as scope evolves;
5. **Recover** at conclusion.

## Pitfalls

- **No Beddoe** before substantial costs;
- **Scope drift** beyond authorised;
- **Misconduct** during litigation;
- **Inadequate documentation**.
