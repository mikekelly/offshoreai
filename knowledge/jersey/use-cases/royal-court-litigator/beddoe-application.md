# Beddoe Application for Trustee Litigation Funding

## The question

The trustee faces (or contemplates) litigation. The trustee
wants **court-sanctioned cost protection** from the trust fund
— a **Beddoe** application. How?

## The short answer

A **Beddoe application** is the trustee's application to the
Royal Court for **authority to bring or defend litigation** with
**costs paid from the trust fund**:

- **Pre-litigation** typically — establishes cost protection
  before incurring significant costs;
- **Indemnity basis** for trustee costs;
- **Court considers** merits, risk, alternatives;
- **Protected** trustee can litigate without personal cost
  exposure;
- **Without Beddoe**, trustee faces personal cost risk if
  litigation goes badly.

## The proper answer

### What Beddoe gives

The trustee receives:

- **Authority** from the Court to bring / defend the specific
  litigation;
- **Indemnity** for costs from the trust fund;
- **Protection** against personal cost orders;
- **Specific scope** of authorised litigation.

### The Re Beddoe principle

From the English case *Re Beddoe* (1893), adopted in Jersey:

- **Trustee's litigation** can expose the trustee personally if
  unsuccessful;
- **Court guidance** before litigating protects the trustee;
- **Fund indemnity** for trustee costs;
- **Court-sanctioned** litigation strategy.

### When to apply

#### Common triggers

- **Hostile litigation** by beneficiary against trustee;
- **Trustee's own claim** against third party;
- **Defence** of trust assets against creditor;
- **Cross-border** trust dispute;
- **Substantial cost exposure** anticipated.

#### Timing

- **Before** incurring substantial costs (ideally);
- **At commencement** of litigation;
- **Mid-litigation** in some cases for changed circumstances.

### Application content

#### Representation

- **Trustee identifies**;
- **Litigation** described;
- **Authority** sought.

#### Affidavit

- **Background** to the dispute;
- **Trustee's position**;
- **Merits assessment** (typically through counsel's opinion);
- **Estimated costs**;
- **Risk analysis**;
- **Alternative resolutions** considered.

#### Counsel's opinion

- **Often attached** in confidential annexure;
- **Privileged** as to beneficiaries (in some cases);
- **Court considers** in its assessment.

### Beneficiary involvement

#### Service

- **Beneficiaries** served typically;
- **Adverse beneficiary** (the one bringing claim) usually
  excluded from some materials;
- **Representative beneficiary** for minors / unborn.

#### Beneficiary position

- **Generally support** trustee's defence of trust assets;
- **Specific beneficiary** may oppose if it's their claim.

### The hearing

#### Considerations

- **Merits** of the litigation;
- **Risk** of failure;
- **Cost estimate**;
- **Trust fund** capacity;
- **Beneficiary interests**;
- **Alternative resolution** options.

#### Outcome

- **Authority granted** with terms (often a specified
  budget);
- **Cost protection** crystallised;
- **Trustee proceeds** with the litigation.

### Continued review

- **Material changes** trigger renewed application;
- **Budget overrun** may require further application;
- **Settlement opportunities** can be brought back to Court.

### Common scenarios

#### Beneficiary suing trustee for breach

- **Trustee defends** under Beddoe protection;
- **Cost from fund** (subject to outcome);
- **If trustee found to have breached**: cost protection may
  be lost.

#### Trustee bringing claim against third party

- **Beddoe authority** before commencing;
- **Cost protection** for the affirmative claim;
- **Recovery** from defendant if successful.

#### Cross-border trust dispute

- **Multi-jurisdictional litigation**;
- **Coordinated** Beddoe-like applications;
- **Major cost** exposure.

#### Defending a foreign-court judgment

- **Jersey trustee** defends in foreign court;
- **Beddoe authority** for the defence;
- **Article 9 firewall** considerations.

### Costs

- **Beddoe application itself**: typically £5k–25k+;
- **Hearing**: 1–2 hours typically;
- **Annual recurrence** for ongoing litigation may be needed.

### Trustee's continuing exposure

Beddoe doesn't fully protect:

- **Bad-faith conduct** by trustee can lose protection;
- **Failure to disclose** material facts;
- **Exceeding** authorised scope of litigation;
- **Failure to follow** Court's directions.

### Coordination with Article 51

Beddoe is closely linked with **Article 51 directions**:

- **Beddoe** typically focuses on cost protection;
- **Article 51** broader — directions on substantive decisions;
- **Often run together** — a single application covers both
  cost protection and substantive directions.

## What to do next

For an active situation:

1. **Identify** anticipated litigation;
2. **Engage** Jersey Advocate;
3. **Draft** Representation and affidavit;
4. **Obtain** counsel's opinion on merits;
5. **File** with Royal Court;
6. **Attend** hearing;
7. **Operate** within authorised scope.

## Pitfalls

- **Late application**: significant costs already incurred;
- **Inadequate merits assessment**;
- **Scope-of-authority** drift during litigation;
- **Failure to renew** for changed circumstances.
