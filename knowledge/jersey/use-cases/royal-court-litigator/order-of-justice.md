# Issuing an Order of Justice — What's Required

## The question

You're starting **hostile civil proceedings** in the Royal
Court. The **Order of Justice (OOJ)** is the principal
originating process. What's required?

## The short answer

The OOJ:

- **Issued** in the Royal Court Samedi Division for hostile
  civil claims;
- **Contains** the plaintiff's claim and prayer for relief;
- **Signed** by an Advocate of the Royal Court;
- **Served** on defendant per Royal Court rules;
- **Followed by** pleadings (Answer, Reply) per the timetable.

Royal Court of Justice **Rules** (Royal Court Rules 2004 as
amended) govern format and procedure.

## The proper answer

### What an OOJ is

The Order of Justice is the **hostile-claim originating
process**:

- **Equivalent** in function to English **Claim Form + Particulars
  of Claim** combined;
- **Specifically Jersey** terminology rooted in customary law;
- **Hostile** in nature — represents an attack rather than
  administrative application.

For **non-hostile** matters (trust directions, probate,
administrative), the **Representation** is the appropriate
originating process instead.

### Content of an OOJ

A typical OOJ includes:

#### Header

- **Royal Court** designation;
- **Plaintiff(s)** and **Defendant(s)** named;
- **Style** of cause;
- **Year** and case number (assigned).

#### Recitations

- **Factual background**;
- **Cause of action** narrated;
- **Parties' relationships**.

#### Prayer

- **Relief sought**;
- **Damages** quantified or claim made for;
- **Specific orders** requested;
- **Costs** and any other ancillary relief.

#### Signature and verification

- **Signed** by an Advocate (or Solicitor for some matters);
- **Verification** of facts where required.

### Procedural framework

#### Filing

- **Submit** to the Judicial Greffe;
- **Pay** appropriate court fees;
- **Receive** issued OOJ with case number.

#### Service

- **On the defendant** — typically by **bailiff** for hostile
  matters;
- **At registered office** for Jersey companies;
- **Hague Convention** for service abroad;
- **Specific Royal Court rules** apply.

#### Timetable

After service:

- **Acknowledgement** of service (specified period);
- **Answer** (defence) filed (specified period);
- **Reply** if needed;
- **Case management** by Court;
- **Trial** scheduled.

### Specific drafting considerations

#### Factual pleading

- **Specific** facts pleaded;
- **Chronological** narrative where helpful;
- **Avoiding** unnecessary detail;
- **Particulars** of fraud / specific matters where required.

#### Causes of action

- **Each cause** identified;
- **Elements** stated;
- **Facts** supporting each.

#### Damages / relief

- **Quantification** where possible;
- **Categories** of loss;
- **Specific orders** sought.

### Common types of OOJ

#### Commercial dispute

- **Breach of contract / tort**;
- **Damages claim**;
- **Specific performance** sometimes.

#### Trust hostile claim

- **Breach of trust** by trustee;
- **Account** of trust property;
- **Damages / restitution**.

#### Property dispute

- **Title / boundary**;
- **Specific Jersey-law** considerations (passation, customary
  law);
- **Public Registry** matters.

#### Recovery of debt

- **Statutory demand** considerations;
- **Specific** procedural alternatives (désastre, etc.).

### When OOJ vs Representation

#### Use OOJ for

- **Hostile claims** between adverse parties;
- **Breach** of contract / tort / trust;
- **Recovery** claims;
- **Adverse possession**, etc.

#### Use Representation for

- **Trust directions** (Article 51);
- **Trust set-aside** (Article 47);
- **Probate** applications;
- **Court-supervised** administrative matters;
- **Beddoe** applications;
- **Non-hostile** trust matters generally.

### Counsel involvement

#### Advocate involvement

- **Senior Advocate** typically engaged for substantive matters;
- **Drafting** typically Solicitor + Advocate collaboration;
- **Signature** by Advocate.

#### Cost considerations

- **OOJ drafting**: £5k–25k+ depending on complexity;
- **Filing fees**: modest;
- **Service**: bailiff fees;
- **Subsequent litigation**: substantial.

### After OOJ issued

- **Service** on defendant;
- **Defendant acknowledges** receipt;
- **Defendant files Answer** within prescribed time;
- **Pleadings** continue;
- **Case management directions** from Court;
- **Discovery / disclosure**;
- **Trial preparation**.

### Common issues

#### Inadequate pleading

- **Vague allegations** rejected;
- **Lack of particulars** for fraud allegations;
- **Insufficient elements** of causes of action.

#### Procedural slips

- **Missing prayer** or unclear relief;
- **Service problems** — wrong address, foreign-service errors;
- **Time-limit** issues.

#### Cost exposure

- **Hostile litigation** has cost consequences;
- **Loser-pays** principle in Royal Court;
- **Cost protection** for trustees through Beddoe.

## What to do next

For an active matter:

1. **Confirm** OOJ is right (vs Representation);
2. **Gather** facts and causes of action;
3. **Draft** with Advocate;
4. **File** with Judicial Greffe;
5. **Serve** on defendant;
6. **Manage** pleadings phase.

## Pitfalls

- **OOJ vs Representation** mismatch;
- **Inadequate particulars** of fraud allegations;
- **Service** errors;
- **Cost** under-estimation.
