# Restoration of Dissolved Companies

## The question

A Jersey company has been **dissolved** — through winding up,
strike-off, or other process. You need it **restored**. How?

## The short answer

Under **Article 213** of the Companies Law:

- **Royal Court application** by interested person;
- **Within prescribed period** after dissolution (typically
  10 years);
- **Court declares dissolution void** if grounds met;
- **Company restored** to active register;
- **Assets, contracts, litigation** resume as if not dissolved.

Common grounds: discovery of significant asset, claim against
or by the company, regulatory necessity.

## The proper answer

### Statutory framework — Article 213

- **Application** by interested person;
- **Royal Court** declares dissolution void;
- **Effect**: company deemed never to have been dissolved;
- **Time limit**: typically 10 years from dissolution.

### Who can apply

- **Former member**;
- **Former director**;
- **Former creditor**;
- **Person with claim** against the company;
- **Person with claim** by the company;
- **Other interested** person.

### Grounds for restoration

#### Discovery of asset

- **Forgotten bank account** with significant funds;
- **Unidentified investment** owned by the company;
- **Property** registered to the company;
- **Without restoration**, asset becomes bona vacantia (Crown).

#### Claim against company

- **Creditor** with unpaid debt;
- **Tort claimant** with new claim;
- **Need** to sue the (former) company;
- **Restoration** required to make defendant available.

#### Claim by company

- **Company has claim** discovered post-dissolution;
- **Restoration** required to pursue the claim.

#### Regulatory necessity

- **Tax-authority** investigation needs the company;
- **Other regulatory** proceedings;
- **Restoration** to enable.

### Procedure

#### Application

- **Representation** to Royal Court;
- **Affidavit** with grounds;
- **Notice** to Attorney General (where bona vacantia
  involved);
- **Notice** to other interested parties;
- **Identification** of successor directors / officers if
  applicable.

#### Hearing

- **Royal Court Samedi**;
- **Bailiff / Commissioner**;
- **Consideration** of grounds and any objections;
- **Order** declaring dissolution void if granted.

#### Effect

- **Company restored** with effect from dissolution date;
- **Assets returned** if escheated as bona vacantia;
- **Legal proceedings** resume as if not dissolved;
- **Directors / officers** reappointed or new appointments
  needed.

### Practical considerations

#### Bona vacantia

- **Asset transfers** to Crown on dissolution;
- **Restoration** can recover bona vacantia assets;
- **AG involvement** in process;
- **Conditions** sometimes imposed.

#### Director / officer status

- **Pre-dissolution** directors may no longer be available;
- **New directors** appointed;
- **Statutory compliance** restored.

#### Tax position

- **Tax filings** for dissolved period;
- **Catch-up** filings;
- **Tax authorities** may have demands.

### Common scenarios

#### Forgotten bank account

- Significant funds in dormant account post-dissolution;
- Former director applies to restore;
- Court orders restoration;
- Funds recovered.

#### New claim against dissolved company

- Tort claim emerges (e.g. asbestos exposure decades after);
- Claimant restores company to sue;
- Insurance / asset recovery possible.

#### Pursuing claim discovered post-dissolution

- Claim against third party emerges after dissolution;
- Company restored to pursue;
- Recovery flows to former owners.

#### Tax-authority investigation

- HMRC or Revenue Jersey needs the company for enquiry;
- Restoration enables proceedings;
- Tax-authority typically applies.

### Cost

- **Application cost**: £15k–50k+;
- **Bona vacantia recovery** can recoup;
- **Beneficiary** (typically former owner) bears cost.

### Time-bar

- **10 years from dissolution** typically;
- **Specific cases** may have shorter or longer windows;
- **Statutory limitation** on the underlying claim still
  applies.

### Cross-border

For dissolved Jersey companies with foreign-asset interests:

- **Jersey restoration** primary;
- **Foreign-jurisdiction** asset recovery procedures may be
  needed;
- **Coordinated** approach.

## What to do next

For a restoration application:

1. **Identify** grounds;
2. **Confirm** within time-bar;
3. **Engage** Jersey Advocate;
4. **Draft** Representation and affidavit;
5. **Serve** AG and interested parties;
6. **Attend** hearing;
7. **Implement** restoration.

## Pitfalls

- **Time-bar** miss;
- **Inadequate notice** to interested parties;
- **Bona vacantia** complications;
- **Director / officer** identification issues.
