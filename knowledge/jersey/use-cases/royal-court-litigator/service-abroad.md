# Service Abroad of Jersey Proceedings

## The question

Your Jersey Royal Court proceedings need to be served on a
**foreign-resident defendant**. How?

## The short answer

Service abroad of Jersey proceedings:

- **Hague Service Convention 1965** for civil/commercial
  matters (Jersey is party);
- **Through Jersey AG** as transmitting authority;
- **Foreign central authority** effects service;
- **Direct service** sometimes permitted depending on
  destination jurisdiction's reservations;
- **Permission of Royal Court** may be needed for service
  outside Jersey.

For commercial litigators: **plan service early** — Hague
Convention service takes months.

## The proper answer

### Permission to serve out

For service of Jersey proceedings on foreign-resident:

#### When permission needed

- **Some categories** of claim;
- **Application** for permission under Royal Court rules;
- **Standard grounds** (defendant within jurisdiction,
  necessary or proper party, etc.).

#### Application

- **Summons** to Royal Court;
- **Affidavit** with grounds;
- **Court order** granting permission.

#### Standard grounds

Analogous to English CPR Practice Direction 6B:

- **Defendant within jurisdiction** for related party;
- **Tort** committed in Jersey;
- **Contract** governed by Jersey law;
- **Trust** governed by Jersey law;
- **Property** in Jersey;
- **Other** specific grounds.

### Hague Service Convention

For foreign service after permission:

#### Process

1. **Apply** through Jersey AG;
2. **AG transmits** to foreign central authority;
3. **Foreign authority** effects service;
4. **Certificate** of service returned;
5. **Service** evidenced for Royal Court.

#### Timing

- **Months** typical — Hague process is slow;
- **Some jurisdictions** faster than others;
- **Plan early**.

#### Costs

- **Application** to Royal Court;
- **AG fees**;
- **Foreign authority** fees in some cases;
- **Translation** if needed.

### Alternative service methods

#### Article 10 (direct service)

- **Hague Convention** allows direct service in some
  configurations;
- **Reservations** apply by jurisdiction;
- **Postal service** to some jurisdictions permitted;
- **Direct service** through process servers in some.

#### Specific bilateral arrangements

- **UK-Jersey** specific arrangements;
- **Crown Dependency** simplified procedures;
- **Common law** jurisdictions sometimes more flexible.

### Defendant in non-Hague country

For service in non-Hague country:

- **Letters rogatory** through diplomatic channels;
- **Slower** process;
- **Compliance** with destination-jurisdiction rules;
- **Specific** procedural advice needed.

### Service on Jersey-related foreign entities

#### Foreign company with Jersey-related business

- **Specific service** rules apply;
- **Branch** registration may simplify;
- **Authorised agent** in Jersey for service in some cases.

#### Foreign trustee of Jersey-law trust

- **Service** as foreign defendant;
- **Hague Convention** routes;
- **Trust deed** may specify service address.

### Common scenarios

#### Hostile claim against foreign-resident defendant

- **Apply for permission** to serve abroad;
- **Hague Convention** through AG;
- **Wait** months;
- **Continue with substantive litigation**.

#### Defendant in EU country

- **Hague Convention** continues to apply post-Brexit (UK +
  Jersey still parties);
- **EU Service Regulation** not applicable to Jersey directly;
- **Specific timeline** per destination.

#### Defendant in non-cooperative country

- **Letters rogatory** through diplomatic channels;
- **Very slow** — sometimes years;
- **Alternative** strategies (interim relief, asset-targeting)
  considered.

#### Multiple-defendant case

- **Coordinate** service across jurisdictions;
- **First-defendant in Jersey**, related defendants abroad;
- **Service-out** for related parties.

### Defending validity

If service abroad is challenged:

- **Procedural compliance** central;
- **Hague Convention** rules adhered to;
- **Certificate of service** authoritative;
- **Defendant's appearance** waives some objections.

### Subsequent proceedings

After service abroad:

- **Defendant's acknowledgement**;
- **Defence** filed within prescribed time;
- **Disclosure** with cross-border considerations;
- **Trial** in Jersey at the Royal Court.

## What to do next

For active service-out:

1. **Apply** for permission to serve abroad;
2. **Prepare** Hague Convention package;
3. **Submit** through AG;
4. **Wait** for service certificate;
5. **Continue** substantive litigation.

## Pitfalls

- **Permission omission** voiding service;
- **Hague Convention** non-compliance;
- **Timing under-estimation**;
- **Translation** failures.
