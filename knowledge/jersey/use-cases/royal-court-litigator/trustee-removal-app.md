# Removal of a Trustee — Application Procedure

## The question

You're acting on an application to **remove a trustee** from
office. What's the procedure and the substantive standard?

## The short answer

Trustee removal applications:

- **Statutory route**: Article 21 of the Trusts (Jersey) Law
  1984;
- **Inherent jurisdiction**: Royal Court's supervisory power;
- **Application** by **Representation** (or OOJ for hostile
  cases);
- **Grounds**: breach of duty, conflict, incapacity,
  unsuitability, loss of beneficiary confidence;
- **Substantive test**: best interests of the trust;
- **Often combined** with appointment of successor.

## The proper answer

### Statutory framework

#### Article 21 — Power of Court to remove

Royal Court may remove a trustee on application of:

- **Beneficiary**;
- **Co-trustee**;
- **Settlor**;
- **Other** with sufficient interest.

Grounds:

- **Breach of duty**;
- **Conflict of interest**;
- **Incapacity** (mental / physical);
- **Persistent failure** to perform;
- **General unsuitability**;
- **Loss of beneficiary confidence**.

#### Article 22 — Discharge

Allied provision for discharge of trustees.

#### Inherent jurisdiction

- **Royal Court** has supervisory jurisdiction over trusts;
- **Supplements** statutory powers;
- **Available** where statute doesn't precisely fit.

### Substantive test

Per Royal Court jurisprudence (e.g. *Re A Trust*):

- The Court is concerned with the **welfare of the
  beneficiaries** and **proper administration**;
- **Friction alone** is not enough;
- Need not be **proof of misconduct** — unfitness sufficient;
- But **clear evidence** of unsuitability required;
- **Best interests of the trust** is the touchstone.

### Application procedure

#### Originating process

- **Representation** if non-hostile (trustee accepts removal);
- **OOJ** if hostile (trustee resists);
- **Either route** through Samedi Division.

#### Content

- **Identify** trust and trustee;
- **Set out grounds** for removal;
- **Propose** successor trustee;
- **Pray** for removal and appointment.

#### Affidavit

- **Detailed evidence** of grounds;
- **Specific incidents** of breach / unsuitability;
- **Beneficiary impact**;
- **Successor identification**;
- **Why successor** is appropriate.

#### Supporting documents

- **Trust deed**;
- **Correspondence** evidencing issues;
- **Trustee's response** if any;
- **Beneficiary statements** of support.

### Hearing

#### Non-hostile

- **Trustee consents** to removal;
- **Brief hearing**;
- **Court approves** if procedural requirements met.

#### Hostile

- **Trustee resists** removal;
- **Full hearing** with cross-examination;
- **Reasoned judgment** required;
- **Appeal** possible.

### Common scenarios

#### Conflict of interest

- **Trustee** has personal interest conflicting with beneficiary
  interests;
- **Continuing in office** untenable;
- **Court removes** and appoints independent successor.

#### Breach of duty

- **Specific** documented breach (e.g. self-dealing,
  inadequate accounting);
- **Beneficiary application** for removal;
- **Court considers** materiality.

#### Incapacity

- **Health issues** preventing performance;
- **Routine** but sensitive;
- **Replacement** appointed;
- **Often** consensual.

#### Persistent inactivity

- **Trustee failing** to administer;
- **Beneficiary frustration**;
- **Court removes** if persistent.

#### Family-dispute-driven

- **Beneficiaries** lost confidence in trustee for family
  reasons;
- **Bar higher** — loss of confidence not enough by itself;
- **Substantive grounds** typically needed.

### Successor trustee

#### Identification

- **Specific successor** named in application;
- **Successor's** acceptance evidenced;
- **JFSC regulated** status if TCB;
- **Capacity** to take on the role.

#### Handover

- **Records, assets, mandates** transferred;
- **Deed of appointment / removal**;
- **Counterparty notifications**.

### Costs

- **Successful applicant**: cost from fund typically;
- **Removed trustee**: may face personal cost order if
  misconduct;
- **Beddoe protection** for trustee's defence costs (with
  limitations).

### Appeal

- **Court of Appeal** from removal order;
- **Trustee** can appeal;
- **Privy Council** with leave.

### Beneficiary-driven removal

For minority-beneficiary application:

- **Strong evidence** required;
- **Cost risk** if unsuccessful;
- **Strategic** alternative: negotiated trustee change.

### Cross-border

For cross-border trust:

- **Jersey court** has jurisdiction over Jersey trust;
- **Foreign trustees** can be removed if trust is Jersey-law;
- **Foreign-court orders** for trustee removal may not bind
  Jersey-law trust without Royal Court endorsement.

## What to do next

For an active application:

1. **Confirm** grounds;
2. **Identify** successor;
3. **Engage** Jersey Advocate;
4. **Draft** Representation and affidavit;
5. **File** and serve;
6. **Attend** hearing;
7. **Implement** removal with proper handover.

## Pitfalls

- **Thin grounds**: "friction" alone insufficient;
- **No successor**: removal without successor problematic;
- **Hostile** trustee resistance underestimated;
- **Cost** management.
