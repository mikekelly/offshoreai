# Winding-Up Applications

## The question

You're advising on a **winding-up** application in the Royal
Court. Procedure and routes?

## The short answer

Jersey winding-up routes:

- **Summary winding up** (Chapter 2, Part 21) — solvent
  voluntary;
- **Just-and-equitable** winding up (Article 155) — court-
  ordered;
- **Creditors' winding up** (Chapter 4, Part 21) — insolvent;
- **Désastre** (Bankruptcy (Désastre) Law 1990) — Viscount-
  administered insolvency.

For litigators: **just-and-equitable** and **creditors' winding-
up applications** are court applications; summary is largely
out-of-court.

## The proper answer

### Just-and-equitable winding up (Article 155)

#### Who can apply

- **Member** of the company;
- **Minister**;
- **JFSC**.

#### Grounds

- **Loss of substratum**;
- **Deadlock** in management or membership;
- **Quasi-partnership breakdown** (trust and confidence);
- **Justifiable lack of confidence** in management;
- **Fraud or oppression**.

#### Procedure

- **Representation** to Royal Court;
- **Affidavit** detailing grounds;
- **Notice** to company and other interested parties;
- **Hearing**;
- **Court order** for winding up if just-and-equitable;
- **Liquidator** appointed.

### Creditors' winding up

#### Routes

- **Company-initiated** (Article 157): members' resolution
  where unable to continue by reason of liabilities;
- **Creditor-initiated** (Article 157A): creditor applies to
  Court.

#### Creditor application procedure

- **OOJ** or Representation depending on facts;
- **Affidavit** of unpaid debt and other grounds;
- **Service** on company;
- **Court hearing**;
- **Order** if grounds met;
- **Provisional liquidator** sometimes appointed pending
  final hearing (Article 157B).

#### Grounds for creditor application

- **Company unable to pay debts**;
- **Statutory demand** unsatisfied (typical);
- **Other** evidence of insolvency;
- **Just and equitable** for Court to wind up.

### Désastre

#### Alternative regime

- **Bankruptcy (Désastre) (Jersey) Law 1990**;
- **Viscount** administers;
- **Older regime** originally for personal bankruptcy;
- **Application** to Royal Court for declaration of désastre.

#### Choice between désastre and creditors' winding up

- **Désastre**: Viscount-administered;
- **Creditors' winding up**: private liquidator;
- **Strategic choice** based on case;
- **Bridge** between regimes possible.

### Common scenarios

#### Member application — deadlock

- **Two 50% members** disagree;
- **Company can't function**;
- **Just-and-equitable** application;
- **Liquidator** appointed.

#### Creditor application — unpaid debt

- **Statutory demand** unsatisfied;
- **Creditor application**;
- **Provisional liquidator** for urgent matters;
- **Final winding-up** order.

#### Family-business breakdown

- **Quasi-partnership** features;
- **Just-and-equitable** application;
- **Liquidator** realises and distributes.

#### Insolvent company orderly wind-down

- **Directors** initiate creditors' winding-up;
- **Member resolution** under Article 157;
- **Creditors' meeting** confirms / replaces liquidator;
- **Distribution** per Part 21 waterfall.

### Provisional liquidator

In creditor applications:

- **Pending** final order;
- **Asset preservation**;
- **Order under Article 157B**;
- **Specific powers** specified by Court.

### Cost

- **Application costs**: £10k–50k+;
- **Provisional liquidator** fees;
- **Notice and service** costs.

### Post-winding-up

- **Liquidator** (Part 21 mechanics);
- **Claw-back claims** (Article 176, 176A, 177, 178);
- **Distribution** waterfall;
- **Dissolution**.

### Cross-border

- **Foreign-court orders** for cross-border insolvency;
- **Recognition** of foreign judgments;
- **UNCITRAL** Model Law: Jersey adoption status varies;
- **Cooperation** with foreign courts in major cases.

## What to do next

For an active situation:

1. **Identify** appropriate route;
2. **Gather** evidence;
3. **Engage** Jersey Advocate;
4. **Draft** application materials;
5. **File and serve**;
6. **Attend** hearings;
7. **Coordinate** with appointed liquidator.

## Pitfalls

- **Wrong route**: désastre vs creditors' winding-up
  miscalibration;
- **Insufficient grounds**: just-and-equitable bar is real;
- **Provisional-liquidator** timing for urgent asset
  preservation;
- **Cost**: substantial.
