# Costs Against Parties Personally vs From Estate

## The question

In trust litigation, can costs be ordered **personally** against
a party (trustee / beneficiary) or **from the trust fund**?
What drives the decision?

## The short answer

Royal Court discretion includes:

- **Personal cost order**: where conduct warrants;
- **Cost from trust fund**: standard for proper trust matters;
- **Mixed**: some costs personal, others from fund;
- **Indemnity basis** personal cost orders for egregious
  conduct.

Key factors:

- **Whose conduct** caused the litigation?
- **Was the party** acting in trust capacity or personal
  capacity?
- **Was the conduct** reasonable?

## The proper answer

### Personal cost orders

Available against:

- **Trustees** for misconduct, breach, bad faith;
- **Beneficiaries** for unreasonable opposition;
- **Third parties** in some circumstances.

#### Trustee personal cost

- **Breach of duty** during the conduct litigated;
- **Bad-faith** approach to litigation itself;
- **Unauthorised litigation** without Beddoe;
- **Unreasonable position** in proceedings.

#### Beneficiary personal cost

- **Unreasonable opposition** in trust application;
- **Bad-faith** participation;
- **Failed claim** in hostile beneficiary-vs-trustee
  litigation.

#### Indemnity-basis personal orders

- **Egregious conduct**;
- **Repeated** unreasonable behaviour;
- **Specific breach** of court orders.

### Trust-fund costs

#### Standard treatment

- **Trustee's proper costs**: from fund (Beddoe-protected);
- **Beneficiary's helpful participation**: from fund;
- **Court-ordered** application costs: from fund;
- **Litigation costs in proper conduct of trust** affairs.

#### When from fund

- **Trust application** by trustee (Article 51, etc.);
- **Trustee's defence** of proper administration;
- **Beneficiary's substantively helpful participation**;
- **Representative beneficiary** for minors / unborn.

### Mixed orders

Common patterns:

#### Trustee bad behaviour + beneficiary bad behaviour

- **Both parties** face personal cost orders;
- **Trust fund** spared;
- **Court** assesses respective faults.

#### Trustee good faith + beneficiary unreasonable opposition

- **Trustee's costs** from fund;
- **Beneficiary's costs** personal;
- **Indemnity** to trustee where appropriate.

#### Trustee misconduct + beneficiary reasonable challenge

- **Trustee personal** liability for own costs and possibly
  beneficiary's;
- **Beneficiary's costs** from fund or personal recovery from
  trustee.

### Examples from case law

#### Trustee mismanagement

- **Significant breach** caused the litigation;
- **Personal cost order** for trustee;
- **Beneficiary cost** from fund or personal from trustee.

#### Beneficiary unreasonable Schmidt application

- **Bar for information** not met;
- **Personal cost order** for beneficiary;
- **Trustee** recovers from beneficiary or fund.

#### Hostile family dispute

- **Family members** as adverse parties;
- **Each side** bears own costs sometimes (no-order-as-to-
  costs);
- **Or** loser-pays standard.

### Specific contexts

#### Article 51 directions

- **Trustee's application**: costs from fund typically;
- **Beneficiary participation**: from fund if helpful;
- **No personal orders** for reasonable participation.

#### Beddoe application

- **Trustee's application**: from fund;
- **Beneficiary involvement**: from fund typically.

#### Article 47 mistake

- **Applicant's costs** from fund typically;
- **All-parties' costs** from fund in many cases;
- **No personal orders** for reasonable involvement.

#### Hostile beneficiary claim

- **Loser-pays** standard;
- **Indemnity** for egregious conduct;
- **Beddoe protection** for trustee.

### Cost-budget management

For Jersey trust litigation:

- **Plan** cost-protection mechanisms;
- **Beddoe** for trustees;
- **Cost-cap** considerations for vulnerable parties;
- **Sealed offers** strategically.

### Cross-border

For cross-border family disputes:

- **Foreign-court orders** for costs different from Jersey;
- **Personal exposure** in some jurisdictions;
- **Article 9** considerations for trust assets recovery.

### Common scenarios

#### Trustee defending breach claim, found liable

- **Personal cost order** for trustee;
- **Beneficiary's costs** from trustee personally;
- **Loss of Beddoe protection** in some cases.

#### Trustee defending breach claim, found not liable

- **Trustee costs** from fund;
- **Beneficiary's costs** personal (loser-pays);
- **Indemnity costs** for unreasonable claim.

#### Article 51 trustee application

- **All costs from fund** typically;
- **No personal orders** for proper participation;
- **Standard** trust-administration pattern.

#### Hostile beneficiary information demand

- **If reasonable**: costs from fund;
- **If unreasonable**: personal against beneficiary.

## What to do next

For an active matter:

1. **Plan** cost strategy at outset;
2. **Apply for Beddoe** for trustees;
3. **Consider** sealed offers to shift cost risk;
4. **Document** reasonableness of approach;
5. **Argue** cost recovery at end based on conduct.

## Pitfalls

- **Underestimating** personal cost exposure;
- **Misconduct** during litigation losing protection;
- **No Beddoe** for trustees in protracted litigation;
- **Unreasonable** position attracting indemnity costs.
