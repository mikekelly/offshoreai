# Enforcement of Jersey Judgments Abroad

## The question

You have a **Jersey Royal Court judgment**. The defendant's
assets are **abroad**. How do you enforce?

## The short answer

Enforcement abroad of Jersey judgments:

- **Bilateral / multilateral** treaty arrangements where
  available (UK, Commonwealth, etc.);
- **Common-law enforcement** in many jurisdictions through
  action on the judgment;
- **Specific jurisdiction's** reciprocal enforcement laws;
- **No general Hague Convention** on enforcement (the 2019
  Convention is recent and limited);
- **Engage foreign counsel** in each enforcement jurisdiction.

**Plan enforcement strategy** before obtaining judgment if
assets are abroad.

## The proper answer

### Routes by destination

#### UK

- **Reciprocal enforcement** under UK statute extends to Jersey
  judgments;
- **Specific procedures** for registration and execution;
- **Standard** and well-established route.

#### Commonwealth jurisdictions

- **Various** bilateral / multilateral arrangements;
- **Reciprocal enforcement legislation** in most Commonwealth
  countries;
- **Specific** procedural requirements per jurisdiction.

#### EU

- **Brussels Recast Regulation** doesn't directly apply to
  Jersey (not EU);
- **Bilateral arrangements** with specific countries vary;
- **Common-law action** typically the route;
- **Hague 2005 Choice of Court Convention** may apply for
  some jurisdiction-clause matters.

#### US

- **Common-law action** on the judgment;
- **State-by-state** recognition;
- **Uniform Foreign-Country Money Judgments Recognition Act**
  in many states;
- **Specific** state requirements.

#### Non-cooperating jurisdictions

- **Difficult**;
- **Common-law action** may be available;
- **Practical** enforcement challenges;
- **Asset-tracing** alternative strategies.

### Common-law enforcement

In many jurisdictions:

- **Bring fresh action** on the Jersey judgment;
- **Foreign court** treats Jersey judgment as **evidence of
  debt** owed;
- **Summary judgment** typically available;
- **Subject to defences**:
  - **Foreign court** lacked jurisdiction;
  - **Defendant** not given fair opportunity;
  - **Fraud**;
  - **Public policy**;
  - **Limitations**.

### Procedural framework

#### Pre-enforcement

- **Identify** location of assets;
- **Engage foreign counsel** in enforcement jurisdiction;
- **Translation** of judgment if needed;
- **Authentication** (apostille / consular legalisation).

#### Foreign-court application

- **Foreign jurisdiction-specific** procedure;
- **File** authenticated Jersey judgment;
- **Application** for recognition / registration;
- **Defendant** can challenge.

#### Execution

- **Once recognised** / registered: like foreign-jurisdiction
  domestic judgment;
- **Execution against assets**: bank account garnishment,
  property seizure, etc.

### Specific assets

#### Bank accounts

- **Garnishment** under foreign-jurisdiction procedure;
- **Account-by-account** typically;
- **Asset disclosure orders** may be available.

#### Real estate

- **Foreign jurisdiction's** real-estate enforcement;
- **Specific** procedural requirements;
- **Often** slower than bank account enforcement.

#### Securities

- **Court-ordered transfer** in foreign jurisdiction;
- **Custodian** cooperation needed;
- **Cross-border** complications.

#### Operating assets

- **Court-ordered sale** if applicable;
- **Trustee-in-bankruptcy** style appointments in some cases;
- **Asset-specific** procedures.

### Pre-judgment planning

For strategic litigation:

- **Identify** asset locations early;
- **Plan enforcement** route during merits litigation;
- **Interim relief** (freezing orders) coordinated;
- **Worldwide** vs jurisdiction-specific freezing.

### Costs

- **Foreign counsel** in each enforcement jurisdiction;
- **Translation and authentication**;
- **Foreign court fees**;
- **Substantial** for cross-border enforcement.

### Common scenarios

#### UK enforcement

- **Most common** for Jersey judgments;
- **Established** procedure;
- **Reliable** outcome typically.

#### Multi-jurisdictional family dispute

- **Judgment in Jersey** against family member abroad;
- **Article 9** Considerations for trust assets;
- **Personal-asset** enforcement in family member's
  residence jurisdiction.

#### Commercial debt recovery

- **Jersey commercial judgment**;
- **Foreign trading-partner** defendant;
- **Standard** common-law enforcement.

#### Sanctions / criminal-related

- **Specific procedural** issues;
- **Foreign cooperation** through different channels;
- **Complex** asset-tracing.

### Hague 2019 Convention

The **Hague Convention on the Recognition and Enforcement of
Foreign Judgments in Civil or Commercial Matters 2019**:

- **Recent** convention;
- **Limited** signatories so far;
- **Future** simplification potential;
- **Jersey adoption** status check.

### Practical advice

- **Don't assume** enforcement is automatic — proper procedural
  steps needed in each jurisdiction;
- **Plan enforcement** during merits;
- **Use interim measures** (freezing orders) where appropriate;
- **Engage foreign counsel** early.

## What to do next

For an active enforcement:

1. **Identify** asset jurisdictions;
2. **Engage** foreign counsel in each;
3. **Authenticate** Jersey judgment;
4. **Apply** for foreign recognition;
5. **Execute** against assets;
6. **Coordinate** across jurisdictions.

## Pitfalls

- **Late enforcement** allowing asset dissipation;
- **Wrong route** in destination jurisdiction;
- **Translation / authentication** errors;
- **Cost** of multi-jurisdictional enforcement.
