# Leave to Appeal to the Privy Council

## The question

You've lost (or partially lost) at the Court of Appeal. You're
considering a further appeal to the **Judicial Committee of the
Privy Council**. How?

## The short answer

Appeal to the Privy Council from Jersey:

- **With leave** from Court of Appeal or Privy Council;
- **Significant question of law** or **public importance**
  typically required;
- **Constitutional matters** sometimes;
- **Hearing in London** at Downing Street;
- **Final** — no further appeal;
- **Bindng on Jersey courts** as authoritative.

Privy Council appeals are **rare** but Jersey trust-law cases
have produced significant Privy Council decisions.

## The proper answer

### Privy Council jurisdiction

The Judicial Committee of the Privy Council:

- **Final appeal** for Jersey;
- **Justices of the UK Supreme Court** sitting as Privy
  Counsellors;
- **Panel of three or five** typically;
- **Sits in London** at Downing Street (formerly Privy Council
  building).

### Leave to appeal

#### Required

- **Most appeals** require leave;
- **Court of Appeal** can grant;
- **Privy Council** itself can grant if Court of Appeal refuses.

#### Grounds for leave

- **Significant question of law**;
- **Public importance** of the issue;
- **Conflict** between Court of Appeal decisions;
- **Constitutional** importance;
- **Wider Commonwealth** significance.

#### Without leave

- **Specific statutory** provisions allowing as of right;
- **Some constitutional** matters;
- **Rare**.

### Application

#### Initial application

- **To Court of Appeal** for leave first;
- **Written submissions** on grounds;
- **Hearing** typically;
- **Court of Appeal** grants / refuses.

#### If refused

- **Application to Privy Council** for leave;
- **Petition** procedure;
- **Privy Council** considers and grants / refuses.

### Notice of appeal

If leave granted:

- **Notice** filed within prescribed time;
- **Detailed grounds**;
- **Bundle** and skeleton arguments;
- **Cost considerations**.

### Hearing

- **One- to three-day** hearing typically;
- **Oral argument** central;
- **Written cases** filed in advance;
- **Reserved judgment** typical;
- **Authoritative reasoned judgment**.

### Outcome

- **Dismiss appeal** (most common in trust cases);
- **Allow** in whole or part;
- **Remit** to Court of Appeal or Royal Court;
- **Settle** authoritative legal position.

### Significance

Privy Council decisions:

- **Bind Jersey courts**;
- **Highly persuasive** elsewhere in Commonwealth;
- **Set** Jersey law authoritatively;
- **Trust law** has had many significant Privy Council
  Jersey decisions.

### Notable Privy Council Jersey trust decisions

- *Schmidt v Rosewood* (2003): beneficiary information rights;
- *Crociani v Crociani* (2014): jurisdiction clauses;
- *Various* trust-set-aside and Article 9 cases.

### Cost

- **Substantial** — Privy Council appeals expensive;
- **Senior London counsel** typically;
- **Total cost**: £500k–2m+;
- **Cost-protection** mechanisms important.

### Timing

- **Leave application**: 2–4 months;
- **Hearing**: 6–12 months from leave;
- **Judgment**: 3–6 months from hearing;
- **Total**: 12–24 months typically.

### Strategic considerations

#### When Privy Council appeal makes sense

- **Significant legal point** with broad application;
- **Major commercial outcome** at stake;
- **Settled-law-question** worth resolving;
- **Client willing** to commit substantial cost.

#### When not to appeal

- **Discretionary decisions** by lower courts respected;
- **Fact-based** outcomes;
- **Disproportionate** cost to outcome;
- **Bad facts** making bad law risk.

### Cross-border considerations

#### Foreign-law recognition

- **Privy Council decisions** on Jersey law authoritative in
  Jersey;
- **Foreign courts** may be persuaded by Privy Council Jersey
  decisions;
- **Standing-of-Jersey law** enhanced by Privy Council
  treatment.

#### Comity

- **Privy Council** has comity considerations in Jersey
  appeals;
- **Different from** UK domestic Supreme Court approach;
- **Constitutional** position of Jersey reflected.

### Counsel selection

#### London counsel

- **KCs / senior barristers** with Privy Council experience;
- **Jersey-law expertise** important;
- **Often** the same KCs who advise at Court of Appeal level.

#### Jersey counsel

- **Jersey Advocate** typically retained alongside London
  counsel;
- **Solicitors** for the substantive Jersey-side work;
- **Coordinated team**.

## What to do next

For Privy Council appeal:

1. **Assess** prospects with appellate counsel;
2. **Apply to Court of Appeal** for leave;
3. **If refused**, apply to Privy Council;
4. **Prepare** comprehensive case;
5. **Manage** substantial cost;
6. **Attend** hearing;
7. **Implement** outcome.

## Pitfalls

- **Cost** substantial — client must commit;
- **Time** — multi-year process;
- **Bad case** can make bad law;
- **Procedural** errors in petition.
