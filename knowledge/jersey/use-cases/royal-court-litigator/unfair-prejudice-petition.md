# Unfair-Prejudice Petitions — Jersey Procedure

## The question

You're acting for a minority shareholder in an
**unfair-prejudice petition** under Article 141 of the Companies
Law. Procedure?

## The short answer

Unfair-prejudice petitions:

- **Application by member** to Royal Court (Article 141);
- **Originating process**: Representation;
- **Grounds**: company's affairs conducted in manner unfairly
  prejudicial to members generally or particular members;
- **Relief**: Court has **wide discretion** under Article 143 —
  most commonly **buy-out order** for the petitioner.

**Buy-out** is the dominant remedy — petitioner exits at
court-set valuation, conduct ceases by separation.

## The proper answer

### Statutory framework

- **Article 141**: member's right to apply;
- **Article 142**: Minister / JFSC right to apply;
- **Article 143**: Court's powers.

### Who can petition

- **Any member**, regardless of holding size;
- **No minimum** threshold;
- **Member at time of conduct** and at application;
- **Former member** has limited standing.

### What counts as unfairly prejudicial

Per case law:

- **Exclusion from management** in quasi-partnership;
- **Diversion of profits** to controllers;
- **Excessive director remuneration**;
- **Failure to declare dividends** unreasonably;
- **Dilution** at less than market value;
- **Information starvation**;
- **Breach of articles** / shareholders' agreement;
- **Breach of legitimate expectation**.

The test:

- **Unfairness** assessed by reference to constitution and
  legitimate expectations;
- **Prejudice** to interests **as a member**;
- **Conduct** of affairs is the focus.

### Procedure

#### Application

- **Representation**;
- **Affidavit** with specific evidence;
- **Allegations** of unfair-prejudicial conduct;
- **Relief sought**.

#### Service

- **Respondents** (company and / or directors);
- **Other affected** members may participate.

#### Pleadings

- **Petition** equivalent;
- **Response** by company / respondents;
- **Particulars** of unfairness alleged;
- **Particulars** of prejudice claimed.

#### Disclosure

- **Discovery** of company records;
- **Specific** documents relevant to allegations;
- **Schmidt v Rosewood** principles for trust-context information.

#### Witness evidence

- **Witness statements** of fact;
- **Expert** valuation evidence (key for buy-out remedy);
- **Cross-examination** at trial.

#### Trial

- **Royal Court** hearing;
- **Often weeks** for substantive trial;
- **Reserved judgment**;
- **Detailed reasoning** required.

### Remedies (Article 143)

Court has wide power:

#### Buy-out order

- **Most common**;
- **Court orders** company / other members to buy petitioner's
  shares;
- **Valuation** by Court (often expert-assisted);
- **Petitioner exits**, conduct ceases by separation.

#### Regulation of future conduct

- **Court orders** specific governance changes;
- **Restraining** specific conduct;
- **Suitable** for continuing relationships.

#### Authorisation of civil proceedings

- **Court authorises** company-level claims against wrongdoers
  brought by petitioner / designated person.

#### Other relief

- **Capital reduction** to buy out;
- **Restraints** on memorandum / articles changes;
- **Any** order the Court considers just.

### Buy-out valuation

The most-litigated remedy issue:

#### Date of valuation

- **Typically order date** or date of judgment;
- **Earlier date** if the conduct depressed value;
- **Court discretion**.

#### Discount for minority

- **Generally not** applied in quasi-partnership cases;
- **Sometimes** applied in pure commercial cases;
- **Case-specific**.

#### Tax adjustments

- **Typically not made** (speculative);
- **Court may consider** in valuation broadly.

#### Expert evidence

- **Both sides** typically have valuation experts;
- **Court appoints** independent expert sometimes;
- **Methodology** disputed.

### Cost considerations

- **Loser-pays** typical;
- **Indemnity costs** for egregious conduct;
- **Cost protection** for petitioner — none standard;
- **Substantial** total cost: £200k–£2m+ for full trial.

### Common scenarios

#### Family business breakdown

- **Family members** excluded by majority;
- **Quasi-partnership** features;
- **Buy-out** typical remedy.

#### Owner-manager dispute

- **Founder + investor** dispute;
- **Diverted profits** allegation;
- **Buy-out** or governance order.

#### Pre-IPO dispute

- **Minority** dispute about restructuring or pre-IPO actions;
- **Specific remedies** sometimes;
- **Time-pressure** for resolution.

### Cross-border

For multinational families / corporates:

- **Foreign-court** considerations;
- **Article 9** for trust-held shareholdings;
- **Cross-border valuation** complexities.

## What to do next

For an active petition:

1. **Identify** specific unfair-prejudicial conduct;
2. **Gather** evidence;
3. **Engage** Jersey Advocate;
4. **Draft** Representation and affidavit;
5. **File and serve**;
6. **Manage** through disclosure / trial;
7. **Implement** Court order.

## Pitfalls

- **Vague allegations**: must be specific;
- **Conduct vs unfortunate outcome**: must show conduct is
  unfair;
- **Inadequate valuation evidence**;
- **Cost** under-estimation.
