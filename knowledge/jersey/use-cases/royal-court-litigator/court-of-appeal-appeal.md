# Appeal from the Royal Court to Court of Appeal

## The question

You've lost (or partially lost) at the Royal Court. What's the
procedure and prospects for an appeal to the **Court of
Appeal**?

## The short answer

Appeals to the Court of Appeal:

- **Notice of appeal** within prescribed time (typically 28
  days);
- **Leave to appeal** required in some matters; available as
  of right in others;
- **Panel of three** Court of Appeal judges (senior English-
  qualified jurists);
- **Hearing** in Jersey at the Royal Court building;
- **Standard appeal grounds**: errors of law, errors of fact
  (limited), exercise of discretion.

The Court of Appeal **rarely interferes** with discretionary
decisions absent clear error.

## The proper answer

### Composition

The Jersey Court of Appeal:

- **Panel of three** judges typically;
- **Drawn from** senior English / Welsh / Scottish / Northern
  Irish jurists (often KCs, retired senior judges);
- **President of the Court of Appeal** (Jersey-resident);
- **Sits in Jersey** at the Royal Court building.

### Jurisdiction

Hears appeals from:

- **Royal Court Samedi** civil decisions;
- **Royal Court Inferior Number** civil and criminal;
- **Royal Court Superior Number**;
- **Some specific statutory** appeals.

### Right of appeal vs leave

Some appeals require **leave** from the Royal Court or Court of
Appeal:

- **Interlocutory** matters typically need leave;
- **Final decisions** often as of right;
- **Specific statutory** provisions vary.

Where leave required, **prompt application** to Royal Court
preferred.

### Notice of appeal

#### Content

- **Identify** the decision being appealed;
- **Grounds** of appeal;
- **Relief** sought;
- **Parties** to the appeal.

#### Filing

- **With Court of Appeal** through Judicial Greffe;
- **Within prescribed time** (typically 28 days from order);
- **Fee** paid.

### Grounds of appeal

#### Errors of law

- **Misinterpretation** of statute / case law;
- **Misapplication** of legal principles;
- **Most likely** to succeed.

#### Errors of fact

- **Limited scope** for appellate intervention;
- **Findings of fact** by trial judge / Jurats respected;
- **Manifest error** or no-evidence required.

#### Discretion

- **Royal Court discretion** rarely disturbed;
- **Wrong principle** applied, or **outside range** of
  reasonable decisions required.

#### Procedural

- **Procedural unfairness** at trial;
- **Inadequate reasons** for decision;
- **Failure to consider** relevant evidence.

### Appeal procedure

#### Pre-hearing

- **Skeleton arguments** filed;
- **Bundle** of relevant documents;
- **Witness evidence** typically based on Royal Court record
  (no new witnesses);
- **Authorities bundle**.

#### Hearing

- **Oral argument** typically 1–3 days;
- **Cross-examination** rare (record-based);
- **Reserved judgment** typical;
- **Reasoned judgment** delivered.

#### Outcome

- **Dismiss** the appeal;
- **Allow** the appeal in whole or part;
- **Remit** to Royal Court for reconsideration;
- **Substitute** different order.

### Costs

- **Standard rules** (loser-pays);
- **Indemnity** for unreasonable appeals;
- **Substantial** cost — appeal hearings expensive.

### Strategic considerations

#### When to appeal

- **Genuine legal error** — best prospects;
- **Outside-range discretion** — moderate prospects;
- **Fresh perspective** sometimes valuable.

#### When not to appeal

- **Findings of fact** with adequate evidence;
- **Reasonable exercise of discretion**;
- **Procedural matters** with no substantive impact.

### Common scenarios

#### Trust dispute appeal

- **Trustee-vs-beneficiary** matter;
- **Schmidt-Rosewood** or Article 51 application;
- **Discretion** considerations limit appeal prospects.

#### Commercial dispute appeal

- **Contractual interpretation**;
- **Legal-error** grounds typically strongest.

#### Procedural appeal

- **Specific** procedural decisions;
- **Limited grounds** but specific issues sometimes arise.

#### Cost order appeal

- **Discretion** of Royal Court strongly respected;
- **Manifest error** required.

### Further appeal

From Court of Appeal:

- **Privy Council** with leave;
- **Most matters** end at Court of Appeal;
- **Constitutional / significant law** matters reach Privy
  Council.

### Timeline

- **Notice of appeal**: 28 days;
- **Pre-hearing prep**: 3–6 months;
- **Hearing**: scheduled by Court;
- **Judgment**: typically weeks-months after hearing;
- **Total**: 6–12 months typically.

## What to do next

For appeal consideration:

1. **Assess** prospects with appellate counsel;
2. **File** notice of appeal within time;
3. **Prepare** skeleton arguments;
4. **Manage** cost exposure;
5. **Attend** hearing;
6. **Implement** outcome.

## Pitfalls

- **Time-limit** miss;
- **Weak grounds** — discretion respected;
- **Cost** exposure;
- **Strategic** under-consideration of stay / interim relief.
