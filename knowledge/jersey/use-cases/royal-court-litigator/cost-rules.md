# Cost Rules in Royal Court Litigation

## The question

How do **costs** work in Royal Court litigation — loser-pays,
specific bases of assessment, scope of recoverable costs?

## The short answer

- **Loser pays** is the general rule;
- **Standard basis** of assessment — reasonable costs
  reasonably incurred;
- **Indemnity basis** — wider recovery, available for
  egregious conduct;
- **Costs in the cause** — abide event;
- **No-order-as-to-costs** in some matters (some trust
  applications);
- **Beddoe protection** for trustees in trust litigation;
- **Cost capping** possible in some cases.

Royal Court has discretion to depart from defaults in
appropriate cases.

## The proper answer

### Standard rules

#### Loser pays

- **Default** in hostile civil litigation;
- **Court discretion** to depart;
- **Successful party** entitled to costs subject to conduct
  considerations.

#### Standard basis of assessment

- **Reasonable costs reasonably incurred**;
- **Court / taxing master** assesses;
- **Detailed bills** to be paid;
- **Typical** recovery: 60–80% of actual costs.

#### Indemnity basis

- **Wider scope** than standard;
- **All costs** unless unreasonable;
- **Available for**:
  - **Egregious conduct** by losing party;
  - **Breach of pre-action protocols**;
  - **Unreasonable rejection** of offers to settle;
  - **Specific contractual provision**.

#### Costs in the cause

- **Abide the eventual outcome**;
- **Common** for interlocutory matters.

#### No order as to costs

- **Each side bears own**;
- **Common** in some administrative matters;
- **Trust applications** where Court directs.

### Beddoe protection

For trustees in trust litigation:

- **Application** before substantive litigation;
- **Trust-fund indemnity** for costs;
- **Specific** to authorised scope of litigation;
- **Protected** trustee not personally liable;
- **Specific Jersey practice** more flexible than English.

### Cost-protection mechanisms

#### Cost capping

- **Royal Court** can cap costs in appropriate cases;
- **Applicant's protection** in disproportionate cost
  exposure;
- **Less common** than English equivalent.

#### Sealed offers / Calderbank

- **Without-prejudice-save-as-to-costs** offers;
- **Loser failing to better** offer may face indemnity costs;
- **Strategic** litigation tool.

#### Security for costs

- **Defendant's protection** against impecunious plaintiff;
- **Application** to court;
- **Order** to deposit costs security.

### Specific contexts

#### Trust litigation

- **Beddoe protection** for trustees common;
- **Indemnity costs** from trust fund typical;
- **Beneficiary participation** costs sometimes from fund;
- **Schmidt-Rosewood** principles may apply.

#### Commercial litigation

- **Standard loser-pays**;
- **Conduct considerations**;
- **Sealed offers** active.

#### Probate

- **Specific** cost rules;
- **Estate-bound** costs in some matters;
- **Personal costs** for unreasonable opposition.

### Cost levels

Indicative Royal Court litigation cost ranges:

- **Interlocutory** application: £5k–25k;
- **Trust directions** (uncontested): £15k–50k;
- **Trust directions** (contested): £100k–500k+;
- **Commercial trial** (substantial): £500k–2m+;
- **Complex multi-jurisdictional**: well into seven figures.

### Recovery

#### Bill of costs

- **Detailed bill** prepared by winning party;
- **Filed** with Court;
- **Opposite party** can challenge;
- **Taxation** by master if disputed.

#### Payment

- **Within prescribed time** of order;
- **Enforcement** through normal civil procedures if unpaid;
- **Interim payments** sometimes ordered.

### Common scenarios

#### Successful claim

- **Court orders** defendant pays costs;
- **Standard basis** typically;
- **Indemnity** for misconduct.

#### Failed claim

- **Plaintiff pays** defendant's costs;
- **Standard basis**;
- **Cost protection** through cost-capping or security
  applications.

#### Beneficiary application against trustee

- **Beneficiary loses**: typically pays trustee costs from
  personal funds (no Beddoe-like protection for beneficiary);
- **Beneficiary wins**: trustee pays from fund typically.

#### Article 51 trustee application

- **Trustee costs** from fund;
- **Beneficiary participation** costs typically from fund;
- **No-order** for or against parties (some categories).

### Cross-border considerations

- **Foreign-court costs** vs Jersey costs separate;
- **Enforcement** of foreign cost orders in Jersey through
  recognition processes;
- **Coordinated** cost management in multi-jurisdictional
  matters.

## What to do next

For an active matter:

1. **Plan** cost strategy at outset;
2. **Set up** appropriate cost-protection mechanisms;
3. **Make sealed offers** strategically;
4. **Monitor** costs vs budget;
5. **Present** cost claim properly at end.

## Pitfalls

- **Cost under-estimation** at outset;
- **Failure to use** sealed offers;
- **Beddoe gap** for trustees;
- **Inadequate documentation** of costs.
