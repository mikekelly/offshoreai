# Interlocutory Injunctions — Mareva, Norwich Pharmacal, Anton Piller

## The question

You need **interlocutory relief** in the Royal Court — freezing
assets, getting third-party disclosure, securing evidence.
What's available in Jersey?

## The short answer

Jersey recognises and applies the **English-law interlocutory
injunction toolkit**:

- **Mareva** (freezing injunction) — preventing asset
  dissipation;
- **Norwich Pharmacal** — third-party disclosure of wrongdoer
  identity;
- **Anton Piller** (search order) — search for evidence;
- **Other** interlocutory relief through Royal Court inherent
  jurisdiction.

Applications are typically **ex parte** (without notice) for
urgent matters, with **return date** for adverse party.

## The proper answer

### Mareva (freezing) injunctions

#### Purpose

- **Freeze** defendant's assets pending substantive resolution;
- **Prevent dissipation** that would defeat eventual judgment;
- **Pre-judgment** asset preservation.

#### Conditions

The applicant must show:

- **Good arguable case** on the merits;
- **Real risk** of asset dissipation;
- **Sufficient assets** in Jersey to be frozen;
- **Balance of convenience** favours injunction;
- **Undertaking** as to damages.

#### Scope

- **Jersey-asset specific** typically;
- **Worldwide freezing** orders possible in some cases (subject
  to comity);
- **Specific assets** named or generic "all assets";
- **Ordinary-living-expenses** carve-out usually.

#### Procedure

- **Ex parte** application typically;
- **Affidavit** evidence;
- **Order granted** with return date;
- **Return date** within days/weeks for full hearing.

### Norwich Pharmacal orders

#### Purpose

- **Identify wrongdoers** through third-party disclosure;
- Third party innocently caught up in wrongdoing;
- Necessary disclosure for substantive claim.

#### Conditions

- **Wrong** done to applicant;
- **Third party** has relevant information;
- **Innocent involvement** of third party;
- **Necessity** of disclosure to pursue wrongdoer;
- **Proportionality**.

#### Examples

- **Bank** disclosure of account holder identity;
- **Internet service provider** disclosure of user identity;
- **Trust company** disclosure of beneficial owners.

### Anton Piller (search) orders

#### Purpose

- **Search premises** for evidence;
- **Prevent destruction** by defendant;
- **Preservation** of evidence before substantive claim.

#### Conditions

The applicant must show:

- **Extremely strong** prima facie case;
- **Very serious damage** if order not made;
- **Clear evidence** of defendant possessing incriminating
  documents;
- **Real possibility** of destruction.

#### Procedure

- **Ex parte** application;
- **Stringent procedural** safeguards;
- **Supervising solicitor** typically;
- **Defendant's rights** protected throughout.

Anton Piller orders are **rare** — very high bar.

### Other interlocutory relief

#### Specific performance

- **Compel performance** of contract pending trial;
- **Where damages inadequate**.

#### Mandatory injunctions

- **Compel action** rather than prevent it;
- **High bar** to grant.

#### Ne exeat orders

- **Restrict travel** of defendant pending matter;
- **Rare** in modern practice.

### Cross-border issues

#### Worldwide freezing orders

- **Jersey court** can grant in some cases;
- **Recognition** in other jurisdictions varies;
- **Coordination** with foreign-court parallel orders.

#### Foreign freezing orders

- **Recognition** in Jersey through Royal Court action;
- **Service** on Jersey-located defendants;
- **Cooperation** between courts.

### Procedural framework

#### Application

- **Representation** or **summons** depending on context;
- **Detailed affidavit** evidence;
- **Counsel** signature;
- **Filing** with Judicial Greffe.

#### Hearing

- **Ex parte** initially;
- **Bailiff or Commissioner** in chambers or open court;
- **Order drafted** at hearing or shortly after;
- **Service** on defendant.

#### Return date

- **Within days/weeks**;
- **Defendant** can challenge / vary;
- **Continuation** or discharge.

### Costs

- **Successful application**: typically defendant pays;
- **Application fails**: applicant pays;
- **Cross-undertaking in damages** for any loss caused by
  injunction;
- **Senior Advocate** rates for urgent applications.

### Common scenarios

#### Pre-action freezing of fraud proceeds

- **Strong case** developing;
- **Defendant** known to be flighty;
- **Mareva** to preserve assets;
- **Substantive** claim follows.

#### Identifying anonymous wrongdoer

- **Wrong** done through online / banking channels;
- **Third party** (bank, ISP) has information;
- **Norwich Pharmacal** to obtain identity.

#### Preserving documents before trial

- **Defendant** likely to destroy;
- **Anton Piller** for preservation;
- **High bar** — used sparingly.

#### Cross-border asset tracing

- **Foreign court** orders + Jersey orders;
- **Coordinated** strategy;
- **Trust assets** Article 9 considerations.

## What to do next

For an active situation:

1. **Assess** which type of order needed;
2. **Gather** evidence;
3. **Engage** senior Advocate;
4. **Draft** application with affidavit;
5. **File** urgently;
6. **Manage** the hearing and return date.

## Pitfalls

- **Insufficient evidence** for ex parte application;
- **Failure to disclose** material facts (disclosure
  obligation in ex parte);
- **Cross-undertaking** under-considered;
- **Service** errors on the injunction.
