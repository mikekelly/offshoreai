# Negative News and Reputational Risk Events

## The question

A **negative news event** about a customer — criminal charges,
regulatory action, business failure, scandal — has emerged. How
do you handle?

## The short answer

Triage immediately:

1. **Verify** the news is about your customer (not just a
   similar name);
2. **Assess** materiality (severity, recency, jurisdiction,
   credibility);
3. **Investigate** internally — review CDD, recent activity;
4. **Decide** on action:
   - **Continue** the relationship with enhanced monitoring;
   - **Reduce** exposure / hold transactions;
   - **Exit** the relationship;
5. **Document** the decision.

Negative news is **a key trigger** for customer review under
the AML / CFT Handbook.

## The proper answer

### Step 1: Verify

- **Confirm** the news refers to your specific customer
  (similar names common);
- **Multiple sources** to confirm reliability;
- **Date** of the news event;
- **Credibility** of the source.

### Step 2: Assess materiality

Factors:

- **Severity** of the alleged conduct;
- **Recency** (old news may have been resolved);
- **Jurisdiction** of the proceeding / action;
- **Customer's role** (subject vs witness);
- **Outcome** if known;
- **Consistency** with customer's prior profile.

### Step 3: Investigate internally

Review:

- **CDD file** for the customer;
- **Source-of-wealth narrative** for any connection;
- **Transaction history** for any related patterns;
- **Other customers** in the same group or with connections;
- **PEP / sanctions** status update.

### Step 4: Risk-classify

Categories of negative news:

#### High-severity

- **Criminal charges** for financial crime / money laundering;
- **Sanctions designation** (different from negative news but
  same trigger);
- **Major regulatory action**;
- **Material allegations** of tax evasion, corruption.

→ **Immediate review and likely material action**.

#### Medium-severity

- **Civil litigation** alleging financial wrongdoing;
- **Bankruptcy / insolvency**;
- **Reputational events** without proven wrongdoing;
- **Family-related disputes** affecting wealth.

→ **Enhanced review and possible action**.

#### Lower-severity

- **Old news** of resolved disputes;
- **Industry-wide** negative perception of customer's sector;
- **Allegations** unsubstantiated and old;
- **Family/personal** events without financial-crime implication.

→ **Document and continue with appropriate awareness**.

### Step 5: Decide

For each category, options:

#### Continue with enhanced monitoring

- Customer relationship continues;
- **Enhanced ongoing monitoring** (lower thresholds, more
  frequent review);
- **Re-EDD** at next opportunity;
- **Senior approval** for material transactions.

#### Reduce / freeze

- **Hold** specific transactions while investigating;
- **Reduce** exposure in line with risk;
- **No new significant activity** until clearer.

#### Exit

- **Plan** orderly exit;
- **FIU consent** for exit transactions if needed;
- **Tipping-off** discipline;
- **Document** reasons.

### Step 6: Document

- **Initial negative-media** identification;
- **Verification** records;
- **Materiality assessment**;
- **Internal investigation** findings;
- **Decision** and reasons;
- **Implementation** records.

### Common scenarios

#### Customer charged with financial crime abroad

- **Material** event;
- **Immediate review**;
- **Likely senior-management** decision needed;
- **Possible exit** depending on circumstances.

#### Customer's company in regulatory dispute

- **Severity-dependent**;
- **Distinguish** between customer's personal conduct and
  company's;
- **Investigate** what the dispute involves.

#### Customer in messy divorce

- **Generally not adverse** for AML purposes;
- But **may affect** beneficial-ownership / structure;
- **Update** CDD as facts emerge.

#### Old allegations resurfacing

- **Old + resolved** → likely lower-priority;
- **Old + unresolved** → still relevant;
- **Re-emergence** with new evidence → fresh review.

### SAR considerations

If negative news triggers **suspicion** of money laundering:

- **Internal escalation** to MLRO;
- **MLRO assessment**;
- **SAR** if threshold met;
- **Standard consent / tipping-off** discipline.

## What to do next

For an active negative-news event:

1. **Verify**;
2. **Assess** materiality;
3. **Investigate** internally;
4. **Risk-classify**;
5. **Decide** action;
6. **Document**.

For programme:

- **Adverse-media** screening tools and process;
- **Trigger-event** internal-escalation channels;
- **Training** on assessment of negative news.

## Pitfalls

- **Premature decision**: acting before facts confirmed;
- **Over-reaction**: exit relationships on thin information;
- **Under-reaction**: dismissing material events;
- **Documentation gaps**.
