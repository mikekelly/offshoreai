# Annual Business-Wide Risk Assessment (BWRA)

## The question

The Money Laundering Order 2008 and JFSC Handbook require a
**business-wide risk assessment (BWRA)** — at least annually.
What goes in it?

## The short answer

The BWRA documents the firm's **AML / CFT risk profile**:

- **Customer-type** risks across the book;
- **Product / service** risks;
- **Jurisdictional** risks of customer base;
- **Delivery channel** risks (in-person vs remote);
- **Specific high-risk** factors (PEPs, complex structures,
  high-risk activities);
- **Overall risk rating** of the firm;
- **Mitigation** measures and controls.

The BWRA drives the firm's **risk-based AML programme**.

## The proper answer

### Why BWRA

The risk-based approach to AML requires the firm to:

- **Understand** its specific risks;
- **Calibrate** controls to those risks;
- **Demonstrate** that controls are adequate;
- **Update** as risks evolve.

The BWRA is the **central documentation** of this analysis.

### BWRA components

#### Customer-type risks

- Map customer types in the book (HNW individuals, family-
  offices, trusts, foundations, corporates, funds);
- Assess **risk profile** of each type;
- Identify **higher-risk subtypes** (PEPs, high-risk-jurisdiction,
  complex structures).

#### Product / service risks

- Map products / services offered (trust administration, fund
  services, corporate services, custody, etc.);
- Assess **risk profile** of each;
- Identify high-risk products (e.g. complex structures, virtual
  assets).

#### Jurisdictional risks

- Map customer-jurisdiction distribution;
- Identify exposure to **high-risk jurisdictions** (FATF lists,
  Jersey-specific, sanctioned countries);
- **Mitigation** for high-risk-jurisdiction customers.

#### Delivery channel risks

- **In-person** onboarding vs **remote / digital**;
- Risk-mitigation for remote (enhanced identification, etc.).

#### Specific high-risk factors

- **PEP population** size and management;
- **Sanctions exposure**;
- **Complex structures** in the book;
- **VASP or virtual-asset** exposure;
- **Cash-intensive** activities.

### Methodology

A typical BWRA process:

1. **Data gathering** — customer / product / transaction
   statistics;
2. **Risk-factor analysis** for each category;
3. **Risk scoring** (often quantified);
4. **Aggregation** to firm-wide risk rating;
5. **Mitigation review** — are controls proportionate?
6. **Action plan** for gaps;
7. **Board / senior management approval**;
8. **Implementation**.

### Output

The BWRA document typically includes:

- **Executive summary** for board / senior management;
- **Methodology** and data sources;
- **Detailed risk analysis** by category;
- **Firm-wide risk rating**;
- **Mitigation framework**;
- **Action plan**;
- **Approval** by board / risk committee.

### Frequency

- **Annual** is the minimum cadence;
- **More frequent** for fast-changing risk environments;
- **Triggered updates** on material changes (new product, new
  customer category, significant incident).

### Use in operations

The BWRA drives:

- **Customer-onboarding** risk classification;
- **CDD intensity** (standard vs EDD);
- **Ongoing-monitoring** intensity;
- **Training** content;
- **Resource allocation**;
- **Internal-audit** focus.

### Common deficiencies

JFSC criticisms of BWRAs commonly include:

- **Generic** — not specific to the firm;
- **Stale** — not updated for material changes;
- **Disconnected** from operational practices;
- **Approval-only** — no real engagement by board;
- **Mitigation-light** — risks identified but controls not
  calibrated.

A robust BWRA addresses these.

## What to do next

For the annual BWRA:

1. **Gather** data on customer / product / jurisdiction
   distribution;
2. **Analyse** risk factors per category;
3. **Score** and aggregate;
4. **Review** mitigation;
5. **Identify** action items;
6. **Document** the analysis;
7. **Approve** at board / senior committee;
8. **Implement** any actions.

For programme:

- **Schedule** annual BWRA cycle;
- **Maintain** data infrastructure to support;
- **Train** team on methodology.

## Pitfalls

- **Template BWRA**: industry templates as a starting point,
  but require firm-specific customisation;
- **Annual rather than living**: update on material changes;
- **Disconnected from operations**: BWRA should drive practice,
  not sit on a shelf;
- **Inadequate board engagement**.
