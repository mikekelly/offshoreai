# Training Programme — Frequency and Content

## The question

What AML / CFT training must staff receive, how often, and on
what topics?

## The short answer

Under the Money Laundering Order 2008 and JFSC Handbook:

- **All relevant staff** must be trained on AML / CFT
  obligations;
- **Initial training** at induction;
- **Refresh training** at least **annually**;
- **Role-specific training** for higher-risk roles;
- **Ad-hoc training** on emerging issues (regulatory changes,
  sanctions, new risks).

Training must be **documented** — JFSC inspections look at
training records.

## The proper answer

### Who is trained

- **All staff** with customer-facing or transaction-handling
  roles;
- **All principal persons** including non-executive directors;
- **Senior management** with oversight responsibility;
- **Compliance and AML team** at higher intensity;
- **Outsourced administration providers** as applicable.

### Topics

Core curriculum:

- **What money laundering is** and how it relates to the firm;
- **Money-laundering offences** under Jersey law;
- **Customer-due-diligence** requirements;
- **Suspicious-activity recognition**;
- **Internal-disclosure** procedures;
- **Tipping-off** rules;
- **Sanctions** compliance;
- **CRS / FATCA** essentials;
- **Code-of-Practice** obligations;
- **Firm-specific** policies and procedures.

Role-specific extensions:

- **Customer-facing** staff: practical CDD scenarios;
- **Transaction-handling**: red-flag recognition;
- **Senior management**: risk oversight;
- **Compliance**: technical regulatory updates.

### Frequency

- **Induction** for new hires;
- **Annual refresh** at minimum;
- **Triggered** on regulatory changes;
- **Triggered** on emerging risks (new sanctions, new fraud
  patterns);
- **Periodic** practical-scenario training.

### Format

- **Online modules** for breadth and consistency;
- **In-person sessions** for case studies and discussion;
- **External speakers** (Jersey-counsel, regulators) for senior
  audiences;
- **Industry workshops** for compliance team;
- **Assessment / testing** to verify retention.

### Documentation

For each session:

- **Date and topics**;
- **Attendees** list;
- **Trainer credentials**;
- **Materials** retained;
- **Assessment** results if applicable;
- **Follow-up** for non-attenders.

### Effectiveness measurement

- **Testing** post-training;
- **Practical exercises** (mock onboarding);
- **Internal audit** review of operational practice;
- **JFSC visit** feedback on staff awareness.

## What to do next

For programme:

1. **Design** a curriculum matching firm risk profile;
2. **Identify** training methods (online + in-person);
3. **Set** annual cadence;
4. **Implement** with documentation;
5. **Measure** effectiveness;
6. **Refresh** content periodically.

## Pitfalls

- **One-size-fits-all**: roles vary in need;
- **Tick-box training**: completion without retention;
- **No assessment**: retention unverified;
- **Stale content**: regulatory environment changes.
