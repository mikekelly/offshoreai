# CDD Refresh Cycle — What Cadence?

## The question

How often should CDD on existing customers be **refreshed**?

## The short answer

**Risk-based cadence**:

- **High-risk** customers (PEP, EDD population): **annual**
  refresh;
- **Medium-risk** customers: **3-yearly** refresh typically;
- **Low-risk** customers: **5-yearly** or trigger-based;
- **Material-event triggers** at any time (negative news,
  sanctions match, transaction profile change).

Cadence should match the **business-wide risk assessment**
(BWRA) and be **documented** in the firm's AML programme.

## The proper answer

### Why refresh

CDD captures a customer profile **at a point in time**. The
profile changes:

- **Customer status changes** (new role triggering PEP status);
- **Beneficial-ownership changes** in entity customers;
- **Wealth profile changes** (acquisition, divorce, inheritance);
- **Risk profile changes** (sanctions, adverse media, regulatory
  action);
- **Documentary evidence ages** (5-year-old passport copy isn't
  current).

Refresh keeps the CDD **live and reliable**.

### Risk-based cadence

The Money Laundering Order 2008 and JFSC Handbook expect a
**risk-based approach**:

#### High-risk customers (annual)

- **PEPs**;
- **Customers with adverse media**;
- **High-risk jurisdiction** customers;
- **Complex structures**;
- **Cash-intensive businesses**.

For these, **annual comprehensive review** is the norm.

#### Medium-risk customers (3-yearly)

- **Standard HNW** clients without elevated factors;
- **Regulated FI** counterparties (where SDD applies);
- **Standard institutional** investor base.

#### Low-risk customers (5-yearly or trigger-based)

- **Routine retail** customers (rare in TCB / fund context);
- **Long-standing low-activity** relationships;
- **Listed-company** counterparties (where SDD applies).

### Trigger-based reviews

Outside the scheduled cycle, **trigger reviews** apply on:

#### Adverse-media hits

- **Negative news** about the customer or related party;
- **Regulatory action** elsewhere;
- **Criminal proceedings** in any jurisdiction.

#### Sanctions / PEP-list updates

- **New designations** matching customer profile;
- **PEP-status changes** (new political role);
- **Customer-jurisdiction** regime changes.

#### Customer-event changes

- **New beneficial owner**;
- **Significant new activity** in transaction profile;
- **Material change** in business model;
- **Significant new transaction** outside normal pattern.

#### Documentary refresh

- **ID expiry** (passport renewal);
- **Address changes**;
- **Regulatory document** changes.

### What a refresh covers

A scheduled refresh typically:

- **Reconfirms** customer identification (re-verify if
  documents stale);
- **Updates** beneficial-ownership / controlling-person
  information;
- **Re-screens** for PEP / sanctions / adverse media;
- **Reviews** transaction history against profile;
- **Updates** source-of-wealth understanding;
- **Reassesses** risk classification;
- **Documents** the refresh outcome.

### Documentation

Maintain:

- **Refresh schedule** for each customer;
- **Refresh records** showing what was done and concluded;
- **Risk-classification** at each refresh;
- **Trigger-event logs** with consequent reviews.

JFSC inspections look at refresh discipline carefully.

### Operational realities

Many TCBs / regulated entities:

- Use **case-management systems** to schedule refreshes;
- **Automated screening** runs more frequently (daily / weekly)
  for sanctions / PEP updates;
- **Comprehensive review** at the scheduled cadence;
- **Risk-committee** oversight of high-risk population.

### Common issues

- **Refresh backlogs** — high-volume customer books outpace
  refresh capacity;
- **Triggered reviews delayed** — events identified but not
  acted on promptly;
- **Documentation gaps** — refresh done but inadequately
  recorded;
- **Risk-classification creep** — customers slowly reclassified
  without explicit risk-committee approval.

## What to do next

For programme readiness:

1. **Set** risk-based cadence in your AML programme;
2. **Implement** scheduling system;
3. **Define** trigger events for off-cycle reviews;
4. **Train** staff on refresh process;
5. **Monitor** programme execution.

For an immediate refresh:

1. **Identify** the customer's current risk classification;
2. **Apply** appropriate refresh scope;
3. **Re-screen** for sanctions / PEP / adverse media;
4. **Update** identification and source-of-wealth;
5. **Reassess** risk classification;
6. **Document**.

## Pitfalls

- **Mechanical refresh** without substantive review;
- **Refresh backlog** that grows beyond manageable;
- **Trigger-event blindness** — events not feeding into reviews;
- **Inadequate documentation** of refresh outcomes.
