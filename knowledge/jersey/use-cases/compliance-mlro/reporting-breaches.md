# Reporting Breaches to the JFSC

## The question

You've identified a **breach of regulatory requirements** — code-
of-practice breach, AML failure, missed filing, inadequate CDD
on a particular customer. Do you need to report this to the
JFSC, and how?

## The short answer

**Material breaches** must be **self-reported** to the JFSC
under the relevant Code of Practice:

- **Significant CDD failures**;
- **Late or missed filings**;
- **Compliance-system failures**;
- **AML / sanctions failures**;
- **Material breaches** of policy or procedure;
- **Breaches identified through internal audit / external
  review**.

Self-reporting is **expected** — failing to self-report a
known breach is itself a worse breach and can attract harsher
penalties.

## The proper answer

### What constitutes a reportable breach

#### Material

- **CDD failures** on individual customers;
- **AML procedure failures**;
- **SAR-process failures**;
- **Sanctions-screening lapses**;
- **Filing failures** (late or missing);
- **Code-of-Practice breaches**;
- **Internal-control failures**;
- **Fit-and-proper issues** with key persons.

#### Less material

- **Routine administrative errors** corrected promptly;
- **Minor procedural deviations** without consequence;
- **Process improvements** identified through audit.

The line between material and immaterial is **judgement-based**;
err toward reporting if in doubt.

### Self-report decision

Factors:

- **Severity** of the breach (impact on customers, financial,
  regulatory);
- **Whether it was systemic** or one-off;
- **Whether it was detected by chance** or robust controls;
- **Whether remediation** is complete;
- **Whether further breaches** are likely.

For material breaches: **self-report**.

### Reporting process

1. **Investigate** the breach internally;
2. **Document** facts, root cause, scope;
3. **Implement** immediate remediation;
4. **Consider** broader implications (similar customers / files);
5. **Prepare** a self-report submission;
6. **Submit** to the JFSC through prescribed channel;
7. **Follow up** with the JFSC's response.

### The self-report submission

Include:

- **Nature** of the breach;
- **When** it occurred and when discovered;
- **How** it was discovered;
- **Root cause** analysis;
- **Scope** (single incident vs systemic);
- **Customers / files** affected;
- **Remediation steps** taken;
- **Prevention measures** going forward;
- **Senior-management** sign-off.

### Why self-report

#### Benefits

- **Demonstrates** good compliance culture;
- **Mitigates penalty severity** in enforcement;
- **Builds trust** with the JFSC;
- **Better engagement** on remediation;
- **Public-statement** language often softer for self-reported
  breaches.

#### Cost of not self-reporting

- **Discovery elsewhere** (JFSC visit, third-party complaint)
  with significantly worse consequences;
- **Enforcement** is harsher for concealment;
- **Reputational** damage if breach surfaces publicly;
- **Personal liability** of MLRO / principal persons increased.

### Common breach scenarios

#### CDD discovered as inadequate during file review

- **Remediate** the specific customer file;
- **Audit** similar customers for the same issue;
- **Self-report** if systemic;
- **Improve** training / process.

#### Missed SAR identified retrospectively

- **File SAR** immediately;
- **Self-report** the missed-SAR to JFSC;
- **Review** decision-making process.

#### Filing missed beyond deadline

- **File** as soon as possible (with late-filing fee);
- **Self-report** if material;
- **Improve** filing-tracking system.

#### Sanctions match missed

- **Address** the match immediately;
- **Self-report** the missed screen;
- **Improve** the screening system.

### Engagement with the JFSC

Post-self-report:

- **Cooperate** with any follow-up;
- **Provide** additional information promptly;
- **Implement** any required remediation;
- **Update** on progress;
- **Close** the matter formally with the JFSC.

The JFSC typically responds with:

- **Acknowledgement** and acceptance;
- **Follow-up questions** if more info needed;
- **Required remediation** (often what's already underway);
- **Possible enforcement** for serious or systemic breaches;
- **Closure** of the matter.

### Documentation

Maintain:

- **Internal investigation** records;
- **Self-report submission** copies;
- **JFSC correspondence**;
- **Remediation completion** evidence;
- **Closure confirmation**.

## What to do next

For an identified breach:

1. **Investigate** quickly;
2. **Document** facts and root cause;
3. **Implement** remediation;
4. **Decide** materiality;
5. **Self-report** if material;
6. **Follow up** with JFSC response;
7. **Document** closure.

## Pitfalls

- **Treating non-reporting as a choice**: it isn't for material
  breaches;
- **Inadequate remediation** before reporting;
- **Defensive submission** that doesn't address root cause;
- **Failing to investigate scope**: a single-customer issue may
  be systemic.
