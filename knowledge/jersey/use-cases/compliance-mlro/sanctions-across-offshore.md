# Sanctions Compliance Across Offshore Jurisdictions

## The question

You're MLRO of a TCB firm with **clients connected to
multiple offshore jurisdictions** (Jersey trust + Cayman fund
+ BVI BC). What's the **multi-jurisdictional sanctions
compliance** picture?

## The short answer

Each offshore jurisdiction has its **own sanctions regime**:

- **Jersey SAFL 2014** — Jersey sanctions;
- **Cayman** sanctions law;
- **BVI** sanctions law;
- **Bermuda** sanctions law;
- **IoM** sanctions law;
- **Guernsey** sanctions law.

**All** mirror **UK / UN sanctions** broadly. **OFSI** (UK)
designations propagate through all Crown Dependencies and
BOTs. **EU sanctions** apply where compatible. **US OFAC**
applies extraterritorially.

For Jersey MLROs:

- **Screen** against Jersey consolidated list (which
  includes UK / UN);
- **Be aware** of cross-jurisdictional designations;
- **Coordinate** with counterparts in other jurisdictions
  for cross-border structures;
- **Consider** US OFAC for USD transactions.

## The proper answer

### Jurisdictional overview

#### Jersey

- **Sanctions and Asset-Freezing (Jersey) Law 2014**;
- **JFSC** publishes consolidated list;
- **UK / UN** mirroring;
- **EU** selective post-Brexit;
- See [`../../aml-cft/safl-articles-index.md`](../../aml-cft/safl-articles-index.md).

#### Guernsey

- **Sanctions (Bailiwick of Guernsey) Law 2018**;
- **GFSC** publishes list;
- **UK / UN** mirroring;
- **Aligned** with Jersey approach.

#### Isle of Man

- **Specific** IoM sanctions legislation;
- **FSA** publishes guidance;
- **UK / UN** mirroring;
- **VAT / customs union** considerations.

#### Cayman

- **Sanctions and Anti-Money Laundering Act 2018** (UK Act
  extended);
- **Cayman-specific** orders;
- **OFSI alignment** through UK extension.

#### BVI

- **Sanctions and Anti-Money Laundering Act 2018** (UK Act
  extended);
- **FSC** publishes guidance.

#### Bermuda

- **Specific** Bermuda sanctions legislation;
- **UK / UN** mirroring through specific Bermuda
  implementing legislation.

### Cross-jurisdictional propagation

UK / UN sanctions **propagate** through:

- **UK Order in Council** extending UK sanctions to Crown
  Dependencies / BOTs;
- **Specific** local implementing legislation in each
  jurisdiction;
- **Consolidated lists** maintained in each jurisdiction;
- **Coordinated** updates.

In practice:

- A person **UK-designated** is **also Jersey-designated**;
- **Also Cayman**, **BVI**, **Bermuda**, **IoM**,
  **Guernsey-designated**;
- **All** propagate from UK / UN actions.

### Specific scenarios

#### Russia sanctions (post-2022)

- **UK** introduced extensive Russia sanctions in 2022;
- **All Crown Dependencies and BOTs** implemented in parallel
  through specific orders / legislation;
- **Crown Dependencies** especially active in implementing;
- **Affecting** Russia-connected trust / company structures
  across all offshore jurisdictions.

#### Designated client with multi-jurisdictional structure

If a client becomes designated, structures spanning
jurisdictions need **coordinated response**:

- **Jersey trust** — comply with SAFL Article 10 (freeze);
- **BVI BC** — comply with BVI sanctions;
- **Cayman fund** — comply with Cayman sanctions;
- **Bermuda** — comply with Bermuda sanctions;
- **Coordinated** licence applications if specific
  transactions needed;
- **Information sharing** subject to local privacy / tipping-
  off rules.

#### Cross-border licensing

For transactions needing licences:

- **Jersey** licence under SAFL Article 15;
- **UK OFSI** licence;
- **EU** licence if relevant;
- **US OFAC** licence for USD;
- **Other jurisdictions** as relevant;
- **Joint** processing in some cases.

#### OFSI as central authority

The **UK OFSI** is the **practical centre**:

- **Maintains** UK consolidated list;
- **Issues** UK licences;
- **Provides** guidance applicable across UK / Crown
  Dependencies / BOTs;
- **Coordinates** with offshore counterparts.

### US OFAC extraterritorial application

US OFAC sanctions apply **extraterritorially**:

- **USD-denominated** transactions involve US dollar clearing
  → US persons → US OFAC jurisdiction;
- **US-person involvement** in transaction → US OFAC
  jurisdiction;
- **Secondary sanctions** can apply to non-US persons doing
  business with sanctioned countries / entities;
- **Specific** US-licensing process separate from offshore.

For Jersey MLROs:

- **Screen** for OFAC SDNs especially for USD transactions;
- **Specific** US OFAC licensing process if needed;
- **Coordinate** with US counsel for complex matters.

### Information sharing

For sanctions matters across offshore jurisdictions:

- **POCL Article 36** information sharing in Jersey
  applies to regulated firms;
- **Equivalent** in other jurisdictions;
- **Group-wide** AML / sanctions sharing typically permitted;
- **Tipping-off** considerations apply across all
  jurisdictions;
- **Specific** procedures for cross-jurisdictional sharing.

### Practical multi-jurisdictional compliance

For a Jersey MLRO with multi-jurisdictional clients:

1. **Map** the client structures by jurisdiction;
2. **Identify** sanctions risks in each jurisdiction;
3. **Coordinate** screening across the structure;
4. **Establish** information-sharing protocols with
   counterparts;
5. **Document** the coordinated approach;
6. **Train** staff on multi-jurisdictional issues;
7. **Engage** with sanctions counsel for complex matters.

## What to do next

For a multi-jurisdictional sanctions matter:

1. **Identify** all jurisdictions in the structure;
2. **Screen** in each jurisdiction;
3. **Determine** designations applicable;
4. **Coordinate** with MLROs / officers in other
   jurisdictions;
5. **Apply** for any necessary licences (multi-jurisdiction
   if needed);
6. **Document** the coordinated compliance approach;
7. **Continue** monitoring as designations evolve.

## Pitfalls

- **Missing** cross-jurisdictional designations;
- **Inconsistent** approach across jurisdictions;
- **Tipping-off** through cross-jurisdictional
  communication;
- **OFAC** issues missed for USD transactions;
- **Inadequate** licence coordination;
- **Specific** issues with sanctioned-jurisdiction-connected
  clients.
