# What Information Goes in a SAR?

## The question

You're filing a SAR with the Jersey FIU. What information should
be included?

## The short answer

A typical SAR includes:

- **Reporting institution** details (firm, MLRO);
- **Subject(s) of the SAR** — customer identity and CDD
  details;
- **Suspicious activity** described factually;
- **Transaction details** — amounts, dates, counterparties,
  flow;
- **Reasons for suspicion** — analytical basis;
- **Supporting documents** (KYC, transaction records,
  background);
- **Consent request** if a transaction is pending.

Use the **FIU's online portal** template; structured fields
guide the content.

## The proper answer

### The FIU SAR portal

Jersey's Financial Intelligence Unit operates an **online SAR
portal** (THEMIS or successor system) for structured submissions:

- **Mandatory fields** for institution / subject identity;
- **Narrative fields** for circumstances of suspicion;
- **Document upload** for supporting evidence;
- **Consent flag** for pending-transaction situations.

### Reporting institution

- Firm name and JFSC registration details;
- **MLRO identity** as the filer;
- Firm's address and contact details.

### Subject of the SAR

For an individual customer:

- **Full legal name**, all known aliases;
- **Date of birth, place of birth, nationality**;
- **Residential address** (current and prior known);
- **Tax residence**;
- **ID documents** referenced (passport / national ID
  numbers);
- **Roles** in any entity structure.

For an entity customer:

- **Full legal name, registered number, jurisdiction**;
- **Registered office and principal place of business**;
- **Directors and officers**;
- **Beneficial owners** identified;
- **Banking relationship details**.

### Description of suspicious activity

A **narrative** describing what happened and why it's suspicious:

- **Chronology** of events;
- **Specific transactions** with dates and amounts;
- **Counterparties** involved;
- **Inconsistencies** with the customer profile;
- **Red flags** specifically identified;
- **The trigger** that prompted internal disclosure.

Write **factually** — describe what you observed, not
conclusions about guilt.

### Reasons for suspicion

The **analytical basis** for the suspicion:

- **What's anomalous** about the activity?
- **Why** is it inconsistent with the customer's profile?
- **What alternative explanations** were considered and why
  they don't fit?
- **What information** would help the FIU investigate further?

This is the **analytical layer** beyond bare facts.

### Supporting documents

Attach as relevant:

- **CDD file** extracts (identification, source of wealth);
- **Transaction records** evidencing the suspicious activity;
- **Account statements** for relevant period;
- **Background documents** (corporate structure, trust deeds);
- **Communications** with the customer (selectively, where
  relevant);
- **Adverse-media** snippets if material.

### Consent request

If a transaction is **pending** and requires FIU consent to
proceed:

- **Flag the consent request** prominently;
- **Provide details** of the specific transaction;
- **Timeline** by which decision is needed;
- **Consequences** of delay (e.g. settlement deadlines).

The FIU has **statutory deadlines** to respond.

### Confidentiality and data protection

- **SAR content** is confidential — not shared with the
  customer;
- **Personal data** protection rules continue to apply;
- **Group-entity sharing** carefully controlled;
- **Retention** per regulatory requirements.

### Practical drafting tips

- **Be specific** with amounts, dates, account numbers;
- **Be factual** in describing observations;
- **Be analytical** in explaining suspicion;
- **Avoid speculation** about criminal conduct beyond what's
  supported;
- **Avoid conclusory language** — let the facts speak;
- **Use plain language** — assume the FIU analyst is not
  familiar with your customer.

### Post-filing

After submission:

- **Retain** the SAR copy in compliance records;
- **Update** the customer file with the SAR reference;
- **Monitor** for FIU follow-up;
- **Manage** the relationship with tipping-off discipline.

## What to do next

For a SAR being filed:

1. **Gather** the facts and supporting documents;
2. **Draft** the SAR using the portal;
3. **Review** for accuracy and clarity;
4. **Submit** via the FIU portal;
5. **Document** the submission internally;
6. **Manage** the customer relationship per consent rules.

## Pitfalls

- **Conclusory drafting**: stating "this is money laundering"
  rather than describing the facts;
- **Inadequate supporting documents**: makes the FIU's job
  harder;
- **Vague narratives**: specifics help the analyst;
- **Missing the consent flag**: if a transaction is pending,
  flag clearly.
