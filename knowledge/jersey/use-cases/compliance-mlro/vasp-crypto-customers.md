# Virtual Asset / Crypto-Related Customers

## The question

You have customers involved in **virtual assets / crypto** —
holding cryptocurrency, operating a VASP, investing in crypto
funds. How do you manage AML / CFT?

## The short answer

Crypto / virtual-asset customers require **EDD** and enhanced
monitoring:

- **Source of funds** for crypto-derived wealth requires
  blockchain-analysis evidence;
- **Travel rule** requirements apply for VASP-to-VASP transfers;
- **VASP customers** must be **JFSC-registered** if Jersey-based;
- **Sanctions screening** including crypto-specific tools;
- **Specific risk factors** (anonymity, decentralisation,
  speed) drive enhanced controls.

The crypto / virtual-asset space is **higher AML risk** than
traditional assets — but legitimate participants exist and
should be served appropriately.

## The proper answer

### VASP definition

A **Virtual Asset Service Provider (VASP)** under FATF Rec 15
and Jersey rules conducts business in:

- **Exchange** between virtual assets and fiat;
- **Exchange** between virtual assets;
- **Transfer** of virtual assets;
- **Custody / administration** of virtual assets;
- **Issuer-related services** for virtual assets.

VASPs operating in / from Jersey must be **JFSC-registered**
under the AML / CFT framework.

### Customer profile types

#### Individual holding crypto

- Personal investor / family member;
- Source of funds: salary / inheritance / business / prior
  crypto investment;
- **EDD** if material wealth in crypto.

#### Crypto-fund investor

- Investing in fund with crypto strategy;
- Source of funds: traditional / crypto-derived;
- Standard fund-investor CDD plus crypto-specific source
  scrutiny.

#### VASP customer

- Business providing crypto services;
- Must be JFSC-registered if Jersey-based;
- Full business CDD including beneficial ownership.

#### Crypto-rich client

- HNW whose wealth was generated through crypto;
- **Significant source-of-wealth diligence**;
- **Blockchain analytics** evidence;
- **Tax-compliance** verification.

### Source-of-wealth for crypto

Documentary evidence for crypto wealth:

- **Wallet records** showing acquisition history;
- **Exchange statements** with deposit / withdrawal records;
- **Blockchain analytics** reports (Chainalysis, Elliptic,
  TRM Labs) showing transaction history;
- **Tax declarations** in relevant jurisdictions;
- **Disposal records** if some crypto sold for fiat.

The narrative should show:

- **Origins** of acquisition (purchased / earned / mined);
- **Chain of holdings** through history;
- **Transactions** demonstrating beneficial ownership;
- **Tax compliance** through the holding period.

### Travel rule

For **VASP-to-VASP transfers** above prescribed thresholds, the
originating VASP must transmit **originator information** and
**beneficiary information** to the receiving VASP:

- **Originator name, account, address**;
- **Beneficiary name, account**;
- Implementation through TRP (Travel Rule Protocol) or similar
  standards.

This is the **AML equivalent** of FATF Recommendation 16 for
traditional wire transfers.

### Sanctions

Crypto sanctions screening:

- **Wallet-address screening** against sanctions lists;
- **Person-level screening** of customers;
- **Specialised tools** (Chainalysis Sanctions Screening,
  similar);
- **Real-time alerts** for transactions touching sanctioned
  addresses.

### Specific risks

#### Anonymity-enhanced

- **Privacy coins** (Monero, Zcash) higher risk;
- **Mixers / tumblers** indicators of obfuscation;
- **DeFi protocols** with less identifiable counterparties.

#### High-risk jurisdictions

- Crypto often crosses borders fluidly;
- Geographic risk from blockchain analytics;
- High-risk jurisdiction crypto exposure requires EDD.

#### Volatility

- Sudden wealth changes;
- Source-of-wealth narratives age quickly;
- Refresh more often.

### Operational tools

For crypto-customer management:

- **Blockchain analytics** subscription (Chainalysis, Elliptic,
  TRM Labs);
- **Wallet screening** integration;
- **Travel rule** compliance tooling;
- **Specialised AML training** for crypto-aware staff.

## What to do next

For onboarding crypto customer:

1. **Identify** crypto involvement;
2. **Conduct EDD** including blockchain-analytics evidence;
3. **Verify** source of wealth;
4. **Set up** ongoing monitoring with crypto-specific tools;
5. **Document**.

For programme:

- **Define** crypto-customer appetite;
- **Acquire** blockchain-analytics tools;
- **Train** staff;
- **Update** policy.

## Pitfalls

- **Traditional CDD insufficient**: crypto-source needs
  blockchain evidence;
- **Wallet-address gaps**: must screen specific addresses;
- **Travel-rule failures** for VASP customers;
- **Static risk assessment**: crypto risk profile changes
  rapidly.
