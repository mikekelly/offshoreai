# What Do I Do on a Sanctions Match?

## The question

Your screening system has identified a **sanctions match**.
What do you do?

## The short answer

Triage immediately:

1. **Stop** any in-flight dealings;
2. **Verify** the match (true positive vs false positive);
3. If **false positive**: document and clear;
4. If **true positive**:
   - **Freeze** affected assets immediately;
   - **Notify** JFSC and FIU;
   - **Apply** for any necessary licences;
   - **Document** everything.

The verification step matters — **most matches are false
positives** based on similar names.

## The proper answer

### Step 1: Stop in-flight activity

Until the match is resolved:

- **Pause** any pending transactions involving the matched
  party;
- **Hold** any pending account changes;
- **Document** the timing of the hold.

### Step 2: Verify

Compare the matched-record details with the customer:

- **Full name** match including middle names;
- **Date of birth** match (often the differentiator);
- **Place of birth / nationality**;
- **Address** if available;
- **Other identifying details** (passport number, etc.).

#### False positive

Common false positives:

- Common name shared with designated person;
- Different DOB / nationality;
- Different supplementary details.

If facts clearly differ:

- **Document** the differentiation;
- **Clear** the match in the screening system;
- **Resume** normal customer activity.

#### True positive

If facts align with the designated person:

- **Confirm** the match;
- **Proceed to freeze**.

#### Uncertain

If partial match without clear differentiation:

- **Investigate** further (e.g. ask customer for verifying
  information without tipping off);
- **Escalate** to MLRO / senior management;
- **Hold** pending clarification.

### Step 3: Asset freeze

For a confirmed match:

- **Identify** all assets / accounts held in the customer's
  name;
- **Apply freeze** — no withdrawals, no transfers, no
  disbursements;
- **Continue holding** for the customer (you don't take title);
- **Document** the freeze with timestamp.

### Step 4: Notifications

Notify:

- **JFSC** through prescribed channel;
- **Financial Intelligence Unit (FIU)** — often parallel SAR;
- **OFSI** in some cases (UK-list designations);
- **Group entities** as appropriate.

The timing is typically **immediate** — within hours of
confirmed match.

### Step 5: Customer interaction

While the freeze is in place:

- **Don't disclose** the sanctions designation;
- **Standard non-disclosing responses** to customer queries;
- **Engage** counsel if customer escalates;
- **Maintain** AML / tipping-off discipline.

### Step 6: Licence applications

If the customer needs specific dealings:

- **Basic needs** (living expenses, in some categories);
- **Legal fees** (defending the designation);
- **Humanitarian** purposes;
- Apply through OFSI / appropriate authority.

The licence is **narrow** — only the authorised activity is
permitted.

### Step 7: Ongoing

After the initial response:

- **Monitor** for changes in designation status;
- **Update** the customer file as facts change;
- **Continue** AML / FIU cooperation;
- **Document** all subsequent activity.

If designation is **lifted**: assets typically unfreeze
automatically (confirm with regulators).

If designation **persists**: the freeze continues; the
relationship may need to wind down through licences.

### Documentation throughout

Maintain:

- **Initial match** record (date, screening system, list);
- **Verification analysis** with reasoning;
- **Freeze instruction** and execution evidence;
- **Notifications** to authorities;
- **Licence applications** if any;
- **Ongoing monitoring** records;
- **Resolution** if / when designation lifted.

### Common issues

#### Customer protests innocence

- **Don't engage** on the merits of the designation;
- **Refer** to legal counsel if needed;
- **Maintain** the freeze.

#### Family member of designated person

- **Family members** may not themselves be designated;
- But **funds traceable to designated person** may be subject
  to freeze;
- **Careful analysis** of the specific asset's origin needed.

#### Designated person seeks essential payments

- **Apply** for OFSI / Jersey licence for specific basic-needs
  payments;
- Don't make payments without licence even if compassionate.

### Group-entity implications

If your group has multiple Jersey entities:

- **Coordinate** the freeze across entities;
- **Same approach** firm-wide;
- **Joint notifications** where appropriate;
- **Avoid** the designated person moving between group entities
  to evade.

## What to do next

For an active match:

1. **Stop dealings**;
2. **Verify** the match;
3. **Freeze** if confirmed;
4. **Notify** authorities;
5. **Document** thoroughly;
6. **Apply** for licences if needed;
7. **Manage** ongoing per tipping-off rules.

## Pitfalls

- **Premature unfreezing** based on customer protest;
- **Inadequate verification** — false positives missed and
  customers wrongly frozen;
- **Documentation gaps**;
- **Group-entity inconsistency**.
