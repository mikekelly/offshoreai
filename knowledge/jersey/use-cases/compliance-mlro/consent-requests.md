# Consent Requests — When and How

## The question

You've identified a suspicious transaction that is **pending
completion**. The customer expects it to settle. How do you
request **FIU consent** to proceed?

## The short answer

When a SAR concerns a **pending transaction**:

- **File the SAR with consent flag** through the FIU portal;
- The **FIU has a statutory period** (typically 7 working days)
  to respond;
- **Don't proceed** with the transaction before consent or
  expiry of the period;
- If **FIU consents** or doesn't respond within the period:
  proceed;
- If **FIU refuses**: don't proceed; restraining order may
  follow.

The consent regime allows the relationship to continue without
tipping off the subject.

## The proper answer

### The legal mechanism

Under the Proceeds of Crime (Jersey) Law 1999:

- **Section 32** (or equivalent) — "appropriate consent" allows
  a person who has made an authorised disclosure to act
  without committing money-laundering offences;
- **FIU consent** is the practical implementation;
- **Statutory deadline** for FIU response.

### When consent is needed

- A **transaction is pending** that you've identified as
  suspicious;
- **Acting** on the transaction would risk a money-laundering
  offence;
- A **SAR is being filed** with the FIU;
- **Consent** is requested for the specific transaction to
  proceed.

### When consent isn't needed

- Historical transactions (already completed) — no consent
  needed but SAR still appropriate;
- Routine activities outside the suspicious pattern;
- Activities the firm can refuse without further explanation
  (e.g. exit the relationship cleanly).

### The consent process

1. **File SAR** through FIU portal, flagging consent request;
2. **Provide details** of pending transaction (amount, parties,
   deadline);
3. **FIU acknowledges** receipt;
4. **FIU has statutory period** to respond (~7 working days);
5. During the period: **do not proceed** with the
   transaction;
6. **FIU decision**:
   - **Consent granted**: proceed;
   - **Consent refused**: do not proceed; further action by
     authorities may follow;
   - **No response within period**: deemed consent — proceed.

### Managing the customer

While awaiting consent:

- **Don't tip off** that a SAR / consent request is in train;
- **Handle delay** with non-disclosing language;
- **Stand by** the operational stance (transaction delayed for
  compliance review).

If the customer presses:

- **Continue standard responses**;
- **Don't elaborate**;
- **Escalate** internally if pressure becomes problematic.

### If consent is refused

- **Don't proceed** with the specific transaction;
- The FIU may **continue investigating**;
- A **restraining order** from the Royal Court may follow,
  freezing assets;
- The firm may need to **engage Jersey counsel** for navigating
  the situation;
- **Continuing customer management** requires careful judgement.

### Multiple transactions

If suspicious activity is **ongoing** rather than a single
transaction:

- The initial SAR with consent request covers the **specific
  transaction**;
- **Continued suspicious transactions** may need further SARs /
  consent requests;
- The FIU may issue **broader directions** about the
  relationship.

### Exit considerations

After SAR / consent:

- **Continuing the relationship** is possible with appropriate
  oversight;
- **Exiting the relationship** may be appropriate;
- **Exit must be handled** without tipping off;
- Consider **FIU consent for the exit transaction itself** if
  it involves moving suspicious assets.

### Documentation

Maintain records of:

- **SAR filing** with consent flag;
- **FIU response** (consent / refusal / no response);
- **Transaction handling** during the consent period;
- **Subsequent activity** with the customer;
- **Exit (if any)** and its handling.

## What to do next

For a current consent situation:

1. **Identify** the pending transaction and its deadline;
2. **File SAR** with consent flag through portal;
3. **Hold** the transaction during the consent period;
4. **Monitor** for FIU response;
5. **Proceed or hold** based on response;
6. **Manage** customer relationship with tipping-off
   discipline.

For programme:

- **Train** staff on identifying consent-needed situations;
- **Maintain** clear internal-escalation channels;
- **Document** consent procedures.

## Pitfalls

- **Proceeding without consent**: serious offence risk;
- **Tipping off** during the consent wait;
- **Inadequate transaction details** in the consent request:
  may delay FIU decision;
- **Forgetting** that no-response = deemed consent: don't
  delay unnecessarily after the statutory period.
