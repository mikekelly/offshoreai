# When is Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD) Required?

## The question

When does CDD need to be upgraded to **EDD** under Jersey AML
rules?

## The short answer

EDD is required when a relationship presents **higher money-
laundering or terrorist-financing risk**. The Money Laundering
(Jersey) Order 2008 and the JFSC AML/CFT Handbook prescribe EDD
for:

- **Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs)** and their family /
  close associates;
- **Customers / transactions** involving **high-risk
  jurisdictions** (FATF grey/black list, Jersey-specified
  lists);
- **Complex ownership structures** (multiple layers, unclear
  beneficial owners);
- **Unusual transactional patterns** inconsistent with normal
  customer profile;
- **Cash-intensive businesses** or business categories with
  elevated AML risk;
- **High-risk activities** (VASPs, certain gaming, certain
  precious metals);
- **Risk-based determinations** by the regulated entity for
  specific relationships.

EDD is a **graduated** response — different triggers may warrant
different intensities.

## The proper answer

### What EDD adds

Beyond standard CDD, EDD typically requires:

- **Senior-management approval** of the relationship;
- **Enhanced source-of-wealth verification** with documentary
  evidence;
- **More frequent** periodic review;
- **Enhanced ongoing monitoring** with lower transaction alert
  thresholds;
- **Adverse-media** ongoing screening;
- **Specific risk-mitigation measures** depending on the
  trigger.

### Triggers in detail

#### PEPs (politically exposed persons)

- **Foreign PEPs**: senior figures in foreign governments and
  state entities;
- **Domestic PEPs**: equivalent Jersey figures (different
  threshold sometimes);
- **International organisation PEPs**: senior figures at UN,
  WTO, etc.;
- **Family members and close associates** also captured;
- **EDD** required throughout relationship.

#### High-risk jurisdictions

- **FATF grey/black list** countries;
- **Jersey-prescribed** high-risk lists (varies over time);
- **Sanctions-impacted** jurisdictions;
- **Corruption-perceived** jurisdictions (Transparency International
  CPI lowest quintile, for example).

#### Complex ownership

- Multiple corporate layers between customer and beneficial
  owner;
- **Nominees** in the structure;
- **Bearer shares** (where they still exist in any
  jurisdiction);
- **Trust / foundation** layers that obscure economic
  ownership.

#### Unusual transactional patterns

- **Pass-through** transactions with no commercial purpose;
- **Round-number** transactions designed to avoid thresholds;
- **Inconsistent** with stated business purpose;
- **Unusual** geographic flows.

#### Cash-intensive sectors

- **Gaming and casinos**;
- **Money service businesses**;
- **Precious metals / antiques** dealing;
- **Cash-heavy retail** chains;
- **Hospitality** in some markets.

#### Specific high-risk activities

- **Virtual asset service providers (VASPs)**;
- **Real estate** in certain jurisdictions;
- **Charity** sectors in conflict zones;
- **Politically-aligned** business activities.

### Risk-based judgement

The Money Laundering Order 2008 calls for a **risk-based
approach**:

- The regulated entity must conduct a **business-wide risk
  assessment (BWRA)**;
- Each customer relationship is assessed against the BWRA;
- The **risk classification** drives CDD intensity;
- **High-risk relationships** trigger EDD by definition;
- The entity has **discretion** to apply EDD to medium-risk
  relationships where specific factors warrant.

### EDD content — practical detail

For an EDD case:

#### Enhanced source-of-wealth verification

- **Detailed narrative** of wealth generation;
- **Documentary evidence**: financial statements, tax returns,
  sale agreements, inheritance documents;
- **Tax-compliance evidence** in home jurisdiction;
- **Independent professional opinions** in complex cases.

#### Senior-management approval

- The TCB / regulated entity's **board or designated senior
  committee** approves;
- **Reasoned decision** documented;
- **Risk-acceptance criteria** applied;
- **Ongoing oversight** of EDD population.

#### Enhanced monitoring

- **Lower transaction alert** thresholds;
- **More frequent** review of activity;
- **Adverse-media** ongoing screening;
- **Trigger reviews** on specific events (political change,
  litigation, regulatory action).

#### Periodic review

- **Annual** (vs typical 3-yearly for standard);
- **Comprehensive** rather than refresh-only;
- **Documented** with current risk assessment.

## What to do next

For onboarding:

1. **Apply BWRA-informed risk assessment**;
2. **Identify EDD triggers**;
3. **Conduct EDD** if triggered;
4. **Document** EDD basis and steps;
5. **Obtain** senior-management approval;
6. **Set up** enhanced ongoing monitoring.

For programme:

- **Review** BWRA annually;
- **Update** EDD triggers as risk landscape evolves;
- **Train** staff on EDD application;
- **Audit** EDD compliance periodically.

## Pitfalls

- **Mechanical EDD application**: should be risk-driven, not
  rote;
- **Senior-management rubber-stamp**: must be substantive
  approval;
- **Failure to document**: undermines the EDD defence;
- **Setting EDD too narrow**: missing risk factors leaves
  exposure.
