# Customer Due Diligence — Standard, Simplified, Enhanced

## The question

You are onboarding a new customer at your JFSC-regulated
business. What CDD do you actually need to do?

## The short answer

CDD level is **risk-based** under the Money Laundering (Jersey)
Order 2008:

- **Simplified due diligence (SDD)** for **lower-risk**
  customers (listed companies, regulated FIs from equivalent
  jurisdictions);
- **Standard CDD** for **most** customers — identification,
  verification, beneficial-owner identification, source of
  funds for transactions;
- **Enhanced due diligence (EDD)** for **higher-risk** customers
  — PEPs, high-risk jurisdictions, complex structures,
  unusual patterns.

The **risk classification** drives the depth.

## The proper answer

### Risk assessment first

Before doing CDD on a specific customer, your business should
have a **business-wide risk assessment (BWRA)** identifying:

- **Customer types** likely to walk in;
- **Product / service** AML risks;
- **Jurisdictional** risks of customer base;
- **Delivery channel** risks (in-person vs remote).

For each prospective customer, assess against the BWRA to
determine **risk classification** (low / medium / high).

### Standard CDD — what to gather

For an **individual customer**:

- **Full legal name**;
- **Date and place of birth**;
- **Residential address**;
- **Nationality**;
- **Identification**: government photo ID (passport, national
  ID) — verified from original or certified copy;
- **Address verification**: utility bill, bank statement,
  government correspondence — recent;
- **Source of funds** for the relationship and significant
  transactions;
- **Source of wealth** for higher-relationship-value
  individuals;
- **Purpose and intended nature** of the relationship.

For an **entity customer**:

- **Legal form** and jurisdiction of incorporation;
- **Constitutional documents**;
- **Directors / officers** (and CDD on senior management);
- **Beneficial owners** (typically natural persons holding 25%+
  ownership or control) — CDD on each as an individual;
- **Authorised representatives** dealing with you;
- **Source of funds** for transactions;
- **Purpose and intended nature** of the relationship.

For a **trust customer**:

- **Trust deed** or summary letter of wishes equivalent;
- **Settlor** (and historical settlors);
- **Trustee** identity (typically a regulated trust company);
- **Protector** if any;
- **Named or principal beneficiaries**;
- **Class of beneficiaries** (where discretionary);
- **Underlying assets**;
- **Source of trust property**.

For a **foundation customer**:

- **Foundation charter** (public document);
- **Regulations** (private — request copy);
- **Council members** identity and CDD;
- **Guardian** identity;
- **Founder** identity and CDD;
- **Beneficiaries or objects** of the foundation;
- **Underlying assets**;
- **Source of foundation property**.

### Simplified due diligence (SDD)

SDD applies to prescribed **lower-risk** categories:

- **Listed companies** on recognised stock exchanges;
- **Regulated FIs** in equivalent jurisdictions;
- **Government bodies**;
- **Certain pension funds**;
- Other prescribed categories.

SDD typically allows:

- **Reduced identity-verification** evidence;
- **Reliance** on the regulated status of the counterparty;
- **Streamlined** ongoing monitoring.

### Enhanced due diligence (EDD)

EDD applies to **higher-risk** categories:

- **Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs)** and their family /
  close associates;
- **Customers from high-risk jurisdictions** (FATF grey/black
  list, Jersey-specified high-risk lists);
- **Complex ownership structures** (multiple layers, nominees);
- **Unusual transaction patterns**;
- **Cash-intensive businesses**;
- **Specific high-risk activities** (gaming, virtual assets,
  precious metals).

EDD typically requires:

- **Senior-management approval** of the relationship;
- **Source-of-wealth verification** (not just source of funds);
- **Enhanced ongoing monitoring**;
- **More frequent periodic review**;
- **Additional documentation** of the customer's affairs.

## What to do next

1. **Classify** the prospective customer (low / medium / high
   risk).
2. **Gather** the appropriate level of CDD documentation.
3. **Verify** identification from reliable sources.
4. **Document** the CDD file with all evidence and conclusions.
5. **Approve** the relationship at the appropriate seniority
   level (senior management for EDD).
6. **Set up** ongoing monitoring proportionate to risk.
7. **Schedule** periodic CDD refresh.

## Pitfalls

- **Standard CDD applied to PEPs**: insufficient — EDD required.
- **Reliance on weak ID**: photocopies of expired passports
  don't satisfy verification.
- **Missing beneficial-owner CDD**: entity CDD without
  beneficial-owner CDD on natural-person controllers is
  incomplete.
- **No source-of-wealth analysis** for high-relationship-value
  individuals.
- **Stale CDD**: not refreshed during the relationship.
