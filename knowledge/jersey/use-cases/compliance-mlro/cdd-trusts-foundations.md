# CDD on Trusts and Foundations as Customers

## The question

A **trust or foundation** is your customer (e.g. opening a bank
account, subscribing to a fund). What CDD applies?

## The short answer

For a **trust customer**:

- Identify and document **settlor, trustees, protector,
  beneficiaries** (or principal members of class);
- Verify the **trust deed**;
- Source of trust property;
- Purpose of the trust.

For a **foundation customer**:

- Identify **founder, council members, guardian, beneficiaries
  / objects**;
- Verify the **charter and regulations** (charter public, get
  regulations);
- Source of foundation property;
- Purpose of the foundation.

For both, classify under **CRS / FATCA** (typically Passive
NFFE or FFI depending on facts).

## The proper answer

### Trust as customer

#### Documentation

- **Trust deed** (verified copy);
- **Settlor's identity** documents;
- **Trustees' identity** documents (typically a TCB — verify its
  regulatory standing);
- **Protector identity** (if any);
- **Beneficiary identity** for named beneficiaries;
- **Class identification** for discretionary trusts;
- **Source of trust property**.

#### Risk assessment

- **Settled** trusts with clear purposes are typically lower-
  risk;
- **PEP settlor / beneficiary** triggers EDD;
- **Complex multi-layer** structures merit closer scrutiny;
- **Source-of-wealth** narrative must support the settled
  amount.

#### Ongoing

- **Refresh** at intervals;
- **Update** on settlement of additional property or change of
  parties;
- **Monitor** transactions against the trust's stated purpose.

### Foundation as customer

#### Documentation

- **Charter** (public, easy to obtain);
- **Regulations** (private — request);
- **Founder identity**;
- **Council member identities**;
- **Guardian identity**;
- **Beneficiary / object identification**;
- **Source of foundation property**.

#### Risk assessment

Similar to trusts:

- **PEP founder / council member / beneficiary** triggers EDD;
- **Pure-purpose foundations** (orphan SPV) without natural-
  person beneficiaries: identify the **purpose** and the
  **controlling parties** of the structure;
- **Civil-law-jurisdiction founders** — common for foundations,
  not problematic per se.

### Trustee / council-member regulatory standing

Where the trustee is a **JFSC-regulated TCB**:

- **Verify** TCB registration;
- **Reduce reliance** on going behind the trustee for some
  CDD (TCB has its own CDD);
- **Note** that BO transparency may require going behind.

Where the trustee is an **individual** or **family PTC**:

- More **direct CDD** on the trustee themselves;
- More **scrutiny** of trustee qualifications.

### CRS / FATCA classification

A trust is typically:

- **FI (Investment Entity)** if professionally managed with
  financial assets;
- **Passive NFFE** otherwise (real-estate-only trusts, etc.).

A foundation is typically:

- **FI (Investment Entity)** if professionally managed with
  financial assets;
- **Passive NFFE** otherwise.

Classification drives **reporting** at the customer level.

### Controlling persons

For Passive-NFFE trusts / foundations, the customer entity does
**not** report — but the regulated entity holding the account
must identify **controlling persons**:

- For trusts: settlor, trustees, protector, named beneficiaries,
  principal class members;
- For foundations: founder, council members, guardian,
  beneficiaries;
- Apply CRS / FATCA reporting to controlling persons who are
  non-Jersey-resident / US-person.

### Common scenarios

#### Family trust opens account

- Standard CDD on settlor / beneficiaries / trustee;
- Documentation of source of family wealth;
- Typically straightforward if regulated TCB trustee.

#### Charity trust account

- Identification of charity registration and purposes;
- Lower-risk profile if Jersey-registered charity with normal
  activities;
- EDD if charity operates in high-risk jurisdictions.

#### Orphan-SPV foundation

- Identification of purposes (no beneficiaries);
- CDD on council members and counterparties;
- Often **lower-risk** despite no beneficiaries — depends on
  structured-finance counterparty quality.

#### Foreign-trust account opening

- Verify the trust's existence and validity under its proper
  law;
- **Foreign trustee** CDD — additional verification of foreign
  regulatory standing;
- **Source of wealth** for non-Jersey-domiciled settlor.

## What to do next

1. **Obtain** the trust deed / foundation documents;
2. **Identify** all parties (settlor, trustee, beneficiaries,
   etc.);
3. **Verify** identification of each;
4. **Assess** risk including source-of-wealth;
5. **Classify** under CRS / FATCA;
6. **Document** the CDD file;
7. **Set up** ongoing monitoring.

## Pitfalls

- **Trust-deed-only CDD**: missing the people behind the
  documents;
- **Class-beneficiary gaps**: identifying just settlor's
  spouse / children misses extended discretionary class;
- **Foundation regulation neglect**: must obtain the private
  regulations, not just the charter;
- **Stale party identification**: settlors / council members /
  trustees change.
