# Sale of the Jersey TopCo — Tax and Procedural Mechanics

## The question

A buyer is interested in **acquiring your Jersey TopCo** (or
the business it holds). What are the **tax and procedural
mechanics** at Jersey level?

## The short answer

For a typical share-sale of a Jersey company:

- **No Jersey CGT** on the gain (Jersey has none);
- **No Jersey stamp duty** on share transfer (subject to LTT
  for property-share property);
- **Foreign-tax** at shareholder level (UK CGT, US capital gains,
  etc.) is the active consideration;
- **Share-transfer mechanics** under Article 42 of the Companies
  Law — typically straightforward.

Alternative structures:

- **Asset sale** (operating subsidiary sold rather than holding
  shares);
- **Merger** under Part 18B for combination with acquirer;
- **Squeeze-out** under Part 18 for compulsory acquisition.

## The proper answer

### Share-sale (most common)

#### Jersey-level mechanics

- **Share-transfer** under Article 42;
- **No Jersey CGT** on capital gain;
- **No Jersey stamp duty** on share transfers (Jersey doesn't
  have stamp duty on shares, unlike UK SDRT);
- **Land Transactions Tax (LTT)** if property-share property
  involved.

#### Procedural

- **Sale and Purchase Agreement (SPA)** — typically governed
  by Jersey or English law;
- **Share transfer form** for the actual share transfer;
- **Stock transfer** in registers;
- **Closing** with payment in escrow / completion mechanics;
- **Post-closing** notifications.

#### Documents

- **SPA**;
- **Warranty deeds**;
- **Disclosure letter**;
- **Pre-closing reorganisation documents** if needed;
- **Power of attorney** if foreign-resident execution;
- **Closing checklist**.

### Asset sale

If the buyer wants the **business but not the entity**:

- **Operating subsidiary** sells assets (typically Jersey
  operating subsidiary);
- **Holding company** retains corporate shell;
- **Tax implications** vary — operating-jurisdiction tax
  generally;
- **More complex** legal mechanics (asset by asset).

### Merger

For combination with the acquirer:

- **Statutory merger** under Part 18B;
- **Universal succession** of assets / liabilities;
- **Single surviving entity** post-merger;
- **Suitable** for friendly transactions integrating into buyer
  group.

### Squeeze-out (Part 18)

If buying out minority post-tender:

- **Tender offer** for shares (Article 116);
- **90% acceptance** triggers squeeze-out (Article 117);
- **Compulsory acquisition** of remaining minority on offer
  terms;
- **Standard public-company** acquisition mechanism.

### Shareholder-level tax

Where the founder owns shares directly:

#### UK shareholder

- **UK CGT** on disposal gain;
- **Entrepreneurs' relief** (now Business Asset Disposal
  Relief — BADR) if qualifying — 10% rate up to lifetime
  £1m;
- **Standard CGT** otherwise (24% top rate as at writing);
- **Tax-residence** of seller drives outcome.

#### US shareholder

- **Capital gains** at US rates;
- **PFIC** considerations if Jersey company classified as
  PFIC;
- **Section 1248** for CFC-classified holdings.

#### Other shareholders

- **Home-jurisdiction** capital gains rules.

### Where shares held through trust / company

Where founder holds through:

- **Jersey trust**: trust receives proceeds; distribution rules
  apply to beneficiaries; Article 9 firewall protects;
- **Personal holding company**: company-level tax (depending
  on jurisdiction); subsequent distribution rules apply;
- **Family foundation**: foundation receives; council manages
  proceeds.

### Pre-sale planning

#### Wealth shifting

- **Pre-sale** transfers can optimise tax;
- **Trust settlements** can route proceeds tax-efficiently;
- **Foreign-jurisdiction-specific** planning critical.

#### Reorganisation

- **Pre-sale** group simplification;
- **Eliminate** unwanted subsidiaries;
- **Realise** wanted asset positions.

### Closing mechanics

#### Conditions precedent

- **Regulatory approvals** (foreign-jurisdiction antitrust, etc.);
- **No material adverse change**;
- **Specific conditions** in SPA.

#### Closing day

- **Document exchange**;
- **Payment** of consideration;
- **Share transfer registered**;
- **Director / officer changes**;
- **Bank-mandate transitions**;
- **Counterparty notifications**.

#### Post-closing

- **Earn-out monitoring** if applicable;
- **Warranty claims** during warranty period;
- **Tax-filing** for the period of ownership;
- **Continued obligations** under SPA.

### Warranty regime

Typical SPA warranties:

- **Title to shares** by seller;
- **Authority** to sell;
- **No undisclosed liabilities** in target;
- **Tax-position** representations;
- **Material-contract** representations;
- **Compliance** representations.

Limitation:

- **Cap** on warranty claims (often a percentage of
  consideration);
- **De minimis** thresholds for claims;
- **Time limits** (typically 18 months general; longer for tax
  warranties).

### Common scenarios

#### Trade-sale to strategic acquirer

- **Friendly negotiated** sale;
- **Earn-out** sometimes;
- **Founder transition** plan;
- **Standard SPA** mechanics.

#### PE buy-out

- **Management buy-out** with PE backing;
- **Founder reinvestment** sometimes;
- **Continuing-role** arrangements;
- **Acquisition-vehicle** structures.

#### IPO with secondary sell-down

- **Pre-IPO restructuring**;
- **Listing**;
- **Lock-up** then phased sell-down;
- **Public-market** dynamics.

#### Family succession sale

- **Sale to family members** at market or favourable terms;
- **Tax-planning** through trusts / foundations;
- **Generational handover** completion.

## What to do next

For a sale process:

1. **Engage** financial adviser / banker if appropriate;
2. **Engage** Jersey counsel + jurisdictional counsel;
3. **Prepare** for due diligence;
4. **Negotiate** SPA terms;
5. **Plan** post-sale founder position (proceeds management);
6. **Execute** the closing.

## Pitfalls

- **Tax-planning gaps** at shareholder level;
- **Warranty negotiations** under-played;
- **Closing-day execution** errors;
- **Post-sale obligations** under-prepared.
