# Where to Hold IP — Jersey or Other Structure?

## The question

Your business has valuable **intellectual property** (patents,
trademarks, copyrights, software, brand). Should the IP be
held in a **Jersey company** or elsewhere?

## The short answer

Jersey IP-holding works when:

- The **IP-substance** test under Substance Law 2019 can be met
  (real IP activities in Jersey);
- The **tax characterisation** in operating jurisdictions is
  accepted;
- The **licensing flows** to operating entities are sustainable;
- **Pillar Two** doesn't override the structure (if group is
  large).

But **Jersey isn't the obvious IP-jurisdiction** for many
groups:

- **Ireland**, **Luxembourg**, **Netherlands** have specific
  IP-box regimes with **treaty network** Jersey doesn't have;
- **Modern OECD rules** (BEPS Action 5 substance) tighten
  IP-shifting generally.

Model carefully — Jersey IP-holding is **not a default tax
strategy**.

## The proper answer

### The structural concept

A typical IP-holding structure:

```
[Jersey TopCo / IP Co]
    |
    licenses IP to
    |
[Operating Cos in various jurisdictions]
    |
    pays licence fees up to Jersey IP Co
```

Jersey IP Co receives **royalties** taxed at **0%** under
zero-ten (for most companies). Operating cos **deduct**
royalties at their statutory rates, reducing their tax burden.

### Why Jersey IP-holding is hard now

#### Substance requirements

Under the **Substance Law 2019**:

- **IP business** is a relevant activity;
- **Full substance** required (not the reduced equity-holding
  test);
- **Decision-making in Jersey** about the IP;
- **CIGAs** (core income-generating activities) in Jersey:
  R&D, licensing decisions, IP enforcement decisions;
- **Adequate employees and premises** in Jersey.

Most IP held offshore historically **doesn't meet** the
substance test — the IP-holding company has no real Jersey
activity beyond receiving royalties.

#### OECD BEPS Action 5

The **modified nexus approach** under OECD BEPS:

- Tax benefits for IP **must be linked to substantial activity**
  generating that IP;
- **Pure royalty-receiving** companies without R&D activity
  don't get IP-regime benefits in most jurisdictions;
- **Jersey lacks an IP-box regime** anyway.

#### Pillar Two

For large groups (€750m+):

- **15% minimum** applies regardless of zero-ten;
- **IP-shifting benefits** within scope reduced or eliminated;
- **Many traditional structures** no longer effective.

### When Jersey IP-holding still works

- **Genuine IP development** in Jersey (rare);
- **Holding-of-acquired IP** with adequate substance maintained;
- **Group simplification** alongside other Jersey-resident
  activities;
- **Pre-IPO restructuring** where IP holding facilitates
  transaction;
- **Operating-business** with IP as integral.

### Alternative IP-holding jurisdictions

#### Ireland

- **IP-box / R&D credit** regime;
- **12.5% rate** (rising to 15% for Pillar Two groups);
- **Extensive treaty network**;
- **Strong R&D ecosystem**.

#### Luxembourg

- **IP-box** regime;
- **Treaty network** for royalty flows;
- **EU-jurisdiction** advantages.

#### Netherlands

- **Innovation Box** regime;
- **Treaty network**;
- **Common holding-IP jurisdiction**.

#### Switzerland

- **Patent Box** under federal rules;
- **Substance** infrastructure.

These typically **out-compete Jersey** for active IP-holding.

### Substance compliance

If Jersey IP-holding is chosen, ensure:

- **Real Jersey activity** around the IP (decisions, oversight,
  some development);
- **Adequate Jersey employees** for the activity;
- **Adequate Jersey premises**;
- **Documented CIGAs** (core income-generating activities) in
  Jersey;
- **Annual reporting** of substance.

### Practical considerations

For most modern groups:

- **IP-holding** in Jersey is **not the default**;
- **Onshore IP-jurisdictions** (Ireland, Luxembourg) more
  efficient;
- **Operating-jurisdiction IP** (e.g. UK IP for UK-operating
  group) often simpler;
- **Substance and Pillar Two** considerations dominate.

If Jersey is chosen, it's typically because:

- Jersey TopCo already exists;
- IP is acquired alongside other Jersey-housed assets;
- Group has Jersey-based decision-makers;
- Pre-IPO simplification.

## What to do next

For an IP-holding decision:

1. **Model** alternative jurisdictions;
2. **Assess** substance feasibility in each;
3. **Apply** Pillar Two analysis;
4. **Choose** based on tax + substance + operational factors;
5. **Engage** specialist tax counsel.

## Pitfalls

- **Default to Jersey**: Jersey isn't the obvious IP-holding
  jurisdiction;
- **Substance under-attention**: full substance required for
  IP business;
- **Pillar Two surprise** for large groups;
- **OECD-rule misalignment**: legacy IP-shifting structures
  often no longer compliant.
