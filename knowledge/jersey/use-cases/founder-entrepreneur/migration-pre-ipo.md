# Migration of Corporate Seat for Pre-IPO Purposes

## The question

Your operating business is in a non-Jersey TopCo. You want to
**migrate to a Jersey TopCo before IPO**. How?

## The short answer

Migrate via **continuance** under Part 18C of the Companies
(Jersey) Law 1991 (or equivalent in current jurisdiction):

- **Foreign jurisdiction permitting outbound continuance** is
  prerequisite;
- **Jersey JFSC consent** required for inbound continuance;
- **Continuity of legal identity** preserved — same entity,
  new jurisdiction;
- **Shareholders, contracts, assets** continue;
- **Timeline**: typically 3–6 months.

Often preferable to **liquidation and re-incorporation** which
loses continuity.

## The proper answer

### Continuance vs merger vs new-company

#### Continuance (Part 18C inbound)

- **Same entity** persists across jurisdictions;
- **No disruption** to contracts / shareholders / assets;
- **Two-stage approval** (foreign exit + Jersey entry);
- **3–6 month** typical timeline.

#### Merger (Part 18B inbound)

- **Two companies merge** into Jersey company;
- **Universal succession** of assets / liabilities;
- **Pre-IPO simplification** combining group structure;
- **More complex** for cross-border.

#### New Jersey TopCo above existing structure

- **Newco Jersey company** acquires existing TopCo or its
  subsidiaries;
- **Tax-event** at shareholder level potentially;
- **Section 110** / similar UK rollover relief may apply;
- **Common approach** when full continuance not feasible.

### Continuance prerequisites

#### Foreign jurisdiction

- **Permits outbound continuance** (US Delaware, BVI, Cayman,
  some EU jurisdictions do; some don't);
- **Specific procedural requirements**;
- **No insolvency / regulatory** impediments.

#### Jersey

- **JFSC consent** under Article 127K-N;
- **Articles of continuance** prepared;
- **Solvency statement** by directors;
- **Foreign-law opinion** on the home-jurisdiction exit.

### Timeline and process

#### Stage 1: Foreign exit

1. **Board approval** in foreign jurisdiction;
2. **Shareholder approval** if required;
3. **Foreign-jurisdiction filings** for outbound continuance;
4. **Foreign-tax** filings completed;
5. **Foreign regulatory** notifications.

#### Stage 2: Jersey entry

1. **JFSC application** under Article 127K;
2. **Articles of continuance** filed;
3. **Solvency statement**;
4. **JFSC determination** (Article 127N);
5. **Certificate of continuance** (Article 127O);
6. **Effective date** — body becomes Jersey company (Article
   127P).

### Tax considerations

#### At foreign-jurisdiction level

- **Exit tax** may apply (some jurisdictions);
- **Filing obligations** through exit;
- **Continuation** of any unrelated foreign-tax matters.

#### At shareholder level

- **No share-disposal event** typically (continuity of entity);
- **Tax-free** for shareholders in most jurisdictions if
  continuance recognised;
- **Confirm** with shareholder-jurisdiction advisers.

#### Going forward

- **Jersey tax regime** applies from effective date;
- **Zero-ten** for most TopCos;
- **Substance** obligations begin in Jersey.

### Shareholder considerations

- **Shareholder approval** may be required (depending on
  foreign-jurisdiction company-law);
- **Class meetings** if affected;
- **Disclosure** to shareholders about consequences;
- **Tax characterisation** in shareholder jurisdictions.

### Listing implications

For pre-IPO continuance:

- **Listing-jurisdiction acceptance** of Jersey companies
  (well-established for LSE / AIM);
- **Listing-rule alignment** post-continuance;
- **Track-record** continuity for listing eligibility.

### Common scenarios

#### BVI / Cayman TopCo migrating to Jersey

- **BVI / Cayman** permit outbound continuance;
- **Jersey** welcomes inbound;
- **Common pre-IPO** approach;
- **2–4 month** timeline.

#### US Delaware TopCo migrating to Jersey

- **Delaware** permits some forms of continuance;
- **Or** more typically: Newco Jersey acquires Delaware TopCo
  via share-for-share;
- **Tax** considerations significant for US shareholders.

#### UK TopCo migrating to Jersey

- **UK doesn't permit** outbound continuance to Jersey directly;
- **Alternative**: Newco Jersey TopCo above UK company;
- **UK** corporation tax considerations;
- **Common** UK-Newco-Jersey approach.

### When migration doesn't work

- **Foreign jurisdiction** doesn't permit outbound continuance;
- **Insolvency / regulatory** impediments;
- **Shareholder opposition** in foreign jurisdiction;
- **Tax cost** too high.

In such cases, **alternative structures** (Newco Jersey TopCo
above existing) typically used.

## What to do next

For a pre-IPO migration:

1. **Confirm** feasibility from foreign-jurisdiction side;
2. **Engage** Jersey counsel + foreign counsel;
3. **Plan** timeline allowing 3–6 months;
4. **Obtain** shareholder approvals;
5. **Apply** for JFSC consent;
6. **Execute** the continuance;
7. **Proceed** with IPO process.

## Pitfalls

- **Foreign-jurisdiction non-permission**: alternative needed;
- **Tax-event surprise** for shareholders;
- **Timeline crunch** vs IPO date;
- **Listing-rule** considerations during transition.
