# Why Jersey for a TopCo?

## The question

Your operating business has reached a scale where group
restructuring makes sense. Should the **TopCo** (the ultimate
holding entity) be a **Jersey company**?

## The short answer

Jersey is a strong TopCo choice when **all** of the following
apply:

- The group's **operating businesses are not in Jersey** (so
  Jersey-corporate tax on operating profits isn't the issue);
- A **0% corporate-tax rate** at the TopCo level is desired;
- A **listing or pre-IPO** is contemplated, and Jersey is
  acceptable to your target listing venue;
- **No Jersey CGT or IHT** is a meaningful factor for
  shareholders;
- **English-style corporate-law familiarity** is helpful
  (Companies Law 1991 has English-influenced concepts).

Jersey is a **poor** choice when:

- The operating business itself is in Jersey (other rates apply);
- The group's tax planning relies on **double-tax-treaty
  benefits** Jersey doesn't offer;
- The investor base **prefers a different jurisdiction** for
  governance reasons.

## The proper answer

### The zero-ten advantage

Under [zero-ten](../../tax/zero-ten.md):

- Most Jersey companies pay **0% corporate tax**;
- **No withholding** on dividends from a 0%-rate Jersey company;
- **No Jersey CGT** on capital gains at the company or
  shareholder level;
- **No Jersey IHT** on transfer of shares.

For a TopCo holding shares in foreign operating subsidiaries:

- Foreign **operating profits** are taxed where earned (foreign
  jurisdictions);
- **Dividends up** to the Jersey TopCo are typically tax-free at
  the Jersey level;
- **Capital gains** on sale of subsidiaries are not Jersey-taxed.

### Pillar Two consideration

If the group is **large** (annual consolidated revenue **EUR
750m+**), Pillar Two applies:

- A **15% minimum effective rate** on Jersey-booked income;
- Jersey collects this via its **MCIT** (Multinational
  Corporate Income Tax) from 2025;
- The advantage of 0% Jersey rate **disappears for large
  groups** — the rate becomes 15% effectively.

For groups **below** the Pillar Two threshold, the 0% Jersey
rate is unaffected.

### Pre-IPO / listing factors

Jersey is **well-accepted** by:

- **LSE Main Market** and **AIM** — many UK-listed companies
  have Jersey TopCos;
- **Euronext** and other European exchanges;
- **NASDAQ** and **NYSE** to some extent (more diligence needed
  for US listings);
- **TISE** (The International Stock Exchange — based in
  Channel Islands).

A pre-IPO Jersey TopCo is **familiar and acceptable** to most
listing venues, allowing flexibility on listing choice closer
to the time.

### Corporate-law advantages

The **Companies (Jersey) Law 1991** offers:

- **No-par-value share capital** (simpler than UK par-value);
- **Solvency-statement-based distributions** (no UK profits-
  available-for-distribution test);
- **Reduction of capital** without court approval where
  solvency statement made;
- **Statutory merger regime** (Part 18B) — cleaner than schemes
  for many transactions;
- **Continuance / migration** (Part 18C) — flexibility to move
  in or out of Jersey as the business evolves.

### Other practical advantages

- **English-language** legal and business environment;
- **Common-law-influenced** legal system familiar to UK / US
  lawyers;
- **Established service-provider infrastructure**;
- **Time zone** advantage (UK time);
- **Regulatory reputation** (white-list with EU, OECD).

### Where Jersey doesn't win

- **No DTA network advantage** — Jersey's DTAs are limited; if
  the group needs treaty benefits (e.g. for cross-border
  dividend / interest flows), other jurisdictions (Luxembourg,
  Ireland, Netherlands) may have better networks;
- **Local-employment costs**: Jersey wages and office costs are
  high relative to some alternatives;
- **Substance requirements** under the 2019 substance Law need
  to be met — the TopCo cannot be purely paper.

## What to do next

1. **Model the tax position** of the proposed Jersey TopCo
   structure against alternatives;
2. **Confirm Pillar Two exposure** if the group is large or
   growing;
3. **Engage Jersey corporate counsel** for incorporation;
4. **Set up the TopCo** with adequate Jersey substance (board,
   directors, registered office, premises);
5. **Coordinate** with home-jurisdiction tax advisers on
   transfer of existing TopCo position to Jersey (potentially
   via continuance from existing jurisdiction).

## Pitfalls

- **Insufficient substance**: a paper Jersey TopCo with no real
  Jersey activity will be challenged under economic-substance
  rules.
- **Underestimating Pillar Two**: a fast-growing group can hit
  the EUR 750m threshold sooner than expected, materially
  changing the tax position.
- **DTA-dependent flows**: any cross-border flow that relies on
  a DTA needs to be checked against Jersey's network — TIEA-
  only relationships don't get withholding reductions.
- **Migration into Jersey** (continuance) has substantive
  documentation and timeline requirements.
