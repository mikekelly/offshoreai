# Employment of the Founder Team in Jersey

## The question

You're a founder setting up a **Jersey topco** with a small
team of founders, executives, and early hires. What Jersey
employment considerations apply?

## The short answer

- **Employment Law 2003** applies once you have employees
  (not before incorporation);
- **CHWL status** must be verified for each hire;
- **Specific** founder vs employee distinction matters
  (directors vs employees);
- **Share schemes / options** subject to Jersey rules;
- **Restrictive covenants** post-termination need careful
  drafting;
- **Specific** considerations for international founders
  relocating to Jersey.

## The proper answer

### Founders as directors vs employees

A common founder configuration:

- **Founders are directors** of the topco;
- **Some founders are also employees** with employment
  contracts;
- **Director duties** (CJL 1991 Article 74) apply
  separately;
- **Employment Law** applies to employed founders.

### Hiring early-stage employees

For the first hires:

#### Status verification

- **CHWL status** of each candidate;
- **Registered status** for newcomers typically requires
  sponsorship;
- **Specific** essential-worker route for skilled hires not
  available locally.

#### Employment contracts

- **EL 2003 Article 3** — written particulars within 4 weeks;
- **Comprehensive** drafting recommended (beyond statutory
  minimum);
- **Confidentiality** undertakings (especially for IP-heavy
  startups);
- **Assignment of IP** (express provisions);
- **Restrictive covenants** post-termination;
- **Equity / option** terms by reference to scheme documents.

#### Share schemes / options

- **Jersey** permits broad share-scheme flexibility;
- **EMI** (UK Enterprise Management Incentives) not directly
  applicable to Jersey but Jersey can replicate similar terms;
- **Specific** Jersey tax treatment for share scheme
  participants;
- **Specific** considerations for non-resident participants.

#### IP assignment

- **Express** IP assignment from employees to company;
- **Specific** Jersey rules on employee inventions;
- **International** IP considerations for cross-border
  work;
- **Specific** confidentiality post-termination.

### Cross-border founder considerations

For founders **relocating to Jersey** from abroad:

- **CHWL** Licensed status (typically via HVR if wealth
  threshold met);
- **Specific** tax considerations on relocation;
- **Specific** employment contract terms for the Jersey
  topco employment;
- **Specific** non-Jersey employment continuing (if
  applicable).

For founders **remaining abroad** with Jersey topco:

- **Director role** without Jersey employment typically;
- **Specific** substance considerations (Jersey board
  meetings, decision-making);
- **Specific** tax treatment of director fees and other
  remuneration.

### Substance considerations

For Jersey topco employing founders / staff:

- **Substance Law 2019** considerations for in-scope
  activities;
- **Jersey CIGAs** (core income-generating activities) by
  Jersey-located employees;
- **Specific** documentation of Jersey activity;
- **Subject to** Substance Law in-scope categories.

See [`substance-for-topco.md`](./substance-for-topco.md).

### Discrimination considerations

The Discrimination Law 2013 applies from the recruitment
stage:

- **Job ads** must not discriminate;
- **Interview questions** must not discriminate;
- **Selection** on non-discriminatory grounds;
- **Subsequent terms** equal across staff;
- **Specific** considerations for international hiring
  (national origin / race risk).

### Data protection

The DPL 2018 applies to employee data:

- **Privacy notice** to employees;
- **Lawful basis** for HR processing;
- **Specific** rules for international transfers (e.g.,
  group HR systems abroad);
- **Specific** rules for special category data (health for
  insurance, etc.).

### Cap table considerations

For early-stage equity:

- **Jersey topco** with no-par-value shares often simpler;
- **Specific** classes of shares for founders, employees,
  investors;
- **Specific** vesting and acceleration provisions;
- **Specific** drag-along / tag-along arrangements (see
  [`drag-tag-rofr.md`](./drag-tag-rofr.md));
- **Specific** investor protections (information rights,
  consent rights).

### Termination of founder

When a founder leaves:

- **Director resignation** procedure (CJL 1991);
- **Employment termination** (EL 2003);
- **Vesting** of shares / options accelerated or paused
  (per cap table);
- **Restrictive covenants** kick in;
- **Specific** ongoing obligations (confidentiality,
  non-compete, non-solicit);
- **Specific** dispute risks.

### Restrictive covenants

For founder / senior departures:

- **Reasonable** in scope, duration, geographic area;
- **Subject to** Jersey common-law unreasonableness review;
- **Specific** Jersey case law on reasonableness;
- **Specific** enforceability through Royal Court injunction;
- **Damages** for breach where covenant unenforceable as
  injunction.

## Specific scenarios

### Co-founder departure

- **Mutual** vs **involuntary** departure;
- **Vesting** acceleration considerations;
- **Cap table** adjustment;
- **Restrictive covenants** enforcement consideration;
- **Specific** legal advice essential.

### Hiring CFO from overseas

- **Status** sponsorship through Population Office;
- **Specific** Licensed status application;
- **Compensation** package including relocation;
- **Specific** tax planning for relocation;
- **Specific** employment contract.

### Acquisition exit

- **Founder / employee** terms in acquisition;
- **Specific** earn-out arrangements;
- **Specific** post-acquisition employment terms;
- **Specific** restrictive covenant negotiation.

## What to do next

For new founder team:

1. **Choose** corporate structure (topco) and incorporate;
2. **Engage** employment lawyer for contracts;
3. **Verify** each hire's CHWL status;
4. **Set up** share scheme / option arrangements;
5. **Document** all founder / employee terms comprehensively;
6. **Establish** HR and payroll systems;
7. **Coordinate** with tax adviser for compensation
   structuring.

## Pitfalls

- **Inadequate** founder agreements;
- **Failure** to verify CHWL status before hiring;
- **Unreasonable** restrictive covenants (unenforceable);
- **Inadequate** IP assignment;
- **Unclear** vesting and equity terms;
- **Discrimination** in recruitment process.
