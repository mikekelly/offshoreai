# How Will My Jersey TopCo Be Taxed Under Zero-Ten?

## The question

Your Jersey TopCo is established. What Jersey tax does it pay
under the **zero-ten regime**?

## The short answer

A standard Jersey TopCo:

- **0% Jersey corporate tax** under Article 123A for most
  activities;
- **0% withholding** on dividends to non-resident shareholders;
- **No Jersey CGT** on capital gains;
- **No Jersey stamp duty** on share transfers (subject to LTT
  for property-share property);
- **Annual return** filed but at 0% rate.

Subject to:

- **10% rate** if classified as a financial-services company
  (Article 123C);
- **20% rate** for specific categories (utility, large
  corporate retailer);
- **15% Pillar Two** for large MNE groups (€750m+);
- **Substance** obligations under the 2019 Substance Law.

## The proper answer

### Standard 0% rate

Most Jersey TopCos fall under **Article 123A** standard 0% rate:

- **Holding company** activity;
- **Investment-management** for own account;
- **General trading** without specific high-rate triggers;
- **Group-services** within reason.

### 10% financial services category

**Article 123C** applies 10% rate to **financial services
companies**:

- **Banking** (under separate Banking Business Law primarily);
- **Investment business** for clients (regulated under
  FS(J)L 1998);
- **Trust company business**;
- **Fund services business**;
- **Insurance underwriting / mediation**.

A typical **founder TopCo** holding shares in operating
companies is **not** a financial-services company. But if the
TopCo provides regulated services, the 10% may apply.

### 20% specific categories

**Article 123D** applies 20% rate to:

- **Utility companies** (water, gas, electricity);
- **Large corporate retailers** (specific definition based on
  turnover);
- **Property-rental income** (Schedule A income at 20%);
- **Specific other categories** in the Law.

### Pillar Two for large groups

For MNE groups with **€750m+** annual consolidated revenue:

- **15% minimum** effective rate applies;
- **Jersey MCIT** (Multinational Corporate Income Tax) effective
  from 2025;
- **Top-up** to 15% if Jersey effective rate is below;
- **Reporting** under Pillar Two methodology.

For groups **below the threshold**, zero-ten 0% rate continues
unaffected.

### Substance obligations

Under the **Substance Law 2019**:

- Jersey-tax-resident companies carrying on **relevant
  activities** must meet substance test;
- For most TopCo activities:
  - **Pure equity-holding**: reduced test (basic compliance +
    adequate people / premises);
  - **Financing** activities: full substance;
  - **IP business**: full substance;
  - **Headquarters** activities: full substance.

Most founder TopCos meet the **reduced test** through their
service-provider arrangements.

### Distributions to shareholders

For a standard TopCo distributing to shareholders:

#### Non-resident shareholders

- **No Jersey withholding**;
- **No Jersey distribution tax**;
- **Foreign tax** in shareholder's home jurisdiction is the
  active question.

#### Jersey-resident shareholders

- **Article 81 distribution regime** applies;
- Distributions taxed in Jersey-resident shareholder's hands at
  20%;
- **Anti-avoidance** for deemed distributions (excess retention
  rules).

### Annual compliance

For a typical TopCo:

- **Annual corporate tax return** filed with Revenue Jersey;
- **0% tax payable** in standard case;
- **Substance attestation** (annually for relevant-activity
  companies);
- **CRS / FATCA** reporting if applicable;
- **Beneficial-ownership** filings.

### Common scenarios

#### Operating-business TopCo holding foreign subsidiaries

- **0% Jersey rate** at TopCo level;
- **Foreign tax** at operating-subsidiary level;
- **Dividends up** to TopCo at 0% Jersey rate;
- **Distributions to shareholders** without Jersey withholding.

#### IP-holding TopCo

- **0% rate** under zero-ten generally;
- **Full substance** required for IP business;
- **Foreign tax** characterisation may add complexity;
- **See [`ip-holding.md`](./ip-holding.md)**.

#### Pre-IPO TopCo

- **0% rate** during pre-IPO operating phase;
- **No exit charge** on IPO restructuring;
- **Listing** doesn't change Jersey tax position.

## What to do next

For an existing TopCo:

1. **Confirm** classification (0% / 10% / 20%);
2. **Confirm** substance compliance;
3. **Check** Pillar Two threshold;
4. **File** annual return.

For a new TopCo:

1. **Plan** activities to align with desired rate;
2. **Establish** substance arrangements;
3. **Monitor** for threshold changes (Pillar Two especially).

## Pitfalls

- **Misclassification**: a TopCo with financial-services
  activities may hit 10% rate;
- **Substance gap**: insufficient Jersey activity for relevant-
  activity classification;
- **Pillar Two threshold**: groups can grow into scope;
- **Distribution-rule** for Jersey-resident shareholders.
