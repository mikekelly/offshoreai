# Private vs Public Jersey Company — When Does the Choice Matter?

## The question

You're forming a Jersey company. Should it be **private** or
**public**? When does the choice make a real difference?

## The short answer

For most founder TopCos: **private** is the default and works.

**Choose public** when:

- The company intends to **offer securities to the public**
  (IPO is the obvious case);
- **Listing-rule requirements** demand public-company status;
- Specific **regulatory frameworks** require public status.

**Key practical differences**:

- **Public** allows prospectus-based public offerings;
- **Public** allows broader transferability of shares;
- **Public** has more disclosure obligations;
- **Private** is restricted to a **closed shareholder base**
  (with limited public offering exceptions).

## The proper answer

### Definitional difference

Under the Companies (Jersey) Law 1991:

#### Private company (Article 3B)

- **Cannot offer securities to the public** generally;
- **Restricted ownership**;
- **Member-count limits** historically (though largely modernised);
- **Light disclosure** obligations.

#### Public company (Article 3A)

- **Can offer securities to the public**;
- **Higher disclosure** obligations;
- **Subject to prospectus** rules for offerings;
- **Standard form** for listed entities.

### When private suffices

**Most Jersey TopCos** are private companies:

- **Owner-operator** founder;
- **Small shareholder base** (founders + investors);
- **No public-offering** intent;
- **Standard private-equity** investment structures;
- **Pre-IPO** working structure;
- **Family-business** vehicles;
- **Holding-company** purposes.

The private-company regime suits the **vast majority** of
business structures.

### When public is necessary

- **Imminent IPO** — listing on Main Market / AIM / Euronext /
  NASDAQ;
- **Crowdfunding or public placement** that requires public
  status;
- **Industry-specific** requirements (some regulated industries
  prefer / require public);
- **Bond issuance** with public offering;
- **Specific listing-rule** prerequisites.

### Conversion possibilities

Conversion is possible:

- **Private → public**: Article 17 procedure;
- **Public → private**: Article 16 procedure;
- **Conversion** typically before IPO or after delisting.

The conversion process is **straightforward** under Jersey law
— no need to start as public to plan for eventual IPO.

### Practical implications

#### For the founder

- **Private** TopCo is easier and lower-cost to operate;
- **Convert** to public closer to IPO if needed;
- **No need** to start public unless specific reason.

#### For investors

- **Private** company shares are illiquid (typical for PE / VC
  investors);
- **Public** company shares are tradeable in principle;
- **Most institutional investors** are comfortable with private
  Jersey companies.

### Common scenarios

#### Tech founder establishing TopCo

- **Private** Jersey company;
- **Founder + early investors** as shareholders;
- **Convert to public** if / when IPO planned;
- **Standard** structure.

#### Pre-IPO restructuring

- **Existing private** TopCo;
- **Convert to public** as part of pre-IPO preparation;
- **Public TopCo** at IPO date;
- **Standard** procedure.

#### Family business with operational subsidiaries

- **Private** Jersey TopCo;
- **Family-only** shareholder base;
- **No public-offering** intent;
- **Private** is correct.

#### Investment-trust-style listed fund

- **Public** company necessary for LSE listing;
- **Specific listing-rule** requirements drive public status.

### Disclosure differences

#### Private

- **Annual confirmation statement**;
- **Filed accounts** (limited public availability for some
  categories);
- **Beneficial-ownership** to central register (not public);
- **Standard** private-company filings.

#### Public

- **Annual confirmation statement**;
- **Filed audited accounts** with public availability;
- **More extensive** filing obligations;
- **Prospectus** for any public offering;
- **Listing-rule** ongoing disclosure if listed.

## What to do next

For a new TopCo:

1. **Confirm** business plan — is public-offering intent immediate
   or later?
2. **Default to private** unless specific reason;
3. **Plan** for conversion if IPO comes;
4. **Engage** Jersey counsel for incorporation.

## Pitfalls

- **Starting public unnecessarily**: extra cost and complexity;
- **Late conversion**: pre-IPO timing can be tight;
- **Mixing up** Jersey private / public distinction with UK /
  US equivalents (similar concepts, different details).
