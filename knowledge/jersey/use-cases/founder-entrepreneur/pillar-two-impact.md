# Pillar Two Consequences if My Group Scales Above €750m

## The question

Your Jersey TopCo's group is growing. **Pillar Two** applies to
groups with **€750m+ consolidated revenue**. What changes when
you cross the threshold?

## The short answer

When the group crosses €750m:

- **Jersey MCIT** (Multinational Corporate Income Tax) applies
  to Jersey constituent entities, effective 2025+;
- **Top-up tax** to **15% effective rate** on Jersey-booked
  income;
- **Compliance burden** significantly increases;
- **Specific tax software / advisers** needed;
- **Other Pillar Two jurisdictions** also apply parallel rules
  to non-Jersey group entities.

The **0% Jersey rate** is no longer the headline result for
large groups — Jersey collects up to 15% via MCIT instead of
the foreign parent jurisdiction collecting it under IIR /
UTPR.

## The proper answer

### Threshold and timing

- **€750m** annual consolidated revenue;
- **Two of four** preceding fiscal years above the threshold
  required for in-scope status;
- **Pillar Two effective** 2025+ in most jurisdictions
  including Jersey;
- **Phase-in** rules may apply.

### What Pillar Two does

For each jurisdiction in which the in-scope group operates:

- **Effective tax rate (ETR)** calculated on **GloBE income**;
- If **ETR < 15%**, top-up tax applies;
- **Top-up** collected by:
  - **QDMTT** (Jersey MCIT) at the source jurisdiction; or
  - **IIR** at parent jurisdiction; or
  - **UTPR** at other group jurisdictions.

Jersey's **MCIT acts as QDMTT** — Jersey collects the top-up
rather than allowing the foreign parent to collect it.

### How Jersey MCIT works

- **Apply Pillar Two rules** to Jersey constituent entities;
- **Calculate GloBE income** under standardised rules;
- **Calculate Jersey ETR** (typically very low under zero-ten);
- **Top-up to 15%** as MCIT;
- **File** Pillar Two return alongside or separate from local
  Jersey tax;
- **Pay** the top-up.

### What changes for the founder

#### Before threshold

- **0% Jersey rate** under zero-ten;
- **Standard substance** compliance;
- **Simple annual return**;
- **Low cost** of Jersey tax compliance.

#### After threshold

- **15% effective rate** on Jersey income;
- **Pillar Two return** alongside standard return;
- **Complex compliance** (GloBE income calculation, ETR analysis);
- **Specialist software** typically used;
- **Specialist tax advisers** for the Pillar Two layer.

### Strategic considerations

#### Approaching the threshold

- **Monitor** group consolidated revenue;
- **Anticipate** Pillar Two crossing;
- **Engage** Pillar Two advisers in advance;
- **Plan** for compliance infrastructure.

#### After crossing

- **Reassess** the Jersey tax strategy — is 15% still attractive
  vs other jurisdictions?
- **Compare** with onshore options (UK, Ireland, etc.);
- **Group simplification** sometimes drives consolidation;
- **Continued substance** still required.

### What stays the same

Even with Pillar Two:

- **Jersey legal advantages** persist (corporate law, no CGT
  at investor level, listing acceptance);
- **Jersey substance** infrastructure remains useful;
- **15% effective** is still competitive with many onshore
  jurisdictions;
- **Group structure** continuity often preserved.

### Who benefits, who loses

#### Benefits

- **Jersey treasury** collects the top-up;
- **Group continuity** preserved;
- **Investor experience** of the company unchanged.

#### Costs

- **Group ETR** rises;
- **Compliance cost** increases;
- **Margin** on Jersey-booked income shrinks.

### What if group is below threshold

- **Standard zero-ten** continues;
- **Most groups** remain below;
- **Substance** continues being the main concern;
- **No Pillar Two filing**.

### Group structure implications

For groups crossing the threshold:

#### Stay in Jersey

- **Continue** Jersey TopCo with MCIT top-up;
- **Familiar** infrastructure;
- **Lower** transition cost.

#### Migrate to onshore

- **Migrate TopCo** to onshore jurisdiction (UK, Ireland, etc.);
- **Match** onshore rate (15% or higher) with onshore-tax
  treatment;
- **Lose** Jersey advantages on other dimensions;
- **Migration cost** material.

#### Continue with assessment

- **Some advantages** to Jersey persist even at 15% ETR;
- **Case-by-case** analysis based on group's specifics.

## What to do next

For monitoring:

1. **Track** group consolidated revenue;
2. **Anticipate** Pillar Two crossing;
3. **Build** compliance capability;
4. **Engage** Pillar Two specialists.

For crossing:

1. **Implement** Pillar Two compliance;
2. **Reassess** structure strategy;
3. **Decide** stay vs migrate.

## Pitfalls

- **Late detection** of threshold crossing;
- **Compliance unpreparedness** when threshold crossed;
- **Strategic complacency**: continuing with no-change
  approach despite changed tax landscape;
- **Migration cost** underestimated if changing jurisdictions.
