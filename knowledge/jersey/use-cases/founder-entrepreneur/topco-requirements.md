# What a Jersey TopCo Needs — Board, Secretary, Registered Office

## The question

You're setting up a Jersey TopCo. What are the **minimum
requirements** for board, secretary, and registered office?

## The short answer

Jersey company law requires:

- **At least one director** (no requirement for Jersey
  residence, but **substance** typically demands at least some
  Jersey directors);
- **Secretary** (typically a corporate-secretary services
  provider);
- **Registered office** in Jersey;
- **Memorandum and articles** filed at incorporation;
- **Statutory registers** maintained;
- **Annual confirmation statement** filed.

For **substance** under the 2019 Substance Law:

- **At least some Jersey-resident directors** typically required
  even for pure equity-holding;
- **Board meetings** held in Jersey;
- **Decision-making in Jersey** for relevant activities.

## The proper answer

### Statutory minimums

#### Directors

- **At least one** director (Article 73);
- **No Jersey-residence** requirement in statute alone;
- **Natural person** or body corporate director permitted;
- **Fit-and-proper** standards apply (especially for regulated
  contexts).

For most TopCos, **2–4 directors** is typical, mixing:

- **Service-provider professional directors** for compliance /
  substance;
- **Founder / management** for strategic decision-making;
- **Independent directors** for governance (especially pre-IPO).

#### Secretary

- **Required** (Article 81);
- **Qualifications** per Article 82;
- **Typically** a corporate-services provider acts;
- **Some structures**: family-member secretary (small / closely-
  held companies).

#### Registered office

- **In Jersey** (Article 67);
- **Specified address** for service of process and statutory
  notices;
- **Typically** the corporate-services provider's office;
- **Listed** on the public Registry.

### Substance requirements

Even where statute doesn't mandate, substance practically
requires:

- **Adequate Jersey-resident directors** to make decisions in
  Jersey;
- **Jersey-resident** at least one director typically;
- **Board meetings in Jersey** with proper minutes;
- **Adequate Jersey premises** (typically the service-provider
  address);
- **Adequate personnel** to discharge duties.

For pure equity-holding TopCos, **standard service-provider
arrangements** typically suffice.

### Setting up

#### Pre-incorporation

- **Choose corporate-services provider** (see [`choosing-service-provider.md`](choosing-service-provider.md));
- **Choose company name** (verify availability with Registry);
- **Draft memorandum and articles**;
- **Identify directors** (provider's professionals + founder /
  management);
- **Identify secretary**;
- **Identify registered office** (service-provider's address).

#### Incorporation

- **File** memorandum and articles with Registry;
- **Pay** incorporation fee;
- **Receive** certificate of incorporation;
- **Open** bank account;
- **Establish** statutory registers.

#### Post-incorporation

- **First board meeting** to formalise establishment;
- **Adopt** bank mandates;
- **Establish** company seal if used;
- **Engage** auditor (if required);
- **Set up** ongoing administration.

### Ongoing maintenance

#### Annual confirmation statement

- **Filed** annually with Registry;
- **Confirms** or updates company information;
- **Annual fee** paid.

#### Statutory registers

- **Register of members**;
- **Register of directors / secretary**;
- **Register of charges**;
- **Minute book** for resolutions and meetings;
- **Maintained current**.

#### Filings

- **Director changes** notified;
- **Major resolutions** filed;
- **Material changes** (registered office, name, capital)
  filed.

### Beneficial-ownership compliance

The **FS(DPI)(J)L 2020** requires:

- **Beneficial-owner identification**;
- **Central register filing** with JFSC;
- **Update** on changes;
- **Significant-persons** identification.

Service provider typically handles this.

### Directors' duties

Once appointed, directors owe Article 74 duties:

- **Honesty and good faith**;
- **Care, skill, and diligence**;
- **Best interests** of the company;
- **No conflict of interest** (or disclosure);
- **No personal benefit** beyond authorisation.

These apply to all directors — founder, professional, independent.

### Common scenarios

#### Pre-IPO holding company

- **3–4 directors**: founder + 2 professional + 1 independent
  (pre-IPO transition);
- **Board meetings** quarterly in Jersey;
- **Robust governance** developing pre-IPO.

#### Family-business TopCo

- **2–3 directors**: family member + professional service
  director;
- **Annual / semi-annual** board meetings;
- **Lower-intensity** governance.

#### PE-backed TopCo

- **Founder / management** + **investor representatives** +
  **professional director**;
- **Regular board meetings**;
- **Investor information rights** add reporting cadence.

#### Listed TopCo

- **5–9 directors** typical;
- **Mix of executive / non-executive**;
- **Independent majority**;
- **Audit / remuneration / nomination** committees;
- **Quarterly+ board meetings**;
- **UK CGC** alignment for LSE Premium.

## What to do next

For a new TopCo:

1. **Engage** corporate-services provider;
2. **Set up** with statutory minimums;
3. **Apply** substance lens — adequate Jersey directors / 
   meetings / premises;
4. **Establish** ongoing maintenance routine.

For an existing TopCo:

- **Review** board composition periodically;
- **Refresh** as needs evolve (pre-IPO governance, etc.);
- **Maintain** substance compliance.

## Pitfalls

- **Bare-minimum compliance**: doesn't satisfy substance;
- **Foreign-resident-only directors**: substance failure for
  Jersey-resident company;
- **Inactive directors**: don't make decisions, undermine
  substance;
- **Stale governance**: not evolving as the company evolves.
