# Investor Information Rights Under Jersey Company Law

## The question

What **information rights** do shareholders / investors have
under Jersey company law? Can the company restrict them?

## The short answer

Jersey company law provides shareholders with:

- **Annual confirmation statement** (publicly accessible);
- **Memorandum and articles** (publicly accessible);
- **Register of members** (member-accessible per Article 41);
- **Register of directors / secretary** (member-accessible);
- **Annual accounts** (must be sent to members or made available);
- **Audited accounts** for public / certain private companies.

Beyond statutory:

- **Shareholders' agreement** typically expands rights;
- **PE / VC investor information rights** standard practice;
- **Listed-company** investors have broader rights under listing
  rules.

## The proper answer

### Statutory rights

#### Register of members

- **Member can inspect** (Article 45);
- **Copies available** on payment of fee;
- **Public access** is restricted to members.

#### Memorandum and articles

- **Publicly accessible** from JFSC Registry;
- **Members entitled to** a copy on payment.

#### Annual accounts

- **Sent to members** annually (Article 105);
- **Audited accounts** for prescribed company categories;
- **Filed accounts** for public companies (publicly accessible).

#### Other registers

- **Directors / secretary**;
- **Charges**;
- **Resolutions filed**.

### Members' meetings

#### Annual General Meeting (AGM)

- **Public companies** must hold AGM;
- **Private companies** can opt out;
- **Member rights** at meetings: speak, propose resolutions
  (subject to procedural rules), vote.

#### Information at meetings

- **Directors' report**;
- **Auditor's report** (for audited companies);
- **Specific business** on the agenda.

### Contractual rights

#### Shareholders' agreement

Typically expands statutory rights:

- **Monthly / quarterly management accounts**;
- **Budgets** at start of year;
- **Strategic plans** annually;
- **Material-event** notifications;
- **Board minutes** (or summaries) for major investors;
- **Tax returns** in some cases;
- **Audit reports** including management letters.

#### PE / VC investor info rights

Standard PE / VC investor information rights:

- **Monthly reporting** with KPIs;
- **Annual budget** and strategic plan;
- **Quarterly board-pack** observer rights or board seats;
- **Audited annual accounts**;
- **Major-event** notifications;
- **Inspection rights** for the company's records;
- **Special-resolution** matters require investor consent.

### Listed-company information

For listed Jersey companies:

#### Listing-rule disclosures

- **Periodic reports** (annual, half-yearly, sometimes
  quarterly);
- **Material events** disclosed to market;
- **Major shareholders** notifications;
- **Insider transactions** disclosed.

#### Continuous disclosure

- **Inside information** must be disclosed promptly;
- **Market-abuse rules** govern timing;
- **Specific procedures** for trading suspensions if needed.

### Limits and restrictions

Jersey company law allows some restrictions:

#### Confidentiality

- **Sensitive business information** can be confidential to
  certain investors per shareholders' agreement;
- **Non-disclosure** mechanisms for receiving;
- **But statutory rights** can't be waived (registers, accounts
  to members).

#### Information control

- **Class-specific** information for different shareholder
  classes;
- **Need-to-know** principles for major decisions;
- **Listed-company** rules prevent selective disclosure.

#### Restricted access

- **Director home addresses**: restricted from public access;
- **Beneficial-ownership**: not publicly accessible;
- **Some commercially-sensitive** information legitimately
  confidential.

### Members' right to information at common law

Jersey courts have recognised that members have **inherent
rights** to information necessary to exercise their rights as
members:

- **Access to register** of members;
- **Adequate accounts**;
- **Information** to vote informedly at meetings;
- **Limits**: not unlimited; not for purposes adverse to the
  company.

### Common scenarios

#### PE / VC investor

- **Standard package** of information rights;
- **Board observer / member**;
- **Quarterly reporting** at minimum;
- **Audited annual** accounts;
- **Material-event** notice;
- **Consent rights** for major matters.

#### Family-shareholder dispute

- **Minority members** demanding information;
- **Schmidt-Rosewood-style** principles for closely-held
  companies;
- **Royal Court application** possible if information unfairly
  withheld.

#### Listed-company investor

- **Listing-rule** disclosures cover most needs;
- **Direct inquiries** through investor relations;
- **General-meeting** rights to ask questions.

### Defensive practices

For a founder protecting commercial information:

- **Articles drafted** to limit information rights beyond
  statutory minimums (for private company);
- **Shareholders' agreement** clearly specifies what's
  delivered;
- **Confidentiality** obligations on recipients;
- **Listed-company**: follow rules strictly to avoid selective
  disclosure.

## What to do next

For an existing company:

- **Audit** information delivery against statutory + contractual
  obligations;
- **Engage** investors on enhanced reporting if appropriate;
- **Document** policies.

For a new company / funding round:

- **Draft** articles and shareholders' agreement carefully;
- **Negotiate** investor information rights to manageable level;
- **Establish** reporting cadence.

## Pitfalls

- **Statutory-right denial**: can't refuse register access etc.;
- **Selective disclosure**: violates market-abuse rules;
- **Inadequate reporting**: investor frustration / litigation;
- **Confidentiality leaks** through under-controlled investor
  population.
