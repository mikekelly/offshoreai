# Distributions to Shareholders — Jersey Tax Mechanics

## The question

Your Jersey TopCo wants to **distribute** to shareholders.
What's the Jersey tax position?

## The short answer

For a Jersey company under zero-ten:

- **No Jersey withholding** on dividends to **non-resident
  shareholders**;
- **No Jersey withholding** on dividends from **Article 123A
  0%-rate** companies generally;
- **Article 81 distribution regime** applies to **Jersey-
  resident shareholders** of zero-rate companies — they pay
  personal income tax at 20%;
- **Distribution mechanics** under Article 115 — directors'
  solvency statement required.

**Article 123C 10%-rate** financial-services companies have
specific distribution-tax mechanics.

## The proper answer

### Mechanics of distribution

Jersey distributions under Article 115:

- **Directors' solvency statement** that the company can pay
  its debts after distribution;
- **Members' approval** if articles require;
- **Distribution** paid;
- **No Jersey corporate tax** on the distribution itself
  (0%-rate company).

### Shareholder-level Jersey tax

#### Non-resident shareholder

- **No Jersey withholding**;
- **No Jersey personal tax**;
- **Foreign tax** in shareholder's home jurisdiction is the
  active question.

#### Jersey-resident shareholder

For Jersey residents:

- **Article 81 distribution regime** applies for 0%-rate
  companies;
- Distribution is **taxable as income** at 20% (subject to
  marginal relief);
- **Tax credit** for any underlying tax paid (typically nil for
  Article 123A companies);
- **Anti-avoidance** for deemed distributions (excess
  retention).

### Deemed distributions

The distribution regime has **anti-avoidance** mechanics:

- A company that **doesn't distribute** but accumulates can be
  deemed to have distributed;
- **Specific triggers**: certain related-party transactions
  treated as deemed distributions;
- **Loans to participators** in some circumstances;
- **Excess-retention** rules.

These are **anti-avoidance** designed to prevent Jersey-resident
shareholders accumulating tax-free in a 0%-rate company.

### Distributions from 10%-rate companies

For Article 123C financial-services companies:

- **10% tax** already paid at company level;
- **Withholding** on distribution to Jersey-resident
  shareholders to bring total to 20%;
- Net effect: Jersey-resident shareholder pays 20% combined.

### Practical mechanics

For a typical founder TopCo distributing to non-resident
shareholders:

1. **Director resolution** approving distribution;
2. **Solvency statement** under Article 115;
3. **Member resolution** if articles require;
4. **Distribution paid** to shareholders;
5. **Accounting record** updated;
6. **No Jersey tax** — but shareholder reports in home
   jurisdiction.

### Capital vs income distributions

Jersey distributions can be:

- **From distributable profits** (revenue);
- **From stated capital** (capital — for no-par-value
  companies, via Article 115);
- **Combined**.

The **distinction matters** primarily for foreign tax purposes.
Jersey's flexible distribution regime allows distribution from
**any source** subject to solvency.

### Common scenarios

#### Operating dividend to founder shareholders

- **0% Jersey tax** on company;
- **No Jersey withholding** to non-resident founders;
- **Foreign tax** on founders in their home jurisdictions.

#### Pre-exit dividend recap

- **Distribute** earnings before sale;
- **Increase** consideration received in sale;
- **Foreign-tax planning** at shareholder level critical.

#### Capital distribution post-listing

- **Listed companies** may distribute capital;
- **Solvency statement** required;
- **Listing-rule** disclosures.

#### Return of capital

- **Capital reduction** through Article 61A solvency-statement
  process;
- **Distribute** the reduced amount to shareholders;
- **Tax treatment** varies by shareholder jurisdiction.

### Tax-planning considerations

Foreign-jurisdiction tax planning often drives:

- **Timing** of distributions (pre / post year-end);
- **Form** of distribution (cash / in-specie / capital);
- **Source** of distribution (revenue / capital);
- **Recipient** structuring (direct vs through trust).

The Jersey-level mechanics are flexible — **foreign-tax
planning** is where value is added.

## What to do next

For a planned distribution:

1. **Confirm** solvency for Article 115;
2. **Board resolution**;
3. **Foreign-tax planning** with shareholders' advisers;
4. **Execute** distribution;
5. **Update** company records.

## Pitfalls

- **Article 81 surprise**: Jersey-resident shareholders may face
  unexpected Jersey personal tax;
- **Solvency-statement defect**: undermines distribution
  validity;
- **Foreign-tax planning gaps**: distribution flexibility at
  Jersey-level value is lost if shareholder-side tax not
  optimised.
