# Choosing a Jersey Corporate-Services Provider

## The question

Your Jersey TopCo needs a **corporate-services provider** —
directors, secretary, registered office, administration. How to
choose?

## The short answer

Options:

- **Major Jersey corporate-services providers**: IQ-EQ, Vistra,
  Sanne (now Apex), Maples, Tridel, and law-firm-affiliated
  (Carey Olsen, Mourant, Ogier, Bedell trust company arms);
- **Boutique** corporate-services firms;
- **Specialist** providers (fund services, family-office focused,
  etc.).

Key factors:

- **Scale** matching your needs;
- **Service quality**;
- **Reputation** with foreign counterparties;
- **Pricing**;
- **Relationship-officer continuity**;
- **Specialist expertise** if needed (PE, listed, fund, family-
  office).

## The proper answer

### What corporate services include

Standard Jersey corporate-services package:

- **Registered office** address in Jersey;
- **Director(s)** — professional directors;
- **Company secretary**;
- **Administration**: bookkeeping, accounts, filings;
- **Compliance**: CDD, AML, regulatory filings;
- **Director services** for board meetings;
- **Liaison** with auditors, lawyers, etc.

### Major providers

#### Large integrated services groups

- **IQ-EQ** — global services group, Jersey strong;
- **Vistra** — global;
- **Apex Group** (Sanne, etc.) — fund-focused historically,
  broader now;
- **Maples Group** — global offshore;
- **Hawksford** — Channel-Islands-focused;
- **Suntera Global** — channel-islands-focused.

#### Law-firm-affiliated trust / corporate services

- **Mourant** services;
- **Carey Olsen** services;
- **Ogier** services (Ogier Global);
- **Bedell Trust**;
- **Walkers** services.

### Boutique firms

Smaller specialists:

- **Family-office focus**;
- **Specific sector specialisation** (e.g. fund-focused,
  property-focused);
- **High-touch service**;
- **Often lower cost**;
- **Capacity limits** for very large structures.

### Decision factors

#### Scale matching

- **Small / simple structure**: boutique may suffice;
- **Medium**: choice of mid-tier or larger;
- **Large / complex**: major-services provider;
- **Listed-company**: major provider with public-company
  experience.

#### Service quality

- **Relationship officer** seniority and continuity;
- **Response times**;
- **Substantive director engagement** vs pure compliance;
- **Proactive advice** on issues.

#### Reputation

- **Counterparty acceptance** — major providers known to
  banks, auditors;
- **Regulatory standing** — JFSC-supervised, good record;
- **International reputation** for cross-border structures.

#### Pricing

- **Fixed fees** + activity-based;
- **Annual range**: £15k–£50k+ for standard private company;
  more for complex / public.

#### Specialist expertise

- **PE / VC**: experience with carry vehicles, fund structures;
- **Listed company**: public-company experience;
- **Family office**: complex family structures;
- **Fund services**: FSB-licensed for funds.

### Evaluation process

1. **Identify** specific needs (current and 3-year horizon);
2. **Shortlist** 3–5 providers;
3. **Request proposals** with scope and pricing;
4. **Site visit** to provider offices;
5. **Meet** proposed relationship team;
6. **References** from existing clients;
7. **Compare** proposals on total value;
8. **Choose** and engage.

### Engagement letter

Cover:

- **Scope** of services clearly defined;
- **Service-level expectations**;
- **Pricing** structure;
- **Director** identities and engagement terms;
- **Termination** rights for both parties;
- **Confidentiality**;
- **Indemnities** and limitations.

### Director selection

If the provider provides directors:

- **Qualifications and experience**;
- **JFSC fit-and-proper** status;
- **Capacity** (number of other directorships);
- **Substantive engagement** vs nominee-only;
- **Continuity** through the relationship.

For listed companies, **public-company-experienced directors**
matter.

### Substance considerations

The provider arrangement should support **economic substance**:

- **Adequate director presence** in Jersey;
- **Director engagement** in decisions;
- **Board meetings** in Jersey;
- **Administrative function** in Jersey;
- **Premises** in Jersey.

For pure equity-holding TopCos, **service-provider arrangements**
typically satisfy substance requirements.

### Working effectively

Once engaged:

- **Clear communication** of expectations;
- **Regular meetings** with relationship officer;
- **Annual reviews** of service;
- **Proactive engagement** on issues;
- **Escalation paths** for problems.

### Switching providers

Reasons to switch:

- **Service deterioration**;
- **Pricing dispute**;
- **Capability gap**;
- **Specialist need** the current provider doesn't have.

Switching process:

- **Notice** to existing provider per agreement;
- **Onboarding** with new provider;
- **Records handover**;
- **Director transitions**;
- **Counterparty notifications**.

## What to do next

For a new TopCo:

1. **Identify** needs and budget;
2. **Shortlist** providers;
3. **Evaluate** with site visits and proposals;
4. **Negotiate** engagement;
5. **Onboard**;
6. **Operate** with clear communication.

For an existing relationship:

- **Review** periodically;
- **Address** concerns proactively;
- **Switch** if necessary.

## Pitfalls

- **Cost-only selection**: cheaper providers may lack capability;
- **Tier mismatch**: large provider for tiny TopCo wasteful;
  boutique for complex structure under-resourced;
- **Director-quality**: nominee-only directors don't satisfy
  substance;
- **Counterparty-acceptance**: smaller providers may face
  banking-relationship challenges.
