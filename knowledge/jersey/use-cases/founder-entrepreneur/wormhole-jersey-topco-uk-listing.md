# Jersey TopCo → UK Opco → LSE listing — compiled walkthrough

> **What this is.** A compiled, citation-complete distillation of the
> founder-entrepreneur TopCo-listing cluster: the end-to-end structure for
> inserting a Jersey holding company above a UK operating business and
> listing it on the London Stock Exchange. Every step carries the
> authoritative corpus citation so the whole answer can be grounded from
> this one node. Derived from the cluster files listed in `derived_from`;
> `draft` status — verify the cited primary files before relying on it.

## End-state structure

```
Public investors → [Jersey TopCo] (listed, LSE) → [UK Opco]
```

## Steps

1. **Incorporate the Jersey TopCo** — a public company limited by shares
   under the **Companies (Jersey) Law 1991**; ~1–3 days; ≥2 directors, a
   Jersey-resident registered office / corporate-services provider, and
   beneficial-ownership lodged with the Registry (private register).
   ([`../../companies/companies-law-1991.md`](../../companies/companies-law-1991.md))

2. **Share-for-share exchange** — UK Opco shareholders exchange their
   shares for new TopCo shares (1:1 or other ratio); TopCo ends up owning
   100% of the UK Opco. **UK CGT** on the exchange is generally deferred by
   **share-for-share rollover relief under TCGA 1992 s.135** (subject to
   anti-avoidance). **0.5% UK stamp duty** may arise on the transfer of UK
   shares (reliefs may apply for genuine reorganisations). No Jersey tax
   arises on the exchange (no Jersey CGT).
   ([`./migration-pre-ipo.md`](./migration-pre-ipo.md))

3. **TopCo Jersey tax position** — the TopCo is Jersey-resident (incorporated
   in Jersey, managed and controlled there). Under the **zero-ten** regime
   (Income Tax (Jersey) Law 1961) a holding company pays the **0% standard
   rate** (10% only for financial-services companies; 20% for
   utility/property/quarrying/cannabis income).
   ([`../../tax/zero-ten.md`](../../tax/zero-ten.md))

4. **Economic substance** — under the **Taxation (Companies — Economic
   Substance) (Jersey) Law 2019**, a pure equity-holding company is subject
   to a **reduced substance test**: adequate people and premises in Jersey,
   but a lower bar than for IP or financing/leasing companies. Pure-paper
   presence does not satisfy it.
   ([`../../tax/economic-substance.md`](../../tax/economic-substance.md))

5. **Pillar Two** — if the consolidated group's annual revenue is **≥ EUR
   750m**, Jersey's **MCIT (effective 1 January 2025)** applies a **QDMTT**
   that tops the TopCo's Jersey income up to **15%**, keeping the top-up in
   Jersey rather than ceding it under another jurisdiction's IIR; UK Pillar
   Two runs in parallel. Below the threshold, Pillar Two does not engage and
   the 0% position continues.
   ([`../../international/pillar-two-mcit.md`](../../international/pillar-two-mcit.md))

6. **Capital structure** — CJL 1991 gives IPO-useful flexibility:
   **no-par-value shares** (standard), **multiple share classes** (founder /
   investor / public, incl. dual-class super-voting), **treasury shares**,
   **redeemable shares**, and **buy-backs**; capital reductions by
   special resolution + solvency statement (less court-intensive than the
   UK). ([`../../companies/companies-law-1991.md`](../../companies/companies-law-1991.md))

7. **LSE listing** — Jersey companies are routinely accepted across the LSE
   segments. **UK Listing Rules** apply (with DTRs, MAR, Prospectus
   Regulation), and the **UK Takeover Code** applies to a Jersey-incorporated
   company with securities on a UK regulated market. A prospectus (or AIM
   admission document) is required, with Jersey legal opinions / corporate
   authorities in the disclosure. ([`./listing-rule-overlay.md`](./listing-rule-overlay.md))

8. **Post-IPO** — the **UK Corporate Governance Code** applies; the JFSC
   does not regulate the TopCo unless it carries on regulated business;
   Registry filings continue. **Distributions** are made on a **solvency
   basis** under CJL 1991 (no retained-earnings test), with **0% Jersey
   withholding** on dividends to non-resident shareholders.
   ([`../../companies/companies-law-1991.md`](../../companies/companies-law-1991.md))

## Pitfalls

- Stamp-duty leakage on the share-for-share exchange if not structured for
  relief; managing the TopCo's tax residence via genuine Jersey-based
  management; Pillar Two if the group is in scope; FCA prospectus / sponsor
  / market-abuse requirements; and real (not paper) substance at TopCo level.
