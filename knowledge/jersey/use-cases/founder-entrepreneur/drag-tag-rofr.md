# Drag-Along, Tag-Along, and ROFR in Jersey Articles

## The question

You want to include **drag-along, tag-along, and right-of-first-
refusal (ROFR)** mechanics in your Jersey TopCo's articles or
shareholders' agreement. Do they work?

## The short answer

Yes — these provisions are **enforceable** in Jersey:

- **Articles of association** can include drag, tag, ROFR;
- **Shareholders' agreement** can supplement;
- **Jersey courts** enforce share-transfer-restriction
  provisions consistent with the Articles 9 / Article 9A
  framework;
- **Standard PE / VC drafting** transferable to Jersey context.

Jersey is a **familiar** corporate-law environment for these
mechanics — no surprises for advisers used to UK or other
common-law jurisdictions.

## The proper answer

### Drag-along

Drag-along clauses **force minority shareholders** to participate
in a sale alongside the majority:

#### Mechanics

- **Trigger threshold** typically 51%, 66.7%, or 75% of
  shareholders;
- **Dragged shareholders** must sell on **same terms** as
  dragging shareholders;
- **Notice** and procedural requirements;
- **Acceleration** of vesting if relevant.

#### Jersey application

- **Articles** typically contain drag-along clause;
- **Members agreement** supplements with detail;
- **Enforcement**: by injunction or specific performance in
  Royal Court if needed;
- **Standard market drafting** works.

### Tag-along

Tag-along (or co-sale) gives **minority shareholders the right
to participate** in a majority-shareholder sale:

#### Mechanics

- **Triggered** when majority shareholder proposes to sell
  above-threshold stake;
- **Other shareholders** can **tag along** at the same terms;
- **Pro-rata** share-sale typically;
- **Notice** and procedural requirements.

#### Jersey application

- **Articles** or **shareholders' agreement**;
- **Specific procedural** requirements drafted carefully;
- **Enforcement**: typically by injunction restraining sale
  without compliance.

### Right of First Refusal (ROFR)

ROFR gives **existing shareholders** preference to acquire
shares being sold:

#### Mechanics

- **Selling shareholder** must offer shares to existing
  shareholders first;
- **At offered terms** (matched-bid mechanism);
- **Pro-rata** or in agreed proportions;
- **Time window** for response;
- **Sale to third party** only if existing shareholders pass.

#### Jersey application

- **Articles** include ROFR;
- **Detailed procedural** clauses for matched bids;
- **Practical operation** typically slows transfers but provides
  shareholder protection.

### Other transfer restrictions

Jersey articles can also include:

- **Right of First Offer (ROFO)** — selling shareholder offers
  first but at no specific price;
- **Total transfer restrictions** for specified periods;
- **Class-specific restrictions**;
- **Permitted transferees** (e.g. family, related entities);
- **Lock-up periods** post-investment.

### Jersey law specifics

#### Article 42 (transfer of shares)

- **Articles can restrict** transfer of shares;
- **Restrictions** are valid and binding on members;
- **Standard restriction-of-transfer** clauses work.

#### Enforcement

- **Royal Court** enforces share-transfer restrictions;
- **Injunction** to prevent breaching transfer;
- **Specific performance** to compel compliant transfer;
- **Damages** if appropriate.

### Drafting considerations

For a Jersey TopCo with PE / VC investment:

#### Drag-along

- **Threshold**: usually 60–75% to drag;
- **Pricing protections**: ensure dragged minority gets fair
  terms;
- **Vesting acceleration**: align with employment / departure
  rules;
- **Anti-dilution coordination**.

#### Tag-along

- **Threshold**: typically applies above 5–10% transfer;
- **Pro-rata** sharing of sale opportunity;
- **Process**: clear notice and election windows.

#### ROFR

- **Operational practicality**: not too cumbersome;
- **Time windows**: balance protection with deal-execution;
- **Match mechanics**: clear pricing match.

### Shareholders' agreement

The **shareholders' agreement** typically:

- **Supplements** articles with detail;
- **Confidential** between shareholders;
- **Includes** non-Jersey-law-specific provisions (e.g. governing
  law for the agreement itself);
- **Standard PE / VC investment** documentation.

Coordination between articles (Jersey-law constitutional) and
agreement (contractual between members) is critical.

### Common scenarios

#### Series A VC investment

- **Drag** triggered by majority preference shares;
- **Tag-along** for all shareholders on exit-related sales;
- **ROFR** among existing shareholders;
- **Vesting reverse-vest** for founders.

#### Pre-IPO

- **Drag-along** to force minority participation in IPO if
  needed;
- **Lock-up** post-IPO;
- **Articles aligned** with listing rules.

#### Family-business with outside investors

- **Family-shareholder protections** for control;
- **Outside investors** tag-along on family-share-sale;
- **ROFR** to maintain family ownership.

## What to do next

For a new investment:

1. **Negotiate** drag / tag / ROFR terms;
2. **Engage** Jersey counsel for articles;
3. **Engage** investment counsel for shareholders' agreement;
4. **Align** articles and agreement;
5. **Execute** at closing.

## Pitfalls

- **Articles-and-agreement misalignment**: differing provisions
  create disputes;
- **Procedural complexity**: too many transfer steps slow
  deals;
- **Threshold confusion**: who can drag, who can tag, at what
  level;
- **Listing-rule conflict** at IPO if articles too restrictive.
