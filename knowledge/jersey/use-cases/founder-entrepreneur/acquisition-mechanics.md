# Continuance / Merger of TopCo as Part of Acquisition

## The question

The acquirer wants to **integrate your Jersey TopCo into their
group**. Should this be done via **continuance**, **merger**,
or **share-sale followed by integration**? What are the
mechanics?

## The short answer

Options:

- **Share-sale** (common): acquirer buys shares of Jersey TopCo;
  TopCo becomes group subsidiary;
- **Statutory merger (Part 18B)**: Jersey TopCo merges into
  acquirer's vehicle; one surviving entity;
- **Continuance (Part 18C)**: Jersey TopCo migrates to
  acquirer's jurisdiction; same entity, new domicile;
- **Asset transfer**: operating-business assets move; Jersey
  TopCo retained or wound down.

Choice driven by:

- **Acquirer's preference** for group structure;
- **Tax efficiency** in both Jersey and acquirer's jurisdiction;
- **Contract and counterparty** continuity needs;
- **Regulatory** considerations.

## The proper answer

### Share-sale (most common)

The simplest structure:

- **Buyer acquires shares** of Jersey TopCo;
- **TopCo continues** as Jersey company, now buyer's subsidiary;
- **No corporate-law restructuring** needed in Jersey;
- **Group consolidation** under buyer's group structure;
- **TopCo can be migrated** later if needed.

Pros:

- **Simple** execution;
- **Continuity** of TopCo operations;
- **Lower legal cost**;
- **Quick** to close.

Cons:

- **Two corporate levels** (acquirer + TopCo) — operational
  duplication;
- **Migration / merger** may come later anyway.

### Statutory merger (Part 18B)

If acquirer wants **single surviving entity**:

- **Merger of acquirer vehicle + Jersey TopCo**;
- **One survives**, other ceases to exist;
- **Universal succession** of assets / liabilities;
- **Cross-border mergers** possible (subject to JFSC consent
  if non-Jersey survivor);
- **Founder shareholders** receive consideration in cash or
  shares of survivor.

Pros:

- **Clean** single-entity outcome;
- **Universal succession** simplifies asset transfers;
- **Tax-efficient** in some configurations.

Cons:

- **Cross-border-merger complexity** if acquirer is non-Jersey;
- **Foreign-jurisdiction recognition** of Jersey merger
  important;
- **JFSC consent** time / cost.

### Continuance (Part 18C)

If acquirer wants the Jersey TopCo **moved to their
jurisdiction**:

- **Continuance out of Jersey** under Part 18C;
- **Same entity** persists in destination;
- **Acquirer's tax / regulatory** regime applies post-
  continuance;
- **Shareholder approvals** required;
- **JFSC consent** required.

Pros:

- **Same legal entity** continues — contracts / licences /
  IP / etc. preserved;
- **No new entity** creation needed;
- **Operational continuity**.

Cons:

- **Destination-jurisdiction's** recognition required;
- **Tax** considerations in destination;
- **6+ months** typical timeline.

### Asset-transfer (operating-business only)

If acquirer wants the **operating business** but not the corporate
entity:

- **Jersey operating subsidiary** sells assets to acquirer;
- **Asset-purchase agreement**;
- **Customer / employee / contract** transitions;
- **Holding company** retained / wound up separately;
- **Founder gets** sale proceeds at operating-subsidiary level.

Pros:

- **Clean acquisition** of business;
- **Acquirer avoids** historical liabilities of corporate shell;
- **Familiar** to acquirers in some jurisdictions.

Cons:

- **Asset-by-asset** transfer mechanics;
- **Contract novations**;
- **Tax** at operating level (often less efficient than share-
  sale).

### Decision factors

#### Acquirer's preference

- **Strategic acquirer** wanting clean integration: merger or
  continuance;
- **PE buyer**: typically share-sale, integration later;
- **Founder-rollover**: share-sale with reinvestment.

#### Tax efficiency

- **Jersey CGT-free** at company level for share-sale;
- **Founder-level CGT** depends on jurisdiction;
- **Step-up basis** at acquirer level (or not) by route.

#### Continuity needs

- **Contract continuity**: continuance preserves; merger does
  via universal succession;
- **Licence / regulatory** continuity: continuance often
  better.

#### Speed

- **Share-sale**: fastest;
- **Merger**: 3–6 months;
- **Continuance**: 3–6 months.

### Founder considerations

Founder concerns:

- **Maximum proceeds** with minimum tax;
- **Reinvestment** opportunity if continuing role;
- **Earn-out** mechanics;
- **Warranty exposure** under SPA / merger documents;
- **Post-sale obligations** (transition, non-compete).

The **legal route** (share / merger / continuance) doesn't
fundamentally change founder economics — the **commercial deal
terms** matter more.

### Common scenarios

#### Trade-sale to UK strategic

- **Often** share-sale;
- **UK acquirer** takes Jersey TopCo as subsidiary;
- **Post-acquisition** integration through internal restructuring;
- **Jersey-TopCo continuance** to UK sometimes follows.

#### PE buy-out

- **Share-sale** standard;
- **Acquisition-vehicle** PE structure;
- **Management rollover** if continuing;
- **Jersey TopCo** continued as part of PE group.

#### Strategic merger of equals

- **Statutory merger** considered;
- **Cross-border** complexity;
- **Often share-sale + later restructuring** simpler.

#### US-listed-acquirer

- **Share-sale** typical;
- **Pre-sale** continuance to US sometimes;
- **NASDAQ listing** continues after acquisition.

## What to do next

For an active sale:

1. **Discuss** structure preferences with acquirer;
2. **Model** tax outcomes for each route;
3. **Engage** Jersey counsel and acquirer-jurisdiction counsel;
4. **Choose** structure;
5. **Execute** with appropriate documentation;
6. **Close** with proper procedural compliance.

## Pitfalls

- **Defaulting to share-sale** without considering alternatives;
- **Tax-route optimisation** missed;
- **JFSC consent** time / cost underestimated;
- **Cross-border merger** complexity;
- **Continuance recognition** in destination.
