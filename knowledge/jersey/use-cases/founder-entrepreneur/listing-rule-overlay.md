# Listing-Rule Overlay on Jersey Company Law

## The question

Your Jersey TopCo is listed (or pre-IPO). **Listing rules** of
the relevant exchange apply on top of Jersey company law. How
do they interact?

## The short answer

Listing rules are **contractual obligations** on the company:

- **Jersey company law** sets the floor (incorporation,
  governance basics, statutory rights);
- **Listing rules** add **further obligations** (disclosure,
  governance, related-party transactions, etc.);
- **Listed company** complies with both;
- **Jersey law prevails** where there is conflict for corporate-
  law matters; **listing rules** are contractual and breach
  triggers listing-rule consequences.

For LSE Main Market Premium Segment, expect **UK Corporate
Governance Code** application; for AIM, lighter rules; for
NASDAQ / NYSE, US-SEC standards.

## The proper answer

### Layered framework

A Jersey-incorporated listed company complies with:

1. **Jersey Companies Law 1991** — incorporation, statutory
   rights, basic governance;
2. **JFSC regulations** — Registry, beneficial-ownership,
   substance, etc.;
3. **Listing rules** of the relevant exchange;
4. **Market-abuse rules** of the listing jurisdiction;
5. **Continuing-disclosure** rules;
6. **Corporate-governance codes** (UK CGC for LSE, others for
   different exchanges).

### Jersey-law vs listing-rule interaction

#### Floor not ceiling

- Jersey law sets **minimum** corporate-law requirements;
- Listing rules typically **exceed** Jersey requirements (e.g.
  higher disclosure, more board structure);
- Company **complies with both**.

#### Where conflict

- **Mandatory Jersey law** prevails over contradictory listing
  rule (e.g. Jersey can't waive statutory directors' duties);
- **Listing rule** typically remains contractually binding for
  listing purposes;
- **Practical conflict** is rare — both regimes aim at corporate
  health.

### LSE Main Market Premium Segment

For a Jersey TopCo on LSE Main Market Premium:

#### UK CGC

- **Independence**: majority independent directors;
- **Separation** of Chairman and CEO;
- **Audit committee** with independent members;
- **Remuneration committee** with independent members;
- **Nomination committee** with majority independent;
- **Annual evaluation** of board;
- **Comply or explain** principle.

#### Listing-rule disclosures

- **Annual report** with prescribed content;
- **Interim accounts** half-yearly;
- **Class 1 and Class 2** transaction disclosures;
- **Related-party transaction** rules;
- **Major shareholder** notifications;
- **Insider transaction** disclosures.

#### Market-abuse

- **MAR** (Market Abuse Regulation) compliance;
- **PDMR transactions** disclosed;
- **Insider lists**.

### AIM

For AIM-listed Jersey TopCo:

- **Lighter regime** than Main Market;
- **NOMAD** (Nominated Adviser) provides ongoing guidance;
- **AIM Rules for Companies** apply;
- **Disclosure** less onerous but still substantial;
- **No specific governance code** but governance expectations
  evolved.

### NASDAQ / NYSE

For US-listed Jersey TopCo:

- **SEC registration** required;
- **Sarbanes-Oxley** for the audit;
- **NASDAQ / NYSE listing standards**;
- **Foreign Private Issuer** election available — lighter US
  disclosure if elected;
- **Form 20-F** annual report (FPI) or **Form 10-K** (full US-
  domestic).

The US disclosure burden is **significantly higher** than UK
counterparts.

### TISE (Channel Islands)

For TISE-listed Jersey company:

- **TISE Listing Rules** apply;
- **Lighter** than UK exchanges typically;
- **Channel-Islands-familiar** regime;
- **Common** for specialist / closed-end vehicles.

### Areas of practical attention

#### Related-party transactions

- **Listing rules** typically require disclosure / approval
  of related-party deals;
- **Jersey company law** (Article 75 — director disclosure)
  has separate requirements;
- **Both** apply to listed Jersey company.

#### Significant acquisitions / disposals

- **Listing rules** (LSE Class 1 / 2) trigger shareholder
  approval for large transactions;
- **Jersey law** typically doesn't require shareholder approval
  for ordinary transactions;
- **Listing rules govern** for listed company.

#### Share buybacks

- **Jersey Article 57** (share buyback) gives the framework;
- **Listing rules** (e.g. Premium Segment) add restrictions
  (5% per year cap, etc.);
- **Both regimes** must be satisfied.

#### Capital increases

- **Jersey** allows authorised capital increases by director /
  member resolution;
- **Listing rules** (pre-emption, dilution caps) restrict
  practice;
- **Practical operations** follow listing-rule constraints.

### Governance documentation

A listed Jersey TopCo typically has:

- **Articles of association** Jersey-law compliant + listing-
  rule features;
- **Board committee terms of reference** following CGC / listing
  expectations;
- **Disclosure policy** for market-abuse rules;
- **Insider-trading policy**;
- **Code of conduct** for officers.

### Continuing compliance

- **Listing-rule annual review** by NOMAD / sponsor;
- **Periodic regulator engagement** (FCA in UK, SEC in US);
- **Disclosure cadence** following exchange rules;
- **Governance refresh** in line with CGC / equivalent.

## What to do next

For pre-IPO planning:

1. **Choose** the listing venue;
2. **Identify** specific listing-rule overlay;
3. **Align** Jersey TopCo governance with listing requirements;
4. **Draft** Jersey articles incorporating listing-rule
   features;
5. **Engage** sponsor / NOMAD / underwriter.

For ongoing compliance:

- **Monitor** listing-rule changes;
- **Refresh** governance practices;
- **Engage** with sponsor / NOMAD on emerging issues.

## Pitfalls

- **Jersey-only focus**: missing listing-rule overlay;
- **Listing-rule-only focus**: missing Jersey-law requirements;
- **Conflict misidentification**: when rules differ, mapping
  to the right one matters.
