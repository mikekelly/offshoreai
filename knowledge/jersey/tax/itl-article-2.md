# ITL Article 2 — Yearly Assessments

Article 2 of the **[Income Tax (Jersey) Law 1961][itl]**
provides for the **annual assessment** of income tax.

## What Article 2 covers

Income tax is **assessed for each year of assessment**:

- The **year of assessment** is the **calendar year**;
- Assessments are made on the **annual basis**;
- **Profits / income of the YA** are the relevant measure;
- **Specific rules** for accounting periods straddling year-end.

The current YA is **2026**; the most recently completed YA is
**2025**.

## Why this matters

Article 2 establishes the **temporal structure** of Jersey
income tax:

- Each YA is a **discrete assessment unit**;
- **Filings** relate to specific YAs;
- **Allowances** and reliefs apply per YA;
- **Marginal-relief** calculations and other annual mechanics
  follow.

For practitioners: ensure filings are by YA, with
appropriate accounting-period mapping where business years
don't align with the calendar.
