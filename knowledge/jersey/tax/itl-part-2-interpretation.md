# ITL Part 2 — Interpretation

Part 2 of the **[Income Tax (Jersey) Law 1961][itl]** provides
the **interpretation provisions** for the Law.

## What Part 2 covers

### Article 3 — General interpretation

Defines key terms used throughout the Law:

- **"Body corporate"**, **"company"**, **"person"**;
- **"Income"** and related concepts;
- **"Trade"**, **"profession"**, **"employment"**;
- **"Resident"**, **"non-resident"**;
- **Other** technical definitions.

### Article 4 — Connected persons; subsidiaries

Defines:

- **"Connected persons"** for various anti-avoidance and
  reporting purposes;
- **Subsidiary / holding-company** relationships;
- **Group** concepts;
- **Control** definitions.

The connected-persons concept is **critical** for:

- Transfer-pricing and related-party rules;
- Anti-avoidance under Article 134A;
- Group relief provisions;
- Specific distribution and substance rules.

### Article 4A — Total income; financial periods

Defines:

- **"Total income"** for personal-tax purposes;
- **Financial periods** for accounting-period mapping;
- **YA / financial-period** alignment rules;
- **Specific** computational provisions.

## Why these matter

Part 2 is the **lookup** for the rest of the Law:

- Every substantive provision uses Part 2 terminology;
- **Misreading** definitions leads to wrong substantive
  application;
- **Specific terms** in Part 2 have detailed meanings.

For practitioners: **read Part 2 carefully** when interpreting
any substantive ITL provision.

## Common areas of complexity

### "Total income"

- Computational concept aggregating various income types;
- **Specific exclusions** and **adjustments**;
- **Different** from US "gross income" concept;
- **Subject to** specific reliefs.

### "Connected persons"

- Broad in some applications;
- **Specific to** Article 134A anti-avoidance;
- **Different from** Companies Law connected-persons (which is
  also broad);
- **Family members**, **trustees**, **shareholders** all
  potentially within.

### Residence concepts

- **Article 3** general residence rules;
- **More detailed** rules elsewhere (Article 126 onwards);
- **Body-corporate residence** under Article 123.
