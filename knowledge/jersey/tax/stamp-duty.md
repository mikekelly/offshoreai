# Stamp Duty and Transaction Taxes — Jersey

Jersey imposes a relatively narrow set of **transaction taxes**:

- **Stamp duty** on certain instruments;
- **Land Transactions Tax (LTT)** on share-transfer purchases of
  qualifying immovable property;
- **Probate fees** (the residual *droit de succession*);
- **Registration fees** in the Public Registry for immovable
  property transactions.

There is **no general** stamp duty on share transfers or
contracts, in contrast to the UK and many European jurisdictions.

## Stamp duty on instruments

Stamp duty under the **Stamp Duties and Fees (Jersey) Law 1998**
applies to specified instruments:

- **Conveyances of immovable property** in Jersey;
- **Probate** of estates of Jersey-resident deceased persons;
- **Certain court orders** and registered instruments;
- **Hypothecs** registered against Jersey immovable property.

Rates are scaled by value, with banded percentages similar in
structure to (but lower in level than) UK SDLT.

## Land Transactions Tax (LTT)

The **Taxation (Land Transactions) (Jersey) Law 2009** imposes a
parallel tax on **share-transfer transactions** that confer
beneficial ownership of Jersey **share-transfer property**
(typically apartments and flats where ownership is held through
share certificates rather than freehold title).

LTT rates broadly mirror conveyance stamp duty, ensuring **fiscal
parity** between freehold and share-transfer ownership.

## Probate fees

Probate of a Jersey-resident estate triggers a **probate fee**,
scaled by estate value. As discussed in
[`no-inheritance-tax.md`](no-inheritance-tax.md), this is a
**registration fee** not a wealth-transfer tax — modest in
amount and unrelated to UK / US IHT levels.

## No general stamp duty

Jersey does **not** impose stamp duty on:

- **Share transfers** of Jersey companies (except in the LTT
  property-share context above);
- **Loan documents**;
- **Commercial contracts**;
- **Trust deeds**.

This is a significant difference from jurisdictions like the UK
(where stamp duty / SDRT applies to UK share transfers) and many
civil-law jurisdictions (with registration taxes on contracts).

## First-time buyer relief

Jersey grants **reduced rates** for **first-time-buyer** purchases
within prescribed value bands — a social-policy measure to
support Jersey residents acquiring their first home.

## Practical implications

For most **transactional** work in Jersey, the absence of broad
stamp duty is a **cost saving** relative to other jurisdictions:

- **M&A transactions** — Jersey share transfers attract no Jersey
  stamp duty (subject to LTT for property-related shares);
- **Restructurings** — no Jersey contract stamp;
- **Trust and family-office work** — no Jersey stamp on settlement
  deeds.

The narrow Jersey transaction-tax base is part of the
jurisdiction's **competitive positioning**.
