# High-Value Residency Tax — Jersey 2(1)(e)

Jersey's **High-Value Residency (HVR)** regime — also called
"**2(1)(e) status**" after the relevant regulation under the
Control of Housing and Work (Jersey) Law 2012 — is a **bespoke
tax and residency regime** for incoming high-net-worth
individuals.

## The 2(1)(e) status

The 2(1)(e) designation:

- Is granted under the Control of Housing and Work (Jersey) Law
  2012, regulation 2(1)(e);
- Allows the individual to **acquire residential property** in
  Jersey (a restricted privilege in Jersey housing law);
- Carries with it a **specific tax regime** distinct from
  ordinary 20% personal income tax.

It is the principal route by which **wealthy non-Jersey
individuals** can become Jersey-resident.

## The tax regime

The bespoke HVR tax position is broadly:

- **Tier 1 income** (up to a specified threshold of Jersey-taxable
  income): taxed at **20%** (the standard rate);
- **Tier 2 income** (Jersey-taxable income above the threshold):
  taxed at **a flat reduced rate** (commonly 1%) up to a higher
  threshold;
- **Investment income from abroad** brought into Jersey can be
  subject to specific provisions agreed at the start of residency.

The exact tier amounts and rates are set out in published Revenue
Jersey guidance and are subject to periodic adjustment.

An **annual minimum tax** (a floor) is typically agreed as part
of the 2(1)(e) approval, ensuring Jersey receives at least a
minimum tax contribution from each HVR resident regardless of
income variation.

## Application process

2(1)(e) status is granted through:

- Application to the **Population Office** / **Locate Jersey**;
- **Assessment** of the applicant's economic and social
  contribution;
- **Comfort with** the applicant's reputation, AML, source of
  funds;
- **Approval** by the relevant minister;
- **Property acquisition** within prescribed timeframes.

The regime is **selective** — Jersey approves a limited number
of new HVR applicants each year and prioritises individuals
contributing to the Jersey economy.

## Why this matters

The HVR regime is a key **inbound migration** route for HNW
individuals choosing Jersey:

- **Tax stability** at a known rate, often more favourable than
  high-tax home jurisdictions;
- **No CGT / no IHT** at the Jersey level (subject to home-
  jurisdiction tax);
- **Common-law legal environment** with English-style courts;
- **Lifestyle factors** — Channel Islands location, security,
  community.

For comparison:

- UK non-dom regime (in its various incarnations) has been the
  closest UK equivalent;
- Italian, Portuguese, and similar HNW regimes offer different
  trade-offs.
