# ITL Part 11 — Interest, Dividends, Distributions

Part 11 (Articles 86–90D) governs **payment deductions**,
**dividend withholding**, **relief provisions**, and **maintenance
payment deductions**.

## Scope

- **Payments deduction** at source for specific income types;
- **Dividend withholding** in some categories;
- **Specific reliefs**;
- **Maintenance payments** (Jersey personal-tax context).

## Withholding mechanics

### Zero-ten companies

Most Jersey companies at 0% rate:

- **No withholding** on dividends to non-resident shareholders;
- **Article 81 distribution regime** for Jersey-resident
  shareholders;
- **Specific 10%-rate** companies have different treatment.

### Article 123C 10% rate

- **Financial-services companies** at 10%;
- **Withholding** on distribution to Jersey-resident
  shareholders;
- **Bring effective rate** to 20% for Jersey-resident
  shareholders;
- **Non-resident** shareholders: no withholding.

### Specific other payments

- **Royalties**: specific Jersey rules;
- **Interest** payments to non-residents: generally no
  withholding;
- **Service payments**: case-by-case.

## Distribution mechanics

The **Article 81 onwards** distribution regime:

- **Anti-avoidance** for Jersey-resident shareholders of 0%
  Jersey companies;
- **Excess retention** rules;
- **Deemed distributions** in some circumstances.

For Jersey-resident shareholders:

- **Distributions taxed** as income at 20%;
- **Tax credit** for underlying tax in some cases;
- **Specific anti-avoidance** for retention.

## Maintenance payments

- **Maintenance to former spouse** specific treatment;
- **Deduction** for payer in some cases;
- **Inclusion** for recipient;
- **Modern reform** has reduced this regime's scope.

## Practical considerations

For Jersey companies:

- **Determine** distribution treatment;
- **Apply** appropriate withholding if 10%-rate company;
- **Report** distributions to shareholders.

For Jersey-resident shareholders:

- **Report** distributions received;
- **Apply** Article 81 anti-avoidance considerations;
- **Foreign-tax** position for foreign-source distributions.
