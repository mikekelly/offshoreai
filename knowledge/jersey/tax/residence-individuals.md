# Tax Residence of Individuals in Jersey

An individual's **tax residence** status determines whether
Jersey income tax applies, and on what scope of income.

## The three categories

Jersey tax law recognises three residence categories:

1. **Resident and ordinarily resident** — Jersey-domiciled, with
   Jersey as their settled centre of life;
2. **Resident but not ordinarily resident** — short-term
   residence in Jersey without the settled-presence quality;
3. **Non-resident** — outside Jersey for tax purposes.

Each category has different **scope of charge**.

## Tests for residence

### The 183-day test

An individual present in Jersey for **183 days or more** in a
year of assessment is treated as **resident** for that year.

### The habitual visit test

An individual making **habitual substantial visits** (e.g. averaged
over 4 years, ~91 days per year) may be treated as resident, even
without hitting 183 days in any single year.

### The available accommodation test

An individual with **accommodation available** for their use in
Jersey, and who visits regularly, may be treated as resident.

(See Revenue Jersey guidance for detailed application.)

## Ordinarily resident

The "ordinarily resident" qualifier reflects the **settled
quality** of residence — Jersey as the individual's principal
home or settled abode, not just a temporary stay.

In practice:

- A new arrival making Jersey their home is typically **resident
  but not ordinarily resident** in the first year, becoming
  ordinarily resident in subsequent years;
- A long-term Jersey resident is **resident and ordinarily
  resident**;
- A short-secondment arrival who plans to leave is **resident**
  but **not ordinarily resident**.

## Scope of charge by category

| Status | Scope of charge |
|---|---|
| Resident and ordinarily resident | **Worldwide income** at 20% (subject to marginal relief) |
| Resident but not ordinarily resident | Jersey-source income; foreign income only as remitted to Jersey |
| Non-resident | Jersey-source income only |

## Becoming non-resident

Leaving Jersey for tax purposes requires:

- **Genuine departure** — not just a paper exit;
- **Substantial absence** — typically more than half the year
  outside Jersey;
- **Loss of settled connection** — disposing of Jersey
  accommodation, family relocation, etc.;
- **Notification** to Revenue Jersey.

Practical compliance involves **careful diary management** and
**evidence preservation** in the year of departure.

## High-Value Residency

For incoming **high-net-worth individuals**, Jersey offers a
bespoke **High-Value Residency** regime under regulation 2(1)(e)
of the Control of Housing and Work Law. The tax position is
modified — see [`high-value-residency-tax.md`](./high-value-residency-tax.md)
(stub) for details.
