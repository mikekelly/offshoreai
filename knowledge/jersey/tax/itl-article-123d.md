# ITL Article 123D — Specific 10% / 20% Rate Categories

Article 123D of the **[Income Tax (Jersey) Law 1961][itl]**
imposes **specific elevated rates** on certain Jersey-tax-
resident companies — typically **utility companies** at 20% and
**large corporate retailers** at a graduated rate.

## What Article 123D covers

### Utility companies

- **Water, electricity, gas** companies serving Jersey;
- **20% rate** on profits;
- **Reflects** local-monopoly status and substance proportionality.

### Large corporate retailers

- **Defined by** turnover threshold (annual revenue exceeding
  prescribed amount);
- **Retail trade** in Jersey;
- **Specific rate** (typically 20% on profits above thresholds
  with graduated approach);
- **Smaller retailers** at 0% under standard zero-ten.

### Other Article 123D categories

- **Specific sectors** as designated by Order;
- **Quarrying** (Jersey-extracted material) historically;
- **Specific** regulated sectors may be added.

## Why 20% / specific rates

- **Local-utility activity** has substantial Jersey-resident
  customer base — pure profits-extraction by 0% would seem
  inappropriate;
- **Large retailers** similarly have Jersey-derived profit
  from Jersey-resident consumer base;
- **Smaller** retailers at 0% to preserve competitive
  environment;
- **Calibrated** between zero (general companies) and full 20%.

## Specific applications

### Jersey utility companies

- **Jersey Electricity, Jersey Water, Jersey Gas** — major
  utilities;
- **20%** corporate tax;
- **Specific** regulatory obligations alongside;
- **Long-standing** rate.

### Large retailers

- **Major supermarkets**, **chain retailers** above threshold;
- **Graduated rate** in some applications;
- **Annual review** of threshold.

### Imported / fuel duties etc.

- Separate excise / duty regimes apply alongside corporate
  income tax;
- Article 123D is the **corporate income tax** layer.

## Distribution mechanics

For Article 123D companies:

- **Withholding** to Jersey-resident shareholders at appropriate
  rate to bring effective rate to 20% combined;
- **Non-resident** shareholders: no Jersey withholding;
- **Specific** mechanics by company type.

## Compliance

- **Annual corporate tax return** at relevant Article 123D
  rate;
- **Substance** compliance;
- **Specific** sector-regulatory obligations;
- **Distribution mechanics**.

## Pillar Two interaction

For Article 123D companies in large MNE groups:

- **Already at 20%** typically — at or above 15% Pillar Two
  minimum;
- **No top-up** typically needed;
- **Confirm** specific Pillar Two effective-rate calculation.

## Common scenarios

### Major Jersey utility

- **20% Article 123D** rate;
- **Effectively** the highest Jersey corporate rate;
- **Substantial** local activity supports.

### Major Jersey supermarket chain

- **Large corporate retailer** classification;
- **Graduated rate** in many cases;
- **Specific** turnover-threshold calculations.

### Smaller retailer

- **Below threshold**: 0% under Article 123A standard;
- **Above threshold**: Article 123D applies;
- **Step-up** as growth occurs.
