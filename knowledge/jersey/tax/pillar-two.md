# Pillar Two — Jersey MCIT / Domestic Minimum Tax

Jersey implements the **OECD Pillar Two** global-minimum-tax
framework through the **Multinational Corporate Income Tax
(Jersey) Law 2024** ("MCIT Law"), effective for financial years
commencing on or after **1 January 2025**.

## What Pillar Two does

Pillar Two sets a **global minimum effective tax rate of 15%**
for **large multinational enterprise groups** (MNE groups with
**EUR 750 million** or more in annual consolidated revenue).

The rules operate through:

- **Income Inclusion Rule (IIR)** — top-up tax paid in the parent
  jurisdiction on low-taxed subsidiaries;
- **Undertaxed Profits Rule (UTPR)** — backstop rule allocating
  top-up tax to other group jurisdictions;
- **Qualified Domestic Minimum Top-up Tax (QDMTT)** — top-up tax
  paid in the low-taxed jurisdiction itself, which then offsets
  IIR / UTPR elsewhere.

## Jersey's response

Jersey is a **low-tax jurisdiction** under Pillar Two — many
Jersey companies pay 0% under zero-ten. Without action, Pillar
Two would have allowed **foreign parent jurisdictions** to
collect the 15% top-up tax on Jersey-booked income.

Jersey chose to **collect the top-up itself** through the MCIT.
Of the three OECD Pillar Two mechanisms, Jersey adopted two:

| OECD mechanism | What it does | **Jersey adopted?** |
|---|---|---|
| **Qualified Domestic Minimum Top-up Tax (QDMTT)** — implemented in Jersey as the **Domestic Top-up Tax (DTT)** | Low-tax jurisdiction collects its own top-up first, before any other jurisdiction can | **Yes** — this is the heart of MCIT |
| **Qualified Income Inclusion Rule (IIR)** | Parent jurisdiction collects top-up on its low-taxed subsidiaries elsewhere | **Yes** — Jersey applies IIR where a Jersey parent has low-taxed foreign subsidiaries |
| **Undertaxed Profits Rule (UTPR)** | Residual / backstop rule allocating top-up to other group jurisdictions when IIR doesn't reach | **No** — not implemented |

So MCIT is structured around **QDMTT (collected domestically as
DTT) + IIR (for Jersey-parent groups with foreign low-taxed
subsidiaries)**, with no UTPR. The DTT is the practical mechanism
that captures top-up tax on the Jersey-source income of in-scope
groups.

## Who is in scope

The MCIT applies to a constituent entity of an MNE group with:

- **Annual consolidated revenue of EUR 750m or more** in at least
  two of the four preceding fiscal years;
- A **Jersey nexus** (Jersey-tax-resident or Jersey-effectively-
  managed).

**Out of scope**:

- Smaller MNE groups (below the €750m threshold);
- Wholly domestic groups;
- Excluded entities (e.g. governmental entities, international
  organisations, pension funds, certain investment funds with
  pure pooled-investment structures).

## The 15% effective rate

The Pillar Two minimum is **15% on GloBE income**, computed
under GloBE Rules — not the same as Jersey local taxable income.

GloBE income starts from **consolidated financial-accounting
income** and applies prescribed adjustments. Many adjustments
differ from Jersey's local tax computation.

The **effective tax rate (ETR)** is computed for each
jurisdiction. If a Jersey ETR is below 15%, a **top-up** is
charged to bring it to 15%.

## Interaction with zero-ten

For an in-scope MNE group with Jersey companies:

- **Zero-ten** continues to be the Jersey local-tax position
  (0% / 10% / 20% as before);
- **MCIT** is **an additional tax** for in-scope groups — a
  top-up to bring Jersey ETR to 15%;
- The total Jersey tax is therefore **at least 15%** for
  in-scope groups, regardless of the zero-ten classification.

For **out-of-scope** groups and entities (the vast majority of
Jersey companies), zero-ten remains the **only** tax — no
Pillar Two top-up.

## Compliance

In-scope groups must:

- **Register** their Jersey constituent entities;
- **File** Pillar Two returns alongside or separately from
  local Jersey tax returns;
- **Calculate** GloBE income, ETR, and top-up using GloBE
  methodology;
- **Pay** the top-up by prescribed deadlines.

Compliance is **complex** — most in-scope groups use specialist
tax software and advisers.
