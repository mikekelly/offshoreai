# Social Security Contributions — Jersey

Jersey **social security contributions** are imposed under the
**Social Security (Jersey) Law 1974** — separately from income
tax, although increasingly integrated administratively.

## The classes

| Class | Who pays | Basis |
|---|---|---|
| **Class 1** | Employees and their employers | Employment earnings |
| **Class 2** | Self-employed | Self-employed earnings |
| **Class 3** | Voluntary contributors | Voluntary participation |

## Class 1 (employees)

Contributions are payable on **employment earnings**:

- **Employee contribution** — a percentage of earnings up to an
  upper earnings limit;
- **Employer contribution** — a percentage of the same earnings
  base, paid additionally;
- A **higher-rate band** applies above a threshold.

Rates are updated periodically — check Revenue Jersey current
guidance for current figures.

## Class 2 (self-employed)

The self-employed pay contributions on their **business
earnings**, computed on a basis similar to (but distinct from)
income tax.

## Class 3 (voluntary)

Individuals not otherwise contributing (e.g. retired-early
persons keeping their record current) may pay **voluntary
contributions** to maintain entitlement to State Pension and
other benefits.

## What contributions fund

- **States of Jersey old age pension**;
- **Sickness, incapacity, and maternity** benefits;
- **Survivor benefits**;
- **Other social-insurance** benefits managed by the Social
  Security Department.

The fund is **separate** from general taxation revenue.

## Relationship with income tax

Social security and income tax are **legally separate** but
**operationally integrated**:

- **Same earnings base** broadly (with some differences in
  computation);
- **Joint administration** through Revenue Jersey collection
  systems;
- **Coordinated** treatment of expatriates and short-term
  workers.
