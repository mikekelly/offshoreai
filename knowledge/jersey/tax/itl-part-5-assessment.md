# ITL Part 5 — Assessment

Part 5 of the **[Income Tax (Jersey) Law 1961][itl]**
(Articles 22–26) governs **tax assessments** by the
Comptroller.

## Scope

- **Article 22**: income assessment;
- **Article 23**: making assessments without returns;
- **Article 24-25**: amendment powers;
- **Article 26**: assessment notices.

## Mechanics

### Standard assessment

- **Based on return** filed by taxpayer;
- **Comptroller reviews** and assesses;
- **Notice issued** confirming or amending.

### Assessment without return

- Where taxpayer **fails to file**;
- Comptroller can assess **on best information**;
- **Burden** on taxpayer to challenge.

### Amendment

- Comptroller may **amend** within prescribed period;
- **New information** can trigger amendment;
- **Taxpayer challenge** rights preserved.

### Assessment notice

- **Formal notice** to taxpayer;
- **Specifies** tax assessed;
- **Time to pay**;
- **Appeal rights** noted.

## Time limits

- **Comptroller** assessment within prescribed period;
- **Extensions** for specific circumstances;
- **Amendment** windows.

## Practical considerations

- **Maintain records** to support returns;
- **Respond** promptly to Comptroller inquiries;
- **Track** assessment notices and deadlines;
- **Appeal** if necessary within time.
