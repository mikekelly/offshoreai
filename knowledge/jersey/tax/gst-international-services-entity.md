# GST International Services Entity (ISE)

The **International Services Entity (ISE)** regime under the
Goods and Services Tax (Jersey) Law 2007 is a **flat-fee
alternative** to ordinary GST registration for entities
predominantly supplying non-Jersey customers.

## The problem ISE solves

Jersey's **finance industry** primarily serves **non-Jersey
clients**:

- Trust companies administer trusts with foreign beneficiaries;
- Fund administrators administer funds for international
  investors;
- Banks service non-resident depositors and borrowers.

If these entities were subject to **full GST registration**:

- They would have to **track input GST** on Jersey-incurred costs
  and **output GST** on Jersey-source supplies;
- The administrative burden would be substantial;
- The actual GST revenue from finance entities serving foreign
  clients would be **modest** anyway.

The ISE regime provides a **flat-fee compromise**: the entity
pays an annual flat fee in lieu of full GST, and its supplies to
non-Jersey customers are **outside Jersey GST**.

## Who can be an ISE

ISE status is open to entities that:

- Carry on a **prescribed type of business** (financial services,
  trust services, fund administration, certain ancillary
  services);
- Supply **predominantly to non-Jersey customers**;
- Meet other prescribed criteria.

## How ISE works

An ISE:

- Pays a **prescribed annual fee** (the "ISE fee") to Revenue
  Jersey;
- Is **not registered for GST** in the ordinary sense;
- **Charges no GST** on its supplies (to non-Jersey customers
  generally) — these are outside the Jersey GST charge;
- **Cannot reclaim** Jersey GST on its own inputs (this is the
  "trade-off" — flat fee in exchange for no input credits).

For supplies to **Jersey customers**, the ISE may need to charge
GST under specific rules; the regime is designed primarily for
**non-Jersey-customer** business.

## Why this matters

The ISE regime is what allows Jersey's **finance industry** to
operate **without GST drag** on its international business. It
is a key part of Jersey's **competitive position** as a finance
centre — competitive with other low-GST/VAT international
finance jurisdictions.

A trust company subject to full Jersey GST on its trustee fees
would face significant administration costs and potential
client-pricing pressure. The ISE regime removes that friction.
