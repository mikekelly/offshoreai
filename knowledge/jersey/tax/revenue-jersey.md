# Revenue Jersey

**Revenue Jersey** is the Jersey government's **tax authority**.
It is part of the **Treasury and Resources Department** of the
States of Jersey.

## What Revenue Jersey does

Revenue Jersey is responsible for:

- **Administering** the Income Tax (Jersey) Law 1961 (corporate
  and personal income tax);
- **Administering** the Goods and Services Tax (Jersey) Law 2007
  (GST);
- **Collecting** social security contributions (separately from
  income tax, but increasingly integrated administratively);
- **Issuing rulings and guidance** on Jersey tax law;
- **Conducting investigations** into suspected tax fraud or
  non-compliance;
- **Exchanging information** with foreign tax authorities under
  CRS, FATCA, TIEAs, and DTAs;
- **Implementing** the OECD inclusive framework rules including
  Pillar Two (MCIT / DMT).

## The Comptroller of Revenue

The **Comptroller of Revenue** is the senior officer of Revenue
Jersey, with statutory powers including:

- Issuing **tax assessments**;
- Determining **disputed positions** subject to appeal;
- Making **directions** under specific provisions of the tax
  laws;
- Approving **tax-clearance applications** (continuance,
  distribution, etc.).

The Comptroller's decisions are appealable to the **Royal Court**
through specified statutory procedures.

## Relationship with the JFSC

Revenue Jersey and the **Jersey Financial Services Commission**
(JFSC) are **separate authorities**:

- **JFSC** — financial-services regulator and Registrar of
  Companies (corporate law);
- **Revenue Jersey** — tax administration;
- They share **information** where statutorily permitted and
  cooperate operationally (e.g. on economic substance compliance).

## Key contact channels

- **Online portal** for personal and company tax filings;
- **Public guidance** at gov.je/TaxesMoney;
- **Comptroller's office** for ruling applications;
- **Compliance and audit unit** for tax investigations.
