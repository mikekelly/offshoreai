# No Capital Gains Tax — Jersey

Jersey **does not have a capital gains tax**. Gains arising on
the **disposal of capital assets** by individuals or companies
are not, in general, taxed in Jersey.

## What this means

- **Individuals**: gain on sale of shares, bonds, real estate
  (when held as investment), antiques, art — **no Jersey tax**.
- **Companies**: gain on sale of investment assets — **no Jersey
  tax** (the 0% rate under zero-ten applies anyway, but more
  fundamentally, capital gains are outside the income-tax base
  altogether).
- **Trusts**: capital gains realised by Jersey-resident trusts
  are **not Jersey-taxed**.

## The income / capital distinction

The crucial qualifier: tax-free only **if the gain is genuinely
capital**. **Trading profits**, even on real estate or shares,
are **income** and are taxed:

| Activity | Treatment |
|---|---|
| Investment in real estate; sale after years of holding | **Capital gain** — tax-free |
| Property dealer flipping multiple properties | **Trading profit** — 20% income tax |
| Long-term shareholding by an individual | **Capital gain** — tax-free |
| Stockbroker's trading-book profit | **Trading profit** — taxable |
| Family company gives shares to next generation | **No tax** (no CGT, no IHT) |

The boundary is **fact-sensitive**. Repeated, organised, short-
holding-period activity in any asset class can be characterised
as trading. Revenue Jersey applies broadly the same **"badges of
trade"** as English tax law:

- Length of ownership;
- Frequency of similar transactions;
- Supplementary work undertaken on the asset;
- Circumstances of acquisition and sale;
- Motive (acquired with intent to resell?);
- Method of finance.

## Property development

Schedule A income-tax provisions in the Income Tax Law tax
**property-development profits** at 20%. Holding land as a
long-term investment and disposing of it is generally **not**
taxed, but **development for sale** is treated as a trade and
fully taxed.

## Why this matters internationally

The absence of Jersey CGT is a **major attraction** for:

- **Investment funds** structured as Jersey companies — fund
  exits are tax-free at the Jersey level;
- **Private equity deal vehicles** — exits attract no Jersey
  tax;
- **Family wealth planning** — share dispositions can be made
  free of Jersey CGT cost.

But foreign tax may still apply:

- **Non-resident sellers** may face their home-jurisdiction CGT;
- **UK-tax-domiciled individuals** are exposed to UK CGT
  regardless of Jersey position;
- **US persons** are exposed to US tax under worldwide-income
  principles.

The Jersey advantage is at the **Jersey level only**; it does not
override foreign tax positions.
