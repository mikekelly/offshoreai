# ITL Part 12 — Personal Allowances and Reliefs

Part 12 (Articles 92–106) governs **personal exemption
thresholds**, **child / spouse allowances**, and various
**personal-tax reliefs**.

## Key provisions

### Exemption thresholds

- **Annual exempt amount** below which no Jersey income tax
  charged;
- **YA 2025 figures**: £20,700 single, £33,200 married;
- **Annual review** in budget statements;
- **Marginal-relief** mechanism for income just above threshold.

### Marginal relief

- **Income just above threshold**: taxed at marginal rate
  bringing total tax burden up gradually;
- **Avoids cliff edge** of full 20% on first £1 above
  threshold;
- **Effective rate** lower than 20% for many marginal-band
  taxpayers.

### Child allowance

- **Per qualifying child** in care;
- **Specific** age and circumstance criteria;
- **Annual increase** with budget reviews.

### Single-parent allowance

- **Additional** for single parents;
- **Specific qualifying** criteria.

### Spouse / civil-partner allowance

- **Married / civil-partnered** taxpayers;
- **Joint or separate** assessment elections;
- **2025 reform** (Schedule 7) changed mechanics.

### Pension / retirement reliefs

- **Pension contributions** deductible up to limits;
- **Retirement annuity** specific rules;
- **Specific** Jersey pension-scheme treatment.

### Specific other allowances

- **Disability**;
- **Specific** medical / caring;
- **Other** statutory reliefs.

## Computation mechanics

### Step-by-step

1. **Determine total income**;
2. **Apply** specific deductions;
3. **Apply** exemption threshold;
4. **Apply** marginal relief if applicable;
5. **Apply** personal allowances;
6. **Calculate** tax at 20% on residual;
7. **Apply** any further reliefs.

### Specific scenarios

- **Standard 20%** taxpayer above threshold;
- **Marginal-relief** taxpayer just above threshold;
- **HVR taxpayer** under bespoke regime;
- **Non-resident** taxpayer with Jersey-source income only.

## 2025 reforms

Schedule 7 changed:

- **Married / civil-partner** taxation moved to independent
  treatment by default;
- **Joint election** still available;
- **Implications** for marriage / civil partnership tax
  planning.

## Practical considerations

For Jersey-resident individuals:

- **Confirm** applicable allowances;
- **Optimise** elections where available;
- **Plan** for pension / retirement contributions;
- **File** personal return with appropriate claims.
