# ITL Article 1 — Charge of Income Tax

Article 1 of the **[Income Tax (Jersey) Law 1961][itl]** is the
**foundation provision**: it charges income tax in Jersey at the
standard rate for the year of assessment.

## What Article 1 covers

Article 1 charges **income tax** for each **year of assessment**:

- On **all properties, profits, gains, or income** described in
  the Schedules (Schedule A — property; Schedule D — trade /
  profession / employment / other);
- At the **standard rate** as fixed for that year (currently
  **20%** — see Article 142 for rate setting);
- Subject to **exemptions, allowances, and reliefs** provided
  elsewhere in the Law.

## Why this matters

Article 1 is the **legal basis** for Jersey income tax. Without
it, no tax is charged. It is the **gateway provision** to the
substantive computation rules elsewhere in the Law.

The 20% standard rate makes Jersey a **moderate-tax**
jurisdiction for individuals — comparable to UK basic rate but
without the higher / additional UK rate bands. For companies,
the standard rate is overridden by zero-ten (Part 17 — Articles
123 onwards).

## Year of Assessment

The "**year of assessment**" (YA) is the **calendar year** (1
January to 31 December). The current YA at this writing is
**2026**; the most recently completed YA is **2025**.
