# ITL Parts 14, 14A, 14B — Double Taxation Relief

These three Parts govern **relief from double taxation** through
treaties and unilateral provisions.

## Part 14 — DTA Relief (Articles 111-114)

### Scope

- **Bilateral DTA** application;
- **Tax credit** for foreign tax on same income;
- **Treaty-modified** rates and rules.

### Jersey's DTA network

- **UK** (most important);
- **Guernsey, Isle of Man** (Crown Dependency DTAs);
- **Cyprus, Estonia, Luxembourg, Malta** (EU partners);
- **Hong Kong, Singapore, UAE, Qatar** (Asian / Middle Eastern);
- **Various** others.

See [`../international/dtas.md`](../international/dtas.md) for
detail.

### Operation

- **Foreign tax** on income;
- **Jersey tax** on same income;
- **DTA-prescribed** relief through credit or exemption;
- **Tax authorities** cooperate on issues.

## Part 14A — Unilateral Foreign-Income Relief (Articles 114A-114C)

### For qualifying companies

- **Specific Jersey companies**;
- **Foreign-source income**;
- **Unilateral relief** in absence of DTA;
- **Avoids** double taxation where treaty unavailable.

### Mechanics

- **Foreign tax** paid;
- **Jersey** allows credit against Jersey tax on same income;
- **Subject to** specific conditions.

## Part 14B — Unilateral Employment Income Relief (Articles 114D-114E)

### For employment income

- **Jersey resident** with foreign employment income;
- **Foreign tax** paid on it;
- **Jersey** allows credit;
- **Specific** computational rules.

### Common scenarios

- **Jersey-resident** working in UK office for foreign employer;
- **Income** taxed in both jurisdictions absent relief;
- **Part 14B** prevents double taxation.

## Pillar Two integration

For large MNE groups under Pillar Two:

- **MCIT** (Multinational Corporate Income Tax) interacts;
- **15% minimum** rate;
- **Specific** allocation rules;
- **Coordination** with DTA / unilateral relief.

## Practical considerations

### For individuals

- **Claim** unilateral or DTA relief for foreign-source income;
- **Documentation** of foreign tax paid;
- **File** appropriate Jersey return supporting claim.

### For companies

- **Larger** unilateral relief regime;
- **Substance** considerations for some claims;
- **Specific** computational rules.

### Cross-border tax planning

- **Identify** all jurisdictions taxing;
- **Apply** highest-relief mechanism;
- **Document** for both Jersey and foreign tax authorities;
- **Coordinate** with foreign tax advisers.
