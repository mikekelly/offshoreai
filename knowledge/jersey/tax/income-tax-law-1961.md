# Income Tax (Jersey) Law 1961 — Statute Overview

The **Income Tax (Jersey) Law 1961** (ITL 1961) is the
**principal income-tax statute** for Jersey, governing both
**corporate** and **personal** income tax. It has been heavily
amended since 1961 and remains the foundation of Jersey income
taxation.

## Why this Law matters

ITL 1961 is the single most important Jersey tax statute. It:

- **Charges income tax** at the prescribed rate (20% by default
  for individuals; 0% / 10% / 20% for companies under zero-
  ten);
- **Defines** what counts as income (Schedule A property income,
  Schedule D trade / profession / employment / other);
- **Provides exemptions**, allowances, and reliefs;
- **Implements** the **zero-ten** corporate tax regime through
  Articles 123–123S;
- **Provides** for double-tax relief, anti-avoidance, collection,
  and enforcement.

## Reading the Law

ITL 1961 is **dense and heavily amended**. Key navigation tips:

- **Charge of tax** is in Article 1 (the foundation);
- **Total income** definition is in Article 4A;
- **Corporate tax regime** (zero-ten) is in Part 17 (Articles
  123–123S);
- **Schedule A** (property income) and **Schedule D** (trade /
  employment / other) are the two main income-classification
  schedules;
- **Personal allowances** are in Part 12;
- **Loss relief** is in Part 13;
- **Anti-avoidance** is in Article 134A.

## Key concepts

- **Year of Assessment (YA)** — the calendar year on which tax
  is charged;
- **Schedule A, B, C, D, E** — historical classification of
  income types (Jersey retains A and D in active use);
- **Zero-ten** — the corporate tax regime introduced in 2009;
- **Distribution rules** — anti-avoidance for shareholders of
  zero-rate companies (Article 81A–81P);
- **Marginal relief** — personal-tax mechanism for low-income
  individuals.

## Annual changes

The Law is amended **annually** through:

- The **annual Income Tax Law amendment** (typically passed in
  December for the following YA);
- **Budget Statement** measures that take statutory effect
  through the amendment;
- **Regulations** under specific delegated powers.

Practitioners and Revenue Jersey closely track these annual
changes. Materially, much of the substantive structure has been
stable since the 2009 zero-ten reforms.
