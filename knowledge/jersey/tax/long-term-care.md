# Long-Term Care Contribution — Jersey

The **Long-Term Care (Jersey) Law 2012** introduced a dedicated
**Long-Term Care (LTC)** scheme funded by a separate contribution
collected through the income-tax mechanism.

## What LTC funds

The LTC scheme funds **long-term care** for Jersey residents
requiring it — primarily elderly care including:

- **Care home placements**;
- **Home-based care packages**;
- **Specialist long-term care services**.

It is a **social-insurance** scheme — contributions during
working life fund care benefits later in life.

## The LTC contribution

The LTC contribution is:

- A **percentage** of taxable income (similar to a small extra
  income-tax band);
- **Collected through** the income-tax return / PAYE system by
  Revenue Jersey;
- **Distinct from** but operationally **alongside** the 20%
  income tax;
- **Distinct from** social security contributions under the
  1974 Law.

## Who pays

Generally:

- **Jersey-resident individuals** with taxable income above
  prescribed thresholds;
- **All age groups** during working life (the eligibility for
  care benefits is age- and need-based, separate from
  contribution liability).

Specific exemptions and reliefs may apply — check Revenue
Jersey current guidance.
