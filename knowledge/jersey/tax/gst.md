# Goods and Services Tax (GST) — Jersey

Jersey's broad-based consumption tax is the **Goods and Services Tax
(GST)**, introduced by the **Goods and Services Tax (Jersey) Law 2007**
and administered by [Revenue Jersey](./revenue-jersey.md). GST is similar
in mechanism to UK VAT and to other VAT-style taxes, but with a much
narrower coverage of zero-rated and exempt categories, a lower rate,
and a specialised regime — the **International Services Entity (ISE)**
— for finance-services businesses supplying non-Jersey clients.

## The rate — 5%

> GST is charged at 5% on the majority of goods and services supplied
> in Jersey for local use, including imports.
>
> — [Government of Jersey][gov-gst]

The standard rate is **5%**. There is no separate higher or
intermediate rate; supplies are taxed at 5%, **zero-rated** (in scope,
but at a 0% rate, with input-tax recovery), or **exempt** (outside the
charge, no input-tax recovery).

GST has applied at 5% since the rate was increased from 3% to 5% in
2011. The rate is not embedded in the long title of the Law; check the
current rate against Revenue Jersey before assuming.

## Who charges GST

A business that is **registered for GST** charges GST on its taxable
supplies of goods and services in Jersey. Registered businesses
collect GST from customers, recover input tax on their GST-bearing
purchases, and pay the **net** GST to Revenue Jersey on a **quarterly**
filing cycle. ([Government of Jersey][gov-gst])

A business that is **not registered** does not charge GST and cannot
recover input tax. Registration is mandatory above a turnover
threshold and optional below it; the current threshold figure should
be verified against Revenue Jersey's business-GST pages before relying
on this file for an advice point.

## What is zero-rated or exempt

GST has a relatively narrow set of carve-outs compared to UK VAT.
The principal categories that are **not** taxed at the 5% standard
rate are:

- **Exports of goods** and most cross-border services to overseas
  customers — zero-rated.
- **Certain finance and insurance supplies** — exempt.
- **Housing-related supplies** governed by specific orders made under
  the GST Law — typically exempt.
- **Healthcare and education** supplies of the kinds carved out by the
  GST (International Services Entities) (Jersey) Regulations and
  related orders — exempt.

Detailed carve-outs are set out in the GST Law and its orders; the
Revenue Jersey "What GST is taxed on" page is the friendly summary.
([Revenue Jersey][what-taxed])

## International Services Entity (ISE) regime

Jersey's offshore-finance industry is built on services supplied to
non-Jersey clients. To avoid GST appearing as a deadweight cost on
those services (it would not be deductible against the foreign
client's home VAT/GST system), Jersey provides a fixed-fee election
called the **International Services Entity (ISE)** regime.

An ISE pays a **flat annual fee** in lieu of GST on supplies it makes
to non-Jersey customers and on supplies it receives where it would
otherwise have to self-account; the trade-off is that the ISE neither
charges GST nor recovers input GST in respect of those supplies.
Trust company businesses, fund services businesses, JFSC-regulated
investment businesses and similar are typical ISE registrants.

The mechanics, current annual ISE fee, and eligible-entity definitions
are dealt with in
[gst-international-services-entity.md](./gst-international-services-entity.md).
*(That file is a stub at this revision.)*

## Imports

Imports of goods into Jersey are subject to GST on entry. Goods
imported through the UK and then onward to Jersey are *not* covered
by UK VAT once they arrive in Jersey, because Jersey is outside the
UK VAT area; instead they are subject to Jersey GST.

## Filing

A GST-registered business files quarterly returns and pays the net
GST due to Revenue Jersey via the customs portal at
[customs.gst.gov.je](https://www.customs.gst.gov.je/). Late returns
and underpayments carry penalties under the GST Law.

## What GST is *not*

- Not a VAT in the EU sense — Jersey has never been within EU VAT,
  and the GST Law is a Jersey-domestic statute rather than an
  implementation of an EU directive.
- Not progressive — the 5% rate is flat across all standard-rated
  supplies.
- Not a substitute for [stamp duty](./stamp-duty.md) on land
  transactions, which is a separate charge under separate Laws.
