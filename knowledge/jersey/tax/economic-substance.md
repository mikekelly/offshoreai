# Economic Substance — Jersey

Jersey's **economic substance** regime requires Jersey-resident
companies carrying on certain **relevant activities** to have
**real economic substance** in Jersey — proportionate to the
income they book here.

## Origin

The regime was introduced in response to **EU Code of Conduct
Group** scrutiny and **OECD BEPS** standards that demanded
low/no-tax jurisdictions ensure substance behind income.

The principal Jersey legislation is:

- **Taxation (Companies — Economic Substance) (Jersey) Law 2019**
  — the substantive substance regime;
- **Articles 143A–143B** of the Companies Law — companies-law
  enforcement (strike-off / winding-up).

## Who is in scope

The regime applies to a **Jersey-tax-resident company** that
carries on any of the **relevant activities**:

- **Banking**;
- **Insurance**;
- **Fund management**;
- **Financing and leasing**;
- **Headquarters** business;
- **Shipping**;
- **Holding company business** (pure equity-holding);
- **Intellectual property** business;
- **Distribution and service centre** business.

Trusts, partnerships, and foundations are **not in scope** of
this Law (they may have separate substance considerations).

## The substance test

For each relevant activity, the company must:

- Be **directed and managed** in Jersey;
- Have **adequate employees / physical presence / expenditure**
  in Jersey, proportionate to the level of relevant-activity
  income;
- Carry on **core income-generating activities** (CIGAs) in
  Jersey.

CIGAs are activity-specific. For banking, they include taking
deposits, providing credit. For insurance, underwriting and
reserving. For fund management, investment decisions and
client interaction.

For **pure equity-holding** companies, the test is **reduced** —
they need only meet the basic Jersey companies-law obligations
(directors, registered office, statutory filings) and have
adequate people and premises to discharge those duties.

## Reporting and assessment

In-scope companies must:

- **Self-assess** their substance position annually;
- **Report** through the corporate tax return whether they have
  met the substance test;
- **Provide** prescribed information (employees, premises,
  expenditure, CIGAs);
- Be subject to **Revenue Jersey audit**.

## Consequences of failure

Failure of the substance test triggers a graduated response:

| Failure | Consequence |
|---|---|
| First failure | Spontaneous information exchange with relevant foreign tax authorities; civil financial penalty |
| Second consecutive failure | Higher penalty; continued information exchange |
| Persistent failure | Companies Law enforcement: Minister application under Article 143A; possible strike-off or wind-up under Article 143B |

The **information-exchange consequence** is often the most
significant — failing the substance test can trigger spontaneous
notification to the tax authority of the parent company's home
jurisdiction, with foreign-tax consequences potentially
out-weighing any Jersey enforcement penalty.

## Relationship with zero-ten

Substance compliance is **a condition** for the zero-ten regime
to work as intended:

- **0% rate** companies that fail substance face significant
  enforcement consequences (foreign-tax exposure on the income
  that would otherwise be tax-free in Jersey);
- The substance regime is, in effect, the **price** of zero-ten —
  the genuine economic activity needed to justify low-tax
  treatment in the eyes of international tax policy.
