# Eligible Investor Fund

The **Eligible Investor Fund** is a Jersey fund regime
positioned **between the Expert Fund and Listed Fund** regimes —
for funds investing **eligible investors** under specific
criteria, with a streamlined disclosure regime.

## Headline features

- **Eligible investor** definition broadly comparable to the
  Expert Fund category but with slightly different threshold
  positioning;
- **Regulated by the JFSC** with appropriate disclosure
  document;
- **Designed for institutional and high-net-worth** investor base;
- **Streamlined supervision** relative to public funds.

## Eligible investor criteria

The "eligible investor" definition draws on:

- **Net-worth** tests;
- **Investment-knowledge / experience** tests;
- **Minimum subscription** thresholds;
- **Institutional-investor** categories.

## When to use

The Eligible Investor Fund tends to be used where:

- The investor base does not quite fit the **expert-investor**
  criteria;
- A more **institutional-friendly** disclosure regime is needed;
- The structure benefits from JFSC oversight without the public-
  fund weight.

In practice, the choice between JPF / Expert / Eligible / Listed
is driven by investor mix, marketing strategy, and fund-manager
preference. Sponsors typically choose the **lightest regime**
that captures their investor base.
