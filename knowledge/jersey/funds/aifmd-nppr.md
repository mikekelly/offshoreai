# AIFMD and NPPR — Marketing Jersey AIFs into the EU/UK

The **EU Alternative Investment Fund Managers Directive (AIFMD)**
regulates **alternative investment fund managers (AIFMs)**
marketing into the EU. Jersey, as a non-EU jurisdiction, accesses
EU markets through the **National Private Placement Regime
(NPPR)** of individual member states.

## The legal anchors

Two pieces of legislation underpin the regime for Jersey:

- **AIFMD Article 42** — the EU directive provision that
  authorises individual EU member states to permit a non-EU AIFM
  to market a non-EU AIF to professional investors in their
  territory, subject to minimum conditions (registration with the
  home regulator, AIFMD-equivalent oversight, cooperation
  arrangements between the home and host regulators, and
  compliance with AIFMD's transparency obligations). **Article 42
  is the legal basis for NPPR**; every NPPR notification a Jersey
  manager makes into Germany, France, Netherlands, etc. is
  ultimately an Article 42 filing under that member state's
  implementing legislation.
- **AIFMD Article 21** — the depository requirements. For each
  AIF marketed under Article 42, the AIFM must ensure a single
  depository is appointed to perform cash monitoring, safekeeping
  of financial instruments, and ownership verification. For Jersey
  AIFs marketed via NPPR, the depository typically sits in Jersey
  under AIFMD-equivalent arrangements; see *The depository
  question* below.
- **The Alternative Investment Funds (Jersey) Regulations 2012**
  — Jersey's domestic implementation that gives the JFSC the
  regulatory framework to certify Jersey AIFs and AIFMs as
  AIFMD-equivalent for NPPR purposes. Sits alongside the JFSC's
  Code of Practice for Alternative Investment Funds.

## What AIFMD requires

For a **Jersey AIF** to be marketed to **EU professional
investors** under Article 42 / NPPR:

- The AIFM must register with the JFSC under the **Alternative
  Investment Funds (Jersey) Regulations 2012**;
- The AIFM must demonstrate **substantive AIFMD-equivalent**
  compliance (capital adequacy, risk management, conflicts of
  interest, depository arrangements per Article 21, transparency
  to investors, regulatory reporting);
- The AIF can then be **notified** to the relevant EU member
  state's regulator under that member state's Article 42 / NPPR
  implementation;
- The **NPPR-approved AIF** can be marketed to that member
  state's professional investors.

## NPPR vs full AIFMD passport

- **NPPR** — country-by-country private-placement approval; not
  EU-wide; subject to each member state's specific NPPR rules
  (Germany and France have stricter rules than some others).
- **Full AIFMD passport** — EU-wide marketing right available to
  EU-AIFM-managed EU AIFs; **not currently** available to Jersey
  AIFMs.

The "Third Country Passport" extension to non-EU jurisdictions
was proposed but has not been implemented.

## Jersey's competitive position

Even without the full passport, Jersey is a **strong AIF
domicile**:

- **AIFMD-equivalent regulation** through the JFSC;
- **NPPR access** to most major EU markets through country-by-
  country notification;
- **UK marketing** through the UK's own NPPR (Brexit having
  somewhat eased the regulatory mapping);
- **Lower cost** than EU-domiciled AIFs;
- **Tax efficiency** under zero-ten regime.

## The depository question

AIFMD requires AIFs to have a **depository** with prescribed
custody and oversight functions. Jersey allows for **lighter
depository** functions for non-EU-marketed funds, with **AIFMD-
equivalent** depository arrangements required for EU-marketed
AIFs through NPPR.

## Brexit and the UK

Post-Brexit, the UK has its own **UK NPPR regime** parallel to
the EU NPPR. Jersey AIFs market into the UK under this regime,
which is broadly cooperative with the Jersey position.
