# CIFL 1988 Article 22 — NAV Calculation

Article 22 governs **Net Asset Value (NAV)** calculation for
Jersey public funds.

## Frequency

NAV calculated per prospectus:

- **Daily** (open-ended liquid funds);
- **Weekly** (some hedge funds);
- **Monthly** (some PE / less liquid);
- **Quarterly** (real estate, illiquid);
- **Specific** per fund design.

## Methodology

NAV calculation:

- **Total assets** at fair value;
- **Less** total liabilities;
- **Result** divided by **units outstanding** for **per-
  unit NAV**;
- **Per-class** NAV for multi-class funds.

## Asset valuation

Different asset types:

### Listed securities

- **Closing price** typically;
- **Mid-market** in some cases;
- **Bid-side** for redemption-priced;
- **Specific** valuation methodology.

### Non-listed securities

- **Fair value** estimation;
- **Valuation models** (DCF, comparable companies);
- **Independent valuer** for material illiquid assets;
- **Specific** Jersey valuation standards.

### Real estate

- **Periodic** independent appraisal;
- **Specific** valuation methodology;
- **Specific** Jersey practices.

### Other assets

- **Specific** valuation per asset type;
- **Specific** Jersey rules.

## Pricing errors

If NAV miscalculation:

- **Material errors** require remediation;
- **Specific** Jersey rules on error thresholds;
- **Compensation** to affected investors;
- **Specific** reporting to JFSC;
- **Specific** procedural rules.

## NAV transparency

- **Investors** receive periodic NAV statements;
- **Specific** reporting cadence;
- **Specific** electronic reporting common;
- **NAV publication** for some fund types.

## Independence

- **Administrator** typically calculates NAV (independent of
  manager);
- **Specific** oversight by depositary / custodian;
- **Audit** of NAV calculation methodology.
