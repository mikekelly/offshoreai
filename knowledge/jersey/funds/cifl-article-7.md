# CIFL 1988 Article 7 — Permit Required for Functionary

Article 7 governs **permits for functionaries** — service
providers to CIFL public funds.

## The provision

A person shall not act as a **functionary** of a public fund
unless they hold a **permit** under the CIFL or are otherwise
authorised.

## Who needs a permit

The Article 7 permit covers functionaries to **CIFL public
funds**. For most other configurations, the equivalent
authorisation is **fund services business (FSB) registration**
under the Financial Services (Jersey) Law 1998.

In practice:

- **Public fund functionaries** — CIFL permit + FSB
  registration (typically both, or a hybrid);
- **Non-public fund functionaries** — FSB registration under
  FSL Article 9;
- **JFSC** treats the two regimes consistently in practice
  through cross-referenced application processes.

## Permit categories

The permit specifies:

- The **role** (manager, trustee, custodian, administrator,
  investment manager);
- The **type of fund** that can be served;
- **Conditions** specific to the holder.

## Application

Application includes:

- **Identity** and **constitutional** documents of the
  applicant;
- **Business plan** — funds proposed to be served, roles
  proposed;
- **Capital** appropriate to functionary scale (typically a
  multiple of operating costs);
- **Principal persons** — fit and proper;
- **Resources** — Jersey staff, systems, premises;
- **AML / CFT** programme;
- **Conduct rules** acknowledgment;
- **Compliance** function;
- **Auditor** engagement;
- **Fees**.

## Substantive grant

The JFSC grants a permit if satisfied that:

- The applicant is **fit and proper**;
- **Principal persons** are fit and proper;
- The applicant has **adequate capital**;
- The applicant has **adequate systems** and **controls**;
- It is **in the public interest**.

## Conditions

The JFSC typically imposes conditions including:

- **Scope** of authorised activities;
- **Capital** maintenance;
- **Reporting** obligations;
- **Senior management** approval for material changes.

## CIFL Code of Practice for Functionaries

In addition to the Law, functionaries comply with the **CIFL
Code of Practice** addressing:

- **Governance** and senior management;
- **Conflicts of interest**;
- **Custody / depositary** standards;
- **Trading and execution** standards;
- **Outsourcing**;
- **Conduct of business**.

## Continuing obligations

A permitted functionary must:

- **Maintain** the conditions of permit;
- **Submit** annual returns;
- **Notify** material changes (principals, ownership, scope);
- **Cooperate** with JFSC supervision;
- **Pay** annual fees.

## Refusal, suspension, revocation

The JFSC may:

- **Refuse** for failure to meet conditions;
- **Suspend** for breaches or pending investigation;
- **Revoke** for material breaches or loss of fit-and-proper.
