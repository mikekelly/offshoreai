# CIFL 1988 Part 1 — Preliminary

## Article 1 — Interpretation

Key definitions:

- **"Collective investment fund"** — defined in Article 2;
- **"Certificate"** — JFSC authorisation to operate a public
  fund;
- **"Permit"** — JFSC authorisation for a functionary;
- **"Functionary"** — manager, trustee, custodian,
  administrator, investment manager, or other defined role
  serving the fund;
- **"Manager"** — entity responsible for operating the fund;
- **"Trustee"** — for unit-trust funds, the entity holding
  fund assets in trust;
- **"Custodian"** — the entity holding fund assets in
  custody;
- **"Administrator"** — the entity providing administrative
  services;
- **"Unit"** — a participation interest in the fund;
- **"Unit-holder"** — an investor in the fund;
- **"Prospectus"** — the offer document;
- **"Public fund"** — a fund marketed to the public
  (triggering the CIFL certificate requirement).

## Public vs non-public funds

The CIFL governs **public funds** (marketed to retail / public
investors).

**Non-public funds** are governed by:

- The **Financial Services (Jersey) Law 1998** for the
  **fund services business** providing services to the fund;
- The **Order** establishing the specific non-public fund
  regime (Expert, Eligible Investor, Private Fund, etc.).
