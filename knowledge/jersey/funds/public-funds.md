# Public Funds — Jersey

**Public Funds** under the **Collective Investment Funds (Jersey)
Law 1988** are funds offered to the **general public** or with
**broad distribution** — distinct from the private / professional
/ expert / eligible funds covered by lighter regimes.

## Headline features

- **Full JFSC permit** for the fund itself under the CIF Law;
- **Full FSB permits** for all service providers (administrator,
  manager, custodian) under FS(J)L 1998;
- **Prospectus** meeting prescribed requirements;
- **Ongoing public-disclosure** regime;
- **Retail-investor protection** through structural and conduct
  rules.

## When to use

Public Funds are used for:

- **Retail-distributed funds** — funds offered to general
  consumers;
- **Funds requiring broad public marketing** in jurisdictions
  with restrictive private-placement rules;
- **UCITS-style funds** where public-fund standing is needed for
  marketing access.

## Service providers

A Public Fund needs **regulated service providers** under
FS(J)L 1998 / FSB permits:

- **Manager** — investment-management decisions;
- **Administrator** — accounting, NAV calculation, transfer
  agency;
- **Custodian** — asset safekeeping;
- **Registrar** — register of unitholders / shareholders;
- **Auditor** — independent audit.

Each of these is JFSC-regulated independently of the fund's own
CIF permit.

## Prospectus regime

The prospectus must include:

- **Investment objective and policy**;
- **Risk factors**;
- **Fees and expenses**;
- **Tax disclosure**;
- **Subscription and redemption procedures**;
- **Service provider details**;
- **Audited historical financials** (for established funds);
- Other prescribed disclosures.

The prospectus is **JFSC-reviewed** before publication.
