# CIFL 1988 Article 6 — Grant of Certificate

Article 6 sets out the **substantive conditions** for grant of
a CIFL certificate.

## The provision

The JFSC may grant a certificate if satisfied:

- The proposed **functionaries** are appropriately permitted /
  registered;
- The **constitutional documents** comply with the Law and
  CIFL Code;
- The **prospectus** discloses all material information;
- The **investment policy and restrictions** are appropriate;
- The **fee structure** is fair and clearly disclosed;
- The **risk warnings** are adequate;
- **Investor protection** measures are in place
  (redemption mechanism, NAV calculation, etc.);
- The **audit** arrangements are adequate;
- The proposed **AML / CFT** procedures meet JFSC standards;
- It is **in the public interest** to grant the certificate.

## CIFL Code of Practice

Beyond the statutory criteria, the JFSC applies the **CIFL
Code of Practice**:

- Detailed **prospectus** requirements;
- **Valuation** methodologies;
- **NAV calculation** standards;
- **Pricing** errors and remediation;
- **Distribution** rules;
- **Conduct of business** for fund operators;
- **Outsourcing** standards.

The Code is **enforceable** and treated as the principal
operational standard.

## Tiering for retail vs sophisticated investors

The CIFL distinguishes between fund types:

### Recognised Funds

- **Highest** retail standard;
- Subject to **detailed regulation**;
- Includes **UCITS-equivalent** funds;
- Marketable across borders subject to mutual recognition.

### Other public funds

- Subject to **standard CIFL** regulation;
- Designed for **wider retail** distribution;
- **Reduced** but still substantial requirements.

## Conditions on certificate

The JFSC may impose **conditions** including:

- **Specific investment restrictions**;
- **Leverage** limits;
- **Concentration** limits;
- **Risk reporting** requirements;
- **Distribution** restrictions;
- **Investor categorisation** requirements.

## Practical considerations

- Public fund certification is a **substantial undertaking** —
  months of pre-application engagement, detailed application,
  prospectus drafting;
- **Most Jersey funds are non-public** — the public fund
  regime is less commonly used than Expert / Eligible Investor
  / Private Fund;
- **UCITS-equivalent** distribution from Jersey is limited
  given Jersey's non-EU status — most Jersey public funds are
  for non-EU distribution or for accredited / institutional
  EU investors.
