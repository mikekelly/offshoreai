# Listed Fund

A **Jersey Listed Fund** is a fund whose **shares (or equivalent
interests) are admitted to listing on a recognised stock
exchange** — typically the **LSE Main Market**, **AIM**,
**Euronext**, **TISE**, or **NASDAQ**.

## Headline features

- **Closed-ended** typically (open-ended listings are less
  common);
- **Stock-exchange listed** for public secondary trading;
- **JFSC-regulated** as a Jersey fund;
- **Continuing disclosure** under stock-exchange rules;
- **Eligible for inclusion** in certain investor portfolios that
  require listed status.

## When to use

The Listed Fund regime is used for:

- **Investment trusts** and **closed-ended investment companies**
  listed on the LSE;
- **REITs** with Jersey domicile (Jersey REIT structures listed on
  the LSE are common);
- **Speciality investment vehicles** in real estate, infrastructure,
  alternative credit.

The combination of:

- **Jersey corporate / tax flexibility**;
- **Stock-exchange listing** giving liquid secondary market access;
- **Familiarity** to UK and international investors;

makes Jersey a popular choice for **listed-fund domicile**.

## Disclosure regime

Listed funds typically follow:

- The **JFSC's CIF Law 1988** framework for the fund itself;
- The **stock exchange's** listing rules and continuing obligations;
- **Prospectus rules** in the listing jurisdiction (e.g.
  UK Prospectus Regulation, applied via local rules);
- **Market-abuse** rules of the listing jurisdiction.

The fund's **Jersey-domicile aspects** are governed primarily by
Jersey law; its **listing aspects** by the listing-jurisdiction
rules.
