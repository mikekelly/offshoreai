# CIFL 1988 Part 2 — Certificates and Permits (residual)

The principal Part 2 provisions (Articles 4, 6, 7) have their
own deep files. This cluster covers Articles 5, 8-12.

## Article 5 — Application for certificate (fund)

The application for a fund certificate includes:

- **Constitutional documents** (articles, trust deed, LP
  agreement);
- **Prospectus**;
- **Investment policy and restrictions**;
- **Management arrangements** (manager, investment manager);
- **Functionary details** (trustee / depositary / custodian,
  administrator, auditor);
- **Fee structures** and **expense recovery**;
- **Subscription / redemption** mechanism;
- **NAV** calculation methodology;
- **Reporting** to investors;
- **AML / CFT** procedures;
- **Documentation** of consents from functionaries;
- **Fees** prescribed.

## Article 8 — Conditions of permit

The JFSC may impose conditions on a functionary permit:

- **Scope** of activity;
- **Capital** requirements;
- **Reporting** obligations;
- **Operational** restrictions;
- **Senior management** approval requirements for material
  changes.

## Article 9 — Variation

A permit holder or certificate holder may apply to vary:

- **Scope** of activity / fund;
- **Conditions** of authorisation;
- **Principal persons**;
- **Other** authorisation details.

Variations are subject to **JFSC approval** and may require
fresh fit-and-proper assessment, capital review, or other
substantive review depending on materiality.

## Article 10 — Suspension

The JFSC may **suspend** a certificate or permit:

- For **breach** of conditions;
- **Pending** investigation;
- For **public-interest** reasons;
- For **material** prudential or conduct concerns.

A suspended fund cannot:

- **Issue new units**;
- **Process redemptions** (subject to specific JFSC directions);
- **Operate normally**;
- And must comply with **emergency directions** issued by the
  JFSC.

## Article 11 — Revocation

Revocation grounds include:

- **Material** breach of conditions;
- **Failure** to remedy breaches;
- **Loss** of fit-and-proper of principals;
- **Adverse** prudential or conduct findings;
- **Public-interest** reasons.

Revocation triggers **wind-down** — orderly redemption or
liquidation under JFSC supervision.

## Article 12 — Appeals

Decisions are subject to **appeal** to the Royal Court:

- Within prescribed period;
- On grounds of procedural fairness, error of law, or
  unreasonableness;
- Court can confirm, vary, or set aside;
- Further appeal to **Court of Appeal**.
