# CIFL 1988 Part 6 — Enforcement and Miscellaneous

Part 6 consolidates enforcement, administrative matters, and
citation.

## Civil penalties

The JFSC may impose **civil penalties** for:

- **Operating** a public fund without certificate;
- **Acting** as a functionary without permit;
- **Breach** of conditions;
- **Breach** of continuing obligations;
- **Material** prospectus misstatements;
- **Failure** to comply with directions.

Penalties are calibrated and substantial in serious cases.

## Public statements

The JFSC may issue **public statements** on enforcement action,
findings, and warnings.

## Criminal offences

Specific criminal offences include:

- **Unlicensed** fund operation (Article 4);
- **Acting** as functionary without permit;
- **False** statements in applications / returns;
- **Specific** market-conduct offences (insider dealing,
  market manipulation engaging market-abuse regime).

## Appeals

Decisions are appealable to the **Royal Court**.

## Regulations and Orders

The Minister can make **regulations** and **Orders** for:

- **Forms and fees**;
- **Specific fund categories** (Expert, Eligible Investor,
  Private Fund — each established by Order);
- **Specific functionary categories**;
- **Implementation** of international standards (IOSCO, AIFMD
  alignment, FATF for AML).

## Non-public fund regimes

The CIFL Article 4 / 7 regime is for **public funds**. Most
Jersey funds use **non-public** regimes established by Order:

- **Expert Fund Guide** — accredited investors;
- **Eligible Investor Fund Guide** — sophisticated
  investors;
- **Jersey Private Fund Guide** — closed-circle (≤50)
  investors;
- **Listed Fund Guide** — exchange-listed funds;
- **Unregulated Fund Guides** — unregulated eligible investor
  / exchange-traded fund regimes (limited use).

These regimes have their own statutory bases (often the FSL
1998 for the FSB / manager) and Order-based fund-level
treatment.

## JFSC Codes of Practice

Codes of Practice apply alongside the Law:

- **CIFL Code of Practice for Certified Funds**;
- **Code of Practice for Fund Services Business**;
- **Code of Practice for Public Funds**;
- **Code of Practice for Jersey Private Funds**.

These Codes are **enforceable** and treated as expected
standards.

## Citation

The Law is cited as the **"Collective Investment Funds
(Jersey) Law 1988"**.

It came into force on **1 January 1988** and has been amended
many times to keep pace with:

- **UCITS** alignment opportunities;
- **AIFMD** transitional provisions (NPPR access);
- **FATF** AML / CFT standards;
- **JFSC** supervisory enhancements;
- **Jersey Private Fund** regime introduction (2017) and
  refinements.

## International alignment

The JFSC works with:

- **IOSCO** for international standards;
- **EU regulators** for AIFMD-NPPR cross-border distribution;
- **Other regulators** through MoUs.
