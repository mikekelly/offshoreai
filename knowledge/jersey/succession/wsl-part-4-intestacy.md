# WSL 1993 Part 4 — Intestacy

Part 4 sets out the **intestacy rules** — what happens to the
estate where there is no valid will (or the will fails or is
revoked).

## Article 21 — Spouse / partner first

The surviving spouse / civil partner has **first call**:

### If there are descendants

- Spouse / partner: **fixed legacy** (typically substantial,
  set by Order) PLUS **personal effects** PLUS **half of the
  residue**;
- Descendants: **other half** of the residue.

### If no descendants but other relatives

- Spouse / partner: **fixed legacy** PLUS **all** of the
  residue;
- (Other relatives take nothing from the estate but **légitime
  may apply** if any).

### If no other relatives

- Spouse / partner: **entire estate**.

## Article 22 — Descendants

Where there is **no spouse / partner** (or partner takes only
part):

- **Descendants** take the residue **per stirpes**;
- **Equal shares** between children;
- **Predeceased child's share** flows to that child's
  children;
- **No descendant takes** until all higher-generation members
  in their line accounted for.

## Article 23 — Parents

Where **no spouse and no descendants**:

- **Parents** of the deceased take;
- **Equal shares** between two surviving parents;
- **Single surviving parent** takes the whole;
- **Stepparents** generally not included unless adoptive.

## Article 24 — Siblings (and their descendants)

Where **no spouse, descendants, or parents**:

- **Siblings** of the deceased take;
- **Equal** between siblings;
- **Per stirpes** for predeceased siblings (their children
  take their share);
- **Full** and **half** siblings — Jersey rules vary;
  typically full siblings preferred.

## Article 25 — More remote relatives

Where **no closer relatives**:

- **Grandparents**;
- **Aunts and uncles**;
- **Cousins**;
- **More remote** kindred per progressive class.

## Article 26 — Bona vacantia

If **no kindred** at all:

- Estate passes to **the Crown** (bona vacantia);
- **States Treasury** receives.

## Real property intestacy

For **immovable property in Jersey**:

- **Customary-law** rules apply;
- **Historically**: specific rules favoring eldest son (now
  modified);
- **Modern**: equal division between descendants is the
  default;
- **Specific** customary-law rules for **principal heir** in
  some configurations.

The customary-law overlay on real-property intestacy means
that **Jersey property succession can differ** from the WSL
intestacy framework for movables.

## Choice of trust over intestacy

For **non-controllable family disputes**, a **lifetime trust
settlement** is often preferred:

- **Avoids** intestacy disputes;
- **Allows** more nuanced distribution than fixed intestacy
  rules;
- **Trustee** can adapt to changing circumstances;
- **No probate** for trust assets (no public-record dispute).
