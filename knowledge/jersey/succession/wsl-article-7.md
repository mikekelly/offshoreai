# WSL 1993 Article 7 — Revocation of Wills

Article 7 specifies how a Jersey will may be **revoked**.

## Methods of revocation

A will may be revoked:

### By subsequent will

- **Express revocation** ("I revoke all prior wills");
- **Implied revocation** (subsequent will inconsistent with
  prior);
- **Most common** method of revocation.

### By destruction

- **Physical destruction** of the will;
- **With intent** to revoke;
- **Tearing, burning, defacing**;
- **By the testator** personally (or by another in testator's
  presence and direction).

Destruction **without** revocation intent is not revocation.

### By marriage / civil partnership

- **Generally** revoked by marriage / civil partnership;
- **Unless** the will was made **in contemplation of** the
  marriage;
- **Specific** Jersey rules;
- **Practical** issue for testators who marry / form
  partnerships.

### By specific written act

- **Document** declaring revocation;
- **Specific** Article 5 formalities;
- **Standalone** revocation without new will.

## Effect of revocation

A revoked will is:

- **Invalid** unless **revived**;
- **No** testamentary effect;
- **Earlier wills** not automatically reinstated;
- **Specific** Jersey rules on revival.

## Revival

A revoked will can be **revived** by:

- **Codicil** referring to the revoked will;
- **Re-execution** with Article 5 formalities;
- **Specific** documented revival intent;
- **Not** by destruction of the revoking will.

## Common scenarios

### Marriage and prior will

- **Marriage revokes** the prior will (general rule);
- **Exception**: will made in contemplation of marriage;
- **Practical**: post-marriage will-drafting essential.

### Multiple wills

- **Most recent will** prevails;
- **Earlier wills** revoked unless saved by partial-
  revocation clause;
- **Specific** drafting considerations.

### Codicils

- **Codicil** can revoke specific parts of a will;
- **Cannot** revoke the whole will (use a new will);
- **Specific** Article 10 codicil rules.
