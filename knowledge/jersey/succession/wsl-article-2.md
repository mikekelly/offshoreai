# WSL 1993 Article 2 — Movable / Immovable Distinction

Article 2 establishes the **fundamental classification** that
drives Jersey succession law — the distinction between
**movable** and **immovable** property.

## The provision

For succession purposes:

- **Immovable property** = real property situated in Jersey
  (land, buildings, fixtures attached to land);
- **Movable property** = everything else (chattels, financial
  assets, intangible property);
- The classification determines the **applicable law** for
  succession.

## Why this matters

The distinction is **load-bearing**:

| Type | Applicable law for succession |
|---|---|
| Jersey immovables | Jersey law (WSL + customary law) |
| Foreign immovables | Foreign law of situs |
| Movables (anywhere) | Law of deceased's domicile |

For a **Jersey-domiciled** testator:

- **Jersey** immovables → Jersey succession law;
- **Foreign** immovables → foreign-situs law;
- **Movables** anywhere → Jersey succession law.

For a **foreign-domiciled** testator:

- **Jersey** immovables → Jersey succession law (situs rule);
- **Movables** anywhere → foreign domicile law;
- The estate may be split between Jersey law (for Jersey
  immovables) and foreign law (for all movables).

## Classification rules

### What is immovable

- **Land** in Jersey;
- **Buildings** on Jersey land;
- **Fixtures** attached to Jersey land (subject to detailed
  rules on tenant fixtures, trade fixtures);
- **Easements** and similar rights over Jersey land;
- **Mortgages** over Jersey land in some configurations.

### What is movable

- **Cash and bank accounts** wherever located;
- **Shares and securities** (subject to specific rules for
  shares in real-property companies);
- **Investments** generally;
- **Chattels** (cars, jewellery, art, furniture);
- **Intellectual property** rights;
- **Contractual** rights;
- **Trust interests** generally.

### Borderline cases

- **Shares in a company owning Jersey property** — generally
  movable, though courts will look through in sham cases;
- **Specific securities** linked to land (mortgages, debentures
  secured by real property) — may be re-characterised;
- **Leasehold** — distinction between long-term leases (more
  property-like) and short-term (more contractual).

## Légitime

The **légitime** regime under WSL Part 3 applies to **movable
estate only**:

- Spouse / children's protected shares come out of movables;
- Immovables follow customary-law rules (which can be more
  restrictive in some cases);
- This **bifurcation** complicates testamentary planning.

## International dimension

For **international Jersey clients**:

- **Jersey resident** with **UK property** — UK succession
  applies to UK property (situs rule);
- **UK resident** with **Jersey property** — Jersey succession
  applies to Jersey property (situs rule);
- **Movables** follow domicile — careful domicile analysis
  needed;
- **Trust structures** can be used to **bypass** situs rules
  for movables transferred to the trust (subject to anti-
  avoidance).
