# WSL 1993 Article 14 — Spouse / Partner Légitime

Article 14 establishes the **statutory protected share** for
the **surviving spouse** or **civil partner** — a Jersey form
of **forced heirship**.

## The provision

A surviving spouse / civil partner is entitled to claim a
**légitime** out of the deceased's **movable estate**:

- **One-third** if there are surviving descendants of the
  deceased (children, grandchildren);
- **Two-thirds** if there are no surviving descendants but
  surviving collaterals (siblings, parents);
- **Full** estate if no descendants and no surviving
  collaterals (intestacy-style).

The légitime is **a right**, not a discretionary remedy.

## Key features

### Right, not entitlement

The légitime is **claimed** by the spouse:

- **Not** automatically taken;
- **Can be waived** by the spouse;
- **Claimed within** a prescribed period (typically 6 months
  after grant of probate);
- **Subject to** the deceased's debts and expenses being
  paid first.

### Movable estate only

The légitime applies to **movable property only**:

- **Cash, bank accounts, investments, chattels**;
- **Not** Jersey real property (which has separate customary-
  law rules);
- **Not** foreign real property (situs law applies);
- This creates **complex planning** for estates with
  significant immovable property.

### Calculated on net estate

The légitime is calculated on the **net movable estate**:

- **Total movable assets**;
- **Less** debts and expenses;
- **Less** specific charges (taxes, funeral expenses);
- The **net** value is divided per the légitime fractions.

## Effect on wills

If the will gives the spouse **less than légitime**:

- The spouse can **claim the légitime** in addition to or in
  place of the will gift;
- The will is **abated** proportionately to fund the
  légitime (other beneficiaries receive less);
- **Specific gifts** typically respected if estate sufficient.

If the will gives the spouse **more than légitime**:

- The spouse takes under the will;
- **No** légitime claim needed.

## Effect on intestacy

Where there is **no will**:

- The spouse takes under intestacy rules (Article 21+) which
  typically provide more than légitime for surviving spouse;
- **Légitime** is the minimum entitlement, not a cap.

## Disinheritance

A Jersey-domiciled testator **cannot effectively disinherit**
their spouse from movable estate:

- **Will purporting to disinherit** is ineffective for movables
  beyond the légitime;
- **Different from English law** which allows disinheritance
  subject to discretionary family-provision applications;
- **Important** in cross-jurisdictional planning.

## Lifetime gifts (gifts in vue d'héritage)

Specific rules address **lifetime gifts** to defeat légitime:

- **Gifts** made within a lookback period before death;
- **Treated** as part of the estate for légitime calculation;
- **Lookback** typically several years;
- **Specific** provisions in WSL Articles 16-20.

## Civil partnership

The Civil Partnership (Jersey) Law extended **all marriage-
related rights** to civil partnerships:

- **Légitime** for civil partner same as for spouse;
- **All other** succession rights aligned;
- **Same-sex marriage** also recognised with equal rights.
