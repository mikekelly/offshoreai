# WSL 1993 Part 5 — Conflict of Laws and Miscellaneous

## Article 27 — Domicile

Article 27 codifies the **domicile-based** conflict-of-laws
rule:

- **Movables** governed by **domicile law** at death;
- **Jersey immovables** governed by **Jersey law**;
- **Foreign immovables** governed by **foreign-situs law**;
- See also [`wsl-article-3.md`](./wsl-article-3.md).

## Article 28 — Recognition of foreign wills

Recognition of foreign wills:

- A will is **valid in Jersey** if it complies with the
  formalities of:
  - **Jersey** (Article 5);
  - **Country of execution**;
  - **Country of domicile** (at execution or death);
  - **Country of nationality** (at execution or death);
  - **Country of habitual residence**;
- The **Hague Convention on the Form of Testamentary
  Dispositions** approach;
- **Translation** required for non-English wills.

## Article 29 — Régime matrimonial

Specific provision for **matrimonial-property** issues:

- **Jersey** has limited matrimonial-property regime (separate-
  property approach historically);
- **Foreign** matrimonial regimes can affect estate
  composition (e.g., community-property states);
- **Specific** rules for international couples;
- **Coordination** with foreign-divorce-court ancillary
  relief.

## Article 30 — Citation, Regulations

The Law is cited as the **"Wills and Successions (Jersey) Law
1993"**.

It came into force in **1993** with amendments since,
including:

- **Civil Partnership (Jersey) Law** integration;
- **Equality** provisions extending to all children equally;
- **Specific** procedural updates;
- **Coordination** with international conventions.

The Minister can make **Orders** for:

- **Forms** and **fees**;
- **Specific** procedural requirements;
- **Amount** adjustments for fixed legacies (intestacy
  spouse share);
- **Other** technical matters.

## International planning considerations

### Pre-2025 UK domicile regime

- **UK-domiciled** persons resident in Jersey would have UK
  IHT exposure on worldwide assets;
- **Jersey-domiciled** persons not subject to UK IHT on
  non-UK assets (subject to deemed-domicile rules);
- **Complex** interaction with Jersey succession law.

### Post-2025 UK residence-based IHT

- **UK long-term residents** subject to UK IHT on worldwide
  assets regardless of domicile;
- **Jersey** IHT considerations independent;
- **Coordination** of both regimes essential;
- **Trust structures** may be more attractive given
  residence-based UK regime.

### EU Succession Regulation (Brussels IV)

- Allows **EU testators** to choose either nationality or
  habitual-residence law for succession;
- **Does not apply to Jersey** (not EU);
- **Jersey property** of EU testators still subject to
  Jersey law (situs rule for immovables);
- **Movables** of EU testators subject to chosen succession
  law under Brussels IV.

### Other forced-heirship regimes

For testators from **forced-heirship** jurisdictions
(France, Spain, Italy, Sharia-law countries):

- **Foreign legitima** may apply to foreign movables (if
  foreign domicile);
- **Jersey property** governed by Jersey rules;
- **Conflict** can arise — coordinated planning needed;
- **Trusts** may be used to avoid foreign forced-heirship
  for assets settled into trust (subject to anti-avoidance).
