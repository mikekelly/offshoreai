# WSL 1993 Article 5 — Formalities of Wills

Article 5 sets out the **execution formalities** required for
a valid Jersey will.

## The provision

A will is **validly executed** in Jersey if:

- It is **in writing**;
- It is **signed by the testator** (or by another in the
  testator's presence and by their direction);
- The testator's signature is **made or acknowledged** in the
  presence of **two witnesses** present at the same time; and
- The two witnesses **attest and sign** in the testator's
  presence.

This is **broadly similar** to English law but with specific
Jersey requirements.

## Witnessing requirements

The two witnesses must:

- Be **at least 18** years old;
- Be of **sound mind**;
- **Not be beneficiaries** under the will (any gift to a
  witness is **void**);
- Not be the **spouse / partner** of a beneficiary (similar
  void rule);
- Be **present** when the testator signs or acknowledges;
- Sign **in the testator's presence**;
- Not need to be present when each other signs (though
  typical).

## The witness-beneficiary rule

A gift to a witness or witness's spouse is **void**:

- The **will remains valid**;
- The **specific gift** to the witness fails;
- **Passes** as part of the residue or under intestacy as
  appropriate;
- **Reason**: prevents witness pressure on the testator.

## Holograph wills (Article 6)

In addition to the formal witnessed will, Jersey recognises
**holograph wills**:

- **Entirely handwritten** by the testator;
- **Dated** and **signed** by the testator;
- **No witnesses** required;
- See [`wsl-article-6.md`](wsl-article-6.md).

## Specific scenarios

### Signing by another

If the testator cannot sign (illness, illiteracy):

- Another person may **sign in the testator's presence and by
  direction**;
- **Witnesses** must still attest;
- **Specific recital** in the will indicating the
  circumstances helpful;
- **Greater scrutiny** on undue influence and capacity.

### Mark or thumbprint

A **mark** (X) or **thumbprint** can suffice as "signature":

- **In the testator's presence**;
- **With witness attestation** confirming it's intended as
  signature;
- **Less common** in modern practice but historically
  important for illiterate testators.

### Foreign-executed wills

A will executed abroad:

- May be **valid under Article 5** if Jersey formalities
  followed;
- **Or valid under Article 9** if foreign-jurisdiction
  formalities followed;
- **Multiple recognition routes** available.

### Translation

A will in a **foreign language**:

- **Valid** if executed per Article 5 or Article 9;
- **Translation** required for probate purposes;
- **Certified translation** typically.

## Specific failures

Failures to comply:

- **No writing** — invalid;
- **No signature** — invalid;
- **One witness only** — invalid;
- **Witnesses not present** at same time — challengeable;
- **Witness signature without testator presence** —
  challengeable.

These can be **fatal** to the will. Where a will fails for
formalities, the testator dies **intestate** (or under
previous will if not revoked).
