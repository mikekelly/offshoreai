# WSL 1993 Part 2 — Wills (residual)

Articles 5 (formalities) and 6 (holograph wills) have their
own deep files. This cluster covers Articles 4, 7-13.

## Article 4 — Capacity

A person can make a will if they have **testamentary
capacity**:

- **Sound mind, memory, and understanding** at the time of
  making;
- Understands they are making a will and its effect;
- Understands the **extent** of their property;
- Understands the **claims** of those who might expect benefit;
- **Not affected** by any insane delusion influencing the
  will.

Minimum age:

- **18 years** for general testamentary capacity;
- **Specific exceptions** for soldiers / sailors on active
  service (lower age).

## Article 7 — Revocation

A will may be **revoked**:

- By a **subsequent will** (express or implied revocation);
- By **destruction** of the will with intent to revoke;
- By **marriage / civil partnership** (generally — unless
  the will was made in contemplation of the marriage);
- By **specific written act** declaring revocation.

A revoked will is **invalid** unless **revived** by a later
codicil or by re-execution.

## Article 8 — Mutual wills

**Mutual wills** are wills made by two or more persons:

- With a **shared scheme** for disposition of their property;
- With **agreement** that they will not be unilaterally
  revoked after the death of the first;
- **Enforceable** as a constructive trust on the survivor's
  property to the extent of the agreement;
- **Complex** in practice — proper documentation essential.

## Article 9 — Foreign wills

A will is **valid in Jersey** if it complies with formalities of:

- **Jersey** (Article 5);
- The **country where executed**;
- The **country of the testator's domicile**;
- The **country of the testator's nationality**;
- The **country of habitual residence**;
- (Alternative routes — multiple recognition options).

This follows the **Hague Convention on the Form of
Testamentary Dispositions** approach (which Jersey applies
through the WSL).

## Article 10 — Codicils

A **codicil** is an amendment to a will:

- **Same formalities** as a will;
- **Reads with** the original will;
- Can **revoke** parts of the original;
- Can **add** new provisions;
- **Revival** of revoked wills possible by codicil reference.

## Article 11 — Specific gifts and legacies

Rules for:

- **Specific** legacies (specific identified property);
- **General** legacies (e.g., "£10,000");
- **Residuary** gift (everything else);
- **Specific** legacy fails if the property is no longer
  owned at death (ademption);
- **General** legacy abates if estate insufficient.

## Article 12 — Lapse

A gift **lapses** if:

- The beneficiary **predeceases** the testator;
- The beneficiary **disclaims**;
- The gift is **impossible** to fulfil;
- Specific **statutory exceptions** (substitution for
  descendants in some cases — anti-lapse provisions).

## Article 13 — Will registration

Jersey does not have **compulsory** will registration during
the testator's lifetime, but:

- Wills can be **deposited** with the Judicial Greffe for
  safekeeping;
- **Probate** registration after death is required for the
  will to take effect;
- **Lost wills** can be proved through specific procedures.
