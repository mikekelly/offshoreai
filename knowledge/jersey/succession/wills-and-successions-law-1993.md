# Wills and Successions (Jersey) Law 1993 — Overview

The **Wills and Successions (Jersey) Law 1993 (WSL)** is the
**principal modern statute** governing Jersey succession. It
modernises and codifies key aspects of Jersey succession law
while preserving important **customary-law** elements.

## What WSL covers

- **Wills** — formalities, capacity, types, revocation;
- **Intestacy** — rules where no valid will exists;
- **Légitime** — protected shares for spouse and children;
- **Movable vs immovable** property distinction;
- **Conflict of laws** — which jurisdiction's law applies;
- **Administration** principles (alongside Probate Law).

## Key features

### Movable / immovable distinction

- **Movable property** (personal property) — governed by the
  law of the **deceased's domicile**;
- **Immovable property** (real property) — governed by the
  law of the **situs** (location);
- Jersey-situate **immovable property** is governed by
  **Jersey customary law plus WSL** regardless of testator's
  domicile.

### Légitime (forced heirship)

Jersey law preserves a form of **forced heirship**:

- **Surviving spouse / civil partner** has a guaranteed
  share;
- **Children** have a guaranteed share;
- **Légitime** can be claimed even if the will purports to
  disinherit;
- **Movable estate** only — légitime does not apply to
  immovable property (which has its own customary rules);
- **Quantum** depends on family composition.

This is **distinctive** from English law (which generally
allows testamentary freedom subject to family-provision
applications under the Inheritance (Provision for Family and
Dependants) Act 1975 in England — but Jersey's légitime is a
**statutory right**, not just a discretionary remedy).

### Will formalities

- **Holograph wills** — handwritten and signed by testator;
- **Witnessed wills** — typed / printed with two-witness
  signature;
- **Mutual wills** — recognised in defined circumstances;
- **Nuncupative wills** — limited recognition (oral wills in
  specific emergency situations).

### Intestacy

Where there is **no valid will** or the will fails:

- **Spouse / civil partner** has first call;
- **Children** in succession;
- **Other relatives** in defined order;
- **Movable / immovable** property may follow different rules.

## Domicile rules

A Jersey-domiciled testator:

- Whose **movables** are subject to Jersey law worldwide;
- Whose **immovables in Jersey** are subject to Jersey law;
- Whose **foreign immovables** are subject to foreign-situs
  law.

A foreign-domiciled testator with **Jersey real property**:

- Foreign domicile determines movables (not Jersey law's
  concern except for movables in Jersey);
- Jersey law determines Jersey real property succession.

This creates **planning complexity** for international
families with Jersey property.

## Interaction with trusts

A common Jersey-planning device:

- **Settle assets into trust** during lifetime;
- Trust **survives** the settlor's death;
- Trust property is **not** part of the settlor's estate;
- **Avoids** légitime / intestacy / probate process;
- Subject to **avoidance** rules (sham, fraudulent transfer
  in some cases).

## Modernisation

The WSL has been amended periodically:

- **Civil partnerships** added (alongside marriage);
- **Specific** technical updates for digital wills, etc.;
- **Procedural** modernisations for probate;
- **Coordination** with international conventions.
