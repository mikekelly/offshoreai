# WSL 1993 Article 15 — Children's Légitime

Article 15 provides for the **children's protected share** —
the second pillar of Jersey's légitime regime.

## The provision

Surviving **children** (descendants) of the deceased are
entitled to a **collective légitime** of:

- **One-third** of the deceased's **movable estate** if the
  deceased is survived by a spouse / civil partner;
- **Two-thirds** if no surviving spouse / civil partner;
- **Per stirpes** distribution within the children's group
  (the share of a predeceased child passes to that child's
  own children).

## Children covered

The légitime applies to:

- **Legitimate children**;
- **Illegitimate children** (post-amendment — historically
  not, but modern Jersey treats them equally);
- **Adopted children** (under Jersey adoption);
- **Children of dissolved marriages / partnerships** (still
  entitled);
- **Stepchildren** generally **not** unless adopted or
  specifically provided for.

## Distribution within the group

Within the children's class:

- **Equal** shares between children of the same generation;
- **Per stirpes** to descendants of predeceased children;
- A predeceased child's share **flows to** their own
  surviving children;
- **No** representation if no descendants of the predeceased
  child.

### Example

Deceased survived by:
- Spouse;
- Three children: A, B, C;
- B has predeceased, leaving two children B1 and B2.

Calculation:
- Spouse légitime: 1/3 of movable estate;
- Children's légitime: 1/3 of movable estate;
- Within children's third:
  - A: 1/3 of children's third (= 1/9 of total);
  - B's representation: 1/3 of children's third, shared
    between B1 and B2 (each 1/18 of total);
  - C: 1/3 of children's third (= 1/9 of total).

The remaining 1/3 of the estate (the "free portion") can be
distributed per the will or, in default, by intestacy.

## Hotchpot

For children's légitime calculation:

- **Lifetime gifts** to children may be **brought into
  hotchpot** — added to the calculation and credited against
  the child's share;
- Purpose: ensure **equal treatment** of children, taking
  account of lifetime advancements;
- **Specific** rules in WSL on what constitutes a hotchpot
  gift.

## Disinheritance

Like spouse légitime, a Jersey-domiciled testator **cannot
effectively disinherit children** from movable estate:

- A will giving children less than légitime is **abated**;
- Children **claim** the légitime;
- **Specific** waivers can be made by children (e.g., adult
  child waiving in favour of surviving parent), subject to
  reasonable formality.

## Effect on planning

The children's légitime affects:

- **Wills** — must respect minimum allocations;
- **Lifetime planning** — hotchpot considerations for gifts
  to children;
- **Trust planning** — trusts settled before death are
  generally **outside the estate** for légitime purposes
  (subject to anti-avoidance);
- **Foreign-jurisdiction planning** — coordination with
  foreign forced-heirship regimes (which may overlap or
  conflict).

## Comparison

| System | Children's protected share |
|---|---|
| Jersey (movable) | Statutory légitime (1/3 with spouse) |
| England | Discretionary family-provision (no fixed share) |
| France | Réserve héréditaire (1/2-3/4 depending on number) |
| Spain | Legitima (2/3 strictly protected) |
| Most civil-law | Strong forced-heirship regimes |
| Most common-law | Testamentary freedom subject to family provision |

Jersey **sits between** common-law and civil-law approaches.
