# WSL 1993 Part 3 — Légitime (residual)

Articles 14 (spouse / partner) and 15 (children) have their
own deep files. This cluster covers Articles 16-20.

## Article 16 — Quantum

Calculation details:

- **Net movable estate** is the base;
- **Debts and expenses** paid first;
- **Specific** funeral / administration costs deducted;
- **Net amount** subject to légitime fractions per Articles
  14, 15;
- **Valuation date** typically date of death.

## Article 17 — Claim procedure

A légitime claim must be made:

- **Within** the prescribed period (typically **6 months**
  from grant of probate);
- **In writing** to the personal representative;
- **Specifying** the claim amount;
- **Subject** to extension by Court in some cases.

## Article 18 — Lifetime gifts (hotchpot)

Lifetime gifts may be **brought into hotchpot**:

- **For children's légitime** calculation;
- **Gifts** within a defined lookback period;
- **Spousal gifts** typically not in hotchpot (different
  regime);
- **Specific exemptions** for small gifts, customary gifts.

The hotchpot rule ensures **equal treatment** of children who
received pre-death gifts versus those who didn't.

## Article 19 — Waivers and renunciations

A beneficiary may **waive** légitime:

- **In writing**;
- **Voluntary** and **informed**;
- **Subject to** any specific procedural requirements;
- **Common** for adult children renouncing in favour of
  surviving parent;
- **Cannot** be done before the testator's death (anticipated
  waivers void).

## Article 20 — Anti-avoidance for légitime

Specific anti-avoidance:

- **Lifetime gifts** specifically intended to defeat légitime
  may be **clawed back**;
- **Sham transactions** can be set aside;
- **Trust settlements** with retained powers may be
  re-characterised in some cases;
- **Time limits** apply.

But:

- **Genuine trust settlements** with **no retained powers**
  are typically **outside the estate** for légitime purposes
  — this is a key Jersey planning device.

## Légitime in practice

For Jersey-domiciled testators:

- **Légitime is a significant constraint** on will-making
  freedom;
- **Planning** typically respects légitime to avoid post-
  death disputes;
- **Lifetime trust settlement** is the principal route to
  avoid légitime constraints (but with careful structuring);
- **International dimension** complicates planning where
  forced-heirship regimes also apply abroad.

For non-Jersey-domiciled testators with Jersey property:

- **Légitime does not apply** to movables (foreign domicile);
- **Légitime does not apply** to immovables (immovables
  follow customary-law rules, which can be **more
  restrictive**);
- **Different planning** approach needed.
