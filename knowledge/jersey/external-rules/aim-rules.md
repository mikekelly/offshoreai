# LSE AIM Rules — Jersey Interaction

**Bridge file.** Documents how the London Stock Exchange's **AIM
Rules for Companies** and **AIM Rules for Nominated Advisers**
(NOMADs) apply to Jersey-incorporated AIM-listed companies and
where they interact with the
[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991](../companies/index.md). For the
substance of any Rule, cite the current LSE publication
directly.

## The AIM market and its rule-set

AIM (formerly the Alternative Investment Market) is the London
Stock Exchange's growth market. It is **not** a regulated market
for the purposes of the EU Markets in Financial Instruments
Directive — it is a UK multilateral trading facility (MTF). That
distinction matters for several Jersey-touching points:

- **Prospectus Regulation reach is narrower** for AIM admission
  than for Main Market admission.
- The **AIM Rules** (LSE-issued, not FCA-issued) are the
  principal admission and ongoing-obligation rule-book.
- The **MAR** (Market Abuse Regulation) regime nonetheless
  applies to AIM issuers — see [`mar.md`](./mar.md).

A **Jersey-incorporated** company is a common AIM TopCo
structure — used to obtain Jersey CJL 1991 governance with UK
market access. See
[`../use-cases/founder-entrepreneur/why-jersey-topco.md`](../use-cases/founder-entrepreneur/why-jersey-topco.md).

## Two parallel rule-sets

### AIM Rules for Companies

Govern the obligations of the **issuer**. Cover admission
eligibility, ongoing disclosure, related-party transactions,
substantial transactions, reverse takeovers, share dealings,
suspension and cancellation, and Class 1–3 transaction
classification thresholds.

### AIM Rules for Nominated Advisers

Govern the obligations of the **NOMAD** — the LSE-approved firm
acting as the issuer's regulatory gateway. Every AIM company
must retain a NOMAD at all times. NOMAD loss without immediate
replacement triggers suspension. The NOMAD's principal duty is
**to the LSE** (not to the issuer), which materially shapes the
NOMAD-issuer relationship.

## Load-bearing Rules for Jersey-incorporated AIM issuers

The agent should know to direct the user to the LSE Rule for
the substantive position. The Rule numbers below are stable
recurring references practitioners use:

### Rule 11 — General disclosure of price-sensitive information

Issuers must, without delay, disclose any new developments
which, if made public, would be likely to lead to a substantial
movement in the price of its AIM securities. This is the AIM
counterpart to the inside-information disclosure obligation
under [MAR](./mar.md); they sit alongside each other.

### Rule 13 — Related-party transactions

Transactions between the AIM issuer and a related party
(directors, substantial shareholders, associates) above a
**5%-class-tests** threshold require the NOMAD to confirm the
terms are **fair and reasonable** as far as shareholders are
concerned. Notification with prescribed content is required.

For a Jersey-incorporated AIM company, Rule 13 sits alongside
the Jersey-domestic position on directors' duties in
[`../companies/directors-duties.md`](../companies/directors-duties.md)
and the **Article 76 CJL 1991** disclosure-of-interest regime.
A related-party transaction can be both an AIM Rule 13 trigger
and a CJL 1991 disclosure event.

### Rule 14 — Reverse takeovers

A transaction qualifying as a reverse takeover (acquisitions
meeting 100%+ class-test thresholds, or fundamental changes in
business) requires **shareholder approval** in general meeting
and re-admission of the enlarged group to AIM. This is the
trigger that drives the Jersey side back to **AGM mechanics
under the CJL 1991** (notice, quorum, voting thresholds) — see
[`../companies/agm.md`](../companies/agm.md) and
[`../companies/notice-of-meetings.md`](../companies/notice-of-meetings.md).

### Rule 21 — Restrictions on deals (closed period)

Directors and applicable employees must not deal in AIM
securities during a **close period** before the announcement of
results or while the issuer is in possession of unpublished
price-sensitive information. Sits alongside the MAR closed-
period regime (which is the more developed source) and any
issuer-specific dealing policy.

### Rule 26 — Website disclosure

AIM companies must maintain a free, publicly accessible website
containing prescribed information — including (notably for
Jersey-incorporated companies) the **corporate governance code
the company has adopted** and how it complies with it. This is
the practical mechanism by which the
[QCA Code](./qca-code.md) or
[UK Corporate Governance Code](./uk-corporate-governance-code.md)
adoption becomes operationally visible.

### Schedule Two — Admission document content requirements

The AIM admission document (analogous to a prospectus but
governed by AIM Rules, not by the Prospectus Regulation) has
prescribed content. Schedule Two is the canonical reference.
For a Jersey-incorporated admission, the content includes
prescribed disclosure of Jersey-law features — registered office,
governing law of the constitutional documents, shareholder
rights, and substantive Jersey CJL 1991 mechanics that differ
from English company law.

## The NOMAD relationship in a Jersey context

The NOMAD's continuing responsibility includes ensuring that
the AIM company **complies with the AIM Rules**. For a Jersey-
incorporated company, the NOMAD's familiarity with Jersey law
becomes part of the NOMAD's competence to act — Jersey CJL 1991
mechanics on distributions, related-party transactions,
shareholder rights and director duties are within scope of the
NOMAD's monitoring.

This is part of the strategic rationale for Jersey-incorporated
AIM TopCos: the NOMAD ecosystem is familiar with Jersey
companies as a routine admission profile.
