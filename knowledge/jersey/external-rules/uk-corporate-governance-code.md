# UK Corporate Governance Code — Jersey Interaction

**Bridge file.** The **UK Corporate Governance Code** is
published by the Financial Reporting Council (FRC) and is the
default governance code for **premium-listed companies on the
LSE Main Market**. For the substance of any principle or
provision, cite the FRC publication directly.

## When the UKCGC applies to a Jersey-incorporated company

The Code applies on a **comply or explain** basis to **premium-
listed** issuers on the LSE Main Market — i.e., companies whose
shares are admitted under Listing Rule 9 to the premium segment
of the Official List. The Listing Rules (Disclosure Guidance and
Transparency Rules — DTR 7.2 — see [`dtr.md`](./dtr.md)) require
disclosure of compliance with the Code.

For a **Jersey-incorporated** company, the UKCGC is in scope if
the company is premium-listed on the LSE Main Market. **It is
not the default code for AIM-listed** Jersey-incorporated
companies — see [`qca-code.md`](./qca-code.md) — but a Jersey
AIM TopCo *may* adopt the UKCGC voluntarily, typically where the
investor base or the scale of the business makes UKCGC
expectations appropriate.

## Comply or explain

The UKCGC operates on a strict **comply or explain** basis: a
company either (a) reports that it has complied with each
provision throughout the year, or (b) reports specific non-
compliance with reasons. The Code's principles and provisions
are more prescriptive than the
[QCA Code](./qca-code.md)'s "apply and explain" structure.

## Five sections

The UKCGC covers (in the most recent FRC structure):

1. **Board leadership and company purpose.**
2. **Division of responsibilities** — chair/CEO split, non-
   executive composition, board balance.
3. **Composition, succession and evaluation** — appointments,
   evaluation, tenure.
4. **Audit, risk and internal control.**
5. **Remuneration.**

Each section comprises principles and detailed provisions. The
detailed provisions are where comply-or-explain is most
demanding — e.g. specific provisions on the proportion of
independent non-executives, the lead non-executive role, board
evaluation cycle, audit committee composition, and remuneration
structure.

## Interaction with Jersey CJL 1991

As with the [QCA Code](./qca-code.md), the UKCGC is a
**governance overlay** on top of the CJL 1991 statutory and
common-law framework. **Article 74 CJL 1991 directors' duties**
(see
[`../companies/directors-duties.md`](../companies/directors-duties.md))
continue to apply unchanged. The Code adds disclosure-driven
governance expectations; it does not change underlying legal
duties.

Where the UKCGC's prescriptive provisions interact with Jersey-
constitutional-documents drafting (e.g. board composition
requirements, audit committee independence, executive
remuneration disclosure), the constitutional documents are
typically drafted **so that the UKCGC provisions can be met
without constitutional amendment** — flexible articles, broad
board powers, audit-committee terms-of-reference set at the
board level rather than entrenched.

## Premium listing implications

A premium listing of a Jersey-incorporated company on the LSE
Main Market is an unusual structure. The combination yields:

- UKCGC application (this file);
- Full [Prospectus Regulation](./aim-rules.md) reach (vs the
  lighter AIM regime);
- [DTR](./dtr.md) periodic-reporting and major-shareholder
  obligations;
- [MAR](./mar.md) market-abuse regime;
- [UK Takeover Code](./uk-takeover-code.md) jurisdiction (the
  Code applies to Jersey-incorporated companies admitted to
  trading on a UK regulated market).

The decision to premium-list rather than to use AIM is driven
by investor-base expectations and capital-raising profile, not
by Jersey-side factors.

## Verification status

Draft quality. The UKCGC is updated periodically by the FRC;
the most recent revisions have moved toward more demanding
expectations on board purpose, culture, and ESG-aligned
disclosure. **Verify against the current FRC publication** for
the precise text of any principle or provision.
