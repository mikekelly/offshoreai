# External Rule-Sets — Jersey Interaction Map

## What this section is about

The Jersey corpus is the canonical source for Jersey law — the
[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991](../companies/index.md), the
[Trusts (Jersey) Law 1984](../trusts/index.md),
the [Financial Services (Jersey) Law 1998](../financial-regulation/financial-services-jersey-law-1998.md),
the JFSC Codes of Practice, the AML/CFT Handbook, and so on.

But Jersey-incorporated entities — especially Jersey-incorporated
companies whose shares are admitted to trading on a UK market —
routinely **collide with external rule-sets** that the corpus
does **not** wikify: the UK Takeover Code, the LSE's AIM Rules,
the EU/UK Market Abuse Regulation, the QCA Code, the UK Corporate
Governance Code, the FCA Handbook, and the UK Disclosure Guidance
and Transparency Rules.

This section contains **bridge files** — short, link-rich files
that document, for each external rule-set:

- when it applies to a Jersey-incorporated entity (the
  applicability test);
- the canonical primary-source URL (we never wikify the foreign
  rule);
- the **interaction points** with Jersey law — where the foreign
  rule intersects the [Companies (Jersey) Law 1991](../companies/index.md),
  the [JFSC framework](../financial-regulation/index.md), or the
  [AML/CFT regime](../aml-cft/index.md);
- the typical questions the agent should be able to answer about
  the interaction.

Bridge files are **not authoritative on the external rule**. An
agent answering a question about, say, the precise wording of UK
Takeover Code Rule 9 should cite the external source directly,
not the Jersey bridge file. The bridge file is for orientation
and for interaction-point identification.

## Why this section exists

Three use cases that the doctrinal corpus cannot answer on its
own:

1. **AIM-listed Jersey plc operational reality.** Most Jersey-
   incorporated plcs that list use AIM. AIM Rules, MAR, DTRs,
   QCA/UKCGC governance choices, and FCA Handbook touchpoints
   are part of the day-to-day operating reality of those
   companies. An agent serving a wealth-group chair needs to be
   able to say "your Jersey-CJL-1991 obligations interact with
   X external rule in Y way."
2. **Public M&A involving Jersey-incorporated companies.** The
   UK Takeover Code (the City Code) applies to many Jersey-
   incorporated companies whose shares are traded on a UK
   market. The Code's Rule 9 mandatory-offer threshold, Rule 21
   frustrating-action restrictions, and irrevocables practice
   are load-bearing for any Jersey-incorporated public-M&A
   conversation.
3. **Cross-border regulated business.** A Jersey-licensed
   investment business with a UK subsidiary or a UK-regulated
   FCA-authorised affiliate needs to navigate the FCA Handbook
   on the UK side and the JFSC Codes on the Jersey side. The
   bridge file documents the touchpoints.

## What this section is *not*

- **Not a substitute for the external source.** Every bridge
  file links to the canonical publication and explicitly tells
  the agent to cite the source, not the bridge file, on matters
  of foreign-rule substance.
- **Not a place to wikify foreign law.** If you find yourself
  writing a long article-level breakdown of the UK Takeover Code
  in this section, stop — that work belongs upstream of the
  Jersey corpus, not inside it. The Jersey corpus stays Jersey-
  focused.
- **Not a generic "external sources" file.** Cross-border tax
  treaties live under
  [`../international/`](../international/index.md). Foreign
  trust-recognition issues live under
  [`../trusts/`](../trusts/index.md) and
  [`../international/`](../international/index.md). This section
  is specifically for **non-Jersey rule-sets** that **govern
  Jersey-incorporated entities or their operations** by virtue
  of those entities' listing venue, regulator footprint, or
  cross-border activity.

## Bridge files

### Listing venue, market conduct, governance

### [`uk-takeover-code.md`](./uk-takeover-code.md)
The UK Panel on Takeovers and Mergers ("the Panel") and the City
Code on Takeovers and Mergers ("the Code"). Applicability to
Jersey-incorporated companies; Rule 9 mandatory offer; Rule 21
frustrating-action restrictions; irrevocables; the Jersey
Takeovers Panel interaction (see also
[`../companies/takeovers-panel-2009-index.md`](../companies/takeovers-panel-2009-index.md)).

### [`aim-rules.md`](./aim-rules.md)
The London Stock Exchange's AIM Rules for Companies and AIM
Rules for Nominated Advisers. AIM admission, ongoing disclosure
obligations, related-party transaction Rules (AIM Rule 13),
reverse takeovers (AIM Rule 14), and the NOMAD relationship — as
they apply to Jersey-incorporated AIM-listed issuers.

### [`mar.md`](./mar.md)
The EU/UK Market Abuse Regulation. Inside information
identification and disclosure, insider lists, PDMR dealing
notifications, and the closed-period regime — for Jersey-
incorporated issuers admitted to trading on UK regulated markets
and on AIM.

### [`qca-code.md`](./qca-code.md)
The Quoted Companies Alliance Corporate Governance Code — the
most commonly adopted governance code for AIM-listed companies.
Adoption / "apply and explain" mechanics; ten principles; how
AIM Rule 26 disclosure interacts.

### [`uk-corporate-governance-code.md`](./uk-corporate-governance-code.md)
The UK Corporate Governance Code — typically adopted by Main
Market premium-listed issuers; relevant where a Jersey-
incorporated company is premium-listed in London.

### [`dtr.md`](./dtr.md)
The UK Disclosure Guidance and Transparency Rules (DTRs). DTR 4
periodic reporting; DTR 5 vote-holder notifications; DTR 6
information disclosure; DTR 7 audit committee composition — as
they apply to Jersey-incorporated issuers.

### Regulated-business interaction

### [`fca-handbook-jersey-touchpoints.md`](./fca-handbook-jersey-touchpoints.md)
The UK Financial Conduct Authority Handbook — touchpoints
relevant to Jersey-licensed groups with UK FCA-authorised
subsidiaries or affiliates. SUP, COBS, CASS, SYSC, MAR, FUND, and
the Senior Managers and Certification Regime as they intersect
Jersey supervisory expectations.

## How an agent should use these files

| The question is about… | Read |
|---|---|
| Does the UK Takeover Code apply to a Jersey-incorporated bid target? | [`uk-takeover-code.md`](./uk-takeover-code.md) |
| AIM Rule 26 disclosure obligation for a Jersey-incorporated AIM company | [`aim-rules.md`](./aim-rules.md) |
| Inside-information disclosure for a Jersey-incorporated AIM issuer | [`mar.md`](./mar.md) |
| Choice between QCA Code and UKCGC for a Jersey AIM TopCo | [`qca-code.md`](./qca-code.md) and [`uk-corporate-governance-code.md`](./uk-corporate-governance-code.md) |
| Major-shareholder notification thresholds for Jersey-incorporated AIM issuer | [`dtr.md`](./dtr.md) (DTR 5) |
| Group governance where a UK FCA-authorised subsidiary sits under a Jersey parent | [`fca-handbook-jersey-touchpoints.md`](./fca-handbook-jersey-touchpoints.md) |

Always pair the bridge file with the underlying Jersey-law
position from the doctrinal corpus. E.g. "the UK Takeover Code
applies via [`uk-takeover-code.md`](./uk-takeover-code.md);
internally, the Jersey-incorporated bidder's board duties are
governed by Article 74 CJL 1991 in
[`../companies/directors-duties.md`](../companies/directors-duties.md)."
