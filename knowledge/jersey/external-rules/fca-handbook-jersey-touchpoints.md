# FCA Handbook — Jersey Touchpoints

**Bridge file.** The UK Financial Conduct Authority (FCA) is the
UK conduct regulator for financial services. Its **Handbook** is
the principal rule-book governing FCA-authorised firms. **Jersey
is a separate jurisdiction with a separate regulator
([JFSC](../financial-regulation/jfsc.md))**, so the FCA Handbook
does **not** directly apply to most Jersey-licensed businesses.

This file documents the **touchpoints** where the FCA Handbook
intersects Jersey-licensed businesses or Jersey-incorporated
entities operating in the UK. For the substance of any
sourcebook provision, cite the FCA Handbook directly.

## When the FCA Handbook touches a Jersey-licensed group

There are six common patterns:

### 1. UK FCA-authorised subsidiary of a Jersey group

A Jersey-licensed wealth manager or fund services business with
a **UK FCA-authorised subsidiary** (an investment adviser,
discretionary manager, AIFM, MiFID firm, or insurance
intermediary). The UK subsidiary is **wholly within FCA
Handbook scope**; the Jersey parent is **wholly within JFSC
scope**. The interaction lives at the **group governance** level
— consolidated supervision, intra-group exposures, outsourcing
between parent and subsidiary.

The relevant FCA sourcebooks for the UK subsidiary will
typically include: **SYSC** (governance), **COBS** (conduct of
business), **CASS** (client assets), **MIPRU/IFPR** (prudential),
and **MAR** (market conduct — distinct from the EU MAR
Regulation; see [`mar.md`](./mar.md)).

### 2. Cross-border marketing of Jersey funds into UK retail/professional investors

A Jersey AIF marketed into UK investors typically operates under
the **UK NPPR (National Private Placement Regime)** —
substantially preserved post-Brexit. The marketing firm (which
may be FCA-authorised or relying on a permission of another
kind) operates within the FCA's promotion-of-funds framework.

The principal FCA sources: **COLL** (collective investment
schemes), **FUND** (alternative investment funds), and **PERG**
(perimeter guidance). Promotion to retail involves further
restrictions under **COBS 4** financial promotions rules.

### 3. Jersey-incorporated issuer admitted to UK trading

Where a Jersey-incorporated company has shares admitted to
trading on a UK market, several FCA-administered regimes apply:

- The [DTRs](./dtr.md);
- [UK MAR](./mar.md);
- Prospectus Regulation (where capital is being raised);
- The Listing Rules (for premium-listed Main Market issuers).

The FCA's role here is as the **competent authority** for these
regimes; the issuer is not "FCA-authorised" as a firm, but is
subject to FCA-administered issuer disclosure and conduct rules.

### 4. Senior managers crossing the JFSC / FCA boundary

The **Senior Managers and Certification Regime (SM&CR)** in the
UK is the FCA's senior-accountability framework. Senior managers
of a UK FCA-authorised subsidiary fall within SM&CR. Where the
same senior manager has a JFSC-recognised role at the Jersey
parent (under the
[principal-persons regime](../financial-regulation/licensing.md)),
the individual has **two parallel regulatory profiles**.
Disciplinary or fit-and-proper action in either jurisdiction is
typically communicated to the other under MoUs.

### 5. Outsourcing to / from Jersey

Where a UK FCA-authorised firm **outsources** to a Jersey-
licensed service provider (a common pattern for trust company
services, fund administration, custody-equivalent services),
the FCA's **SYSC 8** outsourcing rules apply on the UK side.
On the Jersey side, the JFSC outsourcing expectations under the
relevant Code of Practice apply
([cop-tcb-index.md](../financial-regulation/cop-tcb-index.md),
[cop-fsb-index.md](../financial-regulation/cop-fsb-index.md),
[cop-ib-index.md](../financial-regulation/cop-ib-index.md)).
Both regimes have to be satisfied; in practice the contracts and
controls satisfy both simultaneously.

### 6. Sanctions and financial-crime cooperation

Both the FCA and the JFSC operate within the UK/Channel Islands
sanctions framework, but the Jersey sanctions regime under the
[Sanctions and Asset-Freezing (Jersey) Law 2019](../aml-cft/safl-articles-index.md)
operates **separately**. UK sanctions designations made by OFSI
are not automatically Jersey designations; cross-recognition is
through a separate Jersey process. A Jersey-licensed group with
UK customers has to comply with **both** regimes.

## What this means operationally

A Jersey-licensed group with UK exposure typically operates a
**dual-compliance function**: the Jersey-side compliance team
working to the JFSC Codes of Practice and the AML/CFT Handbook,
and the UK-side compliance team working to the FCA Handbook.

The two regimes overlap heavily in substance (governance,
conduct of business, client assets, outsourcing, anti-money-
laundering) but the two regulators expect **separately
documented and discharged** compliance against their respective
sourcebooks.
