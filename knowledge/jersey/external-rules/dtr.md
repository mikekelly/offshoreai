# UK Disclosure Guidance and Transparency Rules (DTR) — Jersey Interaction

**Bridge file.** The **DTRs** are part of the FCA Handbook
("DTR" sourcebook). They implement (with post-Brexit
modifications) the EU Transparency Directive disclosure regime
and apply to issuers admitted to trading on UK markets. For the
substance of any DTR provision, cite the FCA Handbook directly.

## Scope by listing venue

DTR application varies by listing segment:

- **Premium-listed** issuers on the LSE Main Market — full DTR
  application (DTR 1–7).
- **Standard-listed** issuers on the LSE Main Market —
  application of most DTRs other than DTR 7 governance
  provisions.
- **AIM-listed** issuers — **partial** application: principally
  **DTR 5** (vote-holder notifications) and limited portions of
  DTR 1A/1B. Most DTR 4 (periodic reporting) and DTR 6
  obligations do not apply to AIM in the same way; AIM Rule
  disclosure handles the periodic-reporting role.

For a **Jersey-incorporated** issuer the same listing-segment
test applies — DTR scope is driven by listing venue, not by
issuer incorporation. The most common Jersey-incorporated DTR
touchpoint is **DTR 5** on AIM.

## Load-bearing DTR sourcebooks

### DTR 4 — Periodic financial reporting

Premium-listed issuers must publish:

- An **annual financial report** within four months of the
  financial year-end;
- A **half-yearly financial report** within three months of
  the period-end.

The annual report content includes audited financial statements,
management commentary, directors' responsibility statement, and
(for UKCGC-applicable issuers) the governance compliance
statement under DTR 7.

### DTR 5 — Vote-holder notifications

A person who acquires or disposes of voting rights such that
the percentage of voting rights held crosses **3% or any
integer above 3%** (4%, 5%, …) must notify the issuer **and**
the FCA within prescribed time limits. The issuer must in turn
notify the market.

DTR 5 applies to **AIM-listed** Jersey-incorporated issuers
(unusually — most DTRs do not). It is the principal mechanism
by which **substantial shareholder changes are surfaced** to the
market for AIM companies.

The interaction with the **CJL 1991 register of members** in
[`../companies/register-of-members.md`](../companies/register-of-members.md)
is operational: DTR 5 notifications drive the issuer's awareness
of voting-rights concentrations, which in turn drives the
[UK Takeover Code Rule 9](./uk-takeover-code.md) mandatory-offer
analysis at the 30% threshold.

### DTR 6 — Continuing obligations

Including obligations on the form and dissemination of
regulatory information, language requirements, and the use of
prescribed information channels.

### DTR 7 — Corporate governance

Including the comply-or-explain disclosure obligation for the
**[UK Corporate Governance Code](./uk-corporate-governance-code.md)**
(DTR 7.2), audit committee composition requirements (DTR 7.1),
and diversity reporting (DTR 7.2.8A).

DTR 7 applies most directly to **premium-listed** Jersey-
incorporated issuers; the audit-committee composition
requirements interact with the **Jersey CJL 1991 audit
provisions** (the Part 16 audit regime), though the DTR is the
more demanding source.

## Interaction with MAR

DTR provisions on inside information and dissemination operate
**alongside** [MAR](./mar.md). MAR Article 17 is the primary
inside-information disclosure obligation; DTR 6 supplements
with form and dissemination requirements. For most practical
purposes, an issuer satisfying MAR Article 17 will also satisfy
the relevant DTR 6 requirements through the same RNS workflow.

## Interaction with AIM Rules

For AIM-listed Jersey-incorporated companies, the DTRs are
**narrower in scope** than for premium-listed companies. The
AIM Rules handle the role that DTR 4 plays for Main Market
issuers (interim reports, audited annual accounts), with their
own deadlines and content requirements. DTR 5 vote-holder
notification is the most common AIM-DTR touchpoint.
