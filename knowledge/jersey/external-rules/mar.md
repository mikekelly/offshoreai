# Market Abuse Regulation (MAR) — Jersey Interaction

**Bridge file.** Documents how the **Market Abuse Regulation
(MAR)** applies to **Jersey-incorporated** issuers whose
securities are admitted to trading on a UK regulated market or
on a UK multilateral trading facility (MTF) such as AIM. For the
substantive Articles of MAR, cite the FCA Handbook MAR sourcebook
or the underlying EU/UK MAR text directly.

## Two parallel MAR regimes (EU MAR / UK MAR)

Following Brexit, the EU Market Abuse Regulation (Regulation
596/2014) was retained as **UK MAR** with FCA-supervised
application. The EU MAR continues to apply to securities admitted
to trading on EU markets. **A Jersey-incorporated company
admitted to a UK market is in scope of UK MAR**; if it is also
admitted to an EU market, it may be in scope of EU MAR
concurrently.

The two regimes are substantively similar but have diverged in
points of detail. An agent answering an MAR question for a
Jersey-incorporated dual-listed company should flag the dual
applicability and verify the specific point against both texts.

## When MAR applies to a Jersey-incorporated company

UK MAR applies where:

- The company's securities are **admitted to trading** (or
  request for admission has been made) on a UK regulated market
  (the LSE Main Market) **or** on a UK MTF (AIM, AQSE Growth
  Market), **or**
- The company's securities are traded on a UK regulated market /
  MTF with its consent.

This captures the typical Jersey-incorporated AIM TopCo — even
though AIM is an MTF rather than a regulated market, MAR's
**Article 2** in-scope provisions extend to MTFs.

## The four load-bearing obligations

### 1. Inside-information disclosure (Article 17)

A listed/MTF-traded issuer must **disclose inside information
that directly concerns it as soon as possible**. The carve-outs
for legitimate-interest deferral, confidentiality conditions,
and the public-misleading test are in Article 17(4)–(5).

For a Jersey-incorporated AIM issuer, the MAR Article 17
obligation sits alongside **AIM Rule 11** (general disclosure of
price-sensitive information) — see [`aim-rules.md`](./aim-rules.md).
The two largely overlap; the practical mechanism is the same
RNS-via-NOMAD process. The MAR obligation is the more
sophisticated source on deferral and confidentiality.

### 2. Insider lists (Article 18)

Issuers must maintain an **insider list** identifying every
person with access to inside information, with prescribed
content (identity, function, date of access, date of cessation).
Lists must be provided to the FCA on request. The list discipline
applies equally to a Jersey-incorporated issuer.

### 3. PDMR dealing notifications (Article 19)

**Persons Discharging Managerial Responsibilities (PDMRs)** and
their **closely associated persons** must notify the issuer and
the FCA of every transaction in the issuer's securities. The
notification deadline is **three business days** from the
transaction. The threshold is a low **EUR 5,000 per calendar
year** before notification requirements apply.

For a Jersey-incorporated AIM issuer, the PDMR concept overlaps
with the CJL 1991 Article 76 directors' disclosure-of-interest
mechanism — see
[`../companies/directors-duties.md`](../companies/directors-duties.md)
and
[`../companies/director-disclosure.md`](../companies/director-disclosure.md).
The two regimes have different purposes: MAR Article 19 is market
transparency; CJL 1991 Article 76 is conflicts-of-interest
governance. Both apply.

### 4. Closed period (Article 19(11))

PDMRs may not deal in the issuer's securities during a **closed
period of 30 calendar days before announcement of an interim
financial report or year-end report**. Limited exceptions exist
(exceptional circumstances, employee share scheme transactions).

The closed period interacts with **AIM Rule 21** restrictions on
deals and any issuer-specific dealing policy.

## Definition of inside information

MAR Article 7 defines inside information as information of a
**precise** nature, **not generally available**, relating
directly or indirectly to the issuer or its securities, which
if it were generally available would likely have a **significant
effect on the price** of the securities.

The "significant effect on the price" element is the **reasonable
investor test** — would a reasonable investor be likely to use
the information as part of the basis of an investment decision.

## Market manipulation and insider dealing offences

MAR also prohibits:

- **Insider dealing** (Article 8) — dealing on the basis of
  inside information;
- **Unlawful disclosure** of inside information (Article 10);
- **Market manipulation** (Article 12) — trades or orders that
  give false or misleading signals, secure prices at abnormal
  levels, employ fictitious devices.

These prohibitions apply to **any person** within the
jurisdictional reach of UK MAR, not just to issuers and
their PDMRs. For a Jersey-incorporated AIM company's directors
and senior managers, dealing in the company's securities on the
basis of unpublished inside information is an MAR offence
regardless of any AIM-specific or Jersey-specific rule.

## Interaction with the Jersey supervisory framework

The **JFSC does not directly supervise MAR compliance** in
relation to securities admitted on UK markets — the FCA does.
But the JFSC does:

- Supervise the **Jersey-licensed investment business** firms
  that advise / deal in the relevant securities;
- Expect Jersey-licensed firms to operate **market-abuse-aware
  surveillance** consistent with their conduct-of-business
  obligations under the
  [Investment Business Code of Practice](../financial-regulation/cop-ib-index.md);
- Cooperate with the FCA under the relevant MoUs on cross-
  border market-abuse investigations.

For a **Jersey-incorporated AIM-listed plc**, the MAR-compliance
function (RNS workflow, insider-list maintenance, PDMR
notifications, closed-period administration) typically sits with
the company secretarial function under the company's UK NOMAD,
not with a Jersey-licensed regulated entity. This is operational
detail rather than regulatory architecture.
