# Police Procedures and Criminal Evidence (Jersey) Law 2003 — Overview

## What the law does

The **Police Procedures and Criminal Evidence (Jersey) Law
2003** (PPCE 2003) is Jersey's principal criminal-procedure
statute. It:

- Regulates **stop and search** powers;
- Regulates **entry** to premises and **search** of
  premises;
- Establishes the **arrest** powers and procedure;
- Governs **detention** at the police station — time
  limits, reviews, conditions;
- Sets the **rights** of detained persons — legal advice,
  notification, silence;
- Regulates **police interviews** — Codes of Practice;
- Provides for **identification** procedures;
- Governs **searches** of persons in custody;
- Provides **safeguards** for vulnerable persons,
  juveniles, non-English speakers.

It is modelled on the **UK Police and Criminal Evidence
Act 1984** (PACE) but with Jersey-specific provisions
including:

- **Recognition** of the role of the **Honorary Police**
  in arrests and detentions;
- **Interaction** with **Parish Hall Enquiries** for
  minor offences;
- **Specific** Jersey procedural safeguards.

## Codes of Practice

The PPCE 2003 is supplemented by detailed **Codes of
Practice** issued by the Minister:

| Code | Topic |
|---|---|
| **Code A** | Stop and search |
| **Code B** | Searches of premises |
| **Code C** | Detention, treatment, and questioning of persons |
| **Code D** | Identification of persons |
| **Code E** | Audio recording of interviews |
| **Code F** | Video recording (where applicable) |
| **Code G** | Arrest |
| **Code H** | Detention for serious offences (extended) |

These Codes are **binding** in the sense that:

- **Police officers** must have regard to them;
- **Breach** can lead to evidence being excluded by the
  court (Article 76 PPCE-equivalent — the discretion to
  exclude evidence obtained in breach of procedural
  safeguards);
- **Disciplinary** consequences for officers in serious
  cases.

## Key concepts

### Arrest

Police may arrest where:

- **Reasonable grounds** for suspecting the person is
  committing, has committed, or is about to commit an
  offence;
- **Statutory** arrest powers for specific offences;
- **Common-law** arrest in narrow circumstances (e.g.
  breach of the peace).

On arrest, the officer must:

- **Inform** the person that they are under arrest;
- **State** the offence suspected;
- **Caution** the person (formal caution wording);
- **Provide** identification (uniform / warrant card).

See [`arrest.md`](arrest.md).

### Detention

Once at the police station:

- **Custody officer** (an officer of sufficient rank)
  decides whether detention is lawful;
- **Reasons** for detention recorded in the **custody
  record**;
- **Initial limit**: **24 hours** without charge;
- **Extension** by **police inspector** up to 36 hours
  for serious offences;
- **Royal Court warrant** required for further extensions
  (up to 96 hours in the most serious cases).

**Regular reviews** of detention are mandatory — typically
every 6 hours initially, less frequently thereafter.

### Rights of the detained person

The detained person has the right to:

- **Free legal advice** from a solicitor (own choice or
  duty solicitor);
- **Inform someone** of arrest (family / friend);
- **Inform** consular officials if a foreign national;
- **A copy** of the **custody record**;
- **Notice of rights** in writing;
- **An appropriate adult** if a juvenile or vulnerable;
- **Interpretation** if not proficient in English;
- **Medical attention** if needed;
- **Rest periods** between interviews;
- **Adequate food and drink**.

These rights can be **delayed** only in very narrow
circumstances (e.g. legal advice delay in cases involving
terrorism or evidence-preservation grounds for offences
above prescribed threshold).

See [`rights-at-police-station.md`](rights-at-police-station.md).

### The caution

Before being questioned, the suspect is **cautioned**:

> "You are not obliged to say anything unless you wish to
> do so, but what you say may be put into writing and
> given in evidence."

A suspect who declines to answer questions exercises the
**right to silence**. Adverse inferences from silence are
narrower in Jersey than in some UK contexts — generally
inferences from silence at interview are limited by
PPCE's procedural fairness framework.

### Interview rules

Interviews must:

- Be **recorded** (Code E — audio recording open and
  reliable);
- Take place in **suitable conditions** (no oppression,
  breaks, fit to interview);
- Follow the **caution**;
- Allow **legal adviser presence** if requested;
- Be conducted in a language the suspect understands.

A suspect not fit to be interviewed (intoxicated, unwell,
unable to appreciate questions) must not be interviewed.

### Stop and search

Police may stop and search persons or vehicles on
specific grounds:

- **Reasonable suspicion** of carrying weapons, drugs,
  stolen property, or evidence of an offence;
- **Anti-terrorism** statutory powers;
- **Serious violence / disorder** specific powers;
- **Matching a suspect description**.

**Discrimination prohibited**: stops cannot be based on
age, race, ethnic background, nationality, religion, or
prior criminal history alone.

Before searching, the officer must:

- **Identify themselves**;
- **State** the grounds for the search;
- **Explain** the person's rights;
- **Record** the search (officer notebook / form).

See [`stop-and-search.md`](stop-and-search.md).

### Powers of entry

Police may enter premises:

- **With a warrant** issued by the Magistrate's Court;
- **Without a warrant** in specific emergency
  circumstances (breach of the peace, urgent arrest, risk
  to life).

Forced entry permitted where occupier refuses or absent,
or where necessary to prevent obstruction.

See [`powers-of-entry.md`](powers-of-entry.md).

## Procedural safeguards

The PPCE 2003 framework includes specific safeguards:

- **Custody officer** independent of investigating
  officer;
- **Custody record** as a comprehensive record of every
  event;
- **Reviews** of detention at prescribed intervals;
- **Independent legal advice** preserved;
- **Codes of Practice** binding on officers;
- **Court exclusion** of evidence obtained in serious
  breach.

## Vulnerable persons

Specific provisions for:

- **Juveniles** (under 18) — parent / guardian notified;
  **appropriate adult** required for interview;
- **Mentally vulnerable** persons — appropriate adult;
  fitness assessment;
- **Non-English speakers** — interpreter required before
  interview;
- **Hearing / speech difficulties** — appropriate
  support.

## Honorary Police involvement

The PPCE 2003 recognises the dual policing structure (see
[`../road-traffic/honorary-police.md`](../road-traffic/honorary-police.md)):

- **Both** States Police and Honorary Police may exercise
  PPCE powers;
- **Centeniers** play a specific role in charging
  decisions (analogous to a CPS function in the UK);
- **Parish Hall Enquiries** dispose of many minor
  offences pre-court.

## Recent reforms

Codes of Practice are revised periodically. The Minister
periodically updates Codes to reflect operational and
human-rights developments.
