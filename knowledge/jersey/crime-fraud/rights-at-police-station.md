# Rights at the Police Station

This file collects the specific rights a person has when
detained at a Jersey police station under the PPCE 2003
and its Codes of Practice. The full text of each right is
in the Codes — this is the operational summary.

## The headline rights

### 1. Right to free legal advice

You may consult a **solicitor or advocate** at any point
during detention. This is:

- **Free of charge** (legal aid funded for the duty
  solicitor; private retainer also possible);
- **Available 24/7** through the duty-solicitor scheme;
- **Confidential** — privileged communications;
- **Pre-interview** — interviews should normally not
  begin until you have had the opportunity for advice if
  you want it.

**Delay** of this right is permissible only in **very
serious cases** and only on specific statutory grounds
(typically evidence-preservation in the most serious
offences) authorised at superintendent level or above.
Routine delay is **not** permitted.

If you initially decline legal advice but **change your
mind** during interview, **questioning must stop** and you
must be offered the chance to consult a solicitor.

### 2. Right to inform someone of your arrest

You can have **one person** told of your arrest and
location:

- **Family member, friend, or other person**;
- **At public expense** (police phone the person);
- **As soon as practicable**.

This right can be **delayed** in serious cases (e.g.
where notification might alert other suspects, hinder
evidence recovery, or interfere with property recovery)
— but only on specific grounds authorised at inspector
level or above.

### 3. Right to inform consular officials (foreign nationals)

If you are a foreign national, you can have your country's
consulate or High Commission notified. Some countries have
**automatic** notification arrangements with Jersey.

### 4. Right to remain silent

You are **not obliged** to answer questions. The caution
is:

> "You are not obliged to say anything unless you wish to
> do so, but what you say may be put into writing and
> given in evidence."

This is read to you on arrest and again before any
formal interview. Specific things to know:

- **No adverse inference** can typically be drawn from
  silence at police station interview alone (Jersey is
  narrower on this than England post-2003);
- **Silence in court** is a separate matter with
  different rules;
- **Comments you make** without being cautioned may not
  be admissible.

### 5. Right to a copy of the custody record

The **custody record** is a comprehensive log of:

- Time of arrest, of arrival at station, of each event;
- Reasons for detention;
- Reviews of detention by inspector;
- Visits by solicitor;
- Medical examinations;
- Searches;
- Property seized;
- Meals, breaks, rest;
- Interview times.

You are entitled to:

- **Sight of the record** during detention (on request);
- **A copy** on release.

The custody record is **critical evidence** in any
subsequent challenge to procedural fairness — review it
carefully.

### 6. Right to a written notice of rights

On arrival at the station, the custody officer must
provide a **written notice of rights** in your language.
This summarises the above and provides contact details
for legal advice.

### 7. Right to an interpreter

If you don't speak English well enough to follow the
procedure, an **interpreter** must be arranged before
interview:

- **At public expense**;
- **Professional interpreter** (not a friend / family
  member except in exceptional cases);
- **Interview cannot start** without interpretation unless
  delay would create serious harm.

### 8. Right to appropriate adult (vulnerable persons)

For **juveniles under 18**, **mentally vulnerable
persons**, or **those with significant learning
difficulties**:

- A **parent, guardian, or appropriate adult** must be
  present;
- **Interview cannot proceed** without the adult except in
  defined urgent circumstances;
- The adult's role is to assist communication, observe
  fairness, and support the person.

### 9. Right to medical attention

If you are unwell, injured, or in need of medication:

- **Medical examination** to be arranged;
- **Custody officer** to call appropriate care;
- **Records** of medical events kept in the custody
  record.

### 10. Right to adequate physical conditions

- **Cell standards** specified;
- **Meals** at appropriate times;
- **Drinking water**;
- **Rest periods** — typically continuous 8 hours' rest
  in any 24-hour period;
- **Toilet access**;
- **Heat / light** at appropriate levels.

## Specific to interviews

Beyond the general rights, when actually interviewed:

- **Recording**: Code E requires open audio recording —
  start, end, presence of those in the room, breaks all
  recorded;
- **No oppression** — raising voice, intimidation,
  prolonged questioning are prohibited;
- **Breaks** at appropriate intervals;
- **Fitness** to be interviewed — police must not
  interview someone too intoxicated, unwell, or
  distressed to follow;
- **Caution given** at the start of each interview
  session;
- **Solicitor presence** if requested.

## Detention time limits

| Stage | Limit | Authorised by |
|---|---|---|
| Initial detention | **24 hours** | Custody officer |
| First extension (serious offences) | up to **36 hours** | Police inspector or higher |
| Royal Court warrant of further detention | up to **96 hours total** | Royal Court (Magistrate) |
| Beyond 96 hours | **Charge or release** required |

Detention beyond these limits is **unlawful** and provides
grounds for habeas corpus / wrongful imprisonment claim.

## Searches at the station

The PPCE permits specified searches:

- **Personal search** — strip search only by same-sex
  officer, in private, with proper records;
- **Searches of property** seized on arrest;
- **Intimate samples** (blood, urine, etc.) — specific
  consent / warrant required;
- **Non-intimate samples** (fingerprints, mouth swabs) —
  permissible in defined circumstances.

## What to do if your rights are breached

If you believe a right has been breached:

- **Note the time / details** if possible;
- **Tell your solicitor** at first opportunity;
- **Complain** through the formal complaint route after
  release;
- **Material exclusion** of evidence may be sought at any
  trial;
- **Specific** civil claim possible for serious breach.

## Recommendations

If detained:

- **Ask for a solicitor immediately** — every time;
- **Stay calm** and cooperative on procedural matters;
- **Don't answer questions** before legal advice;
- **Note everything** you can remember;
- **Read the custody record** carefully;
- **Keep the notice of rights** and the copy of the
  custody record;
- **Engage a Jersey criminal advocate** post-release for
  any contested matter.
