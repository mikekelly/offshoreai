# Crime and Criminal Procedure — Jersey

## What this section is about

Jersey criminal procedure — the police powers, suspect
rights, custody procedures, court processes, and
sentencing that apply when a person is suspected,
arrested, charged, tried, or sentenced for a criminal
offence in Jersey.

This is **distinct** from the corpus's existing coverage
of:

- **AML/CFT** — see [`../aml-cft/`](../aml-cft/index.md)
  — financial-crime prevention, regulated-business
  obligations;
- **Specific offence statutes** — see substantive sections
  (e.g. [`../road-traffic/`](../road-traffic/index.md)
  for motoring offences;
  [`../residential-tenancy/non-payment-of-rent.md`](../residential-tenancy/non-payment-of-rent.md)
  for unlawful eviction).

This section is for **criminal procedure** — the rules
about how the State investigates, prosecutes, and tries
crime, and what rights the individual has at each stage.

The principal statute is the **Police Procedures and
Criminal Evidence (Jersey) Law 2003** (PPCE 2003), with
detailed Codes of Practice on detention, treatment,
questioning, identification, and audio recording.

## Why this section exists

This is the **sixth gap-fill** from the coverage audit at
[`../COVERAGE-AUDIT.md`](../COVERAGE-AUDIT.md), addressing
gap **#10** (PPCE 2003). Any Jersey resident who is
arrested, questioned, stopped and searched, or whose
property is searched relies on PPCE. The corpus had no
coverage; only the ECHR human-rights file provided
abstract bridging.

## At a glance

| Topic | Position |
|---|---|
| Principal statute | Police Procedures and Criminal Evidence (Jersey) Law 2003 |
| Detention limit without charge | 24 hours (extendable for serious crime by an inspector) |
| Right to legal advice | Free; can be delayed in very serious cases |
| Right to inform someone | Yes; can be withheld in serious cases |
| Caution wording | "You are not obliged to say anything unless you wish to do so, but what you say may be put into writing and given in evidence" |
| Interview procedure | Code of Practice C (detention, treatment, questioning); Code E (audio recording) |
| Custody record | Written notice of rights + copy on release |
| Parish Hall Enquiries | Lesser offences — see [`../road-traffic/parish-hall-enquiries.md`](../road-traffic/parish-hall-enquiries.md) |
| Summary trial | Magistrate's Court |
| Indictable trial | Royal Court — Inferior Number or Superior Number |
| Final appeal | Court of Appeal → Judicial Committee of the Privy Council |
