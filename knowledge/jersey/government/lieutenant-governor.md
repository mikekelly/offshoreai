# Lieutenant Governor of Jersey

## What the Lieutenant Governor is

The **Lieutenant Governor of Jersey** is the **personal
representative of the Sovereign** in Jersey — the highest
office in the Bailiwick on behalf of the Crown.

Appointed by **the Sovereign** on advice (typically of the
Crown's UK government via the Privy Council), the Lt-Governor:

- Serves for a **five-year** term (renewable);
- Is by **convention a retired senior military officer**
  (latterly with diplomatic / civil-service backgrounds also
  represented);
- Holds the office on behalf of the Sovereign.

## Constitutional position

The Lt-Governor's functions are principally:

### Symbolic / representative

- Represents the **Sovereign** at state and public events;
- Hosts visiting dignitaries;
- Is the point of formal communication between the
  Sovereign / Privy Council and Jersey institutions on
  ceremonial matters.

### Statutory powers (limited)

- **Grants Royal Assent** in some cases (most assents are
  through the Privy Council);
- **Promulgates** specific Orders in Council in Jersey;
- **Exercises** the prerogative of mercy in defined
  circumstances on advice;
- **Other specific functions** under Jersey laws (e.g., in
  relation to police, prison administration historically).

### Executive — none in the modern sense

The Lt-Governor does **not**:

- Direct the **Council of Ministers**;
- Direct the **States Assembly**;
- Have **executive** power over Jersey government;
- Have **legislative** veto power.

These powers belong to Jersey's democratic institutions.

## Distinction from Bailiff

The **Lt-Governor** and **Bailiff** are distinct offices:

| Office | Role |
|---|---|
| Lt-Governor | Crown representative — ceremonial / symbolic |
| Bailiff | Head of Royal Court, presiding officer of States Assembly until separation, civic head |

The **Bailiff** historically presided over the States Assembly,
but this function has been separated from the judicial role
in recent constitutional reforms (with a separate **Speaker /
President** for the Assembly).

## Modern role

The Lt-Governor's role has evolved:

- **Ceremonial** dimension preserved;
- **Practical** governance handled by Council of Ministers and
  Chief Minister;
- **Diplomatic** function in liaison with UK government and
  visiting officials;
- **Civic** function in public events and charitable
  patronage.
