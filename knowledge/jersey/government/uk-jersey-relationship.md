# UK-Jersey Constitutional and Practical Relationship

## The constitutional position

Jersey is a **Crown Dependency** — not part of the United
Kingdom but tied to the **British Crown**:

- Jersey is **self-governing** for domestic matters;
- The **UK Government** retains responsibility (on behalf of
  the Crown) for **defence** and **external relations**, with
  modulated arrangements;
- Jersey **legislates** for itself through the **States
  Assembly**, with Royal Assent granted by the Privy Council
  (effectively the UK Cabinet) for material laws;
- The **UK Parliament** does not legislate for Jersey **without
  consent**, except in defined exceptional cases (defence,
  international treaties, fundamental constitutional matters).

## Privy Council process

For Jersey laws requiring Royal Assent:

- The States Assembly **passes** the Law;
- The Bailiff **forwards** the Law to the Privy Council
  (effectively the Ministry of Justice's Crown Dependencies
  team);
- The Privy Council **reviews** for compatibility with the
  UK's international obligations, fundamental constitutional
  principles, and good administration;
- The **Sovereign** grants Royal Assent (on advice of the Privy
  Council).

The process can take **several months**.

## External relations

The UK Government acts for Jersey in:

- **Defence** — UK forces protect Jersey;
- **External relations** — UK signs treaties on Jersey's behalf
  (sometimes "with the territorial extent" of Jersey, sometimes
  not);
- **Some** international organisations.

**Important modulations**:

- Jersey has **its own treaty-making** capacity for tax
  treaties (Letters of Entrustment from the UK enabling Jersey
  to negotiate DTAs and TIEAs directly);
- Jersey is **internationally** active in financial-services
  matters in its own right (OECD, FATF, Moneyval observer
  participation);
- Jersey participates in **bilateral** information-exchange
  agreements.

## Fiscal autonomy

Jersey raises and spends **its own taxes**:

- **No** contribution to UK budget;
- **No** UK transfers to Jersey;
- Jersey's **Treasury** raises personal income tax, corporate
  tax (zero-ten), GST, social security contributions, and
  other revenues;
- Jersey runs **its own budget**.

This fiscal autonomy is **fundamental** to the Crown Dependency
status and underpins Jersey's IFC role.

## EU relationship

Jersey was **never** an EU member (it had specific Protocol 3
arrangements while the UK was in the EU for goods trade only).
Since UK's **Brexit**:

- Jersey is **outside** the EU comprehensively;
- Jersey has a **Trade and Cooperation Agreement** arrangement
  for goods trade with the EU through the UK-EU TCA;
- Jersey's **financial services** access to the EU is via
  **equivalence** / **NPPR** (national private placement
  regimes) rather than passporting.

## Modern issues

### UK political pressure

UK governments periodically raise concerns about:

- **Tax transparency** — beneficial ownership, CRS coverage;
- **AML / CFT** standards;
- **Tax avoidance** (in the political rather than legal
  sense — Jersey's zero-ten regime);
- **Resilience** of Jersey's financial-services sector to
  shocks.

Jersey **responds** through formal channels and informally
through ministerial dialogue.

### Constitutional reform

Periodic debates touch on:

- Jersey's **electoral system**;
- Relations with the **Bailiff** (separation of judicial / civic
  / Assembly roles);
- **Treaty-making** autonomy;
- **Constitutional codification** (Jersey's constitution is
  uncodified, like the UK's).

## Practical guidance for foreign practitioners

- **Don't treat** Jersey as "part of the UK" — it isn't;
- **Don't treat** Jersey law as English law — Jersey law has
  its own sources, courts, and judges;
- **Don't assume** UK regulators apply — Jersey has its own
  JFSC;
- **Don't assume** EU access — Jersey is outside the EU;
- **Do** recognise that English / UK case law is **persuasive
  authority** in many areas of Jersey law (especially trust
  and company law);
- **Do** treat the UK Privy Council as the **ultimate appeal
  court** for Jersey civil matters.
