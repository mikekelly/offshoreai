# BVI FSC — Financial Services Commission

## What the FSC is

The **BVI Financial Services Commission (FSC)** is the
**integrated regulator** of BVI financial services:

- **Established** by the Financial Services Commission Act
  2001;
- **Independent** of government in day-to-day operation;
- **Funded** through fees;
- **Reports** to the BVI Government.

## Functions

The FSC:

- **Licenses** financial-services businesses;
- **Registers** companies, funds, trustees;
- **Supervises** ongoing compliance;
- **Investigates** breaches;
- **Enforces** regulatory standards;
- **Issues** Rules and Guidance Notes;
- **Cooperates** with international regulators.

## Sectors regulated

### Banking

- **Banks**;
- **Trust companies** (under BC Act 2004);
- **Specific** BVI rules.

### Insurance

- **Domestic** insurance;
- **Captive** insurance;
- **Specific** BVI Insurance Act.

### Funds

- **SIBA** for investment business;
- **Mutual Funds Regulations**;
- **Private Investment Funds Regulations**;
- **Specific** fund categories.

### Investment business

- **SIBA** Categories of investment business;
- **Specific** licensing per category.

### Trust services

- **Trust company licensing**;
- **Specific** BVI rules.

### Money services

- **Money transmission**;
- **Specific** licensing.

### AML / CFT

- **Proceeds of Criminal Conduct Act**;
- **Specific** AML regulations;
- **FSC AML / CFT Code of Practice**.

## FSC engagement

For BVI-licensed entities:

- **FSC** authoritative on regulatory matters;
- **Online** registration and reporting systems;
- **Industry** consultations;
- **Specific** Rules updated periodically.

## International cooperation

The FSC participates in:

- **IOSCO** — securities;
- **IAIS** — insurance;
- **FATF / CFATF** — Caribbean Financial Action Task Force;
- **Specific MoUs** with major regulators.
