# BVI Incubator and Approved Fund Regimes

## What they are

The **BVI Incubator Fund** and **BVI Approved Fund** are
two light-touch fund regimes introduced by the **SIBA
(Incubator and Approved Funds) Regulations 2015**. They
sit beneath the BVI Private Fund and Professional Fund
regimes, providing **fast-track launch routes** for:

- **Emerging managers** wanting a manageable regulatory
  burden;
- **Track-record-building** vehicles intended to scale
  later;
- **Friends-and-family** investment structures;
- **Initial capital pools** before a full Private Fund
  launch.

The two regimes are **distinct** but often confused.
Pick one — they are not interchangeable.

## BVI Incubator Fund

The **Incubator Fund** is designed for **emerging
managers building a track record**.

### Key parameters

- **Up to 20 investors**;
- **Minimum investment per investor**: **US$20,000**;
- **Maximum fund assets**: **US$20 million**;
- **Maximum two-year life** as Incubator (must transition
  or wind down after);
- **No mandatory** auditor;
- **No mandatory** administrator;
- **No mandatory** investment manager (but typical for
  marketing reasons);
- **Light-touch** ongoing regulation.

### Transition

After two years (extendable by one further year on
application), an Incubator Fund must:

- **Convert** to a Private Fund, Professional Fund, or
  Public Fund (typically Private); or
- **Wind down**.

The Incubator regime is **intentionally time-limited** —
it's a launch pad, not a permanent home.

### Use case

A new sponsor wants to:

1. **Launch quickly** with limited regulatory friction;
2. **Build a track record** over 12-24 months;
3. **Demonstrate** to professional LPs the strategy
   works;
4. **Convert** to Private or Professional Fund when
   ready to scale.

## BVI Approved Fund

The **Approved Fund** is a permanent light-touch fund
regime designed for **stable, smaller funds**.

### Key parameters

- **Up to 20 investors**;
- **No statutory** minimum investment per investor;
- **Maximum fund assets**: **US$100 million**;
- **No mandatory** time limit (can remain Approved
  permanently);
- **No mandatory** auditor;
- **Administrator** typically required;
- **Investment manager** typically required;
- **Light-touch** ongoing regulation.

### Use case

A small, closely-held fund:

- **Family-office** investment pool;
- **Friends-and-family** vehicle;
- **Boutique** specialist fund staying small by design;
- **Holding vehicle** for a defined investor group.

## Compared

| Feature | Incubator Fund | Approved Fund |
|---|---|---|
| Max investors | 20 | 20 |
| Min investment | US$20,000 | None statutory |
| Max AUM | US$20m | US$100m |
| Time limit | 2 years + 1 year extension | Permanent |
| Auditor required | No | No |
| Administrator typically required | No | Yes |
| Designed for | Emerging manager track-record | Stable small fund |
| Typical conversion exit | To Private / Professional / Public | Stay Approved or convert |

## Comparison with BVI Private and Professional Funds

| Feature | Incubator | Approved | Private Fund | Professional Fund |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Max investors | 20 | 20 | 50 | No max |
| AUM ceiling | US$20m | US$100m | None | None |
| Audit | Not required | Not required | Required | Required |
| Investor minimum | US$20,000 | None | None | Sophisticated only |
| Time limit | 2 years | None | None | None |
| Regulatory intensity | Lowest | Light | Medium | Medium-high |

The BVI regime tier ladder: **Incubator → Approved →
Private Fund → Professional Fund → Public Fund**.
Sponsors pick the lowest tier consistent with their
investor base and size, upgrading as needed.

## Comparison with Jersey equivalents

| BVI | Jersey equivalent |
|---|---|
| Incubator Fund | No direct equivalent — JPF is the typical fast-track |
| Approved Fund | No direct equivalent — JPF most analogous |
| Private Fund (50 investors) | Jersey Private Fund (JPF) |
| Professional Fund | Expert Fund |
| Public Fund | Listed Fund / Public Fund |

The BVI Incubator regime in particular has no direct
Jersey equivalent — Jersey's JPF is a more
mature-management-focused fast-track, whereas the BVI
Incubator is explicitly oriented to brand-new managers
with limited operational infrastructure.

## Approval and ongoing requirements

### Application

For both Incubator and Approved Funds:

- **Application** to the FSC with constitutional
  documents, manager / administrator details (where
  applicable), and offering memorandum;
- **Faster** than Private / Professional Fund
  applications;
- **Approval** typically within weeks.

### Ongoing

- **Annual return** to FSC;
- **Annual fees**;
- **AML/CFT** compliance;
- **Specific** valuation and reporting policies;
- **Substance** considerations where applicable.

## Manager status

Both regimes can be managed by:

- A **BVI Approved Manager** (see
  [`approved-manager.md`](./approved-manager.md));
- A **full-licensed** BVI Investment Business manager;
- A **foreign-licensed** manager (with FSC notification).

The Approved Manager + Incubator Fund / Approved Fund
combination is a common emerging-manager stack.

## Pitfalls

- **Confusing** Incubator with Approved (different
  parameters);
- **Missing** the Incubator time limit and not planning
  conversion;
- **Outgrowing** AUM ceilings without converting;
- **Investor minimum** breach in Incubator;
- **Substance** non-compliance;
- **Marketing** assumptions — these are BVI regulated
  funds but with reduced disclosure; counterparties /
  prime brokers will diligence carefully.
