# BVI Approved Manager Regime

## What it is

The **BVI Approved Manager** regime was introduced by
the **Investment Business (Approved Manager) Regulations
2012** to provide a **fast-track, light-touch**
authorisation route for investment managers of:

- **BVI funds** (Private, Professional, Incubator,
  Approved, or Public funds);
- **Closely-related non-BVI funds** (subject to specific
  qualifying conditions).

The regime sits **below** full BVI Investment Business
licensing (the standard SIBA / FSC-licensed manager).
It's designed for **start-up, smaller, or family-office
managers** who don't need the full licensing apparatus
but do need a recognised regulatory status.

## Why approved-manager exists

Without an intermediate regime, an investment manager
either:

- **Holds full BVI Investment Business Act licence** —
  substantial regulatory infrastructure, capital,
  personnel, and time-to-licence; or
- **Operates without BVI authorisation** — viable only
  for non-BVI clients and creates marketing /
  counterparty difficulties.

The Approved Manager regime fills the gap:

- **Faster** authorisation (a few weeks vs months);
- **Lower** initial and ongoing costs;
- **Proportionate** regulatory standards;
- **Marketing-acceptable** to investors who want a
  regulated counterparty.

## Eligibility

A person can apply as an Approved Manager if:

- Acting as **investment manager / adviser** to:
  - **BVI Private Funds, Professional Funds, Incubator
    Funds, Approved Funds, or Public Funds**; or
  - **Non-BVI funds** that are closely connected to BVI
    funds (e.g. parallel-fund structures);
- **Assets under management** within prescribed thresholds
  (the regime is principally for managers below a defined
  AUM ceiling; check current thresholds);
- **Qualified personnel** with relevant experience;
- **Fit-and-proper** check by the FSC;
- **Specific** office and operational arrangements.

If AUM grows beyond the threshold, the manager must
upgrade to full Investment Business licensing.

## Application process

A typical Approved Manager application:

1. **Engage** a BVI law firm and registered agent;
2. **Submit** the application to the FSC with:
   - Company / partnership details;
   - Manager personnel CVs and fit-and-proper
     declarations;
   - Business plan;
   - AML / compliance framework;
   - Specific operational policies;
3. **FSC review** — typically several weeks;
4. **Approval** issued;
5. **Ongoing** compliance commitments.

The timeline contrasts favourably with full SIBA
licensing.

## Ongoing obligations

An Approved Manager must:

- **Maintain** the fitness and propriety of personnel;
- **File** annual returns with the FSC;
- **Pay** annual fees;
- **Comply** with AML/CFT under the BVI Proceeds of
  Criminal Conduct Act and AMLR;
- **Notify** the FSC of material changes;
- **Maintain** records and documentation accessible to
  FSC inspection;
- **Comply** with economic substance under the BVI
  Economic Substance (Companies and Limited Partnerships)
  Act 2018 where applicable.

## What an Approved Manager can do

The Approved Manager can:

- **Manage** investments of approved BVI / connected
  funds;
- **Advise** on investments;
- **Hold itself out** as a regulated manager (for
  marketing and counterparty purposes);
- **Receive** management fees and performance fees;
- **Engage** sub-advisers / delegates with FSC notice.

## What it cannot do

- **Manage non-qualifying funds** beyond the regime's
  scope;
- **Exceed** AUM / activity thresholds without upgrading
  to full licensing;
- **Act as administrator / custodian** (separate
  licensing regimes apply).

## Comparison with adjacent regimes

| Regime | Jurisdiction | Use case |
|---|---|---|
| **BVI Approved Manager** | BVI | Start-up / small manager of BVI / connected funds |
| **BVI Investment Business Licence** | BVI | Full-scale investment business |
| **Cayman CIMA-registered manager** | Cayman | Full-scale Cayman manager |
| **Jersey AIFM Regulations 2012** | Jersey | Manager of EU-NPPR-marketed AIFs |
| **Bermuda Investment Business Act class** | Bermuda | Bermuda-based investment business |
| **Guernsey GFSC licensing classes** | Guernsey | Various tiers under FSB Law |

## Comparison with Cayman analogues

Cayman has the **Securities Investment Business Act
(SIBA)** regime with:

- **Excluded Persons Regulations 2003** — light-touch
  for narrow categories (similar in concept to BVI
  Approved Manager);
- **Full SIBA licensing** — full regulatory regime.

The BVI Approved Manager is conceptually closer to the
Cayman Excluded Persons regime than to full SIBA
licensing, but with substantive differences in detail.

## Use cases

### Start-up fund managers

A new sponsor launching a first BVI fund typically:

1. **Forms** the GP entity (BVI BC);
2. **Forms** the management entity (BVI BC) as Approved
   Manager;
3. **Forms** the fund (BVI BC or LP under BVI
   Partnership Act);
4. **Operates** with full Approved Manager status from
   inception.

This stack delivers the regulatory cover and
counterparty-acceptance the fund needs without the
overhead of full licensing.

### Family-office structures

Family offices managing related-party BVI funds often
use Approved Manager status for:

- Recognised regulatory standing;
- Counterparty-acceptance with banks, brokers,
  administrators;
- AEOI / CRS reporting clarity.

### Spin-outs and smaller asset managers

Experienced asset managers spinning out from larger
firms commonly use BVI Approved Manager status at
launch, upgrading to full SIBA / full licensing only
when AUM justifies it.

## Pitfalls

- **AUM** growth past the threshold — must upgrade to
  full Investment Business licensing;
- **Scope** drift — managing non-qualifying funds takes
  the activity outside the regime;
- **Substance** — economic substance compliance is
  separate and equally important;
- **AML/CFT** — substantive obligations, not paper
  formality;
- **Misperception** — Approved Manager is a regime, not
  a loophole; FSC will inspect.
