# Continuation Funds and GP-Led Secondaries — The Dominant PE Liquidity Trend

## What this is

A **continuation fund** is a new fund vehicle into which
a sponsor (GP) transfers one or more portfolio assets
from an existing fund, with existing LPs given the
choice to **roll** their interest into the continuation
fund or **cash out** at a negotiated valuation.
**GP-led secondaries** is the broader category, also
including tender offers, strip sales, preferred-equity
transactions, and structured liquidity solutions.

Together these are the **single most active area** of PE
fund structuring in 2024-2026 — driven by:

- **Tough M&A and IPO exit markets** through 2022-2025;
- **LPs demanding DPI** (distributed-to-paid-in
  capital) to recycle into new commitments;
- **GPs unwilling to sell trophy assets** at depressed
  valuations;
- **Secondaries market capital** ready to deploy.

For Crown Dependencies practitioners — particularly
**Guernsey**, which has positioned itself aggressively
in this space — continuation funds are the dominant
fund-formation conversation right now.

## Where it stands (as of 2026-05-18)

### Market size and trajectory

Per the **2026 Global Private Equity Outlook survey**
(referenced by Dechert, Nov 2025):

- **46% of respondents** are using GP-led secondaries
  or continuation vehicles to expedite LP distributions
  — **roughly double last year's figure**;
- **51% of North American** respondents planning to
  increase GP-led secondary dealmaking in next 24
  months;
- **55% of APAC** respondents planning increases;
- **43% of EMEA** respondents planning increases (up
  from 14% in 2024).

The trend is **broad-based and accelerating**.

### Why Guernsey

Guernsey Finance has marketed aggressively. The key
features:

- **Single-asset continuation funds are not regulated as
  collective investment schemes** in Guernsey — a
  significant simplification of the regulatory pathway;
- **Fast-track fund regimes** enabling sign-off within
  **3 business days** maximum, with next-day approval
  for Private Investment Funds (PIFs);
- **Familiar fund-counsel ecosystem** with experienced
  practitioners (Carey Olsen, Ogier, Mourant, Walkers);
- **Tax-neutral** (0% standard corporate tax);
- **Substance-light** for the new continuation vehicle
  itself if asset is the only thing changing hands.

See [`../guernsey/private-investment-fund.md`](../guernsey/private-investment-fund.md).

### Why Jersey

Per Walkers' April 2025 commentary on Jersey PE
secondaries:

- **Jersey Private Funds** can be set up for
  continuation vehicles with **48-hour** JFSC
  authorisation;
- **Designated Service Provider (DSP)** model is
  particularly suited to continuation funds where
  the sponsor wants regulatory efficiency;
- **Familiar GP/LP** mechanics under Jersey LP Law;
- **Article 9 firewall** considerations for trust-
  related rollovers;
- **Specific Jersey** procedural points: existing LP
  consent mechanics; treatment of rolled vs cashed-out
  LPs; carry crystallisation at the rollover point.

See [`../jersey/funds/jersey-private-fund.md`](../jersey/funds/jersey-private-fund.md).

### Why Cayman

Cayman remains dominant for institutional PE
continuation vehicles where US-LP capital is involved.
The **Cayman ELP** under the Private Funds Act 2020
framework is the standard structure. See
[`../cayman/exempted-limited-partnership.md`](../cayman/exempted-limited-partnership.md).

## Typical continuation-fund structure

A simplified walkthrough of a single-asset continuation
fund (other variations: multi-asset, tender-offer,
strip-sale, preferred-equity sidecar):

### Pre-transaction

```
       Existing PE Fund
            |
       [Trophy Asset]
       (other portfolio assets)
```

### Transaction mechanics

1. **GP identifies** trophy asset and forms intent to
   continue holding;
2. **Independent valuation** of the asset and the
   transaction terms;
3. **LPAC consent** for the conflicted transaction
   (essential — GP is buying from itself);
4. **New continuation fund** formed (typically
   Guernsey PIF / Jersey JPF / Cayman ELP);
5. **Secondary investors** subscribe new capital to the
   continuation fund;
6. **Existing LPs** elected to roll into continuation
   fund or cash out at the agreed valuation;
7. **Original fund** receives proceeds, distributes to
   electing-cash-out LPs;
8. **Continuation fund** holds the asset going forward
   with rolled LPs + secondary investors;
9. **GP** typically commits significant new capital and
   adjusted carry economics.

### Post-transaction

```
       New Continuation Fund
            |
       [Trophy Asset]
       
       LPs: rolled-over from original + new secondaries
       GP: continued sponsor with new carry/commit
```

### Carry crystallisation

A central mechanical issue: the original fund's GP carry
**crystallises** on the asset transfer (if the asset
sold at a profit). The continuation fund's GP carry
**resets** at the new entry basis. Tax treatment of the
crystallisation event is a major workstream.

## Hot regulatory and conflict topics

### Conflicts of interest

GP-led secondaries are **inherently conflicted** — the
GP is on both sides. The 2023 SEC Adviser Marketing
Rule, ILPA guidance, and EU ESMA guidance all
emphasise:

- **Independent valuation** by qualified third party;
- **LPAC blessing** as standard practice;
- **Disclosure** of all conflicts to electing LPs;
- **Genuine option** for LPs to cash out vs roll;
- **Auction or status-quo benchmark** for pricing
  defensibility.

### Carry treatment

- Original fund GP carry crystallises;
- Continuation fund GP carry resets;
- Tax treatment depends on jurisdictions of GP entities
  and individual partners;
- **UK carried-interest reform** (see
  [`uk-carried-interest-reform-2026.md`](uk-carried-interest-reform-2026.md))
  changes the post-6-April-2026 economics
  meaningfully.

### LP rights and side letters

- MFN clauses may be triggered;
- Specific consent rights re conflicted transactions;
- ILPA Continuation Fund Guidance (2023) increasingly
  treated as soft-law standard.

### Tax

- **Rollover** treatment for tax purposes — varies by
  LP jurisdiction;
- **US tax** treatment for US LPs (UBTI / ECI
  considerations);
- **Pillar Two** implications for the portfolio asset
  if its group is in scope;
- **Stamp duty** in transaction jurisdictions.

## What practitioners are doing

### For sponsors considering a continuation fund

- **Pre-LPAC** engagement to test reception;
- **Valuation** process from independent firm;
- **Fund-counsel** engagement (Walkers, Carey Olsen,
  Ogier, Mourant typically involved);
- **Secondary advisers** (Evercore, Lazard, Jefferies,
  Campbell Lutyens) to run the process;
- **Tax structuring** to minimise leakage on rollover;
- **Communication strategy** with LPs;
- **Updated** GP carry / commitment economics for the
  continuation fund.

### For LPs facing a continuation-fund offer

- **Information rights** scrutiny — what disclosure am
  I receiving?
- **Independent advice** on rollover vs cash-out;
- **Comparison** to alternative liquidity routes;
- **Tax** modelling for both options;
- **MFN trigger** analysis.

### For Crown Dependencies fund counsel

- Continuation funds are now a **standard product
  line**;
- Guernsey marketing is the **most aggressive** —
  Guernsey Finance, GFSC, and local counsel
  coordinating;
- Jersey practitioners have shifted **inventory** to
  match — multiple Jersey-domiciled continuation funds
  launched in 2025-2026.

## What to watch for next

- **SEC further rulemaking** in continuation-fund
  conflict and disclosure space;
- **EU AIFMD II** disclosure requirements on
  continuation funds (April 2026 onward — see
  [`aifmd-ii-april-2026.md`](aifmd-ii-april-2026.md));
- **ILPA** continuation-fund guidance updates;
- **JFSC** and **GFSC** specific guidance on
  continuation-fund-related conflicts;
- **Tax authority responses** to carry crystallisation
  events on rollover;
- **Market scale** — survey data suggests continued
  doubling-of-volume in 2026.

## Pitfalls

- **Treating** the continuation fund as a routine new
  fund — the inherent conflict requires bespoke
  process;
- **Inadequate** LP disclosure / consent process;
- **Pricing** that doesn't survive auditor / regulator
  scrutiny;
- **Tax leakage** on rollover for specific LP types;
- **Carry mechanics** that misalign rolled LPs and new
  secondary investors;
- **Side-letter / MFN** triggers missed;
- **Sponsor reputational** damage if process feels
  one-sided to LPs.
