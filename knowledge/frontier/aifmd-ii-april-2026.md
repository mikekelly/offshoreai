# AIFMD II — Most Requirements Effective 16 April 2026

## What this is

The first material revision to the EU Alternative
Investment Fund Managers Directive since the original
2011 framework. **Directive (EU) 2024/927** entered into
force on **15 April 2024**, with most member-state
implementation required by **15 April 2026**. Most
substantive operational requirements take effect from
**16 April 2026** — about a month before this file's
`as_of` date.

For **Crown Dependencies fund managers**, the impact is
materially **narrower** than for EU-domiciled AIFMs but
non-trivial — and the competitive positioning of
Jersey / Guernsey relative to EU domiciles is being
actively re-evaluated by sponsors right now.

## Where it stands (as of 2026-05-18)

**Effective 16 April 2026**, AIFMD II:

- **Stricter rules** on AIFM **delegation** to third
  countries;
- **New substance** requirements for AIFM operations;
- **Loan originating funds** rules (significant new
  framework for credit AIFs);
- **Liquidity management** tool requirements for open-
  ended funds;
- **Enhanced Annex IV reporting** to ESMA;
- **Expanded investor disclosures** (pre-investment and
  ongoing);
- **Asset stripping** rules tightened.

The most important point for **Channel Islands managers**:
the directive **preserves Crown Dependencies' access to
EU professional investors via the National Private
Placement Regimes** (NPPRs). Jersey and Guernsey
continue to meet the EU's tax-transparency and AML
criteria.

## What changes for Crown Dependencies managers

### Direct impact (limited)

The directly-applicable changes for Jersey / Guernsey
AIFMs operating under NPPR are concentrated in two
areas:

1. **Expanded investor disclosures** — pre-marketing and
   ongoing disclosure obligations have been widened to
   cover liquidity management, sustainability factors
   (interaction with SFDR — see
   [`sfdr-2-and-sustainable-finance.md`](../jersey/frontier/sfdr-2-and-sustainable-finance.md)),
   and specific loan-originating-fund disclosures where
   applicable;
2. **Annex IV reporting** — significantly enhanced
   regulatory reporting to ESMA via competent
   authorities; new data fields; more granular
   reporting on specific asset categories and risk
   exposures.

### Indirect impact (potentially substantial)

The directive's stricter rules on **AIFM delegation to
third countries** affect how **EU AIFMs use Crown
Dependencies in delegation chains**. Specifically:

- An EU AIFM that delegates portfolio management to a
  Jersey or Guernsey entity must meet enhanced
  substance and oversight requirements;
- The delegating EU AIFM cannot become a "letter-box
  entity" — substantive functions must remain in the
  EU;
- Loan-originating EU AIFMs face specific limits on
  exposure to and delegation of loan-origination
  activity to non-EU entities.

This **reduces** the appeal of "delegate everything to
Jersey / Guernsey while marketing through an EU
shell" structures that some sponsors had been using.

### Competitive positioning

Carey Olsen and Langham Hall commentary identifies a
**counterintuitive consequence**: the tighter EU
substance and delegation rules make a **direct Jersey
or Guernsey AIFM with NPPR marketing** comparatively
more attractive vs an EU AIFM that depends on delegation
to non-EU portfolio managers. Sponsors that previously
ran an EU-AIFM-with-CI-delegate model are revisiting
the case for direct Channel Islands AIFM domiciliation.

Guernsey commentary in particular has been bullish on
the AIFMD II competitive position.

## Jersey AIF Code split

Per Carey Olsen's Spring 2026 update and the JFSC's
March 2026 strategy publication: Jersey's **AIF Code of
Practice** is being split into:

- **EU AIF Code** — applies to Jersey AIFMs marketing
  into the EU via NPPR; aligned with AIFMD II
  requirements;
- **UK AIF Code** — applies to Jersey AIFMs marketing
  into the UK; aligned with UK's evolving post-Brexit
  AIFM framework.

This bifurcation lets Jersey serve both markets without
forcing a single regime to satisfy both regulators
simultaneously. **EU AIF Code expected to be finalised
through 2026.**

Guernsey is expected to follow a similar bifurcation
path through its own code revisions.

## What practitioners are doing

### Compliance projects in flight

- **Annex IV reporting** systems being upgraded to
  capture new data fields;
- **Investor disclosure documents** (PPM updates,
  ongoing transparency reports) being refreshed for
  AIFMD II language;
- **Delegation agreements** between EU AIFMs and
  Crown Dependencies entities being reviewed;
- **Substance** documentation being strengthened.

### Structural responses

- **Direct CI AIFM with NPPR marketing** gaining favour
  vs EU-AIFM-with-CI-delegate models;
- **Loan-originating funds** examining whether to
  domicile in Crown Dependencies vs EU given the new
  EU-side restrictions;
- **Liquidity management tools** being built into
  open-ended fund constitutional documents;
- **Specific commercial conversations** with EU LPs
  about post-AIFMD-II operational impact.

### Marketing window

Some Channel Islands sponsors are **accelerating EU
fundraising** to lock in current arrangements before
full AIFMD II implementation effects bed in across
member states. EU member states' transposition has
been **uneven** — some jurisdictions implementing
faithfully, others tighter, others looser.

## What to watch for next

- **Member-state transposition** variations — some
  jurisdictions (Luxembourg, Ireland) have transposed
  closely to the directive; others (Germany, France)
  more strictly;
- **ESMA Q&A guidance** through 2026 clarifying
  ambiguities;
- **First Annex IV submissions** under new requirements
  expected end-Q2 / early Q3 2026;
- **JFSC EU AIF Code** finalisation;
- **GFSC** equivalent code-bifurcation moves;
- **Pillar Two intersection** with AIFMD II disclosure
  for portfolio-company exposures;
- **Loan-originating fund** market reshape — the new
  rules are restrictive enough that credit-fund
  sponsors are actively reviewing domicile.

## Pitfalls

- **Treating AIFMD II as a small-deltas exercise** —
  the loan-originating-fund rules and substance
  requirements are substantive;
- **Assuming NPPR is unaffected** — disclosure and
  Annex IV requirements have widened;
- **Missing the delegation impact** — EU AIFMs that
  delegate to CI entities face enhanced oversight
  obligations;
- **Member-state variation** — a fund marketed across
  multiple EU jurisdictions may face different
  effective requirements;
- **Sustainability disclosure** overlap with SFDR — the
  two regimes are now interlocking, see SFDR 2.0
  frontier file.
