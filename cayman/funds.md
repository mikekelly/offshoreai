# Cayman Funds — Global Hedge Fund Hub

## Why Cayman dominates fund domiciliation

The Cayman Islands is the **leading global jurisdiction** for:

- **Hedge funds** (estimated 75%+ of offshore hedge funds);
- **Private equity / venture capital** funds;
- **Many** other alternative investment funds.

Reasons:

- **Speed** of establishment (hours to days);
- **Cost** efficiency;
- **Investor familiarity** (industry standard);
- **Sophisticated** professional infrastructure;
- **Regulatory** flexibility;
- **Tax neutrality** (no Cayman tax);
- **CIMA** as well-respected regulator;
- **Existing** Cayman investor base.

## Principal legislation

### Mutual Funds Act

The **Mutual Funds Act**:

- Governs **open-ended** mutual funds;
- **Different** categories of regulated funds;
- **Specific** registration / licensing rules.

### Private Funds Act 2020

The **Private Funds Act 2020**:

- Governs **closed-ended** funds;
- **Private equity, venture capital, real estate**;
- **Specific** registration requirements;
- **CIMA** registration required.

## Mutual Fund categories

### Section 4(3) registered fund

- **Minimum subscription** of US$100,000;
- **CIMA registered**;
- **Most common** category for hedge funds;
- **Sophisticated** investor base.

### Section 4(4) licensed fund

- **Higher** regulatory tier;
- **Retail** or quasi-retail funds;
- **Comprehensive** regulation.

### Master fund

- Where **feeder funds** invest into a master;
- **CIMA registered**;
- **Specific** master-fund requirements.

### Limited Investor Fund

- **15 or fewer** investors;
- **Significant** rights for investors;
- **Lighter** regulation;
- **Used** for small / closed-circle funds.

## Private Funds (closed-ended)

Under the Private Funds Act 2020:

- **Closed-ended** funds previously largely unregulated;
- **Now** subject to:
  - **CIMA registration**;
  - **Annual** filings;
  - **Specific** asset-valuation and -safekeeping rules;
  - **AML / CFT** compliance.
- **Includes** PE, VC, real estate, infrastructure funds.

## Fund vehicles

Cayman funds typically use:

### Exempted company

- **Most common** for hedge funds;
- **Standard** corporate features;
- **Investor base** holds shares.

### Limited Liability Company (LLC)

- **Increasingly** popular;
- **Member-managed** flexibility;
- **US-tax-purposes** considerations.

### Exempted limited partnership (ELP)

- **Principal vehicle** for PE / VC;
- **GP / LP** structure;
- **Cayman ELP Act**;
- **Tax transparency** in most home jurisdictions.

### Unit trust

- **Less common**;
- **Specific** uses.

### SPC

- **Segregated Portfolio Company** umbrella;
- **Multiple cells / portfolios**;
- **Used** for multi-strategy / multi-class funds.

## Service providers

Cayman fund services:

- **Administrator**;
- **Custodian**;
- **Auditor** (typically Big 4);
- **Manager** (often US- or UK-based);
- **Legal counsel** (Cayman + manager's home jurisdiction);
- **Specific** Cayman-licensed providers required.

## Cross-jurisdictional structures

Common cross-jurisdictional configurations:

- **Cayman master** + **Jersey feeder** (for Jersey investors
  / institutional);
- **Cayman fund** + **Delaware GP**;
- **Cayman fund** + **Luxembourg parallel** for EU access;
- **Cayman fund** + **Singapore-managed**;
- **Many** combinations.

## Substance / Pillar Two

Cayman funds:

- **Subject to** Cayman substance rules;
- **In-scope** activities require substance;
- **Investment funds** subject to specific
  requirements;
- **Pillar Two** for large groups (€750m+).
