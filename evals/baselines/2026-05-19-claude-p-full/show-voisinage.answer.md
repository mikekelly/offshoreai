## What voisinage is

**Voisinage** is a **Norman customary-law doctrine** preserved in Jersey that governs the relationship between neighbouring landowners. At its core, it requires each owner to use their property in a way that does not cause **unreasonable harm** to neighbours — harm that exceeds the ordinary inconveniences of living next to someone (`jersey/property/voisinage.md`).

It is distinctively Jersey: most modern systems have abandoned it, and it sits alongside (but is not the same as) English-style private nuisance. Jersey's tort/délit framework lists voisinage as a **specific customary Jersey doctrine**, with strict-liability elements and case law including *Rockhampton Apartments* (`jersey/contract/delit-tort.md`).

## When it bites

To make out a claim under voisinage, the corpus says you need (`jersey/property/voisinage.md`):

1. **Harm** that is objectively identifiable;
2. **Caused by the defendant's use of their property**;
3. **Exceeding ordinary inconveniences** of the neighbourhood;
4. **Without adequate justification**.

Importantly, voisinage **can apply without fault** — it has objective/strict-liability flavour, unlike negligence. It is also **not limited** to the English nuisance categories of "smell, smoke, noise" — its scope is broader (`jersey/property/voisinage.md`).

## Typical fact patterns the corpus says it covers

Per `jersey/property/voisinage.md`, voisinage has been used for:

- excessive noise (including late-night noise from commercial premises and inter-apartment noise);
- smells from livestock, kitchens, or activities;
- smoke / fumes;
- vibrations from machinery or construction (prolonged or excessive construction, not just temporary);
- surface water / drainage;
- tree roots / overhanging branches;
- light obstruction (specific Jersey rules);
- agricultural manure/slurry spreading.

Defences include reasonable use, claimant consent/acquiescence, statutory authority, and (limited) prescription. Remedies the Royal Court can grant include **injunctions**, **damages**, and orders for **specific preventative measures** (`jersey/property/voisinage.md`).

## Two important traps the corpus flags

- **Planning permission does not defeat voisinage.** Holding planning consent is not a complete answer; regulatory compliance is at most evidence of reasonable use (`jersey/property/voisinage.md`).
- **Servitudes don't override voisinage either.** A neighbour with a valid easement can still breach voisinage if they exercise it excessively (`jersey/property/voisinage.md`).

## So does it apply to *your* problem?

The corpus cannot answer that — you haven't told me what the neighbour is doing. The honest pre-screen from `jersey/property/voisinage.md` is:

- Is there objectively identifiable **harm** to you/your property (not just irritation)?
- Is it **caused by how they use their land** (not, e.g., personal conduct unrelated to the property)?
- Does it **exceed normal neighbourhood inconvenience** — i.e., would a reasonable Jersey neighbour in the same area find this beyond the pale?
- Is there **no statutory authority or servitude** properly justifying it?

If yes to all four, voisinage is in scope. The corpus's practical recommendation is to **document the interference** (logs, photographs, recordings), build a **correspondence trail** with the neighbour, **engage experts early** (acoustic, environmental) where relevant, **try mediation first**, and instruct a Jersey advocate familiar with the doctrine before any Order of Justice in the Royal Court (`jersey/property/voisinage.md`).
