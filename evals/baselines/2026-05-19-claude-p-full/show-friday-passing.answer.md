## Answer

Your transfer happens that way because Jersey real-property law requires every conveyance of land to be **"passed" before the Royal Court** — buyer and seller (typically their advocates as attorneys) appear in court, the contract is read or referenced, the Greffier records it, and **legal title passes at that moment in the courtroom**. The transfer is a court act, not a private deed exchange as it is in England (`jersey/property/contract-passing.md`; `jersey/property/real-property-overview.md`).

The forum is the Royal Court's **Samedi Division**. The name itself ("samedi" = Saturday) is a clue: it is so called *after the historical Saturday sittings* — i.e. there is a long-standing tradition that this branch of the Court sits at the end of the week for civil and conveyancing business (`jersey/legal-system/royal-court.md`). In modern practice, contract passings for real-property dealings sit on a **Friday afternoon** slot — the corpus records this explicitly for:

- conventional sales and hypothecs of land (`jersey/property/contract-passing.md` describes the procedure; the Friday-afternoon scheduling is given in the files below),
- **commercial leases of 9 years or more** ("Passing on a Friday afternoon at the Royal Court; Two Jurats sit to witness") (`jersey/property/commercial-leases.md`),
- **flying-freehold transfers** ("Royal Court passing (Friday afternoon)") (`jersey/property/flying-freeholds.md`),
- and is referenced in the contract-law context too: "Royal Court passing required (Friday afternoon hearings) … Court closure historically triggered force-majeure arguments" (`jersey/contract/force-majeure.md`).

**Why a court hearing at all?** The corpus explains the rationale as a Norman-customary-law inheritance: real-property transfers are made *publicly* before the Royal Court so the Court acts as the **repository of property records**, giving **certainty about title transitions** and **public visibility** of who is dealing with what land (price terms remain private) — and the system has been preserved for its historic importance (`jersey/property/contract-passing.md`).

**Why Friday specifically?** The corpus consistently *describes* Friday-afternoon passing as the established practice across at least four files, but it does not give a separate doctrinal reason beyond the Samedi Division's historical end-of-week sitting pattern. So the honest answer is: the Friday-afternoon slot is the modern continuation of the Samedi (Saturday) civil-sitting tradition — **the corpus does not give a more specific reason** than that for the choice of Friday.
