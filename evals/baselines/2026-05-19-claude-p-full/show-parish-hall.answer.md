## What is a Parish Hall Enquiry?

A **Parish Hall Enquiry** is Jersey's distinctive pre-court forum for routine minor offences — most often motoring. It is presided over by a **Centenier**, an *elected, unpaid* honorary police officer of the parish in which the alleged offence occurred. There is no direct equivalent in the UK or other common-law systems: it blends prosecutorial and limited adjudicative functions in one official, justified by long parish tradition (`jersey/road-traffic/parish-hall-enquiries.md`).

At the Enquiry, the Centenier reviews the evidence (typically from the States Police or other Honorary Police), engages with the alleged offender, and may either **caution** (no further action, but recorded), **issue a written fine up to £200 per offence**, or **refer to Magistrate's Court** if the matter is beyond Parish Hall scope. Crucially, a fine or caution can be imposed **only if the offender both admits the offence and accepts the proposed penalty** — anyone who disputes the offence must be sent to court (`jersey/road-traffic/parish-hall-enquiries.md`).

The process is informal — usually 15–30 minutes, no robes, no transcript, no advocate's address — but you have rights: to be informed of the alleged offence in advance, to bring a friend/family member/advocate, to decline to admit, to demand Magistrate's Court at any point, to appeal to Magistrate's Court for a re-hearing, and to a copy of the Centenier's record (`jersey/road-traffic/parish-hall-enquiries.md`).

## When does your case go there?

Your case is **likely** to be heard at Parish Hall if it is a routine motoring or minor offence such as:

- Minor speeding (under 15 mph over the limit)
- Mobile phone offences
- Failing to display an insurance disc
- Some traffic-light offences
- Some minor careless-driving cases
- Parking offences

(`jersey/road-traffic/parish-hall-enquiries.md`)

Your case is **excluded** from Parish Hall — and goes directly to Magistrate's Court — if it involves:

- Speeding 15 mph or more over the limit
- Drink-driving (any reading)
- Drug-driving
- Dangerous driving (Article 23 of the Road Traffic Law)
- Causing death or serious injury by driving
- Driving while disqualified
- Failing to stop/report after an accident (in serious cases)
- Any offence carrying **mandatory disqualification**
- Any offence the Centenier judges to exceed the £200 ceiling

(`jersey/road-traffic/parish-hall-enquiries.md`)

The schedule of Parish-Hall-treatable offences is maintained by the Law Officers' Department and reviewed periodically (`jersey/road-traffic/parish-hall-enquiries.md`).

## Two things worth flagging

- A Parish Hall outcome **is not** a formal criminal conviction for most purposes, but it **is** recorded, **can endorse your driving licence**, and **may be reported** to other authorities (e.g. licensing). Don't assume it's consequence-free (`jersey/road-traffic/parish-hall-enquiries.md`).
- The Centenier **cannot impose disqualification**. If disqualification is on the table, you'll be in Magistrate's Court regardless (`jersey/road-traffic/parish-hall-enquiries.md`).
