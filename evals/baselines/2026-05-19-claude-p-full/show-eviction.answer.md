# Can I evict my tenant for non-payment of rent in Jersey?

**Short answer: No, not unilaterally — but yes, via a statutory court-supervised process.** Non-payment of rent is a breach of the tenancy agreement under the Residential Tenancy (Jersey) Law 2011, which triggers a defined breach-and-eviction procedure. Self-help eviction (changing locks, cutting off utilities, removing the tenant or their possessions) is unlawful and may itself be a criminal offence (`jersey/residential-tenancy/non-payment-of-rent.md`).

## The proper procedure

**Step 1 — Serve a 7-day written breach notice.** The notice must be in writing, signed by or for the landlord, identify the breach (specifying the rent due, period, and amount), and require remedy (payment in full) within at least 7 days. Seven days is the statutory minimum — you can give more, but not less. Service must be in accordance with the agreement or by personal delivery / recorded post to the unit (`jersey/residential-tenancy/non-payment-of-rent.md`).

**Step 2 — Wait out the notice period.** If the tenant pays in full within the period, the breach is remedied and the tenancy continues — you cannot then terminate on those arrears. A partial payment is not "remedy" unless the agreement or notice says so (`jersey/residential-tenancy/non-payment-of-rent.md`).

**Step 3 — Apply to the Petty Debts Court.** If still unpaid after 7 days, apply for: (a) termination of the tenancy, (b) an eviction order, and optionally (c) a money judgment for the arrears. The Petty Debts Court has **exclusive original jurisdiction** under the RTJL — the Royal Court does not handle these at first instance. You will typically need the tenancy agreement, condition report, rent ledger / bank evidence, a copy of the breach notice with proof of service, and a record of engagement attempts (`jersey/residential-tenancy/non-payment-of-rent.md`).

**Step 4 — The hearing.** The court checks whether rent was due and unpaid, whether the 7-day notice was properly given and elapsed, and whether the tenant has any defence (e.g. set-off for landlord's failure to repair, procedural defects, disputed liability). It may refuse, grant immediately, or grant with a **stay of execution** — commonly 14–28 days, longer for vulnerable tenants, families with children, or those awaiting Income Support / rehousing (`jersey/residential-tenancy/non-payment-of-rent.md`).

**Step 5 — Enforcement by the Viscount.** Once the order is in force (and any stay has expired), the Viscount executes the eviction: attends the property, gives the tenant the opportunity to leave, removes them and their possessions with force if necessary, stores realisable items (selling those with value, disposing of others unless claimed within 15 days), and accounts for net proceeds. Viscount fees are a real and often-underestimated cost (`jersey/residential-tenancy/non-payment-of-rent.md`).

## What the tenant can raise as a defence

- **Set-off** for the landlord's own breaches, e.g. unfit premises under the repair / standards regime — this can reduce or extinguish the arrears claim.
- **Procedural defects** in the breach notice — defective notice is fatal to the application.
- **Discrimination** under the Discrimination (Jersey) Law 2013 if the eviction is in reality discriminatory.
- **Harassment** counter-claim if the landlord engaged in pressure tactics (cutting services, unauthorised entry, intimidation) during the notice period — separately a criminal offence (`jersey/residential-tenancy/non-payment-of-rent.md`).

## Practical bottom line

Realistic elapsed time from first missed payment to actual possession is **2–4 months** in a straightforward case, longer if contested or stayed. You cannot shortcut this with self-help — every shortcut (locks, possessions, utilities, physical removal, refusing access) is unlawful and potentially criminal (`jersey/use-cases/tenant-landlord/evicting-non-paying-tenant.md`, `jersey/residential-tenancy/non-payment-of-rent.md`).
