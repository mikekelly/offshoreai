# Marketing a Jersey AIF into Germany via NPPR

Both source files I'm drawing on are dated `2026-05-15` (4 days old, well inside the 180-day freshness window) but carry `status: draft`, so treat the detail as orientation rather than a final compliance checklist.

## The route at a glance

Jersey is non-EU, so the full AIFMD passport is not available to Jersey AIFMs — the "third-country passport" was proposed but never switched on. The practical route into Germany is the **National Private Placement Regime (NPPR)**: a country-by-country private-placement notification, not an EU-wide right (`jersey/funds/aifmd-nppr.md`, `jersey/use-cases/fund-counsel/aifmd-nppr-registration.md`).

## What you have to do

1. **Get the AIFM JFSC-registered under Jersey's AIFMD-equivalent regime.** The JFSC operates a regime designed to be substantively equivalent to AIFMD: capital adequacy proportionate to AIFM size, an AIFMD-Art-15-equivalent risk-management framework, liquidity management for open-ended AIFs, depository arrangements, investor transparency (annual report + periodic reporting), and regulatory reporting back to the JFSC (`jersey/funds/aifmd-nppr.md`, `jersey/use-cases/fund-counsel/aifmd-nppr-registration.md`).

2. **Sort out the depository.** A Jersey AIF marketed into the EU via NPPR needs **AIFMD-equivalent depository arrangements** — custody, oversight, and cash-flow monitoring functions. Jersey permits a "lighter" depository for funds *not* marketed into the EU, but that concession is not available once you're using NPPR. A common pattern is a Jersey JFSC-licensed depository combined with the full AIFMD depository elsewhere (`jersey/funds/aifmd-nppr.md`, `jersey/use-cases/fund-counsel/aifmd-nppr-registration.md`).

3. **File the Germany-specific NPPR notification with BaFin.** Germany is one of the **stricter** NPPR jurisdictions. The Jersey corpus specifically flags, for Germany:
   - Strict NPPR rules under **§330 KAGB**;
   - A **local fact-sheet** requirement;
   - A **local distributor** is sometimes required;
   - **Ongoing reporting** obligations after acceptance.

   The procedural steps are: identify BaFin as the competent authority, prepare the German NPPR notification package, confirm Jersey AIFM-regime equivalence is recognised, submit, and await clearance (timeline varies) (`jersey/use-cases/fund-counsel/aifmd-nppr-registration.md`).

4. **Stay within the NPPR perimeter once cleared.** You may then market only to **professional investors** in Germany — and only by means of active marketing that has been notified. Reverse solicitation (the investor approaches you unsolicited) sits outside NPPR, but member-state authorities scrutinise such claims and "active outreach disguised as reverse solicitation can attract penalties" (`jersey/use-cases/fund-counsel/aifmd-nppr-registration.md`).

## A couple of things worth flagging

- **UK is now separate.** Post-Brexit the UK runs its own NPPR via the FCA, so a Jersey AIF aimed at both Germany and the UK has **two parallel notification streams** (`jersey/funds/aifmd-nppr.md`, `jersey/use-cases/fund-counsel/aifmd-nppr-registration.md`).
- **Pitfalls the corpus calls out** for an NPPR project: active marketing without notification, overclaimed reverse solicitation, and a depository set-up that's lighter than EU-marketed AIFs require (`jersey/use-cases/fund-counsel/aifmd-nppr-registration.md`).
- The corpus does not give the BaFin filing fee, the precise content of the §330 KAGB fact-sheet, or processing-time figures — for those you'd need primary BaFin guidance.
