# Drug-driving in Jersey — current rules and what's coming

## Today (May 2026): impairment, not concentration

Driving while impaired by drugs is an offence under the **Road Traffic (Jersey) Law 1956**. Unlike drink-driving — which has a fixed alcohol limit and a mechanical "over the limit" test — Jersey's drug-driving regime is **impairment-based**: the prosecution must prove on objective evidence that the driver was actually unfit to drive (poor control, slurred speech, failed field sobriety tests, plus blood or urine confirming drug presence) (`jersey/road-traffic/drug-driving.md`).

What Jersey deliberately **does not yet have**:

- No **statutory drug-concentration limits** (no equivalent of the English specified-limit regime);
- No **roadside drug-testing powers** for police (no drug-wipe swabs);
- No **per se offence** — being over a numeric limit is not, on its own, the offence.

The practical consequence: prosecution is evidentially harder than drink-driving, because impairment must be made out (`jersey/road-traffic/drug-driving.md`).

### Procedure currently in use

1. Stop by States or Honorary Police on observed signs of impairment;
2. Field sobriety tests (balance, walking, eye-movement);
3. Arrest on reasonable suspicion;
4. Blood / urine sample at the station;
5. Charge based on impairment evidence **plus** drug presence;
6. Proceedings in the Magistrate's Court (`jersey/road-traffic/drug-driving.md`).

### Substances and defences

The offence is **substance-neutral** — controlled drugs, lawful prescription medication, OTC medicines with drowsiness warnings, and combinations with alcohol all qualify if impairment is real. "I had a prescription" is not a defence to impairment. Substantive defences are limited to identity, impairment not made out, procedural defects in sampling, narrow "innocent ingestion", and lawful-prescription-within-label as relevant only to some charges but not impairment itself (`jersey/road-traffic/drug-driving.md`).

### Sentencing

Tracks drink-driving: mandatory disqualification (typically 12+ months for a first offence), fine up to £2,000, imprisonment up to 6 months, licence endorsement, with aggravation for accidents, passengers, or refusal to cooperate (`jersey/road-traffic/drug-driving.md`).

## What's coming: the February 2026 reform proposal

In **February 2026**, States representatives proposed legislation that would bring Jersey closer to the UK drug-driving regime:

- **Statutory drug limits** for controlled and some prescription substances;
- **Roadside testing powers** for police (swab tests similar to UK drug-wipes);
- **Per se offences** — being over the limit becomes the offence, no impairment required;
- **Mandatory disqualification** on conviction.

The proposal is **not yet enacted** as of May 2026. The States Assembly's timetable suggests possible introduction during 2026 or 2027; until then the impairment-based regime continues to apply (`jersey/road-traffic/drug-driving.md`). The road-traffic index flags this as a "current gap; 2026 reform pending" (`jersey/road-traffic/index.md`).
