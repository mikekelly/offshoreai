Your Jersey real-property transfer happens on a Friday afternoon in court because that is the **scheduled sitting of the Royal Court at which conveyancing contracts are passed**. The corpus describes the mechanics but does not give an underlying historical rationale for the Friday slot specifically.

Key points the corpus supports:

- Jersey real-property transfers are not effected privately (as in English conveyancing). Title passes by an *act of the Royal Court* — the contract is read/referenced, the Greffier records it, and the transfer takes effect at that moment (`jersey/property/contract-passing.md`, `jersey/property/real-property-overview.md`).
- The forum is the **Samedi Division** of the Royal Court — named after its historical Saturday sittings, and now the day-to-day civil/administrative court of the Bailiff or Deputy Bailiff (`jersey/legal-system/royal-court.md`).
- The corpus explicitly identifies **Friday afternoon** as the conveyancing slot for the passing of contracts: it is when the Royal Court takes commercial leases of 9+ years (`jersey/property/commercial-leases.md`), flying-freehold transfers (`jersey/property/flying-freeholds.md`), and sales of Jersey real property generally (`jersey/contract/force-majeure.md`, which also notes that Royal Court closure of that slot has historically triggered force-majeure arguments on delayed completions).
- Two **Jurats** sit to witness the passing, alongside the Bailiff/Deputy Bailiff (`jersey/property/commercial-leases.md`; cf. `jersey/legal-system/royal-court.md` on Samedi composition).
- Lawyers (advocates) typically appear as **attorneys** under powers of attorney for absent parties, so the buyer/seller usually does not have to be physically present (`jersey/property/contract-passing.md`).
- Possession typically transfers the same day; registration in the Public Registry, and registration of any hypothec, follow the passing (`jersey/property/contract-passing.md`).

What the corpus does **not** say: it does not explain *why* the Royal Court allocates Friday afternoons specifically to conveyancing passings (e.g. any historical or administrative origin of the weekly Friday slot). To that narrower "why Friday" question, **the corpus does not answer this question** — it documents the practice without giving its rationale. The general "why a court at all" rationale (Norman customary law, public transfer of real property, the Royal Court as the repository of property records, certainty of title transitions) is given in `jersey/property/contract-passing.md`.
