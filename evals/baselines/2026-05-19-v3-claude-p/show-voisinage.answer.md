## What voisinage is

**Voisinage** is a Jersey **Norman customary-law doctrine** that governs the relationship between neighbouring landowners. It imposes a duty on each owner to use their property in a way that does **not cause unreasonable harm** to neighbours, and is enforced by the Royal Court (`jersey/property/voisinage.md`). The glossary frames it as a Jersey customary "tort concept" — a neighbour obligation not to cause unreasonable harm, with strict-liability elements (`jersey/glossary-extension.md`).

Key features that distinguish voisinage from English nuisance:

- **Customary, not statutory** — derived from Norman customary law (Le Geyt, Pothier) and developed by Royal Court jurisprudence (`jersey/property/voisinage.md`).
- **No fault requirement** — voisinage can apply **objectively**, without proving fault, and is not confined to enumerated English categories like "smell, smoke, noise" (`jersey/property/voisinage.md`).
- **Recognised in Jersey case law** — `Rockhampton Apartments` is cited as illustrative (`jersey/contract/delit-tort.md`).

## Does it apply to your neighbour problem?

The corpus says voisinage **may** apply where there is:

1. **Harm** that is objectively identifiable;
2. Caused by the **defendant's use of property**;
3. **Exceeding the ordinary inconveniences** of neighbourhood living; and
4. **Without adequate justification** (`jersey/property/voisinage.md`).

Typical claims the corpus lists include excessive noise, smells (livestock, kitchens), smoke/fumes, vibration from machinery or construction, surface water/drainage, tree roots and overhanging branches, and light obstruction (`jersey/property/voisinage.md`). It also flags specific commercial-residential, apartment (inter-unit noise/smells), and rural/agricultural contexts. So if your problem fits one of those patterns and the interference goes beyond ordinary neighbourhood inconvenience, voisinage is a plausible cause of action.

Things that **do not** automatically defeat (or save) a voisinage claim:

- **Planning permission does not exclude voisinage** — regulatory compliance is only evidence of reasonable use, not a shield (`jersey/property/voisinage.md`).
- **Valid servitudes** are not overridden by voisinage, but **excessive use of a servitude** can still breach it (`jersey/property/voisinage.md`).
- Available defences include reasonable use, pre-existing state (limited), consent/acquiescence, statutory authority, and prescription (limited) (`jersey/property/voisinage.md`).

If the issue is really about **where the boundary lies** rather than how the neighbour is using their land, voisinage is the wrong frame — that's a boundary-disputes question (deeds, plans, Vue de Vicomte, prescription), though voisinage is cross-referenced there too (`jersey/property/boundary-disputes.md`). If you are a tenant complaining about your landlord or another tenant's interference, voisinage is also referenced alongside quiet-enjoyment and ordinary tort/nuisance (`jersey/residential-tenancy/quiet-enjoyment-and-access.md`).

## Remedies and how to proceed

The Royal Court can grant an **injunction**, **damages**, or **specific performance of preventative measures** (`jersey/property/voisinage.md`). The corpus recommends documenting the interference (logs, photos, recordings), engaging acoustic/environmental experts early, building a correspondence trail with the neighbour, attempting **mediation first**, and instructing a Jersey advocate familiar with voisinage; litigation proceeds by Order of Justice under Royal Court civil procedure, often with a site visit (`jersey/property/voisinage.md`).
