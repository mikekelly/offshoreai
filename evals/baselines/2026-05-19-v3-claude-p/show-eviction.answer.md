# Can I evict my non-paying tenant in Jersey?

**Short answer: no, not directly — you cannot self-evict, but you can obtain a court order and then have the Viscount enforce it.** Jersey residential tenancies are governed by the **Residential Tenancy (Jersey) Law 2011** (RTJL), and non-payment of rent is a breach that triggers a specific statutory procedure (jersey/residential-tenancy/index.md; jersey/residential-tenancy/non-payment-of-rent.md).

## The procedure

**Step 1 — Serve a 7-day breach notice.** Non-payment is a breach of the agreement, but your right to terminate is not automatic when a rent date is missed. You must serve a written notice that (a) is signed by or on behalf of the landlord, (b) identifies the breach specifically (which rent periods, what amount), (c) requires remedy within at least 7 days, and (d) is delivered as the agreement specifies or by personal delivery / recorded post. The 7-day window is a statutory minimum — you can give longer but not less. A partial payment does not remedy the breach unless you've agreed to accept it (jersey/residential-tenancy/non-payment-of-rent.md).

**Step 2 — If unpaid after 7 days, apply to the Petty Debts Court.** The Petty Debts Court has *exclusive original jurisdiction* over residential tenancy disputes under the RTJL — the Royal Court does not handle them at first instance. You apply for (i) termination of the tenancy, (ii) an eviction order, and optionally (iii) a money judgment for the arrears. You'll need the tenancy agreement, condition report, rent ledger / bank statements, copy of the breach notice and proof of service, and a record of attempts to engage the tenant (jersey/residential-tenancy/non-payment-of-rent.md; jersey/residential-tenancy/eviction-procedure.md).

**Step 3 — The hearing.** The court will check that the breach is made out and procedure was followed, consider any tenant defences (e.g. set-off for landlord's failure to repair, discrimination, procedural defects), and assess vulnerability factors. It may **refuse** the order, **grant** it immediately, or **grant with a stay of execution** (commonly 14–28 days, occasionally up to 8 weeks). Stays are routinely granted for families with minor children, tenants on Income Support, or those awaiting social-housing placement (jersey/residential-tenancy/eviction-procedure.md).

**Step 4 — Enforcement by the Viscount.** Only the **Viscount** (a senior judicial officer of the Royal Court, who is Jersey's principal civil-judgment enforcement officer) may lawfully take possession from a resisting tenant. After any stay expires you apply to the Viscount's Department; the Viscount attends, gives the tenant a chance to leave with possessions, removes them with reasonable force if necessary, inventories and stores remaining items (selling realisable items toward fees; disposing of unclaimed items after 15 days), and restores possession to you. Viscount fees can run from hundreds to thousands of pounds (jersey/residential-tenancy/eviction-procedure.md).

## What you must NOT do

Self-help is unlawful and may be criminal under the RTJL:
- Don't change the locks (unlawful eviction; criminal offence; damages);
- Don't cut off utilities (harassment offence);
- Don't remove the tenant's possessions (conversion; criminal);
- Don't enter without consent (except per the agreement, with notice);
- Don't sue for arrears in the Royal Court at first instance (wrong forum) (jersey/residential-tenancy/non-payment-of-rent.md; jersey/residential-tenancy/eviction-procedure.md).

## Realistic timeline

From missed payment to actual possession in a straightforward case: typically **2–4 months**. With a standard 28-day stay, 6–10 weeks from court order; with a longer stay, 10–14 weeks; contested cases with appeals can run many months to a year (jersey/residential-tenancy/non-payment-of-rent.md; jersey/residential-tenancy/eviction-procedure.md).
