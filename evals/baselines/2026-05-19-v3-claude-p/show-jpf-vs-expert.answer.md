For a **club-deal PE fund**, the corpus points clearly to the **Jersey Private Fund (JPF)**.

## Why the JPF fits

The JPF concept file names "**club deal** structures" explicitly as one of the use cases the regime was designed for, alongside "**private equity** funds and **co-investment** vehicles" and "**single-LP**" structures (`jersey/funds/jersey-private-fund.md`). The characteristic shape of a club deal — a small, defined group of sophisticated investors backing a specific opportunity — maps directly onto the JPF's headline parameters:

- **Up to 50 offers / investors** at any one time
- **Professional / eligible investors only** (self-certified)
- **Fast-track JFSC authorisation in ~48 hours**
- **Reduced ongoing supervision** versus public funds
- Light-PPM-only disclosure rather than a full offering memorandum

You can wrap it in whichever vehicle suits the LPs — a Jersey limited partnership is the most common PE shape, but a no-par-value company, unit trust, or PCC/ICC cell are all on the menu (`jersey/funds/jersey-private-fund.md`). Substantive responsibility sits with the Jersey-licensed administrator acting as **Designated Service Provider** (CDD/AML, books and records, JFSC-supervised), which is what lets the fund itself be light-touch.

## When the Expert Fund becomes the right choice instead

The Expert Fund regime is the next step up. Per the corpus comparison table, you'd move to an Expert Fund if any of these apply (`jersey/funds/expert-fund.md`):

- You expect **more than 50 investors** (the JPF cap is hard)
- You need **wider marketing scope** to expert investors across jurisdictions permitting private placement
- You need **AIFMD NPPR** compliance for active EU marketing (the Expert Fund file flags this; see also `jersey/funds/aifmd-nppr.md`, not read here)
- The deal is a "**large private-equity** vehicle exceeding JPF investor count" — verbatim from the file

The trade-offs are longer authorisation (days to weeks rather than ~48 hours), a fuller offering memorandum rather than a light PPM, and a higher investor-qualification bar (the expert-investor net-worth / minimum-investment / institutional tests rather than mere professional/eligible self-certification).

## Practical recommendation

For a **club deal** — by definition small, defined, sophisticated — the JPF is the default. The corpus uses exactly that label for it. Only flip to the Expert Fund if you anticipate breaching 50 investors, or if active cross-border marketing into the EU under NPPR is part of the fundraising plan from day one.
