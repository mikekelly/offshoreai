# Drug-driving in Jersey

## The current position (May 2026)

Driving while **impaired by drugs** is an offence under the Road Traffic (Jersey) Law 1956. Prosecution runs on an **impairment** theory: the police must prove the driver was unfit to drive properly, using objective evidence — poor vehicle control, slurred speech, failed field sobriety tests, and blood or urine results showing drugs in the system (`jersey/road-traffic/drug-driving.md`).

What Jersey **does not yet have**:

- **Statutory drug-concentration limits** (no equivalent of the English specified-limit regime);
- **Roadside drug-testing powers** for the police (no drug-wipe authority beyond impairment-based field tests);
- **Per se offences** — being over a numeric limit irrespective of impairment.

The practical effect is that drug-driving is **harder to prove than drink-driving**, which has a mechanical "over-the-limit" rule (`jersey/road-traffic/drug-driving.md`).

## Substances covered

The impairment offence is **substance-neutral**. Anything that actually impairs counts — controlled drugs (cannabis, cocaine, MDMA, heroin), prescription medication (sedatives, opioids, benzodiazepines, some antihistamines), OTC medicines with warning labels, and combinations of drugs with alcohol. A lawful prescription is **not a defence** if the impairment is real (`jersey/road-traffic/drug-driving.md`).

## How a prosecution runs today

Stop (usually by States or Honorary Police on observed signs of impairment) → field sobriety tests → arrest on reasonable suspicion → blood/urine sample at the station, tested for controlled and prescription drugs → charge based on impairment evidence *plus* drug presence → proceedings in the Magistrate's Court (`jersey/road-traffic/drug-driving.md`).

## Sentencing

Tracks drink-driving sentencing: **mandatory disqualification** (typically 12+ months for a first offence), a fine up to £2,000, imprisonment up to 6 months, and licence endorsement. Accidents, passengers, or refusal aggravate. In combined drink-and-drug cases, the alcohol penalty typically dominates and the drug element is treated as aggravation (`jersey/road-traffic/drug-driving.md`).

## Defences

Identity (were you the driver?), impairment not made out (passed field tests, alternative medical explanation), procedural defect in sampling/testing, innocent ingestion (very narrow), and lawful prescription within label (relevant to intent for some charges, but **not** to impairment itself) (`jersey/road-traffic/drug-driving.md`).

## What's coming — the reform pipeline

In **February 2026** States representatives proposed legislation that would bring Jersey's drug-driving regime closer to the English specified-limit model. The proposals are to:

- Introduce **statutory drug limits** for controlled and some prescription substances;
- Give police **roadside testing powers** (swab tests similar to UK drug-wipes);
- Create **per se offences** so that being over the limit is itself the offence (no need to prove impairment);
- Make **disqualification mandatory** on conviction.

As of May 2026 the proposal is **not yet enacted**; the States Assembly's timetable points to potential introduction during 2026 or 2027. Until then the **impairment-based regime continues to apply** (`jersey/road-traffic/drug-driving.md`).
