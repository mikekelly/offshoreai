# Your rights if arrested in Jersey

Jersey criminal procedure is governed by the **Police Procedures and Criminal Evidence (Jersey) Law 2003** (PPCE 2003) and its Codes of Practice. Below is what the corpus says about what the police must do — and what you can demand — from the moment of arrest through detention at the station. (Both files cited were last verified 2026-05-18, one day before today — fresh.)

## At the point of arrest

The arresting officer (whether States Police or Honorary Police — both have full PPCE arrest powers) must:

- **Tell you that you are under arrest**;
- **State the offence** for which you are being arrested;
- **Caution** you with the Jersey wording: *"You are not obliged to say anything unless you wish to do so, but what you say may be put into writing and given in evidence"*;
- **Identify themselves** (uniform / warrant card);
- **Use no more force than necessary** — force must be reasonable, proportionate and necessary.

Failure to follow these steps can render the arrest unlawful and may ground exclusion of evidence, a wrongful-arrest civil claim, or internal discipline (`jersey/crime-fraud/arrest.md`).

The arrest itself must rest on **objective reasonable grounds** specific to you — not general suspicion — and is reviewable by the custody officer at the station and ultimately by a court (`jersey/crime-fraud/arrest.md`).

A search incident to arrest is permitted for weapons, evidence, or items that could assist further offending; it must be proportionate, recorded, and any strip search done by a same-sex officer in private (`jersey/crime-fraud/arrest.md`).

## After arrest — at the police station

You must be taken to a police station as soon as practicable and presented to an **independent custody officer**, who decides whether continued detention is lawful (`jersey/crime-fraud/arrest.md`). On arrival, the custody officer must give you a **written notice of rights in your language**. The principal rights at the station, drawn from `jersey/crime-fraud/rights-at-police-station.md`, are:

1. **Free legal advice** — a solicitor or advocate, available 24/7 via the duty-solicitor scheme, confidential and free of charge. Interviews should not normally begin until you have had the opportunity for advice. Delay of this right is permitted only in very serious cases on specific statutory grounds, authorised at superintendent level or above. If you initially decline advice but change your mind mid-interview, questioning **must stop** so you can consult a solicitor.
2. **Right to inform one person** of your arrest and location, at public expense, as soon as practicable. Can be delayed in serious cases (e.g. risk of alerting other suspects) on grounds authorised at inspector level or above.
3. **Right to consular notification** if you are a foreign national.
4. **Right to silence** — you do not have to answer questions. The caution is repeated at the start of each interview. The file notes that **no adverse inference can typically be drawn from silence at police-station interview alone** (Jersey is narrower than post-2003 England on this point).
5. **Right to see / receive the custody record** — the comprehensive log of times, reasons for detention, reviews, solicitor visits, medical events, searches, property seized, meals, rest and interviews. You can ask to see it during detention and you are entitled to a copy on release. It is critical evidence in any later challenge to procedural fairness.
6. **Right to a written notice of rights** on arrival.
7. **Right to a professional interpreter** at public expense if you don't speak English well enough; interview cannot start without interpretation except in serious-harm situations.
8. **Right to an appropriate adult** if you are under 18, mentally vulnerable, or have significant learning difficulties — interview cannot proceed without one except in defined urgent circumstances.
9. **Right to medical attention** — examination arranged if you are unwell, injured, or need medication; events recorded in the custody record.
10. **Right to adequate physical conditions** — cell standards, meals, drinking water, toilet access, heat/light, and typically a continuous 8-hour rest period in any 24-hour window.

## Interview rules

Once interviewed, additional protections apply (`jersey/crime-fraud/rights-at-police-station.md`):

- **Open audio recording** is mandatory under Code E (start, end, those present and breaks all recorded);
- **No oppression** — raised voices, intimidation or prolonged questioning are prohibited;
- **Breaks** at appropriate intervals;
- **Fitness to be interviewed** — police must not interview someone too intoxicated, unwell or distressed to follow;
- **Caution** given at the start of each session;
- **Solicitor present** if requested.

## How long you can be held

From `jersey/crime-fraud/rights-at-police-station.md`:

| Stage | Limit | Authorised by |
|---|---|---|
| Initial detention | **24 hours** | Custody officer |
| First extension (serious offences) | up to **36 hours** | Police inspector or higher |
| Royal Court warrant of further detention | up to **96 hours total** | Royal Court (Magistrate) |
| Beyond 96 hours | **Charge or release required** | — |

Detention beyond these limits is unlawful and grounds for a habeas corpus / wrongful-imprisonment claim.

## Samples and searches at the station

Personal search must be by a same-sex officer, in private, properly recorded. **Intimate samples** (blood, urine etc.) require specific consent or warrant; **non-intimate samples** (fingerprints, mouth swabs) are permissible in defined circumstances (`jersey/crime-fraud/rights-at-police-station.md`).

## If your rights are breached

Note the time and details, tell your solicitor at the first opportunity, complain through the formal complaints route after release, and seek **exclusion of resulting evidence** at any trial; a civil claim is possible for serious breach (`jersey/crime-fraud/rights-at-police-station.md`).

## Practical advice the corpus gives you

From both files combined: stay calm, identify yourself truthfully, do not resist physically (challenge an unlawful arrest later through proper channels), **ask for a solicitor immediately**, do not answer questions before getting legal advice, use your phone call to inform someone, don't sign anything without legal advice, note times and names, and read the custody record carefully before you leave (`jersey/crime-fraud/arrest.md`, `jersey/crime-fraud/rights-at-police-station.md`).
