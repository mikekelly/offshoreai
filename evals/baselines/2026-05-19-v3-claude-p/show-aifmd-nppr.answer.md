Based on the corpus, here is what the Jersey knowledge base says about marketing a Jersey AIF into Germany via NPPR.

## The route in outline

Because Jersey is non-EU, there is no AIFMD passport available — the third-country passport extension was proposed but never implemented (`jersey/funds/aifmd-nppr.md`). Marketing into Germany therefore happens through Germany's **National Private Placement Regime (NPPR)** under §330 KAGB, on a country-by-country basis (`jersey/use-cases/fund-counsel/aifmd-nppr-registration.md`).

## What you need to put in place

**1. JFSC AIFM registration.** Your Jersey AIFM must be registered with the JFSC under Jersey's AIFMD-equivalent regime and demonstrate substantive AIFMD-equivalent compliance — capital adequacy, risk management (equivalent to AIFMD Art 15), conflicts-of-interest controls, liquidity management for open-ended AIFs, transparency to investors (annual report, periodic disclosure), and regulatory reporting to the JFSC (`jersey/funds/aifmd-nppr.md`; `jersey/use-cases/fund-counsel/aifmd-nppr-registration.md`).

**2. AIFMD-equivalent depository.** A Jersey AIF marketed into the EU via NPPR needs depository arrangements with the prescribed custody, oversight and cash-flow-monitoring functions. Jersey allows a "lighter" depository for non-EU-marketed funds, but for EU NPPR marketing you need AIFMD-equivalent arrangements — often the Jersey depository (which must be JFSC-licensed) combined with the full AIFMD depository elsewhere (`jersey/funds/aifmd-nppr.md`; `jersey/use-cases/fund-counsel/aifmd-nppr-registration.md`).

**3. German NPPR notification to BaFin.** Germany is treated as one of the stricter NPPR jurisdictions (alongside France). The Jersey corpus flags the specifically German requirements as:
- Strict NPPR rules under **§330 KAGB**;
- A **local fact-sheet** required;
- A **local distributor** sometimes required;
- Ongoing **reporting obligations** after acceptance.

The competent authority is **BaFin**. The general NPPR flow is: confirm BaFin recognises the Jersey AIFM regime as equivalent, prepare Germany's notification package, submit, and wait for clearance (timeline varies). Once cleared, you can market **to professional investors in Germany only** — NPPR is not EU-wide (`jersey/use-cases/fund-counsel/aifmd-nppr-registration.md`).

## Scope and pitfalls

- **Professional investors only** — NPPR clearance lets you market to that member state's professional investors, not retail (`jersey/funds/aifmd-nppr.md`).
- **Reverse solicitation** doesn't trigger NPPR, but BaFin and peer regulators scrutinise reverse-solicitation claims; active outreach dressed up as reverse solicitation attracts penalties (`jersey/use-cases/fund-counsel/aifmd-nppr-registration.md`).
- **Active marketing without a German NPPR notification is a serious enforcement risk** (`jersey/use-cases/fund-counsel/aifmd-nppr-registration.md`).
- Germany-specific drafting (e.g. the local fact-sheet, distributor arrangements) needs to be in place before you push.
