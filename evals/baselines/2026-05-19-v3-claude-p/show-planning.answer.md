## Do I need planning permission for a rear extension on my Jersey house?

**It depends on the size, location, and status of the property.** The Jersey corpus treats rear extensions under the **Planning and Building (General Development) (Jersey) Order 2011** ("GD Order 2011"), which grants automatic planning permission ("permitted development") for minor works that fall fully within prescribed limits.

### When you probably do NOT need planning permission

A **single-storey rear extension** is one of the categories listed as permitted development under the GD Order 2011, provided it stays within the Order's limits on size, depth, and height (`jersey/planning/permitted-development.md`). The extensions-and-conversions file gives a rough rule of thumb that, for a typical Jersey-sized property, a **small rear extension of ~3–4 m projection, single storey** may be permitted development (`jersey/planning/extensions-and-conversions.md`).

### When you DO need planning permission

You will need a full planning application if any of the following apply:

- The extension **exceeds the GD Order's size / depth / height limits**, or breaches any of the conditions (boundary distance, curtilage coverage, no projection forward of the main building line, etc.) — exceeding *any* condition takes the work outside permitted development entirely (`jersey/planning/permitted-development.md`).
- It is **two-storey** — always requires an application under current rules (`jersey/planning/extensions-and-conversions.md`).
- The property is in a **conservation area** — typically a full application is needed even if the proposal would otherwise be within the thresholds (`jersey/planning/extensions-and-conversions.md`; `jersey/planning/permitted-development.md`).
- The property is a **listed building** — listed-building consent is always required, separately from planning permission (`jersey/planning/extensions-and-conversions.md`).
- The site is in the **Coastal National Park / Green Zone**, or under an Article 31 direction removing permitted-development rights (`jersey/planning/permitted-development.md`).

### Even if planning is not needed

You may still need:

- **Building approval** under the Building Bye-Laws 2007 (a separate process from planning);
- To check **restrictive covenants** on the title — a private-law matter that can prohibit works even where planning permits them (`jersey/planning/permitted-development.md`).

### Practical recommendation from the corpus

If there is any ambiguity over whether the proposal falls within permitted development, the corpus strongly recommends applying for a **Certificate of Lawful Development** — cheaper than a full application, provides certainty for sale / refinancing, and protects against later enforcement (`jersey/planning/permitted-development.md`). The corpus also flags the "small ≠ permitted" pitfall: the GD Order limits are often more restrictive than intuition suggests (`jersey/planning/extensions-and-conversions.md`).

The corpus does not reproduce the exact numerical thresholds in the GD Order 2011 itself — for the precise depth/height/area figures you would need to consult the Order directly.
