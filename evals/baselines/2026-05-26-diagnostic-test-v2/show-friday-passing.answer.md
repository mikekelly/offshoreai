## The Friday-afternoon practice — what the corpus says

In Jersey, real-property contracts (and long leases of nine years or more — *contrats héréditaires*) take effect only when **passed before the Royal Court**: the parties (or their advocates as attorneys) appear, the contract is read or referenced, two **Jurats** sit to witness, the **Greffier** records it, and title transfers at that moment. This is a **Norman customary-law** inheritance — real-property transfer in Jersey is a public Court act, not a private deed, and the Royal Court is the repository of property records (`knowledge/jersey/property/contract-passing.md`).

The **Friday-afternoon convention** for that passing sitting is recorded as established practice across several corpus files — commercial leases of 9+ years are passed "on a Friday afternoon at the Royal Court" with "two Jurats sit[ting] to witness" (`knowledge/jersey/property/commercial-leases.md`), the flying-freehold conveyancing walkthrough lists "Royal Court passing (Friday afternoon)" as the standard step (`knowledge/jersey/property/flying-freeholds.md`), and the contract / force-majeure file notes the same Friday-afternoon hearing slot (`knowledge/jersey/contract/force-majeure.md`).

**On the specific "why Friday afternoon" — the corpus is silent**. It records the convention as a fact of Jersey practice (rooted in the broader customary-law requirement for a public Court passing and the Samedi Division's role as the civil and administrative court) but does not document the historical reason the slot settled on Friday afternoon specifically. The contract-passing file in fact describes property passings as happening "most weekdays" before the Samedi Division, which sits in some tension with the Friday-afternoon convention referenced elsewhere — so there is a corpus-internal inconsistency on cadence that you may want to verify against a Jersey advocate or the Judicial Greffe before relying on it.
