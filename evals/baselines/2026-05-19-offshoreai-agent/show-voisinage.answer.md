## What voisinage is

**Voisinage** is Jersey's Norman customary-law doctrine of "good neighbourhood." It requires a property owner not to use their land in a way that causes **unreasonable harm** to a neighbour — broadly similar in result to English nuisance, but rooted in Jersey's customary law and capable of operating **without fault** (i.e. on an objective basis). It can apply to noise, smells, smoke/fumes, vibrations, drainage/surface water, tree roots and overhanging branches, light obstruction, and similar interferences ([`jersey/property/voisinage.md`](jersey/property/voisinage.md)).

## The threshold test

A voisinage claim needs four things ([`jersey/property/voisinage.md`](jersey/property/voisinage.md)):

1. **Harm** that is objectively identifiable;
2. **Caused by** the defendant's use of their property;
3. **Exceeding** the ordinary inconveniences of neighbourhood; and
4. **Without** adequate justification.

Two practical points the corpus highlights:

- **Planning permission does not exclude voisinage** — the fact your neighbour has consent for an activity does not, on its own, defeat a claim.
- Voisinage does **not override valid servitudes**, but **excessive use** of a servitude can still breach it.

## Does it apply to *your* neighbour problem?

The corpus cannot tell you that — and I shouldn't pretend it can. Whether voisinage bites depends on:

- the **type** of interference (noise, smell, drainage, light, roots, etc. — all are within scope in principle);
- whether it **exceeds ordinary neighbourhood inconvenience** (the load-bearing threshold);
- whether the neighbour has a **defence** — reasonable use, your prior consent/acquiescence, statutory authority, or (limited) prescription;
- whether a **servitude** is engaged.

If you can share what the actual interference is (kind, intensity, duration, when it started, what you've already done about it), I can point you at the more specific corpus material — e.g. construction-noise treatment, agricultural smells, commercial-residential interface, or the procedure for an Order of Justice in the Royal Court. The corpus also recommends, as practical first steps: **document the interference** (logs, photographs, recordings), build a **correspondence trail** with the neighbour, **engage relevant experts** (acoustic, environmental) early, and **mediate** before litigating ([`jersey/property/voisinage.md`](jersey/property/voisinage.md)).
