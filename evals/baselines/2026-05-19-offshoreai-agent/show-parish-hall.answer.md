## Parish Hall Enquiry — what it is

A **Parish Hall Enquiry** is a pre-court forum unique to Jersey, presided over by an elected **Centenier** (an honorary, unpaid parish police officer) of the parish where the alleged offence occurred. It is the route by which **most routine motoring offences** are resolved without reaching the Magistrate's Court. There is no direct UK or other common-law equivalent — it has features of a fixed-penalty notice, a prosecutorial decision, and a summary hearing combined.

The Centenier reviews the evidence, decides whether to charge, and can dispose of the matter with a caution, a written fine of up to **£200 per offence**, or referral to court. They can only impose a fine or caution if you **admit** the offence and **accept** the penalty — and you have the right at any point to **opt out and demand a Magistrate's Court hearing instead** ([`jersey/road-traffic/parish-hall-enquiries.md`](jersey/road-traffic/parish-hall-enquiries.md)).

## When your case goes there

Typical Parish Hall matters include:
- minor speeding (**under 15 mph** over the limit)
- mobile phone offences
- failing to display insurance disc
- some traffic-light and minor careless-driving offences
- parking offences

Your case **does not** go to Parish Hall — it is summoned directly to the Magistrate's Court — for:
- speeding **15 mph or more** over the limit
- drink-driving or drug-driving (any reading)
- dangerous driving (Article 23 RTJL)
- causing death or serious injury by driving
- driving while disqualified
- serious failure-to-stop / report-after-accident
- any offence carrying **mandatory disqualification**, or where the Centenier judges the £200 ceiling inadequate
