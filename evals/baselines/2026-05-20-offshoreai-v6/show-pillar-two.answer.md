**Gating threshold first.** Pillar Two only bites if the group has **consolidated revenue of €750m+ in at least two of the four preceding years**. Below that, none of this applies — the Jersey TopCo stays on zero-ten at 0% and the only live concern is economic substance (`knowledge/jersey/international/pillar-two-mcit.md`; `knowledge/jersey/use-cases/founder-entrepreneur/pillar-two-impact.md`). Most founder/holding structures never cross it (`knowledge/jersey/use-cases/founder-entrepreneur/why-jersey-topco.md`).

**The two jurisdictions in this structure are taxed separately under GloBE.** Pillar Two computes an effective tax rate (ETR) jurisdiction-by-jurisdiction and tops up any jurisdiction below 15% (`knowledge/jersey/international/pillar-two-mcit.md`):

- **The UK operating business** is taxed at the UK corporate rate (well above 15%), so the UK-jurisdiction ETR is already compliant and there is no top-up on the operating profits. The corpus treats foreign operating profits as taxed where earned, with dividends flowing up to the Jersey TopCo typically tax-free at the Jersey level (`knowledge/jersey/use-cases/founder-entrepreneur/why-jersey-topco.md`).
- **The Jersey TopCo** sits at ~0% under zero-ten, so the Jersey-jurisdiction ETR is below 15% and the top-up falls on the TopCo's own **GloBE income**. For a pure holding TopCo that income is usually thin — and note GloBE's exclusions (the substance-based income exclusion based on payroll plus tangible assets, and the treatment of qualifying dividends) reduce the base further (`knowledge/jersey/international/pillar-two-mcit.md`).

**Who collects the Jersey top-up.** Jersey's **MCIT** (Multinational Corporate Income Tax), effective for periods beginning **on/after 1 January 2025**, operates as a **Qualified Domestic Minimum Top-up Tax (QDMTT)**. This means **Jersey itself collects the 15% top-up on the TopCo's Jersey-booked income**, rather than ceding it to a parent jurisdiction under the Income Inclusion Rule (IIR) (`knowledge/jersey/international/pillar-two-mcit.md`; `knowledge/jersey/tax/pillar-two.md`). Because the Jersey TopCo is the ultimate parent here, the QDMTT is what matters; the IIR/UTPR backstops are mainly relevant where a domestic top-up is absent.

**Net effect.** Crossing the threshold turns the headline "0% Jersey TopCo" into an effective 15% on the TopCo's Jersey income, plus materially heavier compliance — GloBE ETR computation, the Global Information Return (GIR), and coordination with the UK's own Pillar Two filing for the group (`knowledge/jersey/use-cases/founder-entrepreneur/pillar-two-impact.md`). The non-tax reasons for a Jersey TopCo (no Jersey CGT/IHT, no stamp duty on share transfers, solvency-based distributions, listing acceptance, continuance flexibility) survive unchanged, and 15% remains competitive with onshore alternatives (`knowledge/jersey/use-cases/founder-entrepreneur/why-jersey-topco.md`; `knowledge/jersey/use-cases/international-lawyer/jersey-topco-ma.md`). Economic substance under the Substance Law 2019 continues to apply independently of MCIT.
