Let me read the most relevant cluster.**Short answer: No, you cannot "just" evict — but yes, you have a clear statutory route.** Non-payment of rent is a breach of the tenancy agreement under the **Residential Tenancy (Jersey) Law 2011 (RTJL)**, and the only lawful way to recover possession is by **court order followed by Viscount enforcement**. Self-help — changing locks, removing possessions, cutting off utilities, physically removing the tenant — is unlawful and may be a **criminal offence**.

## The procedure

1. **Serve a 7-day written breach notice.** It must be in writing, signed, identify the specific arrears (period and amount), and require payment within at least 7 days. The 7 days is a statutory minimum; a defective notice is fatal and forces you to start over. If the tenant pays in full within the window, the breach is remedied and you cannot proceed on those arrears. (`knowledge/jersey/residential-tenancy/non-payment-of-rent.md`)

2. **Apply to the Petty Debts Court** if still unpaid. It has **exclusive original jurisdiction** — the Royal Court does not handle residential tenancy disputes at first instance. You bring the tenancy agreement, condition report, rent ledger/bank evidence, the breach notice with proof of service, and a record of your attempts to engage. You can also seek a money judgment for the arrears. (`knowledge/jersey/residential-tenancy/eviction-procedure.md`)

3. **The hearing.** The court checks procedural compliance, whether the breach is made out, and any tenant defences. It may grant termination + eviction (often with a **stay of execution**, commonly 14–28 days, longer for vulnerable tenants), refuse for procedural defects, and award costs. (`knowledge/jersey/residential-tenancy/eviction-procedure.md`)

4. **Viscount enforcement.** Only the **Viscount** — not you, not a private bailiff — may take possession from a resisting tenant. After any stay expires, you apply for execution; the Viscount attends, removes the tenant (with force if needed), inventories/stores/sells possessions, and restores possession. Viscount fees (several hundred to several thousand pounds) are recoverable from the tenant in theory but often borne by the landlord in practice. (`knowledge/jersey/residential-tenancy/eviction-procedure.md`)

## Tenant protections that make this "not easy"

- **Procedural** — your notice and service must be perfect.
- **Set-off** — if you've failed to repair an unfit property, the tenant can reduce or extinguish the arrears claim.
- **Discrimination / retaliation** — eviction on a protected ground (Discrimination (Jersey) Law 2013) or following a complaint is highly contestable.
- **Vulnerability** — children, pregnancy, disability, mental health, or pending Income Support routinely attract a stay; Children (Jersey) Law 2002 and Mental Health (Jersey) Law 2016 considerations apply.

**Realistic timeline:** typically **2–4 months** from first missed payment to actual possession in a straightforward case, longer if contested or stayed. (`knowledge/jersey/use-cases/tenant-landlord/evicting-non-paying-tenant.md`)

## Practical first steps

Calculate the arrears precisely, check the agreement and condition report, serve a properly drafted 7-day notice and document service, and open a parallel conversation about a payment plan — many cases resolve there. Engage a Jersey advocate if it looks contested. (`knowledge/jersey/use-cases/tenant-landlord/evicting-non-paying-tenant.md`)
