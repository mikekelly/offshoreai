## What this means for an emerging sub-US$100m PE manager

**The corpus's own decision framework** points emerging managers below US$100m toward **BVI Approved Manager + Approved Fund**, and EU-marketing-focused funds toward **Jersey JPF or Guernsey PIF** (`knowledge/CROSS-JURISDICTIONAL-MAP.md`, "Where should I form my fund?"). Mapping that onto your four candidates:

- **BVI Approved Fund** is the literal fit for your profile — it is purpose-built for a stable, smaller fund, with a **US$100m AUM ceiling**, **no mandatory auditor**, and the lowest regulatory intensity (`knowledge/bvi/incubator-and-approved-fund.md`). The catch is the **20-investor cap**, which is tight if you expect more than a handful of LPs, and BVI funds carry less institutional-LP familiarity than Cayman. Pair it with the **BVI Approved Manager** regime on the manager side — a fast-track, lower-cost authorisation explicitly designed for start-up/sub-threshold managers (`knowledge/bvi/approved-manager.md`). (Note the corpus also flags the **BVI Incubator Fund** — US$20m cap, 20 investors, 2-year life — as the track-record-building launchpad if you're starting smaller still.)

- **Cayman ELP** is the global PE default: tax transparency for US/UK/EU LPs, an LPA-driven contractual vehicle your LPs will already understand, no investor or AUM cap, and natural compatibility with Delaware parallel structures (`knowledge/cayman/exempted-limited-partnership.md`). It is the right call if you intend to raise from institutional LPs or scale well past US$100m — but it carries more cost and a mandatory audit, which is heavier than an emerging manager strictly needs.

- **Jersey JPF** suits you if EU marketing matters: 50 investors, ~48h authorisation, the DSP-delegation model (no licensed manager required), and NPPR access into EU member states (`knowledge/jersey/funds/jersey-private-fund.md`; for NPPR see `knowledge/jersey/funds/aifmd-nppr.md`). It is the corpus's flagged choice (with Guernsey PIF) for EU-marketing-focused or family-office pools.

- **Guernsey PIF** is the direct JPF competitor — 50 investors, 1-business-day authorisation, also NPPR-capable — but offers **three routes** (licensed manager / family / qualifying-investor), giving more structuring flexibility, especially for family or related-party LP bases (`knowledge/guernsey/private-investment-fund.md`).

**Short version:** if cost-minimisation and staying small are the priority and your LP count is ≤20, **BVI Approved Fund + Approved Manager** is the tightest fit. If you want institutional-LP credibility and room to scale, **Cayman ELP**. If EU marketing is on your roadmap, **Jersey JPF** or **Guernsey PIF** (PIF if you want route flexibility / a family-investor base). The 20-investor BVI cap vs. the 50-investor Channel Islands cap vs. uncapped Cayman is the single sharpest dividing line.
