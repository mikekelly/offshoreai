## Short answer — when to use each

These four are all members of the **cell-company family** (single regulated entity / shared governance, with statutorily ringfenced sub-pools). The Guernsey PCC was the original (1997); Cayman SPC (1998) and Bermuda SAC (2000) are evolutions; the Guernsey ICC is the second-generation "each cell is its own company" form.

| Vehicle | Reach for it when… |
|---|---|
| **Bermuda SAC** | Your structure is **insurance-led** — captives, rent-a-captive, ILS / cat-bond series, collateralised reinsurance. SACs are heavily insurance-oriented and BMA-supervised; this is the default cell vehicle inside the world's leading (re)insurance/ILS market. |
| **Cayman SPC** | You want a **general-purpose, fund-oriented** cell vehicle — multi-strategy / umbrella hedge funds, multiple share-class pools in one entity, where the LP base already expects a Cayman domicile. SPCs are explicitly general-purpose rather than sector-specific. |
| **Guernsey PCC** | You want the **original, lower-cost single-entity** cell form — captive insurance (its founding and principal use case), funds, structured finance — and you're comfortable relying on statutory ringfencing rather than separate legal personality. |
| **Guernsey ICC** | **Foreign-court recognition risk is acute**, a counterparty (prime broker, custodian, regulated entity) insists on dealing with a separate corporate personality, or you anticipate **cell migration** in/out of the structure. Each ICC cell is separately incorporated, so its separate-entity status is clear beyond doubt — at slightly higher cost/complexity than a PCC. |

## The key decision axes

1. **Sector.** Insurance/ILS → **Bermuda SAC** (or a Guernsey PCC cell for a European-parent captive). General funds → **Cayman SPC** or **Guernsey PCC**.
   (`knowledge/bermuda/segregated-accounts-company.md`, `knowledge/cayman/segregated-portfolio-company.md`)

2. **Legal personality of the cell — the PCC vs ICC choice.** A PCC is *one company with ringfenced internal accounts*; an ICC is *a group of separately-incorporated companies under shared governance*. Choose the ICC when statutory segregation alone is too risky — i.e. foreign courts that may not respect a ringfence, counterparties that only deal with companies, or planned cell migration. Otherwise the PCC is more common and cheaper.
   (`knowledge/guernsey/incorporated-cell-company.md`, `knowledge/guernsey/protected-cell-company.md`)

3. **The shared structural risk for all three single-personality forms (SAC / SPC / PCC):** segregation depends on **proper cell identification when contracting** and on **foreign courts respecting the statutory ringfence**. Improper identification is the primary route to "piercing the segregation veil." The ICC is the structural answer to that risk.
   (`knowledge/cayman/segregated-portfolio-company.md`, `knowledge/guernsey/protected-cell-company.md`)

The corpus's own decision frameworks align with this: for a fund, "multi-strategy single-entity → **Cayman SPC** or **Guernsey PCC**"; for insurance/ILS, "**Bermuda SAC** with Class 3 / 3B SPI" or "**Guernsey PCC** cell" for a European-parent captive (`knowledge/CROSS-JURISDICTIONAL-MAP.md`).
