I'll pull the AIFMD II frontier file and the Jersey NPPR doctrinal file in parallel.## What changes for a Jersey NPPR-marketing AIFM under AIFMD II

**The headline: NPPR access survives, but disclosure and reporting obligations widen.** AIFMD II (Directive (EU) 2024/927, most requirements effective 16 April 2026) preserves Crown Dependencies' access to EU professional investors via member-state National Private Placement Regimes — Jersey continues to meet the EU's tax-transparency and AML criteria. The direct impact on a Jersey AIFM is concentrated in two areas (`knowledge/frontier/aifmd-ii-april-2026.md`):

1. **Expanded investor disclosures** — pre-marketing and ongoing disclosure obligations widened to cover liquidity management, sustainability factors (interlocking with SFDR), and loan-originating-fund disclosures where applicable.
2. **Enhanced Annex IV reporting** to ESMA via competent authorities — new data fields and more granular reporting on specific asset categories and risk exposures. First submissions under the new requirements are expected end-Q2 / early-Q3 2026.

**Indirect impact — the delegation squeeze.** AIFMD II's stricter rules on AIFM delegation to third countries hit the *EU-AIFM-with-CI-delegate* model, not the direct-Jersey-AIFM-with-NPPR model. An EU AIFM delegating portfolio management to a Jersey entity must now meet enhanced substance and oversight requirements and cannot become a "letter-box entity." Carey Olsen / Langham Hall commentary flags the counterintuitive consequence: this makes a **direct Jersey AIFM marketing under NPPR comparatively *more* attractive** than an EU shell dependent on non-EU delegation. (`knowledge/frontier/aifmd-ii-april-2026.md`)

**JFSC AIF Code split.** Jersey is bifurcating its AIF Code of Practice into an **EU AIF Code** (for NPPR marketing into the EU, aligned with AIFMD II) and a **UK AIF Code** (for UK marketing). The EU AIF Code is expected to be finalised through 2026 (`knowledge/frontier/aifmd-ii-april-2026.md`).

**Baseline that does *not* change** (`knowledge/jersey/funds/aifmd-nppr.md`): NPPR remains country-by-country, not an EU-wide passport — the third-country passport extension was never implemented. Germany and France retain stricter NPPR rules. AIFMD-equivalent depository arrangements are still required for EU-marketed AIFs.
