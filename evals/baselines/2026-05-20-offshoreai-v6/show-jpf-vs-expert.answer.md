## Answer: JPF — almost certainly the right vehicle for a club deal

A "club deal" — a small group of sophisticated investors pooling capital for a specific PE opportunity — maps directly onto what the JPF was designed for. The JPF concept file lists **"single-LP or club deal structures"** and **"private equity funds and co-investment vehicles"** explicitly among its default use cases (`knowledge/jersey/funds/jersey-private-fund.md`). The deciding factor is investor count: a JPF takes **up to 50 offers/investors**, all professional/eligible, with **fast-track JFSC authorisation in ~48 hours** and a Jersey-licensed administrator acting as designated service provider (DSP) who carries the AML/CDD and ongoing-supervision burden so the fund itself stays light-touch (same file).

**Choose the Expert Fund only if you breach the JPF envelope.** The Expert Fund's stated reason for existing, relative to the JPF, is to take **"large private-equity vehicles exceeding JPF investor count"** — i.e. when you expect more than 50 investors — and it accepts a slower authorisation (days to weeks), a more detailed offering memorandum, and an "expert investor" qualification (≈US$100k minimum or net-worth/institutional thresholds) in exchange for **no specific investor cap** and wider marketing latitude (`knowledge/jersey/funds/expert-fund.md`).

| Decision driver | Points to |
|---|---|
| ≤50 investors, club/co-invest | **JPF** |
| Speed to launch (48h) | **JPF** |
| >50 investors expected | **Expert Fund** |
| Wider expert-investor marketing | **Expert Fund** |

**Two things to confirm before committing:**
- **EU/UK marketing.** If you plan to market into the EU or UK under AIFMD's National Private Placement Regime, that affects the structuring on either route — see `knowledge/jersey/funds/aifmd-nppr.md` (not read here; flag it to your fund counsel).
- **Vehicle form.** A JPF club deal is most commonly a **Jersey limited partnership**, which is also the conventional PE structure for carry and LP tax treatment (`knowledge/jersey/funds/jersey-private-fund.md`).

**Caveats:**
- All three corpus files I relied on are **`draft` status** (last verified 2026-05-15, 5 days ago) — drafted but not yet editorially verified against the primary JFSC source. The investor caps, authorisation timelines, and the expert-investor monetary thresholds in particular should be re-verified against the current JFSC Jersey Private Funds Guide and the Expert Funds Guide before you rely on them.
- The corpus does not give the JPF's precise "professional/eligible investor" statutory definition in these files — verify investor eligibility against the JFSC guide.
