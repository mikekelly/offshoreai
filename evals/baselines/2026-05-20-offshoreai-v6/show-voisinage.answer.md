## Voisinage — and whether it fits your neighbour problem

**What it is.** Voisinage ("good neighbourhood") is a **Norman customary-law doctrine**, preserved in Jersey though abandoned in most modern legal systems, that governs the relationship between neighbouring landowners. It requires each owner to use their land in a way that does **not cause unreasonable harm** to neighbours — harm that exceeds the ordinary inconveniences of neighbourhood, caused by the defendant's use of the property, without adequate justification (`knowledge/jersey/property/voisinage.md`).

**How it differs from English nuisance.** This matters for your problem, because the test is *not* the English one. Voisinage can apply **without fault** (it has objective / strict-liability elements), it is **not confined** to the enumerated "smell, smoke, noise" nuisance categories, and it rests on a specific Jersey customary foundation. The corpus flags "assuming the English nuisance test applies wholesale" as a classic pitfall (`knowledge/jersey/property/voisinage.md`; `knowledge/jersey/contract/delit-tort.md`).

**Does it apply to your problem?** The corpus lists the situations voisinage typically reaches — and most everyday neighbour disputes fall within them:

- excessive **noise** from the neighbouring property;
- **smells** (livestock, kitchens, refuse), **smoke / fumes**;
- **vibrations** from machinery or construction;
- **surface water / drainage**;
- **tree roots / overhanging branches**;
- **light obstruction**;
- inter-unit noise/smells in apartments (interacting with flying-freehold / share-transfer and service-charge mechanisms).

So whether it "really applies" turns on whether your neighbour's *use of their land* is causing you **identifiable harm beyond ordinary neighbourhood inconvenience, without justification**. Temporary construction noise, for example, is usually just ordinary inconvenience; *prolonged or excessive* disturbance may cross the line (`knowledge/jersey/property/voisinage.md`).

**Two things that do not automatically defeat a claim:** the fact your neighbour has **planning permission** does not exclude voisinage, and the existence of a **servitude** (easement) does not license *excessive* use of it. Conversely, available defences include reasonable use, the claimant's consent/acquiescence, and statutory authority (`knowledge/jersey/property/voisinage.md`).

**Practical next steps the corpus recommends:** document the interference (logs, photos, recordings), engage experts early (e.g. acoustic/environmental), keep a correspondence trail with the neighbour, try **mediation first**, and instruct a Jersey advocate familiar with voisinage; contested claims proceed by Order of Justice in the Royal Court (`knowledge/jersey/property/voisinage.md`).
