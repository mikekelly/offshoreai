## How Guernsey's image-rights register works

Guernsey is the **only jurisdiction in the world** with a statutory image-rights register, created by the **Image Rights (Bailiwick of Guernsey) Ordinance 2012**. It converts what most legal systems treat as a fragmented patchwork (passing off, defamation, US-style right of publicity) into a single **registrable property right** with statutory backing. (`knowledge/guernsey/image-rights-register.md`)

**What you register.** You register a *"personnage"* — the protected persona — and then specific image attributes attached to it: your name (real, professional, or stage), voice, signature, likeness/photographs, distinctive appearance, mannerisms, and other identifying attributes. Both natural persons and fictional characters can underlie a personnage.

**The property right itself.** Once registered, the right is transferable (assignable, licensable), inheritable on death, enforceable against unauthorised use, and in some cases mortgageable as collateral. This is what makes it useful as the *asset base* for licensing — you license a registered title rather than a fuzzy bundle of tort claims.

**Registration mechanics.** Filed with the Guernsey Intellectual Property Office; subject to examination and publication; granted after an opposition period; renewable every 10 years (with longer protection — life plus 100 years — for some categories); the register is public; fees are modest relative to commercial trademark filings.

**Enforcement.** A registered personnage gives you standing in the Guernsey courts and statutory remedies — injunction, damages, account of profits, and delivery up of infringing material. The important limitation: against infringers *outside* Guernsey, the regime is sui generis and foreign courts may not give it direct effect. In practice holders pair the Guernsey registration (as evidence of a proprietary interest) with local-jurisdiction enforcement (trademarks, passing off, right-of-publicity claims), and write Guernsey governing-law/forum clauses into licensing agreements.

## Structuring for licensing and succession

The corpus describes the standard celebrity-wealth pattern: the image-rights registration is combined with a **Guernsey trust or holding company**, so that licensing royalties flow to that vehicle and the rights pass under the structure's succession terms rather than your home-jurisdiction succession regime (subject to applicable conflict-of-laws rules). This is presented as the regime's most valuable use — as the licensing/structuring asset base rather than as a standalone enforcement tool. (`knowledge/guernsey/image-rights-register.md`)

**Where Jersey sits:** the corpus notes Jersey has no image-rights statute — neither do Cayman or BVI. Guernsey is the offering for this. So although this corpus is Jersey-anchored, the answer points you to Guernsey.
