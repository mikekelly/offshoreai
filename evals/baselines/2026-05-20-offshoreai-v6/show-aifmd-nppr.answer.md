## Marketing a Jersey AIF into Germany via NPPR

Germany is **not** reached through an EU-wide passport — Jersey AIFMs cannot use the full AIFMD passport (the proposed "third-country passport" extension to non-EU jurisdictions was never implemented). Instead you access Germany through its **National Private Placement Regime (NPPR)**, country-by-country. The route, in sequence:

1. **AIFM registration with the JFSC** under Jersey's AIFMD-equivalent regime.
2. **Demonstrate AIFMD-equivalent compliance** — capital adequacy, risk management, conflicts of interest, depositary arrangements, transparency/disclosure to investors, and regulatory reporting.
3. **AIFMD-equivalent depositary** — for an EU-marketed AIF you need full AIFMD-equivalent depositary arrangements (the "lighter" Jersey depositary functions available to non-EU-marketed funds are not sufficient here).
4. **Notify the German regulator (BaFin)** under Germany's NPPR for that AIF.
5. Once approved, the AIF may be **marketed to German professional investors** only.

Two cautions specific to Germany. First, the corpus flags Germany (with France) as having **stricter NPPR rules than some other member states** — so the German notification is at the more demanding end of the country-by-country spectrum (`knowledge/jersey/funds/aifmd-nppr.md`). Second, NPPR marketing is to **professional investors**, not retail.
