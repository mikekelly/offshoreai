## What changes

UK carried-interest taxation moves from a CGT basis to an **income-tax basis with a partial exclusion**, in force for carry arising on or after 6 April 2026 (`knowledge/frontier/uk-carried-interest-reform-2026.md`):

- **Old regime (to 5 April 2025):** CGT-based, ~28% effective for additional-rate payers.
- **Transition (6 April 2025 – 5 April 2026):** flat **32%** CGT.
- **New regime (from 6 April 2026):** carry treated as **income**, subject to income tax + Class 4 NICs. For **qualifying** carry a **72.5% multiplier** brings only 72.5% into charge — ~**34.1%** effective for a 45% taxpayer. **Non-qualifying** carry attracts full income tax + NICs, effective rates up to **~47%**.

The **qualifying / non-qualifying split** is the load-bearing distinction: qualifying requires genuine investment-management activity and specified fund characteristics. The definition was tightened in the final legislation, though the government dropped the proposed minimum-investment access conditions. Established UK partners at mature sponsors are usually qualifying by default; new entrants, secondaries participants, and non-traditional structures need careful analysis (`knowledge/frontier/uk-carried-interest-reform-2026.md`).

## What PE partners in the Crown Dependencies should do

**Residency planning for UK partners** is the headline response. Jersey's **HVR / 2(1)(e)** route (bespoke tax-rate cap), Guernsey's Open Market, and the IoM tax cap are being marketed more actively to PE partners facing a 34–47% UK charge (`knowledge/frontier/uk-carried-interest-reform-2026.md`; `knowledge/jersey/immigration-residency/high-value-residency.md`; `knowledge/jersey/tax/high-value-residency-tax.md`). But three cautions are flagged: the UK Statutory Residence Test makes *leaving* hard; there is a real **tail liability** on carry that economically accrued while UK-resident even if realised after departure; and the new legislation has specific anti-avoidance provisions around residence changes. Substance in the destination is essential.

**At the structuring level:**
- **GP-entity location** — CD-domiciled GPs (Jersey companies, Guernsey companies acting as GP of Jersey/Cayman ELP funds) may be more attractive as the carry-allocation entity for non-UK partners, but UK look-through / situs rules still bite where a UK-resident partner gets the economic benefit.
- **Fund vehicles are largely unaffected** — Jersey JPF, Guernsey PIF and Cayman ELP are unchanged at fund level; the action is at GP-entity carry allocation and vesting (`knowledge/frontier/uk-carried-interest-reform-2026.md`; `knowledge/jersey/funds/jersey-private-fund.md`).
- **Co-invest vs carry** — co-invest economics remain CGT-treated, so the distinction is now acutely material and must not be misclassified.
- **Sponsor-side** — document carry arrangements carefully to preserve qualifying status, review GP/LP economics, adjust vesting/forfeiture, and prepare for LP questions on re-pricing and key-partner retention.

Note the pre-6 April 2026 window has now closed: through Q1 2026 many partners accelerated crystallisation to land under the 32% CGT regime, but that planning is no longer available for carry arising after the effective date.
