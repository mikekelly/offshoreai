## The emerging-manager stack — three layers

For a BVI emerging manager, the corpus describes a canonical three-tier stack, picked at the lowest tier consistent with your investor base and AUM:

### 1. Manager — BVI Approved Manager
The **Approved Manager** regime (Investment Business (Approved Manager) Regulations 2012) is the fast-track, light-touch authorisation route purpose-built for start-up, smaller, and family-office managers. It sits below full Investment Business (SIBA) licensing: faster approval (weeks vs months), lower cost, proportionate standards, but still a recognised regulated status that banks, brokers, and administrators will accept. It is AUM-capped — grow past the threshold and you must upgrade to full Investment Business licensing. The regime explicitly contemplates managing BVI Incubator and Approved Funds, so it pairs directly with the fund layer below. (`knowledge/bvi/approved-manager.md`)

### 2. Fund — Incubator Fund *or* Approved Fund
Two distinct light-touch regimes from the SIBA (Incubator and Approved Funds) Regulations 2015. They are **not** interchangeable — pick one:

| Feature | **Incubator Fund** | **Approved Fund** |
|---|---|---|
| Max investors | 20 | 20 |
| Min investment | US$20,000 | None statutory |
| Max AUM | US$20m | US$100m |
| Time limit | 2 years (+1 yr extension) | Permanent |
| Auditor | Not required | Not required |
| Administrator | Not required | Typically required |
| Designed for | Emerging manager building a **track record** | Stable small / family-office fund |

As an emerging manager building a record, the **Incubator Fund** is the natural fit — it is explicitly a launch pad, time-limited, and must convert (typically to a Private Fund) or wind down after its life expires. If you expect to stay small and permanent, the **Approved Fund** is the alternative. The full BVI tier ladder above these is: Incubator → Approved → Private Fund (≤50 investors, audited) → Professional Fund → Public Fund. (`knowledge/bvi/incubator-and-approved-fund.md`, `knowledge/bvi/funds.md`)

### 3. Vehicle — BVI Business Company or Limited Partnership
The fund itself is housed in:
- **BVI Business Company (BC)** under the BC Act — the most common fund vehicle; investors hold shares;
- **Limited Partnership** (BVI Partnership Act) — common for PE/VC, GP/LP structure, tax-transparent in investors' home jurisdictions;
- **SPC (Segregated Portfolio Company)** — cell-style umbrella, less prevalent than the Cayman equivalent.

(`knowledge/bvi/funds.md`, `knowledge/bvi/business-company.md`)

## How it assembles
The corpus gives the start-up assembly directly: form the **GP/management entity** (a BVI BC) and authorise it as **Approved Manager**; form the **fund** (BVI BC or LP) as an **Incubator** or **Approved Fund**; operate with Approved Manager status from inception. The "Approved Manager + Incubator/Approved Fund" combination is named as *the* common emerging-manager stack. (`knowledge/bvi/approved-manager.md`, `knowledge/bvi/incubator-and-approved-fund.md`)

## Two things the corpus flags as easy to get wrong
- **Economic substance** (BVI Economic Substance Act 2018) and **AML/CFT** (Proceeds of Criminal Conduct Act + AMLR) are separate, substantive obligations — not paper formalities — and apply across the stack. (`knowledge/bvi/approved-manager.md`)
- **Watch the ceilings:** Incubator's 2-year life and US$20m / US$20k-per-investor limits, the Approved Fund's US$100m cap, and the Approved Manager's AUM threshold all force an upgrade or conversion when breached. Plan the conversion path early. (`knowledge/bvi/incubator-and-approved-fund.md`)
