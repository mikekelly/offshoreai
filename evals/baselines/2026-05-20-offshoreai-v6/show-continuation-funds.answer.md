## Why continuation funds and GP-led secondaries are growing so fast

A continuation fund is a new vehicle into which a sponsor (GP) moves one or more portfolio assets out of an existing fund, giving the original LPs a choice to **roll** their interest into the new vehicle or **cash out** at a negotiated valuation; "GP-led secondaries" is the wider category (tender offers, strip sales, preferred equity, structured liquidity). The growth is demand-driven by four reinforcing pressures: weak M&A and IPO exit markets through 2022–2025, LPs demanding **DPI** (realised distributions) to recycle into new commitments, GPs unwilling to sell trophy assets at depressed valuations, and abundant secondaries-market capital ready to deploy (`knowledge/frontier/continuation-funds-and-gp-led-secondaries.md`).

The trajectory is steep and broad-based. The 2026 Global Private Equity Outlook survey (cited via Dechert, Nov 2025) records **46% of respondents** using GP-led secondaries or continuation vehicles to expedite LP distributions — roughly **double** the prior year — with planned increases over the next 24 months reported by 51% of North American, 55% of APAC, and 43% of EMEA respondents (the EMEA figure up from 14% in 2024) (`knowledge/frontier/continuation-funds-and-gp-led-secondaries.md`).

## Why Guernsey is winning the business

Guernsey is the **leading European jurisdiction** for continuation funds, and the reasons are structural and durable (`knowledge/guernsey/continuation-funds.md`):

- **Single-asset CFs are not treated as collective investment schemes.** Under the Protection of Investors (Bailiwick of Guernsey) Law 2020, a single-asset continuation fund is characterised as a single co-investment vehicle (closer to a joint venture than a pooled, collectively-managed fund), so it can be established **without CIS authorisation**. This is the structurally most important advantage.
- **Speed.** Single-asset CFs requiring GFSC review get a **3-business-day** approval — the fastest in the European market — letting a sponsor go from term-sheet to closing in 2–3 weeks rather than the typical 4–8.
- **Explicit strategic positioning** by the GFSC and Guernsey Finance, who have marketed the product line aggressively to UK and European sponsors.
- **Mature practitioner base** (Mourant, Ogier, Carey Olsen, Walkers, Appleby, plus admin houses Aztec, IQ-EQ, JTC, Apex) that has handled the transaction volume through 2024–2026.
- **Tax neutrality** (0% standard corporate tax), though Guernsey's QDMTT under the Income Tax (Pillar Two) (Guernsey) Law 2024 applies a 15% top-up to in-scope MNE-group sponsors.

By contrast, Jersey is **strong but second**: its Jersey Private Fund offers a 48-hour JFSC authorisation and a Designated Service Provider model well-suited to continuation vehicles, and it remains competitive for sponsors with existing Jersey infrastructure or trust/family-office-adjacent transactions — but most sponsors with strong CF deal flow have defaulted to Guernsey, whose not-a-CIS treatment of single-asset deals is the decisive edge (`knowledge/jersey/funds/continuation-funds.md`, `knowledge/guernsey/continuation-funds.md`).

**Caveats on Guernsey's edge:** the not-a-CIS treatment does not auto-apply (the structural characterisation matters; engage the GFSC early on novel structures); CFs still need genuine Guernsey substance; and the post-16 April 2026 AIFMD II disclosure uplift is not exempted by the fast-track (`knowledge/guernsey/continuation-funds.md`).
