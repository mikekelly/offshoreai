Bermuda is the world's leading captive-insurance domicile and a top-3 global reinsurance market, established as an insurance centre since the 1960s. The structural advantages relevant to a multinational group captive are:

- **Depth and infrastructure** — the largest captive market globally, with the full professional ecosystem (insurance managers, specialist actuaries, brokers, legal and audit firms) and a regulator (the Bermuda Monetary Authority) experienced across every captive type. ([`knowledge/bermuda/insurance.md`](knowledge/bermuda/insurance.md))
- **Solvency II equivalence** — Bermuda's commercial-class regime is recognised by the EU as equivalent, so Bermuda (re)insurers face reduced collateral requirements when reinsuring EU cedants. It is the only offshore jurisdiction with full equivalence. ([`knowledge/bermuda/insurance-classes.md`](knowledge/bermuda/insurance-classes.md); [`knowledge/CROSS-JURISDICTIONAL-MAP.md`](knowledge/CROSS-JURISDICTIONAL-MAP.md))
- **NAIC qualified jurisdiction** — useful if your group has US-facing risk, again via reduced reinsurance collateral. ([`knowledge/bermuda/insurance.md`](knowledge/bermuda/insurance.md))

The trade-off is higher BMA scrutiny and real substance requirements relative to lighter-touch domiciles. The corpus notes the typical split: Bermuda for the largest captives, catastrophe reinsurance and ILS; Cayman for healthcare captives; Guernsey (PCC-based) for European parents wanting an EU-near structure. ([`knowledge/bermuda/insurance-classes.md`](knowledge/bermuda/insurance-classes.md))

## Which Class

The classes turn on **whose risk** the captive writes:

| Class | Use | Fits your group if… |
|---|---|---|
| **Class 1** | Pure single-parent captive — writes only the risks of the parent / group | …you are self-insuring purely your own group's risks (the standard multinational single-parent captive) |
| **Class 2** | Multi-owner / group / association captive, plus *limited* unrelated business | …the captive is multi-parent, or writes some limited third-party risk |
| **Class 3** | Captive writing *significant* unrelated (commercial third-party) business | …you intend to write material third-party risk |

For a single multinational group insuring its own / intra-group risks, the answer is almost always **Class 1** — the pure single-parent captive, which is the lowest regulatory tier (minimum capital, solvency, reporting and audit obligations rise sharply through the scale, so sponsors pick the lowest class consistent with the business). If you bring in unrelated or partner risks, you move to Class 2 or 3. ([`knowledge/bermuda/insurance-classes.md`](knowledge/bermuda/insurance-classes.md))

Two structuring notes:
- If you plan **multiple programmes or lines** through one licensed entity, a **Segregated Accounts Company (SAC)** lets you ring-fence each cell — common for multi-line captive structures. ([`knowledge/bermuda/segregated-accounts-company.md`](knowledge/bermuda/segregated-accounts-company.md), referenced from insurance-classes.md)
- The **long-term (life) classes A–E** are the parallel scale if the captive writes life/long-term business rather than P&C — Class A being the long-term single-parent captive. ([`knowledge/bermuda/insurance-classes.md`](knowledge/bermuda/insurance-classes.md))
