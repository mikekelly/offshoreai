## The short answer

**Cayman Exempted is the default choice for a NASDAQ listing**, with Bermuda Exempted a strong second in specific sectors, and **BVI BC the weakest of the three on exchange acceptance**.

## Why

**Cayman Exempted** is one of the three jurisdictions (alongside BVI and Delaware) routinely accepted by major exchanges, and it is the standard vehicle for US-listed companies with Asia/EMEA operations — the "Alibaba / TikTok holdco pattern," combined with SEC Form F-1/F-4. Its capital flexibility (multiple share classes, founder/investor structures, no UK-style capital maintenance regime) suits pre-IPO and dual-class listing structures. (`knowledge/cayman/exempted-company.md`)

**Bermuda Exempted** is explicitly "routinely accepted as a listing domicile" on NYSE/NASDAQ/LSE, and is the conventional choice where the listing entity is insurance/reinsurance or ILS-related — Bermuda's dominant sector. The distinguishing procedural feature is **BMA pre-vetting** of incorporation, which adds a regulatory filter (and a little friction) but lends counterparty confidence. (`knowledge/bermuda/exempted-company.md`)

**BVI BC** is the cheapest and fastest to incorporate (hours, low fees), but the corpus flags **more limited exchange acceptance** than Cayman. The Cayman comparison table lists BVI listing acceptance as "NYSE, NASDAQ, HKEx (more limited)," and the cross-jurisdictional listing framework notes BVI carries "some exchange-acceptance limits." For a NASDAQ listing specifically, that limitation counts against it. (`knowledge/bvi/business-company.md`; `knowledge/CROSS-JURISDICTIONAL-MAP.md`)

## Decision framing (from the cross-jurisdictional map)

| Profile | Lean toward |
|---|---|
| US-listed tech holdco | **Cayman Exempted** (the Alibaba pattern) |
| Insurance / reinsurance listing | **Bermuda Exempted** |
| Lower-cost smaller listing | BVI BC (subject to exchange-acceptance limits) |

## Recommendation

For a generic NASDAQ listing, **default to Cayman Exempted** unless you are an insurance/reinsurance/ILS business, in which case **Bermuda Exempted** is the more natural home. Reserve BVI BC for cost-sensitive cases where you have confirmed your specific exchange and underwriters will accept it. Note also the Pillar Two overlay if your group is in scope (≥ EUR 750m revenue): Bermuda's CIT 2023 imposes a 15% QDMTT-equivalent from fiscal years beginning on/after 1 Jan 2025, while Cayman/BVI apply their own QDMTT regimes — a tax-modelling input that may matter more than the form itself.

> **Footnote — Jersey:** If you were ever weighing a *UK/LSE* listing, Jersey (or Guernsey) would enter the picture; for **NASDAQ**, Jersey is not the conventional pick and the corpus points to the Caribbean cluster.
