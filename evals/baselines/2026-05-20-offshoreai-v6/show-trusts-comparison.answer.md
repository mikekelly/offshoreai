## Short version

These three are **not interchangeable** — each was built to solve a different problem:

| | Built to solve | Reach for it when |
|---|---|---|
| **Jersey trust** | Maximum protection of the settlor's freedom to settle against foreign attack, plus settlor control | Forced-heirship defence, civil-law family, settlor wants to retain control |
| **Cayman STAR** | Holding assets with **no beneficiaries** — purpose-only or mixed | Orphan SPV, PTC-ownership, family-governance purpose vehicle |
| **BVI VISTA** | Holding **operating-company shares** without trustee meddling in the business | Founder passing a family business into trust but keeping chosen directors running it |

## When to use which

**Jersey trust — the wealth-wrapper / forced-heirship defence.**
The Jersey trust is the default central wealth vehicle. Its distinctive strength is the **Article 9 firewall**: the Royal Court applies *only* Jersey law to validity, settlor capacity, administration and beneficial rights, and **refuses to give effect to foreign forced-heirship or matrimonial rules** — and foreign judgments inconsistent with Article 9 are unenforceable in Jersey (Art 9(4)) (`knowledge/jersey/trusts/firewall.md`, TJL 1984 Art 9). Pair that with **Article 9A reserved powers**, which lets the settlor keep powers (revoke, direct investment, appoint/remove beneficiaries) without invalidating the trust or making the settlor a trustee (`knowledge/jersey/trusts/reserved-powers.md`, Art 9A). Choose Jersey for **maximum forced-heirship defence** and **settlor-control** structures — the cross-jurisdictional decision framework puts "maximum forced-heirship defence" squarely at Jersey via Art 9(4) (`knowledge/CROSS-JURISDICTIONAL-MAP.md`).

**Cayman STAR — the no-beneficiary / purpose trust.**
STAR (Special Trusts — Alternative Regime Act 1997) permits **non-charitable purpose trusts with no beneficiaries**, enforced by a mandatory **enforcer** rather than beneficiaries, with unlimited duration (`knowledge/cayman/star-trusts.md`). The classic use is the **orphan SPV** in securitisation/structured finance (bankruptcy-remote, no beneficiaries to consolidate), and **PTC-holding** or **family-governance** vehicles. Reach for STAR when the structure is purpose-driven rather than people-driven. Note Jersey's own Article 12 non-charitable purpose trust is functionally similar for orphan and family-governance work — the regimes differ in statutory detail, not core function (`knowledge/cayman/star-trusts.md` comparison table).

**BVI VISTA — the operating-business succession trust.**
VISTA (Virgin Islands Special Trusts Act 2003) **disengages the trustee from managing the underlying company**. It removes the classical *Bartlett v Barclays Bank* duty to monitor and intervene, so the trustee holds the designated company's shares as a custodian of legal title while **"office of director rules" (ODRs)** preserve the settlor/family's chosen control over who runs the business (`knowledge/bvi/vista-trusts.md`). Default is **no intervention**; the instrument defines the narrow exceptions (fraud, gross misconduct). Reach for VISTA for **family-business succession** where the founder wants operating-company shares in trust but does not want a professional trustee second-guessing management — the decision framework lists "family-business succession with disengaged trustee" → BVI VISTA (`knowledge/CROSS-JURISDICTIONAL-MAP.md`).

## How they combine

These often appear in the **same** structure rather than as either/or — e.g. a **Jersey trust** as the central wealth wrapper, with a **Cayman STAR** trust owning a PTC and **BVI/Cayman** underlying companies for specific asset classes (`knowledge/CROSS-JURISDICTIONAL-MAP.md`, "Where should I plan my international wealth?").
