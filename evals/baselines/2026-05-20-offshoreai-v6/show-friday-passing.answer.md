Your property transfer happens **in court** because Jersey, uniquely, **passes real-property contracts before the Royal Court** rather than completing them privately. This is a **Norman customary-law inheritance**: the Royal Court (sitting as the **Samedi Division**) is the historic public repository of property-title records, and the transfer of title legally *takes effect* by the Court's act — the contract is read or referenced, the Greffier records and signs it, and title (and possession) pass at that moment. In practice your lawyers appear as attorneys for you under a power of attorney, so you needn't attend personally. ([`knowledge/jersey/property/contract-passing.md`](knowledge/jersey/property/contract-passing.md))

The rationale the corpus gives for the in-court procedure: **public** transfer of real property, the Royal Court as the **repository of property records**, **certainty** about title transitions, and **visibility** of the fact of transaction (though the price is kept private). This contrasts with English conveyancing, which is largely private until completion. ([`knowledge/jersey/property/contract-passing.md`](knowledge/jersey/property/contract-passing.md))

## On the "Friday afternoon" specifically

Here I have to be candid about a gap. The corpus confirms the in-court passing procedure, but it does **not substantiate the Friday-afternoon timing as the reason**:

- The doctrinal file says property contracts are passed in the Samedi Division **"most weekdays"** — not specifically Friday. ([`knowledge/jersey/property/contract-passing.md`](knowledge/jersey/property/contract-passing.md))
- The cross-jurisdictional map lists "Royal Court Friday-afternoon property passings" as a distinctive Jersey feature, but only as an unsourced passing label, with no explanation of *why* Friday. ([`knowledge/CROSS-JURISDICTIONAL-MAP.md`](knowledge/CROSS-JURISDICTIONAL-MAP.md))
- The "Samedi" name itself derives from the court's **historical Saturday sittings**, not Friday. ([`knowledge/jersey/legal-system/royal-court.md`](knowledge/jersey/legal-system/royal-court.md))

So the corpus explains *why your transfer is in court at all*, but **does not cover why conveyancing passings are conventionally scheduled for Friday afternoon**. That convention (widely understood in practice to be the customary day the Royal Court sets aside for property contracts) is not documented or sourced in the corpus, and I won't synthesise a reason from outside it.
