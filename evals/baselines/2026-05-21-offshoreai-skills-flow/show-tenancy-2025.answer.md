## What changed on 15 April 2026

The **Residential Tenancy (Jersey) Amendment Law 2025** came into force on **15 April 2026** — the most significant Jersey residential-tenancy reform in over a decade. From that date the post-amendment regime applies to new tenancies, with transitional rules for existing ones. The headline changes:

- **Fixed terms capped at 3 years**, and **successive fixed terms prohibited** — a tenancy ending at the close of a fixed term either ends outright or converts to periodic (down from the prior 9-year ceiling for non-contract leases).
- **Tenant exit on one month's notice** for *any* tenancy, including a fixed term — no more fixed-term lock-in.
- **Landlord termination of a periodic tenancy** now requires either a permitted ground (breach, sale, owner-occupation, redevelopment) or **12 months' no-fault notice** — replacing the prior 3-month no-fault notice (3 months' notice now only on a justified ground).
- **Rent caps**: increases only **once per year**, **no more than Jersey RPI**, with **2 months' written notice**, unless rent is "significantly below market rent" (a narrow, challengeable carve-out).
- **A new independent Rent Tribunal** to hear rent-increase / RPI / once-yearly / notice disputes (but not social-housing or employment-linked accommodation, and not eviction/breach — those stay with the Petty Debts Court).
- **Strengthened written-form rules** — all tenant fees must be clearly identified; hidden fees are unenforceable and may be an offence.

**Transitional treatment**: new tenancies get the full new regime; pre-existing **periodic** tenancies convert immediately (rent caps, 12-month no-fault notice); pre-existing **fixed-term** tenancies continue under old law until expiry, then convert; successive-fixed-term clauses become unenforceable at expiry of the current term.

What did *not* change: Petty Debts Court eviction/breach jurisdiction, Viscount enforcement, the condition-report regime, the Tenants' Deposit Scheme, the lodging-house exclusion, and the 9-year contract-of-lease ceiling.
