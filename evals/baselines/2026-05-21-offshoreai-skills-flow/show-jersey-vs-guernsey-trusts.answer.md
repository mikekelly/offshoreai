## Jersey vs Guernsey trust — when to pick which

**Short version: the two regimes are close substitutes.** Both descend from a shared Norman customary heritage, both recognise discretionary/fixed-interest/purpose trusts, both allow settlor-reserved powers, both have a statutory firewall, both permit perpetual trusts, and both route final appeals to the Privy Council. For most cross-border family-wealth structures they are competitive, and the choice often turns on practical factors rather than legal substance: sponsor familiarity, existing service-provider relationships, and where the administrator/group infrastructure already sits (`knowledge/guernsey/trust-law-differences-from-jersey.md`).

Where the law actually pushes one way:

### Lean Jersey when…
- **Maximum forced-heirship / matrimonial defence is the priority.** Jersey's **Article 9 firewall** — specifically the **Article 9(4)** non-recognition of inconsistent *foreign judgments*, added by the Trusts (Amendment No. 4) Law 2006 — is more *explicit* than Guernsey's s.14 of the TGL 2007. Guernsey's protection is comparable in outcome but narrower in detail on foreign judgments, so sophisticated defensive structures still marginally prefer Jersey (`knowledge/jersey/trusts/firewall.md`; `knowledge/guernsey/trust-law-differences-from-jersey.md`).
- **You want deep case law on setting aside trustee decisions for mistake.** Both jurisdictions have codified a mistake regime wider than the English *Pitt v Holt* line, but Jersey's **Article 47B–J** jurisprudence (the *Re S&T Trusts* test) is more developed (`knowledge/guernsey/trust-law-differences-from-jersey.md`).
- **You want the largest trust-company industry** and the deepest reserved-powers menu (Jersey's **Article 9A** lists a wide, specific set of retainable powers with trustee protection and a "settlor is not thereby a trustee" rule) (`knowledge/jersey/trusts/reserved-powers.md`).

### Lean Guernsey when…
- **You need a cell-company wrapper for the trustee/structure.** Guernsey offers both the **PCC and the ICC** (it invented the PCC in 1997); Jersey has the PCC but not the ICC.
- **The structure pairs with a Guernsey fund** (PIF rather than Jersey's JPF), or where existing Guernsey administrator relationships and customary-law nuances favour staying in-Bailiwick (`knowledge/guernsey/trust-law-differences-from-jersey.md`).

### Where it's a wash
Reserved-powers capability, confidentiality, perpetual duration, beneficiary information rights (both apply *Schmidt v Rosewood*), trust-company regulation, and EU-marketing context are **broadly equivalent** — you can build the same outcome in either (`knowledge/guernsey/trust-law-differences-from-jersey.md`).

**Bottom line:** default to whichever jurisdiction your advisers and administrator already know; tilt to **Jersey** specifically for maximum forced-heirship defence (Article 9(4)) and mature mistake case law, and to **Guernsey** specifically if you need an ICC or are combining with Guernsey fund infrastructure.
