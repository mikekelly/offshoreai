## Setting up a forced-heirship-proof Jersey trust for an Italian HNW family

The corpus addresses this exact scenario directly. Here is the structure it lays out.

**1. Use a Jersey discretionary trust as the wrapper.**
Settle a discretionary trust under the Trusts (Jersey) Law 1984, with an express choice of Jersey law as the proper law (this is what engages the firewall). The discretionary form is deliberate: no beneficiary holds a fixed entitlement that an Italian court could attach, and the trustee retains distributive flexibility. Trust over foundation here because the discretionary trust is well-understood by foreign tax authorities and the Article 9 firewall is in its most fully developed form for trusts (`worked-example-international-family-wealth.md`, Steps 1–2).

**2. The Article 9 firewall is the core forced-heirship defence.**
Article 9 directs the Royal Court to apply *Jersey law only* to the trust's validity, the settlor's capacity, administration, and beneficial rights — and to disregard foreign forced-heirship rules such as the Italian *legittima*. Crucially, Article 9(2) requires the court to proceed without regard to whether a foreign law treats the disposition as defeating reserved-share rights, and **Article 9(4) makes a foreign judgment inconsistent with Article 9 (e.g. an Italian clawback order) unenforceable in Jersey**. This is the strongest explicit forced-heirship defence among the offshore jurisdictions (`firewall.md`; `worked-example`, Step 5).

**Know the limits before you rely on it.** The firewall binds the Royal Court and Jersey-situs assets — it does *not* bind a foreign court acting *in personam* over an Italian-resident beneficiary, and it cannot protect assets physically located in the foreign jurisdiction. Article 9(2A) also means it cannot validate a disposition the settlor had no power to make, override local-law transfer formalities, or cure an invalid will. The corpus recommends parallel substantive lifetime planning in Italy (e.g. a notarial *patto di famiglia*) rather than relying on the firewall alone (`firewall.md`, "Limits" and "Practical drafting").

**3. Layer in Article 9A reserved powers for family comfort.**
The settlor can reserve powers — investment direction, appointment/removal of trustees, consent over distributions, power to add/remove beneficiaries — without invalidating the trust (Article 9A(1)), and a trustee acting on a properly reserved power does not breach trust (Article 9A(3)). Draft the reserved powers as an *explicit list* (not a catch-all), specify the mechanism of exercise, and address what happens to the powers on the settlor's death or incapacity. Watch the sham line: reserved *powers* are protected, but retained *beneficial ownership* fails the Article 2 existence test and loses firewall protection entirely (`reserved-powers.md`).

**4. Hold assets through underlying companies, with a US blocker.**
Insert holding companies beneath the trust rather than holding investments directly — cleaner administration, counterparty acceptance, and asset-class-specific structuring. The corpus maps the asset classes specifically:
- **US public equities** → a non-US blocker corporation (Cayman or BVI). A non-US-domiciled individual holding US-situs assets directly faces US estate tax above only a ~$60,000 threshold; the blocker means the family never directly owns US assets, so death triggers no US estate tax.
- **EU operating-company shares** → Luxembourg or Jersey holdco for treaty access.
- **Swiss portfolio** → held by the underlying company directly.
- **Caribbean villa** → a BVI BC owning the property (avoids Caribbean estate tax) (`worked-example`, Steps 3 & 7).

**5. Consider a Private Trust Company (PTC) as the structure matures.**
For greater family governance, a Jersey PTC (owned by a purpose trust) can replace the professional TCB trustee, with family, advisers and professionals as directors — typically added once governance becomes the priority rather than at inception (`worked-example`, Step 4).

**6. Plan around the UK-resident child and reporting from day one.**
- The UK-resident adult child triggers UK anti-avoidance analysis (settlements code, transfer-of-assets-abroad, the IHT relevant-property regime, excluded-property questions) — specialist UK tax advice is essential.
- The structure is fully transparent, not secret: **CRS** reporting on controlling persons (Italy, UK, possibly US), **FATCA** if any US person appears, **DAC6** on cross-border arrangements with an EU intermediary, and **UK TRS** if there is UK nexus. Italian tax declarations of the trust are also required (`worked-example`, Steps 6 & 8; `wormhole-international-family-wealth.md`).

**In one line:** a Jersey discretionary trust with an express Jersey-law choice (engaging the Article 9 firewall and Article 9(4) judgment-blocking), Article 9A reserved powers, assets held through underlying companies with a non-US blocker for US equities, paired with Italian-side lifetime planning and full CRS/FATCA/DAC6/TRS reporting.
