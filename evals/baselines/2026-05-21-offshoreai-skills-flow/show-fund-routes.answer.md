## How each fits an emerging PE manager

**Cayman ELP** — the global default for closed-ended PE/VC and the form your LPs will most expect, especially US/institutional money. Tax-transparent, LPA-driven flexibility, routine in parallel structures (Cayman ELP for non-US LPs + Delaware LP for US LPs). The trade-off versus the others: it registers as a private fund under the Private Funds Act 2020, so you carry CIMA registration, annual filings and **audited financials** — heavier than the light-touch CD regimes — and there is **no EU NPPR route** (`knowledge/cayman/exempted-limited-partnership.md`).

**Jersey JPF** — the default Jersey PE vehicle: up to 50 professional/eligible investors, ~48h authorisation, and the **designated service provider (DSP)** model where a Jersey-licensed administrator absorbs CDD/AML and substitutes for direct JFSC oversight. Crucially for fundraising into Europe, Jersey offers **NPPR access to EU member states** (`knowledge/jersey/funds/jersey-private-fund.md`; cross-jurisdictional map).

**Guernsey PIF** — the close competitor to the JPF: also 50 investors, ~1 business day (Route 1), also NPPR-capable. Its differentiator is route flexibility — Route 2 for genuine family/private-relationship pools, Route 3 for qualifying investors — and strong PCC/ICC options for multi-strategy platforms. Note PIFs require audited statements (`knowledge/guernsey/private-investment-fund.md`).

**BVI Approved Fund** — the most explicitly small-and-emerging option, and the one that maps to your "sub-US$100m" framing literally: it is **capped at US$100m AUM and 20 investors**, permanent, with **no mandatory auditor** (administrator typically required) — the lowest cost/friction here. The common emerging-manager stack is **Approved Manager + Approved Fund** (`knowledge/bvi/incubator-and-approved-fund.md`; `knowledge/bvi/approved-manager.md`). One thing to flag: if your real goal is *building a track record* before scaling, the corpus notes the **BVI Incubator Fund** (≤20 investors, US$20k minimum, US$20m max, 2-year+1 life) is the regime purpose-built for brand-new managers — the Approved Fund is for a stable small fund rather than a launch pad (`knowledge/bvi/incubator-and-approved-fund.md`).

## Suggested decision path

- **US / global institutional LPs, expect to scale past US$100m or 20 LPs** → Cayman ELP, accepting the audit + CIMA load.
- **EU LPs or you need to market into Europe** → Jersey JPF or Guernsey PIF (only these two carry NPPR).
- **Small, closely-held, cost-sensitive, no EU marketing, staying under US$100m / 20 LPs** → BVI Approved Fund (or Incubator if it's purely a track-record build).
- **JPF vs PIF tie-breaker**: JPF if you prefer the DSP regulatory-predictability model; PIF if you want route flexibility (family/qualifying-investor) or PCC/ICC multi-strategy segregation.
