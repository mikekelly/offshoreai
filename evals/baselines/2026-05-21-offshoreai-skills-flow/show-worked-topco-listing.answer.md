## Jersey TopCo above a UK Opco, listing on the LSE — the structure

Note up front: because your operating business is a **UK** company, you do **not** migrate it into Jersey by continuance. The UK does not permit outbound continuance to Jersey, so the standard route is to incorporate a fresh Jersey TopCo and bring the UK company underneath it via a share-for-share exchange (`migration-pre-ipo.md` — "UK TopCo migrating to Jersey: UK doesn't permit outbound continuance... Alternative: Newco Jersey TopCo above UK company"). The end state is: public investors → Jersey TopCo (LSE-listed) → UK Opco (`worked-example-jersey-topco-uk-listing.md`).

### Step 1 — Incorporate the Jersey TopCo
A Jersey **public company limited by shares** under the Companies (Jersey) Law 1991: IPO-ready memorandum and articles, no-par-value shares (common), at least two directors, a Jersey-resident corporate-services provider for the registered office, and beneficial-ownership data lodged with the (private) Companies Registry. Incorporation is **1–3 days** (`worked-example-jersey-topco-uk-listing.md`, Step 1).

### Step 2 — Share-for-share exchange
UK shareholders of the operating company exchange their shares for new Jersey TopCo shares (typically 1:1); TopCo ends up owning 100% of the UK Opco. Tax points (`worked-example-jersey-topco-uk-listing.md`, Step 2):
- **UK CGT** — share-for-share rollover relief under **TCGA s.135** generally available (subject to anti-avoidance), deferring gains;
- **UK stamp duty** — 0.5% potentially payable on the transfer of UK shares to TopCo; reliefs may apply for genuine reorganisations — a key leakage point to structure around;
- **Jersey** — no Jersey tax event on the exchange.

### Step 3 — TopCo tax position
The TopCo is Jersey-tax-resident (incorporated there + management and control in Jersey via Jersey-resident directors). Under the zero-ten regime (Income Tax (Jersey) Law 1961) a generic holding company pays **0%** (`worked-example...`, Step 3; `knowledge/jersey/tax/zero-ten.md`). A pure holding company faces the **reduced economic-substance test** under the Taxation (Companies — Economic Substance) (Jersey) Law 2019 — adequate Jersey people and premises, but a low bar (`knowledge/jersey/tax/economic-substance.md`).

### Step 4 — Pillar Two check
If the consolidated group has annual revenue **≥ EUR 750m**, Pillar Two engages and Jersey's **MCIT 2025** QDMTT tops the TopCo's income up to 15% (kept in Jersey rather than ceded to the UK under the IIR), running parallel to UK Pillar Two. Below the threshold, the 0% position holds (`worked-example...`, Step 4; `knowledge/jersey/tax/pillar-two.md`).

### Step 5 — Capital structure
CJL 1991 gives IPO-useful flexibility: multiple share classes (founder / investor / public), no-par-value and redeemable shares, treasury shares, buy-backs, and capital reductions by special resolution + solvency statement (less court-intensive than English law). Dual-class super-voting structures are permitted subject to protections (`worked-example...`, Steps 5 and "Common variants").

### Step 6 — Convert to public and list on the LSE
Convert private→public under **Article 17** of the Companies Law (special resolution + amended M&A) close to the IPO date (`pre-ipo-jersey.md`). Jersey TopCos are routinely accepted across the LSE Main Market (Premium/Standard) and AIM. A prospectus is required (or an AIM admission document), with Jersey legal opinions and corporate authorities; Jersey advocates work alongside UK securities counsel (`worked-example...`, Step 6; `listing-rule-overlay.md`).

The **listing-rule overlay** sits on top of Jersey law (`listing-rule-overlay.md`): Jersey company law is the floor; listing rules add disclosure, related-party rules, Class 1/2 transaction approvals, MAR market-abuse compliance, and — for a Premium listing — the **UK Corporate Governance Code** (independent directors, separation of Chair/CEO, audit/remuneration/nomination committees, comply-or-explain). The **UK Takeover Code** applies to a Jersey-incorporated company with securities on a UK regulated market.

### Step 7 — Post-IPO / dividends
The JFSC does not generally regulate the TopCo unless it carries on regulated business; Jersey Registry filings (annual return, beneficial ownership) continue; keep at least one Jersey-resident director for substance. Dividends are solvency-based under CJL 1991 (no English-style retained-earnings test), with **0% Jersey withholding** on dividends to non-resident shareholders — though UK-resident holders are taxed in the UK on receipt (`worked-example...`, Steps 7–8).

### Main pitfalls
Stamp-duty leakage on the exchange; protecting TopCo's Jersey tax residence through genuine Jersey-based decision-making; Pillar Two if in scope; satisfying substance (no pure-paper presence); and the FCA prospectus / sponsor process (`worked-example...`, "Pitfalls").
