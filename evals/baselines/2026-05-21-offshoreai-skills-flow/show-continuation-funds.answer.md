The growth is demand-driven, and the frontier corpus is explicit that this is **the single most active area of PE fund structuring in 2024–2026**, with adoption roughly doubling year-on-year (46% of respondents in the 2026 Global PE Outlook survey using GP-led secondaries/continuation vehicles, up from ~half that the prior year). Four forces converge:

- **A stalled exit environment** — weak M&A and IPO markets through 2022–2025 left sponsors unable to realise assets conventionally;
- **LP pressure for DPI** (distributed-to-paid-in capital) so LPs can recycle into new commitments;
- **GPs unwilling to sell "trophy" assets** into depressed valuations — a continuation fund lets them keep holding while offering existing LPs a roll-or-cash election;
- **A deep pool of secondaries capital** ready to deploy into these structures.

## Why Guernsey is winning the business

The corpus attributes Guernsey's lead to a small set of **specific and durable** advantages, not marketing alone:

1. **The "not-a-CIS" treatment for single-asset CFs** — the structurally most important factor. A single-asset continuation fund is *not* treated as a collective investment scheme under the Protection of Investors (Bailiwick of Guernsey) Law 2020, because it is characterised as a single co-investment vehicle (closer to a JV than a pooled, collectively-managed fund). That removes the CIS authorisation pathway entirely.
2. **GFSC 3-business-day approval** for single-asset CFs that do need review — the fastest in the European market, taking term-sheet-to-close to ~2–3 weeks rather than 4–8.
3. **Explicit strategic positioning** by the GFSC and Guernsey Finance, who have marketed the product line aggressively to UK and European sponsors.
4. **A mature practitioner ecosystem** — the major Channel Islands firms and admin houses have already handled the transaction volume.
5. **Tax neutrality** (0% standard corporate tax), with substance-light treatment for the new vehicle.

(`knowledge/guernsey/continuation-funds.md`)

**Versus Jersey:** Jersey is "strong but second." It competes via the Jersey Private Fund (48-hour JFSC authorisation) and the Designated Service Provider model, and remains preferred for sponsors with existing Jersey infrastructure or trust/family-office-adjacent transactions — but most sponsors with strong CF deal flow have defaulted to Guernsey through 2024–2026, where the single-asset not-a-CIS regulatory edge is most visible. (`knowledge/jersey/funds/continuation-funds.md`, `knowledge/guernsey/continuation-funds.md`)

**Caveats worth flagging:** Guernsey's speed does not exempt the deal from genuine **substance** (Economic Substance (Guernsey) Law 2018), from **AIFMD II disclosure** obligations post-16 April 2026, or from **GFSC conflicts scrutiny** — the GP sits on both sides of a GP-led secondary, so independent valuation, a fairness opinion, and LPAC consent are standard. The not-a-CIS characterisation is also not automatic; novel structures should be cleared with the GFSC early.
