## AIFMD NPPR route for a Jersey AIF marketing into Germany

Jersey is a non-EU jurisdiction, so it has **no full AIFMD passport** — that is available only to EU-AIFM-managed EU AIFs, and the proposed "third-country passport" extension to non-EU jurisdictions was never implemented. Instead, a Jersey AIF reaches German professional investors through the **National Private Placement Regime (NPPR)**, which is approved country-by-country rather than EU-wide. (`knowledge/jersey/funds/aifmd-nppr.md`)

The route, in sequence:

1. **AIFM registers with the JFSC** under Jersey's AIFMD-equivalent regime.
2. **The AIFM demonstrates substantive AIFMD-equivalent compliance** — capital adequacy, risk management, conflicts of interest, depository arrangements, investor transparency, and regulatory reporting.
3. **The AIF is notified to BaFin** (Germany's regulator) under Germany's NPPR rules. The corpus flags that **Germany (with France) applies stricter NPPR rules than some other member states**, so the German notification is at the more demanding end of the spectrum.
4. **Once approved, the AIF may be marketed to German professional investors.**

**Depository:** an EU-marketed AIF going through NPPR must have **AIFMD-equivalent depository arrangements** (custody and oversight functions), not the lighter depository Jersey permits for non-EU-marketed funds. (`knowledge/jersey/funds/aifmd-nppr.md`)

The common Jersey vehicle for AIFMD-NPPR marketing is the **Expert Fund** (`knowledge/jersey/funds/expert-fund.md`, cross-referenced).
