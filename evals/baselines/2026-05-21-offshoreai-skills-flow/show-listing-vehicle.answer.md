This is a comparative cross-jurisdictional question — I'll pull the relevant company files for the three Caribbean-cluster vehicles plus the Jersey TopCo use-case material for the listing-vehicle framing.## Short answer

For a NASDAQ listing, **Cayman Exempted is the default choice** — it's the standard US-listing holdco vehicle (the Alibaba pattern). **Bermuda Exempted** is the right pick if you're an **insurance/reinsurance** business, where it's the established listing domicile. **BVI BC** is viable mainly for **lower-cost, smaller listings**, and carries some exchange-acceptance friction that makes it the weakest of the three for a US main-board listing.

The decision framework in the cross-jurisdictional map maps directly onto your question (`knowledge/CROSS-JURISDICTIONAL-MAP.md`, "Where should I form my listing-vehicle TopCo?"):

| Profile | Lean toward |
|---|---|
| US-listed tech holdco | **Cayman Exempted** (the Alibaba pattern) |
| Insurance / reinsurance listing | **Bermuda Exempted** |
| Lower-cost smaller listing | **BVI BC** (some exchange-acceptance limits) |

## Why Cayman is the default for NASDAQ

The Cayman Exempted Company is the workhorse US-listing holdco — its explicitly listed use cases include **pre-IPO holdcos** at the top of group structures, and it offers the capital-structure flexibility (multiple share classes, founder/investor classes, par or no-par) that IPO structuring needs. Cayman has no general direct tax and a 20-year (renewable to 30) tax undertaking is available (`knowledge/cayman/exempted-company.md`). This is the most-accepted offshore vehicle on US exchanges.

## When Bermuda wins

Bermuda Exempted companies are **routinely accepted as a listing domicile on NYSE / NASDAQ / LSE**, and the file lists "Listed holdcos on NYSE / NASDAQ / LSE" as a core use case. Bermuda's distinctive procedural feature is **BMA pre-vetting** of incorporations, which delivers regulatory confidence to counterparties — a positive for some listings. It is the natural home for an **insurance/reinsurance** listing given that's Bermuda's dominant sector (`knowledge/bermuda/exempted-company.md`).

## Why BVI is the weakest of the three here

The BVI BC is the cheapest and fastest to incorporate (hours, low fees) and is a familiar international vehicle, but the map flags **"some exchange-acceptance limits"** for listing use, and the corpus positions the BC for volume/standard structures rather than sophisticated listing vehicles (`knowledge/bvi/business-company.md`; `knowledge/CROSS-JURISDICTIONAL-MAP.md`). Reserve it for lower-cost, smaller listings.

## Two things to factor in before deciding

1. **Pillar Two.** All three jurisdictions have enacted a 15% QDMTT-equivalent for in-scope groups (consolidated revenue ≥ EUR 750m): Bermuda's **Corporate Income Tax Act 2023** (15%, effective fiscal years from 1 Jan 2025), Cayman's MCIT 2025, and BVI's MTT 2025 (`knowledge/bermuda/exempted-company.md`; `knowledge/CROSS-JURISDICTIONAL-MAP.md`). If your group is at that scale, the "no direct tax" advantage is materially reduced everywhere, so the choice turns on listing-market acceptance and sector fit, not headline rate.
2. **You named only the three Caribbean vehicles, but Jersey is a live alternative for a US listing too** — though a weaker one for NASDAQ specifically. The corpus notes Jersey TopCos are "well-accepted" by LSE Main Market and AIM, but for **NASDAQ/NYSE it says US listings need more diligence** ("to some extent — more diligence needed for US listings") (`knowledge/jersey/use-cases/founder-entrepreneur/why-jersey-topco.md`). So if NASDAQ is firmly the venue, Cayman remains the cleaner path; Jersey is stronger if a UK/European listing is also in contemplation.
