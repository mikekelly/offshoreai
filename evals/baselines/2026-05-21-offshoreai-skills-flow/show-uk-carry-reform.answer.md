UK carried-interest taxation moves from a **CGT-based regime to an income-tax-based regime with a partial exclusion**:

- Carry arising on/after 6 April 2026 is treated as **income**, subject to **income tax + Class 4 NICs**.
- For **qualifying** carry, a **72.5% multiplier** applies (only 72.5% is brought into charge) → effective rate ~**34.1%** for a 45% additional-rate taxpayer.
- **Non-qualifying** carry: full income tax + NICs, effective rates up to ~**47%**.
- The prior path: ~28% under the old CGT regime, then a flat **32%** in the 6 Apr 2025–5 Apr 2026 transition window.

The qualifying/non-qualifying split turns on genuine investment-management activity and fund characteristics. For established UK-resident partners at mature sponsors, qualifying status is the default; new entrants, secondaries managers, and non-traditional structures need careful analysis. ## What Crown Dependencies PE partners should do

**Pre-6 April crystallisation (now largely closed):** through Q1 2026 many partners accelerated carry realisations — distributions, disposals, restructurings — to land under the 32% CGT basis rather than the new income regime.

**Residency planning for UK partners** — the headline CD angle:
- **Jersey HVR (2(1)(e))** — bespoke high-value-residency route with a tax-rate cap and specific eligibility;
- **Guernsey Open Market** (house-purchase-based, 20% tax) and **IoM tax cap** are also being marketed more actively to PE partners.

But three cautions: the **UK Statutory Residence Test** bites quickly on leaving; there is **tail liability** on carry that economically accrued while UK-resident even if realised after departure; and **real substance** in the destination is essential, with specific anti-avoidance provisions around residence changes.

**Structuring focus:**
- The fund vehicles themselves (**Jersey JPF**, **Guernsey PIF**, **Cayman ELP**) are unchanged — the action is at the **GP entity / carry-allocation level** (partnership profit allocation, carry vesting/forfeiture).
- CD-domiciled GPs may become more attractive for non-UK partners, though UK look-through/situs rules still apply where a UK-resident partner gets the economic benefit.
- The **carry-vs-co-invest** distinction is now acutely material — co-invest economics remain CGT-treated, so misclassification is costly.

**Sponsor-side:** document carry arrangements carefully to preserve qualifying status, review GP/LP economics, and prepare for LP questions on re-pricing and UK-partner retention.

**Watch next:** ongoing HMRC manual guidance, edge-case treatment for secondaries/fund-of-funds/infrastructure, first tribunal appeals (~2027–28), and formal CD competitive marketing of residency programs.
