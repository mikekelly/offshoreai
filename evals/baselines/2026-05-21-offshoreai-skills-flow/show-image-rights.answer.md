## Guernsey's image-rights register

Guernsey is the **only jurisdiction in the world with a statutory image-rights register** for individuals, created by the **Image Rights (Bailiwick of Guernsey) Ordinance 2012**. It converts what most legal systems treat as a fragmented patchwork (passing off, defamation, US state right-of-publicity, personality torts) into a single **registrable property right** that is transferable, licensable, inheritable, enforceable, and in some cases mortgageable. (`knowledge/guernsey/image-rights-register.md`)

**What you register.** You register a *"personnage"* — the persona — and then specific image attributes against it: name (real, professional, or stage), voice, signature, likeness/photographs, distinctive appearance, mannerisms, and other identifying attributes. Both natural persons and fictional characters can sit behind a personnage. (`knowledge/guernsey/image-rights-register.md`)

**Process.** Filed with the Guernsey Intellectual Property Office, examined, published, and granted after an opposition period. Registration is **public**, renewable every 10 years (with protection extending to life-plus-100-years for some categories), and carries modest fees relative to commercial trademark filings. (`knowledge/guernsey/image-rights-register.md`)

**Licensing and structuring** — your core interest. The registered right is most valuable as the **asset base for licensing agreements**. The typical structure: settle the image rights into a Guernsey trust or hold them in a Guernsey/holding company, license use to third parties, and route royalty flow to that vehicle — combining the registration with a Guernsey trust/company and favourable tax for succession and estate planning. (`knowledge/guernsey/image-rights-register.md`)

**Enforcement.** A registered personnage gives you standing in Guernsey courts with statutory remedies — injunction, damages, account of profits, delivery-up of infringing material. (`knowledge/guernsey/image-rights-register.md`)

**The key limitation to understand.** The regime is *sui generis*, so **foreign-court recognition is the main practical question** — courts outside Guernsey may not give the registration direct effect. In practice it is best used alongside local-jurisdiction IP/publicity enforcement, with the Guernsey registration serving as evidence of a proprietary interest and as the licensing asset base, rather than as a standalone global enforcement tool. Note too that the **public** nature of the register may not suit those wanting privacy. (`knowledge/guernsey/image-rights-register.md`)

**Comparative note:** no other corpus jurisdiction has an equivalent — **Jersey, Cayman, and BVI have no image-rights statute**; the closest analogues elsewhere are US state rights of publicity (strong but no unified register) and codified-but-tort-based EU personality rights. (`knowledge/CROSS-JURISDICTIONAL-MAP.md`, `knowledge/guernsey/image-rights-register.md`)
