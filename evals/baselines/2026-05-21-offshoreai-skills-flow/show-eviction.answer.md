## What you cannot do

Self-help is unlawful and may be a **criminal offence** under the Residential Tenancy (Jersey) Law 2011 (RTJL): you cannot change the locks, remove the tenant's possessions, cut off utilities, or physically remove the tenant (`knowledge/jersey/residential-tenancy/non-payment-of-rent.md`; `knowledge/jersey/use-cases/tenant-landlord/evicting-non-paying-tenant.md`).

## The lawful route (three stages)

1. **7-day breach notice.** Non-payment is a breach of the tenancy agreement under the RTJL. Serve a written, signed notice identifying the unpaid rent and requiring payment within at least 7 days. If the tenant pays in full within the window, the breach is remedied and you cannot proceed on those arrears (`knowledge/jersey/residential-tenancy/non-payment-of-rent.md`).
2. **Apply to the Petty Debts Court** — which has *exclusive original jurisdiction* over residential tenancy termination, eviction, and arrears money judgments (the Royal Court does not handle these at first instance). You'll need the tenancy agreement, condition report, rent ledger, and proof the notice was served. The court can terminate the tenancy, order eviction, grant a money judgment for arrears, and award costs — but commonly attaches a **stay of execution** (typically 14–28 days, longer for vulnerable tenants or families with children) (`knowledge/jersey/residential-tenancy/eviction-procedure.md`).
3. **Viscount enforcement.** Only the Viscount may take possession from a resisting tenant. After any stay expires, you apply to the Viscount's Department to attend, remove the tenant (with force if needed), handle their possessions, and restore possession to you (`knowledge/jersey/residential-tenancy/eviction-procedure.md`).

## Tenant protections that make this "not easy"

A defective notice is fatal to the application; tenants may raise **set-off** for landlord disrepair, a **discrimination** cross-claim (Discrimination (Jersey) Law 2013), **harassment** if you applied pressure during the notice period, and **vulnerability**-based stays. Realistic timeline from first missed payment to actual possession is typically **2–4 months**, longer if contested (`knowledge/jersey/residential-tenancy/non-payment-of-rent.md`; `knowledge/jersey/use-cases/tenant-landlord/evicting-non-paying-tenant.md`).

## Practical next steps
Calculate the arrears precisely, serve a properly drafted and served 7-day notice, document everything, and open a parallel conversation about a payment plan (many cases resolve here). Engage a Jersey advocate if it looks contested (`knowledge/jersey/use-cases/tenant-landlord/evicting-non-paying-tenant.md`).
