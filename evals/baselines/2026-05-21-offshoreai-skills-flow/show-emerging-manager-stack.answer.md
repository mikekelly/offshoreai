## The BVI emerging-manager structure stack

For a sub-$100m BVI emerging manager, the corpus describes a three-layer stack, each layer deliberately at the **lightest tier** consistent with your size and investor base:

### 1. Manager layer — BVI Approved Manager
The **Approved Manager** regime (Investment Business (Approved Manager) Regulations 2012) is the fast-track, light-touch authorisation built precisely for "start-up, smaller, or family-office managers." It sits *below* full SIBA Investment Business licensing: authorisation in a few weeks rather than months, lower cost, but still a recognised regulated status that's marketing-acceptable to LPs. It can manage BVI funds (Incubator, Approved, Private, Professional, Public) plus closely-connected non-BVI funds. There's an AUM ceiling — exceed it and you must upgrade to full Investment Business licensing. `knowledge/bvi/approved-manager.md`

### 2. Fund layer — Incubator Fund or Approved Fund
Two distinct light-touch fund regimes under the SIBA (Incubator and Approved Funds) Regulations 2015 — pick one, they are **not interchangeable** (`knowledge/bvi/incubator-and-approved-fund.md`):

| | Incubator Fund | Approved Fund |
|---|---|---|
| Designed for | Building a track record | Stable small fund |
| Max investors | 20 | 20 |
| Min investment | US$20,000 | None |
| Max AUM | US$20m | US$100m |
| Time limit | 2 years (+1 yr extension) | Permanent |
| Auditor | Not required | Not required |
| Administrator | Not required | Typically required |

As a genuine emerging manager building a track record, the **Incubator Fund** is the canonical entry point — fastest launch, no mandatory administrator/auditor/manager — with a built-in two-year runway after which you convert to a Private/Professional Fund or wind down. If you're a stable, closely-held pool that won't outgrow $100m and want a permanent home, the **Approved Fund** is the better fit. The full tier ladder is Incubator → Approved → Private → Professional → Public; take the lowest tier and upgrade as you scale.

### 3. Vehicle layer — BVI Business Company
The fund itself is housed in a corporate vehicle, the standard being the **BVI Business Company (BC)** under the Business Companies Act 2004 — incorporable in hours, low fees, no minimum capital, flexible share classes, single director/shareholder permitted with no residence requirement, registered-agent model. `knowledge/bvi/business-company.md`
