**The threshold question first.** Pillar Two only engages if the consolidated group has **annual revenue of €750m or more** (in at least two of the four preceding fiscal years). Below that threshold — the position for the great majority of founder TopCos — Pillar Two does **not** apply at all, and the Jersey TopCo continues at the **0% standard rate** under zero-ten as a holding company (`knowledge/jersey/tax/pillar-two.md`; `knowledge/jersey/use-cases/founder-entrepreneur/pillar-two-impact.md`).

**If the group is in scope (€750m+).** Two parallel things happen:

1. **At the Jersey TopCo.** Jersey implements Pillar Two through the **Multinational Corporate Income Tax (MCIT)**, in force for financial years beginning on or after **1 January 2025**, structured as a **Qualified Domestic Minimum Top-up Tax (QDMTT)** plus an Income Inclusion Rule. The TopCo's Jersey-booked income — which under zero-ten is typically taxed at 0% — is **topped up to a 15% effective rate**, calculated on GloBE income (not Jersey local taxable income). Crucially, the QDMTT design means **Jersey collects that top-up itself** rather than ceding it to a foreign parent jurisdiction under its IIR/UTPR (`knowledge/jersey/international/pillar-two-mcit.md`; `knowledge/jersey/tax/pillar-two.md`).

2. **At the UK Opco — parallel UK Pillar Two.** The UK operates its own Pillar Two regime, which runs alongside Jersey's. Because the UK operating business is already subject to UK corporation tax (well above the 15% floor), its UK profits would not normally generate a top-up. The corpus states UK Pillar Two "runs in parallel" with the Jersey MCIT for an in-scope group (`knowledge/jersey/use-cases/founder-entrepreneur/wormhole-jersey-topco-uk-listing.md`, step 5). The corpus does not give a detailed worked treatment of the UK leg beyond this parallel-operation point.

**Net effect.** For an in-scope group, the headline "0% Jersey" result disappears for the TopCo's Jersey income, which becomes **at least 15%** — but that 15% is generally collected in Jersey via MCIT rather than abroad, group structure continuity is preserved, and the non-tax Jersey advantages (corporate-law flexibility, no investor-level CGT, 0% withholding on dividends to non-residents, LSE listing acceptance) persist (`knowledge/jersey/use-cases/founder-entrepreneur/topco-tax.md`; `pillar-two-impact.md`). Compliance burden rises substantially (GloBE income computation, ETR analysis, GIR filing).
