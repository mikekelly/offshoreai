## The route in short

Jersey is a **non-EU jurisdiction**, so it cannot use the full AIFMD passport — that is available only to EU-AIFM-managed EU AIFs and has not been extended to third countries. To market into Germany, your AIF goes through Germany's **National Private Placement Regime (NPPR)** — a country-by-country private-placement notification, not an EU-wide right (`knowledge/jersey/funds/aifmd-nppr.md`).

## What's required

**1. JFSC-registered AIFM under Jersey's AIFMD-equivalent regime.** Before any NPPR notification, the AIFM must register with the JFSC and demonstrate AIFMD-equivalent compliance: capital adequacy, risk management (equivalent to AIFMD Art 15), liquidity management for open-ended AIFs, depository arrangements, investor transparency (annual report, periodic reporting), and regulatory reporting (`knowledge/jersey/use-cases/fund-counsel/aifmd-nppr-registration.md`).

**2. AIFMD-equivalent depository.** An EU-marketed Jersey AIF needs depository arrangements covering custody, oversight, and cash-flow monitoring. Jersey's "lighter depository" option is for non-EU-marketed funds only — for NPPR marketing you need full AIFMD-equivalent depository arrangements; a deficient setup is a recognised regulatory mismatch (`knowledge/jersey/funds/aifmd-nppr.md`).

**3. NPPR notification to BaFin (Germany).** Germany is one of the **stricter** NPPR states. The corpus records German-specific conditions under **§330 KAGB**: a local fact-sheet, a local distributor sometimes required, and post-acceptance reporting obligations. The process: confirm BaFin recognises the Jersey AIFM regime as equivalent, prepare the German notification package, submit, and await clearance before any active marketing (`knowledge/jersey/use-cases/fund-counsel/aifmd-nppr-registration.md`).

Once cleared, the AIF can be marketed **to German professional investors** only.

## Two things to factor in for 2026

**AIFMD II is now live.** Directive (EU) 2024/927 took effect from **16 April 2026**. NPPR access for Crown Dependencies is preserved, but two requirements have widened that bear directly on a German notification: **expanded investor disclosures** (pre-marketing and ongoing) and **enhanced Annex IV reporting** to ESMA via competent authorities. Germany is flagged as transposing AIFMD II more strictly than Luxembourg or Ireland. Note also Jersey's **AIF Code is being split into an EU AIF Code and a UK AIF Code**, with the EU code (AIFMD II-aligned, the one relevant to your German marketing) expected to be finalised through 2026 (`knowledge/frontier/aifmd-ii-april-2026.md`).

**Reverse solicitation is not a workaround.** NPPR is required only for *active* marketing. A genuine unsolicited approach by a German investor doesn't trigger NPPR — but BaFin scrutinises reverse-solicitation claims, and active outreach disguised as reverse solicitation attracts penalties (`knowledge/jersey/use-cases/fund-counsel/aifmd-nppr-registration.md`).
