For a **NASDAQ listing, the default and safest choice is the Cayman Exempted Company.** All three are accepted offshore listing domiciles, but they sort by use-case fit:

- **Cayman Exempted** — the standard US-listing vehicle. It is "one of three jurisdictions (alongside BVI and Delaware) routinely accepted by major exchanges as listing vehicles," and the file explicitly notes US-listed companies (the Alibaba / TikTok holdco pattern) list as Cayman exempted companies. Its comparison table lists acceptance on **NYSE, NASDAQ, HKEx**. (`knowledge/cayman/exempted-company.md`)
- **BVI BC** — also accepted on **NYSE / NASDAQ / HKEx but "more limited"** exchange acceptance per the same comparison table. The corpus frames the BC as the volume/standard-structure vehicle rather than the premium listing vehicle. Viable, but with more friction and lower market familiarity for a listed holdco. (`knowledge/cayman/exempted-company.md`; `knowledge/bvi/business-company.md`)
- **Bermuda Exempted** — "routinely accepted as a listing domicile" on **NYSE / NASDAQ / LSE**, but Bermuda's specialism (and where it's the strongest pick) is **(re)insurance and ILS listings**. It also carries a distinguishing procedural step: **BMA pre-vetting consent at incorporation**, which adds a regulatory filter Cayman/BVI don't impose. (`knowledge/bermuda/exempted-company.md`)

The cross-jurisdictional decision framework agrees: for a "US-listed tech holdco" it points to **Cayman Exempted (the Alibaba pattern)**; Bermuda is the lean for an "insurance / reinsurance listing"; BVI is the lower-cost option but with "some exchange-acceptance limits" (`knowledge/CROSS-JURISDICTIONAL-MAP.md`).

## How to choose between them

| If your business is… | Lean toward |

| (Re)insurance, ILS, or cat-bond issuer | **Bermuda Exempted** |
