A **Parish Hall Enquiry** is a distinctively Jersey **pre-court forum**, presided over by a **Centenier** — an elected, unpaid honorary police officer of the parish where the alleged offence took place. It's the route by which the **majority of routine Jersey motoring offences are resolved without ever reaching the Magistrate's Court**. There is no direct equivalent in the UK or other common-law systems, so don't assume it works like a UK fixed-penalty notice or court. (`knowledge/jersey/road-traffic/parish-hall-enquiries.md`)

At the enquiry the Centenier reviews the evidence, decides whether there is enough to charge and whether the public interest requires prosecution, engages with you, and can then **caution, fine (up to £200 per offence), or refer the case to court**. Crucially, a Centenier may only impose a fine or caution if you **admit the offence and accept the proposed penalty** — if you dispute it, the case must go to the Magistrate's Court. You also have the right to be told the offence in advance, to bring a friend or advocate, and to **opt out to court at any point**. (`knowledge/jersey/road-traffic/parish-hall-enquiries.md`)

## When your case goes there

**Routine, minor motoring matters** are typical Parish Hall fare — for example:

- Minor speeding (under 15 mph over the limit)
- Mobile phone offences
- Failing to display an insurance disc
- Some traffic-light and minor careless-driving offences
- Parking offences

**Your case does NOT go to Parish Hall** (it's referred straight to Magistrate's Court) for serious matters, including:

- Speeding **15 mph or more** over the limit
- Drink-driving (any reading) or drug-driving
- Dangerous driving (Article 23 RTJL 1956)
- Causing death or serious injury by driving
- Driving while disqualified
- Any offence carrying **mandatory disqualification**, or where the Centenier judges the £200 ceiling inadequate

The schedule of offences treatable at Parish Hall is maintained by the Law Officers' Department and reviewed periodically. (`knowledge/jersey/road-traffic/parish-hall-enquiries.md`)

## One thing worth weighing

A Parish Hall outcome is faster, cheaper, and isn't a formal court conviction — but it **can still endorse your licence** and create a record, and a Centenier cannot impose disqualification. If you have a genuine defence, or disqualification/endorsement could matter for insurance, opting out to the Magistrate's Court (with advice) may be the better call.
