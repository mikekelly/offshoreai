## Jersey trust vs Cayman STAR vs BVI VISTA — when to use which

The three are not interchangeable: each was built to solve a different structuring problem. The decision turns on **what the trust's job is**.

### The three regimes at a glance

| | Jersey trust | Cayman STAR | BVI VISTA |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Core form** | Classic discretionary trust under the Trusts (Jersey) Law 1984 | Purpose (or mixed person/purpose) trust | Ordinary trust + share-ownership overlay |
| **Defining feature** | Article 9 firewall + Article 9A reserved powers | Enforcer-based; beneficiaries have no standing to enforce | Disapplies the trustee's duty to monitor/intervene in the underlying company |
| **Best for** | Forced-heirship defence; broad settlor control with deep case law | Orphan SPV / no-beneficiary purpose holding | Family-business succession with a deliberately hands-off trustee |

### When to use which

- **Jersey** — the default for **wealth protection and forced-heirship defence**. Article 9 directs the Royal Court to apply Jersey law only and disregard foreign forced-heirship rules; Article 9(4) makes a foreign judgment inconsistent with Article 9 unenforceable in Jersey. Article 9A lets the settlor reserve broad powers (investment, appointment, revocation) without invalidating the trust. Choose Jersey when the driver is *defending the structure against foreign claims* and you want settled jurisprudence. (`knowledge/jersey/trusts/firewall.md`, `knowledge/jersey/trusts/reserved-powers.md`)

- **Cayman STAR** — choose when the structure has **no beneficiaries to serve**, or a *purpose* dominates: an orphan SPV in a securitisation, a holding vehicle for a private trust company (PTC), or a philanthropic/commercial purpose that doesn't fit charitable status. Enforcement runs through a mandatory **enforcer** standing in place of beneficiaries; STAR trusts can have unlimited duration. (`knowledge/cayman/star-trusts.md`)

- **BVI VISTA** — choose when the trust's job is to **hold the shares of a family or operating company** and the family wants the trustee to **stay out of the company's management**. VISTA disengages the trustee from the *Bartlett v Barclays Bank* duty of active intervention; "office of director rules" preserve the settlor's chosen control structure, leaving the directors to run the business. (`knowledge/bvi/vista-trusts.md`)

### The decision in one line

Forced-heirship / control / jurisprudence → **Jersey**; no-beneficiary or purpose-led → **Cayman STAR**; hold company shares with a hands-off trustee → **BVI VISTA**.
