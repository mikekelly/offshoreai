I'll pull the relevant Jersey fund-regime files in parallel.## Recommendation: JPF

For a **club-deal PE fund**, the **Jersey Private Fund (JPF)** is the natural fit. A club deal is by definition a small, defined group of co-investors — comfortably inside the JPF's cap of **up to 50 offers/investors** — and the corpus lists "club deal structures," "private equity funds and co-investment vehicles," and "carry vehicles for fund-management teams" as the JPF's *default* use cases (`knowledge/jersey/funds/jersey-private-fund.md`). You get fast-track JFSC authorisation (typically ~48 hours), reduced ongoing supervision, and a JFSC-licensed designated service provider (DSP) carrying the regulatory/AML load, with structuring as a Jersey limited partnership (the most common PE form), company, unit trust, or cell company.

**When the Expert Fund becomes the better choice instead:** the Expert Fund has *no specific investor cap* (subject to each investor meeting the "expert" threshold — broadly ~US$100,000 minimum investment, prescribed net assets, or professional/institutional status). The corpus flags it for "large private-equity vehicles **exceeding JPF investor count**" and funds needing wider expert-investor marketing (`knowledge/jersey/funds/expert-fund.md`). So the decision hinges on one question: **will your investor base stay at or below 50?** If yes (the typical club deal), use the JPF — it's lighter-touch and faster. If you expect the club to grow past 50 or you want broader marketing latitude, step up to the Expert Fund.

**EU/UK marketing note:** if you intend to market the fund into EU member states or the UK, either regime can be combined with AIFMD National Private Placement Regime compliance — see `knowledge/jersey/funds/aifmd-nppr.md`.
