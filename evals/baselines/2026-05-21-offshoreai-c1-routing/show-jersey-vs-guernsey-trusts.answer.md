## Short answer

For most cross-border family-wealth structures, **Jersey and Guernsey trusts are competitive substitutes** — both descend from a shared Norman customary heritage, both recognise discretionary/fixed-interest/purpose trusts, both allow settlor-reserved powers, both have a statutory firewall, both permit perpetual duration, and both route final appeals to the Privy Council (`knowledge/guernsey/trust-law-differences-from-jersey.md`). The choice usually turns on a few specific factors rather than a wholesale regime difference.

## When to lean Jersey

- **Maximum forced-heirship / matrimonial-claim defence.** Jersey's firewall is the gold standard. Article 9(1) directs the Royal Court to apply only Jersey law to validity, capacity, administration and beneficial-rights questions, and Article 9(4) makes foreign judgments inconsistent with Article 9 unenforceable in Jersey. Guernsey's s.14 (TGL 2007) is comparable but **less explicit on foreign-judgment non-recognition**, so sophisticated defensive structures often still prefer Jersey (`knowledge/jersey/trusts/firewall.md`; `knowledge/guernsey/trust-law-differences-from-jersey.md`).
- **Depth of mistake / *Hastings-Bass* case law.** Both jurisdictions have codified a statutory set-aside-for-mistake regime wider than the English *Pitt v Holt* approach, but Jersey's body of case law under Article 47B–J (the *Re S&T Trusts* test) is **more developed** (`knowledge/guernsey/trust-law-differences-from-jersey.md`).
- **Larger trust-company industry** and, if you're combining the trust with a fund, the **JPF** route (`knowledge/guernsey/trust-law-differences-from-jersey.md`).
- Note: Jersey's Article 9A reserved-powers menu (revocation, variation, advancement, trustee direction, beneficiary appointment, etc.) is broad and explicit, and is the standard vehicle for US-grantor-trust and patriarch/family-office control structures — but Guernsey offers broadly equivalent outcomes here, so this is rarely the deciding factor (`knowledge/jersey/trusts/reserved-powers.md`).

## When to lean Guernsey

- **Cell-company trustee structures.** Guernsey offers both the **PCC and ICC** (it invented the PCC in 1997); Jersey offers the PCC only (`knowledge/guernsey/trust-law-differences-from-jersey.md`; `knowledge/CROSS-JURISDICTIONAL-MAP.md`).
- **Fund-trust combinations using the PIF** rather than the JPF (`knowledge/guernsey/trust-law-differences-from-jersey.md`).
- **Image rights**: if the family has commercially valuable personalities, Guernsey hosts the world's only Image Rights Register, which can sit alongside the trust (`knowledge/CROSS-JURISDICTIONAL-MAP.md`).

## What usually actually decides it

Where the two regimes are substitutes, the practical tie-breakers are **sponsor familiarity, existing service-provider relationships, group/administrator infrastructure location, and specific asset or family considerations** — not a substantive legal difference (`knowledge/guernsey/trust-law-differences-from-jersey.md`).
