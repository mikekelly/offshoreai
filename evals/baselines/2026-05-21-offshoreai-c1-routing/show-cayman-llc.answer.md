## Is the Cayman LLC the right form?

For a **Delaware LLC redomiciling to Cayman, the Cayman LLC is generally the natural target form** — it was introduced by the **Limited Liability Companies Act 2016** specifically to mirror the Delaware LLC and to ease exactly this kind of cross-border restructuring. The practical advantage is that a Delaware LLC can convert **directly** into a Cayman LLC, whereas migrating into a Cayman exempted company would mean shifting into a share-based corporate form first. The LLC keeps the familiar architecture: members (not shareholders), an LLC Agreement as the master document (no shares, no statutory capital), member-managed by default with an optional manager-managed election, and limited liability for all members. (`knowledge/cayman/cayman-llc.md`)

The form is most clearly right when Delaware-LLC parity matters, the counterparty is itself a Delaware LLC, or you want check-the-box flexibility. It is **not** automatically right for every use: for a fund's main pooling vehicle the **Cayman ELP** remains dominant (GP/LP economics, market familiarity), and some investors/counterparties are still unfamiliar with the LLC form and will need it explained. So confirm the LLC fits the structure's role rather than defaulting to it. (`knowledge/cayman/cayman-llc.md`)

## How check-the-box works

The Cayman LLC is eligible for US "check-the-box" classification under the entity-classification regulations:

- **Default** (no election): a foreign entity owned by US tax persons is treated as a **partnership** if it has more than one member, or a **disregarded entity** if it has a single member — both giving flow-through treatment.
- **Elective**: the entity can instead **elect to be treated as a corporation**, which is useful for blocker / GILTI / Subpart F planning.

This elective flexibility is one of the main reasons sponsors pick the LLC over the exempted company, which is generally a per-se corporation. A key pitfall the corpus flags: **the default may not be what your structure needs** — confirm the intended classification before relying on it. (`knowledge/cayman/cayman-llc.md`)

## Migration caveat

The corpus expressly notes that **migration from a Delaware LLC carries tax-residency and Pillar Two implications that need careful handling** — worth scoping early, especially if the group is in scope of the EUR 750m Pillar Two threshold. (`knowledge/cayman/cayman-llc.md`)
