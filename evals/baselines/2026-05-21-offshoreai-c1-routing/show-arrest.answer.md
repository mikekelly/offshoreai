## Your rights on arrest in Jersey

Jersey criminal procedure is governed by the **Police Procedures and Criminal Evidence (Jersey) Law 2003** (PPCE 2003) and its Codes of Practice — Jersey's own statute, not the UK's PACE, though it is closely modelled on it. Your headline rights once detained at the police station are:

**The most important one first:** You have an **absolute right to free legal advice** — a 24/7 duty solicitor or your own choice, in private and privileged, *before any interview*. This can be delayed only on specific statutory grounds in very serious cases (e.g. terrorism, evidence preservation), authorised at senior level; routine delay is not permitted. The practical advice the corpus emphasises: **ask for a solicitor immediately and don't answer substantive questions until you've spoken to one** — declining to look cooperative is the single most common mistake. (`knowledge/jersey/use-cases/jersey-resident/just-arrested-police-station.md`; `knowledge/jersey/crime-fraud/rights-at-police-station.md`)

**Your other core rights:**

- **Have someone informed** — one person (family, friend) told of your arrest and location, police calling at public expense, as soon as practicable. Can be delayed in serious cases at inspector level. (`rights-at-police-station.md`)
- **Consular notification** if you are a foreign national. (`rights-at-police-station.md`)
- **Right to silence** — you need not answer questions. The caution is: *"You are not obliged to say anything unless you wish to do so, but what you say may be put into writing and given in evidence."* Note that **adverse inferences from silence at police-station interview are narrower in Jersey than in post-2003 England**. (`rights-at-police-station.md`)
- **Written notice of rights** in your language on arrival, and a **copy of the custody record** (the full log of your detention) on release — keep it; it's key evidence if you later challenge procedural fairness. (`rights-at-police-station.md`)
- **Interpreter** at public expense if you can't follow proceedings in English; **appropriate adult** if you are a juvenile or vulnerable; **medical attention** if needed; adequate cell conditions and rest (at least 8 continuous hours' rest in any 24). (`rights-at-police-station.md`)

**Detention time limits** (`rights-at-police-station.md`; `just-arrested-police-station.md`; and headlined in `knowledge/jersey/crime-fraud/index.md`):

| Stage | Limit | Authorised by |
|---|---|---|
| Initial detention | **24 hours** | Custody officer |
| Extension (serious offences) | up to **36 hours** | Police inspector |
| Warrant of further detention | up to **96 hours total** | Royal Court (Magistrate) |
| Beyond 96 hours | **Charge or release** | — |

Detention is reviewed periodically (typically every 6 hours initially); holding you beyond these limits is unlawful.

**In interview** (`rights-at-police-station.md`): open audio recording under Code E, the caution at the start of each session, your solicitor present if requested, no oppression, breaks, and no questioning if you're unfit (intoxicated, unwell). You may give a "no comment" interview or a prepared statement through your solicitor.

**If you think a right was breached:** note the time and details, tell your solicitor at the first opportunity, and complain formally after release. Evidence obtained in breach may be excluded at trial, and a civil claim is possible for serious breaches. (`rights-at-police-station.md`)

For lesser offences — particularly routine motoring matters — note that Jersey often diverts cases to a **Parish Hall Enquiry** before the Magistrate's Court, handled by Centeniers of the Honorary Police (`knowledge/jersey/crime-fraud/index.md`, cross-referencing the road-traffic section).
