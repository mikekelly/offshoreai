## Bottom line for an emerging sub-US$100m PE manager

The four vehicles split into two groups: **Cayman ELP** is the institutional global-LP default (heavier, but what large LPs expect), while **JPF / PIF / BVI Approved** are light-touch fast-launch regimes. For a *first*, genuinely small fund, the BVI Approved Fund (and even the Incubator) is the lowest-friction tier; JPF and PIF give you more headroom to scale; Cayman ELP is the choice if your LP base is US/global institutions.

## How to choose, given your profile

**You're sub-US$100m and first-time — so size and cost matter more than institutional optics.**

- **BVI Approved Fund** is purpose-built for exactly your tier: a permanent light-touch regime, **20-investor / US$100m ceiling**, **no mandatory audit**, designed for "stable, smaller funds" and emerging managers (`knowledge/bvi/incubator-and-approved-fund.md`). The catch is the hard 20-investor cap and the US$100m ceiling — if you expect to raise above either, you'll outgrow it and have to convert to a BVI Private/Professional Fund. Note also the **BVI Incubator Fund** (US$20m / 20 investors / 2-year life, no admin or auditor needed) as an even-lighter "track-record-building" launch pad before you commit (`knowledge/bvi/incubator-and-approved-fund.md`).

- **Jersey JPF** — 50 investors, no AUM ceiling, **~48h authorisation**, and the distinctive **DSP (designated service provider) model**: a JFSC-licensed Jersey administrator carries AML/CDD and substantive responsibility, substituting for direct JFSC oversight (`knowledge/jersey/funds/jersey-private-fund.md`). Strong if you want a regime that scales past US$100m without re-papering and value EU-marketing reach via NPPR. Audit is required, and you must engage a DSP from day one.

- **Guernsey PIF** — also 50 investors, no ceiling, ~1-business-day authorisation, but uniquely offers **three routes** (Route 1 licensed manager / Route 2 family / Route 3 qualifying investors), which lets you match governance to your investor base (`knowledge/guernsey/private-investment-fund.md`). Functionally very close to the JPF; the route flexibility is the differentiator, and PCC/ICC forms are a Guernsey strength if you'll run multiple strategies.

- **Cayman ELP** — the heaviest of the four to stand up (LPA negotiation runs weeks-to-months) but the form **global PE/VC LPs expect**: tax-transparent in the US/UK/EU, no statutory capital maintenance, fully LPA-driven economics, and it slots into parallel-fund structures (Delaware LP for US LPs + Cayman ELP for non-US) (`knowledge/cayman/exempted-limited-partnership.md`). Choose this if your raise targets US/institutional money where investor familiarity outweighs the higher setup cost — arguably overkill for a first sub-US$100m friends-and-network raise unless those LPs demand it.

**Practical steer:** if your LPs are institutional/US → Cayman ELP. If you want the cheapest compliant launch and ≤20 investors → BVI Approved (or Incubator first). If you want a fast, scalable light-touch home that won't cap you at US$100m and value EU access → Jersey JPF or Guernsey PIF, with PIF edging ahead if your investors fit its family/qualifying-investor routes.

One gap to flag: the corpus files compare these regimes on structure and thresholds but do **not** give comparative *fee/cost figures* — verify setup and annual costs with providers in each jurisdiction before deciding.
