## Why Bermuda for a multinational-group captive

Bermuda is the world's leading captive-insurance domicile and the natural choice for a large multinational self-insuring its own risks. The corpus gives three reasons it wins for group captives specifically:

- **Market depth** — it is the largest captive market globally, with the deepest pool of specialist insurance managers, actuaries, and BMA supervisory expertise to support a captive programme. (`knowledge/bermuda/insurance-classes.md`)
- **Solvency II equivalence** — Bermuda's solvency regime (the BSCR) is recognised by the EU as equivalent. Bermuda is one of only two offshore jurisdictions with this status (Isle of Man is the other), which reduces collateral requirements when your captive reinsures EU cedants. (`knowledge/bermuda/insurance-classes.md`; `knowledge/CROSS-JURISDICTIONAL-MAP.md`)
- **Scale-up headroom** — the same Insurance Act 1978 class ladder runs from pure captive up to the Class 4 commercial cat-reinsurance market, so a captive that later writes third-party or unrelated business can move up classes within the same regime. (`knowledge/bermuda/insurance-classes.md`)

The trade-off the corpus flags: Bermuda carries **higher BMA scrutiny and substance requirements** than lighter-touch rivals. For comparison — Cayman (Class A–E) is the specialist for **healthcare** captives; Guernsey (PCC-based) is favoured by **European parents** wanting an EU-near structure and smaller scale. (`knowledge/bermuda/insurance-classes.md`)

## Which Class

Under the **Insurance Act 1978** general-business (P&C) ladder:

| Your situation | Class |
|---|---|
| Captive writes **only the risks of your group** (pure single-parent self-insurance) | **Class 1** |
| Captive also writes **limited unrelated risks**, or is a multi-parent/association structure | **Class 2** |
| Captive writes **significant unrelated third-party business** (up to thresholds) | **Class 3** |

For a multinational group insuring its own risks, the default is **Class 1** (pure single-parent captive). If you intend to bring in some unrelated business or share the captive across more than one owner, you move to **Class 2**. The corpus's stated principle is to pick the **lowest class consistent with your business** to minimise regulatory overhead — minimum capital, solvency, reporting, and audit intensity all rise sharply up the ladder. (`knowledge/bermuda/insurance-classes.md`)

If you want multiple distinct programmes or lines ring-fenced from each other under one licensed entity, the captive can be run through a Bermuda **Segregated Accounts Company (SAC)**. (`knowledge/bermuda/insurance-classes.md`, `knowledge/bermuda/segregated-accounts-company.md`)

Note these are the **general-business** classes for property/casualty risk. If your captive is writing **long-term (life)** business instead, the parallel ladder is Class A (long-term captive) through Class E. (`knowledge/bermuda/insurance-classes.md`)
