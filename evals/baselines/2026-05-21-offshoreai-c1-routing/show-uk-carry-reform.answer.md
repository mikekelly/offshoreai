## What changes on 6 April 2026

The UK is replacing the CGT-based carried-interest regime with an **income-tax-based regime with a partial exclusion** (`knowledge/frontier/uk-carried-interest-reform-2026.md`, as of 2026-05-18):

| Period | Treatment | Effective rate (additional-rate payer) |
|---|---|---|
| Pre-6 Apr 2025 | CGT | ~28% |
| 6 Apr 2025 – 5 Apr 2026 | CGT, flat 32% | 32% |
| **From 6 Apr 2026** | **Income tax + Class 4 NICs** | **~34.1% qualifying / up to ~47% non-qualifying** |

From 6 April 2026 carry is taxed as **income**, but for **qualifying** carried interest a **72.5% multiplier** applies (only 72.5% is brought into charge), landing an additional-rate payer at ~34.1%. **Non-qualifying** carry takes the full income-tax + NICs hit, up to ~47%. The qualifying definition (genuine investment-management activity, specified fund characteristics) was **tightened** in the final legislation versus earlier drafts, though the government dropped the proposed minimum-investment "access conditions." For most established UK-resident partners at mature sponsors, qualifying status is the default; new entrants, secondaries participants, and non-traditional structures need careful analysis.

## What PE partners in the Crown Dependencies should do

**Residency planning for UK partners** — the most-marketed response. The Crown Dependencies are being actively promoted as a residence destination (`knowledge/frontier/uk-carried-interest-reform-2026.md`):
- **Jersey 2(1)(e) High-Value Residency** — bespoke route with a tax-rate cap (`knowledge/jersey/immigration-residency/high-value-residency.md`, `knowledge/jersey/tax/high-value-residency-tax.md`);
- **Guernsey Open Market** (house-purchase-based, standard 20% tax) and **IoM tax cap** as alternatives.

But the corpus is explicit on the limits: the **UK Statutory Residence Test** has stringent conditions for leaving, there is **tail liability** on carry that economically accrued while UK-resident (even if realised after departure), **substance** in the destination is essential, and the new legislation contains **specific anti-avoidance provisions around residence changes**. Treating UK departure as a clean shield is flagged as a pitfall.

**Structuring and entity-location moves:**
- **GP-entity location** — Crown Dependencies-domiciled GPs (Jersey/Guernsey companies acting as GP of Jersey/Cayman ELP funds) may become more attractive as the carry-allocation entity for *non-UK* partners, though UK look-through / situs rules still apply where UK-resident partners get the economic benefit.
- **Carry-allocation mechanics at the GP** are the focal point — fund vehicles themselves (Jersey JPF, Guernsey PIF, Cayman ELP) are unchanged; partnership profit allocation and carry vesting are where the rules bite.
- **Co-invest vs carry distinction** is now acutely material — co-invest economics remain CGT-treated, and misclassifying one as the other is a named pitfall.
- **Trust and family-office structures** for deferral, spinouts, and generational transfer.

**Pre-6 April crystallisation** (now largely historical): through Q1 2026 many partners accelerated distributions/disposals to land under the 32% CGT regime.

**Sponsor-side**, advisers should document carry arrangements carefully to preserve qualifying status for each partner, review GP/LP economics, and prepare for LP questions on re-pricing and key-partner retention.
