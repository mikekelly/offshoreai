## The procedure

1. **Serve a written 7-day breach notice.** It must be in writing and signed, identify the breach specifically (which rent periods, how much is owed), and require payment of the arrears within at least 7 days (a statutory minimum — you can give longer, not less). Serve it properly per the agreement or by personal service / recorded post. A defective notice is fatal and forces you to start over. If the tenant pays in full within the window, the breach is remedied and you cannot proceed on the same arrears. (`knowledge/jersey/residential-tenancy/non-payment-of-rent.md`)

2. **Apply to the Petty Debts Court** if the arrears remain unpaid after the notice period. The Petty Debts Court has **exclusive original jurisdiction** over residential-tenancy termination and eviction — the Royal Court does not hear these at first instance. Bring the tenancy agreement, condition report, rent ledger/bank statements, the breach notice with proof of service, and a record of your attempts to engage. You can also seek a money judgment for the arrears. (`knowledge/jersey/residential-tenancy/eviction-procedure.md`, `knowledge/jersey/residential-tenancy/petty-debts-court-tenancy.md`)

3. **The hearing.** The court checks procedural compliance and whether the breach is made out, considers tenant defences, and decides whether to grant a **stay of execution**. If satisfied it can grant termination, an eviction order, a money judgment, and costs — commonly with a stay of 14–28 days (longer for vulnerable tenants). (`knowledge/jersey/residential-tenancy/eviction-procedure.md`)

4. **Enforcement by the Viscount.** Once any stay has expired, you apply to the Viscount's Department to execute. The Viscount is the **only person** lawfully entitled to take possession from a resisting tenant — not you, not a private bailiff. They attend, remove the tenant (with force/police if necessary), handle possessions, and restore possession to you. Viscount's fees can run from several hundred to several thousand pounds, recoverable from the tenant in principle but often borne by you in practice. (`knowledge/jersey/residential-tenancy/eviction-procedure.md`)

## Tenant protections that make this "not easy"
- **Procedural rigour** — your notice and service must be perfect.
- **Set-off** — if you've failed to repair / the premises are unfit, the tenant can reduce or extinguish the arrears claim.
- **Discrimination / retaliation** — eviction on a protected ground (Discrimination (Jersey) Law 2013) or following a tenant complaint is highly contestable.
- **Vulnerability** — children, pregnancy, disability, mental-health issues, or a pending Income Support / rehousing application routinely attract stays of execution.

**Realistic timeline:** typically **2–4 months** from first missed payment to actual possession in a straightforward case, longer if contested or stayed. (`knowledge/jersey/residential-tenancy/non-payment-of-rent.md`, `knowledge/jersey/residential-tenancy/eviction-procedure.md`)

**Practical first steps:** calculate arrears precisely, check the agreement and condition report, serve a properly-drafted signed 7-day notice with documented service, open a payment-plan conversation in parallel (many cases resolve here), and engage a Jersey advocate if it looks contested. (`knowledge/jersey/use-cases/tenant-landlord/evicting-non-paying-tenant.md`)
