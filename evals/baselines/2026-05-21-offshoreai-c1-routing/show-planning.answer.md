## Do you need planning permission for a rear extension?

**It depends on size.** A modest single-storey rear extension may fall within the **permitted-development** exemptions granted automatically by the **Planning and Building (General Development) (Jersey) Order 2011** ("GD Order 2011") — in which case no planning application is needed. But anything that exceeds the Order's limits on **floor area, depth, height, or position** needs a full planning application.

The corpus's practical position:

- **Single-storey rear extension within the GD Order 2011 limits** (the corpus gives an indicative example of roughly a 3–4m projection, single storey) — likely **permitted development**, no application required. (`knowledge/jersey/planning/extensions-and-conversions.md`; `knowledge/jersey/planning/permitted-development.md`)
- **Larger or two-storey rear extension** — a **planning application is required**. Two-storey extensions "always require application" under current rules. (`knowledge/jersey/planning/extensions-and-conversions.md`)

### Important qualifiers

Even if your extension is within the permitted-development thresholds, it can still need consent, and several factors take it back into the application route:

- **Conservation area** — typically needs a full application even within the size thresholds; many PD rights are narrower or don't apply. (`knowledge/jersey/planning/permitted-development.md`)
- **Listed building** — **listed-building consent is always required**, regardless of size. (`knowledge/jersey/planning/extensions-and-conversions.md`)
- **Coastal National Park / Green Zone** — restricted. (`knowledge/jersey/planning/permitted-development.md`)
- **Boundary proximity** — building close to a boundary can trigger neighbour-rights issues. (`knowledge/jersey/planning/extensions-and-conversions.md`)
- **Article 31 directions** — the Minister can strip permitted-development rights in specific areas. (`knowledge/jersey/planning/permitted-development.md`)

Separately, **building approval under the Building Bye-Laws 2007** is a distinct requirement that can apply even where planning permission is not needed. The corpus also warns that the most common mistake is assuming a "modest" extension is automatically permitted when its area/height/position in fact exceeds the GD Order limits. (`knowledge/jersey/planning/permitted-development.md`)

The corpus does not reproduce the exact numeric thresholds (floor area, depth, height) from the GD Order 2011 — both files advise checking the current Order for the precise wording and conditions.

Confirm the specific dimensional limits against the GD Order 2011 itself, and consider a pre-application enquiry with the Planning Department before relying on a permitted-development assumption.
