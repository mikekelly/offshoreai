Let me read the dedicated amendment file.On **15 April 2026**, the **Residential Tenancy (Jersey) Amendment Law 2025** came into force, the most significant Jersey residential-tenancy reform in over a decade. From that date the post-amendment regime applies to all new tenancies, with transitional rules for existing ones (`knowledge/jersey/residential-tenancy/2025-amendment-law.md`).

## Headline changes

- **Fixed terms capped at 3 years.** The initial fixed term may not exceed 3 years (down from the prior 9-year ceiling), and *successive fixed terms are prohibited* — an expired fixed term either ends or converts automatically to a periodic tenancy.
- **Tenant exit on one month's notice.** A tenant may terminate *any* residential tenancy on one month's written notice, even one within a fixed term — no more fixed-term lock-in.
- **Landlord termination — justified or 12 months.** To end a periodic tenancy a landlord must either rely on a permitted ground (breach, sale, owner-occupation, redevelopment) or give 12 months' no-fault notice. This replaces the old 3-month no-fault notice; 3 months' notice now requires a justified ground.
- **Rent caps and once-yearly rule.** Rent on periodic tenancies may rise only once per year, by no more than Jersey RPI, with at least 2 months' notice — unless the rent is "significantly below market rent" (a narrow, challengeable carve-out).
- **New Rent Tribunal.** An independent tribunal now hears disputes over rent increases (RPI breach, the below-market carve-out, once-yearly rule, notice compliance). It does *not* handle eviction/breach (still Petty Debts Court), social housing, or employment-linked accommodation (`knowledge/jersey/residential-tenancy/rent-tribunal.md`).
- **Strengthened written-form rules.** Agreements must clearly identify all fees chargeable to the tenant; hidden fees are unenforceable and may be an offence.

## Transitional treatment

- New tenancies (on/after 15 April 2026): full post-amendment regime.
- Pre-existing **periodic** tenancies: convert immediately to the new regime (rent caps, 12-month no-fault notice).
- Pre-existing **fixed-term** tenancies: continue under the old law until expiry, then the new rules apply; any successive-fixed-term clause becomes unenforceable at expiry.

## What did *not* change

Petty Debts Court jurisdiction over eviction/breach, Viscount enforcement, the condition-report regime, the Tenants' Deposit Scheme, the lodging-house exclusion, and the 9-year Royal-Court contract-of-lease ceiling.
