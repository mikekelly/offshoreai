## The three-layer stack

For a BVI emerging manager, the corpus describes a deliberately light-touch ladder at each layer:

### 1. Manager layer — Approved Manager
The **BVI Approved Manager** regime (Investment Business (Approved Manager) Regulations 2012) is the fast-track, light-touch authorisation route built precisely for "start-up, smaller, or family-office managers" — it sits *below* full SIBA Investment Business licensing. It gives you recognised regulated status (marketing-acceptable to LPs) with authorisation in a few weeks rather than months, lower cost, and proportionate ongoing obligations (annual returns, AML/CFT under the Proceeds of Criminal Conduct Act + AMLR, fit-and-proper maintenance, economic-substance compliance where applicable). It can manage BVI Private/Professional/Incubator/Approved/Public funds and closely-connected non-BVI funds. **If AUM grows beyond the prescribed ceiling, you must upgrade to full Investment Business licensing.** `knowledge/bvi/approved-manager.md`

### 2. Fund layer — Incubator *or* Approved Fund
Two distinct light-touch regimes from the SIBA (Incubator and Approved Funds) Regulations 2015 — pick one, they're not interchangeable:

| | Incubator Fund | Approved Fund |
|---|---|---|
| Max investors | 20 | 20 |
| Min investment | US$20,000 | None statutory |
| Max AUM | US$20m | US$100m |
| Time limit | 2 years (+1 yr extension) | Permanent |
| Auditor | Not required | Not required |
| Administrator | Not required | Typically required |
| Designed for | Building a track record | Stable small fund |

As an *emerging manager*, the **Incubator Fund** is the natural starting point — a launch pad to build a 12–24 month track record, after which you convert to a Private/Professional Fund (or wind down). The **Approved Fund** is the alternative if you intend to stay small permanently (e.g. a friends-and-family or boutique pool). The full ladder above these is Incubator → Approved → Private (≤50 investors, audited) → Professional → Public. `knowledge/bvi/incubator-and-approved-fund.md`, `knowledge/bvi/funds.md`

### 3. Vehicle layer — BC or Limited Partnership
The fund's legal form is typically one of:
- **BVI Business Company (BC)** under the Business Companies Act 2004 — the most common fund vehicle; investors hold shares. Incorporates in hours, low cost, one director minimum, no residence requirements, registered agent + registered office mandatory, BO information held by the agent. `knowledge/bvi/business-company.md`, `knowledge/bvi/funds.md`
- **Limited partnership** (GP/LP) — common for PE/VC where LPs want tax transparency in their home jurisdictions. `knowledge/bvi/funds.md`
- **SPC (Segregated Portfolio Company)** — a cell-style umbrella, but less prevalent in BVI than the Cayman SPC. `knowledge/bvi/funds.md`

## Putting it together
A typical emerging-manager stack: **you (or your management entity) authorised as an Approved Manager → managing an Incubator Fund → constituted as a BVI Business Company** (or a limited partnership if the strategy is closed-ended PE/VC and LPs need transparency). As you build track record and AUM, the fund converts up the ladder and the manager upgrades to full SIBA licensing.

A note on scope: this is the *light-touch emerging-manager* path. The corpus's BVI section is explicitly "high-level… for full BVI practice, engage BVI counsel" (`knowledge/bvi/index.md`), and the comparative framing in `knowledge/CROSS-JURISDICTIONAL-MAP.md` confirms BVI Approved Manager + Approved/Incubator Fund as the canonical emerging-manager combination.
