## What changes for a Jersey NPPR AIFM under AIFMD II (effective 16 April 2026)

**The headline: NPPR access survives.** AIFMD II (Directive (EU) 2024/927) preserves Crown Dependencies' access to EU professional investors via member-state National Private Placement Regimes — Jersey continues to meet the EU's tax-transparency and AML criteria, so the route itself is intact (`knowledge/frontier/aifmd-ii-april-2026.md`). The baseline NPPR mechanics — JFSC registration under Jersey's AIFMD-equivalent regime, country-by-country notification, AIFMD-equivalent depository for EU-marketed funds — are unchanged (`knowledge/jersey/funds/aifmd-nppr.md`).

### Direct changes that hit a Jersey NPPR AIFM
1. **Expanded investor disclosures** — pre-marketing and ongoing disclosure obligations widened to cover liquidity management, sustainability factors (interlocking with SFDR), and loan-originating-fund disclosures where applicable. PPMs and ongoing transparency reports need refreshing.
2. **Enhanced Annex IV reporting to ESMA** — new data fields and more granular reporting on asset categories and risk exposures; reporting systems are being upgraded, with first submissions under the new requirements expected end-Q2 / early-Q3 2026.

(Source for both: `knowledge/frontier/aifmd-ii-april-2026.md`.)

### Indirect change — the delegation squeeze (potentially the bigger story)
AIFMD II tightens rules on **EU AIFM delegation to third countries**. An EU AIFM delegating portfolio management to a Jersey entity faces enhanced substance/oversight requirements and cannot become a "letter-box entity." This reduces the appeal of "delegate everything to Jersey, market through an EU shell" structures — and, counterintuitively, makes a **direct Jersey AIFM marketing via NPPR comparatively more attractive** than an EU-AIFM-with-CI-delegate model. Sponsors are revisiting the case for direct Channel Islands domiciliation (`knowledge/frontier/aifmd-ii-april-2026.md`).

### Jersey-specific regulatory response
The JFSC is **splitting its AIF Code of Practice** into an **EU AIF Code** (for NPPR-into-EU marketing, aligned to AIFMD II) and a **UK AIF Code** (for UK marketing). The EU AIF Code is expected to be finalised through 2026, so a Jersey EU-marketing AIFM should track that finalisation (`knowledge/frontier/aifmd-ii-april-2026.md`).

### Watch-outs
- **Loan-originating funds** face a substantive new EU framework with restrictions on non-EU delegation — credit-fund sponsors are reviewing domicile.
- **Member-state transposition is uneven** (Luxembourg/Ireland close to the directive; Germany/France stricter), so a fund marketed across several EU states may face different effective requirements.
- Don't treat NPPR as wholly unaffected — disclosure and Annex IV obligations have widened.
