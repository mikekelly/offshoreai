The most on-point file is the founder use case. Let me read it plus the compiled wormhole.## Pillar Two and a Jersey TopCo above a UK Opco

**The threshold gate comes first.** Pillar Two only engages if the consolidated group has **annual consolidated revenue of EUR 750m or more** in at least two of the four preceding fiscal years. Below that, Pillar Two does not apply at all — the Jersey TopCo continues on the **zero-ten 0% standard rate** as a holding company, and nothing changes. (`knowledge/jersey/international/pillar-two-mcit.md`; `knowledge/jersey/tax/pillar-two.md`; `knowledge/jersey/use-cases/founder-entrepreneur/wormhole-jersey-topco-uk-listing.md` step 5)

**Above the threshold — the Jersey side.** Jersey implements Pillar Two through the **Multinational Corporate Income Tax (Jersey) Law 2024 (MCIT)**, effective for financial years beginning on or after **1 January 2025**. Because a typical Jersey TopCo pays 0% under zero-ten, its Jersey effective tax rate is below 15%, so a **top-up** is charged to bring the Jersey-booked income up to a **15% effective rate**. The MCIT is structured as a **Qualified Domestic Minimum Top-up Tax (QDMTT)** plus an Income Inclusion Rule — the key point being that **Jersey collects the top-up itself** rather than ceding it to a foreign parent jurisdiction. (`knowledge/jersey/tax/pillar-two.md`; `knowledge/jersey/international/pillar-two-mcit.md`)

**The UK-Opco interaction.** This is the structurally important part for your fact pattern. The group has tax presence in two relevant jurisdictions:

- **Jersey income (the TopCo)** — Jersey's QDMTT/MCIT tops up the TopCo's Jersey-source income to 15% and keeps that revenue in Jersey. Because the QDMTT is "qualified," it offsets/displaces top-up tax that the UK (or any other parent jurisdiction) would otherwise collect on the Jersey income under its own Income Inclusion Rule or UTPR. (`knowledge/jersey/international/pillar-two-mcit.md`; `knowledge/jersey/use-cases/founder-entrepreneur/wormhole-jersey-topco-uk-listing.md` step 5)
- **UK Opco income** — the UK runs its own Pillar Two regime **in parallel** on the UK operating company's profits. The UK Opco is already taxed at UK corporation-tax rates well above 15%, so it typically generates no top-up; in any case the UK Pillar Two charge is the UK's to administer, not Jersey's. The corpus states explicitly that "**UK Pillar Two runs in parallel**." (`knowledge/jersey/use-cases/founder-entrepreneur/wormhole-jersey-topco-uk-listing.md` step 5)

So a Jersey QDMTT means the **15% floor on the TopCo's Jersey income is captured in Jersey, not exported to the UK** under the UK's IIR — that is the whole point of Jersey having implemented a QDMTT rather than leaving the gap for foreign parents to fill.

**What changes operationally above the threshold.** GloBE-income computation per jurisdiction, ETR analysis, registration of Jersey constituent entities, a Pillar Two return alongside the local Jersey return, and the Global Information Return — materially heavier compliance, typically requiring specialist software and advisers. The substance-based income exclusion (payroll + tangible assets, sliding-scale carve-out) can reduce the in-scope amount in early years. (`knowledge/jersey/international/pillar-two-mcit.md`; `knowledge/jersey/use-cases/founder-entrepreneur/pillar-two-impact.md`)

**Note also:** Jersey has **not** implemented an Undertaxed Profits Rule (UTPR) — it operates the QDMTT and IIR only (per the cross-jurisdictional/trajectory context). And economic substance under the **Taxation (Companies — Economic Substance) (Jersey) Law 2019** applies to the holding-company TopCo independently of Pillar Two (reduced test for a pure equity holder, but real people and premises still required). (`knowledge/jersey/tax/economic-substance.md` via `knowledge/jersey/use-cases/founder-entrepreneur/wormhole-jersey-topco-uk-listing.md` step 4)
