> **Note on source status:** the corpus file I'm relying on is marked `draft` — the content is written but not yet verified against the primary source (the Image Rights (Bailiwick of Guernsey) Ordinance 2012). Treat the specifics below as indicative and verify against the Ordinance before relying on them. It was last verified 2026-05-18, so it is fresh.

**The headline.** Guernsey is the only jurisdiction in the world with a statutory image-rights *register* for individuals, created by the **Image Rights (Bailiwick of Guernsey) Ordinance 2012**. Where most jurisdictions protect image only through a patchwork of tort claims (passing off, defamation, US-style rights of publicity), Guernsey turns it into a **registrable property right** — meaning it is transferable, licensable, inheritable, enforceable, and in some cases mortgageable. (`knowledge/guernsey/image-rights-register.md`)

**What you register.** You register a "**personnage**" — the identifying attributes attached to a personality. That can cover your name (real, professional or stage), voice, signature, image/likeness, distinctive appearance, and mannerisms. Both real persons and fictional/non-real characters can be associated with a personnage. (`knowledge/guernsey/image-rights-register.md`)

**How registration works.** You file with the **Guernsey Intellectual Property Office**; the application is examined and published, granted after an opposition period, and is renewable every 10 years (with longer post-death protection for some categories). The register is public, and fees are modest relative to commercial trademark filings. (`knowledge/guernsey/image-rights-register.md`)

**Licensing — your core use case.** The registered personnage is most valuable precisely as the **asset base for licensing arrangements** rather than as a standalone enforcement tool. You license registered attributes to brand partners/product lines, typically with Guernsey-law governing law and forum clauses, and route royalties to a holding vehicle. (`knowledge/guernsey/image-rights-register.md`)

**Enforcement.** A registered personnage gives you standing in the Guernsey courts and statutory remedies — injunction, damages, account of profits, delivery-up of infringing material. The important caveat: the regime is *sui generis*, so **foreign courts may not give it direct effect**. Against infringers outside Guernsey you'll usually combine local-jurisdiction enforcement (trademarks, passing off, publicity rights) with the Guernsey registration used as evidence of a proprietary interest. (`knowledge/guernsey/image-rights-register.md`)

**Structuring and succession.** The registration is commonly wrapped in a **Guernsey trust or holding company**, which then licenses use and collects royalties — combining the registered right with Guernsey trust law and favourable tax for a succession/estate-planning outcome (image rights survive death and can pass outside your home-jurisdiction succession regime, subject to conflict-of-laws rules). (`knowledge/guernsey/image-rights-register.md`)

**Privacy trade-off.** Because the register is public, the regime may not suit someone wanting full privacy, though the corpus notes the specifics of a registration can be calibrated. (`knowledge/guernsey/image-rights-register.md`)

**One Jersey-corpus point worth flagging:** this is a *Guernsey* offering — Jersey, Cayman, and BVI have no equivalent image-rights statute. So if image-rights structuring is the priority, the vehicle points you to Guernsey, even if other parts of a family structure (e.g. a Jersey trust under the Article 9 firewall) sit elsewhere. (`knowledge/guernsey/image-rights-register.md`; `knowledge/CROSS-JURISDICTIONAL-MAP.md`)
