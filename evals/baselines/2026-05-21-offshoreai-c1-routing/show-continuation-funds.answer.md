## Why continuation funds and GP-led secondaries are growing so fast

A continuation fund is a new vehicle into which a GP transfers one or more portfolio assets from an existing fund, letting existing LPs either **roll** their interest into the new vehicle or **cash out** at a negotiated valuation; GP-led secondaries is the broader category (tender offers, strip sales, preferred-equity deals, structured liquidity). The corpus identifies four converging drivers behind the surge:

- **Weak exit markets** — tough M&A and IPO conditions through 2022–2025 left GPs unable to exit cleanly;
- **LP pressure for DPI** — LPs want distributions back to recycle into new commitments;
- **GPs unwilling to sell trophy assets** at depressed valuations, preferring to keep holding them;
- **Secondaries capital ready to deploy.**

The scale is striking and accelerating: the 2026 Global Private Equity Outlook survey (cited in the file) records **46% of respondents** using GP-led secondaries or continuation vehicles — roughly double the prior year — with EMEA respondents planning increases jumping from 14% (2024) to 43%. ## Why Guernsey is winning the business

Guernsey Finance has marketed aggressively, and the corpus attributes its lead to a specific set of structural advantages:

- **Single-asset continuation funds are not regulated as collective investment schemes** in Guernsey — a major simplification of the regulatory pathway;
- **Fast-track regimes** with sign-off in a maximum of **3 business days**, and next-day approval for Private Investment Funds (PIFs);
- A **deep, familiar fund-counsel ecosystem** (Carey Olsen, Ogier, Mourant, Walkers);
- **Tax neutrality** (0% standard corporate rate);
- **Substance-light treatment** of the new continuation vehicle where the asset is the only thing changing hands.

By contrast, Jersey competes on a **48-hour JPF authorisation** and its Designated Service Provider model, and Cayman remains dominant where US-LP capital is involved (Cayman ELP under the Private Funds Act 2020). The corpus notes Jersey practitioners have shifted inventory to match, but Guernsey's marketing is "the most aggressive." ([`knowledge/frontier/continuation-funds-and-gp-led-secondaries.md`](knowledge/frontier/continuation-funds-and-gp-led-secondaries.md), with depth in [`knowledge/guernsey/private-investment-fund.md`](knowledge/guernsey/private-investment-fund.md) and [`knowledge/jersey/funds/jersey-private-fund.md`](knowledge/jersey/funds/jersey-private-fund.md))

**A caveat worth flagging:** the regulatory edge — that single-asset continuation funds escape CIS classification in Guernsey — is the load-bearing claim here and turns on a specific Guernsey regulatory treatment.
