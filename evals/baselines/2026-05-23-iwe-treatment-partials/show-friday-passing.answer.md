## Why your Jersey property transfer happens before the Royal Court

In Jersey, **real-property contracts are "passed" before the Royal Court** rather than completed privately as in England. The buyer and seller (typically represented by their advocates acting as attorneys) appear before the Court; the contract is read or referenced; the Greffier records it; two Jurats sit to witness; and the **transfer takes effect at that Court act** — the property changes hands at that moment, not on signature of the contract (`knowledge/jersey/property/contract-passing.md`; `knowledge/jersey/property/commercial-leases.md` for the Jurats / Friday-afternoon detail).

The corpus explains the procedure as a **Norman customary law inheritance**: it makes the transfer of real property **public**, places the Royal Court as **repository of property records**, and gives **certainty** about title transitions — quite different from the largely private English conveyancing model (`knowledge/jersey/property/contract-passing.md`).

## Why specifically *Friday afternoon*

The Friday-afternoon convention is **referenced in the corpus** as the standard slot for property passing — `commercial-leases.md` notes leases of 9+ years are passed "on a Friday afternoon at the Royal Court", `flying-freeholds.md` notes "Royal Court passing (Friday afternoon)", and `contract/force-majeure.md` notes that historic Court closures on the Friday-afternoon slot have triggered force-majeure arguments — but **the corpus does not contain a substantive explanation of the historical origin of the Friday-afternoon timing itself**. It is described as established Royal Court practice for property passings, not derived from a specific statute.

The contract-passing file notes that the Samedi Division sits "most weekdays" and that property contracts are routinely passed there, with Friday afternoon being the conventional slot in practice — but the *why-Friday* (as opposed to *why-the-Royal-Court*) is not in the corpus.
