# Marketing a Jersey AIF into Germany via NPPR

## The route in summary

Germany is **not** accessible via an EU passport for a Jersey AIF — Jersey is non-EU, and the AIFMD third-country passport was proposed but never implemented. The practical route is **country-by-country private placement under the National Private Placement Regime (NPPR)**, which for Germany sits under **§330 KAGB** and is supervised by **BaFin**. Germany is one of the stricter NPPR jurisdictions (alongside France).

## What you need in place

**1. JFSC AIFM registration in Jersey.** The AIFM must be registered with the JFSC under Jersey's AIFMD-equivalent regime and demonstrate AIFMD-equivalent compliance — capital adequacy, AIFMD Art 15-equivalent risk management, liquidity management (for open-ended AIFs), AIFMD-equivalent depository arrangements, investor transparency (annual report, periodic reporting), and regulatory reporting to the JFSC. (`knowledge/jersey/funds/aifmd-nppr.md`; `knowledge/jersey/use-cases/fund-counsel/aifmd-nppr-registration.md`)

**2. Depository.** A Jersey AIF marketed into the EU typically needs **AIFMD-equivalent depository arrangements** covering custody, oversight, and cash-flow monitoring. Jersey AIFs often combine a JFSC-licensed Jersey depository (lighter functions) with full AIFMD-depository functions performed elsewhere. (`knowledge/jersey/funds/aifmd-nppr.md`; `knowledge/jersey/use-cases/fund-counsel/aifmd-nppr-registration.md`)

**3. The BaFin NPPR notification (§330 KAGB).** Notify BaFin as Germany's competent authority. Germany-specific requirements flagged in the corpus:
- a **local fact-sheet**;
- a **local distributor** is sometimes required;
- **ongoing reporting** obligations after acceptance.

You can only market to **professional investors** in Germany once cleared, and only after notification — active marketing without notification is a serious enforcement risk. (`knowledge/jersey/use-cases/fund-counsel/aifmd-nppr-registration.md`)

## AIFMD II — now in force (effective 16 April 2026)

Most AIFMD II requirements (Directive (EU) 2024/927) took effect **16 April 2026** — about five weeks ago. The directive **preserves Crown Dependencies' NPPR access**, but for Jersey AIFMs marketing into the EU it tightens two areas you should expect to feel:

- **Expanded investor disclosures** — pre-marketing and ongoing disclosures now cover liquidity management, sustainability factors (SFDR interaction), and loan-originating-fund disclosures where applicable;
- **Enhanced Annex IV reporting** to ESMA — new data fields, more granular reporting on asset categories and risk exposures.

The JFSC is also splitting its AIF Code into an **EU AIF Code** (aligned with AIFMD II, for NPPR marketing) and a **UK AIF Code**; the EU AIF Code is expected to be finalised through 2026. (`knowledge/frontier/aifmd-ii-april-2026.md`)

## Reverse solicitation caveat

NPPR registration is only required for **active marketing**. Genuine reverse solicitation by a German investor approaching the AIF unsolicited does not trigger NPPR — but BaFin and other member-state authorities scrutinise reverse-solicitation claims, and active outreach disguised as reverse solicitation attracts penalties. (`knowledge/jersey/use-cases/fund-counsel/aifmd-nppr-registration.md`)
