# UK carried-interest reform — 6 April 2026

## What changes

The UK has moved carried interest from a **CGT-based regime** to an **income-tax-based regime with a partial exclusion**. The arc:

- **Pre-6 April 2025**: CGT, effective ~28% for additional-rate payers.
- **6 April 2025 – 5 April 2026** (transition): flat **32% CGT**.
- **From 6 April 2026**: carry is **income**, subject to income tax + Class 4 NICs. For **qualifying** carry, a **72.5% multiplier** applies (only 72.5% of the carry is in charge), giving an effective rate of approximately **34.1%** for a 45% additional-rate payer. **Non-qualifying** carry attracts the full income + NICs charge — up to ~**47%**.

The qualifying/non-qualifying split turns on whether the partner is performing genuine investment-management activity and whether the fund meets specified characteristics; the definition was tightened in the final Finance Bill 2025-26. Established UK-resident PE partners at mature sponsors will typically be qualifying; new entrants, secondaries managers and non-traditional structures need careful analysis (`knowledge/frontier/uk-carried-interest-reform-2026.md`).

## What PE partners in the Crown Dependencies should do

**Pre-6 April 2026 was crystallisation season** — accelerated distributions, disposals and restructurings to lock in the 32% CGT basis. That window is now closed; the question is post-effective-date positioning.

### For individuals
- **Residency planning** — Jersey **2(1)(e) HVR**, Guernsey Open Market and IoM tax-cap routes are being marketed more actively to UK partners. But the UK **Statutory Residence Test** bites quickly, **tail liability** on carry that economically accrued while UK-resident continues post-departure, **substance** in the destination is essential, and the new legislation contains **specific anti-avoidance provisions around residence changes**.
- **Don't conflate co-invest with carry** — co-invest economics remain CGT-treated, so misclassification is now materially more costly.
- **Trust and family-office structures** for deferral, spinout and generational transfer are active areas of advice.

### For sponsors / GPs
- **Document carry arrangements** carefully to preserve qualifying status partner-by-partner.
- **Review GP / LP economics** for tax-efficient allocations to non-UK partners — Crown Dependencies-domiciled GPs (Jersey/Guernsey companies acting as GPs of Jersey or Cayman ELP funds) become more attractive as the carry-allocation entity for non-UK partners, though UK look-through rules still apply where a UK-resident partner receives the economic benefit.
- **Adjust vesting / forfeiture mechanics** and prepare LP communications: gross-of-tax economics may need re-pricing, and LPs are asking explicit retention questions about key UK-based partners.
- **Fund-level vehicles** (JPF, Guernsey PIF, Cayman ELP) are unchanged — the bite is at the **GP entity and carry allocation** layer.

A typical Jersey carry vehicle is a Jersey LP whose limited partners are the management team and which holds the carry interest in the main fund — Jersey-level tax is generally zero for non-resident participants, so the substantive issue has always been the recipients' home-jurisdiction tax. The 6 April 2026 reform sharply raises that home-jurisdiction cost for UK-resident recipients, which is why the structuring conversation now centres on partner residence, qualifying-status documentation, and the carry/co-invest boundary (`knowledge/jersey/use-cases/fund-counsel/carry-vehicle.md`).
