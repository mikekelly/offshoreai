## What AIFMD II changes for a Jersey AIFM marketing into the EU under NPPR

**NPPR access is preserved.** The directive maintains Crown Dependencies' access to EU professional investors through member-state National Private Placement Regimes; Jersey and Guernsey continue to meet the EU's tax-transparency and AML criteria, so the route itself is intact (`knowledge/frontier/aifmd-ii-april-2026.md`).

**Direct impact on a Jersey AIFM is concentrated in two areas:**

1. **Expanded investor disclosures.** Pre-marketing and ongoing disclosure obligations are widened — covering liquidity-management, sustainability factors (interlocking with SFDR), and loan-originating-fund-specific disclosures where applicable.
2. **Enhanced Annex IV reporting** to ESMA via competent authorities — new data fields and more granular reporting on specific asset categories and risk exposures. First submissions under the new requirements are expected end-Q2 / early Q3 2026 (`knowledge/frontier/aifmd-ii-april-2026.md`).

**Indirect — and arguably more important — impact via delegation rules.** AIFMD II tightens substance and oversight obligations on **EU AIFMs that delegate portfolio management to third countries** (including Jersey). EU AIFMs cannot become "letter-box entities," and loan-originating EU AIFMs face specific limits on delegation of origination to non-EU entities. The counter-intuitive consequence flagged by Carey Olsen and Langham Hall: a **direct Jersey AIFM marketing under NPPR** is comparatively *more* attractive than an EU-AIFM-with-Jersey-delegate model (`knowledge/frontier/aifmd-ii-april-2026.md`).

**Jersey-side regulatory response — the AIF Code split.** The JFSC is bifurcating Jersey's AIF Code of Practice into an **EU AIF Code** (aligned with AIFMD II for NPPR marketing) and a **UK AIF Code** (aligned with the UK's evolving post-Brexit AIFM framework). The EU AIF Code is expected to be finalised through 2026 (`knowledge/frontier/aifmd-ii-april-2026.md`).

**Other substantive AIFMD II features** — loan-originating-fund rules, liquidity-management-tool requirements for open-ended AIFs, and tightened asset-stripping rules — apply primarily within the AIFMD II framework itself, but feed through to NPPR-marketed Jersey AIFs via the disclosure and reporting strands above.

**Practical pitfalls** the frontier file flags: treating AIFMD II as a small-deltas exercise (the loan-originating-fund and substance rules are substantive); assuming NPPR is unaffected (disclosure/reporting widened); missing the delegation impact on EU-AIFM-CI-delegate structures; and uneven member-state transposition (Luxembourg/Ireland closer to the directive; Germany/France stricter) meaning a fund marketed across multiple EU jurisdictions may face differing effective requirements.
