## The BVI emerging-manager stack

The corpus describes a canonical three-layer stack for a BVI emerging manager.

### 1. The manager — BVI Approved Manager

Authorised under the **Investment Business (Approved Manager) Regulations 2012**: a fast-track, light-touch route below full SIBA / Investment Business licensing. Authorisation typically runs weeks rather than months, with lower initial and ongoing costs and AUM-capped scope. Eligible to manage BVI Private, Professional, Incubator, Approved or Public Funds (and closely-connected non-BVI funds). If AUM grows past the threshold, you upgrade to a full Investment Business licence (`knowledge/bvi/approved-manager.md`).

### 2. The fund — Incubator Fund or Approved Fund (SIBA 2015)

Two distinct light-touch regimes from the **SIBA (Incubator and Approved Funds) Regulations 2015** — pick one, they're not interchangeable:

| | Incubator Fund | Approved Fund |
|---|---|---|
| Max investors | 20 | 20 |
| Min investment per investor | US$20,000 | None statutory |
| Max AUM | US$20m | US$100m |
| Time limit | 2 years (+1 yr extension) → convert or wind down | Permanent |
| Auditor | Not required | Not required |
| Administrator | Not required | Typically required |
| Designed for | Emerging manager building a track record | Stable small fund / family-office pool |

For an "emerging manager", the **Incubator Fund** is the file's named target use case: launch quickly, build 12–24 months of track record, then convert to a Private Fund (50 investors), Professional Fund, or Public Fund (`knowledge/bvi/incubator-and-approved-fund.md`).

### 3. The vehicle layer — BVI Business Companies (BCA 2004) and/or LPs

The Approved Manager file lays out the canonical stack literally:

1. **GP entity** — BVI Business Company (BC) under the BVI Business Companies Act 2004;
2. **Management entity** — BVI BC, authorised as the Approved Manager;
3. **Fund vehicle** — BVI BC, or a limited partnership under the BVI Partnership Act (`knowledge/bvi/approved-manager.md`).

The BC is "the principal vehicle for international business in the BVI" — incorporated in hours, low fees, registered-agent model, no residence requirement for directors or shareholders, flexible share classes (`knowledge/bvi/business-company.md`). Choose BC vs LP at the fund level based on investor preference (LPs are typical for closed-ended PE-style funds with carried interest mechanics).

### Cross-cutting obligations

- **Economic substance** — BVI Economic Substance (Companies and Limited Partnerships) Act 2018 applies to in-scope activities of the manager and any in-scope underlying entities; reduced substance for pure equity holding (`knowledge/bvi/business-company.md`, `knowledge/bvi/approved-manager.md`).
- **AML/CFT** — BVI Proceeds of Criminal Conduct Act and AMLR; substantive, not paper formality (`knowledge/bvi/approved-manager.md`).
- **Beneficial ownership** — registered-agent-held via BOSS; authority access, no public access currently (`knowledge/bvi/business-company.md`).

### Caveats

All three cited files are `status: draft` in the corpus — drafted but not yet editorially verified against the primary statutes and FSC handbooks. Engage **BVI counsel** before launch — the corpus explicitly notes this at `knowledge/bvi/index.md`. Verify current AUM thresholds, fee levels, and the Approved Manager AUM ceiling against the BVI FSC primary materials before relying on the numbers above; the Incubator US$20m / Approved US$100m caps are statutory but the Approved Manager AUM threshold is stated as "check current thresholds" in the corpus itself.
