## Today (May 2026) — impairment-based regime

Drug-driving in Jersey is prosecuted as **driving while impaired by drugs** under the Road Traffic (Jersey) Law 1956. Police must prove **actual impairment** on objective evidence (poor control, failed field sobriety tests, blood/urine confirming drugs) — there are **no statutory drug-concentration limits, no per se offences, and no roadside swab-testing powers** equivalent to the UK regime (`knowledge/jersey/road-traffic/drug-driving.md`).

The offence is **substance-neutral**: controlled drugs, prescription medication (sedatives, opioids, benzodiazepines), OTC medicines with sedating warnings, and drug-plus-alcohol combinations all count. A valid prescription is **not a defence** if impairment is real (`knowledge/jersey/road-traffic/drug-driving.md`).

## Sentencing on conviction

Tracks drink-driving: **mandatory disqualification** (typically 12+ months on a first offence), fine up to **£2,000**, imprisonment up to **6 months**, and licence endorsement. Accidents, refusal to cooperate, or presence of passengers aggravate (`knowledge/jersey/road-traffic/drug-driving.md`).

## What's coming — reform pipeline

In **February 2026**, States representatives proposed legislation to:

- introduce **statutory drug limits** for controlled and some prescription substances;
- give police **roadside testing powers** (UK-style drug-wipe swabs);
- create **per se offences** — being over the limit is the offence, regardless of impairment;
- impose **mandatory disqualification** on conviction.

The proposal is **not yet enacted** as of May 2026; the States Assembly timetable points to potential introduction during 2026 or 2027. Until then, the impairment-based regime continues (`knowledge/jersey/road-traffic/drug-driving.md`).
