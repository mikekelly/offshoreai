# Pillar Two — Jersey TopCo above a UK Opco

## Threshold test — does Pillar Two engage at all?

Pillar Two only engages for **MNE groups with annual consolidated revenue of EUR 750 million or more** in at least two of the four preceding fiscal years (`knowledge/jersey/tax/pillar-two.md`; `knowledge/jersey/international/pillar-two-mcit.md`). Below that threshold, the Jersey TopCo continues at its **zero-ten** rate (typically 0% for a pure holding company), and the UK Opco continues to pay UK corporation tax on its trading profits, with no top-up mechanics (`knowledge/jersey/use-cases/founder-entrepreneur/topco-tax.md`; `knowledge/jersey/use-cases/founder-entrepreneur/worked-example-jersey-topco-uk-listing.md`, Step 4).

## In-scope groups — how the rules apply to the structure

If the consolidated group is at or above the €750m threshold:

- **Jersey TopCo (the parent / holding entity).** Under zero-ten the TopCo's Jersey ETR will typically be 0%, well below the 15% GloBE minimum. Jersey has implemented the **Multinational Corporate Income Tax (Jersey) Law 2024** ("MCIT"), effective for financial years commencing on or after **1 January 2025**, comprising a **Qualified Domestic Minimum Top-up Tax (QDMTT / DTT)** and an **Income Inclusion Rule (IIR)** (`knowledge/jersey/tax/pillar-two.md`; `knowledge/jersey/international/pillar-two-mcit.md`). The QDMTT tops Jersey-booked income up to a 15% ETR; because Jersey collects it locally, the UK does not collect it under its own IIR (`knowledge/jersey/use-cases/founder-entrepreneur/worked-example-jersey-topco-uk-listing.md`, Step 4: "the TopCo's Jersey-source income is topped up to 15% rather than ceded to the UK under the IIR").
- **UK Opco.** UK corporation tax on the operating profits is well above 15%, so no Pillar Two top-up is generated at the UK level on those profits. The corpus notes that "UK Pillar Two implementation runs in parallel" — i.e. the UK has its own GloBE-aligned regime — but does not give the UK CT rate itself; verify the UK-side ETR computation with a UK tax adviser (`knowledge/jersey/use-cases/founder-entrepreneur/worked-example-jersey-topco-uk-listing.md`, Step 4).
- **Net cash-tax effect.** Total Jersey tax on the TopCo becomes **at least 15%** for in-scope groups regardless of the zero-ten classification (`knowledge/jersey/tax/pillar-two.md`, *Interaction with zero-ten*). The headline 0% Jersey rate is no longer the result for the in-scope tier (`knowledge/jersey/use-cases/founder-entrepreneur/pillar-two-impact.md`).

## Mechanics worth flagging

- **GloBE income ≠ Jersey taxable income.** ETR is computed on GloBE income (consolidated-accounting-derived, with prescribed adjustments), not on Jersey local taxable income (`knowledge/jersey/tax/pillar-two.md`, *The 15% effective rate*).
- **Substance-based income exclusion** (formulaic payroll + tangible-asset carve-out) reduces top-up in early years on a sliding scale (`knowledge/jersey/international/pillar-two-mcit.md`).
- **Substance Law 2019** continues to apply in parallel — Pillar Two does not displace it (`knowledge/jersey/international/pillar-two-mcit.md`; `knowledge/jersey/use-cases/founder-entrepreneur/worked-example-jersey-topco-uk-listing.md`, Step 3).
- **Compliance burden** is material: MCIT registration, GloBE income and ETR calculation, top-up computation, GIR filing, and coordination with the group's home-country Pillar Two filings (`knowledge/jersey/international/pillar-two-mcit.md`; `knowledge/jersey/use-cases/founder-entrepreneur/pillar-two-impact.md`).

## Out-of-scope holdings — the practical baseline

Most founder TopCo structures are well below €750m; for them, the Jersey TopCo continues at 0% under zero-ten, with no Pillar Two filings (`knowledge/jersey/use-cases/founder-entrepreneur/topco-tax.md`; `knowledge/jersey/tax/pillar-two.md`).
