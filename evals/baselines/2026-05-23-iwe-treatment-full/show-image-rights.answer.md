## Guernsey's image-rights register — how it works

Guernsey is the **only jurisdiction in the world** with a statutory image-rights register for individuals, established by the **Image Rights (Bailiwick of Guernsey) Ordinance 2012**. It converts what other jurisdictions treat as a patchwork of tort claims (passing off, defamation, right of publicity) into a **registrable property right** that is transferable, licensable, inheritable, mortgageable, and directly enforceable in Guernsey courts (`knowledge/guernsey/image-rights-register.md`).

### What you can register

You register a **"personnage"** — covering any combination of: name (real, professional, or stage), voice, signature, image/likeness, distinctive appearance, mannerisms, and other personality-identifying attributes. Both natural persons and fictional characters can be associated with a personnage, which is why the regime also gets used for animated characters and brand mascots (`knowledge/guernsey/image-rights-register.md`).

### How the registration works

- Filed with the **Guernsey Intellectual Property Office**;
- Subject to examination, publication, and an opposition period before grant;
- Renewable every 10 years, with longer terms (life + 100 years) for certain categories;
- Public on the register; fees modest compared with commercial trade-mark filings (`knowledge/guernsey/image-rights-register.md`).

### Enforcement and licensing

A registered personnage gives you **standing in Guernsey courts** and statutory remedies — injunction, damages, account of profits, and delivery up of infringing material. For overseas infringement, the Guernsey registration is typically used as evidence of proprietary interest in support of local-jurisdiction proceedings, with licensing agreements drafted under Guernsey-law governing law and forum (`knowledge/guernsey/image-rights-register.md`).

### Structuring (what most celebrities actually do)

The registered right is usually held inside a wealth-structuring wrapper — most commonly:

- Image rights **settled into a Guernsey trust**, or
- Image rights **owned by a Guernsey holding company** that licenses use and receives royalty flow;
- Combined with **estate planning** so that the rights pass outside the personality's home-jurisdiction succession regime (subject to applicable conflict-of-laws rules) (`knowledge/guernsey/image-rights-register.md`).

### Practical limitations to weigh

- **Foreign-court recognition** is the main open question — the regime is sui generis and other courts may not give the Guernsey right direct effect, so it is best paired with local-jurisdiction IP / publicity-rights enforcement;
- The register is **public**, which may not suit individuals wanting maximum privacy (though what is registered can be calibrated);
- Its highest-value use is as the **asset base for licensing arrangements** rather than as a standalone enforcement tool (`knowledge/guernsey/image-rights-register.md`).
