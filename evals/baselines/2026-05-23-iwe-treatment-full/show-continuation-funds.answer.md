## Why continuation funds and GP-led secondaries are growing fast

A continuation fund is a new vehicle into which a sponsor transfers one or more portfolio assets from an existing fund, with existing LPs given the choice to **roll** their interest into the new vehicle or **cash out** at a negotiated valuation. GP-led secondaries is the broader category (tender offers, strip sales, preferred-equity, structured liquidity). The growth drivers are converging:

- **Tough M&A and IPO exit markets** through 2022–2025, leaving GPs unable or unwilling to sell trophy assets at depressed valuations;
- **LP demand for DPI** (distributed-to-paid-in capital) to recycle into new commitments;
- **Plenty of secondaries dry powder** ready to deploy.

The **2026 Global Private Equity Outlook survey** (cited by Dechert, Nov 2025) records 46% of respondents using GP-led secondaries or continuation vehicles to expedite LP distributions — roughly double the prior year — with 51% of North American, 55% of APAC, and 43% of EMEA respondents (up from 14% in 2024) planning to *increase* GP-led dealmaking over the next 24 months. ([`knowledge/frontier/continuation-funds-and-gp-led-secondaries.md`](knowledge/frontier/continuation-funds-and-gp-led-secondaries.md))

## Why Guernsey is winning the work

Per the corpus, Guernsey has positioned itself most aggressively, and the package has five specific features:

1. **Single-asset continuation funds are not regulated as collective investment schemes in Guernsey** — a material simplification of the regulatory pathway. This is the structural advantage at the centre of the pitch.
2. **Fast-track approval** — sign-off within 3 business days maximum, with **next-day approval for Private Investment Funds (PIFs)**. The PIF Route 1 (licensed manager) is the most common path and is typically authorised in one business day.
3. **A familiar fund-counsel ecosystem** (Carey Olsen, Ogier, Mourant, Walkers) with practitioners already running these processes;
4. **Tax neutrality** — 0% standard corporate tax;
5. **Substance-light treatment** for the new continuation vehicle itself where the asset is the only thing changing hands.

Coordination between **Guernsey Finance, the GFSC, and local counsel** is described as the most aggressive of any IFC in this space. ([`knowledge/frontier/continuation-funds-and-gp-led-secondaries.md`](knowledge/frontier/continuation-funds-and-gp-led-secondaries.md); [`knowledge/guernsey/private-investment-fund.md`](knowledge/guernsey/private-investment-fund.md))

## How Jersey compares

Jersey is competing rather than ceding the ground: **Jersey Private Funds** can be set up for continuation vehicles with **48-hour JFSC authorisation**, the **Designated Service Provider (DSP)** model suits sponsors who want regulatory efficiency, LP/GP mechanics are familiar under Jersey LP Law, and multiple Jersey-domiciled continuation funds were launched in 2025–2026. Jersey's structural near-equivalent — `48h JPF vs 1-day Guernsey PIF` — is close enough that the choice often turns on counsel preference, route flexibility (Guernsey offers three PIF routes; Jersey's JPF is one DSP-delegated route), and the sponsor's existing administrator relationships. ([`knowledge/frontier/continuation-funds-and-gp-led-secondaries.md`](knowledge/frontier/continuation-funds-and-gp-led-secondaries.md); [`knowledge/guernsey/private-investment-fund.md`](knowledge/guernsey/private-investment-fund.md))

Cayman remains dominant where US-LP institutional capital sets the structuring centre of gravity, using the Cayman ELP under the Private Funds Act 2020 framework. ([`knowledge/frontier/continuation-funds-and-gp-led-secondaries.md`](knowledge/frontier/continuation-funds-and-gp-led-secondaries.md))

## What to watch

- **UK carried-interest reform** (effective 6 April 2026) changes post-rollover carry economics meaningfully;
- **AIFMD II** continuation-fund disclosure requirements bite from April 2026 onward;
- **Conflict and disclosure** scrutiny continues to tighten — SEC rulemaking, ILPA's 2023 Continuation Fund Guidance increasingly treated as soft-law, independent-valuation and LPAC-blessing standards hardening.
