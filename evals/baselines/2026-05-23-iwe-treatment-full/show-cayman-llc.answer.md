## Is the Cayman LLC the right form?

For a Delaware LLC redomiciliation, **yes — the Cayman LLC is the form purpose-built for this**. The **Limited Liability Companies Act 2016** was introduced specifically to mirror the Delaware LLC and to facilitate cross-border restructurings involving Delaware LLCs. The corpus is explicit on the direct-conversion advantage: a Delaware LLC can be **directly converted** into a Cayman LLC, whereas converting it into a Cayman exempted company would require first converting it to a corporate form ([`knowledge/cayman/cayman-llc.md`](knowledge/cayman/cayman-llc.md)).

The LLC form is "particularly useful" — per the corpus — precisely in scenarios like yours: restructurings involving a Delaware LLC redomiciling to Cayman, where Delaware-LLC parity and check-the-box flexibility matter ([`knowledge/cayman/cayman-llc.md`](knowledge/cayman/cayman-llc.md)).

Key features carried over from the Delaware model:
- **Members** (not shareholders), limited liability for all
- **LLC Agreement** as the master contractual document (no M&A, no statutory capital)
- **Member-managed by default**, or manager-managed by election
- Migration in/out is **permitted**; mergers and conversions with foreign LLCs available
- **50-year tax undertaking** available
- No CIMA registration solely for the form; substance and activity-triggered regulation apply ([`knowledge/cayman/cayman-llc.md`](knowledge/cayman/cayman-llc.md))

## How check-the-box works for a Cayman LLC

Cayman LLCs are eligible for the US "check-the-box" classification regime ([`knowledge/cayman/cayman-llc.md`](knowledge/cayman/cayman-llc.md)):

- **Default classification** (as a foreign entity owned by US persons): **partnership** if more than one member; **disregarded entity** if a single member.
- **Elective**: the entity can affirmatively elect to be treated as a **corporation** instead.

By contrast, a Cayman **exempted company** is generally a **per se corporation** in most cases — no flow-through election. The check-the-box flexibility is one of the main reasons sponsors pick the LLC over the exempted company for US-tax-sensitive structures: flow-through allows direct pass-through of income/loss, while a corporate election is useful for **blocker, GILTI, and Subpart F planning** ([`knowledge/cayman/cayman-llc.md`](knowledge/cayman/cayman-llc.md)).

## Watch-outs the corpus flags

- **Migration tax-residency consequences**: the corpus explicitly warns that migrating from a Delaware LLC has tax-residency and **Pillar Two** implications that need careful handling ([`knowledge/cayman/cayman-llc.md`](knowledge/cayman/cayman-llc.md)). For Cayman's Pillar Two response, see [`knowledge/cayman/tax.md`](knowledge/cayman/tax.md) (MCIT 2025 — QDMTT for in-scope groups, per [`knowledge/CROSS-JURISDICTIONAL-MAP.md`](knowledge/CROSS-JURISDICTIONAL-MAP.md)).
- **Default check-the-box treatment** may not be what the structure needs — if the existing Delaware LLC has elected corporate treatment, the Cayman successor needs an equivalent affirmative election; the default does not necessarily replicate the prior classification.
- **Member-managed default**: without an explicit manager-managed election in the LLC Agreement, every member may have management authority by default.
- **LLC Agreement drafting**: the entire structure rests on it; "sloppy drafting is fatal" per the corpus.
