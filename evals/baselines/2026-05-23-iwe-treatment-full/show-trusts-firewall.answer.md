## How Article 9 handles foreign forced-heirship

**Article 9 of the [Trusts (Jersey) Law 1984](knowledge/jersey/trusts/firewall.md)** — the "firewall" in its current form from the **Trusts (Amendment No. 4) (Jersey) Law 2006** — directs the Royal Court to apply **Jersey law alone** to questions of trust validity, settlor capacity, administration, trustees' powers, and beneficial rights, and to refuse effect to foreign rules that would constrain the settlor's freedom to settle (`knowledge/jersey/trusts/firewall.md`).

### The mechanics against forced-heirship

1. **Article 9(1) — headline rule.** Validity of the trust *and of any transfer or disposition into it*, capacity of the settlor, administration, and beneficial rights "shall be determined in accordance with the law of Jersey and no rule of foreign law shall affect such question."
2. **Article 9(2) — express disregard.** The Royal Court proceeds "without consideration of whether the trust avoids or defeats rights, claims, or interests conferred by any foreign law" and without regard to whether the foreign system recognises the trust concept at all. This is the operative line against *réserve héréditaire*, *légitime*, and German *Pflichtteilsergänzung*-style clawback.
3. **Article 9(3A) — no renvoi.** Jersey conflict-of-laws rules (other than Article 9 itself) do not apply, so a heir cannot argue Jersey choice-of-law sends the question back to the deceased's domicile.
4. **Article 9(4) — foreign judgments.** A foreign judgment inconsistent with Article 9 — including one setting aside the transfer into the trust as a fraud on heirship — is **unenforceable in Jersey**.
5. **Donner et retenir ne vaut disapplied.** The customary rule against giving while retaining control is switched off for trust purposes, removing a structural attack vector that pairs with reserved-powers planning under Article 9A ([`knowledge/jersey/trusts/reserved-powers.md`](knowledge/jersey/trusts/reserved-powers.md)).

### Savings — what the firewall does *not* do

- **Article 9(2A)** preserves foreign rules on **ownership and formalities of transfer** (so the firewall cannot turn theft into title, nor override foreign real-estate transfer formalities), and does not validate testamentary dispositions invalid under the testator's domicile law.
- **Article 9(3)** preserves **Jersey's own *légitime*** where the settlor is **Jersey-domiciled** — a Jersey-domiciled settlor cannot use a Jersey trust to escape Jersey forced-heirship.

### Practical limits

The firewall binds the Royal Court and Jersey-situated assets — it does not bind foreign courts. So in practice:

- A foreign court with **in personam jurisdiction over a beneficiary** resident there may compel that beneficiary to act on trust assets, regardless of Article 9.
- **Assets located in the forced-heirship jurisdiction** may be reached directly by the foreign court.
- The deed must make an **express choice of Jersey law** (Article 4) for the firewall to engage; and clauses that re-import foreign succession rules by the back door can undercut it.
- A trust that fails the Article 2 existence test (a sham) is not protected — Article 9 protects trusts, not non-trusts.
- Jersey **public policy** is a residual override in egregious cases.
