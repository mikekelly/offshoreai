## The Friday-afternoon convention

The corpus confirms that **Royal Court "passing" of Jersey real-property transfers happens on Friday afternoon** as a matter of standing practice. The procedure is:

- The contract is **passed before the Royal Court** (the [Samedi Division](knowledge/jersey/legal-system/royal-court.md) is the day-to-day civil forum) — title transfers at that Court act, not at signature;
- **Two Jurats sit to witness** the passing alongside the Bailiff / Deputy Bailiff;
- The passing is then **registered in the Public Registry**;
- This applies to freehold sales, hypothecs, and **leases of 9+ years** (*contrat héréditaire*); shorter commercial leases avoid Court passing.

(See `knowledge/jersey/property/commercial-leases.md` for the Friday-afternoon + two-Jurats statement, `knowledge/jersey/property/flying-freeholds.md` for the same convention on freehold passings, and `knowledge/jersey/contract/force-majeure.md` noting that historic Court closures on Fridays generated genuine force-majeure arguments because conveyancing cannot complete without the passing.)

## Why "Friday afternoon" specifically — corpus limitation

On the structural *why* of the procedure, the corpus is clear: contract passing is a **Norman customary law inheritance**, where real-property transfer is a public Court act rather than a private exchange — giving certainty about title transitions and public visibility of who owns what (`knowledge/jersey/property/contract-passing.md` — "Why this procedure exists").

But on **why that public act is timetabled to a Friday afternoon** (as opposed to general weekday scheduling, banking settlement cycles, or historical Court roster reasons), **the corpus does not give a substantive explanation** — it records the Friday-afternoon convention as a practice fact only. There is a mild tension in the corpus on this: `contract-passing.md` says passings happen "most weekdays" in the Samedi Division generally, while the property-specific files (`commercial-leases.md`, `flying-freeholds.md`) and `force-majeure.md` all describe the Friday-afternoon hearing as the operative convention for *property* contract passings.

## Caveats

All four cited files are **draft** status — the substantive convention is recorded but has not been editorially verified to `review` / `stable`. Treat the Friday-afternoon convention as load-bearing practice information, but if the timing matters to your transaction (e.g. for a force-majeure or delayed-completion argument), verify the current Royal Court sittings list with your Jersey advocate or Judicial Greffe.
