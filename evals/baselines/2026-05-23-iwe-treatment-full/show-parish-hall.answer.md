## What it is

A **Parish Hall Enquiry** is a pre-court forum presided over by a **Centenier** — an elected, unpaid honorary police officer — of the parish where the alleged offence took place. It's the route by which most routine Jersey motoring offences (and some other minor offences) are resolved without ever reaching the Magistrate's Court. There is no direct UK equivalent — it has features of a fixed-penalty notice and an English court but is its own thing (`knowledge/jersey/road-traffic/parish-hall-enquiries.md`).

The Centenier reviews the evidence, decides whether to charge, and can **caution**, **fine up to £200 per offence**, or **refer to Magistrate's Court**. They can only fine or caution if you **admit** the offence and **accept** the penalty — if you dispute it, the case must go to court (`knowledge/jersey/road-traffic/parish-hall-enquiries.md`).

## When your case goes there

Typical Parish Hall offences include:

- Minor speeding (under 15 mph excess)
- Mobile-phone offences
- Failing to display an insurance disc
- Some traffic-light and minor careless-driving cases
- Parking offences

The case is **excluded** from Parish Hall — and goes straight to Magistrate's Court — for: speeding 15 mph+ over the limit; drink- or drug-driving (any reading); dangerous driving (Article 23 RTJL); causing death or serious injury by driving; driving while disqualified; failing to stop/report (serious cases); any offence carrying mandatory disqualification; or anything where the £200 ceiling is inadequate (`knowledge/jersey/road-traffic/parish-hall-enquiries.md`).

## Your rights

You have the right to be told the alleged offence in advance, to bring a friend / family member / advocate, to decline to admit the offence, and crucially to **demand the case be heard at Magistrate's Court instead** — at any point, before or after the Parish Hall outcome. You can also appeal a Parish Hall outcome by requesting a Magistrate's Court re-hearing (`knowledge/jersey/road-traffic/parish-hall-enquiries.md`).
