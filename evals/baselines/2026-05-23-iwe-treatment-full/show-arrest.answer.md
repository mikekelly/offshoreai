# Your rights if arrested in Jersey

Jersey criminal procedure is governed by the **Police Procedures and Criminal Evidence (Jersey) Law 2003** (PPCE 2003) and its Codes of Practice. The rules below apply to arrest by both States Police and Honorary Police (Centeniers etc. have full PPCE arrest powers).

## At the point of arrest

The officer must tell you that you are under arrest, state the offence, identify themselves, and give you the **caution**:

> "You are not obliged to say anything unless you wish to do so, but what you say may be put into writing and given in evidence."

Force used in effecting the arrest must be reasonable, proportionate and necessary; failure to follow the arrest formalities can render the arrest unlawful and lead to exclusion of evidence or civil claims for wrongful arrest. [`knowledge/jersey/crime-fraud/arrest.md`](knowledge/jersey/crime-fraud/arrest.md)

## At the police station — your headline rights

1. **Free legal advice from a solicitor or advocate** — available 24/7 through the duty-solicitor scheme, confidential, and normally pre-interview. Delay is only permissible in very serious cases on specific statutory grounds authorised at superintendent level or above. If you initially decline but change your mind during interview, **questioning must stop**.
2. **Have one person told of your arrest and location** — at public expense, as soon as practicable. Can be delayed in serious cases on inspector-level authorisation.
3. **Consular notification** if you are a foreign national.
4. **Right to remain silent** — you are not obliged to answer questions; Jersey is narrower than post-2003 England on drawing adverse inferences from silence at police-station interview alone.
5. **Custody record** — entitled to view it on request during detention and to a copy on release. It is critical evidence in any later challenge.
6. **Written notice of rights** in your language, given by the custody officer on arrival.
7. **Free professional interpreter** if you don't speak English well enough; interview cannot start without it (absent serious-harm urgency).
8. **Appropriate adult** if you are under 18, mentally vulnerable, or have significant learning difficulties — interview cannot proceed without them (defined urgent exceptions aside).
9. **Medical attention** if unwell, injured, or needing medication.
10. **Adequate physical conditions** — including a continuous 8 hours' rest in any 24-hour period, meals, water, toilet access. [`knowledge/jersey/crime-fraud/rights-at-police-station.md`](knowledge/jersey/crime-fraud/rights-at-police-station.md)

## Interview rules

Interviews are **audio-recorded** under Code E; the caution is given at the start of each session; oppression (intimidation, raising voice, prolonged questioning) is prohibited; you must be fit to be interviewed; a solicitor may be present if requested. [`knowledge/jersey/crime-fraud/rights-at-police-station.md`](knowledge/jersey/crime-fraud/rights-at-police-station.md)

## How long you can be held without charge

| Stage | Limit | Authorised by |
|---|---|---|
| Initial detention | **24 hours** | Custody officer |
| First extension (serious offences) | up to **36 hours** | Inspector or higher |
| Further detention by warrant | up to **96 hours total** | Royal Court (Magistrate) |
| Beyond 96 hours | **charge or release** required |

Detention beyond these limits is unlawful and grounds for habeas corpus / wrongful imprisonment proceedings. [`knowledge/jersey/crime-fraud/rights-at-police-station.md`](knowledge/jersey/crime-fraud/rights-at-police-station.md)

## Practical advice from the corpus

Stay calm, identify yourself truthfully, don't resist physically (challenge unlawful arrest through later channels), **ask for a solicitor immediately**, don't answer questions beyond basic identifying information before legal advice, don't sign anything without advice, and use your phone call. Read the custody record carefully and keep the notice of rights and your copy of the custody record. [`knowledge/jersey/crime-fraud/arrest.md`](knowledge/jersey/crime-fraud/arrest.md), [`knowledge/jersey/crime-fraud/rights-at-police-station.md`](knowledge/jersey/crime-fraud/rights-at-police-station.md)
