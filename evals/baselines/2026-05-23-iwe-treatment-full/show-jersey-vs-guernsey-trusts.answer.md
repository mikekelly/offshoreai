Both are siblings — modernised Norman-customary trust regimes with statutory firewalls, settlor reserved powers, non-charitable purpose trusts, perpetual duration, *Schmidt v Rosewood* beneficiary information rights, Royal Court supervision, and final appeal to the Privy Council. For most cross-border family-wealth structures they are **competitive substitutes**, and the choice often turns on sponsor familiarity, administrator location, and existing service-provider relationships rather than legal substance (`knowledge/guernsey/trust-law-differences-from-jersey.md`).

The real differentiators are these.

### Lean Jersey when…

- **Maximum forced-heirship / matrimonial-claim defence is the dominant concern.** Jersey's **Article 9(4)** of the Trusts (Jersey) Law 1984 expressly renders unenforceable in Jersey any foreign judgment inconsistent with the firewall — including judgments based on forced-heirship, matrimonial-property, or anti-trust foreign rules. Guernsey's **s.14 of TGL 2007** is comparable in concept but **less explicit on foreign-judgment non-recognition**; sophisticated defensive structures continue to prefer Jersey for the explicit statutory language (`knowledge/jersey/trusts/firewall.md`; `knowledge/guernsey/trust-law-differences-from-jersey.md`).
- **You may need to unwind a mistaken trustee or settlor decision.** Both jurisdictions have a statutory mistake jurisdiction wider than English law post-*Pitt v Holt*, but Jersey's **Articles 47B–47H regime** (inserted by Trusts (Amendment No. 6) (Jersey) Law 2013) has substantially deeper case-law development — notably the *Re S & T Trusts* [2015] JRC259 three-limb test (`knowledge/jersey/trusts/article-47-set-aside.md`). Guernsey codified a parallel mistake regime in TGL 2007, but with less developed jurisprudence (`knowledge/guernsey/trust-law-differences-from-jersey.md`).
- **You want a comprehensive, expressly-listed reserved-powers menu.** Jersey's **Article 9A** sets out an explicit (and open-ended) list of permissible reserved powers — revocation, variation, advancement, direction, beneficiary appointment, change of proper law, restriction of trustee discretion, etc. — with an express statutory confirmation that a trustee following such a direction does not commit a breach (Article 9A(3)) and that the settlor is not thereby a trustee (Article 9A(3A)) (`knowledge/jersey/trusts/reserved-powers.md`). Guernsey has substantively similar provisions, but the explicit Jersey menu is often preferred for US-grantor-trust planning and family-office structures.
- **You want to combine the trust with a JPF.** If the surrounding structure includes a Jersey Private Fund, keeping the trust in Jersey aligns the administrator, regulator, and counsel (`knowledge/CROSS-JURISDICTIONAL-MAP.md`).

### Lean Guernsey when…

- **You need a trustee or PTC structure built on a cell company.** Guernsey **invented the PCC (1997)** and is the only Channel Island with an **ICC** (Incorporated Cell Company) as well — useful for multi-family-PTC platforms or segregated-trustee arrangements (`knowledge/CROSS-JURISDICTIONAL-MAP.md`).
- **The combined fund vehicle is a PIF.** The Guernsey Private Investment Fund pairs naturally with a Guernsey trust where the rest of the structure is GFSC-supervised (`knowledge/CROSS-JURISDICTIONAL-MAP.md`).
- **Image rights are part of the family's commercially-valuable assets.** Guernsey runs the world's only **Image Rights Register**, and pairing it with a Guernsey trust keeps the assets and the trustee in one jurisdiction (`knowledge/CROSS-JURISDICTIONAL-MAP.md`).

### Roughly equivalent

- **Confidentiality / beneficial-ownership reporting** — both private trusts with non-public BO registers accessible to specified authorities.
- **EU / UK marketing context for any fund overlay** — both NPPR-route jurisdictions.
- **Trust company regulation** — Jersey TCBs under Financial Services (Jersey) Law 1998; Guernsey under the Regulation of Fiduciaries Law 2000; broadly equivalent (`knowledge/guernsey/trust-law-differences-from-jersey.md`).
