## The four are siblings, not substitutes

All four create **statutorily ringfenced cells inside one corporate envelope**; they diverge on (a) whether each cell is its own legal entity and (b) which industry the regime is *built around*. Guernsey invented the form in 1997 (PCC), Cayman copied in 1998 (SPC), Bermuda in 2000 (SAC), and Guernsey then evolved the second-generation ICC. ([`knowledge/guernsey/protected-cell-company.md`](knowledge/guernsey/protected-cell-company.md); [`knowledge/cayman/segregated-portfolio-company.md`](knowledge/cayman/segregated-portfolio-company.md); [`knowledge/bermuda/segregated-accounts-company.md`](knowledge/bermuda/segregated-accounts-company.md); [`knowledge/guernsey/incorporated-cell-company.md`](knowledge/guernsey/incorporated-cell-company.md))

## When to use each

### Bermuda SAC — insurance, ILS, rent-a-captive
Reach for the SAC when the underlying activity is **insurance-shaped**: corporate captive housing multiple lines (D&O, property, BI) in one BMA-licensed entity; a repeat **ILS / cat-bond** issuer where each series sits in its own account, bankruptcy-remote at the series level; or a **rent-a-captive** provider delivering captive economics to third parties. Bermuda's ILS market is the world's largest and SACs are central to it; the BMA pre-vets the SAC and the underlying activity. ([`knowledge/bermuda/segregated-accounts-company.md`](knowledge/bermuda/segregated-accounts-company.md); [`knowledge/CROSS-JURISDICTIONAL-MAP.md`](knowledge/CROSS-JURISDICTIONAL-MAP.md))

### Cayman SPC — multi-strategy funds and general-purpose segregation
The SPC is the **default fund-side cell vehicle**: multi-strategy hedge funds (equity L/S + credit + macro in separate portfolios), securitisation conduits issuing multiple bond series, family-office multi-strategy pools, and insurance segregation within the Cayman insurance market. Used wherever the sponsor wants one CIMA-registered entity, one board, and portfolio-level economics. The corpus notes Cayman has **no statutory ICC equivalent** — if you need each cell to be its own company in Cayman you must form separate companies. ([`knowledge/cayman/segregated-portfolio-company.md`](knowledge/cayman/segregated-portfolio-company.md))

### Guernsey PCC — captive insurance (the original), Europe-proximate funds
The PCC is the **original 1997 invention** and remains Guernsey's workhorse. Reach for it for: **captive insurance programmes** (Guernsey is Europe's largest captive jurisdiction by vehicle count, and the PCC was built for this), multi-class / multi-strategy Guernsey funds, securitisation conduits, and any sponsor wanting a single-entity cell vehicle in a European-time-zone IFC with NPPR access. Comparable in substance to Cayman SPC / Bermuda SAC; differentiated by Guernsey's captive depth and (for Europe-facing structures) NPPR routing. ([`knowledge/guernsey/protected-cell-company.md`](knowledge/guernsey/protected-cell-company.md); matrix at [`knowledge/CROSS-JURISDICTIONAL-MAP.md`](knowledge/CROSS-JURISDICTIONAL-MAP.md))

### Guernsey ICC — when each cell must look like a real company
The ICC is the upgrade you reach for when **single legal personality is the binding constraint**. Use it when:
- **Foreign-court recognition risk is acute** — each ICC cell is separately incorporated and unambiguously a distinct legal entity, eliminating residual cross-cell pooling risk;
- **Counterparties (prime brokers, custodians, regulated counterparties) insist on dealing with "a company"**, not "a cell of a company";
- **Cell migration** is contemplated — an ICC cell can leave the ICC and continue as a standalone (or join another ICC) because it already has its own corporate personality;
- A **specific regulatory regime** treats cells differently from companies and the sponsor wants company-level treatment.

The trade-off is slightly higher administrative cost (separate registrations and filings per cell) and more governance overhead. The corpus is explicit that **Guernsey is alone in offering both PCC and ICC** — if the structure requires separate-personality cells, Guernsey is the only choice in this set. ([`knowledge/guernsey/incorporated-cell-company.md`](knowledge/guernsey/incorporated-cell-company.md))

| Use case | First-pick vehicle |
|---|---|
| Single-parent captive, multi-line | **Bermuda SAC** *or* **Guernsey PCC cell** |
| Commercial cat reinsurance, $100m+ | **Bermuda** (Class 4 + SAC/SPI for ILS) |
| ILS / sidecar / cat-bond series | **Bermuda SAC** |
| European-parent captive | **Guernsey PCC** |
| Multi-strategy hedge fund | **Cayman SPC** |
| Multi-series securitisation conduit | **Cayman SPC** or **Guernsey PCC** |
| Family-office multi-strategy pool | **Cayman SPC** or **Guernsey PCC** |
| Fund/captive where counterparties require separate-company status per cell | **Guernsey ICC** |
| Cell migration planned | **Guernsey ICC** |

## Common pitfalls (all four)

The risk profiles are similar: **sloppy cell identification in contracts**, undocumented inter-cellular dealings, common counterparties exposed across multiple cells, and foreign-court non-recognition of statutory segregation in specific jurisdictions. The PCC/SAC/SPC depend on the segregation being respected by the foreign forum; the ICC's separate-personality construction is the structural answer to that risk. ([`knowledge/cayman/segregated-portfolio-company.md`](knowledge/cayman/segregated-portfolio-company.md); [`knowledge/bermuda/segregated-accounts-company.md`](knowledge/bermuda/segregated-accounts-company.md); [`knowledge/guernsey/protected-cell-company.md`](knowledge/guernsey/protected-cell-company.md))
