# Marketing a Jersey AIF into Germany via NPPR

## The route at a glance

Jersey is a non-EU jurisdiction, so a Jersey AIF cannot use the EU-wide AIFMD passport (the proposed third-country passport extension was never implemented). The practical route into Germany is **country-by-country notification under Germany's National Private Placement Regime (NPPR)**, addressed to **BaFin** under **§330 KAGB**, after which the AIF can be marketed to **German professional investors only** (`knowledge/jersey/funds/aifmd-nppr.md`; `knowledge/jersey/use-cases/fund-counsel/aifmd-nppr-registration.md`).

## What you need in place before notifying BaFin

1. **JFSC-registered AIFM** under Jersey's AIFMD-equivalent regime, demonstrating substantive AIFMD-equivalent compliance: capital adequacy, risk management, conflicts of interest, liquidity management for open-ended AIFs, transparency to investors (annual report, periodic reporting), and regulatory reporting to the JFSC (`knowledge/jersey/funds/aifmd-nppr.md`; `knowledge/jersey/use-cases/fund-counsel/aifmd-nppr-registration.md`).
2. **AIFMD-equivalent depository** arrangements (custody, oversight, cash-flow monitoring). For Jersey AIFs marketed to the EU this typically means a Jersey JFSC-licensed depository often combined with full AIFMD depository functions elsewhere — Jersey allows lighter depository for non-EU-marketed funds, but EU-marketed AIFs need the equivalent set (`knowledge/jersey/funds/aifmd-nppr.md`; `knowledge/jersey/use-cases/fund-counsel/aifmd-nppr-registration.md`).
3. **Cooperation arrangements** — Jersey's tax-transparency and AML credentials underpin the EU's continued willingness to accept NPPR from Crown Dependencies, which AIFMD II preserves (`knowledge/frontier/aifmd-ii-april-2026.md`).

## Germany-specific NPPR conditions (§330 KAGB)

The corpus flags Germany (with France) as one of the stricter NPPRs. Distinctive features:

- **§330 KAGB** is the governing provision;
- **Local fact-sheet** required;
- **Local distributor** sometimes required;
- **Post-acceptance reporting** obligations to BaFin (`knowledge/jersey/use-cases/fund-counsel/aifmd-nppr-registration.md`).

The general notification mechanics are: identify BaFin as competent authority, prepare the German-specific notification package, confirm BaFin recognises Jersey's AIFM regime as equivalent, submit, and await clearance — timelines vary (`knowledge/jersey/use-cases/fund-counsel/aifmd-nppr-registration.md`).

## What AIFMD II changes (effective 16 April 2026)

Directive (EU) 2024/927 preserves NPPR access for Crown Dependencies but adds two operational uplifts a Jersey AIFM marketing into Germany must absorb:

- **Expanded investor disclosures** — pre-marketing and ongoing, covering liquidity management, sustainability factors (SFDR interaction), and loan-originating-fund disclosures where applicable;
- **Enhanced Annex IV reporting** to ESMA via competent authorities, with new fields and more granular asset/risk reporting.

The JFSC is splitting Jersey's AIF Code into an **EU AIF Code** (aligned with AIFMD II for NPPR marketing) and a **UK AIF Code**, with the EU AIF Code expected to be finalised through 2026 (`knowledge/frontier/aifmd-ii-april-2026.md`).

## Reverse solicitation caveat

NPPR notification is required only for **active marketing**. Unsolicited investor approaches do not trigger NPPR, but you must be able to evidence genuine reverse solicitation — BaFin and peer authorities scrutinise the documentation closely (`knowledge/jersey/use-cases/fund-counsel/aifmd-nppr-registration.md`).
