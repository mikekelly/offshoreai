## What voisinage is

**Voisinage** is a Norman customary-law doctrine, preserved in Jersey but abandoned in most other modern legal systems, that governs the relationship between neighbouring landowners. It requires each owner to use their property in a way that does **not cause unreasonable harm** to neighbours — exceeding the ordinary inconveniences of neighbourhood, without adequate justification. It is *distinct* from English nuisance even though the outcomes often overlap (`knowledge/jersey/property/voisinage.md`).

Two features make it worth flagging up front:

- **It can apply without fault** (objective liability), unlike English nuisance in some formulations.
- **There is no closed list of "smell, smoke, noise" categories** — the doctrine captures a broader range of conduct, including vibrations, drainage and surface water, tree roots and overhanging branches, light obstruction, and inter-unit issues in apartments (`knowledge/jersey/property/voisinage.md`).

## Does it apply to your neighbour problem?

The corpus cannot answer that for your specific facts, but the **threshold test** it states is:

1. **Harm** that is objectively identifiable;
2. **Caused by** the defendant's use of their property;
3. **Exceeding** the ordinary inconveniences of neighbourhood;
4. **Without** adequate justification (`knowledge/jersey/property/voisinage.md`).

Two practical points worth knowing before you decide if it fits:

- **Planning permission does not exclude voisinage** — your neighbour having consent for what they're doing is not a complete answer. Regulatory compliance may be *evidence* of reasonable use, but it is not a shield (`knowledge/jersey/property/voisinage.md`).
- **A valid servitude** (a Jersey easement) is a different matter — voisinage does not override one, but *excessive use* of a servitude can still breach voisinage (`knowledge/jersey/property/voisinage.md`).

**Defences** your neighbour might raise include reasonable use, a pre-existing state of affairs (limited), your consent or acquiescence, statutory authority, or prescription (limited) (`knowledge/jersey/property/voisinage.md`).

## Practical next steps the corpus recommends

- **Document the interference** — logs, photographs, recordings.
- **Engage experts early** where the harm needs measuring (acoustic, environmental).
- Build a **correspondence trail** with the neighbour.
- **Mediate first** where possible — voisinage matters often resolve there.
- If it escalates, the **Royal Court** hears voisinage claims by Order of Justice, with site visits and expert evidence common (`knowledge/jersey/property/voisinage.md`).

The most-cited *pitfall* in the file is **assuming the English nuisance test applies wholesale** — engage a Jersey advocate familiar with voisinage rather than relying on English-law instincts (`knowledge/jersey/property/voisinage.md`).
