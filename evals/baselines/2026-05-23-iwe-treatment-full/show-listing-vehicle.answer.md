I'll check the cross-jurisdictional synthesis surface and the per-jurisdiction listing-vehicle files in parallel.# US-listed (NASDAQ) holdco — recommendation

**Default choice: Cayman Exempted Company** — the standard US-listing vehicle (the "Alibaba pattern"). The corpus's cross-jurisdictional decision framework for "US-listed tech holdco" points to Cayman Exempted as the default (`knowledge/CROSS-JURISDICTIONAL-MAP.md`). The Cayman Exempted Company is expressly used as a **pre-IPO holdco** form and has the flexible capital structure (multi-class shares, par or no-par, redeemable shares, no UK-style capital maintenance regime) that US listings require (`knowledge/cayman/exempted-company.md`).

## Brief comparison

| Vehicle | NASDAQ fit |
|---|---|
| **Cayman Exempted** | Market standard for US-listed tech/holding companies. The dominant non-US listing-vehicle domicile on NYSE/NASDAQ. 1–2 day formation, flexible share classes, no AGM requirement, 20-year (renewable to 30) tax undertaking available (`knowledge/cayman/exempted-company.md`). |
| **Bermuda Exempted** | "Routinely accepted as a listing domicile" on NYSE / NASDAQ / LSE — most natural where the underlying business is **(re)insurance or ILS**, which is Bermuda's specialism. BMA pre-vetting at incorporation adds friction but delivers regulatory credibility (`knowledge/bermuda/exempted-company.md`). The cross-jurisdictional decision framework points to Bermuda specifically for "insurance / reinsurance listing" (`knowledge/CROSS-JURISDICTIONAL-MAP.md`). |
| **BVI BC** | Cheaper and faster, but the cross-jurisdictional map flags "some exchange-acceptance limits" for listing vehicles — generally used for "lower-cost smaller listings" rather than a NASDAQ main-market float (`knowledge/CROSS-JURISDICTIONAL-MAP.md`; `knowledge/bvi/business-company.md`). |

## How to choose

- **Generic tech / operating-business holdco listing on NASDAQ → Cayman Exempted.** This is the well-trodden path; US underwriters, SEC counsel, and transfer agents all have settled playbooks for it.
- **The business is (re)insurance or ILS → Bermuda Exempted** is the natural fit because Bermuda is the deep (re)insurance domicile and NASDAQ accepts it routinely.
- **BVI BC** is generally not the first-choice NASDAQ vehicle given the noted exchange-acceptance friction; consider it only for smaller / specialty listings where cost dominates.

## Pillar Two flag (applies to all three)

If the listed group will have **consolidated revenue ≥ EUR 750m**, all three jurisdictions now have in-force domestic top-up regimes for in-scope groups: Cayman MCIT 2025, BVI MTT 2025, Bermuda CIT 2023 (effective 1 Jan 2025) — Bermuda's 15% CIT on in-scope multinationals is explicitly documented (`knowledge/bermuda/exempted-company.md`; `knowledge/CROSS-JURISDICTIONAL-MAP.md`). Below the threshold, the historic no-direct-tax position continues in each.
