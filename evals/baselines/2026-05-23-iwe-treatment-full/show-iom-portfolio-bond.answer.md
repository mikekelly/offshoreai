## Why a UK HNW investor uses an Isle of Man portfolio bond

A portfolio bond is a **single-premium investment wrapped in a life-assurance policy**, issued by an IoM insurer and holding a wide range of underlying assets (funds, equities, fixed income, alternatives) — with the investment strategy typically directed by the policyholder's chosen investment manager (`knowledge/isle-of-man/life-assurance.md`).

For a UK-resident HNW investor, the principal attractions are:

- **The UK 5% rule (tax-deferred withdrawals).** Up to **5% of the original premium can be withdrawn each year, tax-deferred, for 20 years**. The chargeable event arises only on final encashment, when it is taxed as deferred income. **Top-slicing relief** then reduces the effective rate for higher- and additional-rate payers. The governing UK regime is **Chapter 9 of Part 4 ITTOIA 2005**. This delivers materially better cash-flow tax treatment than ordinary investment (`knowledge/isle-of-man/life-assurance.md`).
- **A specialist, well-regulated home jurisdiction.** The IoM is the world's leading offshore life-assurance jurisdiction, with major UK groups (Quilter, Standard Life International, Royal London, Canada Life International, Utmost) writing through IoM subsidiaries; the IOMFSA supervises under the **Insurance Act 2008**, and the IoM holds **EU Solvency II equivalence** — the only offshore jurisdiction other than Bermuda to do so (`knowledge/isle-of-man/life-assurance.md`; `knowledge/CROSS-JURISDICTIONAL-MAP.md`).
- **Investment flexibility inside the wrapper.** Wide asset universe, discretionary-manager designation, and surrender flexibility (subject to product penalties) — used alongside ISAs and pensions as a long-term wrapper (`knowledge/isle-of-man/life-assurance.md`).
- **Estate-planning utility.** The life-assurance wrapper provides estate-planning efficiency in some jurisdictions; combined with trust structuring it is a recurring component of HNW family-wealth plans — including the cross-jurisdictional family-wealth pattern in this corpus (`knowledge/CROSS-JURISDICTIONAL-MAP.md`).

### Pitfalls to flag

- **Adviser suitability** — IoM bonds are sold through intermediaries; not appropriate for non-sophisticated retail investors.
- **Product misclassification** in the policyholder's tax jurisdiction can break the expected treatment.
- **Solvency II equivalence** is a watch-list item (currently stable but reviewed periodically).
- AML/CFT exposure runs through the intermediary chain (`knowledge/isle-of-man/life-assurance.md`).
