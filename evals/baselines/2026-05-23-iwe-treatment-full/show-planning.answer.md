## Rear extension on a Jersey house — do you need planning permission?

**It depends on size and context.** Under the **Planning and Building (General Development) (Jersey) Order 2011** ("GD Order 2011"), a typical single-storey rear extension that stays within the Order's defined floor-area, depth, and height limits is **permitted development** — no application needed. A small rear extension (e.g. roughly 3-4m projection, single storey) on a standard Jersey dwelling will often fall within those limits. Anything beyond them — larger projection, two-storey, or close to a boundary — needs a **full planning application** under the **Planning and Building (Jersey) Law 2002** (`knowledge/jersey/planning/extensions-and-conversions.md`; `knowledge/jersey/planning/permitted-development.md`).

**Key carve-outs that make a full application mandatory regardless of size:**
- **Conservation area** — typically need full application even if within thresholds.
- **Listed building** — listed-building consent is always required (and is separate from planning permission).
- **Two-storey extension** — always requires application under current rules (`knowledge/jersey/planning/extensions-and-conversions.md`).

**Other things to budget for, even if planning is fine:**
- **Building approval** under the Building Bye-Laws 2007 is a separate consent — you need both.
- **Private restrictive covenants** in the title deed can prohibit works even where planning permits them (`knowledge/jersey/property/restrictive-covenants.md` is cross-referenced from the source file).
- If you're unsure, you can apply for a **Certificate of Lawful Development** to confirm PD status before building.

**Caveats.** The two files I rely on (`extensions-and-conversions.md`, `permitted-development.md`) are status `draft` — drafted but not yet editorially verified against the GD Order 2011 schedules. The exact GD Order thresholds (precise depth, floor-area, and height limits) are not enumerated in the corpus; before building, verify the current GD Order 2011 limits and any subsequent amendments directly, or obtain pre-application advice from the Planning Department.
