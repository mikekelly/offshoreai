## Recommendation: JPF, almost certainly

A **club deal** is the JPF's archetypal use case — the JPF concept file explicitly lists "**Single-LP** or **club deal** structures" alongside "**private equity** funds and **co-investment** vehicles" as the regime's default applications (`knowledge/jersey/funds/jersey-private-fund.md`).

### Why JPF fits the club-deal pattern

| Feature | JPF | Expert Fund |
|---|---|---|
| Investor cap | **50** | No specific cap |
| Investor qualification | Professional / eligible | Expert (typically US$100k+ / net-worth thresholds) |
| Authorisation | **~48 hours** | Days to weeks |
| Offering doc | Light PPM | Fuller offering memorandum |
| Ongoing supervision | DSP-substituted (light-touch) | More direct JFSC engagement |

Source: `knowledge/jersey/funds/jersey-private-fund.md`, `knowledge/jersey/funds/expert-fund.md`.

Club deals are typically a small group of co-investors (often <20, almost always <50) chasing a single target or a tight pipeline — that maps directly onto the JPF's **50-investor / professional-investor / 48-hour authorisation** envelope, with a JFSC-licensed administrator acting as **Designated Service Provider** carrying CDD and admin (`knowledge/jersey/funds/jersey-private-fund.md`). You'd most commonly structure it as a **Jersey limited partnership** (the most common JPF form).

### When to switch to Expert Fund instead

Per the Expert Fund file, choose Expert Fund if:
- The club is likely to **exceed 50 investors**;
- You need **wider expert-investor marketing latitude** across multiple jurisdictions; or
- You require **AIFMD NPPR compliance** for EU marketing at a scale where a more substantive offering memorandum is warranted (`knowledge/jersey/funds/expert-fund.md`).

For a true "club" — small, named, professional co-investors, single deal or short series — the JPF is the default and the Expert Fund is the upgrade path only if the headcount or marketing footprint outgrows it.

### Caveats

- Both source files are **status: `draft`** (last verified 2026-05-15) — the content is drafted but not stable-verified; confirm headline numbers (50-investor cap, 48-hour turnaround, expert-investor thresholds) against the JFSC's current JPF Guide and Codes of Practice before filing.
