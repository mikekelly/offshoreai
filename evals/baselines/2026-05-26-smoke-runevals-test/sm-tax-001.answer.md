**Headline Jersey rate.** A Jersey-incorporated trading company that isn't a "financial services company" (Article 123D), utility (Article 123C(3)), large corporate retailer (Article 123I), hydrocarbon trader (Article 123CAA(1)), cannabis trader (Article 123DA), or earning Jersey-land/property income is taxed at the **0% standard rate** under **Article 123C(2)** of the Income Tax (Jersey) Law 1961. Assuming your group is a straightforward trading business in one of those neutral categories, your Jersey corporate tax rate at company level is **0%**. ([`knowledge/jersey/tax/zero-ten.md`](knowledge/jersey/tax/zero-ten.md))

**Pillar Two does add on top.** Your group is above the **EUR 750m consolidated-revenue threshold**, so for accounting periods beginning on or after **1 January 2025** the Jersey **Multinational Corporate Income Tax (MCIT)** brings the effective rate on Jersey profits up to the GloBE **15% minimum**. Jersey's MCIT is structured as a **Qualified Domestic Minimum Top-up Tax (QDMTT)** — meaning Jersey collects the top-up itself rather than ceding it to the parent jurisdiction's Income Inclusion Rule (IIR). Jersey has implemented a Qualified IIR alongside the QDMTT but has **not** implemented a UTPR. ([`knowledge/jersey/international/pillar-two-mcit.md`](knowledge/jersey/international/pillar-two-mcit.md); [`knowledge/jersey/history/finance/trajectory.md`](knowledge/jersey/history/finance/trajectory.md))

**Net answer for you.** Statutory rate 0% under zero-ten, but because consolidated group revenue exceeds EUR 750m the effective rate on in-scope Jersey profits is **topped up to 15%** by MCIT 2025. Compliance: MCIT return, GloBE ETR calculation, GIR coordination with the parent jurisdiction. Substance Law 2019 obligations sit alongside MCIT and are unaffected by it.

**Caveats.**
- Both cited files are **`status: draft`** (not yet `stable` / `review`) — the substance reflects the 2025 regime as understood, but verify against Revenue Jersey and the GloBE primary sources before acting.
- This assumes the trading business doesn't fall into one of the 10% or 20% categories — if it's regulated under FSJL 1998 / BBL 1991 / IBL 1996 / CIFL 1988, or trades hydrocarbons, cannabis, or Jersey property, the pre-Pillar-Two rate is different (though MCIT still tops the effective rate to 15% for in-scope groups).
- Group structure matters for the EUR 750m test: it's measured at consolidated level over 2 of the preceding 4 years, with carve-outs for excluded entities and the substance-based income exclusion.
