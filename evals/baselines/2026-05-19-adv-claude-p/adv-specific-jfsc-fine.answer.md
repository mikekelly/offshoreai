## Answer

The corpus does not answer this question as posed — there is no Article-specified monetary cap for unauthorised trust company business in the materials I read. The corpus distinguishes two distinct regimes, neither of which gives the numeric maximum the question presumes:

**1. Unauthorised TCB is a criminal offence under Article 7, not a JFSC-imposed civil penalty.**
Article 7 of the Financial Services (Jersey) Law 1998 is the operative prohibition: a person "shall not" carry on financial service business (including the TCB class) in or from within Jersey without JFSC registration. The corpus describes the consequences of breach as a **criminal offence** carrying "substantial fines," imprisonment of up to **7 years** for individuals, civil consequences (voidable contracts, disgorgement) and JFSC public statements naming unauthorised providers — but it does **not** state a specific monetary cap for the Article 7 fine, and the prosecution is brought by the Attorney General, not the JFSC. (`jersey/financial-regulation/fsl-article-7.md`, `jersey/financial-regulation/fsl-part-5-enforcement.md`.)

**2. JFSC civil financial penalties sit under Article 24 — but apply to registered firms, not unauthorised ones, and Band 3 has no statutory upper limit.**
Article 24 enables the JFSC's civil financial penalty (CFP) regime, with amounts set under the Civil Financial Penalties (Financial Services Business) (Jersey) Order 2015. The corpus describes three bands — Band 1 typically £10k–£100k, Band 2 £100k–£500k, Band 3 £500k+ with **"no statutory upper limit"** — and notes the CFP regime is for **regulated entities** (registered firms) calibrated by entity type, seriousness and conduct. The corpus is explicit elsewhere that CFPs are imposed on regulated entities through the JFSC's enforcement powers, while unauthorised activity is handled through criminal prosecution referral. (`jersey/financial-regulation/fsl-article-24.md`, `jersey/financial-regulation/enforcement.md`, `jersey/financial-regulation/financial-services-jersey-law-1998.md`.)

So: the closest Article on penalty caps is **Article 24**, but it expressly has **no statutory upper limit** at Band 3 and is the wrong regime for *unauthorised* business. For unauthorised TCB the corpus points to **Article 7** criminal liability without quoting a numeric fine cap. If the question is asking for a specific JFSC-imposable maximum tied to an Article cap for *unauthorised* trust company business, **the corpus does not answer this question** — you'd need to verify against the consolidated text of the FSL 1998 itself (the sources link to https://www.jerseylaw.je/laws/unofficialconsolidated/Pages/13.225.aspx).
