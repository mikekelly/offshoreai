- The **use-case** entry (`jersey/use-cases/trust-officer/article-47-set-aside-for-mistake.md`, lines 148–160) lists three reasons drawn from the same Collas Crill summary as part of a broader "common reasons for refusal" list — and the M Settlement footnote is attached to two of them:
  1. The applicants could not show that the disposition **would not have been made but for the mistake** (Article 47's causation limb failed); and
  2. The relief sought was **partial rescission framed as a selective re-write** of the transaction — the Court was "wary of being asked to re-write history" and required any unwind to undo the impugned step in full, not selectively. (The third bullet — "commercial misjudgement rather than mistake about effect, consequences or fact" — is listed generally but not specifically tied to M Settlement in the corpus footnoting.)

So the **single ground the corpus most directly attributes to M Settlement** is the "no re-writing of history" point: the partial-rescission relief sought was a selective re-write of the transaction rather than a true unwinding, which the Royal Court would not grant. The causation-limb failure is the other ground the corpus footnotes to the same case.
