Under the post-2025 amendment regime (in force from 15 April 2026), rent increases on **periodic** residential tenancies are subject to a cap tied to the **Jersey Retail Prices Index (RPI)**. The increase must not exceed Jersey RPI for the relevant period — typically the 12 months ending at the most recently published RPI figure prior to the notice.

**How the cap is applied:**

- **Frequency**: at most **one increase per rolling 12-month period**, measured from the previous increase (not from the tenancy start). A second increase inside 12 months is unenforceable.
- **Notice**: minimum **2 months' written, signed notice** identifying the property/parties, stating the new rent, showing the RPI figure relied upon, and specifying the effective date.
- **Calculation discipline**: the landlord must identify the specific Jersey RPI figure used, show the arithmetic in the notice, and round consistently (typically to the nearest pound).
- **Narrow carve-out**: an above-RPI increase is only permitted where the current rent is **"significantly below market rent"** for comparable units — a narrow exception requiring comparable-lettings evidence and tested by the **Rent Tribunal**, which may permit, refuse, or set a lower figure.
- **Enforcement**: if a landlord imposes an above-RPI increase without Tribunal approval, the tenant is not required to pay the excess and refusal to pay the excess does not count as rent arrears.
- **Scope/exclusions**: applies to all periodic tenancies in existence on or entered after 15 April 2026 (including pre-existing ones — above-RPI review clauses in older agreements are unenforceable to that extent). The cap does **not** apply to **initial rent** at commencement of a new tenancy, nor to social housing, employment-linked accommodation, or lodging-house arrangements.

Fixed-term tenancies (≤3 years post-2025) may specify a review date but must still observe the once-yearly and RPI substance unless the "below market" carve-out applies.
