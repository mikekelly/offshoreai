## Why continuation funds and GP-led secondaries are growing so fast

Per the **2026 Global Private Equity Outlook survey** (cited via Dechert, Nov 2025), **46% of respondents** are now using GP-led secondaries or continuation vehicles to expedite LP distributions — **roughly double last year's figure** — with 51% of North American, 55% of APAC, and 43% of EMEA respondents planning further increases over 24 months. The drivers are structural:

- **Tough M&A and IPO exit markets** through 2022–2025 left assets stuck;
- **LPs demanding DPI** (distributed-to-paid-in capital) to recycle into new commitments;
- **GPs unwilling to sell trophy assets** at depressed valuations;
- **Secondaries market capital** is ready to deploy into precisely this structure.

A continuation fund lets the GP keep the trophy asset, hand existing LPs a roll-or-cash election, and bring in new secondary capital — solving all four pressures at once (`knowledge/frontier/continuation-funds-and-gp-led-secondaries.md`).

## Why Guernsey is winning the business

Guernsey has positioned aggressively with a regulatory package tuned to the product:

- **Single-asset continuation funds are not regulated as collective investment schemes in Guernsey** — a material simplification versus jurisdictions where the new vehicle has to clear full CIS authorisation;
- **Fast-track sign-off — Private Investment Fund (PIF) Route 1 next-business-day, 3 business days maximum** — competitive with (and arguably ahead of) Jersey's 48-hour JPF;
- **Three PIF routes** (licensed manager / family-or-private-relationship / qualifying private investors) give sponsors more structuring flexibility than the single-route JPF DSP model;
- **PCC and ICC vehicle forms** are particularly strong in Guernsey for multi-strategy or multi-asset continuation structures;
- **0% standard corporate tax** and a **substance-light profile** for the new continuation vehicle when the asset is the only thing changing hands;
- A **familiar fund-counsel ecosystem** (Carey Olsen, Ogier, Mourant, Walkers) coordinating with Guernsey Finance and the GFSC on marketing the niche.

Jersey competes on the same product line — JPFs with 48-hour JFSC authorisation under the DSP model — and Cayman remains dominant where US-LP capital is involved via the ELP. But the **single-asset-CV-not-a-CIS** point plus the three-route PIF flexibility is what the corpus identifies as Guernsey's marketed edge (`knowledge/frontier/continuation-funds-and-gp-led-secondaries.md`, `knowledge/guernsey/private-investment-fund.md`).
