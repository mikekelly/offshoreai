## What changes on 6 April 2026

UK carried interest moves from a **CGT-based regime** (28%, then 32% during the 2025–26 transition year) to an **income-tax-based regime with a partial exclusion** ([`knowledge/frontier/uk-carried-interest-reform-2026.md`](knowledge/frontier/uk-carried-interest-reform-2026.md)).

From 6 April 2026:

- Carry is treated as **income** for income-tax purposes, subject to **income tax + Class 4 NICs**.
- **Qualifying** carry benefits from a **72.5% multiplier** — only 72.5% of the carry is brought into charge. Effective rate for an additional-rate (45%) taxpayer is approximately **34.1%**.
- **Non-qualifying** carry attracts full income-tax + NICs at effective rates up to **47%**.

The qualifying / non-qualifying split turns on whether the partner is performing genuine investment-management activity and whether the fund meets specified characteristics. Final legislation **tightened** the qualifying definition vs prior drafts, though the government dropped some access conditions (e.g. minimum partner co-invest). Most established UK-resident partners at mature sponsors will default to **qualifying** status; new entrants, secondary-market participants, and non-traditional structures need closer analysis ([`knowledge/frontier/uk-carried-interest-reform-2026.md`](knowledge/frontier/uk-carried-interest-reform-2026.md)).

## What PE partners in the Crown Dependencies should do about it

### Residency planning for UK partners considering relocating

The three Crown Dependencies routes being actively marketed to PE partners:

- **Jersey 2(1)(e) HVR** — bespoke high-value-residency route with a specific tax-rate regime
- **Guernsey Open Market** — house-purchase-based residency; standard 20% tax
- **Isle of Man tax cap** — annual cap on individual income tax

The caveats are load-bearing:

- The **UK Statutory Residence Test** has stringent conditions for leaving.
- **Tail liability** is real — carry that economically accrued while UK-resident remains chargeable in the UK even if realised after departure.
- **Substance** in the destination must be genuine.
- The new legislation includes **specific anti-avoidance provisions around residence changes**.

(All from [`knowledge/frontier/uk-carried-interest-reform-2026.md`](knowledge/frontier/uk-carried-interest-reform-2026.md); doctrinal detail at [`knowledge/jersey/immigration-residency/high-value-residency.md`](knowledge/jersey/immigration-residency/high-value-residency.md) and [`knowledge/jersey/tax/high-value-residency-tax.md`](knowledge/jersey/tax/high-value-residency-tax.md).)

### GP-entity and fund-vehicle implications

- **Jersey JPF**, **Guernsey PIF**, and **Cayman ELP** vehicles are unchanged at the fund level — the new rules bite at the **GP entity** on carry allocation and vesting.
- Crown-Dependencies-domiciled GPs may become more attractive for **non-UK partners**, but UK look-through / situs rules continue to apply where UK-resident partners receive the economic benefit.
- **Documentation** of carry arrangements needs tightening to clearly establish qualifying status partner-by-partner.
- The **carry vs co-invest distinction** is now more acutely material — co-invest economics remain CGT-treated, so misclassification is meaningfully expensive ([`knowledge/frontier/uk-carried-interest-reform-2026.md`](knowledge/frontier/uk-carried-interest-reform-2026.md)).

### Pre-6 April crystallisation (mostly past tense now)

Through Q1 2026 many partners accelerated distributions, disposals, and restructurings to lock in the 32% CGT basis. If a partner has any remaining pre-6 April basis to crystallise it is now largely too late — but transitional questions about which side of the line specific receipts fall on remain live ([`knowledge/frontier/uk-carried-interest-reform-2026.md`](knowledge/frontier/uk-carried-interest-reform-2026.md)).

### Pitfalls to flag to clients

- Treating the 72.5% multiplier as a fixed cliff — the qualifying boundary is fact-sensitive
- Assuming UK departure shields against UK charges — the tail liability is real
- Misclassifying co-invest as carry or vice versa
- Sponsor documentation that doesn't clearly establish qualifying status per partner
- Cross-border interaction with French / German / Italian partners — peer-jurisdiction reforms diverge
