## Pillar Two and a Jersey TopCo over a UK operating business

**First gate — is the group in scope?** Pillar Two applies only to MNE groups with **consolidated revenue of €750m or more in at least two of the four preceding fiscal years** (`knowledge/jersey/tax/pillar-two.md`). Below that threshold, nothing changes: the Jersey TopCo stays on zero-ten (0% as a holding company), the UK OpCo pays UK corporation tax as usual, no top-up arises.

If the group is **in scope**, the analysis runs jurisdiction by jurisdiction:

### At the Jersey TopCo level

Jersey implements Pillar Two via the **Multinational Corporate Income Tax (Jersey) Law 2024**, effective for financial years commencing on or after **1 January 2025** (`knowledge/jersey/tax/pillar-two.md`). MCIT adopts **two** of the three OECD mechanisms — a **Qualified Domestic Minimum Top-up Tax (QDMTT)**, delivered as the **Domestic Top-up Tax (DTT)**, and a **Qualified Income Inclusion Rule (IIR)** for Jersey parents with low-taxed foreign subsidiaries — and pointedly **does not adopt the UTPR** (`knowledge/jersey/international/pillar-two-mcit.md`).

For a Jersey TopCo of an in-scope group, this means:

- **DTT (QDMTT)** charges a top-up to bring the **Jersey-jurisdictional ETR to 15%** on GloBE income booked in Jersey. The TopCo's holding-company income (dividend flows from the UK OpCo, treasury yield, capital gains routed via Jersey) would otherwise sit at 0% under zero-ten; MCIT collects the difference. The result is that the **Jersey tax becomes "an additional tax" on top of zero-ten — a top-up to bring Jersey ETR to 15%** (`knowledge/jersey/tax/pillar-two.md`).
- **IIR** could apply at the Jersey TopCo if the group has *low-taxed* foreign subsidiaries elsewhere. The UK OpCo is not such a subsidiary — UK corporation tax (currently 25% main rate) is above the 15% floor — so practically the IIR at the Jersey TopCo bites only on third-country low-taxed sub-subsidiaries, not on the UK OpCo.

A practical nuance: much of a holding company's income is **excluded dividend income** under GloBE (intra-group dividends from substantial shareholdings are generally excluded from GloBE income), so the DTT often hits residual items (treasury income, gains on disposals, certain non-dividend flows) rather than every pound that passes through the TopCo. The corpus describes the substance-based income exclusion (formulaic carve-out for payroll plus tangible assets, on a sliding scale) but does not work the dividend exclusion in detail (`knowledge/jersey/international/pillar-two-mcit.md`).

### At the UK OpCo level

The corpus does not analyse the UK-side Pillar Two mechanics directly. What it does say: because the Jersey DTT is a **QDMTT**, it is collected at source **before any other jurisdiction can** (`knowledge/jersey/international/pillar-two-mcit.md`). That is the point of Jersey's design — to capture the top-up domestically rather than ceding it to the parent's home country under IIR. In a Jersey-TopCo / UK-OpCo structure there is no UK parent above the Jersey TopCo, so the UK IIR question doesn't arise for Jersey income; UK OpCo income is taxed under ordinary UK corporation tax and is generally above the 15% floor in its own right.

### Net effect on the structure

- **Below €750m**: zero-ten continues; the Jersey TopCo is a 0% holding company; **no Pillar Two filing** (`knowledge/jersey/use-cases/founder-entrepreneur/pillar-two-impact.md`).
- **In scope**: the headline "0% Jersey" result disappears at the TopCo — Jersey-booked GloBE income tops up to **15%** via DTT; group ETR rises; compliance becomes materially more complex (GloBE income calculation, ETR analysis, MCIT return, GloBE Information Return coordination with the rest of the group) (`knowledge/jersey/use-cases/founder-entrepreneur/pillar-two-impact.md`).
- **What stays**: the non-tax reasons for a Jersey TopCo — corporate-law flexibility under CJL 1991, no CGT at investor level, UK-market listing acceptance, established substance infrastructure — are unaffected by MCIT (`knowledge/jersey/use-cases/founder-entrepreneur/pillar-two-impact.md`).

### Caveats

The three Pillar Two files above are all **status: draft** with `last_verified` between 15–17 May 2026 — fresh, but draft rather than stable. They are also generic-MNE-shaped; the corpus does **not** contain a worked example specifically for a Jersey-TopCo-over-UK-OpCo group with numbers (e.g. ETR calculation on a particular dividend flow), and does not analyse UK-side Pillar Two mechanics from a UK source. For a real engagement, the GloBE income / ETR calculation needs specialist tax advice and modelling.
