# Jersey vs Guernsey trust — when to pick which

The two regimes are **siblings**, both descended from Norman customary law and modernised by mid-20th-century statutes. They overlap heavily — discretionary, fixed-interest, charitable, purpose, and reserved-powers trusts all work in either; both allow perpetual duration; both have a Royal Court supervisory jurisdiction with final appeal to the Privy Council; both have full statutory firewalls; both maintain non-public BO registers. For most cross-border family-wealth structures they are **competitive substitutes**, and the choice often turns on sponsor familiarity, existing service-provider relationships, and administrator location rather than on substantive law.

That said, three doctrinal differences materially tip the choice.

## Lean **Jersey** when…

- **You need the strongest forced-heirship / matrimonial-regime defence.** Jersey's **Article 9** firewall — and in particular **Article 9(4)** rendering inconsistent foreign judgments unenforceable in Jersey — is more **explicitly worded** than Guernsey's **s.14** of the Trusts (Guernsey) Law 2007. The protection is broadly comparable in outcome, but sophisticated forced-heirship-defensive structures still tend to prefer Jersey for the explicit statutory language ([`knowledge/guernsey/trust-law-differences-from-jersey.md`](./knowledge/guernsey/trust-law-differences-from-jersey.md); [`knowledge/jersey/trusts/firewall.md`](./knowledge/jersey/trusts/firewall.md)).
- **You want the most developed mistake / *Hastings-Bass*-style set-aside case law.** Both jurisdictions have codified mistake regimes wider than English law post-*Pitt v Holt*, but Jersey's **Articles 47B–H** (introduced by the Trusts (Amendment No. 6) (Jersey) Law 2013, with the three-limb *S&T Trusts* test) has substantially more developed case law than Guernsey's parallel regime ([`knowledge/jersey/trusts/article-47-set-aside.md`](./knowledge/jersey/trusts/article-47-set-aside.md); [`knowledge/guernsey/trust-law-differences-from-jersey.md`](./knowledge/guernsey/trust-law-differences-from-jersey.md)).
- **You're combining a trust with a Jersey TopCo, a JPF, or a tokenisation play.** Jersey carries the deeper trust-company industry and the trust will sit naturally alongside other Jersey product (CJL 1991 TopCo, JPF speed-to-market, the tokenisation infrastructure) ([`knowledge/CROSS-JURISDICTIONAL-MAP.md`](./knowledge/CROSS-JURISDICTIONAL-MAP.md)).
- **You need broadly stronger Article 9A reserved-powers framing.** Both regimes are equivalent in *outcome* on reserved powers, but Jersey's **Article 9A** menu is more explicitly enumerated (revocation, advancement, amendment, directive, beneficiary appointment, change of proper law, etc.) and Article 9A(3A) confirms a settlor reserving powers does not thereby become a trustee ([`knowledge/jersey/trusts/reserved-powers.md`](./knowledge/jersey/trusts/reserved-powers.md)).

## Lean **Guernsey** when…

- **You want a trustee structure using a cell company.** Guernsey invented the PCC (1997) and also offers the **ICC** — the ICC is not available in Jersey. For multi-family-office trustee businesses segregating client trusts into cells, Guernsey is the home market ([`knowledge/CROSS-JURISDICTIONAL-MAP.md`](./knowledge/CROSS-JURISDICTIONAL-MAP.md)).
- **The structure pairs naturally with Guernsey-specific product.** A **PIF** (3 routes, 1-business-day) on the fund side, the **Image Rights Register** (only in the world) for commercially-valuable personalities, **captive insurance** (Europe's largest by vehicle count), or **continuation-fund** infrastructure — these all sit naturally with a Guernsey trust ([`knowledge/CROSS-JURISDICTIONAL-MAP.md`](./knowledge/CROSS-JURISDICTIONAL-MAP.md)).
- **Sponsor / administrator familiarity sits in Guernsey.** The two regimes are equivalent enough that an existing Guernsey trustee relationship is itself usually a sufficient reason to stay in Guernsey rather than restructure across the channel.

## What the choice does *not* turn on

- **EU NPPR / AIFMD position** — both Crown Dependencies use NPPR equivalently ([`knowledge/CROSS-JURISDICTIONAL-MAP.md`](./knowledge/CROSS-JURISDICTIONAL-MAP.md)).
- **Pillar Two exposure** — both have implemented parallel QDMTTs for in-scope groups; trust-vehicle level is generally below the EUR 750m threshold ([`knowledge/CROSS-JURISDICTIONAL-MAP.md`](./knowledge/CROSS-JURISDICTIONAL-MAP.md)).
- **Confidentiality / BO-register architecture** — both have private trust documents plus non-public BO registers accessible to specified authorities ([`knowledge/guernsey/trust-law-differences-from-jersey.md`](./knowledge/guernsey/trust-law-differences-from-jersey.md)).
- **Beneficiary information rights** — both apply *Schmidt v Rosewood* with statutory refinements that are broadly similar in result ([`knowledge/guernsey/trust-law-differences-from-jersey.md`](./knowledge/guernsey/trust-law-differences-from-jersey.md)).
