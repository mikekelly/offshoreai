Three vehicles, three different problems. Pick by the structural job you need done.

## Jersey trust (Trusts (Jersey) Law 1984)

The **default wealth-structuring vehicle**. Reach for it when:

- **Forced-heirship / matrimonial-regime defence is the priority.** The **Article 9 firewall** instructs the Royal Court to apply Jersey law to validity, capacity, administration, and beneficial-rights questions, and refuses to enforce foreign judgments inconsistent with it — including civil-law *réserve héréditaire*, community-property regimes, and foreign divorce orders against trust assets (`knowledge/jersey/trusts/firewall.md`).
- **The settlor wants to retain control without invalidity risk.** **Article 9A** lists permitted reserved powers (revocation, variation, advancement, direction over investment/distribution, appointment of beneficiaries, change of proper law) and confirms a trustee acting on a reserved-power direction does not breach trust (`knowledge/jersey/trusts/reserved-powers.md`). This is the workhorse for **US grantor-trust planning** and **family-office patriarchs** who want directive powers over a professional trustee.
- **General discretionary or fixed-interest family trust** with a normal beneficiary class. The Article 47B–H statutory mistake regime is also available if a settlement decision needs unwinding (cross-jurisdictional decision matrix at `knowledge/CROSS-JURISDICTIONAL-MAP.md`).

## Cayman STAR trust (Special Trusts (Alternative Regime) Act 1997)

A **purpose trust** — STAR's signature feature is that it can exist **without beneficiaries**, enforced by a mandatory **enforcer** (`knowledge/cayman/star-trusts.md`). Reach for it when:

- **Orphan SPV / structured-finance vehicle.** The classic STAR use case: a STAR trust holds the SPV's shares so it consolidates to no settlor and no beneficial owner — bankruptcy-remote, ownerless.
- **PTC (private trust company) ownership.** STAR holds the PTC shares above the family trusts.
- **Non-charitable purposes** that are not strictly charitable but need a trust wrapper — family-governance committees, multi-generational purpose vehicles, commercial-purpose holdings.
- **No defined beneficiary class** — STAR removes the *requirement* for one.

What STAR is **not for**: ordinary family wealth where you want identifiable beneficiaries with rights to information and distributions. Jersey (or a Cayman ordinary trust) is the right tool there.

## BVI VISTA trust (Virgin Islands Special Trusts Act 2003)

A **share-ownership trust** that **disengages the trustee from the underlying company's management** — explicitly disapplying the *Bartlett v Barclays Bank* duty to monitor and intervene in the company's affairs (`knowledge/bvi/vista-trusts.md`). Reach for it when:

- **Family-business succession.** A founder wants operating-company shares in trust, but wants chosen directors (often family) to run the business without trustee second-guessing. VISTA's **Office of Director Rules (ODRs)** preserve that control structure while legal title sits with the trustee.
- **Single-asset / illiquid holdings** (typically a designated BVI BC) where the classical trustee diversification / oversight duty is a mismatch for the commercial reality.
- **Professional trustee reluctance.** Without VISTA, many trustees won't take on operating-business shares because of management-oversight liability. VISTA gives them a statutory basis to accept the role.

VISTA's forced-heirship defence is **weaker than Jersey's** (`knowledge/bvi/vista-trusts.md`), and VISTA doesn't remove the beneficiary requirement (so it doesn't solve the orphan-SPV problem STAR solves).

## Quick decision matrix

| If the priority is… | Pick |
|---|---|
| Forced-heirship / cross-border defence + reserved powers | **Jersey** (Article 9 + 9A) |
| Orphan SPV / no-beneficiary purpose trust | **Cayman STAR** |
| Family business where the founder/chosen directors must run the company | **BVI VISTA** |
| Standard discretionary family wealth, identifiable beneficiaries | **Jersey** (default) |
| PTC ownership / family-governance committee | **Cayman STAR** |
| US-grantor-trust planning | **Jersey** (reserved powers) |

The three are also **stackable** — e.g., a Cayman STAR holding shares in a PTC that trustees a Jersey family trust; or a BVI VISTA designated company sitting inside a wider Jersey settlement (`knowledge/CROSS-JURISDICTIONAL-MAP.md` "Where should I form my trust?").
