## The current rules (as of May 2026)

Drug-driving in Jersey is prosecuted under the **Road Traffic (Jersey) Law 1956** on an **impairment basis only**. Police must prove the driver was actually unfit to drive properly because of drug use — on objective evidence such as poor vehicle control, failed field sobriety tests, and a blood/urine sample showing drugs. Jersey has **no statutory drug-concentration limits**, **no roadside drug-wipe powers**, and **no per se "over the limit" offence** — unlike the English specified-limit regime. The impairment offence is **substance-neutral**: it covers controlled drugs, prescription medication, and OTC drugs alike, and "I had a prescription" is not a defence to actual impairment.

Sentencing tracks drink-driving: mandatory disqualification (typically 12+ months for a first offence), fines up to £2,000, imprisonment up to 6 months, and licence endorsement. Refusing to give a sample is itself an offence.

## What's coming

In **February 2026**, States representatives proposed legislation that would introduce **statutory drug limits**, give police **roadside swab-testing powers**, create **per se offences**, and impose **mandatory disqualification** on conviction. The proposal is **not yet enacted** as of May 2026; potential introduction is during 2026 or 2027. Until then, the impairment-based regime continues to apply.
