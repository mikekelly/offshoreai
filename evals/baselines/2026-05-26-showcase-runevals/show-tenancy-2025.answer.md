On **15 April 2026** the **Residential Tenancy (Jersey) Amendment Law 2025** came into force, the most significant Jersey residential-tenancy reform in over a decade. The headline changes for tenancies entered on or after that date:

## Headline changes

- **Fixed terms capped at 3 years.** The initial fixed term may not exceed three years (down from the prior 9-year ceiling), and successive fixed terms are prohibited — at expiry, a tenancy ends or converts automatically to periodic.
- **Tenant exit on 1 month's notice.** A tenant may terminate **any** residential tenancy on one month's written notice, regardless of any fixed term — fixed-term "lock-in" is gone.
- **Landlord termination: justified or 12 months.** A landlord ending a periodic tenancy must either justify on a permitted ground (3 months' notice) or give **12 months' notice** for no-fault termination (up from the prior 3-month no-fault notice).
- **Rent caps.** Rent may be raised **once a year**, **capped at Jersey RPI**, with **at least 2 months' written notice** — unless rent is "significantly below market rent" (narrow carve-out, challengeable).
- **New Rent Tribunal.** An independent tribunal hears disputes over above-RPI increases, the once-yearly rule, the 2-month notice, and the market-rent carve-out. Eviction / breach disputes remain with the **Petty Debts Court**; the **Viscount** still enforces eviction orders.
- **Strengthened written-form rules.** Tenancy agreements must clearly identify all fees chargeable to the tenant; hidden fees are unenforceable and may constitute an offence.

## Transitional rules

- Pre-existing **periodic** tenancies convert immediately to the new regime (rent caps, 12-month no-fault notice).
- Pre-existing **fixed-term** tenancies continue under pre-amendment law until expiry; post-amendment rules apply thereafter.
- Successive-fixed-term clauses become unenforceable at expiry of the current term.

What did **not** change: Petty Debts Court jurisdiction over eviction, the condition-report regime, the Tenants' Deposit Scheme, the lodging-house exclusion, and the 9-year ceiling above which a lease becomes a Royal-Court-passed contract of lease.
