I'll pull the BVI files on the manager regime, fund options, and the underlying company vehicle.# BVI emerging-manager structure stack

The canonical BVI emerging-manager stack has three layers, plus a GP entity if you use an LP form. Each layer was deliberately built to sit beneath the full-licensed equivalents, and they are designed to compose.

## 1. Manager — BVI Approved Manager

Authorised under the **Investment Business (Approved Manager) Regulations 2012**: a fast-track, light-touch route below full SIBA Investment Business licensing. Designed precisely for start-up, smaller, or family-office managers — authorisation in weeks rather than months, lower initial/ongoing cost, fit-and-proper review, and recognised regulatory standing for marketing and counterparty acceptance. Can manage BVI Private, Professional, Incubator, Approved, or Public funds plus closely-connected non-BVI funds, subject to an AUM ceiling; once you grow past it you must upgrade to a full Investment Business licence. ([`knowledge/bvi/approved-manager.md`](knowledge/bvi/approved-manager.md))

## 2. Fund — Incubator or Approved Fund

Both regimes come from the **SIBA (Incubator and Approved Funds) Regulations 2015** and sit beneath the Private Fund / Professional Fund tier. Pick one — they are not interchangeable:

| | Incubator Fund | Approved Fund |
|---|---|---|
| Max investors | 20 | 20 |
| Min investment per investor | US$20,000 | None statutory |
| Max AUM | US$20m | US$100m |
| Time limit | 2 years (+1 year extension) | Permanent |
| Auditor | Not required | Not required |
| Administrator | Not typically required | Typically required |
| Designed for | Emerging manager building a track record | Stable small / family-office fund |

The Incubator is a launch pad — at the end of the runway you convert to Private / Professional / Public Fund or wind down. The Approved Fund can remain Approved indefinitely if you stay within the parameters. ([`knowledge/bvi/incubator-and-approved-fund.md`](knowledge/bvi/incubator-and-approved-fund.md))

## 3. Vehicle — BVI Business Company (and a GP if you go LP)

The **BVI Business Company** under the **BVI Business Companies Act 2004** is the default underlying form: hours to incorporate, low fees, no minimum capital, par-value or no-par-value shares, single shareholder/director permitted, no residence requirement, corporate directors allowed, registered-agent and registered-office obligations in BVI, BO information lodged with the agent (BOSS — not currently public). ([`knowledge/bvi/business-company.md`](knowledge/bvi/business-company.md))

The corpus's canonical start-up stack puts it together as: **GP entity (BVI BC) + Management entity (BVI BC) as Approved Manager + Fund (BVI BC or LP under the BVI Partnership Act)** — operating with full Approved Manager status from inception. If you use an LP fund form the GP is itself a BVI BC.

## Cross-cutting overlays

- **Economic substance** under the **Economic Substance (Companies and Limited Partnerships) Act 2018** is a separate compliance track for in-scope activities — directed-and-managed test, qualified employees, premises, annual substance return. Equity-holding companies get a reduced regime. Treat it as parallel to the regulatory regime, not subsumed by it.
- **AML/CFT** under the Proceeds of Criminal Conduct Act and the AMLR is substantive, not a paper formality — the Approved Manager carries real obligations.
- **AUM growth** is the principal upgrade trigger across the stack: past the Approved Manager ceiling you go to full Investment Business licensing; past US$20m or 2 years on Incubator you convert; past US$100m on Approved you convert.
