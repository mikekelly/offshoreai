## AIFMD II — what changes for a Jersey AIFM marketing into the EU under NPPR

**Bottom line:** NPPR access is preserved — Jersey continues to meet the EU's tax-transparency and AML criteria — but the operational obligations on a Jersey AIFM marketing into the EU under NPPR widen materially from 16 April 2026.

### Direct changes (apply to your Jersey AIFM)

1. **Expanded investor disclosures.** Pre-marketing and ongoing disclosures widen to cover **liquidity-management arrangements**, **sustainability factors** (interlocking with SFDR), and, where applicable, **loan-originating-fund** specifics. PPMs and ongoing transparency reports need refreshing for AIFMD II language (`knowledge/frontier/aifmd-ii-april-2026.md`).
2. **Enhanced Annex IV reporting to ESMA** via competent authorities — new data fields, more granular reporting on asset categories and risk exposures. First submissions under the new requirements are expected end-Q2 / early-Q3 2026 (`knowledge/frontier/aifmd-ii-april-2026.md`).
3. **Loan-originating funds** — a substantive new framework if your AIF originates credit; the rules are restrictive enough that credit-fund sponsors are actively reviewing domicile (`knowledge/frontier/aifmd-ii-april-2026.md`).
4. **Liquidity-management tools** — open-ended AIFs must build prescribed LMTs into constitutional documents (`knowledge/frontier/aifmd-ii-april-2026.md`).

### Indirect (but potentially the bigger commercial point) — delegation

AIFMD II tightens the rules on **EU AIFMs delegating portfolio management to third countries**. An EU AIFM that delegates to a Jersey entity faces enhanced substance and oversight obligations and cannot become a "letter-box entity." The counterintuitive consequence — flagged in Carey Olsen and Langham Hall commentary — is that a **direct Jersey AIFM marketing under NPPR** becomes comparatively more attractive than an EU-AIFM-with-CI-delegate model. Sponsors are revisiting that structural choice (`knowledge/frontier/aifmd-ii-april-2026.md`).

### Jersey-side regulatory response

The **JFSC AIF Code of Practice is being split** into an **EU AIF Code** (aligned with AIFMD II, for NPPR marketing into the EU) and a **UK AIF Code** (for marketing into the UK under the UK's post-Brexit NPPR). **The EU AIF Code is expected to be finalised through 2026** — watch for the final text (`knowledge/frontier/aifmd-ii-april-2026.md`). The underlying JFSC AIFMD-equivalent registration regime itself is unchanged (`knowledge/jersey/funds/aifmd-nppr.md`).

### What is not changing

- **NPPR access is preserved** — no Third Country Passport is being switched on; the country-by-country private-placement model continues, with the usual member-state variation (Germany / France stricter than some others) (`knowledge/jersey/funds/aifmd-nppr.md`, `knowledge/frontier/aifmd-ii-april-2026.md`).
- The **depository regime** position for EU-marketed Jersey AIFs (AIFMD-equivalent depository arrangements) is unchanged at the doctrinal level (`knowledge/jersey/funds/aifmd-nppr.md`).
