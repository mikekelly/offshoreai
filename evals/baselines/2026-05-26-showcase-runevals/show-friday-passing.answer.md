Let me search for the Friday-specific convention.Your Jersey property transfer happens **before the Royal Court** because Jersey real property cannot change hands by private deed — it must be "passed" as a Court act. This is a **Norman customary-law inheritance**: the Royal Court is the public repository of property records, and a public passing gives certainty about title transitions and visibility of who owns what (though the price stays private). [`knowledge/jersey/property/contract-passing.md`]

The **Friday-afternoon** slot is the conveyancing convention for the sitting that handles 9+ year leases, flying-freehold transfers, and other real-property contracts: **two Jurats sit to witness**, the contract is read or referenced, the Greffier records it, and the transfer takes effect at that moment. Your lawyers usually appear as attorneys under power of attorney, so you don't have to be there yourself. [`knowledge/jersey/property/commercial-leases.md`, `knowledge/jersey/property/flying-freeholds.md`]
