## The strategic split

Two real choices for an emerging sub-US$100m manager:

**Light-touch tier (BVI Approved or Guernsey PIF Route 1 or Jersey JPF).** If your LP base is friends-and-family / family office / a small set of professional LPs and you don't need an audit, the BVI Approved Fund is the cheapest and lightest — no mandatory auditor, no time limit (unlike the BVI Incubator), AUM ceiling sized for you (`knowledge/bvi/incubator-and-approved-fund.md`). The Approved Fund + BVI Approved Manager stack is the standard emerging-manager BVI configuration. If you want broader LP marketability and credibility, Jersey JPF (DSP-delegation model, 48h turnaround) or Guernsey PIF Route 1 (licensed-manager model, 1 BD) deliver similar speed with audited financials and stronger institutional perception — and Jersey/Guernsey give you AIFMD NPPR access into individual EU member states that BVI/Cayman do not.

**Institutional tier (Cayman ELP).** If you expect institutional LPs (endowments, pensions, fund-of-funds) and a US-touching investor base, they overwhelmingly expect a Cayman ELP — it's the global PE/VC default, with the most LPA contractual flexibility, deepest market familiarity, and tax-transparent treatment in the US/UK/EU (`knowledge/cayman/exempted-limited-partnership.md`). It's heavier (Private Funds Act 2020 registration with CIMA, audited statements, annual filings) and there is no EU passport — Cayman has no AIFMD-equivalent NPPR. Parallel Cayman ELP + Delaware LP structures are routine where you have mixed US / non-US LPs.

## Key structural notes

- **BVI Approved vs BVI Incubator** — don't confuse them. Incubator caps at US$20m and **forces conversion or wind-down after 2 years (extendable by 1)**. Approved is permanent and caps at US$100m. For a fund you intend to keep at sub-US$100m, Approved is the right choice; if you plan to scale past US$100m, an Incubator is the launch pad that converts to a Private Fund.
- **No direct BVI/Cayman EU passport** — only Jersey and Guernsey offer NPPR-style EU marketing routes. If even one tier-2 EU LP is in scope, that tilts toward Jersey or Guernsey.
- **Substance** — all four require some economic-substance attention for the GP/manager entity, not just the fund vehicle. The fund itself usually benefits from an investment-fund carve-out, but the manager doesn't.
- **Conversion path** — JPF / PIF / BVI Approved can all be converted to a higher-tier regulated fund (Expert Fund / Authorised Fund / BVI Private Fund) when you outgrow them; design your LPA / constitutional docs with that in mind.

## My read on your profile

For a **first fund, sub-US$100m, emerging manager**, the corpus-supported sequence is:

1. **If LP base is family/HNW/friendly professionals, no EU LPs**: **BVI Approved Fund + BVI Approved Manager** — cheapest, lightest, sized for you.
2. **If LP base includes EU professionals or you want stronger institutional wrapper**: **Jersey JPF** or **Guernsey PIF Route 1** — fast, audited, NPPR-capable.
3. **If LP base is US-touching institutional and EU marketing is irrelevant**: **Cayman ELP** under the Private Funds Act 2020.

You don't pick the regime in isolation from your LP base — the LP base picks it for you.
