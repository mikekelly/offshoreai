## What is a Parish Hall Enquiry?

A **Parish Hall Enquiry** is a distinctively Jersey pre-court forum, presided over by a **Centenier** — an elected, unpaid honorary police officer of the parish in which the alleged offence took place. It is the route by which the **majority of routine Jersey motoring offences are resolved without ever reaching the Magistrate's Court**. There is no direct equivalent in UK or other common-law jurisdictions — it has features of a fixed-penalty notice, a Scottish fiscal fine, and a court hearing, but its own procedure (`knowledge/jersey/road-traffic/parish-hall-enquiries.md`).

The Centenier reviews evidence, decides whether to charge, and can impose **a caution or a written fine of up to £200 per offence** — but **only** if the offender admits the offence and accepts the penalty. A defendant who disputes the matter, or who simply prefers formal court treatment, has the right at any point to demand that the case be heard in **Magistrate's Court** instead (`knowledge/jersey/road-traffic/parish-hall-enquiries.md`).

## When your case goes to Parish Hall

**Routine motoring offences** are the typical Parish Hall matter — including minor speeding (under 15 mph excess), mobile phone offences, failing to display an insurance disc, some traffic-light offences, minor careless driving, and parking offences (`knowledge/jersey/road-traffic/parish-hall-enquiries.md`).

## When it does NOT go to Parish Hall

The Parish Hall route is **excluded** for more serious matters, which go directly to Magistrate's Court by summons:

- **Speeding 15 mph or more over the limit**
- **Drink-driving** (any reading) or **drug-driving**
- **Dangerous driving** (Article 23 RTJL 1956)
- **Causing death or serious injury** by driving
- **Driving while disqualified**
- **Failing to stop / report** after an accident (in serious cases)
- **Any offence carrying mandatory disqualification**
- Any offence the Centenier judges the £200 ceiling inadequate for

## A couple of practical points worth knowing

- You can bring a **friend, family member, or advocate** for support.
- A Parish Hall outcome **is not a formal criminal conviction**, but it **is recorded**, can lead to **endorsement of your driving licence**, and may be relevant to insurance.
- If you admit and accept the penalty, the whole process is typically 15–30 minutes and resolved the same day; court takes several weeks.
