## The eight steps

### 1. Incorporate the Jersey TopCo

A Jersey **public company limited by shares** under the **Companies (Jersey) Law 1991** (CJL 1991). Key inputs: IPO-ready M&A, no-par-value shares, ≥2 directors initially, Jersey-resident registered office through a corporate-services provider, beneficial-ownership filing with the Companies Registry (private register). Incorporation: **1–3 days**.

### 2. Share-for-share exchange

UK shareholders of the Opco exchange their UK shares for Jersey TopCo shares (typically 1:1). Result: TopCo owns 100% of UK Opco; shareholders hold equivalent stakes in Jersey TopCo. Tax watchpoints:

- **UK**: TCGA s.135 rollover relief is generally available subject to anti-avoidance — defers CGT on the exchange.
- **UK stamp duty**: 0.5% potentially payable on transfer of UK shares into TopCo; structure for relief.
- **Jersey**: no Jersey CGT; Jersey-source income falls under zero-ten.

### 3. TopCo Jersey tax position

Jersey-resident by incorporation + management and control. Under the **Income Tax (Jersey) Law 1961** zero-ten regime, a generic holding company is at **0%** (10% bracket for financial-services companies; 20% for utilities / quarrying / cannabis / property income). Economic substance under the **Taxation (Companies — Economic Substance) (Jersey) Law 2019**: a pure holding company benefits from the **reduced substance test** — adequate Jersey people + premises but the bar is low.

### 4. Pillar Two consideration

If the group's annual revenue is ≥ **EUR 750m**, Pillar Two engages. Jersey's **MCIT Law 2025** (a QDMTT-equivalent + an IIR) is effective from accounting periods beginning on or after **1 January 2025**, topping Jersey-source income up to 15% in Jersey rather than ceding it to the UK under its IIR. Below the threshold, the 0% position continues.

### 5. Capital structure

CJL 1991 gives substantial pre/post-IPO flexibility: multiple share classes (founder / investor / public), no-par-value as standard, treasury shares, **capital reductions by special resolution + solvency statement** (no court process), redeemable shares, buy-backs. Useful for class structures, post-IPO buybacks, and (where permitted) anti-takeover features.

### 6. Listing on the LSE

Jersey TopCos are routinely accepted on Premium / Standard / High-Growth segments. The **UK Listing Rules, DTRs, MAR, and Prospectus Regulation** apply to the issuer's UK-market activities; the **UK Takeover Code** applies to a Jersey-incorporated company with securities admitted to a UK regulated market. The prospectus requires Jersey legal opinions and Jersey corporate-authority confirmations — Jersey advocates work alongside UK securities counsel.

### 7. Post-IPO governance

The **UK Corporate Governance Code** applies (Jersey companies admitted to UK regulated markets are in scope). The JFSC does not generally regulate the TopCo itself unless it carries on regulated business. Ongoing Jersey filings continue (annual return, BO information). Board typically mixes execs and NEDs, with at least one Jersey-resident director supporting substance.

### 8. Dividends and distributions

Distributions under CJL 1991 are **solvency-based** (no English-style retained-earnings test). Jersey applies **no withholding tax** on dividends to non-resident shareholders. UK-resident shareholders are taxed under UK personal dividend rules.

## Common variants

- **AIM** rather than Main Market — same structural logic, admission document rather than full prospectus.
- **Dual-class** founder/ordinary share structures — CJL 1991 permits subject to protections.
- **Pre-listing reorg** can bolt on spin-offs, founder-share settlements into family trusts, management-equity programmes, TopCo-level debt refinancing.

## Principal pitfalls

- Stamp-duty leakage on the share-for-share exchange if not structured for relief.
- **UK tax-residence creep** of TopCo — must be managed via genuinely Jersey-based directors and decision-making, not paper-only.
- Pillar Two triggering once the group crosses EUR 750m.
- Listing-side requirements (FCA prospectus approval, sponsor/NOMAD relationship, MAR compliance).
- Substance — pure-paper Jersey presence won't pass.
