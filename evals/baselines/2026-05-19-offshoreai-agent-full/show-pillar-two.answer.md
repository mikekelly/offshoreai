## Pillar Two — Jersey TopCo above a UK operating business

### Threshold

Pillar Two only engages once the group's **consolidated revenue is €750m+** in at least two of the four preceding fiscal years. Below that, neither Jersey MCIT nor UK MTT applies, and a Jersey TopCo continues to pay 0% under zero-ten (`jersey/tax/pillar-two.md`, `jersey/use-cases/founder-entrepreneur/pillar-two-impact.md`).

### What changes at Jersey level when the group is in scope

Jersey enacted the **Multinational Corporate Income Tax (Jersey) Law 2024 ("MCIT Law")**, effective for financial years on/after **1 January 2025**. The MCIT contains both:

- a **Qualified Domestic Top-up Tax (QDMTT‑equivalent / Domestic Top‑up Tax)** — Jersey itself collects the top-up to a 15% effective rate on Jersey constituent entities' GloBE income; and
- an **Income Inclusion Rule (IIR)** — applied at a Jersey parent in respect of low-taxed foreign subsidiaries.

For the Jersey TopCo as **ultimate parent entity**:
- Its own Jersey income (typically taxed at 0% under zero-ten) is **topped up to 15%** by Jersey's MCIT, rather than that revenue being ceded to a foreign parent jurisdiction under IIR/UTPR (`jersey/international/pillar-two-mcit.md`, `jersey/tax/pillar-two.md`).
- Zero-ten continues to be the **local** Jersey computation; MCIT is an **additional** top-up on top of it (`jersey/tax/pillar-two.md`).

### What changes at the UK operating company

The corpus is Jersey-centric and does **not contain a dedicated file on the UK's Pillar Two implementation** (the UK Multinational Top‑up Tax and Domestic Top‑up Tax). The corpus does, however, set out the cross-border mechanics it relies on:

- UK operating profits taxed at UK corporation tax (well above 15%) generally produce a UK ETR above the 15% floor, so **no top-up should fall on the UK opco's UK income** under GloBE methodology (general statement of the regime in `jersey/international/pillar-two-mcit.md`).
- The Jersey TopCo's IIR, if it applied, would reach **low-taxed** subsidiaries — a UK opco taxed at standard UK CT rates would not be one (`jersey/international/pillar-two-mcit.md`).
- Where Jersey collects QDMTT on Jersey-booked income, that should reduce the residual amount any other in-scope jurisdiction could collect under IIR or UTPR on those same Jersey profits (the policy rationale stated in `jersey/international/pillar-two-mcit.md`).

For the UK-side mechanics (UK MTT scope, UK QDMTT, UK IIR/UTPR ordering, interaction with UK CT, GIR filing in the UK, etc.) the corpus is silent — verify against UK primary sources / HMRC guidance.

### Compliance burden the corpus does flag

In-scope groups must register Jersey constituent entities, **compute GloBE income and ETR**, file Pillar Two returns, and pay the top-up. The corpus flags that this is materially more complex than the historical Jersey return and is typically handled with specialist software/advisers (`jersey/tax/pillar-two.md`, `jersey/use-cases/founder-entrepreneur/pillar-two-impact.md`).
