## Scope caveat — the corpus is Jersey-centric

The offshoreai corpus covers **Jersey** comprehensively; it does **not** contain dedicated concept files on Cayman **STAR** trusts (Special Trusts — Alternative Regime, Cayman Trusts Law Part VIII) or BVI **VISTA** trusts (Virgin Islands Special Trusts Act 2003). I can describe Jersey's offering and the points where the corpus expressly contrasts Jersey with STAR; for STAR-specific and VISTA-specific rules you should consult Cayman / BVI primary sources directly. All files cited below are `draft` status, meaning the content is drafted but not yet verified to `review` / `stable` — treat as orientation, not authority.

## What Jersey offers

Jersey's two analogues to STAR / VISTA are the **non-charitable purpose trust (NCPT)** under **Article 12** of the Trusts (Jersey) Law 1984, and the **reserved-powers trust** under **Article 9A**.

- **Article 12 NCPTs** validly hold assets for purposes (not beneficiaries), provided an **enforcer** is appointed; the enforcer cannot be a trustee. Typical uses: orphan SPVs (securitisation, aviation/shipping finance), holding the shares of a private trust company (PTC), and family-governance vehicles. (`jersey/trusts/purpose-trusts.md`)
- **Article 9A reserved powers**: a settlor may retain revocation, variation, advancement, investment-direction, beneficiary-appointment, change-of-proper-law and other powers without invalidating the trust, and trustees acting on those directions are protected from breach-of-trust liability (Art. 9A(3)). This is what makes Jersey workable for control-retaining settlors — e.g., US-grantor-trust planning. (`jersey/trusts/reserved-powers.md`)

## Where the corpus directly contrasts the three

The Article 12 file expressly addresses STAR — see `jersey/trusts/purpose-trusts.md` §"STAR-like applications": "Jersey NCPTs are functionally similar to (though not identical with) the Cayman STAR trust regime." Per the corpus, when choosing between Jersey NCPT and Cayman STAR, the differentiators are:

1. **Enforcer identity and qualifications** — both regimes require an enforcer, but the rules on *who* can act differ.
2. **Redomiciliation options** between the two regimes.
3. **Regulatory and tax treatment** of the underlying entities.

VISTA is **not discussed** in the corpus; I will not synthesise a comparison from training-data knowledge of the BVI statute. VISTA's distinctive feature (relieving the trustee of the prudent-man duty to monitor or intervene in an underlying company's affairs — the "anti-Bartlett" function) has no statutory analogue in Jersey; under Jersey law the trustee's monitoring duty would have to be modified by deed and managed within Article 9A reserved-powers / director-of-underlying-company drafting.

## Practical "when to use Jersey" pointers from the corpus

From `jersey/use-cases/family-office-adviser/family-governance-overlay.md` and the Article 12 / 9A files, Jersey is well-suited where:

- the settlor wants **strong reserved powers** with statutory protection (Article 9A) — particularly for **US-grantor-trust** treatment;
- the structure is an **orphan SPV** (securitisation, aviation/shipping leasing) or a **PTC-share holder**;
- the family wants either a **purpose trust** with an enforcer (common-law-comfortable) **or** a **foundation** (civil-law-comfortable) — Jersey offers both in the same legal system;
- the client values Jersey's **Article 9 firewall** for conflict-of-laws insulation (forced heirship, foreign matrimonial / creditor claims).

For Cayman vs Jersey *fund* domicile choice (a different question) the corpus does have a file: `jersey/use-cases/fund-counsel/jersey-vs-cayman-fund-choice.md` — but that is funds, not trusts.

## Honest answer to "which do I use?"

The corpus does **not** contain a STAR / VISTA / Jersey decision matrix. The most accurate corpus-grounded answer is:

- If you have already decided the structure should be **Jersey**, the choice within Jersey is between Article 12 NCPT, Article 9A reserved-powers ordinary trust, or a Foundations Law 2009 foundation — and the family-governance-overlay file walks through that selection.
- If the live question is **Jersey vs Cayman STAR vs BVI VISTA**, the corpus only supports the Jersey side. You will need Cayman Trusts Law Part VIII and the BVI Virgin Islands Special Trusts Act 2003 (and any 2013 / later amendments) as primary sources, plus a Cayman / BVI advisor — those are outside the offshoreai Jersey corpus's coverage.
