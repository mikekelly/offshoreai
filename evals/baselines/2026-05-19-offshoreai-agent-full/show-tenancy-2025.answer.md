## What changed on 15 April 2026

The **Residential Tenancy (Jersey) Amendment Law 2025** came into force on 15 April 2026 — the most significant Jersey residential-tenancy reform in over a decade. The headline changes (`jersey/residential-tenancy/2025-amendment-law.md`):

- **Fixed-term cap at 3 years.** The initial fixed term may not exceed 3 years, and successive fixed terms are prohibited — at expiry the tenancy ends or auto-converts to periodic.
- **Tenant exit on 1 month's notice.** A tenant may terminate *any* residential tenancy (including a fixed term) on one month's written notice.
- **Landlord termination: justified, or 12 months' notice.** No-fault termination of a periodic tenancy now requires 12 months' notice (up from the prior 3 months); a 3-month notice still works only on a permitted ground (breach, sale, owner-occupation, redevelopment).
- **RPI rent cap, once per year.** Rent on periodic tenancies may rise only once per year, by no more than the Jersey RPI, on at least 2 months' written notice — unless the rent is "significantly below market rent" (a narrow, challengeable carve-out).
- **New Rent Tribunal.** An independent Rent Tribunal handles rent-increase disputes (RPI breach, carve-out scope, once-yearly rule, notice compliance). It does not handle eviction/breach (still Petty Debts Court) or social-housing/employment-linked accommodation.
- **Stronger written-form rules.** All fees chargeable to a tenant must be clearly identified in the agreement; hidden fees are unenforceable and may be an offence.

**Transitional treatment** (also in the same file): pre-existing periodic tenancies convert immediately to the new regime; pre-existing fixed terms continue under pre-amendment law until expiry; successive-fixed-term clauses become unenforceable at expiry of the current term.

**What did *not* change**: Petty Debts Court jurisdiction over eviction/breach; Viscount enforcement; the 2014 condition-report regime; the Tenants' Deposit Scheme; the lodging-house exclusion; the 9-year ceiling above which a lease becomes a Royal-Court-passed contract of lease.
