## Do I need planning permission for a rear extension on my Jersey house?

**Short answer: Probably yes, unless it's modest enough to fall fully within "permitted development" under the Planning and Building (General Development) (Jersey) Order 2011 ("GD Order 2011").**

### How to think about it

The GD Order 2011 grants automatic planning permission for a range of minor works, including **single-storey rear extensions** — but only if they fall within specific numeric limits on floor area, depth, height, eaves height, distance from boundaries, and materials. Anything outside those thresholds, or a **two-storey** rear extension of any size, needs a full planning application under the Planning and Building (Jersey) Law 2002 (`jersey/planning/extensions-and-conversions.md`; `jersey/planning/permitted-development.md`).

For a typical Jersey-sized property, a small single-storey rear extension (e.g. ~3–4m projection, single storey) may be permitted development; anything larger or two-storey almost always requires application (`jersey/planning/extensions-and-conversions.md`).

### Important overlays that narrow or remove permitted development

- **Listed building** — listed-building consent is needed even where permitted development would otherwise cover the works.
- **Conservation area** — many exemptions don't apply or are narrower; a full application is typical even for modest works.
- **Green Zone / Coastal National Park** — restricted to protect landscape.
- **Article 31 directions** — the Minister can remove permitted-development rights in specific locations.

### Even if planning permission isn't needed

- **Building approval** under the **Building Bye-Laws 2007** is a separate consent and is still required for the technical standards (structure, insulation, fire, drainage).
- **Private restrictive covenants** on the title can prohibit works that planning would otherwise allow — check the title (`jersey/planning/extensions-and-conversions.md`; `jersey/property/restrictive-covenants.md` is cross-referenced).

### If you're unsure

Apply for a **Certificate of Lawful Development** — a lower-fee route that confirms whether your proposal falls within permitted development and gives certainty for future sale, refinancing, or against enforcement (`jersey/planning/permitted-development.md`; `jersey/use-cases/jersey-resident/extension-planning-permission.md`).

### What happens if you build without permission

Unauthorised development is a **criminal offence** under the Planning and Building (Jersey) Law 2002: unlimited fines (imprisonment in serious cases), enforcement notices requiring reversal at your cost, stop notices halting work, sale and mortgage/insurance complications, and no guarantee that a retrospective application will be granted (`jersey/use-cases/jersey-resident/extension-planning-permission.md`).
