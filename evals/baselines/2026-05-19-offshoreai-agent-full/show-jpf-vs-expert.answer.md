## Short answer

For a **club-deal PE fund**, the default Jersey regime is the **Jersey Private Fund (JPF)** — provided you can credibly cap the structure at ≤ 50 offers / investors, all of whom are professional / eligible. The corpus explicitly names "club deal structures" as a paradigm JPF use case ([jersey/funds/jersey-private-fund.md](jersey/funds/jersey-private-fund.md), "When to use a JPF"). Go to **Expert Fund** only if you expect to breach the 50-investor cap or if your investors won't credibly self-certify as professional/eligible but will meet the higher expert-investor net-worth / minimum-investment thresholds.

## Why JPF fits a club deal

The JPF is Jersey's light-touch private-fund regime, with:

- a **≤ 50 offers / investors** ceiling,
- **professional / eligible investors only**,
- **~48-hour JFSC authorisation**,
- a **JFSC-licensed administrator as Designated Service Provider (DSP)** carrying CDD/AML and standing in for direct JFSC oversight of the fund.

That economic profile — small investor count, sophisticated LPs, fast time-to-market, low ongoing supervisory burden — is exactly what a club deal needs. ([jersey/funds/jersey-private-fund.md](jersey/funds/jersey-private-fund.md))

The fund-counsel decision tree puts it the same way: ≤ 50 non-retail investors → **JPF**; > 50 expert investors → **Expert Fund** ([jersey/use-cases/fund-counsel/choosing-fund-regime.md](jersey/use-cases/fund-counsel/choosing-fund-regime.md), "Decision tree" and "Specific scenarios" — "PE / VC fund with up to 50 LPs: JPF. Fast, light, costs aligned with closed-ended PE economics").

## When to switch to Expert Fund instead

The Expert Fund regime is the right answer if any of the following hold:

- The deal will be **offered to more than 50 potential investors** (note: the cap counts *offers*, not just final subscribers — undershoot the cap by design).
- You expect to **scale into a larger PE vehicle** beyond the JPF cap.
- You need **wider expert-investor marketing latitude** or **AIFMD NPPR** positioning for active EU distribution.

Expert Funds have no fixed investor cap but require investors to meet expert criteria (typically US$100k minimum investment or prescribed net-worth thresholds), a more detailed offering memorandum, and slower authorisation (days to weeks rather than 48 hours) ([jersey/funds/expert-fund.md](jersey/funds/expert-fund.md)).

## Pitfall the corpus flags specifically

> "Going too light: a 'JPF' for what should be an Expert Fund will be re-categorised by the JFSC if you go over the 50-investor cap; better to size up at outset." ([jersey/use-cases/fund-counsel/choosing-fund-regime.md](jersey/use-cases/fund-counsel/choosing-fund-regime.md), "Pitfalls")

For a club deal — by definition a small, known investor group — that risk is normally low, which is why JPF is the default. But model the investor count honestly *including offers* before committing.
