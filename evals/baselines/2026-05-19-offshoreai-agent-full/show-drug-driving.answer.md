## Drug-driving in Jersey — current rules

### What applies today (May 2026)

Drug-driving in Jersey is prosecuted as **driving while impaired by drugs** under the **Road Traffic (Jersey) Law 1956**. Unlike drink-driving's mechanical "over the limit" test, prosecution depends on **proving impairment** on objective evidence — observed control, field sobriety tests, and blood/urine showing drug presence (`jersey/road-traffic/drug-driving.md`).

What Jersey **does not** currently have:

- Statutory drug-concentration limits (no equivalent to the English "specified limit" regime);
- Police powers for roadside drug-wipe testing (only impairment-based field tests);
- Per se offences (being over a numeric limit irrespective of impairment).

The offence is **substance-neutral** — it captures controlled drugs (cannabis, cocaine, MDMA, etc.), prescription medication (sedatives, opioids, benzodiazepines), OTC medicines that impair, and drug/alcohol combinations. "I have a prescription" is not a defence to actual impairment.

**Sentencing** tracks drink-driving: mandatory disqualification (typically 12+ months on a first offence), a fine up to £2,000, imprisonment up to 6 months, and licence endorsement, with aggravation for accidents or refusal to cooperate.

### What's coming

In **February 2026**, States representatives proposed legislation to:

- Introduce **statutory drug limits** for controlled and some prescription substances;
- Give police **roadside drug-testing powers** (swab tests, akin to UK drug-wipes);
- Create **per se offences** (over the limit = offence, no impairment proof needed);
- Make **disqualification mandatory** on conviction.

As of May 2026 the proposal is **not yet enacted**; the States Assembly timetable suggests potential introduction during 2026 or 2027. Until then, the impairment-based regime continues to govern (`jersey/road-traffic/drug-driving.md`).
