Three things stack up to give you that Friday-afternoon slot.

**1. In Jersey, transfer of real property is an act of Court — not a private completion.** Unlike English conveyancing (where "completion" happens between solicitors), a Jersey property contract has to be **passed before the Royal Court**: the parties (usually their Advocates as attorneys) appear, the contract is read or referenced, the Greffier signs and records it, and **the transfer takes legal effect at that moment** — there is no separate completion event (`knowledge/jersey/property/contract-passing.md`; `knowledge/jersey/property/real-property-overview.md`).

**2. The forum is the Samedi Division, sitting with two Jurats.** Real-property passings are heard in the Royal Court's **Samedi Division** — its civil day-to-day forum, named after its historical **Saturday** sittings (*samedi* = Saturday in French). For a property passing, **the Bailiff or Deputy Bailiff sits with two Jurats** to witness the contract — the convention that has settled in modern practice is **Friday afternoon** as the dedicated property-passing slot (`knowledge/jersey/legal-system/royal-court.md`; `knowledge/jersey/property/commercial-leases.md`; `knowledge/jersey/property/flying-freeholds.md`). The same Friday-afternoon convention is treated as load-bearing enough that historic Royal Court closures on a passing day have triggered force-majeure arguments in Jersey contracts (`knowledge/jersey/contract/force-majeure.md`).

**3. Why a Court act at all — the Norman-customary-law root.** Public transfer of real property before the Court is a **Norman customary law** inheritance: the Royal Court is the repository of property records, the transfer is visible (parties and the fact of passing are public, though price is typically private), and title certainty flows from the Court act rather than from a private exchange of deeds (`knowledge/jersey/property/contract-passing.md`; `knowledge/jersey/property/real-property-overview.md`).
