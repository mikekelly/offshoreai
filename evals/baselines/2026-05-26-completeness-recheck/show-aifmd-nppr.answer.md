## The route for marketing a Jersey AIF into Germany

Germany is one of the **stricter NPPR jurisdictions** — the route works, but with more conditions than (e.g.) Luxembourg or the Netherlands.

### 1. Jersey-side prerequisites

The AIFM must be **JFSC-registered under Jersey's AIFMD-equivalent regime** and demonstrate equivalent compliance — capital adequacy, risk management, AIFMD-equivalent depository arrangements, transparency to investors, and regulatory reporting to the JFSC (`knowledge/jersey/funds/aifmd-nppr.md`; `knowledge/jersey/use-cases/fund-counsel/aifmd-nppr-registration.md`).

### 2. German-side notification (§330 KAGB)

Notification is filed with **BaFin** under §330 of the German Kapitalanlagegesetzbuch (KAGB). German-specific overlays the corpus flags:

- **Local fact-sheet** required;
- **Local distributor** sometimes required;
- **Ongoing reporting** obligations after acceptance;
- Marketing is to **professional investors** only — the semi-professional / retail routes are tighter and rarely used by Jersey AIFs (`knowledge/jersey/use-cases/fund-counsel/aifmd-nppr-registration.md`).

### 3. AIFMD II overlay (effective 16 April 2026 — six weeks ago)

NPPR access is **preserved** under AIFMD II, but two operational deltas now bite for any Jersey AIFM marketing into Germany:

- **Expanded investor disclosures** — pre-marketing and ongoing disclosure widened (liquidity management, sustainability factors interacting with SFDR, loan-originating-fund disclosures where applicable);
- **Enhanced Annex IV reporting** to ESMA via BaFin — new data fields, more granular asset and risk reporting. First submissions under the new requirements expected end-Q2 / early-Q3 2026.

Germany has transposed AIFMD II **on the stricter end** of the member-state spectrum, so BaFin's NPPR conditions sit at the more demanding end of EU practice. The JFSC's **EU AIF Code** (the AIF Code of Practice split into EU and UK tracks) is being finalised through 2026 specifically to align Jersey AIFMs marketing into the EU with AIFMD II (`knowledge/frontier/aifmd-ii-april-2026.md`).

### 4. Watch-outs

- **No third-country passport** — the proposed extension was never implemented, so Germany is country-by-country, separate from any other EU member-state notification;
- **Reverse solicitation** doesn't trigger NPPR but BaFin scrutinises the claim — active outreach disguised as reverse solicitation attracts penalties;
- **Depository mismatch** — a "lighter" Jersey depository alone is a regulatory mismatch for an EU-marketed AIF; the AIFMD-equivalent depository functions must be in place.
