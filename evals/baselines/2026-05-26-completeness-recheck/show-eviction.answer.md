Let me read the dedicated landlord use-case and the underlying procedure files.## Short answer

**No, you cannot just evict** — Jersey requires you to go through the **statutory breach procedure** under the **Residential Tenancy (Jersey) Law 2011** (RTJL), with a court order from the **Petty Debts Court** and physical enforcement by the **Viscount**. Self-help (changing locks, removing possessions, cutting off utilities, physically removing the tenant) is unlawful and may itself be a **criminal offence** under the RTJL.

## The procedure

1. **7-day breach notice** — in writing, signed, identifying the unpaid rent (period and amount), and requiring payment within at least 7 days. A defective notice is fatal; you start over. If the tenant pays in full within the 7 days, the breach is remedied and you cannot proceed on the same arrears.
2. **Apply to the Petty Debts Court** (exclusive original jurisdiction) for an order **terminating** the tenancy and **evicting** the tenant — you can also seek a money judgment for the arrears. Evidence pack: tenancy agreement, condition report, rent ledger, breach notice and proof of service, communications log.
3. **Hearing** — the court checks procedural compliance and considers any tenant defences (set-off for landlord disrepair, discrimination, harassment during the notice period, procedural defects). It typically grants the order with a **stay of execution** (commonly 14–28 days, longer for families with children or vulnerable tenants).
4. **Viscount enforcement** — after the stay expires, the Viscount (the only person lawfully entitled to take possession from a resisting tenant) attends, removes the tenant if necessary, inventories and stores possessions, and restores possession to you. Viscount fees run from several hundred to several thousand pounds.

**Realistic timeline:** **2–4 months** from first missed payment to actual possession in a straightforward case; longer if contested or stayed.

## Tenant protections worth knowing

- **Set-off** for landlord breach (e.g. unfit premises under the Public Health and Safety (Rented Dwellings) (Jersey) Law 2018) can reduce or extinguish arrears.
- **Discrimination defence** under the Discrimination (Jersey) Law 2013 if eviction is in substance on a protected ground.
- **Retaliation** — courts scrutinise evictions that follow a complaint or repair request.
- **Vulnerability** — children, pregnancy, disability, mental-health, Income Support / social-housing placement all attract stays.

## What to do tomorrow morning

Calculate arrears precisely; check the agreement and condition report; serve a properly drafted, signed 7-day notice with documented service; open a parallel payment-plan conversation (many cases resolve here); if unresolved, gather evidence for the Petty Debts Court application; engage a Jersey advocate if the matter is contested.
