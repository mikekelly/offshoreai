## Why continuation funds and GP-leds are growing

A **continuation fund** is a new vehicle into which a GP transfers one or more portfolio assets from an existing fund, with existing LPs given a choice to **roll** their interest in or **cash out** at a negotiated valuation. **GP-led secondaries** is the broader category (tender offers, strip sales, preferred-equity sidecars, structured liquidity).

The growth driver is structural, not cyclical noise:

- **Tough M&A and IPO exit markets** through 2022-2025 left GPs sitting on trophy assets they wouldn't sell at depressed valuations.
- **LPs are demanding DPI** (distributed-to-paid-in capital) to recycle into new commitments.
- **Secondaries-market capital** is sitting ready to deploy.

The **2026 Global Private Equity Outlook survey** (cited by Dechert, Nov 2025) records that **46% of respondents** are now using GP-led secondaries / continuation vehicles to expedite LP distributions — **roughly double the 2024 figure**. **51% of North American**, **55% of APAC**, and **43% of EMEA** respondents plan to *increase* GP-led secondary dealmaking over the next 24 months (EMEA was 14% in 2024). The trend is broad-based and accelerating.

## Why Guernsey is winning the European share

Guernsey's lead is built on four specific, durable features:

1. **Single-asset CFs are not-a-CIS.** A single-asset continuation fund in Guernsey is **not treated as a collective investment scheme** under the **Protection of Investors (Bailiwick of Guernsey) Law 2020**, because the structure is closer to a single co-investment vehicle / JV than a pooled managed fund. The practical effect: it can be established **without** CIS authorisation. (Multi-asset CFs still use the standard authorised regimes or PIF Class 1.)
2. **GFSC 3-business-day approval** for single-asset CFs requiring review — the fastest in Europe. Term-sheet to close in 2-3 weeks vs 4-8 weeks elsewhere.
3. **Explicit strategic positioning** — the GFSC and Guernsey Finance have actively marketed the product line through guidance, practitioner engagement, and case-handling.
4. **Mature Channel-Islands practitioner base** (Mourant, Ogier, Carey Olsen, Walkers, Appleby) plus specialist admin houses (Aztec, IQ-EQ, JTC, Apex) who have handled the volume through 2024-2026.

Typical winning-deal profile: EUR 100m – EUR 1bn trophy asset from a 2018-2020 vintage, lead institutional secondary buyer (HarbourVest, Coller, Pantheon), 30-60% LP roll rate, 8-12 weeks to close.

## How Jersey compares

Jersey is **second to Guernsey but competitive**, particularly where the sponsor already has Jersey infrastructure or the deal is trust-and-family-office-adjacent. The **JPF** offers **48-hour JFSC authorisation** and is the standard Jersey continuation-fund vehicle; the **DSP** model gives regulatory efficiency. Where Guernsey wins on the not-a-CIS structural categorisation and explicit market positioning, Jersey wins on JPF speed and trust-law adjacency.

## Caveats

- **Inherent conflict** — the GP is on both sides. Independent valuation, LPAC consent, fairness opinion, and genuine cash-out option are all market-standard mitigants; ILPA Continuation Fund Guidance (2023) is increasingly soft-law.
- **AIFMD II disclosure uplift** from **16 April 2026** materially raises the bar for EU-marketing CFs; the GFSC fast-track does not exempt.
- **Carry crystallisation** at rollover is a tax workstream of its own; the **UK carried-interest reform effective 6 April 2026** changes the post-reform economics meaningfully for UK-resident GPs.
- **Pillar Two** — Guernsey's QDMTT (effective 1 January 2025) and Jersey's MCIT 2025 both apply 15% top-up to in-scope MNE-group sponsors.
