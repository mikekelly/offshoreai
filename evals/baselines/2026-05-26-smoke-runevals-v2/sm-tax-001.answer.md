## Jersey corporate tax — the headline rate

A Jersey-incorporated **trading** company (i.e. not a financial-services company, utility, large corporate retailer, hydrocarbon or cannabis trader, and not earning Jersey land/property income) is taxed at the **0%** standard rate under **Article 123C(2) of the Income Tax (Jersey) Law 1961** — the "zero" leg of the **zero-ten** regime ([`knowledge/jersey/tax/zero-ten.md`](knowledge/jersey/tax/zero-ten.md)).

One sanity-check worth flagging: if "trading group" means a **retail** trade selling goods to consumers in Jersey with annual Jersey turnover of £2m+, you fall under **Article 123I** (large corporate retailer) and would be taxed at **20%** above the £750k profits band, with a taper between £500k–£750k. The 0% answer assumes you're not in that category.

## Pillar Two — yes, MCIT applies at €900m

Yes — at ~€900m consolidated revenue you are **in scope** of Jersey's **Multinational Corporate Income Tax (MCIT)**, in force for accounting periods beginning on or after **1 January 2025** ([`knowledge/jersey/international/pillar-two-mcit.md`](knowledge/jersey/international/pillar-two-mcit.md)):

- The MCIT applies to groups with consolidated revenue of **€750m+** in at least 2 of the preceding 4 years — you're materially above that.
- It functions as a **Qualified Domestic Minimum Top-up Tax (QDMTT)**: Jersey itself collects the top-up so that your in-scope Jersey profits bear a **15% effective rate** under GloBE methodology, rather than the 0% headline.
- A **Qualified Income Inclusion Rule (IIR)** also applies for in-scope groups; Jersey has **not** implemented an Undertaxed Profits Rule (UTPR).
- The **substance-based income exclusion** (formulaic carve-out for payroll + tangible assets, tapering down over the early years) can reduce the top-up base.
- **Substance Law 2019** obligations are separate and continue to apply on top of MCIT for any relevant-activity income.

So in short: your headline Jersey rate on trading profits is **0%**, but because the group is over the €750m Pillar Two threshold, MCIT tops that up to a **15% effective rate** on the in-scope Jersey income.
