## CDD for a new institutional client under the MLO 2008

The Money Laundering (Jersey) Order 2008 imposes a **risk-based CDD obligation**, so the depth depends on what kind of institution this is and how you risk-rate the relationship.

### The likely SDD route

If your new institutional client is a **regulated financial institution in an equivalent jurisdiction**, a **listed company on a recognised exchange**, a **government body**, or a **prescribed pension fund**, it falls into the **simplified due diligence (SDD)** category. SDD typically allows reduced identity-verification evidence, reliance on the counterparty's regulated/listed status, and streamlined ongoing monitoring (`knowledge/jersey/use-cases/compliance-mlro/customer-due-diligence.md`; `knowledge/jersey/aml-cft/money-laundering-order-2008.md`).

You still need to **document why the SDD category applies** (the BWRA-anchored risk classification) — SDD is not "no CDD."

### Standard CDD for an entity customer

If the institution doesn't fit a prescribed SDD bucket, you apply standard CDD on the entity (`knowledge/jersey/use-cases/compliance-mlro/customer-due-diligence.md`):

- **Legal form** and jurisdiction of incorporation
- **Constitutional documents**
- **Directors / officers** (with CDD on senior management)
- **Beneficial owners** — natural persons holding 25%+ ownership or control, each CDD'd as an individual
- **Authorised representatives** dealing with you
- **Source of funds** for transactions
- **Purpose and intended nature** of the relationship

Plus the cross-cutting MLO 2008 obligations: **ongoing monitoring**, **record-keeping**, periodic refresh, and documentation of the file (`knowledge/jersey/aml-cft/money-laundering-order-2008.md`).

### When EDD bites instead

EDD applies — and overrides any SDD assumption — if any of the following are present: **PEPs** in beneficial ownership/control, **high-risk jurisdiction** exposure (FATF grey/black list or Jersey-specified), **complex layered ownership**, or unusual patterns. EDD requires senior-management approval, **source-of-wealth verification** (not just source of funds), enhanced ongoing monitoring, and more frequent review (`knowledge/jersey/use-cases/compliance-mlro/customer-due-diligence.md`).

### The procedural sequence

1. Run the prospective relationship against your **business-wide risk assessment (BWRA)** to classify low / medium / high
2. Gather CDD at the appropriate depth (SDD / standard / EDD)
3. Verify ID from reliable independent sources
4. Approve at the appropriate seniority (senior management if EDD)
5. Set up risk-proportionate ongoing monitoring and a CDD refresh schedule
