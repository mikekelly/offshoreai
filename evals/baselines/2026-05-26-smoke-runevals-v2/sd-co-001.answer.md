This is one of the most distinctive features of the LLC Law 2018, drawing on the Delaware model, and a deliberate departure from the Jersey company under CJL 1991 where directors' fiduciary duties are largely non-modifiable.

## The irreducible floor — two limits remain

Even with maximum drafting freedom, Article 47 keeps two guard-rails that cannot be contracted out of:

1. **The duty of good faith** cannot be eliminated. Where the LLC agreement is silent, a member or manager exercising rights or powers in respect of the LLC owes the LLC and the other members a duty of good faith; that default cannot be drafted away.
2. **Liability for fraud, wilful default, or gross negligence** cannot be eliminated. The LLC agreement may calibrate duties downward but cannot purport to shield a member or manager from acts or omissions that fall into those three categories.

Within those guard-rails, the LLC agreement can replace the default fiduciary framework with a contractually-specified one — which is what makes the vehicle attractive as a fund GP, JV vehicle, or family-office GP where the conflict-of-interest perimeter is defined by contract rather than left to default fiduciary doctrine.

## Comparator point

By contrast, a Jersey company under the Companies (Jersey) Law 1991 has directors' fiduciary duties that are largely non-modifiable — so if you need contractual flexibility on duties, the LLC is the vehicle that gets you there. The Jersey position mirrors the Delaware and Cayman LLC floors.
