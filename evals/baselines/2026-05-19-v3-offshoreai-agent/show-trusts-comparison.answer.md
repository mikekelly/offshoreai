## Jersey NCPT vs Cayman STAR vs BVI VISTA — when to use which

The three regimes solve **two different problems**, which is the key to the choice.

### Two purpose-trust regimes vs one share-ownership regime

**Jersey Article 12 non-charitable purpose trust (NCPT)** and **Cayman STAR** are both **purpose-trust regimes** — they let a trust exist for a stated purpose without beneficiaries, enforced by a mandatory **enforcer**. Both have unlimited duration and are functionally similar; the differences are statutory detail, who can act as enforcer, redomiciliation options, and trustee licensing regime (`jersey/trusts/purpose-trusts.md`; `cayman/star-trusts.md`).

**BVI VISTA** solves a **different problem**. It is a **share-ownership regime** for trusts holding shares of a designated BVI Business Company. Its signature feature is **disengaging the trustee from the *Bartlett v Barclays Bank* duty** to monitor and intervene in the underlying company's affairs. The trustee holds legal title but is not required (and not liable for failing) to second-guess directors. "Office of Director Rules" in the trust instrument preserve settlor/family control over management (`bvi/vista-trusts.md`).

### When to use which

| Use case | Pick |
|---|---|
| Holding shares of an **operating family business** where the settlor wants chosen directors to run it without trustee interference | **BVI VISTA** — the regime is purpose-built for this. STAR/NCPT do not directly disapply the Bartlett duty |
| **Orphan SPV** in securitisation, aviation/shipping finance, structured finance (no beneficiaries; SPV off-balance-sheet) | **Jersey NCPT** or **Cayman STAR** — interchangeable on the legal mechanics; choose by trustee location, redomiciliation flexibility, and tax/regulatory fit of the SPV |
| Holding the shares of a **Private Trust Company (PTC)** | **Jersey NCPT** or **Cayman STAR** — both are standard. Jersey is the natural choice if the PTC and its underlying trusts are otherwise Jersey-based |
| **Family-governance** purpose vehicle (heritage assets, defined non-charitable purposes) | **Jersey NCPT** or **Cayman STAR** — pick by the jurisdiction of the surrounding family structure. Jersey foundations are an adjacent vehicle worth considering |
| **Mixed beneficiary + purpose** trust | **Cayman STAR** specifically permits this in statute; Jersey accommodates it via separate provisions |
| Settlor wants **regulatory and licensing** centre with deep TCB ecosystem and Royal Court supervision | **Jersey NCPT** |

### Caveats

- All three source files are **`draft` status** in this corpus — the comparison is drafted but the editorial team has not yet promoted them to `review`/`stable`. Verify the statutory detail (in particular VISTA's office-of-director rules and STAR Act 1997 enforcer provisions) against the underlying statutes before relying on this in a transaction.
- The corpus is **Jersey-primary**; Cayman and BVI files exist as adjacent-jurisdiction comparison material. For deep Cayman-only or BVI-only structuring questions, a Cayman/BVI-specialist source is preferable.
- A Jersey **foundation** can sometimes substitute for an NCPT in family-governance contexts (`jersey/foundations/foundations-law-2009.md`) — worth considering before settling on a purpose trust.
