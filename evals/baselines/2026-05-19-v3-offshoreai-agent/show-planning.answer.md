# Planning permission for a rear extension in Jersey

## Short answer

**Probably yes — but a modest single-storey rear extension may fall within "permitted development" under the Planning and Building (General Development) (Jersey) Order 2011 ("GD Order 2011"), in which case no planning application is needed.** A two-storey rear extension, anything exceeding the GD Order's size/depth/height limits, or extensions on listed buildings or in conservation areas / the Green Zone / Coastal National Park will almost always require a full planning application under the **Planning and Building (Jersey) Law 2002** (jersey/use-cases/jersey-resident/extension-planning-permission.md; jersey/planning/permitted-development.md).

## The detail

**Permitted development cover.** The GD Order 2011 grants automatic planning permission for certain single-storey rear extensions, subject to conditions on maximum floor area added, maximum projection from the original rear wall, maximum height, maximum eaves height, distance from boundaries, and matching materials. Exceeding *any* condition takes the work outside permitted development. Two-storey extensions are not within the rear-extension permitted-development class (jersey/planning/permitted-development.md).

**Listed / conservation / Green Zone.** Permitted-development rights are typically narrower or removed entirely if your property is listed, in a conservation area, or in the Green Zone / Coastal National Park. Listed buildings will additionally need listed-building consent (jersey/planning/permitted-development.md).

**Building approval is separate.** Permitted-development cover only relieves you from needing planning permission — you still need **building approval** under the Building Bye-Laws 2007 for the technical work (structure, insulation, fire, drainage) (jersey/use-cases/jersey-resident/extension-planning-permission.md).

**If unsure — Certificate of Lawful Development.** Where the proposal is borderline, the recommended step is to apply for a Certificate of Lawful Development: lower fee than a full planning application, confirms the works fall within permitted development, and gives certainty on future sale or enforcement (jersey/planning/permitted-development.md).

**Consequences of getting it wrong.** Unauthorised development is a criminal offence under the PBL 2002, with unlimited fines, possible enforcement notices requiring removal at your cost, stop notices, and impact on later sale, mortgage and insurance (jersey/use-cases/jersey-resident/extension-planning-permission.md).

**Don't forget private-law restrictions.** Even if planning permits the works, restrictive covenants on your title can independently prohibit them (jersey/property/restrictive-covenants.md cross-reference).

## Caveats

Both cited files are **draft** status (last verified 2026-05-18). The general framework is reliable, but the GD Order 2011 contains specific numeric thresholds (exact maximum projection, floor area, height) that the corpus describes indicatively rather than quoting verbatim — verify the current numeric limits against the GD Order itself before relying on a self-assessment that your extension is permitted development.
