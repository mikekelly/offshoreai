## The corpus does not directly answer the Friday-afternoon scheduling question

The Jersey corpus covers **why** real-property transfers happen in court at all, but it does not specifically document the well-known practitioner convention that contract passing is scheduled for Friday afternoons. The closest material is:

**What the corpus does cover.** Jersey real-property transfers are effected by *contract passing* before the **Samedi Division of the Royal Court** — the buyer and seller (typically through lawyers acting as attorneys) appear, the contract is read or referenced, the Greffier records it, and title transfers at that moment (`jersey/property/contract-passing.md`). The Samedi Division is described as the Royal Court's day-to-day civil forum and is "named after the historical Saturday sittings" (`jersey/legal-system/royal-court.md`). The contract-passing file itself says only that the Samedi Division sits "most weekdays" and does not single out Fridays.

**Why the procedure exists at all.** This is a **Norman customary-law inheritance**: public transfer of real property before the Royal Court, with the Court as the repository of property records, ensures certainty and visibility of title transitions — different from English conveyancing, which is largely private until Land Registry entry (`jersey/property/contract-passing.md`; `jersey/property/real-property-overview.md`).
