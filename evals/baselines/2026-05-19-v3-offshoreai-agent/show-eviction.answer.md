## Short answer

**No — you cannot just evict.** Self-help (changing locks, removing the tenant's possessions, cutting utilities, physically ejecting the tenant) is unlawful in Jersey and may be a criminal offence under the **Residential Tenancy (Jersey) Law 2011** (RTJL). You must use the statutory breach-and-court procedure.

## The procedure

1. **Serve a 7-day written breach notice** on the tenant identifying the unpaid rent (which period(s), how much) and requiring payment within at least 7 days. The notice must be in writing, signed, and properly served (per the agreement, or by personal service / recorded post). Defective notice is fatal — you would have to start over. (`jersey/residential-tenancy/non-payment-of-rent.md`)
2. **If unpaid after 7 days, apply to the Petty Debts Court** — which has **exclusive original jurisdiction** over residential tenancy termination, eviction, and arrears judgments under the RTJL. The Royal Court does not handle these at first instance. You bring the tenancy agreement, condition report, rent ledger, the breach notice with proof of service, and your correspondence schedule. (`jersey/residential-tenancy/eviction-procedure.md`)
3. **At the hearing**, the court tests whether the breach is made out, whether procedure was compliant, and whether the tenant has a defence (set-off for landlord disrepair, discrimination, harassment, procedural defects). If satisfied, it grants a termination order, an eviction order, usually a **stay of execution** (commonly 14–28 days, longer for vulnerable tenants or families with children), a money judgment for arrears if sought, and costs. (`jersey/residential-tenancy/eviction-procedure.md`)
4. **Enforcement is by the Viscount** — the only person lawfully entitled to take possession from a resisting tenant. After any stay expires, you apply to the Viscount's Department; the Viscount attends, removes the tenant (with reasonable force and police assistance if needed), inventories and stores/sells possessions, and restores possession to you. (`jersey/residential-tenancy/eviction-procedure.md`)

## Realistic timeline and cost

**2–4 months** from first missed payment to actual possession in a straightforward case; longer if contested or stayed. You bear court fees, Viscount's fees (tariffed — typically several hundred to several thousand pounds), and lock-change/storage costs. These are recoverable from the tenant under the lease/judgment but often borne in practice by the landlord if the tenant is judgment-proof. (`jersey/use-cases/tenant-landlord/evicting-non-paying-tenant.md`, `jersey/residential-tenancy/eviction-procedure.md`)

## What you must not do

Change the locks, cut off utilities, remove the tenant's possessions, enter without consent, or physically remove the tenant. Each is unlawful — exposure to damages, an unfair-eviction or harassment offence under the RTJL, and (for the possessions) potential conversion/criminal liability. Self-help also destroys your case at court. (`jersey/residential-tenancy/non-payment-of-rent.md`)

## Tenant protections to expect

- **Set-off** for landlord disrepair (e.g. unfit premises) can reduce or extinguish the arrears claim.
- **Discrimination defence** under the Discrimination (Jersey) Law 2013 if the eviction is in substance on a protected ground.
- **Retaliation** scrutiny if eviction follows a complaint or repair request.
- **Vulnerability** — children, mental health, pregnancy, disability, Income Support / social-housing applications routinely attract stays of execution. (`jersey/residential-tenancy/non-payment-of-rent.md`)

## Practical sequence tomorrow

Calculate arrears precisely; check your tenancy agreement and condition report; serve a properly-drafted, signed 7-day notice with documented service; keep open a parallel payment-plan conversation (many cases resolve here); if unresolved after 7 days, prepare the Petty Debts Court application and consider engaging a Jersey advocate, especially if the tenant is represented. (`jersey/use-cases/tenant-landlord/evicting-non-paying-tenant.md`)
