## Drug-driving in Jersey

### The position today (May 2026)

Driving while **impaired by drugs** is an offence under the **Road Traffic (Jersey) Law 1956**. Prosecution runs on an **impairment** theory — police must prove the driver was unfit to drive properly, on objective evidence (poor control, failed field sobriety tests, drugs in blood or urine). Crucially, Jersey has **no statutory drug-concentration limits**, **no roadside drug-screening power** (beyond impairment-based field tests), and **no per se offence** of being over a numeric limit regardless of impairment. That makes the evidential threshold higher than for drink-driving's mechanical "over the limit" rule. ([jersey/road-traffic/drug-driving.md](jersey/road-traffic/drug-driving.md))

The offence is **substance-neutral** — impairment by controlled drugs (cannabis, cocaine, MDMA, heroin), prescription medication (sedatives, opioids, benzodiazepines), OTC medicines, or a drug/alcohol combination all qualify. A lawful prescription is not a defence to impairment. Sentencing tracks drink-driving: **mandatory disqualification** (typically 12+ months on a first offence), fine up to **£2,000**, imprisonment up to **6 months**, and licence endorsement. ([jersey/road-traffic/drug-driving.md](jersey/road-traffic/drug-driving.md))

### What's coming

In **February 2026**, States representatives proposed legislation to bring Jersey closer to the English specified-limit regime by:

- introducing **statutory drug limits** for controlled and some prescription substances;
- giving police **roadside testing powers** (swab tests akin to UK drug-wipes);
- creating **per se offences** (over the limit = the offence, no impairment proof needed);
- imposing **mandatory disqualification** on conviction.

The proposal is **not yet enacted** as of May 2026; the States Assembly's timetable suggests possible introduction during 2026 or 2027. Until then, the impairment-based regime continues to apply. ([jersey/road-traffic/drug-driving.md](jersey/road-traffic/drug-driving.md))
