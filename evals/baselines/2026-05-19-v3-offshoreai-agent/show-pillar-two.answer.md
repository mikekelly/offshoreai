# Pillar Two applied to a Jersey TopCo above a UK operating business

## Threshold — does Pillar Two apply at all?

Pillar Two only bites where the **MNE group has consolidated revenue of €750m+** in at least two of the four preceding fiscal years (`jersey/tax/pillar-two.md`; `jersey/international/pillar-two-mcit.md`). For a Jersey TopCo / UK OpCo structure **below** that threshold, nothing changes: the Jersey TopCo continues under zero-ten (0% in most cases) and there is no Pillar Two filing (`jersey/use-cases/founder-entrepreneur/pillar-two-impact.md`).

The rest of this answer assumes the group is **in-scope** (€750m+).

## Where the top-up is collected — Jersey, not the UK

Pillar Two operates through three coordinated rules: **IIR** (top-up at the parent jurisdiction), **UTPR** (residual backstop), and **QDMTT** (top-up paid in the low-taxed jurisdiction itself, which then offsets IIR/UTPR elsewhere) (`jersey/tax/pillar-two.md`).

Jersey implemented the **Multinational Corporate Income Tax (Jersey) Law 2024 ("MCIT")**, effective for financial years commencing on or after **1 January 2025**, structured as a Jersey **QDMTT-equivalent** (an Income Inclusion Rule plus a Domestic Top-up Tax) (`jersey/tax/pillar-two.md`; `jersey/international/pillar-two-mcit.md`).

The practical consequence for a Jersey TopCo / UK OpCo group:

- The **Jersey TopCo's** GloBE-jurisdictional ETR (typically very low under zero-ten, often 0%) is **topped up to 15% inside Jersey** by the MCIT, rather than the UK or any intermediate jurisdiction collecting it under IIR/UTPR (`jersey/international/pillar-two-mcit.md`).
- The **UK OpCo** sits in a separate GloBE jurisdiction. The UK has its own Pillar Two implementation (multinational top-up tax and domestic top-up tax). UK corporation tax on the trading profits will normally exceed the 15% GloBE floor, so the UK jurisdictional ETR is usually fine on its own and the question is really about what happens to the **Jersey-booked** income above it.

Result: the top-up that the UK (or any other parent jurisdiction in the chain) might otherwise have collected via IIR on the Jersey TopCo's low-taxed income is instead **collected in Jersey via MCIT**, because Jersey's QDMTT is designed to be creditable against foreign IIR/UTPR (`jersey/international/pillar-two-mcit.md`; `jersey/tax/pillar-two.md`).

## Interaction with zero-ten

Zero-ten is not displaced — it remains the Jersey **local-tax** position (0% / 10% / 20% by category). MCIT is an **additional** layer that sits on top for in-scope groups, ensuring the Jersey jurisdictional ETR reaches 15% (`jersey/tax/pillar-two.md`). For a typical 0% Jersey TopCo above a UK OpCo, that means: Jersey local tax = 0%; MCIT top-up ≈ 15% of GloBE income; total Jersey tax ≈ 15%.

If the Jersey TopCo is a **financial-services company** (10% zero-ten band) or a **utility/large retailer** (20%), MCIT still tops up to 15% only where the GloBE ETR is below that floor (`jersey/international/pillar-two-mcit.md`).

## Mechanics for the Jersey constituent entity

In-scope Jersey constituent entities must (`jersey/tax/pillar-two.md`; `jersey/international/pillar-two-mcit.md`):

- **Register** as MCIT taxpayers;
- **Compute GloBE income** from consolidated financial-accounting income with prescribed adjustments (not the same as Jersey local taxable income);
- **Calculate the Jersey jurisdictional ETR** under GloBE methodology;
- **Apply the substance-based income exclusion** (formulaic payroll + tangible-assets carve-out, taper sliding in early years) — this is often immaterial for a holding-only TopCo because it has little payroll or tangible assets in Jersey;
- **Pay the top-up** and file the MCIT return plus coordinate with the GloBE Information Return (GIR) at group level.

## Strategic points specific to a Jersey TopCo above UK trade

Drawing the threads together (`jersey/use-cases/founder-entrepreneur/pillar-two-impact.md`; `jersey/use-cases/international-lawyer/jersey-topco-ma.md`):

- **The headline "0% Jersey rate" pitch disappears** for in-scope groups — the effective rate on Jersey-booked income becomes ~15%.
- **Other Jersey advantages survive**: no Jersey CGT on a share-sale of the TopCo, no Jersey stamp duty on share transfers, Article 115 distribution flexibility, listing acceptance, and the Companies (Jersey) Law 1991 corporate toolkit. These are unaffected by Pillar Two.
- **Migration is a live question.** Some groups crossing the threshold reassess Jersey TopCo vs an onshore (UK / Irish) TopCo — Pillar Two narrows the rate gap, so the case turns on the non-tax Jersey advantages and migration cost. **Continuance** under Companies Law Part 18C is the standard exit mechanism.
- **Substance still matters separately.** Jersey's economic-substance regime continues alongside MCIT — it is not subsumed by Pillar Two.
