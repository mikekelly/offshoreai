## What a Parish Hall Enquiry is

A **Parish Hall Enquiry** is a pre-court forum unique to Jersey, presided over by a **Centenier** — an elected, unpaid honorary police officer of one of Jersey's 12 parishes. It is the route by which the **majority of routine motoring and minor criminal matters** are resolved without ever reaching the Magistrate's Court. It has no direct equivalent in the UK or other common-law systems (`jersey/road-traffic/parish-hall-enquiries.md`).

The Centenier reviews the evidence, decides whether to charge, and — if the matter falls within Parish Hall scope — can **caution**, **issue a written fine of up to £200 per offence**, or **refer the case to Magistrate's Court**. A fine or caution can only be imposed if the defendant **admits** the offence **and accepts** the penalty; otherwise the matter must go to court.

## When your case goes there

Typical Parish Hall matter (`jersey/road-traffic/parish-hall-enquiries.md`):

- Minor speeding (under 15 mph over the limit)
- Mobile-phone offences
- Failing to display an insurance disc
- Some traffic-light offences
- Minor careless driving (some cases)
- Parking offences

## When it does **not** go to Parish Hall

The Parish Hall route is excluded for, and the case is sent straight to **Magistrate's Court**, when the offence involves (`jersey/road-traffic/parish-hall-enquiries.md`):

- Speeding **15 mph or more** over the limit
- **Drink-driving** (any reading) or **drug-driving**
- **Dangerous driving** (Article 23 RTJL 1956)
- Causing death or serious injury by driving
- Driving while disqualified
- Failing to stop / report after a serious accident
- Any offence carrying **mandatory disqualification**
- Any matter where the Centenier judges the £200 ceiling inadequate

## Your key procedural rights

You can **bring a friend, family member, or advocate**, **decline to admit** the offence, and at any point **demand that the case be heard at Magistrate's Court instead** — including appealing a Parish Hall outcome by requesting a Magistrate's Court re-hearing.
