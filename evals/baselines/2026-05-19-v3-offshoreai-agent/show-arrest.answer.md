# Your rights on arrest in Jersey

Your rights are set out in the **Police Procedures and Criminal Evidence (Jersey) Law 2003** (PPCE 2003) and its Codes of Practice. The single most important practical step: **ask for a solicitor immediately and decline to answer substantive questions until you've spoken to one**.

## Headline rights

| Right | Position |
|---|---|
| **Free legal advice** | 24/7 duty solicitor or your own choice; free; confidential; before any interview. Delay only on specific statutory grounds in very serious cases, authorised at senior level. |
| **Inform someone** | One person told of your arrest and location, at public expense. Delayable on narrow grounds at inspector level. |
| **Consular notification** | If a foreign national, your consulate/High Commission can be notified. |
| **Right to silence** | Caution: *"You are not obliged to say anything unless you wish to do so, but what you say may be put into writing and given in evidence."* No adverse inference from police-station silence is typically drawn (narrower than England post-2003). |
| **Written notice of rights** | Provided by the custody officer in your language on arrival. |
| **Custody record** | Comprehensive log of every event; sight on request during detention; copy on release. |
| **Interpreter** | If you don't speak English well enough, at public expense, before any interview. |
| **Appropriate adult** | For juveniles under 18, mentally vulnerable persons, or those with significant learning difficulties — interview cannot proceed without one (save defined urgent circumstances). |
| **Medical attention** | If unwell, injured, or needing medication. |
| **Physical conditions** | Meals, drinking water, toilet access, heat/light; **at least 8 continuous hours' rest** in any 24-hour period. |

## Detention limits

| Stage | Limit | Authorised by |
|---|---|---|
| Initial | **24 hours** | Custody officer |
| Extension for serious offences | up to **36 hours** | Police inspector or higher |
| Warrant of further detention | up to **96 hours total** | Royal Court (Magistrate) |
| Beyond 96 hours | Charge or release required | — |

Detention beyond these limits is unlawful and grounds for habeas corpus / wrongful-imprisonment claim. The custody officer must review detention at prescribed intervals.

## Interview safeguards

- **Open audio recording** under Code E; caution at the start of each session.
- **No oppression** (raised voice, intimidation, prolonged questioning prohibited).
- **Solicitor present** if requested; if you change your mind mid-interview, questioning must stop.
- Police must not interview you if you are too intoxicated, unwell, or distressed to follow.

## If a right is breached

Tell your solicitor immediately, note times and details, formal complaint after release, and seek **exclusion of resulting evidence** at any trial; serious breaches may ground a civil claim.
