## Jersey corporate tax for your group

### The headline rate — 0%

A Jersey-incorporated trading company is taxed at the **0% default standard rate** under **Article 123C(2)** of the Income Tax (Jersey) Law 1961. The 10% rate applies only to defined "financial services companies" (regulated banking, TCB, fund services, investment business, insurance permit holders, etc. — Article 123D), and the 20% rate is reserved for a closed list of categories: utility companies (Article 123C(3)), large corporate retailers with £2m+ Jersey turnover and £500k+ profits (Article 123I), hydrocarbon oil trades (Article 123CAA(1)), cannabis trades (Article 123DA), and income from Jersey land. A generic trading group sits outside those carve-outs and is taxed at **0%**.

**Caveat — check the carve-outs apply to you.** "Trading group" is a broad description. If your Jersey-incorporated entities are in fact carrying on a retail trade in Jersey above the £2m gateway, importing/supplying hydrocarbons, in cannabis, or earning income from Jersey land, one of the 20% rates would bite. The 0% answer assumes your trade falls outside all those categories.

Citation: `knowledge/jersey/tax/zero-ten.md`.

### Pillar Two — yes, it adds a top-up

Your group revenue (~EUR 900m) **is above** the **EUR 750m consolidated-revenue threshold** that brings in-scope multinational groups within Jersey's **Multinational Corporate Income Tax (MCIT)**, effective **1 January 2025**. MCIT is Jersey's **Qualified Domestic Minimum Top-up Tax (QDMTT)** under the OECD GloBE rules: it tops Jersey-source profits up to a **15% effective rate**.

So in practical terms:

- Underlying Jersey rate on your trading profits: **0%** under Article 123C(2).
- **MCIT top-up:** Jersey collects the difference to reach a **15% effective tax rate** on the in-scope Jersey profits (assuming the EUR 750m threshold is met in at least 2 of the 4 preceding years, per the standard GloBE scope test).
- The substance-based income exclusion (formulaic carve-out for payroll + tangible assets, sliding-scale in early years) may reduce the GloBE tax base; the international-shipping exclusion may apply if relevant.

Jersey has implemented MCIT as a **QDMTT** and an **IIR** for in-scope groups; it has **not** implemented a UTPR. If MCIT were not in place, the top-up would be collected by the ultimate-parent jurisdiction's IIR instead — MCIT simply keeps the revenue in Jersey.
