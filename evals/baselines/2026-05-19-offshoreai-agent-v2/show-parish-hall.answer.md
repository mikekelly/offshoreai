## What a Parish Hall Enquiry is

A **Parish Hall Enquiry** is a distinctive Jersey **pre-court forum** presided over by a **Centenier** — an elected, unpaid honorary police officer of the parish where the alleged offence took place. It is the route by which the **majority of routine Jersey motoring offences are resolved without ever reaching the Magistrate's Court**. There is no direct equivalent in the UK or other common-law jurisdictions, so don't assume it works like a UK fixed-penalty notice or English court (`jersey/road-traffic/parish-hall-enquiries.md`).

The Centenier reviews the evidence, decides whether there is a case, and — crucially — may only impose a sanction if the **defendant admits the offence and accepts the proposed penalty**. The Centenier's powers are: caution (no further action), a written fine of **up to £200 per offence**, or referral to Magistrate's Court. A defendant can opt out at any point and demand a Magistrate's Court hearing instead.

## When your case goes to Parish Hall

Routine motoring matters typically dealt with at Parish Hall include:

- Minor speeding (under 15 mph over the limit)
- Mobile phone offences
- Failing to display insurance disc
- Traffic-light offences (in some cases)
- Minor careless driving (in some cases)
- Parking offences

## When it does *not* go to Parish Hall

The Parish Hall route is **excluded** for, among others:

- Speeding **15 mph or more** over the limit
- **Drink-driving** (any reading) or drug-driving
- **Dangerous driving** (Article 23 RTJL 1956)
- Causing death or serious injury by driving
- Driving while disqualified
- Any offence carrying **mandatory disqualification**
- Any offence the Centenier judges to exceed the £200 ceiling

These are referred directly to **Magistrate's Court** by summons.
