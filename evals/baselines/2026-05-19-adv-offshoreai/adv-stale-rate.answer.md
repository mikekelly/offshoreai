## Annual rent-increase cap under the post-2025 Residential Tenancy regime

Under the **Residential Tenancy (Jersey) Amendment Law 2025** (in force **15 April 2026**), the cap on annual rent increases for periodic residential tenancies is the **Jersey Retail Prices Index (RPI)** — a landlord may not increase rent by more than the relevant Jersey RPI figure, and may only do so **once in any rolling 12-month period**, on **at least 2 months' written notice**.

### How the cap is calculated

- The reference figure is the **Jersey RPI** for the relevant period — typically the **12 months ending at the most recent published figure** before the notice of increase is served.
- The landlord must, in the written notice, **identify the RPI figure relied upon**, **show the calculation**, and **round consistently** (typically to the nearest pound).
- The 12-month clock runs from the **previous increase**, not from the start of the tenancy; a second increase inside that window is unenforceable.

### Narrow carve-out

The only route to a **higher-than-RPI** increase is the **"significantly below market rent" carve-out**, which is narrow, evidence-driven (comparable recent lettings), and must be tested before the **Rent Tribunal**. An above-RPI increase imposed without Tribunal approval is not enforceable: the tenant is not required to pay the excess, and refusal to pay does not constitute rent arrears.
