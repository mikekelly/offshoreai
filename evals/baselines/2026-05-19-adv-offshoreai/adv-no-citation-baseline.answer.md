## The corpus does not give a specific years-of-experience figure

The Jersey corpus describes the JFSC's fit-and-proper test for principal persons of a TCB in qualitative terms but does **not** quote a specific quantum of trust officer experience (e.g. "five years" or "ten years") that the JFSC typically expects. Both relevant files are also marked `draft`, so I will not cite them as settled authority.

### What the corpus does say

For principal-person approval at a Jersey TCB, the JFSC applies a fit-and-proper assessment by reference to:

- **Honesty, integrity and reputation**;
- **Competence and capability for the role**;
- **Financial soundness**;
- **Regulatory history** in any jurisdiction;
- **Criminal record**;
- **Conflicts of interest**;
- **Track record in prior similar roles**.

The test is applied both at the individual level and at the level of the organisation as a whole, and runs as a **continuing** obligation — adverse events (criminal conviction, regulatory action, insolvency) trigger re-assessment ([`jersey/financial-regulation/licensing.md`](jersey/financial-regulation/licensing.md), draft, last verified 2026-05-15).

A new principal person also requires **prior JFSC consent before the appointment takes effect**, supported by a principal-person questionnaire and references ([`jersey/use-cases/trust-officer/fit-and-proper-changes.md`](jersey/use-cases/trust-officer/fit-and-proper-changes.md), draft, last verified 2026-05-15).

### What the corpus does not cover

The corpus does **not** quote:

- a minimum number of years of fiduciary / trust officer experience the JFSC looks for;
- expected professional qualifications (e.g. STEP, ICSA / CGI, ACCA);
- whether expectations differ between principals of a large bank-owned TCB and a boutique TCB;
- how the JFSC treats overseas trust experience as a substitute for Jersey experience.

These are the kinds of specifics that, in practice, sit in the **JFSC Codes of Practice for Trust Company Business** and the JFSC's **fit-and-proper guidance notes** — primary sources the corpus references but does not reproduce the quantitative detail of.

### Caveats and recommendation

- Both files cited above are status `draft` — drafted but not yet editorially verified against the primary sources. Treat them as orientation, not authority.
- For a definitive answer on what trust officer experience the JFSC expects in practice for principal-person approval, verify directly against the JFSC TCB Code of Practice and the JFSC fit-and-proper guidance (jerseyfsc.org), and consider speaking to JFSC supervision in advance for novel cases.
