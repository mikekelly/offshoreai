## What the corpus does say about Article 47G and amendment history

The Article 47B–47J statutory mistake regime — including **Article 47G** (set-aside of an exercise of powers in relation to a trust for mistake) — was **introduced by the Trusts (Amendment No. 6) (Jersey) Law 2013**. The corpus describes the regime as a single statutory mechanism enacted by that amendment, not by any later amendment:

> "The regime was introduced by the **Trusts (Amendment No. 6) (Jersey) Law 2013** to put on a clear statutory footing the Royal Court's previous case-law-developed approach to mistake."
> — `jersey/trusts/article-47-set-aside.md`

The Articles Index reinforces this: "These nine Articles work as a single statutory mechanism introduced by the 2013 amendment" (`jersey/trusts/articles-index.md`). The same index identifies the two most material amendments to the Trusts Law as **Amendment No. 4 of 2006** (reserved powers, perpetuities) and **Amendment No. 6 of 2013** (mistake regime) — Amendment No. 7 is not mentioned.

The closest the corpus comes to acknowledging post-2013 amendment activity is a note in the Law overview that the Government published a **2024 consultation on amendments**, with re-verification recommended against the States Assembly (`jersey/trusts/trusts-law-1984.md`).
