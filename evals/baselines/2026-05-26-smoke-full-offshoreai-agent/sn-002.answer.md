Under the MLO 2008, a "new institutional client" — read as a **regulated financial institution** counterparty — still attracts the full Article 5 CDD obligation; what differs is the **calibration** the JFSC AML/CFT Handbook permits when the institution is regulated in an equivalent jurisdiction.

## Core MLO 2008 obligations (apply to every new customer)

Article 5 requires the supervised business to apply CDD measures comprising:

- **Identification** of the customer and any agent;
- **Verification** of identity using reliable independent sources;
- **Identification** of the **beneficial owner** (where the customer is not an individual), with verification to a reasonable extent;
- **Information** on the **purpose and intended nature** of the relationship (Article 9 — purpose, expected volume / frequency / geography, source of funds, source of wealth for higher-risk);
- **Ongoing monitoring** (Article 10 — transaction monitoring, periodic refresh, trigger-based review).

(`knowledge/jersey/aml-cft/mlo-article-5.md`; `knowledge/jersey/aml-cft/mlo-part-3-cdd.md`)

Article 6 confirms the trigger: full CDD is required **at establishment** of the relationship, regardless of any de minimis threshold, and must be **completed before** the relationship proceeds beyond preliminary steps (limited delayed-completion exceptions apply). (`knowledge/jersey/aml-cft/mlo-article-6.md`)

## Calibration for an institutional / regulated-institution customer

If the new client is itself a **bank, MiFID-equivalent investment firm, AIFMD-equivalent fund manager, Solvency-II-equivalent insurer, or licensed TCB/CSP** in an **equivalent jurisdiction** (FATF member / clean MoneyVal record / on any JFSC-published equivalent list, and not FATF Black/Grey), the Handbook permits a more targeted approach:

- **Identification**: legal name and structure via public registry; **regulatory licence(s)** verified on the home regulator's public register (FCA, BaFin, SFC, etc.); senior officers / beneficial owners identified to a depth appropriate to risk (often light for listed groups, fuller for private institutions); recent regulatory standing / enforcement history.
- **Simplified Due Diligence (SDD)** may be available for **listed financial institutions** on recognised markets (beneficial-owner ID typically not required), **wholly-owned subsidiaries** of regulated entities, and **government-owned** financial institutions in equivalent jurisdictions. SDD reduces verification — it does **not remove** identification or monitoring.
- **Reliance regime**: where the institutional customer introduces or holds underlying clients through the relationship, the Jersey firm may rely on the institution's own CDD on those underlying clients **only if** (i) the institution is regulated for AML/CFT in an equivalent jurisdiction, (ii) there is a **written reliance arrangement**, (iii) the Jersey firm is satisfied the institution applies equivalent standards, and (iv) the Jersey firm can **obtain CDD records on demand**. Accountability stays with the Jersey firm; ongoing monitoring and JFSC/LEA record-production obligations are not delegated.

A customer regulated in a **non-equivalent** jurisdiction reverts to full CDD with likely EDD. (`knowledge/jersey/aml-cft/amlh-customer-regulated-institution.md`)

## Operational implications

- Capture the Article 9 information (purpose, expected activity, source of funds / wealth) even for a regulated counterparty — it anchors monitoring and risk rating.
- Document the equivalent-jurisdiction assessment and SDD eligibility; do not assume.
- If relying, paper the reliance arrangement and **test record-access** rather than waiting for a JFSC request.
