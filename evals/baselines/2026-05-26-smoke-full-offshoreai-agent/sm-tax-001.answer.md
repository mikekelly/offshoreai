## The headline answer

A Jersey-incorporated trading group is taxed at the **0% standard rate** under Article 123C(2) of the Income Tax (Jersey) Law 1961 — that is the default for any Jersey company that is not a financial-services company (10%), a utility (20%), a large corporate retailer (20% with taper), a hydrocarbon or cannabis trader (20%), or in receipt of Jersey land / property income (20%). A pure trading group sits in the default bucket. (`knowledge/jersey/tax/zero-ten.md`)

## Pillar Two — yes, it applies on top

At ~€900m consolidated revenue you are over the **€750m** Pillar Two threshold (which bites where the group is at or above €750m in at least 2 of the 4 preceding years). Jersey has implemented Pillar Two as the **Multinational Corporate Income Tax (MCIT)** with effect from accounting periods beginning on or after **1 January 2025**, structured as a **Qualified Domestic Minimum Top-up Tax (QDMTT)** plus an Income Inclusion Rule. The effect for an in-scope group is that the **effective Jersey rate becomes 15%** — Jersey collects the top-up itself rather than ceding it to the parent's home jurisdiction. (`knowledge/jersey/international/pillar-two-mcit.md`)

So in concrete terms for your group:

- **Headline domestic rate:** 0% under Article 123C(2)
- **Pillar Two overlay:** because you are over €750m, MCIT tops the Jersey effective rate up to **15%** on in-scope profits
- **Substance:** the Taxation (Companies — Economic Substance) (Jersey) Law 2019 sits alongside this and is a separate obligation — verify substance compliance independently (`knowledge/jersey/tax/zero-ten.md`)

## Caveats

- Both corpus files I have cited are **`status: draft`** — the content is drafted but flagged as not yet at `stable`/`review` editorial state. Verify the MCIT mechanics and the precise scope-test against the Income Tax (Jersey) Law 1961 as amended and Revenue Jersey guidance before relying on this for a filing position.
- The €750m test looks at consolidated group revenue in **at least 2 of the 4 preceding years** — "around €900m" current revenue is likely in scope, but the lookback rule matters if revenue has fluctuated.
- The €750m threshold is a **group** test on the consolidated ultimate parent. The framing "we're a Jersey-incorporated trading group" implies the Jersey entity is at or near the top of the group; if it is instead a sub-group of a larger MNE, scope is tested at the ultimate-parent level.
- Both files are reasonably fresh (last_verified 2026-05-14 and 2026-05-17 against today 2026-05-26), but for a live tax position the primary sources (jerseylaw.je for the ITL 1961 as amended, gov.je Revenue Jersey for MCIT guidance) should be checked.
