## Article 19 vs Article 21 — Terrorism (Jersey) Law 2002 SAR duties

Both articles create a failure-to-disclose offence in respect of terrorism-financing (specifically suspected offences under [Articles 15, 16, or 16A](knowledge/jersey/aml-cft/terrorism-article-19.md)), but they apply to **different populations** and use a **different mental-element test**.

### Article 19 — general SAR duty

- **Who it applies to:** anyone whose information came to their attention in the **course of a trade, profession, business or employment** — i.e. the whole working population, not just the regulated sector.
- **Mental element:** the person **believes or suspects** that another person has committed an Article 15/16/16A offence — a **purely subjective** test.
- **Duty:** disclose to the FIU as soon as reasonably practicable.
- **Defence:** reasonable excuse.

Source: `knowledge/jersey/aml-cft/terrorism-article-19.md`.

### Article 21 — failure to disclose: financial institutions

- **Who it applies to:** persons in a **regulated business** as defined in Schedule 2 to POCL 1999 — i.e. the financial-institution / DNFBP perimeter.
- **Mental element:** the person knows, suspects, **or has reasonable grounds for knowing or suspecting** — adding an **objective limb** on top of Article 19's subjective test.
- **Duty:** disclose to the FIU as soon as reasonably practicable.
- **Defence:** reasonable excuse, including a legal-professional-privilege carve-out.

Source: `knowledge/jersey/aml-cft/terrorism-article-21.md`.

### The practical difference in one line

Article 19 catches **actual subjective suspicion** by anyone in any business; Article 21 additionally catches **negligent failure** by a regulated-sector person to spot the suspicious facts in front of them — that "reasonable grounds" limb is what drives JFSC AML/CFT Handbook CDD, monitoring, and SAR disciplines for CFT. Both shift to [Article 35](knowledge/jersey/aml-cft/terrorism-article-19.md) (tipping off) as the disclosure-side constraint once a SAR is filed; the POCL Article 34A ML counterpart sits in parallel.
