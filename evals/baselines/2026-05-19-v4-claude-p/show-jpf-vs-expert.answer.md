## Recommendation: **JPF** is the default for a Jersey club-deal PE fund

The corpus answers this question quite directly. The Jersey Private Fund regime is purpose-built for exactly this fact pattern, and "club deal structures" is explicitly named as one of its default use cases.

### Why JPF fits a club deal

A club deal — by definition a small number of known, sophisticated co-investors aligned around a specific transaction or strategy — sits squarely inside the JPF design envelope:

- **Investor cap of up to 50 offers/investors** at any time, which comfortably accommodates the typical club's 5–25 LPs. The cap is on *offers* as well as holders, so structure your fundraise accordingly.
- **Professional / eligible investors only** — appropriate for the institutional and HNW participants typical of club deals.
- **48-hour fast-track JFSC authorisation**, which matters when a club deal is organised around a specific opportunity with a real closing window.
- **Designated Service Provider (DSP) substitution model** — a Jersey-licensed administrator carries the CDD/AML and ongoing supervision burden, so the fund itself is light-touch from the manager's perspective.
- **Structural flexibility** to use a Jersey LP (the standard PE form), a no-par-value company, a unit trust, or a PCC/ICC cell company, picked on investor tax preference.

The fund-counsel use-case file is unambiguous on the scenario: *"PE / VC fund with up to 50 LPs: JPF. Fast, light, costs aligned with closed-ended PE economics."* It explicitly flags going Expert Fund-heavy for a club deal as a pitfall — *"a public-fund-style filing for a club deal is expensive and delays time-to-market."*

### When you would step up to an Expert Fund instead

Per the comparison table and the Expert Fund concept file, you'd move to Expert Fund only if:

- The club is likely to exceed **50 investors/offers** (Expert has no specific cap, subject to expert criteria);
- You need **wider expert-investor marketing latitude** beyond limited private placement; or
- The deal requires fuller **AIFMD NPPR** positioning for EU marketing (Expert is explicitly identified as supporting this; JPF marketing is described as "limited private placement").

The Expert Fund authorisation timeline is "few days to weeks" rather than ~48 hours, the offering document is a fuller offering memorandum rather than a light PPM, and disclosure expectations are higher. The "Going too light" pitfall warns that if you organise a JPF and then breach 50 investors, the JFSC will re-categorise you — so size honestly at outset.

### Practical answer for a club deal

Default to **JPF, structured as a Jersey limited partnership** (the most common PE form per the JPF concept file), with a Jersey-licensed administrator as DSP. Only reach for Expert Fund if your club is genuinely scaling past ~50 LPs/offers, or if EU NPPR distribution is core to the strategy.
