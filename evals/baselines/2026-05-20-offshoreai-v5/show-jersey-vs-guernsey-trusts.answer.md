This is a comparative question, so I'll pull the Jersey firewall/reserved-powers files and the Guernsey trust-law-differences file in parallel, then check freshness.## Jersey vs Guernsey trust — when to pick which

The honest headline: **for most cross-border family-wealth structures the two regimes are competitive substitutes.** Both descend from a shared Norman customary heritage, both have been modernised (Jersey by the Trusts (Jersey) Law 1984; Guernsey by the Trusts (Guernsey) Law 2007), and they now overlap heavily — discretionary/fixed-interest/purpose trusts, settlor reserved powers, a statutory firewall, *Schmidt v Rosewood* beneficiary-information principles, perpetual duration, and a Royal Court supervisory jurisdiction with final appeal to the Privy Council all exist in both (`knowledge/guernsey/trust-law-differences-from-jersey.md`).

The choice usually turns on a handful of differentiators.

### Lean Jersey when…

- **You want maximum forced-heirship / matrimonial-claim defence.** Jersey's **Article 9 firewall** is the gold standard. Article 9(1) sends validity, capacity, administration, and beneficial-rights questions to Jersey law alone; Article 9(2) disregards whether a foreign law recognises trusts or treats the disposition as defeating foreign-law rights; and the 2006-inserted **Article 9(4)** makes foreign judgments inconsistent with the Article unenforceable in Jersey. Guernsey's **s.14** is comparable in effect but **less explicit on foreign-judgment non-recognition**, so sophisticated maximum-defence structures still marginally prefer Jersey (`knowledge/jersey/trusts/firewall.md`; `knowledge/guernsey/trust-law-differences-from-jersey.md`).
- **You expect to rely on the statutory mistake / *Hastings-Bass* regime.** Both jurisdictions have codified a setting-aside regime wider than the English *Pitt v Holt* approach, but **Jersey's case law under Article 47B–J is more developed** (the *Re S&T Trusts* three-limb test and its progeny) (`knowledge/jersey/trusts/article-47-set-aside.md`; `knowledge/guernsey/trust-law-differences-from-jersey.md`).
- **You need deep reserved-powers / US-grantor-trust drafting.** Jersey's **Article 9A** provides a wide, specific statutory menu (revocation, variation, advancement, trustee direction, beneficiary appointment, change of proper law, etc.), confirms the trust still takes immediate effect, protects the trustee who acts on a reserved power, and confirms the settlor is not thereby a trustee. Guernsey has equivalent provisions; outcomes are broadly the same, but Jersey's menu is the more frequently-cited reference (`knowledge/jersey/trusts/reserved-powers.md`).
- **Trust-company industry depth matters** — Jersey's TCB industry is the larger of the two.

### Lean Guernsey when…

- **You want a PCC *or* ICC for the trustee/structuring layer** — Guernsey offers both the protected cell company and the incorporated cell company; Jersey offers the PCC.
- **The structure pairs with a Guernsey fund** (PIF rather than Jersey's JPF), or existing administrator infrastructure / service-provider relationships sit in Guernsey.
- **Guernsey-specific assets** (e.g. a Guernsey image-rights register interaction) or sponsor familiarity point that way.

### What does *not* differentiate them

EU-marketing posture (both run NPPR routes), confidentiality (both private, both with beneficial-ownership registers accessible to authorities), and duration (both permit perpetual trusts) are effectively equivalent (`knowledge/guernsey/trust-law-differences-from-jersey.md`).

In practice the deciding factors are often **sponsor familiarity, existing service-provider relationships, group/administrator location, and specific asset or family considerations** rather than a doctrinal gap.
