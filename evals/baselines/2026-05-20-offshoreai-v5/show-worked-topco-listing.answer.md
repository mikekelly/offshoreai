## Jersey TopCo above UK Opco → LSE listing

**End-state structure**: public investors → **Jersey TopCo (listed)** → **UK Opco (100%)**.

**Step 1 — Incorporate the Jersey TopCo.** A Jersey public company limited by shares under the Companies (Jersey) Law 1991, with IPO-ready M&A, no-par-value shares, at least two directors, a Jersey-resident corporate services provider for the registered office, and beneficial-ownership lodged with the (private) Companies Registry. Incorporation takes ~1–3 days.

**Step 2 — Share-for-share exchange.** UK TechCo shareholders exchange their shares for TopCo shares (e.g. 1:1); TopCo ends up owning 100% of UK Opco. Tax flags: UK rollover relief under TCGA s.135 generally defers CGT (subject to anti-avoidance); 0.5% UK stamp duty may arise on the transfer of UK shares (reliefs may apply for genuine restructurings); no Jersey corporate tax issue on the exchange.

**Step 3 — TopCo tax position.** Jersey-resident company taxed under the zero-ten regime — **0%** for a generic holding company. A pure holding company gets the **reduced economic-substance test** (adequate people/premises in Jersey, low bar) under the Taxation (Companies — Economic Substance) (Jersey) Law 2019.

**Step 4 — Pillar Two check.** If consolidated group revenue ≥ EUR 750m, Jersey's MCIT Law 2025 (QDMTT-equivalent, 15%, effective 1 Jan 2025) tops the TopCo's Jersey income up to 15%, keeping the top-up in Jersey rather than ceding it to the UK IIR. Below the threshold, the 0% position continues.

**Step 5 — Capital structure.** CJL 1991 gives IPO-useful flexibility: multiple share classes (founder/investor/public), no-par-value and redeemable shares, treasury shares, buy-backs, and capital reductions via special resolution + solvency statement (less court-intensive than the UK).

**Step 6 — LSE listing.** Jersey TopCos are routinely accepted across Premium/Standard/High-Growth segments. UK Listing Rules, DTRs, MAR and the Prospectus Regulation apply; a prospectus (or AIM admission document) is required; the **UK Takeover Code applies** to Jersey companies admitted to a UK regulated market. Jersey legal opinions/corporate authorities feature in the prospectus.

**Step 7 — Post-IPO governance.** UK Corporate Governance Code applies; the JFSC does not generally regulate the TopCo unless it carries on regulated business; Jersey registry filings (annual return, BO) continue; keep at least one Jersey-resident director for substance.

**Step 8 — Dividends.** Solvency-based distributions under CJL 1991 (no UK-style retained-earnings test); typically **0%** Jersey withholding on dividends to non-resident shareholders (UK shareholders still taxed in the UK).

**Main pitfalls**: stamp-duty leakage on the exchange if not structured for relief; protecting TopCo's Jersey (not UK) tax residence via genuine Jersey-based management; Pillar Two if in scope; FCA prospectus/sponsor and market-abuse requirements; and avoiding a paper-only Jersey presence that fails substance.
