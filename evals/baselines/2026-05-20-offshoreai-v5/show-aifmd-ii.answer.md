I'll pull the AIFMD II frontier file and the Jersey NPPR doctrinal file, and check freshness on both since this is a "what's changing" question.## What changes for a Jersey AIFM marketing into the EU under NPPR

The headline: **NPPR access survives.** AIFMD II (Directive (EU) 2024/927) preserves Crown Dependencies' access to EU professional investors through the National Private Placement Regimes — Jersey continues to meet the EU's tax-transparency and AML criteria, so the country-by-country private-placement route is intact. ([`knowledge/frontier/aifmd-ii-april-2026.md`](knowledge/frontier/aifmd-ii-april-2026.md))

The directly-applicable changes from **16 April 2026** for a Jersey AIFM on the NPPR route are concentrated in two areas:

1. **Expanded investor disclosures** — pre-marketing and ongoing disclosure obligations widen to cover liquidity management, sustainability factors (interlocking with SFDR), and loan-originating-fund disclosures where applicable. PPMs and ongoing transparency reports need refreshing for AIFMD II language.
2. **Enhanced Annex IV reporting to ESMA** — significantly more granular regulatory reporting via competent authorities, new data fields, more detail on specific asset categories and risk exposures. First submissions under the new requirements are expected end-Q2 / early-Q3 2026.

([`knowledge/frontier/aifmd-ii-april-2026.md`](knowledge/frontier/aifmd-ii-april-2026.md))

### Indirect (delegation) impact — relevant if the structure relies on an EU AIFM

The stricter third-country **delegation** rules don't bite on a direct Jersey AIFM marketing under NPPR, but they reshape the *EU-AIFM-with-Jersey-delegate* model: the delegating EU AIFM faces enhanced substance/oversight obligations and cannot become a "letter-box entity." Counterintuitively, this makes a **direct Jersey AIFM with NPPR marketing comparatively more attractive** than an EU shell that depends on delegation to a Jersey portfolio manager — sponsors running the delegate model are revisiting direct Channel Islands domiciliation. ([`knowledge/frontier/aifmd-ii-april-2026.md`](knowledge/frontier/aifmd-ii-april-2026.md))

### Jersey-side regulatory response: the AIF Code split

The JFSC is **splitting its AIF Code of Practice** into an **EU AIF Code** (for Jersey AIFMs marketing into the EU via NPPR, aligned with AIFMD II) and a **UK AIF Code** (for UK marketing, aligned with the UK's post-Brexit framework). The EU AIF Code is expected to be finalised through 2026, so a Jersey AIFM should expect to align with the new EU Code as it lands. ([`knowledge/frontier/aifmd-ii-april-2026.md`](knowledge/frontier/aifmd-ii-april-2026.md))

### Two pitfalls flagged in the corpus
- **Don't assume NPPR is unaffected** — the route survives, but disclosure and Annex IV obligations have widened.
- **Member-state transposition is uneven** — Luxembourg/Ireland transposed closely; Germany/France stricter; others looser. A fund marketed across several EU states may face different effective requirements. ([`knowledge/frontier/aifmd-ii-april-2026.md`](knowledge/frontier/aifmd-ii-april-2026.md))

### Baseline mechanics (for context)
The underlying NPPR mechanics are unchanged: the AIFM registers with the JFSC under Jersey's AIFMD-equivalent regime, demonstrates AIFMD-equivalent compliance (capital adequacy, risk management, conflicts, depository, transparency, reporting), then notifies each target member state's regulator country-by-country. There is still **no full AIFMD passport** for Jersey AIFMs — the proposed third-country passport was never implemented. ([`knowledge/jersey/funds/aifmd-nppr.md`](knowledge/jersey/funds/aifmd-nppr.md))
