## Do you need planning permission for a rear extension on your Jersey house?

**Probably yes — unless the extension is modest enough to fall entirely within "permitted development."** Single-storey rear extensions are one of the categories that *can* be permitted-development, but only if they stay within the specific size, depth, height, eaves-height, boundary-distance and materials conditions set out in the **Planning and Building (General Development) (Jersey) Order 2011**. Exceed any one of those conditions — or build a **two-storey** rear extension (which needs a full application regardless of size) — and you need planning permission under the **Planning and Building (Jersey) Law 2002**. (`knowledge/jersey/use-cases/jersey-resident/extension-planning-permission.md`; `knowledge/jersey/planning/permitted-development.md`)

### Key points

- **Two consents, not one.** Permitted-development cover only relieves you from *planning permission*. You still need separate **building approval** under the **Building Bye-Laws (Jersey) 2007** for the technical standards (structure, insulation, fire, drainage). (`knowledge/jersey/use-cases/jersey-resident/extension-planning-permission.md`; `knowledge/jersey/planning/permitted-development.md`)

- **Special areas strip away the exemption.** If your house is a **listed building**, in a **conservation area**, or in the **Green Zone / Coastal National Park**, most permitted-development rights don't apply and even a small rear extension will usually need a full application. The Minister can also remove permitted-development rights for specific locations by an **Article 31 direction**. (`knowledge/jersey/planning/permitted-development.md`)

- **Don't assume "small" = permitted.** The Order's numeric limits are often more restrictive than intuition, and this is the most common mistake. (`knowledge/jersey/planning/permitted-development.md`)

- **If unsure, get certainty.** Apply for a **Certificate of Lawful Development** (lower fee than a full application) to confirm your proposal is within permitted development before you build. The Department for the Environment also runs a free pre-application drop-in. (`knowledge/jersey/planning/permitted-development.md`; `knowledge/jersey/use-cases/jersey-resident/extension-planning-permission.md`)

- **Building without permission is criminal.** Unauthorised development under the PBL 2002 can mean unlimited fines, an enforcement notice requiring removal at your cost, and problems on sale, mortgage and insurance. (`knowledge/jersey/use-cases/jersey-resident/extension-planning-permission.md`)

- **Check your title too.** A **restrictive covenant** can prohibit the works even where planning would permit them — a separate private-law matter. (`knowledge/jersey/planning/permitted-development.md`)

### Caveats

- The corpus does **not** record the exact numeric thresholds (maximum floor area, projection depth, height) in the GD Order 2011 — it flags that you must "check the current Order for exact wording and conditions." For a go/no-go decision on a specific design, those figures need to be read from the Order itself.
