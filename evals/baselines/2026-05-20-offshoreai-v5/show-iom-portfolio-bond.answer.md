## Why a UK HNW investor holds an IoM portfolio bond

The portfolio bond is the Isle of Man's defining life-assurance product: a **single-premium investment wrapped in a life-assurance policy**, into which you can hold a broad range of underlying assets (funds, equities, fixed income, alternatives), typically directed by your investment manager. The reasons a UK HNW investor uses one:

1. **The UK 5% rule (the core tax driver).** You can withdraw **5% of the original premium each year, tax-deferred, for up to 20 years** — no UK income tax at the point of withdrawal. The tax charge crystallises only at a "chargeable event" (final encashment), when the gain is treated as deferred income. **Top-slicing relief** then reduces the effective rate for higher-/additional-rate payers. This gives materially better cash-flow tax treatment than holding the same assets directly. The corpus attributes the statutory basis to Chapter 9 of Part 4 ITTOIA 2005 (UK law — outside this corpus's primary scope, so verify independently).

2. **Tax deferral on growth inside the wrapper** — gains roll up without an annual UK tax drag, alongside ISAs and pensions as a long-term planning layer.

3. **Estate-planning efficiency** in some scenarios, and a recognised wrapper for trust/family-wealth structuring.

4. **Jurisdictional credibility.** The IoM is the world's leading offshore life-assurance domicile, regulated by the IOMFSA under the **Insurance Act 2008**, with **Solvency II equivalence** and long-established UK insurer subsidiaries (Quilter, Standard Life International, Canada Life International, Utmost, etc.). The insurer is tax-neutral, so the wrapper itself doesn't add a tax layer.

**Caveats the corpus flags:** product misclassification in the policyholder's tax jurisdiction, adviser-suitability concerns, and AML/CFT exposure through the intermediary chain. Bonds are sold via intermediaries (UK FCA-authorised advisers, IFAs, private banks), not direct.

Why IoM specifically over alternatives: Dublin/Luxembourg serve EU-focused business (they're inside the EU); the IoM is the competitive choice for **UK-focused** business post-Brexit. Jersey and Guernsey have only smaller / specialty life-assurance offerings (`knowledge/isle-of-man/life-assurance.md`, comparison table).
