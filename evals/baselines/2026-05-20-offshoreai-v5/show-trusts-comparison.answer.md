## The short answer — they solve three different problems

These three trusts are not really competitors; each is the dominant tool for a *different* structuring problem.

| | Jersey trust | Cayman STAR | BVI VISTA |
|---|---|---|---|
| Core statute | Trusts (Jersey) Law 1984 | Special Trusts (Alternative Regime) Act 1997 | Virgin Islands Special Trusts Act 2003 |
| Problem it solves | Forced-heirship / creditor / matrimonial attack on settled wealth; settlor control | Trusts with **no beneficiaries** (purpose trusts) | Holding **operating-company shares** without the trustee's duty to interfere |
| Signature feature | Article 9 firewall + Article 9A reserved powers | Non-charitable purpose + mandatory enforcer, unlimited duration | Trustee disengagement from company management via "office of director rules" |

## When to use Jersey

Reach for a **Jersey trust** when the structuring goal is **maximum defensive certainty around the settled wealth itself** — and where the settlor wants to retain control without invalidating the trust.

- **Forced-heirship defence is paramount.** Article 9 instructs the Royal Court to apply only Jersey law to validity, capacity, administration and beneficial-rights questions, and to refuse effect to foreign forced-heirship/matrimonial rules — including refusing to enforce inconsistent foreign judgments (Article 9(4)). This is described in the corpus as the "gold standard" firewall. (`knowledge/jersey/trusts/firewall.md`)
- **Settlor wants to keep control** (revocation, investment direction, power to appoint/remove). Article 9A confirms a long menu of reserved powers does not invalidate the trust and protects the trustee who acts on them — central to US grantor-trust and first-generation family-office planning. (`knowledge/jersey/trusts/reserved-powers.md`)
- This is the **central wealth wrapper** in the classic HNW mix, with underlying Cayman/BVI companies for specific assets. (`knowledge/CROSS-JURISDICTIONAL-MAP.md`)

Jersey *also* offers a purpose-trust route (Trusts Law Article 12), so the STAR/VISTA features below are not exclusively offshore-Caribbean — but the Cayman and BVI statutes are the market-standard tools for those two niches.

## When to use Cayman STAR

Reach for **STAR** when you need a trust with **no beneficiaries** — a purpose trust.

- **Orphan SPVs** in securitisation/structured finance: the classic use. STAR holds the SPV shares so the vehicle is bankruptcy-remote with nobody to consolidate it. (`knowledge/cayman/star-trusts.md`)
- **Private Trust Company (PTC) ownership** — STAR holds the PTC shares to create an orphan PTC.
- **Family-governance or quasi-charitable purposes** that don't fit a beneficiary trust or strict charitable status.
- Enforced by a **mandatory enforcer** standing in place of beneficiaries; **unlimited duration**. (`knowledge/cayman/star-trusts.md`)

The corpus decision framework lists STAR as the lean for "orphan SPV / no-beneficiary purpose trust." (`knowledge/CROSS-JURISDICTIONAL-MAP.md`)

## When to use BVI VISTA

Reach for **VISTA** when the trust will hold **shares in an operating business** and the settlor wants chosen directors (often family) to run it **without trustee second-guessing**.

- VISTA disengages the trustee from the *Bartlett v Barclays* duty to monitor and intervene in the company's affairs — the trustee holds legal title but need not (and is not liable for failing to) intervene. (`knowledge/bvi/vista-trusts.md`)
- Control is preserved through mandatory **office of director rules (ODRs)** specifying who appoints/removes directors and how the trustee is restrained.
- Intervention is the exception (fraud, misconduct, defined triggers), not the default.
- The corpus framework lists VISTA as the lean for "family-business succession with disengaged trustee." (`knowledge/CROSS-JURISDICTIONAL-MAP.md`)

## Decision rule of thumb

- **Defend settled wealth from heirs/creditors, keep settlor control** → **Jersey**
- **No beneficiaries — orphan SPV, PTC, or pure purpose** → **Cayman STAR**
- **Hold an operating company, family runs it, trustee stays out** → **BVI VISTA**

These combine in practice: a Jersey trust as the wealth wrapper can sit above a BVI VISTA holding the family business, or a Cayman STAR holding an orphan PTC.
