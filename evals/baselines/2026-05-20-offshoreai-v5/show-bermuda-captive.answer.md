I'll pull the Bermuda insurance-class material and the cross-jurisdictional captive framing.## Why Bermuda for a group captive

Bermuda is the world's leading captive-insurance domicile, and the structural reasons that matter for a multinational group are:

- **Depth and supervisory familiarity** — the largest captive market globally, with the Bermuda Monetary Authority (BMA) experienced in single-parent and group structures.
- **Solvency II equivalence** — Bermuda is recognised by the EU as Solvency II-equivalent, so a Bermuda captive/reinsurer benefits from reduced collateral requirements when reinsuring EU cedants. The corpus flags this as a significant Bermuda competitive advantage and notes it is the **only offshore jurisdiction** (alongside Isle of Man) with that equivalence. ([`knowledge/bermuda/insurance-classes.md`](knowledge/bermuda/insurance-classes.md); [`knowledge/CROSS-JURISDICTIONAL-MAP.md`](knowledge/CROSS-JURISDICTIONAL-MAP.md))
- **Class 4 cat-reinsurance ecosystem and ILS market** — proximity to the deepest commercial reinsurance and insurance-linked-securities market if your programme ever needs to access that capacity. ([`knowledge/bermuda/insurance-classes.md`](knowledge/bermuda/insurance-classes.md))

The trade-off the corpus names: **higher BMA scrutiny and substance requirements** than lighter-touch domiciles. (For comparison: Cayman leans to healthcare captives, Guernsey to European-parent PCC-cell captives.)

## Which Class

Bermuda classifies P&C ("general business") captives under the **Insurance Act 1978** by how much *unrelated* business they write:

| Class | Fits if your captive… |
|---|---|
| **Class 1** | writes **only the risks of your own group** (pure single-parent self-insurance) |
| **Class 2** | is **multi-owner / group / association**, or writes group risks **plus a limited amount of unrelated** business |
| **Class 3** | writes **significant unrelated (third-party) business** up to specified thresholds |

**For a multinational group self-insuring its own risks, the natural starting point is Class 1** (pure single-parent captive). It moves to **Class 2** if the structure is multi-parent/association or takes on some third-party risk, and to **Class 3** once unrelated business becomes significant. The corpus's guidance is to pick the **lowest class consistent with the business**, since minimum capital, solvency, reporting and BMA engagement all rise sharply up the scale. If you want multiple programmes or lines run through one licensed entity, a **Segregated Accounts Company (SAC)** is the structuring tool. (All: [`knowledge/bermuda/insurance-classes.md`](knowledge/bermuda/insurance-classes.md))

Note these are *general business* classes — the parallel **Class A–E** ladder is for **long-term (life)** captives, relevant only if the captive insures group life/long-term risks.
