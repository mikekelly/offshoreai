## The four regimes side by side

| Feature | **Jersey JPF** | **Cayman ELP** | **BVI Approved Fund** | **Guernsey PIF** |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| What it is | Light-touch private-fund regime | A *vehicle* (partnership form), not a regime — registers as a private fund under the Private Funds Act 2020 | Permanent light-touch fund regime for stable small funds | Fast-track private-fund regime (3 routes) |
| Max investors | 50 | No statutory cap on the ELP itself | **20** | 50 |
| AUM ceiling | None | None | **US$100m** | None |
| Investor type | Professional / eligible only | Set by the LPA (typically sophisticated/professional) | No statutory minimum investment | Professional/sophisticated (R3), family (R2), or via licensed mgr (R1) |

| Audit required | Yes | (Private Funds Act applies) | **No mandatory audit** | Yes |

## How this maps to your profile (emerging manager, first fund, sub-US$100m)

**The corpus's own decision framework points to BVI for exactly your profile.** The cross-jurisdictional map's "Where should I form my fund?" table lists *"Emerging manager, sub-$100m, BVI-friendly → BVI Approved Manager + Approved Fund"* (`knowledge/CROSS-JURISDICTIONAL-MAP.md`). The Approved Fund is purpose-built for "emerging managers wanting a manageable regulatory burden" and "track-record-building vehicles intended to scale later," with no mandatory auditor and the lowest regulatory intensity tier (`knowledge/bvi/incubator-and-approved-fund.md`). The **catch is the 20-investor cap and the US$100m ceiling** — if you outgrow either, you must convert up to a BVI Private (50 investors) or Professional Fund. Pair it with the Approved Manager regime, which gives you "marketing-acceptable" regulated status without full SIBA licensing (`knowledge/bvi/approved-manager.md`).

The choice really turns on **your LP base**, which the corpus frames clearly:

- **Predominantly US institutional LPs / Delaware-parallel structure** → **Cayman ELP** is the global default. LPs expect the form, it gives tax transparency in the US/UK/EU, and it pairs naturally with a Delaware LP for US investors (`knowledge/cayman/exempted-limited-partnership.md`). It carries no 20-investor cap, so it doesn't box you in the way the BVI Approved Fund does — but it is more expensive to run.

- **You need to market into the EU/UK** → **Jersey JPF** or **Guernsey PIF**, because only these two offer AIFMD **NPPR** routes (`knowledge/CROSS-JURISDICTIONAL-MAP.md`). The JPF (50 investors, 48-hour authorisation, DSP model) and PIF (50 investors, 1-business-day Route 1) are close competitors; the corpus notes the JPF's distinctive feature is DSP delegation for "regulatory predictability," while the PIF's edge is route flexibility — Route 2 for family pools, Route 3 for qualifying investors (`knowledge/guernsey/private-investment-fund.md`).

- **Family-and-friends / club-deal first fund** → both the **JPF** (explicitly lists "family-and-friends investment partnerships" and "carry vehicles") and the **Guernsey PIF Route 2** suit this (`knowledge/jersey/funds/jersey-private-fund.md`; `knowledge/guernsey/private-investment-fund.md`).

**Bottom line for a first sub-$100m fund:** if cost and speed dominate and your investor count is small, the **BVI Approved Fund + Approved Manager** is the lowest-friction home and the corpus's named recommendation for your profile — accepting the 20-investor / US$100m ceilings as a "launch pad, not a permanent home." If you have US institutional LPs, default to the **Cayman ELP**. If EU/UK marketing is essential, you're choosing between **Jersey JPF** and **Guernsey PIF**.
