## The emerging-manager stack in BVI

For a BVI-based emerging manager, the corpus describes a three-layer stack, with the lowest-friction option at each layer:

### 1. Manager — BVI Approved Manager
The **Approved Manager** regime (Investment Business (Approved Manager) Regulations 2012) is the fast-track, light-touch authorisation route designed explicitly for "start-up, smaller, or family-office managers." It sits below full Investment Business (SIBA) licensing: faster (weeks not months), lower cost, proportionate standards, but still a *recognised regulated status* that banks, brokers and administrators will accept. It's subject to an AUM ceiling — if you grow past it, you must upgrade to full Investment Business licensing. (`knowledge/bvi/approved-manager.md`)

### 2. Fund — BVI Incubator Fund or Approved Fund
Two light-touch regimes under the SIBA (Incubator and Approved Funds) Regulations 2015. For an *emerging manager building a track record*, the **Incubator Fund** is the targeted choice:

| | Incubator Fund | Approved Fund |
|---|---|---|
| Max investors | 20 | 20 |
| Min investment | US$20,000 | None |
| Max AUM | US$20m | US$100m |
| Time limit | 2 years (+1 yr extension) | Permanent |
| Auditor / administrator | Neither required | Administrator typically required |
| Designed for | Emerging-manager track-record | Stable small fund |

The Incubator is intentionally time-limited — a launch pad, not a permanent home. After two years (extendable by one) it must convert to a Private/Professional/Public Fund or wind down. If you'd rather stay small permanently, the Approved Fund is the better fit. (`knowledge/bvi/incubator-and-approved-fund.md`)

### 3. Vehicle — BVI Business Company (or LP)
The fund itself, the GP entity, and the management entity are each typically incorporated as a **BVI Business Company** under the BVI Business Companies Act 2004 — incorporable in hours, low cost, no minimum capital, flexible share classes, one director (no residence requirement). A fund can alternatively be a limited partnership under BVI partnership law. (`knowledge/bvi/business-company.md`)

### The assembled stack
The corpus gives the canonical start-up sequence (`knowledge/bvi/approved-manager.md`):

1. Form the **GP entity** (BVI BC);
2. Form the **management entity** (BVI BC) and obtain **Approved Manager** status;
3. Form the **fund** (BVI BC, or LP under BVI partnership law) as an **Incubator** or **Approved Fund**;
4. Operate with full Approved Manager status from inception.

The "Approved Manager + Incubator/Approved Fund" pairing is explicitly called out as the common emerging-manager combination.

### Two cross-cutting obligations
- **Economic substance** — the BVI Economic Substance (Companies and Limited Partnerships) Act 2018 applies separately to in-scope activities and is not satisfied by the light-touch fund/manager regimes.
- **AML/CFT** — substantive obligations under the Proceeds of Criminal Conduct Act and the AMLR, with FSC inspection. The corpus stresses these regimes are "a regime, not a loophole."
