A **Parish Hall Enquiry** is a distinctively Jersey pre-court forum, presided over by a **Centenier** — an elected, unpaid honorary police officer of the parish where the alleged offence occurred. It is the route by which the **majority of routine Jersey motoring offences are resolved without ever reaching the Magistrate's Court**. There is no direct equivalent in the UK or other common-law systems — don't assume it works like a UK fixed-penalty notice or fiscal fine. (`knowledge/jersey/road-traffic/parish-hall-enquiries.md`)

The Centenier reviews the evidence, decides whether to charge, and can issue a **caution**, a **written fine of up to £200 per offence**, or **refer the matter to the Magistrate's Court**. Critically, a Centenier may only fine or caution if you **admit** the offence and **accept** the penalty. You can decline to admit, bring a friend or advocate, and **demand the case be heard at Magistrate's Court instead** at any point.

## When your case goes there

**Goes to Parish Hall** — routine, lower-level motoring matters, e.g.:
- Minor speeding (under 15 mph excess)
- Mobile-phone offences
- Failing to display insurance disc
- Some traffic-light / minor careless-driving offences
- Parking offences

**Does NOT go to Parish Hall** (referred directly to Magistrate's Court):
- Speeding **15 mph or more** over the limit
- Drink-driving or drug-driving (any reading)
- Dangerous driving (Article 23 RTJL 1956)
- Causing death or serious injury by driving
- Driving while disqualified
- Any offence carrying **mandatory disqualification**, or where the Centenier judges the £200 ceiling inadequate

The Centenier cannot impose disqualification — so any case where a ban is on the table goes to court. The schedule of Parish-Hall-eligible offences is maintained by the Law Officers' Department.
