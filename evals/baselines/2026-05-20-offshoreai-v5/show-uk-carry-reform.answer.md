UK carried-interest taxation moves from a CGT-based regime to an **income-tax-based regime with a partial exclusion** (`knowledge/frontier/uk-carried-interest-reform-2026.md`):

- **Pre-6 April 2025:** CGT-based, ~28% effective for additional-rate payers.
- **6 April 2025 – 5 April 2026 (transition):** flat 32% CGT.
- **From 6 April 2026 (now in force):** carry is treated as **income**, subject to income tax + Class 4 NICs. For **qualifying** carry a **72.5% multiplier** applies (only 72.5% brought into charge), giving an effective rate of ~**34.1%** for a 45% taxpayer. **Non-qualifying** carry attracts full income tax + NICs, up to ~**47%**.

The qualifying / non-qualifying split is fact-sensitive: qualifying status turns on the partner performing genuine investment-management activity and the fund meeting specified characteristics. The definition was **tightened** versus earlier drafts, though the government dropped the additional access conditions (e.g. a minimum-investment-by-partner test) in the final legislation. For established UK-resident partners at mature sponsors, qualifying is the default; new entrants, secondaries participants, and non-traditional structures need care.

## What Crown Dependencies PE partners should do

The corpus frames the response in three buckets (`knowledge/frontier/uk-carried-interest-reform-2026.md`):

**Residency planning for UK partners.** Jersey **HVR (2(1)(e))**, Guernsey Open Market, and IoM's tax cap are being marketed more actively. But three big caveats: the UK **Statutory Residence Test** makes leaving hard; there is a **tail liability** on carry that economically accrued while UK-resident even if realised after departure; and the new legislation contains **specific anti-avoidance provisions around residence changes**. Real substance in the destination is essential.

**GP-entity and carry-allocation structuring.** Crown Dependencies-domiciled GPs (Jersey/Guernsey companies acting as GP of Jersey/Cayman ELP funds) may become more attractive as the carry-allocation entity for non-UK partners — but UK look-through / situs rules still apply where a UK-resident partner gets the economic benefit. The fund vehicles themselves (Jersey JPF, Guernsey PIF, Cayman ELP) are unchanged; the action is at the GP-entity profit-allocation and vesting level.

**Documentation and the co-invest line.** Sponsors should document carry arrangements carefully to preserve qualifying status, and the **carry-vs-co-invest distinction is now acutely material** — co-invest economics remain CGT-treated. Misclassifying one as the other is a flagged pitfall.

Pre-6-April crystallisation (accelerating realisations to land under the 32% regime) was the Q1 2026 play; that window has now closed for carry arising on or after 6 April 2026.
