Let me read them.**Short answer: no, you cannot "just" evict them — but yes, you have a clear statutory route to eviction for non-payment.** Non-payment of rent is a breach of the tenancy agreement under the **Residential Tenancy (Jersey) Law 2011 (RTJL)**, and the law channels you through a defined procedure rather than self-help. Self-help — changing the locks, removing possessions, cutting off utilities, or physically removing the tenant — is unlawful and may itself be a **criminal offence**. (`knowledge/jersey/residential-tenancy/non-payment-of-rent.md`, `knowledge/jersey/use-cases/tenant-landlord/evicting-non-paying-tenant.md`)

## The procedure

1. **Serve a written 7-day breach notice** — signed, identifying the unpaid rent (which periods, how much), requiring payment within at least 7 days, and properly served (per the agreement or by personal service / recorded post). 7 days is the statutory minimum; a defective notice is fatal and you start over. If the tenant pays in full within the window, the breach is remedied and you cannot proceed on the same arrears. (`non-payment-of-rent.md`)

2. **Apply to the Petty Debts Court** if still unpaid — it has **exclusive original jurisdiction** (not the Royal Court at first instance). You bring the tenancy agreement, condition report, rent ledger/bank evidence of arrears, a copy of the notice with proof of service, and a record of communications. You can also seek a money judgment for the arrears. (`non-payment-of-rent.md`, `knowledge/jersey/residential-tenancy/eviction-procedure.md`)

3. **The hearing** — the court checks the breach is made out and the procedure was compliant, considers tenant defences, and may grant termination + an eviction order, often with a **stay of execution** (commonly 14–28 days, longer for families with children or vulnerable tenants). (`eviction-procedure.md`)

4. **Enforcement by the Viscount** — only the Viscount may take possession from a resisting tenant. After any stay expires, the Viscount attends, removes the tenant (with force if necessary), inventories/stores possessions, and restores possession to you. (`eviction-procedure.md`)

## Tenant protections to expect

Procedural (perfect notice and service required), **set-off** for landlord disrepair, a **discrimination** cross-claim if the eviction is in substance on a protected ground (Discrimination (Jersey) Law 2013), **harassment** liability for pressure tactics during the notice period, and **stays of execution** for vulnerability (children, Income Support, mental-health/capacity considerations). (`non-payment-of-rent.md`, `eviction-procedure.md`)

## Realistic timeline and cost

Typically **2–4 months** from first missed payment to actual possession in a straightforward case — longer if contested or appealed. Budget for court fees plus Viscount's fees (several hundred to several thousand pounds), which are recoverable from the tenant per the lease/judgment but often borne in practice if the tenant is judgment-proof. (`non-payment-of-rent.md`, `eviction-procedure.md`)
