## Pillar Two on a Jersey TopCo above a UK operating business

### 1. It only bites above the size threshold
Pillar Two applies only to MNE groups with **€750m+ consolidated revenue** in at least two of the four preceding fiscal years. Below that, nothing changes: the Jersey TopCo stays on zero-ten (0%) and the structure is unaffected (`knowledge/jersey/tax/pillar-two.md`; `knowledge/jersey/use-cases/founder-entrepreneur/pillar-two-impact.md`). Most TopCo groups never cross it.

### 2. Above the threshold, Jersey collects the top-up itself
Jersey is a low-tax jurisdiction — the TopCo's Jersey-booked income is taxed at 0% under zero-ten, so its Jersey GloBE effective rate sits below 15%. Jersey closes that gap through the **Multinational Corporate Income Tax (Jersey) Law 2024 (MCIT)**, effective for financial years beginning on or after **1 January 2025**. The MCIT is structured as a **Qualified Domestic Minimum Top-up Tax (QDMTT)** plus an **Income Inclusion Rule (IIR)**; Jersey has **not** implemented the UTPR backstop (`knowledge/jersey/tax/pillar-two.md`; `knowledge/jersey/international/pillar-two-mcit.md`; trajectory synthesis at `knowledge/jersey/history/finance/trajectory.md`).

The practical effect: instead of the foreign parent jurisdiction collecting the 15% top-up via its IIR, **Jersey collects it** as QDMTT. The TopCo's Jersey effective rate becomes **15%**, layered on top of (not replacing) its zero-ten classification (`knowledge/jersey/tax/pillar-two.md` — "Interaction with zero-ten").

### 3. The UK operating business is not where the top-up arises
This is the structural point for your fact pattern. The top-up is computed **per jurisdiction**. The UK trading profits are taxed at the UK corporation-tax rate, which is **above the 15% GloBE floor**, so the UK jurisdictional blend produces little or no top-up — the action concentrates on the **low-taxed Jersey layer**, not the UK opco. (The UK-side mechanics — UK corporation tax rates and the UK's own Multinational/Domestic Top-up Tax — are UK law and **the corpus does not cover them**; treat that sentence as general context, not a corpus-cited claim. The nearest corpus material is the Jersey-side QDMTT/IIR analysis cited above.)

Two further wrinkles the corpus flags:
- **Substance-based income exclusion** — a formulaic carve-out (payroll + tangible assets) reduces the GloBE income subject to top-up, on a sliding scale in early years. A TopCo with thin Jersey substance gets little benefit here (`knowledge/jersey/international/pillar-two-mcit.md` — "Excluded income").
- **TopCo as Ultimate Parent Entity** — if the Jersey TopCo is the group UPE, Jersey's IIR is the rule that would top up any *other* low-taxed group entities; a UK opco taxed above 15% would not generate an IIR charge (`knowledge/jersey/international/pillar-two-mcit.md` — "Income Inclusion Rule"). Note investment-fund and certain holding structures can be excluded entities — check scope.

### 4. Compliance and the strategic read
In-scope groups must register Jersey constituent entities, compute GloBE income/ETR, file the Pillar Two return (alongside the local return), coordinate with the GloBE Information Return, and pay the top-up — materially heavier than the simple zero-ten annual return (`knowledge/jersey/tax/pillar-two.md`; `knowledge/jersey/use-cases/founder-entrepreneur/pillar-two-impact.md`). The corpus's strategic note: 15% is still competitive with onshore options, and Jersey's non-tax advantages (corporate law, no investor-level CGT, listing acceptance) persist, so most groups keep the Jersey TopCo rather than migrate (`knowledge/jersey/use-cases/founder-entrepreneur/pillar-two-impact.md`).
