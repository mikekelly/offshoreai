## Your rights on arrest in Jersey

Your rights flow from the **Police Procedures and Criminal Evidence (Jersey) Law 2003** (PPCE 2003) and its Codes of Practice. The single most important: **ask for a solicitor immediately and don't answer substantive questions until you've spoken to one.**

**The core rights:**

- **Free legal advice** — an absolute right to consult a solicitor or advocate, free of charge via the 24/7 duty-solicitor scheme (or your own), in private, *before* any interview. Can be delayed only in very serious cases (e.g. terrorism/evidence-preservation) on specific statutory grounds, authorised at senior level — never routinely. If you initially decline but change your mind mid-interview, questioning must stop.
- **Right to silence** — you need not answer questions. The caution: *"You are not obliged to say anything unless you wish to do so, but what you say may be put into writing and given in evidence."* A "no comment" interview is legitimate, and adverse inferences from police-station silence are narrower in Jersey than in England.
- **Inform someone** — one person told of your arrest and location, police calling at public expense, as soon as practicable (delayable in serious cases on specific grounds).
- **Consular notification** — if you're a foreign national.
- **Written notice of rights** and access to your **custody record** (sight during detention; a copy on release — keep it, it's key evidence if procedure is later challenged).
- **Interpreter** if you don't speak English well enough; **appropriate adult** if you're under 18 or vulnerable; **medical attention** if needed; adequate cell conditions and rest (at least 8 continuous hours in any 24).

**Detention limits:**

| Stage | Limit | Authorised by |
|---|---|---|
| Initial | **24 hours** | Custody officer |
| Extension (serious offences) | up to **36 hours** | Police inspector |
| Royal Court warrant of further detention | up to **96 hours total** | Royal Court (Magistrate) |
| Beyond 96 hours | must **charge or release** | — |

Detention beyond these limits is unlawful. The custody officer must review the grounds at prescribed intervals.

**In interview:** open audio recording (Code E), the caution at the start of each session, your solicitor present if requested, no oppression, breaks, and no questioning if you're unfit (intoxicated/unwell). Don't sign anything without legal advice.

**If a right is breached:** note the time and details, tell your solicitor immediately, complain through formal channels after release. Evidence obtained in breach may be excluded at trial, and serious breaches can ground a civil claim.
