Your transfer happens **in the Royal Court** because Jersey real-property contracts must be *passed before the Court* — a Norman customary-law inheritance, not a UK-style private conveyance. The buyer and seller (usually their lawyers acting as attorneys under powers of attorney) appear, the contract is read/referenced, and the **Greffier records it**; the property changes hands at that Court act, and the transaction is recorded in the Public Registry. The forum is the **Samedi Division**, the Royal Court's day-to-day civil court. ([`knowledge/jersey/property/contract-passing.md`](knowledge/jersey/property/contract-passing.md); [`knowledge/jersey/legal-system/royal-court.md`](knowledge/jersey/legal-system/royal-court.md))

On the specific "**Friday afternoon**": the corpus documents this as the established practical convention — property contracts pass on a Friday afternoon at the Royal Court — across several files ([`knowledge/jersey/property/commercial-leases.md:54`](knowledge/jersey/property/commercial-leases.md), [`knowledge/jersey/property/flying-freeholds.md:161`](knowledge/jersey/property/flying-freeholds.md), [`knowledge/jersey/contract/force-majeure.md:124`](knowledge/jersey/contract/force-majeure.md)). The contract-passing file itself describes passings as happening "most weekdays" in the Samedi Division, so the Friday slot is best understood as the **customary scheduling convention** for conveyancing passings rather than a statutory requirement.
