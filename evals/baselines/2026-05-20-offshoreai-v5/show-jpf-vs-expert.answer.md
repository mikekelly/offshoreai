I'll pull the JPF and Expert Fund files in parallel to ground the comparison.## JPF — for a club deal, the JPF is the natural fit

A **club-deal PE fund** is exactly the use case the JPF regime is built for. The corpus lists "**single-LP or club deal structures**", "private equity funds and co-investment vehicles", and "carry vehicles" as the JPF's default targets (`knowledge/jersey/funds/jersey-private-fund.md`). The JPF gives you:

- **Light-touch authorisation** — typically issued within ~48 hours of application;
- **Up to 50 offers/investors**, professional/eligible only — a club deal almost always sits well inside that;
- A **JFSC-licensed administrator as designated service provider (DSP)** carrying CDD/AML and substituting for direct JFSC oversight;
- Structural flexibility — most PE club deals use a **Jersey limited partnership**, but company, unit trust, or cell forms are available (`knowledge/jersey/funds/jersey-private-fund.md`).

## When you'd reach for the Expert Fund instead

The decisive variable is **investor count**. The Expert Fund has **no specific investor cap** (subject to expert-investor criteria) and is the corpus's recommended route for "large private-equity vehicles exceeding JPF investor count" — but it carries more disclosure, a fuller offering memorandum, and a longer authorisation timeline (days to weeks vs ~48 hours) (`knowledge/jersey/funds/expert-fund.md`). It's also the regime the corpus flags where **AIFMD NPPR compliance for EU marketing** is in play.

So the decision:

| If your club deal… | Use |
|---|---|
| Has ≤50 professional/eligible investors (the typical club deal) | **JPF** |
| Will exceed 50 investors, or needs wider expert-investor marketing | **Expert Fund** |

For a true "club deal" — a handful of known sophisticated co-investors — **JPF** is the answer.
