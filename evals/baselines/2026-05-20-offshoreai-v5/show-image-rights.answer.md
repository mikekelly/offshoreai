## How Guernsey's image-rights register works

Guernsey is the **only jurisdiction in the world** with a statutory image-rights register for individuals, created by the **Image Rights (Bailiwick of Guernsey) Ordinance 2012**. It converts what most legal systems treat as a fragmented patchwork (passing off, defamation, right of publicity, GDPR/personal-data claims) into a **single registrable property right** with statutory backing. ([`knowledge/guernsey/image-rights-register.md`](knowledge/guernsey/image-rights-register.md))

**What you register** — a *"personnage"*, the registered persona, against which specific attributes are protected: name (real, professional or stage), voice, signature, image/likeness, distinctive appearance, mannerisms, and other personality-identifying attributes. Both natural persons and fictional/non-real characters (mascots, animated characters) can be associated with a personnage.

**The property right it gives you** — once registered, your image rights become:
- **Transferable** — assignable and licensable (this is the basis for your licensing agreements);
- **Inheritable** — they pass on death and can survive it;
- **Mortgageable** — capable of being used as collateral in some cases;
- **Enforceable** — against unauthorised use.

**Registration mechanics** — filed with the Guernsey Intellectual Property Office, subject to examination and publication, granted after an opposition period, **renewable every 10 years** (with protection running to life-plus-100-years for some categories), public on the register, at modest fees relative to commercial trademark filings.

**Enforcement** — a registered personnage gives you standing in the Guernsey courts and statutory remedies (injunction, damages, account of profits, delivery up of infringing material).

## The practical caveats that matter for you

1. **Foreign-court recognition is the main limitation.** The regime is *sui generis*, so courts outside Guernsey may not give the registration direct effect. In practice it is best used **alongside** local-jurisdiction enforcement (trademarks, passing off, right-of-publicity claims), with the Guernsey registration serving as evidence of a proprietary interest to support foreign litigation.
2. **It is most valuable as an asset base for licensing**, rather than as a standalone global enforcement tool. Licensing agreements are typically drafted with Guernsey governing law and forum.
3. **Registration is public** — which may not suit a celebrity wanting privacy, though the specifics registered can be calibrated.

## Structuring it for protection + licensing

The file describes the typical wealth-structuring combination: settle the image rights into a **Guernsey trust** or hold them via a **Guernsey (or other) holding company** that licenses use, route licensing/royalty income through that vehicle, and use the structure for succession planning outside your home-jurisdiction succession regime (subject to applicable conflict-of-laws rules). This combination of registration + trust/company + favourable tax is what makes the regime attractive for high-profile individuals. ([`knowledge/guernsey/image-rights-register.md`](knowledge/guernsey/image-rights-register.md))

Note that **Jersey, Cayman and BVI have no equivalent regime**; the closest analogues elsewhere (US state right-of-publicity, UK common-law protections, codified EU personality rights) are not registrable property rights.
