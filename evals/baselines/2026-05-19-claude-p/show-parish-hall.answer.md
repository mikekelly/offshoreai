## What's a Parish Hall Enquiry?

A **Parish Hall Enquiry** is a distinctively Jersey, pre-court forum presided over by a **Centenier** — an elected, unpaid honorary police officer of the parish in which the alleged offence took place. It has no direct UK or other common-law equivalent: it's not quite a fixed-penalty notice, not quite a court, and combines prosecutorial and limited adjudicative roles in one person, justified by long parish tradition (`jersey/road-traffic/parish-hall-enquiries.md`).

At the Enquiry, the Centenier reviews the evidence (usually from the Honorary or States Police), decides whether to charge or to resolve the matter at parish level, hears from the alleged offender, and can:

- **Caution** (recorded, no further action),
- **Issue a written fine** of up to **£200 per offence**, or
- **Refer the case to Magistrate's Court** if it's beyond Parish-Hall scope.

The Centenier may only fine/caution if the offender **admits** the offence **and accepts** the proposed penalty. Anyone who disputes the offence — or simply prefers a formal hearing — has the right to demand the case go to Magistrate's Court instead, at any point and even after a Parish Hall outcome. They can also bring a friend, family member, or advocate, and appeal any outcome by requesting a Magistrate's Court re-hearing (`jersey/road-traffic/parish-hall-enquiries.md`).

## When does your case go there?

Parish Hall is the routine route for **minor motoring matters**, including (`jersey/road-traffic/parish-hall-enquiries.md`):

- Minor speeding (under 15 mph excess),
- Mobile-phone offences,
- Failing to display an insurance disc,
- Some traffic-light offences,
- Some minor careless-driving cases,
- Parking offences.

It is **excluded** — case goes straight to Magistrate's Court by summons — for:

- Speeding 15 mph or more over the limit,
- Drink-driving (any reading) and drug-driving,
- Dangerous driving (Article 23 RTJL),
- Causing death or serious injury by driving,
- Driving while disqualified,
- Failing to stop/report after an accident (serious cases),
- Any offence carrying mandatory disqualification,
- Any offence for which the Centenier thinks the £200 ceiling is inadequate.

Process is typically 15–30 minutes and informal — no robes, no transcripts, no advocate's address (`jersey/road-traffic/parish-hall-enquiries.md`).
