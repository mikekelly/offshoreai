## Route

A Jersey AIF marketing into Germany goes via the **National Private Placement Regime (NPPR)** under **AIFMD Article 42** — the EU directive provision that authorises a member state to permit a non-EU AIFM to market a non-EU AIF to professional investors in its territory, implemented in German law by **§330 KAGB** with **BaFin** as the competent authority.

### Sequence

1. **Register the AIFM with the JFSC** under the **Alternative Investment Funds (Jersey) Regulations 2012**, which is Jersey's domestic AIFMD-equivalent framework (sits alongside the JFSC's AIF Code of Practice).
2. **Stand up AIFMD-equivalent compliance** — proportionate capital adequacy, risk-management framework, liquidity management for open-ended funds, transparency to investors, regulatory reporting, and an **AIFMD Article 21 depository** (for Jersey AIFs marketed into the EU, the JFSC-licensed Jersey depository must perform cash monitoring, safekeeping of financial instruments, and ownership verification on AIFMD-equivalent terms).
3. **Notify BaFin under §330 KAGB**. Germany is one of the stricter NPPR jurisdictions: expect to file a **local fact-sheet**, in some cases appoint a **local distributor**, and accept ongoing **reporting obligations** post-acceptance.
4. **Market only to German professional investors** once cleared. Active marketing without notification is a serious enforcement risk; **reverse solicitation** is available but Germany scrutinises overclaims.

### What changed on 16 April 2026 — AIFMD II is now live

**Directive (EU) 2024/927** is in force as of 16 April 2026 (you are about six weeks past the application date). For a Jersey AIFM marketing into Germany via NPPR, the directly-applicable deltas are:

- **Expanded investor disclosures** (pre-marketing and ongoing — liquidity management, sustainability factors interacting with SFDR, loan-originating disclosures where applicable);
- **Enhanced Annex IV reporting** to ESMA via BaFin — new data fields, more granular reporting on specific asset categories;
- **Germany has transposed more strictly than Luxembourg or Ireland** — assume the higher bar.

The JFSC is bifurcating its **AIF Code of Practice into an EU AIF Code and a UK AIF Code**, with the EU AIF Code being aligned to AIFMD II requirements and expected to be finalised through 2026 — track it before your next notification.

### Caveats

- The doctrinal corpus files cited are `draft` status (drafted, awaiting verification against the primary statute and BaFin guidance) — verify the §330 KAGB current text and BaFin's NPPR notification pack before filing.
- The AIFMD II frontier file has no `last_verified` date and was written before transposition was fully complete; re-check Germany's implementing measures (BaFin guidance and any KAGB amendments) before relying on the disclosure / Annex IV detail.
- The **full AIFMD third-country passport** is not available; NPPR remains the route.
