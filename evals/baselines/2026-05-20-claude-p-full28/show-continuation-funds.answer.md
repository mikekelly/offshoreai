## Why continuation funds and GP-led secondaries are growing so fast

A **continuation fund** is a new vehicle into which a GP transfers one or more portfolio assets out of an existing (often expiring) fund, letting existing LPs either **roll** their interest into the new vehicle or **cash out** at a negotiated valuation. **GP-led secondaries** is the wider category — also covering tender offers, strip sales, preferred-equity deals, and other structured liquidity solutions (`knowledge/frontier/continuation-funds-and-gp-led-secondaries.md`).

The corpus attributes the surge to a convergence of four pressures (`knowledge/frontier/continuation-funds-and-gp-led-secondaries.md`):

1. **Weak exit markets** — tough M&A and IPO conditions through 2022–2025 closed off traditional exit routes.
2. **LP demand for DPI** (distributed-to-paid-in capital) — LPs need realised cash back to recycle into new commitments.
3. **GPs unwilling to dump trophy assets** at depressed valuations — a continuation fund lets them keep holding quality assets while still generating liquidity.
4. **Secondaries market capital** ready and waiting to be deployed.

The scale data backs the "fast-growing" framing: per the 2026 Global Private Equity Outlook survey (cited via Dechert), **46% of respondents** are now using GP-led secondaries or continuation vehicles to expedite LP distributions — roughly **double the prior year**. Planned increases over the next 24 months run at 51% (North America), 55% (APAC), and 43% (EMEA, up sharply from 14% in 2024). The corpus describes the trend as "broad-based and accelerating," with survey data pointing to continued doubling of volume in 2026.

## Why Guernsey is winning that business

The corpus is explicit that Guernsey "has positioned itself aggressively" and that Guernsey Finance "has marketed aggressively" in this space. The specific advantages it lists (`knowledge/frontier/continuation-funds-and-gp-led-secondaries.md`):

- **Regulatory carve-out**: single-asset continuation funds are **not regulated as collective investment schemes** in Guernsey — a major simplification of the regulatory pathway. This is the standout structural edge.
- **Speed**: fast-track regimes with sign-off within **3 business days** maximum, and **next-day approval** for Private Investment Funds (PIFs).
- **Practitioner ecosystem**: a familiar, experienced fund-counsel bench (Carey Olsen, Ogier, Mourant, Walkers).
- **Tax neutrality**: 0% standard corporate tax.
- **Substance-light** treatment for the new continuation vehicle itself where only the asset is changing hands.
- **Coordinated marketing**: Guernsey Finance, the GFSC, and local counsel are described as actively coordinating — the "most aggressive" marketing among the Crown Dependencies.

The underlying vehicle is typically a **Guernsey PIF** (`knowledge/guernsey/private-investment-fund.md`), Guernsey's fast-track private-fund regime (launched 2016, refined by 2021 Rules). Its Route 1 (licensed-manager) path can be approved in **1 business day**, capped at 50 investors, with light-touch ongoing regulation — well suited to forming a continuation vehicle quickly. Guernsey's strength in **PCC/ICC** structures also supports multi-strategy/multi-asset variations.

For contrast, the corpus notes the competing offerings: **Jersey** (JPF with 48-hour JFSC authorisation and the DSP model) and **Cayman** (ELP under the Private Funds Act 2020, still dominant where US-LP capital is involved). Jersey practitioners have shifted inventory to match Guernsey, with multiple Jersey-domiciled continuation funds launched in 2025–2026 — but the corpus frames Guernsey as the front-runner on this particular product.
