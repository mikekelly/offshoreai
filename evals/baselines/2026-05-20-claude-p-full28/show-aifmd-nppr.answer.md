## Marketing your Jersey AIF into Germany under AIFMD NPPR

Germany is one of the EU member states Jersey funds reach through the **National Private Placement Regime (NPPR)** rather than the full AIFMD passport. The full EU-wide passport is only available to EU-AIFM-managed EU AIFs; the proposed "third-country passport" extension to non-EU jurisdictions like Jersey was proposed but never implemented. So NPPR is the practical route. [knowledge/jersey/funds/aifmd-nppr.md, knowledge/jersey/use-cases/fund-counsel/aifmd-nppr-registration.md]

### Step 1 — Get your AIFM Jersey-registered first
Before any German notification, your AIFM must be **registered with the JFSC under Jersey's AIFMD-equivalent regime**, and must demonstrate substantive AIFMD-equivalent compliance:
- capital adequacy proportionate to AIFM size;
- a risk-management framework equivalent to AIFMD Art 15;
- liquidity management (for open-ended AIFs);
- depository arrangements;
- transparency to investors (annual report, periodic reporting);
- regulatory reporting to the JFSC.

Only once Jersey-AIFMD-registered can you pursue the German notification. [knowledge/jersey/funds/aifmd-nppr.md, knowledge/jersey/use-cases/fund-counsel/aifmd-nppr-registration.md]

### Step 2 — Notify BaFin under §330 KAGB
Germany's competent authority is **BaFin**, and its NPPR rules sit under **§330 KAGB**. The corpus flags Germany (alongside France) as one of the *stricter* NPPR regimes. The German-specific requirements noted are:
- a **local fact-sheet**;
- a **local distributor** (sometimes required);
- **reporting obligations** after the notification is accepted.

The general notification mechanics: identify the authority (BaFin), confirm that the Jersey AIFM regime is recognised as equivalent by Germany, prepare the German notification package, submit, and await clearance (timeline varies). Once cleared, you may market **to professional investors in Germany**. [knowledge/jersey/use-cases/fund-counsel/aifmd-nppr-registration.md]

### Step 3 — Depository
A Jersey AIF marketed into the EU via NPPR typically needs **AIFMD-equivalent depository arrangements** covering custody, oversight and cash-flow monitoring. Jersey often runs a "lighter" depository for non-EU-marketed funds, so for German marketing you need to ensure the depository meets the AIFMD-equivalent standard — the corpus explicitly warns that a lighter Jersey depository for EU-marketed AIFs is a regulatory mismatch and a pitfall. [knowledge/jersey/funds/aifmd-nppr.md, knowledge/jersey/use-cases/fund-counsel/aifmd-nppr-registration.md]

### Two things to keep in mind
- **Reverse solicitation:** NPPR/BaFin notification is only triggered by *active* marketing. If a German investor approaches you unsolicited, that doesn't require NPPR — but member-state authorities scrutinise reverse-solicitation claims, and active outreach disguised as reverse solicitation attracts penalties. [knowledge/jersey/use-cases/fund-counsel/aifmd-nppr-registration.md]
- **A common Jersey vehicle** for AIFMD-NPPR marketing is the Expert Fund, per the cross-references (not read in full here). [knowledge/jersey/funds/aifmd-nppr.md]

### A caveat the corpus raises elsewhere
AIFMD II applies in the EU from **16 April 2026**, and per the corpus's frontier/trajectory layer its impact on Jersey funds runs mainly through tightened NPPR conditions, disclosure, and Annex IV reporting uplift. The two files above don't yet incorporate AIFMD II changes to the German route, so confirm current BaFin conditions against the AIFMD II transposition before relying on the steps above. [knowledge/jersey/history/finance/trajectory.md]
