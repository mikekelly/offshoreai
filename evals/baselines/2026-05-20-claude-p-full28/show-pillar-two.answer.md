## Pillar Two applied to a Jersey TopCo above a UK operating business

**The threshold gate comes first.** Pillar Two only engages if the *consolidated group* (Jersey TopCo + UK Opco + any other subsidiaries) has annual consolidated revenue of **EUR 750m or more** in at least two of the four preceding fiscal years. Below that threshold, nothing changes: the Jersey TopCo stays on zero‑ten at 0% and there is no Pillar Two filing (`knowledge/jersey/use-cases/founder-entrepreneur/worked-example-jersey-topco-uk-listing.md`, step 4; `knowledge/jersey/use-cases/founder-entrepreneur/pillar-two-impact.md`). Most founder/family structures sit below this line.

**If the group is in scope, two things run in parallel:**

1. **Jersey side — MCIT as a QDMTT.** Jersey implements Pillar Two through the **Multinational Corporate Income Tax (Jersey) Law 2024 ("MCIT")**, effective for financial years beginning on or after **1 January 2025**. It is structured as a Qualified Domestic Minimum Top‑up Tax (a "QDMTT‑equivalent"), combined with an Income Inclusion Rule and a Domestic Top‑up Tax. Because the Jersey TopCo's local effective rate under zero‑ten is typically 0%, the MCIT charges a **top‑up to bring the Jersey effective rate to 15%** on GloBE income. The whole point of Jersey collecting this itself is so the top‑up tax is *kept in Jersey* rather than ceded to a foreign parent jurisdiction under the IIR/UTPR (`knowledge/jersey/tax/pillar-two.md`; `knowledge/jersey/international/pillar-two-mcit.md`; worked-example step 4).

2. **UK side — UK Pillar Two runs in parallel.** The corpus states that "UK Pillar Two implementation runs in parallel" on the group's UK-side entities, with group consolidation tests applying across both (`worked-example-jersey-topco-uk-listing.md`, step 4). The corpus describes the Jersey TopCo's Jersey-source income as being "topped up to 15% rather than ceded to the UK under the IIR" — i.e. MCIT pre-empts the foreign-parent top-up.

**What the 15% bites on.** The minimum is **15% on GloBE income**, which starts from consolidated financial-accounting income with prescribed GloBE adjustments — *not* the same base as Jersey local taxable income. A jurisdictional ETR is computed; if Jersey's ETR is below 15%, the top-up applies. A **substance-based income exclusion** (a formulaic carve-out keyed to payroll and tangible assets, reducing on a sliding scale in early years) reduces the income subject to top-up (`knowledge/jersey/tax/pillar-two.md`; `knowledge/jersey/international/pillar-two-mcit.md`).

**What stays the same / what changes:**
- Zero‑ten remains the *local* Jersey position (0%/10%/20%); MCIT is an *additional* top‑up layer, so total Jersey tax for an in‑scope group is **at least 15%** (`knowledge/jersey/tax/pillar-two.md`).
- Economic substance obligations under the **Taxation (Companies — Economic Substance) (Jersey) Law 2019** continue independently — a pure holding TopCo gets the reduced substance test but still needs adequate Jersey people and premises (`worked-example...`, step 3; `knowledge/jersey/use-cases/founder-entrepreneur/topco-tax.md`).
- Compliance burden rises materially: registration of Jersey constituent entities, GloBE income/ETR computation, MCIT returns, GloBE Information Return (GIR) obligations, and coordination with the UK's parallel Pillar Two compliance — typically requiring specialist software and advisers (`knowledge/jersey/tax/pillar-two.md`; `knowledge/jersey/international/pillar-two-mcit.md`; `pillar-two-impact.md`).
- The strategic consequence the corpus flags: once at a 15% effective rate, the founder should reassess whether Jersey is still preferable to an onshore TopCo (UK, Ireland), weighing migration cost against Jersey's non-tax advantages (corporate-law flexibility, no investor-level Jersey CGT, LSE listing acceptance) (`pillar-two-impact.md`).

**Caveats for the reader.** All five source files carry `status: draft`. On directional precision: in this structure the Jersey TopCo is the *parent* and the UK Opco is the *subsidiary*; the corpus's phrase "rather than ceded to the UK under the IIR" describes MCIT pre-empting a foreign top-up but does not work through which specific GloBE charging rule (IIR vs UTPR) the UK would otherwise apply given that the UK entity sits *below* the Jersey parent — the corpus does not analyse that mechanical detail.
