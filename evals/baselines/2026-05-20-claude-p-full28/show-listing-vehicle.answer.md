Based on the corpus, here's how the three vehicles compare for a NASDAQ listing.

## The headline guidance

The cross-jurisdictional decision framework directly addresses listing-vehicle TopCos. Its recommendations split by business profile rather than naming one universal winner:

- **US-listed tech / general operating holdco → Cayman Exempted** (the "Alibaba pattern")
- **Insurance / reinsurance listing → Bermuda Exempted**
- **Lower-cost smaller listing → BVI BC**, but with the explicit caveat of "some exchange-acceptance limits" (`knowledge/CROSS-JURISDICTIONAL-MAP.md`).

## The exchange-acceptance point (the deciding factor for NASDAQ)

All three are recognised offshore listing domiciles, but the corpus flags a real difference in how readily exchanges accept them:

- **Cayman Exempted** is described as one of three jurisdictions (alongside BVI and Delaware) "routinely accepted by major exchanges as listing vehicles," and is expressly the form used by many US-listed companies with Asia/EMEA operations — the Alibaba / TikTok holdco pattern. Its listing-acceptance row reads "NYSE, NASDAQ, HKEx" without qualification (`knowledge/cayman/exempted-company.md`).
- **Bermuda Exempted** is "routinely listed on NYSE / NASDAQ / LSE," and is noted as a preferred listing domicile particularly for insurance / reinsurance and specialty businesses, partly because the Bermuda Monetary Authority pre-vets every incorporation, which "delivers regulatory confidence to counterparties" (`knowledge/bermuda/exempted-company.md`).
- **BVI BC** carries the most qualified acceptance. The Cayman file's own comparison table lists BVI listing acceptance as "NYSE, NASDAQ, HKEx (**more limited**)," and the cross-jurisdictional map ties the BVI recommendation to "lower-cost smaller listing" with "some exchange-acceptance limits" (`knowledge/cayman/exempted-company.md`, `knowledge/CROSS-JURISDICTIONAL-MAP.md`). The BVI file itself frames the BC as a volume/standard-structure vehicle rather than a sophisticated-listing one (`knowledge/bvi/business-company.md`).

## Recommendation per the corpus

For a NASDAQ listing of a normal operating/tech group, the corpus points to **Cayman Exempted** as the default — it has the cleanest exchange acceptance and is the established US-listing TopCo pattern, with highly flexible capital structure (multiple share classes, redeemable shares) suited to pre-IPO structuring (`knowledge/cayman/exempted-company.md`).

Choose **Bermuda Exempted** instead if you are an **insurance / reinsurance or specialty** group — that is the profile the corpus explicitly steers to Bermuda, and the BMA pre-vetting is a feature there rather than a drag (`knowledge/bermuda/exempted-company.md`, `knowledge/CROSS-JURISDICTIONAL-MAP.md`).

Treat **BVI BC** as the cost-driven option for a smaller listing, accepting that exchange acceptance is "more limited" — the corpus does not present it as the first choice for a NASDAQ TopCo (`knowledge/bvi/business-company.md`, `knowledge/CROSS-JURISDICTIONAL-MAP.md`).

## Two factors the corpus does not resolve

- **NASDAQ-specific listing rules** (foreign-private-issuer eligibility, governance carve-outs, etc.) are not covered — the corpus speaks only at the jurisdiction-acceptance level, not NASDAQ's own rulebook. The corpus does not answer that part.
- **Pillar Two exposure** differs and may matter if your group is large (consolidated revenue ≥ €750m): Bermuda's Corporate Income Tax (15%) is in force for fiscal years from 1 Jan 2025; Cayman has a similar MCIT regime; the BVI file notes no equivalent top-up tax "yet" (`knowledge/bermuda/exempted-company.md`, `knowledge/cayman/exempted-company.md`, `knowledge/bvi/business-company.md`). Below that threshold, all three remain no-direct-tax.
