## Why a UK HNW investor holds an Isle of Man portfolio bond

Drawing on `knowledge/isle-of-man/life-assurance.md`, the corpus gives a clear set of reasons.

**The product itself.** An IoM portfolio bond is a *single-premium* investment vehicle wrapped inside a life-assurance policy. You put a lump sum in, and inside the wrapper you can hold a wide range of underlying assets — funds, equities, fixed income, alternatives — with the investment strategy designated by you (usually run through an investment manager). The IoM insurer handles policy administration, and you have surrender flexibility (subject to product penalties).

**The core UK tax driver — the "5% rule."** This is the headline reason for a UK resident. The corpus describes it as a defining UK tax mechanism for offshore life-assurance bonds:

- You can withdraw **5% of the original premium each year, tax-deferred, for up to 20 years**.
- The chargeable-event charge is deferred until final encashment, when it is treated as deferred income.
- **Top-slicing relief** then reduces the effective rate for higher-rate / additional-rate taxpayers.
- The statutory basis is Chapter 9 of Part 4 ITTOIA 2005.

The net effect, per the corpus, is "materially better cash-flow tax treatment than ordinary investment for HNW UK-residents" — alongside ISAs and pensions — plus tax deferral on growth and estate-planning efficiency in some jurisdictions.

**Why the Isle of Man specifically (rather than another offshore centre).** The corpus calls the IoM "the world's leading offshore life-assurance jurisdiction" for these products. Its distinguishing strengths:

- **Solvency II equivalence** with the EU — a competitive advantage no other offshore life-assurance domicile in the comparison table holds, giving its insurers regulatory standing and EU-market reach.
- A **long-established, specialist industry** — major UK and global insurers (Quilter, Standard Life International, Royal London, Canada Life International, Utmost) run IoM subsidiaries, and the IOMFSA has deep sector-specific regulatory expertise.
- **Tax-neutral treatment of the wrapping insurer**, and a reputable, mature financial-services jurisdiction.

In the corpus's cross-jurisdiction comparison, the IoM is the "largest offshore life-assurance domicile," while Jersey has only "some specialty; smaller than IoM," Guernsey runs specialty captive life vehicles, and Dublin/Luxembourg serve EU-focused (rather than UK-focused) business. Post-Brexit, the corpus notes the IoM "remains a competitive alternative for UK-focused business."

**Caveats the corpus flags.** Watch for product misclassification in the policyholder's tax jurisdiction, adviser-suitability concerns (these are designed for HNW/sophisticated investors, sold through FCA-authorised advisers, specialist IFAs, and private banks — not mass retail), and AML/CFT exposure in the intermediary chain.
