## AIFMD II and the Jersey NPPR-marketing AIFM

The headline point: **NPPR survives.** AIFMD II (Directive (EU) 2024/927, most operational requirements effective 16 April 2026) does *not* remove Crown Dependencies' route into EU professional-investor markets. The directive preserves access via member states' National Private Placement Regimes, and Jersey continues to meet the EU's tax-transparency and AML criteria (`knowledge/frontier/aifmd-ii-april-2026.md`). Jersey was already a non-EU jurisdiction relying on country-by-country NPPR notification rather than the (never-implemented) third-country passport, so the baseline route is unchanged (`knowledge/jersey/funds/aifmd-nppr.md`).

What actually changes for a Jersey-based AIFM marketing under NPPR falls into a few buckets:

**Direct, but limited, changes (`knowledge/frontier/aifmd-ii-april-2026.md`):**
1. **Wider investor disclosures** — pre-marketing and ongoing disclosure obligations are expanded to cover liquidity management, sustainability factors (interlocking with SFDR), and loan-originating-fund specifics where relevant. PPMs and ongoing transparency reports are being refreshed for AIFMD II language.
2. **Enhanced Annex IV reporting** — significantly more granular regulatory reporting to ESMA via competent authorities, with new data fields and asset/risk-exposure detail. Firms are upgrading reporting systems; first submissions under the new requirements are expected end-Q2/early-Q3 2026.

**Jersey-side regime change:** the JFSC is splitting its **AIF Code of Practice** into an **EU AIF Code** (for NPPR-into-EU marketing, aligned with AIFMD II) and a **UK AIF Code** (for UK marketing). The EU AIF Code is expected to be finalised through 2026. This lets Jersey serve both markets without one regime straining to satisfy both regulators (`knowledge/frontier/aifmd-ii-april-2026.md`).

**Indirect effects on structures (`knowledge/frontier/aifmd-ii-april-2026.md`):** AIFMD II tightens rules on EU AIFMs *delegating* to third countries — enhanced substance/oversight, no "letter-box" EU entity, and limits on delegating loan-origination to non-EU entities. This bites on the "EU-AIFM-with-a-Jersey-delegate" model, not directly on a direct Jersey AIFM. Counterintuitively, commentary (Carey Olsen, Langham Hall) suggests this makes a **direct Jersey AIFM marketing under NPPR comparatively more attractive** than the delegation model; sponsors are revisiting direct Channel Islands domiciliation.

**Things that don't change but warrant care:**
- NPPR remains country-by-country, with Germany (§330 KAGB) and France (AMF) stricter than the Netherlands/Luxembourg/Ireland; member-state transposition of AIFMD II is uneven, so a fund marketed across several states may face differing effective requirements (`knowledge/jersey/use-cases/fund-counsel/aifmd-nppr-registration.md`; `knowledge/frontier/aifmd-ii-april-2026.md`).
- The depository requirement persists — EU-marketed Jersey AIFs need AIFMD-equivalent depository arrangements (`knowledge/jersey/funds/aifmd-nppr.md`; `knowledge/jersey/use-cases/fund-counsel/aifmd-nppr-registration.md`).
- Reverse solicitation still avoids NPPR, but is scrutinised (`knowledge/jersey/use-cases/fund-counsel/aifmd-nppr-registration.md`).
- The corpus flags pitfalls: do not treat AIFMD II as "small deltas" (loan-originating and substance rules are substantive) and do not assume NPPR is wholly unaffected (disclosure and Annex IV have widened) (`knowledge/frontier/aifmd-ii-april-2026.md`).
