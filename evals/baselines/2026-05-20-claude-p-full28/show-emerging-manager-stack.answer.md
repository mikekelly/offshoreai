## The BVI emerging-manager structure stack

For a BVI-based emerging manager, the corpus describes a three-layer stack — **manager ��� fund → vehicle** — built entirely from BVI light-touch regimes that are explicitly designed for new sponsors who want recognised regulatory standing without the cost and lead-time of full licensing.

### 1. The manager — BVI Approved Manager

Your management entity should take **Approved Manager** status under the **Investment Business (Approved Manager) Regulations 2012**. This is a fast-track, light-touch authorisation sitting *below* a full BVI Investment Business (SIBA) licence — designed precisely for "start-up, smaller, or family-office managers." It gives you:

- Authorisation in a few weeks rather than months, at lower initial and ongoing cost;
- The ability to hold yourself out as a regulated manager (which banks, prime brokers and LPs want to see), to manage and advise BVI funds, and to charge management and performance fees;
- An AUM ceiling — if you grow past the prescribed threshold you must upgrade to full Investment Business licensing.

Ongoing, you must keep personnel fit-and-proper, file annual returns, pay annual fees, comply with AML/CFT and (where in scope) economic substance. It can manage BVI Private, Professional, Incubator, Approved or Public funds, plus closely-connected non-BVI funds. (`knowledge/bvi/approved-manager.md`)

### 2. The fund — Incubator Fund or Approved Fund

Both are light-touch regimes under the **SIBA (Incubator and Approved Funds) Regulations 2015**, sitting beneath the Private and Professional Fund tiers. As an *emerging* manager, the natural choice is the **Incubator Fund**, which is explicitly oriented to brand-new managers building a track record:

| | **Incubator Fund** | **Approved Fund** |
|---|---|---|
| Max investors | 20 | 20 |
| Min investment | US$20,000/investor | None |
| Max AUM | US$20m | US$100m |
| Time limit | 2 years (+1 year extension) | Permanent |
| Auditor | Not required | Not required |
| Administrator | Not required | Typically required |
| Investment manager | Not required (but typical) | Typically required |
| Purpose | Track-record build, then convert | Stable small/family fund |

The Incubator is intentionally time-limited — a launch pad. After two years you must convert (typically to a Private Fund) or wind down. The Approved Fund is the alternative if you intend to stay small and permanent (e.g. a family-office or friends-and-family pool up to US$100m). Both funds can be run by an Approved Manager — the corpus calls "Approved Manager + Incubator Fund / Approved Fund" a **common emerging-manager stack**. (`knowledge/bvi/incubator-and-approved-fund.md`)

### 3. The vehicle — BVI Business Company (or limited partnership)

The fund itself, and the related entities, are usually formed as **BVI Business Companies (BCs)** under the **BVI Business Companies Act 2004** — the principal offshore vehicle in the BVI (400,000+ active, incorporable in hours, low fees, one director/one shareholder, no residence requirement, registered agent + registered office mandatory, BO held by the agent via BOSS). The BC is described as the **most common** fund vehicle. The alternative form is a **limited partnership** (GP/LP), common for closed-ended PE/VC where LPs want tax transparency; a Segregated Portfolio Company (SPC) is available for cell-style umbrellas but is less prevalent than in Cayman. (`knowledge/bvi/business-company.md`, `knowledge/bvi/funds.md`)

### Putting it together — the start-up stack

The Approved Manager file lays out the typical first-fund sequence directly:

1. Form the **GP entity** (BVI BC);
2. Form the **management entity** (BVI BC) and obtain **Approved Manager** status;
3. Form the **fund** (BVI BC, or LP under the BVI Partnership Act), as an **Incubator Fund** (or Approved Fund);
4. Operate with full Approved Manager status from inception, upgrading to full SIBA licensing / a Private or Professional Fund only when AUM justifies it.

(`knowledge/bvi/approved-manager.md`)
