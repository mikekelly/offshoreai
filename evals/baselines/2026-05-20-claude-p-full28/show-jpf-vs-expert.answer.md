## Recommendation: a JPF, almost certainly

For a **club-deal PE fund**, the corpus points clearly at the **Jersey Private Fund (JPF)**.

**Why the JPF fits a club deal directly.** The JPF concept file lists its use cases explicitly, and "**club deal** structures" is one of them — alongside private equity funds, co-investment vehicles, and carry vehicles for the management team (`knowledge/jersey/funds/jersey-private-fund.md`). A club deal — a small group of known, sophisticated investors pooling capital into a specific deal or strategy — is squarely what the regime was built for.

**The mechanics line up:**
- **Investor count.** A JPF allows up to **50 offers/investors** at any time (`knowledge/jersey/funds/jersey-private-fund.md`). A club deal is by nature a small, curated group, so the 50-investor cap is rarely a constraint.
- **Investors must be professional/eligible** — not a retail public offer — which matches a club of sophisticated PE participants (`knowledge/jersey/funds/jersey-private-fund.md`).
- **Speed.** JFSC authorisation is typically issued within **~48 hours**, versus "a few days to weeks" for an Expert Fund (`knowledge/jersey/funds/jersey-private-fund.md`; comparison table in `knowledge/jersey/funds/expert-fund.md`). For a deal-driven club structure, speed-to-market matters.
- **Light-touch supervision** via the designated service provider (DSP) model: a Jersey-licensed administrator handles CDD/AML and is JFSC-supervised, substituting for direct oversight of the fund itself, with only a light PPM rather than a full offering memorandum (`knowledge/jersey/funds/jersey-private-fund.md`).
- **Structure.** It can be set up as a Jersey limited partnership — the most common and the usual PE form — or a company, unit trust, or cell company, chosen on investor tax preferences (`knowledge/jersey/funds/jersey-private-fund.md`).

**When you'd instead reach for the Expert Fund.** The Expert Fund becomes the better choice when you outgrow the JPF's frame — specifically a **large private-equity vehicle that exceeds the JPF investor count**, or where you need wider expert-investor marketing latitude (`knowledge/jersey/funds/expert-fund.md`). It has no specific investor cap (subject to expert-investor criteria), but in exchange you accept a more detailed offering memorandum and a slower registration (`knowledge/jersey/funds/expert-fund.md`). A "club deal" almost by definition is *not* a 50+-investor raise, so these advantages don't bite.

**One thing to confirm separately — EU/UK marketing.** If you intend to actively market the fund into the EU or UK, note that the Expert Fund file flags AIFMD NPPR compliance as one of its use cases, and both regimes cross-reference the NPPR (`knowledge/jersey/funds/expert-fund.md`; `knowledge/jersey/funds/index.md`). The JPF file itself doesn't discuss how the JPF interacts with NPPR marketing — **the corpus does not, in these files, spell out the JPF's NPPR marketing position**, so if cross-border marketing is central to your club deal, check `knowledge/jersey/funds/aifmd-nppr.md` before finalising (I have not read it here).

**Bottom line:** for a club-deal PE fund, choose the **JPF** — the corpus names club deals and PE as its core use case, the ≤50 professional-investor cap suits a small club, and you get ~48-hour authorisation and light-touch supervision. Move to an Expert Fund only if the investor base will exceed the JPF's 50-investor limit.
