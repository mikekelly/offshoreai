## Comparison for an emerging sub-US$100m PE manager

You're at the size and stage where all four jurisdictions have a purpose-built "light-touch, fast-launch" private-fund product. They cluster into two groups: the **50-investor sophisticated-investor fast-track** (Jersey JPF, Guernsey PIF) and the **20-investor capped-AUM regime** (BVI Approved Fund), with the **Cayman ELP** sitting apart as the dominant global PE *vehicle form* rather than a capped light-touch regime.

### Jersey Private Fund (JPF)
Jersey's light-touch closed-investor regime. Up to **50 offers/investors**, professional/eligible investors only. Fast-track JFSC authorisation **typically within 48 hours**. Its defining feature is the **Designated Service Provider (DSP) model**: a Jersey-licensed administrator carries out CDD/AML, keeps books and records, and substitutes for direct JFSC oversight of the fund itself. The corpus calls the JPF the "default Jersey fund vehicle" for PE funds, co-investment vehicles, closed-ended real estate, club deals and carry vehicles. It can be a Jersey company, limited partnership (most common), unit trust, or cell company. (`knowledge/jersey/funds/jersey-private-fund.md`)

### Guernsey Private Investment Fund (PIF)
Guernsey's direct competitor to the JPF, launched 2016 (substantive Rules 2021). Also capped at **50 investors**, approvable in **~1 business day** (Route 1). Its distinguishing feature is **three routes**:
- **Route 1** — appoint a GFSC-licensed Guernsey manager who takes regulatory responsibility (fastest, most common);
- **Route 2** — restricted to a genuine family/private-relationship investor class, no licensed manager required;
- **Route 3** — restricted to qualifying professional/sophisticated investors, no licensed manager required.

PIFs require **audited financial statements** and ongoing GFSC supervision. The corpus frames PIF vs JPF as competing offerings: the PIF wins on route flexibility (especially the family/qualifying-investor routes), the JPF wins for sponsors who prefer the DSP delegation model's regulatory predictability. PIFs also support PCC/ICC cell structures, which is a particular Guernsey strength for multi-strategy platforms. (`knowledge/guernsey/private-investment-fund.md`)

### BVI Approved Fund
This is the regime that most precisely matches your "sub-US$100m" framing. It is a **permanent** light-touch regime for stable, smaller funds: **up to 20 investors**, **maximum fund assets of US$100 million**, **no statutory minimum investment**, **no mandatory auditor**, with an administrator and investment manager typically required. Approval is faster than the Private/Professional Fund route (typically within weeks). Note the trade-off versus Jersey/Guernsey: only **20 investors** (vs 50) and a hard **US$100m AUM ceiling** — outgrow either and you must convert up the ladder (Approved → Private Fund (50 investors) → Professional Fund). (`knowledge/bvi/incubator-and-approved-fund.md`)

Two BVI nuances for an emerging manager specifically:
- The corpus notes the **Incubator Fund** (20 investors, US$20m AUM cap, two-year time limit, no admin/auditor needed) is the regime "explicitly oriented to brand-new managers with limited operational infrastructure" — if you're truly building a track record from scratch, that's the more emerging-manager-tuned option, with the Approved Fund as the permanent step up. (`knowledge/bvi/incubator-and-approved-fund.md`)
- BVI pairs these funds with the **Approved Manager** regime (Investment Business (Approved Manager) Regulations 2012) — a fast-track (a few weeks), low-cost, light-touch manager authorisation explicitly designed for "start-up, smaller, or family-office managers" below an AUM threshold. The corpus describes the "Approved Manager + Approved Fund" combination as "a common emerging-manager stack," and the typical first-fund stack as GP entity + management entity (Approved Manager) + fund, all BVI. This is the most complete *out-of-the-box emerging-manager package* of the four. (`knowledge/bvi/approved-manager.md`, `knowledge/bvi/incubator-and-approved-fund.md`)

### Cayman Exempted Limited Partnership (ELP)
Different in kind from the other three. The ELP is not an investor-capped light-touch *regime* — it's the **dominant closed-ended PE/VC vehicle form globally**, governed by the ELP Act, with the **LPA as the master contractual document** and the statute supplying default rules. A closed-ended ELP fund typically registers as a **private fund under the Private Funds Act 2020** (CIMA registration, annual filings, **audited financial statements**, CIMA oversight) — so there's no 20/50-investor cap, but also no "skip the audit / 48-hour" lightness. Why managers reach for it anyway: **tax transparency** across US/UK/EU investors, total **LPA flexibility** over governance and waterfall, **investor familiarity** (global LPs expect the form), and **parallel-structure compatibility** (Cayman ELP for non-US LPs alongside a Delaware LP for US LPs). The corpus also documents standard fund economics it accommodates (2%/20%/8% pref, 5-year investment period, 10-year term). (`knowledge/cayman/exempted-limited-partnership.md`)

### How to read this for your decision

| Dimension | Jersey JPF | Guernsey PIF | BVI Approved Fund | Cayman ELP |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Max investors | 50 | 50 | 20 | No cap (form, not regime) |
| AUM ceiling | None stated | None stated | **US$100m** | None |
| Speed | ~48 hours | ~1 business day (Route 1) | Weeks | 1–2 days to register the entity; PFA registration separate |
| Audit required | Required (per Guernsey-comparison table) | Required | **Not required** | Required (under PFA 2020) |
| Defining feature | DSP delegation model | 3 routes (incl. family/qualifying) | Permanent capped light-touch | Contractual LPA flexibility; global standard |
| Best fit | EU-marketing (NPPR), trust-law depth, PE default | Route flexibility, family pools, cell platforms | Genuinely small/stable fund, lowest cost/burden | Global LP base, US parallel structures, scaling intent |

The corpus does not rank these for you or give cost figures, and per the cross-jurisdictional decision framework it places **"EU-marketing-focused / NPPR" → Jersey JPF or Guernsey PIF**, and **"emerging manager, sub-$100m, BVI-friendly" → BVI Approved Manager + Approved Fund** (`knowledge/CROSS-JURISDICTIONAL-MAP.md`, transcluded). The practical read: if your LP base is European and you'll market under NPPR, Jersey/Guernsey; if you want the lightest, cheapest emerging-manager stack and can live within 20 investors / US$100m, BVI; if you have (or expect) a global/US institutional LP base and may scale past these caps, Cayman ELP is the form built for that even though it carries full audit/CIMA registration.

One caveat the corpus flags repeatedly: across all four, **economic substance** for the GP/manager entity and **substantive (not paper) AML/CFT** are live obligations — "Approved Manager is a regime, not a loophole."
