## Jersey vs Guernsey trust — when to pick which

**Start from the baseline that they're siblings, not opposites.** Both descend from shared Norman customary law and both have been modernised by statute — Jersey by the Trusts (Jersey) Law 1984, Guernsey by the Trusts (Guernsey) Law 2007 (TGL 2007). Both recognise discretionary, fixed-interest, accumulation and purpose trusts; both allow settlor-reserved powers without invalidating the trust; both provide a firewall against foreign forced-heirship; both permit perpetual (unlimited-duration) trusts; both apply *Schmidt v Rosewood* beneficiary-information principles; both keep trust documents private (while maintaining beneficial-ownership registers for authorities); and both are supervised by a Royal Court with final appeal to the Privy Council. For most cross-border family-wealth structures they are **competitive substitutes** (`knowledge/guernsey/trust-law-differences-from-jersey.md`).

So the choice turns on a handful of specific factors.

**Lean Jersey when:**

- **You want maximum forced-heirship / matrimonial-claim defence.** Jersey's Article 9 firewall is the "gold standard." The 2006 Article 9(4) amendment explicitly disapplies foreign *judgments* based on inconsistent law. Guernsey's equivalent (s.14 TGL 2007) is comparable in effect but **less explicit**, and its foreign-judgment provisions are narrower in detail. For aggressive-defence structures, Jersey's Article 9(4) language remains the marginally preferred choice.
- **You expect to need the statutory mistake / set-aside regime and want the deepest case law.** Both jurisdictions have codified a mistake/inadequate-deliberation regime wider than the English post-*Pitt v Holt* approach, but Jersey's Article 47B–J case law (the *Re S&T Trusts* three-limb test and its progeny) is **more developed**.
- **You value the larger trust-company industry**, or you're combining the trust with a Jersey Private Fund (JPF) (`knowledge/guernsey/trust-law-differences-from-jersey.md`).

**Lean Guernsey when:**

- **You need cell-company structuring around the trustee.** Guernsey offers both the PCC *and* the ICC (Jersey offers the PCC only).
- **You're pairing the trust with a Guernsey fund** (the PIF, Guernsey's fast-track fund vehicle, vs Jersey's JPF).
- **You have an image-rights angle** — per the cross-jurisdictional decision framework, a trust combined with the image-rights register points to Guernsey, which holds the only image-rights register in the world (`knowledge/CROSS-JURISDICTIONAL-MAP.md`).

**Factors that don't decide it:**

- Reserved powers (Jersey's Article 9A menu vs Guernsey's Part II) — different in detail, broadly equivalent in outcome; you can build the same arrangement in either.
- EU-marketing context — both equivalent.
- Confidentiality and beneficiary information rights — broadly equivalent.

**The practical tie-breakers.** Because the regimes are so close, the real-world choice often comes down to non-doctrinal factors: sponsor familiarity with one regime, existing service-provider relationships, where the administrator/group infrastructure sits, and the specific asset/family circumstances (`knowledge/guernsey/trust-law-differences-from-jersey.md`).

**Bottom line:** default to either for a standard discretionary family trust; pick **Jersey** when forced-heirship defence or mistake-regime case-law depth is the priority; pick **Guernsey** when you need ICC/PCC trustee structuring, a PIF pairing, or image-rights integration (`knowledge/guernsey/trust-law-differences-from-jersey.md`, `knowledge/CROSS-JURISDICTIONAL-MAP.md`).
