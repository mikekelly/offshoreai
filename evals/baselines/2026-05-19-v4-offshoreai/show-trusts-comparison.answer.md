# Jersey trust vs Cayman STAR vs BVI VISTA — when do I use which?

These three regimes solve **different structural problems**. Pick by the problem first, jurisdiction second.

## The three regimes in one line each

- **Jersey trust** (Trusts (Jersey) Law 1984) — the **wealth-protection workhorse**: classical discretionary / fixed-interest trust hardened by the **Article 9 firewall** against foreign forced-heirship and matrimonial-regime claims, and the **Article 9A reserved-powers** regime allowing settlor control without invalidity (`knowledge/jersey/trusts/firewall.md`, `knowledge/jersey/trusts/reserved-powers.md`).
- **Cayman STAR** (Special Trusts (Alternative Regime) Act 1997) — the **purpose-trust solution**: a non-charitable purpose trust with **no required beneficiaries**, enforced by an **enforcer**, unlimited duration; designed for cases where the trust exists to hold something or pursue a purpose rather than benefit identifiable people (`knowledge/cayman/star-trusts.md`).
- **BVI VISTA** (Virgin Islands Special Trusts Act 2003) — the **share-ownership solution**: holds shares in a **designated BVI Business Company** and **switches off the Bartlett v Barclays Bank duty** of trustee intervention, so chosen directors can run the underlying business without trustee second-guessing; managed via "office of director rules" in the trust deed (`knowledge/bvi/vista-trusts.md`).

## Decision framework — pick by the problem

| If your problem is… | Use |
|---|---|
| Maximum defence against forced-heirship / civil-law clawback against a wealth-holder's family disposition | **Jersey** — Article 9 firewall is the strongest in the cluster (`knowledge/jersey/trusts/firewall.md`); `knowledge/CROSS-JURISDICTIONAL-MAP.md`) |
| Settlor wants to retain revocation / investment-direction / appointment powers without invalidating the trust (incl. US grantor-trust planning) | **Jersey** — Article 9A statutory menu (`knowledge/jersey/trusts/reserved-powers.md`) |
| Standard discretionary family trust with deep, predictable jurisprudence and Royal Court directions jurisdiction | **Jersey** (`knowledge/CROSS-JURISDICTIONAL-MAP.md`) |
| Family-owned **operating business** — founder wants trustee to hold shares but chosen directors (often family) to keep running the company without trustee monitoring or intervention | **BVI VISTA** — the Bartlett disengagement is the whole point (`knowledge/bvi/vista-trusts.md`) |
| **Orphan SPV** for securitisation / structured finance — trust must hold shares with no beneficiaries and be bankruptcy-remote from any settlor | **Cayman STAR** (`knowledge/cayman/star-trusts.md`; `knowledge/CROSS-JURISDICTIONAL-MAP.md`) |
| **Private Trust Company (PTC) holding vehicle** — orphan ownership of PTC shares | **Cayman STAR** (or Jersey Article 12 purpose trust as functional equivalent) (`knowledge/cayman/star-trusts.md`) |
| Pure non-charitable **purpose** trust (family governance, charitable-like-but-not-strictly-charitable purposes, multi-generational philanthropic structures with no defined beneficiaries) | **Cayman STAR** (`knowledge/cayman/star-trusts.md`) |

## Combining them

These are not mutually exclusive. A common HNW pattern uses **Jersey at the top** as the family wealth wrapper (firewall + reserved powers), with **Cayman STAR or BVI BCs as underlying holding vehicles** for specific assets, and a **BVI VISTA** layer where a family operating business sits in the structure (`knowledge/CROSS-JURISDICTIONAL-MAP.md` — "Where should I plan my international wealth?").

## Caveats

- All four cited files are currently **`status: draft`** — the substance reflects the corpus editorial team's working understanding but has not yet been re-verified to `stable`. Treat as orientation and verify the statutory cite (TJL 1984 Articles 9 / 9A, Cayman STAR Act 1997, BVI VISTA Act 2003) against the primary source before relying on it for transactional purposes.
- Tax characterisation (UK HMRC, US IRS, settlor-jurisdiction substance-over-form) is a **separate question** from trust-law validity in any of the three regimes — Article 9A in particular makes a trust valid as a matter of Jersey law but cannot dictate foreign tax treatment (`knowledge/jersey/trusts/reserved-powers.md`).
- The Jersey **Article 12 non-charitable purpose trust** is the functional Jersey equivalent of Cayman STAR for orphan / PTC-holding use cases; the corpus notes the two regimes are "functionally similar" but statutorily distinct (`knowledge/cayman/star-trusts.md`).
