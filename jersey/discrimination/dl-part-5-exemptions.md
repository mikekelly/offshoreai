# DL 2013 Part 5 — Exemptions

Part 5 contains **exemptions** from the DL — specific
circumstances where less-favourable treatment is permitted.

## Article 41 — Occupational requirements

Direct discrimination may be **lawful** where having a
particular protected characteristic is an **occupational
requirement**:

- **Genuine** and **determining** requirement for the role;
- **Proportionate** means of achieving a legitimate aim;
- **Examples**:
  - Female-only roles in specific contexts (women's refuge
    staff, fitting models);
  - Roles requiring **specific religion or belief**
    (religious organisation leadership);
  - Roles requiring **specific national identity** for
    cultural authenticity;
  - **Specific** age requirements for legitimate purposes.

## Article 42 — Positive action

**Positive action** is permitted:

- **Specific** measures to address disadvantage suffered by
  protected groups;
- **Encouragement** for under-represented groups to apply;
- **Targeted** training programs;
- **Subject to** specific Jersey statutory rules.

**Positive discrimination** (favouring a less-qualified
candidate purely on protected-characteristic grounds) is
generally **not** permitted.

## Article 43 — Age-specific exemptions

Specific age-related exemptions:

- **Retirement** ages where genuinely justified;
- **Length-of-service** based pay differentials;
- **Benefits** for specific age groups (parental benefits,
  young-worker schemes);
- **Sports** age categories;
- **Subject to** proportionate-means / legitimate-aim test.

## Article 44 — Pregnancy and maternity exemptions

Specific limited exemptions:

- **Specific** insurance / risk-based decisions during
  pregnancy (subject to medical evidence);
- **Specific** workplace safety measures.

## Article 45 — Religious / belief exemptions

Jersey does **not** generally include religion / belief as a
protected characteristic, but specific religious
considerations affect:

- **Religious schools** — specific exemptions for religion-of-
  pupil decisions;
- **Religious organisations** — leadership / membership rules;
- **Specific** religious accommodation in employment.

## Article 46 — Single-sex exemptions

Specific single-sex contexts:

- **Single-sex schools**;
- **Single-sex hostels / shelters** (e.g., women's refuges);
- **Single-sex sports** in specific competition contexts;
- **Specific** changing rooms, toilets, etc.

## Article 47 — Sport

Sports-specific exemptions:

- **Single-sex** competition;
- **Age-band** competition;
- **Disability sport** categorisation;
- **Specific** sport-specific rules.

## Articles 48-50 — Other exemptions

Other specific contexts:

### Charity exemptions

- **Charity** for benefit of specific group (e.g., disabled
  persons, women, specific ethnic group);
- **Subject to** charitable-purpose justification.

### Family circumstances

- **Specific** family-context exemptions;
- **Owner-occupier** sharing accommodation.

### Insurance

- **Risk-based** insurance with **actuarial** justification;
- **Subject to** specific rules and supervision.

### National security

- **Specific** national-security related exemptions;
- **Subject to** specific Jersey rules.

## Practical application

Exemptions must be:

- **Narrowly construed**;
- **Justified** in each specific case;
- **Documented**;
- **Proportionate**.

The DL is **fundamentally** designed to prohibit
discrimination — exemptions are **departures** from the
default rule.
