# CL 2014 Part 3 — Charity Commissioner

## The office

The **Charity Commissioner** is:

- **Appointed** by the States Assembly;
- **Independent** of the Council of Ministers;
- **Reports** to the States annually;
- **Funded** through charity fees and States subvention;
- **Holds** a defined term, renewable.

## Functions

The Commissioner:

### Registration

- **Receives** applications;
- **Adjudicates** on charitable status;
- **Maintains** the register (three sections);
- **Removes** charities as appropriate.

### Supervision

- **Receives** annual returns and accounts;
- **Reviews** material changes;
- **Identifies** concerns through reporting and complaints;
- **Engages** with charities on compliance.

### Investigation

- **Investigates** suspected misconduct;
- **Issues** directions to trustees;
- **Protects** charitable assets;
- **Refers** matters to AG for criminal investigation;
- **Coordinates** with JFSC where regulated trustees involved.

### Guidance

- **Publishes** guidance for charities and trustees;
- **Specific guidance** on application process, public
  benefit, governance;
- **Reports** on themes and trends;
- **Education** of the sector.

### Cooperation

- **Cooperates** with **UK Charity Commission**;
- **Cooperates** with **foreign charity regulators**;
- **Cooperates** with **JFSC** (regulated charity trustees);
- **Cooperates** with **Comptroller of Revenue** (tax-status
  matters).

## Powers

The Commissioner can:

- **Require information** from registered charities;
- **Inspect** charity records;
- **Issue directions** for compliance;
- **Suspend** trustees in serious cases;
- **Appoint** interim trustees in extremis;
- **Refer** to Royal Court for orders;
- **Impose** civil penalties (limited);
- **Remove** charity from register.

## Trustee fitness

The Commissioner assesses **fit-and-proper** of charity
trustees:

- **Good character**;
- **No** disqualifying convictions / bankruptcies;
- **Adequate** time and capacity;
- **Understanding** of trustee duties;
- **No** conflicts that compromise the role;
- **Specific** factors for high-profile / international
  charities.

## Reporting

The Commissioner:

- **Publishes** annual report on charity sector;
- **Reports** on key statistics (number of charities, sector
  health);
- **Reports** on enforcement and themes;
- **Reports** to the States Assembly.

## Accountability

The Commissioner:

- **Subject to** judicial review;
- Decisions **appealable** to Royal Court (Article 63);
- **Funded** transparently;
- **Accountable** to the States through reporting;
- **Independent** of Council of Ministers in decision-making.
