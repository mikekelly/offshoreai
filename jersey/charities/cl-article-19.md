# CL 2014 Article 19 — Charity Commissioner Functions

Article 19 sets out the **functions of the Jersey Charity
Commissioner** — the independent statutory regulator
established under the Charities (Jersey) Law 2014.

## General duty

The Commissioner has the general duty:

- **To determine** applications for charity registration;
- **To maintain** the charity register;
- **To regulate** registered charities;
- **To promote** compliance with charity law;
- **To act** in the public interest.

## Specific functions

Article 19 specifies that the Commissioner:

- **Determines** registration applications under Article 7;
- **Maintains** the public registered charity register;
- **Maintains** the restricted section;
- **Maintains** the historic section;
- **Issues** guidance on charity law;
- **Conducts** investigations under Part 5;
- **Issues** directions / orders;
- **Reports** annually to the States Assembly.

## Powers in support of functions

The Commissioner has powers to:

- **Request** information from charities;
- **Inspect** charity records;
- **Issue** directions for compliance;
- **Apply** to the Royal Court for protective orders;
- **Refer** for prosecution where criminal offences
  suspected.

## Independence

The Commissioner is:

- **Independent** in the exercise of functions;
- **Not subject** to direction by Ministers;
- **Reports** to the States Assembly;
- **Specific** appointment / removal procedures.

## Coordination with other regulators

The Commissioner coordinates with:

- **JFSC** for charities that are TCB clients;
- **Revenue Jersey** for tax-related issues;
- **JOIC** for data-protection issues;
- **Comptroller** for AML / regulatory overlap.

## Practical issues

For charity trustees:

- **First point of contact** for registration questions;
- **Guidance** issued on practical issues;
- **Charity register** is searchable public record;
- **Specific** statutory powers shape Commissioner
  interaction.
