# CL 2014 Article 39 — Investigations

Article 39 empowers the **Charity Commissioner to conduct
investigations** into registered charities and persons
purporting to operate as charities.

## Grounds for investigation

The Commissioner may investigate where there is:

- **Reasonable suspicion** of breach of charity law;
- **Reasonable suspicion** of trustee misconduct;
- **Concerns** about charity assets;
- **Concerns** about charity purposes / public benefit;
- **Public-interest** grounds;
- **Complaint** received and merits investigation.

## Scope of investigation

An investigation may cover:

- **Charity activities** and operations;
- **Charity finances** and accounts;
- **Trustee conduct**;
- **Compliance** with the Law and registration conditions;
- **Allegations** of mismanagement / misconduct.

## Powers in investigation

The Commissioner may:

- **Require** production of documents;
- **Require** persons to answer questions;
- **Enter** charity premises (with appropriate authority);
- **Seek** Royal Court orders for compulsion;
- **Issue** directions during investigation.

## Procedure

- **Written notice** of investigation initiation;
- **Specific** scope and purpose stated;
- **Cooperation** required from trustees;
- **Records** of investigation kept;
- **Outcome** report.

## Outcomes

Following investigation, the Commissioner may:

- **Take no further action**;
- **Issue directions** under Part 5;
- **Apply** to the Royal Court for orders;
- **Refer** for prosecution;
- **Remove** charity from register;
- **Apply** for trustee removal / appointment.

## Rights of the charity

The investigated charity has rights to:

- **Be informed** of the investigation scope;
- **Make representations**;
- **Receive** reasoned decisions;
- **Appeal** outcomes under Part 7;
- **Royal Court** challenge of unreasonable conduct.

## Practical issues

- **Investigations** are typically confidential during
  conduct;
- **Public** disclosure may follow conclusion;
- **AML / tax** parallel investigations possible;
- **Specific** procedural fairness requirements.
