# CL 2014 Article 56 — Offences

Article 56 establishes the **criminal offences** under the
Charities (Jersey) Law 2014.

## Principal offences

It is an offence to:

- **Misrepresent** an entity as a registered charity when
  it is not;
- **Use** "charity" / "charitable" terminology for an
  unregistered body in regulated contexts;
- **Make false statements** in registration applications;
- **Provide false information** to the Commissioner;
- **Fail to comply** with Commissioner directions;
- **Obstruct** investigations under Part 5;
- **Misappropriate** charity assets (with associated
  trustee offences);
- **Specific** procedural breaches.

## Trustee liability

Where the charity is a body corporate or trust:

- **Individual trustees** may be liable;
- **Specific** offences for breach of trustee duty;
- **Connivance, consent, or neglect** of officers
  attributable to them.

## Penalties

Article 56 specifies penalty levels:

- **Summary conviction** — fines / short imprisonment;
- **Indictable** offences — higher penalties for serious
  conduct;
- **Disqualification** from acting as charity trustee;
- **Specific** statutory penalty bands.

## Defences

Specific statutory defences may apply:

- **Reasonable belief** in lawfulness of conduct;
- **Due diligence** taken;
- **Acting under** Commissioner direction;
- **Specific** statutory defences for trustees.

## Procedural matters

- **Magistrate's Court** for summary offences;
- **Royal Court** for indictable offences;
- **Specific** evidential requirements;
- **Rights** of accused trustees / officers.

## Practical issues

For charity trustees:

- **Compliance** culture essential;
- **Documentation** of decisions important;
- **Specific** advice on borderline conduct;
- **Insurance** coverage for trustees (specific products
  available).

## Interaction with other laws

Conduct may also attract:

- **POCL 1999** — fraud / money laundering;
- **Fraud (Jersey) Law 2010** — specific fraud offences;
- **CJL 1991** — director / officer offences (where charity
  is corporate);
- **TJL 1984** — trust-related offences (where charity is a
  trust).
