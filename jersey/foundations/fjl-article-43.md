# FJL 2009 Article 43 — Distribution on Dissolution

Article 43 governs the **distribution of remaining property**
when a foundation is dissolved.

## The distribution waterfall

On dissolution, the council (or liquidator) must:

### 1. Discharge debts and liabilities

- **All creditors** paid in full (if solvent);
- **Specific** priority if insolvent (similar to BDL);
- **Contingent liabilities** provisioned;
- **Specific** tax obligations satisfied.

### 2. Distribute remaining property

After debts paid, remaining property goes per:

- The **charter / regulations** (typically prescribing
  beneficiaries or successor purposes);
- **Court order** (Article 42 dissolution);
- **Default rules** if charter / regulations silent.

### 3. Default distribution

If charter / regulations do not specify:

- To **named beneficiaries** if any;
- To **the founder** as residual (in some configurations);
- To **the Crown as bona vacantia** if no other claimant.

## Charitable foundations

For charitable foundations:

- **Cy-près** principle preferred over distribution to private
  parties;
- **Similar charitable purpose** identified;
- **Attorney General** typically convened;
- **Royal Court** approval of cy-près scheme;
- **Specific** charity law (Charities (Jersey) Law 2014)
  considerations.

## Tax considerations

Distribution on dissolution:

- **Jersey side**: typically no tax (foundations zero-rated);
- **Beneficiaries' jurisdictions**: distribution may be
  taxable in beneficiary's home jurisdiction;
- **Specific** considerations for receipt of foundation
  property by individuals.

## Creditor priority (insolvent)

For insolvent dissolution, similar priority to BDL:

1. **Costs** of dissolution / liquidation;
2. **Secured** creditors to extent of security;
3. **Preferential** creditors;
4. **Unsecured** creditors pari passu;
5. **Subordinated** creditors;
6. **Residual** to beneficiaries (rare in insolvency).

## Final filings

After distribution:

- **Final return** to Registrar;
- **Dissolution certificate** issued;
- **Foundation removed** from public register;
- **Records** retained per Article 32 (10+ years).

## Specific scenarios

### Family foundation winding up

- **Distribution** to family beneficiaries per regulations;
- **Tax planning** for receipt;
- **Documentation** of distribution decisions;
- **AML / CDD** confirmation on beneficiaries.

### Charitable foundation winding up

- **Cy-près** scheme typically;
- **Receiving charity** identified;
- **Court approval**;
- **Specific** Charity Commissioner involvement.

### Orphan structure winding up

- **Specific** purposes fulfilled;
- **Residual** to specified purposes;
- **Court guidance** if regulations unclear.
