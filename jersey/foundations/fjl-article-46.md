# FJL 2009 Article 46 — Applications to the Court

Article 46 is the **operative provision** for council /
guardian directions applications — the foundation analogue of
**Article 51 of the Trusts (Jersey) Law 1984**.

## The provision

The council or guardian may apply to the Royal Court for:

- **Directions** as to the proper exercise of a power or
  discretion;
- **Approval** of a proposed course of action (a
  *Public Trustee v Cooper* category-2 application);
- **Resolution** of a deadlock or impasse;
- **Construction** of the charter / regulations;
- **Order** that any specified act may be done.

Other persons with standing under Article 45 may also apply.

## When to apply

Typical scenarios warranting an Article 46 application:

- **Momentous decisions** — disposing of a major asset,
  restructuring a portfolio, distributing significantly;
- **Surrender of discretion** to the Court (rare, but
  appropriate where the council cannot or will not decide);
- **Construction** of unclear charter / regulations;
- **Conflicts** between council and guardian;
- **Beneficiary / founder disputes** requiring guidance;
- **Hostile parties** seeking standing or relief;
- **Insolvency-adjacent** decisions where comfort is needed.

## The *Public Trustee v Cooper* framework

The Royal Court applies the **four-category framework** from
*Public Trustee v Cooper* to council / guardian applications:

| Category | Scenario | Court's role |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Doubt as to construction or power | Court determines the law |
| 2 | Momentous decision, council needs comfort | Court "blesses" if rational and not in breach of duty |
| 3 | Council won't decide, surrenders to Court | Court decides in council's place |
| 4 | Disputed claim affecting the foundation | Court adjudicates between parties |

Category 2 (**blessing**) is by far the most common
application form.

## Procedure

An Article 46 application is brought by **representation**:

- **Representation** filed with the Royal Court;
- **Convenors** identified (parties whose views the Court
  wishes to hear);
- **Evidence** filed (affidavits, exhibits);
- **Convening hearing** to identify and join parties;
- **Substantive hearing** with submissions;
- **Order** made.

The procedure parallels Article 51 trust directions.

## Beddoe equivalent

For litigation by or against the foundation, the council can
apply for a **costs order**:

- Authorising the council to bring / defend proceedings;
- Providing for **costs from foundation assets** (subject to
  outcomes);
- Protecting council against personal liability for costs;
- Analogous to **Beddoe** in trust practice.
