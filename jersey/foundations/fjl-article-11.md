# FJL 2009 Article 11 — Initial Endowment

Article 11 governs **endowment** of a Jersey foundation —
the transfer of property to the foundation.

## The provision

A foundation:

- **May** be established with an **initial endowment** of
  property;
- **May** be established with **no initial property** (a
  "shelf" foundation);
- **May** receive further endowments subsequently;
- **From** the founder or third parties;
- **Subject to** AML / CDD on the source of funds.

## No minimum capital

Unlike some corporate vehicles, there is **no minimum
endowment** for a Jersey foundation:

- Foundations can be established with **nominal** initial
  endowment (e.g., £100);
- **Substantive** endowment later as needed;
- **Flexibility** for planning purposes.

## Endowment process

Property is transferred to the foundation:

- **By the founder** (initially) or later by other persons;
- **Documented** in the foundation's records;
- **AML / CDD** on the transferor;
- **Source of funds** verified;
- **Tax** considerations for the transferor in their home
  jurisdiction.

## Sources of endowment

Common endowment sources:

### Founder's lifetime gift

- **Most common**;
- **Documented** as gift to the foundation;
- **Tax-effective** in many home jurisdictions;
- **Subject to** anti-avoidance rules.

### Founder's will / estate

- **Testamentary** endowment;
- **Specific** bequest to the foundation;
- **Subject to** the founder's home succession law;
- **Specific** Jersey foundation receives the bequest.

### Third-party donations

- **Donors** other than the founder;
- **Subject to** specific AML / CDD;
- **Tax** considerations for donor;
- **Acknowledgement** by the foundation.

### Income / appreciation

- **Foundation's** own investments generating returns;
- **Reinvestment** in the foundation;
- **Different** from external endowment.

## Subsequent endowments

Endowments after establishment:

- **Same process** — transfer of property with AML / CDD;
- **Records** updated;
- **Specific** considerations for foreign-tax treatment in
  donor's home jurisdiction.
