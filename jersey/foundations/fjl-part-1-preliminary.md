# FJL 2009 Part 1 — Preliminary

Part 1 of the Foundations (Jersey) Law 2009 contains the
**interpretation provisions** and the **threshold definitions**
that anchor the rest of the Law.

## Article 1 — Interpretation

Article 1 sets the **definitions** used throughout the FJL. Key
defined terms include:

- **"Charter"** — the constitutional document of a foundation,
  registered (and public) under Part 2;
- **"Council"** — the body of one or more council members who
  manage the foundation;
- **"Foundation"** — a legal person established under the Law;
- **"Founder"** — the person endowing the foundation initially;
- **"Guardian"** — the supervisory office under Part 5;
- **"Object"** — the purpose or class of beneficiaries for whose
  benefit the foundation is established;
- **"Qualified member"** — a member of the council who is a
  Jersey-regulated qualified person;
- **"Qualified person"** — a person registered for **trust
  company business** under the Financial Services (Jersey) Law
  1998;
- **"Regulations"** — the private constitutional document
  governing more detailed matters (beneficiaries, dispositive
  rules, council appointment / removal);
- **"Registrar"** — the Registrar of Foundations (a function
  within the JFSC).

## Article 2 — Meaning of "qualified person"

A **qualified person** for the purposes of the Law is:

- A person registered under Article 9 of the **Financial
  Services (Jersey) Law 1998** to carry on **trust company
  business**; or
- An exempted class prescribed by the Minister.

The **mandatory qualified member** of the council (Article 16)
must be a qualified person — this is the regulatory anchor of
the foundation in Jersey. In practice, every Jersey foundation
has a TCB-registered firm on its council.

## Article 3 — Foundations subject to this Law

The Law applies to:

- **Foundations established** under the Law;
- **Foundations continued** into Jersey under Part 10
  (migrating in from another jurisdiction); and
- **Foundations whose charter so provides** (in transitional /
  historical cases).

A foreign foundation that has not been continued into Jersey is
**not** governed by the FJL — even if Jersey persons act for
it. Practitioners advising on a "Liechtenstein Anstalt" or
"Panama foundation" should not assume FJL applies.
