# FJL 2009 Article 26 — Founder

Article 26 governs the **founder**'s position in relation to
the foundation.

## The provision

The **founder**:

- Is the person who **signs the charter and regulations**;
- May be an **individual, body corporate, or nominee**;
- **Endows** the foundation (immediately or later);
- May reserve **powers** in the charter / regulations;
- Has **no inherent ongoing position** in the foundation
  beyond what the charter / regulations grant;
- Is **not a trustee** (this is not a trust);
- Is **not automatically a beneficiary** (a separate matter).

## Reserved powers

The founder may **reserve**:

- Power to **appoint / remove** council members;
- Power to **appoint / remove** the guardian;
- Power to **consent** to specified matters;
- Power to **direct** distributions (subject to council's
  fiduciary check);
- Power to **revoke** the foundation (rare but possible);
- Power to **amend** the charter / regulations;
- Power to **designate** beneficiaries.

These reserved powers are **analogous to** but distinct from
**Article 9A reserved powers** for trusts. The reservation is
a function of the **constitutional document** (charter /
regulations), not of a separate statutory reservation regime.

## Tax / regulatory characterisation

Reserved powers can affect:

- **Foreign tax** treatment — a founder retaining
  revocability or distribution control may be the deemed
  owner under foreign tax regimes (similar to US grantor-
  trust treatment);
- **AML** treatment — the founder remains a key principal for
  ongoing CDD;
- **Beneficial-ownership** registration — founders are
  registrable beneficial owners under the FS(DPI)(J)L 2020 (as
  applied to foundations);
- **Substance** for the foundation — founder-directed
  decisions may compromise the assertion that decisions are
  taken in Jersey.

## Founder's death

On the founder's death:

- Reserved powers **terminate** unless the charter /
  regulations provide for successor exercise;
- The **successor** (often spouse, then children, then
  professional) takes the role specified;
- The **charter / regulations** should be drafted to
  anticipate this transition.

## Nominee founder

A **nominee founder** is permitted — typically a Jersey law
firm or service provider acting for an undisclosed principal.
Key points:

- The **underlying principal** must be identified to the
  qualified member for AML purposes;
- The **public record** shows the nominee;
- The nominee has **no economic interest**.
