# FJL 2009 Article 21 — Indemnity

Article 21 provides for **indemnification** of council
members.

## Statutory indemnity

A council member is **entitled to be indemnified** out of
foundation assets against:

- **Liabilities properly incurred** in acting as council
  member;
- **Costs properly incurred** in defending litigation;
- **Other expenses** properly incurred in the performance of
  duties.

## Limits

The indemnity does **not extend to**:

- **Fraud** by the council member;
- **Wilful default** of duty under Article 17;
- **Wilful breach** of trust / duty;
- **Liabilities outside** the scope of council duties;
- **Criminal** acts.

## Contractual extension

The charter / regulations can:

- **Extend** the indemnity beyond the statutory minimum;
- **Restrict** the indemnity in specific contexts;
- **Subject to** public policy limits (cannot indemnify
  against fraud / wilful misconduct);
- **Cover** specific litigation contexts.

## Indemnity insurance

The foundation can purchase **indemnity insurance** for
council members:

- **Standard practice** for professional council members;
- **D&O-style** insurance;
- **Specific** coverage limits;
- **Subject to** specific exclusions.

## Qualified member's contractual indemnity

The **qualified-member firm** typically has:

- **Contractual** indemnity in engagement letter;
- **In addition** to statutory indemnity;
- **Specific** carve-outs (firm's own negligence);
- **Limits** of liability;
- **Insurance** backing.

## Costs of defending claims

For ordinary acts in good faith, council members are
**entitled to costs of defending claims**:

- **From the foundation**;
- **Even if** ultimately unsuccessful in defence (subject to
  wilful default test);
- **Court can review** reasonableness;
- **Insurance** typically covers as well.

## Successor liability

When a council member retires:

- **Past acts** properly performed remain covered;
- **Indemnity continues** post-retirement for past acts;
- **Specific** continuing rights documented in retirement
  documentation;
- **Subject to** specific claims-discovery limits.
