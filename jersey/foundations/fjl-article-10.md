# FJL 2009 Article 10 — Address in Jersey

Article 10 requires every foundation to have a **registered
office in Jersey**.

## The requirement

Every Jersey foundation must:

- **Have** a registered office in Jersey at all times;
- **Notify** the registered office address in the charter or
  by separate filing with the Registrar;
- **Use** the registered office for **service of process**;
- **Keep** specified records at the registered office.

## Typical registered office

In practice, the registered office is:

- The **office of the qualified-member** (TCB firm) typically;
- **Specific** Jersey office;
- **Maintained** through the qualified-member engagement.

## What the registered office is used for

The registered office is:

- The **address for service** of legal process;
- The **address** at which **registers and records** are kept
  (unless otherwise notified);
- The **address** for **regulatory** correspondence;
- The **principal** contact point for the foundation in
  Jersey.

## Change of registered office

- **Notify** the Registrar of any change;
- **Specific** filing requirement;
- **Effective** on filing;
- **Updated** in public register.

## Co-location with qualified member

Typically:

- **Registered office** at qualified-member's office;
- **Records** maintained by qualified-member;
- **Service** handled by qualified-member;
- **Specific** engagement-letter terms cover this.

If the foundation **changes qualified member**, the registered
office typically changes too.
