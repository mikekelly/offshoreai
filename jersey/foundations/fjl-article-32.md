# FJL 2009 Article 32 — Accounting Records

Article 32 requires every foundation to maintain
**accounting records**.

## The provision

The council must maintain **accounting records** that:

- Are **sufficient** to show and explain the foundation's
  transactions;
- **Disclose** the foundation's financial position with
  reasonable accuracy at any time;
- Enable preparation of **any required accounts**;
- Are **kept** at the registered office or another place
  notified to the Registrar;
- Are **preserved** for at least **10 years**.

## Content

Accounting records include:

- **Cash books** and **bank records**;
- **Receipts** and **invoices** for transactions;
- **Asset registers**;
- **Distribution records**;
- **Records** of transactions with founder, guardian,
  beneficiaries, and connected parties;
- **Income** sources;
- **Expenditure** categories.

## Formal accounts

Most foundations do **not** prepare audited financial
statements unless:

- **Required** by the regulations / charter;
- **Required** by the JFSC for the qualified-member firm;
- **Required** for tax purposes in beneficiaries'
  jurisdictions;
- **Voluntary** for governance reasons (larger
  foundations).

Typically:

- **Management accounts** prepared annually by qualified-
  member;
- **Income / expenditure** statements;
- **Balance sheet**;
- **Specific** activity reports.

## Distribution records

Particular attention to **distributions**:

- **Date** of distribution;
- **Beneficiary** identification;
- **Amount** and **purpose**;
- **Decision-making** documentation (council resolution,
  guardian consent if required);
- **AML / CDD** on the recipient;
- **Specific** documentation requirements.

## Location and access

Accounting records:

- **At** the registered office typically (or notified
  location);
- **Accessible** to council, guardian, Registrar;
- **Available** for Royal Court order;
- **Confidential** to other parties (subject to specific
  rules).

## Audit

If audit is required:

- **Auditor** appointed by council;
- **Independent** auditor;
- **Annual** audit;
- **Audit report** to council and guardian;
- **Specific** purposes and scope.

Most family / private foundations do **not** require audit
beyond JFSC requirements for the qualified-member firm.

## Failure to maintain records

Failure is:

- **Regulatory** breach;
- **Council members** personally liable;
- **JFSC** action against qualified member;
- **Specific** criminal offences in serious cases;
- **Practical** difficulty in administering the foundation.

## Comparison with TJL trustee records

Trust trustee record-keeping is similar:

- **TJL Article 21**: trustee duty to keep accounts;
- **TJL Article 29**: disclosure of accounts to
  beneficiaries (subject to specific rules);
- **Same** general principles as FJL Article 32.
