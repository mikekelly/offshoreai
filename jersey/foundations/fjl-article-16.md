# FJL 2009 Article 16 — Qualified Member

Article 16 is one of the **load-bearing** provisions of the FJL
2009. It requires every foundation to have a **qualified
member** on its council.

## The provision

A foundation **must at all times** have a council member who
is a **qualified person** within the meaning of Article 2 — in
practice, a Jersey-regulated **trust company business** entity.

If the foundation **ceases to have** a qualified member (e.g.,
the qualified-member firm resigns or loses its TCB
registration), the foundation must **appoint a replacement
qualified member** without delay.

## Why this matters

The qualified-member requirement is the **regulatory anchor**
of every Jersey foundation:

- **JFSC oversight** — the qualified-member firm is regulated
  by the JFSC for TCB and is subject to AML / CFT and
  prudential supervision;
- **AML / KYC** — the qualified member conducts CDD on the
  founder, beneficiaries, and source of funds;
- **Substance** — the qualified-member firm provides Jersey
  substance for the foundation (officers, decisions in Jersey,
  records);
- **Accountability** — the JFSC can take enforcement action
  against the qualified-member firm if the foundation is used
  for illicit purposes;
- **Continuity** — the qualified member ensures filings,
  records, and good standing.

## Qualified member's role

The qualified member is **a council member** — not a
service-provider standing outside the council. Practically:

- **Files** the charter and ongoing returns;
- **Maintains** records and registers;
- **Conducts** AML / CDD on founder, beneficiaries, and
  source of funds;
- **Participates** in council decisions;
- **Receives** council fees and / or admin fees per the
  engagement letter.

## Consequences of failure

If a foundation **fails to maintain** a qualified member:

- **Reportable** to the JFSC by the outgoing member;
- The **Royal Court** can intervene under Article 45;
- The **Attorney General** can apply for striking off;
- **Offences** under Article 53 may attach to the council
  members.

## Practical considerations

- The qualified-member firm typically has **resignation rights**
  in the engagement letter (with notice);
- A **successor qualified member** must be in place before
  resignation takes effect;
- The qualified member should be **briefed thoroughly** on the
  family / purposes;
- The qualified member is **paid** out of foundation assets per
  the engagement letter.
