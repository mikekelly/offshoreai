# FJL 2009 Article 28 — Beneficiary Defined

Article 28 defines who is a **beneficiary** of a Jersey
foundation.

## The provision

A **beneficiary** is a person who:

- Is **designated** in the charter or regulations as a
  beneficiary; or
- Is a **member of a class** so designated; or
- Is **otherwise entitled** under the charter or regulations
  to benefit from the foundation.

A foundation **may** have:

- **No beneficiaries** (a pure-purpose foundation);
- **Beneficiaries only**;
- **Both** beneficiaries and purposes.

This flexibility is one of the foundation's principal features
— a single vehicle can serve **mixed objects** in a way that
trust law has historically resisted.

## Designation

Beneficiaries are typically designated:

- **By name** in the regulations (rarely in the charter, for
  privacy);
- **By class** (e.g., "the children and remoter issue of the
  founder");
- **By relationship** (e.g., "the spouse of the founder for
  the time being");
- **By criteria** (e.g., "such persons as the council may
  determine from time to time");
- **By future addition** under regulations granting council /
  founder power to add or remove.

## Effect of designation

Being a beneficiary does **not** mean:

- **Entitlement** to foundation assets as of right (Article
  29);
- **Equitable interest** in foundation property;
- **Information rights** (Article 30 — heavily constrained);
- **Standing** to challenge council decisions in the broad
  way trust beneficiaries can.

Being a beneficiary **does** mean:

- The council can **make distributions** to that person within
  the objects;
- The beneficiary has **the protections** granted by Articles
  29-31 and the charter / regulations;
- The beneficiary has **standing** to apply to the Royal Court
  under Article 46 in specified circumstances.

## Practical consequences

For practitioners:

- **Treat designation as a power, not a right** — beneficiaries
  are objects of distribution discretion;
- **Draft information rights carefully** — default position
  is no rights;
- **Identify** beneficiaries privately in the regulations;
- **Consider** US grantor-trust analogies if useful for
  intended tax treatment.
