# FJL 2009 Article 12 — Capacity

Article 12 gives a Jersey foundation **legal capacity**.

## The provision

A foundation has the **capacity of an individual of full age
and capacity**, **except** as restricted by:

- The **Law itself** (the foundation must act for its
  objects);
- The **charter** (the objects clause is a real
  restriction);
- The **regulations** (which may further restrict council
  powers);
- **Specific** statutory restrictions.

## Wide capacity

The default capacity is **wide**:

- **Contract**;
- **Acquire, hold, dispose of property**;
- **Borrow, lend**;
- **Issue** specific debt securities;
- **Sue and be sued**;
- **Any lawful** civil capacity.

## Limitations

The capacity is **constrained by**:

### The objects

- The **charter's objects clause** defines what the
  foundation can do;
- Acts **outside the objects** are **ultra vires** (Article
  13);
- Specific Court jurisdiction to set aside ultra vires acts.

### The regulations

- The **regulations** may restrict the council's powers
  internally;
- Acts contrary to regulations may be **valid externally**
  but breach internal governance.

### Specific statutory limits

- **Specific** Jersey regulatory limits;
- **AML / CFT** obligations;
- **Sanctions** restrictions;
- **Tax** obligations.

## Practical implications

For counterparties dealing with a Jersey foundation:

- **Foundation** is presumed to have capacity;
- **Specific** charter / regulations check advisable for
  significant transactions;
- **Specific** Article 13 considerations for ultra vires
  concern.
