# Pillar Two and the Multinational Corporate Income Tax (MCIT)

## What Pillar Two is

The **OECD/G20 Inclusive Framework's "Pillar Two"** is a
global agreement to ensure that **large multinational
groups** pay a **minimum effective tax rate of 15%** on their
profits, wherever earned.

The mechanism applies to:

- **Multinational groups** with **consolidated revenue of
  €750 million+** in at least two of the four preceding
  years;
- All **jurisdictions** in which such groups operate;
- Implemented through **GloBE rules** (Global Anti-Base Erosion
  Rules) adopted by participating jurisdictions.

## Jersey's response — the MCIT

Jersey introduced the **Multinational Corporate Income Tax
(MCIT)** with effect from **1 January 2025**:

- **15% rate** on in-scope multinationals' Jersey profits;
- **Top-up** mechanism where Jersey-source income would
  otherwise be taxed below 15%;
- **Coexists** with the existing zero-ten regime for
  out-of-scope groups (smaller groups continue at 0%);
- **Aligned** with OECD GloBE rules.

## How MCIT interacts with zero-ten

| Group type | Tax treatment |
|---|---|
| **In-scope** (€750m+ revenue) | MCIT — 15% effective rate |
| **Out-of-scope** small / mid-cap | Zero-ten preserved — 0% / 10% / 20% by category |
| **Financial services** in-scope | MCIT supersedes 10% (top-up to 15%) |
| **Utilities / large retail** in-scope | MCIT supersedes 20% / specific 10% (top-up to 15%) |

For most Jersey financial-services subsidiaries of major
international groups, the **effective rate becomes 15%** under
MCIT.

## Mechanics

### Income Inclusion Rule (IIR)

The IIR applies at the **parent jurisdiction**:

- Parent collects **top-up tax** on under-taxed subsidiaries;
- If a Jersey subsidiary is taxed below 15%, the parent's home
  country collects the difference;
- Without Jersey's own implementation, this would mean the
  parent country (often UK / US / EU) collects the top-up
  rather than Jersey.

### Qualified Domestic Top-up Tax (QDMTT)

Jersey's **MCIT** functions as a **QDMTT**:

- Jersey **collects** the top-up itself rather than ceding it
  to parent jurisdictions;
- **Preserves** Jersey's tax revenues from in-scope groups;
- **Aligned** with OECD model rules for QDMTT.

### Under-Taxed Profits Rule (UTPR)

A residual rule where the IIR is not collected:

- Other jurisdictions impose **top-up tax** on the under-taxed
  income;
- Increasingly relevant as Pillar Two adoption spreads.

## Scope details

### Group consolidated revenue threshold

- **€750m+** in at least 2 of preceding 4 years;
- Groups **below** threshold remain on zero-ten;
- **Joint ventures**, **stand-alone subsidiaries**, and
  certain structures handled per GloBE rules.

### Excluded entities

- **Government entities**;
- **International organisations**;
- **Non-profit organisations**;
- **Pension funds**;
- **Investment funds** that are ultimate parent entities (for
  fund-of-funds, this matters);
- **Real-estate investment vehicles** that meet specific
  conditions.

### Excluded income

- **International shipping** income (subject to specific
  conditions);
- **Substance-based income exclusion** — formulaic carve-out
  for in-substance economic activity (payroll plus
  tangible assets), reducing in early years on a sliding
  scale.

## Compliance

Jersey in-scope groups must:

- **File** MCIT returns;
- **Compute** effective tax rate per GloBE methodology;
- **Calculate** top-up tax due;
- **Comply** with global GloBE information return
  (GIR) obligations;
- **Coordinate** with home-country Pillar Two implementation
  for the group.

## Implications for Jersey's IFC role

### Positive

- Jersey **collects** the top-up tax that would otherwise go
  to other jurisdictions — revenue preservation;
- **Reputational** alignment with OECD norms;
- **Compliance** signal to international counterparties.

### Challenging

- **Reduced** tax-rate advantage for in-scope groups;
- **Increased** compliance complexity;
- **Smaller** groups still benefit from zero-ten — Jersey
  attractive for SMEs, family offices, mid-cap structures;
- **Substance requirements** (Substance Law 2019) remain
  relevant alongside MCIT.

## What practitioners should know

- **Identify** group consolidated revenue early — €750m
  threshold determines MCIT in-scope status;
- **Model** the effective tax rate under GloBE methodology;
- **Coordinate** Jersey MCIT compliance with home-country
  Pillar Two compliance;
- **Substance** requirements continue separately;
- **Smaller groups** remain in zero-ten — most family-office
  and private-client structures unaffected.
