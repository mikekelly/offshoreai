# EU List of Non-Cooperative Jurisdictions and Jersey

## What the list is

The **EU Council's List of non-cooperative jurisdictions for
tax purposes** (the **"EU list"**, sometimes called the EU
**"blacklist"** plus a separate **"grey list"** or watchlist)
identifies jurisdictions that the EU deems not to meet **good
tax governance** standards:

- **Tax transparency** — exchange of information;
- **Fair taxation** — no harmful preferential regimes;
- **Anti-BEPS** — implementation of minimum standards.

## Jersey's history with the list

Jersey was on the **grey list** (subject to commitments)
through the late 2010s, principally over **economic substance**
concerns about its zero-ten regime:

- The EU Code of Conduct Group identified Jersey's zero-ten
  as potentially harmful given the absence of substance
  requirements;
- Jersey **committed** to introducing substance requirements;
- Jersey **delivered** the **Taxation (Companies — Economic
  Substance) (Jersey) Law 2019** (Substance Law 2019);
- Jersey was **removed** from the grey list following
  implementation.

## Current position

As of 2026, Jersey is:

- **Not** on the EU list (neither blacklist nor grey list);
- **Compliant** with the substance, transparency, and
  fair-taxation commitments;
- **Continuing** to engage with the EU Code of Conduct Group
  on emerging matters.

## Implications of being on the list

For jurisdictions on the **blacklist**:

- **EU Member States** impose defensive measures:
  - **Withholding tax** at penal rates on payments to
    blacklisted jurisdictions;
  - **CFC** acceleration;
  - **No tax deductibility** for payments to blacklisted
    jurisdictions;
  - **Specific** anti-abuse measures.
- **Reputational** damage;
- **Investment** flow disruption.

For jurisdictions on the **grey list**:

- **No** automatic defensive measures (but commitments must
  be met);
- **Reputational** concern;
- **Monitoring** by the EU.

## Why this matters for Jersey IFC

### Demonstrating good standing

- Jersey's **removal** from the grey list (and absence from
  the blacklist) is a **reputational asset**;
- Jersey markets itself as a **white-listed** offshore
  jurisdiction;
- **Foreign clients** comparing offshore options see Jersey
  vs. blacklisted alternatives (e.g., some Caribbean
  jurisdictions during periods on the list).

### Sustaining good standing

Jersey must continue to:

- **Comply** with substance commitments;
- **Maintain** information-exchange standards;
- **Avoid** introducing harmful preferential regimes;
- **Implement** BEPS / Pillar Two commitments;
- **Engage** constructively with the EU Code of Conduct
  Group.

### Substance Law 2019 — the operative regime

The **Substance Law 2019** is the principal Jersey mechanism
supporting EU-list good standing:

- **In-scope activities**: banking, insurance, fund
  management, financing / leasing, IP, headquarters,
  shipping, distribution-and-service-centre, pure equity
  holding;
- **Substance requirements**: directed in Jersey, qualified
  employees, premises, expenditure, CIGAs in Jersey;
- **Annual return** to the Comptroller of Revenue;
- **Penalties** and ultimate strike-off for non-compliance.

See [`../tax/substance-law-2019.md`](../tax/substance-law-2019.md)
*(if exists)*.

## Comparison with other offshore jurisdictions

The EU list has affected various offshore jurisdictions:

- **Cayman Islands** — on / off the list at various times;
- **Bermuda** — short period on the list, then removed;
- **BVI** — periods on grey list, then removed;
- **Bahamas** — periodically scrutinised;
- **UAE** — historically on list, removed after substance
  reforms;
- **Various** Caribbean / Pacific jurisdictions periodically
  listed.

Jersey's **continuous** clean status since 2019 is a
competitive advantage among offshore jurisdictions.
