# Multilateral Instrument (MLI)

## What the MLI is

The **Multilateral Convention to Implement Tax Treaty Related
Measures to Prevent BEPS** — usually known as the **MLI** — is
a single multilateral treaty that **modifies** existing
bilateral tax treaties without the need for individual
bilateral renegotiation.

It implements **BEPS Action 6** (treaty abuse) and **BEPS
Action 15** (multilateral instrument) and related actions.

## How the MLI works

For each bilateral treaty:

- **Both** treaty parties must be MLI signatories;
- **Both** must designate the bilateral treaty as a "Covered
  Tax Agreement";
- **Both** must select compatible options for each MLI
  provision;
- The MLI provisions then **modify** the underlying bilateral
  treaty.

## Jersey's MLI participation

Jersey **signed** the MLI in **2017** and **ratified** in **2019**:

- **Covered Tax Agreements**: all of Jersey's bilateral DTAs
  designated;
- **Principal Purpose Test (PPT)** adopted as the
  anti-treaty-abuse rule;
- **Other** key MLI provisions adopted:
  - Saving clause provisions;
  - Permanent establishment provisions (PE avoidance);
  - Dual-resident entity provisions;
  - Mutual agreement procedure (MAP) provisions.

## Key MLI provisions affecting Jersey treaties

### Principal Purpose Test (Article 7)

The **PPT** denies treaty benefits where:

- A **principal purpose** of any arrangement or transaction
  was to obtain the treaty benefit;
- And granting the benefit would not be in line with the
  object and purpose of the treaty.

The PPT applies to **all of Jersey's DTAs** with treaty partners
that have signed compatible options.

The PPT is **subjective** in application — facts-and-
circumstances analysis of arrangements. Common examples:

- **Treaty shopping** through Jersey: arrangements lacking
  commercial substance designed to access a Jersey DTA
  benefit;
- **Conduit arrangements**: Jersey vehicle routing income
  to/from non-treaty partners;
- **Specific avoidance**: Jersey vehicle established
  pre-transaction specifically to capture treaty benefit.

### Saving clause

Treaties may apply notwithstanding domestic tax rules of the
state of source/residence — **subject to listed exceptions**
(e.g., exemptions, foreign-tax credit).

### Dual resident entities (Article 4)

Where an entity is resident in both treaty states, the
**competent authorities** of the two states determine residence
by **MAP** — not the traditional tie-breaker tests in many
older treaties.

### Permanent establishment (Articles 12-15)

The MLI **expands** the definition of permanent establishment
to capture:

- **Commissionaire** and similar arrangements;
- **Specific** preparatory / auxiliary exclusions narrowed;
- **Splitting** of contracts to avoid the 12-month
  construction threshold.

### Mutual Agreement Procedure (MAP) (Article 16)

The MLI **strengthens** MAP:

- **Faster** access to MAP for treaty disputes;
- **Time-limited** MAP cases;
- **Mandatory binding arbitration** (Part VI of MLI — Jersey
  opted-in to mandatory binding arbitration for compatible
  partners).

## Practical implications

### For Jersey vehicles relying on DTAs

Test the structure against:

- **PPT** — is there a non-tax commercial purpose?
- **Substance** under the Substance Law 2019 — separate but
  reinforcing test;
- **PE** — is there inadvertent PE creation given the wider MLI
  definition?
- **Residence** — single resident, or dual-resident requiring
  MAP determination?

### For tax-treaty-driven transactions

- Pre-transaction **opinion** addressing the MLI provisions;
- **Documentation** of commercial purposes;
- **Substance** in Jersey supporting the treaty position;
- **Coordination** with treaty-partner counsel.

### For cross-border transactions

- **PE** risk assessment under expanded MLI definitions;
- **Withholding tax** matching to treaty rates considering
  PPT;
- **Anti-abuse** clauses in transactional documents.

## DTAs affected

All of Jersey's bilateral DTAs are subject to MLI modification
where compatible. See [`dtas.md`](dtas.md) for the list of
DTAs.
