# Civil Partnerships and Same-Sex Marriage in Jersey

## The framework

Two routes are available to same-sex couples:

- **Civil partnership** — under the **Civil Partnership
  (Jersey) Law 2012**;
- **Marriage** — under the **Marriage and Civil Status
  (Jersey) Law 2001** as amended by the **Marriage and
  Civil Status (Jersey) Order 2018** (permitting same-sex
  marriage).

Opposite-sex couples can also enter civil partnerships
(per later amendments aligning Jersey with the post-2019
position in England).

## Civil partnership

A **civil partnership** is a formal, legally registered
relationship producing rights and obligations parallel
(but not identical) to marriage:

- **Formation** — by registration at the Superintendent
  Registrar's office;
- **Dissolution** — by **dissolution order** in the
  Royal Court Family Division;
- **Financial relief** on dissolution — parallel to
  divorce ancillary relief;
- **Parental responsibility** — see
  [`parental-responsibility.md`](parental-responsibility.md);
- **Succession** — légitime / douaire equivalents apply.

## Same-sex marriage

From the **2018 Order**, same-sex couples may marry in
Jersey:

- **Formation** — by civil or (in some cases) religious
  ceremony;
- **Religious bodies** opting in or out — some Jersey
  religious bodies marry same-sex couples, others do not;
- **Identical** civil status to opposite-sex marriage;
- **Identical** rights on divorce, succession, parental
  responsibility (subject to the historical Article 7
  adultery wording — opposite-sex only, an anomaly).

## Recognition of foreign relationships

Jersey recognises:

- **Foreign same-sex marriages** valid in the country of
  celebration;
- **Foreign civil partnerships / civil unions** valid in
  the country of formation;
- **Conversion** from civil partnership to marriage in
  jurisdictions that permit it.

Jersey does **not** recognise:

- **Customary marriages** not registered through formal
  civil procedure;
- **Religious-only** marriages that lacked a parallel
  civil component (specific rules).

## Dissolution vs divorce

The procedural mechanics for ending a civil partnership
parallel divorce:

- **Dissolution petition** in the Family Division;
- **Grounds** mirror MCJL Article 7 (with appropriate
  modifications for the relationship structure);
- **Decree nisi → decree absolute** equivalent;
- **Financial relief** under the CPJL equivalent of
  MCJL Articles 25-31.

## Children

Same-sex couples and civil partners:

- **Adoption** — available (Adoption Law 1961 amendments
  recognising same-sex adopters);
- **Surrogacy** — pending the Children and Civil Status
  (Amendments) (Jersey) Law 2024;
- **Parental responsibility** — civil partner / spouse
  of a biological parent does **not automatically**
  acquire PR; agreement or order needed.

## Succession on death

- **Civil partner / same-sex spouse**: identical
  succession rights to opposite-sex spouse (légitime,
  douaire, intestacy entitlements);
- **Cohabitant** — no automatic rights; see
  [`cohabitation.md`](cohabitation.md).

## Pitfalls

- **Civil partner of biological parent** assuming PR —
  it isn't automatic;
- **Adultery ground** in Article 7 not opening to same-
  sex conduct (use alternative grounds);
- **Religious ceremony** without civil registration —
  some same-sex couples have been caught by this.
