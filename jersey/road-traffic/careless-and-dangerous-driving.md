# Careless and Dangerous Driving in Jersey

## The two offences

The Road Traffic (Jersey) Law 1956 distinguishes:

- **Careless driving** (Article 25) — driving **without
  due care and attention**, or **without reasonable
  consideration** for other road users;
- **Dangerous driving** (Article 23) — driving in a way
  that **falls far below** what would be expected of a
  competent and careful driver, **and** would be obvious
  to a competent and careful driver as **dangerous**.

The distinction matters: dangerous driving is **far more
serious** in both forum and penalty.

## Careless driving

### Threshold

The driver's standard fell **below** the standard of a
competent and careful driver. Examples:

- Failing to look properly at a junction;
- Pulling out without due care;
- Driving too close to another vehicle;
- Brief inattention causing risk;
- Following too closely;
- Inappropriate lane changes;
- Aggressive but not dangerous overtaking.

### Forum

Careless driving **can** be dealt with at:

- **Parish Hall Enquiry** — if minor and the defendant
  admits (max £200 fine);
- **Magistrate's Court** — for more serious instances or
  if disputed.

### Penalty

- **Fine** (typically several hundred pounds);
- **Endorsement** of licence;
- **Discretionary disqualification** in serious cases;
- **No mandatory disqualification**.

## Dangerous driving (Article 23)

### Threshold

The driver's standard fell **far below** the competent
and careful standard, **and** was obviously dangerous. The
"obvious" part is an objective test — would a competent
and careful driver have recognised the danger?

Examples:

- High-speed overtaking on blind bends;
- Racing on public roads;
- Sustained excessive speed in built-up areas;
- Reckless overtaking near pedestrians;
- Driving with seriously impaired vehicle (e.g. brakes
  failed and driver knew);
- Pursuing or being pursued;
- Phone use combined with other failures.

### Forum

Dangerous driving **cannot** be dealt with at Parish Hall.
It goes to:

- **Magistrate's Court** initially;
- **Royal Court** for the most serious cases or where
  death / serious injury caused.

### Penalty

- **Mandatory disqualification** (typically minimum 12
  months);
- **Re-test** before licence restored;
- **Fine** — substantial;
- **Imprisonment** — available for the most serious cases;
- **Endorsement**.

## Causing death by driving

The most serious driving offences:

- **Causing death by dangerous driving**;
- **Causing death by careless driving while under the
  influence**;
- **Causing serious injury by dangerous driving**;
- **Causing serious injury by careless driving while
  under the influence**.

These go to the **Royal Court** and carry substantial
imprisonment plus mandatory long disqualifications.

The 2020s amendments expanded these grounds — see
[`road-traffic-law-1956.md`](road-traffic-law-1956.md).

## Distinguishing the two

Whether conduct is **careless** or **dangerous** is often
the central issue in prosecution. Indicators that push
into dangerous territory:

- **Sustained** poor driving rather than a momentary
  lapse;
- **High speed** in a vulnerable area;
- **Multiple failures** combining;
- **Vehicle defects** the driver knew about;
- **Aggressive intent**.

Indicators of careless:

- **Momentary** lapse;
- **Mid-range** speeds;
- **Single failure**;
- **No bad intent**;
- **Quick correction** by driver.

## Defences

- **Identity** — was you the driver?
- **Standard met** — the conduct did meet the competent
  and careful standard given conditions;
- **Mechanical failure** beyond the driver's control;
- **Sudden medical event** beyond the driver's control;
- **Provocation / emergency** — narrow.

## Mitigation

- **Clean record**;
- **Prompt admission**;
- **Cooperation** with investigation;
- **Genuine remorse**;
- **Loss of licence consequences**.
