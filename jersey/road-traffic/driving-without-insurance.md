# Driving Without Insurance

## The offence

Driving (or using, or causing or permitting use of) a motor
vehicle on a road without **at least third-party motor
insurance** is an offence under the **Motor Traffic (Third
Party Insurance) (Jersey) Law 1948** and the **Road Traffic
(Jersey) Law 1956**.

The third-party insurance must cover liability for:

- **Death or bodily injury** to a third party;
- **Damage to third-party property** (subject to specified
  limits).

## The offences

There are related offences:

- **Using** without insurance (strict liability — the
  user is liable);
- **Causing or permitting** use without insurance (e.g.
  lending the car to an uninsured driver);
- **Failing to display** the windscreen insurance disc
  (Article 16 RTJL — lesser offence);
- **Failing to produce** the insurance certificate when
  required (Article 17 RTJL — lesser offence).

## Penalties

- **Fine** — typically £300-£1,500 depending on
  circumstances;
- **Endorsement** of licence;
- **Discretionary disqualification** — common for blatant
  cases;
- **Possible custodial sentence** for repeat offenders.

For knowingly uninsured driving combined with another
offence (e.g. drink-driving), the sentencing aggravates
sharply.

## Forum

Driving without insurance can be dealt with at:

- **Parish Hall** — for minor / inadvertent cases (admin
  lapse, momentary cover gap), with admission;
- **Magistrate's Court** — for serious or repeat cases.

## Defences

- **You were not the user / cause / permitter**;
- **Insurance was in force** — produce the cover note or
  policy schedule;
- **Honest belief in cover** — narrow; the courts treat
  insurance as a strict obligation;
- **Identity** — were you the driver?

A common scenario: a renewal was not paid on time, but
the driver believed it was. The Magistrate may take this
as **mitigation** but it is **not a substantive defence**
to a strict-liability offence.

## Practical recommendations

- **Verify cover** at every renewal — direct
  acknowledgement from the insurer;
- **Keep the certificate** in the vehicle or accessible
  digitally;
- **Display the disc** where required;
- **Don't lend** the car without confirming the driver's
  cover;
- **Hire cars** — confirm the rental includes cover.

## Third-party motor insurance — minimum

The **Motor Traffic (Third Party Insurance) (Jersey) Law
1948** sets the compulsory minimum cover. Comprehensive
policies are standard but third-party-only meets the legal
requirement.
