# Jersey Sustainable Finance

## What this is

The **sustainable finance** vertical in Jersey covers the
structuring, regulatory, and disclosure considerations for
investment products positioned on environmental, social,
or governance (ESG) characteristics. The Jersey industry
has positioned sustainable finance as a **growth vertical**
since 2021, with Jersey Finance projecting sustainable-
fund AUM reaching **£123 billion by 2026**.

This file is the **index** for Jersey's sustainable-
finance content. The doctrinal layer covers SFDR
treatment, fund classification under EU sustainable-
finance regimes, the taxonomy regulation, greenwashing
risk, and the JFSC's supervisory posture. Live
developments — including SFDR 2.0 — are tracked in
[`../../frontier/sfdr-2-and-sustainable-finance.md`](../../frontier/sfdr-2-and-sustainable-finance.md).

## The regulatory landscape

Jersey is **not in EU MiCA scope** and is **not directly
subject to EU SFDR**. But Jersey-domiciled funds marketed
into the EU under the [AIFMD NPPR](../funds/aifmd-nppr.md)
route are expected to comply with SFDR-equivalent
disclosure where they make sustainability claims. See
[`./sfdr.md`](./sfdr.md) for the SFDR mechanics.

The **JFSC** has published sustainable-finance guidance
that adopts a **MiCA-compatible posture**: Jersey funds
positioning as Article 8 or Article 9 under SFDR
equivalent are expected to make the same level of
disclosure, evidence the same level of sustainability
integration, and avoid greenwashing on the same standards
as the EU regime.

## The Jersey strategic posture

The strategic positioning has been articulated through:

- **Jersey Finance Sustainable Finance Action Plan**
  (multi-year framework);
- **JFSC sustainable-finance guidance** (multiple updates
  through 2022-2026);
- **Government of Jersey Sustainable Finance Action Plan**;
- The **JFSC 2026-2030 Strategy**, which names sustainable
  finance as one of two strategic growth verticals (the
  other being digital assets / tokenisation).

The intent is to position Jersey as a **trusted offshore
domicile for sustainable funds and structures**, with
JFSC oversight and Jersey trust / fund / TopCo
infrastructure underneath.

## Product application

Sustainable finance touches multiple product lines:

### Sustainable funds

Jersey funds (JPF, Expert Fund, Eligible Investor Fund,
Listed Fund) can be positioned as sustainable, ESG-focused,
or impact-oriented. Disclosures track SFDR Article 8 /
Article 9 categorisation. See [`./sfdr.md`](./sfdr.md).

### Sustainable corporate vehicles

TopCos and corporate vehicles incorporated in Jersey can
adopt sustainability reporting frameworks (TCFD, ISSB)
without requiring change to their underlying corporate
form.

### Green bonds and sustainable debt

Jersey vehicles can issue green / social / sustainability-
linked bonds, with the listing typically on the LSE or
Euronext. The Jersey legal framework supports the issuance;
the substantive sustainable-finance requirements come from
the listing venue and the underlying ICMA / EU GBS
standards.

### Sustainable trust and family-office structures

Trust and family-office structures increasingly include
ESG governance provisions, impact-investment mandates,
and sustainability reporting at the trust or underlying-
company level.

## Greenwashing risk

The dominant practitioner concern. The greenwashing risk
profile is heightened by:

- The **growth of sustainable funds** as a category,
  which raises scrutiny;
- The **maturation of EU regulators** — particularly
  the ESAs (EBA, ESMA, EIOPA) — in identifying
  inconsistencies between fund disclosure and actual
  portfolio composition;
- The **SFDR 2.0 proposal** explicitly responds to
  greenwashing concerns and tightens the regime — see
  [`../../frontier/sfdr-2-and-sustainable-finance.md`](../../frontier/sfdr-2-and-sustainable-finance.md);
- The **JFSC supervisory focus** on sustainable-finance
  disclosure consistency.

Practitioner mitigants:

- **Conservative fund categorisation** — when in doubt,
  Article 8 rather than Article 9;
- **Robust sustainability-integration documentation**;
- **Periodic third-party assurance** on sustainability
  disclosures;
- **Clear demarcation** of marketing language vs
  regulated disclosure.

## What's coming

The major live development is **SFDR 2.0** — the European
Commission's formal proposal published 20 November 2025,
with implementation expected 2027-2028. SFDR 2.0 is
expected to introduce a **revised product-classification
system** (replacing or evolving the Article 6 / 8 / 9
categorisation), tighter sustainability-claim regulation,
and stronger data architecture (the European Single Access
Point, or ESAP).

Jersey is following the development closely and will
update guidance to align with the final regime. See
[`../../frontier/sfdr-2-and-sustainable-finance.md`](../../frontier/sfdr-2-and-sustainable-finance.md).

## Pitfalls

- **Assuming Jersey is outside SFDR scope** — the
  marketing channel into the EU brings the requirement;
- **Article 9 positioning without substantive
  integration** — high greenwashing risk;
- **Static disclosure** — sustainability disclosure is
  expected to be dynamic (regular updates, periodic
  review);
- **Ignoring SFDR 2.0** — even if 2027-2028
  implementation seems distant, fund structures launched
  now should consider the transition;
- **Tokenisation overlap** — sustainable-tokenised-
  asset structures combine two regulated areas; cross-
  reference [`../digital-assets/index.md`](../digital-assets/index.md).
