# Succession — Jersey

## What this section is about

This section covers **Jersey succession law** — the rules
that govern what happens to a person's property on their
death.

Jersey succession law has **Norman customary law** roots
overlaid with **statute**:

- **Wills and Successions (Jersey) Law 1993 (WSL)** — the
  principal modern statute;
- **Customary law** of succession — partially preserved,
  particularly for **real property** (immovable);
- **Probate (Jersey) Law 1949** — administration of estates;
- **Specific Orders** for forms and procedures.

The system distinguishes between **movable** property
(personal property — bank accounts, investments, chattels)
and **immovable** property (real property — Jersey land and
buildings).

## Customary law principles

Beyond the WSL, Jersey customary law preserves:

- **Real property** succession rules with specific
  reservation for **descendants** in some configurations;
- **Tuteur** / **curatelle** concepts for minor heirs;
- **Specific procedural** requirements for real-property
  succession;
- **Dévolution** by operation of law for certain estates.

Jersey's succession law is **substantially modernised** by
the WSL but retains **customary-law overlays** that
distinguish it from English / common-law succession.
