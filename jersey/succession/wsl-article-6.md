# WSL 1993 Article 6 — Holograph Wills

Article 6 preserves the **holograph will** — a will entirely
handwritten and signed by the testator, with **no witnesses
required**.

## The provision

A **holograph will** is valid if:

- Written **entirely** in the **testator's own handwriting**;
- **Dated**;
- **Signed** by the testator;
- **No witnesses** required.

## Why holograph wills exist

The holograph will is a feature of **civil-law** systems
(originally French / Norman customary law) that Jersey
preserves:

- **Simplicity** — no witness coordination needed;
- **Privacy** — no need to disclose to witnesses;
- **Last-minute** wills possible without witnesses;
- **Emergency** wills during illness or travel;
- **Personal** expression in the testator's own hand.

## Requirements detail

### Entirely handwritten

- **Cannot** be typed, printed, or partly printed;
- **Cannot** combine handwritten and typed elements;
- **Letterhead** is permitted (not part of the substantive
  will);
- **Pre-printed forms** with handwritten content typically
  fail (the printed part is not in the testator's hand).

### In the testator's own hand

- **Personally** by the testator;
- **Not** another person writing for them;
- **Forms** prepared elsewhere and copied fail (must be
  composed by the testator in their hand).

### Date

- **Day, month, year** required;
- **Verifiable** date (e.g., from internal references) may
  suffice if not explicit;
- **No date** — invalidity risk.

### Signature

- **Below or after** the dispositive content (conventional);
- **Below additions** — additions after signing may be
  invalid;
- **Mark** acceptable in same circumstances as Article 5.

## Probate of holograph wills

For probate:

- **Identification** of the handwriting (handwriting
  comparison if disputed);
- **Affidavit** from someone familiar with the testator's
  handwriting;
- **Compared** with known samples;
- **Disputed** holographs may go to evidentiary hearing.

## Practical use

Holograph wills are **commonly used** for:

- **Simple estates** with straightforward gifts;
- **Family arrangements** that don't need complex drafting;
- **Emergency wills** during travel or illness;
- **Updates** to existing wills (codicils in holograph form);
- **Specific gifts** added to existing will scheme.

For **complex estates** or international planning, a **fully-
witnessed Article 5 will** is generally preferred for
robustness.

## Combining wills

A testator may have:

- **Main will** under Article 5 (witnessed);
- **Codicil** in holograph form (Article 6);
- Both read together to give effect to the testator's wishes.
