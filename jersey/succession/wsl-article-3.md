# WSL 1993 Article 3 — Application of the Law

Article 3 sets out **when WSL applies** — the conflict-of-laws
framework determining whether Jersey succession law governs.

## The provision

WSL applies to:

- **Movable property** of a person **domiciled in Jersey** at
  the time of death, **wherever** located;
- **Immovable property** in Jersey, **regardless** of the
  deceased's domicile;
- Specific other configurations.

WSL does **not apply** to:

- **Movable property** of a person **not domiciled** in
  Jersey at death (foreign-domicile law applies);
- **Immovable property** outside Jersey (foreign-situs law
  applies).

## Domicile analysis

**Domicile** is a legal concept distinct from residence:

- **Domicile of origin** — typically the domicile of the
  father at the time of birth (legitimate child) or the
  mother (illegitimate);
- **Domicile of choice** — acquired by physical presence in a
  country with the intention to remain there permanently or
  indefinitely;
- **Domicile of dependence** — for minors and (historically)
  married women;
- Jersey applies broadly the same domicile concepts as
  English law.

A person can have **only one domicile** at a time.

## Determining domicile at death

In practice:

- **Long-term Jersey resident** with no continuing ties to
  another country — Jersey domicile likely;
- **Recent arrival** maintaining ties to country of origin —
  origin domicile may persist;
- **Foreign business owner** with Jersey base — domicile
  depends on intention;
- **Death-bed** considerations — domicile at the precise
  moment of death matters.

## Practical scenarios

### Jersey-domiciled person

- **Worldwide** movables governed by Jersey succession;
- **Jersey** immovables governed by Jersey;
- **Foreign** immovables governed by foreign situs law;
- Need **separate** succession planning in each foreign
  jurisdiction with property.

### Foreign-domiciled person with Jersey property

- **Foreign** law applies to movables (typically a will valid
  in the foreign jurisdiction is recognised);
- **Jersey** law applies to Jersey real property;
- May need **Jersey will** for Jersey immovables (or relied-
  upon foreign will recognised in Jersey).

### Newly relocated Jersey resident

- **Domicile may not have changed** to Jersey yet;
- **Old will** under previous domicile may apply to movables;
- **Jersey immovables** would still be Jersey law;
- **Planning review** essential.

### UK-resident-non-domiciled

- Particularly relevant **before the 2025 UK IHT reforms** to
  residence-based;
- **Post-2025**: UK residence-based IHT considers Jersey
  succession less directly;
- **Jersey deemed-domicile** rules in the UK (now moved away
  from domicile) historically applied.

## Will validity

A will may be **valid** under WSL if it complies with:

- **Jersey** formalities (Article 5); or
- **Formalities** of the country where executed; or
- **Formalities** of the country of the testator's domicile;
- Multiple alternative recognition routes (the **Hague
  Convention on the Form of Testamentary Dispositions**
  approach).
