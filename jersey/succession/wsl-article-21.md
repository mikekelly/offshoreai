# WSL 1993 Article 21 — Intestacy and Spouse / Partner Rights

Article 21 sets out the **first call** of the surviving
spouse / civil partner on the deceased's estate where there
is **no valid will**.

## Where descendants survive

The surviving spouse / partner takes:

- **Fixed legacy** (a prescribed amount, currently typically
  £30,000 or higher per Order);
- **PLUS personal effects**;
- **PLUS half of the residue** of the movable estate;

The **other half** of the residue passes to descendants.

## Where no descendants but other relatives

The surviving spouse / partner takes:

- **Fixed legacy** (PLUS interest);
- **PLUS all of the residue** of the movable estate;

Other relatives **take nothing** from movable estate (but
**légitime** may apply for movable estate if claimed).

## Where no other relatives

The surviving spouse / partner takes the **entire estate**.

## Real property

For **Jersey real property**:

- **Customary inheritance rules** apply, distinct from
  movable intestacy;
- **Different** distribution to movable estate;
- See [`../property/real-property-succession.md`](../property/real-property-succession.md).

## Specific definitions

- **"Spouse"** includes **civil partner**;
- **"Surviving"** typically requires surviving by **specific
  period** (28 days commonly);
- **"Fixed legacy"** is set by Minister Order, periodically
  reviewed.

## Relationship to légitime

Even with intestacy:

- **Légitime** may apply if spouse / children would prefer
  the légitime measure;
- **Intestacy** typically more generous than légitime for
  surviving spouse;
- **Election** between intestacy and légitime in some
  configurations.

## Specific scenarios

### Single spouse, no children

- Spouse takes all movable estate;
- Real property per customary rules.

### Spouse + children

- Spouse: fixed legacy + half residue + personal effects;
- Children: other half of residue per stirpes.

### Spouse + parents but no children

- Spouse: fixed legacy + all residue (no other relatives'
  share);
- Parents take nothing on intestacy.
