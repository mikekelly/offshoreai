# Trusts (Jersey) Law 1984

The **[Trusts (Jersey) Law 1984][tjl]** is Jersey's principal trusts
statute, with a long title to the effect of "A LAW relating to trusts,
trustees and persons interested under trusts and for connected
purposes". It has been amended a number of times since enactment,
notably to introduce non-charitable purpose trusts (Article 12), the
settlor-reserved-powers regime (Article 9A) and the Article 47
mistake-based set-aside jurisdiction.

> Subject to this Law, a trust shall be recognised by the law of Jersey
> as valid and enforceable.
>
> — Article 2 of the Trusts (Jersey) Law 1984 (per the consolidated
> text on jerseylaw.je).

This page is an orientation to the structure of the Law. Individual
Articles are dealt with in the topic-specific files in this section.

## Key Articles to know

- **Article 2 — Definition and recognition.** A Jersey-law trust is
  recognised and enforceable, subject to the Law.
- **Article 9 — Applicable law (the firewall).** Jersey law governs
  the validity of a Jersey trust, the rights of beneficiaries, the
  capacity of the settlor in relation to the trust property and the
  rights and duties of trustees. Foreign forced-heirship rules and
  similar non-trust regimes are not applied. See
  [firewall.md](firewall.md). *(File is a stub.)*
- **Article 9A — Reserved powers.** A Jersey trust is not invalid by
  reason only of the settlor reserving any of a wide menu of powers
  (powers of revocation, powers to direct investment, powers to add or
  remove beneficiaries, powers to direct distributions, etc.). See
  [reserved-powers.md](reserved-powers.md). *(File is a stub.)*
- **Article 12 — Purpose trusts.** A Jersey trust may be created for
  a non-charitable purpose, subject to certain requirements (an
  enforcer, suitable wording, and the purpose being lawful and not
  contrary to public policy). See [purpose-trusts.md](purpose-trusts.md).
  *(File is a stub.)*
- **Article 47 — Mistake and set-aside.** The Royal Court has a
  statutory jurisdiction to set aside the exercise of a fiduciary power
  in relation to a trust where the power-holder acted under a
  qualifying mistake. This replaced reliance on the common-law
  *Hastings-Bass* rule. See [article-47-set-aside.md](article-47-set-aside.md).
  *(File is a stub.)*
- **Article 51 — Directions applications.** The Court's jurisdiction
  to give directions to trustees on the proper exercise of their
  powers — the workhorse procedure for "blessing" a trustee decision.

## What sits alongside the Law

- The **Foundations (Jersey) Law 2009** (see
  [foundations-law-2009.md](../foundations/foundations-law-2009.md))
  provides a separate civil-law-flavoured vehicle without
  beneficiaries-as-of-right.
- The **Financial Services (Jersey) Law 1998** regulates the
  conduct of **trust-company business** (the provision of trustee
  services in the course of business) — see
  [financial-regulation/index.md](../financial-regulation/index.md).
- The **Probate (Jersey) Law 1998** governs grants of representation.

## Recent activity

The Government of Jersey published a **consultation on amendments to
the Trusts (Jersey) Law 1984** in mid-2024; some of those proposals
have moved into draft legislation and some remain under consideration.
Re-verify against the [States Assembly][sa] before relying on this
file for a current-amendment point.
