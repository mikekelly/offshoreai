# Article 48 — Application of Part 3

Article 48 is the **scope-of-Part-3** provision in the
**[Trusts (Jersey) Law 1984][tjl]**. It states simply:

> This Part of this Law shall apply only to a foreign trust.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][tjl].)

## What this means

Part 3 (Articles 48–49) of the Law contains the **rules specifically
for foreign trusts** — trusts whose proper law is not Jersey law
(see [`proper-law.md`](proper-law.md) for the proper-law
determination).

Where [Part 2](application-of-part-2.md) governs **Jersey trusts**
exhaustively, Part 3 is shorter — only [Article 49](foreign-trust-enforceability.md)
is substantive — because Jersey law largely defers to the foreign
proper law on substantive questions and applies its own rules only
on a narrow class of points.

Foreign trusts that come before the Royal Court (typically under
[Article 5](court-jurisdiction-over-trusts.md) jurisdiction because
the trustee is Jersey-resident, the property is Jersey-situated, or
the trust is administered in Jersey) are dealt with under:

- the **substantive provisions** of the foreign proper law, on
  validity, interpretation, trustee duties, beneficial rights, etc.;
- the **Part 3 provisions** (Articles 48–49) of the Trusts Law, on
  Jersey-specific recognition and the public-policy carve-out;
- the **Part 4 provisions** (Articles 50–57) — see
  [`application-of-part-4.md`](application-of-part-4.md) — which
  apply to both Jersey and foreign trusts where context admits.
