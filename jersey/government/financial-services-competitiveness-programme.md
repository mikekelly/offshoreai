# Financial Services Competitiveness Programme (FSCP)

## What it is

The **Financial Services Competitiveness Programme**
(FSCP) is the Government of Jersey's **10-year strategic
economic-policy plan** for the financial-services sector,
running through **2035**. It was **launched April 2025**
and the **final report and action plan was published March
2026**.

The FSCP is the Government's articulation of the policy
environment that will support the financial-services
industry over the coming decade. It is **complementary to**
the [JFSC 2026-2030 Strategy](../financial-regulation/jfsc-strategy-2026-2030.md) —
the JFSC strategy is the regulator's plan; the FSCP is the
government's economic-policy plan.

## Why it matters

Financial services constitutes **38.5% of Jersey's
national income in 2024** (Statistics Jersey — see
[`../../history/sources.md`](../../history/sources.md)). It
is the dominant economic sector and the primary source of
Government revenue. A coordinated long-horizon policy plan
is therefore strategically essential.

The FSCP is also a **signal of policy continuity**. By
articulating a 10-year horizon, the Government provides
the industry with the policy stability needed for long-
horizon investment decisions (industry build-out, training,
infrastructure, sponsor commitment to Jersey-based
structures).

## The four workstreams

The FSCP organises its action plan into four workstreams:

### Workstream 1 — Tax

Includes:

- Pillar Two implementation (the
  [MCIT 2025](../international/pillar-two-mcit.md));
- Long-term competitiveness of the
  [Zero-Ten regime](../tax/zero-ten.md);
- International tax compliance ([CRS](../international/crs.md),
  [FATCA](../international/fatca.md), [DAC6](../international/dac6.md),
  CARF — see [`../international/carf.md`](../international/carf.md));
- Bilateral and multilateral tax-treaty work;
- High-Value Residency tax regime ([HVR tax](../tax/high-value-residency-tax.md))
  as a partner-attraction mechanism.

### Workstream 2 — Regulation

Includes:

- Coordination with the
  [JFSC 2026-2030 Strategy](../financial-regulation/jfsc-strategy-2026-2030.md);
- AML/CFT supervisory effectiveness (post-MoneyVal-2024);
- Substance regime ongoing operationalisation
  ([Economic Substance Law 2019](../tax/economic-substance.md));
- Innovation Council coordination on digital-assets
  regulatory development (see
  [`../financial-regulation/innovation-council.md`](../financial-regulation/innovation-council.md));
- Sustainable-finance regulatory framework
  ([`../sustainable-finance/index.md`](../sustainable-finance/index.md)).

### Workstream 3 — External growth

Includes:

- International market access (UK, EU, US, Asia,
  Africa, Middle East);
- Bilateral engagement on regulatory equivalence;
- Marketing of Jersey's competitive position;
- Trade-mission and industry-event engagement;
- Jersey Finance support and coordination.

### Workstream 4 — Skills

Includes:

- Pipeline of qualified financial-services professionals;
- Coordination with Digital Jersey, the College, and the
  university;
- Continuous-professional-development infrastructure;
- Immigration policy as it supports professional skills
  needs;
- High-Value Residency programme as a senior-skills
  attraction route ([HVR](../immigration-residency/high-value-residency.md)).

## How it interacts with the JFSC Strategy

The FSCP and the JFSC Strategy are **coordinated but
distinct**:

| Dimension | FSCP | JFSC 2026-2030 Strategy |
|---|---|---|
| Issuer | Government of Jersey | JFSC |
| Time horizon | 10 years (to 2035) | 5 years (to 2030) |
| Domain | Economic policy | Regulation and supervision |
| Workstreams | Tax / Regulation / External Growth / Skills | International standards / Digital assets / Sustainable finance / Substance-AML-resilience |
| Coordination mechanism | Cross-government and JFSC engagement | Government engagement on regulatory direction |

The two plans **explicitly reinforce each other**:

- **Tax workstream of FSCP** ↔ JFSC's regulatory framework
  applies to tax-driven structures;
- **Regulation workstream of FSCP** ↔ JFSC's supervisory
  programme runs underneath;
- **Digital-assets growth vertical (JFSC)** ↔ External-
  growth workstream (FSCP);
- **Sustainable finance growth vertical (JFSC)** ↔
  External-growth workstream (FSCP);
- **Skills workstream of FSCP** ↔ JFSC's regulator-staff
  pipeline and industry talent.

## What's coming

Implementation runs through **2035**. Annual reviews are
expected. The most consequential near-term deliverables:

- **JFSC EU AIF Code** publication (mid-late 2026);
- **Tokenisation legislation review** (if needed beyond
  existing framework);
- **SFDR 2.0 implementation** alignment (2027-2028);
- **MoneyVal follow-up reporting** on 5th-round outcomes;
- **Multi-year skills-pipeline build-out**;
- **External-market positioning** for the next decade.

## The strategic narrative

Read together, the FSCP and the JFSC Strategy articulate
**a coherent multi-decade plan**:

- **Compliance baseline maintained** (MoneyVal, Pillar
  Two, substance);
- **Two growth verticals targeted** (digital, sustainable);
- **External market access** prioritised;
- **Skills pipeline** built out;
- **Policy continuity** signalled over 10 years.

This is the most coordinated multi-stakeholder strategy
articulation Jersey's financial-services sector has had —
see the **trajectory** synthesis at
[`../../history/trajectory.md`](../../history/trajectory.md)
for how this fits into the four-act narrative.

## Pitfalls

- **Treating the FSCP as government marketing** — it is
  a resource-allocated, deliverable-bound plan;
- **Assuming the FSCP replaces the JFSC role** — they
  are complementary, not substitutable;
- **Underestimating the skills workstream** — it is the
  most operationally consequential for industry growth;
- **Ignoring the policy-continuity signal** — the 10-
  year horizon is itself the message.
