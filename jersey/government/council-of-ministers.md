# Council of Ministers

## What the Council of Ministers is

The **Council of Ministers** is the **executive arm** of
Jersey government — the body of senior ministers who direct
policy and administration.

It comprises:

- The **Chief Minister** (head of government);
- **Ministers** with portfolios (Treasury, External Relations,
  Home Affairs, Health, Education, Economic Development,
  etc.);
- **Assistant Ministers** supporting Ministers.

## Election and composition

- **Chief Minister** is elected by the States Assembly (the
  legislature) after each general election;
- **Ministers** are appointed by the Chief Minister, subject
  to **States Assembly approval** of nominees;
- The number of Ministers and their portfolios is set by
  legislation and political agreement, typically **8-10
  Ministers**;
- Ministers must be **States members** (sitting in the
  States Assembly).

## Roles

The Council of Ministers:

- **Sets** government policy direction;
- **Coordinates** ministerial action across portfolios;
- **Brings** legislation to the States Assembly for debate;
- **Manages** government finance through the Treasury Minister;
- **Represents** Jersey externally (in cooperation with the
  External Relations Minister);
- **Responds** to crises (Covid, financial crisis, economic
  shocks).

## Individual Ministers

Each Minister:

- **Heads** a department / portfolio;
- **Directs** civil servants in that area;
- **Brings** propositions and laws to the Assembly within
  their portfolio;
- **Answers** questions in the Assembly;
- **Accountable** to the Assembly through scrutiny.

## Key Ministerial portfolios

- **Chief Minister** — head of government, overall direction;
- **Treasury** — finance, tax, fiscal policy;
- **External Relations** — UK / EU / international relations;
- **Home Affairs** — justice, police, immigration;
- **Health and Social Services**;
- **Education**;
- **Economic Development** — financial services, tourism,
  industry;
- **Environment**;
- **Infrastructure**;
- **Social Security**.

## Distinction from civil service

- **Ministers** are **politicians** — elected to the Assembly,
  appointed to portfolios;
- **Civil servants** (the "**Public Service**") implement and
  advise — politically neutral;
- **Chief Executive** of the public service is the senior
  civil servant supporting the Council of Ministers.

## Constitutional position

The Council of Ministers operates under the **Constitution of
Jersey** (rooted in customary law, statute, and convention):

- **Accountable** to the States Assembly;
- **Subject to** judicial review of administrative decisions
  by the Royal Court;
- **Operates** within the broader Crown Dependency
  framework — Jersey's executive answers to its own
  legislature, not directly to UK ministers.

## Scrutiny

The Council of Ministers is held to account by:

- The **States Assembly** through questions, motions, no-
  confidence;
- **Scrutiny panels** of backbench members reviewing
  policy areas;
- The **Public Accounts Committee** for financial accountability;
- The **Comptroller and Auditor General** for value-for-money;
- The **media** and public.
