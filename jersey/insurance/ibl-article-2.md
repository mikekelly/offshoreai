# IBL 1996 Article 2 — Meaning of "Insurance Business"

Article 2 is the **gateway** to the IBL regime — what
constitutes "insurance business" requiring a JFSC permit.

## The provision

A person carries on insurance business if they:

- **Effect contracts of insurance** with policyholders; or
- **Carry out contracts of insurance** in their capacity as
  insurer (paying claims, managing the policy lifecycle).

## What is a contract of insurance

A contract of insurance has these classical features
(*Prudential Insurance v Inland Revenue Commissioners*):

- The **insurer** agrees to pay the **insured** money or
  provide a corresponding benefit;
- On the occurrence of an **uncertain event** adverse to the
  insured;
- In return for the **premium**;
- The insured has an **insurable interest** in the subject
  matter.

Contracts that **lack** these features are typically not
insurance:

- **Investment** contracts (no uncertain adverse event);
- **Service** contracts with payment for service (not for risk
  transfer);
- **Warranty** / **guarantee** arrangements (where the test is
  fact-dependent);
- **Hedging** financial-market contracts.

## Long-term vs general business

The Law distinguishes:

### Long-term business

- **Life** insurance — death, term, whole-of-life;
- **Annuities** — paying periodic income;
- **Permanent health** insurance — long-term disability;
- **Capital redemption** — investment-linked policies with
  guaranteed return at maturity;
- **Pensions** — annuity-style retirement income.

Long-term business is subject to **specific prudential rules**
reflecting its long liability tails.

### General business

- **Property** insurance;
- **Liability** insurance;
- **Marine, aviation, transport** (MAT);
- **Motor** insurance;
- **Credit / surety**;
- **Specialty** lines (cyber, professional indemnity,
  political risk).

General business has shorter liability tails but is subject to
catastrophe / cyclical risks.

## Reinsurance

**Reinsurance** is insurance of insurers — a key Jersey market
segment:

- **Captive reinsurance** — reinsurance into a captive
  vehicle;
- **Commercial reinsurance** — third-party reinsurance market
  participation;
- **Treaty** vs **facultative** reinsurance.

Reinsurance is **within Article 2** — reinsurers also require
permits.

## Excluded activities

Article 2 excludes:

- **Self-insurance** (no separate insurer / insured);
- **Mutuals** structured as non-insurance vehicles;
- **Specific exempted** categories by Order;
- **Public-sector** insurance arrangements.

## Practical scope questions

### Warranty / extended-warranty products

- Where the product **transfers risk** between independent
  parties for premium, often classified as insurance;
- Where it is **embedded** in a sale (retailer-provided
  warranty), may not be insurance;
- **Fact-specific** analysis required.

### Captive cells

- A **cell** of a Jersey PCC operates as a separate underwriter;
- Each cell must comply with the IBL through the cell company;
- See [`ibl-part-6-captives.md`](ibl-part-6-captives.md).

### Crypto / DeFi insurance

- Decentralised insurance products may or may not be
  "insurance" depending on substance;
- New JFSC guidance addresses crypto / DeFi scope;
- **Pseudo-insurance** structures avoiding the IBL are
  scrutinised.
