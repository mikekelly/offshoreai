# IBL 1996 Article 3 — Categories of Permit

Article 3 divides Jersey insurance permits into two
**categories**, reflecting fundamentally different business
models and regulatory regimes.

## Category A — Domestic / Jersey policyholder business

Category A permits insurers that:

- **Write policies** to Jersey-resident policyholders;
- **Conduct domestic** insurance business in the Jersey
  market;
- Are subject to **full retail / consumer protection** rules;
- Are subject to **higher prudential** standards reflecting
  retail policyholder protection.

Category A insurers in Jersey are relatively limited in
number — most domestic policyholders are served by branches /
agencies of UK insurers, not Jersey-incorporated Category A
insurers.

See [`category-a-permit.md`](category-a-permit.md).

## Category B — International / Captive business

Category B permits insurers that:

- **Do not write** Jersey-resident policyholder business;
- **Write** international / non-Jersey business;
- Are typically **captive** insurers (group-owned) or
  **international reinsurers**;
- Are subject to **proportionate prudential** rules
  reflecting the sophisticated nature of the policyholder
  (corporate / institutional);
- Are subject to **reduced retail conduct** rules (no Jersey
  retail consumers).

Category B is the **dominant** Jersey insurance category. Most
Jersey insurers are captives or international reinsurers
under Category B.

See [`category-b-permit.md`](category-b-permit.md).

## Why two categories

The split reflects Jersey's role as an **international
financial centre** with **limited domestic market**:

- **Domestic** business is relatively small;
- **International / captive** business is the principal
  insurance activity;
- **Regulatory calibration** differs — Category B can be
  lighter on retail conduct (no retail customers) but full on
  prudential and AML;
- **Cross-jurisdictional** taxonomy — many jurisdictions
  distinguish similar "domestic" vs "international" categories
  (Guernsey, Bermuda, Cayman, IoM).

## Practical implications

### Choosing the category

- **Category A** if Jersey retail policyholders are targeted;
- **Category B** for everything else (captive, reinsurance,
  high-net-worth, international).

### Cross-border distribution

- A **Category A** insurer can passport limited business
  internationally subject to host-country rules;
- A **Category B** insurer **cannot** write Jersey
  policyholder business (would need to upgrade to Category A);
- **Tied agency** and **introduced** policyholder mechanisms
  used for cross-border distribution.

### Conversion

- An insurer can apply to **convert** from one category to
  another;
- Conversion engages full re-licensing requirements;
- Most insurers settle on one category long-term.
