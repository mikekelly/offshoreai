# IBL 1996 Part 2 — Permits (residual)

Articles 4 and 6 have their own deep files. This cluster
covers Articles 5, 7, 8.

## Article 5 — Application for permit

The application includes (depending on category):

- **Identity** of the applicant — corporate or other;
- **Constitutional documents**;
- **Business plan** detail:
  - Lines of business proposed;
  - Markets (geography, customer types);
  - Distribution channels;
  - Pricing methodology;
  - Volume projections (3-5 years);
- **Capital plan** — initial and maintained;
- **Solvency margin** approach;
- **Reinsurance** programme;
- **Investment** policy and strategic asset allocation;
- **Reserving** methodology;
- **Principal persons** — directors, senior officers,
  controllers;
- **Actuarial** engagement (mandatory for long-term);
- **Auditor** engagement;
- **Outsourcing** arrangements;
- **AML / CFT** programme;
- **Local presence** — Jersey premises, key personnel;
- **Group structure** — parent insurer, sister entities;
- **Fees** prescribed.

## Article 7 — Conditions of permit

The JFSC may impose **conditions** including:

- **Scope** — specific lines of business;
- **Capital** requirements above statutory minimum;
- **Reinsurance** requirements (retention limits,
  approved counterparties);
- **Distribution** restrictions (e.g., wholesale only);
- **Reporting** beyond standard returns;
- **Senior management** approval for material changes;
- **Specific risks** — e.g., cyber, catastrophe limits.

Conditions are **enforceable** and breach is a regulatory
offence.

## Article 8 — Refusal, suspension, revocation

The JFSC may:

### Refuse

For failure to meet Article 6 conditions, inadequate principals,
insufficient capital, unsuitable business plan, or adverse
public-interest considerations.

### Suspend

For breaches, pending investigation, or material concerns.

### Revoke

For material breaches, loss of fit-and-proper, prudential
failure, AML / CFT material failure, or other serious cause.

Decisions are subject to:

- **Notice** with grounds;
- **Right** to representations;
- **Reasoned** decision;
- **Appeal** to Royal Court.
