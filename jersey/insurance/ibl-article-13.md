# IBL 1996 Article 13 — Capital and Solvency

Article 13 governs **capital and solvency** requirements for
Jersey insurers.

## The provision

An insurer must maintain:

- **Capital** at the level prescribed by JFSC Order / Code;
- **Solvency margin** — assets in excess of technical
  provisions and other liabilities;
- **Composition** of capital meeting prescribed quality
  standards;
- **Reporting** of capital and solvency in prescribed returns;
- **Stress-testing** of capital adequacy.

## Minimum capital

Minimum capital depends on:

- **Category** (A or B);
- **Class** of business (long-term, general, captive);
- **Lines** within class;
- **Scale** of business.

Indicative minimums (subject to current JFSC schedules):

- **Category A general** — £50,000+ depending on lines;
- **Category A long-term** — £100,000+ depending on lines;
- **Category B captive** — proportionate, often £100,000+
  with parent / group support;
- **Category B reinsurer** — substantial, typically several
  £m+;
- **PCC core** — separate requirements for core and cells.

These are **statutory minimums** — the JFSC will require
**more** for material business and risks.

## Solvency margin

The solvency margin is the **excess of admissible assets** over:

- **Technical provisions** (claims reserves, IBNR, unearned
  premium, mathematical reserves);
- **Other liabilities**.

The required solvency margin depends on:

- **Business class** and **lines**;
- **Volume** of business (premium / claims);
- **Risk profile** (lines, geographies, reinsurance);
- **Stress-scenario** outcomes.

For Category A and significant Category B insurers, **Solvency
II-equivalent** approaches apply:

- **Solvency Capital Requirement (SCR)** — capital sufficient
  to absorb a 1-in-200-year loss;
- **Minimum Capital Requirement (MCR)** — absolute floor
  below which the insurer cannot operate;
- **Own Risk and Solvency Assessment (ORSA)** — insurer's own
  assessment of capital adequacy.

For smaller / captive insurers, **proportionate** approaches
apply.

## Admissible assets

For solvency calculations:

- **High-quality** assets count fully (cash, government bonds,
  rated corporate bonds);
- **Equity** subject to haircuts and concentration limits;
- **Real estate** subject to haircuts;
- **Intra-group** assets subject to restrictions
  (concentration limits, parent support requirements);
- **Intangibles** typically excluded.

## Group support

For **captives**, the JFSC may accept:

- **Letters of guarantee** from creditworthy parents;
- **Reinsurance** from group reinsurers (subject to
  counterparty assessment);
- **Capital commitment** letters.

These reduce the captive's own capital requirements but tie
the captive's solvency to parent strength.

## Capital reporting

Insurers report capital and solvency in:

- **Annual** prudential return (audited);
- **Quarterly** management returns (larger insurers);
- **Ad hoc** notifications for material movements;
- **Annual** ORSA report.
