# Category B Insurance Permit — Jersey

A **Category B permit** under the Insurance Business (Jersey)
Law 1996 authorises an insurer to write **international,
specialist, or captive business** — typically not direct Jersey
retail.

## Who uses Category B

- **Captive insurers** — single-parent or group captives
  insuring their parent's risks;
- **Specialist reinsurers** — writing reinsurance for specific
  industries or risk types;
- **Run-off vehicles** — managing closed insurance portfolios;
- **Industry-mutual** structures;
- **Cell-company captives** — individual cells within PCC / ICC
  structures (Part 18D Companies Law).

## Why Category B

The split between Category A and Category B reflects a **policy
calibration**:

- **Retail-protection considerations** (Category A) apply to
  Jersey-consumer business and demand stringent solvency, conduct,
  and continuity standards;
- **Sophisticated-client business** (Category B) involves
  clients who can assess insurer creditworthiness themselves;
  the regime is **proportionate**.

This calibration is what makes Jersey **competitive** as a
captive jurisdiction — appropriate regulatory burden for the
sophisticated client base.

## Cell-company captives

A common Category B structure:

- A **Protected Cell Company (PCC)** establishes individual
  **cells** for separate insurance programmes;
- Each cell operates as a **virtual insurer** within the PCC;
- The cellular-segregation rules (Article 127YT of the Companies
  Law) keep each cell's liabilities ring-fenced;
- The **PCC core** holds general capital and provides the
  governance shell.

This allows multiple captive owners to **share infrastructure**
in a single legal entity while keeping their risks segregated —
a "**rent-a-captive**" model.
