# Jersey Digital Assets and Tokenisation

## What this is

The **digital-assets** vertical in Jersey covers
**tokenisation**, **VASP operations**, **security
tokens**, **stablecoins**, **tokenised real-world assets
(RWAs)**, and **tokenised fund interests**.

By Oct 2025, **14 registered VASPs** operated in Jersey
(see [`../registries/vasp-register.md`](../registries/vasp-register.md)),
and the [Innovation Council](../financial-regulation/innovation-council.md)
had been established as the strategic-coordination body.
The [JFSC 2026-2030 Strategy](../financial-regulation/jfsc-strategy-2026-2030.md)
names digital assets and tokenisation as one of two
strategic growth verticals (alongside sustainable
finance).

This file is the **index** for Jersey's digital-assets
content. Live frontier-tracking — including specific
developments through 2026-2027 — lives at
[`../../frontier/tokenisation-jersey-2026.md`](../../frontier/tokenisation-jersey-2026.md).

## The regulatory architecture

Jersey does **not** have dedicated tokenisation legislation
(yet). The digital-assets activity is regulated under the
**existing financial-services framework**:

- **VASP supervision** under the
  [Proceeds of Crime (Jersey) Law 1999 (POCL)](../aml-cft/proceeds-of-crime-law-1999.md)
  framework — see [`../registries/vasp-register.md`](../registries/vasp-register.md);
- **Securities token treatment** — tokenised securities are
  regulated as securities under existing securities laws
  ([Financial Services (Jersey) Law 1998](../financial-regulation/financial-services-jersey-law-1998.md));
- **Fund tokenisation** — tokenised fund interests are
  regulated as fund interests under the existing fund
  regulation ([JPF](../funds/jersey-private-fund.md),
  [Expert Fund](../funds/expert-fund.md), etc.);
- **Tax** — tokenised entities subject to standard tax
  regime ([Zero-Ten](../tax/zero-ten.md), and
  [MCIT 2025](../international/pillar-two-mcit.md) where in
  scope);
- **AML/CFT** — full
  [AML/CFT regime](../aml-cft/index.md) applies;
- **CARF** — see [`../international/carf.md`](../international/carf.md)
  for tax-information reporting on crypto-assets;
- **Substance** —
  [Economic Substance Law 2019](../tax/economic-substance.md)
  applies to Jersey-resident digital-assets businesses.

## The strategic posture

Jersey's positioning, articulated through the
[Innovation Council](../financial-regulation/innovation-council.md)
and the [JFSC Strategy](../financial-regulation/jfsc-strategy-2026-2030.md):

- **Regulated** — not "anything-goes"; the existing
  framework applies;
- **MiCA-compatible** — aligning with the EU Markets in
  Crypto-Assets Regulation despite Jersey not being in
  MiCA scope;
- **Selective** — targeting specific verticals (tokenised
  RWAs, tokenised fund interests, security tokens) rather
  than retail-crypto or speculative-token flows;
- **Sponsor-led** — domicile of choice for established
  sponsors moving into tokenisation, not for early-stage
  retail crypto;
- **Substance-required** — tokenisation businesses need
  Jersey substance like any other.

## Product application

### Tokenised real-world assets (RWAs)

Tokenisation of physical or off-chain assets — real
estate, art, infrastructure, commodities, carbon credits.
The tokenised representation is typically a security token
falling within existing securities regulation. Use cases:

- Fractional ownership of high-value assets;
- Liquidity for traditionally illiquid asset classes;
- Programmable rights (income flows, voting, transfer
  restrictions);
- Tokenised carbon credits and sustainable assets.

### Tokenised fund interests

Tokenisation of LP / shareholder interests in funds. The
tokenised interest is typically still a fund interest under
existing fund regulation. Use cases:

- Secondary-market liquidity for traditionally illiquid
  fund interests;
- Programmable governance (LP voting);
- Programmable distributions;
- Integration with on-chain investment workflows.

The interplay between tokenised fund interests and the
[JPF regime](../funds/jersey-private-fund.md) is one of
the most active practitioner areas.

### Security tokens

Pure tokenised securities — issued, traded, and settled
on-chain. Regulated as securities under existing law.

### Stablecoins

Fiat-backed stablecoins issued from Jersey are regulated
under the existing securities and financial-services
framework. Jersey is following the EU MiCA approach to
asset-referenced tokens (ARTs) and e-money tokens (EMTs).

### Tokenised carbon credits

Combination of digital-assets and sustainable-finance
verticals. Tokenised carbon credits issued from Jersey
sit at the intersection of [sustainable
finance](../sustainable-finance/index.md) and digital
assets.

## VASP operations

Jersey-registered VASPs operate under the
[VASP register](../registries/vasp-register.md)
framework. Activities include:

- Crypto-asset exchange services;
- Custody and administration of crypto-assets;
- Operating crypto-asset trading platforms;
- Issuance and offering of crypto-assets in certain
  arrangements.

Operational view at
[`../use-cases/compliance-mlro/vasp-crypto-customers.md`](../use-cases/compliance-mlro/vasp-crypto-customers.md).

## Comparison with adjacent jurisdictions

| Jurisdiction | Approach | Status |
|---|---|---|
| **Jersey** | Existing framework + Innovation Council; VASP regime; MiCA-compatible | 14 registered VASPs; substance-required |
| **Cayman** | VASP Act 2020; broader retail-crypto activity | Larger VASP population; less substance-focused |
| **EU (MiCA)** | Dedicated regulation; phased application from 2024-2026 | Comprehensive but EU-only |
| **UK** | FCA registration; FSMA fall-back; no dedicated regulation | Practitioner uncertainty |
| **Gibraltar** | DLT regulation; early mover | Smaller market |

Jersey's positioning is closer to the **EU MiCA** posture
(regulated, substantive, restrictive) than to Cayman's
more permissive approach.

## What's coming

- **Continued VASP register growth**;
- **Innovation Council pilots** in tokenised RWAs and
  carbon credits;
- **Possible specific tokenisation legislation** — if
  the existing framework proves inadequate;
- **CARF implementation** — first reports due 2027 (see
  [`../international/carf.md`](../international/carf.md));
- **EU MiCA implementation tail** — Jersey aligning with
  MiCA framework standards;
- **SFDR 2.0 / sustainable-finance overlap** with
  tokenised sustainable assets.

## Pitfalls

- **Assuming Jersey is permissive** — the framework is
  substantive and selective;
- **Underestimating substance requirements** — applies
  fully to digital-assets businesses;
- **Confusing VASP registration with CARF reporting** —
  both required;
- **Treating tokenised securities as outside securities
  law** — they're not; existing securities regulation
  applies;
- **Ignoring MiCA-compatibility** — Jersey will not
  diverge from EU framework in a way that creates
  arbitrage.
