# FSL 1998 Part 4 — Supervision

Article 19 (Codes of Practice) has its own deep file. This
cluster covers Articles 16-18 and 20-23.

## Article 16 — Information powers

The JFSC may require a registered firm to:

- **Produce documents**;
- **Provide information**;
- **Explain** specific matters;
- **Confirm** facts;
- Within **specified timeframes**.

Failure to comply is a **regulatory offence** and grounds for
supervisory action.

The information powers apply:

- For **routine** supervision (annual returns, ad hoc
  inquiries);
- For **themed** reviews (sector-wide topics);
- For **specific** investigations.

## Article 17 — Inspections

The JFSC may **inspect** a registered firm:

- **Premises** (Jersey-based);
- **Records and IT systems**;
- **Staff interviews**;
- **Outsourced functions** at provider locations.

Types of inspection:

- **Routine** — typically 1-3 year cycle depending on risk;
- **Themed** — sector-wide topic-focused (e.g., AML
  effectiveness, conduct);
- **Cause-based** — specific concerns;
- **Pre-launch** — for new permitted activities;
- **Group** — joint with home regulator for cross-border
  groups.

Inspection reports:

- **Confidential** but actionable;
- **Findings letters** with rated issues (high / medium /
  low);
- **Remediation** required within specified timeframes;
- **Follow-up** monitoring.

## Article 18 — Skilled persons reports

The JFSC may require a firm to **engage a skilled person**:

- Independent **third party** (typically an audit firm,
  specialist consultancy);
- **Paid by the firm**;
- **Reporting to the JFSC** on specific matters;
- **Common** topics: AML effectiveness, risk management,
  governance, systems and controls;
- **Material** for serious supervisory concerns.

A skilled persons report is **expensive** for the firm
(often six- or seven-figure project costs) and signals
serious JFSC concern.

## Article 20 — Directions

The JFSC may issue **directions** to a registered firm:

- **Cease** specified activities;
- **Take** specified steps (strengthen capital, controls,
  AML);
- **Limit** exposures;
- **Provide** specific information;
- **Apply** specific procedures.

Directions are:

- **Enforceable** through Court order;
- **Subject** to appeal to Royal Court;
- **Public** in some cases;
- **Persistent** until varied or lifted.

## Article 21 — Suspension

The JFSC may **suspend** registration:

- For **breach** of conditions;
- **Pending** investigation;
- For **public-interest** reasons (e.g., suspected illegal
  activity);
- For **prudential** concerns.

A suspended firm:

- **Cannot** carry on the regulated activity during suspension;
- **Must** comply with **emergency directions**;
- **Must** continue to pay fees and report;
- **May** continue to wind-down operations under JFSC
  supervision.

## Article 22 — Additional reporting

The JFSC may require **additional reporting** beyond standard:

- **Specific risk** indicators;
- **Specific transactions**;
- **Specific outsourced functions**;
- **Frequent** (e.g., weekly or daily during stress) reports;
- **Confidential** to JFSC and not generally public.

## Article 23 — Cooperation and information sharing

The JFSC cooperates with:

- **Other regulators** (UK FCA / PRA, ECB, SRB, foreign
  equivalents);
- **AML** authorities (Jersey FIU, foreign FIUs);
- **Other JFSC divisions**;
- **Foreign supervisors** through MoUs.

Information sharing is subject to **confidentiality
safeguards**.
