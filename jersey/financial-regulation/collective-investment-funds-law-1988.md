# Collective Investment Funds (Jersey) Law 1988

The **Collective Investment Funds (Jersey) Law 1988** (CIF Law)
is the principal statute regulating **public-distributed
collective investment funds** in Jersey.

## What is a "collective investment fund"

A **fund** under the CIF Law is, broadly:

- A vehicle for **pooling capital** from multiple investors;
- For **collective investment** in a specified asset class or
  strategy;
- With investors generally **passive** (not exercising
  day-to-day control).

The CIF Law applies to **public funds** — funds offered to the
general public or with broad distribution. **Private funds**
(typically with limited investor numbers or restricted to
sophisticated investors) are regulated under different
frameworks including:

- **Alternative Investment Funds (Jersey) Regulations 2012**
  (AIFs);
- **Private Fund Service Provider** arrangements;
- **Expert Fund** rules and similar JFSC-published frameworks.

## Public fund regulation

A public Jersey fund typically requires:

- A **permit** under the CIF Law for the fund itself;
- **Service-provider registrations** under FS(J)L 1998 for its
  administrator, manager, custodian (FSB registrations);
- A **prospectus** complying with prescribed requirements;
- Ongoing **regulatory supervision** by the JFSC.

## Fund vehicles

Jersey public funds are typically structured as:

- **Unit trusts** (a trust holds the assets; investors are
  unitholders);
- **Companies** (typically open-ended investment companies);
- **Limited partnerships** under the relevant partnership
  legislation;
- **Cell-company structures** (PCC or ICC under Part 18D of the
  Companies Law).

The choice of vehicle is driven by **tax characterisation in
investor jurisdictions**, **operational preferences**, and
**investor expectations**.

## Why CIF matters

Jersey is a **significant funds jurisdiction**:

- Home to a wide range of **hedge funds**, **private equity
  funds**, **real estate funds**, and other alternative strategies;
- Strong **service-provider infrastructure** (administrators,
  custodians, lawyers, auditors);
- **CIF-listed funds** can market into many other jurisdictions
  including the UK and EU (subject to local rules).
