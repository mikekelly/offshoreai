# JFSC 2026-2030 Strategy

## What it is

The **JFSC 2026-2030 Strategy** is the
[JFSC's](./jfsc.md) five-year strategic plan, published
**23 March 2026**. It is the regulator's first explicit
multi-year strategic articulation since the post-MoneyVal-
2016 supervisory build-out programme, and marks a
**meaningful posture shift** in Jersey's regulatory
direction.

The strategy operates **in parallel** with the
**Government of Jersey's 10-year Financial Services
Competitiveness Programme** (see
[`../government/financial-services-competitiveness-programme.md`](../government/financial-services-competitiveness-programme.md)) —
the JFSC strategy is the regulator's plan, the FSCP is the
government's economic-policy plan. Together they constitute
the most coordinated articulation of regulator-and-
government strategic intent the industry has had.

## Why it's strategically significant

For most of the past decade Jersey's regulatory posture was
**defensive** — managing MoneyVal-4th-round risk, EU/UK
listing risk, BEPS pressure, substance-regime build-out,
and Pillar Two negotiation. With the MoneyVal 5th round
top-tier result (July 2024), the MCIT 2025 implementation,
and the post-Brexit equilibrium settled, the regulator
moved to an **offensive growth posture**.

The 2026-2030 Strategy is the formal articulation of that
shift. It is the first time the JFSC has explicitly named
**growth verticals** as supervisory priorities.

## The four priorities

The Strategy articulates four strategic priorities for the
period:

### Priority 1 — International standards and competitive positioning

Maintain Jersey's **top-tier international standards** —
particularly on [AML/CFT](../aml-cft/index.md) (building on
the MoneyVal 5th-round outcome) — while **preserving and
extending** competitive positioning in chosen verticals.

The framing: international compliance is the **non-
negotiable foundation**, but it is the entry ticket, not
the strategy. Growth comes from positioning above and
beyond compliance.

### Priority 2 — Digital assets and tokenisation as a growth vertical

The Strategy names **digital assets and tokenisation** as a
strategic growth vertical. This formalises the direction
that the [Innovation Council](./innovation-council.md) was
established in late 2025 to drive.

Implementation work:

- Tokenisation guidance updates aligning with the EU MiCA
  framework where appropriate;
- VASP register growth and supervision quality (see
  [`../registries/vasp-register.md`](../registries/vasp-register.md));
- Selective tokenisation pilots;
- International engagement on digital-assets supervisory
  standards.

### Priority 3 — Sustainable finance as a growth vertical

The Strategy names **sustainable finance** as the second
growth vertical. The Jersey Finance projection of £123bn
sustainable-fund AUM by 2026 sits behind this.

Implementation work:

- Sustainable-finance guidance updates as SFDR 2.0
  develops (see [`../sustainable-finance/index.md`](../sustainable-finance/index.md)
  and [`../../frontier/sfdr-2-and-sustainable-finance.md`](../../frontier/sfdr-2-and-sustainable-finance.md));
- ESG fund supervision intensity;
- Greenwashing risk monitoring;
- Coordination with EU regulatory developments.

### Priority 4 — Substance, AML/CFT, and operational resilience

The Strategy retains **substance, AML/CFT, and operational
resilience** as ongoing supervisory priorities. These are
the foundational compliance areas; growth verticals do not
displace them.

Operational resilience is newly emphasised — the JFSC has
signalled increased supervisory focus on operational-
resilience programmes (post-COVID and post-Brexit
infrastructure stress having sharpened the issue).

## What's changed from the prior posture

| Dimension | Pre-2026 posture | 2026-2030 Strategy |
|---|---|---|
| Strategic horizon | Tactical (annual) | Five-year |
| Headline framing | Defending standards | Growth above standards |
| Digital assets | Cautious / catching up | Strategic growth vertical |
| Sustainable finance | Following EU | Strategic growth vertical |
| Operational resilience | Implicit | Explicit supervisory priority |
| Coordination with Government | Informal | Coordinated with FSCP |

## Implementation structure

The Strategy commits the JFSC to:

- **Annual progress reporting** against the four
  priorities;
- **Stakeholder engagement** through industry consultations
  (currently active: MLCO reforms, Schedule 2 Guidelines —
  see [`../../frontier/jersey-finance-industry-2026.md`](../../frontier/jersey-finance-industry-2026.md));
- **Resource alignment** — the JFSC's resource allocation
  is expected to follow the four priorities;
- **International engagement** in OECD, FATF, MoneyVal,
  and bilateral channels.

## How it lands for practitioners

For Jersey practitioners and structuring counsel:

- **Compliance baseline is non-negotiable** — top-tier
  AML/CFT, substance, and resilience are the entry
  ticket;
- **Two growth verticals are explicitly supported** —
  digital assets / tokenisation and sustainable finance;
- **Regulatory engagement** is welcomed on innovative
  structures within the supported verticals;
- **Structuring outside the supported verticals**
  continues to be supported (the strategy is additive,
  not exclusionary) but the regulator's focus is
  vertical-led.

## How it interacts with the FSCP

The **Government's Financial Services Competitiveness
Programme** is the parallel 10-year economic-policy plan.
The JFSC Strategy is broadly **consistent and
complementary** with the FSCP — both reflect the
post-MoneyVal pivot to growth, both emphasise digital and
sustainable, both maintain compliance as the foundation.

The Government and the JFSC have coordinated explicitly on
the relationship between the two plans.

## What's coming over the strategic period

Through 2026-2030:

- **Annual reviews** of strategy progress;
- **JFSC EU AIF Code** publication (post-AIFMD II
  implementation work);
- **Tokenisation pilots** translating to mainstream
  product;
- **SFDR 2.0 implementation** (2027-2028) with JFSC
  guidance alignment;
- **MoneyVal 6th round** in the early 2030s — the next
  major compliance milestone;
- **Pillar Two evolution** — possible OECD changes to
  thresholds, scope, mechanics;
- **MoneyVal follow-up** to the 5th round outcomes.

## Pitfalls

- **Treating the strategy as marketing** — it is a real
  resource-allocation document;
- **Assuming the growth verticals replace compliance** —
  they sit above it;
- **Underestimating the operational-resilience emphasis**
  — newly explicit and supervisory attention is rising;
- **Missing the FSCP coordination** — the two plans
  should be read together.
