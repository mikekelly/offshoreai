# Data Protection (Jersey) Law 2018 — Overview

The **Data Protection (Jersey) Law 2018 (DPL)** is Jersey's
**comprehensive data protection statute**, modelled closely
on the **EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)** and
came into force alongside the EU GDPR (May 2018).

## Why GDPR equivalence

Jersey's DPL is **substantively aligned** with GDPR for two
key reasons:

### Adequacy

The European Commission considers Jersey to have **adequate
data protection** for purposes of EU-Jersey data flows:

- Free movement of personal data from EU to Jersey;
- No need for Jersey-EU **Standard Contractual Clauses**;
- Critical for Jersey IFC role serving EU clients;
- Adequacy is **maintained** through ongoing alignment.

### Business reality

Jersey businesses serving EU clients need to **comply with
GDPR** in any event (extraterritorial reach of GDPR). Jersey
DPL **harmonises** the compliance burden.

## Key concepts

### Personal data

**Personal data** is any information relating to an
**identified or identifiable natural person**:

- Names, identifiers, location data;
- IP addresses (in context);
- Online identifiers;
- Genetic, biometric data;
- Pseudonymous data (if re-identification possible);
- **Not** anonymised data (irreversibly).

### Special category data

Special protections for:

- **Race / ethnic origin**;
- **Political opinions**;
- **Religious / philosophical beliefs**;
- **Trade union membership**;
- **Genetic / biometric** data for identification;
- **Health** data;
- **Sex life / orientation**;
- **Criminal convictions**.

### Controller vs processor

- **Controller** — determines purposes and means of
  processing;
- **Processor** — processes on behalf of controller;
- **Different** liabilities and obligations;
- **Most Jersey** TCB / FSB firms are controllers;
- **IT outsourcers** typically processors.

## Principal obligations

### Legal basis for processing

Processing requires a **legal basis**:

- **Consent**;
- **Contract** with the data subject;
- **Legal obligation**;
- **Vital interests** of the subject;
- **Public task**;
- **Legitimate interests** of the controller (with balancing).

For **special category** data, **additional** legal basis
needed.

### Data subject rights

- **Right of access**;
- **Right to rectification**;
- **Right to erasure** ("right to be forgotten");
- **Right to restrict processing**;
- **Right to data portability**;
- **Right to object**;
- **Rights related to automated decision-making**.

### Data protection by design and default

- **Privacy considered** from project inception;
- **Privacy-enhancing** technologies;
- **Default** settings minimising data collection.

### Security

- **Appropriate technical and organisational measures**;
- **Encryption** where appropriate;
- **Confidentiality, integrity, availability**;
- **Resilience** to incidents.

### Breach notification

- **Personal data breaches** notified to the **JOIC** within
  **72 hours**;
- **High-risk** breaches also notified to **data subjects**;
- **Detailed reporting** of facts, mitigation, advice.

### International transfers

Transfers outside Jersey to **non-adequate** jurisdictions
require:

- **Adequacy decision**;
- **Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs)**;
- **Binding Corporate Rules (BCRs)**;
- **Approved code of conduct**;
- **Specific derogations** (consent, contract necessity, etc.).

### Records of processing

Controllers and processors must maintain:

- **Records of processing activities**;
- **Available to JOIC** on request;
- **Specific** content prescribed.

## Penalties

Substantial fines available:

- **Up to 4% of global annual turnover** OR **£10 million**
  (whichever higher) for major breaches;
- **Up to 2% / £5 million** for lesser breaches;
- **Aligned** with GDPR penalty structure;
- **Criminal offences** for specific intentional breaches.

## JOIC

The **Jersey Office of the Information Commissioner** is the
supervisory authority:

- **Independent** office;
- **Investigates** complaints;
- **Conducts** audits;
- **Imposes** penalties;
- **Issues** guidance.
