# DPL 2018 Part 5 — International Transfers

Part 5 governs **transfers of personal data outside Jersey**
— the regime that supports / restricts cross-border data
flows.

## Article 44 — General principle

Transfers of personal data outside Jersey are **prohibited
unless**:

- The destination country has an **adequacy decision**;
- **Appropriate safeguards** are in place;
- A **specific derogation** applies;
- **Other** statutory basis.

## Articles 45-46 — Adequacy

Jersey recognises certain countries as **adequate**:

- **EU / EEA** countries (mutual adequacy);
- **United Kingdom** (post-Brexit adequacy);
- **Specific countries** recognised by Jersey decision
  (typically mirroring EU adequacy decisions);
- **List** maintained by JOIC.

Transfers to adequate countries proceed **without further
safeguards**.

## Articles 47-50 — Safeguards

For non-adequate destinations:

### Standard Contractual Clauses (SCCs)

- **Approved** by JOIC (typically mirroring EU SCCs);
- **Contractual** safeguards between Jersey controller / 
  processor and foreign recipient;
- **Most common** route for non-adequate destinations.

### Binding Corporate Rules (BCRs)

- **Group-wide** data protection policies;
- **Approved** by JOIC;
- **Specific** safeguards and review mechanisms;
- For large international groups.

### Approved codes of conduct

- **Industry codes** with international transfer provisions;
- **Approved** by JOIC.

### Approved certification

- **Specific certification** mechanisms;
- **Approved** by JOIC.

## Articles 51-53 — Specific derogations

Transfers permitted on **specific grounds**:

- **Explicit consent** of data subject (with information
  about risks);
- **Contract necessity**;
- **Public interest**;
- **Legal claims**;
- **Vital interests**;
- **Public register** transfers;
- **Specific compelling legitimate interests** (narrow).

## Article 54 — Onward transfers

Data transferred internationally **further transferred**:

- **Subject to** same safeguards;
- **Contractual** chain through SCCs / BCRs;
- **No degradation** of protection.

## Articles 55-56 — Post-Schrems II considerations

Following EU **Schrems II** decision (2020):

- **SCC** transfers require **assessment** of destination
  country surveillance / access laws;
- **Supplementary measures** may be needed;
- **Jersey** approach mirrors EU practice;
- **EU-US Data Privacy Framework** (2023) relevant for US
  transfers.

## Specific destinations

### Transfers to UK

- UK is **adequate** for Jersey purposes;
- **No additional** safeguards required;
- **Volume** of Jersey-UK data flow significant;
- **Aligned** regulatory regimes.

### Transfers to EU

- EU is **adequate** for Jersey purposes (mutual);
- **No additional** safeguards required.

### Transfers to US

- **Not generally adequate**;
- **SCCs** typically used;
- **EU-US DPF** (Data Privacy Framework) provides adequacy
  for certified US recipients;
- **Jersey** approach typically aligned with EU position.

### Transfers to other offshore jurisdictions

- **Guernsey, IoM** generally adequate;
- **Cayman, BVI, Bermuda** typically not adequate — SCCs
  used;
- **Specific** assessment per destination.

## Practical implications

For Jersey businesses with international operations:

- **Map** all international data flows;
- **Document** legal basis for each flow;
- **SCCs** in place for non-adequate destinations;
- **Transfer impact assessments** where needed;
- **Update** as adequacy decisions evolve.
