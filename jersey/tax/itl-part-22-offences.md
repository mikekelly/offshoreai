# ITL Part 22 — Prosecutions and Penalties

Part 22 (Articles 136–141) governs **criminal offences** for
tax fraud and **penalty provisions** for non-compliance.

## Key offences

### Tax evasion / fraud

- **Deliberate evasion** of tax;
- **False statements** in returns;
- **Concealment** of income;
- **Criminal penalties** including imprisonment.

### Failure to comply

- **Non-filing** of returns;
- **Late filing**;
- **Failure to pay**;
- **Civil financial penalties** plus criminal in serious
  cases.

### Aiding and abetting

- **Persons assisting** in evasion;
- **Professional advisers** in some cases;
- **Family members** complicit;
- **Personal liability**.

### Specific offences

- **Falsifying records**;
- **Obstructing inspectors**;
- **Tipping off** in some categories;
- **Other** specific Part 22 provisions.

## Penalty levels

### Civil financial penalties

- **Scaled** by amount of tax involved and severity;
- **Comptroller** assesses;
- **Appeal** rights;
- **Common** outcome for non-deliberate failures.

### Criminal penalties

- **Magistrate's Court** for lesser offences;
- **Royal Court** for serious;
- **Imprisonment up to 7 years** for some offences;
- **Substantial fines**.

### Specific offences and penalties

- **Tax fraud**: significant imprisonment and fines;
- **False statements**: imprisonment + fines;
- **Failure to file**: typically civil penalty;
- **Concealment**: criminal.

## Comptroller enforcement powers

### Investigation

- **Document production** requirements;
- **Witness examination**;
- **Premises entry** (under Part 22A separately);
- **Specific investigative** powers.

### Settlement

- **Civil-financial-penalty** settlements common for non-
  deliberate;
- **Disclosure programmes** sometimes available;
- **Compounding** of some offences possible.

### Prosecution referral

- **Comptroller** can refer to Attorney General;
- **Criminal** investigation by police;
- **Royal Court** prosecution.

## Common scenarios

### Late filing of tax return

- **Civil penalty** automatic;
- **Standard** outcome;
- **Engagement** with Comptroller for relief in genuine
  hardship.

### Underdeclared income identified on audit

- **Penalty** scaled by amount;
- **Voluntary disclosure** reduces penalty;
- **Deliberate** concealment increases / criminal exposure.

### Failure to register as employer

- **Penalty** for non-registration;
- **Catch-up** ITIS obligations;
- **Compliance** requirement going forward.

### Fraud / serious evasion

- **Criminal investigation** by police;
- **Royal Court** prosecution;
- **Substantial** penalties on conviction.

## Voluntary disclosure

For taxpayers identifying past non-compliance:

- **Disclosure programmes** sometimes published;
- **Reduced penalty** for voluntary disclosure;
- **Specific** procedural requirements;
- **Engage** Jersey tax adviser.

## Practical considerations

- **Compliance discipline** essential;
- **Tax-adviser engagement** where uncertain;
- **Records** maintained for prescribed periods;
- **Voluntary disclosure** preferred over discovery if past
  issues exist.
