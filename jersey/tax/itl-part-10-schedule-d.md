# ITL Part 10 — Schedule D

Part 10 (Articles 61–85H) is the **largest substantive part** of
the Income Tax Law, governing **Schedule D** — the residual
catch-all income classification covering:

- **Trade and profession** (Cases I–II);
- **Employment** (Case III);
- **Foreign income** (Cases IV–V);
- **Annuities, interest, and other** (Case VI);
- **Other** specified cases (VII–IX);
- **Partnerships**;
- **Loss relief**;
- **Specific computational alternatives**.

## Schedule D cases

### Case I — Trade

- **Profits of a trade** in Jersey;
- **Tax-resident trader** taxed on worldwide trade profits;
- **Standard computational rules**;
- **Allowable deductions**.

### Case II — Profession

- **Profession or vocation**;
- **Similar treatment** to trade;
- **Specific profession-related** rules.

### Case III — Employment

- **Salary, wages, bonuses**;
- **Benefits in kind** (Schedule 2);
- **Pension contributions**;
- **Specific employment-tax** rules.

### Cases IV-V — Foreign income

- **Interest, dividends, other** foreign-source investment
  income;
- **Specific computational** rules;
- **DTA / unilateral relief** considerations.

### Case VI — Annuities and other

- **Annuities** and **specified other income**;
- **Catch-all** for items not in other Cases.

### Cases VII-IX — Other

- **Specific** statutory categories;
- **Less commonly invoked**.

## Partnerships

Partnership tax rules:

- **Partnership not taxed** as separate entity;
- **Partners** taxed on their share;
- **Partnership return** allocates;
- **Specific** Jersey rules for LP / LLC partnerships.

## Loss relief

- **Trade losses** can offset against other income;
- **Carry-back** and **carry-forward** mechanics;
- **Specific** loss rules in Part 13;
- **Anti-avoidance** for loss-utilisation.

## Capital allowances

- **Plant and machinery** allowances;
- **Specific Part 12A** treatment;
- **Annual investment** thresholds.

## Practical considerations

For Schedule D income:

- **Classify** income correctly across Cases;
- **Compute** with allowable deductions;
- **File** comprehensive return;
- **Claim** loss relief where applicable.
