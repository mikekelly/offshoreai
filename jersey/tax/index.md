# Tax — Jersey

Jersey has its own tax system, administered by **[Revenue Jersey](revenue-jersey.md)**,
a department within the Treasury and Resources Ministry. The two principal
statutes are the **Income Tax (Jersey) Law 1961** and the
**Goods and Services Tax (Jersey) Law 2007**, both consolidated on
[jerseylaw.je][jl]. ([Revenue Jersey — Companies][rj-co])

A user asking a Jersey-tax question is usually trying to work out one of
three things:

1. **How is a Jersey company taxed?** Almost certainly at **0%** under the
   "zero-ten" regime, but 10% if it falls within a defined list of
   financial-services activities and 20% if it falls within a defined
   list of capped or regulated activities (utilities, large retailers,
   the hydrocarbon trade, the cannabis trade, etc.). See
   [zero-ten.md](zero-ten.md).
2. **How is a Jersey-resident individual taxed?** At a flat standard
   rate of **20%**, capped by **marginal relief** so that a low-income
   resident pays much less in effective terms — but never more than
   20% on total income. See [personal-income-tax.md](personal-income-tax.md).
3. **What other taxes apply?** **GST at 5%** on most goods and services;
   **social-security contributions** and a **Long-Term Care contribution**
   on earnings; **stamp duty** on land transactions and on probate. There
   is **no capital gains tax** and **no inheritance tax**. See the files
   linked below.

## Year of Assessment

Jersey personal and company income tax are charged on a "Year of
Assessment" basis (the **YA**), which is the calendar year. As at the
file's last-verified date (May 2026), the current YA is **2026** and
the most recently completed YA is **2025**. Tax-allowance numbers in this
corpus carry the YA they belong to in the relevant file — see e.g.
[personal-income-tax.md](personal-income-tax.md) for the YA 2025
exemption thresholds.
