# ITL Article 123C — Financial Services 10% Rate

Article 123C of the **[Income Tax (Jersey) Law 1961][itl]**
imposes a **10% rate** on **financial-services companies** —
the principal exception to the standard 0% zero-ten rate.

## What Article 123C covers

A Jersey-tax-resident **financial-services company** is taxed
at **10%** on its profits.

## What is a "financial-services company"

The definition is **carefully drawn**:

- **JFSC-regulated** entity in a specified financial-services
  category;
- **Carrying on** financial-services activity:
  - **Trust company business** (TCB);
  - **Fund services business** (FSB);
  - **Investment business**;
  - **Banking business**;
  - **Insurance** (some categories);
  - **Money services business** (MSB);
  - **General insurance mediation** (GIMB).

For most **regulated entities** in Jersey financial services,
Article 123C applies.

## Why 10% (not 0%)

The 10% rate reflects:

- **Substantial activity** of these businesses in Jersey;
- **Substance proportionate** to the income generated;
- **Foreign-tax authority comfort** that low-tax jurisdictions
  apply some tax to substantive regulated activity;
- **Effectively** brings these businesses' tax burden to a
  modest level while still preserving competitive position.

## Distribution mechanics

Distributions from Article 123C 10%-rate companies:

- **Withholding** applies to Jersey-resident shareholders to
  bring total tax to 20%;
- **Effectively**: 10% at company + 10% at shareholder = 20%
  combined;
- **Non-resident shareholders**: no Jersey withholding.

This contrasts with Article 123A 0%-rate companies where the
Article 81 anti-avoidance regime applies to Jersey-resident
shareholders.

## Compliance

Article 123C companies:

- **Annual corporate tax return** at 10%;
- **Substance compliance** under 2019 Law (full substance test
  typically);
- **JFSC regulatory** obligations;
- **AML / CFT** compliance;
- **Distribution withholding** mechanics.

## Specific scenarios

### Trust company

- **TCB-registered** firm;
- **10% rate** on profits;
- **Distributions** to Jersey-resident owners: withholding
  apply.

### Fund administrator

- **FSB-registered** firm;
- **10% rate**;
- **Distributions** at withholding.

### Bank

- **Banking-permit** Jersey company;
- **10% rate**;
- **Specific** banking-supervision overlays.

### Investment business

- **FS(J)L 1998** investment-business licence;
- **10% rate**;
- **Client-services** specific.

## Why not 0%

A common question: why not extend 0% to financial services?

- **Substance proportionality**: financial-services activity is
  substantive in Jersey;
- **International expectations**: low-tax regimes for
  substantive financial-services activity face scrutiny;
- **Specific BEPS Action 5** considerations historically;
- **Calibrated** between zero (for general companies) and
  20% (for utilities / large retailers).

## Pillar Two for large groups

For large MNE groups (€750m+):

- **Pillar Two MCIT** brings rate to 15%;
- **10% Jersey** + **5% top-up** = 15%;
- **Or** parent-jurisdiction IIR / UTPR.
