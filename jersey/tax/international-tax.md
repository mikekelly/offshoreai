# International Tax — Jersey Overview

Jersey's tax system sits within several **international tax
frameworks**:

- **Double Taxation Agreements (DTAs)** with treaty partners;
- **Tax Information Exchange Agreements (TIEAs)** with non-treaty
  partners;
- **Common Reporting Standard (CRS)** — automatic information
  exchange of financial-account information;
- **FATCA** — US-specific automatic information exchange;
- **OECD BEPS** measures including **Pillar Two**;
- **EU Code of Conduct Group** scrutiny resulting in the economic
  substance regime;
- **Treaty network** through specialised bilateral and multilateral
  agreements.

## DTAs (Double Taxation Agreements)

Jersey has DTAs with selected jurisdictions, the most important
being:

- **UK** — comprehensive DTA covering tax of individuals,
  companies, and specific income types;
- **Guernsey** — DTA between Crown Dependencies;
- **Isle of Man** — DTA between Crown Dependencies;
- **A handful of others** — Cyprus, Malta, Luxembourg, Singapore,
  Hong Kong, etc.

DTAs provide:

- **Tie-breaker rules** for dual-resident individuals and
  companies;
- **Reduced withholding rates** on cross-border income;
- **Mutual agreement procedures** for tax disputes;
- **Information exchange** on request.

Jersey's DTA network is **smaller** than that of major
jurisdictions — many international tax questions involving
Jersey are resolved through TIEAs and the OECD-MLI framework
rather than full DTAs.

## TIEAs (Tax Information Exchange Agreements)

Jersey has TIEAs with most major economies — over 30 in total.
TIEAs provide:

- **Information exchange on request** between tax authorities;
- **No reduced withholding** (unlike DTAs);
- **Specific case-by-case** information requests rather than
  automatic exchange.

TIEAs were a major part of Jersey's response to **early-2000s
international pressure** on offshore finance centres and helped
Jersey transition from "uncooperative" to "white-list" status.

## CRS

The **Common Reporting Standard** is the **OECD-driven framework**
for **automatic exchange of financial-account information**
between participating jurisdictions.

Jersey participates fully as a **reporting jurisdiction**.
Jersey financial institutions (banks, trust companies, fund
administrators, insurers) classify their accounts and report
non-Jersey-resident account holders to Revenue Jersey, which
forwards the data to relevant foreign authorities.

See [`../international/crs.md`](../international/crs.md) for
detail.

## FATCA

The **US Foreign Account Tax Compliance Act** is Jersey-relevant
through an **intergovernmental agreement** (IGA) between Jersey
and the US.

Jersey financial institutions identify and report **US-person**
account holders to Revenue Jersey, which forwards to the US IRS.

See [`../international/fatca.md`](../international/fatca.md) for
detail.

## OECD BEPS and Pillar Two

Jersey has implemented:

- **Economic substance** rules (BEPS Action 5);
- **Country-by-country reporting** for MNE groups (BEPS Action
  13);
- **MLI provisions** modifying DTAs (BEPS Action 15);
- **Pillar Two** minimum tax (effective 2025) — see
  [`pillar-two.md`](pillar-two.md).

## EU lists

Jersey has historically been on / off the EU's **list of
non-cooperative jurisdictions**. The Jersey policy response
(substance, CRS, etc.) has been driven significantly by EU Code
of Conduct Group expectations.

As at this writing, Jersey is **on the EU's white-list** /
cooperative-jurisdiction status.
