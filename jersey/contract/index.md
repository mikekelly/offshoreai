# Contract and Obligations Law — Jersey

## What this section is about

This section covers **Jersey contract and obligations law** —
the rules governing legally enforceable agreements and other
sources of obligation.

Jersey contract law is **distinctive**:

- **Customary-law** roots (Norman law, Pothier);
- **Civil-law** influences (cause, performance);
- **Common-law** practical convergence (much of commercial
  practice mirrors English);
- **Pothier** routinely cited in Jersey Royal Court;
- **Jersey-specific** doctrines preserved.

## Sources of obligations

Following Pothier and the Norman tradition, Jersey recognises
**multiple sources of obligation**:

1. **Contract** (convention);
2. **Quasi-contract** (no agreement but legally implied
   obligation — e.g., negotiorum gestio, unjust enrichment);
3. **Délit** (tort / civil wrong);
4. **Quasi-délit** (similar, less intentional);
5. **Statute** (modern statutory duties).
