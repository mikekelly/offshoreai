# Performance and Breach in Jersey Contract Law

## Performance

### Standard

A party must perform:

- **As specified** in the contract;
- **In good faith** (specific Jersey view);
- **With reasonable care** where applicable;
- **By the agreed date** or within reasonable time;
- **At the agreed place**;
- **In the agreed manner**.

### Tender

**Tender of performance** by an obligor:

- **Genuine** attempt to perform;
- **Communicated** to the obligee;
- **At the appropriate time and place**;
- **If refused** wrongfully — discharges obligor from liability
  for delay.

### Delivery and payment

Specific Jersey rules on:

- **Delivery** of goods (place, time, condition);
- **Payment** (means, currency, timing);
- **Coordination** under sale of goods principles.

### Good faith

Jersey law recognises a **duty of good faith** in performance:

- **Civil-law** influence;
- **Specific** Jersey case-law application;
- **Broader** than typical English law approach (which has
  good-faith elements but not a general duty);
- **Specific** application in commercial contexts.

## Breach

A **breach** occurs when a party fails to perform their
contractual obligation:

### Types of breach

#### Repudiatory breach

- **Fundamental** breach going to the root of the contract;
- **Innocent party** can terminate and claim damages;
- **Or affirm** and claim damages while continuing;
- **Examples**: complete non-performance, defective major
  performance, anticipatory breach with clear repudiation.

#### Breach of warranty

- **Less fundamental** breach;
- **Damages** but no termination right;
- **Examples**: minor delays, minor defects.

#### Anticipatory breach

- **Pre-performance** clear repudiation;
- **Innocent party** can accept the breach and terminate
  immediately;
- **Or affirm** and wait for performance.

#### Innominate / intermediate terms

- **Neither** strictly conditions nor warranties;
- **Effect of breach** determined by **seriousness** of
  consequences;
- **Subject to** *Hong Kong Fir Shipping* approach
  (persuasive in Jersey).

## Remedies for breach

### Damages

- **Compensatory** principle;
- **Expectation** loss typically (put claimant in position as
  if contract performed);
- **Reliance** loss in some cases;
- **Restitution** in unjust enrichment cases;
- **Specific** Jersey rules;
- See [`damages.md`](damages.md).

### Specific performance

- **Broader** in Jersey than English equity;
- **Available** for specific categories;
- **Subject to** practicability and supervision concerns;
- See [`specific-performance.md`](specific-performance.md).

### Termination

- **Right to terminate** for repudiatory breach;
- **Notice** required;
- **Specific** Jersey procedural rules;
- **Affirmation** can preclude.

### Injunction

- **Restraining** specific conduct;
- **Negative covenants**;
- **Specific** Jersey rules.

### Account

- **Account of profits** in some cases;
- **Specific** Jersey rules on availability.

## Defences

To a claim for breach:

### Performance

- **Already performed** the obligation;
- **Tendered** performance which was refused.

### Discharge by agreement

- **Variation** of contract;
- **Waiver** of breach;
- **Release** by the innocent party.

### Frustration

- **Specific** unforeseen events;
- **Performance impossible** or radically different;
- **Specific** Jersey rules.

### Estoppel

- **Innocent party's conduct** precludes claim;
- **Specific** Jersey rules.

### Limitation

- **Statutory** limitation periods;
- **Specific** Jersey rules (typically 6 years for contract,
  10 years for some specific cases).

## Frustration

A contract is **frustrated** when:

- **Supervening event** without fault of either party;
- **Performance** becomes **impossible** or **radically
  different**;
- **Not** mere increased difficulty / cost;
- **Examples**: physical destruction of subject matter, legal
  impossibility, supervening illegality.

### Effect

- **Contract** discharged from the moment of frustration;
- **Specific** Jersey rules on adjustments and restitution;
- **Frustrated Contracts (Jersey) Law** addresses specific
  apportionment.
