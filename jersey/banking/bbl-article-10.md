# BBL 1991 Article 10 — Continuing Obligations

Article 10 governs the **continuing obligations** of a
registered bank — the post-registration prudential and
operational requirements.

## The provision

A registered bank must, on an ongoing basis:

- **Maintain** the registration conditions (Article 6
  standards continuing);
- **Hold** adequate capital and liquidity;
- **Maintain** systems and controls;
- **Notify** the JFSC of material changes;
- **Submit** prescribed returns (financial, prudential,
  operational);
- **Cooperate** with JFSC supervision;
- **Conduct** business in a manner consistent with public
  trust.

## Capital and liquidity

Ongoing compliance with:

- **Capital ratios** — CET1, Tier 1, total capital per Basel
  III / JFSC implementing rules;
- **Buffer requirements** — capital conservation buffer,
  counter-cyclical buffer, systemic buffer (where applicable);
- **Liquidity coverage** ratio (LCR);
- **Net stable funding** ratio (NSFR);
- **Internal capital adequacy** assessment (ICAAP);
- **Internal liquidity adequacy** assessment (ILAAP).

## Concentration and exposure

- **Large exposure** limits (typically 25% of capital for a
  single counterparty);
- **Connected counterparty** aggregation;
- **Sectoral** concentrations;
- **Geographic** concentrations.

## Reporting

A registered bank must submit:

- **Quarterly** capital and liquidity returns;
- **Annual audited** financial statements;
- **ICAAP** and **ILAAP** documents;
- **Resolution plans** (subject to thresholds);
- **Material change** notifications;
- **Adverse event** reports (significant losses, IT outages,
  regulatory action).

## Material change notifications

The bank must notify the JFSC of:

- **Changes** in ownership (controllers above thresholds);
- **Changes** in principal persons (directors, senior
  officers, MLRO);
- **Significant** restructuring;
- **Material** litigation;
- **Adverse** regulatory action by home regulator;
- **Material** breaches of internal limits;
- **IT** outages or cyber-events of materiality;
- **Other** events affecting fit-and-proper or prudential
  position.

## Public trust standard

A registered bank must conduct business:

- In a **fit-and-proper** manner;
- With **integrity**;
- With **due skill, care, and diligence**;
- With **adequate resources**;
- With **proper market conduct**;
- With **regard** for customers' interests.

This standard supports JFSC supervisory action even where
specific rules are not breached but conduct falls short.

## Consequences of breach

Failure to comply with Article 10:

- **Civil penalties** under Part 6;
- **Supervisory measures** (additional capital, restrictions,
  intensified supervision);
- **Public statements** by the JFSC;
- **Suspension / revocation** of registration in serious
  cases;
- **Criminal** liability for specified breaches.
