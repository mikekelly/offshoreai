# BBL 1991 Article 24 — Depositor Compensation Scheme

Article 24 provides for the **Jersey Bank Depositors
Compensation Scheme** — protection for retail depositors if
a bank fails.

## Coverage

The Scheme:

- Covers **eligible deposits** up to **£50,000 per depositor
  per bank** (current threshold);
- **Funded** by levy on participating banks;
- **Pay-outs** within **7 days** of failure;
- **Administered** by the States Treasury with JFSC
  involvement.

## Eligible deposits

Covered deposits typically include:

- **Bank account** deposits;
- **Specific** retail / small business deposits;
- **Subject to** £50,000 per-depositor cap.

## Excluded deposits

Generally excluded:

- **Wholesale** deposits above retail thresholds;
- **Specific** institutional deposits;
- **Specific** other carve-outs.

## Funding

- **Levy** on participating banks;
- **Pre-funding** typically maintained;
- **Ex-post** levy in crisis if needed;
- **Specific** Jersey government backstop in some
  configurations.

## Comparison with UK / EU schemes

| Scheme | Coverage | Specific features |
|---|---|---|
| Jersey | £50,000 per depositor per bank | Specific Jersey scheme |
| UK FSCS | £85,000 per depositor per bank | UK-wide |
| EU DGS | €100,000 per depositor per bank | EU harmonised |

## Trigger

The scheme pays out when:

- A **bank fails** (insolvency / resolution);
- **JFSC determines** the scheme should be triggered;
- **Specific** procedural rules;
- **Specific** payment mechanism.

## Practical implementation

- **JBA (Jersey Bankers Association)** participates in
  scheme governance;
- **Specific** Jersey government oversight;
- **Specific** depositor information requirements
  (transparency to customers);
- **Specific** branch / subsidiary considerations.
