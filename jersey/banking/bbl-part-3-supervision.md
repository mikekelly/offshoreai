# BBL 1991 Part 3 — Supervision

Article 10 (continuing obligations) has its own deep file.
This cluster covers Articles 11-13.

## Article 11 — Information requirements

The JFSC may require a registered bank to:

- **Provide information** about its business;
- **Produce documents** in its possession;
- **Provide explanations** of its records or transactions;
- **Confirm** specific facts;
- **Within** a specified timeframe.

Failure to comply is an **offence** and grounds for
**supervisory action**.

The information powers can be exercised:

- **Routinely** as part of supervision;
- **Specifically** in response to concerns;
- **In support** of investigations under Article 12.

## Article 12 — On-site inspections

The JFSC may **inspect** a registered bank's premises and
records:

- With **reasonable notice** (typically months for routine
  inspection, immediate for cause);
- **Without notice** in some cases (e.g., suspected
  misconduct);
- **Through delegated** auditors / consultants in some cases;
- With **access** to all premises, records, staff, IT
  systems.

Inspections include:

- **Routine** prudential / conduct review (annual / biennial
  for most banks);
- **Themed** reviews (specific risk areas across sector);
- **Cause-based** (specific concerns about an institution);
- **Joint** with home regulator for groups.

The bank must:

- **Cooperate fully**;
- **Provide** requested access;
- **Make staff available**;
- **Implement** remedial actions identified.

## Article 13 — Directions

The JFSC may issue **directions** to a registered bank:

- To **cease** specified activities;
- To **take** specified steps;
- To **limit** specified exposures;
- To **strengthen** specific arrangements (capital, liquidity,
  controls);
- To **provide** specified information.

Directions:

- Are **enforceable** through Court order or supervisory
  escalation;
- May be **public** in some cases;
- Are **subject to appeal** to the Royal Court;
- May be **temporary** or **permanent**.

## Supervisory cooperation

The JFSC cooperates with:

- **Home regulators** of group banks (e.g., PRA, ECB, FRB);
- **Other JFSC divisions** (TCB, investment business);
- **FIU** for AML matters;
- **Bank of England** for resolution coordination;
- **Foreign FIUs** through Egmont and treaty channels;
- **EBA / FSB** through colleges of supervisors for global
  groups.
