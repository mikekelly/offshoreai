# BBL 1991 Part 7 — Miscellaneous

Part 7 contains **administrative architecture** — regulations,
forms, fees, public registers, citation.

## Regulations

The Minister may make **regulations** under the Law to:

- **Prescribe** capital, liquidity, and prudential ratios;
- **Specify** application forms and procedures;
- **Define** terms ambiguously stated in the Law;
- **Implement** international standards (Basel III, FSB);
- **Address** specific risks (e.g., crypto, e-money interface);
- **Update** the regime in response to JFSC needs and policy.

## Codes of Practice

The JFSC issues **Codes of Practice** for banking:

- **Code of Practice for Deposit Takers** — the principal
  Code;
- Implementing **prudential** rules in detail;
- Addressing **conduct** and **operational** expectations;
- Referenced in supervisory action — treated as evidence of
  expected standard.

## Forms and fees

The JFSC publishes:

- **Application forms** (registration, variation, principal-
  person changes);
- **Reporting forms** (capital, liquidity, large exposures);
- **Fee schedules** (application, annual, transaction fees).

Fees are typically reviewed **annually** by the JFSC and
Minister.

## Public register

A **public register** of registered banks is maintained:

- **Name** and trading name;
- **Status** (active, suspended);
- **Conditions** of registration (where public);
- **Date** of registration.

The register is accessible through the JFSC website and
supports public confidence in identifying licensed
institutions.

## Confidentiality

A complex balance:

- The **JFSC** holds **confidential** supervisory information;
- **Information sharing** with home regulators is provided
  for;
- **Public information** is published;
- **Whistleblower** information is protected.

## International cooperation

The Law authorises:

- **Memoranda of understanding** with foreign regulators;
- **Information sharing** under safeguards;
- **Inspections** by home regulators (with JFSC oversight);
- **Joint supervisory** activities;
- **College of supervisors** participation.

## Citation and commencement

The Law is cited as the **"Banking Business (Jersey) Law
1991"**.

It came into force on **1 January 1991** and has been amended
many times, including:

- **2014** — substantial revision aligning with Basel III;
- **2018** — implementation of recovery / resolution regime;
- **2020s** — alignment with FATF and JFSC supervisory
  enhancements.
