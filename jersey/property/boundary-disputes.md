# Boundary Disputes in Jersey

## The question

Two adjoining Jersey landowners disagree about the boundary
between their properties. What's the legal framework, what
evidence matters, and how are disputes resolved?

## Sources

Jersey boundary law sits across:

- **Customary law** (Norman-derived rules);
- **Title deeds** (contrats passed in Royal Court);
- **Public Registry** records;
- **Boundary statutes** (specific Loi provisions);
- **Modern surveying** (cadastral records, drawings).

## Title deeds as primary evidence

The starting point is the **chain of title**:

- **Original** contrat passed in Royal Court;
- **Verbal** descriptions ("from the corner of X to the
  hedge at Y...");
- **Specific** Norman terminology preserved;
- **Plans** attached in modern conveyancing.

## Verbal descriptions

Historical Jersey deeds typically describe boundaries by:

- **Natural features** (hedges, walls, watercourses);
- **Adjacent owners** ("bordering on the lands of...");
- **Roads / paths**;
- **Specific Norman** units of measurement (vergée, perche).

## Plans and surveys

Modern deeds increasingly include plans:

- **OS-style** maps;
- **Cadastral** references;
- **Specific** Royal Court practice on plan attachment;
- **Discrepancies** between verbal description and plan —
  customary rules.

## Vue de Vicomte

A **traditional Jersey procedure** for boundary disputes:

- **Application** to the Royal Court;
- **Vicomte** (or Vicomte's officer) inspects;
- **Specific** historical procedure;
- **Modern** Royal Court process via Order of Justice or
  Representation now usual;
- **Survey evidence** typically presented.

## Adverse possession (prescription)

Jersey recognises **prescription** for real property:

- **Long, continuous, uninterrupted** possession;
- **Specific** statutory periods (40 years for some
  contexts, 10 years for others);
- **Specific** customary-law rules;
- **Distinguished from** English adverse possession.

## Acquiescence and estoppel

Where one party has acted on the basis of a boundary
position:

- **Acquiescence** by the other party may be relevant;
- **Estoppel-equivalent** doctrines applied;
- **Specific** Jersey case law on conduct-based boundary
  determination;
- **Pothier-aligned** good-faith considerations.

## Hedges, walls, and fences

Specific Jersey rules govern boundary features:

- **Bornes** (boundary markers) — formal markers;
- **Murs mitoyens** (party walls) — shared ownership;
- **Hedges** — specific Jersey customary rules on ownership;
- **Trees** — specific rules on overhanging branches.

## Watercourses

For streams and ditches:

- **Mid-line** of watercourse typically the boundary;
- **Specific** Jersey rules on alteration;
- **Servitudes** of water flow / drainage;
- **Specific** statutory and customary provisions.

## Specific causes of dispute

### Encroachment

- **One party's structure** crosses the boundary;
- **Remedies** — injunction, damages, specific performance;
- **Royal Court** procedure;
- **Specific** Jersey case law on de minimis encroachments.

### Misalignment of plans vs deeds

- **Verbal description** typically prevails over plan in
  older deeds;
- **Modern** practice increasingly plan-prevailing;
- **Specific** Jersey case law on construction.

### Hedge / wall position

- **Customary rules** govern responsibility;
- **Specific** Jersey rules on maintenance;
- **Conduct-based** evidence.

### Overhanging trees

- **Right of self-help** (cutting back to boundary);
- **Specific** Jersey rules on neighbour cooperation;
- **Servitude** issues.

## Evidence in disputes

Typical evidence in a Jersey boundary dispute:

- **Title deeds** (chain of title);
- **Plans** (historic and modern);
- **Surveyor** reports;
- **Photographs** (historic and current);
- **Neighbour testimony** (long-standing knowledge);
- **Public Registry** searches;
- **Specific** Jersey expert evidence.

## Procedure

Modern disputes typically proceed by:

- **Initial** correspondence and surveyor input;
- **Mediation** (often successful);
- **Order of Justice** if litigation needed;
- **Royal Court** civil procedure (Royal Court Rules);
- **Surveyor** expert evidence;
- **Site visit** by Jurats / judge;
- **Specific** Jersey procedural requirements.

## Remedies

- **Declaration** of boundary position;
- **Injunction** against encroachment;
- **Damages** for trespass / nuisance;
- **Specific performance** for boundary rectification;
- **Costs** orders (often substantial in boundary cases);
- **Specific** Jersey case law.

## Voisinage

The Jersey customary doctrine of **voisinage** governs
neighbour relations:

- **Reasonable use** of property;
- **No unreasonable** interference with neighbour;
- **Norman customary** origin;
- **Specific** Jersey case law;
- **Distinguishable from** English nuisance.

## Pitfalls

- **Relying** solely on modern plans without title-deed
  review;
- **Missing** customary-law rules on specific features;
- **Failing** to engage surveyor early;
- **Misunderstanding** prescription periods;
- **Underestimating** litigation costs;
- **Ignoring** mediation as cost-effective first step.

## Practical recommendations

- **Title search** through chain back to original passing;
- **Survey** by Jersey-experienced surveyor;
- **Photograph** boundary features;
- **Document** neighbour communications;
- **Mediate** before litigating where possible;
- **Engage** Jersey advocate from outset.
