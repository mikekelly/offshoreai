# CIFL 1988 Article 3 — Functionaries

Article 3 defines the **functionaries** that serve a fund —
the **service providers** delivering specific functions in
fund operation.

## The provision

A **functionary** in relation to a fund is:

- The **manager** of the fund;
- The **trustee** (for unit-trust funds) or **directors**
  (for company funds);
- The **custodian** of fund assets;
- The **administrator** providing fund administration;
- The **investment manager** (if separate from the
  manager);
- Any **other defined role** prescribed by the Minister.

## Functionary roles

### Manager / general partner / corporate director

- **Operational responsibility** for the fund;
- **Strategic decisions** on the fund's behalf;
- **Liaison** with regulators and investors;
- **Approval** of material matters.

### Trustee (for unit trust funds)

- **Holds** scheme property in trust for unit-holders;
- **Custody** and asset-protection function;
- **Oversight** of manager (limited fiduciary role to
  unit-holders).

### Investment manager

- **Investment decisions** within the scheme's mandate;
- **Portfolio** construction and trading;
- **Discretionary** authority typically;
- Often separate from the manager.

### Custodian / depositary

- **Safe-keeping** of fund assets;
- **Settlement** of trades;
- **Cash management**;
- For AIFMD-compliant funds, depositary has a broader
  oversight role.

### Administrator

- **NAV calculation**;
- **Investor register** maintenance;
- **Subscription / redemption** processing;
- **Reporting** to investors;
- **AML / CFT** on investors (typically).

### Auditor

- **Annual financial audit**;
- Independent from manager and administrator.

## Functionary permits

Each functionary providing services from Jersey:

- Must hold a **permit** under Article 7 (for CIFL public
  funds); or
- Must be registered for **fund services business (FSB)**
  under the **Financial Services (Jersey) Law 1998** (for
  non-public funds and for many functionaries generally).

## Fit and proper

Functionaries must be:

- **Fit and proper** entities;
- With **fit and proper** principals;
- **JFSC-supervised** and rated for the role;
- **Adequately resourced** for the function;
- **Independent** as required (auditor, depositary).

## Common configurations

### Self-managed corporate fund

- The **board** is the manager;
- An **investment manager** is engaged;
- A **custodian** and **administrator** are engaged;
- An **auditor** is engaged;
- All are functionaries with their own permit / FSB
  registration.

### Externally-managed AIF

- A separate **AIFM** is the manager;
- The fund **board** is non-executive;
- **Investment manager / advisor** sub-delegated.

### Master-feeder

- The **master** fund has functionaries;
- Each **feeder** also has functionaries;
- Coordinated across the structure.
