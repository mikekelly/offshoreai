# CIFL 1988 Article 4 — Certificate Required for Public Fund

Article 4 is the **operative prohibition** for public funds.

## The provision

A person shall not establish, operate, or promote in Jersey a
**public collective investment fund** unless the fund holds a
**certificate** under the Law.

## "Public" fund

A fund is **public** if it is:

- **Marketed to the public**;
- **Promoted** to retail / individual investors generally;
- **Offered** through public-facing channels (not by
  invitation only to defined categories).

A fund is **not public** (and so outside Article 4 / requires a
different regime) if it is:

- Restricted to **expert investors** (Expert Fund);
- Restricted to **eligible investors** (Eligible Investor
  Fund);
- Restricted to a **closed circle** of 50 or fewer investors
  (Jersey Private Fund);
- **Exchange-listed** within the Listed Fund category;
- **Unregulated** under specific Unregulated Fund regimes
  (UIEF, UIETF).

## What "establish, operate, promote" covers

The prohibition is broad:

- **Establish** — set up the fund structure;
- **Operate** — manage, administer the fund's affairs;
- **Promote** — solicit investment.

Each of these acts triggers the certificate requirement if
done in Jersey for a public fund.

## Penalty

Operating a public fund without a CIFL certificate:

- Is a **criminal offence**;
- Carries **substantial fines**;
- Carries **imprisonment** up to **7 years** for individuals;
- Triggers **investor protection** issues (subscription
  contracts may be voidable).

## Application

Application is made under **Article 5** with:

- Fund **constitutional documents** (articles, trust deed, LP
  agreement);
- **Prospectus** (offer document);
- **Manager** and other **functionary** details;
- **Investment policy** and restrictions;
- **Fee structures**;
- **Risk warnings** and **disclosures**;
- **Audit** arrangements;
- **AML / CFT** procedures;
- **Fees** prescribed.

## Substantive grant (Article 6)

The JFSC issues a certificate if satisfied:

- **Functionaries** are appropriately permitted;
- **Constitutional documents** comply with the Law;
- **Investment policy** is appropriate for the fund type;
- **Investor protection** measures are adequate;
- The fund is **in the public interest**.

## Distinguishing certificate vs permit

Two different authorisations:

- **Certificate** under Article 4 — authorises the **fund**
  itself to be a public collective investment fund;
- **Permit** under Article 7 — authorises **functionaries** to
  serve funds.

A public fund needs **both**:

- A CIFL **certificate**;
- Each functionary with an appropriate **permit** (or FSB
  registration under FSL 1998).
