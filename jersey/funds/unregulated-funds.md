# Unregulated Funds — Jersey

The **Unregulated Fund** category permits certain Jersey fund
vehicles to operate **without JFSC fund-level authorisation** —
relying entirely on the regulation of their **service providers**
and on **investor sophistication / eligibility**.

## Categories

Two principal subcategories:

- **Unregulated Eligible Investor Fund** — for eligible investors
  only (high-net-worth / institutional);
- **Unregulated Exchange Traded Fund** — for funds listed on a
  recognised exchange.

## Headline features

- **No JFSC fund-level permit** — light-touch from the fund's
  perspective;
- **JFSC-regulated administrator** still required;
- **No retail distribution** — the regime relies on investor
  qualification as a substitute for fund-level supervision;
- **No prospectus review** by the JFSC;
- **Most flexibility** in fund operations.

## When to use

Unregulated Funds are used for:

- **Smaller / niche** strategies where regulatory cost would be
  disproportionate;
- **Single-family-office vehicles**;
- **Fund-of-funds and feeder structures**;
- **Vehicles requiring maximum operational flexibility**.

## Comparison with JPF

The JPF and Unregulated Eligible Investor Fund overlap. Choice
depends on:

- **Speed** of authorisation (Unregulated is faster — no JFSC
  filing);
- **Investor cap** (JPF has 50; Unregulated has investor-eligibility
  cap);
- **Investor preference** for some JFSC overlay (some prefer
  JPF for that signal);
- **Marketing strategy**.

Trends have favoured the **JPF** over Unregulated Funds for many
typical use cases, but Unregulated Funds remain relevant for
specific scenarios.
