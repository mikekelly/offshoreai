# Jersey Court of Appeal

The **Jersey Court of Appeal** is the **intermediate appellate
court** for Jersey, hearing appeals from the Royal Court and
sending its own decisions onwards (with leave) to the Privy
Council.

## Composition

The Court of Appeal sits in panels of (typically) **three
judges** drawn from a roster of:

- Senior **English, Welsh, Scottish, and Northern Irish jurists**
  appointed as Judges of the Jersey Court of Appeal;
- Frequently **King's Counsel** and former High Court / Court of
  Appeal judges;
- A **Jersey-resident President** of the Court of Appeal.

The use of senior **non-Jersey jurists** brings substantial UK
legal expertise to bear on Jersey appeals.

## Jurisdiction

The Court of Appeal hears:

- **Civil appeals** from the Royal Court Samedi Division;
- **Criminal appeals** from the Royal Court Inferior Number and
  Superior Number;
- **Specific statutory** appeals where Laws designate the Court
  of Appeal as appellate forum;
- **Both questions of law and questions of fact** (with some
  restrictions on fact-appeals);
- **Discretion appeals** (subject to limitations).

## Procedure

Appeals are usually:

- **On the record** — based on Royal Court evidence and findings;
- **Argued in writing** plus **oral hearing**;
- **Heard in Jersey** at the Royal Court building;
- Disposed of by **written judgment** delivered after hearing.

## Where to find judgments

Jersey Court of Appeal judgments are published on **jerseylaw.je**
and have **binding precedent** value for the Royal Court.
