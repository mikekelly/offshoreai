# Bailiff of Jersey

The **Bailiff of Jersey** is the **senior judicial officer** of
the Island — presiding over the Royal Court and the States
Assembly as its civic and constitutional head.

## Roles

The Bailiff combines several functions:

- **Senior judge** — presides over the most significant Royal
  Court hearings; **judge of law** alongside the lay Jurats who
  are judges of fact;
- **President of the States Assembly** — civic head presiding
  over the parliamentary chamber (a distinctive Jersey
  arrangement keeping legislative-judicial roles connected);
- **Civic head** of the Island — representing Jersey
  ceremonially and constitutionally;
- **Returning Officer** for Jersey elections;
- **Head of Bailiff's Chambers** — the office supporting the
  Bailiff's functions.

The **Deputy Bailiff** assists and substitutes for the Bailiff.
**Commissioners** sit as judges of the Royal Court when needed.

## Appointment and tenure

- Appointed by the **Crown** on the recommendation of the
  Lieutenant-Governor and Chief Minister;
- **Senior Jersey lawyer** by background;
- **Tenure** is during good behaviour up to a retirement age.

## In practice

A practitioner appearing before the Royal Court typically faces:

- The **Bailiff** or **Deputy Bailiff** as presiding judge of
  law;
- **Two Jurats** as judges of fact (or more for Superior Number
  matters);
- A **Commissioner** in some cases instead of the Bailiff.
