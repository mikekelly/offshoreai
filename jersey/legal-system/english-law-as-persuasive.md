# English Law as Persuasive Authority in Jersey

## The basic position

**English law is not binding** on Jersey courts but is
**highly persuasive**, especially in:

- **Modern commercial** law areas;
- **Trust** law;
- **Company** law;
- **Banking** and **finance**;
- **Contract** (where Pothier hasn't departed);
- **Procedural** matters;
- **Statutory** interpretation principles.

In contrast, **Jersey customary law** is binding (where
applicable), as are:

- **Jersey statutes**;
- **Jersey case law** (Royal Court, Court of Appeal);
- **Privy Council decisions in Jersey appeals** (binding).

## Why English law is so influential

Several reasons:

### Constitutional connection

- Jersey is a **Crown Dependency**;
- **Privy Council** is Jersey's final court;
- **Privy Council** is staffed by English / UK Supreme Court
  judges;
- **Long-standing** legal connection.

### Cross-border commerce

- Jersey is **deeply integrated** with UK financial markets;
- **Jersey firms** operate within UK / Crown legal traditions;
- **Most Jersey lawyers** trained in England and Wales (or
  similar common-law jurisdictions);
- **Practical** common-law approach.

### Common law foundations

- **Trust law** developed in English equity, then transplanted;
- **Company law** structurally English-derived;
- **Banking** practices English-derived.

### Modernisation

- **Modern statutes** in Jersey often mirror UK developments;
- **Industry codes** track UK best practice;
- **Specific** regulatory alignment.

## When English law is most persuasive

### Trust law

- **Jersey Trusts Law 1984** broadly aligned with English
  trust concepts;
- **English cases** on settlor / trustee / beneficiary
  routinely cited;
- **Specific** Jersey divergences (Article 47 mistake is
  wider than English *Pitt v Holt*);
- **Pre-Trusts Law 1984** Jersey trust law was largely
  English-derived.

### Company law

- **Companies Law 1991** broadly aligned with English company
  law;
- **English case law** on director duties, capital
  maintenance, minority protection cited routinely;
- **Specific** Jersey provisions (par-value / no-par-value
  flexibility) diverge.

### Commercial law

- **Sale of goods**;
- **Agency**;
- **Banking**;
- **Insurance** (alongside Jersey-specific insurance law);
- **Specific** financial-services contexts.

### Procedure

- **Royal Court Rules** often track English Civil Procedure
  Rules;
- **Specific** Jersey procedural variations;
- **Substantive** procedural principles broadly aligned.

## When English law is less persuasive

### Property law

- **Norman customary** roots;
- **Hypothec** / **rentes** unique to Jersey;
- **Specific** customary rules for inheritance.

### Succession

- **Légitime** is statutory in Jersey, discretionary
  family-provision in England;
- **Specific** customary rules for real property.

### Contract

- **Pothier-derived** elements;
- **Cause** doctrine (contested);
- **Specific** civil-law-flavoured aspects.

### Specifically Jersey statutes

- **Substantive** Jersey statute always trumps English;
- **English** cases on similar UK statutes may inform
  interpretation but cannot override.

## How English law is cited

In Jersey Royal Court:

- **UK Supreme Court** / **House of Lords** decisions
  generally highest weight;
- **Court of Appeal (England and Wales)** highly persuasive;
- **High Court** decisions persuasive but second-tier;
- **First instance** judgments influential where in
  point.

## Jersey divergence from English law

Specific notable Jersey divergences:

### Trust law

- **Article 47 mistake** wider than *Pitt v Holt*;
- **Article 9 firewall** robust against foreign forced-
  heirship in ways English law doesn't address;
- **Schmidt v Rosewood** principles applied with Jersey
  statutory overlay.

### Company law

- **No-par-value shares** possible (unlike pre-2025 England);
- **Specific** Jersey financial-assistance rules;
- **Cell companies** (PCC, ICC) distinct Jersey features.

### Tax

- **Zero-ten** regime distinct;
- **No** capital gains tax;
- **No** general inheritance tax.

### Bankruptcy / insolvency

- **Désastre** distinct from English bankruptcy /
  liquidation;
- **Specific** Jersey avoidance rules.

## Practical guidance

For practitioners:

- **Always start** with applicable Jersey statute;
- **Then** check Jersey case law (Royal Court, Court of
  Appeal, Privy Council);
- **Then** check Jersey customary law if relevant;
- **Then** consider English authority as persuasive;
- **Be alert** for conscious Jersey divergences;
- **Don't assume** English answer is Jersey answer.

For foreign lawyers approaching Jersey law:

- **Treat** Jersey law as **its own jurisdiction**;
- **Engage** Jersey counsel for opinions;
- **Don't extrapolate** from English law unilaterally;
- **Recognise** Jersey distinctive features.
