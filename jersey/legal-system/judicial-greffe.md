# Judicial Greffe — Jersey

The **Judicial Greffe** is the **court registry and
administrative office** of the Royal Court — the operational
backbone of Jersey's civil and criminal justice system.

## Functions

The Judicial Greffe:

- **Receives** court filings (proceedings, applications, appeals);
- **Records** Royal Court orders and judgments;
- **Maintains** the **Public Registry** of immovable-property
  contracts (passations);
- **Administers** probate of Jersey-resident estates;
- **Manages** court listings and hearing schedules;
- **Supports** the Bailiff, Deputy Bailiff, Jurats, and other
  judicial officers operationally.

## Head: The Judicial Greffier

The **Judicial Greffier** is the head of the office — a
**senior court officer** of long standing in Jersey constitutional
tradition.

## Why it matters

For practitioners and litigants:

- **All court filings** route through the Greffe;
- **Probate** applications are submitted to the Greffe;
- **Public Registry** records of land transactions are kept by
  the Greffe;
- **Judgment / order** copies are obtained from the Greffe.
