# Companies — Jersey

This section covers Jersey corporate vehicles: ordinary companies, cell
companies, limited liability partnerships, and the four kinds of
partnership (general, limited, separate limited, incorporated limited).
The headline statute is the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]**, with
companion Laws for the partnership and LLP vehicles. ([JFSC — All
legislation][jfsc-legis])

For the tax treatment of a Jersey company, see
[tax/zero-ten.md](../tax/zero-ten.md). For the regulatory question of
whether the company needs JFSC consent before doing business, see
[financial-regulation/index.md](../financial-regulation/index.md). For
the **economic-substance** test applicable to certain
Jersey-tax-resident companies, see
[tax/economic-substance.md](../tax/economic-substance.md).

## How a typical Jersey company comes into existence

A short walk-through, to anchor the section before the detail files:

1. **Choose a vehicle.** Almost always a private company limited by
   shares under the Companies Law 1991. Use of LP, LLP, cell company,
   foundation or trust is driven by the commercial purpose — see
   [types-of-company.md](types-of-company.md).
2. **Engage a Jersey administered-service provider.** A Jersey
   regulated trust company business (see
   [financial-regulation/index.md](../financial-regulation/index.md))
   acts as registered agent / company secretary and is in practice
   required for incorporation.
3. **Obtain consent to incorporate.** A consent under the Control of
   Borrowing (Jersey) Order 1958 is required from the Registrar of
   Companies (a function exercised within the JFSC). The application
   includes prospective shareholder, director, and beneficial-owner
   details.
4. **Incorporate.** Standard service is 24 hours; a 2-hour fast-track
   is available on payment of the higher fee. See
   [incorporation.md](incorporation.md).
5. **Run the company** within the Companies Law: meetings (Part 15),
   accounts (Part 16), distributions only after passing the
   Article 61A solvency statement, and directors' duties under
   Article 74.
6. **Wind up** by solvent members' winding-up (Part 21) or, if
   insolvent, by creditors' winding-up or *désastre* — see
   [winding-up.md](winding-up.md).
