# Article 217 — General Powers of the Court

Article 217 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** is the
**general powers provision** for the Royal Court in Companies
Law matters.

## What Article 217 covers

The Royal Court may make orders:

- **Ancillary** to any specific power in the Companies Law;
- **Procedural** in nature to support the conduct of Companies
  Law proceedings;
- **Declaratory** to determine questions arising under the
  Companies Law;
- **Injunctive** to restrain conduct in breach of the Companies
  Law;
- **Of any other character** the Court considers just in the
  particular matter.

The Court has its **inherent jurisdiction** in addition to any
specific statutory powers.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

Statutory provisions cannot **anticipate every situation**.
Article 217 gives the Royal Court the **flexibility** to make
appropriate orders for the matter at hand:

- Where a **specific power** is needed but not expressly
  provided;
- Where **inherent-jurisdiction** orders supplement statutory
  ones;
- Where **emergency or interim relief** is required.

The article also acknowledges the Court's **independent role**
in supervising Jersey company law — it is not reduced to a mere
implementer of explicit statutory steps.
