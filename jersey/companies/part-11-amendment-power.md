# Article 59 — Power of States to Amend Part 11

Article 59 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** is the
**amendment-power** provision for Part 11 (Redemption and Purchase
of Shares). The States may amend Part 11 by Regulations.

## What Article 59 says

The States may modify provisions governing redeemable shares,
purchases, and treasury shares through **regulatory amendments**.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this power exists

Share-redemption and buyback mechanics evolve with **market
practice**. The Article 59 power lets the States update:

- **Treasury-share limits** (under Article 58B) as commercial
  practice changes;
- **Redemption-funding rules** as capital-management practices
  develop;
- **Buyback-procedure** specifics where simplification is
  warranted.

The Regulations route is faster than primary-law amendment.

## Relationship to other amendment powers

One of the family of amendment-power Articles in the Companies
Law — see also Articles 2B, 17D, 40C, 66A, 85A, 113H, 115B, 124A,
127GA, 127YN, 194A. Each is limited to the Part it references.
