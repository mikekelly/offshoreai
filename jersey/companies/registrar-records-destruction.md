# Article 204 — Destruction of Records

Article 204 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** allows
the Registrar to **destroy or archive** old records after
prescribed periods.

## What Article 204 covers

The Registrar may, after prescribed retention periods, destroy
or transfer to archival storage:

- Records of **dissolved companies** older than a prescribed
  period (commonly 10–20 years post-dissolution);
- Superseded documents (e.g. old versions of memorandum/articles
  replaced by current versions);
- Other records the retention of which is no longer required.

**Permanent records** (incorporation certificates, dissolution
records, etc.) are preserved indefinitely.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

The Registrar holds **enormous volumes** of records:

- Tens of thousands of active companies plus historical records;
- Each company generates many documents over its life;
- Storage costs scale with volume.

Article 204 lets the Registrar manage **archive volume** through
controlled destruction of superseded records while preserving the
core historical-record function.
