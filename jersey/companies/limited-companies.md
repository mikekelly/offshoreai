# Article 3C — Limited Companies

Article 3C of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** defines
the **limited company** category. A Jersey limited company is one
whose members' liability is **limited by shares** or **by
guarantee** (or both).

## What Article 3C says

A **limited company** is a company that is either:

- a **par-value** or **no-par-value** company with **limited shares**;
  or
- a **guarantee** company.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Three flavours of limited

The Article 3C definition captures three structurally distinct
"limited" forms:

### 1. Par-value limited by shares

Shares have a **nominal value** stated in the memorandum
(e.g., £1 each). Members are liable to the company for the unpaid
amount on their shares — once shares are fully paid, member
liability is effectively zero.

See [Article 3E par-value companies](par-value-companies.md).

### 2. No-par-value limited by shares

Shares have **no nominal value** — they are issued for whatever
the company decides to charge. Members are liable to the company
for unpaid amounts on their shares; once issued and paid for in
full, member liability is again zero.

See [Article 3F no-par-value companies](no-par-value-companies.md).
This is the **modern default** in Jersey practice.

### 3. Guarantee

Members agree to contribute up to a **specified amount** if the
company is wound up. There are no shares; members are guarantors.
Used for not-for-profit and certain charity-style vehicles.

See [Article 3G guarantee companies](guarantee-companies.md).

## Why limited liability matters

The fundamental feature of a limited company: members' personal
assets are not exposed to the company's debts beyond their
agreed contribution. This is the **structural premise** of modern
corporate practice and underlies the use of Jersey companies as:

- **operational entities** that contract on their own account;
- **holding companies** that own subsidiaries and investments;
- **fund GP vehicles** that act for limited partnerships;
- **trustee companies** (TCBs, PTCs) that take on trustee
  obligations.

## Contrast with unlimited

An **unlimited company** under [Article 3D](unlimited-companies.md)
has no limit on member liability — members are personally liable
for the company's debts on wind-up. Unlimited Jersey companies are
rare and used for specific structuring reasons (typically tax
characterisation in a foreign jurisdiction, or to avoid certain
disclosure obligations).
