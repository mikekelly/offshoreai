# Article 206 — Form of Company's Records

Article 206 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** permits
companies to keep their **statutory records** in **electronic
form** rather than paper.

## What Article 206 covers

Records required by the Companies Law may be kept in:

- **Hard copy** (paper) form;
- **Electronic** form (database, file storage);
- **Other forms** prescribed by the Minister or Registrar;

provided that the records are:

- **Capable of being reproduced** in legible form;
- **Adequately secured** against alteration;
- **Accessible** for inspection where the Companies Law requires
  inspection.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

Companies have moved overwhelmingly to **electronic records**:

- **Cost savings** in storage and retrieval;
- **Searchability** of digital records;
- **Backup and redundancy** through cloud storage;
- **Integration** with accounting and ERP systems.

Article 206 confirms that statutory record-keeping obligations
can be met electronically, provided the records remain
**reproducible and reliable**.
