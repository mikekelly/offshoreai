# Article 208 — Production and Inspection of Records Where Offence Suspected

Article 208 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** gives
**enhanced powers** to compel production of company records
where an **offence is suspected**.

## What Article 208 covers

Where the JFSC, the police, or other authorised persons have
**reason to believe** that an offence under the Companies Law (or
a related offence) has been or is being committed:

- They may require **production** of specified records;
- They may **inspect** records on the company's premises;
- The **Royal Court** may issue **warrants** for search and
  seizure;
- Standard **privilege** protections apply.

Failure to comply is an offence.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

Article 208 is the **standalone enforcement tool** for offences
under the Companies Law, complementing the inspector regime under
Part 19. It allows **quicker, more targeted action** without the
full inspector machinery:

- Suspected **filing falsifications**;
- Suspected **breaches of director duties**;
- Suspected **fraudulent or wrongful trading**;
- Other Companies Law offences.
