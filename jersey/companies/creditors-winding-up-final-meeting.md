# Article 169 — Final Meeting and Dissolution

Article 169 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** sets
out the **conclusion** of a creditors' winding up — the final
meeting and dissolution of the company.

## What Article 169 covers

When the affairs of the company have been **fully wound up**:

- The liquidator **prepares a final account** showing how the
  winding up has been conducted and the disposition of all
  property;
- The liquidator **convenes a final meeting** of creditors (and
  members);
- The meeting **considers** the final account;
- The liquidator **files** the final account with the registrar;
- After a prescribed period, the company is **dissolved**.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

The final meeting and dissolution **close out** the liquidation:

- Creditors **confirm** the final account;
- The liquidator is **discharged** from further obligations;
- The company **ceases to exist** as a legal person;
- The register reflects the dissolution.

After dissolution, no further claims against the company are
possible (subject to limited reinstatement procedures).
