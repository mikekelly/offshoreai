# Article 78 — Disqualification Orders

Article 78 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** is the
**disqualification regime** — the Royal Court can order that a
person be disqualified from acting as a director (or in other
corporate roles) for serious breaches or unfitness.

## What Article 78 covers

The Royal Court may make a **disqualification order** against a
person, preventing them from:

- acting as a **director** of a Jersey company;
- acting as a **secretary** or other officer;
- being **involved in the management** of any Jersey company;
- (depending on order) other related corporate involvement.

Disqualification can apply for a **stated period** — typically
years rather than indefinite — after which the person may resume
director eligibility.

(For the verbatim text see [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Grounds for disqualification

Typical grounds:

- **Serious breach of directors' duties** under
  [Article 74](directors-duties.md);
- **Fraudulent or wrongful trading** in insolvency contexts
  (Articles 177–178);
- **Repeated regulatory breaches**;
- **Conduct showing unfitness** for company directorship;
- **Conviction** of certain offences (under the Companies Law
  or other statutes affecting commercial integrity).

The Royal Court considers the **gravity** of the conduct and the
**period** appropriate for protection.

## Who can apply

Applications typically come from:

- The **JFSC** in regulatory cases;
- The **Attorney General** in criminal-conduct cases;
- The **Liquidator** of an insolvent company where the conduct
  occurred in the wind-up context;
- Other appropriate applicants.

## Acting while disqualified

[Article 79](disqualified-personal-liability.md) addresses the
**consequences** of acting in breach of a disqualification order:

- **Personal liability** for the company's debts incurred during
  the disqualified period;
- **Criminal offence** under the Companies Law;
- **Further extension** of the disqualification period
  potentially.

## Cross-border effect

A Jersey disqualification order:

- Bars the person from Jersey-company directorship;
- May be **recognised** in other common-law jurisdictions
  applying disqualification regimes;
- Should be **considered** by the person's home jurisdiction's
  regulators and authorities.

The cross-border effect varies; the Jersey order is canonical
for Jersey companies.

## Pitfalls

- **Continuing to act after disqualification** — significant
  personal liability and criminal exposure.
- **Failure to disclose** a foreign disqualification when
  taking up a Jersey directorship — invites disclosure
  enforcement.
