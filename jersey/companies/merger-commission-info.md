# Article 127FH — Commission May Require Further Information

Article 127FH of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** gives
the JFSC **information-gathering power** in support of an
Article 127FF consent application.

## What Article 127FH covers

The JFSC may, in writing, require the applicant or any merging
body to provide **further information** the Commission considers
necessary to determine the application, including:

- **Financial information** about all merging bodies and the
  merged body;
- **Beneficial-owner** identity and source-of-funds information;
- **Foreign-law opinions** on the merger's effect;
- **Director / officer fit-and-proper** information;
- Information about the **purpose** of the merger.

Failure to comply within a reasonable period typically results in
the application being **refused** or **deemed withdrawn**.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

The consent process under Article 127FF is **substantive, not
formal**. The JFSC is not a rubber-stamp — and to do its job
properly it needs **full disclosure** from applicants. Article
127FH backs up that expectation with a **legal obligation**.

Applicants generally provide far more information voluntarily
than the article would require, in the interest of efficient
processing — but where they don't, the Commission has the
statutory teeth to insist.
