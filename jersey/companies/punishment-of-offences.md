# Article 215 — Punishment of Offences

Article 215 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** sets
out the **penalty regime** for offences under the Companies Law.

## What Article 215 covers

Penalties for offences under the Companies Law are:

- **Set out in Schedule 1** of the Law (the offences schedule);
- **Calibrated** to the seriousness of the offence:
  - **Lesser offences** — fines of moderate amount;
  - **Serious offences** — significant fines and/or imprisonment
    (typically up to 5 years for matters like fraudulent trading,
    false solvency statements);
- May include both **fines and imprisonment** for serious cases;
- Apply to **directors, officers, and other natural persons** as
  well as companies themselves (where applicable).

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl] and Schedule
1.)

## Why this matters

The penalty regime is what gives the Companies Law its **teeth**:

- **Solvency-statement offences** (Articles 61A, 146, 127E, etc.)
  carry significant imprisonment risk;
- **Filing offences** are mostly fine-based;
- **Fraudulent-trading offences** (Article 178) carry the
  heaviest penalties.

Article 215 anchors all these in a coherent penalty framework
through Schedule 1.
