# Article 127D — Merger Agreement

Article 127D of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** sets
out the **required contents of the merger agreement** between
merging bodies.

## What Article 127D covers

The merger agreement must specify, in prescribed form, matters
including:

- The **names** of the merging bodies and identification of the
  **merged body**;
- The **constitutional documents** of the merged body (or, if it
  is a newly formed Jersey company, its proposed memorandum and
  articles);
- The **consideration** (if any) for cancellation, conversion or
  continuation of securities in the merging bodies;
- The **manner in which** securities of each merging body will
  be cancelled, converted into securities of the merged body, or
  otherwise dealt with;
- The **directors** of the merged body;
- Any **other matter** prescribed.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

The merger agreement is the **commercial heart** of the merger
— it determines:

- **Who owns the merged body** (share exchange ratios);
- **What rights survive** (treatment of preference shares,
  loan notes, options);
- **Who governs the merged body** (board composition).

It is annexed to the documents filed with the registrar and is
the controlling document for the merger's effect.
