# Article 3D — Unlimited Companies

Article 3D of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** defines an
**unlimited company** — one in which **no person is a member by
reason of holding a limited share**.

## What Article 3D says

An **unlimited company** is one in which no person is a member by
reason of holding a limited share. In substance: a company with
shares that **do not limit member liability**, or a company without
shares whose members are not limited by guarantee.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why use an unlimited form

Unlimited companies are rare in Jersey practice but are sometimes
used for:

- **US "check-the-box" tax characterisation** — a US person may
  prefer a foreign company that is "transparent" for US tax
  purposes. An unlimited company may achieve this characterisation
  more cleanly than a limited company under US Treasury rules;
- **Avoidance of public filing of accounts** — historically, Jersey
  unlimited companies had reduced public disclosure obligations;
- **Specific group-structuring** purposes where unlimited liability
  is acceptable or even desirable for tax reasons.

## Risk

The key risk of an unlimited company: **members are personally
liable** for the company's debts on wind-up. A member of an
unlimited Jersey company that becomes insolvent may be required to
contribute to the wind-up to meet the company's liabilities.

This is structurally very different from a limited company and is
the reason unlimited form is rare.

## Members must understand the exposure

Anyone holding shares in a Jersey unlimited company should:

- understand the **personal liability** on wind-up;
- consider whether to **insure** against the exposure;
- model the **tax effects** in their home jurisdiction (the US
  check-the-box treatment may be the point of the exercise, but
  other jurisdictions may also re-characterise the structure);
- review the **company's solvency** before becoming a member.
