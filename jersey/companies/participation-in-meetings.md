# Article 86 — Participation in Meetings

Article 86 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** is the
**meeting-participation** rule for Jersey companies — members may
attend in person, by proxy, or by electronic means.

## What Article 86 says

Members may participate in general meetings:

- **in person**; or
- **by proxy**;

with provisions allowing attendance through **electronic means**
as permitted by the **articles**.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Electronic participation

Modern Jersey deeds typically permit **electronic participation**
in meetings:

- **Telephone conferencing**;
- **Video conferencing**;
- **Web-based meeting platforms**.

A member participating electronically is treated as **present at
the meeting** for quorum, voting, and other purposes, provided
the deed contemplates the arrangement.

This is operationally important for:

- **Geographically-dispersed** member bases;
- **Cross-border** family-office and fund structures;
- **Listed-company** virtual or hybrid AGMs.
