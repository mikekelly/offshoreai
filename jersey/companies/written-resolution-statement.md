# Article 95ZC — Circulation of Written Resolution and Statement

Article 95ZC of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]**
specifies the **content** of materials circulated with a written
resolution under Articles 95ZA / 95ZB.

## What Article 95ZC says

The company must provide members **clear copies** of:

- **Proposed resolutions**;
- **Supporting statements**.

Materials must **explain**:

- The resolution's **purpose**;
- Any **director recommendations**.

Companies **cannot alter** the resolution text during circulation.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## The accompanying statement

The supporting statement gives members the **context** to make an
informed vote:

- **What the resolution does** — the substantive effect;
- **Why it is proposed** — the underlying rationale;
- **Director recommendation** — for or against, with reasons;
- **Effect on classes of members** — if different members are
  affected differently;
- **Tax or financial consequences** — where material.

For **director-proposed** resolutions (Article 95ZA), the
statement is prepared by the company. For
**member-proposed** resolutions (Article 95ZB), the
requisitioners typically prepare the statement.

## No alteration

The Article prohibits the company **altering the resolution text**
during circulation. This protects against:

- **Tampering** by management with the substantive text
  proposed by members;
- **Confusion** if different members see different versions;
- **Procedural challenges** based on inconsistent
  documentation.

If the company believes the resolution as proposed has problems,
the proper response is:

- **Engage** with the proposer to amend before circulation; or
- **Circulate** as proposed and explain concerns in the
  accompanying statement.

## Pitfalls

- **Inadequate statements** — members can't make informed
  decisions and the resolution's validity may be challenged.
- **Bias** in statements — director-prepared statements that
  unfairly disparage a member-proposed resolution invite
  procedural challenge.
