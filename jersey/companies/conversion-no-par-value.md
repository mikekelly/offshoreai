# Article 40B — Conversion of Shares in No-Par-Value Companies

Article 40B of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** is the
**no-par-value-side** counterpart to Article 40A — permitting a
no-par-value company to convert its shares between classes or
back to par-value.

## What Article 40B permits

A no-par-value company may **convert** its shares:

- between **share classes** within no-par-value;
- between **no-par-value and par-value** structures (converting
  back to par-value);
- between **currencies**.

Subject to:

- **shareholder resolution** (special resolution typically);
- **class-rights variation procedures** where applicable;
- **Registrar notification** and updated memorandum.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## When this is used

No-par-value → par-value conversion is **less common** than the
reverse direction. It is sometimes done for:

- **Foreign-jurisdiction listing** requirements that mandate
  par-value (rare in modern markets);
- **Foreign-law-of-shareholder** requirements that favour par-
  value;
- **Group-alignment** with par-value parent or subsidiary
  structures.

Most modern Jersey conversions go par-value → no-par-value
(under Article 40A), not the reverse.

**Class-to-class conversion** within no-par-value (e.g., ordinary
to preference, or one currency class to another) is operationally
more common and uses the same Article 40B mechanism.

## Mechanics

The conversion process mirrors Article 40A:

1. **Special resolution** of members.
2. **Memorandum amendment** reflecting the new structure.
3. **Allocation of stated capital** — converting back to
   par-value requires re-allocating between nominal capital and
   share premium.
4. **Registration** with the Registrar.

## Class-rights consequences

Same as Article 40A — class-affected conversions require
class-rights variation procedure under Article 52.

## Tax

Same as Article 40A — generally not a Jersey taxable event;
foreign-jurisdiction tax consequences require pre-modelling.

## Pitfalls

- **Reversed conversion arithmetic** — converting back to
  par-value requires splitting stated capital into nominal +
  share premium. Get the allocation right per the deed.
- **Listing-rule compatibility** if a foreign listing is
  contemplated post-conversion.
