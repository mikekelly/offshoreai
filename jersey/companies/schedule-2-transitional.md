# Schedule 2 — Transitional Provisions

Schedule 2 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** sets
out the **detailed transitional provisions** linking the 1991
Law to its 1861 predecessor (and amendments).

## What Schedule 2 covers

Schedule 2 maps:

- **Companies in existence** at the 1991 Law commencement —
  treated as continuing under the new Law;
- **Memoranda and articles** of pre-existing companies — deemed
  to continue, with specified mappings to new concepts;
- **Pre-existing share-capital structures** — mapped to the
  par-value framework of the 1991 Law;
- **Old-form resolutions and meetings** — validity preserved;
- **Pre-existing officers** — continuing in office under the new
  Law;
- **Pre-1991 winding-ups** — continuing under either the old or
  new regime as specified.

The provisions are designed to ensure **no disruption** to
existing companies on the cut-over date and to provide a
manageable migration path to the new regime.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

The 1991 Law modernised Jersey company law fundamentally:

- It introduced **concepts** that did not exist under the 1861
  Loi (e.g. modern director-duty articulation, public/private
  split, etc.);
- It needed to **continue** all existing companies without
  forcing reformation;
- It needed to **preserve** the validity of pre-1991 acts.

Schedule 2 makes the transition workable.
