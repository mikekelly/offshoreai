# Article 137 — Expenses of Investigating a Company's Affairs

Article 137 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** sets
out **who pays the expenses** of a Part 19 investigation.

## What Article 137 covers

The expenses of an investigation (inspector's fees, expenses,
counsel fees, accountants, search expenses, etc.) may be
recovered from:

- **Persons convicted** of offences as a result of the
  investigation;
- **Persons against whom civil orders** are made in proceedings
  brought as a result of the investigation;
- **The company itself**, where the JFSC determines this
  appropriate;
- **The applicants** who triggered the investigation (e.g.
  members), in defined circumstances;
- The **Treasury** as a default backstop.

Recovery is enforced as a **civil debt**.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

Investigations can be **very expensive** — large fraud cases run
into millions in inspector and adviser fees. Article 137 ensures:

- The **wrongdoers** are first in line to pay;
- The **company** (and through it, the shareholders) pays where
  recoveries are insufficient;
- The **public purse** is a last resort.

This creates **incentives**:

- Wrongdoers know they will face cost orders alongside other
  penalties;
- Applicant members must consider whether their investigation
  request is justified — frivolous applications can result in
  cost exposure.
