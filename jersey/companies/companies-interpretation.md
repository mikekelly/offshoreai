# Article 1 — Interpretation (Companies (Jersey) Law 1991)

Article 1 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** is the
**definitions** Article. It provides the terms of art used
throughout the Law — "company", "director", "member", "memorandum",
"articles", "shares", and the family of structural definitions
that distinguish Jersey company types.

## Key defined terms

| Term | Definition (summarised — verbatim text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl]) |
|---|---|
| **Company** | A company registered under this Law, or an "existing company" preserved by transitional provisions. |
| **Director** | A person occupying the position of director by whatever name called (capturing *de facto* and shadow directors as well as formally appointed directors). |
| **Memorandum** | The memorandum of association of a company (Article 4 family). |
| **Articles** | The articles of association of a company (Article 5). |
| **Member** | A person on the register of members (subject to specifics in [Article 25](definition-of-member.md)). |
| **Officer** | A director, secretary, or designated officer of a company. |
| **Shares** | Shares in the capital of the company. |
| **Subsidiary / holding body** | As defined in [Article 2](subsidiary-and-holding-body.md). |

Other defined terms cover the JFSC ("the Commission"), the Registrar
of Companies, "ordinary resolution", "special resolution" (further
specified in [Article 90](special-resolution.md)), and a wide range
of structural matters.

## "Existing company"

The reference to "existing company" in the definition of "company"
preserves the validity of companies incorporated under the previous
Jersey companies regime (the 1861 Loi sur les sociétés à
responsabilité limitée) that were carried over into the 1991 Law on
its commencement. These legacy companies are subject to the 1991
Law from commencement, with savings in the
**Schedule 2 Transitional Provisions**.

## "Director" — broad capture

The Article 1 definition of "director" captures more than the
formally-appointed board:

- **De facto directors** — those acting as directors without
  formal appointment;
- **Shadow directors** — those whose directions and instructions
  the formally-appointed board is accustomed to follow (with the
  standard "professional advice" carve-out for genuine professional
  advisers giving advice in that capacity);
- **Alternate directors** — those acting in place of a formally-
  appointed director under deed-of-appointment mechanisms.

All such persons owe the **statutory duties** in
[Article 74](directors-duties.md). This is operationally important
for Jersey TCBs, PTCs, and family-office governance structures —
the substance of control matters, not just the formal appointment.

## "Officer" — wider than director

"Officer" is wider than "director" and includes the **secretary**
([Article 81](secretary.md)) and other persons holding office in
the company. For some Articles (notably the indemnity provisions
in [Article 77](officer-indemnity.md)), "officer" is the operative
term.

## Pitfalls

- **Shadow-director risk.** A person who advises a Jersey company
  without taking a formal directorship may still be a "director"
  under Article 1 if the board is accustomed to following their
  directions. The Article 74 statutory duties — and Part 21
  wrongful-trading exposure — follow. Be careful about
  documented "advisory" relationships that drift into directing.
- **Carryover "existing companies".** Pre-1991 companies operate
  under the 1991 Law with Schedule 2 transitional rules; check
  the transitional rules when dealing with very old companies.
