# Article 121 — Applications to the Court

Article 121 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]**
provides the **judicial oversight backstop** for the squeeze-out
and sell-out regime. A dissenting shareholder served with an
Article 117 notice, or an offeror facing an Article 119 demand,
may apply to the **Royal Court**.

## What Article 121 covers

Within 6 weeks of an Article 117 notice (or in connection with
an Article 119 demand), a person to whom the notice or demand
relates may apply to the **Royal Court** for an order that:

- The shares **should not be acquired** by the offeror;
- The acquisition should be on **different terms**; or
- Such other relief as the court considers just.

The court has **wide discretionary power** to make any order it
thinks fit, including specifying terms different from the offer.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

The squeeze-out is a **compulsory deprivation of property**. Even
with the 90% acceptance threshold and same-terms requirement,
unfairness can creep in:

- Where some accepting shareholders had **side inducements** not
  available to the minority;
- Where the offer terms reflect **inside information** not
  available to the market;
- Where the offer was made at a time when the market price was
  **artificially depressed**.

Article 121 lets the Royal Court scrutinise these cases.

## Standard of review

In practice the Royal Court is **reluctant to interfere** with the
terms of an offer that 90% of the shares to which it related have
accepted. The applicant must show:

- That **special facts** distinguish their position;
- Or that the offer was **not made on the same terms** (within
  Article 116);
- Or that the offer process was **tainted** by misleading
  statements or omissions.

The bar is high but not insuperable.

## Procedural timing

- **Squeeze-out**: court application must be made within **6
  weeks** of the Article 117 notice. The squeeze-out is stayed
  pending the court's decision.
- **Sell-out**: court application may be made at any time before
  acquisition is completed.
