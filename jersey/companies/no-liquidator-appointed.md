# Article 164 — No Liquidator Appointed

Article 164 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** covers
the **edge case** where no liquidator is appointed in a creditors'
winding up.

## What Article 164 covers

Where no liquidator is in office (creditors' meeting failed to
appoint, court has not appointed, prior liquidator has vacated):

- The **Viscount** (the principal Crown officer) may act in the
  interim;
- An **application to the Royal Court** may be made under Article
  175 for appointment;
- The **company's affairs are preserved** by the Viscount or by
  court order pending appointment.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

A creditors' winding up without a liquidator is a **legal
vacuum** — there is nobody with authority to act on the
company's behalf. Article 164 fills that vacuum so the company's
affairs do not deteriorate while the appointment process plays
out.
