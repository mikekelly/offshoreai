# Article 189 — Corrupt Inducement Affecting Appointment as Liquidator

Article 189 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]**
creates an **offence** of corruptly inducing or accepting
inducement in connection with **liquidator appointments**.

## What Article 189 covers

A person commits an offence if they:

- **Give, offer, or agree to give** any inducement, reward, or
  benefit to procure a particular person's appointment (or
  non-appointment) as liquidator;
- **Receive, accept, or agree to receive** such inducement;
- **Conceal** such arrangements.

Penalties include **imprisonment** and **fines**.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

The liquidator's appointment is a **significant commercial
decision** with potential for corruption:

- A complicit liquidator could **soft-pedal** investigations
  against directors;
- Could **accept** preferential payments without challenge;
- Could **inflate** their fees without proper scrutiny.

Article 189 protects the **integrity** of the appointment
process. Directors and creditors negotiate appointments openly,
based on professional reputation and creditor preference — not
through hidden inducements.
