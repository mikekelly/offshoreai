# Article 127U — Determination of Application for Authorization to Seek Continuance Overseas

Article 127U of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** sets
out how the **JFSC determines** an application for authorization
to seek continuance overseas.

## What Article 127U covers

The JFSC considers:

- Whether the company meets the **eligibility requirements** of
  Article 127H;
- Whether the proposed continuance falls within **restrictions**
  in Article 127I;
- Whether **member approval** under Article 127Q has been
  properly obtained;
- Whether **creditor notice** under Article 127R has been
  properly given;
- Whether the **solvency statement** under Article 127W is
  supported;
- Whether **member objections** under Article 127S have been
  resolved;
- Whether the **destination jurisdiction** is acceptable;
- Whether the **public interest** supports the migration.

The JFSC may:

- **Grant** authorization (with or without conditions);
- **Refuse** authorization with reasons.

Refusal is appealable to the Royal Court.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

Outbound authorization is a **substantive permission** to leave
Jersey. The JFSC's primary considerations are:

- **Reputation** — is Jersey being used as a stepping-stone to
  evade obligations elsewhere?
- **Creditor protection** — are creditors adequately protected?
- **Member protection** — have minorities been properly heard?
- **Tax** — has the Jersey tax footprint been settled?

The Commission generally takes a **facilitative** approach to
legitimate redomiciliation while being firm on the **gatekeeping
function**.
