# Article 203 — Enforcement of Company's Duty to Make Returns

Article 203 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** gives
the Registrar and the Royal Court tools to **enforce companies'
filing obligations**.

## What Article 203 covers

Where a company fails to file required documents within
prescribed periods:

- The Registrar may **issue notice** requiring filing;
- The Registrar may impose **late filing fees**;
- The Royal Court may make **mandatory orders** on application
  of the Registrar or other interested person;
- **Persistent failure** can lead to strike-off under Article
  205.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

The **public register** depends on companies filing on time.
Without enforcement:

- Records would become **stale**;
- Information would be **unreliable**;
- The registry's value as a **due-diligence resource** would
  collapse.

Article 203 backs up filing obligations with **graduated
enforcement** — from fees to court orders to ultimate strike-off.
