# Article 217A — Limitation of Liability

Article 217A of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]**
**limits the liability** of the Registrar, the JFSC, and others
acting under the Companies Law from claims for conduct in good
faith.

## What Article 217A covers

No civil action lies against:

- The **Registrar**, **deputy registrars**, or registry staff;
- The **JFSC**, its officers, employees, and agents;
- The **Minister** or Ministry officials;
- **Other persons** acting under the Companies Law in an
  official capacity;

for any act or omission **in the discharge of their functions**
under the Companies Law, **unless** the act or omission was
**not in good faith** (or was otherwise outside the protection).

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

Public officials acting under regulatory legislation face
potential **liability exposure**:

- Decisions that prove wrong in hindsight;
- Strike-off actions that affect commercial interests;
- Investigation decisions that prove unfounded;
- Filings rejected on grounds later determined invalid.

Without statutory protection, officials would be paralysed by
liability concerns. Article 217A provides the **good-faith
shield** — analogous to similar provisions in other regulatory
statutes — to allow officials to make decisions on the merits
without personal litigation risk.
