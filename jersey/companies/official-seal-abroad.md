# Article 23 — Official Seal for Use Abroad

Article 23 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** permits a
Jersey company that already has a common seal to adopt an
**official seal for use outside Jersey** — typically for foreign-
jurisdiction transactions where local custom or law expects a
seal.

## What Article 23 says

Companies with a common seal **may adopt an official seal for
foreign use**, marked with:

- "**Branch Seal**"; or
- the **name of the location** where it is to be used.

Documents properly sealed with such an official seal **bind the
company as if it had been sealed with the company's common seal**.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## When this is used

Common scenarios:

- **Foreign real-estate transactions** where local registries
  require a sealed deed and a Jersey notarisation or apostille is
  inconvenient;
- **Foreign branch operations** that need to execute documents
  locally without sending the document back to Jersey for sealing;
- **Group structures** where a Jersey TopCo has operating
  subsidiaries abroad and needs to seal documents in those
  jurisdictions.

The official seal serves the practical function of letting the
company **operate locally** in jurisdictions that still expect
sealing, without the inconvenience of physically sending documents
to Jersey.

## Format

The seal must be marked with either:

- **"Branch Seal"** (generic); or
- the **specific location** (e.g., "London Branch Seal", "Paris
  Branch Seal").

The location-specific format is preferred for clarity and to
avoid confusion if multiple branch seals exist.

## Effect

A document sealed with an Article 23 official seal **binds the
company as if** the company's common seal had been applied. There
is no qualitative difference in effect.

This means:

- Counterparties can treat an Article 23-sealed document as a
  fully-authorised Jersey-company document.
- Foreign registries that recognise the company's authorisation
  to seal abroad can accept the document.

## Authorisation

The company's deed should specify:

- **Who may authorise** application of the official seal;
- The **custody arrangements** for the official seal abroad;
- Any **recording or reporting** back to Jersey for control
  purposes.

The Article doesn't require Jersey-side approval for each
sealing, but good corporate governance typically does.

## Pitfalls

- **Treating the official seal as a different legal animal**.
  It has the same legal effect as the common seal — no special
  validation or qualification.
- **Loss of seal control** — an official seal abroad must be
  held with the same custody discipline as the common seal.
