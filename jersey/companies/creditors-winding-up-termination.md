# Article 185A — Termination of Creditors' Winding Up

Article 185A of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]**
provides a mechanism to **terminate a creditors' winding up**
where circumstances have changed.

## What Article 185A covers

A creditors' winding up may be terminated by:

- **Court order** on application of the liquidator, a creditor,
  a member, or other interested party;
- Subject to **creditor approval** in prescribed form;
- Where the basis for the winding up has fallen away or where
  termination is just and equitable.

The Court will consider:

- **Creditor consent** and protection;
- **Solvency** of the post-termination company;
- **Continuing obligations** the company faces.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

Creditors' winding ups are generally **one-way** — once an
insolvent company is liquidating, it usually goes through to
dissolution. But occasionally:

- **Rescue financing** restores solvency;
- A **scheme of arrangement** compromises debts and removes the
  insolvency;
- **Successful litigation** brings in unexpected funds.

In these cases, terminating the winding up and restoring the
company may produce **better outcomes** than continuing the
liquidation. Article 185A provides the procedural route.
