# Article 149 — Appointment of Liquidator (Summary Winding Up)

Article 149 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]**
governs **liquidator appointment** in a summary winding up.

## What Article 149 covers

The **members** appoint the liquidator:

- In the **special resolution** under Article 146; or
- By **subsequent resolution** if not appointed in the original
  resolution;

The liquidator must:

- Meet the **qualification requirements** under Article 188 (not
  a body corporate, not a disqualified person, etc.);
- **Consent** in writing to the appointment;
- **File** notice of appointment with the registrar.

A **vacancy** in the liquidator's office (resignation, death) is
filled by a further members' resolution.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

The members' choice of liquidator is the **single most important
decision** in a summary winding up:

- The liquidator **takes control** of the company;
- The liquidator's **competence and integrity** affect creditor
  outcomes;
- The liquidator's **costs** affect member returns.

Insolvency practitioners are most often chosen. Officeholders
without specific qualifications can be appointed, but a complex
winding up benefits from experienced practitioners.
