# Article 188 — Qualifications of Liquidator

Article 188 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** sets
out **who may act as liquidator** of a Jersey company in
winding up.

## What Article 188 covers

A person is **qualified to act as liquidator** only if they:

- Are a **natural person** (not a body corporate);
- Are **not disqualified** from being a director or liquidator
  (e.g. by Article 78 disqualification order);
- Are not **bankrupt** or otherwise insolvent personally;
- Are not a person under specified incapacities;
- Meet any **prescribed qualifications** for the type of winding
  up.

Specific exclusions:

- The **company being wound up**, or any director or officer of
  it (in their personal capacity);
- A person who is or has been a **creditor** for a prescribed
  period (subject to disclosure exceptions);
- Other persons whose **independence** would be compromised.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

The liquidator must be **independent** and **competent**:

- **Independence** ensures the liquidator does not favour
  particular creditors or members;
- **Competence** ensures the winding up is conducted properly.

Article 188's restrictions reflect the **policy minimum**.
Industry practice — through the Joint Insolvency Examination Board
and similar — adds professional qualifications expected of
insolvency practitioners.
