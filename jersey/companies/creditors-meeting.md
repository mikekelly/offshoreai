# Article 160 — Meeting of Creditors (Non-Court-Ordered)

Article 160 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]**
requires a **first meeting of creditors** in a creditors'
winding up commenced by members' resolution (not by court order).

## What Article 160 covers

Where creditors' winding up is initiated under Article 157
(company / members resolution), a **meeting of creditors** must
be convened within prescribed time (typically 14 days of the
resolution):

- **Notice** to all known creditors with prescribed information;
- **Attendance** by directors or other person designated to
  present the company's affairs to creditors;
- **Confirmation or replacement** of any liquidator appointed by
  members;
- **Appointment of a liquidation committee** (Article 162).

At the meeting, creditors may:

- **Replace** the members-appointed liquidator with one of their
  choice;
- **Appoint** a liquidation committee;
- **Direct** the liquidator on specified matters;
- **Receive** the company's statement of affairs from the
  directors.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

The first creditors' meeting is the **central forum** for
creditor governance of the winding up. Creditors get:

- Their **say** on who liquidates the company;
- **Information** about what assets exist and what's recoverable;
- **Standing** to influence strategy.

It is what makes "creditors' winding up" actually creditor-driven,
rather than just member-driven with creditor consequences.
