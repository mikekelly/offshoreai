# Article 34 — Nature and Numbering of Shares

Article 34 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** establishes
the **basic nature of shares** in a Jersey company — that shares
are **transferable interests** governed by the articles, and sets
the rules on share numbering and the **prohibition on bearer
shares**.

## What Article 34 says

- **Members' interests are transferable** per the company's
  articles (Article 34(1));
- Shares **may lack individual numbers** if **fully paid with
  equal rights**, or if documented by **numbered certificates**
  (Article 34(2));
- A company **may not issue bearer shares** (Article 34(3)).

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## What this means

### Transferability

A share is a **transferable interest**. The terms of transfer
are governed by the company's articles — typically including
provisions on:

- **Board approval** (common in private companies);
- **Pre-emption rights** (existing members offered first refusal);
- **Drag and tag** provisions for control transactions;
- **Restrictions** on transfers to specified persons.

See [`share-transfer.md`](share-transfer.md) — Article 42 — for
the registration mechanics.

### Numbering

The Article permits **unnumbered shares** where:

- the shares are **fully paid**; and
- the shares have **equal rights**.

This simplifies modern share-register administration —
fully-paid ordinary shares are the standard case and don't need
individual numbers.

Where shares carry **different rights** (e.g., partly-paid
shares, shares with rights subject to special arrangements), the
shares may need to be **numbered** or documented by numbered
certificates so the company can track which share has which
entitlement.

### Bearer-share prohibition

Article 34(3) **prohibits bearer shares** in Jersey companies.
Bearer shares — shares represented by a certificate that confers
title on the physical holder, with no registered owner — were
historically used in some offshore jurisdictions for anonymity
purposes.

The Jersey prohibition reflects:

- **AML/CFT policy** — bearer shares are inconsistent with the
  beneficial-ownership transparency expected of modern
  jurisdictions;
- **OECD inclusive-framework expectations**;
- **Moneyval review** outcomes.

A Jersey company **cannot issue bearer shares**. Where a Jersey
company is in a group with foreign bearer-share-issuing
subsidiaries, the Jersey-law position constrains only the Jersey
entity.

## Pitfalls

- **Bearer-share-style arrangements** — sometimes structures use
  features that resemble bearer shares (e.g., transferable
  warrants, certain crypto-style tokenisations). The
  characterisation matters; if it amounts in substance to bearer,
  the structure may breach Article 34(3).
- **Unnumbered partly-paid shares** — don't omit numbering for
  shares that are partly paid; the Article only permits
  unnumbering for fully-paid equal-right shares.
