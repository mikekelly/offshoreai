# Article 127GA — Power of States to Amend Part 18B

Article 127GA of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** gives
the **States of Jersey** power to amend Part 18B (Mergers) by
**Regulations**.

## What Article 127GA covers

The States may by Regulations amend Part 18B, including:

- Adding or modifying **categories of eligible bodies** (Articles
  127B / 127C);
- Adjusting the **approval thresholds** and procedural steps;
- Adjusting the **creditor and member protections**;
- Calibrating the **JFSC consent regime** for cross-border
  cases;
- Adding or removing **categories of merger offence**.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

The merger regime in Part 18B was introduced as a modernisation
of Jersey corporate law and has been amended periodically as
international practice has evolved (the EU Cross-Border Mergers
Directive influenced thinking even though Jersey is not in the
EU). Article 127GA allows further evolution **without primary
legislation** each time.

## Pitfalls

- **Check Regulations as well as the principal Law** — Part 18B
  is one of the more frequently-tuned parts of the Law.
