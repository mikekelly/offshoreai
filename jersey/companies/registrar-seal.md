# Article 197 — Registrar's Seal

Article 197 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]**
provides for the **official seal** of the Registrar of Companies.

## What Article 197 covers

The Registrar has an **official seal** used to authenticate:

- **Certificates of incorporation**;
- **Certificates of change of name**;
- **Certificates of registration** of mergers, continuances,
  cell creation;
- Other documents requiring the Registrar's **formal
  authentication**.

A **facsimile** of the seal may be used on documents issued in
the ordinary course.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

The Registrar's seal is the **mark of authenticity** for
official documents:

- It distinguishes **genuine** Registrar documents from forgeries;
- It satisfies **foreign-court evidentiary requirements** (often
  in conjunction with apostille certification);
- It provides **commercial certainty** for counterparties relying
  on certificate copies.
