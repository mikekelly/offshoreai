# Article 127Y — Offences Relating to Continuance

Article 127Y of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]**
creates **criminal offences** for false or deficient conduct in
connection with a Part 18C continuance.

## What Article 127Y covers

Offences include:

- **Making a solvency statement** under Article 127W without
  reasonable grounds;
- **Knowingly delivering false documents** to the JFSC or the
  registrar in connection with a continuance application;
- **Failing to give creditor notice** required by Article 127R
  (outbound) or its inbound counterpart;
- **Concealing material information** from the JFSC during the
  application process.

Penalties include **imprisonment and fines** at significant
levels.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

As with the merger regime, continuance trusts directors to:

- Make a **truthful solvency statement**;
- Disclose **all material facts** to the JFSC;
- Notify **creditors** properly.

Article 127Y creates **personal criminal accountability** for
directors. The offences are not a formality — they reflect that
continuance is a **significant corporate event** with real
consequences for creditors, members and the public interest.
