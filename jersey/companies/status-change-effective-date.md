# Article 17B — Effective Date of Change of Status

Article 17B of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** specifies
**when** a public-to-private or private-to-public conversion under
Articles 16 or 17 actually takes effect.

## What Article 17B says

Status changes become **effective upon the Registrar issuing an
appropriate certificate of incorporation** after receiving:

- the **special resolution** copy; and
- any other required documents.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

The effective-date rule means the change of status is **not
instant** on the member vote — there's a gap between:

- the **resolution date** (when members vote); and
- the **effective date** (when the Registrar issues the new
  certificate).

In that gap, the company is still in its **old form**. The old-
form rules apply to:

- accounts and audit obligations;
- prospectus requirements (for a private-to-public conversion);
- AGM requirements;
- any other public-vs-private rules.

The gap is typically short — a few business days — but matters in
edge cases (e.g., a year-end transition where audit obligations
turn on the company's status at a specific date).

## Practical timing

The standard sequence:

1. **Day 0**: Special resolution passed.
2. **Day 1–5**: Filing with the Registrar; the resolution and
   altered memorandum are delivered.
3. **Day 5–7**: Registrar processes the filing and issues the
   altered certificate.
4. **Effective date**: The date on the altered certificate.

If timing matters — e.g., year-end transitions, transaction
closings — the company should plan for the gap and may wish to
pay for fast-track Registrar processing.

## Interaction with auto-conversion (Article 17)

The Article 17B effective-date rule applies to **voluntary**
conversions under Article 16 / 17 (the special-resolution route).
For **automatic conversion** under Article 17 (e.g., on exceeding
30 members or circulating a prospectus), the effective date is
**the trigger date** (the date on which the threshold is crossed
or the prospectus circulated), not a later certificate date.

This is an important distinction:

- **Voluntary conversion**: effective on certificate issue
  (Article 17B).
- **Auto-conversion**: effective on trigger (Article 17 itself).

Auto-conversion can therefore catch the company **retrospectively**
— it may be deemed public from a past date once the trigger is
recognised.

## Pitfalls

- **Treating the resolution date as the effective date** for
  voluntary conversion — the change isn't legal until the
  Registrar issues the new certificate.
- **Forgetting to file promptly** — Registrar filings should be
  the same-day priority on resolution.
- **Confusing voluntary with auto-conversion timing** — they have
  different effective-date rules.
