# Article 38B — Rate of Exchange for Currency Conversions

Article 38B of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** specifies
the **rate of exchange** that applies when a Jersey company
converts share capital from one currency to another — a routine
operation for multi-currency capital structures.

## What Article 38B says

For currency conversions, the **rate applicable** is:

- the **prevailing rate on the date of conversion**; or
- as specified in **company resolutions**.

The rule ensures **consistency** in capital accounting across
different currency denominations.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

A Jersey company can have **share capital in multiple
currencies** — typical for fund vehicles with classes in GBP,
USD, EUR, and JPY simultaneously. When the company needs to:

- **Aggregate capital** across currencies for accounting;
- **Convert** a class from one currency to another;
- **Consolidate** classes after a re-pricing event;
- **Report** capital figures in a single reference currency,

Article 38B provides the **conversion-rate convention**.

## The two permitted rates

### Prevailing rate on date of conversion

The default — typically the **spot rate** quoted by a recognised
source on the conversion date. Operationally straightforward;
suitable for routine conversions.

### Rate specified in company resolutions

The company can **fix a different rate** by resolution. This is
useful for:

- **Forward-looking arrangements** where the conversion rate is
  set in advance;
- **Hedging-related conversions** where the company has locked in
  a specific rate;
- **Commercial alignment** with related transactions priced at a
  different rate.

The resolution-based rate must be **commercially sensible** —
arbitrary rates that prejudice creditors or other classes risk
being challenged.

## Practical scenarios

| Scenario | Typical rate |
|---|---|
| Routine accounting consolidation | Spot rate on consolidation date |
| Class conversion (e.g., GBP class to USD class) | Resolution-specified or spot |
| Tax-driven currency redesignation | Resolution-specified to match tax-event date |
| Group reorganisation with multi-currency rebalancing | Pre-agreed transaction rate |

## Pitfalls

- **Inconsistent rates** across related operations — accounting,
  registration, regulatory filings. Use the same rate throughout
  a single conversion.
- **Cherry-picking rates** to advantage some shareholders over
  others — engages directors' duties and class-rights
  protections.
