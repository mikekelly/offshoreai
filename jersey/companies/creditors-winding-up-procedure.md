# Article 157 — Procedure (Creditors' Winding Up)

Article 157 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** sets
out the **procedure for commencing creditors' winding up** by
the company itself.

## What Article 157 covers

A company may resolve to enter creditors' winding up by:

- **Special resolution** of members where the company is **unable
  to continue its business by reason of liabilities**;
- The resolution typically follows a **directors' determination**
  that the company cannot continue;
- Notice of the resolution must be given to the **registrar**;
- A **meeting of creditors** must be convened within prescribed
  time (Article 160).

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

Where directors realise the company is insolvent, they often
prefer to **enter winding up voluntarily** rather than wait for
creditor action:

- It allows **timely orderly wind-down** — earlier intervention
  preserves more value;
- It limits **wrongful-trading exposure** (Article 177) — once
  in liquidation, no further unauthorised trading occurs;
- It allows the **directors to participate** in the choice of
  liquidator subject to creditor confirmation.

Article 157 is the **company-initiated** insolvency route, in
contrast to the **creditor-initiated** route in Article 157A.
