# Article 216 — Accessories and Abettors

Article 216 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** extends
criminal liability for Companies Law offences to **accessories
and abettors** of the principal offender.

## What Article 216 covers

A person who **aids, abets, counsels, or procures** the commission
of an offence under the Companies Law is liable to be **tried
and punished** as a principal offender.

This standard formulation covers:

- **Directors** who facilitate offences committed by other
  directors;
- **Professional advisers** who knowingly assist in offences;
- **Employees** who participate in fraudulent conduct;
- **Co-conspirators** in fraudulent trading or other coordinated
  offences.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

Companies Law offences are often **collective enterprises**:

- Fraudulent trading needs participants beyond a single director;
- Falsification of solvency statements may involve professionals;
- Concealment of assets requires multiple actors.

Article 216 ensures that **all participants** face liability —
not just the directly named offender. The standard formulation
follows established principles of Jersey criminal law.
