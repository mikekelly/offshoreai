# Article 127F — Approval of Merger Agreement

Article 127F of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** sets
the **members' approval requirement** for a Part 18B merger.

## What Article 127F covers

The merger agreement must be approved by:

- A **special resolution** of the members of each merging Jersey
  company; and
- Where the merger affects a particular **class of members**
  differently, a separate special resolution of that class.

The notice of meeting / written resolution must:

- Be accompanied by a **copy of the merger agreement** and the
  directors' resolutions and certificates under Article 127E;
- Contain prescribed information about the merger;
- Be given to **all members** entitled to receive notice.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

Special-resolution approval is the **member-protection backbone**
of the merger regime. Unlike a scheme of arrangement, the merger
is not court-sanctioned — so the **two-thirds super-majority**
required for a special resolution is what stands between an
unwilling minority and dilution / disenfranchisement.

The **class-resolution requirement** further protects minority
classes: a merger that prejudices preference shareholders or
non-voting shareholders must be approved by those classes
separately.
