# Article 113L — Commission May Require Recognized Auditors to Give Information

Article 113L of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** gives the
**JFSC investigative powers** over recognised auditors.

## What Article 113L covers

The **Commission** possesses **broad investigative powers** to
require auditors to furnish:

- **information**;
- **documents**;
- **explanations**;

— necessary to assess compliance with applicable standards and
regulations.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## When the power is used

The JFSC may invoke Article 113L:

- In **routine audit-quality** reviews;
- In response to **specific concerns** raised about an audit;
- Following **complaints** about an auditor;
- In **broader market investigations** affecting multiple
  auditors;
- For **information-sharing** with other regulators (subject to
  confidentiality limits under Article 113P).

## Auditor cooperation

The auditor must:

- **Provide** the requested information promptly;
- **Cooperate** with the JFSC's investigation;
- **Not obstruct** the inquiry.

Failure can lead to **enforcement** under
[Article 113O](rules-enforcement.md).

## Pitfalls

- **Treating JFSC requests as optional** — they are statutory
  and obligatory.
