# Article 3 — Method of Formation of a Company

Article 3 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** is the
**formation** gateway. It sets out who may apply to form a Jersey
company and the basic procedural framework.

## What Article 3 says

- "**Any 2 or more persons associated for a lawful purpose may
  apply**" to form a **public** company;
- **Any person or group** may form a **private** company;
- The **Registrar** may restrict applications **exceeding 30
  persons** unless the **Commission (JFSC) approves**.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## What this means in practice

### Public vs private — minimum number of incorporators

- A **public company** requires **at least 2** persons applying.
- A **private company** can be formed by **a single person** — a
  single-shareholder Jersey private company is permitted.

In modern practice almost every newly-formed Jersey company is a
**single-shareholder private company limited by shares**. The
single-shareholder option is operationally convenient for:

- holding-company structures;
- private trust companies (where the PTC's shareholder is itself a
  trust or another corporate vehicle);
- family-office vehicles;
- pre-IPO structuring (a holding company below the public listing
  vehicle).

### "Lawful purpose"

The Article requires the persons to be associated for a **lawful
purpose**. The "lawful purpose" requirement is broad — Jersey does
not require commercial purposes; not-for-profit and special-purpose
vehicles are permitted, as long as the underlying purpose is not
illegal.

### 30-person threshold and JFSC approval

The Article gives the Registrar power to **restrict applications
exceeding 30 persons** unless the JFSC approves. This historical
provision is tied to the **30-member limit** for private companies
(see [Article 17D](30-member-limit-abolition.md)) and to the
broader policy framework on private-vs-public distinction.

In modern practice the 30-person threshold rarely bites at
incorporation because:

- most incorporations are with **1-5 founding shareholders**;
- the 30-member limit for private companies has been
  **abolished by Order** under Article 17D (the States having
  exercised that power); private companies can have any number of
  members now.

For the rare incorporation with a large number of founding
applicants, JFSC approval is the gating step.

## The incorporation process in outline

A Jersey company incorporation typically involves:

1. **Engagement** of a Jersey-regulated registered agent (a TCB or
   law firm acting as registered agent), which acts as
   intermediary with the Registrar.
2. **Consent to incorporate** under the [Control of Borrowing
   (Jersey) Order 1958][cob] — the Registrar's pre-incorporation
   approval of the proposed company.
3. **Preparation** of the memorandum (Article 4 family) and
   articles (Article 5) of association.
4. **Delivery** of the application documents to the Registrar
   under [Article 7](incorporation-documents.md).
5. **Registration** under [Article 8](registration.md) — standard
   24-hour service or 2-hour fast-track on payment of premium fees.
6. **Issue of certificate of incorporation** under
   [Article 9](effect-of-registration.md).

Article 3 is the legal anchor for the question of **who may apply**.
The procedural detail is in Articles 4–12.

## Pitfalls

- **Treating Article 3 as the whole process.** Article 3 deals
  with the gateway. The actual procedural mechanics are in
  Articles 7–12 and in the JFSC's published incorporation guidance.
- **Choosing public form unnecessarily.** A public company is
  more heavily regulated; most Jersey vehicles should be private
  unless there is a specific reason for public form (e.g., a
  contemplated public offering).
