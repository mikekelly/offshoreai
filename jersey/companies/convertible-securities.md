# Article 124 — Convertible Securities

Article 124 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** brings
**convertible securities** within the scope of the Part 18
takeover regime — treating them, for relevant purposes, as
shares of the class into which they may convert.

## What Article 124 covers

For Part 18 purposes, **securities convertible into shares** of
a company (e.g. convertible loan notes, warrants, options) are
treated as shares of the class into which they are convertible:

- A takeover offer can be made for **both shares and convertible
  securities** of the same target;
- The **90% threshold** can be computed on an "as-converted"
  basis where relevant;
- Holders of convertible securities have **squeeze-out exposure
  and sell-out rights** equivalent to shareholders.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

Public-company capital structures commonly include securities
that, while not currently shares, would dilute the share register
if converted. A bidder can't get to **100% economic ownership**
without either:

- Acquiring the convertibles at the same time as the shares; or
- Living with a residual convertible holder who could later
  convert and become a stranded minority shareholder.

Article 124 brings convertibles within the same regime so that
**convertible holders can be squeezed out** alongside
shareholders, and so they can **invoke sell-out rights**.

## Mechanics

- The takeover offer can extend to convertibles in the same
  document;
- Acceptance of the offer by convertible-holders counts towards
  the squeeze-out threshold (with prescribed conversion ratios);
- The Article 117 notice can be served on dissenting convertible-
  holders;
- The consideration may be the same as for shares, or different
  consideration appropriate to the convertibles.

## Pitfalls

- **Out-of-the-money options** — bidders sometimes overlook
  options that are out-of-the-money at the offer price. They
  remain convertible securities and a strict view of the regime
  may require their inclusion in the offer.
- **Vesting and lapse** — employee share options with vesting
  conditions need careful Part 18 treatment.
