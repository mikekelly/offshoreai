# Article 53 — Members' Right to Object to Variation

Article 53 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** gives a
**minority of class holders** the right to **challenge a
class-rights variation** in the Royal Court — even though the
75% threshold under Article 52 has been met.

## What Article 53 says

**Members holding at least 15%** of the issued shares in an
affected class may **challenge a variation decision**.

These dissident members have the right to **petition the Court
within a specified timeframe**.

> Members holding 15% of shares in the affected class may apply
> to court to have variation cancelled.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why the right exists

A 75% threshold under Article 52 means up to 25% of the class
can be **outvoted** on a variation that affects them. Article 53
gives that dissenting up-to-25% a **safety valve**: if they hold
at least 15% (i.e., a substantial minority), they can ask the
Court to **set aside** the variation.

The Article 53 challenge does **not** require the dissenter to
have voted against — they can have abstained, been absent, or
even (in some readings) voted in favour if subsequent
information changes their position.

## Procedure

The application is by **Representation** in the Royal Court
(Samedi Division for civil matters). Procedural elements:

- **Within the timeframe** specified by the Law (typically 21
  days from the variation taking effect);
- Supported by **affidavit evidence** of the alleged unfair
  treatment;
- **Convening** the company and the class holders who consented
  to the variation;
- The **Court considers** whether the variation has been made
  fairly and in good faith, and whether the dissenters have
  been unfairly prejudiced.

## What the Court considers

The Royal Court applies a similar approach to that applied in
**unfair-prejudice** petitions under Part 20:

- Was the variation **for a proper purpose**?
- Did the 75% majority act **in good faith**?
- Is the variation **unfairly prejudicial** to the dissenters?
- Was the **procedure** properly followed?
- What is the **commercial substance** of the variation?

The Court has wide discretion to:

- **Confirm** the variation (no relief);
- **Set aside** the variation;
- **Modify** the variation;
- Impose **conditions** on the variation's continued effect.

## Strategic considerations

For the company contemplating a class-rights variation:

- **Engage with dissenters early** to reduce the 15%-objection
  risk;
- **Document the commercial rationale** — the Court will look
  for legitimate purpose;
- **Time the variation** to avoid the 21-day challenge window
  becoming a transaction-completion issue;
- **Consider Beddoe-style pre-clearance** in marginal cases — a
  pre-emptive Article 51-style application could resolve
  uncertainty.

## Pitfalls

- **Inadequate consultation** with the class before the
  variation — invites Article 53 challenge.
- **Procedural shortcuts** that undermine the legitimacy of the
  variation.
- **Missing the 21-day window** — a dissenter who wants to
  challenge must act promptly.
