# Article 201A — Keeping of Records by Registrar

Article 201A of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]**
requires the Registrar to **keep records** of all filings and
related information.

## What Article 201A covers

The Registrar must maintain:

- A **register** of all companies (current and former);
- **Files** of documents delivered to the Registrar;
- **Indexes and search systems** for public access;
- **Electronic records** in addition to or in place of paper.

The records constitute the **official public register** of
Jersey companies.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

The public register is the **foundation** of corporate trust and
search in Jersey:

- **Counterparties** can verify a company's existence and status;
- **Investors** can access basic information about prospective
  investments;
- **Regulators** can monitor compliance;
- **Historical researchers** and journalists can investigate.

Article 201A makes the **record-keeping obligation** explicit
and statutory.
