# Article 127E — Resolutions and Certificates

Article 127E of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** sets
out the **resolutions and certificates** that the directors and
members of each merging Jersey company must produce in connection
with a Part 18B merger.

## What Article 127E covers

The directors of each merging Jersey company must:

- **Resolve** that the merger is, in their opinion, in the best
  interests of the company;
- **Certify** that they are not aware of any **circumstance** that
  would make the company **unable to pay its debts** during the
  prescribed period following the merger (the **solvency
  certificate**, broadly equivalent to the Article 61A solvency
  statement);
- **Recommend** the merger to members for approval.

The certificates must:

- Be in **prescribed form**;
- Be made by **all directors** (or a majority, with reasons for
  abstention);
- Be filed alongside other merger documents with the registrar.

Falsity of the solvency certificate is an **offence**.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

The merger regime is a **directors-driven, court-free** mechanism.
That places considerable weight on the **directors' personal
attestation** that the merger is sound. The solvency certificate
gives:

- **Creditors comfort** that the merged body will be solvent;
- **The State a basis** for trusting court-free mergers without
  case-by-case judicial scrutiny;
- **Directors a clear personal exposure** point if their
  judgement was wrong.
