# Article 67A — Relief from Breach of Duty in Unavoidable Circumstances

Article 67A of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** provides
a **discretionary relief** mechanism for officers (and former
officers) who have breached their duties in **unavoidable
circumstances**.

## What Article 67A says

The **Court may grant relief** to **officers or former officers**
who **breach duties in "unavoidable circumstances"** involving
**unforeseen events beyond their reasonable control**.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## When relief is available

The Article applies where:

- An **officer** (director, secretary, etc.) has **breached** a
  duty under the Companies Law;
- The breach occurred in **unavoidable circumstances**;
- The circumstances involve **unforeseen events** that were
  **beyond the officer's reasonable control**.

Examples might include:

- **Force majeure** events disrupting the company's ability to
  comply with filing deadlines;
- **Sudden incapacity** of the officer leaving administrative
  matters unattended;
- **Third-party failure** beyond the officer's ability to
  prevent or correct;
- **Catastrophic events** (natural disaster, civil unrest)
  affecting compliance.

## What relief looks like

The Royal Court has wide discretion. Possible outcomes:

- **Full relief** — the officer is excused entirely;
- **Partial relief** — the officer is excused on conditions
  (e.g., remedial action, restitution);
- **Refusal** — where the circumstances were avoidable on
  proper care, no relief.

## The narrowness of the relief

Article 67A is **not a general "I tried my best" defence**. The
relief requires:

- **Unavoidability** — not just difficulty;
- **Unforeseen** events — not events the officer should have
  anticipated;
- **Beyond reasonable control** — not events the officer could
  have managed with proper systems.

Day-to-day operational lapses, inadequate systems, or routine
oversights are **not** "unavoidable" within the Article 67A
sense. The relief is for genuinely extraordinary circumstances.

## Pitfalls

- **Treating Article 67A as a general defence** — it isn't. The
  threshold is high.
- **Failing to apply for relief** in genuine cases — relief
  must be **sought**; it is not automatically granted.
