# Article 127FF — Consent of Commission Required for Mergers Involving Bodies Other than Companies

Article 127FF of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** is
the **JFSC consent gateway** for mergers that include a body
other than a Jersey company — typically **cross-border mergers**
with foreign bodies corporate.

## What Article 127FF covers

Where any merging body or merged body is **not a Jersey company**
under the Companies Law (i.e. is a **foreign body corporate**),
the merger **may not proceed** without the **prior consent of the
Jersey Financial Services Commission**.

The JFSC will consider:

- Whether the merger is **consistent with Jersey's reputation**
  for sound regulation;
- Whether the foreign jurisdiction has a **comparable merger
  regime** with adequate creditor and member protections;
- Whether the **commercial substance** of the transaction is
  sound;
- Any **fit-and-proper** issues with the foreign body's owners
  or directors.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

Cross-border mergers can be used legitimately — for international
group restructurings, redomiciliation projects, regulatory
optimisation — but they can also be vehicles for:

- **Asset stripping** of Jersey creditors;
- **Regulatory arbitrage** across less-rigorous jurisdictions;
- **Reputation damage** to Jersey if the foreign counterparty is
  unfit.

Article 127FF gives the JFSC a **case-by-case veto** — the
gatekeeping mechanism that allows Jersey to permit cross-border
mergers while protecting the jurisdiction's standards.
