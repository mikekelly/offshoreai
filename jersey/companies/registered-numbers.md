# Article 198 — Registered Numbers

Article 198 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** provides
for **company registration numbers** — the unique identifier
each Jersey company receives.

## What Article 198 covers

- The Registrar assigns a **unique registered number** to each
  company on registration;
- The number is **permanent** — it does not change on:
  - **Change of name**;
  - **Continuance**;
  - **Reorganisation**;
- The number must appear on the company's **statutory
  identification** (e.g. correspondence under Article 70).

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

The registered number is the **unambiguous identifier** of a
Jersey company:

- Two companies can share a name (in different periods); they
  cannot share a number;
- **Foreign databases** index by registered number for clarity;
- **Public searches** use the number for exact matching.

This is the **technological foundation** of the public register.
