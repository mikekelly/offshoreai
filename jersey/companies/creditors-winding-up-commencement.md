# Article 159 — Commencement and Effects of Creditors' Winding Up

Article 159 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** sets
the **commencement date** of creditors' winding up and the
**legal effects** that flow from it.

## What Article 159 covers

Creditors' winding up commences:

- On the **passing of the resolution** under Article 157
  (company-initiated); or
- On the **date of the court order** under Article 157C
  (creditor-initiated).

On commencement:

- The **directors' powers cease** (subject to liquidator
  delegation);
- The **liquidator (or provisional liquidator)** takes control;
- The company **continues to exist** for winding-up purposes;
- **No transfer of shares** or alteration of members' status is
  effective except with court sanction or liquidator consent;
- **Legal proceedings** against the company may be **stayed**
  pending liquidator review.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

The **commencement moment** is critical because:

- **Claw-back periods** for transactions at undervalue (Article
  176), preferences (Article 176A), and similar are calculated
  by reference to it;
- **Wrongful trading** (Article 177) exposure ends — trading
  beyond that point is liquidator-authorised or stops;
- **Asset preservation** kicks in;
- **Statutory moratorium-like effects** apply to share transfers
  and certain dealings.
