# Article 209 — Legal Professional Privilege

Article 209 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]**
**preserves legal professional privilege** as a defence against
production requirements under the Companies Law.

## What Article 209 covers

Nothing in the Companies Law requires a person to:

- **Disclose** information protected by legal professional
  privilege;
- **Produce** documents protected by legal professional
  privilege;

except in circumstances where that privilege does not apply
(e.g. iniquity exception, waiver, joint privilege issues).

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

Legal professional privilege is a **fundamental safeguard** of:

- The **client-lawyer relationship**;
- **Access to legal advice** in regulated environments;
- **Constitutional-level protections** under Jersey law.

Article 209 is a **horizontal** privilege provision applying
across the Companies Law, complementing the specific Article 139
privilege provision for Part 19 investigations.
