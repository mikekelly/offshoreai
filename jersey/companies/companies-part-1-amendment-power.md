# Article 2B — Power of States to Amend Part 1

Article 2B of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** confers a
**Regulations-making power** on the **States Assembly** to amend
**Part 1** (the interpretation and group-definition Part). This is
one of a family of similar amendment powers scattered through the
Law — see also Articles 40C (Part 8), 59 (Part 11), 66A (Part 12),
85A (Part 14), 113H (Part 16), 115B (Part 17), 124A (Part 18),
127GA (Part 18B), 127YN (Part 18D), 194A (Part 21).

## Why these powers exist

Modern Jersey legislation routinely includes **delegated amendment
powers** to let the States Assembly update statutory definitions and
operational details by **Regulations** (a faster legislative
process) rather than by primary law amendment.

For Part 1 specifically, the amendment power lets the States update:

- **definitional terms** as financial-market practice evolves;
- the **subsidiary/holding-body** tests as group structures become
  more complex;
- the **interaction** with other Jersey laws as those laws are
  themselves amended.

Without the Article 2B power, every such change would require a
full amendment to the Companies Law itself.

## Limits on the power

The power is to amend "Part 1" — the interpretation Part. It does
not extend to amendments to the substantive provisions of other
Parts. Where a substantive change is needed elsewhere in the Law,
the corresponding Part's amendment power (or a primary-law
amendment) is used.

The States Assembly procedure for Regulations includes Assembly
debate and approval, providing democratic oversight.
