# Article 65 — Liability of Members on Reduced Shares

Article 65 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** addresses
the **continuing member liability** position after a capital
reduction.

## What Article 65 says

Members remain liable on reduced shares **only to the extent of
amounts still unpaid**.

**Creditors may not pursue members** for debts incurred before
the reduction became effective (beyond the still-unpaid amount).

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## What this resolves

A historical concern: when a company **reduces its capital**, what
happens to:

- **Partly-paid shares** with continuing call obligations?
- **Creditor claims** based on the reliance interest that
  pre-existed the reduction?

Article 65 sets the position:

- **Members' liability is the unpaid balance** on reduced
  shares — they are not subject to expanded liability;
- **Creditors are not entitled** to pursue members beyond that
  unpaid amount, even for debts incurred before the reduction.

## The creditor-protection limit

The creditor-protection at the **reduction stage** is via:

- The **Article 61A solvency statement** (if used) — directors
  confirm the company can meet debts as they fall due;
- The **court-confirmation route** under Article 62 — the court
  considers creditor interests;
- Notification to creditors and an opportunity to object before
  the reduction takes effect.

Once the reduction is effective, creditors generally cannot
claw back the reduction by pursuing members for additional
amounts beyond the unpaid balance on shares.

## Practical implications

For a company contemplating reduction:

- **Identify partly-paid shareholdings** — the unpaid amounts
  represent member exposure post-reduction;
- **Communicate** the position to members so they understand
  their continuing liability (if any);
- **Document** member-liability modifications in the deed and
  court order (if applicable).

For a member of a company that has been reduced:

- The **unpaid amount on shares** is the continuing liability;
- **No additional creditor pursuit** is permitted for pre-
  reduction debts beyond that amount;
- **Be cautious** about reductions in marginally-solvent
  companies — directors making false solvency statements can be
  liable, but that doesn't help an unpaid creditor.

## Pitfalls

- **Treating the reduction as eliminating all member liability**
  — partly-paid shares retain the unpaid balance as continuing
  obligation.
- **Assuming creditor reach** — Article 65 limits creditor
  pursuit of members to the unpaid amount.
