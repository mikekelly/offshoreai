# Article 111 — Registration as a Recognized Auditor

Article 111 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** sets the
**application process** for becoming a recognised auditor on the
JFSC register.

## What Article 111 covers

A person may **apply for registration** as a recognised auditor
if they:

- meet **qualification requirements** under recognised
  professional bodies' rules; or
- demonstrate **equivalent competence** through alternative
  means approved by the Commission.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Routes to registration

### Through a recognised professional body

The standard route — the applicant holds qualification from a
**recognised professional body** (chartered accountancy
institute, etc.), giving them entitlement to register.

### Alternative competence demonstration

Where a person doesn't have a recognised professional
qualification but can demonstrate **equivalent competence**, the
Commission can approve registration. This route is **narrower**
and more discretionary.

## What the Commission considers

In assessing an application:

- **Qualifications** (formal credentials);
- **Experience** in auditing work;
- **Fit-and-proper** status;
- **Independence** (capacity to maintain independence under
  Article 113E);
- **Insurance** and professional standing.

## Pitfalls

- **Application gaps** in fit-and-proper detail can lead to
  refusal or conditions on registration.
