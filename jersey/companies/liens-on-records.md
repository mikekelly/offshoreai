# Article 174 — Unenforceability of Liens on Records

Article 174 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** makes
**liens over company records** unenforceable against the
liquidator.

## What Article 174 covers

A lien over the company's **books, accounts, records, or
documents**:

- Is **unenforceable** to the extent that it would prevent the
  liquidator's access;
- The **underlying claim** survives — the lienholder retains
  their unsecured claim;
- The **records must be produced** to the liquidator.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

Without Article 174, professionals holding the company's records
(accountants, lawyers, bookkeepers) could **withhold them** to
secure payment of pre-liquidation fees:

- The **liquidator's investigation** would be defeated;
- **Claw-back claims** would be impossible to assess;
- **Creditor reporting** could not be done.

Article 174 makes clear that **information takes priority** —
professionals lose their records lien on commencement of
liquidation but retain their claim (which they prove in the
winding up like any other creditor).
