# Article 218 — Power to Make Rules
Article 218 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]**
empowers the **Royal Court** (or in some cases the Chief
Minister) to make **Rules** for the practical operation of
specified provisions of the Companies Law.

## What Article 218 covers

Rules may govern:

- **Procedure in Companies-Law proceedings** in the Royal Court
  (e.g. winding-up, just-and-equitable, scheme-of-arrangement
  applications);
- **Forms and templates** for filings and applications;
- **Detailed mechanics** delegated by the principal Law;
- **Costs and fees** for court proceedings.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

The Companies Law sets out **substantive law**; Rules set out
**operational procedure**. Separating them:

- Keeps the **principal Law** focused on principle;
- Allows **procedural updates** without primary legislation;
- Provides **detail** appropriate to court-rule format;
- Lets the **Royal Court** (which knows its procedures best) set
  the operational detail.
