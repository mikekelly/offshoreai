# Article 127YC — Creation of Cells

Article 127YC of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** is
the **creation mechanic** that brings an incorporated cell into
legal existence.

## What Article 127YC covers

On receiving a complete cell-creation application under Article
127YA, the registrar:

- **Registers** the cell;
- **Issues a certificate of incorporation** for the cell;
- **Assigns a registration number** distinct from the ICC's;
- **Adds the cell** to the public register as an incorporated
  cell of the named ICC.

The cell **comes into existence** as a body corporate on the
**date of the certificate** of incorporation.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

The cell is a **separate legal person** from the moment of
issuance of the certificate. From that moment:

- It can contract in its own name;
- It can hold assets in its own name;
- It can sue and be sued;
- It is **separately liable** for its own debts (subject to any
  cross-cell or ICC-level arrangements).
