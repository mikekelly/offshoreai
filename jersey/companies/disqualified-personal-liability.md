# Article 79 — Personal Liability for Acting While Disqualified

Article 79 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]**
establishes **personal liability** for persons who act as
directors (or in other corporate roles) **while disqualified**
under [Article 78](disqualification-orders.md).

## What Article 79 covers

A person who acts in breach of a disqualification order may be:

- **personally liable** for the **company's debts** incurred
  during the period of the breach;
- **subject to criminal proceedings**;
- **subject to extension** of the disqualification.

(For the verbatim text see [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## The personal-liability mechanism

The substantive bite: a disqualified person who acts as director:

- becomes **jointly and severally liable** with the company for
  debts of the company incurred during their improper
  involvement;
- can be **sued** by creditors directly;
- has no usual **separate-legal-entity** protection.

This is one of Jersey-law's most direct piercings of the
corporate veil — and it exists specifically to deter
acting-while-disqualified.

## Who can pursue

Creditors of the company may pursue the disqualified-but-acting
person:

- in **civil proceedings** for the debt;
- as part of a **wind-up** of the company under Part 21;
- via a **Liquidator's claim** where the company is in
  liquidation.

## Avoiding the trap

A disqualified person who:

- **Genuinely steps back** from director-style activity;
- **Ensures** they are not on the board;
- **Does not direct** the company's affairs informally;
- **Documents** their distance from management;

avoids the Article 79 exposure. The disqualification is from
acting; it doesn't bar the person from being a shareholder,
beneficiary, or third-party adviser.

The risk arises where the disqualified person continues to direct
the company's affairs in fact — formally or informally.

## Pitfalls

- **Shadow-director-style involvement** by a disqualified person
  — captured by the broad
  [Article 1 "director" definition](companies-interpretation.md)
  and exposes the person to Article 79 liability.
- **Family-office structures** where a disqualified principal
  continues to direct via family members — substance over form;
  the disqualified person may still be acting.
