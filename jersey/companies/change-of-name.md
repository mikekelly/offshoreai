# Article 14 — Change of Name

Article 14 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** governs
how a Jersey company **changes its name** after incorporation.

## What Article 14 says

- A company may **change its name** by **special resolution**;
- The change takes effect **from the date on which the altered
  certificate is issued** by the Registrar;
- Companies holding **immovable property interests** must notify
  the **Judicial Greffier within fourteen days** of the name
  change;
- The name change **does not affect** the company's legal rights
  or existing proceedings.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## How a name change works

The mechanics:

1. **Board proposal** — the directors resolve to propose a name
   change and a meeting (or written resolution) is convened.
2. **Special resolution** of the members under Article 90
   typically requires a two-thirds majority (or the deed's higher
   threshold) of votes cast on the resolution.
3. **Name availability check** — the new name must satisfy
   [Article 13](name-requirements.md) requirements.
4. **Filing** of the resolution with the Registrar, with the
   memorandum amendment reflecting the new name.
5. **New certificate** — the Registrar issues an **altered
   certificate of incorporation** showing the new name.
6. **Effective date** — the change takes effect from the
   altered-certificate date.

## Legal continuity

The Article expressly preserves **legal continuity**:

- The company remains the **same legal person**;
- **Existing contracts** are not affected by the name change;
- **Pending proceedings** in the company's old name continue
  unaffected (though parties typically amend the title of
  proceedings to reflect the new name);
- **Property held in the old name** continues to belong to the
  company.

This is operationally important: a name change is **administrative**,
not a transfer of business or assets. Counterparties dealing with
the company after a name change need only refer to the new name;
no novation is required.

## Land registration notification

Where the company holds **interests in Jersey immovable property**
(real estate), the **Judicial Greffier** must be notified within
**14 days** of the name change. The Greffier updates the
Public Registry of contracts so that property records reflect the
current name.

Failure to notify can lead to confusion in subsequent property
transactions but does not invalidate the name change itself.

## Practical pattern

A typical Jersey company name change in commercial practice:

- **M&A or restructuring** — acquirer renames target post-
  acquisition;
- **Brand alignment** — operating-company name aligned with
  parent's brand;
- **Listing or capital raise** — name simplification before
  going public;
- **Rebranding** — business-name change reflected in legal name.

The process typically takes a few business days to a week.

## Pitfalls

- **Operating under the old name** after the effective date —
  contracts and correspondence should switch to the new name
  promptly to avoid name-on-correspondence offences
  ([Article 69](name-on-correspondence.md) *(planned)*).
- **Missing the 14-day land-registration notification** — should
  be on the post-change checklist for property-holding companies.
- **Forgetting third-party updates** — banks, registries,
  service providers, contractual counterparties all need to be
  notified.
