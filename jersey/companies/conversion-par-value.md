# Article 40A — Conversion of Shares in Par-Value Companies

Article 40A of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** permits a
par-value Jersey company to **convert** its shares from one type
to another, including the **important option** to convert to
**no-par-value**.

## What Article 40A permits

A par-value company may **convert** its shares:

- between **share classes** (e.g., ordinary to preference);
- between **par-value and no-par-value** structures (converting
  to no-par-value under this Article);
- between **currencies** (with the Article 38B exchange rate
  rules).

The conversion is subject to:

- **shareholder resolution** (special resolution typically
  required);
- **class-rights protections** under Articles 52–54 if classes
  are affected differently;
- **Registrar notification** and updated memorandum.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Par-value → no-par-value conversion

This is the **most commonly-asked-about** conversion in modern
Jersey practice. Many older Jersey companies were incorporated
with **par-value** share capital under the original 1991 regime
and have since converted to **no-par-value** to access the
operational advantages:

- **Simpler distribution** mechanics under Article 39A
  stated-capital;
- **Cleaner currency conversion** under Article 38B;
- **Removal of share-premium-account constraints** under
  Article 39;
- **Familiarity for international counterparties** used to
  Cayman / BVI / Bermuda no-par-value norms.

The conversion mechanics:

1. **Special resolution** of members to alter the memorandum.
2. **Memorandum amendment** specifying no-par-value structure
   (Article 3F status).
3. **Stated-capital allocation** — the existing nominal capital
   and share-premium balances merge into the new stated-capital
   account.
4. **Registration** of the altered memorandum with the
   Registrar; conversion takes effect on registration.

## Class-rights interaction

A conversion that affects different classes differently is a
**class-rights variation** under Article 52, requiring:

- a **three-fourths majority** in writing of the affected class;
  or
- a **separate class meeting** with class-special-resolution.

For a uniform par-value → no-par-value conversion across all
classes (which is the typical case), the basic Article 40A
procedure suffices.

## Tax considerations

A par-value → no-par-value conversion is **generally not** a
taxable event for Jersey purposes (the company's tax position
under [zero-ten](../tax/zero-ten.md) is unaffected).

**Foreign tax** consequences may be different — pre-model the
conversion in any foreign tax regime relevant to the company's
shareholders or activities.

## Pitfalls

- **Failing to handle class-rights variations** correctly — a
  conversion that disadvantages a particular class requires
  class-rights variation procedure even though the wider
  conversion is by special resolution.
- **Forgetting share-premium balances** — these merge into
  stated capital on conversion; ensure the accounting reflects
  this.
- **Cross-border tax surprises** — pre-model in the
  shareholders' home jurisdictions.
