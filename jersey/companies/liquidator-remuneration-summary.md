# Article 152 — Remuneration of Liquidator (Summary Winding Up)

Article 152 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** covers
**how the liquidator gets paid** in a summary winding up.

## What Article 152 covers

The liquidator's remuneration is determined by:

- **Members' resolution** — typically passed at the appointment
  meeting, fixing the basis (hourly, scale, percentage of
  realisations);
- **Members' subsequent resolution** if the original basis needs
  to be varied;
- The **Royal Court** if members fail to agree or if a dispute
  arises.

Remuneration is **paid out of the company's assets** as an
expense of the winding up, ahead of creditors and members
(Article 150).

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

The liquidator works on the **company's assets** and is paid
from them. Without a clear remuneration mechanism:

- Liquidators could not predict their fees;
- Members would not know what return to expect;
- Disputes about fees could deadlock the winding up.

The Court backstop ensures the procedure does not break down
where members and the liquidator disagree.
