# Article 127P — Effect of Issue of Certificate of Continuance Within Jersey

Article 127P of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** is the
**substantive effect provision** for inbound continuance — what
actually happens, in law, when the certificate of continuance is
issued.

## What Article 127P covers

On the effective date specified in the certificate of continuance:

- The body **becomes a Jersey company** under the Companies Law;
- The body **continues to be the same legal entity** as it was
  in its home jurisdiction (no new entity is created);
- All **property, rights, assets, liabilities and contracts** of
  the body remain vested in it — **no transfer or conveyance** is
  needed;
- All **legal proceedings** by or against the body continue
  unaffected;
- The **articles of continuance** become the memorandum and
  articles of the Jersey company;
- The **members** of the body continue as members of the Jersey
  company on the terms set out in the articles of continuance.

The continuance **does not**:

- Constitute a dissolution and re-incorporation;
- Create a new entity;
- Affect any contract or obligation of the body.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

The **continuity-of-legal-identity** principle is the **whole
point** of the continuance regime. Without it, the same outcome
would require:

- Dissolving the foreign body;
- Incorporating a new Jersey company;
- Transferring all assets and contracts;
- Novating all counterparty relationships.

This is impractical for large operating businesses and creates
**counterparty-consent risk** at every stage.

Article 127P delivers the **commercial benefit** that makes
continuance useful: the same entity continues, just in a
different legal jurisdiction.

## Pitfalls

- **Foreign-jurisdiction recognition** — Article 127P establishes
  continuity in Jersey law. Whether the body is treated as
  ceasing to exist in its home jurisdiction depends on **home-
  jurisdiction law**. A reliable foreign-law opinion is
  essential.
- **Counterparty contracts** — even though legal identity
  continues, some contracts contain **redomicile clauses** that
  treat the continuance as a triggering event.
