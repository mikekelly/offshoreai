# Article 150 — Application of Assets and Dissolution

Article 150 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** sets
the **distribution waterfall** and **dissolution** in summary
winding up.

## What Article 150 covers

In summary winding up, the liquidator applies the company's
assets in the following order:

1. **Costs and expenses** of winding up (including liquidator's
   remuneration);
2. **Preferential debts** (wages, taxes, etc. as defined by
   law);
3. **Other creditors** in proportion to their claims (pari
   passu);
4. **Subordinated creditors** in their priority order;
5. **Members** — distributed according to the articles and the
   members' rights.

When all creditors have been paid in full and any surplus
distributed to members:

- The liquidator **files final account** with the registrar;
- After a prescribed period, the company is **dissolved**;
- The dissolution is **noted on the register**.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

Article 150 is the **endgame** of the summary winding-up
procedure — the point at which the company finally **ceases to
exist** as a legal person.

The distribution waterfall is straightforward in a solvent
liquidation: there is **enough money to pay everyone**, so
priority matters only for timing. In the **rare case** of a
summary winding up that turns out to be insolvent, the procedure
converts to creditors' winding up under Article 151.
