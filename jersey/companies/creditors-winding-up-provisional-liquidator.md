# Article 157B — Appointment of Provisional Liquidator

Article 157B of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** gives
the Royal Court power to **appoint a provisional liquidator**
pending the final hearing of a creditors' winding-up application.

## What Article 157B covers

After the creditor's application under Article 157A is filed but
before final order under Article 157C, the Royal Court may
appoint a **provisional liquidator** if:

- The company's **assets are at risk** of dissipation;
- The **directors are not credible custodians** during the
  pendency;
- **Urgent action** is needed to preserve the company's affairs.

The provisional liquidator typically has:

- **Power to take control** of company assets;
- **Power to prevent dispositions**;
- **Limited powers to trade** (subject to court authorisation);
- **Power to preserve records**.

The appointment lasts until final winding-up order or until
discharged.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

The **gap between application and final order** can be long —
months in contested cases. Without protection:

- **Directors** could dissipate assets;
- **Records** could be destroyed;
- **Preferential payments** could be made to favoured creditors;
- The eventual liquidator could find an empty shell.

The provisional liquidator **secures the company** while the
court process runs its course.
