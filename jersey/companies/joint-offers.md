# Article 122 — Joint Offers

Article 122 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** adapts
the Part 18 takeover regime to **joint offers** — where two or
more persons make the offer together.

## What Article 122 covers

Where a takeover offer is made **jointly** by two or more persons,
the references in Articles 116–121 to the "offeror" are construed
as references to the **joint offerors** acting together.

In particular:

- Shares **held by any one** of the joint offerors at the date of
  the offer are excluded from the "shares to which the offer
  relates";
- The **90% threshold** applies to the joint offerors' combined
  holdings;
- Squeeze-out and sell-out rights are exercised by / against the
  joint offerors **collectively**.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

Joint bids are common where:

- A **financial sponsor partners with a strategic acquirer**;
- A **consortium** combines for size, financing, or risk-sharing
  reasons;
- A **management team partners with a private-equity buyer** in a
  management buyout.

Article 122 ensures the joint bidders are treated as a single
acquiring entity for Part 18 purposes — they cannot game the
threshold by structuring as multiple "separate" offerors and they
collectively bear the obligations of an offeror.

## Pitfalls

- **Allocation of acquired shares** between joint offerors after
  squeeze-out / sell-out — needs to be agreed contractually
  between them.
- **Joint and several liability** for offeror obligations — the
  joint offerors should think carefully about indemnity
  arrangements between them.
