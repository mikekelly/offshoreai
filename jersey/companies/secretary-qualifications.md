# Article 82 — Qualifications of Secretary

Article 82 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** sets the
**qualifications** for a Jersey company secretary.

## What Article 82 covers

Article 82 specifies who is **eligible** to serve as company
secretary. Typical requirements:

- For **public companies**, the secretary must hold appropriate
  professional qualifications (e.g., chartered company secretary,
  legal, or accountancy qualifications);
- For **private companies**, the requirements are typically less
  formal — any competent person can serve;
- **Disqualified persons** cannot serve as secretary.

(For the verbatim text and specific qualification list, see
[jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Practical patterns

In modern Jersey practice:

- **Most private companies** use their **registered agent** as
  secretary — a JFSC-regulated trust-company business with
  professional secretarial staff;
- **Public companies** typically retain a **specialist company
  secretary** with relevant qualifications;
- **Listed Jersey companies** often have an **in-house secretary**
  with senior corporate-governance experience, plus the
  registered agent for Jersey-side filings.

## Corporate vs individual secretary

A **corporate body** (such as a TCB) may serve as secretary,
subject to its own qualifications. The corporate secretary
acts through its **officers** — typically a designated person
within the TCB has primary responsibility.

## Pitfalls

- **Under-qualified secretary** for a public company — invites
  Registrar query and regulatory scrutiny.
- **Disqualified persons** — same as for directors, a person
  disqualified under Article 78 cannot serve as secretary.
