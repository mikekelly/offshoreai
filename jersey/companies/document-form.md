# Article 200 — Form of Documents to be Delivered to the Registrar

Article 200 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** allows
the Registrar to **prescribe forms** for documents filed under
the Companies Law.

## What Article 200 covers

The Registrar may **prescribe** standard forms for:

- **Incorporation documents**;
- **Annual confirmation statements**;
- **Director / secretary appointment and change notices**;
- **Share-issue and change-of-capital filings**;
- **Merger, continuance, demerger filings**;
- **Strike-off and winding-up filings**.

Documents must be in the **prescribed form** to be accepted.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

Standard forms:

- Make filings **predictable** for users;
- Make **processing efficient** for the Registrar;
- Enable **automated systems** to validate and accept filings;
- Reduce **errors and rejections**.

The Registrar's online filing system reflects these standard forms.
