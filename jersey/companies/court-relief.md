# Article 212 — Power of Court to Grant Relief in Certain Cases

Article 212 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** is the
**"honest and reasonable" relief** provision — the Royal Court
may relieve an officer from liability for **honest and reasonable**
conduct.

## What Article 212 covers

If, in any proceedings for **negligence, default, breach of duty,
or breach of trust** against:

- A **director or officer** of a Jersey company;
- An **auditor**;

it appears to the Court that the person:

- Has **acted honestly**;
- Has **acted reasonably**;
- **Ought fairly to be excused** in the circumstances;

the Court may **relieve** the person, wholly or partly, from
liability on such terms as it thinks fit.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

Directors and auditors face significant **liability exposure**:

- For decisions made in good faith but proving unfortunate;
- For technical breaches of complex obligations;
- For consequences they could not have reasonably foreseen.

Article 212 provides a **judicial safety valve** for honest
mistakes — analogous to UK section 1157 (CA 2006). It is
**rarely used** as a primary defence but provides comfort that
the regime is not strict-liability for honest actors.
