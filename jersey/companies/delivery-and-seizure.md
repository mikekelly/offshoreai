# Article 180 — Delivery and Seizure of Property

Article 180 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** gives
the liquidator power (or court-backed authority) to **compel
delivery** and **seize property** belonging to the company.

## What Article 180 covers

Any person who has possession or control of:

- **Property of the company**;
- **Books, papers, or records** of the company;
- **Money** belonging to the company;

may be required by the liquidator to **deliver them** to the
liquidator. The Royal Court may issue **seizure orders** where
delivery is refused or where there is risk of dissipation.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

Recovering company property is the **first practical task** of
the liquidator. Property is often:

- **Held by former directors** at their homes or offices;
- **Held by related entities** that the directors control;
- **Held by counterparties** with disputed entitlement;
- **Stored** at third-party premises (warehouses, lawyers,
  bankers).

Article 180 gives the liquidator the **legal authority** to
compel delivery and the **court-backed enforcement** for
recalcitrant possessors.
