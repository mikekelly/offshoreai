# Article 51A — Uncertificated Securities

Article 51A of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** permits
Jersey companies to issue and hold **uncertificated securities** —
shares (or other securities) **recorded electronically** without
physical certificates.

## What Article 51A says

Companies may issue shares **without physical certificates**
through **electronic systems**, provided **proper procedures
ensure adequate documentation and member protection**.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## What "uncertificated" means

An uncertificated share:

- Is **recorded in the register** of members electronically;
- **Has no physical certificate** issued;
- **Transfers and dispositions** are recorded by electronic
  register update;
- **Title is evidenced by the register entry**, not a separate
  certificate.

The register entry serves the function that the certificate
historically served.

## Why uncertificated form is used

Modern reasons for uncertificated holding:

- **Listed-company efficiency** — modern market settlement
  systems (CREST in the UK, DTC in the US) operate on
  uncertificated systems;
- **Fund-vehicle administration** — fund managers and
  administrators run participant accounts electronically;
- **Avoiding lost-certificate problems** — no physical
  certificate, no risk of loss;
- **Cost saving** — no certificate printing or postage;
- **Speed** — instantaneous transfer recording.

For **private Jersey companies**, uncertificated form is also
becoming common — typically through the registered agent's
electronic system.

## Procedural requirements

Article 51A requires:

- **Proper procedures** for issuing and transferring
  uncertificated shares;
- **Adequate documentation** — typically electronic records
  with appropriate audit trail;
- **Member protection** — members must have means to verify
  their holdings and confirm transactions.

The detailed mechanics are typically governed by:

- The **company's articles**;
- The **specific electronic system** (CREST rules, DTC rules,
  or the registered agent's internal system);
- **Member-side agreements** with the system operator.

## Listed-company context

For a Jersey company listed on the LSE or other major
exchange:

- The shares are typically held in **uncertificated form** via
  CREST or equivalent;
- The **register** is operated by a **specialist registrar**
  (Computershare, Equiniti, Link, etc.);
- **Transfers settle electronically** through the market system;
- **Beneficial holders** are typically a step removed from the
  register, holding via custodians and nominees.

## CREST-eligible Jersey companies

For shares listed on the LSE or AIM:

- The Jersey company must be **CREST-eligible** under the deed
  and Article 51A;
- The deed typically includes specific provisions accommodating
  CREST mechanics;
- The Jersey register is operated in conjunction with the CREST
  system.

## Pitfalls

- **Mixed certificated / uncertificated holdings** in a single
  company can complicate administration; the deed should
  address.
- **System failures or data loss** — uncertificated holdings
  depend on the electronic register; ensure backup and
  reconstruction procedures.
- **Foreign-system compatibility** — different markets have
  different uncertificated regimes; cross-market listings need
  reconciliation.
