# Article 127FM — Registration of Notices as to Merger

Article 127FM of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** is
the **registration mechanic** for a Part 18B merger — the act
that makes the merger legally effective.

## What Article 127FM covers

On receiving the complete pre-registration document bundle (under
Article 127FJ, 127FK or 127FL as applicable), the registrar:

- **Registers** the merger documents;
- **Issues a certificate** of merger (or equivalent) recording
  the **effective date** of the merger;
- **Updates the register** to reflect:
  - The **dissolution** (without winding up) of the merging
    bodies that do not survive;
  - The **continuation** of the merged body (or its **new
    registration** if newly formed);
  - The **transferred share capital, members and contracts**
    where applicable.

The registrar's certificate is **conclusive evidence** that the
statutory requirements have been complied with.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

The **effective date** of the merger is the moment from which:

- The merging bodies cease to exist as separate legal persons;
- The merged body owns all the assets and liabilities;
- Contracts continue with the merged body;
- The members of the merging bodies become members of the merged
  body (on the terms set out in the merger agreement).

Article 127FM gives this moment a clear, public, **statutorily
documented** time — which is essential for third parties relying
on the merger.
