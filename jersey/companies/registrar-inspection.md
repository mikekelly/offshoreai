# Article 202 — Inspection and Production of Records Kept by Registrar

Article 202 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** is the
**public-access provision** for the Registrar's records.

## What Article 202 covers

Any person may:

- **Inspect** the public records held by the Registrar on
  payment of any prescribed fee;
- **Obtain copies** (paper or electronic) of registered
  documents;
- **Obtain certificates** of registration, change of name, good
  standing, etc.

Certain information may be **restricted** for privacy or
security reasons (typically director home addresses; in some
cases beneficial-ownership information beyond what is required
to be public).

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

The **public register** depends on **public access** to be
useful. Article 202 ensures:

- **Due-diligence searches** can be conducted by anyone;
- **Counterparties** can verify information;
- **Beneficial-owner transparency** (within prescribed limits)
  is meaningful;
- **Journalist and civil-society scrutiny** is possible.

The balance between public access and personal privacy is set by
the prescribed restrictions.
