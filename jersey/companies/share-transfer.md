# Article 42 — Transfer and Registration of Shares

Article 42 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** governs
**share transfers** — how a Jersey company processes a transfer
of shares from seller to buyer.

## What Article 42 says

- Shares **transfer per the company's articles**;
- The company **must register transfers** within specified
  timeframes;
- **Registration occurs when the transferee's name enters the
  register of members**, completing the legal transfer.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## How a transfer works

The mechanics:

1. **Sale or transfer agreement** between seller and buyer
   (the legal agreement to transfer).
2. **Stock transfer form** (or equivalent under the deed)
   executed by seller (and sometimes buyer).
3. **Delivery to the company** — usually via the registered agent.
4. **Board / committee approval** (if the deed requires).
5. **Pre-emption / consent procedures** (if the deed requires).
6. **Entry on the register** — the transferee's name is added,
   the transferor's removed (or holding reduced).
7. **Issue of new certificate** to the transferee, if applicable.

The **legal transfer is effective on entry on the register** —
not on the sale agreement, not on the stock-transfer form, not
on delivery.

## Deed-imposed restrictions

A Jersey company's articles typically include various
**restrictions on transfer**:

- **Board approval** (common in private companies — directors
  can refuse on stated grounds);
- **Pre-emption rights** — existing members offered first refusal;
- **Tag-along / drag-along** — minority-protection or
  majority-driving provisions;
- **Lock-up** periods after IPO;
- **Restrictions on transfer to specified persons** (e.g.,
  competitors, US persons in non-US-marketing structures).

Until all deed requirements are met, the **transfer is not
effective** even if the parties have executed the transfer
agreement.

## Timeframes

The Article requires registration within **specified timeframes**.
For a routine, deed-compliant transfer, the timeframe is typically
**short** — a few business days from receipt of complete transfer
paperwork. Where the deed permits the board to refuse, the board
must act **promptly** to refuse or the transfer may be deemed
accepted.

## Pitfalls

- **Failure to follow deed pre-emption** — the transferee never
  becomes a member; the transferor remains the legal holder.
- **Director-approval refusal in bad faith** — directors who
  refuse to register on improper grounds can face unfair-
  prejudice or other claims.
- **Stamp duty / transfer taxes** in the seller's or buyer's
  home jurisdiction — Jersey itself doesn't impose share-transfer
  duty, but other jurisdictions may.
