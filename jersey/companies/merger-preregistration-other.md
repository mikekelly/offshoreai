# Article 127FL — Pre-Registration Steps: Other Cases

Article 127FL of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** is
the **catch-all** pre-registration article — covering Part 18B
mergers that do not fall into the simple "all-Jersey" case
(Article 127FJ) or the "merged body not a company" case
(Article 127FK).

## What Article 127FL covers

Other typical scenarios captured:

- **Inbound mergers** — one or more foreign bodies merge into a
  Jersey company (the foreign body is the merging body; the
  Jersey company is the merged body);
- **Mixed mergers** — multiple bodies of different jurisdictions
  merge into a Jersey company;
- **Mergers involving non-corporate bodies** (where Regulations
  permit).

In each of these cases, the document bundle filed with the
registrar must include, in addition to the standard documents:

- Evidence of the **JFSC's consent** under Article 127FF;
- **Foreign-law opinions** confirming that the foreign body's
  jurisdiction recognises the merger and the disappearance of
  the foreign body;
- **Foreign-law evidence** of compliance with foreign procedural
  requirements (member approvals, creditor protections);
- Evidence that **liabilities** of the foreign body are properly
  passed to the merged body under foreign law.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

Inbound mergers are commonly used for:

- **Re-domiciliation into Jersey** of an offshore business that
  benefits from Jersey corporate law, regulatory standing, or
  tax positioning;
- **Group simplification** where multiple foreign entities are
  consolidated into a Jersey holding;
- **Post-acquisition integration** where a Jersey-based group
  absorbs a foreign target by merger rather than acquisition-
  plus-liquidation.

The registrar needs **foreign-law assurance** that the
disappearance of the foreign body is **complete and effective in
its home jurisdiction** — otherwise a zombie entity persists
abroad with potential liabilities.
