# Article 40C — Power of States to Amend Part 8

Article 40C of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** confers on
the **States Assembly** a **Regulations-making power** to amend
**Part 8** (Share Capital). It is one of the family of similar
amendment powers in the Law — see also Articles 2B (Part 1), 59
(Part 11), 66A (Part 12), 85A (Part 14), 113H (Part 16), 115B
(Part 17), 124A (Part 18), 127GA (Part 18B), 127YN (Part 18D),
194A (Part 21).

## What Article 40C says

The **States may by Regulations** amend **Part 8** (Share
Capital — Articles 34–40C) of the Companies Law.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this power exists

Share-capital mechanics are an area where **commercial practice
evolves faster** than primary legislation. The Article 40C
power lets the States update:

- **Currency-conversion** rules as international financial
  markets change;
- **Share-premium / stated-capital** transfer requirements as
  accounting and audit standards develop;
- **Fractional-share** rules as fund-product structures evolve;
- **Conversion mechanics** between share types.

The Regulations procedure is faster than primary-law amendment
and lets Jersey keep its share-capital regime competitive with
peer offshore jurisdictions.

## Limits

The power is limited to **Part 8** — i.e., share-capital
provisions. It does not extend to other Parts of the Law, each
of which has its own (or no) amendment power.
