# Article 64 — Registration of Act and Minute of Reduction

Article 64 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** completes
the **court-confirmation reduction** procedure: upon court
confirmation, the **court order** and the **minute of reduction**
must be **registered** with the Registrar for the reduction to
take effect.

## What Article 64 says

Upon **court confirmation** under
[Article 63](court-confirmation-order.md), the court's **Act
(order)** and the **minute of reduction** must be **registered
with the Registrar** for the reduction to take effect.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## What gets registered

Two documents:

- The **Act of the Court** — the court's order confirming the
  reduction (with any conditions);
- The **minute of reduction** — the board / member record
  authorising the reduction.

Both must be **delivered to the Registrar** together.

## Effective date

The reduction takes effect on **registration**. As with the
solvency-statement route (under
[Article 61B](solvency-registration.md)), the **registration is
the operative step** — until then, the reduction is not
effective even though the court has confirmed.

## Cross-reference

The parallel registration provision for the solvency-statement
route is [Article 61B](solvency-registration.md). Both routes
require registration as the trigger for effective reduction.

## Pitfalls

- **Delay in registration** after court confirmation — leaves
  the reduction in limbo. File promptly.
- **Conditions in the court order** — some orders impose
  conditions that must be satisfied before registration; ensure
  these are met first.
