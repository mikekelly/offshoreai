# Article 199 — Size, Durability, etc. of Documents Delivered to Registrar

Article 199 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]**
prescribes **physical and electronic format** requirements for
documents filed with the Registrar.

## What Article 199 covers

The Registrar may require documents to meet specifications for:

- **Size** (A4 paper, scanned-document parameters);
- **Durability** of paper / archival quality;
- **Print quality** for legibility;
- **Electronic format** (PDF/A, file size limits, signatures);
- **Page layout** (margins, single-sided, etc.).

Non-conforming documents may be **rejected** by the Registrar.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

The Registrar maintains a **permanent public record** that must
be:

- **Legible** decades later;
- **Searchable** electronically;
- **Archivable** in standard formats;
- **Transmissible** through online filing systems.

Article 199 enables the Registrar to set the standards needed to
make the register work as a long-lived, searchable record.
