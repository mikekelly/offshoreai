# Article 72 — Service of Documents

Article 72 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** specifies
where legal documents can be **served on a Jersey company**.

## What Article 72 says

Legal documents may be served on companies:

- at their **registered office**; or
- at such **other addresses as designated in the memorandum and
  articles**.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## Why this matters

A party that needs to serve **legal proceedings** or **formal
notice** on a Jersey company needs to know where service is
**effective**. Article 72 makes this clear:

- The **registered office** is always a valid service address;
- The **deed** may designate **alternative addresses** for
  specific purposes.

Service at the registered office is **always good**; service at
other addresses may be good if the deed permits it.

## The registered-office default

In practice, almost all service on Jersey companies is at the
**registered office** — which is typically the office of the
registered agent. The registered agent:

- Receives service of process;
- **Forwards** documents to the company / its directors;
- Maintains a record of service receipts.

This works because the registered agent is professionally
equipped to receive and respond to legal process.

## Deed-designated alternative addresses

Some deeds specify alternative addresses — typically for:

- **Member communications** going to a particular member's
  address;
- **Specific contracts** designating an operational address for
  service;
- **Subsidiary or branch** operations with their own service
  addresses.

Where the deed designates an alternative, service at that
address is also valid (within the deed's terms).

## Pitfalls

- **Stale registered office** — if the company has moved
  registered office without updating the Registry, service at
  the old address may technically still be valid until the
  change is filed. Files at the registered office may not reach
  the company's current operating team.
- **Service at non-designated addresses** — service at, e.g.,
  a director's home address may not be valid unless the deed
  designates it.
