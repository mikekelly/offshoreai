# Article 4 — Memorandum of Association

Article 4 of the **[Companies (Jersey) Law 1991][cjl]** is the
**general-purpose memorandum** provision. It sets out what every
memorandum of a Jersey company must contain, regardless of the type
of company. Specialised follow-on Articles deal with the additional
content for share companies (Article 4A), guarantee companies
(Article 4B), and limited-duration companies (Article 4C).

## What Article 4 says

A memorandum of association:

- must be in the **English or French language**;
- must be **printed** (subject to the electronic-delivery
  exception);
- must state the **company's name**;
- must state the company's **public / private status**;
- must classify **share type** (where applicable);
- must record **subscriber details**;
- must be **signed in the presence of at least one witness**, who
  attests the signature.

For **natural-person subscribers**: full name and address.
For **corporate subscribers**: name and address of registered or
principal office.

**Electronic delivery** under the **Electronic Communications
(Jersey) Law 2000** may waive the printing and witnessing
requirements where the prescribed form is followed.

(See the consolidated text at [jerseylaw.je][cjl].)

## What the memorandum does

The memorandum is one of the company's two **constitutional
documents** (the other being the [articles of association](articles-of-association.md)
under Article 5). Once registered, it:

- **binds** the company and its members **as if signed covenants
  existed between them** (under [Article 10](effect-of-memorandum-articles.md)
  *(planned)*);
- is **public** — filed with the JFSC Registrar and available to
  third parties;
- can be **altered** only by special resolution under
  [Article 11](alteration-of-memorandum-articles.md) *(planned)*,
  subject to specific protections for member liability.

## Bilingual provision

The English-or-French permission reflects Jersey's bilingual
constitutional heritage. In practice, modern Jersey memoranda are
almost universally in English. A French-language memorandum is
permitted but rare outside specific historical or family contexts.

## Modern practice

In practice every Jersey company is set up with:

- a **standard-form memorandum** drafted by the registered agent;
- the **content required by Article 4** plus the company-type-
  specific additions (Article 4A for shares, 4B for guarantee,
  4C for limited duration);
- **electronic delivery** under the Electronic Communications Law
  — the printed-and-witnessed requirements are not the operational
  default for modern incorporations.

## Pitfalls

- **Misnaming or misclassification** in the memorandum can lead to
  Registrar rejection or to operational confusion later. Get the
  type designation (public vs private, par-value vs no-par-value,
  limited vs unlimited) right at incorporation.
- **Witnessing errors** for paper memoranda — the witness must
  attest the subscriber's signature. Missing witness signatures
  are a regular Registrar query.
