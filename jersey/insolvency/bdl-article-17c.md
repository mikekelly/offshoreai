# BDL 1990 Article 17C — Fraudulent Transfers

Article 17C is an **avoidance power** for **fraudulent
transfers** made by the debtor.

## Definition

A **fraudulent transfer** is one made with **intent to
defraud creditors**:

- **Intent** is the key element — the transferor must have
  intended to put property beyond reach of creditors;
- **Insolvency** at the time is **not required** (unlike
  preferences and undervalue);
- **No statutory lookback** in the strict sense — the
  challenge can be made under common-law / customary-law
  fraudulent-transfer doctrines as well as statutory powers.

## Indicators of intent

Courts look for:

- **Timing** — close to insolvency or known dispute;
- **Connected party** transferees;
- **Continued use** by debtor after transfer (settlor of
  trust still benefits);
- **Concealment** — transfer not disclosed;
- **Inadequate** consideration;
- **Specific** other badges of fraud.

## Examples

- **Transfers to a trust** specifically to defeat a known
  creditor;
- **Asset transfers** at the threshold of insolvency to
  family members;
- **Disposing of assets** while litigation is being prepared
  by creditors;
- **Specific** sham transactions.

## Effect

If proven:

- The transfer is **set aside**;
- Property is **restored** to the estate;
- **Third-party recipients** (subsequent purchasers) may
  have BFPV defences;
- **Specific** equitable considerations.

## Customary-law base

Jersey's **Pauliana doctrine** (from Roman/Norman customary
law) supports fraudulent-transfer claims **independently** of
the statute:

- Relevant for transactions **outside** statutory lookback
  periods;
- **Foreign-jurisdiction** transfers with Jersey nexus;
- **Non-désastre** actions by individual creditors;
- **Specific** Jersey case law applying Pauliana.

## Specific contexts

### Asset-protection trusts

- **Genuine** asset-protection well in advance of insolvency
  is permissible;
- **Sham** or close-to-insolvency transfers vulnerable;
- **Specific** Jersey planning principles.

### Family business transfers

- **Pre-insolvency** transfers to family members;
- **Specific** scrutiny;
- **Specific** customary-law analysis.

### Foreign-jurisdiction transfers

- **Jersey** transferor moves assets abroad pre-insolvency;
- **Jersey court** can reach across borders through CICL /
  MLA;
- **Specific** Jersey jurisprudence.
