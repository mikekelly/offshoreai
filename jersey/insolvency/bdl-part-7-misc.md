# BDL 1990 Part 7 — Miscellaneous

Part 7 covers **foreign insolvency cooperation**, regulations,
fees, and citation.

## Foreign insolvency

Jersey désastre law works with foreign insolvency through:

### Recognition

The Royal Court can **recognise**:

- **Foreign bankruptcy** orders;
- **Foreign liquidations**;
- **Foreign restraint** orders in insolvency context;
- **Foreign officeholder** appointments;
- **Subject to** Jersey public policy and procedural fairness
  standards.

### Cooperation

The Royal Court will **cooperate** with foreign courts:

- **Letters of request** for assistance;
- **Joint** proceedings in appropriate cases;
- **Information sharing** with foreign officeholders;
- **Asset turnover** to foreign liquidators where
  appropriate;
- **Recognition** of foreign discharges.

The Royal Court relies on **inherent jurisdiction** more than
statutory provisions for cross-border insolvency — Jersey is
not a party to the **UNCITRAL Model Law** on Cross-Border
Insolvency.

### Notable areas

- **UK insolvency** — close historical and procedural
  alignment;
- **EU insolvency** — pre-Brexit cooperation under EU
  Insolvency Regulation, post-Brexit through bilateral
  cooperation;
- **US bankruptcy** — Chapter 15 ancillary proceedings in
  the US enable foreign main / foreign nonmain
  characterisation of Jersey désastre;
- **Cayman, BVI, Bermuda** — Crown / Commonwealth
  cooperation;
- **Other** — case-by-case.

## Regulations and Orders

The Minister may make **regulations** for:

- **Forms and fees**;
- **Procedural rules**;
- **Specific** asset-type rules;
- **Implementation** of international cooperation
  arrangements;
- **Updates** to lookback periods, thresholds, exempt
  categories.

## Specific issues

### Trustee désastre

A trustee's désastre:

- Affects the **trustee personally** but **not** the trust
  property;
- Trust property remains **insolvency-remote** under TJL
  1984 Article 54;
- Trustee's **personal estate** is the désastre estate;
- **Successor trustee** typically appointed to take over the
  trust;
- See [`../trusts/trustee-estate-and-insolvency.md`](../trusts/trustee-estate-and-insolvency.md).

### Trustee fees

The trustee's right to **fees and indemnity** from trust
property survives désastre — but it is exercised through the
trust, not as part of the personal estate.

### Foundation insolvency

A foundation can be **subject to désastre**:

- Designated as the debtor;
- **Trust-like** insolvency-remoteness does **not** apply (the
  foundation owns its property);
- **Council members** may have personal exposure if duties
  breached;
- **Foundations Law** dissolution may run in parallel.

### Cross-border crypto / digital assets

Désastre over digital assets:

- **Vesting** in the Viscount applies;
- **Practical realisation** depends on access to private keys
  and exchange accounts;
- **Foreign-platform** assets challenging to recover;
- **Specialist** advice often needed.

## Citation

The Law is cited as the **"Bankruptcy (Désastre) (Jersey) Law
1990"**.

It came into force in **1990** and has been amended several
times, including:

- **2008** — substantial revisions to align with international
  best practice;
- **2016** — updates to avoidance regime;
- **Periodic** updates to fee schedules.
