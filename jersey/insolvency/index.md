# Insolvency — Jersey

## What this section is about

This section covers **insolvency law in Jersey** — the regimes
that apply when a person (individual or corporate) cannot pay
its debts.

Jersey has **two principal insolvency regimes** that operate
in parallel:

1. **Désastre** under the **Bankruptcy (Désastre) (Jersey) Law
   1990 (BDL)** — a court-supervised procedure that applies to
   both **individuals** and **companies**;
2. **Winding-up** under the **Companies (Jersey) Law 1991** —
   applies only to **companies**, in **summary** (members' /
   solvent) or **creditors'** (insolvent) form.

Jersey's customary-law **cessio bonorum** historically allowed
voluntary surrender of estate but is now largely superseded by
the statutory regimes.

## Jersey insolvency principles

- **Pari passu** — equal treatment of unsecured creditors of
  the same rank;
- **Priority** — secured creditors first; certain preferential
  creditors (wages, certain taxes) ahead of unsecured;
- **Avoidance** — preferences, transactions at undervalue, and
  fraudulent transfers can be set aside;
- **Trust property** is **not** part of the trustee's estate
  (insolvency-remote);
- **Cross-border** cooperation through Royal Court inherent
  jurisdiction and treaty arrangements.
