# BDL 1990 Article 3 — Application for Declaration of Désastre

Article 3 governs **who can apply** for a declaration of
désastre and **how**.

## Who can apply

### The debtor (voluntary application)

A debtor may apply for **their own désastre**:

- **Acknowledging** insolvency;
- **Avoiding** harassment by creditors;
- **Achieving** discharge after the prescribed period;
- **Subject to** the Court's discretion to refuse if abuse.

Voluntary applications are **less common** for companies
(directors typically prefer creditors' winding-up to retain
some control) but **more common** for individuals.

### A creditor

A creditor may apply if:

- They hold a **claim** of at least the **minimum threshold**
  (currently £3,000);
- The debt is **due and unpaid**;
- The debtor has **failed** to comply with a **statutory
  demand** (typically 21 days); or
- The debtor has **otherwise demonstrated** insolvency.

Multiple creditors may join in a single application.

### Other applicants

In specific circumstances:

- **Receiver** of property of an undischarged person;
- **Personal representative** of a deceased insolvent
  (estate insolvency);
- **Specific** statutory officers (in narrow cases).

## Application content

An application includes:

- **Identity** of the applicant;
- **Identity** of the debtor;
- **Grounds** relied on for the declaration;
- **Evidence** of insolvency or other ground;
- **Statement** of known assets and liabilities (creditor
  applications include best information available);
- **Notice** of the application to the debtor (if not the
  debtor's own application);
- **Court fee**.

## Statutory demand

For **creditor applications**, a **statutory demand** is
typically the precursor:

- **Formal demand** for payment of the debt;
- **Served** on the debtor;
- **21 days** to comply (or longer in specified cases);
- **Non-compliance** is evidence of insolvency for Article
  3 purposes.

The statutory demand procedure parallels English bankruptcy
practice and is well-established in Jersey.

## Procedure

After application:

- **Hearing** scheduled before the Royal Court;
- **Debtor** has opportunity to be heard;
- **Court** examines evidence of insolvency / grounds;
- **Court** considers debtor's position (voluntary cases
  generally granted; creditor cases more scrutinised);
- **Declaration** made if grounds satisfied (Article 4);
- **Order** specifies date of declaration.
