# Bail in Jersey Criminal Proceedings

## What bail is

**Bail** is release from custody, subject to conditions,
on the basis that the person will return to the police
station or court at a specified time. Bail can be granted:

- **At the police station** — police bail after arrest
  but before charge, or after charge but before first
  court appearance;
- **At court** — by the Magistrate's Court or Royal
  Court pending trial or sentence;
- **Post-conviction** — pending appeal.

## The presumption

Following ECHR Article 5 (incorporated into Jersey law
by the Human Rights (Jersey) Law 2000) and PPCE / bail
provisions:

- **General presumption** in favour of bail;
- **Exceptions** where specific grounds for denial are
  established;
- **Conditions** may be imposed to address risks short of
  outright refusal.

## Grounds to deny bail

Bail may be denied where the court is satisfied of
substantial grounds for believing:

- The defendant would **fail to surrender** to custody;
- The defendant would **commit further offences** while on
  bail;
- The defendant would **interfere with witnesses or
  obstruct justice**;
- **Welfare** of the defendant or others requires
  detention;
- The defendant is **charged with an indictable offence**
  while already on bail for another;
- **Public protection** in serious / violent offences.

## Conditions of bail

Common conditions:

- **Residence** at a specified address;
- **Curfew** with electronic monitoring possible;
- **Reporting** to a police station at specified
  intervals;
- **Surety** — third party pledges money forfeit if the
  defendant fails to surrender;
- **Security** — deposit lodged;
- **Surrender of passport**;
- **No contact** with complainant / witnesses;
- **Exclusion** from specified areas;
- **No alcohol** / no drugs (for relevant offences);
- **Engagement** with treatment / support;
- **Specific** to the alleged offence (e.g. driving ban
  pending trial).

## Procedure

### Police bail

After arrest, the custody officer may:

- **Release without charge** subject to a return-to-
  station date;
- **Bail to court** pending first appearance;
- **Charge and bail** to court;
- **Charge and detain** if grounds exist (overnight or
  for early court appearance).

### Court bail

At the first court appearance:

- **Bail application** by the defence;
- **Crown's position** stated (oppose / not oppose /
  specific conditions);
- **Magistrate or judge** decides;
- **Reasons** required for refusal;
- **Conditions** stated and recorded.

## Appeals on bail

A defendant refused bail can:

- **Apply afresh** if circumstances change;
- **Appeal** to the Royal Court within prescribed time;
- **Further apply** at subsequent hearings.

The Crown can also appeal bail decisions in narrow
circumstances.

## Breach of bail

A defendant who:

- **Fails to surrender** at the time specified — separate
  offence (failure to surrender);
- **Breaches a condition** — bail may be revoked, and
  separate offence in some cases;
- **Commits an offence** while on bail — aggravating
  factor at sentence.

## Bail post-charge

Even after charge, bail typically applies pending trial.
The court considers:

- **Strength of the case**;
- **Severity** of the alleged offence;
- **Defendant's history**;
- **Bail track record**;
- **Specific risks** identified;
- **Adequacy of conditions** to mitigate risks.

## Bail post-conviction

Pending appeal:

- **Available** but with stricter test;
- **Defendant must show** good arguable grounds of
  appeal;
- **Lengthy custodial sentence** typically counts against
  bail.

## Sureties

A surety:

- **Pledges** a specified sum forfeit if the defendant
  fails to surrender;
- **Must satisfy** the court of financial standing;
- **Cannot be a co-defendant** or person with conflict;
- **Released** from suretyship if the defendant
  surrenders or the bail is otherwise discharged.

## Vulnerable defendants

Specific considerations for:

- **Juveniles** — typically released to parent / guardian
  unless serious;
- **Mentally ill defendants** — mental-health treatment
  conditions, medical liaison;
- **Defendants with caring responsibilities** — court may
  prefer community-based conditions.

## Practical recommendations

For a defendant facing bail decisions:

- **Engage a Jersey criminal advocate immediately**;
- **Prepare** information helpful to bail — fixed address,
  employment, family support, sureties available;
- **Be realistic** about likely conditions and accept
  reasonable ones;
- **Comply scrupulously** with conditions if granted —
  any breach has serious consequences.
