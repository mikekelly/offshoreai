# Forms of Tenancy

Jersey recognises three principal forms of residential
occupation:

## Periodic tenancy

A tenancy that **continues from period to period** until
ended by notice. The period matches the rent-payment
frequency — monthly rent → monthly periodic tenancy.

- **Default** form where no fixed term is specified;
- **Continues indefinitely** until terminated by notice;
- **Notice periods** — tenant 1 month, landlord 3 months
  (justified) or 12 months (no-fault) — see
  [`notice-periods.md`](notice-periods.md);
- **Post-2025**: rent capped at RPI, once-yearly increases
  only;
- **Governed** by the RTJL 2011 throughout.

## Fixed-term tenancy

A tenancy for a **defined period** with a stated end date.

- **Maximum initial term: 3 years** (post-15 April 2026);
- **Successive fixed terms prohibited** — at expiry, the
  tenancy ends or converts to periodic;
- **Tenant may exit on 1 month's notice** regardless of
  fixed term (post-2025 generalisation);
- **Landlord cannot exit** before expiry except via the
  breach procedure;
- **Notice of non-renewal** — landlord must give 3 months'
  written notice before fixed-term end to prevent
  conversion to periodic.

## Contract lease (≥ 9 years)

A lease of **9 years or more** is **not** a residential
tenancy under the RTJL — it is a Royal-Court-passed
**contract héréditaire**:

- **Royal Court passing** required;
- **Stamp duty** payable per duration;
- **Registered** in the Public Registry;
- Governed by commercial / customary lease law, not RTJL;
- See [`../property/commercial-leases.md`](../property/commercial-leases.md).

## Choosing the form

| Want... | Choose... |
|---|---|
| Maximum flexibility | Periodic |
| Defined commitment for the landlord | Fixed term (≤ 3 years) |
| Long-term security and registration | Contract lease (≥ 9 years) |
| Sub-letting arrangement | Sub-lease (subject to head landlord consent) |

## Pre-2025 fixed-term arrangements

Tenancies entered before 15 April 2026:

- **Continue under pre-amendment law** until expiry of the
  current fixed term;
- **Post-expiry**, the post-amendment regime applies —
  cannot be renewed as a successive fixed term, only
  converted to periodic;
- **Periodic tenancies already in existence** convert
  immediately to the new regime (rent caps, 12-month
  no-fault notice).

## Pitfalls

- **Drafting a 5-year fixed term** post-2025 — unenforceable
  beyond 3 years;
- **Drafting successive fixed terms** — the second is
  unenforceable;
- **Conflating fixed-term with locked-in** — tenants can
  still exit on 1 month's notice;
- **Drafting a long fixed term to avoid Royal Court** —
  any term over 9 years requires Royal Court passing
  regardless.
