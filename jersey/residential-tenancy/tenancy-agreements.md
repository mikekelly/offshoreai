# Tenancy Agreements — Required Form and Contents

## Mandatory written form

Every residential tenancy must be in a **written
agreement**, signed by or on behalf of both landlord and
tenant. Oral arrangements are unenforceable under the
RTJL and risk recharacterisation as lodging (outside
the RTJL).

## Prescribed contents

The agreement must include:

- **Parties** — landlord (and managing agent, if any),
  tenant(s);
- **Property** — full description of the unit;
- **Term** — fixed (≤ 3 years) or periodic;
- **Commencement and termination dates** (where
  applicable);
- **Rent** — amount, frequency, payment method;
- **Deposit** — amount, custody arrangement under the
  Tenants' Deposit Scheme;
- **Rent review** provisions (subject to the post-2025
  RPI cap rules);
- **Contents inventory** — landlord's items in the unit;
- **All fees** chargeable to the tenant (post-2025
  amendment requirement — hidden fees unenforceable);
- **Reference** to the mandatory condition report.

## Mandatory implied terms (cannot be excluded)

The RTJL implies several terms that cannot be contracted
out:

- Tenant may **remove personal fixtures**, subject to
  making good any damage;
- Landlord cannot **unreasonably withhold consent** where
  the agreement requires it (e.g. alterations,
  assignment);
- Tenant has **no obligation** to buy items from the
  landlord or pay key money / premiums.

## Prohibited terms

A clause is unenforceable and may be an offence if it:

- **Excludes** any part of the RTJL;
- Requires the tenant to **pay a premium** or **key money**
  on top of rent and deposit;
- Imposes **hidden fees** not disclosed in the agreement;
- Permits the landlord to **enter** without reasonable
  notice except in emergency.

## Pre-signing requirements

The landlord must:

- Provide the tenant **at least one working day** to read
  the agreement before signing;
- Furnish the **executed agreement** to the tenant as soon
  as possible after signature;
- Furnish a **deposit receipt** as soon as possible after
  the deposit is paid.

Failure to comply is an offence.

## Letting-agent agreements

Where a managing agent is involved:

- The agent's identity and address must be in the
  agreement;
- The agent is bound by the same RTJL requirements as the
  landlord;
- Service on the **landlord** is still required for tenant
  notices unless the agreement explicitly permits service
  on the agent.

## Joint tenancies

Where there are multiple tenants:

- All **sign** the agreement;
- Liability is typically **joint and several** for rent and
  damage;
- Notice from / to one tenant is generally binding on all,
  subject to specific clauses;
- **Sub-letting** typically requires landlord consent.
