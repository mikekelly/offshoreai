# Quiet Enjoyment, Access, and Harassment

## Quiet enjoyment

A residential tenant has the implied right to **quiet
enjoyment** of the unit — the landlord may not interfere
with the tenant's reasonable use and occupation.

This is a baseline obligation that cannot be contracted
out and applies regardless of whether the agreement
mentions it.

## Landlord access

The landlord may enter the unit only:

- **By the tenant's consent**;
- **In emergency** (e.g. burst pipe, fire);
- **For inspection or repair** — typically with **24-48
  hours' written notice** at **reasonable times**, per the
  agreement;
- **For viewings** in the last weeks of a tenancy that's
  ending — with proper notice and the tenant's
  cooperation.

A landlord who enters without consent or notice may
commit:

- A **breach** of the tenancy agreement;
- **Trespass** (a délit / civil wrong);
- **Harassment** if repeated or accompanied by intimidating
  conduct.

## Harassment as a specific offence

The RTJL and adjacent statutes treat **harassment** of a
tenant as a serious matter. Examples:

- Repeated entry without notice;
- Cutting off utilities (water, gas, electricity);
- Removing doors or windows;
- Threats of eviction outside the lawful procedure;
- Anti-social conduct (loud noise, abusive language,
  intimidation);
- Refusing to accept rent in an attempt to manufacture
  arrears;
- Removing the tenant's possessions.

A tenant facing harassment may:

- **Report to the police** (criminal aspect);
- **Apply** to the Petty Debts Court for an injunction;
- **Sue** for damages;
- **Defend** any eviction application by reference to the
  harassment.

## Specific scenarios

### "My landlord keeps showing up unannounced"

- Document times and dates;
- Write formally asking for adherence to the 24-48 hours'
  notice rule;
- Report to police if it persists, especially if
  intimidating;
- Consider Petty Debts Court injunction.

### "My landlord cut off the water"

- Major harassment offence;
- Report to police immediately;
- Document with photographs;
- Restore service via the utility company if possible
  (some allow tenants to take over the account);
- Consider damages claim.

### "My landlord wants to show the property to buyers"

- Reasonable in the last weeks of tenancy;
- Should be by appointment, with notice, at reasonable
  hours;
- Tenant may refuse outside reasonable parameters;
- Cooperation expected but not unlimited.

### "My neighbours are abusive — what about the landlord's duty?"

- The landlord's quiet-enjoyment obligation extends to
  acting against nuisance by **other tenants** in the
  building (where applicable);
- Less applicable where the nuisance is from third
  parties not on the landlord's property;
- The voisinage doctrine and ordinary tort / nuisance
  routes may apply to neighbours directly.
