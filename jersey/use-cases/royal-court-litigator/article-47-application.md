# Article 47 Mistake / Hastiness Application — Practice Points

## The question

A trustee or settlor wants to set aside a trust decision (or
trust settlement) under **Article 47** of the Trusts Law for
**mistake** or **hastiness**. Practical procedure?

## The short answer

Article 47 applications:

- **Articles 47B–47H** of Trusts (Jersey) Law 1984;
- **Royal Court** application by **Representation**;
- **Affidavit** evidence on the mistake / hastiness;
- **Beneficiaries** served and represented;
- **Tax authorities** sometimes notified;
- **Hearing** and reasoned judgment;
- **Set-aside** retrospectively if granted.

Article 47 is **wider** than English *Pitt v Holt* — Jersey is
a friendly venue for mistake claims.

## The proper answer

### The Article 47 regime

- **Article 47B**: trustee's mistake — set aside trustee
  decisions made under mistake of serious character;
- **Article 47G**: settlor's mistake — set aside dispositions
  by settlor under mistake;
- **Other Articles**: related provisions (hastiness, etc.).

### Application content

#### Representation

- **Trustee or settlor** identifies as applicant;
- **Trust** and decision being challenged;
- **Mistake / hastiness** alleged;
- **Set-aside** relief sought.

#### Affidavit

The key document:

- **Circumstances** of the decision;
- **What the applicant intended** at the time;
- **What actually happened**;
- **Mistake / hastiness** identified specifically;
- **Consequences** of leaving the decision standing;
- **Why** the relief is just;
- **Foreign-tax** implications.

#### Supporting documents

- **Trust deed**;
- **Decision documents** (resolution, deed of distribution, etc.);
- **Professional advice** at the time (or absence thereof);
- **Subsequent communication** demonstrating mistake;
- **Tax-authority** correspondence if relevant.

### Three-limb test

Per *S & T Trusts* (2018):

1. **Was there a mistake?** — operative mistake at the relevant
   moment;
2. **What was the nature** of the mistake? — sufficient
   character?
3. **Would it be just and equitable** to grant relief?

This is **less restrictive** than English Pitt v Holt.

### Beneficiary involvement

#### Service

- **All affected beneficiaries** typically served;
- **Representative beneficiary** for minors / unborn;
- **Some confidential** material may be restricted.

#### Beneficiary position

- **Support** (common — most parties prefer set-aside avoiding
  adverse consequence);
- **Neutral**;
- **Oppose** (rare).

### Tax-authority engagement

For tax-driven mistake applications:

- **Foreign tax authority** sometimes notified;
- **HMRC**: typically respects Royal Court set-aside;
- **IRS**: more uncertain — engages independently;
- **Other tax authorities**: case-by-case.

The Jersey set-aside provides the **strongest possible basis**
for the foreign-tax position.

### Hearing

#### Standard

- **Royal Court Samedi**;
- **Bailiff / Commissioner** sitting;
- **Counsel** presents;
- **Affidavit evidence** central;
- **Cross-examination** possible but uncommon.

#### Outcome

- **Set-aside ordered** with effective date typically the
  original decision;
- **Retroactive** effect;
- **Tax-authority position** then engaged separately.

### Common scenarios

#### Tax-trigger trust distribution

- **Distribution made** without realising tax consequence;
- **HMRC** would tax the distribution at penal rate;
- **Article 47B** set-aside;
- **Distribution unwound**;
- **Beneficiary's tax position** restored.

#### Mistaken trust settlement

- **Settlor settled** trust on incorrect tax advice;
- **Significant tax cost** of leaving in place;
- **Article 47G** set-aside;
- **Trust unwound** retroactively;
- **Settlor's tax position** restored.

#### Inadequate advice scenario

- **Trustee acted** without proper consideration of consequences;
- **Mistake of serious character**;
- **Article 47B** set-aside.

### Time considerations

- **Move quickly** — delay can harm application;
- **Specific limitation** rules apply;
- **Earlier is better** for fresh, well-documented mistake.

### Cost

- **Trustee cost** from fund (Beddoe protection sometimes
  applied);
- **Beneficiary cost** from fund typically;
- **Tax authority involvement** can add complexity.

### Specific drafting points

#### Mistake specificity

- **What the applicant thought** vs **reality**;
- **Material difference** between expectation and outcome;
- **Documented** in contemporaneous records where possible.

#### Sufficiency of character

- **Major consequence** of leaving in place;
- **Not** trivial regret;
- **Serious enough** to warrant Court intervention.

#### Just-and-equitable

- **Third-party rights** considered;
- **Beneficiary impact**;
- **Practical** consequences of set-aside.

## What to do next

For an active situation:

1. **Identify** the mistake / hastiness specifically;
2. **Move quickly** — engage counsel;
3. **Document** the circumstances;
4. **Draft** Representation and affidavit;
5. **Serve** beneficiaries;
6. **Coordinate** with tax authorities if relevant;
7. **Attend** hearing;
8. **Implement** set-aside.

## Pitfalls

- **Delay**: weakens the application;
- **Insufficient evidence** of mistake;
- **Foreign-tax-authority complications** post-set-aside;
- **Third-party rights** affected.
