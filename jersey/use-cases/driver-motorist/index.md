# Driver / Motorist — Use Cases

## Who this section is for

You are someone **driving or owning a vehicle in Jersey** —
a resident motorist, a new arrival registering a car, a
visitor, a fleet manager, a parent of a new driver, or
someone facing a traffic-offence prosecution.

Your primary statutes are the **Road Traffic (Jersey) Law
1956**, the **Motor Traffic (Third Party Insurance) (Jersey)
Law 1948**, the **Motor Vehicle Registration (Jersey) Law
1993**, and the **Highways (Jersey) Law 1956**. Traffic
offences are tried in the **Magistrate's Court** with
appeals to the Royal Court.

This persona is currently **a scaffold** — see
[`../../COVERAGE-AUDIT.md`](../../COVERAGE-AUDIT.md), where
Road Traffic (Jersey) Law 1956 is ranked the #2 gap.

## Canonical questions

### Licensing

- What licence do I need to drive in Jersey? How does it
  compare to a UK licence?
- Provisional licences, learner drivers, new-driver rules
- Exchanging a foreign licence for a Jersey one
- Medical fitness and licence renewal

### Vehicle ownership

- Registering a vehicle in Jersey
- Importing a vehicle from the UK or EU
- Vehicle Emissions Duty (VED-Jersey)
- MOT-equivalent inspections (or absence of)
- Insurance — third-party minimum requirement

### Offences

- Speeding — fines, points, totting up
- Driving without insurance
- Driving while disqualified
- Drink-driving — the limit, breath tests, refusal to
  provide a sample
- Drug-driving
- Careless driving vs dangerous driving
- Failing to stop / failing to report an accident

### Procedure

- Stopped by police — what are my rights?
- Penalty notices vs court summons
- Magistrate's Court traffic procedure
- Appealing to the Royal Court
- Disqualification — how long, what factors

### Civil

- I was in a car accident — who pays?
- The Motor Insurers' Bureau (MIB)-equivalent in Jersey
- Whiplash / personal injury claims
- Subrogation between insurers

### Parishes and parking

- Parking fines — challenging them
- Parking in St Helier — discs, paycards, residents'
  schemes
- Parish vs States enforcement
