# Designing a Multi-Jurisdictional Offshore Structure

## The question

You're advising an international family / business on an
**offshore structure** that may use **multiple jurisdictions**.
How do you decide which jurisdiction does what?

## The short answer

Each major offshore jurisdiction has **specific strengths**:

- **Jersey** — trust law (Article 9A), banking depth, EU /
  UK proximity;
- **Guernsey** — captive insurance (largest European), PCC
  origin;
- **Isle of Man** — eGaming, life insurance, personal tax
  cap;
- **Cayman** — hedge fund / PE fund domiciliation, STAR
  trusts;
- **BVI** — volume / cost-efficient corporate vehicles,
  VISTA trusts;
- **Bermuda** — captives and reinsurance, ILS, Solvency II
  equivalent.

Designing a structure typically involves **matching
jurisdictions to functions**.

## The proper answer

### Strategic framework

For a multi-jurisdictional structure, allocate functions:

#### Holding / trust function

Where does the **ultimate trust** or **holding company** sit?

- **Jersey** for trust matters (Article 9A reserved powers,
  Article 9 firewall);
- **Guernsey** for similar;
- **Cayman** for STAR trust orphan structures;
- **BVI** for VISTA trust holding family business shares.

#### Operating / investment vehicles

Where do operating subsidiaries / investment vehicles sit?

- **BVI** for **volume** holding companies;
- **Cayman** for fund investments;
- **Jersey** for sophisticated commercial holding companies;
- **Bermuda** for insurance investment vehicles.

#### Insurance function

Where is **captive insurance** or **reinsurance**?

- **Bermuda** for largest captives, reinsurance;
- **Guernsey** for established captives;
- **Cayman** for healthcare captives;
- **IoM** for life insurance specialty;
- **Jersey** for smaller captive market.

#### Fund function

Where is the **fund** domiciled?

- **Cayman** for hedge fund and PE;
- **Jersey** for EU-focused fund;
- **Bermuda** for ILS funds;
- **BVI** for cost-sensitive funds.

### Common structures

#### Family-office structure

**Typical layout**:

- **Top**: Jersey or Cayman trust (with Jersey reserved
  powers OR Cayman STAR trust);
- **Holding companies**: BVI BCs;
- **Operating subsidiaries**: appropriate jurisdiction;
- **Captive insurance**: Bermuda (if material) or Guernsey;
- **Investment fund**: Cayman (if external investors) or
  internal LP.

#### PE fund structure

**Typical layout**:

- **Fund**: Cayman ELP;
- **GP**: Cayman or Delaware;
- **Manager**: Jersey, Cayman, or onshore;
- **Carry vehicle**: Jersey trust;
- **Portfolio holding companies**: BVI or jurisdiction-
  specific.

#### Reinsurance group structure

**Typical layout**:

- **Top trust**: Bermuda or Jersey;
- **Bermuda** reinsurance entities (operating);
- **Jersey / Cayman** holding companies for non-Bermuda
  business;
- **Specific** sectoral subsidiaries elsewhere.

### Choosing jurisdictions — decision factors

#### Tax

- Jersey: zero-ten plus GST;
- Cayman / BVI: no income tax (with Pillar Two for in-scope);
- Bermuda: 15% corporate income tax for in-scope (2025);
- Specific Pillar Two implications.

#### Substance

- Modern substance regimes apply in all offshore
  jurisdictions;
- Substance requirements differ by activity;
- Substance capacity differs:
  - Jersey, Guernsey, IoM: good capacity;
  - Cayman, Bermuda, BVI: variable.

#### Regulator quality

- **All** major offshore regulators are reputable;
- Specific strengths differ;
- Bermuda BMA for insurance is world-leading;
- Cayman CIMA for funds is dominant;
- Jersey JFSC for trust / multi-sector is well-respected.

#### Time zone

- Jersey, Guernsey, IoM: European time zone;
- Cayman, BVI: US East Coast time zone;
- Bermuda: US East Coast time zone.

#### Privacy

- All offshore jurisdictions have BO transparency to
  authorities;
- Public access varies — most have limited public access;
- Specific privacy considerations.

#### Cost

- BVI lowest for volume;
- Cayman competitive;
- Jersey, Guernsey, Bermuda higher costs but more
  sophistication.

### Practical structure design

For a typical international family wealth structure:

#### Step 1: Identify functions

- Trust / governance;
- Holding investments;
- Operating businesses;
- Family residences;
- Captive insurance;
- Charitable;
- Specific other needs.

#### Step 2: Match to jurisdictions

Map each function to the most appropriate jurisdiction based
on:

- Specific legal features needed;
- Tax considerations (home + offshore);
- Family preferences;
- Existing relationships;
- Operational considerations.

#### Step 3: Integration

Design how jurisdictions integrate:

- **Trust at top** with subsidiary holdings;
- **Cross-border** documentation;
- **Specific** Royal Court / equivalent recognition;
- **Specific** tax treaty considerations.

#### Step 4: Substance

For each in-scope entity:

- Where will substance be?
- Who will direct and manage?
- What documents needed?
- Specific compliance obligations.

#### Step 5: Implementation

- Engage **counsel in each jurisdiction**;
- **Coordinate** establishment;
- **Implement** governance procedures;
- **Train** key personnel.

## Common combinations

### Jersey trust + Cayman fund

- **Jersey trust** holds shares in Cayman fund manager / GP;
- **Cayman fund** holds investments;
- **Common** for hedge fund manager wealth planning.

### Jersey trust + Bermuda captive

- **Jersey trust** as ultimate beneficial owner;
- **Bermuda captive** insurer;
- **Common** for high-net-worth family with self-insurance.

### Jersey trust + BVI holding companies

- **Jersey trust** at top;
- **BVI BCs** holding specific assets;
- **Common** for asset segregation.

### Multi-jurisdictional family office

- **Jersey trust** at ultimate top;
- **BVI BC** for general holdings;
- **Cayman fund** for managed investments;
- **Guernsey PCC** for captive elements;
- **IoM** life insurance for specific tax wrappers.

## What to do next

For a multi-jurisdictional structure:

1. **Map** all functions needed;
2. **Identify** specific jurisdictional preferences for each
   function;
3. **Coordinate** legal advice across jurisdictions;
4. **Design** integration carefully;
5. **Document** the structure clearly;
6. **Implement** in coordinated sequence;
7. **Maintain** ongoing compliance across all
   jurisdictions.

## Pitfalls

- **Over-complicating** the structure;
- **Mismatching** function to jurisdiction;
- **Inadequate** substance in any jurisdiction;
- **Inconsistent** documentation;
- **Specific** unintended tax / regulatory interactions;
- **Inadequate** ongoing compliance.
