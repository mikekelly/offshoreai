# Jersey as a Mixed Legal System — Briefing for Foreign Lawyers

## The question

You're a foreign lawyer approaching a Jersey matter. You've
been told Jersey law is **"like English law but a bit
different"**. What do you actually need to know?

## The short answer

Jersey is a **mixed legal system**:

- **Norman customary law** foundation (Pothier, Le Geyt);
- **Civil-law** influences (cause, broader specific
  performance);
- **Common-law** practical convergence (especially in
  commercial, trust, company law);
- **Distinct** from English law in real-property,
  succession, contract foundations;
- **Modern statutes** in most commercial areas have been
  designed to be familiar to English / common-law lawyers.

For **most commercial matters**, the practical outcome
broadly aligns with English law. For **traditional areas**
(property, succession, certain contract issues), Jersey
diverges significantly.

## The proper answer

### Sources of Jersey law

1. **Jersey statute** (Bailiwick laws);
2. **Jersey case law** (Royal Court, Court of Appeal, Privy
   Council in Jersey appeals);
3. **Customary law** (Norman-derived, with Le Geyt as
   principal authority);
4. **English law** as **persuasive authority** (not binding,
   but highly influential in commercial matters);
5. **ECHR** through Human Rights (Jersey) Law 2000.

See [`../../legal-system/sources-of-law.md`](../../legal-system/sources-of-law.md).

### Areas of practical convergence with English law

The following Jersey areas are **practically aligned** with
English law (specific statutes notwithstanding):

#### Companies law

- **CJL 1991** aligned with UK Companies Act structure;
- **Director duties** broadly similar;
- **English company law cases** persuasive;
- **Specific** Jersey divergences (no-par-value historically,
  cell companies).

#### Trust law

- **TJL 1984** aligned with English trust principles;
- **English trust cases** highly persuasive;
- **Specific** Jersey divergences:
  - **Article 47B-J** mistake regime wider than *Pitt v
    Holt*;
  - **Article 9** firewall robust against forced heirship;
  - **Article 9A** reserved powers expansive.

#### Banking / commercial

- **Broadly** aligned with English commercial law;
- **English banking** law persuasive;
- **Specific** Jersey rules (top-50 policy, JFSC
  supervision).

#### Insurance

- **Insurance Business Law 1996** with Solvency II-aligned
  concepts;
- **English insurance** law principles persuasive.

#### Tort

- **Negligence** principles aligned;
- **English cases** persuasive;
- **Specific** Jersey doctrines (voisinage).

### Areas of significant divergence

#### Real property

- **Norman customary** foundation;
- **Hypothec** (not English mortgage);
- **Contract passing** before Royal Court (not English-style
  conveyancing);
- **Servitudes** (specific Jersey rules);
- **Customary inheritance** for real property;
- **Loi (1880) sur la propriété foncière**.

See [`../../property/index.md`](../../property/index.md).

#### Succession

- **WSL 1993** with statutory **légitime** (forced heirship
  for spouse / children of movable estate);
- **Customary inheritance** for real property;
- **Different** from English testamentary freedom subject
  to family-provision applications.

See [`../../succession/index.md`](../../succession/index.md).

#### Contract

- **Pothier**-derived elements;
- **Cause** doctrine (civil-law foundation);
- **Specific** Jersey rules on formation, performance,
  remedies;
- **Specific performance** broader in Jersey than English
  equity.

See [`../../contract/index.md`](../../contract/index.md).

#### Customary law generally

- **Norman customary** foundation throughout;
- **Le Geyt** as principal source;
- **Cited** in modern Royal Court cases;
- **Specific** customary doctrines preserved.

See [`../../legal-system/customary-law.md`](../../legal-system/customary-law.md).

### The role of English authority

In Jersey:

- **English cases** are **persuasive** authority;
- **UK Supreme Court** / **House of Lords** highly weighted;
- **Court of Appeal (E&W)** highly weighted;
- **Persuasive** to the Royal Court but **not binding**;
- **Jersey** has consciously diverged in specific areas.

For foreign lawyers approaching Jersey law:

- **Don't assume** Jersey law is English law;
- **Engage** Jersey advocates / solicitors for Jersey
  opinions;
- **Specific** statutes and case law apply;
- **Specific** Jersey rules need specific Jersey advice.

### Privy Council role

Final appeal from Jersey lies to the **Judicial Committee of
the Privy Council**:

- **English-staffed** typically;
- **Binding** on Jersey Royal Court when in Jersey appeals;
- **Persuasive** otherwise;
- **Significant** Privy Council jurisprudence in Jersey
  trust / commercial law.

See [`../../legal-system/privy-council.md`](../../legal-system/privy-council.md).

### Practical implications

#### For drafting Jersey contracts

- **Choice of law** clauses should specify Jersey law where
  intended;
- **Specific** Jersey rules on formation may surprise
  English drafters;
- **Restrictive covenants** subject to Jersey unreasonableness
  review (similar but not identical to English);
- **Entire agreement** clauses subject to Jersey rules.

#### For litigation strategy

- **Royal Court procedure** different from English CPR;
- **Specific** Jersey procedural concepts (Representation,
  Order of Justice, Doleance);
- **Jurat** fact-finding (specific Channel Islands feature);
- **Engage** Jersey advocates from outset.

#### For transactional work

- **Property transfers** require Royal Court passing;
- **Specific** Jersey conveyancing procedures;
- **Specific** Jersey CDD requirements;
- **Specific** Jersey beneficial-ownership rules.

#### For estate planning

- **Légitime** constraints on movable estate;
- **Customary** real-property inheritance;
- **Specific** Jersey will formalities;
- **Cross-jurisdictional** planning essential for
  international families.

## Common foreign-lawyer misconceptions

### "Jersey is just like the UK"

- **No** — Jersey is its own jurisdiction;
- **Not part of UK**, not subject to UK law;
- **Different** statutes, courts, regulator.

### "Jersey trust law is the same as English"

- **Similar** but distinctively Jersey;
- **TJL 1984** has specific provisions (Article 9, 9A, 47B-J);
- **Engage Jersey** counsel for opinions.

### "English contracts work in Jersey"

- **Choice-of-law** matters;
- **Specific Jersey** rules apply in some areas;
- **Specific** Jersey customary considerations.

### "Jersey property is easy to deal in"

- **Specific** Royal Court process;
- **CHWL** restrictions on non-Entitled buyers;
- **Specific** Jersey procedural requirements;
- **Engage** Jersey property lawyers.

### "English law is binding in Jersey"

- **No** — only persuasive;
- **Jersey statute** prevails;
- **Specific** Jersey divergences need specific analysis.

## What to do next

For foreign lawyer approaching Jersey matter:

1. **Identify** whether the matter is in a Jersey-divergent
   area (property, succession, customary law);
2. **Engage** Jersey counsel from the outset for opinions;
3. **Read** the topic-specific Jersey section before
   discussing with Jersey counsel;
4. **Don't** rely on English-law extrapolation;
5. **Use** the Jersey corpus as a starting point but
   confirm with Jersey advocates.

## Pitfalls

- **Assuming** Jersey law is English law;
- **Drafting** with English-law assumptions;
- **Not engaging** Jersey counsel early;
- **Specific** unrecognised Jersey divergences;
- **Procedural** errors in Royal Court matters.
