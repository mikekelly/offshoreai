# Foreign-Domiciled Settlor — UK IHT of Jersey Trust

## The question

A **foreign-domiciled settlor** (or now under UK's residence-
based rules, non-long-term-resident) settles assets into a
Jersey trust. What's the UK IHT position?

## The short answer

Historically (pre-April 2025):

- **Non-UK-domiciled settlor** could settle **excluded property**
  trust (non-UK assets) outside UK IHT;
- **UK-situs assets** always in UK IHT;
- **Deemed-domicile** after 17 of 20 years brings worldwide
  assets in.

Post-April 2025 (UK IHT reform):

- **Residence-based** test replaces domicile;
- **Long-term resident** (10+ years UK-resident) brings
  worldwide trust assets into UK IHT;
- **Pre-existing trusts** have transitional protections;
- **Significant change** for cross-border family planning.

Model for the **specific timeline** of UK residence.

## The proper answer

### Pre-April 2025 framework

The traditional **domicile-based** UK IHT rules:

- **Excluded property trust**: non-UK assets settled by non-UK-
  dom settlor were outside UK IHT;
- **UK situs**: always within UK IHT;
- **Deemed domicile**: long UK-residence (17 of 20 years
  historically) caused deemed-UK-domicile for IHT.

### April 2025 reforms

Major reform to **residence-based** rules:

- **Long-term resident** = 10+ years UK-resident in last 20;
- **Once long-term resident**, worldwide assets brought into UK
  IHT;
- **Trusts settled by long-term resident** in scope;
- **Transitional rules** for previously-settled trusts.

### Practical scenarios

#### Pre-reform settled trust by non-dom

- **Excluded-property** status historically;
- **Transitional protection** post-April 2025 for trusts settled
  before specific dates;
- **Continued review** needed.

#### Newly settled trust by long-term-resident

- **In UK IHT** under new rules;
- **Relevant property regime** applies;
- **10-year anniversary and exit charges**.

#### Truly non-resident settlor

- **Not long-term resident** of UK;
- **Non-UK situs assets** in trust outside UK IHT;
- **UK situs assets** in trust within UK IHT (always).

### Practical advice for cross-border counsel

#### Identify residence status

- Has settlor been UK-resident 10+ years in last 20?
- What's their trajectory? Approaching threshold?
- Any specific residence-based exemptions or transitional
  protections?

#### Pre-settlement planning

- **Settle before long-term-resident status** possible;
- **Choice of assets**: non-UK situs preferable for non-LTR
  settlor;
- **Trust structure**: discretionary typically;
- **Beneficiary selection** to manage future distributions.

#### Post-settlement maintenance

- **Continued review** of residence trajectory;
- **Adjust** structure if settlor becomes LTR;
- **Plan distributions** considering 10-year-anniversary timing.

### Foreign settlor specific points

#### Connection to UK

- **UK assets** in trust always within UK IHT;
- **UK-situs vs non-UK-situs** determination by asset type;
- **Beneficial-ownership** look-through in some cases.

#### Settlor's intent

- **Genuine non-UK** settlor with non-UK assets and non-UK
  beneficiaries: typically outside UK IHT;
- **Mixed** (UK-resident beneficiaries, UK-situs assets):
  partial UK IHT exposure;
- **UK-connected** settlor (long-term resident): worldwide
  exposure.

#### Beneficiary-level UK exposure

Even if settlor's settlement was outside UK IHT:

- **UK-resident beneficiaries** receiving distributions can
  trigger UK income tax / CGT under attribution rules (TOAA,
  s.87);
- **Beneficiary-level** UK tax separate from trust-level UK
  IHT;
- **Coordinated planning** essential.

### Jersey trust mechanics

The Jersey trust itself:

- **Article 9 firewall**: jurisdiction-of-law protection;
- **Article 9A**: reserved powers don't invalidate;
- **Article 47**: mistake-based set-aside available;
- **Tax-efficient at Jersey level**: no Jersey CGT / IHT.

The **Jersey-side** is generally favourable; the **UK-side**
under reformed rules is the active concern.

### Cross-jurisdictional coordination

For foreign-domiciled settlors with cross-border family:

- **UK-tax adviser** essential for UK exposure modelling;
- **Settlor-jurisdiction** tax adviser for home-country
  position;
- **Beneficiary-jurisdiction** tax advice;
- **Jersey counsel** for trust structuring.

### Common scenarios

#### EU-domiciled retiree moving to UK

- Pre-move: non-UK-dom, excluded property historically;
- Post-move: residence accruing toward long-term resident;
- **Window** for non-UK-IHT settlement planning;
- **Becomes** in scope after 10 years.

#### Long-term UK-resident expat

- **Long-term resident** under new rules;
- **Worldwide** assets in UK IHT;
- **Jersey trust** offers no UK-IHT shelter for new settlements;
- **Pre-settled** trusts may retain transitional protection.

#### Non-UK family with UK property

- **UK property** always within UK IHT regardless of structure;
- **Jersey trust holding UK property** doesn't shield;
- **Non-UK assets** treated per general rules.

## What to do next

For UK-connected planning:

1. **Map** settlor's UK-residence trajectory;
2. **Identify** asset situs;
3. **Engage** UK tax adviser;
4. **Plan** timing of settlement vs UK-residence triggers;
5. **Document** the analysis;
6. **Refresh** annually as residence evolves.

## Pitfalls

- **Stale planning**: 2025 reform changed materially;
- **Beneficiary-level UK tax** missed;
- **Asset-situs determinations** glossed over;
- **Transitional rules** misapplied.
