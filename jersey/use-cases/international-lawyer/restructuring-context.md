# Continuance / Merger of Jersey Companies in Restructuring Context

## The question

Your client's group includes Jersey companies and is undergoing
**restructuring** (debt-and-equity restructuring, group
simplification, geographic migration). What are the Jersey
options?

## The short answer

For restructuring involving Jersey companies:

- **Continuance** (Part 18C) for migration of Jersey entity
  to another jurisdiction (or vice versa);
- **Statutory merger** (Part 18B) for combination of two or
  more entities into one;
- **Scheme of arrangement** (Article 125) for creditor / member
  compromise with court sanction;
- **Demerger** (Part 18BA) for splitting Jersey entity into
  multiple resulting entities;
- **Reduction of capital** (Article 61A) and **share buybacks**
  (Article 57) for capital adjustments.

Many cross-border restructurings combine multiple of these.

## The proper answer

### Continuance (Part 18C)

For jurisdiction migration:

- **Outbound**: Jersey to foreign — JFSC consent required;
- **Inbound**: foreign to Jersey — JFSC consent for entry;
- **Same legal entity** continues across the move;
- **6-month** typical timeline.

Common scenario: post-acquisition relocation of acquired
Jersey entity to acquirer's jurisdiction.

### Statutory merger (Part 18B)

For combining entities:

- **Two or more bodies** merge into one;
- **Cross-border** permitted (with JFSC consent);
- **Universal succession** of assets / liabilities;
- **Sole surviving entity** post-merger.

Common scenario: group simplification eliminating intermediate
holding entities.

### Scheme of arrangement (Article 125)

For creditor / member compromise:

- **Court-sanctioned** procedure;
- **75% in value + majority in number** per class;
- **Binds dissenters**;
- **Used** for debt restructurings, M&A, complex
  reorganisations.

Common scenario: debt-for-equity restructuring of insolvent
Jersey corporate.

### Demerger (Part 18BA)

For splitting:

- **One entity** divides into multiple;
- **Court-free** typically;
- **Universal succession** in reverse — assets allocated to
  resulting entities.

Common scenario: spinning off a business unit ahead of
divestment or separate listing.

### Reductions of capital and share buybacks

For capital adjustments:

- **Article 61A reduction**: directors' solvency statement,
  member resolution, no court approval needed;
- **Article 57 buyback**: share repurchase by company;
- **Both routes** facilitate returns to shareholders /
  rationalisation.

### Combining instruments

Complex restructurings often combine:

- **Reduction of capital** + **share buyback** for return of
  capital;
- **Demerger** + **continuance** for geographic split and
  migration;
- **Statutory merger** + **scheme** for negotiated combinations
  with creditor compromise;
- **Sequential** transactions for staged outcomes.

### Tax and regulatory considerations

#### Jersey level

- **No CGT** at Jersey level;
- **Substance** continuing through restructuring;
- **JFSC** notifications / consents for some changes.

#### Cross-border

- **Foreign-jurisdiction** tax implications;
- **Recognition** of Jersey procedures abroad;
- **Treaty** considerations;
- **Pillar Two** impact for large groups.

### Foreign-lawyer considerations

For non-Jersey counsel:

- **Jersey-procedural** knowledge needed;
- **Engage Jersey counsel** for Jersey-side work;
- **Coordinate** with foreign-jurisdiction procedures;
- **Allow time** — Jersey procedures take months.

### Common scenarios

#### Distressed multi-jurisdiction group

- **Schemes of arrangement** in multiple jurisdictions
  including Jersey;
- **Coordinated approvals** across creditor groups;
- **Survival entity** in one jurisdiction;
- **Continuance / merger** to simplify post-restructure.

#### Pre-IPO simplification

- **Mergers** to consolidate holding structures;
- **Continuance** to move TopCo to listing jurisdiction;
- **Demergers** to separate non-IPO businesses;
- **Reductions of capital** to clean balance sheet.

#### Post-acquisition integration

- **Continuance** of acquired Jersey entity to acquirer
  jurisdiction;
- **Merger** with acquirer's existing entity;
- **Restructuring** of operations under new ownership.

### Insolvent restructuring

If Jersey entity insolvent:

- **Creditors' winding-up** under Part 21;
- **Scheme of arrangement** as alternative to liquidation;
- **Désastre** under Bankruptcy (Désastre) Law 1990;
- **Cross-border insolvency** coordination.

## What to do next

For a restructuring:

1. **Identify** the desired outcome;
2. **Choose** the appropriate Jersey instruments;
3. **Engage** Jersey counsel;
4. **Coordinate** with cross-border procedures;
5. **Execute** with proper compliance.

## Pitfalls

- **Procedural complexity**: cross-border restructurings have
  many moving parts;
- **Timing mismatches** across jurisdictions;
- **Tax-event surprises** at each step;
- **Substance gaps** during transition.
