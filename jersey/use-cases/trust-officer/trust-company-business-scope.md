# What Counts as "Trust Company Business"?

## The question

You are setting up an entity to provide trust / company / family-
office services in Jersey. Will it be **trust company business
(TCB)** requiring **JFSC registration**?

## The short answer

TCB is defined in **Article 2 of the Financial Services (Jersey)
Law 1998** as **carrying on by way of business** any of:

- Acting as a **professional trustee**;
- Acting as a **foundation council member** or **guardian** in a
  qualified-member capacity;
- Acting as a **company director / secretary** on a commercial
  basis;
- Providing a **registered office** on a commercial basis;
- Acting as a **partner in a partnership** on a commercial basis;
- **Administering** trusts, foundations, companies, partnerships
  on a commercial basis.

"**By way of business**" means **commercially provided** —
typically for remuneration, repeatedly, and to multiple clients.

## The proper answer

### The "by way of business" test

The threshold concept is **commercial provision**. The
indicators:

- **Remuneration** — fees, commissions, etc.;
- **Repeated provision** — not a one-off;
- **Multiple clients** — not a single arrangement;
- **Holding out** — advertising / marketing the service;
- **Organisational infrastructure** — premises, staff, systems.

A **family member** acting as trustee for a family trust **on a
gratuitous basis** is typically **not** carrying on TCB.

A **professional trust company** is squarely within TCB.

### Specific activities captured

#### Acting as professional trustee

The classic TCB activity:

- Acting as **sole trustee** of a trust;
- Acting as **co-trustee**;
- Through a **corporate trustee** entity;
- For multiple unrelated clients.

#### Foundation roles

After the Foundations Law 2009:

- Acting as **qualified member** of a foundation council on a
  commercial basis;
- Acting as **guardian** for foundations on a commercial basis.

#### Company services

The "**company services provider**" subset:

- **Director services** — providing professional directors to
  client companies;
- **Secretarial services** — company secretary on a commercial
  basis;
- **Registered office services** — providing the corporate
  address;
- **Nominee services** — holding shares as nominee.

#### Administration services

Even where the entity isn't the trustee / director itself:

- **Administering** trusts on behalf of trustees;
- **Administering** foundations on behalf of councils;
- **Administering** companies on behalf of directors;
- **Bookkeeping, records-keeping, secretariat support**.

### Exemptions

#### Family-PTC exemption

A **Private Trust Company (PTC)** acting only for **trusts of a
single family** can typically operate under a JFSC exemption
rather than full TCB registration:

- **Family-only client base** (specifically defined);
- **Reduced regulatory burden** but still some oversight;
- Common structure: foundation owns the PTC, family-member
  directors plus a regulated TCB providing services.

#### Single-trust exemption

A person acting only as trustee of **a single trust** that is
**not held out for business** is typically not TCB.

#### Family-member trustees

A family member who is **trustee gratuitously** for the family
trust is not TCB.

### Consequences of being TCB

If the activity is TCB:

- **Registration with JFSC** is required;
- **Fit-and-proper** standards apply to principal and key
  persons;
- The entity is subject to the **TCB Code of Practice**;
- **AML / CFT obligations** apply;
- **Annual fees** and reporting;
- **Civil and criminal liability** for unauthorised TCB
  activity.

## What to do next

1. **Map** the proposed activities against the TCB definition;
2. **Identify** any potential exemption (family PTC, etc.);
3. **If TCB**, engage with the JFSC about registration timing
   and structure;
4. **Prepare** the registration application;
5. **If exempt**, document the exemption basis carefully;
6. **Establish** ongoing compliance arrangements proportionate
   to the regime.

## Pitfalls

- **Drift into TCB**: a structure starting as single-family can
  expand to TCB territory by gradual addition of unrelated
  clients;
- **Hidden TCB activity**: providing services through related
  entities can still be TCB;
- **Treating family-PTC exemption as automatic**: must be
  confirmed with the JFSC for the specific structure.
