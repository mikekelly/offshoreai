# Trust / Fiduciary Officer — Use Cases

The questions a trust officer at a Jersey-licensed **trust-company
business (TCB)** actually has, in the order in which they tend to come
up.

The TCB regime is set under the **[Financial Services (Jersey) Law
1998][fsjl]**: a person carrying on **trust company business** (broadly,
the provision of fiduciary services in the course of business) must
be registered with the JFSC and must comply with the **TCB Code of
Practice**. Most users of this directory will be regulated principal
persons or key persons in a registered TCB; some of the
question-answers below also apply to in-house family-office trustees
(typically structured as **private trust companies** under an
exemption).

## Status

This is a deep-but-narrow build: three questions are written end-to-end
to demonstrate the depth, the rest are stubs with intended scope. The
three written questions cover the **most common daily decisions** by a
trust officer and exercise the **most-asked-about Articles** of the
Trusts (Jersey) Law 1984 (Articles 47, 51 and the disclosure
jurisdiction inherited from *Schmidt v Rosewood*).
