# Par-Value vs No-Par-Value Share Capital

## The question

Jersey allows both **par-value** and **no-par-value** companies.
Which should your TopCo be?

## The short answer

**No-par-value** is the **modern default** for Jersey companies:

- **Simpler** capital structure;
- **No nominal value** to track;
- **Stated capital account** instead;
- **More flexible** for distributions, reductions, restructurings;
- **Familiar** to international counterparties.

**Par-value** is sometimes used:

- For **historical compatibility** with predecessor entities;
- For **specific tax characterisations** in certain jurisdictions;
- For **listing-rule** prerequisites (some exchanges prefer
  par-value).

## The proper answer

### Par-value company (Article 3E)

Traditional company with shares of **nominal value**:

- Shares have **par value** (e.g. £1 each);
- **Share premium** above par recorded separately;
- **Capital structure**: par value × number of shares + share
  premium;
- **Reductions of capital** affect par value separately from
  premium.

### No-par-value company (Article 3F)

Modern Jersey form without nominal value:

- Shares have **no par value**;
- All consideration credited to **stated capital account**;
- **Capital structure**: stated capital;
- **Reductions of stated capital** affect the single account.

### Why no-par-value typically wins

- **Simpler** accounting and capital structure;
- **More flexible** for distributions and reductions;
- **No artifice** of par value (which doesn't usually reflect
  economic value);
- **Familiar** to PE / institutional investors;
- **Conversion** between share classes simpler;
- **No share-premium / par split** to maintain.

### When par-value applies

#### Conversion from existing company

- If the Jersey company is **continued from** a foreign par-
  value company, the par-value structure may be retained;
- **Conversion to no-par-value** possible under Article 40A.

#### Specific tax characterisation

- Some jurisdictions characterise par-value vs no-par-value
  differently for tax;
- **Modelling** by foreign tax advisers may indicate one over
  the other.

#### Listing rules

- Some exchanges prefer par-value;
- **LSE**, **Euronext** generally accept both;
- **Confirm** specific exchange requirements.

#### Counterparty preference

- Some counterparties (especially older or conservative
  institutions) may prefer par-value.

### Distribution mechanics

#### Par-value

- **Distribution** of share premium possible under specific
  procedures;
- **Reduction of par** requires Article 61 process;
- **Distribution sources** distinguished (capital vs revenue
  for tax purposes in some jurisdictions).

#### No-par-value

- **Distribution from stated capital** straightforward via
  Article 115 distribution mechanics;
- **Reduction of stated capital** through Article 61A solvency
  statement;
- **Simpler** overall.

### Practical setup

For a new Jersey TopCo:

- **Choose no-par-value** unless specific reason for par-value;
- **Memorandum and articles** specify the company type;
- **Conversion later** possible if circumstances change.

## What to do next

For a new TopCo:

1. **Default to no-par-value**;
2. **Check** foreign-tax modelling for any par-value
   preference;
3. **Confirm** listing requirements if pre-IPO;
4. **Document** the choice.

## Pitfalls

- **Defaulting to par-value** out of habit (or because foreign
  advisers default to it);
- **Foreign-tax surprise**: some jurisdictions characterise
  share-premium / paid-in capital differently;
- **Conversion timing** if listing rules change requirement.
