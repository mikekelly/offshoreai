# Economic Substance — What Does My TopCo Need to Do?

## The question

The **Taxation (Companies — Economic Substance) (Jersey) Law
2019** applies to your Jersey TopCo. What substance do you
need?

## The short answer

For a typical TopCo holding shares in operating subsidiaries:

- **Pure equity-holding** classification typically applies;
- **Reduced substance test**: basic Jersey company-law
  compliance + adequate people / premises to discharge
  obligations;
- Met by your **service-provider arrangements** (registered
  office, directors, secretary) in most cases.

For TopCos with **active business activities** (financing, IP,
distribution, fund management, etc.):

- **Full substance** required;
- **Directed and managed in Jersey**;
- **Adequate employees, premises, expenditure**;
- **CIGAs** (core income-generating activities) in Jersey.

## The proper answer

### Substance Law scope

The 2019 Law applies to Jersey-tax-resident companies (and
LPs / LLCs) carrying on **relevant activities**:

- Banking;
- Insurance;
- Fund management;
- Financing and leasing;
- Headquarters;
- Shipping;
- **Holding company business** (pure equity holding);
- IP business;
- Distribution and service centre.

### Pure equity-holding test

For a TopCo that **only** holds shares in subsidiaries (no
financing, no IP, no operational activity), the **reduced test**
applies:

- **Basic Jersey company-law compliance**:
  - Annual confirmation statement;
  - Maintained registered office;
  - Director / secretary in place;
  - Statutory records;
- **Adequate people** to discharge the obligations (typically
  the service-provider team);
- **Adequate premises** (the service-provider office address).

Met by **standard service-provider arrangements** for most
founder TopCos.

### Full substance for other activities

If the TopCo does more than pure equity-holding:

- **Financing activities** (intra-group loans, third-party
  lending): finance and leasing classification, full substance;
- **IP licensing or holding**: IP business, full substance;
- **Headquarters services** to group: HQ classification, full
  substance;
- **Operational** activities: depending on type, may trigger
  full substance.

Full substance requires:

- **Directed and managed in Jersey** — board meetings here,
  strategic decisions here;
- **Adequate employees** in Jersey for the activity;
- **Adequate premises** in Jersey;
- **Adequate operating expenditure** in Jersey;
- **CIGAs in Jersey** — the core activities generating income
  for the activity.

### What to classify

Examine your TopCo's actual activities:

- **Holding shares only** → pure equity holding;
- **Holding shares + intra-group loans** → could trigger
  financing classification;
- **Holding shares + IP** → IP business if material;
- **Providing group management services** → headquarters;
- **Multiple activities** → multiple classifications.

### Common founder-TopCo scenarios

#### Standard founder TopCo

- **Holds operating-subsidiary shares**;
- **Receives dividends**;
- **Distributes to shareholders**;
- **No financing / IP / HQ activities**;
- **Classification**: pure equity holding;
- **Substance**: reduced test met through service provider.

#### TopCo financing group entities

- **Holds shares + extends intra-group loans**;
- **Classification**: pure equity holding **and** financing /
  leasing;
- **Substance**: full substance for financing activity required;
- **Decision-making** about loans in Jersey;
- **Relevant personnel** in Jersey.

#### TopCo holding IP

- **IP business** classification;
- **Full substance** required;
- **Decisions** about IP, licensing, enforcement in Jersey;
- **R&D or strategic management** of IP in Jersey;
- **Higher bar** than pure equity holding.

### Reporting

Substance is reported through the **annual corporate tax
return**:

- **Self-assess** substance compliance;
- **Identify** relevant activities;
- **Provide** prescribed information (employees, premises,
  expenditure, CIGAs);
- **Confirm** test met or explain failure.

### Failure consequences

- **First failure**: spontaneous exchange of information with
  relevant foreign tax authorities (often material consequence);
  civil financial penalty;
- **Persistent failure**: higher penalties; Companies-Law
  strike-off / wind-up potential (Articles 143A-143B).

### Practical compliance

For a founder TopCo:

- **Classify** activities annually;
- **Document** substance arrangements;
- **Schedule** Jersey board meetings if needed;
- **Confirm** service-provider arrangements meet test;
- **File** annual return with substance information.

## What to do next

1. **Identify** TopCo activities;
2. **Classify** under Substance Law categories;
3. **Apply** appropriate substance test;
4. **Document** compliance arrangements;
5. **Engage** service providers ensuring substance maintained;
6. **File** annual returns.

## Pitfalls

- **Drift into substance categories**: TopCo that starts as
  pure equity-holding may add financing or IP activity
  inadvertently;
- **Service-provider inadequacy**: minimum service-provider
  presence may not meet test;
- **Documentation gaps**: substance must be demonstrable;
- **Foreign-authority spontaneous-exchange surprise**.
