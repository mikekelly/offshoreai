# Jersey TopCo for a UK or US IPO — What to Plan For

## The question

Your Jersey TopCo is preparing for an **IPO** — likely on the
LSE Main Market, AIM, NASDAQ, or another exchange. What's
involved?

## The short answer

Jersey IPOs are **routine** for the LSE and increasingly so for
NASDAQ / NYSE. Key steps:

- **Convert private to public** (Article 17 of Companies Law);
- **Engage** Jersey counsel, listing-jurisdiction counsel,
  nominated adviser / underwriter;
- **Prospectus / listing-document** drafting under listing-rule
  requirements;
- **Corporate-governance** alignment with listing-rule
  expectations;
- **Restructure** if needed (pre-IPO reorganisation);
- **Dual-jurisdiction** coordination (Jersey + listing
  jurisdiction).

The **Jersey angle** is supported and accepted by all major
listing venues.

## The proper answer

### Why Jersey for IPO

Jersey TopCos are common for IPOs:

- **Established acceptance** by LSE, AIM, Euronext, NASDAQ;
- **Familiar legal regime** for advisers;
- **Tax-efficient** structure;
- **No Jersey CGT** for investors (subject to foreign-tax
  considerations);
- **No Jersey stamp duty** on share transfers (subject to LTT
  for property-share property);
- **Flexible corporate law** for restructuring needs.

### Pre-IPO preparation

#### Convert to public

- **Article 17** procedure;
- **Special resolution** of members;
- **Amend** memorandum and articles to public-company form;
- **Notify** registrar.

Typically done close to IPO date.

#### Corporate-governance alignment

For LSE Main Market:

- **UK Corporate Governance Code** expectations;
- **Independent directors** in appropriate numbers;
- **Audit, remuneration, nomination committees**;
- **Specific disclosure** practices.

The Jersey company can structure to meet UK CGC even though
Jersey company law doesn't itself mandate it.

For NASDAQ / NYSE:

- **SEC compliance** (significant complexity for non-US issuers);
- **Sarbanes-Oxley** compliance for the audit;
- **Foreign-private-issuer** elections often used.

#### Pre-IPO restructure

Often involves:

- **Group simplification**;
- **Migrate** of operating subsidiaries if needed;
- **IP-and-asset rationalisation**;
- **Capital-structure** adjustments (no-par to par, share-class
  rationalisation);
- **Director / management** transitions.

### The IPO process

#### Legal documentation

- **Prospectus / listing document** — primary disclosure
  document;
- **Underwriting agreement** with banks;
- **Lock-up agreements** with existing shareholders;
- **Service agreements** for directors / management;
- **Listing-jurisdiction-specific** documents.

#### Due diligence

- **Legal**, **financial**, **commercial**, **tax** due
  diligence;
- **Jersey-law** dimension covered by Jersey counsel;
- **Listing-jurisdiction-law** dimension covered by lead
  counsel.

#### Pricing and allocation

- **Banks** lead the pricing process;
- **Founder shareholders** typically have lock-up periods;
- **Listing** completes with capital raise (typically).

### Tax considerations at IPO

- **Jersey-level**: typically tax-neutral for the listing
  itself;
- **Foreign-shareholder tax**: home-jurisdiction tax on
  pre-listing gains often arises;
- **Stamp / SDRT**: Jersey-shares typically not subject to
  Jersey or UK SDRT;
- **Post-listing**: continuing tax-efficient under zero-ten
  (subject to Pillar Two for large groups).

### Listing rules

Each exchange has specific listing rules:

- **LSE Main Market**: Premium Segment vs Standard Segment;
- **AIM**: lighter rules, nominated adviser oversight;
- **NASDAQ / NYSE**: SEC-compliant standards;
- **TISE**: Channel Islands-based exchange with specific rules.

Jersey company law **is accepted by all** as the underlying
corporate-law regime.

### Common scenarios

#### LSE Main Market premium listing

- **Jersey TopCo** common;
- **UK CGC** alignment;
- **Specific Jersey-Co disclosures** in prospectus;
- **Standard** approach.

#### AIM listing

- **Jersey TopCo** very common (large proportion of AIM
  companies are Jersey or BVI);
- **Lighter listing rules**;
- **Nomad oversight**.

#### NASDAQ listing

- **Jersey TopCo** workable but less common;
- **More complex** for non-US issuer;
- **SEC compliance** is the heavy lift.

#### Dual listing

- **Jersey TopCo** can list on multiple exchanges;
- **Cross-jurisdictional** complexity;
- **Specialised counsel** essential.

### Post-IPO

- **Listing-rule** continuing obligations;
- **Periodic financial reporting**;
- **Market-abuse** rules;
- **Continuing Jersey corporate** compliance;
- **Investor relations** programme.

## What to do next

For IPO preparation:

1. **Engage** lead counsel and bank;
2. **Plan** restructuring 12+ months ahead;
3. **Engage** Jersey counsel for corporate-law work;
4. **Convert** to public closer to IPO date;
5. **Execute** the listing.

## Pitfalls

- **Late preparation**: IPO timelines are long;
- **Pre-IPO restructuring** challenges (tax, operational);
- **Listing-rule misalignment**: governance changes needed;
- **Banker / counsel disconnect**: coordination essential.
