# Granting vs Operating Charity Structures

## The question

The family's philanthropic vehicle will either **make grants**
to other charities / causes (grant-making) or **directly run
charitable activities** (operating). Which model and what
structural implications?

## The short answer

**Grant-making** structures are simpler:

- **Receive endowment**;
- **Invest** the endowment;
- **Make grants** to qualifying charities / projects;
- **Reporting** on grant activity.

**Operating charity** structures directly run programmes:

- **Receive endowment**;
- **Run** schools, hospitals, research, services directly;
- **Employees, contracts, premises** in the vehicle's name;
- **Complex governance** including operational management.

Both work in Jersey vehicles (foundation or trust). Operating
charities typically favour **foundation** for body-corporate
identity.

## The proper answer

### Grant-making model

The grant-making philanthropic vehicle:

- **Endowment** invested to generate income / gains;
- **Income** funds annual grant programme;
- **Grants** to qualifying recipients;
- **No direct operations**.

#### Advantages

- **Simple governance** — board approves grants;
- **Lower operating cost**;
- **Diversified impact** across multiple recipients;
- **Adapts** easily as priorities change.

#### Disadvantages

- **Indirect impact** — depends on grantee execution;
- **Grantee monitoring** required;
- **Less family engagement** with mission.

### Operating charity model

The operating philanthropic vehicle:

- **Directly runs** programmes / institutions;
- **Employs** staff;
- **Owns or leases** premises;
- **Provides** services directly;
- **Examples**: school, hospital, research institute, museum,
  shelter.

#### Advantages

- **Direct mission impact**;
- **Family engagement** through direct involvement;
- **Quality control** over delivery;
- **Identity and brand** in the philanthropic field.

#### Disadvantages

- **Complex governance** including operational management;
- **Higher operating cost** (staff, premises);
- **Employment / regulatory** complexity;
- **Less flexibility** as committed to specific activities.

### Vehicle choice

#### For grant-making

- **Foundation** works well;
- **Charitable trust** equally works;
- **Choice** less important — operations are simple.

#### For operating

- **Foundation** preferred — body-corporate identity for
  employment, contracts, premises;
- **Charitable trust** can work but body-corporate easier;
- **Charity-company** (Jersey company limited by guarantee
  registered as charity) another option.

### Governance differences

#### Grant-making governance

- **Board / council** meets periodically (quarterly typical);
- **Grant proposals** reviewed;
- **Approval** of grants;
- **Programme review** annually;
- **Operations**: administered by family-office or service
  provider.

#### Operating governance

- **Board / council** with oversight of operations;
- **Executive team** running day-to-day;
- **Audit, risk, finance committees** for material operations;
- **Strategic planning** for institution growth;
- **Stakeholder management** (beneficiaries, regulators,
  donors).

### Hybrid models

Some philanthropic vehicles combine both:

- **Grant-making** as primary mode;
- **Specific operating programmes** for high-priority areas;
- **Pilots and incubation** of new approaches before scaling
  through grantees;
- **Convening** function (workshops, conferences) alongside
  granting.

### Common scenarios

#### Family with strong cause commitment

- **Single-cause focus** suggests operating model;
- **Family members involved** in delivery;
- **Family-mission embedded** in operations.

#### Family with diverse philanthropic interests

- **Grant-making** allows breadth;
- **Multiple causes** supported through varied grants;
- **Flexibility** to evolve priorities.

#### Memorial / legacy philanthropy

- **Operating institute** (e.g. research institute named for
  deceased family member);
- **Permanent endowment** funding the institution;
- **Long-term identity**.

#### Hybrid family philanthropy

- **Combine** grant-making for breadth with one or two operating
  flagships;
- **Flexibility** plus depth.

### Tax considerations

For both models:

- **Charity registration** with Jersey Charity Commissioner;
- **No Jersey tax** on investment income at vehicle level
  (subject to specifics);
- **Donor tax-deductibility** in foreign jurisdictions varies;
- **Cross-border philanthropy** has specific tax implications.

### Operational considerations

#### Grant-making

- **Investment management** of endowment;
- **Grant administration** (applications, review, approval,
  monitoring);
- **Reporting** to Charity Commissioner;
- **Governance** light.

#### Operating

- **Employment** of staff;
- **Premises** leasing / ownership;
- **Programme management**;
- **Stakeholder reporting**;
- **Regulatory compliance** (e.g. school inspection if running
  school);
- **Risk management** of operations.

## What to do next

For a family philanthropic establishment:

1. **Discuss** family philanthropic philosophy;
2. **Choose** grant-making, operating, or hybrid;
3. **Choose** vehicle type (foundation typical for operating);
4. **Engage** Jersey counsel;
5. **Set up** with appropriate governance;
6. **Operate**.

## Pitfalls

- **Over-ambition**: operating models often more complex than
  family anticipates;
- **Mission creep**: operating charity drifts from original
  purpose;
- **Inadequate funding**: insufficient endowment for chosen
  model;
- **Governance burden**: operating requires substantive board
  engagement.
