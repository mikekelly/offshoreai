# Spouse and Minor-Children Planning

## The question

How do Jersey vehicles support **spouse and minor-children**
planning — both ongoing welfare and contingent (death,
divorce, capacity)?

## The short answer

Jersey vehicles support spouse / minor-children planning through:

- **Discretionary trusts** including spouse and children;
- **Specific-purpose trusts** for education / welfare;
- **Reserved-powers** for settlor influence;
- **Protector / guardian** roles for ongoing supervision;
- **Court applications** for variation as circumstances evolve.

Key tension: **flexibility** for changing circumstances vs
**certainty** for spouse / children. Jersey's discretionary
trust model favours flexibility.

## The proper answer

### Spouse provision

Common approaches:

#### Spouse as discretionary beneficiary

- **Class beneficiary** alongside settlor / children;
- **Trustee distributes** as appropriate;
- **Letter of wishes** indicates expected spouse support;
- **Flexibility** if marriage circumstances change.

#### Spouse-specific trust

- **Separate trust** or **sub-fund** dedicated to spouse;
- **Spouse as primary beneficiary** with remainder to children;
- **More certainty** for spouse but less flexibility.

#### Spouse as protector / co-trustee

- **Governance role** for spouse;
- **Influence** over distributions to others;
- **Not appropriate** in all marital dynamics.

### Children's provision

#### Discretionary class

- **Children included** in discretionary class;
- **Trustee discretion** on distributions to each child;
- **Adapts** to each child's circumstances over time.

#### Specific trusts per child

- **Separate trust** for each child;
- **Certainty** for each child's entitlement;
- **Less ability** to redirect across children if needs evolve.

#### Education / welfare trusts

- **Specific purpose** of paying education / welfare costs;
- **Ends** when purpose served (e.g. children complete
  education);
- **Useful** alongside broader family-wealth trust.

### Minor-children considerations

For minor children:

#### Trustee discretion

- **Generally** appropriate for minors;
- **Conservative** distribution approach;
- **Education and welfare** typical;
- **Larger distributions** typically held back to maturity.

#### Age contingencies

- **Trust deed** may specify ages at which:
  - Beneficiary becomes adult discretionary beneficiary;
  - Beneficiary gains right to information;
  - Specific funds vest absolutely;
- **Typical ages**: 21, 25, 30, sometimes later.

#### Guardian arrangements

- **Legal guardian** for minor children under home-jurisdiction
  law;
- **Trust deed coordination** with guardianship;
- **Educational and welfare** decisions coordinated.

### Contingent planning

#### Settlor death

- **Trust continues** beyond settlor's death;
- **Letters of wishes** guide trustee post-death;
- **Protector role** if appointed;
- **Continuity** of family wealth management.

#### Spouse death

- **Class beneficiary** loses one member;
- **Remaining beneficiaries** continue;
- **Children** typically primary beneficiaries.

#### Divorce

- **Trust assets** typically protected by Article 9 firewall;
- **English Family Division** may apply personal pressure on
  settlor / beneficiary;
- **Pre-existing** trusts more defensible than late-settled;
- **Foreign-court order** may not bind Jersey trustee.

See [`asset-protection.md`](asset-protection.md) for detail.

#### Capacity loss

- **Settlor / family member** loss of mental capacity;
- **Trustee continues** managing under deed;
- **Protector / family** may assume specific roles;
- **Power-of-attorney** coordination.

### Common scenarios

#### Young family establishing trust

- **Settlor and spouse** as primary beneficiaries;
- **Children** as discretionary class;
- **Education / welfare** focus during minor years;
- **Reserved powers** for settlor control;
- **Adaptive** structure as family grows.

#### Family with adult children

- **Children added** as adult discretionary beneficiaries;
- **Specific sub-funds** for each adult child sometimes;
- **Family-governance** discussions begin;
- **Letter of wishes** clarifies expected distributions.

#### Pre-divorce settling

- **Article 9** protection but practical pressure on settlor;
- **Genuine** wealth-management purpose required;
- **Sham allegations** if obviously divorce-motivated;
- **Caution** with timing.

### Letters of wishes

Detailed letters of wishes typically:

- **Identify** family member priorities;
- **Indicate** expected distribution patterns;
- **Address** education, healthcare, major events;
- **Adapt** to specific child characteristics;
- **Refresh** as family evolves.

### Information flow

Coordinate:

- **Trustee** with family / family-office;
- **Spouse and adult children** as beneficiaries;
- **Minor children** through guardians / parents until adult;
- **Schmidt v Rosewood** principles on disclosure.

## What to do next

For family planning:

1. **Discuss** family priorities for spouse and children;
2. **Identify** specific arrangements (sub-funds, age-
   contingencies, specific purposes);
3. **Engage** Jersey counsel for deed drafting;
4. **Document** family-specific intent in letters of wishes;
5. **Establish** trustee relationship;
6. **Refresh** as family evolves.

## Pitfalls

- **Over-specifying**: rigid trust deed loses flexibility;
- **Under-specifying**: vague terms produce disputes;
- **Pre-divorce settling**: pattern matters;
- **Inadequate spouse provision**: contingent planning gaps.
