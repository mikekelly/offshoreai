# Should the Family Member Move to Jersey Under HVR?

## The question

A high-net-worth family member is considering **moving to
Jersey under the 2(1)(e) High-Value Residency (HVR) regime**.
What are the considerations?

## The short answer

HVR can be a strong move for:

- **HNW individuals** willing to commit annual minimum tax
  contributions (typically £200k+);
- **Genuinely** wanting Jersey residence as their home;
- **Comparing favourably** to home-jurisdiction tax profile;
- **Family** willing to relocate and acquire qualifying property.

Not for:

- **Tax-shopping** without genuine residence intent;
- **Family** unwilling to commit to Jersey lifestyle;
- **Tax-resident jurisdictions** with strong departure rules
  that complicate exit.

Model carefully — HVR is a **multi-decade decision** with
material implications.

## The proper answer

### The HVR proposition

Under regulation 2(1)(e) of the Control of Housing and Work
(Jersey) Regulations 2013:

- **Licensed** status under Jersey housing law;
- **Acquisition** of qualifying Jersey residential property;
- **Bespoke tax regime**: typically 20% on first tier of
  Jersey-taxable income; **lower rate** (often 1%) on higher
  tier;
- **Annual minimum tax** floor agreed at approval (often £150–
  300k+);
- **Family members** can be brought as derivative status.

### Application process

1. **Initial enquiry** with **Locate Jersey**;
2. **Detailed application** with supporting documentation
   (wealth verification, business background, character
   references, tax position);
3. **Due diligence** by Government departments;
4. **Approval** by Chief Minister;
5. **Property acquisition** within prescribed timeframes;
6. **Tax-regime engagement** with Revenue Jersey.

Process **typically 6–12 months**.

### Tax calculation

The HVR tax position (illustrative):

- **First tier** of Jersey-taxable income: at **20%** (standard
  rate);
- **Second tier** (above threshold): at **reduced rate** (often
  1%);
- **Worldwide income** generally taxable in Jersey (subject to
  bespoke arrangements);
- **Annual minimum** floor regardless of actual income;
- **No Jersey CGT**;
- **No Jersey IHT**.

The exact rates and thresholds vary; check current Revenue
Jersey guidance.

### Comparison with home jurisdictions

#### UK comparison

- **UK personal tax**: up to 45% income tax + 20% CGT (or 24%);
  + 40% IHT on death;
- **HVR Jersey**: bespoke modest rate plus minimum tax;
- **Effective savings** can be substantial for genuinely-high-
  income HNWs.

But:

- **UK non-dom** regime evolution (April 2025 reforms);
- **UK CGT departure** rules and **temporary non-residence**;
- **Genuine residence-shift** required (real, not paper).

#### EU jurisdictions comparison

- **Italian flat-tax** regime;
- **Portuguese non-habitual-resident** regime;
- **Maltese residence programmes**;

HVR vs these: case-specific.

#### US comparison

- **US citizens** face worldwide-income US tax regardless of
  residence;
- **HVR doesn't shield** from US tax for US citizens;
- **Renouncing US citizenship** is a separate massive decision;
- For **non-US-citizens** moving from US: HVR may work.

### Family implications

HVR family considerations:

- **Spouse / partner** typically included;
- **Children's schooling** in Jersey (limited but established
  international schools);
- **Children's adult-life** plans;
- **Aged-care** for senior family members;
- **Family-business** management if continues abroad.

### Property acquisition

HVR requires:

- **Qualifying residential property** acquisition in Jersey;
- **Specific property categories** (typically high-value bands);
- **Within prescribed timeframe** after approval;
- **Genuine occupation** required.

Jersey real estate at the HVR-qualifying level is **scarce and
expensive** — plan early.

### Lifestyle considerations

Jersey realities:

- **Island life** — small, water-locked, tight community;
- **Weather** variable, generally temperate;
- **Travel** to UK / Europe accessible but airport-dependent;
- **Limited** retail / cultural amenities compared to major
  cities;
- **Strong** professional services / healthcare for high-end
  needs.

### Exit considerations

If HVR doesn't work:

- **Leaving Jersey** typically straightforward;
- **Property** can be sold;
- **Tax-resident shift** to new jurisdiction with appropriate
  planning;
- **Status** lost on departure.

But: **changing residence is a major event** — don't enter HVR
without serious commitment.

## What to do next

For an HVR consideration:

1. **Model** the tax outcome carefully — Jersey HVR rate plus
   home-jurisdiction exit;
2. **Compare** with alternative jurisdictions;
3. **Discuss** family willingness to relocate;
4. **Initial enquiry** with Locate Jersey;
5. **Engage** counsel for application;
6. **Property search** in parallel;
7. **Decide** with full information.

## Pitfalls

- **Tax-driven only**: family unhappiness with Jersey life
  defeats the move;
- **Inadequate property timing**: scarce HVR-grade properties;
- **Exit-jurisdiction complexity**: not all home jurisdictions
  release cleanly;
- **US-citizen** non-shield surprise;
- **Future-tax-reform** risk in Jersey or home jurisdiction.
