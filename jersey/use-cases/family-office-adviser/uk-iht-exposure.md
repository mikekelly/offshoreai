# UK IHT Exposure of a Jersey Trust Settled by UK-Domiciled Settlor

## The question

The client is **UK-domiciled** and wants to settle assets into a
**Jersey trust**. What is the **UK Inheritance Tax (IHT)**
exposure?

## The short answer

A UK-domiciled settlor's transfer of assets into a Jersey trust
typically triggers:

- **Entry charge** of up to 20% on the value transferred (if
  using relevant-property trust mechanics);
- **10-year anniversary charges** of up to 6% of trust value;
- **Exit charges** on distributions (calculated against the
  10-year rate);
- **Continued UK IHT** on the trust property even after the
  settlor's death.

The Jersey trust **does not escape UK IHT** for a UK-domiciled
settlor. The Jersey advantage is **no Jersey IHT** — but the UK
overlay is the dominant cost.

For **non-UK-domiciled** settlors, the position is different
(historically more favourable) but **2025 reforms** changed
much of this.

## The proper answer

### UK IHT framework (simplified)

The UK relevant-property regime applies to **most discretionary
trusts**:

- **Settlor transfers value** into the trust;
- **Trust holds property** as "relevant property";
- **Anniversary charges** at 10-year intervals;
- **Exit charges** on distributions;
- **Effective rates** up to 6% per 10-year period.

The position is the **same whether trust is UK-resident or
Jersey-resident** if the settlor is UK-domiciled.

### Settlor's UK position

A **UK-domiciled settlor**:

- Is **liable for UK IHT** on worldwide assets transferred
  into trust;
- **Lifetime transfers** can be chargeable lifetime transfers
  (CLT) or potentially exempt transfers (PET);
- Most trust settlements are **CLT** (immediately chargeable);
- **20% lifetime rate** for above-nil-rate-band transfers;
- **Cumulative** with prior CLTs.

### 2025 reforms

Until 2025, **non-UK-domiciled settlors** could often achieve
better positions:

- **Excluded property** treatment for non-UK assets;
- Trust outside UK IHT entirely;
- "Domicile of choice" planning.

**From 6 April 2025**, UK IHT reform replaced domicile-based
rules with **residence-based rules**:

- **UK-resident for 10+ years** brings worldwide assets into UK
  IHT;
- **Long-term-resident** trusts have similar treatment;
- Many previously **excluded-property trusts** are now in UK IHT
  scope;
- Transitional rules and complexity.

This is **major change** — Jersey trusts settled before 2025 may
remain protected; new settlements face the new regime.

### Trust ongoing position

For a Jersey trust within UK IHT:

#### 10-year anniversaries

- **Calculated** as if a hypothetical chargeable transfer;
- Rate up to **6%**;
- Based on **trust value** at the anniversary.

#### Exit charges

- **Proportional** to the time since last anniversary;
- Apply to **distributions to beneficiaries**;
- **Particularly painful** within first 10 years.

#### Beneficiary-level

For UK-resident beneficiaries receiving distributions:

- **Income** distributions taxed at marginal rates;
- **Capital** distributions may trigger UK CGT or income-tax
  attribution (TCGA s.87, ITA s.731);
- **Stockpiled gains** and **deemed income** mechanics.

### Tax-planning considerations

#### Settlement timing

- **Pre-domicile / pre-long-term-resident** settlements
  historically better;
- **Post-2025** options narrowed significantly;
- **Continuing legacy benefits** for older settlements.

#### Asset selection

- **UK situs assets** always within UK IHT;
- **Non-UK situs assets** treatment per new residence rules.

#### Beneficiary structuring

- **Non-UK beneficiaries** avoid UK IHT exposure of
  distributions to them;
- **UK-resident beneficiaries** face attribution rules.

### Jersey's role

Jersey provides:

- **No Jersey IHT** at trust or beneficiary level;
- **No Jersey CGT**;
- **Mature trust law** (Trusts Law 1984);
- **Article 9 firewall** against foreign-court claims;
- **Established professional infrastructure**.

But **none of this overrides UK tax** for UK-connected parties.

### Common scenarios

#### UK-domiciled grandparent settles for UK-resident grandchildren

- **CLT** at settlement (20% above NRB);
- **Anniversary and exit charges** ongoing;
- **Beneficiary income / gains** taxed in UK;
- **Jersey provides** asset-protection and admin advantages but
  no UK tax shelter.

#### Long-resident non-domiciled individual

- **Post-2025**: largely within UK IHT after 10-year residence
  threshold;
- **Pre-2025**: excluded-property advantages historically;
- **Transitional** rules complex.

#### Truly non-UK-resident settlor

- **UK IHT** applies only to **UK-situs** assets;
- **Non-UK assets** in Jersey trust generally outside UK IHT;
- **No UK exposure** for fully-non-UK family.

## What to do next

For client consideration:

1. **Confirm** settlor's UK tax status (domicile, residence,
   long-term residence);
2. **Model** UK IHT consequences at each stage;
3. **Compare** with alternatives;
4. **Make** documented decisions;
5. **Implement** with UK tax adviser engagement.

## Pitfalls

- **Treating Jersey trust as UK-tax-free**: it isn't for
  UK-connected parties;
- **Missing 2025 reforms**: residence-based rules changed the
  landscape;
- **Stockpiled gains** surprise in trust history;
- **Beneficiary attribution** under TOAA / s.731.
