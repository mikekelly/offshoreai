# Continuance / Migration of a Jersey Fund Vehicle

## The question

You want to **move your Jersey fund** to (or from) another
jurisdiction — for tax, regulatory, or commercial reasons.
How does fund continuance work?

## The short answer

A Jersey fund vehicle can be migrated under the **Part 18C
continuance regime** of the Companies Law (for Jersey companies)
or equivalent provisions for LPs / unit trusts:

- **Outbound** — Jersey company to foreign jurisdiction (subject
  to JFSC authorisation under Article 127T-U);
- **Inbound** — foreign body to Jersey company (subject to
  JFSC authorisation under Article 127K-N);
- **Legal identity continues** — same entity, different
  jurisdiction;
- **Investor base** typically maintained;
- **Tax** consequences in both jurisdictions need careful
  modelling.

LP and unit-trust migration uses **different mechanics** but
similar principles.

## The proper answer

### Reasons to migrate a fund

#### Outbound (Jersey to elsewhere)

- **EU access** if AIFMD passport becomes attractive;
- **Tax-treaty positioning** in destination jurisdiction;
- **Investor preference** for the destination;
- **Manager relocation** of the management business.

#### Inbound (elsewhere to Jersey)

- **Jersey advantages** (zero-ten, no CGT, no IHT, listing
  acceptance, infrastructure);
- **Group simplification** with existing Jersey vehicles;
- **Investor preference** for Jersey-domicile;
- **EU-list considerations** (Jersey white-list status).

### The Companies Law continuance route

For a **Jersey-company fund** migrating out:

1. **Member approval** by special resolution (Article 127Q);
2. **Creditor notice** (Article 127R);
3. **Member objection window** (Article 127S);
4. **JFSC consent application** (Article 127T);
5. **JFSC determination** (Article 127U);
6. **Effective date** — fund ceases to be Jersey-incorporated
   (Article 127V).

The fund **continues** as a body in the destination
jurisdiction — same legal identity.

For an **inbound** migration:

1. **JFSC application** (Article 127K) with articles of
   continuance, name, solvency statement;
2. **JFSC determination** (Article 127N);
3. **Certificate of continuance** (Article 127O);
4. **Effective date** — body becomes a Jersey company (Article
   127P).

### Investor consent

For fund migrations, investor consent / notification is critical:

- **PPM / LPA terms** may require investor approval for
  migration;
- **MFN-protected investors** may opt for terms in connection
  with migration;
- **Side-letter compliance** may need addressing;
- **Practical** — investor confidence depends on transparent
  communication.

### Tax consequences

#### At Jersey level

- **Outbound**: Jersey company ceases to be Jersey-tax-resident;
- **Inbound**: foreign body becomes Jersey-tax-resident;
- **No exit charge** in Jersey for outbound (no CGT, no
  formal exit tax for fund migration);
- **Tax filings** must be completed for the relevant period.

#### At destination level

- Destination jurisdiction's tax regime applies;
- **Entry charge** or step-up may apply in some
  jurisdictions;
- **Tax-treaty** position post-migration;
- **Regulated-fund tax classification** in destination.

#### At investor level

- Investors typically have **no direct tax event** from
  migration (legal identity continues);
- **Tax characterisation** of the fund may change in investor
  jurisdictions;
- **CRS / FATCA** classification may change;
- Investor advisers should be **engaged** to confirm.

### LP and unit-trust migration

For non-company fund vehicles:

- **Jersey LP** to foreign LP: typically requires **dissolution
  and reformation** rather than continuance (some jurisdictions
  permit LP continuance, others don't);
- **Jersey unit trust** to foreign unit trust: change of trustee
  / proper law, sometimes effectively a new trust structure;
- **More commercial complexity** than company continuance.

Practically, **company migration is the cleanest** and often
preferred where the choice of vehicle is open.

### Operational handover

The migration involves:

- **Bank account** changes in the new jurisdiction;
- **Service provider** transitions or arrangements;
- **Counterparty notifications**;
- **Listing changes** (if listed);
- **Regulatory de-registration** in Jersey, registration in
  destination.

## What to do next

For a planned migration:

1. **Confirm** the commercial / tax case for migration;
2. **Model** consequences at fund, manager, and investor level;
3. **Engage** counsel in both jurisdictions;
4. **Obtain** member / investor approvals;
5. **Apply** for JFSC authorisation;
6. **Apply** for destination-jurisdiction registration;
7. **Coordinate** the cut-over;
8. **Complete** post-migration filings and communications.

## Pitfalls

- **Tax-surprise** at destination on entry;
- **Investor objection** triggering material delay;
- **Service-provider transition** disruptions;
- **Reg-gap** between Jersey de-registration and destination
  registration.
