# Side Letters, MFN, and Most-Favoured-Nation Governance

## The question

Your fund has investors of different sizes and types. Several
have requested **side letters** modifying or supplementing the
fund's main terms. How do you manage side letters and MFN
mechanics?

## The short answer

Side letters are routine in institutional fund-raising but
require careful management:

- **Side letter** is a contract between the fund / GP and a
  specific investor modifying / supplementing main terms;
- **MFN (most-favoured-nation)** clauses give a side-letter
  investor the right to **opt into** other investors' side-
  letter terms;
- **Tracking and disclosure** is essential — the GP must know
  what was committed and to whom;
- **Equality of treatment** principles in the fund documentation
  and applicable law constrain what can be granted privately.

## The proper answer

### Why side letters exist

Investors negotiating fund commitments often want:

- **Fee discounts** for large commitments;
- **Reporting** beyond the standard cadence;
- **Co-investment** rights or first-look on opportunities;
- **Governance** participation (advisory committee seats);
- **Specific compliance** representations (tax, ERISA, AIFMD
  reverse-solicitation);
- **Specific exclusions** from particular investments
  (sanctions, jurisdictions, sectors).

Side letters allow **bespoke** investor terms without rewriting
the fund's main LPA.

### Common side-letter categories

#### Fee economics

- **Reduced management fee** for large or anchor investors;
- **Reduced carry** in some structures;
- **Hurdle adjustments** in negotiated cases;
- **Fee offset** mechanics for transaction / monitoring fees.

#### Information rights

- **Quarterly reporting** beyond standard;
- **Real-time access** to portfolio data;
- **ESG reporting** specifics;
- **Tax reporting** for specific jurisdictions.

#### Governance

- **Advisory committee** seats;
- **Veto rights** on specified matters;
- **Approval rights** for related-party transactions.

#### Co-investment

- **Pro-rata** co-investment rights;
- **First-look** on opportunities exceeding fund mandate;
- **No-fee co-investment** for anchor investors.

#### Representations and exclusions

- **Tax representations** for sovereign / pension investors;
- **ERISA representations** for US pension investors;
- **Sanctions exclusions** for specific investor categories;
- **Sector exclusions** (e.g. tobacco, weapons) for ESG investors.

### MFN mechanics

A side letter with **MFN protection** typically provides:

- The investor can **opt into** other investors' more favourable
  side-letter terms;
- Subject to **scope limitations** (e.g. only economic terms,
  not governance);
- **Disclosure** of other side letters required;
- **Opt-in window** for the MFN-protected investor.

MFN administration:

- **GP discloses** new side letters to MFN-protected investors;
- **Investors elect** within window;
- **Side-letter terms cascade** to MFN-protected investors;
- **Tracking** is operationally significant.

### Disclosure of side letters

Best practice:

- **Disclose existence** of side letters in the PPM (typical
  practice);
- **Specific disclosure** of categories of side-letter terms
  granted;
- **MFN disclosure** to MFN-protected investors of relevant
  other letters.

Some jurisdictions / fund regimes require **fuller disclosure**
to all investors. Jersey doesn't statutorily require this — but
SOMA / market practice in some segments expects it.

### Tracking side letters

The GP / counsel must maintain:

- **Side-letter register** of all letters granted;
- **Term-by-term** tracking of commitments;
- **MFN matrix** showing which investors have MFN, scope, and
  current opt-ins;
- **Compliance log** of side-letter-specific obligations
  (reports, certificates, communications).

Specialist software is used by larger fund managers; smaller
ones use spreadsheets.

### Equality-of-treatment principles

While side letters are permitted:

- **Fund documentation** may have equality clauses limiting
  scope;
- **Applicable law** (e.g. AIFMD-equivalent for fund
  marketing) has fair-treatment principles;
- **Reputational** considerations — if disclosure to broader
  investor base would be embarrassing, the side letter is
  probably inappropriate.

The rule of thumb: side letters should be **defensible** if
fully disclosed.

## What to do next

For a new fund:

1. **Anticipate** likely side-letter requests in PPM drafting;
2. **Set policy** on what's negotiable and what's not;
3. **Develop** template side-letter language;
4. **Establish** tracking system;
5. **Engage** counsel for negotiated letters.

For an existing fund:

- **Audit** existing side letters;
- **Confirm** MFN compliance;
- **Update** tracking as new letters are signed;
- **Discharge** ongoing obligations to side-letter investors.

## Pitfalls

- **MFN cascade**: granting a generous term to one investor
  cascades widely;
- **Forgotten obligations**: side-letter commitments lost in
  administration churn;
- **Inconsistent grants**: same term granted differently to
  similar investors;
- **Conflict with main LPA**: side letter inconsistent with
  fund-level commitments.
