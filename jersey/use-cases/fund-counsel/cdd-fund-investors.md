# CDD on Fund Investors — What's Required?

## The question

Investors are subscribing to your JPF / Expert / Listed fund.
What CDD must the fund (or its administrator) collect?

## The short answer

CDD on fund investors follows the **Money Laundering (Jersey)
Order 2008** plus the **JFSC AML/CFT Handbook**:

- **Individual investors**: identify and verify, source of
  funds, PEP / sanctions screening;
- **Entity investors**: entity-level CDD plus controlling-
  person CDD;
- **Regulated counterparty investors**: simplified due diligence
  may apply;
- **Eligibility verification**: investors must demonstrate they
  qualify (professional / expert / eligible investor status).

The fund's **Designated Service Provider** (for a JPF) or
**FSB-licensed administrator** typically performs CDD.

## The proper answer

### Investor-eligibility verification

For each fund regime, investors must demonstrate qualification:

- **JPF**: professional / eligible investor;
- **Expert Fund**: expert investor (net-worth / institutional
  criteria);
- **Eligible Investor Fund**: eligible investor;
- **Public Fund**: open to retail (lighter qualification check).

The qualification documentation is **separate from but parallel
to** CDD — typically a subscription document with investor
self-certification and supporting evidence.

### Standard CDD on individual investors

- **Identification**: passport / national ID;
- **Verification** of identity (certified copies, in-person
  verification, etc.);
- **Residential address** verification;
- **Tax residence** (for CRS / FATCA);
- **Source of funds** for the subscription amount;
- **Source of wealth** for HNW investors;
- **PEP screening**;
- **Sanctions screening**.

### CDD on entity investors

- **Constitutional documents**;
- **Directors / officers**;
- **Beneficial owners** (25%+ ownership / control);
- **Authorised signatories** dealing with the fund;
- **Sanctions screening** on entity and controlling persons;
- **Source of funds** for the subscription;
- **Purpose** of the investment.

### Regulated counterparty SDD

For investors that are **regulated FIs in equivalent
jurisdictions**:

- **SDD** typically applies;
- **Reduced documentation** — rely on the counterparty's own
  regulated status;
- **Statement** from the counterparty confirming compliance;
- **Sanctions screening** still required.

### High-risk profiles

For PEP investors, high-risk-jurisdiction investors, complex
structures:

- **EDD** applies;
- **Enhanced source-of-wealth verification**;
- **Senior-management approval** of admission;
- **Enhanced ongoing monitoring**.

### Master-feeder / fund-of-funds

For investors that are themselves funds:

- CDD on the **investing fund** at entity level;
- **Reliance** on the investing fund's CDD on its investors
  (subject to equivalence and documentation);
- **Look-through** to controlling persons where the investing
  fund is not itself an FFI.

### Periodic refresh

CDD is not static:

- **Refresh** at scheduled intervals (annual / 3-yearly /
  risk-based);
- **Update** for material changes (new beneficial owners,
  significant additional subscriptions);
- **Trigger reviews** on adverse events (sanctions matches,
  negative news).

### Documentation

Maintain a **CDD file** for each investor containing:

- Identification and verification documents;
- Source-of-funds evidence;
- Eligibility certifications;
- Screening results (PEP, sanctions, adverse media);
- Approval / risk-assessment records;
- Ongoing monitoring records.

## What to do next

For an investor onboarding:

1. **Send** subscription pack with CDD requirements;
2. **Receive** completed documents;
3. **Verify** identification and source of funds;
4. **Screen** for PEP, sanctions, adverse media;
5. **Risk-assess** the relationship;
6. **Approve** at appropriate seniority;
7. **Admit** the investor;
8. **Set up** ongoing monitoring.

## Pitfalls

- **Subscription rush**: pressure to onboard before fund close
  shouldn't compromise CDD;
- **Reliance on introducer**: introducers don't substitute for
  the fund's own CDD;
- **Eligibility check vs CDD confusion**: both required,
  separately;
- **Stale CDD** on long-term investors.
