# How Do I Assess an Internal Disclosure?

## The question

You're the MLRO and a staff member has filed an **internal
disclosure**. How do you assess it and decide whether to file a
SAR externally?

## The short answer

Assess the disclosure systematically:

1. **Read** the disclosure carefully;
2. **Investigate** — review CDD, transaction history, related
   parties, any negative information;
3. **Consider** alternative explanations for the conduct;
4. **Form a view** on whether suspicion is reasonable;
5. **Decide** to file SAR or close internally, with reasons
   documented;
6. **Document** the assessment.

If suspicion is reasonable, **file the SAR** with the FIU. If
not, **close** with documented reasoning.

## The proper answer

### The MLRO's role

The MLRO is the firm's **gateway to external disclosure**:

- **Receives** all internal disclosures;
- **Investigates** further as needed;
- **Decides** on external SAR filing;
- **Owns** the decision (personal statutory responsibility).

The MLRO must be:

- **Sufficiently senior** to act independently;
- **Adequately resourced** to investigate;
- **Trained** in AML / CFT law;
- **Empowered** to make and defend decisions.

### Step 1: Read and understand

When a disclosure comes in:

- **Read carefully** — what exactly is the concern?
- **Identify** the customer, transaction, parties involved;
- **Note** the discloser's specific observations and any
  judgment expressed;
- **Avoid** premature judgement.

### Step 2: Investigate

Within the firm's records:

- **Review** the customer's full CDD file;
- **Examine** transaction history — patterns, counterparties,
  timing;
- **Check** for related parties (other customers in same group,
  related transactions);
- **Run** fresh PEP / sanctions / adverse-media screening;
- **Note** any prior internal disclosures or reviews.

Where appropriate, **discuss** with:

- The discloser (for clarification);
- The relationship manager (without tipping off the customer);
- Senior management (for high-profile cases).

### Step 3: Consider alternative explanations

For each red flag, ask:

- **What innocent explanation** could account for this?
- **How does it compare** to the customer's overall profile?
- **Is it consistent** with the source-of-wealth narrative?
- **What would a knowledgeable observer** make of this?

The goal is **calibrated judgement**, not paranoia.

### Step 4: Form a view

Apply the suspicion threshold:

- **Knowledge or suspicion** = reasonable doubt requiring
  external disclosure;
- **Less than suspicion** = doesn't require SAR but may
  warrant enhanced monitoring;
- **No concern** = close the internal disclosure.

Document the reasoning fully.

### Step 5: Decide

The decision matrix:

| Suspicion threshold met | Action |
|---|---|
| Yes | **File SAR** with FIU; manage relationship per consent regime |
| Possibly — investigate further | **Additional investigation** before deciding |
| No, but concerning | **Enhanced monitoring** going forward |
| No | **Close** internal disclosure |

For "no but concerning", consider:

- **Documented enhanced monitoring** of the relationship;
- **Pre-set** trigger thresholds for re-escalation;
- **Future re-assessment** if patterns emerge.

### Step 6: Document

Every decision should be documented:

- **Date** of internal disclosure receipt;
- **Investigation steps** taken;
- **Information considered**;
- **Alternative explanations considered**;
- **Reasoning** for conclusion;
- **Outcome** (SAR filed / not filed);
- **Date** of decision.

This is essential for:

- **Defensibility** of decisions;
- **JFSC inspection** preparation;
- **Future similar cases** consistency;
- **MLRO personal protection**.

### Common scenarios

#### Customer reluctance to update CDD

- **Possible**: change of circumstances they want to hide;
- **Investigate**: re-engage, gather background;
- **Decide** based on what comes out.

#### Adverse media match

- **Verify** the match is the same person;
- **Assess** severity of the adverse content;
- **Consider** age of allegations;
- **Decide** based on materiality.

#### Unusual transaction

- **Compare** to customer's normal pattern;
- **Seek explanation** from relationship manager (not customer);
- **Assess** business rationale;
- **Decide**.

#### Pressure from relationship manager not to file

- **Resist** unless the substantive concern is genuinely
  unfounded;
- **Document** any pushback;
- **MLRO independence** is fundamental.

### After the decision

If filing SAR:

- **Prepare and submit** SAR to FIU;
- **Maintain** post-SAR documentation;
- **Manage** the relationship per consent rules;
- **Train** discloser on next-steps (don't tip off).

If not filing:

- **Document** the decision;
- **Communicate** to discloser (in appropriate terms);
- **Set up** any enhanced monitoring agreed;
- **Calendar** future re-review if appropriate.

## What to do next

For an immediate internal disclosure:

1. **Read** and process;
2. **Investigate** systematically;
3. **Consider** alternative explanations;
4. **Decide** with documented reasoning;
5. **Implement** the decision.

For programme:

- **Train** staff on internal-disclosure process;
- **Maintain** documented MLRO decision criteria;
- **Audit** decisions periodically.

## Pitfalls

- **Premature dismissal**: closing without proper investigation;
- **Premature filing**: filing without adequate basis;
- **Inconsistent decision-making**: similar facts treated
  differently;
- **Pressure compromise**: caving to business pressure to not
  file.
