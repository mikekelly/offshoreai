# Sanctions Licences — Permitted Dealings with Designated Persons

## The question

Your customer is sanctions-designated and assets are frozen.
The customer needs **basic-needs payments** or **legal fees**.
What licence options are available, and how do you apply?

## The short answer

**General licences** authorise specific categories of activity
across all affected institutions. **Specific licences**
authorise particular transactions for named persons.

Common licence categories:

- **Basic needs** (food, shelter, medical, education);
- **Legal fees** (defending the designation or unrelated legal
  needs);
- **Humanitarian** purposes;
- **Routine maintenance** of frozen assets (insurance,
  property maintenance).

Apply through **OFSI** (for UK-list designations) or
**equivalent Jersey authority**.

## The proper answer

### General licences

Some categories have **standing general licences** — no specific
application needed:

- **Basic-needs payments** up to specified thresholds;
- **Routine asset-management** activities;
- **Specific humanitarian** categories.

Check the **current general-licence regime** — it changes
with sanctions developments.

### Specific licences

For named-customer specific activities:

1. **Identify** the activity needed and its legal basis;
2. **Engage** Jersey counsel and OFSI counsel as appropriate;
3. **Draft** the application with detailed justification;
4. **Submit** to OFSI / Jersey authority;
5. **Wait** for decision (varies — sometimes weeks);
6. **Operate** within the licence terms once granted.

### Common licence categories

#### Basic needs

For a designated individual:

- Food, accommodation, medical care, education for dependants;
- **Reasonable amounts** within prescribed limits;
- **Documented** purpose for each disbursement.

#### Legal fees

For legal representation:

- **Defending the designation** itself;
- **Unrelated legal matters** (family proceedings, etc.);
- **Reasonable rates** typically required;
- **Detailed billing** documentation.

#### Frozen-asset maintenance

For maintaining frozen assets:

- **Insurance premiums**;
- **Property maintenance** (rates, repairs);
- **Custody fees** on financial assets;
- **Bank account maintenance**.

#### Humanitarian

Special-purpose disbursements:

- **Medical emergencies**;
- **Funeral arrangements**;
- **Other humanitarian** needs.

### Licence scope

Each licence is **narrow** — only the specifically authorised
activity is permitted:

- **Specific transactions** vs **standing authority**;
- **Time-limited** typically;
- **Amount-limited** typically;
- **Counterparty-limited** in some cases.

The firm must **operate strictly** within the licence terms.

### Documentation

For each licence:

- **Application materials** retained;
- **Licence document** received;
- **Transactions executed** under the licence (date, amount,
  purpose, counterparty);
- **Reporting** to OFSI / Jersey authority if required;
- **End-of-licence** report.

### Common scenarios

#### Designated person needs to pay legal fees to defend designation

- **Apply** for legal-fee licence;
- **OFSI typically grants** within reason;
- **Reasonable rates** required.

#### Designated person needs basic-needs cash

- **Apply** for basic-needs licence;
- **Modest standing limit** typical;
- **Documented purpose** for disbursements.

#### Designated person's property needs maintenance

- **Apply** for maintenance licence;
- **Specific** items (insurance, property tax, repairs);
- **Receipts** required.

### Group-entity coordination

If the customer is across multiple firms:

- **Coordinate** licence applications where appropriate;
- **Avoid** the customer obtaining funds across firms beyond
  licensed amounts;
- **Communicate** with regulators about cross-firm exposure.

## What to do next

For a specific licence need:

1. **Identify** the activity and category;
2. **Engage** counsel (Jersey + UK as appropriate);
3. **Draft** the application;
4. **Submit** to OFSI / Jersey authority;
5. **Wait** for decision;
6. **Operate** strictly within licence terms;
7. **Document** activities under the licence.

For programme:

- **Maintain** knowledge of current general licences;
- **Train** staff on licence-needed identification;
- **Document** procedures for licence requests.

## Pitfalls

- **Acting beyond licence scope**: serious offence risk;
- **Inadequate documentation**: undermines licence defence;
- **Stale general licences**: regime changes can revoke;
- **Cross-firm coordination gaps**.
