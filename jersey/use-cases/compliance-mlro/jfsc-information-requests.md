# JFSC Information Requests — What's Required

## The question

The JFSC has issued an **information request** under its
statutory powers. What must you provide?

## The short answer

The JFSC has **broad statutory information powers** under the
relevant regulatory Laws. A regulated entity must:

- **Respond** within the prescribed period;
- **Provide** requested information accurately and completely;
- **Preserve** related records;
- **Cooperate** with any follow-up.

Failure to comply is **a regulatory breach** and can trigger
enforcement action.

## The proper answer

### Sources of JFSC information powers

- **FS(J)L 1998** — for FSB / TCB / Investment Business / GIMB
  / MSB-regulated entities;
- **Banking Business Law 1991** — for banks;
- **CIF Law 1988** — for fund services and funds;
- **Insurance Business Law 1996** — for insurers;
- **Codes of Practice** elaborating statutory powers.

The combined effect: **JFSC can request information on any
matter relevant to its supervision**.

### Common request types

- **Specific customer files** for review;
- **Transaction records** for specified periods;
- **Compliance documentation** (BWRA, policies, training
  records);
- **Financial information** about the firm;
- **Officer / principal-person information**;
- **Information about specific incidents** identified.

### Process

1. **Receive** the request — note the deadline and scope;
2. **Acknowledge** receipt promptly;
3. **Identify** what's required;
4. **Gather** the materials;
5. **Review** for accuracy and completeness;
6. **Submit** through prescribed channel;
7. **Cooperate** with follow-up.

### Confidentiality

JFSC requests are confidential — generally don't disclose to:

- **The customer** subject of the request (potential tipping
  off);
- **Staff** without need to know;
- **Third parties** (lawyers, advisers) without authorisation.

### Privilege

Legal professional privilege survives JFSC information powers
— see Article 209 of the Companies Law analogue and equivalent
provisions in the regulatory Laws. Privileged materials should
be:

- **Identified** in any response;
- **Withheld** with explanation;
- **Available** for JFSC review only if privilege is waived.

### Failure to comply

Failure attracts:

- **Civil financial penalties** under the CFP regime;
- **Public statements** in serious cases;
- **Conditions or suspension** of registration;
- **Personal liability** of MLRO and principal persons.

## What to do next

For an active request:

1. **Acknowledge** receipt;
2. **Assess** scope and effort;
3. **Gather** materials;
4. **Review** for accuracy;
5. **Submit**;
6. **Follow up** on any clarifications.

## Pitfalls

- **Missing the deadline**;
- **Incomplete response** that prompts follow-up;
- **Tipping off** the customer by inadvertent disclosure;
- **Privilege waiver** through inadvertent disclosure.
