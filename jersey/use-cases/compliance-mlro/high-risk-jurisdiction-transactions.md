# High-Risk Jurisdiction Transactions

## The question

How do you handle transactions involving **high-risk
jurisdictions** — sending funds to, receiving funds from, or
counterparties based in such countries?

## The short answer

For transactions involving high-risk jurisdictions:

- **Apply EDD** to the customer and the transaction;
- **Verify** counterparty identity and legitimacy carefully;
- **Document** the commercial purpose of the transaction;
- **Screen** counterparties against sanctions / PEP / adverse-
  media lists;
- **Senior-management approval** for material transactions;
- **Enhanced monitoring** ongoing.

Specific Jersey AML rules apply to **high-risk
third countries** identified by FATF / Jersey-prescribed
lists.

## The proper answer

### Defining "high-risk jurisdiction"

High-risk for AML / CFT purposes typically means:

- **FATF black list** (Non-Cooperative Countries and
  Territories);
- **FATF grey list** (Countries Under Increased Monitoring);
- **Jersey-specific** high-risk lists (as published);
- **Sanctioned jurisdictions** (Iran, North Korea, etc.);
- **Conflict zones** and **failed states**;
- **Specific concern jurisdictions** the firm has identified
  through BWRA.

### EDD requirements

For high-risk-jurisdiction connected customers / transactions:

- **Senior-management approval** of the relationship and
  material transactions;
- **Enhanced source-of-wealth / source-of-funds** verification;
- **Detailed transaction-purpose** documentation;
- **Counterparty diligence** including BO identification;
- **Adverse-media** screening on counterparties;
- **Specific transaction approval** for higher-risk dealings.

### Specific issues

#### Sending funds to high-risk jurisdictions

- **Verify** the recipient's identity and legitimacy;
- **Document** the purpose (commercial / family / charitable);
- **Confirm** no sanctions exposure;
- **Specific approval** for material amounts;
- **Bank counterparty** likely to scrutinise also.

#### Receiving funds from high-risk jurisdictions

- **Verify** source of funds;
- **Confirm** legitimate origin;
- **Source-of-funds evidence** beyond ordinary;
- **Specific approval**;
- **Document**.

#### Counterparties based in high-risk jurisdictions

- **Verify** counterparty existence and ownership;
- **Sanctions / PEP screen**;
- **Reputation / adverse-media** review;
- **Operating-history** verification.

### FATF list-based handling

#### FATF Black list

- **Mandatory countermeasures** required;
- **Very high friction** for any business;
- Often **complete prohibition** by firm policy.

#### FATF Grey list

- **Enhanced due diligence** mandatory;
- **Specific identification** of customers / transactions
  affected;
- **Risk-based** mitigation.

### Sanctioned jurisdictions

For dealings with sanctioned jurisdictions:

- **Often complete prohibition** by sanctions law;
- **Licensed exceptions** only for permitted dealings;
- **Documentation** of licence basis.

### Practical compliance

For each high-risk-jurisdiction-related transaction:

1. **Identify** the high-risk element;
2. **Conduct EDD** on customer / counterparty;
3. **Verify** transaction purpose;
4. **Obtain** senior approval;
5. **Document** thoroughly;
6. **Monitor** for adverse outcomes.

### Banking and correspondent considerations

- Correspondent banks **scrutinise high-risk transactions**;
- **Pre-emptive disclosure** to correspondent often necessary;
- **De-risking** by correspondents can affect the firm's
  ability to process.

### Documentation

For each high-risk transaction:

- **Risk assessment**;
- **EDD documentation**;
- **Approval records**;
- **Transaction details**;
- **Commercial-purpose evidence**;
- **Outcome monitoring**.

## What to do next

For an active situation:

1. **Identify** high-risk element;
2. **Apply EDD**;
3. **Obtain** senior approval;
4. **Document** comprehensively;
5. **Execute** with appropriate monitoring.

For programme:

- **Maintain** current high-risk-jurisdiction list;
- **Train** staff on identification;
- **Document** policy for handling.

## Pitfalls

- **Missing high-risk identification**: customer-jurisdiction
  is not always obvious;
- **Inadequate EDD**: high-risk requires more than standard;
- **Senior-approval drift**: approval becomes pro-forma;
- **Banking de-risking** consequences underestimated.
