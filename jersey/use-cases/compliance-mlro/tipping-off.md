# Tipping Off — What Can and Cannot Be Said?

## The question

After a SAR has been filed (or considered), what can be said to
the customer? The **tipping-off offence** under the Proceeds of
Crime (Jersey) Law 1999 has serious consequences.

## The short answer

The tipping-off offence is committed by **disclosure** that:

- **Knowledge or suspicion** of money laundering has been
  disclosed (or might be); or
- **An investigation** is being or might be conducted; **and**
- Disclosure is **likely to prejudice** any consequent
  investigation.

**Don't tell the customer** that a SAR has been filed, is
contemplated, or that their activities are under review.
**Don't share** SAR-related information with anyone not
authorised. Standard customer-service explanations must be
carefully crafted.

## The proper answer

### Who can know

- **MLRO** and senior management approving the SAR;
- **Discloser** and others who internally disclosed;
- **Legal counsel** providing advice;
- **JFSC and FIU** through formal channels;
- **Group entities** in some controlled-disclosure
  circumstances.

### Who cannot know

- **The customer** (subject of the SAR);
- **The customer's representatives** (unless authorised
  legal counsel for the firm);
- **Other staff** without legitimate need;
- **Third parties** generally.

### Common situations and language

#### Customer asks why transaction is delayed

- **Don't say**: "We've filed a SAR" or "We've reported you";
- **Can say**: "We're conducting standard compliance review",
  "Operational delay", "Pending verification";
- **Be vague but professional** without dishonesty.

#### Customer asks about CDD-refresh request

- **Don't say**: "We're concerned about your activity";
- **Can say**: "Periodic review per our regulatory
  requirements", "Routine refresh";
- **Frame as policy rather than customer-specific**.

#### Customer asks to close account / move accounts

- **Don't say**: "We can't process this because of the SAR";
- **Can say**: "We need to follow standard exit procedures",
  "Process will take typical time";
- **Comply** with FIU consent requirements;
- **Don't act in a way** that telegraphs the SAR.

#### Customer asks why account is closed

- After a properly-handled exit:
- **Can say**: "Commercial decision based on review";
- **Don't elaborate**.

### Defence and protection

The Proceeds of Crime Law provides limited defences:

- **Disclosure within the regulated sector** under specified
  circumstances;
- **Disclosure to legal advisers** for the firm;
- **Disclosure to law enforcement** under their direction;
- **Specific information requests** properly handled.

Outside these, **default to non-disclosure**.

### Practical risks

- **Casual conversation** with the customer: easy slip;
- **Body language and tone**: indirect tipping off;
- **Documentation accidentally shared**: emails forwarded to
  customer, internal notes mishandled;
- **Group-entity disclosure**: ensure group-wide tipping-off
  policy.

### Training and protocol

Firm-wide training should cover:

- **Default non-disclosure** to customer;
- **Approved standard responses** to common questions;
- **Escalation** when customer questions become probing;
- **Communication protocol** internally;
- **Documentation discipline** to avoid leaks.

## What to do next

For a specific situation:

1. **Identify** what the customer is asking;
2. **Apply** the standard approved responses;
3. **Avoid** detail that telegraphs the SAR;
4. **Document** the interaction;
5. **Escalate** to MLRO if pressure increases.

For programme:

- **Establish** standard tipping-off-safe language;
- **Train** staff on application;
- **Audit** compliance.

## Pitfalls

- **Inadvertent disclosure** through body language or
  inadequately crafted explanation;
- **Documentation slip** — internal notes reaching the customer;
- **Group-entity leak**;
- **Lawyer-confidentiality misunderstanding** — not all legal
  advice protects.
