# Outsourcing of Compliance Functions — When Permitted?

## The question

Can your firm **outsource** compliance functions — KYC, AML
screening, internal audit, MLRO support?

## The short answer

Most operational compliance functions **can be outsourced**
subject to Code-of-Practice conditions:

- **KYC / CDD operations**: yes (with proper oversight);
- **AML / sanctions screening tools**: yes (data is the
  outsourced element);
- **Internal audit**: yes (often outsourced to specialist
  firms);
- **MLRO support functions**: yes (but the MLRO role itself
  cannot be outsourced).

**Cannot outsource**:

- **MLRO role** itself (must be a person within the regulated
  entity);
- **Board / governance** functions;
- **Statutory decision-making authority**;
- **Ultimate responsibility** for compliance.

## The proper answer

### Code-of-Practice conditions

Under the JFSC Code of Practice for the relevant class:

- **Risk assessment** before outsourcing;
- **Written agreement** with the provider;
- **Continuing responsibility** of the regulated entity;
- **Right of audit** by the entity and JFSC;
- **Exit strategy**;
- **JFSC notification** for material outsourcing.

### What can be outsourced

#### CDD / KYC operations

- **Document collection and verification**;
- **Screening tools** (sanctions / PEP / adverse media);
- **CDD file maintenance**;
- **Periodic refresh** administration.

The **MLRO retains** decision-making on accept / decline.

#### AML screening

- **Sanctions / PEP screening** through specialist providers
  (Refinitiv, Dow Jones, WorldCheck);
- **Adverse-media monitoring**;
- **Transaction monitoring** tools.

Match-handling decisions remain with the firm.

#### Internal audit

- **Outsourced internal audit** is common;
- **Independence** from operations required;
- **Reports** to the firm's audit committee.

#### Tech / systems

- **Compliance technology platforms**;
- **Case-management systems**;
- **Document storage / e-discovery**.

### What cannot be outsourced

#### MLRO

The MLRO must:

- Be a **person** (not an entity);
- Be **within the regulated entity** (employed or appointed
  officer);
- Be **suitably senior**;
- Have **direct access** to senior management.

The role **itself** cannot be transferred to a third party.
**Support functions** can be outsourced; **the role** cannot.

#### Board / governance

- **Board membership** must be the entity's own directors;
- **Board oversight** cannot be delegated outside the
  entity.

#### Statutory decision-making

- **JFSC-required decisions** by principal persons cannot be
  delegated;
- **Fit-and-proper** assessments rest with the entity.

### Group-internal outsourcing

Outsourcing to **group entities** is common:

- TCB outsources to group operations centre in another
  jurisdiction;
- **Group services agreement** documents the arrangement;
- **Same Code requirements** apply (oversight, audit rights,
  etc.);
- **Easier** to maintain quality and control within group.

### Outsourcing to non-Jersey providers

For outsourcing to providers outside Jersey:

- **JFSC consent** typically required for material outsourcing;
- **Data-protection** considerations;
- **Continuity** plans for cross-border issues;
- **Audit access** preserved.

### Common outsourcing scenarios

#### Outsourced KYC platform

- Specialist provider runs CDD platform;
- Customer documents stored in platform;
- Firm's compliance team accesses for decisions;
- Provider screens against sanctions / PEP lists;
- **MLRO retains** decision authority.

#### Outsourced internal audit

- External firm provides annual / periodic audits;
- **Independent** of management;
- **Reports** to audit committee;
- Findings drive remediation.

#### Outsourced sanctions screening

- Specialist provider's screening tool integrated;
- Daily / weekly automated screening;
- Match-clearing process by firm staff;
- **MLRO retains** match-handling decisions.

### Effectiveness

Outsourced functions must be:

- **Operating as designed** (regular review);
- **Adequately resourced** by provider;
- **Subject to firm oversight**;
- **Resilient** to provider disruptions.

The firm **cannot delegate accountability** by outsourcing —
ultimate responsibility remains.

## What to do next

For new outsourcing:

1. **Risk-assess** the proposed arrangement;
2. **Negotiate** the outsourcing agreement;
3. **Notify** JFSC if material;
4. **Implement** oversight mechanisms;
5. **Operate** with ongoing monitoring.

For existing outsourcing:

- **Review** periodically;
- **Confirm** continuing fitness;
- **Address** any concerns.

## Pitfalls

- **MLRO outsourcing**: cannot outsource the role;
- **Loss of oversight**: outsourcing without monitoring;
- **Provider failure**: business-continuity exposure;
- **Audit-right gap**: must preserve in agreement.
