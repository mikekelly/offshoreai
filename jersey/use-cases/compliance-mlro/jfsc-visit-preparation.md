# JFSC Compliance Visit — How to Prepare

## The question

The JFSC has notified your firm of an **upcoming compliance
visit / thematic review / risk-mitigation programme** visit.
How do you prepare?

## The short answer

Preparation steps:

1. **Confirm** the visit scope, date, and information requests
   from the JFSC notification;
2. **Assemble** internal team — MLRO, compliance officer,
   department heads, senior management;
3. **Gather** documentation responsive to the JFSC's areas of
   focus;
4. **Prepare** materials and dry-run interview responses;
5. **Brief** staff on what to expect and how to engage;
6. **Conduct** the visit professionally with documented
   responses;
7. **Follow up** on action items from the JFSC's report.

JFSC visits are **regular** in TCB / regulated-entity life;
they should be treated as **professional dialogue**, not
adversarial events.

## The proper answer

### Types of JFSC engagement

#### Compliance visit (full)

- **Comprehensive review** of the firm's compliance arrangements;
- **Typically multi-day** with multiple JFSC personnel;
- **Document review** and **interviews**;
- **Detailed report** with findings and required actions.

#### Thematic review

- **Specific subject focus** (e.g. AML on PEPs, CRS controls,
  outsourcing);
- **Sample-based** review across firms;
- **Industry-wide report** typically;
- **Firm-specific follow-up** if findings.

#### Risk-mitigation programme (RMP) visit

- **Follow-up** after identified concerns;
- **Specific remediation** progress check;
- **Targeted scope**.

#### Authorisation / change visit

- **Pre-registration** of new firms;
- **Material change** in principal persons / structure;
- **Operational due-diligence** by JFSC.

### Pre-visit preparation

#### Confirm scope

From the JFSC notification:

- **Date and duration**;
- **Areas of focus**;
- **Information requested** in advance;
- **JFSC personnel** attending;
- **Specific files** they want to see.

Respond to information requests **comprehensively and
promptly**.

#### Internal team

Assemble:

- **MLRO** as primary AML / CFT point;
- **Compliance officer** for broader compliance;
- **Department heads** for area-specific questions;
- **Senior management** for governance / policy questions;
- **Operations** for specific transactions / files;
- **Legal** for any contested issues.

#### Documentation review

For the focus areas, ensure:

- **Policies and procedures** current and approved;
- **BWRA** current;
- **Training records** current;
- **CDD files** organised for sample review;
- **Transaction records** retrievable;
- **Minute books** current;
- **Regulatory correspondence** organised;
- **Reports** to the board / committees current.

#### Dry-run interviews

If senior people will be interviewed:

- **Brief** them on likely topics;
- **Practice** key explanations;
- **Refresh** them on policy commitments;
- **Plan** for difficult questions (known issues, recent
  incidents).

### During the visit

#### Logistics

- **Reserve meeting rooms**;
- **Provide** workspaces for JFSC personnel;
- **Make** documents available promptly when requested;
- **Coordinate** lunch / refreshments;
- **Plan** debriefs at end of each day.

#### Tone

- **Professional and collaborative**;
- **Transparent**;
- **Don't volunteer** information beyond what's asked;
- **Don't withhold** information that's requested;
- **Document** the engagement.

#### Difficult questions

When asked about identified weaknesses or incidents:

- **Acknowledge** the issue;
- **Explain** the cause;
- **Describe** the remediation;
- **Avoid** defensive posturing.

### After the visit

#### Initial debrief

- **JFSC typically debriefs** at end of visit;
- **Document** the debrief points;
- **Identify** issues raised.

#### Formal report

- **JFSC issues report** with findings and required actions;
- **Review** carefully;
- **Respond** with action plans;
- **Track** completion of action items;
- **Notify** the JFSC of completion.

#### Follow-up

- **Implement** required changes;
- **Report** progress to JFSC;
- **Address** any sub-issues that emerge;
- **Document** the closure of action items.

### Common JFSC focus areas

Recurring themes in JFSC visits:

- **PEP and high-risk CDD** quality;
- **SAR procedures** and decision-making;
- **Sanctions screening** robustness;
- **CRS / FATCA** classification accuracy;
- **Outsourcing** governance;
- **Board oversight** of compliance;
- **Training programme** effectiveness;
- **Record-keeping** discipline.

### Defending the firm

If criticism is expected:

- **Engage** counsel early;
- **Prepare** factual responses;
- **Acknowledge** what can be acknowledged;
- **Contest** where there is genuine disagreement;
- **Document** the position.

The objective is **constructive engagement** — the JFSC's role
is supervisory, not adversarial in normal circumstances.

## What to do next

For a notified visit:

1. **Read** the JFSC notification carefully;
2. **Respond** to advance information requests;
3. **Assemble** the team;
4. **Prepare** documents and dry-run interviews;
5. **Conduct** the visit professionally;
6. **Follow up** on JFSC findings.

For programme readiness:

- **Mock visits** internally / by external advisers;
- **Documentation discipline** ongoing;
- **Training and refresh** regular;
- **Issue-management** discipline (track and remediate
  weaknesses).

## Pitfalls

- **Defensive posturing**: damages the relationship;
- **Document chaos** when JFSC requests files;
- **Inconsistent answers** across team members;
- **Failure to follow up** on required actions.
