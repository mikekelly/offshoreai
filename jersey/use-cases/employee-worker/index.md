# Employee / Worker — Use Cases

## Who this section is for

You are a **Jersey-based employee** dealing with a real
employment problem from the employee side — being dismissed,
underpaid, harassed, discriminated against, denied leave,
sick, pregnant, made redundant, or unsure what your contract
actually entitles you to.

The other employment-facing persona (founder-entrepreneur)
covers the **employer's** view — drafting contracts, hiring,
managing performance. This persona covers the **worker's**
view: what protections do I have, when can I bring a claim,
how does the Employment & Discrimination Tribunal work?

Your primary statutes are the **Employment (Jersey) Law
2003** (already covered at article-level) and the
**Discrimination (Jersey) Law 2013** (also article-level
covered). Statutes still uncovered include the **Health and
Safety at Work (Jersey) Law 1989** and the **Jersey Advisory
and Conciliation Service (JACS)** statutory framework.

## Canonical questions

### Pay and conditions

- Minimum wage in Jersey — current rate, who's exempt
- Itemised pay statements — what I'm entitled to see
- Deductions from wages — what's lawful, what isn't
- Holiday pay — accrual, carrying over, on termination
- Sick pay — what's contractual vs statutory
- Tips and gratuities

### Working time and leave

- Maximum hours, rest breaks, time off in lieu
- Annual leave entitlement
- Maternity, paternity, parental, and adoption leave
- Compassionate leave / bereavement leave
- Sick leave

### Discrimination and harassment

- Protected characteristics under the Discrimination Law
- Direct vs indirect discrimination
- Harassment — sexual, racial, etc.
- Victimisation
- Reasonable adjustments for disability
- Pay-equality claims

### Dismissal and redundancy

- Unfair dismissal — qualifying service, grounds
- Wrongful dismissal — contractual notice
- Constructive dismissal
- Redundancy — process, consultation, pay
- Whistleblowing protections
- Notice periods — statutory vs contractual

### Tribunal procedure

- Employment & Discrimination Tribunal — what it does
- JACS conciliation — when and how
- Time limits — 8 weeks default
- Remedies — reinstatement, compensation, declaration
- Costs

### Specific situations

- "I'm being bullied at work"
- "I was sacked for raising a concern"
- "My contract says one thing, my boss does another"
- "I'm pregnant and the offers have suddenly stopped"
- "My employer changed my hours without consulting me"

### Health and safety

- HSWL 1989 employer duties (worker perspective)
- Reporting an unsafe workplace
- Refusing dangerous work
- Stress and mental-health claims
