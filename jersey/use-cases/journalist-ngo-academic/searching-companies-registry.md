# Searching the Jersey Companies Registry

## The question

You are a journalist, researcher, or NGO investigator. What
information about a Jersey company can you find publicly, and
how?

## The short answer

The Jersey Companies Registry, run by the JFSC, is **publicly
searchable** online. For each company you can typically see:

- **Name** and registered number;
- **Status** (active / struck off / etc.);
- **Date of incorporation**;
- **Company type**;
- **Registered office** (the corporate-services-provider address,
  usually);
- **Directors** (current and historical);
- **Secretary**;
- **Memorandum and articles** of association (with appropriate
  fees);
- **Annual confirmation statement filings**;
- **Special resolutions** filed.

You **cannot** see beneficial-ownership detail in the central
public register (this is restricted to law enforcement and
authorities). You **cannot** see day-to-day operational
information (contracts, finances of private companies).

## The proper answer

### How to search

The JFSC Registry has an **online portal** at
**jerseyfsc.org/registry**:

- **Free** basic searches by company name or number;
- **Paid** access to detailed documents (memorandum, articles,
  filed resolutions) — typically a few pounds per document;
- **Certificate of good standing** orders for a fee.

For systematic research:

- **Bulk data** is **not freely available** — the Registry sells
  bulk extracts at commercial rates, primarily to compliance
  / KYC / data providers;
- Various **third-party providers** (Bureau van Dijk, Refinitiv,
  Companies House Hub-style services) aggregate Jersey data.

### What you can see

For any Jersey company, basic public information includes:

| Field | Public? | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Name | Yes | Registry search |
| Company number | Yes | Registry search |
| Status | Yes | Registry search |
| Type (private / public / limited / etc.) | Yes | Registry search |
| Date of incorporation | Yes | Registry search |
| Registered office address | Yes | Registry search |
| Directors and secretary | Yes | Registry filings |
| Beneficial owners | **No** (in the public register) | Restricted to authorities |
| Share capital | Yes (in memorandum) | Filed memorandum |
| Auditor | Yes (where filed) | Annual filings |

### What you cannot see (publicly)

- **Beneficial ownership** — held in the **central register**
  under the FS(DPI)(J)L 2020 but **not publicly accessible**;
- **Detailed financial information** for private companies
  (private companies need not file public accounts in Jersey, in
  contrast to UK);
- **Day-to-day operations**;
- **Contracts and commercial dealings**.

### Trust company services involvement

Many Jersey companies are **administered by trust companies**:

- The **registered office** is typically a trust-company address;
- The **directors** are often **trust-company nominees** or
  **professional directors**;
- The **company secretary** is often the trust company.

This is **lawful and common** but means the registry **doesn't
reveal the ultimate beneficial owner** without further
investigation.

### Beneficial-ownership transparency

Beneficial-ownership information is:

- **Collected** by Jersey under the FS(DPI)(J)L 2020;
- **Verified** by service providers (trust companies, lawyers)
  under AML rules;
- **Held centrally** in a register accessible to **law
  enforcement, the JFSC, Revenue Jersey**, and authorised
  foreign authorities;
- **Not publicly accessible** at this writing.

Some categories (e.g. "Persons with Significant Control") may be
in a more limited public register, but the position evolves.

For comparison:

- **UK PSC register**: publicly accessible (with privacy
  exemptions);
- **Jersey**: authorities-only;
- **Cayman, BVI, Bermuda**: authorities-only.

This is a **moving regulatory area** — international pressure
(EU, FATF, OECD) continues to push for greater transparency.

### Royal Court records

Separately:

- **Royal Court judgments** are published on **jerseylaw.je**;
- **Probate records** are at the Judicial Greffe;
- **Land transactions** are in the Public Registry.

### Practical investigation tips

For journalism / NGO research on Jersey-company structures:

1. **Start with the public Registry** for basic identification;
2. **Order constitutional documents** for share structure;
3. **Map directors and secretary** identities — these are often
   trust-company employees, giving you the **service provider**
   identity;
4. **Search Royal Court** records for any litigation involving
   the company;
5. **Check news / litigation databases** for the same;
6. **Consider** information-request avenues if the matter is
   public-interest (FATF / Moneyval / regulator information
   exchanges may be relevant in specific cases).

## What to do next

For an active investigation:

1. **Identify** the specific Jersey companies of interest;
2. **Run searches** through the JFSC Registry portal;
3. **Build** a registered-office / director / secretary map to
   identify service providers and possible nominees;
4. **Cross-reference** with other-jurisdiction company registers
   (UK, BVI, Cayman, etc.) for related entities;
5. **Search** Royal Court judgments and news databases for any
   publicly-known information about beneficial owners;
6. **Consider** information-request routes for legitimate
   investigation purposes.

## Pitfalls

- **Assuming nominee directors mean wrongdoing** — nominee
  arrangements are common, legitimate, and disclosed to the
  service provider for KYC purposes.
- **Conflating registered-office address with beneficial
  ownership** — the registered office is the service-provider
  address, not the owner's.
- **Overstating** what you can know — Jersey is **less
  transparent than UK** on beneficial ownership and more
  transparent than some other offshore jurisdictions; pitch
  accordingly.
