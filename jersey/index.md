# Jersey

## What this is and where you are

You are at the **entry point** to the Jersey knowledge base — one
jurisdiction within the broader [Offshore AI corpus](../README.md).

**Jersey** is a **British Crown Dependency**, a self-governing
island jurisdiction in the Bay of Mont-Saint-Michel, roughly 14
miles off the coast of Normandy and 85 miles south of England. It
has approximately 105,000 residents.

**The single most important fact about Jersey** for everything that
follows: **Jersey is not part of the United Kingdom and never was
part of the European Union**. The King is head of state and the UK
Government carries formal responsibility for defence and (to some
extent) foreign affairs, but Jersey has its **own** legislature,
courts, tax system, and financial-services regulator. Every section
below describes a system that is **Jersey's own**, not a Jersey
adaptation of UK rules — even where there is family resemblance to
English law. ([gov.je — relationship with the UK and EU][gov-rel])

## What kinds of questions live in this knowledge base

Use this corpus when you have a question of any of the following
kinds:

1. **"What is the Jersey rule on X?"** — pure-Jersey-law questions:
   how a Jersey trust works, how a Jersey company is taxed, how the
   JFSC regulates a trust company business. Land in the relevant
   topical section (Trusts, Companies, Tax, Financial Regulation).
2. **"How does Jersey interact with [foreign jurisdiction] on Y?"** —
   cross-border questions: UK tax of a Jersey trust, EU marketing
   of a Jersey AIF, US grantor-trust treatment, forced-heirship
   recognition. Start in [International](international/index.md)
   and the relevant topical section.
3. **"As a [persona], what do I need to know about Z?"** — operator
   questions where you have a specific role and a specific task.
   Start in [Use Cases](use-cases/index.md), find your persona,
   read the relevant question file.
4. **"What does Article X of [Jersey statute] say?"** — primary-law
   questions. Each statute has an Articles index that lists every
   provision; the Trusts Law 1984 index is complete, the Companies
   Law 1991 index is partial-and-growing.

If you're not sure which kind of question you have, the persona-
driven [use-cases](use-cases/index.md) are usually the best entry
point.

## At a glance — Jersey facts an agent should know on landing

These are the facts that come up in almost every Jersey question.

| | |
|---|---|
| Constitutional status | British Crown Dependency. Self-governing in domestic matters. Not part of the UK. Not part of the EU. [→ details](government/crown-dependency.md) |
| Head of state | The King, represented locally by the [Lieutenant-Governor](government/lieutenant-governor.md) |
| Legislature | The [States Assembly](government/states-assembly.md) — 49 elected members (37 Deputies + 12 Connétables) for the current term; the role of Senator returns at the 2026 election |
| Executive | [Council of Ministers](government/council-of-ministers.md), led by a Chief Minister |
| Top court (domestic) | The [Royal Court](legal-system/royal-court.md), and on appeal the [Jersey Court of Appeal](legal-system/court-of-appeal.md) |
| Final appeal | The Judicial Committee of the Privy Council (JCPC) in London |
| Financial regulator | [Jersey Financial Services Commission (JFSC)](financial-regulation/jfsc.md) |
| Tax authority | [Revenue Jersey](tax/revenue-jersey.md) (part of the Treasury) |
| Standard corporate tax rate | **0%** (the "[zero-ten](tax/zero-ten.md)" regime) |
| Standard personal income-tax rate | [**20%**](tax/personal-income-tax.md) (with marginal relief for low incomes; no higher band) |
| Goods & Services Tax | [**5%**](tax/gst.md) |
| Capital gains tax | **None** |
| Inheritance tax | **None** |
| Currency | Pound sterling (Jersey issues its own banknotes at par with GBP) |
| Customs | In a [customs union with the UK](international/uk-relationship.md); covered by the UK–EU Trade and Cooperation Agreement for goods (post-Brexit; Protocol 3 fell away) |

they orient on everything else.

   not) do for Jersey.

   what licensed status the typical service provider has.

## Sections

Each section has its own `index.md` which is the cold-reader entry
point for that section. Click through for orientation; click the
sub-files within a section for detail.

### [Government](government/index.md)
The Crown relationship, the States Assembly, the Council of Ministers,
the Bailiff and Lieutenant-Governor, the parishes, and the elections.
Read this section to understand Jersey's **constitutional position**
— the foundation of everything else.

### [Legal system](legal-system/index.md)
Sources of Jersey law (Norman customary law, statute, case law),
the **Royal Court** (Samedi Division, Inferior Number, Superior
Number), the Court of Appeal, appeals to the Privy Council, the
Bailiff, Jurats, the Judicial Greffe, and the legal profession
(Advocates and Solicitors).

### [Tax](tax/index.md)
The **zero-ten** corporate regime (0% standard, 10% for financial-
services companies, 20% for utilities / large retailers / hydrocarbon
/ cannabis / property income), personal income tax (20% with
marginal relief), **GST** at 5%, social-security contributions,
the Long-Term Care contribution, **international tax** (CRS, FATCA,
DTAs, TIEAs, OECD **Pillar Two**), **economic substance**, stamp
duty, and what Jersey *doesn't* tax (no capital gains, no
inheritance, no wealth).

### [Companies](companies/index.md)
The [Companies (Jersey) Law 1991](companies/companies-law-1991.md):
types of company (public / private / par-value / no-par-value /
guarantee / cell companies — PCC and ICC), incorporation, share
capital, directors' duties, accounts, distributions and the
**Article 115 cash-flow solvency test**, mergers, **continuance**
(redomiciliation), and winding-up. Also LLPs and the various
flavours of limited partnership.

### [Trusts](trusts/index.md)
The [Trusts (Jersey) Law 1984](trusts/trusts-law-1984.md): the
Jersey trust as a wealth-structuring vehicle, types (discretionary,
fixed-interest, charitable, non-charitable purpose, reserved-powers),
the **firewall** in Article 9, settlor reserved powers in Article 9A,
the **statutory mistake regime** at Articles 47B–H, and the Royal
Court's directions jurisdiction at Article 51. Per-Article coverage
of the Trusts Law is **complete**.

### [Foundations](foundations/index.md)
The [Foundations (Jersey) Law 2009](foundations/foundations-law-2009.md):
a civil-law-flavoured body-corporate vehicle with a **council**, a
mandatory **guardian**, and a Jersey-regulated **qualified member**;
how Jersey foundations differ from trusts (beneficiaries do not
have beneficial ownership of the assets as of right).

### [Funds](funds/index.md)
The Jersey fund regimes by investor sophistication — Jersey Private
Fund (JPF), Eligible Investor Fund, Expert Fund, Listed Fund — and
the public-fund regime under the Collective Investment Funds
(Jersey) Law 1988. AIFMD National Private Placement Regimes (NPPR)
for marketing into individual EU member states and the UK.

### [Banking](banking/index.md)
The Banking Business (Jersey) Law 1991 and the deposit-taking
regime; the historic "top-50" bank policy; depositor compensation.

### [Insurance](insurance/index.md)
The Insurance Business (Jersey) Law 1996; Category A and B permits;
captive use; insurance cell-company use.

### [Financial regulation](financial-regulation/index.md)
The JFSC: its statutory basis under the [Financial Services
Commission (Jersey) Law 1998](financial-regulation/jfsc-law-1998.md);
the principal regulatory laws and the regulated-activity classes
under each (trust company business, fund services business,
investment business, general-insurance mediation, money services
business, banking business, insurance business); the JFSC codes of
practice; licensing and fit-and-proper; enforcement.

### [AML/CFT](aml-cft/index.md)
The Proceeds of Crime (Jersey) Law 1999, the **Money Laundering
(Jersey) Order 2008**, the JFSC AML/CFT Handbook, the Sanctions and
Asset-Freezing (Jersey) Law 2014, and Moneyval evaluations.

### [International](international/index.md)
The UK relationship, the post-Brexit EU relationship (Protocol 3
fell away; UK–EU TCA applies for goods), **CRS**, **FATCA**, DTAs
and TIEAs, the OECD inclusive framework, and **Pillar Two**
implementation.

### [Immigration & residency](immigration-residency/index.md)
The Control of Housing and Work (Jersey) Law 2012 categories —
*Entitled*, *Licensed*, *Entitled for Work*, *Registered* — and the
**2(1)(e) High-Value Residency (HVR)** route with its bespoke tax
regime.

### [Registries](registries/index.md)
JFSC Registry (companies, foundations, partnerships, business
names, VASPs), the **non-public beneficial-ownership register**
under the FSDI Law 2020, the Land Registry, and the charities
register.

### [Insolvency](insolvency/index.md)
The **Bankruptcy (Désastre) (Jersey) Law 1990** — Jersey's
principal insolvency statute. Désastre procedure, avoidance
powers (preferences, undervalue transactions, fraudulent
transfers), distribution priority, discharge, and cross-border
insolvency cooperation. Operates alongside creditors' winding-
up under Companies Law Part 21.

### [Succession](succession/index.md)
The **Wills and Successions (Jersey) Law 1993**: will
formalities (including holograph wills), légitime (forced
heirship for spouse and children of the movable estate),
intestacy rules, and the movable / immovable property
distinction. Real-property succession remains substantially
under customary law.

### [Charities](charities/index.md)
The **Charities (Jersey) Law 2014**: charitable purposes,
public-benefit test, Charity Commissioner, registered-charity
status, three sections of the charity register, trustee
duties, cy-près.

### [Data Protection](data-protection/index.md)
The **Data Protection (Jersey) Law 2018** — Jersey's GDPR-
equivalent: lawful bases, data subject rights, controller /
processor obligations, breach notification, international
transfers, Information Commissioner (JOIC) supervision. Tier
1 / Tier 2 fines mirror GDPR.

### [Employment](employment/index.md)
The **Employment (Jersey) Law 2003**: written particulars,
minimum wage, working time, family-friendly leave, unfair
dismissal, redundancy, and the Jersey Employment and
Discrimination Tribunal.

### [Discrimination](discrimination/index.md)
The **Discrimination (Jersey) Law 2013**: protected
characteristics added in phases since 2014 (race, sex,
sexual orientation, gender reassignment, pregnancy and
maternity, age, disability), direct and indirect
discrimination, reasonable adjustments, equal pay.

### [Property](property/index.md)
Jersey real-property law: the **Loi (1880) sur la propriété
foncière**, hypothec (Jersey real-property security), contract
passing before the Royal Court, servitudes (Jersey easements),
leases, **Security Interests (Jersey) Law 2012** for personal
property, and customary real-property succession.

### [Residential tenancy](residential-tenancy/index.md)
Residential lettings under the **Residential Tenancy (Jersey)
Law 2011** as amended by the **2025 Amendment Law** (in force
15 April 2026): forms of tenancy, condition reports, deposits,
notice periods (1 month tenant / 3 or 12 months landlord),
RPI-capped rent reviews, the Rent Tribunal, eviction by
Petty Debts Court and Viscount enforcement. The first gap-fill
from the [coverage audit](COVERAGE-AUDIT.md).

### [Family law](family/index.md)
Divorce, ancillary relief, child arrangements, care and
protection of children, civil partnerships, cohabitation, and
adoption. Principal statutes: **Matrimonial Causes (Jersey)
Law 1949**, **Children (Jersey) Law 2002**, **Civil
Partnership (Jersey) Law 2012**, **Adoption (Jersey) Law
1961**. Forum: the **Royal Court Family Division**. The
second gap-fill from the coverage audit.

### [Road traffic](road-traffic/index.md)
Motor vehicle offences and enforcement under the **Road
Traffic (Jersey) Law 1956**, plus the distinctive
**Parish Hall Enquiry** layer where Centeniers (Honorary
Police) resolve most routine motoring offences before they
reach the Magistrate's Court. Includes drink-driving
(80 mg / 100 ml), speeding, careless / dangerous driving,
driving without insurance, disqualification. The third
gap-fill from the coverage audit.

### [Contract and obligations](contract/index.md)
Jersey contract / obligations law: formation (four classical
requirements per Pothier including cause), performance and
breach, damages, specific performance (broader in Jersey than
English law), misrepresentation, unjust enrichment / quasi-
contract, and délit / tort (including voisinage).
