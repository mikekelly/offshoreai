# EL 2003 Part 4 — Working Time and Leave

## Articles 23-26 — Working time

The EL contains working-time provisions:

### Maximum weekly hours

- **Average** hours worked over a reference period;
- **Default cap** at prescribed level (typically 48 hours per
  week);
- **Opt-out** available with worker consent;
- **Specific** exemptions for senior management, household
  staff, transport workers.

### Rest periods

- **Daily rest** — minimum 11 hours between working periods;
- **Weekly rest** — minimum 24 hours per week (or 48 hours
  fortnightly);
- **Rest breaks** during shifts over a threshold (typically
  6+ hours).

### Night work

- **Limits** on night work hours;
- **Health assessment** for night workers;
- **Specific** sectoral rules.

## Articles 27-30 — Holiday entitlement

### Statutory holiday

- **Minimum** annual leave (prescribed in days per year —
  typically 2 weeks plus statutory holidays);
- **Pro-rata** for part-time;
- **Holiday pay** at average rate of pay including regular
  overtime.

### Bank holidays

- **Treatment** depends on contract;
- **Default** is paid bank holidays in addition to statutory
  leave;
- **Specific** sectoral practices.

### Carry-over

- **Limited** carry-over of unused leave;
- **Pro-rata** payments on termination for unused leave;
- **Specific** rules for sickness during leave.

## Articles 31-35 — Family-friendly rights

### Maternity leave

- **52 weeks** maximum maternity leave;
- **Pay** through Social Security maternity benefit plus
  contractual enhancement (if any);
- **Right to return** to same / suitable role;
- **Protection** from dismissal during leave.

### Paternity leave

- **Specific** prescribed period (typically 2 weeks);
- **Pay** through Social Security paternity benefit plus
  contractual;
- **Right to return**.

### Adoption leave

- **Equivalent** to maternity leave for adoptive parents;
- **Specific** rules for inter-country adoption.

### Parental leave

- **Unpaid** parental leave (prescribed weeks per child);
- **Specific** notification requirements;
- **Available** for each parent up to child's threshold age.

### Time off for dependants

- **Unpaid** emergency time off;
- **Reasonable** time to make arrangements;
- **No specific cap** but reasonable.

### Flexible working

- **Right to request** flexible working after qualifying
  period;
- **Employer must consider** reasonably;
- **Reasons** for refusal must be valid statutory grounds;
- **Specific** procedural requirements.
