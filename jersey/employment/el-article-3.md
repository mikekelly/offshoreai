# EL 2003 Article 3 — Statement of Written Particulars

Article 3 requires every employer to provide a **written
statement of particulars** to each employee.

## The provision

Within **4 weeks** of the employment start date, the employer
must provide a **written statement** containing:

- Names of **employer and employee**;
- **Date employment began**;
- **Date continuous employment began** (if different);
- **Job title** or brief description of work;
- **Place of work** (or indication of mobility);
- **Pay rate** and **frequency**;
- **Hours of work**;
- **Holiday entitlement** and **holiday pay**;
- **Sick pay** terms;
- **Pension** information;
- **Notice periods** (each side);
- **Disciplinary** and **grievance** procedures;
- **Collective agreements** affecting the role;
- **Specific** terms for work outside Jersey lasting
  over a month.

## Modifying particulars

Changes to particulars require:

- **Written notification** within **4 weeks** of the change;
- **Specific** form for material changes;
- **Consent** in some configurations where contract terms
  change;
- **Coordination** with general contract-amendment law.

## Failure to provide

Failure to provide particulars:

- **Tribunal** can determine what the particulars **should be**;
- **No** automatic financial penalty for failure;
- **May affect** other claims (e.g., breach of contract);
- **Reputational** issue with the Tribunal in subsequent
  matters.

## Standalone vs combined

Practitioners often combine:

- **Written particulars** under Article 3;
- **Contract of employment** including all terms;
- **Specific policies** (handbook, code of conduct);
- **Pension** documentation;
- **Specific** sectoral collective agreements where relevant.

A single comprehensive **employment contract** typically
satisfies Article 3 requirements while providing fuller terms.

## Specific scenarios

### Senior executives

- **Detailed** contracts beyond Article 3 minimum;
- **Long-term** incentive arrangements;
- **Restrictive covenants** (post-termination restrictions);
- **Specific** confidentiality and IP provisions.

### Part-time / job-share

- **Same particulars** required;
- **Pro-rata** terms specified;
- **Equal treatment** with comparable full-time workers
  (subject to specific exceptions).

### Zero-hours / casual

- **Particulars still required**;
- **Hours of work** stated as "as agreed from time to time";
- **Specific** Jersey rules on casual / zero-hours workers
  (mirroring UK developments).

### Cross-border employment

- **Specific** terms for working abroad longer than a month;
- **Currency** of pay;
- **Specific** benefits and allowances;
- **Tax** treatment.
