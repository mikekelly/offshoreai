# EL 2003 Part 5 — Termination of Employment

Part 5 governs **how employment ends** — notice, wrongful
dismissal, summary dismissal, resignation.

## Articles 36-40 — Notice

### Statutory minimum notice

The EL prescribes **minimum statutory notice** periods:

- **Less than 1 month** service: nil notice;
- **1 month to 2 years**: 1 week;
- **2-5 years**: 2 weeks;
- **5-10 years**: 4 weeks;
- **10+ years**: 8 weeks.

Contracts may **enhance** these periods. **Cannot** reduce
below statutory minimum.

### Employee's notice

Employees must give:

- **1 week** notice after 1 month service (statutory minimum);
- **Contractual** notice may be longer;
- **Specific** notice for senior roles.

### Payment in lieu of notice (PILON)

- **Common** mechanism — employer pays salary for notice
  period in lieu of actual work;
- **Contractually authorised** typically;
- **Tax** treatment: PILON treated as employment income;
- **Restrictive covenants** survive notice (with or without
  PILON).

## Article 41 — Wrongful dismissal

**Wrongful dismissal** = breach of contract:

- Dismissal **without** proper notice;
- Dismissal **without** valid contractual reason;
- **Remedy**: damages for lost pay during notice period that
  was not given;
- **Common law** claim through Royal Court typically (though
  Tribunal jurisdiction in some configurations);
- **Distinct from** unfair dismissal (statutory claim).

## Article 42 — Summary dismissal

Summary dismissal = **without notice**:

- Permitted for **gross misconduct**;
- **Specific** examples: theft, violence, gross
  insubordination, dishonesty;
- **Fact-specific** — what counts as gross misconduct depends
  on circumstances;
- **Procedural fairness** required even for summary
  dismissal (Tribunal still scrutinises).

## Articles 43-48 — Statutory matters

### Constructive dismissal

Where employer's conduct entitles employee to resign:

- **Fundamental** breach of contract by employer;
- Employee **resigns** in response (within reasonable time);
- Treated as **dismissal** for EL purposes;
- **Unfair-dismissal** claim possible;
- **Examples**: pay reduction without consent, demotion,
  workplace bullying.

### References

- **No automatic** right to reference;
- **If given**, must be honest and not misleading;
- **Specific** professional-services rules (regulated
  industries — JFSC requires accurate references).

### Final pay

- All amounts due **paid promptly**;
- Including **accrued holiday pay**;
- **Bonus** and other amounts depending on contract;
- **PILON** if applicable;
- **Specific** items: pension, benefits run-off.

## Articles 49-50 — Specific scenarios

### Transfer of employment (TUPE-equivalent)

- **Specific** Jersey rules on transfer of employment on
  business sale / outsourcing;
- **Employee rights** preserved on transfer;
- **Information and consultation** obligations.

### Specific industry termination rules

- **Construction** industry has specific notice rules;
- **Domestic workers** have specific rules;
- **Certain** sectors with collective agreements.
