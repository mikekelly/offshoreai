# EL 2003 Article 79 — Right to Redundancy Pay

Article 79 establishes the **right to statutory redundancy
pay** for qualifying employees.

## Eligibility

An employee made redundant has the right to statutory
redundancy pay if:

- They have **at least 2 years** continuous service;
- They are within scope of the EL;
- They are **made redundant** (not dismissed for other
  reasons).

## Redundancy situation

A redundancy situation exists where:

- The **employer ceases business** (closure);
- The **employer ceases business at the place** where the
  employee was employed;
- The **requirements** for employees to do work of a
  particular kind have **ceased or diminished**.

The reason must be **genuine**:

- **Sham redundancies** to dismiss specific employees are
  challengeable;
- **Specific** Tribunal scrutiny of redundancy claims;
- **Documented** business reasons required.

## Calculation

Statutory redundancy pay is calculated:

- **Weekly pay** × **years of service** (capped at 20 years
  typically);
- **Age factor** (older employees get higher multiplier);
- **Weekly pay capped** at the statutory maximum (prescribed
  annually);
- **Specific** Jersey formula varies somewhat from UK.

## Specific Jersey formula

Example (subject to current statutory rates):

- **Under 22**: 0.5 weeks per year of service;
- **22-40**: 1 week per year;
- **41+**: 1.5 weeks per year;
- **Up to** maximum 20 years of service counted;
- **Weekly pay capped** at the statutory cap.

## When statutory pay only

Statutory redundancy pay is the **minimum**:

- **Contractual** enhancement common in larger employers;
- **Discretionary** additional payments often;
- **Specific** sectoral practices.

## Loss of redundancy entitlement

The employee may lose entitlement by:

- **Refusing** a suitable alternative offer of employment;
- **Specific** procedural failures;
- **Misconduct** during the redundancy process;
- **Specific** other circumstances.
