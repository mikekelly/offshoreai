# EL 2003 Part 1 — Preliminary

## Article 1 — Interpretation

Key definitions:

- **"Employee"** — individual working under a contract of
  employment (employer-employee relationship);
- **"Worker"** — wider class than employee, including those
  working under "other contracts to perform services"
  (excludes genuine self-employed, includes most agency
  workers);
- **"Employer"** — counterparty to the employee under the
  contract;
- **"Contract of employment"** — express or implied contract
  of service;
- **"Continuous employment"** — period of continuous service
  with the same employer (subject to specific rules);
- **"Effective date of termination"** — used for qualifying
  period and time-limit calculations;
- **"Tribunal"** — the Jersey Employment and Discrimination
  Tribunal;
- **"Week's pay"** — calculation method specified in the Law
  (used for redundancy and unfair-dismissal awards).

## Article 2 — Application

The EL applies to:

- **All Jersey-based employees** and workers;
- **Specific** statutory exclusions (e.g., specific Crown
  servants, sailors on certain routes);
- **Cross-border** employees subject to specific tests
  (where Jersey law is the governing law of the contract,
  where Jersey is the place of work, etc.).

## Employee vs worker vs self-employed

The distinction matters because:

| Status | Rights under EL |
|---|---|
| Employee | Full EL rights (unfair dismissal, redundancy, family leave, etc.) |
| Worker | Minimum wage, working time, family leave (some), but not unfair dismissal or redundancy |
| Self-employed | Generally outside EL; rely on contractual rights only |

The classification is a **factual** question — courts /
tribunals look beyond labels to the **reality** of the
relationship.
