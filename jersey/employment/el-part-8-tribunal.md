# EL 2003 Part 8 — Tribunal

Part 8 establishes the **Jersey Employment and Discrimination
Tribunal**.

## The Tribunal

Constituted by:

- **Chair** — qualified lawyer (advocate or solicitor);
- **Two lay members** — typically one with employer-side
  experience, one with employee-side / worker representative
  background;
- **Specific** disability / other expertise where relevant.

## Jurisdiction

The Tribunal hears:

- **Unfair dismissal** claims;
- **Redundancy pay** claims;
- **Unauthorised deductions from wages**;
- **Minimum wage** claims;
- **Family-leave** claims;
- **Discrimination** claims (under Discrimination Law);
- **Whistleblowing** detriment claims;
- **Specific** other statutory rights;
- **Working time** claims (some);
- **Equal pay** claims (under Discrimination Law).

The Tribunal does **not** hear:

- **Pure breach of contract** claims (Royal Court);
- **Tort** claims (Royal Court);
- **Specific** claims requiring criminal process.

## Procedure

### Filing

- **Claim form** filed with Tribunal;
- **Time limit** (8 weeks for unfair dismissal; various for
  other claims);
- **Fee** in some cases (or fee-free in most cases);
- **JACS** conciliation available pre-claim.

### Defence

- Employer files **response** within prescribed period;
- **Reply** to specific allegations;
- **Counter-claims** in some cases.

### Disclosure and preparation

- **Document disclosure** by both parties;
- **Witness statements** exchanged;
- **Bundles** prepared for hearing;
- **Specific** procedural directions from Tribunal.

### Hearing

- **Less formal** than Royal Court;
- **Hearing** in Jersey (specific Tribunal room);
- **Self-representation** common (though legal representation
  helpful);
- **Witnesses** give evidence on oath;
- **Cross-examination** by other side / Tribunal;
- **Submissions** at end.

### Decision

- **Reserved** typically, with written reasons;
- **Orders** for remedy if claim succeeds;
- **Specific** orders for costs in some cases.

## Costs

- **Default** is each party bears own costs;
- **Tribunal can award costs** for:
  - **Frivolous / vexatious** claims;
  - **Unreasonable conduct** of proceedings;
  - **Non-compliance** with orders;
- **Costs** generally modest compared with Royal Court
  litigation.

## Appeals

- **Appeal to Royal Court** on **point of law** only;
- **Specific** procedure;
- **Time limit**;
- **Further** appeal to Court of Appeal possible.

## JACS

The **Jersey Advisory and Conciliation Service** is the
**ACAS-equivalent** for Jersey:

- **Pre-claim** conciliation;
- **Tribunal-stage** conciliation;
- **Voluntary** but encouraged;
- **Settlement** through JACS-recorded agreements;
- **Effective alternative** to formal Tribunal proceedings.
