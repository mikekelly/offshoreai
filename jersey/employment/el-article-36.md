# EL 2003 Article 36 — Statutory Minimum Notice

Article 36 prescribes **statutory minimum notice** periods
for employment termination.

## Notice by employer

The employer must give:

- **Less than 1 month** service: **nil** statutory notice;
- **1 month to 2 years**: **1 week**;
- **2-5 years**: **2 weeks**;
- **5-10 years**: **4 weeks**;
- **10+ years**: **8 weeks**.

## Notice by employee

The employee must give:

- **1 week** notice after 1 month service (statutory
  minimum);
- **Contractual** notice may be longer.

## Contractual enhancement

Contracts may **enhance** these statutory minimums:

- **Higher senior managers** typically have longer notice
  (3-12 months);
- **Specific** sectoral practices;
- **Cannot reduce** below statutory minimum.

## Payment in lieu of notice (PILON)

- **Common** mechanism — employer pays salary for notice
  period in lieu of work;
- **Contractual** authority typically required;
- **Tax** treatment: PILON treated as employment income;
- **Restrictive covenants** survive notice (with or without
  PILON).

## Notice during sickness

- **Specific** Jersey rules for notice during periods of
  sickness;
- **Statutory sick pay** considerations;
- **Specific** procedural protections.

## Garden leave

- **During notice period**, employee may be placed on
  garden leave;
- **Specific** contractual authority typically;
- **Continuing** employment obligations during garden leave;
- **Specific** restrictive-covenant impact.

## Summary dismissal exception

For **gross misconduct**:

- **No notice** required;
- **See Article 42** — summary dismissal;
- **Specific** procedural fairness still required;
- **Specific** Tribunal scrutiny.
