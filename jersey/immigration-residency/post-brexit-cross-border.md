# Post-Brexit Cross-Border Workers and Residents

## Constitutional position

Jersey is part of the **Common Travel Area** with the UK,
Ireland, and the other Crown Dependencies:

- **No** internal immigration controls within the CTA;
- **UK immigration law** controls entry to the CTA;
- **Jersey** controls internal Jersey residency / employment
  separately (CHWL 2012).

## Pre-Brexit position

Before Brexit (pre-31 Jan 2020):

- **EU citizens** had freedom of movement under EU treaties;
- **Could** enter Jersey freely;
- **Subject to** CHWL for housing / employment within Jersey;
- **EU residents** of Jersey had specific protections.

## Post-Brexit position

Post-Brexit (after 31 Dec 2020):

- **EU citizens** are **non-CTA nationals** (with exceptions
  for Irish citizens);
- **UK immigration rules** apply for entry to CTA (including
  Jersey);
- **Visa** requirements for EU nationals depending on purpose
  and length;
- **Settled status** schemes for EU citizens who were
  resident before Brexit cut-off.

## EU Settlement Scheme

EU / EEA / Swiss citizens resident in Jersey before
**31 Dec 2020**:

- **Pre-settled** or **settled status** depending on length
  of residence;
- **Application** through specific Jersey process aligned
  with UK scheme;
- **Permanent** Jersey residency rights preserved;
- **Subject to** continued residence requirements.

## Frontier workers

Pre-Brexit, **frontier workers** (commuting from France) had
EU rights. Post-Brexit:

- **Specific** frontier worker permit scheme;
- **Limited** to those exercising rights before Brexit cut-
  off;
- **Coordinated** with UK / French frontier-worker rules;
- **Transitional** in nature.

## Visa categories for new EU arrivals

Post-Brexit, EU citizens require visas like other non-CTA
nationals:

- **Skilled worker** visa (sponsored by employer);
- **Innovator / global talent** visas;
- **Student** visas;
- **Specific** sectoral schemes;
- **HVR** route for high-value individuals.

## Specific Jersey arrangements

Some specific arrangements:

### Agriculture / horticulture

- **Seasonal worker** schemes;
- **Permits** for short-term agricultural workers;
- **Specific** post-Brexit arrangements with French / EU
  workers.

### Hospitality

- **Specific** post-Brexit shortage occupation acknowledgments;
- **Seasonal** licences for hospitality businesses;
- **Specific** routes for skilled hospitality workers.

### Healthcare

- **NHS-equivalent** Jersey health-service hires;
- **Specific** professional recognition arrangements;
- **Streamlined** processes for shortage-specialty doctors,
  nurses.

### Financial services

- **Skilled worker** routes for financial services;
- **JFSC** approvals separate but coordinated;
- **High demand** for specific skills (compliance, AML,
  technology).

## Jersey-specific challenges

Post-Brexit issues for Jersey:

### Workforce supply

- **Reduced** flow of EU workers historically reliant on for
  hospitality, agriculture, construction;
- **Higher costs** for businesses;
- **Wage pressure** in shortage sectors;
- **Specific** policy response through CHWL and immigration.

### Sectoral resilience

- **Healthcare** workforce;
- **Education** workforce;
- **Tourism** workforce;
- **Specific** Jersey responses including local training.

### Property market

- **Reduced** demand from EU buyers in some segments;
- **HVR** market continues to attract international
  high-net-worth;
- **Specific** market segmentation post-Brexit.
