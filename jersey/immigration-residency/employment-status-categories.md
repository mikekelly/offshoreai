# Employment Status Categories — Jersey

The Control of Housing and Work (Jersey) Law 2012 applies the
**same four-status framework** to employment as to housing —
determining who an employer may engage and in what roles.

## The categories applied to work

### Entitled

- Can work **any job**;
- Most established and flexible status from the employer's
  perspective.

### Licensed

- Can work **for the specific licensed employer** in the
  licensed role;
- The licence is **employer-and-role specific** — moving to
  another employer or role typically requires a new licence;
- Reflects the **bringing-in-skills** policy.

### Entitled for Work

- Can work **any job** like Entitled — the housing distinction
  matters more than the work distinction at this status;
- Common path for spouses of Entitled persons.

### Registered

- Can work in jobs **registered** to that role under prescribed
  rules;
- More restricted than Entitled or Entitled for Work;
- Typical for shorter-term and lower-priority positions in the
  Jersey economy.

## Employer obligations

Employers must:

- **Verify status** before employing;
- **Apply for Licensed status** for new hires not otherwise
  qualified;
- **Report** changes in employment status to the Population
  Office;
- **Comply** with the prescribed status-specific rules.

Failure to comply is an offence.

## Inter-employer mobility

Within Jersey, status-specific employment rules govern movement:

- **Entitled / Entitled for Work** persons can move freely
  between roles;
- **Licensed** persons need a new licence to change employer /
  role;
- **Registered** persons can change roles within the registered
  framework.
