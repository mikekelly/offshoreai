# AML/CFT — Jersey

Jersey's anti-money-laundering and counter-financing-of-terrorism
regime sits on three pillars:

1. The **[Proceeds of Crime (Jersey) Law 1999][pocl]** — the
   underlying criminal offences and the basis on which "supervised
   business" is brought within scope.
2. The **Money Laundering (Jersey) Order 2008** — the operative
   compliance obligations (customer-due-diligence, record-keeping,
   reporting, training, internal controls).
3. The **JFSC AML/CFT Handbook** — the regulator's expectations of
   how those obligations are met, treated as evidence of regulatory
   standard.

Sanctions are administered through the **Sanctions and Asset-Freezing
(Jersey) Law 2014** and related orders, alongside automatic
co-ordination with UK sanctions regimes.

Jersey is evaluated periodically by **Moneyval**, the Council of
Europe's AML/CFT mutual-evaluation body.
