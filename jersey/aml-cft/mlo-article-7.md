# MLO 2008 Article 7 — Identification and Verification

Article 7 sets out **what** identification and verification
must achieve.

## Identification — the question of "who"

Identification means gathering information sufficient to
**know who the customer is**:

For an **individual**:

- **Full legal name**;
- **Date of birth**;
- **Residential address**;
- **Nationality**;
- **Other identifiers** (employer, occupation as risk
  indicator).

For an **entity** customer:

- **Legal name** and any trading names;
- **Legal form** (company, partnership, foundation, trust);
- **Jurisdiction** of registration / formation;
- **Registered office address**;
- **Registration number**;
- **Directors / officers**;
- **Beneficial owners** (see Article 8).

## Verification — the question of "how do we know"

Verification means **confirming** the identification information
using **reliable independent sources**:

### For individuals

- **Government-issued ID** with photograph (passport,
  driving licence, national identity card);
- **Certified copy** if not original — certification by a
  professional in a recognised category (advocate / solicitor,
  notary, embassy, regulated financial institution);
- **Proof of address** — recent (typically within 3 months)
  utility bill, bank statement, tax notice;
- **Electronic verification** — regulated systems checking
  against credit reference agency data, government registers,
  and other independent sources;
- **Sanctions and PEP** screening at the same time.

### For entities

- **Official register** extracts (Companies Registry, JFSC
  registers, foreign equivalents);
- **Articles of association** or equivalent;
- **Certified copies** of constitutional documents;
- **Resolutions** authorising the relationship;
- **Powers of attorney** if applicable.

## Non-face-to-face verification

Where the customer is not met in person:

- **Enhanced verification** typically required;
- **Multiple sources** of evidence;
- **Electronic verification** combined with documentary
  evidence;
- **Senior management** approval often required for higher
  risk;
- **First-transaction monitoring** is heightened.

## Reasonable extent for beneficial owners

For beneficial owners (Article 8), verification is to a
"**reasonable extent**":

- The standard is **proportionate** to the role of the BO and
  the customer risk;
- For **transparent** structures (publicly listed company at
  top), reduced verification may suffice;
- For **opaque** structures, full verification of BOs is
  expected;
- For **PEPs** as BOs, full verification plus EDD.

## Failure of verification

If verification cannot be completed:

- **Do not proceed** with the relationship or transaction;
- **Apply** Article 5 termination / refusal rules;
- **Consider** SAR (failure itself may indicate concerns);
- **Document** the decision.
