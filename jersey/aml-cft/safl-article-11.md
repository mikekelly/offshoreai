# SAFL 2014 Article 11 — Making Funds / Economic Resources Available

Article 11 prohibits **making funds or economic resources
available** to (or for the benefit of) designated persons.

## The provision

A person must **not** make funds or economic resources:

- **Available**, directly or indirectly;
- To **a designated person**, or
- For the **benefit** of a designated person;
- **Unless** authorised by licence.

The prohibition is **complementary** to Article 10 — Article
10 prevents **dealings with** existing assets of designated
persons; Article 11 prevents **new assets** flowing to
designated persons.

## "Making available"

This is broader than just **paying money**:

### Direct provision

- **Paying** the designated person;
- **Transferring** assets to them;
- **Lending** to them;
- **Providing services** to them;
- **Selling** goods to them.

### Indirect provision

- **Paying** an entity controlled by the designated person;
- **Paying** a family member where benefit flows to the
  designated person;
- **Routing** funds through intermediaries to the designated
  person;
- **Providing services** that the designated person consumes
  even where contractually contracted with someone else.

### "For the benefit of"

- **Funds applied** to a designated person's debts;
- **Services** consumed by the designated person;
- **Goods delivered** to the designated person's premises or
  for use;
- **Maintenance** of property the designated person owns or
  uses.

## "Funds or economic resources"

Same broad definitions as Article 10 — covering virtually any
financial or non-financial asset or service.

## Practical applications

### Bank account operations

- **No incoming transfers** to designated-person accounts;
- **No outgoing transfers** allowed if they benefit
  designated person;
- **Interest** crediting may need licensing;
- **Account closure** procedures with proper handling.

### Service providers

- **TCBs** cannot accept designated persons as clients;
- **Existing relationships** must be reviewed and either
  exited or licensed;
- **Trust distributions** to designated beneficiaries
  prohibited;
- **Foundation** distributions to designated beneficiaries
  prohibited.

### Family / third-party scenarios

- **Spouse** of designated person — payments to spouse may
  benefit designated person if shared household;
- **Children** of designated person — payments may benefit
  designated person;
- **Charities** funded by designated person — receipts may
  be restricted;
- **Specific** humanitarian and basic-needs licences address
  these scenarios.

### Cross-border concerns

- **Payments to designated persons abroad** through Jersey
  systems prohibited;
- **Investments** by Jersey persons in designated-person
  businesses prohibited;
- **Trade finance** subject to scrutiny if involving
  designated persons / regimes.

## Defences

The recipient may defend on grounds:

- **No knowledge** that the recipient was designated (subject
  to constructive knowledge — would a reasonable person have
  known?);
- **Authorisation** by licence;
- **Specific** exception applies.

The **knowledge / constructive knowledge** test is **strict**
— professional firms are expected to have robust screening,
and ignorance is rarely a successful defence.
