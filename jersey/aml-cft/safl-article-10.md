# SAFL 2014 Article 10 — Asset-Freezing Prohibition

Article 10 is the **principal substantive prohibition** of the
SAFL — preventing dealings with the funds and economic
resources of designated persons.

## The provision

A person must **not deal** with funds or economic resources
that are:

- **Owned, held, or controlled** by a designated person;
- **Where** the dealing would benefit the designated person;
- **Unless** authorised by licence (Article 15) or specific
  exception.

## What "dealing" covers

The prohibition is **broad**:

- **Transferring** funds / resources;
- **Receiving** funds / resources for a designated person;
- **Releasing** funds held for / on behalf of a designated
  person;
- **Allowing** dealings (banks allowing transactions on
  designated-person accounts);
- **Using** designated-person funds / resources;
- **Selling**, **leasing**, or **otherwise disposing of**
  resources;
- **Transferring** ownership or control.

## "Funds"

**Funds** include:

- **Cash** and bank balances;
- **Cheques** and money orders;
- **Securities** (stocks, bonds, derivatives);
- **Loans** and receivables;
- **Dividends** and interest;
- **Letters of credit**;
- **Documents** showing entitlement to financial assets;
- **Any** financial assets.

## "Economic resources"

**Economic resources** include:

- **Tangible assets** (real estate, vehicles, machinery);
- **Intangible assets** (intellectual property, contracts);
- **Inventory** and **stock**;
- **Anything** that could be **converted to funds, goods, or
  services**;
- **Including** assets that don't appear to be financial
  but could be sold.

## Who is bound

The prohibition applies to:

- **Any person** in Jersey;
- **Jersey nationals** anywhere;
- **Jersey legal entities** anywhere;
- **Any business** operating in or from Jersey;
- **Foreign branches / subsidiaries** of Jersey businesses.

## Practical implications

Jersey businesses must:

- **Screen** customers, BOs, counterparties against the
  consolidated sanctions list;
- **Identify** potential matches (true matches vs false
  positives);
- **Freeze** assets immediately on confirmed match;
- **Notify** JFSC and relevant authorities;
- **Refuse** new transactions involving designated persons;
- **Refer** to licensing (Article 15) for permitted exceptions.

## Beneficial ownership

A designated person's interest can arise through:

- **Direct** ownership;
- **Indirect** ownership (through corporate / trust
  structures);
- **Control** even without ownership (e.g., shadow director,
  controlling person);
- **Beneficial** interest in trusts / foundations;
- **Nominee** arrangements concealing the BO interest.

Sanctions screening must look **through structures** to
identify designated-person interests — this is where
beneficial ownership investigation intersects with
sanctions compliance.

## Specific concerns

### Trust beneficiary as designated person

- The **trust assets** may need to be **frozen** to the
  extent of the designated person's interest;
- **Other beneficiaries' interests** may be unaffected;
- **Trustee** must seek **licence** for distributions to
  others if needed;
- **Court application** may be required for complex
  scenarios.

### Director as designated person

- The **company** may not be designated;
- The director's **personal interests** (shares, salary,
  dividends) are frozen;
- The **company's normal business** may continue subject
  to specific safeguards;
- **Removal** of the designated director typically required.
