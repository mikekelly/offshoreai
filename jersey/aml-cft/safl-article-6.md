# SAFL 2014 Article 6 — EU Listings (Legacy / Discretionary)

Article 6 addresses **EU sanctions listings** — historically
applicable through UK incorporation, post-Brexit
discretionary.

## Pre-Brexit position

Before Brexit (UK leaving EU):

- **EU sanctions** had **direct effect** in the UK;
- **UK extensions** brought EU sanctions to Jersey;
- **All EU-listed persons** were Jersey-designated;
- **Automatic** propagation.

## Post-Brexit position

After Brexit (31 January 2020 / end of transition):

- **EU sanctions** are **not automatically applicable** to
  Jersey (post-Brexit constitutional position);
- Jersey **may** give effect to EU listings through Jersey
  Orders (discretionary);
- **In practice**, substantial overlap between EU and UK
  sanctions reduces gap;
- Where **divergence** exists (rarely), Jersey typically
  follows the **UK line**.

## EU-only listings

For persons listed by EU but not UK:

- **Not automatically** Jersey-designated;
- **Subject to** specific Jersey decision (Minister
  designation Article 3);
- **Specific** consideration of EU-Jersey relationship;
- **Cooperation** considerations.

## Practical implication

For Jersey businesses:

- **Screen** against Jersey consolidated list (which
  generally includes UK / UN);
- **Be aware** of EU-only listings for due-diligence
  purposes;
- **Specific** EU-tax / regulatory compliance for EU
  customers;
- **Coordination** with EU counterparties / counsel for
  cross-border matters.
