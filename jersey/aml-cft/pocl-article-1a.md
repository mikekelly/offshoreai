# POCL 1999 Article 1A — Criminal Conduct

Article 1A is the **all-crimes anchor** of the Jersey AML
regime — the provision that ties the substantive money-
laundering offences and confiscation regime to a wide range
of predicate offences.

## The provision

"Criminal conduct" means:

- **Conduct that constitutes an offence in Jersey**; or
- **Conduct that would constitute an offence in Jersey if it
  occurred in Jersey** (the "dual criminality" / overseas
  conduct branch).

This **all-crimes** scope distinguishes Jersey's regime from
older proceeds-of-crime regimes that limited predicate offences
to specific (typically drug-related) categories.

## What this means in practice

For Jersey MLRO / counsel purposes, any of the following can be
a predicate offence:

- **Drug trafficking** offences (the original POCL predicate
  category);
- **Tax evasion** — including foreign tax evasion (a critical
  feature for offshore practice);
- **Fraud and dishonesty offences**;
- **Bribery and corruption**;
- **Theft**;
- **Sanctions breach**;
- **Insider dealing / market abuse**;
- **Cybercrime** and computer-misuse offences;
- **Migration-related offences** (people-smuggling, trafficking);
- **Counterfeiting**;
- **Environmental crimes**;
- **Any other indictable offence**.

## Foreign tax evasion

Foreign tax evasion as a POCL predicate has been a major theme
since the 2010s:

- **Common Reporting Standard** (CRS) information exchanges
  reveal previously hidden tax-evasion patterns;
- Jersey's **financial institutions** routinely identify
  potential foreign-tax-evasion in legacy structures;
- The **dual criminality** test asks whether the conduct
  **would be an offence in Jersey if done in Jersey** — Jersey
  has its own tax-evasion offences, so foreign tax evasion
  generally satisfies the test;
- This drives a significant proportion of Jersey SARs.

## Conduct overseas

The Law captures **conduct overseas**:

- The conduct **does not have to occur in Jersey** for the
  proceeds to be "criminal property";
- The defendant **does not have to be in Jersey** when the
  conduct occurs (though Jersey jurisdiction requires Jersey
  nexus);
- The dual-criminality test prevents capturing **conduct
  legal in Jersey** (e.g., political dissent in some
  jurisdictions where it constitutes an offence locally but
  not in Jersey).

## Limits

Despite the broad scope:

- **Civil wrongs** that are not also criminal are **not**
  predicate (e.g., breach of contract);
- **Foreign-only offences** that have **no Jersey analogue**
  do not satisfy dual criminality;
- **Disproportionate** prosecution is constrained by
  prosecutorial discretion and Article 6 ECHR.
