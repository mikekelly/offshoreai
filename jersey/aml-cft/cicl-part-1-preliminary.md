# CICL 2001 Part 1 — Preliminary

## Article 1 — Interpretation

Key definitions:

- **"Foreign authority"** — a judicial or prosecuting authority
  of a state other than Jersey, requesting cooperation;
- **"Letter of request"** — a formal request from a foreign
  authority for legal assistance;
- **"Designated country"** — a country listed under regulations
  as eligible for MLA;
- **"Restraint order"** — order preventing dealings with
  property pending criminal proceedings;
- **"Confiscation order"** — order requiring payment / transfer
  of property representing proceeds of crime;
- **"Mutual legal assistance"** — formal cooperation in
  criminal matters between sovereign jurisdictions;
- **"Attorney General"** — Jersey's principal prosecuting
  authority and the **central authority** for MLA purposes.

## Article 2 — Application

The Law applies:

- For **requests received** by Jersey from foreign authorities;
- For **requests made** by Jersey to foreign authorities;
- For **enforcement** of foreign restraint and confiscation
  orders in Jersey;
- For **specific** offences related to foreign criminal
  conduct.

Jersey acts under CICL through:

- The **Attorney General** as the principal MLA channel;
- The **Royal Court** for orders requiring judicial backing;
- The **Viscount** for asset realisation;
- The **JFSC** for regulated-entity cooperation.

## The MLA landscape

CICL operates against the backdrop of:

- **UK MLA framework** — Jersey leverages UK treaty relationships;
- **Bilateral MLA treaties** — Jersey has direct treaty
  relationships with specific countries;
- **EU MLA Convention 2000** — limited post-Brexit
  applicability;
- **UN Convention against Transnational Organized Crime
  (UNTOC)**;
- **UN Convention against Corruption (UNCAC)**;
- **Specific** bilateral / multilateral arrangements.
