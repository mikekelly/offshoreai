# SAFL 2014 Part 2 — Designations (residual)

Articles 3, 4, and 5 (Minister, UN, UK designations) have
their own deep files. This cluster covers Articles 6-9.

## Article 6 — EU listings (legacy / discretionary)

Pre-Brexit, EU sanctions had direct effect in Jersey
(through UK incorporation):

- **Now**: EU sanctions are **not** automatically applicable
  to Jersey (post-Brexit constitutional position);
- Jersey **may** give effect to EU listings through Jersey
  Orders (discretionary);
- In practice, **substantial overlap** between EU and UK
  sanctions reduces the gap;
- Where divergence exists (rarely), Jersey follows the **UK
  line** typically.

## Article 7 — Specific Jersey designations

Jersey may make **specific designations**:

- **Independent** of UN / UK / EU lists;
- **For** Jersey-specific concerns;
- **Subject to** the Minister's autonomous designation power
  (Article 3);
- **Rare in practice**.

## Article 8 — Review of designations

Every designation is subject to **review**:

- **Periodic** review (typically annually);
- **Application** by designated person at any time;
- **Court appeal** on grounds of legality, proportionality;
- **Lifting** if grounds no longer apply;
- **JFSC notification** of designation changes.

## Article 9 — Notification

The Minister / JFSC must:

- **Publish** designations on the Jersey sanctions list;
- **Update** the list as designations change;
- **Notify** regulated businesses through industry channels;
- **Provide** guidance on implementation;
- **Maintain** records of designations and changes.

The Jersey **consolidated sanctions list** is published by
the JFSC and is the authoritative reference for Jersey-
applicable sanctions.
