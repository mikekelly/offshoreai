# MLO 2008 Article 17 — Record Keeping

Article 17 specifies the **records** that a supervised
business must keep and the **retention periods**.

## Records required

The supervised business must keep:

### CDD records

- **Identification documents** (passport copies, certifications);
- **Verification evidence** (utility bills, register extracts);
- **Beneficial-ownership** analysis and documentation;
- **Source-of-funds / wealth** evidence;
- **Risk-rating** decisions;
- **EDD / SDD** rationale.

### Transaction records

- **All transactions** processed for the customer;
- **Source** and **destination** of funds;
- **Amount** and **currency**;
- **Date**;
- **Parties** involved;
- **Purpose**.

### Internal disclosures and SARs

- **Internal disclosure** forms / records;
- **MLRO assessments**;
- **External SARs** filed;
- **FIU communications**;
- **Consent decisions**.

### Risk assessments

- **BWRA** documents (current and historical);
- **Customer risk ratings** (current and historical);
- **Periodic refresh** documentation.

### Training records

- **Training programmes** delivered;
- **Attendance** records;
- **Test / assessment** results;
- **Refresher** schedules.

### Policies and procedures

- **Current** policies and procedures;
- **Historical** versions;
- **Approval** records by board / senior management.

## Retention period

The general retention period is **at least 5 years**:

- **CDD records** — 5 years from the end of the business
  relationship;
- **Transaction records** — 5 years from the date of the
  transaction;
- **Internal disclosures and SARs** — 5 years from filing;
- **Risk assessments** — 5 years from supersession.

In practice, many firms retain **longer** (10+ years):

- For **litigation** preservation;
- For **regulatory** scrutiny (JFSC can ask for records
  several years old);
- For **operational** continuity;
- For **historical** analysis of patterns.

The **Comptroller of Revenue** and **Royal Court** may also
require records for tax / litigation purposes — these
obligations may extend retention.

## Format and accessibility

Records must be:

- **Accessible** within reasonable time on JFSC / FIU
  request;
- **Legible** and **retrievable**;
- **Original or certified copies** as appropriate;
- **Secure** (data protection, confidentiality);
- **Backed up** to protect against loss.

**Electronic records** are acceptable provided integrity is
preserved and they can be reliably reproduced.

## Disposal

After the retention period:

- **Records can be destroyed**;
- **Destruction** should be documented;
- **Confidential destruction** methods used (shredding,
  certified data deletion);
- **Special categories** (e.g., subject of investigation) may
  require longer retention notwithstanding the general period.
