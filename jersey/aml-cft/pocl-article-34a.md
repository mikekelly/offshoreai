# POCL 1999 Article 34A — Failure to Disclose (Nominated Officers)

Article 34A creates a **parallel failure-to-disclose offence**
for **nominated officers** (MLROs) within the regulated
sector.

## The provision

A nominated officer commits an offence if:

- They receive an **internal disclosure** in their capacity as
  nominated officer;
- They **know or suspect**, or have **reasonable grounds for
  suspecting**, that another person is engaged in money
  laundering;
- They do **not** make an **external disclosure** to the FIU
  as soon as practicable;
- They are not protected by an exception.

## Decision to make external disclosure

The MLRO must, on receipt of an internal disclosure:

- **Assess** the information against ML / TF indicators;
- **Form a view** on whether the threshold (knows / suspects /
  reasonable grounds) is met;
- **Document** the assessment;
- If threshold met: **file external SAR** with the FIU;
- If threshold not met: **document the reasons** and retain
  the internal disclosure file for the retention period.

The MLRO **cannot** simply "sit on" an internal disclosure
that meets the threshold.

## "As soon as practicable"

The standard:

- Allows for **reasonable assessment time**;
- But does not permit **strategic delay**;
- Typically external SAR is filed within **24-72 hours** of
  the MLRO's determination;
- Significant delay risks Article 34A liability.

## Quality of the SAR

While Article 34A is about *making* the disclosure, MLROs
must ensure SAR quality:

- **Detail sufficient** for the FIU to assess and act;
- **Identification** of all relevant parties;
- **Documents** referenced or attached;
- **Suspicion basis** clearly articulated;
- **Consent request** if needed.

A poor-quality SAR may be **insufficient** to discharge the
disclosure obligation.

## Penalty

Same as Article 34: up to **5 years' imprisonment** and
unlimited fine on indictment.

## Practical regime

For Jersey TCBs and banks:

- The **nominated officer** (MLRO) is named in the firm's AML
  procedures and notified to the JFSC;
- The MLRO is **personally accountable** for Article 34A
  compliance;
- The firm should have **deputy MLRO** arrangements for
  absence / conflict;
- **Regular training** for the MLRO on threshold assessment;
- **Records of decision** maintained meticulously.
