# POCL 1999 Articles 4-7 — Assessment of Benefit and Recoverable Amount

Articles 4-7 set out the **assessment** process for
confiscation orders.

## Article 4 — Benefit assessment

The Court must determine:

- The **benefit** the defendant obtained from the offence;
- And from any **course of criminal conduct**;
- **Aggregated** across multiple incidents;
- **Calculated** per Article 3 (gross of expenses).

## Article 5 — Criminal lifestyle assumptions

Where the defendant has a **criminal lifestyle**:

- The Court **may make assumptions** (Article 6);
- **Statutory criteria** for criminal lifestyle (specific
  indictable offences, repeated offences, etc.).

## Article 6 — Assumptions

The assumptions are:

### Property acquired post-conviction-lookback

- **All property** acquired by defendant in the **six-year
  lookback** from the start of proceedings is treated as
  benefit;
- **Defendant** must rebut with evidence of legitimate
  source.

### Expenditure in same period

- **All expenditure** in the lookback period treated as
  funded by criminal conduct;
- **Subject to** rebuttal.

### Transfers

- **Specific** rules for property transfers in the lookback.

### Rebuttable

- Defendant can **lead evidence** showing legitimate source;
- **Specific** documentary requirements;
- **Court** determines on balance of probabilities.

## Article 7 — Recoverable amount

The **recoverable amount** is:

- **Lesser of**:
  - The **benefit** (Article 4-6);
  - The **available amount** (defendant's assets practicably
    realisable).

The Court orders payment of the **recoverable amount**.

## Available amount

This includes:

- **Realisable property** (Article 2) — defendant's assets and
  caught gifts;
- **Less** specific senior claims (mortgages, prior secured
  creditors);
- **Specific** practical realisability tests.

## Defendant's challenge

The defendant can:

- **Challenge** the criminal-lifestyle finding;
- **Rebut** specific assumptions;
- **Argue** specific property was lawful source;
- **Challenge** available-amount calculation;
- **Subject to** balance-of-probabilities standard.
