# POCL 1999 Part 6 — Supervised Business

Part 6 sets up the **supervised-business** regime — Schedule 2
businesses subject to specific AML / CFT compliance, registration,
and supervision.

## Article 37 — Supervised business

A business is **supervised business** if it falls within
Schedule 2:

- **Financial-services businesses** regulated by the JFSC
  under the Financial Services (Jersey) Law 1998 (TCB, FSB,
  Investment Business, GIMB, MSB);
- **Banking businesses** regulated under the Banking Business
  (Jersey) Law 1991;
- **Insurance businesses** regulated under the Insurance
  Business (Jersey) Law 1996;
- **Fund services businesses** under the CIFL 1988;
- **Lawyers** in specified activities;
- **Accountants** in specified activities;
- **Estate agents**, **high-value dealers**, **casinos**,
  **VASPs**.

Supervised businesses are **subject to**:

- **AML / CFT compliance** obligations under the **Money
  Laundering (Jersey) Order 2008**;
- **Supervision** by the relevant supervisory body
  (typically the JFSC);
- **Registration** with the JFSC as supervised business;
- **Reporting** of breaches and SARs.

## Article 38 — Register of supervised businesses

The **Registrar** (typically JFSC for financial-services
sectors) maintains a register of:

- **Supervised business** names;
- **Permitted activities** within Schedule 2;
- **Designated** MLRO and Compliance Officer;
- **Status** (active, suspended, struck off).

## Article 39 — Application and registration

A person carrying on supervised business **must register**:

- **Before** commencing supervised business;
- **By application** to the JFSC (or relevant supervisor);
- With **fit and proper** assessment of principals;
- With **payment** of prescribed fees;
- Subject to **continuing obligations** (annual returns,
  ongoing compliance).

For TCBs, FSBs, and other JFSC-regulated entities, AML
registration is **integrated** with FSL Article 9 / 10
authorisation.

## Articles 40-41 — Supervision and enforcement

The JFSC (and other supervisors):

- **Conducts on-site visits** and off-site supervision;
- **Issues** the **AML / CFT Handbook** with practical guidance;
- **Investigates** failures and breaches;
- **Imposes** civil penalties for compliance failures;
- **Refers** criminal matters to the Attorney General;
- **Coordinates** with the FIU and law enforcement.

Civil penalties for AML breaches can be **substantial** —
JFSC fines of £100k+ are not uncommon for material failures.
