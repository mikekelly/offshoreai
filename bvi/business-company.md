# BVI Business Company — The Standard Offshore Vehicle

## What a BC is

The **BVI Business Company (BC)** is the **principal vehicle**
for international business in the BVI:

- **Established** under the **BVI Business Companies Act
  2004**;
- **Replaced** the older International Business Company (IBC)
  regime;
- **400,000+** active BCs;
- **Standard** vehicle in international tax planning;
- **Familiar** to international counterparties globally.

## Key features

### Speed of incorporation

- **Hours** to incorporate;
- **Shelf companies** also available;
- **Online** processes through registered agents;
- **Standard** form documentation.

### Cost efficiency

- **Low** registration fees;
- **Low** annual fees;
- **Specific** Pillar Two and substance considerations may
  add cost for in-scope groups.

### Flexible capital

- **No minimum** capital requirements;
- **Par-value** or **no-par-value** shares;
- **Various** share classes permitted;
- **Specific** Cayman-style flexibility.

### Minimal local requirements

- **Registered agent** in BVI (mandatory);
- **Registered office** in BVI;
- **Beneficial ownership** information held by registered agent;
- **No** requirement for BVI directors or shareholders;
- **No** requirement for BVI residence of directors.

### Directors

- **At least one** director;
- **No** residence requirement;
- **Corporate directors** permitted;
- **Standard** fiduciary duties;
- **Specific** BC Act provisions on duties.

### Constitutional documents

- **Memorandum and Articles** of Association;
- **Standard** templates;
- **Flexible** to customise.

### Shareholders

- **Single** shareholder permitted;
- **No** residence requirement;
- **Corporate** shareholders permitted;
- **Specific** beneficial-ownership obligations through
  agent.

## Uses of BCs

### Holding company

The **classic** use:

- **Holding** investments;
- **Bilateral / multilateral** tax treaty considerations
  (limited — BVI has limited treaty network);
- **Hong Kong / Asia** investors particularly common;
- **Specific** structuring considerations.

### Special-purpose vehicle (SPV)

- **Acquisition** vehicle in M&A;
- **Specific** project financing;
- **Bond / note** issuance vehicle;
- **Structured finance** vehicle.

### Trading company

- **International** trading;
- **Specific** sectors (e.g., shipping, commodities).

### Joint venture

- **JV vehicle** for international joint ventures;
- **Specific** customisation through articles.

### Trust holding

- **Underlying company** held by a trust (VISTA or
  otherwise);
- **Common** in offshore wealth structures.

## Comparison with Jersey companies

| Feature | Jersey CJL 1991 | BVI BC Act 2004 |
|---|---|---|
| Speed to incorporate | Days to weeks | **Hours** |
| Cost | Higher | **Lower** |
| Reporting | Detailed annual return | Lighter |
| Public access | Significant register | Less public (registered-agent model) |
| Substance | More on-the-ground for in-scope | Less typically |
| Specialty | Sophisticated commercial structures | Volume / standard structures |

## Beneficial ownership

### Pre-transparency BVI

- Historic **strong privacy** with no public BO register.

### Modern BVI

- **Beneficial Ownership Secure Search System (BOSS)**;
- **Beneficial owners** identified to registered agent;
- **Authorities have access** through specific routes;
- **No public access** currently (subject to international
  pressure for change).

### Comparison

BVI BO regime is **similar to Jersey** in approach (authority
access, limited public access).

## Annual obligations

A BC must:

- **Pay annual fees** to BVI government;
- **Maintain registered agent** and registered office;
- **Maintain BO information** with the agent;
- **Specific** substance reporting if in-scope under
  Economic Substance Act;
- **No** typically required annual filing of accounts.

## Wind-up / dissolution

- **Voluntary** liquidation through standard process;
- **Strike-off** for non-compliance (e.g., unpaid fees);
- **Restoration** possible within specific period.

## Substance considerations

The **Economic Substance Act 2018** applies to:

- **In-scope** activities (similar to Jersey list);
- **Substance** requirements (directed, qualified employees,
  premises);
- **Annual** substance return;
- **Penalties** for non-compliance.

For **pure equity holding** companies, **reduced** substance
required.
